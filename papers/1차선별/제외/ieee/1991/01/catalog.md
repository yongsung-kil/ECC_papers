## Producing soft-decision information at the output of a class IV partial response Viterbi detector

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Soft-decision (SOVA) output from PRML detector; channel detection/non-LDPC, no transferable binary LDPC decoder technique.
- **ID**: ieee:162473
- **Type**: conference
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: K. J. Knudson, J. K. Wolf, L. B. Milstein
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/162473
- **Abstract**: A method is described for producing soft-decision information at the output of a Class-IV partial response maximum-likelihood (PRML) detector, when the additive noise samples are independent, Gaussian random variables. This method directly applies the soft-decision Viterbi algorithm, presented by J. Hagenauer and P. Hoeher (1989), to the Class-IV partial response channel. The PRML detector is augmented to produce a reliability value for each binary symbol in the maximum-likelihood (ML) sequence estimate of the binary input. The reliability value is a function of the estimated probability of error for the binary symbol. The soft-decisions are used to enhance the performance of an outer error correction code.<>

## Map representations and coding-based priors for segmentation

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Bayesian image segmentation with coding-based priors (MDL); not channel coding, no LDPC ECC relevance.
- **ID**: ieee:139727
- **Type**: conference
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: K. Keeler
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/139727
- **Abstract**: The Bayesian segmentation model developed is motivated by consideration of the information needed for higher-level visual processing. A segmentation is regarded as a collection of parameters defining an image-valued stochastic process by separating topological (adjacency) and metric (shape) properties of the subdivision and intensity properties of each region. The prior selection is structured accordingly. The novel part of the representation, the subdivision topology, is assigned a prior by universal coding arguments, using the minimum description-length philosophy that the best segmentation allows the most efficient representation of visual data.<>

## Block coded modulation: an application to frequency/phase modulation and a procedure for the construction of trellis diagrams

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Block coded modulation with trellis construction; non-LDPC coded modulation, no transferable binary LDPC technique.
- **ID**: ieee:160686
- **Type**: conference
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: E. Paravalos, S. Fleisher
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/160686
- **Abstract**: Block coded modulation (BCM) is a bandwidth efficient scheme that results from the combination of block coding and modulation. The concept of BCM is applied to frequency/phase modulation (FPM) schemes, and the coding gain over uncoded M-PSK, BCM M-PSK, and TCM FPM systems is demonstrated. It is found that block code configurations of low complexity yield coding gains on the order of 3 dB over uncoded modulation while higher complexity codes further improve the performance. A procedure is described for the construction of BCM trellis diagrams, and examples that demonstrate the applicability of the procedure are included.<>

## Multistage decoding using erasing technique for multilevel coding

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Multistage decoding with erasing for BCM/multilevel coding; non-LDPC coded modulation, no transferable binary LDPC technique.
- **ID**: ieee:188489
- **Type**: conference
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: K. Fazel, A. Chouly
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/188489
- **Abstract**: Families of BCM (block-coded modulation) that allow the use of suboptimal multistage decoding procedures having performance/complexity advantages over MLD (maximum likelihood decoding) are proposed. The process of decoding is performed in two steps: a first estimate of the codeword is obtained by a hard decision device, and the associated decoding is carried out to correct the eventual errors. The criterion of erasing, i.e. how to define the erasing procedure in the first step of decoding in order to maximize the coding gain, is discussed for both PSK and QAM modulations. The erasing technique is performed by defining some 'erasing-areas'. The analytic results show that significant coding gain can be achieved with respect to a nonerased equivalent scheme.<>

## On maximum likelihood soft decoding of some binary self dual codes

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: 바이너리 self-dual(Golay 등) 대수부호 전용 ML 소프트 디코딩, LDPC BP로 이식 불가
- **ID**: ieee:217739
- **Type**: conference
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: M. Ran, J. Snyders
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/217739
- **Abstract**: The authors present and compare the performance of maximum likelihood (ML) soft decoding algorithms for binary linear self-dual (SD) codes. The codes considered have a minimum distance of 4, but due to their different weight distributions they differ in their coding gain. Remarkably, there is a code, denoted F/sub 24/, which has a decoder nearly 4 times faster than the fastest published decoder (6) for the (24,12,8) Golay code. Nontheless, these two codes have almost equal coding gains. Simulation results are given to compare between the codes. An analytical discussion of the results appears in an Appendix.<>

## The use of random coset coding for bounding the probability of error in coded modulations schemes

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Random coset coding error-probability bound for coded modulation; pure theory bound, no decoder/HW/construction for NAND LDPC.
- **ID**: ieee:217736
- **Type**: conference
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: H. Herzberg, G. Poltyrev
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/217736
- **Abstract**: A random coset coding for coded modulation structure is presented. This approach enables the authors to calculate easily an upper bound for the probability of decoding error of an average coset code. The random coding method they present, and the upper bound obtained, are applicable to most of the known coded modulation schemes, any transmitted signal constellation, and Gaussian as well as other (not necessarily additive noise) channels. Furthermore, by transmitting according to this method, an equal probability of decoding error is provided for each transmitted sequence.<>

## Fault diagnosis for space-division digital switching network in large-scale SPC telephone switching systems

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Fault diagnosis for telephone switching networks via parity checkers; hardware fault testing, not LDPC ECC.
- **ID**: ieee:184404
- **Type**: conference
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: Yang Fang-chun
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/184404
- **Abstract**: A practical fault diagnosis algorithm for the space-division digital switching network of SPC switching systems is proposed. Its main idea is to sensitize multiple faults to the output of parity checkers in the network using a very short test code sequence. Its advantages over other algorithms are lower diagnosis time, higher fault covering rate and no fault dictionary. The fault types include both stuck-at and bridging faults. Functional faults are also detected.<>

## A cell loss recovery method using FEC in ATM networks

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: FEC for ATM cell-loss recovery; networking/erasure application, no transferable binary LDPC ECC technique.
- **ID**: ieee:108684
- **Type**: journal
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: H. Ohta, T. Kitami
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/108684
- **Abstract**: A cell loss recovery method to be applied to the virtual paths (VPs) of asynchronous transfer mode (ATM) networks is introduced. The method uses forward error correction (FEC). Its advantages over conventional methods on virtual circuits (VCs) include reduced coding/decoding delays and facility sharing. In addition to consecutive cell loss compensation, another feature of this method is that cell loss compensation facilities are only required at VP terminating nodes. When a cell is discarded due to buffer overflow, successive cells also tend to be discarded. Performance estimations show an outstanding reduction in cell loss rate.<>

## Runlength-limited codes for mixed-error channels

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Runlength-limited (d,k) constrained codes for mixed-error channels; non-LDPC constrained coding, no transferable binary LDPC decoder/HW/design technique.
- **ID**: ieee:104318
- **Type**: journal
- **Published**: 1991
- **Authors**: O. Ytrehus
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/104318
- **Abstract**: The mixed-error channel (MC) combines the binary symmetric channel and the peak shift channel. The construction of (d, k) constrained t-MC-error-correcting block codes is described. It is demonstrated that these codes can achieve a code rate close to the (d, k) capacity. The encoding and decoding procedures are described. The performance of the construction depends on a particular partitioning of (d, k) constrained block codes. This partitioning is discussed and various tables of codes are included. Examples on encoding/decoding and on code performance are given.<>
