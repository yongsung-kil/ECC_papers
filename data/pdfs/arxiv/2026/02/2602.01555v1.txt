Abstract—This paper presents a novel design framework for protograph-based LDPC codes that simultaneously achieves full diversity in block-fading channels (BFCs) and nearcapacity performance in additive white Gaussian noise channels (AWGNCs). By leveraging a Boolean approximation-based analysis—Diversity Evolution (DivE)—we derive structural constraints for generalized rootchecks that guarantee full diversity. Based on these constraints, we propose a protograph template tailored for two-block BFCs. Furthermore, we employ a genetic algorithm guided by density evolution to optimize the protograph edges within this template for superior AWGNC performance. The resulting codes effectively bridge the gap between diversityoriented and capacity-oriented designs, exhibiting robust performance across both channel environments.
Index Terms—protograph, block-fading channels, iterative BP decoding, genetic algorithm, LDPC codes
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1], [5] are sparse-graph linear block codes that achieve capacityapproaching performance for large block lengths. Beliefpropagation (BP) decoding is particularly well-suited for practical implementation [8]. Consequently, LDPC codes have been adopted in a wide range of communication standards such as Digital Video Broadcasting (DVB)-S2/C2/T2, IEEE 802.11 wireless local area network (WLAN), Advanced Television Systems Committee (ATSC) 3.0, and 3rd Generation Partnership Project (3GPP) 5th Generation-New Radio (5GNR) [13].
Most existing LDPC code designs are optimized for ergodic channel models such as AWGNCs [3]–[5]. This aligns with modern wireless standards, where the codes are typically optimized for AWGNCs and fading effects are addressed through a bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) scheme [12], [13].
This work was partly supported by Institute of Information & communications Technology Planning & Evaluation (IITP) grant funded by the Korea government (MSIT) (RS-2024-00398449, RS-2024-00397216) and Basic Science Research Program through the National Research Foundation of Korea (NRF) funded by the Ministry of Science and ICT (RS-2024-00343913).

In parallel, a substantial body of research has investigated coding techniques for nonergodic channel models, most notably block-fading channels (BFCs) [16]–[22]. In this setting, performance is governed by diversity rather than ergodic capacity [14], [15]. To address this challenge, root-LDPC and root-protograph-based LDPC codes have been developed to achieve diversity-optimal performance over BFCs [16], [17]. More recently, generalized-root-protograph (GRP) LDPC codes have further extended this framework to higher-rate and hybrid-ARQ scenarios [18], [19].
Despite achieving optimal diversity over BFCs, existing root and GRP LDPC codes rely on highly constrained protograph structures, which limit coding gain and lead to suboptimal AWGNC performance. This raises a fundamental question: Can a single LDPC code simultaneously achieve diversityoptimal performance over BFCs and strong coding gain over AWGNCs? For a BFC with M = 2 blocks, we propose a protograph-based LDPC design framework that achieves diversity optimality while providing enhanced structural flexibility and improved AWGNC performance.
The contributions of this work are as follows. First, we propose a protograph template that induces a protograph family and guarantees diversity-optimal performance over BFCs through iterative BP decoding, guided by a low-complexity diversity analysis tool for LDPC codes, termed Diversity Evolution (DivE) [22]. Second, within the proposed template, we employ a genetic algorithm (GA) [23], [24] to select protographs with strong AWGNC performance. As a result, the proposed framework enables the design of LDPC codes that simultaneously achieve full diversity over BFCs and strong performance over AWGNCs.
II. PRELIMINARIES
A. Low-Density Parity-Check Codes
LDPC codes are linear block codes defined by a sparse parity-check matrix H and an equivalent Tanner-graph representation [1], [2]. The Tanner graph consists of variable nodes

(VNs) and check nodes (CNs), with an edge between VN i and CN j if Hji = 1. This representation enables iterative message-passing decoding, commonly referred to as BP decoding [8]. In practice, low-complexity approximations of BP, such as min-sum (MS) decoding, are widely used. Under MS decoding, VN updates sum the channel log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) and incoming CN messages, whereas CN updates apply sign multiplication and a minimum-magnitude rule to the incoming VN messages [8].

III. DIVERSITY ANALYSIS OF LDPC CODES UNDER BP DECODING IN BLOCK-FADING CHANNELS
A. BFC Model and Coded Diversity
We consider an M -block BFC with binary phase-shift keying (BPSK), where M denotes the number of fading blocks [14], [20]. A codeword is partitioned into M subblocks as c = (c0, c1, . . . , cM−1), where ci is transmitted over the i-th fading block. Each subblock ci is modulated to si, and the i-th block is modeled as ri = hisi + ni for i = 0, . . . , M − 1, where si and ri denote the transmitted and received symbol vectors, respectively, ni is AWGN, and hi ∼ CN (0, 1) is constant within the block and independent across blocks [20], [21]. In this paper, only BPSK is considered. But the results can be readily extended to higher-order modulations.
For transmission over BFCs, achieving diversity across fading blocks is essential for reliable communication. Since BFCs are nonergodic, performance is evaluated in terms of the BLER and its high-SNR slope, which reflects the diversity order [14],

In AWGNCs, performance is closely related to Hamming distance and coding gain, whereas over BFCs it is governed by the blockwise Hamming distance. As a result, LDPC codes optimized for AWGNCs do not necessarily achieve full diversity over BFCs, and vice versa [20]. Motivated by this mismatch, we focus on the M = 2 case and develop an LDPC design framework that achieves both full diversity over BFCs and strong performance over AWGNCs.
B. Diversity Analysis of LDPC Codes under BP Decoding
We introduce Diversity Evolution (DivE) [22] to analyze the diversity behavior of protograph-based LDPC codes over BFCs under iterative BP decoding. We first employ a Boolean approximation Am of the fading coefficient hm for each block m ∈ {0, . . . , M − 1} by thresholding the fading/nonfading state, Am = 1|hm|2γ≥ρ0 , where ρ0 is a threshold and 1E denotes the indicator function of an event E [20], [21]. Let A = (A0, . . . , AM−1) ∈ {0, 1}M denote the set of Boolean fading variables, where Ai is the Boolean approximation of the i-th block.
DivE proceeds by tracking the evolution of message-level fading behavior through iterative BP decoding via Boolean CN/VN updates (Fig. 1), as summarized in Algorithm 1. These updates are symbolic: DivE iteratively updates Boolean algebraic expressions in A, which we refer to as Boolean fading functions. These functions capture the associated diversity behavior [22].
Although implementing Boolean algebra can be tricky (for large M ), DivE can be efficiently derived by running numerical computation of the fading result of MS decoding for all possible individual fading states (2M cases). The function is called fadingMSD [22], which returns a binary vector of length n, the fading state of VNs after MS decoding.
Eventually, DivE results in Boolean fading functions (or the truth tables) corresponding to n VNs, respectively. The diversity order of each VN is determined directly from its final Boolean fading function [21]. If all information VNs achieve

IV. PROTOGRAPH DESIGN FRAMEWORK FOR BLOCK-FADING AND AWGN CHANNELS
The proposed framework has two stages: (i) defining a protograph template that guarantees full diversity for BFC with M = 2, and (ii) selecting protographs within this template using a genetic algorithm, with RCA-DE serving as the fitness metric to optimize the AWGNC decoding threshold.

A. Generalized Rootcheck Structure
We briefly review the rootcheck structure of [16], which achieves full diversity over the BFC after a single (one) BP iteration.
Definition 5 (Rootcheck [16]). A check node cj ∈ C is said to be a rootcheck for a vi ∈ V if π(i) = m for some m ∈ {0, . . . , M − 1} and there exists n ∈ {0, . . . , M − 1} \ {m} such that π(u) = n, ∀ u ∈ Nc(j) \ {i}.

The following proposition confirms that the existence of a generalized rootcheck indicates an improvement of diversity state of the code across multiple decoding iterations where M = 2.

Proposition 1. For the BFC with M = 2, if vi is connected to a generalized rootcheck at iteration ℓ and is the root node, then vi attains full diversity after iteration ℓ.
Proof. Assume vi is assigned to block 0, and let cj be a generalized rootcheck adjacent to vi at iteration ℓ for M = 2. Under DivE, the CN update (AND) combines the incoming functions from Nc(j) \ {i}. By the generalized rootcheck condition, these include the opposite fading variable A1 (and possibly A0 + A1, i.e., a full diversity expression). Since A1 ·A1 = A1 and (A0 +A1)·A1 = A1 in Boolean algebra, we obtain Fc(jℓ→ ) vi = A1. The subsequent VN update (OR) yields Fi(ℓ) = A0 + A1, i.e., vi attains full diversity.

For M = 2, the above result implies a sufficient condition for full diversity.

Proposition 2. For M = 2, if every information VN becomes a generalized root node at some decoding iteration, the resulting LDPC code achieves full diversity.

B. Family of Protographs Achieving Full Diversity
Using the generalized rootcheck structure, we define a family of protographs using the following protograph template that guarantees full diversity over the BFC of M = 2.
Definition 7 (Protograph template). A protograph template is specified by T and induces a constrained protograph family H(T ). For each Hp = [hji] ∈ H(T ), the template fixes a subset of entries of Hp to 0 or 1 and specifies the designable index set D, where (j, i) ∈ D implies hji = b with b ∈ {0, 1}. We focus on the rate-1/2 case (k = n/2), the maximum rate achieving full diversity for M = 2 BFCs under the Singletonlike bound. Here, v0, . . . , vn/2−1 are information VNs and vn/2, . . . , vn−1 are parity VNs.
It can be proved that every protograph in the associated constrained protograph family H(T ) achieves full diversity over the BFC with M = 2.
Theorem 1. Consider a protograph template T and the induced protograph family H(T ). Suppose that the following conditions hold:
- Block mapping (πT (·)): vi is assigned to block 0 for i ∈ [0, n/4) ∪ [3n/4, n), and to block 1 for i ∈ [n/4, 3n/4).
- Row-weight constraint: For j ∈ {n/4 − 2, n/4 − 1, n/2 − 2, n/2 − 1}, { i : (j, i) ∈ D and hji = 1 } ≥ 1.
Under the above conditions, any protograph Hp ∈ H(T ) achieves full diversity over M = 2 block BFC under iterative BP decoding within at most n/4 iterations.
Proof. The proposed protograph template is constructed so that, for each ℓ = 1, . . . , n/4 the information VNs vℓ−1 and vn/4+ℓ−1 are guaranteed to become full diversity within iteration ℓ. We establish this guarantee by tracking generalized rootcheck propagation under DivE.
1) Rootcheck guarantee (ℓ = 1): By the fixed entries imposed by the protograph template, v0 and vn/4 are the root nodes of rootchecks c0 and cn/4, respectively. Hence, they are guaranteed to attain full diversity after one BP iteration.

2) Generalized rootcheck guarantee (ℓ ≥ 2): For ℓ = 2, . . . , n/4, the protograph template constraints guarantee that vℓ−1 and vn/4+ℓ−1 are connected to generalized rootchecks (cℓ−1 and cn/4+ℓ−1). The remaining neighbors satisfy either the opposite fading variable or a full diversity expression (obtained in previous iterations). Therefore, by Proposition 1, each of these VNs is guaranteed to become full diversity within iteration ℓ.
3) Iteration upper bound: Since two specific information VNs are guaranteed per iteration, all information VNs are covered within at most n/4 iterations. Hence, the sufficient condition of Proposition 2 holds for M = 2, and any Hp ∈ H(T ) achieves full diversity under BP decoding. This is an iteration upper bound; depending on which designable entries are set to b = 0 within the lowertriangular regions (e.g., (j, i) with 0 ≤ j < i < n/4 or n/4 ≤ j < i < n/2), the generalized-rootcheck propagation can be accelerated, so full diversity may be achieved in lower iterations.
Theorem 1 guarantees full diversity over the M = 2 BFC for all protographs in H(T ). Within this constrained family, we can search for a protograph with improved coding gain.
Remark 2. Such degree-1 components in the proposed protograph template are introduced to improve finite-length LDPC performance [4] and reduce the search space.

VI. CONCLUSION
We proposed a protograph-based LDPC design framework that achieves full diversity over the BFC with M = 2 while maintaining strong AWGNC performance. Full diversity is guaranteed at the protograph template level via the Boolean approximation-based DivE analysis and generalized rootcheck propagation, and AWGNC performance is then optimized within the induced constrained protograph family using an RCA-DE-guided genetic algorithm targeting low decoding thresholds. Numerical results confirm that the resulting QCLDPC codes exhibit full diversity over the BFC with M = 2 and achieve better BLER performance over the AWGNC across multiple code lengths compared with the considered baselines. As future work, extension of the proposed framework to BFCs of M ≥ 2 and high-rate code design can be considered.
