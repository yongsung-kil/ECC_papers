Abstract—Decoders for Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes are usually tailored to an application and optimized once the specific content and structure of the parity matrix are known. In this work we consider the parity matrix as an argument of the Min-Sum decoder, and provide a GPU implementation that is independent of the content of the parity matrix, and relies only on its dimensions.
Index Terms—Low density parity check codes, Min-Sum decoder, map-reduce optimization, LDPC, GPU.

I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes made their way into several standards and technologies, spanning telecommunications and storage [1]. Prior to adoption, it is sometimes the case that several codes are considered, stemming from different constructions or parameter tuning of a single construction [15]. In this early, short-lived stage, decoder optimizations that are a function of the specific parity matrix are not readily available. In this stage, a high throughput decoder helps eliminate or choose from a selection of codes, often having a parity matrix of similar dimensions. This work is concerned with the optimization of the Min-Sum decoder when only the dimensions of the parity matrix H are known.

VI. EXPERIMENTAL RESULSTS
We use a Quaci-Cyclic 8176,1022 code from [3] to test decoder throughput. For comparison, we implemented a pure CPU-oriented of the Min-Sum decoder in Python with as much usage of the Numba package [9] to compile code directly to the machine language as possible. This is a good approach if the content of the sparse parity matrix H is known, and many of the calculations could be discarded beforehand.
