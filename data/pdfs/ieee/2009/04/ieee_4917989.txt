Thresholds Calculation of LDPC Code Ensembles Using a Novel Gaussian Approximation Algorithm

Piming Ma
UWB Communication Research Center Incheon, Korea
mapiming@sdu.edu.cn
Abstract—A novel Gaussian approximation algorithm is proposed for calculating the thresholds of low-density paritycheck (LDPC) code ensembles under sum-product decoding. The updating rules of means and error probability are derived. Numerical results show that the thresholds obtained by the proposed algorithm closely coincide with the values computed by density evolution (DE). Moreover, our algorithm can calculate the thresholds of irregular LDPC codes with higher accuracy than the algorithm proposed by Lehmann and Maggio.
Keywords- Gaussian approximation, LDPC codes, threshold, density evolution
I. INTRODUCTION
Density evolution (DE) is an algorithm for determining the capacity of low-density parity-check codes under message passing decoding [1][2]. Sae-Young Chung [3] proposed a Gaussian approximation (GA) technique, to simplify density evolution, which converts the infinite-dimensional problem of iteratively calculating message densities into the onedimensional problem of updating means of Gaussian densities. In [4], Lehmann and Maggio developed a one-dimensional map which directly represented the error probabilities based on Gaussian densities.
In this paper, we present a novel Gaussian approximation for iterative analysis of LDPC codes under sum-product decoding. Rather than updating the means of check messages using the φ function in [3], we combine Gallager’s original results [5] with the well-known Q function, to obtain the means from the error probabilities. This algorithm can effectively calculate the thresholds of LDPC code ensembles for AWGN channel.
The paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews the preliminaries LDPC codes. Section III presents the novel Gaussian approximation algorithm in detail and gives the recursive equations for means and error probability. Then, we demonstrate the threshold calculation in Section IV. In Section V we conclude the paper.
II. PRELIMINARIES OF LDPC CODES
Low-density parity-check codes are defined by sparse parity-check matrices. A parity-check matrix H can be represented by a bipartite graph. If there is a 1 at the jth row and the ith column in H, there is an edge between the ith variable node and the jth check node. The number of edges connected to a node is the degree of this node.

The limit properties of tl (s) , rl (s) and pl (s) are determined by λ(x) , ρ(x) and the value of s . If and only if λ(x) , ρ(x) and s satisfy t < f (s,t) for 0 ≤ t < ∞ ,

satisfied, which implies that rl (s) → ∞ and pl (s) → 0 as l→∞.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a novel approximation algorithm was proposed for calculating the thresholds of LDPC code ensembles under sum-product decoding.
1) The updating rules for means and error probability were derived. This provides greater insight into decoding of LDPC codes .
2) The proposed algorithm can be used to calculate the thresholds of LDPC code ensembles. We verified that the

thresholds obtained by our algorithm closely coincide with the values computed by density evolution. Moreover, our algorithm calculated the thresholds of irregular LDPC codes with a higher accuracy than the algorithm in [4].
