Improved Approximately Lower Triangular Encoding of LDPC Codes

Peng Zhang, Fei Yang, Rui Lv, and Jinlun Chen
Communication University of China Beijing, China
trellis_cn@sina.com

Abstract—Concerning the high encoding complexity of lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes, an improved approximately lower triangular (ALT) encoding method is proposed, which is able to take full advantage of the characteristics of the sparse parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of row weight. Experimental results show that, the proposed method can reduce dramatically the memory requirements in hardware implementation, while maintaining the computational complexity nearly.
Keywords-Approximately lower triangular; LDPC codes; Encoding method; CMMB
I. INTRODUCTION
In the past decade, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] had been attracting more and more attentions due to their excellent error-correcting ability and low decoding complexity. They are the most promising forward error correction (FEC) candidates in many applications, such as telecommunications and data storages. Although LDPC codes are the forceful competitors to Turbo codes, the serious drawback of obviously high encoding complexity obstacles the former. Straightforward encoding algorithms, for example, generation matrices that are usually dense, involve dense matrix multiplications and thereby result in the computational complexity quadratic to the codeword length.
Since the rediscovery of LDPC codes, lots of efforts had been made to develop computationally efficient encoders by many coding investigators. MacKay’s lower triangular method [2], Haley’s iterative encoding method [3], and Johnson’s quasi-cyclic method [4] are all aiming at the LDPC codes with specific ensembles but not suitable for arbitrary ones. The allpurpose linear encoding method is first introduced by Neal, i.e. LU decomposition method [5]. Its principle is very simple, and its key is to decompose a matrix into the product of a lower and upper triangular matrix as sparse as possible [6]. Approximately lower triangular encoding method [7] proposed by Richardson has complexity almost linear to the codeword length. Besides plenty of preprocessing [8], its trivial encoding procedure is made up of several steps. Those impose burden on programmers.
For the LU decomposition encoding method, lower and upper triangular matrices are still sparse, but denser than the parity-check matrix. Among all the matrices used by the ALT

The values and column indexes of nonzero elements of the sparse matrices are stored. As for the dense and small matrix, unlikely, the values of all elements are stored, and several consecutive values are stored together at same memory address. So, it employs a little memory and has high parallel speed, especially for binary codes. According to the analysis above, we can draw the conclusion that, despite trivial preprocessing and encoding procedure, the ALT encoding method with less memory and lower complexity is preferable.
For the ALT encoding method, the parity-check matrix after elementary permutations is divided into a few submatrices, which lose its characteristics. In allusion to the shortcoming, an improved ALT encoding method is proposed. The proposed method is capable of making the best of the inherent characteristics of the parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of nonzero elements’ number per row, such that the memory requirements can be decreased greatly.

C. Performance Analysis
Among all the matrices used by the original or improved ALT encoding method, only one matrix Q−1 or Q′−1 with small order g × g is very dense, the others are quite sparse.
Economical strategy is that, the values and column indexes of nonzero elements of the sparse matrices are stored, while the values of all elements of Q−1 or Q′−1 are stored. For binary
codes, the values of nonzero elements of the sparse matrices can be omitted.

In general, all the sub-matrices employed in the ALT encoding method have different row weights themselves. However, the improved method utilizes the whole rows of V ′HM ′ or H at the first and third steps. If row weights of H
are same, the proposed method is probably of higher speed, easier implementation, and less memory requirements. The reason is that it is unnecessary to save and decide the number of nonzero elements every row. If H is cyclic, its structure can be depicted by exceedingly little memory. In the case, saving memory resource is the most remarkable advantage of the improved method.

IV. EXPERIMENTS
Taking example for the LDPC codes adopted by China Mobile Multimedia Broadcasting (CMMB) standard [9] [10], the performance of the improved ALT encoding method is verified.
There are two binary LDPC codes with different rate 1/2 and 3/4 in CMMB system. The former is a (9216, 3, 6) regular code, and the latter is a (9216, 3, 12) regular one. In the standard, the sparse parity-check matrices are specified, which are cyclic [11] but not quasi-cyclic [12]. Technically, it is difficult to encode the both LDPC codes. For the rate 1/2 (3/4), the entire parity-check matrix can be derived from the first 18 (9) rows, which shift 36 columns cyclically every 18 (9) rows. Since H is cyclic and has equal row weights, it is reasonable to believe that the proposed method is quite suitable for the two LDPC codes.

180 and 43 for the rate 1/2 and 3/4 respectively. It is noticeable to point out that, for the (9216, 3, 6) regular code, g ≈ 0.0195n , and it is very close to the expected value
0.017n [7].

The two LDPC encoders are implemented on ALTERA Cyclone III EP3C55 FPGA. Matrices and vectors are stored in the M9K block RAM on chip. For the sake of saving memory, storage strategy is optimized by splitting big matrices and incorporating small matrices. From Table I, both methods utilize a small quantity of logic cells. However, the improved ALT encoding method outperforms remarkably the original one in terms of RAM consumption. The former uses less 91 blocks of M9K block RAM than the latter, which admits choosing cheap FPGA.
The improved ALT encoders adopt two stages pipeline architectures, and maximum work frequency reaches up to 156.93 MHz. Under 100 MHz, pure encoding rate can achieve 15.710 Mbps (the rate 1/2) and 29.542 Mbps (the rate 3/4), which satisfy sufficiently the specifications in CMMB standard: 10.852 Mbps and 16.243 Mbps correspondingly.

V. CONCLUSION
Combining with the partial trapezium encoding method, we propose an improved approximately lower triangular encoding method, which is capable of making the most of the characteristics of the sparse parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of row weight.
Experimental results show that, the proposed method is extremely memory-efficient, keeping basically computational complexity at the same time. The improved approximately lower triangular encoding method is of practical value because of low cost. Our work solves the technical difficulty of encoding LDPC codes and is expected to be helpful to the research and development of CMMB base stations.
