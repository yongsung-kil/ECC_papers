Improving Performance of Some Irregular LDPC Codes By Means of Three Optimization Techniques
Li Peng, Guangxi Zhu
Department of Electronics and Information Engineering, Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Wuhan, Hubei, China 430074
Email: pengli1502@yahoo.com

Abstract—In this paper we present three optimization techniques of improving the performance of some irregular low-density parity-check ensemble with determinate structure and finite block length. Firstly, it is permutation matrix optimization. We present a new random permutation matrix called as the Q-matrix. We substituted Q-matrix for identity matrix in base matrix array which can be expanded into H-matrix of the irregular QC-LDPC codes recommended in IEEE 802.16e standard draft and can make the performance of this QC-LDPC codes improve about 0.4dB. Secondly, it is large girth optimization technique. We design H-matrix of the irregular LDPC ensemble and make it at least not include 8-cycle. Thirdly, it is column weight optimization technique in H-matrix of the irregular LDPC ensemble. For the presented irregular LDPC ensemble, it is not such case that the larger the column weight is and the better the performance is, but the column weight should be a moderate value1.

Index Terms—Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, sparse parity-check matrix, zigzag matrix, permutation matrix.

Ⅰ INTRODUCTION

Despite their excellent error-correcting properties, low-density

parity-check (LDPC) codes with random-like structure have several

shortcomings. The most important of these is the lack of

mathematical structure in the H-matrices of such codes, which leads

to increased encoding complexity and prohibitively large storage

requirements. These issues can usually be resolved by using

structured LDPC codes, but at the cost of some performance loss.

This performance loss may be attributed to the fact that algebraic

code design techniques introduce various constraints on the set of

code parameters influencing the performance of belief propagation

decoding, so that it is hard to optimize the overall structure of the

code.

In this paper, we focus our attention on the S-LDPC codes which

From capacity-achieving degree distribution pair to structure H-matrix, from threshold performance of infinite length codes to asymptotical performance of finite length codes, all these processing behaviors follow the performance loss in the different extent. The main aim of this paper is to look into efficient techniques to make the structural S-LDPC codes reach up to optimization. According to (1), H p matrix is determinate structure and does not need to redesign, so major optimizing work is focused on how to design structural Hd -matrix. In fact, the structure of Hd -matrix of the S-LDPC codes is similar to the structure of H-matrix of the QC-LDPC codes. A large number of literatures studied various structure design of H-matrix of the QC-LDPC codes [1 – 3], but from which we present different designing methods for the S-LDPC codes and obtain better effects in some extent. For Hd -matrix design, we considered three improving methods: new permutation matrix, distribution of permutation matrix in Hd -matrix and appropriate selecting column weight of Hd -matrix.
The paper [1] gives the exact description of permutation matrix starting from a point of a circulant (matrix): A circulant is a square matrix in which each row is the cyclic shift of the row above it, and the first row is the cyclic shift of the last row; each column is the downward cyclic shift of the column on its left, and the first column is the cyclic shift of the last column. The row and column weights of a circulant are the same, say w . If w = 1 , then the circulant is a permutation matrix. Obviously, identity matrix, for short I-matrix, satisfies the above circulant property, so it is permutation matrix. But the bidiagonal matrix, such as H p , does not satisfy this, so it is not.
The paper [2] employed the structure of multiplicative groups over finite fields to design the distribution and the circulant-shift times, here called as circulant-shift “subscript”, of identity matrix within the H-matrix so as to form the determinate structural regular QC-LDPC codes. The paper [3] considered a family of QC-LDPC codes closely related to array codes, and derived simple necessary and sufficient conditions for such codes to have girth larger than six or eight. Subsequently, codes with large girth were constructed by the aid of computer search strategies which rely on randomly generating integers until the conditions of the theorem are met. We derived two algorithms of constructing Hd -matrix without 4-cycle and 6-cycle by using the theorem and the concepts of subscript matrix and subscript difference presented in [3].

A. Introduction to Q-Matrix

In fact, except identity matrix being permutation matrix which has regular structure, the permutation matrix satisfying the above circulant property should still include the other form, for example, algebraic structure and irregular structure. What we have designed algebraic structure permutation matrix is D-matrix based on arithmetical progression. What the D-matrix substitutes for I-matrix for performance improvement of QC-LDPC codes has slight effect, so here save to introduce it. Next, we are interested in introducing Q-matrix based on N-queen searching algorithm, a kind of irregular and random permutation matrix.
Definition 2.1: The Q-matrix is a b× b square matrix in which there is only one “1”element in each row, each column and each diagonal, the rest position elements are all “0”.
Definition 2.2: The n-queen problem requires one to place n queens on a n × n chessboard, so that no two queens are in the same row, in the same column, or in the same diagonal [4].
Comparing the above two definitions, if regarding a n × n chessboard as a n × n square matrix and denoting queen by using “1” element, we can obtain a conclusion that the description of the n-queen problem is equivalent as the description of the defined Q-matrix. From this reason, the Q-matrix gets its name.
As the distribution of n-queen can get by using n-queen searching algorithm in existence, we can also search Q-matrix by using it. For the general, a b× b Q-matrix denotes the number of solution by Q(b) . There have been a number of independent proofs showing

Ⅳ. THE OPTIMUM COLUMN WEIGHT OF Hd -MATRIX FOR
IMPROVING THE PERFORMANCE OF THE S-LDPC CODES
After using two techniques of optimum permutation matrix and increasing girth, comparing with regular QC-LDPC codes, the S-LDPC codes perform very well, and the sparse degree of H-matrix of (1) is minimal, e.g. 50% column weights are less than 3 and 50% column weights are equal to 3, which means that encoding and decoding calculating complexity and hardware description complexity reach up lowest extent. For further improving the performance of the S-LDPC codes and making them surpass the performance of the irregular QC-LDPC codes afforded by IEEE 802.16e standard draft, an effective measure is increasing column weight, but at the cost of sacrificing the complexity of encoding and decoding algorithm.

Ⅴ. CONCLUSIONS
What we give the optimum structure of Hd matrix is that the permutation matrix is Q-matrix, minimum girth is 8 and moderate column weight is q = 6 for 1/2 rate S-LDPC codes. When using this optimum structure, we made the performance of the S-LDPC codes with half-rate and finite code length achieve about 1.3dB under 10-5 over AWGN channel, which is better than one of the irregular QC-LDPC codes, but the cost of complexity of encoding and decoding algorithm is only slightly larger than one of the irregular QC-LDPC codes in IEEE 802.16e standard draft.
