I. INTRODUCTION
L
OW-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1]-[3] are con-
sidered as a class of codes with near Shannon limit
performance under belief-propagation decoding [4]-[7]; in
particular, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes have proven to
be very practical [8]. The LDPC codes are constructed by
random-like [5],[7] and combinatorial methods (including
algebraic and geometric methods) [4],[8]-[20], to mention just
a few. A drawback of the random-like construction method is
that it is hard to obtain high-rate medium-length codes with
good distance parameters using this method. Another problem
with the random-like codes is that, in general, the encoding
complexity of these codes is not low. The algebraic and
geometric constructions using circulant permutation matrices
[8],[10]-[12],[17]-[22] produce quasi-cyclic codes with good
minimum distance and girth properties. These QC codes can
be encoded with simple shift registers [23]-[24].
In [25] a systematic recursive method to construct long-
length high-rate LDPC codes has been given. The so con-
structed codes preserve the girth and minimum distance of the
core code. The construction method given in [12] also provides
a systematic code construction method based on a partial
Kronecker product operation between two configurations.
Among the combinatorial structures with rich mathematical
properties are integer lattices. These structures have been used
in [18]-[19] to construct QC LDPC codes. It is known that the

the constructed codes do not match with the lattice codes, and
hence a comparison between our codes and the lattice codes
given in [18]-[19] is not meaningful, no such comparison is
given in this paper.

VI. CONCLUSION
Following the idea of using a partial Kronecker product
operation between two given configurations, we applied this
technique on a given integer lattice to generate long-length
high-rate well-structured LDPC codes. The codes constructed
by this recursive systematic approach have girth 6, a desired
condition for graph-based iterative decoding algorithms. Due
to their simple representation, these codes posses a low-
complexity encoding process. It was shown that there is
a strong yet simple relation between the minimum weight
codewords in the constructed codes and those in the core code.
From rate perspective, the results verify the advantage of the
constructed codes against the lattice based ones. According
to the simulation results, from performance perspective, the
constructed codes are at least as good as their random-like
counterparts.
