Design of a New Kind of LDPC Decoder

Hanhong Jiang
School of electric and information engineering, Naval University of engineering, NUE Wuhan, China

Guifeng Zhong, Qing Li, Wendan Wu
School of electric and information engineering, Wuhan University of technology, WHUT Wuhan, China

Abstract ü The properties of the LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) error-correcting codes are bearing down on Shannon limit, but it also needs to solve the problem of reducing the complexity of decoding as much as possible in practical applications. A new type of (1008, 3, 6) rules LDPC decoder was introduced in this paper. It adopted the Min-Sum-Plus-Correction-Factor decoding algorithm and part parallel structure, which not only reduced the complexity of the hardware realization, but also improved the decoding speed. The maximum decoding rate could achieve 128Mbit/s. These properties laid a good foundation for the actual application of LDPC.

Keywords-LDPCt; decoder;MSPCF;FPGA
1. INTRODUCTION
Proposed by Gallager in 1962, Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes have the superior performance of approaching the Shannon limit as the same as the turbo codes [1]. Compared with turbo codes, the LDPC decoding algorithm is simple and supporting parallel realization, and now more and more people pay attention to it. LDPC codes can exert its superiority when the length of codes is longer, which means that the scale of dealing encoding and decoding circuit will increase relatively in proportion, and also the size of chips will become bigger. Therefore, how to reduce the complexity of the decoder in actual application has become the focus of research in the field of channel coding. When there is a high SNR, the Min-Sum-Plus decoding algorithm will suffer a greater degree of decoding performance decline, so the Min-Sum-Plus-Correction-Factor (MSPCF) decoding algorithm is adopted in this paper, which can effectively improve decoding performance in this case [2]. Considering the hardware realization of resource occupancy and processing speed, compromising the advantages and disadvantages of parallel structure and serial structure, we adopt part parallel structure in the whole structure design of decoder, so as to improve the decoding speed and reduce the complexity of the decoder.

4. THE WHOLE STRUCTURE DESIGN OF THE DECODER
Since large decoding delay is brought by the only one degree of parallelism in serial structure, general LDPC decoder rarely takes serial structure in decoding structure. Parallelism structure increases the degree of parallelism, which brings quite desirable decoding ratio; however, it also leads to a corresponding exponential growth in hardware resources [5]. Considering the hardware realization of resource occupancy and processing speed, compromising the advantages and disadvantages of parallel structure in the whole structure design of decoder, so as to improve the decoding speed and not occupy too much chip resources.
4.1. Part parallel structure in decoder
The overall structure of LDPC decoder is shown in Fig.2, whose whole FPGA structure consists of six modules: input data control module, operation state control module, check nodes update module, bit nodes update module, data storage module and matrix check module [6]. First, the 3bits long data from the channel is converted to a 1008 bits long data by serial-to-parallel, then divided into all-parallel 24-channel, each channel contains 1008/24=42 bits. Like this it
correspondingly needs wb = 24 BFU (Bit Function Unit) to
calculate the information update module, each BFU needs to process 42 bits data in iteration. The number of the corresponding CFU (Check Function Unit) is half of the
variable node unit, that is wc = 12 , each CFU also needs to
handle 42 bits data in iteration.

5. FPGA SIMULATION AND ANALYSIS
This paper realized the all-parallel structure and part parallel structure of the Min-Sum-Plus decoding algorithm and the Min-Sum-Plus-Correction-Factor decoding algorithm. The (1008,3,6) ruler LDPC decoder is designed, which adopts EP2SGX60EF1152C3 device of Altera-Statix II series FPGA, uses Verilog HDL language to program, and compiles under the Quartus 5.1 development tool.
