AbstractŌĆöThis paper presents a soft reliability-based binary message-passing algorithm for decoding LDPC codes. This algorithm outperforms the existing weighted bit-flipping algorithms with much less computational complexity. It is particularly effective for decoding LDPC codes constructed based on finitegeometries and finite fields. The proposed algorithm can be simplified for applications in communication or storage systems where either soft reliability information is not available to the decoder or a simple decoder is needed.
I. INTRODUCTION
The ever-growing needs for cheaper, faster, and more reliable communication systems have forced many researchers to seek means to attain the ultimate limits on reliable communications. LDPC codes are currently the most promising coding technique to achieve Shannon capacity for a wide range of channels. These codes were first discovered by Gallager in 1962 [1] and then rediscovered in late 1990ŌĆÖs [2], [3]. Ever since their rediscovery, a great deal of research effort has been expended in design, construction, encoding, decoding, generalizations, performance analysis and applications of these codes. Many classes of well performing LDPC codes have been constructed, and various algorithms for decoding these codes have been devised. The decoding algorithms that have been devised provide a wide spectrum of trade-offs between error performance, decoding complexity and decoding speed.
This paper presents a novel iterative soft reliability-based algorithm for decoding LDPC codes. This algorithm is devised based on the simple orthogonal check-sums concept used in the one-step majority-logic decoding (OSMLGD) algorithm [4] in conjunction with some kind of soft reliability measures of received symbols. The reliability measure of each received symbol is improved through decoding iterations. An important feature of this algorithm is that the messages passed between the variable- and the check-nodes of the Tanner graph [5] of an LDPC code are binary. This feature of binary message-passing significantly simplifies the decoder implementation, especially wiring between the variable-node processors and the checknode processors. The proposed decoding algorithm significant outperforms the existing weighted bit-flipping (WBF) algorithms for decoding LDPC codes with much less decoding complexity. Furthermore, decoding with the proposed decod-

ing algorithm converges very fast. Another feature of the proposed algorithm is that it allows parallel decoding of all the received symbols in each decoding iteration in contrast to the existing WBF-algorithms which only allow flipping one received bit at a time in each decoding iteration. Parallel decoding is important for some communication systems where high decoding speed is required. Compared to the sum-product algorithm (SPA) [3], [6], the proposed algorithm provides an efficient trade-off between performance and decoding complexity.
The proposed algorithm can be simplified for applications in communication or storage systems where either soft reliability information is not available to the decoder or a simple decoder is needed.

IV. A SIMPLIFIED ALGORITHM
There are communication and storage systems for which either soft reliability information of received bits are not available to the decoder or a simple decoder is required. In this case, an effective hard-decision decoding algorithm is needed to achieve certain required coding gain with low decoding complexity. The WISRB-MGLD-algorithm can be modified by replacing the soft reliability measures of received bits by some type of hard reliability measures. Such modification results in an iterative hard reliability-based (IHRB)-MLGD-algorithm.
To start the IHRB-MLGD process, we must assign to each received bit an initial reliability measure. Consider the harddecision zj of a sample yj at the output of the receiver sampler given by (1). For BPSK transmission over a binary-input AWGN with 0 mapped into ŌłÆ1 and 1 into +1, it is known in communication theory that: 1) the more negative the sample yj, the more reliable the estimate zj of the transmitted bit vj being a ŌĆ£0ŌĆØ is; and 2) the more positive the sample yj, the more reliable of the estimate zj of the transmitted bit vj being

a ŌĆ£1ŌĆØ is. Consider the decoding function ╬ōj for the OSMLGDalgorithm given by (2). The value of ╬ōj is an integer in the range of [ŌłÆ╬│, +╬│]. V. CONCLUSION AND REMARKS
In this paper, we have presented a reliability-based iterative binary message-passing algorithm for decoding LDPC codes, called the WISRB-MLGD-algorithm. This algorithm is developed based on the simple concept of orthogonal check-sums used in the one-step majority-logic decoding. Only binary logical operations and real-number additions are required in the proposed decoding algorithm. Since only binary messages

are passed between the variable- and check-nodes of the Tanner graph of a code, the wires that connect the variablenode processors and check-node processors can be very simple in contrast to the SPA or the MSA. Experimental results show that the proposed WISRB-MLGD-algorithm performs close to the SPA and the MSA and it outperforms two best performing WBF-algorithms with less computational complexity per iteration. As shown by the experimental results, decoding with the WISRB-MLGD-algorithm converges very fast, much faster than the two best performing WBF-algorithm. Furthermore, the WISRB-MLGD-algorithm allows decoding received bits in full or partially parallel in contrast to the existing BF- or WBF-algorithms which only allow to flip one received bit at a time in each decoding iteration. As a result, high speed decoder can be implemented.
Also presented in this paper is a simplified version of the WISRB-MLGD-algorithm, called the IHRB-MLGDalgorithm, which is suitable for communication and storage systems where soft reliability information is either not available or a simple and fast decoder is needed.
Another possible simplification of the WISRB-MLGDalgorithm is to quantize the samples of the received sequence y uniformly into 2b intervals and represent each interval by an integer in the range [ŌłÆ(2bŌłÆ1 ŌłÆ 1), +(2bŌłÆ1 ŌłÆ 1)]. Then, the integer representations of the samples of y are used as the reliability measures of the received bits of z. By doing this, we will obtain an algorithm that requires only logical operations and integer additions.
The WISRB-MLGD- and the IHRB-MLGD-algorithms are particularly effective for LDPC codes whose RC-constrained parity-check matrices have large column weights, such as the structured LDPC codes constructed based on finite geometries and finite fields [7], [8]. Compared to the SPA, these two binary message-passing algorithms offer efficient trade-offs between performance and decoding complexity.
