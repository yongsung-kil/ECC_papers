A Cost efficient LDPC decoder for DVB-S2
Van Ying, Dan Bo, Shuangqu Huang, Bo Xiang, Yun Chen * ,Xiaoyang Zeng *

Abstract - Based on the Min-Sum algorithm, this paper proposes an LDPC decoder integrating the TDMP schedule, which could achieve low complexity as well as good performance. The LDPC decoder is for DVB-S2, which includes 11 kinds of code rates with a block size of 64800. Based on SMIC 0.13/lm standard CMOS process, the LDPC decoder has an estimation area of 14mm2, a throughput of 135Mbps with a frequency of105MHz and maximum iteration number of30, which shows advantage over previous DVB-S2 LDPC decoders}.
Index Terms - LDPC coeds, DVB-S2, min-sum, TDMP schedule
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes are originally proposed by Gallager in his 1960 doctoral dissertation [1], and they were not used for many years because at that time the technology was not mature for their practical implementation. In the mid-1990's, LDPC codes were resurrected with the contribution of Mackay and Neal [2]. The remarkable error correction performance and low implementation complexity of LDPC codes led to their recent inclusion in broadcasting systems, such as next generation digital video broadcasting via satellite (DVB-S2) [3], and have been optional FEC techniques in networks, such as Wireless-LAN (802.11n) [4] and Wireless-MAN (IEEE 802.16e) [5], etc.
LDPC are linear block codes defined by a sparse parity check matrix. The total number of ones in each row and column is ultra smaller in comparison with the total elements of all rows and columns. The parity check matrix can be viewed as a bipartite graph with two kinds of nodes: checknodes corresponding to the rows and variable-nodes corresponding to the columns of the parity check matrix, as shown in Fig 1.

The Digital Video Broadcasting via Satellite, Second Generation (DVB-S2) protocol [3] has been developed and standardized as an evolution of the Digital Video Broadcasting via Satellite (DVB-S) for European satellite broadcasting by the European Telecommunications Standards Institute (ETSI). DVB-S2 retains the robust operational features of DVBS while introducing new features that promise to improve performance and efficiency of broadcast links, such as additional modulations, dynamic operating modes, and advanced Forward Error Correction (FEC) schemes. The DVB-S2 standard changes the FEC algorithm from CC (convolutional codes) and RS (Reed-Solomon) codes in the DVB system to LDPC(Low-density party-check) and BCH codes, and because of this change, the prominent performance stand near the Shannon limit from 0.7dB to 1.2 dB.
In this paper, we present a DVB-S2 compliant LDPC decoder architecture which combines the TDMP schedule. This design improves usage of area and achieves good error correction performance and high throughput.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section II briefs the simplified LDPC decoding algorithms, i.e, the MinSum algorithm, which implements message update. Section III presents the TDMP schedule. Section N describes The LDPC architecture, while in Section V the numerical simulation and implementation results are analyzed. Finally, Section VI concludes the paper.

IV. LDPC DECODER ARCHITECTURE
A. Partially-Parallel Decoder Architecture
For the fully-parallel architecture, each node is assigned a hardware process cell, which makes it possible to maximum the throughput. But the partially-parallel structure is more practical to balance the hardware costs and throughputs [9] .
The proposed LDPC decoder contains a processor-array consisted of 360 equal processor cells. All the processor cells have the same structure and perform the same operations.
With the idea of single instruction multiple data (SIMD) processor for reference, we adapt our architecture to the microinstruction controller. Instruction memory based controller is the key block that enables the decoder to support multi-rate and multiple maximum numbers of iterations. The state of decoding process and corresponding information of parity check matrix are stored in the instruction memory. The controller reads the instruction information and decodes it to control the data flow in or out of different blocks and the course of serial message processing.
Non-volatile memory can be used for the instruction memory. If we use EPROM or other programmable storages, tiny modification of rewriting parameters in the programmable storages will enable the decoder to support other code rates and code lengths. It is an obvious advantage of this programmable controller.

The messages corresponding to one row of parity check matrix are in a compressed form with four elements, Le., 1) min: the smallest magnitude; 2) submin: the second smallest magnitude; 3) index: the index of the second smallest magnitude; 4) sgn: individual sign of the prior messages in the row; and 5) sgnall: the XOR result of all the sgns in the row. In proposed architecture, the storage of sgnall can be saved; we will elaborate on it in below.
As mentioned above, after the first time process, the messages Lveare obtained and written in L-RAM temporarily. In the second time process, they are read out by the processarray and do not need to be stored again by L-RAM for the next iteration, so the most large row-weight of the parity check matrix is enough for the depth of the L-RAM. The Harddecision RAM stores the hard decision results and propagates them to parity check unit to check whether equation (6) is satisfied.

In the proposed architecture, the updated individual signs of each Lve are not written back immediately, we process them in the second line scan by XOR them with the updated sgnall, then written them back in R-RAM, which results in combining the information of sgnall with each sgn after each line scan, and do not need to store sgnall specially.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper we present a LDPC decoder for DVB-S2 by using the Min-Sum algorithm and SIMD architecture, together with the TDMP schedule, it enables good performance and low complexity. Compared with the published works, our decoder has the advantage of area efficiency.
