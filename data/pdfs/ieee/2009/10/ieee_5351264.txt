Algorithms for LDPC Codes

Abstract—This paper presents two novel iterative reliabilitybased majority-logic algorithms for decoding LDPC codes. Both algorithms are binary message-passing algorithms and require only logical operations and integer additions. Consequently, they can be implemented with simple combinational logic circuits. They either outperform or perform just well as the existing weighted bit-flipping or other reliability-based decoding algorithms for LDPC codes in error performance with a faster rate of decoding convergence and less decoding complexity. Compared to the sum-product algorithm for LDPC codes, they offer effective trade-offs between performance and decoding complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION LDPC codes were first discovered by Gallager in 1962 [1] and then rediscovered in late 1990’s [2], [3]. Ever since their rediscovery, a great deal of research effort has been expended in design, construction, encoding, decoding, generalizations, performance analysis and applications of these codes. Hundreds of papers have been published on these subjects, many classes of well performing LDPC codes have been constructed, and various algorithms for decoding these codes have been devised. The decoding algorithms that have been devised provide a wide spectrum of trade-offs between error performance, decoding complexity and decoding speed. This paper presents two novel reliability-based iterative decoding algorithms for LDPC codes that provide efficient tradeoffs between performance and decoding complexity. These algorithms are devised based on the simple orthogonal checksums concept used in the one-step majority-logic decoding (OSMLGD) algorithm [4] in conjunction with some kind of reliability measures of received bits. The reliability measure of each received symbol is improved through decoding iterations. These algorithms require very low computational complexity and yet achieve very good error performance for LDPC codes. Decoding with these algorithms also converges very fast. They either outperform or perform just as well as the existing bitflipping (BF) or weighted bit-flipping (WBF) algorithms and

Next, we compare the decoding complexity of the ISRB-

MLGD-algorithm with that of some well known BF- and

WBF-algorithms for LDPC codes. The simplest BF-algorithm

was proposed by Kou, Lin and Fossorier in [7] and it is a

hard-decision decoding algorithm. For convenience, we call it

KLF-BF- algorithm. It is a hard-decision decoding algorithm.

Decoding with the KLF-BF-algorithm, the received bits with

the largest parity-check failures are flipped in each iteration.

Among the existing WBF-algorithms, the best one in terms of

error performance is the algorithm proposed by Jiang, Zhao,

Shi and Chen in [9], called the JZSC-WBF algorithm, and the

one that performs close to the JZSC-BF-algorithm but requires

less computational complexity is the algorithm proposed by

Zhang and Fossorier in [10], called the ZF-WBF algorithm. Both JZSC-WBF and ZF-WBF algorithms flip one bit at a time in decoding and they converge slowly. They take a large number of iterations (say 100 iterations) for the decoding to converge. Both algorithms require only binary logical operations and integer additions and hence can be implemented with simple combinational logic circuits using flip-flops to store reliability measures of the received bits.
