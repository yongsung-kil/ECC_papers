On Asymptotic Ensemble Weight Enumerators of LDPC-Like Codes
Chung-Li Wang, Student Member, IEEE, and Marc P. C. Fossorier, Fellow, IEEE

AbstractвҖ”For LDPC-like codes such as LDPC, GLDPC, and DGLDPC codes, it is well known that the error floor can be caused by the codewords of small weights or stopping sets of small sizes. In this paper, we investigate the computation of asymptotic weight enumerators such that it becomes a convenient tool to determine a good distribution of code ensembles. In addition, by analyzing the first order approximation, we derive a condition to obtain a negative asymptotic growth rate of the codewords of small linear-sized weights, which is an important constraint for distribution optimization. Also the weight enumerators of turbo and repeat-accumulate codes are investigated. Furthermore, we extend our results to nonbinary DGLDPC codes. Generalization to N -layer and convolutional code based LDPC-like codes are also developed.
Index TermsвҖ”Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, Generalized LDPC codes, Ensemble weight enumerator, Nonbinary LDPC codes, Degree distribution, Convex optimization, Dual variables.

I. INTRODUCTION

L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes were proposed by Gallager [1] in the 1960s. Tanner described the decoding of LDPC codes using a Tanner graph [2] and proposed generalized LDPC (GLDPC). In [11], doubly generalized LDPC (DGLDPC) codes were introduced. By increasing the complexity of variable nodes, check nodes, or both, the error floor at high SNR can become low enough for general applications. Nonbinary LDPC codes were investigated in [19], where it was shown that an LDPC code constructed over a higher order Galois field provides better performance and has a larger minimum distance than its binary counterpart. Similar conclusions were obtained in [25]. In addition, some of well-known codes using convolutional codes as component codes, such as parallel and serial turbo codes [3] [4] and repeat-accumulate codes [5], can also be represented within this framework. This paper considers all these codes as LDPClike codes.
The code performance highly depends on its weight distribution, and the number of small weight codewords affects the BER at high SNR. As an asymptotic analysis is a tractable method for turbo-like codes, we are interested in the asymptotic properties of the average weight enumerating function (WEF) of LDPC-like codes when the code length goes to infinity. In this paper, we integrate these works and provide a simple condition for a negative asymptotic growth rate of the codewords of small linear-sized weights, for both binary and nonbinary codes, based on an approximation method that was originally proposed in [14] and refined in this paper. The paper is organized as follows. First, we introduce notations and derive an asymptotic analysis of weight enumerators of the variable node and check node sides for binary codes. In the following two sections, a computation method of the asymptotic exponential growth rate of weight enumerators is presented, using dual variables. Next, we conclude our discussion with a simple condition for binary codes and generalize our results to N -layer codes and nonbinary codes. In the final section, we develop the weight enumerator of LDPC-like codes using convolutional codes as component codes, which model turbo codes and repeat-accumulate codes.
