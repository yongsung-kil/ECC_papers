Efficient Encoding of QC-LDPC Codes Related to Cyclic MDS Codes
Norifumi Kamiya, Member, IEEE , and Eisaku Sasaki

Abstract‚ÄĒIn this paper, we present an efficient systematic encoding algorithm for quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes that are related to cyclic maximum-distance separable (MDS) codes. The algorithm offers linear time complexity, and it can be easily implemented by using polynomial multiplication and division circuits. We show that the division polynomials can be completely characterized by their zeros and that the sum of the respective numbers of their zeros is equal to the parity-length of the codes.
Index Terms‚ÄĒQC-LDPC codes, Cyclic MDS codes, Lineartime encoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
R ECENTLY, a number of LDPC codes constructed with the help of classical algebraic coding theory have been presented (cf. [1], [5], [7], [9], [12], [18]). It has been shown in [1] that q-ary Reed-Solomon codes of dimension two can be used to construct LDPC codes with good error-performance. Shown in [9] and [18] is that finite fields can be successfully used to construct several classes of binary and non-binary QC-LDPC codes. Further, several classes of good QC-LDPC codes derived on the basis of finite geometry codes have been presented in [5], [7], and the relationship between the codes and q-ary MDS codes of length q + 1 has been clarified [5]. The parity-check matrices of many of these algebraic LDPC codes will not be full-rank, unlike randomly constructed sparse parity-check matrices, which tend to be full-rank or almost full-rank. Thus, these codes will have higher coding rates than those from randomly generated matrices of the same size. The performance of regular and semi-regular LDPC codes can be enhanced by designing codes to have redundant sparse paritycheck matrix [7], [13].
This paper addresses the issue of efficient systematic encoding of the algebraic QC-LDPC codes that are related to cyclic MDS codes. In general, encoding can be performed by multiplying an information vector with a generator matrix (or with a lower triangular parity-check matrix). Richardson et al. [16] have presented a method for finding a parity-check matrix in an approximate lower triangular form from a given sparse parity-check matrix. The encoding method presented in [16] is general and thus can be applied to any LDPC codes.

It involves, however, a procedure with quadratic complexity. Furthermore for QC-LDPC codes, encoding methods which make full use of its cyclic symmetry can be expected and would appear to be promising, since such encoding methods might be easily implemented by using shift-registers. With regard to encoding of QC-LDPC codes, Li et al. [10] have presented methods for finding a generator matrix in a block-circulant form from a given block-circulant parity-check matrix. While a block-circulant generator matrix obtained using these methods yields efficient encoding implementations because of its cyclic symmetry, there is no guarantee that the generator matrix found will be in a systematic form when the parity-check matrix has a number of redundant rows, such as does that obtained from cyclic MDS codes. In such a case, the encoding will not be systematic, in the sense that there will be information bits which do not appear directly as a part of the encoded codewords.
In this paper, we show that the algebraic nature of the QCLDPC codes related to cyclic MDS codes makes it possible to design a systematic encoding algorithm with linear time complexity. The algorithm can be easily implemented by using polynomial multiplication and division circuits. The division polynomials can be completely characterized by their zeros, and the sum of the respective numbers of their zeros will be equal to the parity-length of the codes. This paper is organized in the following way. In Section II, we present and investigate a class of QC-LDPC codes obtained from a matrix related to cyclic MDS codes of length q + 1, where q is a power of 2. We determine explicitly the dimension of the codes and show that their parity-check matrix has a number of redundant rows. The codes are equivalent to those presented previously in [5] in the sense that the former can be obtained from the latter by applying a fixed permutation. In Section III, we present an efficient encoding algorithm for these codes. In Section IV, we consider a class of QC-LDPC codes related to a cyclic MDS code of length q ‚ąí 1, a class which was originally presented by Djurdjevic et al. [1] and Lan et al. [9]. We show that the encoding algorithm presented in Section III can also be applied to these codes.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have investigated a class of binary QCLDPC codes obtained from cyclic MDS codes. The paritycheck matrices of these codes are not full-rank, unlike randomly constructed block-circulant parity-check matrices, which tend to be either full-rank or almost full-rank. We have presented an explicit formula for the dimension of the codes and shown that their parity-check matrices can be put into a triangular form and that this yields an efficient encoding implementation. The encoding is systematic and has linear time complexity. A special case of the encoding implementation has been reported for a microwave radio system in [6]. The codes employed there are of length 4095 and dimensions 4023 ‚ąí 42r, r = 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, which are equivalent to Cr(65), r = 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, as noted in Section II. The results of this paper can be regarded as a generalization of those in [6].
