An Improved Algorithm for Constructing QC-LDPC Codes Based on the PEG Algorithm

Zhiyong Fan, Weibo Zhang, Xingcheng Liu, and Haohui Cheng Department of Electronic and Communications Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University
Guangzhou, Guangdong, 510275, China {scuteefzy; zhangweibo.sysu; isslxc}@gmail.com

Abstract—The Progressive edge-growth (PEG) algorithm was
proven to be a simple and effective approach in designing Lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes. However, the Tanner graph constructed with the PEG algorithm is non-structured, which leads to random distributions of the positions of 1’s in the corresponding parity-check matrix. In this paper, an improved algorithm based on the PEG algorithm for constructing quasicyclic (QC) LDPC codes is proposed. The proposed algorithm can eliminate short cycles efficiently and gain low error-floors. What is more important, the proposed QC-LDPC codes construction method is hardware-friendly because of its quasicyclic structure. Our simulation results demonstrate that the codes constructed with the proposed algorithm has better performance than the PEG algorithm over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel and Rayleigh flat fading channel.
Keywords-QC-LDPC code; PEG algorithm; parity-check matrix; error-floor

performance. In this paper, we proposed a method to construct QC-LDPC matrix with unreliable factor. With this method we can avoid the unreliable messages transferring as far as possible.
The structure of this paper is as follows, Sections II and III introduce the definition of LDPC codes and the original PEG algorithm. The progressed construction method of QC-LDPC codes base on PEG algorithm will be introduced in Section IV. Simulation results are presented in Section V. Finally, Section VI is devoted to our conclusions.

I.

INTRODUCTION

Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are originally proposed by Gallager [1] in 1962, and rediscovered by Mackay and Neal [2] in 1996. Similar to Turbo codes, LDPC codes are also a class of Shannon limit approaching codes [2][3][4] when decoding with soft-decision belief propagation algorithms such as the sum-product algorithm. Many LDPC codes were constructed by random construction methods and quite a lot of memories are needed for storing parity-check matrices when the length of codes employed is very long. It is also hard to encode such LDPC codes efficiently because they have no fixed structure.

The QC-LDPC code is a class of LDPC codes that can reduce the complexity of encoding and decoding, and lower the requirements of memory. These codes can be encoded with simple shift registers and decoded in parallel [5]. At present, most QC-LDPC codes are constructed based on geometry and algebra theories [6][7][8]. However, the codeword length and code rate defined by the parity-check matrices constructed with these theories and the corresponding design rules are restricted by the parity-check matrix, can not be pre-defined and chosen. So the code parameters can not be determined flexibly. For these reasons, random construction methods were employed to construct QC-LDPC code [9]. III.

THE PEG ALGORITHM

PEG algorithm, proposed by X. Hu, et al. [11], is a construction method of LDPC codes which tries to optimize the girth during the edge growth. The girth of a Tanner graph is the length of the shortest cycle in the graph. The PEG algorithm can keep the cycle length as large as possible each time a new edge is placed, though it can not guarantee the Tanner graph is the best. In this algorithm, a few parameters such as the number of check nodes, the number of symbol nodes and the degree distribution of symbol nodes have to be given before construction. The LDPC codes generated with the above algorithm have a large girth, which is one of the important factors that affect the error correction performance of LDPC codes. In decoding LDPC codes, the sum-product decoding algorithm is based on the assumption that the messages transferred between nodes are independent. However, the existence of cycles in the Tanner graph breaks the independence assumption, which will affect the decoding accuracy and decoding convergence speed. Therefore, a large girth, which the PEG algorithm can guarantee in LDPC code construction, is an important indicator for designing good LDPC codes.

IV.

CONSTRUCTION OF PROPOSED QC-LDPC CODES

The parity-check matrix H of the proposed QC-LDPC codes can be characterized with two small matrices, M and P.

The matrix M of dimension g×t is called the mother matrix, and the matrix P of dimension g×t is called the shift-times matrix. If a parity-check matrix of dimension m×n is required to be constructed with the circulant sub-matrices of dimension L×L, the parameters g and t can be computed according to the expressions g = m/L and t=n/L. Here, the cyclic-shift identity matrix and the full-zero matrix of dimension L×L are used as the sub-matrix of parity-check matrix to avoid creating the cycle set [10] inside the sub-matrix.
The mother matrix M of dimension g×t can be put in shape based on the PEG algorithm. Then the parity-check matrix H can be constructed through replacing each element ‘0’ in matrix M with a full-zero matrix of dimension L×L and each element ‘1’ in matrix M with a cyclic-shift identity matrix of dimension L×L. Each element in matrix P represents the shift times of the identity matrix in the parity-check matrix H. Before defining the shift-times matrix P, it is required to realize the property of the circulant sub-matrix. Each row (column) in a circulant sub-matrix has equivalent effect to the girth. This characteristic efficiently simplifies the process of sub-matrix construction because the construction can be finished once the construction of the first row (column) of a circulant sub-matrix is finished. We define the first column of a sub-matrix as the critical column of the sub-matrix, and the other columns as relative columns of the critical column. According to the structural relationship between matrix M and check matrix H, we similarly define the j-th column of H as the critical column of H, and the (j+1)-th to (j+L-1)-th columns of H as the relative columns of critical column j, where j=r×L, L is the dimension of sub-matrix and r∈{0, 1, …, t-1}.
It is well known that cycles, especially short ones in the Tanner graph are detrimental to the error correction performance of LDPC codes. Generally speaking, the nodes constructing shorter cycle have worse reliability on the messages, which also means worse effect on the performance of LDPC codes. So we introduce a coefficient β called unreliable factor to measure the reliability of messages of a node. The β of a node constructing cycles relate to the number of cycles and the cycle’s length, the more the cycles is and the shorter the length of cycles is, the larger the magnitude of β is, which means less reliability of the messages from this node.

The so-constructed parity-check matrix H has the property of quasi-cyclic and flexible matrix-parameters. We figure out that the proposed algorithm can eliminate short cycles efficiently and gain low error-floors. As we can see in the simulation, the matrix constructed with the proposed algorithm has excellent performance over AWGN channel and Rayleigh flat fading channel. Far more important is that the proposed QC-LDPC codes are more hardware-friendly because of its quasi-cyclic structure.

V.

SIMULATION RESULTS

In our simulation, two groups of LDPC codes with different codeword lengths are constructed, the parameters (n, k) of which are (1008, 504) and (4608, 4096), respectively. Where n

is the codeword length and k (k=n-m) is the information bits length. For these two codes, the Tanner graph is generated with a uniform symbol node degree ds=3. To make the comparison fair, the code words are constructed with the same parameters.
Simulations are carried out with the BPSK modulation over the AWGN channel and Rayleigh flat fading channel. For encoding of LDPC codes, the Gaussian elimination is employed to yield the generator matrix while the sum-product algorithm is adopted for the iterative decoding. The maximum number of iterations for decoders is set to 80.
The comparison of the BER/FER performance between the QC-LDPC codes based on the proposed algorithm and the random LDPC codes generated by the original PEG algorithm [12], both with code rate-1/2 with the BPSK modulation over the AWGN channel, are shown in Fig. 2.
Fig. 2 presents the results of (1008, 504) codes, where the proposed QC-LDPC codes are constructed with the circulant size of 56×56. In this figure, the proposed codes have excellent performance. Especially in high SNR region, the proposed codes gain about 0.1dB compared with the LDPC codes constructed with the random PEG algorithm. Also, the proposed algorithm lowers the error-floors significantly.
Fig. 3 demonstrates the performance of code rate-8/9 QCLDPC (4608, 4096) code constructed with dimension L=128 of the circulant sub-matrix with the BPSK modulation over the AWGN channel. As shown in this figure, the BER performance of the proposed QC-LDPC codes also outperforms the LDPC codes constructed with the random PEG algorithm, though it is not significantly.
Fig. 4 shows the BER/FER performance of LDPC codes with n=1008, m=504, L=56 with the BPSK modulation over Rayleigh flat fading channel. As the figure shown, the proposed codes gain about 0.1dB compared with the LDPC codes constructed with the random PEG algorithm at BER=10-5. This means the proposed QC-LDPC codes also perform better than the codes constructed with the random PEG algorithm over Rayleigh flat fading channel.

VI.

CONCLUSION

This paper proposes an improved construction method of QC-LDPC codes based on the PEG algorithm. The proposed algorithm can eliminate short cycles efficiently and avoid transferring unreliable messages, which improves the performance and lowers error-floors of QC-LDPC codes. Simulation results shows that both the proposed rate-1/2 codes and rate-8/9 codes have better performance than those codes

constructed with the random PEG algorithm. Furthermore, the proposed codes are easy to implement on hardware because of its quasi-cyclic structure. In addition, the parameters of the codes are flexible to be adjusted, compared to the codes generated by other methods of geometry and algebra. Therefore, the QC-LDPC codes constructed by the proposed algorithm are more preferable in applications.
