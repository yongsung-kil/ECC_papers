3. PARITY AUGMENTATION

In this paper we explore alternatives to BP decoding based ex-
plicitly on the idea of increasing the probability of even parity for
each of the checks. Two methods are presented. The first increases
the the probability of error by a direct augmentation method. The second poses an explicit optimization problem for which gradient ascent (or other line search methods) may be employed. The performance of these algorithms is compared with BP decoding. While BP is still a generally superior, there are regimes (such as low available computation) where these methods provide lower
complexity than BP with no performance loss. Furthermore, they
make use of only row information from the parity check matrix, alleviating some ot the hardware complexity ot representing the
interleavers.

as to increase the probability of even parity for those bits. As a
concrete example we explain it here for the case of K = 3 bits.

7. CONCLUSIONS
Parity augmentation has been shown to exhibit lower complexity than belief propagation for decoding LDPC codes while still retaining strong decoding behavior. The performance depends highly on the number of bits involved in PE, providing another indication why such decoding methods are primarily successful on sparse matrices. We proved that every iteration of the augmentation step
increases PE, and that at most one probability may flip per iteration. We also established on O(K) per check complexity by means
of Gallager's lemma.
