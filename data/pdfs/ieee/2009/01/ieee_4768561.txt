Design of Irregular LDPC Codes for BIAWGN Channels with SNR Mismatch
Hamid Saeedi, Member, IEEE, and Amir H. Banihashemi, Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—Belief propagation (BP) algorithm for decoding lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes over a binary input additive white Gaussian noise (BIAWGN) channel requires the knowledge of the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) at the receiver to achieve its ultimate performance. An erroneous estimation or the absence of a perfect knowledge of the SNR at the decoder is referred to as “SNR mismatch”. SNR mismatch can significantly degrade the performance of LDPC codes decoded by the BP algorithm. In this paper, using extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) charts, we design irregular LDPC codes that perform better (have a lower SNR threshold) in the presence of mismatch compared to the conventionally designed irregular LDPC codes that are optimized for zero mismatch. Considering that min-sum (MS) algorithm is the limit of BP with infinite SNR over-estimation, the EXIT functions generated in this work can also be used for the efficient analysis and design of LDPC codes under the MS algorithm.
Index Terms—Irregular LDPC codes, signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) mismatch, channel estimation error, LDPC code design, EXIT charts, belief propagation (BP) algorithm, min-sum algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION AND MOTIVATION
I N the belief propagation (BP) algorithm for decoding LDPC codes over a binary input additive white Gaussian noise (BIAWGN) channel, the knowledge of the channel signal to noise ratio (SNR) is necessary to achieve the ultimate performance. Estimating SNR accurately however may be a difficult task. This motivates the design of codes or decoding algorithms that are robust against SNR estimation errors. In [1], [2], modifications of min-sum (MS) algorithm [3], that perform close to BP for irregular codes, have been devised. Similar to MS, the modifications do not require the knowledge of the channel SNR and are thus robust against SNR estimation errors. The approach of [1] is to scale the MS messages at the output of check nodes by a factor that is optimized for a given degree distribution and in each iteration. In [2], the MS messages at the output of both the variable nodes and the check nodes are scaled or offset. The scaling and offset factors are optimized for a given degree distribution in [2], and although they remain unchanged throughout the

In this work, we use a different approach to mitigate the effects of SNR estimation errors for irregular codes. Instead of the decoding algorithm, we focus on the design of codes for BP that are robust against SNR mismatch. Our results, both asymptotically and at finite lengths, show that at the expense of a slight loss of performance at the absence of SNR estimation errors, one can design codes that are significantly more robust against such errors.
Consider a BIAWGN channel with input X that can take values ±1, and has output Y = X +N , where N is a Gaussian random variable with mean zero and variance σn2 , independent of X. In the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) domain, the input to the BP algorithm for each bit should be 2Y /σn2 . Starting from such initial messages, which is based on the perfect knowledge of the noise power σn2 , the BP algorithm performs optimally in the sense that it provides the a-posteriori probabilities (APP) for bits if the Tanner graph for the code is tree-like (cyclefree). Assuming that the all-one code word is transmitted, the input LLR values for all the bits have the distribution N (2/σn2 , 4/σn2 ), i.e., Gaussian with mean 2/σn2 and variance 4/σn2 [4]. We call such a distribution, with the variance-tomean ratio equal to two, consistent.1
Consider the case where the noise power is estimated erroneously at the decoder as σe2s. This is referred to as “SNR mismatch,” and the SNR mismatch ratio η is defined by σn2 /σe2s. The value of η in dB is called “SNR offset”. The SNR is said to be over-estimated (under-estimated) if σe2s < σn2 (σe2s > σn2 ). In the presence of SNR mismatch, the input LLR values to the BP algorithm have the distribution N (2/σe2s, 4σn2 /σe4s), which is not consistent anymore.
For turbo codes and LDPC codes of large length, it is wellknown that any offset value causes the performance of BP to degrade and that SNR under-estimation is more detrimental than SNR over-estimation [5]-[10]. Similar results for bitinterleaved coded modulation with iterative decoding (BICMID) have been obtained in [11]. The performance of regular and irregular LDPC codes under the BP algorithm in the presence of SNR mismatch is analyzed in [12]. It is observed that the sensitivity to mismatch depends much on the code ensemble degree distributions. For regular codes of fixed rate, the sensitivity grows by increasing variable node and check

Different methods have been proposed for designing irregular LDPC codes. One well-known method is density evolution [4]. For codes on BIAWGN channel however, this method has high complexity. Other methods, such as those in [14], [15], [16], have been proposed based on Gaussian approximation of densities to reduce the complexity. In particular, the approximation proposed in [15], [16] is based on keeping track of the mutual information between the transmitted bit and the output messages of variable nodes and check nodes through extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) charts. This method is based on the assumption that there is no SNR mismatch and thus the initial messages at the input of the decoding algorithm are consistent. Moreover, the messages maintain this condition throughout the iterations [4]. In the mismatch case, since the variance-to-mean ratio of the initial Gaussian message is no longer 2 and since this ratio for the exchanged messages between the variable nodes and the check nodes changes with iterations, the EXIT charts can no longer be generated by the method of [15], [16].

II. PROBLEM FORMULATION
For a given ensemble of codes and a given SNR offset η, SNR threshold α(η) is defined as the infimum of all channel SNR values such that the bit-error rate (BER) converges to
zero as the iteration number tends to infinity. It is demonstrated in [12] that for any value of η, α(η) > α(0), and the difference Δα(η) = α(η) − α(0) (dB) is used to measure the sensitivity of an ensemble to mismatch, i.e., the larger Δα(η), the more sensitive the ensemble is at an SNR offset of η.
Consider an SNR offset range of interest [η1, η2], where η1 and η2 are real numbers, usually negative and positive respectively. In this paper, we are interested in designing an
irregular code ensemble of certain rate for which the largest SNR threshold over the offset range [η1, η2] is minimized over all possible degree distributions. This is a min-max
optimization problem. In most cases, the search over the
degree distributions is constrained by the maximum variable and check degrees or by certain constraints on the degrees of
the constituent variable and check nodes.
It is well-known that SNR offset degrades the performance of BP, and that the larger the offset, the worse the performance. Therefore, if 0 < η1 < η2 (η1 < η2 < 0), the problem is reduced to minimizing α(η2) (α(η1)). It is also well-known that under-estimation of SNR is more deteriorating than overestimation [5]-[10]. In the case that η1 < 0 < η2 and η1 ≤ −η2, the optimization problem is then reduced to minimizing α(η1). This includes the special case of η1 = −η2, which may be of more practical interest. Finally, for the case where η1 < 0 < η2 and η1 > −η2, the problem reduces to minimizing the maximum of α(η1) and α(η2).
