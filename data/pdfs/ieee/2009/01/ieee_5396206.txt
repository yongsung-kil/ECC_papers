Full Custom Design of Comparators for LDPC decoder

FAN Yong-jie TAN Yue-kang JIANG Xiao-bo
School of Electronic and Information Engineering South China University of Technology Guangzhou, China 510641 Email: tanyuekang@gmail.com

Abstract—Comparator is the key module in LDPC decoder that consumes most of the power and area. In this paper, a new comparator based on pass transistors and dynamic logic is proposed to decrease the power and the area. The comparator is implemented with full custom design at SMIC 0.18 1P6M technology. The simulation result of layout shows that the power drops by 11.4% and the area is reduced by 30% compared with the circuit mentioned in reference which we implement with the same technology.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low density parity check (LDPC) was designed by Gallager in 1962[1]. It is broadly used in different communication system. However, the implementation of LDPC decoder is complex and power consuming. This affects its application. Therefore, it is important to decrease the power and area of the decoder [2-3]. In the decoder, comparator is the most complex and consumes most of the power. For higher operational speed and lower complexity, some innovation and improvement is necessary. What’s more, comparator is also one of the most basic and important modules in digital system. It’s widely used in calculation and communication region.
Traditional comparator circuit is based on truth table, leading to large area, low speed and more power. C-H, Hang [4] developed a priority encoder based on logic and module, which could decrease logical depth. S-W Cheng [5] employed the conditional sum adder to make the implementation more efficient. J-Y Kim and H-J Yoo [6] proposed a design without arithmetic operation, which reduces the area and power in further. Since asynchronous circuit has the potential in low power consumption, comparator based on asynchronous circuit [7] becomes more and more important in research.
The comparators mentioned above improve their performance by the design methodology, but their basic cells like AND, OR, NOT gate are based on complementary CMOS. Compared with traditional CMOS design, pass transistors [8] can decrease the area and power efficiently. The all N-type pass transistors technology, which can decrease the transistor number needed by allowing the original input to drive the gate and the source, is adopted in this design, realizing the specific function with fewer transistors. In this paper, the comparator based on pass transistors is advanced which reduces 30% of

area and drops the power by 11.4% compared to the comparator mentioned in reference.
There are three parts in this comparator, including preencoder circuit, bitwise competition logic and decision logic. Pre-encoder circuit encodes the inputs A and B bit by bit for A*=A B and B*= B A . Pre-encoder also excludes the
situation that two inputs values are 1 at the same time, which means that it allows only one of them can be 1 in any bit. The single bitwise competition logic generates 8 clock signals continuously, which activate the comparison signal from MSB to LSB bit by bit, making every bitwise competition logic circuit generate the result to the decision logic. So the circuit can reflect the result of the bitwise competition logic from MSB to LSB. Compared with the comparator in reference [6], the comparator based on pass transistors can reduce the area and power efficiently.
II. COMPARATOR DESIGN
There are three parts in this design: pre-encoder, bitwise competition logic, decision logic. Pre-encoder encodes the two inputs A and B bit by bit to reduce the states. Bitwise competition logic compares the datas encoded from MSB to LSB. At last the result is acquired from decision logic.
A. Pre-encoder based on pass transistors
Pre-encoder encodes the two inputs A and B bit by bit. It makes A[i] = B[i] = 0 if A[i] = B[i], and nothing is changed if A[i] ≠ B[i]. The circuit based on pass transistors is realized in Fig. 1. Here A[i] and B[i] represent the [i] bit of the input datas A and B.

When clk = 0, outA* and outB* are pre-charged to 1. From clk7 to clk0, the interval time between every two continuous clk signal is made by two inverters. When clk = 1, the comparator circuit is activated from MSB to LSB bit by bit. The process above starts from clk7. When the first high signal is detected in bit [i], outA* and outB* pulse low, the output of decision logic Bwin or Awin also pulses low to generate the comparison result. III. PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS OF CIRCUIT
Fig. 7 shows the block diagram of an 8-bit comparator based on pass transistors. The correct comparison result will come out after pre-encoder, bitwise competition logic and decision logic.
The comparator introduced in this paper is realized by SMIC 0.18μm 1P6M metal technology. Data path is realized by full custom design method with pass transistors and CMOS logic unit. The schematic capture of the circuit is done by Schematic Editing of Cadence Virtuoso, and layout design is done by Layout Editing. After layout design, we use Mentor Calibre to do DRC, LVS and PEX, and simulate the SPICE netlist file extracted with RC parameters by Synopsys Hspice. After the repeated design, verification and adjustment, the layout of the whole comparator is shown in Fig. 8.
The circuit is simulated by HSPICE under SMIC 0.18μm 1P6M technology. Fig. 9 shows the situation of clk1, clk0, outA*, outB*, Awin and Bwin when the inputs A=11111101 and B=11111110. Because of the effect of clk1, outB* pulse low, to make Awin in decision logic pulse low, and the decision logic stops the latter signals immediately. With the effect of clk0, though outA* is set low too, the output still reserves the result which is detected first due to the screening of decision logic. As can be seen in the waveform, the function of circuit is correct.

IV. CONCLUSION
Comparators consume most of the power in LDPC decoder circuit. In this paper, one low-power comparator design based on pass transistor is advanced, which can reduce area and power efficiently. Under SMIC 0.18μm 1P6M metal technology, the comparator in this paper reduces 30% area and saves 11.4% of the power compared to the comparator mentioned in the reference, meaning an obvious advantage over existing design. Further research includes: a complete LDPC decoder circuit based on pass transistors; further research in the application of asynchronous circuit [10-11] for LDPC decoder to reduce the circuit’s power consumption.
