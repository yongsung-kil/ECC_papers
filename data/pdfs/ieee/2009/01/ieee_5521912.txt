The Construction of Tanner Graph for Iterative PN Sequence Acquisition

Yanchao Kang, Kai Niu
Key Lab of Universal Wireless Communications, Ministry of Education, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications,China yanchao.kang@gmail.com

Keywords: Pseudonoise(PN) code acquisition, Tanner graph, PEG (Progressive Edge Growth)
Abstract
This paper proposes an improved algorithm of PEG, CPEG (Constrained Progressive Edge Growth), for constructing tanner graphs to represent the constraints of PN sequence, which makes the iterative acquisition method applicable for general PN sequences. And simulation results show that the performance of iterative acquisition using CPEG graph is improved by 2.5dB, and the complexity is reduced to the half.
1 Introduction
The introduction of turbo codes in the mid-1990s marked the beginning of a lot of activities in the field of research, development, and standardization addressing the performance analysis, the design, and the application of iterative decoding in digital communications [1]. The thought of iterative process, also known as Turbo principle, is devoted into many areas in communication and signal processing. An iterative method based on paradigm of message passing on graphical models proposed in [2] is an important technical innovation for achieving PN acquisition.
The traditional technologies for acquiring PN sequence are full search, serial search, and hybrid search. These methods take advantage of correlation feature of PN sequence, which are accomplished by forming correlation between the noisy observation and a local reference generated by the receiver. The main disadvantage of these traditional methods is that they can not achieve fast acquisition with low complexity.
In the approach proposed in [2], no correlation is formed. Rather, the acquisition is approached through the detection of the chips in observed sequences, which is accomplished by the iterative message passing algorithm on graphical model representing the constraints of PN sequence. And a set of local constraints rather than the global constraint are used to establish the graph model. The generating polynomial of PN sequence, i.e. feed-back constraint, was used as the local constraint.
The iterative method can search all the possible PN phases in parallel with complexity significantly lower than optimal full parallel search. However, due to the regular structure of feed-back constraint, the performances of message passing

algorithm and the corresponding iterative acquisition are greatly limited. Hence, this approach is only applicable to the situation when the feedback coefficients of PN sequence are very sparse. When the number of feedback coefficients is lager than four, the girth of the graph constructed using feedback constraint is usually equal to 4, which makes the performance of the proposed method catastrophically degraded.
The iterative message passing algorithm based on graphical model was originally applied to the decoding of LDPC (low density parity check) codes in [3], where the graphical model was called Tanner graph. LDPC codes attracted considerable attention due to the capacity approaching performance [4] and the low decoding complexity. The message passing algorithm based on cycleโfree tanner graph provides optimum decoding, hence itโs natural to minimize the influence of the cycles in the iterative decoding process.
PEG construction is a simple but efficient method for constructing Tanner graph having a large girth for LDPC codes in a best-effort sense in [5]. This method progressively establishes edges between symbol and check nodes in an edge-by-edge manner. Simulation results reveal that the PEG tanner graphs perform better than those constructed randomly under message passing algorithm.
Inspired by the construction of LDPC codes, we propose an algorithm, called CPEG, to construct tanner graph representing the constraints of chips in PN sequence, which makes the iterative acquisition algorithm applicable for general PN sequences. The graphical model presented in [2] is one special case of CPEG algorithm.
This paper is organized as follows. In section 2, we present the signal models and some concepts in graphical model. The CPEG algorithm is proposed in section 3. In section 4, we give some simulation results and come to the conclusion.

3.3 iterative acquisition algorithm using message passing
After the tanner graph has been constructed, we can use the message passing algorithm to estimate the chips in observed m sequences.
The algorithm is carried out on graph. Every node in graph sends messages to its adjacent nodes, and updates the messages it sends out using the messages it receives iteratively. A very important principle is that the message a node sends out along an edge can not contain the message the node receives along the edge.
Figure (3) shows the procedure of message passing algorithm. Symbol node sj sends message msc(j, i) to check node ci, which represents the probability or the LLR (Loglikelihood ratio) of the chip that node sj represents. Check node ci sends message mcs(i, j) to symbol node sj, which also represents the probability information of symbol node sj.

5 Conclusion
The traditional PN sequence acquisition method can not achieve fast acquisition with low complexity. The iterative method proposed in [2] can not be used for general PN sequence due to lack of effective way to construct the constraints for PN sequence.
In this paper, we propose an algorithm called CPEG to construct tanner graph presenting the constraint of PN sequence chips. Simulation results show that CPEG matrix can significantly improve the acquisition performance and reduce the complexity of iterative acquisition method using massage passing algorithm, which makes the iterative algorithm applicable to the PN sequence acquisition in complex mobile communication environments.
