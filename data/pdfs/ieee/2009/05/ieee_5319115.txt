A Novel Log-BP Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes
Jun Wang Shu-Hui Yang College of Photoelectric Information and Communication Engineering
Beijing Information Technology University, Beijing, China
yanhaowong@163.com， yangshuhui2008@yahoo.cn

Abstract — In this paper, we present a simplification of log-BP algorithm which is able to reduce iterative times of decoding algorithm and save the hardware resource of decoder for LDPC codes. This simplification designs an improved layerediterative decoding method without reducing the decoding performance, of which speed the updating of check nodes and variable nodes. Simulation results show that the improved layered-iterative log-BP algorithm with six-time iteration exhibits similar decoding performance to the traditional logBP algorithm with ten-time iteration. When the improved logBP and traditional log-BP both are simulated in ten-time iteration, the SNR of improved layer-iterative method decreases about 0.25 dB than that of traditional log-BP in the
BER of 1e-2.
Key words: LDPC Codes; Belief Propagation; Decoding Algorithm; Layered-iterative; Decoding Performance
Ⅰ. INTRODUCTION
Low density parity check (LDPC) codes ， was introduced first by Gallager[1][2]， had been applied to many wireless and wireline digital communication system due to its good performance that is able to approach to capacity of Shannon limit [3]within 0.0045dB.
Many decoding algorithms[4] for LDPC codes have been the focus of intense research in the recent years. One of the widely used algorithm[5][8] is iterative decoding algorithm based on belief propagation [6], BP algorithm for short, which analyses each decoding bit in reliability, based on parity-check-sum. But it is unsuitable to its hardware implementation, because it employes too much multiplication. So a simplification of BP algorithm that can reduce the complexity of decoding proceeding has been derived by operating in the log-likelihood domain. The log-BP[7] algorithm converted multiplication into addition, which reduce the complexity of calculation.
Based on the log-BP algorithm, this paper presents an improved layered-iterative method which simplifies the standard log-BP algorithm without decreasing the decoding performance. Employing this method may reduce the iterative times and save the hardware resource.

Ⅴ. CONCLUSIONS

Based on traditional log-BP algorithms, this paper presents an improved layered-iterative method, which can simplify the decoding process of traditional log-BP. For the (3,6) regular LDPC code, the improved layered log-BP algorithm with six-time iteration exhibits the similar decoding performance to the traditional log-BP algorithm with ten-time iteration. With the same 10-time iteration, the improved method exhibits higher performance than the traditional algorithm does.
