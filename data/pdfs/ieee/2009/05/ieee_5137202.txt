Construction of Irregular Structured LDPC Codes with Low Encoding Complexity for High-Rate Applications

Puripong Suthisopapan and Virasit Imtawil Department of Electrical Engineering, Faculty of Engineering, Khon Kaen University,
Khon Kaen 40002 Thailand Email: 5050400072@stdmail.kku.ac.th,virasit@kku.ac.th

AbstractโThis paper presents a method to construct irregular structured Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes based on choosing different identity matrices. The parity-check matrix of the proposed code can be easily generated with the flexible parameters such as code rate, block length or sparsity. The proposed code is well structured which can provide a lowcomplexity implementation. The encoder of the proposed code has linear encoding complexity making it suitable for hardware implementation. When iteratively decoded with the sum-product algorithm (SPA), the constructed irregular LDPC codes exhibit a very good performance especially for high rate codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a class of linear block codes which was first introduced by Gallager [1] and was rediscovered by MacKay and Neal [2]. These codes have been adopted in a wide range of applications [3]. The name of these codes comes from the characteristic of their paritycheck matrix which contains only a few non-zero elements in comparison to the number of zero elements. Their main advantage is that they provide a performance which is very close to the theoretical limit under iterative decoding for a many different channels.
The main disadvantage of LDPC codes is high encoding complexity. T.Richardson and R.Urbanke [4] presented the techniques that can resolve the encoding problem. They stated that LDPC codes can be encoded using the parity-check matrix directly by transforming the parity-check matrix into upper triangular form and using back substitution to find its corresponding parity bits.
Structured LDPC codes can be used to significantly reduce the encoding problem and simplify hardware implementation. Some of the prominent structured LDPC codes are introduced here. A. Fan proposed a class of algebraically constructed array codes that are called array LDPC (ALDPC) codes [5] and which can be decoded with iterative decoding. Subsequent to Fanโs work, E.Eleftheriou and S.Oยจ lcยธer [6] proposed a modified array LDPC (MAC) codes that can be easily encoded because their parity-check matrix is in upper triangular form. Several classes of structured LDPC codes are discovered, such as the recent works of Y. Kai et al. [7] and G. Liva et al. [8].
Luby et al. [9] showed that the irregular LDPC codes outperform the regular LDPC codes. The performance of general LDPC codes with iterative decoding depends on the

girth of the codes. The girth of an LDPC code is defined as the length of the shortest cycle in its Tanner graph [10]. If the overlap of non-zero elements between any two columns of parity-check matrix is one, the parity-check matrix has no cycle of length 4.
Although low-rate LDPC codes perform very close to the theoretical limit, in some applications such as digital subscriber line or magnetic storage, high-rate LDPC codes must be considered. In this paper, a class of irregular structured LDPC code with low encoding complexity is introduced. The parity-check matrix of the proposed code can be constructed with various lengths and rates with a guarantee of no cycle of length 4.
The remainder of the paper is outlined as follows. Section II provides the necessary background for the subsequent sections. Section III presents the code-design method and necessary conditions. The encoding complexity of the proposed code is discussed in Section IV and the computer simulations are shown in Section V. Finally, some concluding remarks are presented in Section VI.
II. DEFINITION AND BACKGROUND
In this section, the principle of LDPC codes is briefly introduced. As mentioned above, LDPC codes are a class of linear block codes so LDPC codes can be described in the same way as classical linear block codes. Dimension or message length of LDPC codes are denoted by k and length (usually called block length) of LDPC codes are denoted by n. The code rate of LDPC codes are denoted by R = k/n. The message and codeword are conventionally denoted by m = [m1, m2, . . . , mk] and c = [c1, c2, . . . , cn] respectively. The parities are denoted by u = [u1, u2, . . . , unโk], where the number of elements is equal to the number of parity bits. It is known that a linear block code can be defined by generator and parity-check matrix, so let G and H represent generator and parity-check matrix of LDPC code respectively.
The biggest difference between LDPC codes and classical linear block codes is how they are decoded. LDPC codes are decoded iteratively using a graphical representation of their parity-check matrix which is often called sum product algorithm [11]. VI. CONCLUSION
In this work a family of irregular structured LDPC codes based on choosing different identity matrices has been proposed. The parity-check matrices constructed by the proposed method have the following advantages: 1) Easily constructed 2) The design provides LDPC codes with various lengths and rates 3) The proposed codes can be constructed without girth-4 and they perform well with the sum-product decoding algorithm 4) Encoding process can be obtained without the need to compute G matrix and the complexity of encoding is linear with block length. We observe that the LDPC codes generated by the proposed procedure perform very similar to MAC codes with the same block length and rate. In this paper, the performance of the LDPC codes generated by more or less than three submatrices is not shown in this paper and will be further explored as the future work.
