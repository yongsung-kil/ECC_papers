Optimized Decoding Schedule for Irregular LDPC Codes
Xuwei Chen*, Hui Yu, Youyun Xu
*Institute of Wireless Communication Technology Department of Electronic Engineering
Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, 200240 Email: xiaoweizai@sjtu.edu.cn

Keywords: low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, layered decoding schedule, irregular, regular
Abstract
Recently, the concept of layered decoding schedule for Low-Density Parity-Check codes has been introduced. But the advantage of layered decoding schedule for irregular LDPC codes is not as obvious as regular LDPC codes. The major drawback of the layered irregular LDPC codes versus the layered regular LDPC codes is its comparative low convergence decoding speed. Simulation results show that different bit node degrees of irregular LDPC codes significantly contribute to the decoding performance. The decoding performance of bit nodes with different degrees is of great difference. Bit nodes with small degrees always converge slower than larger ones. In this paper, we propose an optimized decoding schedule for irregular LDPC codes to accelerate the decoding convergence rate. Meanwhile, for the irregular LDPC decoder, the faster decoding speed means a higher throughput and almost the same error performance.

Low-Density Parity-Check codes were first proposed by Gallager in the 1962[1] and were brought back into prominence in the late nineties by MacKay and Neal[2] due to the excellent error-correction capability close to Shannon limit under belief-propagation[3] or the sum-product decoding algorithm. Nowadays, more and more wireless communication standards, e,g, DVB-S2[4], 802.16e[5], 802.11n[6], WiFi, adopt LDPC for the channel coding scheme. It was shown that decoding performance of irregular LDPC codes has exceeded Turbo codes and become one of the best channel coding [7]. The novel contribution of this paper is that we propose a new decoding schedule for irregular LDPC codes. The simulation results show this optimized decoding schedule for irregular LDPC codes achieves significantly complexity reduction, with no performance loss. It significantly gains the performance/complexity trade-off. Moreover, for a given code, the decoder can also get a great improvement in throughput by taking this decoding schedule.

5 Conclusion
In this paper, the decoding convergence speed of irregular LDPC codes is considered. An optimized decoding schedule has been proposed to accelerate the convergence speed of irregular LDPC codes. This new schedule offers different trade-off performance versus complexity based on the CLBP algorithm. Simulation results for a random construction irregular LDPC codes of rate=0.5 and length N=2304 show that the degradations introduced by the optimized decoding schedule under 10 iterations compared with standard schedule under 20 iterations is below 0.05dB. And for 802.16e LDPC codes of rate=0.75 and length N=2304, the new decoding schedule also shows a good result. For the two irregular LDPC codes, both up to 25% complexity reduction can be obtained without any significant degradation of the performance.
