A Low-complexity Layered Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes

Wang Zhongxun
Institute of science and technology for opto-electronic information
Yan Tai University Yantai 264005 China e-mail: ytwzx3@tom.com
Abstract—A Low-complexity Layered Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes is proposed.Our modified algorithm reduces the number of operations in variable nodes to lower LDPC decoder power consumption. The modified parts of LDPC decoder architecture is also described. Simulation results show that comparing with SPA and MSA, our algorithm reducing the number of operations nearly to 60% with little performance loss.
Keywords-LDPC code; Layered Min-sum Algorithm; Lazy scheduling;
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, which have channel capacity approaching performance, were first invented by Gallager in 1962 [1] and is crucial for the mobile applications. LDPC decoding requires huge number of messages passed between check nodes and bit nodes with complex operation performed. To reducing the complexity of LDPC decoding and lower power consumption is to reduce the total number of operations required to decode a LDPC codeword. A method for decoding Low Density Parity Check Codes (LDPC) is the sum-product algorithm proposed by Gallager(Gallager, 1962). Results show that there is no significant difference in BER performance between the optimal and the proposed algorithm. It is difficulty for the decoding hardware to account the hyperbolic
( ) cotangent function Φ • in check nodes. It could reduce ( ) greatly complexity by min(x) replacing Φ • in Layered
decoding with min-sum algorithm with little performance loss[2][3][4].

In LDPC decoding, a large percentage of variable nodes reaches high reliability within only a few iterations in two phase decoding. When the magnitude of total information reaches high confidence, putting these variable nodes into sleep mode for the consequent iterations will have little impact on the correctness of the decoding result. By disabling the incoming and outgoing message update of these variable nodes, considerable amount of operations can be saved. While L(Q) reaches a high reliability, sign bit of L(r) will not affected by L(q) and (3) could be simplified to (7).This simplification saves one memory read access to check information.
Appropriate threshold Tv can be chosen based on the tradeoff between the amount of operation reduction and BER performance. However, if the threshold is chosen too low, error floor appears due to the wrong decisions in the processing nodes. We proposed an effective solution to eliminate error floor effect. The decoder wakes up the sleeping nodes periodically to update their messages. The
update interval of wake up iterations is specified by iu .The exec (Tv , L(Q), iu ) logic determines whether the variable
node should participate in the process of update if the L(Q)
is less then Tv or current iteration reaches iu . All nodes is
waken up to update messages for one iteration. After such iterations, all nodes decide their working status based on the newly updated information.

V. SIMULATION RESULTS
To evaluate the algorithm, simulations were performed using the LDPC codes with a code length of 2304 bits and a code rate of 1/2. AWGN channel, BPSK modulation and maximum 30 iterations are used in our simulation. The simulation results show that our improved algorithm
( R − L − MSA, Tv = 10, iu = 5) reducing the variable node
computation nearly 60 percent.
