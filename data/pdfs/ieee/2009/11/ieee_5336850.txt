Linear LLR Approximation for
Iterative Decoding on Wireless Channels
Raman Yazdani, Student Member, IEEE, and Masoud Ardakani, Member, IEEE
AbstractвАФOn a fading channel with no channel state infor-
mation at the receiver, true log-likelihood ratios (LLR) are
complicated functions of the channel output. It is assumed in
the literature that the power of the additive noise is known and
the expected value of the fading gain is used in a linear function
of the channel output to find approximate LLRs. This approach,
however, is not optimal in the sense of bit error rate performance.
In this paper, we introduce a measure of accuracy for the
approximate LLRs based on their probability density function
and we show that this measure provides a very convenient tool
for finding good approximate LLRs. Assuming that the power of
the additive noise is known, and using the proposed measure, we
find a linear LLR approximation whose performance is extremely
close to that of the true LLR calculation on an uncorrelated
Rayleigh fading channel. These results are then extended to the
case that the noise power is also unknown and a performance
almost identical to the previous case is obtained.
Index TermsвАФLog-likelihood ratio (LLR), channel uncertainty,
iterative decoding, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, wire-
less fading channels.
I. INTRODUCTION
I
N recent years, there have been many advances in iterative
decoding techniques and it has been shown that using
graphical codes such as LDPC codes [1] and turbo codes [2]
associated with iterative decoding, the Shannon limit on many
channels can be approached, e.g., see [3]. Therefore, these
codes have also been proposed for wireless fading channels
[4].
Application of LDPC codes on the Rayleigh fading channel
is pioneered in [4], where a detailed study of performance
and code design is conducted. This work is later extended to
time-selective complex fading channels [5], to Rician fading
channels [6], and also to Rayleigh block fading channels [7].
The application of turbo codes on Rayleigh fading channels
is also studied in [8].
Iterative decoding of graphical codes is based on message
passing algorithms [9]вАУ[12]. The messages are usually log-
likelihood ratios (LLR). This is mainly due to the fact that the
message update rules for LLRs are usually less complex than
the update rules for other types of messages [10]. Moreover,

In an iterative decoder which uses LLR messages, the
decoder is initialized with channel LLRs. The process of
computing channel LLRs depends on whether or not perfect
knowledge of the channel parameters exists at the receiver. The
capacity of the channel is also affected with the availability
of channel parameters at the receiver [8]. With partial or no
knowledge of the channel parameters available at the receiver,
LLRs are usually complicated functions of the channel output
[15], thus approximated LLRs are suggested and used in the
literature (e.g., see [4], [15], [16]).
In this work, we put our focus on uncorrelated fading chan-
nels modeled with a fading gain рЭСЯand an additive Gaussian
noise рЭСІвИЉрЭТ©(0, рЭЬО2

perfect side information (SI) and binary modulation is used,
LLRs are linear functions of the channel output [4]. In order to
have SI at the receiver, channel estimation techniques must be
used. These techniques increase the complexity of the system,
can cause a significant overhead, and are themselves subject
to imperfections. For high throughput wireless applications,
the receiver may not be able to tolerate the extra delay which
is the result of the increased complexity or overhead.
This paper provides an alternative solution which does not
require channel fading gain estimation, yet provides better
performance compared to the existing solutions that use fixed
fading gain estimates in the decoder and assume that рЭЬОрЭСІis
known.
The proposed solution is based on approximating LLRs
with a linear function of the channel output. Similar solutions
already exist in the literature, where the coefficient of this
linear function depends on a fixed estimate ЋЖрЭСЯof the channel
fading gain and knowledge of рЭЬОрЭСІ. For example in [4], [15],
[16], it is assumed that рЭЬОрЭСІis known and the expected value
of рЭСЯis used as ЋЖрЭСЯ. While the expected value of the fading gain
is the minimum mean square error estimation of рЭСЯ, it is not
guaranteed that this choice provides the optimum performance
in the decoder.
In a general setup (which includes famous fading channel
models such as uncorrelated Rayleigh and Rician fading
channels), we propose the following question: Assume that
the pdf of рЭСЯis known at the receiver, but the channel
instantaneous fading gain is not. Also assume that LLRs are
to be computed as linear functions of the channel output.

VII. CODE DESIGN UNDER MCLA
In this part we show that good LDPC codes can be
optimized for uncorrelated fading channels in the absence of
SI based on MCLA.
In general, two LDPC code design processes associated with
two measures of performance can be defined. One can be
defined as maximizing the threshold of the code over its degree
distributions given a target code rate [3], [4] and another one,
can be defined as maximizing the code rate over its degree
distributions given the channel LLR pdf [26]. In our case,
this pdf depends on the noise power, the pdf of рЭСЯ, and the
availability of SI. When no SI is available and (8) is used,
the channel LLR pdf also depends on рЭЫЉin (8). We use both
of these code design approaches in this paper and clarify
which one is used. We refer to the first design approach as
the threshold maximization design and to the second one as
the rate maximization design.

VIII. CONCLUSION
We investigated uncorrelated fading channels when the
fading gain is not known at the receiver and discussed several
methods for linear LLR approximations. We then introduced
a new measure for the accuracy of the approximate LLRs and
proved that this measure is maximized only with true LLRs.
Based on the proposed measure, we showed that the procedure
of finding the optimum linear LLRs is a convex optimization
problem and can be solved efficiently.
On an uncorrelated Rayleigh fading channel, compared to
existing work, which uses the expected value of the fading gain
for LLR calculation, we reported considerable performance
improvement at no extra decoding cost. Also, compared to
true LLR calculation we reported a very minor performance
loss (in the order of hundredth of a dB). We then extended
our approach to the cases that the additive noise power of the
fading channel is also unknown. With a careful choice of linear
LLR calculation, we were able to obtain a performance almost
identical to the case, where the additive noise power was

Our measure of LLR accuracy can also be used in cases
that a non-linear LLR approximation is sought. This can be
done by optimizing the parameters of the non-linear LLR
approximationn according to this measure. For example, in
higher order modulations, even when SI is available, LLRs
may no longer be linear functions of the channel output. In
[28], for soft Viterbi decoding of convolutional codes in the
HIPERLAN/2 standard [29], for the case that SI is available,
LLRs have been approximated by piecewise linear functions.
Our accuracy method could also be applied to these cases for
finding a piecewise linear function.
Although the results were presented for iterative decoding
of LDPC codes on uncorrelated Rayleigh fading channels, our
measure of accuracy is more general and it is applicable to soft
decoding methods that use LLRs such as Viterbi decoding and
iterative decoding on different uncorrelated fading channels.
Overall, for applications that channel estimation results in
significant overheads or suffers from severe imperfections, our
proposed solution can be of interest.
