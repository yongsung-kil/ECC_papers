Adaptive Turbo Equalizer With Stopping Rule Based on LDPC Codes
Myung-Kyu Lee and Kyeongcheol Yang, The Department of Electronics and Electrical Engineering Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH),
Pohang, Kyungbuk 790-784, Korea Email: {mklee.kcyang}@postech.ac.kr

Abstract- In this paper we propose a stopping rule for adaptive turbo equalizers based on low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. Using an extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart, we analyze the convergence behavior of a turbo equalizer and devise an adaptive turbo equalizer with stopping rule. It monitors the status of iterative decoding and stops performing equalization when the decoding messages are reliable enough to converge successfully only by LDPC decoding. Simulation results show that the proposed scheme has lower complexity than the conventional schemes, while it has a similar performance.
I. INTRODUCTION
Many practical communication systems over frequencyselective channels suffer from intersymbol interference (lSI) which drastically distorts the received signal. Douillard et al. demonstrated that their performance can be remarkably improved over lSI channels by employing turbo equalization which is an iterative processing of equalization and decoding [1]. The turbo equalizer by Douillard et al. uses the maximum a posteriori (MAP) equalizer with a convolutional code. This scheme has some weakness such as high complexity and low coding gain. Several algorithms were proposed to reduce the computational complexity of turbo equalizers [2], [3]. In recent years, turbo equalizers based on capacity-approaching codes such as turbo codes and low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have been extensively studied to benefit from their high coding gain [3], [4], [5], [11], [15].
In this paper we propose an adaptive scheduling with stopping rule to reduce the overall complexity of turbo equalizers based on LDPC codes. The main idea of our algorithm is that the receiver monitors the reliability of decoded messages and checks whether additional equalization is required. The receiver stops performing equalization when the messages are reliable enough to converge successfully by only LDPC decoding. By applying our algorithm, the receiver can reduce the execution number of equalization and this makes the receiver more efficient. We use an extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart [9] to analyze the convergence behavior of the iterative process and devise an adaptive turbo equalizer with stopping rule. In addition, simple metrics for measuring the reliability of the messages are considered in order to reduce additional complexity caused by the algorithm.

B. Adaptive Scheduling With Stopping Rule
An adaptive scheduling with stopping rule monitors the reliability of decoded messages and stops performing equalization. In order to do so, we consider a practical convergence property as well as an asymptotic one of iterative decoding. From the view points of an asymptotic property, decoding can be converged successfully by performing only LDPC decoding when SNRE is higher than the noise threshold of the given LDPC code over AWGN channels, where the messages from the equalizer are assumed to be symmetric Gaussian distributed as those over AWGN channels [11]. Therefore, we estimate the effective SNR and compare it with the noise threshold of the given LDPC code.
In the case of finite-length codes, some decoding trajectories are stuck near the bottleneck region in the EXIT chart when the messages are unreliable. Under this situation, additional equalization is required to improve the reliability. On the other hand, the possibility that decoding is successful by performing only LDPC decoding becomes higher if the decoding trajectory escapes from the bottleneck region and is located at high mutual information region. Hence, our algorithm also checks whether the decoding trajectory escapes from the bottleneck region.

C. Complexity Analysis
The computational complexity of the BCJR algorithm and LDPC decoding are considered to analyze the complexity of turbo equalizers. The additional complexity by our algorithm is also dealt with in this section. The sum-product algorithm is employed for LDPC decoding. Their computational complexities are summarized in Table I. The complexity of the BCJR algorithm comes from the computation of forward and backward recursions, the branch metrics for all trellis sections, and the a posteriori LLRs. The operations of the VND and CND for a regular LDPC code are considered for the complexity of sum-product algorithm. If we assume BPSK modulation, a (3,6)-regular LDPC code and an lSI
channel with L == 5, the complexity of the BCJR algorithm is
about 19 times that of the sum -product algorithm with respect to multiplication operation. The complexity of linear MMSE equalization is well described in [2]. Its complexity is also higher than that of LDPC decoding.
In our algorithm, N EDGE comparisons and N comparisons are required to compute NYND and NEQ, respectively. These are easily implemented by addition operation. Since there is no multiplication operation and the complexity for calculating the metrics is much lower than that of one LDPC decoding, our algorithm does not cause a significant increase in complexity. Though memories for storing the metrics and the threshold values are required, they also do not increase significantly memory complexity.

IV. NUMERICAL RESULTS

Steps 1 and 2 in the algorithm compute the metrics and Step 3 checks whether the messages are reliable enough to converge successfully by only LDPC decoding. The conventional scheduling can be easily transformed into our algorithm by adding metric computation and stopping rule.
The remaining work is to decide SNRT and IT. It is hard to find the optimum threshold values since the cost function such as complexity can not be defined in a close form. Hence finding the threshold values for good performance and high efficiency depends on trial and error. It is recommended that SNRT is set to a value higher than the noise threshold of the employed LDPC code over an AWGN channel and IT a value escaped from the bottleneck region.

V. C ONCL USIO N
We analyzed an asymptotic and practical property of the convergence behavior of turbo equalizers based on LOPC

codes by the EXIT chart. By exploiting these properties we
devised an adaptive scheduling with stopping rule for turbo
equalizers based on LOPC codes. The algorithm employed
simple metrics to estimate the reliability of messages and
stopped performing equalization to make the receiver more efficient. Simulation results show that there is a negligible
performance loss between the proposed algorithm and the
conventional one.
