Abstract—By using the minimum mean square error (MMSE) criterion, we show the design of the scaled and offset belief propagation (BP)-based decoding for short low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes on the channels of additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) and uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading with side information (SI). Based on the MMSE criterion, the detailed derivations and numerical calculations on the optimum factors for these two improved BP-based algorithms are provided. The simulation results for several short (3,6) regular LDPC codes on the AWGN and Rayleigh fading channels demonstrate that the scaled and offset BP-based algorithms with our proposed factors can achieve the performance almost the same to, and even better than that of the BP algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, proposed by Gallager [7], have been intensively studied since their rediscovery [9] in the 1990s. An LDPC code is a linear block code defined by its sparse parity-check matrix [7]. With the sparse matrix, we can associate a graph known as Tanner graph [10], which consists of variable nodes, check nodes, and a certain number of edges. For the parity-check matrix, each column corresponds to a variable node, and each row corresponds to a check node. The iterative decoding of the LDPC code is performed by passing messages between the neighboring variable nodes and check nodes.
It is well known that the belief-propagation (BP) [9] (sumproduct [5]) algorithm provides a powerful tool for iterative decoding of LDPC codes. With this algorithm, LDPC codes can achieve fairly good performance on the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN), as well as many fading channels. In order to get a good performance-complexity tradeoff, various iterative decoding algorithms [2]-[4],[6] based on the BP algorithm have been presented, recently. The uniformly most powerful (UMP) BP-based algorithm [6] needs only real additions, and is universal as it does not need any a priori information about the channel state. The UMP BP-based algorithm reduces the decoding complexity of BP significantly, but degrades the decoding performance as well. Nevertheless, two improved versions named scaled BP-based1 and offset BP-based algorithms were introduced [2],[3].
1 In [2] and [3], the similar algorithm is called normalized BP-based algorithm.

This paper is devoted to the design of these two improved BP-based algorithms for short regular LDPC codes. For the short codes, Chen and Fossorier [2] used the criterion of equal magnitude mean (EMM) to get the factor of scaled BP-based algorithm. Actually, as indicated by Kim et al. [8], the EMM criterion is not optimal, and the minimum mean square error (MMSE) criterion is much better. However, the analysis in [2] and [8] is limited to the case of scaled BP-based algorithm on the AWGN channel. In this paper, we deal with the scaled and offset BP-based algorithms on the channels of AWGN and uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading with perfect side information (SI). Our proposed method for designing the both BP-based algorithms is by using the MMSE criterion.

III. MMSE-BASED DESIGN OF SCALED AND OFFSET BP-BASED ALGORITHMS
A. Determinations of α and β under the MMSE criterion
For each of these BP-based algorithms, there may be performance degradation compared with the BP decoding since the simplification for processing check-node incoming messages is introduced by some approximation. For short LDPC codes, as indicated in [2], the accuracy of the soft values delivered by check-node decoder at the first iteration will affect the bit error rate (BER) performance of the overall decoding. The factor of the scaled BP-based algorithm, determined by the EMM [2] or MMSE criterion [8], is just by modifying the first-iteration outgoing messages from the check-node decoder.
At the first iteration, from (1) we can get v = u(0) since the messages from any check node to its neighboring variable nodes are set to be zeros. In the following, we will consider the case that such messages are the incoming of a checknode decoder. For the scaled BP-based algorithm, factor α determined by the MMSE criterion is by minimizing the mean square error E |u| − |u2| 2 = E |u| − α · |u1| 2 . V. CONCLUSIONS
On the channels of AWGN and uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading with SI, we showed the design of the scaled and offset BP-based decoding algorithms for short LDPC codes by using the MMSE criterion. On these two channels, the simulation results for several short (3,6) regular LDPC codes indicate that the scaled and offset BP-based algorithms with the factors determined by MMSE can achieve the performance almost the same to, and even better than that of the BP algorithm. Through the simulation-performance comparison, we observed that the criterion of MMSE is superior to that of EMM.
