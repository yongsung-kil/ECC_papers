LDPC Decoder

1. IMCS, East China Normal University, Shanghai, 200062, China 2. Engineering Research Center for Nanophotonics & Advanced Instrument, Ministry of Education, East China Normal University, Shanghai, 200062, China

Abstract—In this paper, we present a uniform all-integer quantization for irregular LDPC decoder. The LLR values at the variable nodes and the check nodes are mapped to integer with their integer part of their value directly. Variable nodes are quantized to 6-bit integer and check nodes are quantized to 4-bit integer after the input channel messages are scaled up, and at a given iteration number the messages are scaled down. A lookup table is used to store the configuration parameters for quantization, and the hardware architecture of quantization is proposed. Simulations of (3048,7493) show the performance is better than the floating point in normalized min-sum and close to floating point in offset min-sum. The uniform all-integer quantization is easy to be implemented and does not require the floating point operations and so reduces the hardware complexity and lower power.
Keywords-uniform;all-interger;quantization;LDPC
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] were first introduced in the 1960’s and rediscovered in the 1990’s, which has received much attention because of their excellent performance and the large degree of parallelism that can be exploited in the decoder. LDPC codes are being widely considered as the most promising candidate FEC scheme for many applications such as telecommunications, storage devices and other fields. For example, LDPC code was used as channel coding in DVB-S2[2], IEEE 802.11n/16e[3] and DMB-TH [4].
Efficient hardware implementation of the LDPC decoder has become a topic of increasing interest. The finite precision effect on decoding performance is an important issue in hardware design of LDPC decoders. Novel versions of quantization for LDPC decoder have been proposed, which can be classed into two types, namely uniform quantizer and nonuniform quantizer. For a uniform quantizer, let (q : f) denotes the quantization scheme in which a total number of q bits are used, of which f bits are used for the fractional part of the value. IV. SIMULATION RESULT
Let Q(bv, bc) denote the quantization scheme, of which the bv denotes the quantization bits of variable nodes and bc denotes quantization bits of check nodes. The value of variable nodes is represented as a sign bit and bv-1 message bits and the value of check nodes is represented as a sign bit and bc-1 message bits. Uniform quantization means mapping of a number to the closest integer multiple of a small number. In this paper, the LLR values at the VN and the CN are mapped to integer with their integer part of their value directly. When the value is beyond the quantization range, it is replaced with the max value. For the (3048, 7493) code, the degree distribution polynomials of this code are λ(x) = 0.1273x6 +0.8727x7 with respect to variable nodes and ρ (x) = 0.3382x2+0.2168x3+ 0.4x10 with respect to check nodes. Fig. 5 shows the performance comparison of floating point and its quantized versions in normalized min-sum. Q(6,3) and Q(6,4) mean that they are only scaled at initial stage. Improved Q(6,3) and improved Q(6,4) mean that they are not only scaled at the initial stage, but also are scaled down at given iterations. With maximum number of iterations equal to 20, it can be observed that the improved Q(6,4) is better than floating point. Especially, when Eb/N0<2.0dB, the performance of Q(6,3) and Q(6,4) is better than that of floating point. Fig. 6 shows that the performance comparison of floating point and different quantization methods in offset min-sum. It can be observed that the performance of improved Q(6,4) is closed to that of floating point. When Eb/N0<1.5 dB, the performance of Q(6,3) and Q(6,4) is closed to that of floating point .

V. CONCLUSIONS
We present a novel uniform all-integer quantization for irregular LDPC decoder. The performance of Q(6,4) for normalized min-sum overperformances that of floating point, and the performance of Q(6,4) for offset min-sum is closed to that of floating point. A lookup table is used to store the configuration parameters. The uniform all-integer quantization is simple and easy to be implemented with little modification during the updating of channel, variable node and check node message in hardware architecture.
