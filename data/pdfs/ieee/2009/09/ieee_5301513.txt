An Iterative Decoding Method Based on Gaussian Approximation for LDPC Coded Modulations
Ping Huang, Ming Jiang,Chunming Zhao Department of Electrical Engineering , HoHai University National Mobile Communications Research Laboratory (NCRL), Southeast University
Nanjing, Jiangsu, P.R.China, 210096 Email: huangpinghope@163.com, {jiangming,cmzhao}@seu.edu.cn

Abstract— In this paper, we propose an improved iterative demapping/decoding method for short length Low-density paritycheck (LDPC) coded bit-interleaved coded modulations(BICM). For short length LDPC, there exist cycles in the bipartite graph, thereby leading to the high correlation among the extrinsic information of bits out from BP decoder. Based on the Gaussian assumption of the extrinsic log-likelihood ratios (LLRs)delivered by BP decoder, we multiply the extrinsic output of the decoder by a scaling factor and then pass it as a priori information to the demodulator for the next iteration. In this way, we can decrease the correlation of coded bits within the symbol and thus reduce the error propagation. Simulation results show that the proposed method can achieve noticeable performance gains for short length LDPC coded BICM-ID with a minor computation complexity increase.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes can approach the Shannon limit on various channels with relatively low complexity using the Belief Propagation (BP) decoding algorithm [1]–[3]. LDPC codes coded high-order signaling systems also show near-capacity performance [4], [5].
Bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) [6] outperforms trellis-coded modulation (TCM) in Rayleigh fading channel because the bit interleaver increases the time diversity. However, the improvement is achieved at the expense of the reduced free Euclidean distance, leading to a degradation over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels. The BICM with iterative decoding between demodulator and decoder (BICM-ID) was investigated in [7]. Convolutional codes coded BICM-ID can outperform TCM both over AWGN and Rayleigh channels. Soft-decision feedback is the key to realize the inherent gains in BICM while mitigating error propagation between the coded bits mapped to the same symbol.
In [7], when the soft demodulator calculates the bit metric for each coded bits, it assumes the feedback information from the decoder are independent each other within the symbol due to the good interleaver. The extrinsic information generated by the decoder is applied directly to the demapper as a priori loglikelihood ratio (LLR) input for the next iteration. But in the short length LDPC coded BICM-ID systems (LDPC-BICMID), the cycles existing in the bipartite graph of short length LDPC will lead to the high correlation among the extrinsic information from BP decoder. If we update the a priori LLR

for the demodulator as in [7], then it will incur the error propagation between the bits in the symbol. As a result, in this case, we should find other methods to generate the a priori input for the demodulator based on the extrinsic information from BP decoder.
For Turbo codes, the use of a scaling factor to the extrinsic LLRs has been applied in iterative decoding to improve the performance of soft-output Viterbi algorithm (SOVA) [9]. The factor is computed according to the Gaussian assumption of the extrinsic information from the concatenated decoder [8]. Because the output LLRs of BP decoder are also Gaussianlike [10], we can extend the method in [9] to our LDPCBICM-ID system. In this paper, we multiply the extrinsic output of BP decoder by a scaling factor and then pass it as a priori information to the demodulator for the next iteration. In the rest of this paper, we refer to this factor as Gaussian factor. The method is similar to the annealing processing to the output information of BP decoder. In this way, we can decrease the correlation of coded bits within the channel symbol. Simulation results show that the proposed method is effective especially for short length LDPC coded BICM-ID with a minor computation complexity increase.

C. Computational Analysis
Compared with the traditional method in [7], the proposed method scales the extrinsic output from the decoder by Gaussian factor. In simulation, we find that after a couple of blocks, the Gaussian factor tends to a constant. We also observe that this factor is insensitive to the changing SNR in the region of interest. As a result, in the practical application, the Gaussian factor can be estimated during systems setup. Since the proposed demapping/decoding algorithm only have a minor increase both in the computation complexity and storage, it can be applied in practice.

IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose an improved iterative demapping/decoding method based on the Gaussian approximation for short length LDPC coded BICM systems. In this way, we can decrease the correlation of coded bits within the channel symbol and reduce the error propagation. Compared with the traditional algorithm [7], simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can achieve significant performance gain with a small increase in the computation complexity especially for short length LDPC-BICM-ID. We also confirm that for a certain LDPC code, the Gaussian factor can converge to a fixed value independently of the changing SNR, thus make the proposed method be attractable for the view of engineering. It will also work well in scenariors where Gray mapping is not possible such as 32QAM and 128QAM.
