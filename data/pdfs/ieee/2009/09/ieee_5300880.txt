Girth 4 and Low Minimum Weights’ Problems of LDPC Codes in DVB-S2 and Solutions*

Xi Huang1, Jun Fan2, Yang Xiao1
1Institute of Information Science Beijing Jiaotong University Beijing 100044, China 2Office of Academic Affairs Minzu University of China Beijing 100081, China Email: yxiao@center.njtu.edu.cn
Abstract—Though LDPC codes have been accepted by DVB-S2, the LDPC encode of DVB-S2 (Digital Video Broadcasting Satellite Second Generation) are difficult to be implemented for their huge code lengths of 16200 bits and 64800 bits). To shorten the codes will lead the problems of girth-4 and lower weight codes. This paper analyzed the possible reasons leading to the problems, and revised the current code construction of DVB-S2. The simulation results in the AWGN channel show that the BER performance of the improved LDPC codes is much better than that of the former shorten codes. Keywords-Communication; Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes; DVB-S2 standard; Encoding algorithm；BER performance
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC code has nowadays been tentatively applied to deep space communication, optical fiber communication, satellite digital video, audio broadcasting and the like and the encoding method based on LDPC code has been adopted by DVB-S2, the standard for the next-generation satellite digital video broadcasting [2]. However, [1] found that the LDPC codes used in DVB-S2 was not good codes, for they contained a comparatively high error floor, and [1] also pointed out that the bi-diagonal structure of the sub-matrix incorporated in the parity-check matrix was responsible for the error floor. Leaving out all the existing LDPC codes in DVB-S2, [1] put forward a new design of LDPC, which is a variety of irregular quasi-circular LDPC code relying on the Tanner code, in an effort to replace the LDPC code in DVB-S2. But is it possible to obtain good LDPC codes by revising partly the available LDPC codes in DVB-S2? The question above is the main problem with which the paper is grappling. The paper proposes a parity-check matrix for the LDPC encoder with quasi-diagonal sub-matrix, the parity-check matrix can have no girth-4 and the codes of lower weights.

III. THE DESIGN OF THE DVB-S2 LDPC CODE AND ITS
PROBLEMS
The DVB-S2 error correcting code is a kind of concatenating code, employing the BCH code as the outer code, the LDPC code as the inner code. We center our attention around whether the DVB-S2 LDPC code satisfies the requirements of Theorem1 and 2. According to the DVB-S2 standard, the code-length of the LDPC is 16200 or 64800. In this paper, we employ the code-rate equal to 1/2 and the codelength equal to 16200 so as to elaborate on the design method of the parity-check matrix of the DVB-S2 LDPC code.

diagonal-structure sub-matrix introduced into the design

process of the parity-check matrix, a very high error floor

crops up in the BER simulation. The introduction of the bi-

a must for the iterative encoding? That is the problem with

which the present paper is concerned and coping.

matrix of IEEE 802.16e [3], and proposed a quasi-bi-diagonal

V. CONCLUSIONS
This paper reveals that the girth-4s and lower weights’ codes exist in the shorten DVB-S2 LDPC code, they lead to the BER performance of the code to be comparatively poor. As for the reason, the 4-girths in the parity-check matrix of the DVB-S2 LDPC code and the existence of the low-weight codes lead to the poor BER performance. In this paper, the problem of poor BER performance has been solved by revising the parity-check matrix of the existing DVB-S2 LDPC code. The simulation indicates that the proposed scheme improve the BER of the shorten LDPC code for DVBS2 tending towards that of the random code.
