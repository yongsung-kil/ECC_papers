Two Bit-Flipping Decoding Algorithms for Low-Density Parity-Check Codes
Telex Magloire Nkouatchah Ngatched, Member, IEEE, Martin Bossert, Senior Member, IEEE, Achim Fahrner, Member, IEEE, and Fambirai Takawira, Member, IEEE

Abstract—In this letter, a low complexity decoding algorithm for binary linear block codes is applied to low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes and improvements are described, namely an extension to soft-decision decoding and a loop detection mechanism. For soft decoding, only one real-valued addition per code symbol is needed, while the remaining operations are only binary as in the hard decision case. The decoding performance is considerably increased by the loop detection. Simulation results are used to compare the performance with other known decoding strategies for LDPC codes, with the result that the presented algorithms offer excellent performances at smaller complexity.
Index Terms—Belief propagation algorithm, bit-flipping decoding, hard-decision decoding, low-density parity-check codes, soft-decision decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes were originally invented by Gallager [1] and rediscovered by Mackay and Neal [2] and Sipser and Spielman [3]. Their performance has recently been the subject of much experimentation and analysis. The interest in these codes stems from their near Shannon limit performance, their simple descriptions and implementations, and their amenability to rigorous theoretical analysis. LDPC codes have become strong competitors to turbo codes [4]–[7] for error control in many communication and digital storage systems where high reliability is required.
LDPC codes can be decoded with various decoding methods, ranging from low to high complexity and from reasonably good to very good performance. These decoding methods include: One-step majority-logic (MLG) decoding [8], [9], Gallager’s bit flipping (BF) decoding [1], weighted MLG decoding (WMLG) [10], weighted BF decoding (WBF) [11], the algorithm proposed in [12] which we call improved weighted bit flipping (IWBF), a posteriori probability (APP) decoding [1], iterative decoding based on belief propagation (IDBP)

MLG and BF decoding belong to harddecision decoding methods. APP, IDBP/SPA decoding and its approximations are soft-decision schemes. WMLG, WBF, and IWBF decoding are hybrid decoding methods.
In this paper, two new decoding algorithms for LDPC codes are presented which are based on the algorithm [19], [20]. The idea for decoding is the fact that, in the case of low-density parity-check matrices, the syndrome weight increases with the number of errors on average until error weights much larger than half the minimum distance (for the analysis, see [20]). Therefore, the idea is to flip one bit in each iteration, and the bit to be flipped is chosen such that the syndrome weight decreases. It should be noted that not only rows of the paritycheck matrix can be used for decoding, but in principle all vectors of the dual code with minimum (or small) weight. While in [21], the extension of the algorithm in [19], [20] to soft-decision was similar to belief propagation (BP), in our new variant the scaled reliability value from the channel is used only through an addition to the corresponding position in the code after calculating binary operations only. Surprisingly, this low complex method shows excellent performance which can be further improved by the loop detection mechanism. The new algorithms belong to the class of so called bit flipping algorithms and in some cases the changing of bits can enter a loop which means that bits are changed and after some steps are changed back again ad infinitum if no stop criterion is applied. The loop detection recognizes such behavior and resolves this situation such that in many cases a correct decoding is possible.
The remainder of the paper is organized as follows: In the next section, notation, definitions, and the proposed algorithms are presented. In Section III, we evaluate the complexity of the algorithms. Some simulation results are provided in Section IV to illustrate the performance of the proposed algorithms and, finally, conclusions are drawn in Section V.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, two new decoding algorithms for LDPC codes are presented. The first algorithm is a hard-decision method, and the second one is a modification of the first to include reliability information of the received symbols. The new algorithms belong to the class of bit flipping algorithms. Simulation results show that both algorithms work well for general LDPC codes, and that, at BER of 10−5, performances only 0.53 dB worse than BP can be achieved with the second algorithm at much lower complexity.
