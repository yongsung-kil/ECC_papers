The main advantages of LDPCC over turbo codes are: 1) the somewhat simpler decoder (based on belief propagation) that could be fully parallelizable, allowing greater decoding speed; 2) the decoding is such that it is always possible to know if the final decoded word is a codeword (not necessarily the right one) or not, i.e., error detection comes for free. For turbo codes, the encoder is simple, and the decoder is quite complex: on the contrary, in LDPCC the encoder complexity poses the main difficulties. A solution is proposed in [7]; another one, obtained by construction of the encoder exploiting the graph based description of LDPCC, will be presented in another contribution.
Moreover, in this paper we give new distributions and numerical re( sults for LDPCC with high code rates: TS H8 I , PI 8HQ and 0DUPV8 0 . These rates are of interest for near-earth satellite data applications [8]. It is here shown, with numerical results, that irregular LDPCC have a two-slopes behavior of the performance curve. It is worthwhile noting that the same phenomenon, known as ”error floor”, also characterizes turbocodes [5].
Finally, to explain the above-mentioned two-slopes performance behavior, an original method to investigate the minimum distance of specific LDPCC (not the code ensemble) is proposed: we found that all our irregular LDPCC instances have quite low minimum distance, justifying the error floor observed by Monte-Carlo simulations.
II. THEORETICAL BOUNDS
Let us start by some results from information theory related to bounds on the performance of error correcting codes. These will serve to assess the design of irregular LDPCC.
Bounds on error probability for codes with given code rate, allowing the code length going to infinity, are a well-known result of Shannon information theory [9]. However, since we are interested in block codes, we must constraint the codeword length to be finite. In [9] Gallager derives an analytical formulation on performance bounds averaging over the set of all possible codes with the desired code rate and block length. This ensemble bound gives us information about the performance of good codes.

A. Some considerations on the error floor for irregular LDPCC

As can be noticed by observing the previous results, al-

though for quite low values of the error probability, the inves-

ptirgoabteadbiliirtryegvusl.ar«t Lª D8 p PCCcurpvreess.enItt

a slope change in the error can be conjectured that this

behavior is typical of powerful codes reaching performances

very near to Shannon limits, as it has been observed both in

turbo codes, in product (turbo) codes and in irregular LDPCC.

In order to verify the presence of low weight codewords, we

adopted the following steps:

step 1: encode the ”all zero bits” information frame

step 2: Add Gaussian noise

step 3: Decode the received vector. Now, since we are dealing with LDPCC, there are three possible decoding events: E1: All the parity check equations are verified, and the detected vector is the all zero vector; E2: Even after the maximum allowed iterations, some parity check equations are not verified, hence the detected vector is not a codeword; E3: All the parity check equations are verified, and the detected vector is different from the all zero vector. In this last event, the detected vector is a valid codeword since all the parity check codes are verified. Moreover, with low noise, there is a high probability that E3 is due to a decoder failure in choosing between ”near” codewords. This is the original approach we have used to find low weight codewords. The results of this search procedure are summarized in Table VII, where it can be noted that the minimum distance of irregular LDPCC is quite small, and an error floor should be expected. It should be remarked that these results have been obtained for specific instances of LDPCC with the distribution derived in Section III.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper we have designed good distributions for irregular LDPCC with rates 7/8, 8/9 and 15/16 and various block lengths. Performance in terms of BER and CER over AWGN channels for some specific instances of codes having these distributions have been evaluated; it has been found that irregular LDPCC perform similar or better than turbo codes in the same conditions .
Finally, we have investigated the minimum Hamming distance of some specific codes, revealing the presence of the ”error floor” phenomenon.
