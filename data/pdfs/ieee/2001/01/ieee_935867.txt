Abstract - Some twenty years ago Margulis [l]proposed an algebraic construction of LDPC codes. In this paper we mimick the construction of Margulis and we describe a method for algebraically constructing regular and irregular LDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
For the construction of Tanner graphs of codes which are decoded iteratively there are several rules of thumb for getting good decoding performance. One of them is that the girth should be large (or that the graph has a good local-girth distribution) so as to assure the independence of the messages as long as possible. Furthermore, the graphs should have irregular degrees as proposed in [2].
