Novel Construction and Cycle Analysis of Quasi­ Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check Codes

Wenwen Li, Mao Ai, Jie Qiu, Lili Liu
Jiuquan Satellite Launch Center in China JiuQuan, China
liwenwennudt@outlook. com

Ahstract- Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC­ LDPC) codes have practical significance in the field of engineering, thus their construction algorithm is a hot issue in the field of LDPC codes. Based on existing construction methods, particularly the method based on Progressive Edge-Growth (PEG) algorithm, a new construction method of shift matrix is proposed. It can effectively avoid the copy of cycle structures, especially short cycles in base matrix after extension. Besides, it also relieves the time cost caused by random search, and greatly improves the length of cycles when compared with algorithms based on methods such as quadratic congruence and arithmetic sequence. Those algorithms can only eliminate short cycles with length 4, while our method can provide QC-LDPC codes without length 6 at least. Cycle analysis and simulation results show that the newly constructed QC-LDPC codes have good cycle structure and error correction performance.
Keywords: Quasi-cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check Codes, Shift Matrix, Cycle Analysis

I.

INTRODUCTION

Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1], a good kind of erro correction code approaching Shannon limit, are widely used in communication areas such as deep space and aerospace, wireless local area network (WLAN) and hardware storage. Compared with other linear block codes, LDPC codes have low decoding complexity, excellent error correction performance and other advantages. Thus it becomes a hot issue in the field of error correction coding during recent years.
LDPC codes can be generally divided into two kinds: randomly constructed LDPC codes and structured LDPC codes. Though randomly constructed LDPC codes have better performance, their high complexities of realization are not conducive to engineering. Structured LDPC codes can well reduce encoding complexity through their own structural characteristics, so they have been widely used in practice. Quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes, which can be achieved through a set of fast encoding shift register, are a kind of typical structured LDPC codes. They have the traits like small storage for parity check matrix, parallel decoding and good error correction performance, thus they are drawing more and more attention. As for the construction of QC-LDPC codes, those methods can be divided into two categories: construction methods based on finite geometry [2]-[3] and construction methods based on the cyclic shift matrix [4]-[9].

Dedicated to construct base matrix meeting the row-column (RC) constraints, construction methods based on [mite geometry can effectively ensure that the girth of QC-LDPC code is at least 6. However, those methods lack of flexibility in the choice of code length, rate and other parameters. Construction methods based on the cyclic shift matrix primarily control the number and length of cycles through base matrix and shift matrix jointly. Aiming at increasing the girth of base matrix, the Progressive Edge-Growth (PEG) algorithm [10] is generally adopted during its construction. In terms of the construction of shift matrix, the method of random search is used in [5], which is able to enlarge the girth through repeated searching. However, the time cost and the amount of computation will increase rapidly as the pre-set girth increases. And there will possibly be no parity check matrix found if the pre-set girth is relatively big. Another way to construct shift matrix is to use the algorithms like quadratic congruence [7] and arithmetic sequence [9]. These methods can eliminate the cycles with length 4 in party check matrix, which guarantees the girth is at least 6. However, they do not make further consideration for cycles with length more than 6.
Based on existing literatures, a novel construction method of shift matrix is proposed on the basis of cycle analysis in this paper. The algorithm can: I) improve the length of cycles by avoiding the copy of short cycles in base matrix after extension; 2) further increase the girth of QC­ LDPC codes while avoiding time cost and calculation caused by random search. Thus the decoding independency and erro correction performance are improved obviously.
The paper is organized as follows. The representation and cycle structure of QC-LDPC codes based on PEG algorithm are given in Section II. In Section Ill, a novel construction method of shift matrix is proposed on the basis of cycle structure of QC-LDPC codes. And the cycle structure under the construction algorithm is analyzed subsequently. Section IV presents the simulation results of newly constructed QC-LDPC codes as well as appropriate analysis and comparison. Lastly Section V concludes the paper.
