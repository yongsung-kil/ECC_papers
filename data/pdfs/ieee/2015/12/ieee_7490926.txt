An Efficient Early Stopping Analog Decoder for LDPC Codes

Yuan Gao, Yujie Lin, Bowen Zeng, Zhe Zhao, Hao Zheng
School of Information and Electronics Beijing Institute of Technology Beijing, China dsform@126.com

AbstractвҖ”The analog implementation of error control decoders is a competitive methodology compared with the digital implementation in power efficiency and throughput. In order to detect uncorrectable frames and early stop the iterative decoding of low-density parity check (LDPC) analog decoders, a probability stopping block using probabilities of satisfied checks is proposed. Simulation results show that the proposed criterion can greatly reduce the average number of iterations at low SNR, and has better tradeoff between complexity and performance than other stopping criteria using extrinsic information.
KeywordsвҖ”analog LDPC decoders; early stopping criterion; circuit dynamic behavior
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check codes were first proposed by R. G. Gallager [1] and rediscovered by Mackay [2] in 1996. As LDPC codes are applied to a wide range of applications from digital video broadcasting (DVB) to wireless sensor networks (WSNs) , it is of great interest to implement low-cost iterative decoders with high throughput and low power consumption especially on the consumer electronics.
Analog continuous-time iterative decoding was proposed a few years ago. Inspired by WibergвҖҷs work on iterative graph decoding [3], HagenauerвҖҷs [4] and LoeligerвҖҷs [5] groups independently proposed the novel analog electronic network to decode error correcting codes in 1998, and successfully realized analog iterative decoders in BiCMOS technology [6,7]. Since the research efforts aimed at moving from the proof-ofconcept design toward real-world applications, various prototypes of on-chip analog decoders were designed. The successful implementation of a chip for decoding a (132, 40) Turbo code defined in the UMTS standard was reported by Vogrig et al. in [8], and the prototype showed an outstanding improvement in power efficiency and speed compared with their digital counterparts, with only a little error-correcting performance loss.
In [9], analog decoders for turbo-style code and tail-biting trellis code were proposed, which provided a different way for LDPC decoders implementations. Several analog decoders for (32, 8) LDPC code has been proposed [3, 5, 6] using different decoding algorithms, such as Min-Sum (MS) algorithm and Belief Propagation (BP) algorithm.

As the analog decoders share the similar algorithms as their digital counterparts, the decoding delay (correspond to the setup-time of analog decoder) is the prime factor that affects the throughput of decoder. Since the decoding delay increases linearly with the number of decoding iterations, in digital implementations, the BP algorithms terminate when either the estimated codeword satisfies all parity checks or pre-set maximum iteration number is reached. Such standard stopping criterion (SSC) cannot distinguish uncorrectable blocks from correctable ones, which often occur at low to medium Signalto-Noise Ratio (SNR), and cause great waste of power and decoding time. Additionally, SSC is not suitable for analog decoders due to the continuity of decoding process.
To reduce the decoding time of analog decoders, an factor graph based LDPC analog decoder for CCSDS standard with early stopping criteria block is proposed. The calculation of check-satisfied probability uses probabilities of satisfied checks, which could be obtained through check nodes in the factor graph. Firstly, the LDPC decoding over memoryless channels and factor graph representation is briefly introduced and the probabilities of satisfied checks based early stopping criteria based on factor graph is investigated. Then the analog decoding architecture is investigated and circuit implementation of analog LDPC decoders is introduced. Moreover, a behavioral model for the analog implementation of LDPC decoders with early stopping criteria block is proposed. Finally, simulation results show that compared with APP implementation, which is the most efficient one using the extrinsic information, the proposed LDPC analog decoder can achieve better balance decoding delay and performance.

V. CONCLUSIONS
A novel architecture of analog decoder with early stopping block for LDPC decoding is proposed in this paper. The LDPC decoding over memoryless channels and factor graph representation is briefly introduced and the implementation of early stopping criteria based on factor graph is presented. Simulation results show that the BER and FER performance of the proposed analog decoder is better than that of the traditional criterion which is based on digital implementation in some circumstances, and could shorten the setup-time of analog decoders. Moreover, results also reveal that the any variations in presetting value of P larger than 0.5 does not influence the performance of LDPC decoders.
