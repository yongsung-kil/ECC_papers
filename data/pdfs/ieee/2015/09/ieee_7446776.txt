Structured LDPC, such as RA, IRA, ARA, QC-LDPC etc., are some important LDPC codes, these codes usually have very good performance and facilitate the implementation of encoding and decoding for the structural features they have. A new method for the encoding of the structured LDPC codes has been presented, namely FFT-based parallel encoding algorithm, which uses FFT to realize the parallel encoding of LDPC codes by achieving the circular convolution in the frequency domain. The study shows that the proposed algorithm has lower computational complexity than the previously proposed encoding algorithm. Although transforming to the frequency domain requires complex operations in real domain which increasing the number of the quantization points, the throughput of the encoder has greatly improved because of the increase of the degree of parallelism. The simulation results show that the computational complexity of the FFT-based encoding algorithm has an approximately linear relationship with the size of sub-circulate matrix, and when this size goes larger, the newly proposed algorithm will possess a lower computational complexity and a higher throughput compared with the previously proposed algorithms.

The authors in [9] have analysed the circulate property of generator matrix of protograph LDPC codes in detail, and find that the parity check matrix of protograph LDPC codes are circulate, and if they are non-singular, the generator matrix of protograph LDPC codes have the property of circulate, that is to say the generator matrix is composed of sub-circulate matrices, thus, they can be encoded with simple shift registers, this operation is actually circular convolution as will be showed in this paper. With the nature of the circular convolution, a FFT-based encoding algorithm has been proposed with reduction of computing complexity and great improvement of the throughput.

3 FFT-Based Parallel Encoding for Structured LDPC Codes

To simplify the description here, we temporarily call the previously proposed encoding algorithm (such as SRAAbased encoding [11] [12]) the time-domain encoding, since they are all calculated in time domain in contrast to the FFT-based encoding algorithm which is calculated in the frequency domain.
For time-domain encoding, given the size of sub-
circulate matrix m , it will take m2 times multiplications
and m(m 1) times additions, that is to say it suffers a
quadratic encoding complexity with the growth of m .
Usually, calculating a m points DFT needs 4m2 times multiply operation, so it seems that the method that has been proposed above are less computational effectiveness than the time-domain encoding.
Actually, by using the fast Fourier transform, the computational load will be decreased tremendously from quadratic to linear, leading to the great reducing of the computational complexity for the proposed algorithm. Besides, the nature of parallelism of FFT ensures a parallel encoding of LDPC codes, resulting in a rising of the throughput. In this section, some studies on the computational complexity and throughput of the FFT-based parallel encoding algorithm are given.

3.2 Analysis of the Throughput Gain

FFT has a parallel architecture inherently, the parallelism degree it has is equal to the number of points it calculates, for FFT-based encoding algorithm, and this number is namely the size of the sub-circulate matrix m . For most of the structured LDPC codes that is used in space telecommunication, usually, m is relatively large, for example, in the protocols of CCSDS, m takes an value from 128 to 8192, and for DVB-S2/T2/S2X, m becomes to 360. This high level of parallelism makes the high throughputs possible.
Here, two types of FFT structure are given for the analysis of the throughput gain of the proposed method, namely Pipelined FFT and Burst FFT.
Pipelined FFT is given by Figure 3, from this figure, we can see that there are log2 m stages of butterfly units, each has a memory to store the calculating result of this stage. Data inputs from the first stage of butterfly unit, after log2 m times of pipelined operation, the result will be outputted from the last stage continuously.
Compared with the time-domain encoding, namely SRAA-based parallel encoding, the algorithm we have proposed has a throughput gain of m / c ( m , c are defined in Section 1) theoretically, usually c has a much smaller value than m , for the 7/8 LDPC codes in CCSDS, this gain will be 36. Suppose frequency of the working clock is fclk , then the throughput of pipelined FFT can be
expressed as mRfclk , where R is the code rate. For
fclk 100MHz , a throughput about 44.8Gbps will achieved by the FFT-based parallel encoding using Pipelined FFT.

4 Simulation Results

In this section, a simulation of the computational complexity of the FFT-based parallel encoding algorithm is given using MatLab as the simulation tool. In order to present the computational complexity, the average computing time was chosen to accomplish the goal, and it is an average time of 1000 calculations. A comparison has been made between proposed encoding algorithm and the time-domain encoding.
As showed in Figure 5, which the dotted line with diamond represent the computing time (s) of the time-domain encoding while the solid line with circle shows that of the FFT-based parallel encoding. The figure shows that the computational complexity of the proposed algorithm has an approximately linear relationship with the size of subcirculate matrix m , and when m ! 28 , the proposed algorithm will achieve a lower computational complexity than that of the time-domain encoding.
Figure 6 is the ratio of the average computing time of the proposed algorithm Tf to that of the time-domain
encoding Tt . It shows that as the growth of the size of the sub-circulate matrix m , the ratio has a slower decline which indicates the advantage in the computational complexity of the proposed method is no longer increase noticeably, especially when m ! 4000 .
