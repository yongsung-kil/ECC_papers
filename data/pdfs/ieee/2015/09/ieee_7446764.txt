A SIMPLIFED JOINT DEMAPPING AND DECODING FOR LDPC CODED BICM SYSTEMS

Ping Huang1, Yueheng Li1, Meiyan Ju1
1Department of Computer Information, Hohai University, Nanjing, China huangpinghope@163.com, yueheng-li, jmy @hhu.edu.cn

Keywords: BICM, LDPC, Joint Iterative Demapping and Decoding, LLRs Optimization.
Abstract
To make full use of the characteristics of LDPC decoding, Stephan ten Brink had proposed a joint iterative demapping and decoding algorithm for LDPC coded BICM systems. Based on Brink proposed algorithm, we propose a modified joint iterative scheme to further reduce the computation complex. Different from the algorithm presented by Brink, in the process of iterative demapping and decoding, the proposed method simplifies the update calculation of the LDPC parity check nodes by adopting the UMP-BP. More importantly, to compensate for the performance degradation brought by UMP-BP, LLRs optimization based on certain optimality criterion is employed for the proposed method. Numerical analysis shows that the complexity of the proposed method is less than traditional joint iterative algorithm. Simulation results also show that by LLRs optimization the proposed method achieves noticeable performance improvement compared with the BICM-ID and the joint iterative algorithm.
1 Introduction
Bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) [1] has been widely adopted in wireless standards thanks to its excellent performance and flexibility. The BICM with iterative decoding (BICM-ID) [2] between demapper and decoder can improve error performance both in the AWGN channel and Rayleigh fading channel.
For low density parity check (LDPC) coded BICM system, to reduce the computation complex of the traditional BICM-ID receiver, Brink [3] proposed a joint demapping and decoding algorithm based on the LDPC bipartite graph decoding structure. The joint iterative algorithm incorporates the demapping procedure into the belief propagation (BP) iterative decoding, preventing the soft information loss of demapping process as in the traditional BICM-ID schemes. The simulation in [4] confirmed better bit error ratio (BER) performance and much less complexity of the joint iterative algorithm compared with the BICM-ID.
Based on the joint iterative algorithm, we propose a novel joint demapping and decoding method to further reduce the complexity. As the update calculation of the LDPC parity check nodes is the major deciding factor for the total complexity, we adopt simplified uniformly most

powerful (UMP-BP) decoding [5] to decrease the update calculation complexity of the parity check nodes. As shown in the literature [6], UMP-BP incurred the decoding performance degradation because of the degree of accuracy of check nodes output information decline. Especially for large column weight LDPC codes, the decoding performance loss is more than 1 dB. To avoid the loss, we propose LLRs optimization based on certain optimality criterions. Firstly, as the BP decoder is sensitive to imperfect initial channel information, we correct the initial demapper output LLRs according to generalized mutual information (GMI) maximization [7] [8]. Secondly, due to the sub-optimality of the simplified parity check nodes calculation, we scale the check nodes output LLRs in each iterative process. The scalar factor is searched by the rule of consistency condition [9]. Simulation results verify that the proposed method can achieve noticeable performance gains with a minor complexity compared with BICM-ID.

3.3 Complexity Analysis
Compared with the standard joint iterative scheme, as can be seen from Equation (3) and Equation (11), the proposed scheme replaces the exponent operation with minimum comparison operation. So the proposed scheme reduces the complexity significantly.
On the other side, due to the LLR optimization, the proposed scheme will increase the complexity inevitably. We adopt some strategy to reduce the complexity in the LLRs optimization process. Firstly, in the optimization of the demapper output initial channel LLRs before iterative decoding, from GMI calculation of Equation (9) we use sum operation to replace expectation operation to reduce the complexity. Secondly, in the optimization of check nodes output LLRs, as can be seen from Equation (14), scalar factors are obtained by finding probability density function (pdf) of the check node output LLRs. Actually, by simulation, we observe that in the SNR region of interest, the scalar factor of per bit channel tend to a constant during the iterative decoding process. So in the actual application, it is with no need to compute the factor in each iterative process.
Figure 3 shows the BER curves if adopting different scalar factors for check nodes output LLRs in (504, 252) LDPC coded BICM system with SP labelling 8PSK. Xaxis represents scalar factor, Y-axis represents bit error rate. As shown in Figure 3, in the SNR of 2.5 dB, 2.0 dB, 1.5 dB, the optimal scalar factors are about 0.8. Thus the check nodes output LLRs are scaled by a constant 0.8 during each iteration.
