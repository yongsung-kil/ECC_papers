A Local Flat Histogram Method for Evaluating the Performance of LDPC Codes

Yuan Liu∗ ∗School of Electronic Information Engineering
Tianjin University Tianjin, 300072, China
Abstract—The performance of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes can be evaluated by using the dual adaptive importance sampling (DAIS) method. To further accelerate the convergence speed of simulation, an improved method is proposed in which the biased distribution is multiplied with an auxiliary biased function. In this proposed method, samples are limited in the restricted region, where most of the events result in decoding failure, by multiplying the original biased distribution of DAIS method by a second bias. This scheme speeds up the convergence of simulation in the restricted region. Compared with the traditional DAIS method, simulation results for two specific LDPC codes verify that this proposed method achieves the significant simulation gain without sacrificing the accuracy.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes and some of their relatives with large code length can achieve the capacityapproaching error correction performance when iteratively decoded [1]–[3]. The performance of these codes is usually measured using the bit error rate (BER) or frame error rate (FER) after decoding. When the conventional simulation method, for example, the Monte Carlo (MC) method is employed, the accuracy of estimates of BER/FER depends on the number of error events which are sampled. Furthermore, LDPC codes show the superior error floor performance compared with the known turbo codes considering the relatively large minimum distance. Therefore, for the case of estimating the extremely low error BER/FER of LDPC codes, the MC method needs numerous samples in order to obtain a certain number of error events and to achieve a precise estimation since the convergence of the estimator variance using the MC method is quite slow.
Fortunately, many methods have been developed to accelerate the MC simulation in the literature. Importance sampling (IS) technique drives the error events, which leads to the decoding failure, to emerge more frequently, and then can reduces the estimator variance efficiently [4]–[6]. In this approach, the selection of the biasing distribution is a crucial issue. Performance of the regular LDPC code is evaluated by using standard IS method in [7], where the code structure and error region are considered in the choice of biasing distribution. However, this method encounters obstacles if the code structure is complex. In order to evaluate the irregular LDPC code, methods using IS approach in conjunction with the trapping sets (TSs) identification is adopted in [8]–[12], with considering that TSs contribute to the error events at high

signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs). Since searching all the trapping sets is NP hard, estimates obtained by these approaches are underestimated.
Instead of finding the concrete formula of the optimal biasing immediately, in [13] and [14], two improved versions of IS method, i.e., dual adaptive importance sampling (DAIS) and fast flat histogram (FFH) obtain the optimal biasing iteratively. Based on the multicanonical Monte Carlo method [15]–[18], using a control quantity, these two methods adopt the iterative updating scheme which modifies the biasing in each of the iterations and converges to the optimal. Thus error events in the tail of probability density function (pdf) of control quantity are sampled adaptively and more frequently. Furthermore, an improved DAIS method is presented in [19] to reduce the decoding complexity of the simulation. However, the convergence speed is still slow when very low error probabilities of moderate-length LDPC codes are estimated with these two types of methods.
To further accelerate the convergence speed in the simulation using the DAIS method, an improved method called local flat histogram (LFH) method is proposed. In this method, original biasing offered by adaptive importance sampling (AIS) is multiplied by the auxiliary function [20], which is the second biasing artificially enhancing the occurrence probability of samples in the restricted region Ω. Therefore, the histogram in Ω tends to be flat rapidly, and the estimates of probability mass functions (pmfs) approximate accurate values rapidly. Once the histogram in Ω is flat and the estimate is accurate, the error probability will be obtained. In terms of the histogram in Ω, the speed of tending to be flat is accelerated. Then, the convergence of estimates is speed up. Furthermore, it reduces the number of samples and saves the simulation time.
The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section II describes the traditional efficient DAIS method. Section III proposes the LFH method and gives a comparison between the proposed method and the DAIS method. Section IV shows the simulation results using two specific LDPC codes. Section V concludes this work.

III. LOCAL FLAT HISTOGRAM METHOD
Though DAIS is very efficient, the speed of estimates approaching accurate values in traditional DAIS is still slow, when the performance of moderate-length LDPC codes is evaluated. Therefore, an improved DAIS method employing the auxiliary biasing is proposed to deal with this problem.

A. Auxiliary biasing
The auxiliary biasing is introduced in [20] to obtain the accurate estimates of pmf in Ω, where the events are important for simulation. By multiplying the original biasing distribution by an auxiliary biasing, the sampling probability in Ω is enhanced. Finally, the estimates are weighted to remove the bias. The form of the auxiliary biasing which can be set arbitrarily should consider the following principles. In Ω, the value of auxiliary biasing varies slowly or remains constant. However, in the exterior of Ω, the value varies with |k − kmin| or |k − kmax| increasing, where the changes in speed can be set arbitrarily.

B. Proposed method

Two biased AIS simulations are performed to compute the error probabilities of LDPC codes in our presented approach. Both of the two simulations include two biases, which are the original biasing and the auxiliary biasing.

C. Comparison with traditional DAIS method
1) The convergence speed: Error probability is computed in a region in Ω where fluctuations in the estimates are quite small. Thus, we focus on the convergence speed of estimates in this region.
The traditional DAIS method is a technique based on the overall flat histogram. The histogram in the small k region tends to flat first in the early iterations, and the corresponding probabilities in this region converge to the accurate values. In the subsequent iterations, the main purpose of simulation is to estimate the probabilities in the large k region. These estimates are not accurate until the histogram in the whole region of [0, L − 1] tends to flat. However, the histogram tends to flat slowly with j increasing, which is illustrated in Fig. 1 (a). Therefore, the estimates of probabilities in the large k region converge to the accurate values slowly.
The proposed method solves the problem above efficiently. When the auxiliary biasing is introduced, nearly all the samples contribute to obtaining the estimates in Ω in the subsequent iterations. As j increases, the histogram in Ω tends to flat rapidly, which is illustrated in Fig. 1 (b). In this way, the estimates of probabilities are obtained rapidly.
2) The smooth region of estimates: Fig. 2(a) and (b) show the results of the first AIS simulations at different SNRs when control quantities are different. In the two figures, the direction of the arrow represents the direction of SNR increasing; the interval of V in the rectangular box represents the region where error probability is computed by using (2). In Fig. 2(a), it is evident that the smooth region of Perr,k curve (denoted with circles) becomes small, and statistical uncertainty becomes large when SNR increasing. The smooth region refers to the part of the curve with small fluctuations. This leads to the result that we could not obtain the accurate estimates using (2) in the large V region at higher SNRs. However, as is shown in Fig. 2(b), when the control quantity is chosen as (5), the smooth region of Perr,k curve (denoted with circles) is much wider, and then the error probability can be easily obtained using (2).
In summary, LFH method can accelerates the convergence speed. The accurate estimates of both Perr|k and Pk|err in the large k region are obtained rapidly using this method. Then, Perr is obtained.

V. CONCLUSION
A new method called local flat histogram method is proposed to accelerate the speed of histogram tending to be flat in the restricted region by introducing an auxiliary biasing in traditional DAIS. Therefore, the proposed method can speed up the convergence of simulation and save the simulation time. The proposed approach can be used to evaluate the error probability of moderate-length LDPC codes. Simulation results with two specific LDPC codes demonstrate that simulation gain can be achieved at very low error probabilities while maintaining the statistical accuracy. In the future, we will investigate this method using some special kind of LDPC codes, for example, the Margulis code (2640,1320), or codes with the more practical code lengths or error floor performance [25], [26]. Extending the investigation to some other channels such as the Poisson channel could also be interesting considering the practical applications in satellite optic communications [27].
