LDPC Codes for Low-Complexity Analog Decoders

Hao Zheng∗, Xuhui Ding∗, Zhiyuan Wang∗, Yimin Li† and Haikun Luo‡ ∗School of Information and Electronics, Beijing Institute of Technology, 100081, Beijing, China
†Beijing Institute of Remote Sensing Equipment, Beijing, China ‡Science and Technology on Electronic Information Control Lab, 610036, Chengdu, China
Email: 3120130330@bit.edu.cn

Abstract—Compared with their digital counterparts, LDPC analog decoders own higher power efficiency which is suitable for low-power-consumptions applications. However, the high designing complexity of analog decoders with long block length has impeded their real-world applications. This paper presents a method to construct LDPC codes that can lower the designing complexity significantly by using identical models to form the whole decoder, as well as rules that codes should satisfy. Simulation results show that the proposed LDPC codes could lower the designing complexity with little performance lose and have better error-correcting performance than codes in CCSDS standard.
Keywords—LDPC codes; Low-complexity analog decoders; Factor graphs; Identical building block
I. INTRODUCTION
Wireless data transmission for long distance is developing towards higher speed and lower power consumption, as one of efficient ways to provide more channel margin, channel codes attracts much attentions. LDPC codes [1], which have excellent correction capability, have become one of the most common channel codes widely used in wireless data transmission. Recent years, analog LDPC decoders implemented on CMOS circuits have been proved to own outstanding improvements in power efficiency compared to their digital counterparts with less error-correcting performance loss [2]–[10] . However, researches of analog decoders are still in concept design, and are still far from real-world applications.
One of the main problems in LDPC analog decoder applications is that block lengths of decoders are not long enough, while the fact has been disclosed that LDPC codes could get good error-correcting performance when the block length is large enough [1], [11]. But, to maintain the continuous time operation in analog decoder, the parallel flood strategy is employed, which leads to the circuit complexity to grow linearly with the block size. Hence, the designing complexity of analog decoders, which could be measured by the number of wires mapped and gates placed, is too high for LDPC codes that fit applications.
In this paper, the method to construct LDPC codes that lower the designing complexity of analog decoders is proposed, as well as rules that the codes should satisfy. The codes are built from carefully rearranging row vectors of several basic matrices so that corresponding analog decoders could be implemented by connecting several identical building blocks, which allows the designing complexity to reduce from mapping the whole decoder to single block. Moreover, a series of decoders with different block lengths could be implemented by the identical building cell.

The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II illustrates the relationship between LDPC codes’ factor graphs and its analog decoder, and presents rules that codes should satisfy to lower the designing complexity. Section III introduces the method to build LDPC codes and elucidates how the proposed codes could satisfy those rules. Examples of proposed codes and corresponding experimental results are shown in Section IV. Section V concludes this paper.
II. RULES FOR LOWERING DESIGN COMPLEXITY
A. Factor Graphs and Analog Decoders of LDPC Codes
The decoding problem of LDPC codes could be converted into Marginalized Product of Functions (MPF) problem through regarding its linear equations and bits as functions and variables. The electronic network of analog decoder could be acquired by translating nodes into ”probability gates”, and wired them together according to the topology of factor graphs [12], [13]. Then the decoding procedure of LDPC codes could be conducted in factor graphs by massages passing following sum-product algorithm [12].
In analog circuits, the function of check nodes and variable nodes could be implemented by Soft-XOR gates and Soft-Equal gates, respectively [2]. However, message passing between these two kinds of nodes through one connection in factor graphs would be conducted by 4 wires in analog decoders, which are transmitting four currents or voltages representing probabilities of 0 and 1 in both directions [2]. Furthermore, as the parallel flood strategy is employed in analog decoders, there is no way to multiplex any wires or soft gates. Hence, the number of wires need to be mapped in analog decoders is four times of the number of connections in factor graphs, which grows linearly with the block length.
B. Rules That Factor Graphs Should Obey
As stated that the designing complexity growth linearly with block length, which impedes the real-world application of LDPC analog decoders possessing long block length. To achieve an analog decoder owning long block length without too much designing complexity growth, the way is considered that constructing decoders with several identical cells. The identical cell is the basic building block in layouts of analog decoders, which could reduce the designing complexity measured by the number of wires and gates designed from mapping the whole network of decoder to single building block. Then there are some rules that the factor graphs of LDPC codes should obey to build decoder with identical building blocks.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, rules are first introduced that subgraphs of LDPC codes’ factor graph should be isomorphic to allow corresponding analog decoder implemented by several identical models, and then the method to construct these LDPC codes is proposed. Analysis of designing complexity indicates the advantages of proposed codes in the number of wires and gates mapped, and simulations results show that the proposed codes have better performance compared with codes based on CCSDS standard and that performances of decoders built on identical cells are close to BP algorithm. Furthermore, the limitation of extension factor is also explored, which is the indicator for the number of decoder that could be built from one identical blocks.
