Genie-aided Adaptive Normalized Min-Sum Algorithm for LDPC Decoding
Yue Liu, Kewu Peng, Changyong Pan, Liwen Fan
Tsinghua National Laboratory for Information Science and Technology (TNList), Beijing 100084, P. R. China National Engineering Lab. for DTV (Beijing)
Department of Electronic Engineering, Tsinghua University, Beijing 100084, P. R. China Shenzhen City Key Laboratory of Digital TV System, P. R. China liuyue 14@mails.tsinghua.edu.cn

Abstract-This paper proposes an algorithm for the decoding of low-density parity check (LDPC) codes, aiming to retrieve the performance degradation of conventional normalized min-sum algorithm, especially for irregular LDPC code. In our proposed algorithm, normalization factors are adjusted dynamically and adaptively during iterative decoding process according to look-up tables obtained by training and simulation in our proposal. The look-up tables construct the relationship between normalization factor a and the failed parity-check equation proportion p, which could be calculated in each decoding process. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm can achieve a performance closer to the sum-product algorithm and a coding gain of around 0.2dB compared to the conventional normalized min-sum algorithm for rate 2/3 irregular LDPC codes over the AWGN channel. Our proposal is independent from the channel characteristics and retains low complexity. The implementation facility and the excellent performance make the proposed algorithm expect a wide application.
Index Terms-Low-density parity check codes; iterative decoding; belief propagation algorithm; genie-aided adaptive normalization.
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes [1], first discovered by Gallager in the 1960s, and later rediscovered by MacKay and Neal [2], [3], could achieve near Shannon-limit performance [4], [5] with wise decoding proposal.
LDPC codes could achieve superior performance via soft decoding, which use the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) instead of probabilities. Soft decoding could be implemented by iterative decoding employing the belief propagation algorithm [6] (referred to as the sum-product algorithm (SPA) in [3]). Unfortunately, SPA is prohibitive in practical system due to its high hardware implementation complexity. To avoid the implementation complexity, min-sum algorithm (MSA) is proposed in [7] and [8] and this algorithm is universal for the independence from the channel characteristics. A performance improved algorithm referred to as the normalized min-sum algorithm (NMSA) is proposed in [9], which introduces a constant normalization factor a. The normalization factor a makes the soft information value calculated by MSA equal to that by SPA during iterative decoding process on the average sense. Adaptive normalized min-sum algorithm (ANMSA) is proposed in [10] wherein a is adaptively determined by failed parity-check equation proportion p. This algorithm improve

the performance of NMSA for the estimation of a is more accurate and simple.
Theoretically [11], a is determined by degree of check nodes de and the priori average mutual information I e between check node inputs and the transmitted code bits, which alters in the process of iteration. I e is relevant to channel characters while de is determined by specific LDPC code. Thus, ANMSA should also be revised, especially for irregular LDPC code. In [12] , [13] , a is required to be estimated afresh in each iteration, and in [14], two component factors are estimated to seek more accurate revision.
To achieve better performance than ANMSA and keep the virtue of low implementation complexity, this paper proposes a Genie-aided adaptive normalized min-sum algorithm (Genie-aided ANMSA) for LDPC decoding. This proposal utilizes multiple normalization look-up tables. The look-up table characterizes the relationship between normalization factor a and the failed parity-check equation proportion p for a specific check node degree de, wherein a is adaptively determined by p at each decoding iteration. Obviously, p could be easily measured in decoding process. Furthermore, the upper performance bound of MSA, referred to as Genieaided bound, could be obtained by the proposed algorithm via Genie-Aided Training, which calculates the accurate value of a and update the soft information in each decoding iteration. The normalization factor changes adaptively according to the normalization look-up tables throughout the decoding process. Thus, the accuracy of the soft information delivered in the process of the MSA is improved for all SNRs and all iterations.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Several existing algorithms would be reviewed In section II. In Section-III, our Genie-aided ANMSA proposal is proposed and the process to generate look-up tables and Genie-aided bound is provided. BER simulation is performed and the results are presented in Section IV. Finally, conclusions are given in Section V.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposes an algorithm for the decoding of low-density parity check (LDPC) codes, which could simplify the computation of SPA and outperform the conventional NMSA apparently. In the proposed algorithm, several lookup tables, which are the discrete format of adaptive functions, are generated via training and simulation. Moreover, general-adaptive functions and aiming-adaptive functions are generated for different situations. Normalization factors carried during iterative decoding process are adjusted dynamically and adaptively according to look-up tables. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm can achieve a performance closer to the SPA and a coding gain of around 0.24dB compared to the conventional NMSA over the AWGN channel.
This algorithm is also suitable for hardware application and could be adopted as an efficient decoding proposal.
