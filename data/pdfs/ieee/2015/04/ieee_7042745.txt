Recoverability of Variable Nodes in Periodically Punctured LDPC Convolutional Codes
Hua Zhou, Member, IEEE, and Norbert Goertz, Senior Member, IEEE

AbstractвҖ”The concept of transformed codes is introduced to algebraically evaluate the recoverability of punctured nodes for periodically punctured low-density parity-check convolutional codes (LDPC-CCs) by a simple matrix-based approach. The proposed very efficient algorithm allows to identify and exclude those candidate puncturing patterns in a computer search that contain unrecoverable nodes that would lead to bad error-correction performance. Moreover, a novel upper bound on the maximum punctured code rate is obtained, above which any punctured code must contain unrecoverable punctured nodes.
Index TermsвҖ”Low-density parity-check convolutional codes (LDPC-CCs), spatially-coupled codes, recovery tree, transformed codes, recoverability of punctured nodes.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check convolutional codes (LDPCCCs) [1], also known as spatially-coupled codes, outperform the corresponding LDPC block codes (LDPC-BCs) with better iterative decoding threshold [2] and equivalent computational complexity [3]. Some recent work on LDPC-CCs with quasi-cyclic (QC) structure was presented in [4] and [5]. A comparison between irregular LDPC-CCs and QC LDPC-BCs was discussed in [6] with respect to delay and complexity issues.
Rate-compatible (RC) codes, a type of rate-adaptive codes, are designed for time-varying channels. Puncturing and graph extension are frequently used techniques to obtain RC codes. Based on graph extension, Nguyen et al. [7] presented a method to obtain RC LDPC codes with low encoding complexity and thresholds within 0.2 dB of capacity, and Si et al. [8] constructed RC LDPC-CCs achieving the capacity of the BEC. On the other hand, Ha et al. [9] proposed a puncturing scheme to obtain RC LDPC-BCs based on the recoverability of punctured nodes. Recently, Asvadi et al. [10] obtained RC punctured LDPC-BCs by evaluating the number of short cycles with low approximate cycle extrinsic message degree.
Periodical puncturing, introduced by Hagenauer [12], is particularly designed for conventional RC convolutional codes by choosing the optimal distance spectra. However, the sparsity of LDPC-CCs results in high complexity of computing the distance spectrum. Alternatively, Zhou et al. [11] proposed

The recoverability of punctured variable nodes is determined by constructing the corresponding recovery tree, and is denoted by m-SR nodes (m-step recoverable nodes) [9]вҖ“[11]. The parameter m indicates the number of decoding iterations required to recover a punctured node. If m is infinite, the punctured variable nodes are unrecoverable, and they are denoted by вҲһ-SR nodes. It has been shown that a good punctured code should eliminate вҲһ-SR nodes as they significantly degrade decoding performance [11].
In this letter, the concept of transformed LDPC-CCs is introduced to simplify the identification of the recoverability of punctured nodes. The recoverability of a punctured node is obtained by evaluating the product of the transformed puncturing pattern and the weight matrix of the corresponding transformed polynomial syndrome former matrix. It converts the node connections in the recovery tree into a simple matrixbased description. Based on this approach, an algorithm with low complexity is proposed to compute the recoverability of punctured nodes without having to know the specific connections between nodes. Also, we obtain an upper bound on the maximum punctured code rate above which any punctured code must contain вҲһ-SR nodes.

IV. CONCLUSION
This paper introduces a simple matrix-based method to identify the recoverability of variable nodes for periodically punctured LDPC-CCs by algebraically evaluating the punctured transformed code. The proposed algorithm is of low complexity and can be used for both block and convolutional LDPC codes. A new upper bound Rf on the maximum punctured code rate is obtained: any punctured code with rate above that bound must contain unrecoverable (вҲһ-SR) nodes.
