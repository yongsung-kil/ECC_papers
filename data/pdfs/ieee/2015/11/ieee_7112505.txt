The Design of Protograph LDPC Codes for 2-D Magnetic Recording Channels

Pingping Chen1, Lingjun Kong2, Yi Fang3, and Lin Wang4

1Fuzhou University, Fuzhou 350015, China 2Nanjing University of Posts and Communication, Nanjing 210044, China
3Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou 510006, China 4Xiamen University, Xiamen 361006, China

This paper investigates the performance of the protograph-based low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for 2-D intersymbol interference (ISI) channels with magnetic recording density. The protograph LDPC codes have been shown to possess simple hardware implementation and excellent error performance over additive white Gaussian noise channels and over partial response channels. We propose a design scheme and construct two new protograph LDPC codes for 2-D ISI channel, with the help of extrinsic information transfer chart analysis and asymptotic ensemble weight distribution analysis. The theoretical analyses and simulated results show that the proposed codes outperform the previously optimized irregular LDPC code in both low- and high-SNR regions. Thus, the proposed codes are good alternatives compared with other error-correction codes for the use in magnetic recording systems.

L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes can perform very close to the Shannon limit over an additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel [1]. The error floor of the LDPC codes [2], however, is a major issue in LDPC codesbased systems that require very low error floors and high throughputs, such as data storage systems. The density evolution [3] and extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) function [4] have been proposed to address this issue.
Different from the AWGN channel, the intersymbol interference (ISI) channel is modeled and used in bit-patterned media recording, heat (or microwave) assisted magnetic recording, and shingled writing/2-D magnetic recording (TDMR) systems. One challenge that severely degrades the error performance of such TDMR systems with ultrahigh recording density 4 Tb/in2 is 2-D ISI, which consists of 1-D ISI and intertrack interference [6]. Turbo equalization has been proposed to mitigate the ISI by iteratively decoding between an inner detector and an outer decoder [7]. In this paper, we conduct an in-depth investigation on the error performance of protograph LDPC codes in 2-D ISI magnetic recording channels. Our aim is to improve the error performance of such 2-D ISI channels by applying good channel codes that not only can be implemented easily, but also provide superior decoding performance. B. Protograph LDPC Codes
A protograph can be seen as a Tanner graph containing a relatively small number of nodes [14]. A protograph consists of a variable-node set, a check-node set, and an edge set, respectively. Each edge connects a variable node and a check node. However, unlike LDPC codes, a protograph allows parallel edges. We denote the adjacency matrix corresponding to a protograph as a base matrix. A large protograph can be further derived by performing a copy-and-permute operation on the base matrix [14]. The resulting protograph LDPC codes are amenable to high speed decoding, and provide outstanding performance at a high code rate in AWGN channels [14], and in partial response channels [12].

IV. PROPOSED PROTOGRAPH CODES
For the AWGN channel, the channel decoder directly decodes from the channel messages and messages are simply exchanged between the variable-node set and the check-node set. However, the turbo decoder for a 2-D ISI channel consists of an inner channel 2-D-DET and an outer LDPC decoder. The channel messages distorted by 2-D ISI and noise are first decoded by the inner detector. Then, the output extrinsic messages are exchanged between the inner detector and the outer decoder in each turbo iteration. Through Monte Carlo simulation, we found that the conventional protograph AR4JA and AR3A codes [13] that were optimized for AWGN channels perform bad in such 2-D ISI channels. Furthermore, for the conventional protograph code with punctured nodes, there will be an additional component for puncturing in both encoder and decoder in ISI systems. Therefore, instead of using the conventional protograph codes, we aim to propose the new protograph codes without punctured nodes for 2-D ISI channels.
The degree-2 variable nodes in the protograph contribute to good error performance in the waterfall region but too many degree-2 variable nodes will introduce an error floor in the high SNR region [14]. Hence, the desired protograph of low error floor for 2-D ISI channels may have a small proportion of degree-2 variable nodes but not too large. Based on the above observations, we consider the following rule to design the protograph codes for 2-D ISI channels.
1) Avoid introducing punctured nodes in the protograph to simplify the encoding and the decoding implementations for 2-D ISI channels.
2) Increase the proportion of degree-2 variable nodes to reduce decoding threshold, and reduce the proportion of degree-2 variable nodes to lower error floor.
Based on the proposed design algorithm, we construct two new types of protograph LDPC codes, called 2-D-P1 code and 2-D-P2 code, for the use in 2-D ISI channels. Fig. 2 shows that the protograph of the 2-D-P1 code has one degree-2 node for every 3n + 6 variable nodes. Next, we reduce the proportion of degree-2 variable nodes in the 2-D-P1 code by adding a new edge to connect the degree-2 node to another check node, and form the 2-D-P2 code, as shown in Fig. 3. A. EXIT Chart Analysis of the Proposed Codes
In the following, we evaluate the thresholds of the proposed codes by finding the minimum channel SNR Eb/N0 to fit the EXIT curve of the 2-D-DET as closely as possible to the curve of the channel decoder, while ensuring that they do not cross over. Fig. 4 plots the EXIT curves of the proposed 2-D-P1 code and the 2-D-P2 code in a 4 Tb/in2 ISI channel, Eb/N0 = 4 dB with BCJR 2-D-DET, and Eb/N0 = 4.3 dB with IRCSDFA-GA 2-D-DET. The EXIT curves of the optimized irregular LDPC code [13] for such a channel is also plotted for comparison.
We define the region between the two EXIT curves (one for the inner detector and one for the outer channel decoder) of a code as the decoding tunnel. We can observe from Fig. 4 that the decoding tunnel of the irregular LDPC code is narrower than that of the 2-D-P2 code, which is slightly narrower than that of the 2-D-P1 code. The results imply that irregular code performs worst and the 2-D-P1 performs best among the three codes in the waterfall region. By fitting the two curves close to each other for the three codes, we obtain that the decoding thresholds of the irregular code, 2-D-P2 and 2-D-P1, with BCJR 2-D-DET, are 4, 3.96, 3.93 dB, respectively, and 4.23, 4.19, and 4.15 dB, respectively, with IRCSDFA-GA 2-D-DET. In particular, the decoding thresholds of the 2-D-P1 codes with BCJR 2-D-DET are only ∼0.1 dB away from the SIR [10]. Note also that the minimum distance can generally be improved if the protographs have less degree-2 variable nodes, while the 2-D-P2 code without degree-2 nodes has a slightly higher decoding threshold than the 2-D-P1 code with a degree-2 node.

VI. CONCLUSION
This paper has proposed two capacity-approaching protograph LDPC codes, namely, 2-D-P1 code and 2-D-P2 code, for a 2-D ISI channel based on the design rule. We have evaluated the performance of the proposed protograph codes by asymptotic analyses of iterative decoding threshold and ensemble weight enumeration. The theoretical analyses and simulation results have verified that the two proposed protograph codes of more practicality outperform the irregular LDPC code optimized for 2-D ISI channels. In particular, one of the two proposed protograph codes, i.e., the 2-D-P2 code, exhibits a much lower error floor than the irregular code in the high-SNR region.

B. Asymptotic Weight Distribution for the Proposed Codes
This section studies the asymptotic weight distribution of the two proposed codes. Fig. 5 shows that the typical minimum distance ratios of the 2-D-P1 code and the 2-D-P2 code are 0.023 and 0.031, respectively. The typical minimum distance ratio (growth rate δmin) is a characteristic of the specific protograph from which the LDPC code ensemble is derived. Since the δmin is greater than zero, the LDPC code ensembles constructed from the proposed protographs are guaranteed to have linear minimum distance growth. Moreover, the 2-D-P2 code having a larger growth rate δmin appears to have larger minimum distance and then a lower error floor in the high SNR region, as compared with the 2-D-P1 code.
