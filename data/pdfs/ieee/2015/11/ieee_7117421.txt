Protograph-Based Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Coding for Ultrahigh
Density Magnetic Recording Channels
Lingjun Kong1, Liwen He1, Pingping Chen2, Guojun Han3, and Yi Fang3
1School of Computer Science and Technology, Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Nanjing 210003, China 2College of Physics and Information Engineering, Fuzhou University, Fuzhou 350001, China
3School of Information Engineering, Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou 511300, China
In this paper, protograph-based quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are proposed for the efficient structure coding of LDPC codes in 2-D intersymbol interference (ISI) ultrahigh density magnetic recording channels, such as bit-patterned magnetic recording and 2-D magnetic recording, where a reduced-complexity 2-D detector based on the iterative row–column soft detection feedback with Gaussian approximation is employed instead of the full 2-D Bahl–Cocke–Jelinek–Raviv detector. The proposed protograph-based codes, whose base matrices are constructed according to the degree sequences for 2-D-ISI channels, have a low-complexity QC encoder structure with a readily parallelizable protograph decoder structure. Simulation results show that the proposed protograph-based QC-LDPC codes outperform the random LDPC codes optimized for additive white Gaussian noise channels. Furthermore, the performance of the QC codes is similar to that exhibited by ISI-optimized random LDPC codes designed for 2-D-ISI channels, while retaining the implementational benefits.
Index Terms— 2-D intersymbol interference (ISI) channels, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, protograph LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes.

I. INTRODUCTION
D UE to continued strong demand for increasing storage areal densities, bit-patterned media recording, heat- (or microwave-) assisted magnetic recording, and shingled writing/2-D magnetic recording have been proposed to push the storage density of future hard disks beyond 1 Tb/in2 [1]. One way to boost the storage density is to reduce the separation between recording tracks. However, this results in more crosstalk between the tracks, called intertrack interference (ITI). From a signal processing and coding point of view, the most important technical challenge, which severely degrades the performance of data detection, is the 2-D intersymbol interference (ISI), which is constituted by both 1-D ISI and ITI [2], [3].
Although the symbol-based 2-D Bahl–Cocke–Jelinek– Raviv (BCJR) channel detector [4], [5]—hereinafter called full 2-D channel detector—can provide optimal detection over such 2-D ISI channels, it has prohibitive complexity for the typical 2-D data size. Suboptimal 2-D channel detectors with lower complexity have been proposed in [5]–[7], but their computational burden remains a bottleneck of the receiver. In [8], the well-known suboptimal 2-D channel detector, an iterative row–column soft decision feedback algorithm (IRCSDFA) [6], has been further simplified using Gaussian approximation (GA), the resultant simplified 2-D detector (2-D-DET) is referred as IRCSDFA-GA. It suffers performance loss compared with the original IRCSDFA (as expected), but the loss is minimized by careful decoding-detection scheduling design.
Besides 2-D channel detection, coding also provides a means to combat 2-D-ISI [9]–[13]. Coding for 1-D-ISI channel

[9] and Varnica and Kavcic [10] designed low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes optimized for partial response channels by means of a density evolution analysis. In [11], an optimization algorithm was derived for LDPC codes based on a GA of the messages exchanged in the graph. Experiments show that LDPC codes optimized for additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels may not yield good performance when applied to 1-D-ISI channels with equalization. Likewise, in [12], new LDPC codes optimized for 2-D-ISI channels detected using the 2-D BCJR detector have been designed, and are shown to achieve better performance than LDPC optimized for AWGN channels. However, the LDPC codes in [12] were designed for the full 2-D channel detector, which is highly complex. Random-like LDPC codes optimized for 2-D-ISI channels detected using 2-D IRCSDFA-GA detector in [13] exhibit excellent error-correction capabilities, especially for high codeword lengths. However, random-like codes cause complex hardware implementations due to the high-complexity descriptions, and the quadratic encoding complexity growing with the block length.
In this paper, we design protograph-based quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes for ultrahigh density magnetic recording channels, using reduced-complexity 2-D IRCSDFA-GA detector. The base matrix is constructed according to the degree sequences for 4 Tb/in2 2-D-ISI channels with slight modification. The proposed protograph-based codes have a low-complexity QC encoder structure with a readily parallelizable protograph decoder structure.

V. CONCLUSION
New protograph-based QC-LDPC codes have been proposed for an ultrahigh density 2-D-ISI magnetic recording channel detected by the reduced-complexity IRCSDFA-GA detector. Monte Carlo simulation results have verified that our proposed protograph-based QC-LDPC codes can perform very close to the unstructured 2-D-ISI optimized random LDPC codes, and achieve better performance than the random codes optimized for AWGN channel. Hence, the magnetic recording system performance is maintained while both the LDPC coding and decoding complexities have been drastically reduced due to the protograph-based QC structure.

In Fig. 3, we compare the bit error rate (BER) performance of our proposed protograph-based QC-LDPC codes with the 2-D-ISI-optimized random codes (Scheme 2) and AWGN-optimized random codes (Scheme 3) for three different code lengths in a 2-D-ISI channel with recording density of 4 Tb/in2, where the degree sequences of Schemes 2 and 3 are given in Table II and the degree sequence of the proposed code is based on Algorithm 1 and that of Scheme 2.
Fig. 3 shows that the proposed protograph-based QC-LDPC codes of Scheme 1 (red lines) has a performance gain of ∼0.55 dB over the codes of Scheme 3 (black-dashed lines) at BER of 10−5 for all three different code lengths (Nc = 4608, 9216, 13 824) in a 2-D-ISI channel with recording density of 4 Tb/in2. Furthermore, there is only 0.05 dB loss in the performance of the protograph-based QC-LDPC codes when compared with the significantly more complex, unstructured 2-D-ISI-optimized codes at the BER of 10−5 for all three different code lengths, and nearly not any compromise at the high SNR region. Based on the modified degree sequences for 4 Tb/in2 2-D-ISI channels, new protograph-based QC-LDPC codes have been designed for 2-D-ISI channels. Monte Carlo simulations demonstrated that the new LDPC codes can achieve a larger performance gain over 2-D-ISI channels than codes optimized for AWGN channels, and the achievable performance is comparable with that exhibited by random-like optimized codes of [12].
