A Full Layer Parallel QC-LDPC Decoder for WiMAX and Wi-Fi

Wenchao Zhang1, Song Chen1*, Xuefei Bai1, Dajiang Zhou2

1 Dept. of Electronic Sci. & Tech., University of Science and Technology of China, Hefei 230027, China
2 Faculty of Science and Engineering, Waseda University, Kitakyushu 808-0135, Japan * Email: songch@ustc.edu.cn

Abstract This paper presents a full layer parallel quasi-cyclic low
density parity check (QC-LDPC) code decoder for IEEE 802.16e (WiMAX) and IEEE 802.11n (Wi-Fi). By adopting three techniques including reusable fully parallel check node unit (CNU) structure, path rerouting network (PRN) and reusable permutation network (PN), the proposed decoder gets Gbps level throughput and could support two standards and most code modes of them with low hardware cost. By using turbo-decoding message-passing normalized min-sum (TDMP-NMS) decoding strategy, the bit error rate (BER) of the proposed decoder is decreasing fast to 10-5 at 2.2 dB. It only takes 30~40/30~60 clock cycles in each decoding iteration for WiMAX/WiFi. Using SMIC 40nm low leakage HS RVT CMOS process and 7-bit quantization, the proposed decoder attains 789~2227/470~1879 Mbps for WiMAX/Wi-Fi at 290 MHz, 10 iterations, and only occupies 2.26 mm2 area.
1. Introduction Nowadays QC-LDPC code [1] is widely adopted in
many wireless communication standards, such as WiMAX, Wi-Fi, WPAN (802.15.3c), etc. TDMP scheduling are mainly used for QC-LDPC codes because it has the higher convergence rate which is nearly twice than twophase message-passing (TPMP) [2] and the flexibility with multi-rate support. In order to make tradeoff between performance and complexity, the NMS [3] algorithm is proposed, which is more friendly to hardware.
Some TDMP-based decoders called partially-parallel decoder [4] choose to process the non-zero sub-blocks (NZS-block) block by block. This architecture suffers from low throughput and low parallelism caused by large clock cycle number of each iteration when facing high code rate. To improve the performance, [5] proposed an architecture using two groups of processing elements (PE) to process two NZS-blocks in one clock cycle, and they also made some matrix reordering to reduce the clock cycle number of each iteration. But because of the data dependency problem it could not bring double promotion.
Considering the parallelism, we propose a full layer parallel (FLP) decoder based on TDMP-NMS algorithm. FLP means the NZS-blocks of one layer is processed concurrently. To realize the FLP structure, we propose a fully parallel CNU structure in PE to find the min-value in one clock cycle without long critical paths. Considering of the

hardware cost and the reconfigurability, the proposed decoder could support the code rate 1/2, 2/3 and 3/4 both of WiMAX and Wi-Fi. We also make the matrix reordering operation of the base parity check matrix (PCM) of WiMAX code rate 1/2, which makes the CNU structure fully reused supporting two-layer parallel decoding without adding new components. The proposed decoder uses 30~40 clock cycles each iteration for WiMAX, as well as 30~60 for Wi-Fi. The throughput of the decoder reaches 2.23/1.88 Gbps of WiMAX/Wi-Fi at 290 MHz. Facing the interconnection problem between PN and the PEs, the PRN is brought in. The quantization of 7-bit reduces the chip area with no more than 0.2 dB BER losing.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section 2 introduces the architecture of the proposed FLP-decoder. Section 3 presents the details of PE. Section 4 describes the implementation results and shows the comparisons with previous work. In the end, section 5 draws the summary of this paper.

2.2 Decoder Overall Architecture The proposed decoder architecture is shown in Fig.2.
Here the PN is necessary to make the APP messages be sent to the right PE. The proposed decoder has the most counts of NZS-blocks in one layer to process when the code rate is 3/4, which number is 14 or 15. So eliminating the rightmost 0 elements, we only need 13 shifters here. The rest NZS-blocks without shifting are sent to the PRN I directly. The design of PN’s shifter is inspired by [4]’s work. This kind of shifter is constructed as 128×128 MUX-array to support various input/output number, especially when z is no more than 32 or 64, the shifter can be reused to shift 4 or 2 code frame concurrently, which increases the parallelism of decoder greatly. So it is the reason why PE array’s number needs to be 128.
After being shifted, the APP messages are sent to the PE array. Each PE here computes a whole row of one layer at the same time, so the total array computes the whole layer simultaneously. However, the PN’s output is the messages composed sub-block by sub-block, but the input of PE array needs to be composed of rows. So the PRN I here is used to adjust the messages of shifted sub-blocks arranged in the form of rows, and then send them to the right ports of PE array. Fig.3 shows the workflow of PRN

3. Details of PE 3.1 Top View of PE
As mentioned above, each PE needs 15 β-VNUs (variable node unit) to compute βmn. After βmn obtained, we put the absolute value of them into one CNU, as well as the sign and cn. Since cn is updated at the end of one iteration, we put the parity check operation into the processing of each layer of next iteration, which can reduce the whole calculating amount. CNU computes the formula (2) in one clock cycle. During the time of finding min-value, CNU also needs to find the position of min-value. Then the minvalue with position information and sign value are put into 15 Ln-VNUs, which give the new APP messages of the corresponding code bits. Meanwhile, the parity check information is sent to the DECISION UNIT. The structure of PE is shown in Fig.4.

3.2 CNU Design Description The proposed CNU architecture calculates the min-
value, sign function and parity check together in one clock cycle without long logical circuit delay. [6] needs to find the min-value and get its position at first, then they can find the 2nd min-value. This kind of solution will provide too much long delay. We notice that in the min-value finding tree (MVFT) approach of CNU as shown in Fig.5(b), the alternative 2nd min-values are all already compared with the final min-value. So we can compare the candidates immediately after the min-value being found in every stage. Using this method, CNU only spends a little more time to get the 1st/2nd min-value and the min-value position information simultaneously.
Same as [6], we use a 2-mVU as the smallest unit to compare 2 values, moreover, our 2-mVU also computes the sign function and parity check simultaneously. As shown in Fig.5(a), we also provide one 2-mVU_upd which could compute the 1st and 2nd min-value together. These two 2-mVU construct the whole architecture of CNU, 2-mVU computes the input data to generate both 1st and 2nd min-candidates, and the rest stages use the 2-

mVU_upd. As shown in Fig.5(b), in this structure, we only need four stages to get the results, which reduces the critical path greatly. And because the essence of parity check and sign function is just XOR operation, so the CNU can use the MVFT to get the results of them easily. On account of the input number is 15, so we supply one zero signal to match with the 15th input signal.
Especially for code rate 1/2 of WiMAX, we can reuse the sub-MVFT structure in Fig.5(b). In this situation, we put the data of two layers into the CNU together, and at the 3rd stage of MVFT we can get both the results of two layers concurrently, which resolves the two-layer parallel processing problem very well.

4. Implementation and Comparison Here we compare 3 kinds of quant-bit TDMP-NMS de-
coders with the floating point one and the TPMP scheduling. They are 8-bit (1-5-2, 1 sign-bit, 5 integer-bit and 2 float-bit), 7-bit (1-4-2) and 6-bit (1-4-1). These three quant-bit decoders are implemented in Verilog HDL. RTL simulation was performed on WiMAX code rate 1/2 in AWGN channel with BPSK modulation. The floating point decoders are simulated by MatLab. As Fig.6 shows, the BER curve of TDMP scheduling is converging faster than the TPMP one. For the quant-bit ones, the curve of 6-bit is the worst, which causes even more than 0.4 dB performance loss at 10-6 BER. The 8-bit and 7-bit ones’ curves get better performance, which only get 0.1 dB and 0.2 dB loss compared with the TDMP-NMS floating point decoder. So the 7-bit one is chosen based on the tradeoff between decoding performance and hardware cost.

Table 1 summarizes the proposed decoder and other three previous work. By using TDMP scheduling, the proposed decoder and [5] could support more code modes than [7]. The proposed decoder and [8] support multistandard decoding. For all the four designs, the proposed decoder reaches the highest throughput and it does not get large area by using 40 nm process and 7-bit quantization.

The full layer parallel architecture is the key of improving the throughput.
5. Summary This paper presents a full layer parallel QC-LDPC de-
coder. With taking advantage of TDMP-NMS algorithm, reusable fully parallel CNU, path rerouting network and reusable permutation network, the proposed decoder gets Gbps level throughput and could support two standards and most code modes of them without high hardware cost. Therefore, the decoder features high throughput, great flexibility and can be easily adapted to other applications.
