Area Efficient Half Row Pipelined Layered LDPC Decoder for Gigabit Wireless Communications

Sabooh Ajaz and Hanho Lee
Department of Information and Communication Engineering, Inha University, Incheon, Korea

Abstract— This work presents an area efficient half row partially parallel pipelined LDPC decoder architecture for IEEE 802.11ad standard. It provides better area and throughput tradeoff by overcoming the low throughput bottleneck in conventional half row decoders and high complexity bottleneck in fully parallel decoders. The proposed architecture is implemented using 40-nm CMOS technology. The proposed half-row pipelined decoder achieves the maximum required throughput of 8.4 Gb/s for IEEE 802.11ad standard and shows superior area efficiency compared to previous works.
Keywords-LDPC code; half row; layered; pipelined; decoder.

I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity check (LDPC) codes are widely used in error correction systems primarily because of their superior bit error rate and high throughput potential. Many LDPC decoder architectures are presented in the past decade to achieve better area and throughput tradeoff. One of the attractive option for area reduction is half row parallel layered decoder [1] compared to fully parallel layered decoder [2, 3]. But half row decoder suffers from low throughput problem. This work improves conventional half row decoder and achieves better throughput and better area efficiency compared to other decoder available in literature.

It is worth mentioning that two separate barrel shifter blocks are used for CNUs and VNUs, because they operate on different layer in each clock cycle due to pipelining. CNU shown in Fig. 2 implements Eq. (1) and (2), while VNU implements Eq. (3). Outputs of barrel shifters are distributed to CNUs and VNUs, which are 1-stage pipelined to facilitate the processing of two half splits of H-matrix concurrently as shown in Fig. 3. Pipeline sections XC, YC, XV and YV shown in Fig. 2 are depicted with H-matrix layers and H-matrix splits in Fig. 3. For instance during fifth clock cycle, L3 split-A is processed by XC, while YC process L2 split-B and generates Rl-1 [k] for section YV (VNU L2 split-A). CNU generates Rl [k] that is stored in memory and given to VNU as Rl-1 [k] in next clock cycle. VNU generates Pl-1 [k] that is given to barrel shifters again as Pl-2 [k] in next clock cycle. The flip-flop at the end of min-sorter in CNU stores the intermediate min result (of split-A) for one clock cycle. Valid min result is produced in next clock cycle after processing of split-B for a layer.
III. ANALYSIS AND COMPARISON RESULTS
Table I shows the analysis, implementation and comparison results of the proposed decoder. The decoder is implemented using TSMC 40-nm CMOS technology. Analysis results shows that it reduces the complexity of nearly all components by half compared to fully parallel pipelined layers decoder design given in [3]. Although the clock cycles are increased, but still it achieves the maximum throughput of 8.4 Gb/s, required by IEEE 802.11ad standard. The proposed decoder also reduces the major area consuming components like adders, sign magnitude conversion (SMC), and min comparators compared to non-pipelined layered decoder [2] and flooding decoder [5]. The conventional half row layered decoder [1] shows reduced complexity for barrel shifters and subtractors, but it consumes about 33% more clock cycles compared to proposed decoder. Hence, the proposed pipelined half row decoder achieves 33% better throughput compared the conventional half row decoder. The proposed decoder consumes an area of 0.21 mm2. But it achieves a better area efficiency compared to the conventional half row decoder [1].

IV. CONCLUSIONS
The proposed work presents an area efficient half row layered LDPC decoder architecture, which provides a better throughput compared to conventional half row decoder. It also saves a considerable area compared to fully parallel designs. The proposed decoder can shows an attractive option for next generation gigabit wireless communication system.
