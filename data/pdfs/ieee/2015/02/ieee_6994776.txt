Bit-Level Soft-Decision Decoding of Double and Triple-Parity Reed-Solomon Codes Through Binary Hamming Code Constraints
Xiuni Wang and Siyun Tang

Abstract—This paper discusses bit-level soft-decision decoding of double and triple-parity Reed-Solomon (RS) codes through binary Hamming code constraints. The new expressions of paritycheck equations which possess a certain inherent structure in GF(2) are derived. This structure enables representation of a non-binary parity-check equation as that of a compound binary Hamming code. Such technique makes it possible to decode [n, n-2, 3] and [n, n-3, 4] RS codes by Belief-Propagation (BP) algorithm. Simulation results show that the performance comparable to the existing RS decoding techniques is achievable with the presented methodology.

In this paper, we study bit-level soft decoding of double and triple-parity RS codes. We will show that a favorable decoding performance can be obtained just by the iterative BP decoding algorithm on the binary image. We first investigate the paritycheck equations of the binary image of RS code and rewrite them to new expressions. Then a new Tanner Graph for iterative BP decoding is devised. The associated constraint nodes are not single parity-check codes but the compound binary Hamming codes. This is the key point for us to achieve a better decoding performances when compared with those in [3] and [4].

I. INTRODUCTION
B ELIEF PROPAGATION (BP) decoding algorithm [1] has demonstrated empirical success in numerous applications including low-density parity-check codes, turbo codes and so on. In such, information is passed along the edges of the code’s graph (Tanner Graph) and iteratively updated using some update r ules. It could converge to the optimal solution if a cycle-free Tanner Graph was provided.
Recently, BP decoding algorithm has been applied to the soft-decision decoding of Reed-Solomon (RS) codes. The first successful iterative RS decoding technique was developed by Jiang and Narayanan in 2004 [2]. To alleviate deterministic errors caused by BP decoding on the dense RS parity-check matrix, a stochastic shifting schedule based on the cyclic structure of RS codes is proposed. Significant improvement in performance over algebraic hard decision decoding is obtained for short RS codes. It indicates that the algebraic structure of RS codes can be used properly to improve the iterative BP decoding performance. The automorphism group of codes has been considered in [3] and [4] for iterative decoding of RS codes. The foundation of both papers is to build the automorphism group. An explicit algorithm to compute automorphism group of [n, n − 2, 3] RS code is given in [3] and an effective search algorithm to identify automorphism group of a general [n, n−3, 4] RS is proposed in [4]. VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper we have proposed a bit-level soft-decision decoding of double and triple-parity RS codes. Based on the binary image of RS code, we first present the new expressions of the associated parity-check equations. These expressions indicate that each parity-check equation of RS code with roots αi(0 < i < 3) over GF(2q) can be viewed as parity-check equations of a compound Hamming code over GF(2). Then the massage passing decoding algorithm is proposed based on a new Tanner graph composed of Hamming constraint nodes. Simulation results show that our decoding algorithm performs well when compared with the existing decoding algorithms. Even though the idea in this paper can be generalized to other eligible nonbinary cyclic codes with roots more than three, yet the decoding performances are not so good as what we imagined. Further works should be done to improve the proposed decoding algorithm for other codes.
