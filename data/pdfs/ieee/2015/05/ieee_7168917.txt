A 3.46 Gb/s (9141,8224) LDPC-based ECC scheme and on-line channel estimation for Solid-State Drive Applications

Kin-Chu Ho, Chih-Lung Chen, Yen-Chin Liao, Hsie-Chia Chang, and Chen-Yi Lee Department of Electronics Engineering and Institute of Electronics, National Chiao Tung University, Hsinchu, Taiwan
e-mail: chu.ee97g@nctu.edu.tw, {lung,hcchang}@si2lab.org

Abstract—As the reliability of NAND Flash memory keeps degrading, Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are widely proposed to extend the endurance of Solid State Drive (SSD). However, implementing powerful decoding algorithm such as soft min-sum algorithm with high decoding speed comes along with higher hardware cost. To achieve efficient hardware cost, we propose a multi-strategy ECC scheme which consists of modified gradient descent bit-flipping (MGDBF), hard min-sum, and soft min-sum decoders. The MGDBF decoder aims to correct most of the erroneous codewords with advantages of high decoding throughput and low hardware cost, while the soft min-sum decoder is targeted to correct codewords with large number of errors under moderate decoding throughput and reasonable hardware cost. In addition, we propose a bi-sectional channel estimation technique which enables on-line estimation of distribution to generate accurate soft information for LDPC decoding with low complexity. The ECC codec and the complete Toggle DDR 1.0 NAND interface control circuits are integrated and fabricated in 90nm CMOS process. The throughput of proposed MGDBF decoder achieves 3.46 Gb/s which satisfies the throughput requirement of both toggle DDR 1.0 and 2.0 NAND interfaces.
Index Terms—LDPC codes, Solid State Drive, NAND Flash memory.
I. INTRODUCTION
The evolution of storage device has been moved from conventional Hard Disk Drive (HDD) to the Solid State Drive (SSD). In the meantime, the NAND flash device also migrates toward techniques of aggressive process geometry scale-down, multiple bits per cell, and 3D vertical gate to increase the storage capacity. However, the drawbacks of shrinking noise margin and program/erase disturbance among neighboring cells severely degrade the endurance of flash memory. How to keep the reliability of stored data while increasing the storage capacity is still challenging. Fig. 1 shows the circuit structure and the scenario of multi-level storage in NAND flash memory. Since the noise margin becomes smaller, the distribution drifts due to read/program/erase disturbance, data retention, and the temperature variation will introduce high raw bit-error rate if the sensing voltage are not adjusted accordingly. Nonetheless, the hardware overhead of dynamically changing the sensing voltage is still expensive. Therefore, modern error-correcting code (ECC) and advanced memory signal processing algorithm become two essential ways to extend lifetime of flash memory.
Recently, Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1], [2] received much attention in NAND Flash application due to its powerful correcting capability. However, the expensive implementation cost of powerful decoding algorithm such as minsum algorithm, together with long sensing latency for reading soft information, have blocked the rapid adoption of LDPC codes in SSD application. This situation will be changed if there exists a low-complexity decoder which can correct most of the data quickly with short sensing latency, while there

To achieve this, we propose a multi-strategy ECC scheme which includes modified gradient descent bit-flipping (MGDBF), hard min-sum and soft min-sum decoders. The MGDBF decoder can decode quickly when the Raw BER is low, as well as only hard information is needed to avoid long sensing latency. Meanwhile, the soft min-sum decoder can provide stronger protection in order to extend the endurance of SSD.
As the noise margin becomes smaller, the sensing voltage must be changed accordingly when the distribution drifts occur [3]. Otherwise, the Raw BER will be increased to an uncorrectable level. Moreover, the correcting capability of soft min-sum decoders is highly dependent on the accuracy of soft information. It implies that the estimation technique of distribution is critical to guarantee the reliability of SSD. Therefore, we propose a bi-sectional channel estimation technique which can estimate the mean of distribution accurately. Besides, it enables on-line estimation to handle the often changed distribution with very low-complexity. The simulation results show that the proposed technique successfully assists soft min-sum decoder to extend the SSD endurance to P/E cycle 1200 in arbitrary unit (a.u.).
Finally, we integrated the Toggle Double Date Rate (DDR) 1.0 [4] NAND interface control circuits and the multi-strategy ECC scheme to provide real-time performance evaluation. The bi-sectional channel estimation technique is embedded in the firmware level in SSD. We adopt an quasi-cyclic (QC) (9141, 8224) LDPC code and the ECC codec includes encoder, MGDBF, hard min-sum, and soft min-sum decoders together with shuffled scheduling [5]. The test chip is implemented in 90nm UMC CMOS process and occupies 6.48mm2 area. The proposed MGDBF decoder can achieve decoding throughput of 3.46 Gb/s with only 43K gate count under clock frequency of 300MHz. The high decoding throughput can satisfy the throughput requirement of both toggle DDR 1.0 and 2.0 NAND interfaces [4], [6].

The functional block of a SSD is presented in Fig. 2 and the proposed ECC scheme is located inside the memory controller. The data from host is managed by flash file system and then encoded by ECC codec. After encoding, the data is written to one of the 8 channels. Each channel consists multiple NAND memories. The conventional ECC scheme is BCH codec, LDPC codec, or mixed of both [7], [8]. The BCH code is hard decoding algorithm which can correct errors within correcting capability. Once the error number is higher than error correcting ability of BCH code, the decoding fails. For LDPC code, it supports hard min-sum decoder and soft min-sum decoder if soft information is available. However, the decoding latency is long if the soft min-sum decoder is activated. Nevertheless, the gate count of min-sum decoder is also high, leading to another popular solution - the combination of BCH and LDPC code. Unfortunately, it suffers from code-rate loss and long latency since the codec needs encoding and decoding twice. In this paper, we propose a multi-strategy ECC scheme to overcome the drawbacks mentioned above.
Fig. 3 shows the flow control and block diagrams of the proposed multi-strategy ECC scheme. The readout data from NAND flash memory will be fed to MGDBF decoder first, and then followed by hard min-sum decoder and soft minsum decoder. The syndrome checking is performed after each decoder. Once the syndrome is all zeros, the decoding terminated immediately. The proposed scheme can be further classified into two decoding phases: coarse correcting phase and fine correcting phase. The coarse correcting phase is trying to decode most codewords with short sensing latency in NAND Flash memories.

In the ECC codec architecture, there are page buffers for buffering data from the host interfaces for encoding and data from the Flash memories for decoding. When data from Flash memory is being decoded by GDBF decoders, it is also written to page buffers. Once the GDBF decoding fails, the data is fed from page buffer to min-sum decoder for stronger correction. Therefore, the GDBF decoders will not be idle and can continue decoding new data. In addition, it plays an important role in the scheme as it can decode quickly with low hardware cost. The decoding throughput of ECC scheme can be enhanced easily by implementing multiple GDBF decoders without huge hardware overhead.
B. The simulation results
The code we adopt is an (9432, 8512) QC-LDPC code which is constructed by an algebraic code construction algorithm in [9]. The parity-check matrix is composed of 7 x 72 QC blocks, where each QC block size is 131 by 131. The row and column degree of this code are 72 and 7, respectively. For convenience to NAND Flash interfaces, only 128 bits out of 131 bits are used, and the redundant 3 bits are fixed with zeros and shortened into (9141, 8224) QC-LDPC code.
The BER performances of the proposed MGDBF, hard minsum and soft min-sum decoders under AWGN channel are shown in Fig. 4. The performance curve of BCH code is also listed for comparison. It is observed that the proposed MGDBF decoder with 30 iterations can achieve similar performance to BCH code. The Raw BER of three pages at P/E cycles between 900 (a.u.) and 1200 (a.u.) are also pointed out on the curve of raw BER at code rate 0.9. The simulation results show that the MGDBF decoder can cover almost all the error pattern in Lower page. It implies that the Lower page can be accessed at its maximum speed without the need of 2-bit soft information. In addition, the 2-bit soft min-sum decoder can cover all error patterns in Middle and Upper Pages. It guarantee the reliability of SSD even when the Raw BER is high. It should be noticed that the proposed MGDBF decoder can take 2-bit soft information to perform soft decoding if better performance

III. BI-SECTIONAL CHANNEL ESTIMATION
As the distribution drift occurs frequently and unpredictably, a real time estimation of distribution is essential for adjusting the sensing voltage dynamically. Fig. 5 illustrates the concept of proposed bi-sectional channel estimation technique to predict the ideal readout voltages as well as the step size of delta Vread for sensing the soft information [10]. Assume the NAND flash channel is modeled by Gaussian distribution, the mean of a Gaussian pdf coincides with the peak. The integral of the pdf can be approximated by counting number of sensed voltages N (v1, v2) within threshold voltages v1 and v2. The estimation of mean value is described in Fig. 5: 1) calculate integral of left part N 1 and right part N 2; 2) if N 1 > N 2, the mean must fall in the range [v1, (v1 + v2)/2]; otherwise the mean falls in [(v1 + v2)/2, v2]; 3) adjust the value of v1/v2 and repeat step 1 and 2. After few iterations, the mean is estimated quickly with only simple computation. The low-complexity scheme enables on-line estimation for different NAND dies to generate the soft values for the ECC codec.

Fig. 6 shows the simulation results of proposed ECC scheme with estimation technique. The realtime bi-sectional channel estimation technique is embedded in the firmware level in SSD system. After simulating 10000 patterns to investigate the usage percentage of each decoder along different P/E cycles, we only show the middle page with the worst Raw BER due to the limitation of pages. In the coarse correcting phase, the proposed

MGDBF algorithm can correct all patterns before P/E cycle 600 (a.u.) and most of the patterns at P/E cycle 900 (a.u.). This can greatly improve the decoding latency since only few patterns will activate the min-sum decoders. In addition, less than 1% patterns need 2-bit soft information for soft min-sum decoding at P/E cycle 900 (a.u.). As a result, the SSD is promised with high data throughput up to P/E cycle 900 (a.u.) due to short sensing latency. The experiment results also prove that the 2-bit soft min-sum decoder can correct all patterns in Middle page up to P/E cycle 1200 (a.u.), which agrees with the BER performances in Fig. 4.
IV. IMPLEMENTATION RESULTS
In this section, we will introduce the MGDBF decoder architecture and the implementation results of the whole system. The proposed MGDBF algorithm is a modified version of GDBF algorithm [11]. The new features include shuffle decoding with forward and backward (FB) scheduling, as well as dynamic threshold scaling. In conventional shuffle decoding [5], the decoder updates messages along sub-groups in a fixed direction, which is usually from left to right. Therefore, each sub-group can only receives updated messages from a fixed neighbor subgroup and the flow of message passing is lack of diversity. The proposed FB scheduling changes the update direction in opposite way in consecutive iterations. The change in update direction increases the diversity of message flow in the decoding process. Moreover, in conventional GDBF algorithm, there is a pre-defined threshold determining each codeword bit to be flipped or not. In order to improve the BER performance, we update the threshold dynamically along the decoding process. Fig. 7 shows the simulation results of MGDBF among three pages at P/E cycle 900 (a.u.). The two proposed techniques improve the FER and BER performances with no overhead in average number of decoding iteration.
In Fig. 8, the decoder architecture of MGDBF algorithm is presented. When decoding starts, the MGDBF decoder takes 128bits input in each cycle and performs partial syndrome calculation of 131*7 check equations in parallel, indicting 72 cycles are needed to complete calculation of one iteration. When operating in 300MHz, the decoding throughput of MGDBF decoder can reach 3.46 Gb/s at 10 iterations, which is six times higher than the min-sum decoders in [3]. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we present the multi-strategy ECC scheme which can extend the endurance of SSD with efficient hardware cost. The proposed MGDBF decoder plays an important role to provide high decoding speed and cover most of the erroneous codewords. Meanwhile, the soft min-sum decoder provides stronger protection to guarantee the reliability of SSD. In addition, we propose a bi-sectional channel estimation technique which enables realtime estimation of distribution. The estimation requires low computing complexity and is embedded in the firmware level in SSD. Finally, the test chip including proposed ECC codec and complete Flash memory interface control circuits are fabricated in 90nm CMOS Process. The proposed MGDBF decoder occupies 43K gate count only and can achieve decoding throughput of 3.46Gb/s at 10 iterations.

The hard and soft min-sum decoders are implemented with shuffle scheduling and share the same hardware unit. The only difference is that they receive hard and soft information for decoding respectively. Once the hard min-sum decoding fails, the messages inside decoder is reset and soft information is fed to decoder as new channel value. The parity-check matrix is divided into 72 sub-groups and each sub-group takes several cycles for message update in order to minimize the hardware cost. The early termination technique is also adopted for power saving.
Fabricated in 90nm 1P9M CMOS process, our test chip integrates the proposed multi-strategy ECC scheme with Toggle DDR 1.0 NAND interface control circuits. Key features of test chip are given in Fig. 9. The decoder occupies 6.48mm2 area. The chip integrates PLL and flash memory interface to provide reliable clock source, thorough test plan, and on-board realtime evaluation.
