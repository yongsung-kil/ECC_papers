Anytime Properties of Protograph-Based Repeat-Accumulate Codes

University of South Australia, SA 5095, Australia †Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, New Jersey Institute of Technology, Newark, NJ 07102, USA Email: {zhany014, noomy004}@mymail.unisa.edu.au, badri.n.vellambi@ieee.org, khoa.nguyen@unisa.edu.au

Abstract —In this paper, a highly efficient protograph-based
repeat-accumulate (P-RA) anytime code is proposed, which is
derived from the combination of protograph-based low-density
parity-check convolutional (P-LDPCC) and spatially coupled low-
density parity-check convolutional (SC-LDPCC) codes. Density
evolution technique is applied to demonstrate the anytime prop-
erties of the P-RA codes over the binary erasure channel (BEC).
The simulation results show that P-RA codes perform better
than SC-LDPCC and P-LDPCC codes. More importantly, the
finite-length performance of P-RA codes is only dependent on
the decoding delay rather than the decoding message position.
I. INTRODUCTION
In control theory, control signals need to be reliably transmitted to the controller across noisy channels in real-time, i.e., with strict decoding delays [1]. Hence, we need practical real-time encoding and decoding schemes with appropriate reliability for controlling such systems. For such control applications, anytime reliable coding is essential in separating communication and controller designs [2], [3]. In anytime coding, information symbols trickle into the encoder in real time and the encoder encodes the symbols causally. In anytime decoding, at each time instant, the decoder recovers the current as well as previous information symbols. The important requirement of anytime coding is that the decoding error decays exponentially with delay [4].
Two types of LDPC based anytime codes were presented in [5], [6] and [7] [8], respectively. The protograph based LDPCC (P-LDPCC) codes [5], [6] are easy to construct due to its fixed code structure and low encoder complexity. However, the decoding performance decreases over time due to the constant increase of check node degree as time progresses. In other words, the reliability-delay tradeoff of a message depends on its index. Although this issue can be fixed by a feedback link as proposed in [9], a feedback link may not ideal. The anytime codes proposed in [7], [8] are based on spatially coupled LDPCC (SC-LDPC) codes. In the finite-length regime, SCLDPC anytime codes offer index-independent features, due to its fixed degrees for both variable and check nodes. However, the finite-delay performance of SC-LDPC codes is not as good as that of the P-LDPCC counterpart.
The aforementioned observation suggests that introducing fixed degrees of variable and check nodes into P-LDPCC code could keep its finite-length performance independent

with the message index while maintaining the finite-delay performance. This work develops an efficient protographbased repeat-accumulate (P-RA) anytime code by combining the SC-LDPCC codes with P-LDPCC codes. We show that PRA codes asymptotically achieve the desired anytime property. Our proposed P-RA codes can be theoretically analyzed, and provide more flexible trade-offs between decoding performance and anytime characteristics. The contributions of this paper are summarized as follows.
• We develop an improved anytime reliable code known as P-RA codes, which have fixed variable and check node degrees.
• Through density evolution analysis, we show the anytime properties of the P-RA code.
• We show by simulations over the BEC that P-RA codes possess index-independent characteristics, and the performance of P-RA codes outperform those of SC-LDPCC and P-LDPCC codes.
The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces a formal description of anytime transmission. A brief description about the anytime codes proposed in [6], [7] is given in Section III. Section IV presents the P-RA code construction in detail. The finite-length performance and the performance comparison with other anytime codes are given in Section V. The asymptotic analysis and delay exponent comparison of P-RA codes is detailed in Section VI. Finally, this work is concluded in Section VII.
