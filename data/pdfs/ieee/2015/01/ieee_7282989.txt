Iterative Soft-decision Decoding of Reed-Solomon Codes Using Informed Dynamic Scheduling
Huang-Chang Lee, Guan-Xuan Huang, Chung-Hsuan Wang, and Yeong-Luh Ueng.

Abstract—In this paper, an iterative soft-decision decoding algorithm is proposed for Reed-Solomon (RS) codes. The proposed decoding algorithm combines the concepts of adapting the parity-check matrix and informed dynamic scheduling. Before each iteration, the parity-check matrix is re-arranged according to the reliability of the codeword bits, meaning that the influence of the least reliable variable nodes on the decoding process can be reduced. Consequently, the important decoding messages can be scheduled to be updated first, and the reliability of the least reliable bits can be enhanced. The simulation results show that the proposed decoding algorithm can provide significant improvement in the error-rate performance. By using the proposed algorithm, a gain of 0.5 dB can be achieved compared to the conventional adapting belief propagation algorithm.
Index Terms— Reed-Solomon codes, belief propagation, informed dynamic scheduling, iterative soft decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
Reed-Solomon (RS) codes are popular for many applications [1]-[7]. For the linear-block codes with the same length, RS codes can achieve the largest possible minimum distance, and has been adopted in many applications. However, most practical RS codes are decoded using an algebraic hard-decision decoding (HDD) algorithm. The potential of their error-correcting capabilities has not yet been thoroughly explored. Consequently, research focused on the soft decision decoding of RS codes has received significant attention [8][10].
Iterative belief propagation (BP) based on the sum-product algorithm (SPA) is one of the most popular soft decision decoding schemes. It can be applied to the bipartite graphs transferred from the parity-check matrices of linear block codes. The non-zero values in the matrices are mapped to the connections between variable nodes and check nodes, which respectively correspond to codeword bits and check equations.
Iterative BP generates the messages based on the assumption that the decoding bipartite graph is cycle free, and the messages from variable nodes are uncorrelated. Unfortunately, the density of the parity check matrices for RS codes is high, and the corresponding decoding graphs contain a large

In order to mitigate the correlation, the adapting belief-propagation (ABP) decoder [8] is proposed, combining the iterative BP with the adapting parity check matrix (ADP) algorithm. The ADP algorithm modifies the parity-check matrices using row operations after each iteration, and then sorts the variable nodes according to their reliability. The least reliable variable nodes (LRVNs) are mapped to a systematic part of the parity check matrix, i.e., are connected to only one check node, then the influence of the unreliable messages from these degree-1 variable nodes can be reduced.
The decoding messages for the ABP algorithm are updated based on the flooding schedule, where all messages are propagated simultaneously in a single iteration, and the number of decoding messages received at each variable node is equal to the number of neighboring check nodes. The influence of the LRVNs on the other variable nodes can be mitigated by using the matrixes obtained by the ADP algorithm, since the degree of each of these LRVNs is only 1. It can be expected that if these LRVNs are updated with more messages the received vectors can be correctly decoded more often.
Informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) can assist in updating the degree-1 LRVNs more than once in each iteration. In this paper, the layered residual BP (LRBP) decoder is proposed as the first IDS decoder designed for RS codes. Similar to the ABP, the LRBP is also applied to the matrices obtained using the ADP algorithm. Since each check node is connected to one of the LRVNs, the LRBP decoder ensures that all check nodes have an equal chance to propagate their check to variable node (C2V) messages by sequentially selecting a single message from each of the check nodes. Although a variety of IDS decoders have been applied to low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [11]-[14], the LRBP decoder is the first IDS decoder designed for RS codes that have high density parity check matrices. By following the dynamic schedule arranged using the LRBP decoder, the LRVNs can receive more decoding messages compared to using a flooding schedule. At the same time, the number of updates to the most reliable variable nodes (MRVNs) is reduced. The simulation results show that, with the more fair assignment of decoding resources, a significant improvement in the error-rate performance is achieved using LRBP.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. The background to the proposal is introduced in Section II, and the proposed LRBP is presented in Section III. Section IV gives an overview of the simulation results, and Section V concludes the paper.

B. The ABP algorithm
Since the density of Hb is high and its corresponding bipartite graph contains a large number of short cycles, the correlation between messages increases fast after a samll number of iterations. The ABP decoder proposed in [8] is equipped with the ADP algorithm in order to mitigate the correlation by modifying the parity check matrix, as well as the corresponding bipartite graph, after each iteration. Since the decoding messages are propagated along the edges of the bipartite graph, the paths of the propagations are changed along with the modification of the parity check matrix. Hence, the accumulation of the correlation will be slowed down.
An additional purpose for modifying the parity check matrix is to minimize influence of the information from the LRVNs. After each iteration, the variable nodes are first sorted based on their reliability, which can be evaluated using the the absolute LLR values. By using the ADP algorithm, the modified parity check matrix is then derived, where each of the first (n − k) less reliable variable nodes is connected to only one check node. III. PROPOSED LAYERED RESIDUAL BELIEF PROPAGATION

When the iterative BP algorithm is applied to RS codes, the received vectors are more likely to be correctly decoded if all variable nodes collect enough decoding messages and contain high reliability. However, when the ABP decoder is operated on the parity check matrix that was obtained using the ADP algorithm, the decoding messages are propagated by following the flooding schedule. Then the LRVNs mapped to the systematic part can be updated using only a single C2V message in each iteration. In other words, when the influence of the LRVNs on the other MRVNs is minimized, the chances that their reliability will increase is also reduced.
Therefore, the proposed LRBP decoder is designed to arrange the update sequence for the C2V messages, and to enable the LRVNs to be updated more frequently. For this purpose, the C2V residual, which was first included in the RBP decoder described in [11], can be a suitable metric. The C2V residual is defined as the absolute value of the difference between the current C2V value and the C2V value about to be updated. The equation is shown below:

Since only a single C2V message is updated at any one, each check node takes turns to generate C2V messages several times during a single iteration. It is worth noting that, when using the LRBP decoder, the messages about to be used to update the LRVNs will not always be selected. After the first update, the reliability of the LRVNs can be increased, then it may be more urgent to update the variable nodes mapped to the non-systematic part. A single LRVN can wait until its neighboring check node gathers enough contributive information from the decoding messages exchanged in the non-systematic part before being updated again. exchanged in the non-systematic part.

B. Simulation Results
The frame error rate (FER) performances when the LRBP decoder is applied to the (31, 25) and (63, 55) RS codes are shown in Fig. 4 and Fig. 5, respectively. Compared to a conventional algebraic hard-decision decoder and other soft decoding algorithms, including the ABP decoder, the proposed decoder can provide a gain of at least 0.5 dB at an FER of 10−3. Moreover, for the (31, 25) RS code, a near maximumlikelihood (ML) performance can be achieved when using the proposed decoding method. For both ABP and LRBP decoders, 60 iterations are used, and conventional non-binary algebraic hard decoding is appended.
The proposed decoder can also improve the performance of long-length RS codes. Fig. 6 shows that, when the LRBP decoder is applied to the (255, 239) RS code, gain of about 0.3 dB and 0.6 dB can be achieved at an FER of 10−3 compared to the ABP decoder and the Koetter-Vardy (KV) decoder [9], respectively.

V. CONCLUSION
Being the first IDS decoder designed for RS codes, the proposed LRBP decoder can dynamically arrange the update sequence for decoding messages such that the distribution of the decoding resources is no longer limited by the parity check matrix. Using the LRBP decoder, the LRVNs can receive additional decoding messages, and the influence from these LRVNs on other MRVNs can be simultaneously reduced. For RS codes with a variety of lengths and rates, a significant improvement in FER performances can be obtained compared to previously reported iterative soft decoding algorithms
