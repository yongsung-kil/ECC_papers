Invertible Subset QC-LDPC Codes for PAPR Reduction of OFDM Signals
Si Shu, Daiming Qu, Li Li, and Tao Jiang, Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—A type of invertible subset low density paritycheck (IS-LDPC) codes was proposed to reduce the peak-to-average power ratio for orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing systems with low complexity recently, where the key idea is that each invertible subset can be independently inverted to generate other valid codewords of the LDPC code. In this paper, we firstly extend the idea of invertible subsets to quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes, and this new family of QC-LDPC codes is called as invertible subset QC-LDPC (IS-QC-LDPC) codes. Moreover, we propose two types of subset partitioning and deeply discuss the properties of IS-QC-LDPC codes with different subset partitioning. After declaring that interleaved partitioning is more appropriate for IS-QC-LDPC codes than the block partitioning, we propose a novel progressive edge graph-like algorithm to construct good IS-QC-LDPC codes with interleaved partitioning. The simulation results show that the IS-QC-LDPC codes exhibit good error-correcting performance, which is very close to that of the corresponding QC-LDPC codes of the IEEE 802.16e, for a wide range of subset number.
Index Terms—Orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM), peak-to-average power ratio (PAPR), invertible subset, quasi-cyclic low density parity-check codes (QC-LDPC), progressive-edge growth (PEG).

Some of the well-known examples include Digital Audio Broadcasting (DAB), Terrestrial Digital Video Broadcasting (DVB-T), IEEE 802.11, and IEEE 802.16. To reduce PAPR, several methods have been proposed in the literature, such as: coding [5]–[14], companding transforms [15]–[18], partial transmit sequence (PTS) [19]–[22] and tone reservation [23], [24].
Recently, a new kind of low density parity-check (LDPC) codes, called as invertible subset LDPC (IS-LDPC) codes, was proposed in [5], of which each invertible subset can be independently inverted to generate other valid codewords of the LDPC code. Obviously, IS-LDPC codes belong to the type of coding-based candidate generation schemes [12]–[14], and the major idea is to generate candidate codewords and select the candidate codewords with low PAPR for transmission. It has been concluded that the PAPR reduction scheme with IS-LDPC codes has good error-correcting and significant PAPR reduction performance. Moreover, it supports multiple-OFDM-symbol frames very well, by dramatically reducing the searching complexity [5]. Since LDPC codes have been widely adopted in broadcasting standards, such as DTTB (Digital Television Terrestrial Broadcasting), T-MMB (Terrestrial-Mobile Multimedia Broadcasting) and DVB-T2 (Digital Video Broadcasting – Second Generation Terrestrial), it would be interesting to further investigate if the IS-LDPC code could serve as a PAPR reduction scheme for broadcasting systems.
Obviously, IS-LDPC codes belong to random (or randomlike) codes that are generated by computer search based on certain design guidelines and structural properties of the Tanner graphs [25]–[27]. However, the types of structured LDPC code [28], [29] are preferred in practice due to their reduced encoding complexity and efficient memory utilization. It is well known that quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes are typical structured LDPC codes and they can be encoded with simple shift registers, resulting in linear complexity [29]. Moreover, well designed QC-LDPC codes can also perform close to the Shannon limit. Due to the good performance and the easy implementation, QC-LDPC codes have been adopted in several wireless communication standards such as IEEE 802.16e and IEEE 802.11n. Therefore, it is necessary to extend the idea of invertible subsets to QC-LDPC codes.

II. BRIEF REVIEW OF IS-LDPC CODES

An LDPC code is a linear block code defined by a sparse
parity check matrix H of dimension M × N, where the entry
at the mth row and nth column is denoted as hmn. An LDPC code could also be represented by a Tanner graph denoted by
(V, C, E), where V = {v0, v1, . . . , vN−1} is the set of variablenodes, C = {c0, c1, . . . , cM−1} is the set of check-nodes, and E is the set of edges such that E ⊆ V ×C, with edge (vn, cm) ∈ E if and only if hmn = 0. An LDPC code is called regular if the numbers of “1” s in each row and column are constant,
respectively, otherwise it is called irregular.

Subset S is an invertible subset if, for any valid codeword A of A, codeword A = [a0, a1, . . . , aN−1] is a valid codeword of A, where

According to the above definitions, for an IS-LDPC code of inversion freedom U, these U subsets can be inverted independently, and all 2U generated codewords are valid codewords of the IS-LDPC code. To reduce the PAPR of an OFDM signal, the generated codeword with the lowest PAPR is selected for transmission.
Denote SIS as an invertible subset and SOT as the subset of the coded bits that do not belong to SIS. It is worth noting that SOT may include other invertible subsets. Let vector AIS and AOT denote the corresponding coded bits of

SIS and SOT, respectively. The coded bits are reordered such that the codeword A = [AIS, AOT]. Similarly, variable-node set V is also divided into two subsets VIS and VOT, where VIS and VOT corresponds to SIS and SOT, respectively. As a Tanner graph corresponds to a unique parity-check matrix, the parity-check matrix H also consists of two submatrices, i.e., H = [HIS, HOT], where HIS and HOT are submatrices of H, and the columns of HIS and HOT correspond to the variable nodes in subset VIS and VOT, respectively. Given the above notations, the following theorem is reached in [5].
Theorem 1: A necessary and sufficient condition for SIS to be an invertible subset of the LDPC code is that, each row of HIS has even Hamming weight, i.e., HIS[1, 1, . . . , 1]T = 0 over GF(2).
Based on Theorem 1, a modified PEG algorithm was proposed to construct IS-LDPC codes in [5]. The IS-LDPC codes exhibit good error-correcting performance and the PAPR reduction scheme based on IS-LDPC codes significantly reduces the PAPR of OFDM signals. Compared with the existing coding-based candidate generation schemes, this scheme dramatically reduces searching complexity when the codeword is transmitted by multiple OFDM symbols [5].
IS-LDPC codes proposed in [5] is a kind of random LDPC codes. Although random LDPC codes exhibit good errorcorrecting performance close to the Shannon limit, there are some challenging issues relating to application of random LDPC codes, such as high encoding complexity and prohibitively large storage requirements of the parity-check matrix. In an effort toward the algebraic constructions of LDPC codes, QC-LDPC codes are getting more attention due to their linear-time encodability and small size of required memory. These codes can be encoded with simple shift registers and decoded in parallel. Furthermore, well-designed QC-LDPC codes perform as well as random LDPC codes in terms of error-correcting performance and error floor [29]. Due to the good performance and the ease of implementation, QC-LDPC codes are adopted in several wireless communication standards such as IEEE 802.16e and IEEE 802.11n. Therefore, we aim at improving the practicability of IS-LDPC codes by extending the idea of invertible subsets to QC-LDPC codes in this paper.

Invertible subset partitioning refers to a process performed

IS-QC-LDPC codes, the block characteristic of QC-LDPC

zero matrices should be considered. More specifically, for ∀j = 0, 1, . . . , Nb −1, if the locations of 1’s in the first column of the jth column block Hcol( j) are given, the locations of other 1’s in Hcol( j) are uniquely determined. employed. Considering the characteristic, two types of subset

(2) Interleaved partitioning: Variable nodes of the same column block Hcol( j) are partitioned into V0, V1, . . . , VU−1 in an interleaved manner, i.e., {vjL+u, vjL+U+u, . . . , vjL+(L/U−1)U+u} ⊆ Vu for ∀j ∈ {0, 1, . . . , Nb − 1} and ∀u ∈ {0, 1, . . . , U − 1}. D. Comparisons Between Block Partitioning and Interleaved Partitioning
Comparing the block and interleaved partitioning, two conclusions are reached as follows.
• The QC-LDPC codes usually support different code length through varing L. For an OFDM system with a given number of subcarries, when the code length of the IS-QC-LDPC code changes, the number of invertible subsets U should be changed accordingly in order to maintain the PAPR reduction performance. The IS-QC-LDPC code with interleaved partitioning exactly satisfies this requirement, since its U is directly proportional to L. Hence, the interleaved partitioning is more preferred for QC-LDPC codes.
• With the block partitioning, the construction algorithm in [5] could be applied in construction of IS-QC-LDPC codes, and the only required change is to treat the column block as a single variable node in the construction. While for the interleaved partitioning, a novel construction algorithm is needed.
Therefore, this paper focuses on how to construct good IS-QC-LDPC codes with the interleaved partitioning in the followings.
IV. CONSTRUCTION OF THE IS-QC-LDPC CODES WITH INTERLEAVED PARTITIONING
It has been demonstrated that the PEG algorithm can construct regular and irregular QC-LDPC codes with good performance [30]. Similarly, we propose a PEG-like algorithm to construct IS-QC-LDPC codes based on Theorem 3. Here, we call PEG-like algorithm because it constructs a Tanner graph of large girth in a best-effort sense by progressively adding nonnegative aij to B, whereas the PEG algorithm constructs a Tanner graph of large girth in a best-effort sense by progressively establishing edges between variable and check nodes.
In this section, we aim at constructing good IS-QC-LDPC codes that have a similar structure as the QC-LDPC codes in IEEE 802.16e. In the next section, we will illustrate that the constructed IS-QC-LDPC codes are nearly as good as their corresponding QC-LDPC codes of IEEE 802.16e in error-correcting performance.

This helps to reduce the number of short cycles that only contain variable nodes of small degrees, which are one of the sources of the error-floor behavior in the iterative decoding of LDPC codes [32].
• In addition, we should make sure that target B has even number of nonnegative aij, due to Theorem 3.

VI. CONCLUSION AND REMARKS
In this paper, we proposed a new type of QC-LDPC code, called as IS-QC-LDPC code. It is illustrated that interleaved partitioning is more appropriate for IS-QC-LDPC codes than block partitioning. We also designed a construction algorithm for good IS-QC-LDPC codes with the interleaved partitioning of invertible subsets. The advantages of the proposed IS-QC-LDPC code could be summarized as follows:
• The IS-QC-LDPC codes inherit the advantages of IS-LDPC codes such as remarkable PAPR reduction performance with low searching complexity [5]. Simulations demonstrate that the IS-QC-LDPC codes support very high inversion freedom, which ensures significant PAPR reduction for OFDM systems. For instance, the IS-QC-LDPC codes support inversion freedom 96 for code length N = 2304.
• In terms of FER performance, the proposed IS-QC-LDPC codes exhibit error-correcting performance very close to that of the corresponding QC-LDPC codes in IEEE 802.16e, for a wide range of inversion freedom.
• The proposed IS-QC-LDPC codes share the same base matrix structure with the QC-LDPC codes of IEEE 802.16e, which maintain the advantage of fast encoding with linear complexity. Therefore, the proposed IS-QC-LDPC codes have stronger practicability compared with that of IS-LDPC codes.
With all advantages mentioned above, the proposed IS-QC-LDPC codes could serve as an attractive PAPR reduction solution for future multicarrier communication systems.
