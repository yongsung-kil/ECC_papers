Construction of Protographs for Large-Girth 
Structured LDPC Convolutional Codes 
Junho Cho 
Bell Labs, Alcatel-Lucent 
Holmdel, New Jersey, USA 
Email: junho.cho@alcatel-Iucent.com 
Abstract-In this paper, we present a method to construct 
girth-6 protographs that lead to the shortest constraint length 
in the convolutional structure. A stringent structural constraint 
is imposed on the protographs such that the decoder can be 
implemented efficiently. Then, given the structural constraint, it is 
shown that finding the aforementioned protographs is equivalent 
to solving a simple algebraic problem. Based on this mathematical 
formulation, girth-6 protographs are created without having 
to resort to a graph search. Using the girth-6 protographs, 
we derive good low-density parity-check (LDPC) convolutional 
codes by using periodic quasi-cyclic lifting. The performance of 
such constructed codes is compared with AR4JA-based LDPe 
convolutional codes. 
I. INTRODUCTION 
One of the best known constructions of structured low­
density parity-check (LDPC) codes is based on small multi­
edge-type bipartite graphs, so called protographs [1]-[5]. 
For a protograph-based construction, a protograph with an 
optimized degree distribution is first selected and copied S 
times, where S is called the lifting factor. The replicated edges 
are then permuted within each set of S copies to derive a 
particularly good instance of an LDPC code ensemble. The 
copy-and-permute operation is often referred to as lifting the 
protograph. The lifted code inherits the degree distribution of 
the protograph if the permute operations are performed within 
each set of edge copies only. 
The design of protographs and permutation matrices is a 
central problem in the construction of structured LDPC codes. 
With regard to LDPC block codes, there have been a number 
of approaches to solving this problem. Large-girth quasi­
cyclic (QC) LDPC codes have been derived from complete 
protographs such that the derived codes perform well in the 
high signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) regime [6], [7]. A certain 
class of codes have been constructed using finite geometries 
[8], where elimination of 4-cycles is ensured by construction. 
One approach has derived a particularly successful family of 
codes called accumulate repeat-4 jagged-accumulate (AR4JA) 
codes [2]. In the lifting process of the AR4JA protograph, 
heuristics such as the progressive edge-growth (PEG) [9] and 
the approximate cycle extrinsic message degree (ACE) [10] 
algorithms may be used to optimize the girth profile of the 
final code. 
Successful approaches have pursued the construction of 
good LDPC block codes. Regarding the construction of LDPC

In this paper, we study the construction of LDPC convolu­
tional codes of rate (c - 1) / c, with c integer. We investigate 
a method to create girth-6 protographs having the shortest 
constraint length in the convolutional structure. An algebraic 
problem is formulated to find the girth-6 protographs, and by 
solving this, we can enumerate all the protographs with the 
shortest constraint length given the structure and the degree 
distributions. High-girth LDPC convolutional codes are then 
derived from the girth-6 protographs by periodic QC lifting. 
In contrast to the existing approaches that rely on a graph 
search and computer heuristics, our method consists of two 
successive design routines, where the first part (finding the 
girth-6 protographs) is an analytic process and a minimum 
effort to the second part (optimizing the QC lifting) yields 
good structured LDPC convolutional codes.

VI. CONCLUSION 
We presented a simple two-step approach to constructing 
large-girth structured LDPC convolutional codes. We first 
obtained girth-6 protographs having the shortest constraint 
length by solving a simple algebraic problem. Then we con­
structed LDPC block codes by tail biting the protographs and 
LDPC convolutional codes by periodic QC lifting such that 
the girth of the derived graph is further increased. The short 
constraint length, corresponding to the block length for LDPC 
block codes, reduces the decoder memory, and the stringent 
structural constraint imposed on the convolutional proto graph 
lead to greatly simplified decoder architectures. Using the 
suggested method, LDPC convolutional codes with very small 
decoder complexity can be easily constructed while attaining 
the superior error performance offered by spatial coupling.
