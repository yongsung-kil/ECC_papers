AbstractŌĆöIn [6], a construction of cyclic one-step majoritylogic decodable codes based on idempotent polynomials is given. However, the search for the feasible Parity-Check Idempotent runs through all possible combinations of cyclotomic cosets modulo n, satisfying some algebraic constraints, consequently, increasing the code length may result in very large dimension space search, and the search for the solution becomes more difficult. In this paper, we propose a Genetic Algorithm that aimes to construct new moderate and high lengths Binary Cyclic OSMLD codes, considered as LDPC codes, with high correction capacities. Our construction is very efficient and provide codes with high lenghts and high rates.

Difference-Set Codes were discovered independently by Rudolph [1] and Weldon [2] . These codes are known as being completely orthogonalisable and represent a particular class of One-Step Majority-Logic Decodable (OSMLD) codes where the length of the code is in the form n = J(J ŌłÆ 1) + 1. Weldon has shown in [2] that these codes are approximately as powerful as the best cyclic codes for given values of efficiency and length, and are very easily implemented. The formula for calculating the number of parity-check equations was introduced by Graham [3] and MacWilliams [4] . In [9], it was shown that cyclic DS codes may be considered as LDPC codes. Also, in [15] it was shown that cyclic OSMLD codes are LDPC codes with a Tanner graph that has no cycles of length 4. The construction methods introduced by [6] and [7] for short and moderate length LDPC codes over GF(2m) show that every cyclic OSMLD Code may be generated from its dual code, using a parity-check Idempotent polynomial E(x), built from the cyclotomic cosets modulo n. B. Binary Cyclic OSMLD codes based on cyclotomic cosets
Definition 5 (OSMLD code). Consider an (n, k) linear code C with parity-check matrix H. The row space of H is an (n, n ŌłÆ k) cyclic code, denoted by C , which is the dual code of C, or the null space of C. The code C is a One-Step Majority-Logic Decodable code if for any j Ōłł {0, 1, ..., n ŌłÆ 1} there is a set Žē of J vectors in the dual code C , with the following properties :
1) The jth component of each vector Žēi is a 1, for i Ōłł {0, 1, 2, ..., J ŌłÆ 1}.
2) For k = j there is at most one vector Žēi whose kth component is a 1.
These J vectors are said to be orthogonal on the jth digit position. We call them orthogonal vectors. [8]

A. Problematic and discussions

The determination of the set ╬® is essential and very influent for the construction of cyclic codes in general. For the generation of OSMLD codes, the polynomial E(x) is subject to several conditions and mathematical constraints.The choice of ╬® must be carried out following subsection II.C.

The construction method of the parity-check idempotent E(x) of OSMLD codes is based on the union of primitive idempotents, so it depends especially on the number of cyclotomic cosets nc. Indeed, by increasing the code length n, we have noticed that nc increases significantly, which explains the difficulty for finding feasible E(x) (checking all conditions given in subsection II.C) from a large set of potential solutions.
Consequently, an exhaustive search of a feasible E(x) will not be suitable in terms of time complexity, and in terms of memory complexity. In order to avoid this complexity we decide to use an optimisation method. This has lead us to develop a new construction algorithm, based on genetic algorithms, and able to build several OSMLD codes of different lengths. The proposed algorithm will allow us to extend and to generalize it to both cyclic LDPC codes and OSMLD codes.

C. OSMLD-GA construction algorithm
1) Genetic Algorithms:
Genetic algorithms (GAs) were invented by John Holland [13] in the 1960s and were developed by Holland and his students and colleagues at the University of Michigan in the 1960s and the 1970s. In contrast with evolution strategies and evolutionary programming, HollandŌĆÖs original goal was not to design algorithms to solve specific problems, but rather to formally study the phenomenon of adaptation as it occurs in nature and to develop ways in which the mechanisms of natural adaptation might be imported into computer systems. HollandŌĆÖs 1975 book Adaptation in Natural and Artificial Systems presented the genetic algorithm as an abstraction of biological evolution and gave a theoretical framework for adaptation under the GA.
HollandŌĆÖs GA is a method for moving from one population of "chromosomes" (e.g., strings of ones and zeros, or "bits") to a new population by using a kind of "natural selection" together with the geneticsŌłÆinspired operators of crossover, mutation, and inversion. Each chromosome consists of "genes" (e.g., bits), each gene being an instance of a particular "allele" (e.g., 0 or 1). The selection operator chooses those chromosomes in the population that will be allowed to reproduce, and on average the fitter chromosomes produce more offspring than the less fit ones. Crossover exchanges subparts of two chromosomes, roughly mimicking biological recombination between two singleŌłÆchromosome ("haploid") organisms; mutation randomly changes the allele values of some locations in the chromosome; and inversion reverses the order of a contiguous section of the chromosome, thus rearranging the order in which genes are arrayed [13].

The principle is to generate an initial population of Ni individuals. The parameter Ni is called the population size. Then we have to calculate the value of the fitness function f (x1, x2, ..., xM ) for each individual E(x). If one of the individuals has a zero fitness value, we stop the algorithm, and we take the individual E(x) as the solution. Alternatively, if the initial population does not contain a solution, the genetic algorithm is executed according to the following steps:
1) Selection of parent individuals from the current population.
2) Crossing parents for creating children. The crossing is performed according to a crossover probability pc.
3) Mutation of individual children obtained to ensure a genetic diversity for future generations. The mutation is performed according to a mutation probability pm.
4) Calculation of the value of the fitness function for each child.
5) New generation based on elitism. The elitism is an operation which select the best individuals of the current generation based on fitness values.
The same steps are repeated for different values of M to produce different weight of E(x). For each value of M , we perform several iterations of the algorithm, in order to find all possible weights or of all possible combinations of the weights of primitive idempotents.

Given the problematic presented in the previous section, the fact that space search for code lengths is very large, we chose to develop a new construction algorithm for cyclic binary OSMLD codes based on meta-heuristic techniques, known to be very useful for optimization problems. We chose the use of Genetic Algorithms.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper we have presented a construction algorithm of cyclic binary OSMLD codes based on genetic algorithms, providing new high code rates and good correction capacities OSMLD codes. We have constructed two new cyclic DS codes with parameters (n, k, dmin) = (16513, 14325, 130) and (65793, 59231, 258). Indeed to our knowledge the generators of these codes were unknown. These codes are good LDPC codes. Due to the low-complexity encoding and decoding offered by these codes, they could be proposed for industrial applications, providing an attractive solution for high data rate wireless transmissions and for data storage.
