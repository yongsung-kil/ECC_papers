Abstract—In this paper, we propose a design rule of a modified
quasi-cyclic structure for bit-interleaved coded modulation with
iterative decoding (BICM-ID). The proposed structure is espe-
cially favorable for low-complexity hardware implementation. In
addition, it will be shown that the proposed structure effectively
removes low-weight codewords, which in turn greatly improves
error-floor behaviors as compared to quasi-cyclic interleaver.
I. INTRODUCTION
The theoretical limit of transmission rate for a coded mod-
ulation system with equiprobable signaling over the constel-
lation is called the coded modulation (CM) capacity [4]. It is
well-known that for some modulation schemes the bit inter-
leaved coded modulation (BICM) can provide performances
approaching the CM capacity [1] when a Gray mapping and
a powerful binary error-correcting code such as low-density
parity-check (LDPC) codes are serially concatenated through
a random interleaver. However, for some other modulation
schemes such as frequency shift keying (FSK) regardless of
bit mapping rules and error-correcting codes, the maximum
transmission rate of BICM, called BICM capacity suffers a
considerable loss from the CM capacity. A great deal of studies
[2]–[4], [6] have investigated how to reduce the capacity gap
and found that BICM with iterative decoding (BICM-ID) can
achieve performances very close to the CM capacity even
without resorting to powerful error-correcting codes.
Recently, as an effort of developing modulation schemes for
beyond 4G cellular wireless systems, a combination of fre-
quency and quadrature-amplitude modulations called FQAM
is studied in [5]. In the work, it is shown that by employing
the FQAM in the downlink, the distribution of inter-cell-
interference (ICI) in the cell edge becomes notably deviated
from a Gaussian distribution and has a heavier tail. Such a
unique feature enables a cell-edge user to have a considerably
higher transmission rate as compared to the case with the
conventional quadrature amplitude modulation (QAM). How-
ever, unfortunately, it is found that FQAM has a considerable
amount of gap between its CM and BICM capacities. Thus,
a demand on developing efficient wireless transceiver for
orthogonal modulation is on the rise.
BICM-ID has been considered for non-coherent orthogonal
modulation in [7] where the authors demonstrated that a
significant performance improvement over BICM is achiev-
able with iterations between the demodulator and channel
decoder. More recently, capacity-approaching∗codes for non-
coherent orthogonal modulation was extensively investigated
by Fabregas et al in [3]. In particular, the authors in [3]
proposed a code structure in which an outer irregular repetition
code and inner recursive encoder/modulator are serially con-
catenated. In such a serial concatenation, the inner recursive
encoder/modulator is employed for the purpose of removing
error-floors after decoding convergence [6]. Meanwhile, the
outer code is designed with the curve fitting technique to
match with the inner recursive encoder/modulator. To achieve
capacity-approaching performance, the outer code must be
flexible enough that the curve fitting results are well matched
with the inner recursive encoder/modulator. While an irregular
repetition code is adopted as the outer code in [3], it is possible
to design the outer code with other irregular codes, e.g.
punctured convolutional codes of various rates in [6]. How-
ever, the irregular repetition code may be a practically more
favorable choice since the belief-propagation (BP) algorithm
for decoding the repetition code can be implemented with
simple addition operations. Moreover, the algorithm can be
easily parallelized for a higher throughput. The design rule in
[3], [6] can also be applicable to other wireless communication
systems based on orthogonal modulations including FQAM.
In this paper, we consider the BICM-ID [3] with the FQAM
scheme [5]. In this setting, we propose a modified quasi-cyclic
(QC) structure for low-complexity hardware implementation.
In particular, the proposed structure effectively removes low-
weight codewords, which lower lower error-floors as compared
to the existing design of quasi-cyclic interleaver.
In Section II, the system model will be introduced. In
Section III-A, we raise design issues associated with inter-
leaver with the QC structure, and Section III-B gives a new
interleaver design which overcomes the technical issues we
discussed in III-A. Also, section III-B shows performance
evaluations of the BICM-ID systems with the proposed, QC
and random interleavers will be carried out. The results are
also compared each other, which confirms that the proposed
interleaver significantly improves the error-floor behaviors as
compared to the QC interleaver.

It should be noted that the irregular repetition code may pro-
duce low weight codewords for low weight message vectors,
which results in a poor error-floor. For example, suppose that
dv,i = minj dv,j. Then, for a message vector of weight one, m
in which mj = 0 ∀j except for mi = 1, the codeword c will
have a codeword of weight dv,i. Thus, if the interleaver is not
carefully designed, it is possible that the resulting codeword
b will also have a low weight. In the next section, we will
show that the hardware friendly QC structure leads to low
weight codewords b for low weight message vectors, which
significantly deteriorates error-floor performances. To avoid
this, we propose a modified QC structure.

interleaver, called QCDI to avoid low weight codewords. The
modified interleaver simply permutes the way to take coded

increases from 0 to µ −1, rows of Π are taken in the order

IV. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we showed that BICM system with QC
interleaver must be carefully designed to avoid low weight
codewords which significantly deteriorate error-floor perfor-
mances. Such claim was substantiated in both a quantitive
and qualitative ways. It was also shown that by introducing a
modification to QC interleaver, error-floors can be significantly
lowered.
