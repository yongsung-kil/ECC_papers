Novel Forward Error Correction Concepts for Coherent Optical Communications
Laurent Schmalen Alcatel-Lucent Bell Laboratories, Lorenzstr. 10, 70435 Stuttgart, Germany, first.last@alcatel-lucent.com

Abstract-We discuss and present several new directions in forward error correction for coherent optical communications. In particular, we show how the class of spatially coupled codes can lead to new FEe schemes outperforming conventional codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Modern high-speed optical communication systems require
high-performing Forward Error Correction (FEC) implemen­ tations that support throughputs of 100 Gbitls or multiples thereof, that have low power consumption, that realize Net Coding Gains (NCGs) close to the theoretical limits at a target Bit Error Rate (EER) of 10-1 5, and that are preferably adapted
to the peculiarities of the optical channel. Especially with the advent of coherent transmission schemes and the utilization
of high resolution Analog-to-Digital Converters (ADCs), soft­
decision decoding has become an attractive means of reli­ ably increasing the transmission reach of lightwave systems. Currently, there are two popular classes of codes for soft­ decision decoding that are attractive for implementation in
optical receivers at decoding throughputs of 100 Gbitls and above: Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes and Block Turbo Codes (BTCs). Both schemes can be used to realize
coding schemes with high NCGs, but require either large block lengths of more than 100,000 bits to realize codes with small overheads, or do no longer perform well, at least under hard­
decision decoding [1]. New coding schemes promise to realize
unprecedented practically achievable coding gains, have low­ complexity decoding architecture and are well suited for the streaming-like mode of operation of optical communications.
II. NEW FORWARD ERROR CORRECTION CONCEPTS
Recently, the class of Spatially Coupled (SC) codes [2]
has gained widespread interest due to the fact that these codes are asymptotically capacity-achieving, have appealing encoding and decoding complexity and show outstanding practical decoding performance. SC codes are an extension of existing coding schemes by a superimposed convolutional structure. The technique of spatial coupling can be applied to most existing codes, the most popular are however LOPC
codes [2] and BTCs [3]. The latter have found use in opti­ cal communications (staircase codes) and show outstanding
performance, operating within 0.5 dB of the capacity of the hard-decision AWGN channel.

Spatially Coupled (SC)-LOPC codes were introduced more than a decade ago [4] and were originally called LDPC convolutional codes. Their outstanding properties have only
been fully realized recently, when Lentmaier et al. noticed
[5] that the estimated decoding performance of a certain
class of terminated protograph-based SC-LOPC codes with a
simple message passing decoder is close to the performance
of the underlying code ensemble under Maximum Likelihood (ML) decoding as ngrows, which was subsequently proven rigorously in [2], [6], if certain particular conditions on the
code structure are fulfilled. A left-terminated SC-LOPC code is
basically an LOPC code with a structured, infinitely extended

III. COMPARISON OF CODES
In this section, we aim at comparing Polar codes and SC­ LDPC codes with block LDPC codes, one of the currently most widespread coding schemes in long-haul optical commu­ nications for soft-decision decoding. As a comparison between block codes and convolutional-like codes is difficult, we con­ strain the codes and decoders to process always an approx­
imately identical number of bits, leading to equal decoding latencies. We further constrain the decoding complexity per
bit to be roughly equal in all schemes. We construct codes
with an overhead of 25 % (rate 0.8), which is an ideal rate to realize, e.g., 100 Gbitls systems at baud rates of 32 Gbaud/s
operating in a 50 Ghz DWDM scenario. We assume that the number of bits that the decoder handles is around 32,000. The codes we consider are:
