Joint Design of QC-LDPC Codes for Cascade-Based Multisource Coded Cooperation

Shunwai Zhang, Rongfang Song
College of Telecommunications and Information Engineering, Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Nanjing, China

Fengfan Yang
College of Electronic and Information Engineering Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics
Nanjing, China

Abstract—Quasi-cyclic low-density-parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes are jointly designed for cascade-based multisource coded cooperation system. Firstly, QC-LDPC codes based on the base matrix and exponent matrix are introduced, and then two types of girth-4 cycles in QC-LDPC codes employed by the sources and relay are explicated. Secondly, we propose joint design method of QC-LDPC codes employed by the sources and relay, by which all girth-4 cycles including both Type-I and Type-II are canceled. Numerical simulations show that the jointly designed QC-LDPC coded cooperation well combines cooperation gain and channel coding gain, and outperforms the coded noncooperation under the same conditions. Furthermore, the bit error rate (BER) performance of the coded cooperation employing jointly designed QC-LDPC codes is better than those of random LDPC codes and separately designed QC-LDPC codes over AWGN channels.

I. INTRODUCTION
The basic idea of cooperative communications [1] is that the mobile users equipped with single antenna share their antennas and form virtual antenna arrays to emulate multipleinput multiple-output (MIMO) system. Recent studies have shown that cooperative communications can exploit the spatial diversity. There are three main categories of protocols that support cooperative communications, i.e., amplify-andforward (AF) [1] detect-and-forward (DF) [2] and coded cooperation protocols [3]. Due to high transmission reliability, many previous studies have been concentrated on the coded cooperation protocol, in particular, using various low-densityparity-check (LDPC) codes. For multisource coded cooperation or multi-access relay systems, various LDPC codes are designed to improve the performance of systems [47]. In [4] a joint network LDPC code is designed by a new generalized Gaussian approximation method. The authors in [5] propose a novel physical layer network coded LDPC code structure for non-orthogonal relay channel. However, the encoding complexity of these designed LDPC codes is high. Meanwhile, these designed LDPC codes require a large sum of memory when implemented in the hardware, especially when codes are long. Fortunately, quasi-cyclic LDPC (QCLDPC) codes have linear encoding complexity and require much less memory due to the quasi-cyclic structure of their parity-check matrices [8]. V. SIMULATION RESULTS
We compare the BER performance of the systems employing the proposed jointly designed QC-LDPC codes, the random LDPC codes and the separately designed QC-LDPC codes. The separately designed QC-LDPC codes mean only Type-I girth-4 cycles are avoided in the joint parity-check matrix. The based matrices corresponding to the sources and
relay are 5 ×10 with 3 “1”, 6 “1” and 10 × 30 with 3 “1”, 9
“1” in each column and row, respectively. B=20. The joint “Min-Sum” iterative decoding algorithm [11] and BPSK modulation are adopted.
A. BER Performance of Cascaded-based Coded Cooperation versus that of Noncooperation over Rayleigh Fading Channels
In view of the destination, equal QC-LDPC codes as the proposed scheme are employed by the noncooperation. Fig. 4 compares BER performances of the cascade-based coded cooperation and coded noncooperation. The results show that the performance of coded cooperation clearly outperforms the coded noncooperation in all range of SNR. For instance, with ten decoding iterations and at the BER of 10−4 , the coded cooperation achieves about 5dB gain over its respective coded noncooperation. Furthermore, the BER of coded cooperation decreases much faster than that of coded noncooperation, which means it achieves higher diversity order. The significant gain and higher diversity order can be due to this fact: by the joint iterative decoding, we jointly decode three signals from two sources and the relay. Hence it can overcome the signals fading and the diversity gain is achieved.

VI. CONCLUSION
Based on the base matrix and exponent matrix corresponding to QC-LDPC codes, we jointly designed QCLDPC codes for cascade-based multisource coded cooperation system. All girth-4 cycles including both Type-I and Type-II are canceled in the jointly designed QC-LDPC codes. Theoretical analysis and simulation results demonstrate the advantages of the jointly designed QC-LDPC codes. In the future work, we will extend the joint design method of QCLDPC codes for two sources coded cooperation to a general multisource multirelay scenario, and further analyze its validity and advantages.
