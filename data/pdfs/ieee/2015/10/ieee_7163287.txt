A New Scheme of High Performance Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes With Girth 6
Sina Vafi and Narges Rezvani Majid

Abstract—The letter presents a new technique for constructing regular and irregular quasi-cyclic (QC) low density parity check (LDPC) codes with girth 6. Codes are designed by the combinatorial concept, in which subsets representing circulant matrices are originated from a particular subset. Conducted simulations confirm that implemented codes have low error floor, while they provide good performance in the waterfall region.
Index Terms—Quasi-cyclic LDPC codes, combinatorial designs, circulant matrix.
I. INTRODUCTION
Q UASI-CYCLIC Low Density Parity Check (QC-LDPC) codes are the promising codes for the broadband networks and storage systems. This is due to their low-complex encoding and efficient parallel iterative decoding, while the existence of short cycles (in particular cycles of 4) is prohibited in their Tanner graph. Depending on the structure of the parity check matrix, which is formed by a combination of some circulant matrices, these codes are either in regular or irregular form. In regular codes, row and column weights of the circulant matrices are constant, while in irregular ones they are different.
There are several methods for constructing binary QC-LDPC codes, which are free of cycle-4 or have girth 6. The high rate regular codes are obtained by using finite fields, finite geometries [1], [2] or the affine plane approach [3].
Irregular codes with high performance can be designed by a suitable masking technique conducted to circulant matrices. One of the masking matrices is based on a proper Approximate Cycle Extrinsic message degree (ACE), which optimizes the code performance at both waterfall and error floor regions [4].
QC-LDPC codes can also be constructed by combinatorial designs such as Balance Incomplete Block Design (BIBD) or Steiner sequences. They mainly apply cyclic difference families (CDF) concept in structure of their circulant matrices, in which every specific number of elements defined in the subsets of a group occurs only once [5], [6]. Recently, algebraic QCLDPC codes with girth 8 were proposed by the combination of finite fields and combinatorial designs in conjunction with a masking technique, which provides reliable performance at both waterfall and error floor regions [7].
This letter presents a new algebraic method for constructing high performance regular and irregular binary QC-LDPC codes with girth 6. Our regular codes are single-row of circulant matrices. Corresponding to each circulant matrix, there exists a set, whose elements present a location of 1s in the matrix. In these subsets, the difference between any two elements of a subset is unique. In order to gain codes with better performance, parity check matrices of codes are designed in the block-circulant form. They are constructed in irregular format aiming to minimise the number of circulant matrices, while their high performance is maintained. Based on a proper selection of subsets, codes with high performance are expected so as they are comparable with the well-known QC-LDPC codes with girth 8.

V. CONCLUSION
A new scheme of QC-LDPC codes with girth 6 was presented, whose circulant parity check matrices are formed by combinatorial design. This was basically accomplished by defining subsets in which the difference between any two elements is distinct. Simulation results proved that codes have very close performance to well-known codes with girth 8.
