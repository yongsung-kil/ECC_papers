Multimode Radix-4 SISO Kernel Design for Turbo/LDPC Decoding
Cheng-Hung Lin, Member, IEEE, and Chih-Shiang Yu

Abstract— To increase the quality of wireless transmission, wireless communication systems employ advanced forward error correction codes such as turbo codes and low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes. To achieve smooth transition between turbo and LDPC decoding in different communication standards, we propose a VLSI design of a multimode radix-4 soft-input soft-output (SISO) kernel in this paper. The proposed radix-4 SISO kernel composed of three recursive processing elements alternatively employs a radix-4 forward–backward algorithm for LDPC decoding and a radix-4 single-binary/double-binary enhanced max-log-maximum a posteriori probability algorithm for turbo decoding by efficiently sharing computational units. The proposed radix-4 SISO kernel achieves an area reduction of 21.8% when compared with the uncombined SISO kernels for alternatively decoding the turbo and LDPC codes. The proposed radix-4 SISO kernel is verified by implementing it in an application-specific integrated circuit of 0.45 mm2 core area by using a 90-nm CMOS process with a maximum area efficiency of 10.65 bits/mm2. In addition, the throughput rates of the long-term evolution and worldwide interoperability for microwave access schemes for both LDPC and turbo decoding can be achieved using eight proposed radix-4 SISO kernels.
Index Terms— Long-term evolution (LTE), low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, turbo codes, worldwide interoperability for microwave access (WiMAX).

III. PROPOSED MULTIMODE RADIX-4 SISO KERNEL DESIGN
The proposed multimode radix-4 SISO kernel that supports LTE/WiMAX turbo decoding and WiMAX rate-1/2 LDPC decoding is presented in this section. Because the radix-4 SB/DB EML-MAP and the radix-4 FBA have common operations favorable for unification, the proposed radix-4 SISO kernel employs these two decoding algorithms to achieve shared computational units. The hardware of the proposed SISO kernel can then be efficiently used.

A. Overall Architecture of the Proposed SISO Kernel
The block diagram of the proposed multimode radix-4 SISO kernel is shown in Fig. 4. The architecture of the proposed multimode radix-4 SISO kernel is very similar to that of a conventional warmup hybrid-window (HW) MAP kernel shown in [9, Fig. 10(a)]. The key computational components are:
1) a branch metrics calculator unit (BMU); 2) a branch metrics cache (BMC); 3) radix-4 multifunction recursion processing elements
(MFRPs) that compute the state metrics of the radix-4 SB/DB EML-MAP or the extrinsic LLR values of the radix-4 FBA; 4) a state metrics cache (SMC); 5) a multifunction a posteriori calculator unit (MFACU) that calculates a posteriori LLR of the radix-4 SB/DB EML-MAP or radix-4 FBA; 6) an extrinsic calculator unit (ECU).
The control signals of multimode radix-4 SISO kernel are LDPC/turbo (L/T ) mode and single/double (S/D) binary turbo mode. However, to support LDPC decoding, the MFRPs and the MFACU are modified to include bit node and check

D. Multifunction a Posteriori Calculation Unit
The proposed MFACU is the other key module that generates the bit node updates of the radix-4 FBA. As can be seen in Fig. 11(a), the MFACU is composed of one SB/DB trellis switch, four &MAX units, one saturation unit, and one SB/DB MAP LLR calculator. The architecture of the &MAX unit is shown in Fig. 11(b). In the turbo mode, the switch provides the correct path of the SBTC/DBTC for the state and branch metrics. The four &MAX units calculate (00),
(01), (10), and (11) using α, β, and γ values based on [18, eqs. (12) and (14)]. The SB/DB MAP LLR calculator computes the final a posteriori values. The related SB/DB MAP LLR calculator architecture can be referred to in [18]. To support the radix-4 FBA with maximum Wc = 6, four additional adders and 24 additional multiplexers are added to the first level of the &MAX unit. The first- and second-level adders of the &MAX unit are used to calculate the 4 bit node summations of L Q in (11) in the LDPC mode. Therefore, 16 columns of L Q can be calculated simultaneously using the four &MAX units. For WiMAX LDPC decoding, however, only 12 columns of L Q are required in a cycle, and the related hardware can be reduced. The saturation unit is used to calculate the Lq in (6) and prevent L Q from overflowing, thus reducing the bit length of the BNM. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we presented a three-RP multimode radix-4 SISO kernel that employs both radix-4 SB/DB EML-MAP for turbo decoding and radix-4 FBA for LDPC decoding. By sharing the computational units of these two decoding algorithms, the proposed radix-4 SISO kernel achieves area usages of 100% and 62% in the turbo and LDPC modes, respectively. In addition, the proposed radix-4 SISO kernel requires lesser area than the uncombined radix-4 SB/DB EML-MAP and radix-4 FBA kernels. The proposed kernel is implemented in an ASIC with a high-area efficiency by using the 90-nm CMOS process. Furthermore, eight multimode radix-4 SISO kernels satisfy the high-throughput requirements of WiMAX turbo/LDPC and LTE turbo decoding applications.
