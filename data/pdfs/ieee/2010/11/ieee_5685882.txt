Reduced-Complexity Decoding of High-Rate
LDPC Codes over Partial-Response Channels
Zhiliang Qin, Kui Cai, and Songhua Zhang Data Storage Institute, Singapore, 117608 E-mail: {qin_zhiliang; cai_kui; zhang_ songhua}@dsi.a-star.edu.sg

Abstract—In this paper, we consider high-rate low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for magnetic recording systems. We propose an efficient approach to reduce the computational complexity of the sum-product algorithm (SPA) for LDPC decoding by restricting the check operation to a small subset of input messages. Furthermore, the first-order MacLaurin Series is used to simplify the core operation and an attenuation factor is introduced to improve bit-error-rate (BER) performance by scaling down log-likelihood ratio (LLR) reliabilities in the decoding process. Simulation results show that the proposed decoder can obtain a noticeable performance gain over the conventional SPA at high signal-to-noise ratios (SNR) when used in turbo equalization schemes; while achieving a significantly lower computational complexity.
Index Terms—Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) Codes, Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA), Turbo Equalization
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a class of linear block codes defined by large sparse parity-check matrices. LDPC codes were first proposed by Gallager [1] in early 1960s and rediscovered by MacKay [2] in 1999. It is shown that LDPC codes can achieve bit-error-rate (BER) performance close to the Shannon limit by using an iterative soft-input/soft-output (SISO) decoding algorithm over the Tanner graph, such as the sum-product algorithm (SPA) [1]-[5].
Based on methods of construction, LDPC codes can be divided into two categories: random codes [2] and structured codes [6]-[8]. Of particular interest to magnetic recording systems are structured high-rate quasi-cyclic (QC) codes, whose encoders can be easily implemented by using linear feedback shift registers [9]. Despite that the encoding complexity can be effectively reduced, the decoding complexity for high-rate LDPC codes still remains an issue due to much larger check-node degree dc as compared with low-rate codes. The original form of the probability-domain SPA [2] involves many multiplications in the computation and also requires a normalization procedure at each step to ensure numerical stability. In [3], the SPA that operates in the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) domain was proposed, where the check-node update was performed efficiently based on forward and backward recursions over a single-state trellis of single-parity-check (SPC) codes. To avoid the evaluation of the hyperbolic tangent function, the core operation is performed

based on the Jacobian algorithm, where correction terms are implemented by using look-up tables or linear piecewise functions. The forward/backward implementation of the conventional SPA in [3] has a complexity growing linearly with dc, which is computationally expensive for large values of dc.
In this paper, we propose an efficient approach to reduce the complexity of the check-node-update by restricting the computation to a small set of input messages corresponding to the smallest q reliabilities, 2qdc. To minimize performance degradation, an attenuation factor is introduced to suppress the reliabilities of output messages from check nodes. Furthermore, the core operation is simplified by using the first-order MacLaurin Series to approximate the correction term, which can be efficiently implemented by using high-speed adders and comparators. Simulation results show that the proposed decoder can obtain a noticeable performance gain over the conventional SPA in [3] when used in a turbo equalizer over partial-response channels; while achieving a significantly lower computational complexity.
This paper is organized as follows. In Section II, the system description is given. In Section III, the proposed low-complexity decoder is presented. In Section IV, simulation results are presented over ideal ME2PR4 channels. Finally, the conclusion is drawn in Section V.

C. Attenuation of LLR reliabilities

For long LDPC codes whose Tanner graphs are free of cycles, it is well known that the SPA provides optimal performance and converges to the maximum a posteriori decoding. However, for codes at short-to-medium block lengths, there is no guarantee that the SPA is optimal. In this case, incoming messages and outgoing messages along each edge are no longer independent. In [14], it is observed that the outgoing messages from check nodes provided by the SPA over a graph with short cycles tend to have a higher reliability, or a lower uncertainty, on average as compared with those obtained under cycle-free assumption.
Our simulation results show that the proposed decoder in (8) further exacerbates the reliability over-estimation by limiting the check computation to the q least reliable input messages instead of considering all dc inputs as in the conventional SPA. To compensate for the resulting performance degradation, a multiplicative factor Į can be introduced in (8) to scale down LLR reliabilities output from check nodes, i.e.,

Fig. 2 shows that the simplest MinSum algorithm [3] suffers a noticeable performance loss of 0.6 dB from the FC SPA at the BER of 10-5. By slightly increasing the computational complexity, the proposed decoder with q=2, which keeps two inputs for the check operation, achieves a performance gain of 0.2 dB over the Min-Sum algorithm. If we further increase the value of q to 4, a gain of 0.4 dB can be obtained. At the BER of 2×10-6, the proposed decoder with q=4 has a performance loss of 0.1 dB compared with the FC SPA, which, however, achieves a reduction of 93.75% in the decoding complexity. Fig. 2 also shows that the proposed decoder with q=8 has negligible performance degradation from that of the FC SPA throughout the simulated SNR range by restricting the check operation to 8 inputs among all 64 inputs.
In Fig. 3, we show the performance of the proposed decoder based on the MacLaurin Series approximation in (15) versus the attenuation factor a at Eb/No=6.8 dB from the first overall iteration to the fifth overall iteration. The value of q is set to 4. It can be seen that the optimal amount of attenuation required increases with the number of iterations. At the first iteration, BER is not very sensitive to the attenuation factor. At the fifth iteration, however, the choice of the factor has a significant impact on BER performance. When the optimal factor a=0.5 is used in the decoding, we obtain the BER of 2×10-6 at Eb/No=6.8 dB, which achieves improvement by nearly two orders of magnitudes compared with the case where no attenuation is used (i.e., a=1). We notice that the BER is actually a good cost function for choosing the attenuation factor in that the curve is convex-shaped and has a unique global minimum.

Ideally, for a certain LDPC code used in turbo equalization schemes, the optimal attenuation factor should be chosen according to SNR values and the number of overall iterations, which, however, may be computationally infeasible when determined by simulation.
For the sake of simplicity, we assume that the attenuation factor a remains constant through the decoding process. Through comprehensive simulations, we choose this factor at the SNR value whose corresponding BER at the fifth overall iteration is in the order of 10-6. This is the SNR range that manifests the effect of attenuation. In fact, little performance improvement can be obtained no matter how we change the factor from one iteration (or SNR) to another. Fig. 5 shows that by incorporating the attenuation factor a=0.5, the proposed decoder based on the MacLaurin Series approximation (curve labeled “q=4, MacLaurin, a=0.5”) performs very well and even outperforms the FC SPA by a non-negligible gain of 0.2 dB at the BER of 10-6. In addition, all three algorithms with near-optimal attenuation factors produce almost identical performance through the simulated SNR range. The performance improvement, however, is not surprising, since the SPA is by no means optimal for codes with short cycles; while LDPC codes based on m-w RS codewords are known to contain many length-6 cycles in their Tanner graphs [11]. Therefore, it is reasonable that a low-complexity decoder with properly adjusted parameters outperforms the conventional SPA when applied to partial-response channels.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed an efficient approach to reduce the computational complexity of the SPA decoding for high-rate LDPC codes over partial-response channels. The proposed decoder restricts the check operation to a small set of least reliable input messages. The first-order MacLaurin Series approximation is used to simplify the core operation and an attenuation factor is introduced to improve BER performance. Simulation results show that the proposed decoder can achieve a noticeable performance gain over the conventional SPA at high SNR regions when used in turbo equalization schemes.
