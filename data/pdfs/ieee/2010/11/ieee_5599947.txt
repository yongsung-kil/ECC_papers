A Unified Early Stopping Criterion for Binary and Nonbinary
LDPC Codes Based on Check-Sum Variation Patterns
Guojun Han and Xingcheng Liu, Member, IEEE
Abstract—In order to detect uncorrectable error frames and
early terminate their iterative belief-propagation(BP) decoding
for binary or nonbinary LDPC codes, a unified early stopping
criterion using check-sum variation patterns is proposed. In
addition to low computational overhead and easy choice of
threshold parameters, it can be adapted to different channels.
Simulation results show that the proposed criterion greatly
reduces the average number of iterations (ANI) at low to medium
signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) while keeping the error performance
degradation negligible over a wide SNR region.
Index Terms—Belief-propagation (BP), early stopping crite-
rion, iterative decoding, LDPC codes, nonbinary.
I. INTRODUCTION
L
DPC codes exhibit excellent error performance when
decoded with belief-propagation (BP) iterative decoding
algorithm over the factor graph of the code [1]. The standard
BP algorithm for binary and nonbinary LDPC codes [2] termi-
nates iterative decoding process when all checks are satisfied
or the pre-set maximum number of iterations is reached. Since
this stopping criterion can not avoid unnecessary decoding
iterations and there are a large number of uncorrectable frames
during BP iterative decoding at low to medium SNR, it causes
the average number of iterations(ANI) and the BP decoding
latency to greatly increase at the SNR region.
In order to avoid unnecessary decoding iterations, various
early stopping criteria have been proposed for BP algorithm
in recent years. These criteria can be classified into two
types: 1) Log-likelihood ratio (LLR) based criteria, such as
the criteria proposed by Kienle et al [3] and Li et al [4] for
binary LDPC codes. This type of criteria has a relatively high
computational overhead and can not be adapted to different
channels easily due to the threshold parameters changing with
channel characteristics. Especially, optimizing the threshold
parameters is quite time consuming. 2) Hard decision based
criteria, such as the criteria proposed by Cui et al [5] and
Chen et al [6] for binary LDPC codes. This type of criteria has
much lower extra computation. However, the former needs to
estimate channel SNR while the latter obviously suffers error
performance degradation, when the pre-set maximum number
of iterations is larger than 8.
In this letter, by analyzing the check-sum variation patterns
during BP iterative decoding and the fraction of error frames

II. CHARACTERISTICS OF ERROR FRAMES
For LDPC codes with infinite block length, threshold pa-
rameters can be calculated using Density Evolution(DE) [7]
or Extrinsic Information Transfer(EXIT) charts [8]. If channel
noise is below the threshold, the mean magnitude of LLR mes-
sages will increase continually with the number of iterations
and a valid codeword will be found. If channel noise is above
the threshold, the mean magnitude of LLR messages will stop
increasing after several iterations and it converges to an invalid
codeword [9]. However, for LDPC codes with finite length,
due to existence of cycles in factor graphs, the behavior of
BP is quite different from the one for infinite block length.
A. Convergence Behavior of BP for Finite LDPC Codes
From the view point of decoding results, error frames can
be classified into three types described as follows:
Type 𝐼-The decoder converges to an invalid codeword;
Type 𝐼𝐼-The decoder oscillates with iterations;
Type 𝐼𝐼𝐼-The decoder converges to a valid codeword but
not the transmitted one.
If channel SNR is very low, the behavior of a decoder for
finite length is similar to the one for infinite length and all
error frames can be classified into type 𝐼. From low to high
SNR, all the three types of error frames appear and the fraction
of each type changes with SNR. Simulation results presented
by Lechner [9] show that the fraction of error frames of type
𝐼gradually reduces with the increasing of SNR. But even in
medium SNR, it still accounts for a large fraction.
B. Bit Probability Transition
For binary LDPC codes, error frames can also be analyzed
by bit probability transition as follows:
Type 𝐴-Bit probability converges to 1 or 0;
Type 𝐵-Bit probability converges to a certain value between
0 and 1;
Type 𝐶-Bit probability oscillates.
Simulation results presented by Lee et al [10] show that
error frames of type 𝐵are dominant at low SNR. With the
increasing of SNR, the fraction of this type of error frames
gradually reduces.

C. Check-Sum Variation Patterns
For both binary and nonbianry LDPC codes, we define
the check-sum as the number of unsatisfied checks at each
iteration. Since the check-sum uses hard decision of bit LLR
or symbol probability, the variation pattern of check-sum can
be used to identify error frames. In order to examine check-
sum variation patterns, we firstly designed a GF4-WiMAX576
nonbinary LDPC code by filling the parity check matrix of
1/2 rate 576 block length WiMAX LDPC code [11] with
uniformly distributed GF(4) elements. And then we performed
simulations on MN1008, MN504 binary LDPC codes gen-
erated by Mackay [1] , QC-PEG1008 binary LDPC codes
presented in [12] and GF4-WiMAX576 nonbinary LDPC
code. Through analyzing simulation data, all error frames for
binary and nonbianry LDPC codes can be classified into three
variation patterns (𝐸𝑏/𝑁0 for variation patterns demonstration
is 1.5dB) as shown in Fig. 1:
1) Check-sum keeps constant;
2) Check-sum oscillates periodically with iterations;
3) Check-sum oscillates randomly with iterations.
The fraction of error frames with constant check-sum varia-
tion pattern is also shown in Fig. 1. It can be seen that this type
of error frames accounts for a very large fraction at low SNR.
With the increasing of SNR, it gradually reduces. However, at
the region of 1.0 ∼1.5 dB, for binary LDPC codes, this type
of error frames still accounts for a large fraction.
III. NEW EARLY STOPPING CRITERION
The error frame which converges to an invalid codeword, or
the one whose bit or symbol posteriori probability converges
to a certain value between 0 and 1, can be identified by
the constant check-sum variation pattern. This type of error
frames accounts for a large fraction at low to medium SNR.
The other two types account for a small fraction, especially
in low SNR region. To correctly identify these types of error
frames is difficult because some correctable frames also appear
with oscillation during BP decoding. Since a large number of
error frames appear during BP decoding at low to medium
SNR and error frames with constant check-sum account for a
large fraction, to identify this type of error frames and early
terminate their decoding can greatly reduce ANI at this region.
The proposed criterion is to efficiently identify error frames
with constant check-sum and terminate their iterative decod-
ing. The threshold parameter of 𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟is adopted in the new
criterion, in which 𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟denotes the number of consecutive
iterations that the check-sum keeps constant. The new stopping
criterion is described as follows:
1) Initialization: Set the values of 𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟. Reset check-sum
counter C.
2) Check-sum variation pattern identifying: At the end of the
i-th iteration, examine check-sum, if the i-th check-sum keeps
same as the (i-1)-th check-sum, then set C=C+1, otherwise,
reset counter C.
3) Early stopping: If the counter C≥𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟or i=𝐼𝑚𝑎𝑥, ter-
minate the iterative decoding; otherwise continue to the next
iteration.
Since the performance of early stopping criterion is deter-
mined by threshold parameters, the way how to find optimum
threshold parameters is quite important. Compared with the
existing criteria, the new criterion has a much simpler way
to choose such threshold values. For threshold 𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟, it can be
determined by half the length of the shortest cycle in factor
graphs of the corresponding LDPC codes because it is the
smallest number of cycle free iterations.
The criterion is also advantageous for much lower compu-
tational overhead which has nothing to do with block length.
It only requires 3 memory units and does not need special
computation of real numbers.
IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In order to examine and compare the results with the
previously proposed early stopping criteria, we have simu-
lated the new criterion on MN1008 binary LDPC code and
GF4-WiMAX576 nonbinary LDPC code over BIAWGN and
Rayleigh channels. The maximum number of iterations is
set to be 𝐼𝑚𝑎𝑥=100. For nonbinary LDPC codes, we used
FFT-BP [13] decoding algorithm. Both binary and nonbinary
decoding algorithms use sequential updating schedule based
on check nodes [14] and we refer these two algorithms as
CSBP for simplicity. CSBP-SSC is referred to as CSBP using
standard stopping criterion. CSBP-CSVP(𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟) is referred to
as CSBP using early stopping criterion based on checksum
variation patterns (CSVP) criterion with parameter 𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟.
CSBP-CMM(𝜆, P) is referred to as CSBP using convergence
of the mean magnitude (CMM) [4] criterion with parameters of
𝜆and P. CSBP-CAET(Ite, Thr) is referred to as CSBP using
Channel-Adaptive Early Termination (CAET) strategy [6] with
parameters of Ite and Thr.
Fig. 2 and Fig. 3 show the error performance and ANI
of CSBP using different early stopping criteria for MN1008
binary LDPC code and GF4-WiMAX576 nonbinary LDPC
code over BIAWGN channel respectively. From Fig. 2 we
see that with the increasing of 𝜃𝑡ℎ𝑟, ANI increases at low
to medium SNR. We also see that CSVP(3) criterion has
almost the same error performance and ANI as CMM(0.001,
2) criterion. For nonbinary LDPC code, from Fig. 3 we see
that CSVP(4) greatly reduces ANI at low SNR while keeping
negligible error performance degradation at low to high SNR.

V. CONCLUSION
Through analyzing check-sum variation patterns of uncor-
rectable frames, we found that the frames with constant check-
sum variation pattern account for a large fraction at low to
medium SNR. The proposed criterion can efficiently identify
this type of error frames. As compared with existing criteria,
the new criterion has much lower computational overhead and
its parameters can be chosen in a very simple way. Simulation
results show that the new criterion greatly reduces ANI at
low to medium SNR while keeping the error performance
degradation negligible over a wide SNR region. It also shows
that the new criterion can be adapted to different channels.
