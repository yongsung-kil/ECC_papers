Abstract‚ÄîBased on discrete density evolution (DDE), we develop closed form expressions to predict the error floor of LDGM codes. The first, rougher, approximation is obtained by assuming perfect message passing between systematic and parity bit nodes in DDE. The second, finer, expression leads to a more involved formulation. While the rougher approximation matches well to simulation results and DDE analysis for high signal to noise ratio (additive white Gaussian noise, AWGN, channel) or low crossover probability (binary symmetric channel, BSC), the finer approximation shows a good match for a wider range in the channel quality.
I. INTRODUCTION
With the introduction of turbo codes [1] and low-density parity check (LDPC) codes [2]‚Äì[4], the Shannon theoretical limit can be closely approached if the information block lengths are long enough. In practical implementations, turbo codes have a high decoding complexity, while standard LDPC codes present a high encoding complexity. Interestingly, it is possible to achieve a comparable channel coding performance to those of standard LDPC and turbo codes by using systematic linear codes with low-density generator matrices (LDGM) codes [5]‚Äì[7].
Since generator matrices of LDGM codes are sparse, LDGM codes are a particular subset of LDPC codes. The disadvantage of standard LDGM codes is that they present an error floor that does not disappear by increasing the codeword length. Thus, these codes were first recognized as bad codes [4], and they were treated as ‚Äúerror-reduction‚Äù codes [8], or erasure correcting codes [9], and utilized as pure error correction codes only for high-rate applications [10]‚Äì[12]. However, it was shown in [5], [6], that the serial concatenation of two LDGM codes can substantially lead to a reduction of the error floor. In [7], parallel concatenated LDGM codes were introduced aiming at reducing the error floor. All these developments allow the use of LDGM codes as error correcting codes for a wide range of rates.
Since the error floor observed in LDGM codes is independent of their block length, it is of great importance to develop tools to analyze this behavior. Density evolution (DE) [13], and specifically, discrete density evolution (DDE) [14], is a widely used algorithm to analyze LDPC codes over a broad class of noisy channels. Since LDGM codes are a subclass of LDPC codes, DDE can be easily extended to analyze the performance of LDGM codes, including its error floor [7]. However, DDE is an algorithmic method based on message passing decoding,

and does not lead to closed expressions. In this paper, based on the DDE framework, we present two approximations that lead to closed analytical formulas to compute the probability of error for LDGM codes in the error floor region. Both BSC and AWGN channels are considered.

II. LDGM CODES
Systematic regular LDGM codes are linear codes with a sparse generator matrix G = [I | P ], and a parity check matrix H = [P T I]. The systematic bits are denoted as u = [u1, ¬∑ ¬∑ ¬∑ , uK ]. The coded (parity) bits are generated as c = uP = [c1, ¬∑ ¬∑ ¬∑ , cM ], so that [u c] are sent through the channel. At the decoder, the corrupted sequence is [u c ], where u = [u1, ¬∑ ¬∑ ¬∑ , uK ] with uk = uk + ek, c = [c1, ¬∑ ¬∑ ¬∑ , cM ] with cm = cm + em, and ek, em are the error patterns (or AWGN noise) introduced by channel. The rate of
the above LDGM code is K/(K + M ). We will use notation (dv, dc) to indicate the degrees of a regular LDGM code, where dv and dc are the degrees of systematic nodes and the degrees of parity nodes, respectively 1. Fig. 1 shows the factor

We have presented two closed formulas for error floor prediction in LDGM codes over both BSC and AWGN channels, which leads to a reduction in the calculation complexity when compared with other techniques such as DDE. The error floor predictions obtained using the proposed closed expressions agree with those provided by simulation results and DDE.
