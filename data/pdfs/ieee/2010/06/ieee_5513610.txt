Abstract— In this paper, a new construction of quasi-cyclic lowdensity parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes for unequal error protection (UEP) is proposed. We first give a new class of UEP block codes. QC-LDPC codes with binomial-term parity-check matrices which can avoid girths less than 8 and achieve an enlarged upper bound on the minimum distance are also introduced. An effective UEP scheme based on the proposed UEP block codes and QCLDPC codes which can provide flexible choice of protection levels is then presented. Simulation results show that the new QC-LDPC codes can achieve good performance and low error floors. The bits with different designed protection levels can indeed have unequal bit-error-rate performance.
I. INTRODUCTION
Unequal error protection (UEP) [1], [2] means that the codeword bits can be divided into parts with different errorcorrecting capability. Important messages can be provided with more error protection than less important ones. UEP finds many practical applications as broadcast channels, packetswitching networks, visual communication systems, etc.
Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes [3]–[7] are LDPC codes [8] whose parity-check matrices are usually composed of permutation matrices. They can achieve near-Shannon-limit performance, with the advantages that they can be encoded efficiently and its algebraic structure allows for many practical implementations. However, QC-LDPC codes have a limited upper bound on the minimum distance [3]. In [4], [7], Type-II QC-LDPC codes with binomial-term paritycheck matrices were proposed to enlarge the upper bound on the minimum distance. In [7], the structure of Type-II (3, 4)regular QC-LDPC codes was analyzed. However, the general construction for Type-II (3, t)-regular QC-LDPC codes was not provided. Also there is no guarantee that the girth is larger than 6 for codes proposed in [7].
Since LDPC codes have outstanding performance, UEP LDPC codes are desirable. There are two common constructive methods for UEP LDPC codes. The first one is by puncturing [9], [10]. UEP is achieved by puncturing the bits of a mother code to obtain required protection levels. The second one is to design UEP according to the code structure. In [11]–[15], irregular UEP LDPC codes were designed by considering the degree distribution of variable nodes. In [16], a lowertriangular form of the parity-check matrix was introduced for UEP, but only two levels could be provided. In [17], the modified |u|u + v| structure of Plotkin-type construction was

In this paper, we first propose a new construction of UEP block codes, of which the parity-check matrices are of the simple stairway structure. A new construction of QC-LDPC codes with binomial-term parity-check matrices is also given, which can achieve an enlarged upper bound on the minimum distance and guarantee the girth to be at least 8. Based on the proposed UEP block codes and QC-LDPC codes, UEP QCLDPC codes with desired protection levels can be obtained. The low error floor and UEP capability of the new codes are then verified by computer simulations.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. A general construction of UEP block codes is described in Section II. Section III presents a new construction of regular QC-LDPC codes, along with the upper bound on the minimum distance and conditions for the girth to be at least 8. Then in Section IV, new UEP QC-LDPC codes are given. Simulation results are shown in Section V for performance verification. Finally, the work is concluded in Section VI.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a new general construction of UEP block codes with simple stairway parity-check matrices is provided. Moreover, new QC-LDPC codes are introduced. By employing binomial terms in the parity-check matrix, not only the girth of

at least 8 can be guaranteed but also the larger upper bound on
the minimum distance can be obtained. New UEP QC-LDPC
codes can then be obtained based on the above two approaches.
Simulation results show that the new QC-LDPC codes can
have better BER performance at high SNRs and lower error
floors than previously constructed codes. And our UEP QC-
LDPC codes can indeed achieve different error protection
levels, with some parts of bits having better performance
than those of equal error protection QC-LDPC codes. Possible
future work can include extension of the constructions to
higher-rate codes and the encoding problem of information
bits in different UEP levels.
