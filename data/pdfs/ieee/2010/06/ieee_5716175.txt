for constructing (3, k) regular low density parity check (LDPC)

from rank-deficiency of H and high encoding complexity. The

1. 
INTRODUCTION 
In 1960's, regular low density parity check (LDPC) codes 
were invented by Gallager by randomly constructing parity 
check matrix (H) with constant number of j ones in each 
column, constant number of k ones in each row and zeros 
elsewhere using j = 3 and k 
= 4. These regular codes were 
designated as (j, k) regular LDPC codes. In his regular LDPC 
codes, there are at least (j - 1) rows of H which are linearly 
dependent [1]. 
Other structured construction of regular LDPC codes also 
has some dependent rows in H such as algebraic [2] - [9] and 
combinatorial [10] - [13]. Regular quasi cyclic (QC) LDPC 
codes as one example of algebraic codes have at least (j - 1) 
dependent rows in H as stated in [5], [14]. 
A generator matrix (G) that encodes the information bits is 
derived from H. Generally, obtaining G from H involves 
Gauss elimination that may require computation of the order 
of O(N') where N is the code length. In case Hhas some rows 
that are linearly dependent, it is not possible to manipulate H 
to get G. Therefore, it is important to start with a full rank of 
H in the design of regular LDPC codes. Many existing 
techniques either erase the linearly dependent rows in H or 
replace it with another independent row. Since the parity part 
of G is generally a dense matrix, encoding complexity of 
LDPC codes becomes prohibitively complex ! 0 (NZ), since 
the value of N in LDPC codes is large, around hundreds to 
thousands of bits or more. QC LDPC codes solve high 
encoding complexity in regular LDPC codes since they have 
sufficient structure to allow simple encoding with simple shift 
registers based on their generator polynomial in G [5], [8]. 
Unfortunately, this type of encoding is only useful for QC and 
cyclic codes. In spite of the fact that the LDPC codes employ 
a very low complexity decoding, the rank-deficiency of Hand 
high encoding complexity are serious issues. 
This paper proposes a novel code-construction method for 
constructing (3, k) regular LDPC codes in order to overcome 
such problems in regular LDPC codes. The proposed H is 
designed to avoid girth of length 4 and to generate various 
code rates (R) by only changing the number of columns in H. 
The choices of high rate (3, k) regular LDPC codes are taken 
since MacKay and Davey [15] showed that (3, k) regular 
LDPC codes with N ::; 4000 are not suitable for high code rate 
application (R > 0.875) as the BER performance not as good 
as the BER of regular codes with j > 3. Therefore, high code 
rate of (3, k) regular LDPC codes that shows a good BER 
performance is still an open problem. The remainder of the 
paper is organized as follows: The proposed code-construction 
is described in section II. Section III explains encoding. 
Section IV presents results and discussion. Conclusion and 
future direction is explained in section V. 
II. 
CODE-CONSTRUCTION 
The proposed code-construction method follows the 
construction method of QC LDPC codes that use right cyclic 
shift. The proposed H = [Hinf IHpar] consists of information 
part and an approximate lower triangular parity part as given 
in Fig. 1. There are three stages in the proposed code­
construction. The first stage builds the parity part of H using 
expanded deterministic base matrix and the second stage 
builds the information part of H with another expanded base 
matrix. Both stages are combined together while in the third 
stage, each element is expanded by one expansion factor.

IV. 
RESULT AND DISCUSSION 
The proposed (3, k) regular LDPC codes in this paper using 
the above code-construction have no rank-deficiency of Hand 
non-singular parity part. No rank-deficiency of H means that 
all the rows of H are linearly independent. Therefore, there is 
no offline process in H in order to maintain the code rate for 
erasing and replacing some linearly dependent rows with the 
new ones. Non-singular parity part of H yields an invertible 
matrix so that the proposed codes have low encoding 
complexity when they are encoded by Richardson-Urbanke 
method. The value of girth in the proposed H is maintained to 
have at least girth of 6-cycles. 
The results of this paper are obtained in term of block error 
rate (BLER) and bit error rate (BER) versus signal to noise 
ratio (SN R). Simulation model of the proposed code­
construction is developed based on LDPC encoder-decoder of 
MATLAB software version 7.4 using binary input, binary 
phase shift keying (BPSK) modulation and A WGN channeL 
The proposed (3, k) regular LDPC codes in this paper are 
encoded by H using Richardson-Urbanke method [16]. 
According to [8], [17], the minimum distance (dmin) of} = 3 is 
at least 4 that gives the ability of correcting the error of at least 
1 error. The result of the proposed code is obtained by 
simulating across 200 errors at each point of SNR. The 
number of iterations used in sum product decoding algorithm 
(SPA) in Fig. 8 is set to 200 iterations while in Fig. 9 is set to 
50 iterations. Fig. 8 uses 200 iterations since the compared 
regular QC and regular random LDPC codes in this figure use 
200 iterations. 
From the BLER result in Fig. 8, the proposed (3, 13) 
regular LDPC codes outperform (3, 13) regular QC LDPC and 
regular Gallager LDPC code constructed from [4] when the 
code rate R = 0.769. The proposed (3, 13 ) regular LDPC codes 
in Fig. 8 have encoding complexity ! 0 (1.034 N). In Fig. 8, 
(3, 13) regular Gallager LDPC codes have encoding 
complexity in the order of 0 (JV2) with code length N = 1057 
while (3, 13) regular QC LDPC codes have linear encoding 
complexity if these codes are encoded by shift registers based 
on their generator polynomial in G.

V. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE DIRECTION 
In this paper, we have introduced a novel code-construction 
method to construct (3, k) regular LDPC codes with finite code 
length. The proposed H is designed to generate various code 
rates (R) by changing the value of parameter ki• All results of 
5 
the proposed (3, k) regular LDPC codes presented in this paper 
are having no rank-deficiency of H, no girth of 4-cycles, non­
singular parity part and low encoding complexity. 
The proposed (3, 13) regular outperform regular QC and 
regular Gallager LDPC code when the value of code rate 
R = 0.769. It is also shown that (3, 42) regular LDPC code 
performs 0.97 dB from Shannon limit at BER 10,6 when the 
value of code rate R = 0.928. 
Future direction of this work is testing and investigating the 
proposed codes into hardware implementation and its cost 
analysis.
