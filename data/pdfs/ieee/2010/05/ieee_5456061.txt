Construction of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes Based on a Two-Dimensional MDS Code
Chao Chen, Baoming Bai, Member, IEEE, and Xinmei Wang, Member, IEEE

Abstract—In this letter, we first propose a general framework for constructing quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QCLDPC) codes based on a two-dimensional (2-D) maximum distance separable (MDS) code. Two classes of QC-LDPC codes are defined, whose parity-check matrices are transposes of each other. We then use a 2-D generalized Reed-Solomon (GRS) code to give a concrete construction. The decoding parity-check matrices have a large number of redundant parity-check equations while their Tanner graphs have a girth of at least 6. The minimum distances of the codes are very respectable. Experimental studies show that the constructed QC-LDPC codes perform well with the sum-product algorithm (SPA).

I. Introduction
C YCLIC and quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are attractive for two main reasons: they can be constructed to perform fairly well with iterative decoding for practical lengths; their structural properties can potentially facilitate efficient hardware implementations. Among them, finite geometry (FG)-LDPC codes [1] and finite field based LDPC codes [2] form two large classes.
In contrast to many other LDPC codes, FG-LDPC codes possess an appealing feature: their decoding parity-check matrices have a large number of redundant (i.e., linearly dependent) parity-check equations while guarantee no length4 cycles in the corresponding Tanner graphs [1]. Codes with this feature have very respectable minimum distances as far as LDPC codes are concerned. In this letter, we propose a new and systematic construction of such codes of QC form based on a two-dimensional (2-D) maximum distance separable (MDS) code. Using the proposed method, many new codes with the desirable feature are obtained.
Our idea comes from the work of [2] and [4]. In [2], Lan et al. showed that finite fields can be used to construct well-performed QC-LDPC codes. The method essentially boils down to the construction of a matrix over a finite field with certain constraints. The matrix is called the base matrix for code construction. In [4], Chen et al. generalized the work of [3] and gave a construction of the base matrix using the minimum-weight codewords of a 2-D Reed-Solomon (RS) code. IV. A Concrete Construction Based on a 2-D GRS Code

Codes Based on a 2-D MDS Code
Consider an (n, 2, n − 1) MDS code over GF(q) of length n, dimension 2 and minimum distance n − 1. According to Theorem 6 of Chapter 11 in [6], the numbers of weight n − 1 and weight n codewords are n(q − 1) and (q + 1 − n)(q − 1), respectively. All the q2 −1 nonzero codewords can be partitioned into q + 1 disjoint classes, W0, W1, · · · , Wq, with the following properties: 1) each class consists of q − 1 codewords; 2) if a codeword in Wi is denoted by wi = (wi,0, wi,1, · · · , wi,n−1), then Wi = {wi, αwi, · · · , αq−2wi}; 3) for 0 ≤ i ≤ n − 1, the ith component of all codewords in Wi is equal to 0; 4) any two codewords from two different classes differ in at least n − 1 positions. Then the q + 1 classes can be divided into
two groups: one with n classes whose codewords have weight n − 1; the other one with q + 1 − n classes whose codewords have weight n. Any codeword wi in the class Wi is called the representative of the class.
