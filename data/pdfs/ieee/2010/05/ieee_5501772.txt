Semi-Random and Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes Based on Multiple Parity-Check Codes
Xueqin Jiang, Yier Yan, and Moon Ho Lee Senior Member, IEEE Department of Electronics and Information Engineering Chonbuk National University, Korea Email:{jiangxueqin, year0080, moonho}@chonbuk.ac.kr

Abstract—This paper introduces a class of Semi-Random (SR) Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes and a class of QuasiCyclic (QC) LDPC codes. Both of them are derived from Multiple Serially Concatenated Multiple Parity-Check (M-SC-MPC) codes and interleavers, therefore, have low encoding complexities. These two codes are called M-SR-LDPC codes and M-QC-LDPC codes, respectively, in this paper. The M-SR-LDPC codes are designed with the modified Progressive Edge-Growth (PEG) algorithm and the M-QC-LDPC codes are designed with the existing cyclecondition formula. Consequently, the proposed codes have large girths. Simulation results show that our codes perform very well over the AWGN channel with iterative decoding.
Index Terms—Semi-Random (SR), PEG, Quasi-Cyclic (QC), LDPC codes, Multiple Parity-Check (MPC) codes, Interleavers.

I. INTRODUCTION
S INGLE Parity-Check (SPC) codes used in multidimensional schemes can yield very good error rate performance. Tee et al. proposed the Multiple Serially Concatenated SPC (M-SC-SPC) codes in [1]. An M-SC-SPC code consists of M SPC encoders and M −1 random interleavers and offers a flexible code rate and code length with low encoding complexity. In [2], Baldi et al. replaced SPC codes and interleavers of the M-SC-SPC codes with Multiple Parity-Check (MPC) codes to construct Multiple Serially Concatenated MPC (MSC-MPC) codes. The M-SC-MPC codes can be viewed as LDPC codes and therefore can be decoded with the iterative decoding.
The girths of M-SC-MPC codes, however, are lowerbounded by only 6 and the maximum code lengths are limited. To overcome these drawbacks, in this paper, SemiRandom LDPC (SR-LDPC) codes and Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes with large girth and longer maximum code length are proposed. Our proposed code consists of M MPC encoders and M carefully designed interleavers. So we call them M-SR-LDPC codes and M-QC-LDPC codes, respectively. The M-SR-LDPC codes can be designed with the modified Progressive Edge-Growth (PEG) algorithm [3] and the M-QC-LDPC codes can be designed with the existing cycle-condition formula [4]. The main difference between the M-SC-SPC codes and the proposed codes is that the interleavers in the M-SC-SPC codes are random interleavers while the interleavers in the proposed codes are carefully designed to prevent short cycles. The M-SC-MPC codes and the proposed codes are also different in that there is no interleaver in the M-SC-MPC codes.

This paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews the M-SC-SPC codes and the M-SC-MPC codes. Section III introduces how to design the M-SR-LDPC codes with the modified PEG algorithm and Section IV introduces how to design the M-QC-LDPC codes with the existing cyclecondition formula. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.

V. CONCLUSION
By inserting an interleaver before each component code of the M-SC-MPC code, we can construct M-SR-LDPC codes and M-QC-LDPC codes with fast encoding speed and large girths. The parity-check matrix of the M-SR-LDPC codes can be designed with the modified PEG algorithm and the MQC-LDPC codes can be designed with the existing cyclecondition formula. After the parity-check matrix is designed, each interleaver before the i-th component encoder is constructed according to the component parity-check matrix Hi.
