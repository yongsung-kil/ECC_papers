Joint Multiuser LDPC Coding Scheme for Error Control in DS-CDMA Systems
Hamid Saber∗, Ehsan Olfat∗ and Hamidreza Amindavar† ∗School of ECE, College of Engineering, University of Tehran
Email: h.saber@ece.ut.ac.ir, e.olfat@ece.ut.ac.ir †EE Department, Amirkabir University of Technology
Email: hamidami@aut.ac.ir

Abstract—In this paper, we introduce the application of low density parity check (LDPC) codes to multiuser detection through additive white gaussian noise (AWGN) channels. An ensemble of binary LDPC codes with an irregular degree distribution is used. We introduce Joint Multiuser LDPC (JML) Tanner graph for joint decoding of information bits of all users. The decoding of information sources is done by a modified iterative belief propagation (BP) algorithm, which deals with vector messages rather than scalar ones. Good degree distribution pairs are extracted by maximizing the threshold of the decoder. Next, performance of the generated degree distribution pairs is evaluated over AWGN channels. Furthermore, some performance analysis criteria of the proposed method such as multiuser efficiency and bit error rate (BER) are compared with optimum and minimum mean square error (MMSE) receivers and also with receivers, which use convolutional codes. Finally simulation results demonstrate that the new proposed method outperforms these receivers for some good degree distribution pairs.
Index Terms—LDPC codes; BP decoding; Code-Division Multiple Access (CDMA); Multiuser Detection; Tanner Graph.
I. INTRODUCTION
The multiuser detection deals with the separation of mixed sources of information transmitted in the same channel. CodeDivision Multiple Access (CDMA) is one of the most promising methods used in the multiuser detection systems. The conventional single user matched filter (MF) receivers can be used in many circumstances in which an appropriate signal to noise ratio is provided while having a low receiver complexity at the same time. The optimum receiver combats the drawback of low multiuser efficiency of the MF receiver by taking the presence of interferences from other users into account [1]. In [2], the authors introduced linear CDMA receivers. Other simple sub-optimum linear detectors such as minimum mean square error (MMSE) detector are introduced to substitute MF detectors. On the other hand, low density parity check (LDPC) codes are one of the best error control codes in digital communication and are widely used to make reliable communication possible at rates very close to the Shannon limit. LDPC codes have linear decoding complexity with the length of code. A large class of decoding algorithms called message passing algorithms is associated with these codes. The achievable capacity with LDPC codes under message passing decoding algorithms is analyzed in [3] and [4]. The asymptotic performance analysis of LDPC codes can be performed by density evolution. In [5] an application of LDPC codes with

a channel estimation method, based on expectation maximization, is provided. A combination of space-time block coding (STBC), orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) and CDMA spreading systems are presented in [6]. The authors in [7] applied sparse graph codes to finite-dimensional CDMA systems. In this paper, we present binary LDPC coding based on joint multiuser LDPC (JML) graph. It should be mentioned that our proposed method can be applied to an asynchronous system in a straightforward manner. In fact, an asynchronous system with L-users and frame length M can be considered as a synchronous system with (2M + 1)L users [8].
In our work an ensemble of LDPC codes with a specific degree distribution is used by generating a random graph. The main contribution of our work is that the receiver constructs a JML Tanner graph that is applied to all users by using an equivalent codebook in which code symbols are vectors rather than scalars. After doing so, it extracts the information bits of all users by running a modified version of BP algorithm. The performance of this scheme is also evaluated for the mentioned JML Tanner graph and the corresponding vector representation. Then, at the receiver the decoding process separates information sources by iterative BP decoding. The multiuser performance of this scheme is also evaluated.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In Section II, the basic system model and some preliminaries in this context are described. In Section III, the structure of the proposed iterative multiuser detector is presented in details. In Section IV, density evolution equations are derived, which then can be used to design good degree distribution pairs. Finally, in Section V, we evaluate the multiuser performance of the proposed method by simulation, and some comparisons between the proposed method and traditional multiuser detectors with and also without a convolutional coding scheme are performed.

C. BP decoder
Like the common process for LDPC codes in which all messages are passed in the Tanner graph of the code, our algorithm is also implemented on a Tanner graph with iterative messages passed from variable nodes to check nodes and vice versa. The BP decoder estimates the value of variable nodes and sends them over the edges of the graph. The JML Tanner graph consists of n variable vector nodes and m check vector nodes. The message transmitted on every edge of JML Tanner graph is a posteriori probability of variable vector node connected to the edge conditioned on the observed values of variable vector nodes in a neighborhood graph of the edge. Suppose that the elements of GF (2L) are assigned with indices 0 through 2L − 1 (this can be done by converting the binary representation of each symbol to the decimal representation). We assign each L-tuple over GF (2L) a vector of length L by converting 1 to -1 and 0 to 1 so, there is a one to one relation between a column vector over {−1, 1}L and an index i ∈ {0, . . . , 2L − 1} and thus the function f : {0, . . . , 2L − 1} → {−1, 1}L is well-defined. The messages are probability vectors of length 2L in the form of (p0, . . . , p2L−1) where pi is the probability that the value of the corresponding variable vector node is equal to f (i). III. DENSITY EVOLUTION

The messages of BP decoder are random variables and thus have a probability density function. It is shown in [4] that under independence assumption it may be possible to track these densities through iteration t to iteration t + 1 [4]. Note that the BP decoding algorithm defines two message calculating functions: variable-to-check message calculating function ϕvc = ϕvc(x0, x1, . . . , xdv−1) where x0 is the message at iteration 0 at node v and x1 through xdv−1 are messages

received from check nodes Cv\c, and check-to-variable message calculating function ϕcv = ϕcv(x1, . . . , xdc−1) where x1 through xdc−1 are messages received from variable nodes Vc\v. Also, initial message calculating function is denoted by ϕ(0). Note that mv(0()j) = ϕ(0)(yˆ(j)). V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we introduced a new method for error control of a CDMA multiuser detection system. Like many other

receivers the proposed method uses the outputs of the bank of matched filters to decide on the value of information bits. This task is done by joint decoding of information bits of all users. Decoding of noisy data is done by BP algorithm on JML Tanner Graph, which extracts the time diversity of LDPC codes. The performance of this method depends on degree distribution of the LDPC ensemble used. One good objective to extract good degree distribution can be maximizing the LDPC decoder threshold. As it is shown this detection method results in lower BER and higher multiuser efficiency while having low detection complexity, which is linear with the length of the code.
