High Parallel Variation Banyan Network Based Permutation Network for Reconfigurable LDPC Decoder

Xiao Peng, Zhixiang Chen, Xiongxin Zhao, Fumiaki Maehara, Satoshi Goto
The Graduate School of Information, Production and Systems Waseda University, Kitakyushu, Japan pengxiao@suou.waseda.jp

Abstract—Permutation network plays an important role in the reconfigurable QC-LDPC decoder for most modern wireless communication systems with multiple code rates and various code lengths. In this paper, we propose the variation Banyan network (VBN) based permutation network architecture for the reconfigurable QC-LDPC decoders and give the control signal generating algorithm for cyclic shift. Through introducing the bypass network, we put forward the nonblocking scheme for any input number and shift number. In addition, the optimized VBN is proposed for WiMAX and WiFi standard, which can shift at most 4 groups of input data, and greatly reduce the hardware complexity. The synthesis results using the 90nm technology demonstrate that the proposed permutation network can be implemented with the gate count of 18.3k and the frequency of 600 MHz.
Keywords-Permutation, Banyan network, LDPC decoder, Reconfigurable
I. INTRODUCTION
In recent years low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have received great attention in the channel coding area because of their excellent error correction capability. The high performance and parallel property make the structured quasicyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes become part of almost every modern wireless standard, such as wireless metropolitan area networks (WiMAX, IEEE 802.16e) [1] and wireless local area networks (WiFi, IEEE 802.11n) [2].
The parity check matrix (PCM) in QC-LDPC codes is composed of numerous cyclic shift submatrices which specify the interconnection between the variable nodes and check nodes. In modern wireless communication systems the PCMs defined contain several different sizes of submatrices. For example, the WiMAX LDPC codes have 19 kinds of submatrices with the length varying from 24 to 96. As a result, the permutation network in the reconfigurable LDPC decoder needs to provide cyclic shift ability for multiple input number (IN) and shift number (SN).
Currently, the reconfigurable permutation network designed for QC-LDPC codes are mainly based on barrel shifter or the Benes network [3]. Generally, barrel shifter is a natural approach for performing cyclic shift, but it would face difficulty for various IN and SN. C. H. Liu et al [4] add a frontend stage and a back-end stage to barrel shifter, which makes the whole network support the IEEE 802.16e and 802.11n. The shortcoming is that hardware cost of the additional subnetwork

would become much larger for more IN and SN. Also, it will cost much more area to realize parallel permutation by using barrel shifter.
On the other hand, the N×N Benes network is composed of 2log2N-1 stages of 2×2 switch units. To properly control all the switch units and realize the desired permutation, normally the dedicated control signals can be stored in the memory [5]. However, the area consumption would be too large for various IN and SN. In order to avoid big memory, J. Tang et al [6] proposed the scheme consists of two Benes networks, one of which is used to execute the permutation and the other is brought to generate the control signals. J. Lin et al [7] and D. Oh et al [8] stated their own control signal generating algorithm respectively, which can be easily implemented using regular decoders. For WiMAX, [7] optimized the Benes network by cutting off the unused switches, replacing the unchanged switches with wires and combining some of special switches, while [8] reduced the hardware complexity by using the 3×3 network to replace some of the 4×4 network. Nonetheless, these approaches based on the Benes network would always face the latency problem not only because the number of stages is large but also the control signals are generated stage by stage. In this paper, the proposed network based on VBN has only log2N+1 stages and the control signals of all stages is not related to the previous stages, but simply depended on the initial SN of input, which would greatly reduce the latency. Moreover, the proposed network could shift at most 4 groups of data simultaneously for short block length, which can significantly increase throughput of LDPC decoder.
This paper is organized as follows. Section II briefly reviews the decoder architecture of QC-LDPC codes, Benes network and VBN. Section III presents the cyclic shifting algorithm. In section IV, the hardware architecture and implementation will be addressed. Finally, section V draws the conclusion of this paper.

As shown in Figure 7, in 1212 VBN, IN is 10 and SN is 2. If the input 9 and 10 go thought the bypass network, and finally be chosen by the last multiplexer stage, there would be no blocking situation.
Generally, for cyclic shift, the lowest SN inputs should be transferred to the top SN position, if we regard that the network has its height. The conflicting situations comes from that some of the input which are not the lowest, such as the input 9 and 10 in Figure 7, should go to the top position. If these inputs are transformed by other paths, the collision will never occur.
As Figure 7 shows, the proposed network which could perform cyclic shift with any IN and SN should contain two VBNs. The last multiplexer stage is used to judge the final outputs selecting the original network outputs or bypass network outputs.

D. Parallelism design
According to the partial parallel QC-LDPC decoder architecture, the number of working PE is the number of expansion factor of the PCM. That is in LDPC decoder for WiMAX [9], if the expansion factor is 24, the usage of PEs is only 25%, which would waste hardware and lead to low throughput of LDPC decoder. In order to use those idle PEs, the permutation network should permute other frames of data concurrently and feed them into the proper PEs. Therefore, we propose the parallelism design scheme for the permutation network.
When the IN is less than 48, such as the block length 28, Ă, 48, defined by WiMAX, and the block length 27, defined by WiFi, the proposed network could perform two cyclic shifts in parallel with a small justification in the controller. Moreover, when the IN is 24, 4 groups of data could be cyclically shifted simultaneously.
As shown in Figure 13, according to the recursive property of the Banyan network, if we set all the control signals of the first stage to “0”, the first 48 input data are fed into the upper subnetwork and the rest data go into the lower subnetwork. Therefore, the similar shift algorithm and controller design could be used for both of the two subnetworks. The only difference is that there are two SN fed into the controller.
In the same way, for the 4-parallel way design, we could just set all the switches in stage 1 and stage 2 to bar state, and give the controller 4 SN.

[7] make optimization according to WiMAX standard, which lead to the input number can only be 24 to 96 with increment of 4. Our proposed network can use little hardware cost to realize the two and four parallelism because of the high symmetry property of VBN.
