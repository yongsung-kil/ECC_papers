Ruling out small stopping sets and small girth in Tanner graph of QC-LDPC code

Lingjun Kong∗ and Yang Xiao
Institute of Information Science, Beijing Jiaotong University, Beijing 100044, P. R. China

Abstract: The existing constructions of quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes do not consider the problems of small stopping sets and small girth together in the Tanner graph, while their existences will lead to the bit error rate (BER) performance of QC-LDPC codes being much poorer than that of randomly constructed LDPC codes even decoding failure. To solve the problem, some theorems of the specific chosen parity-check matrix of QC-LDPC codes without small stopping sets and small girth are proposed. A novel construction for QC-LDPC codes with long block lengths is presented by multiplying mmin or the multiple of mmin, which is the minimum order of the identity matrix for the chosen parity-check matrix. The simulation results show that the specific chosen parity-check matrix of QC-LDPC codes can effectively avoid specified stopping sets and small girth and exhibit excellent BER performance than random LDPC codes with the same longer codes length.
Keywords: quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check codes, stopping set, stopping distance, girth, circulant matrices.
DOI: 10.3969/j.issn.1004-4132.2010.01.021
1. Introduction
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have been shown to perform extremely well within tenths of a decibel of the capacity limit with iterative message-passing decoding in the codes’ Tanner graph [2−5]. QC-LDPC codes are a class of important structured LDPC codes and manifest excellent performance under iterative decoding. The algebraic structure of the codes makes them particularly suitable for high-speed VLSI implementation [6−10].
It is well known that certain combinatorial structures in the Tanner graph of an LDPC code exhibit a strong influence on its performance under iterative decoding. These structures include girth, stopping sets [11] and minimum distance. Small stopping sets are the failure cause of iterative decoding of LDPC codes over the BEC [12, 13]. The

The algebraic construction is seen to outperform the random construction for short to moderate the block lengths of the LDPC codes [16]. To solve the problems, theorems of parity-check matrix of QC-LDPC codes without small stopping sets and small girth are proposed.
2. Stopping sets and stopping distance
Definition 1 A stopping set S in an LDPC code is a subset of the variable nodes of the associated Tanner graph, so that all neighbors of S are connected with S at least twice.
A stopping set in an LDPC code is a set of column indices such that the sub-matrix formed by the corresponding columns of H does not contain a row of weight one(see Fig. 1). By default, we assume LDPC codes without singly connected variables, every stopping set contains cycles.

4. Simulation results

To illustrate the above theorems and corollaries, we design the (3, 6)-regular rate-1/2 QC-LDPC codes with different code lengths. In the computer simulation, we assume that the LDPC codes are modulated by BPSK and transmitted over AWGN channel. The iterative BP algorithm which allows a maximum of 20 decoding iterations is used for decoding. The BP decoder stops when either a valid codeword is found or the maximum number of decoding iterations is reached. 5. Conclusions
The theorems of the chosen parity-check matrix of QCLDPC codes without small stopping sets and small girth are proposed. A construction based on circulant matrices for designing good QC-LDPC codes with different block lengths without small stopping sets and small girth is presented. The simulation results show that the designed QCLDPC codes can effectively avoid specified stopping sets and small girth, and obtain the significant performance gain. It proves that the algebraic construction can be as good as the random construction for QC-LDPC codes with long block lengths.
