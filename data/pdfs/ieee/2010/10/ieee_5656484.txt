I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-check (LDPC) codes were proposed by Gallager [1] in 1962. This coding technique was largely forgotten for more than 30 years. The mid-1990s were highlighted by the rediscovery of Low-Density Parity-Parity check (LDPC) codes in the field of channel coding [2]. After 1993, there have been many contributions to the design and analysis of LDPC codes.
Recently a flurry of work has been conducted on the design of LDPC codes with imposed sub-structures, starting with the introduction of multi-edge-type codes in [3] and [4]. LDPC codes with protograph structure are a subclass of multi-edgetype LDPC codes [5]. Protograph LDPC codes have the advantage of high-speed decoding, low error floor and low iterative decoding threshold [6-10]. A protograph is a Tanner graph with a relatively small number of nodes. A ‚Äúcopy-andpermute‚Äù operation [11] can be applied to the protograph to obtain larger derived graphs of various sizes.
The Jacket transform has been proposed in [12], and its applications have involved in the areas of engineering as signal process, image compression, cryptography, coding theory and wireless communication. In this paper, we propose a method to construct the protograph LDPC codes based on Jacket matrices.

VI. CONCLUSION
The Jacket matrix is an orthogonal matrix with practical value in representing signal and images processing, especially for the data compression, multidimensional sub-sampling, spectrum analyzers, cryptography information hiding and signal scrambler. In this paper, we have proposed a method to construct the protograph LDPC codes based on Jacket matrices. Furthermore, we design a protograph LDPC codes based on Jacket matrices for mobile digital multimedia. The simulation results in AWGN channels show that the BER performance of the proposed codes is better by about 0.5 dB than that of GB20600 LDPC codes.
