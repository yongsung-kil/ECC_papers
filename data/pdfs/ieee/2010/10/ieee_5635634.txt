Regular Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes With Girth 6 From Prime Fields

Qingji Zheng1, Xiangxue Li2,3,†, Dong Zheng3, Baoan Guo4 1Department of Computer Science, University of Texas at San Antonio 2Department of Computer Science and Technology, East China Normal University 3 School of Information Security Engineering, Shanghai JiaoTong University
4 Aisino Corporation Inc
† Corresponding author: xxli@cs.sjtu.edu.cn

Abstract—The short paper proposes a method for constructing regular quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices via simple prime field operations. The main advantage is that regular QC LDPC codes with a variety of block lengths and rates can be easily constructed which have no cycles of length four or less. Simulation results show that within only a maximum of ten decoding iterations of sumproduct algorithm(SPA) the constructed regular codes of high rates have no error floor down to the bit-error rate of 10−7.
Keywords-quasi-cyclic LDPC; error floor; prime field; Shannon limit;
I. INTRODUCTION
Ever since Shannon [10] proved that for any channel there exist block codes that can achieve arbitrarily small probability of error at any communication rate up to the capacity of the channel, researchers have been exploring such codes for many decades. Low density parity check (LDPC) codes introduced in 1962 and rediscovered in 1990s raise considerable attention recently because of their performance approaching Shannon capacity limit [3], [7], [8]. Generally the methods of constructing LDPC codes may be categorized as random constructions or structural constructions[5], [9]. Superior to codes by structural constructions, codes of long length by random constructions have been shown to closely approach Shannon limit over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. For moderate length LDPC codes, [6] showed that irregular LDPC codes are not better than regular ones and codes by structural constructions can outperform those by random methods.
Due to their special structures, QC LDPC codes can be encoded by using shift-registers as far as hardware implementation is concerned and can approach linearly complexity proportional to the code length or to the parity check bits in different circumstances. Much work has been done in studying QC LDPC codes [1], [2], [4]. In this letter, one new method for constructing QC LDPC codes based on the circulant matrices is proposed. This simple method can be used to construct from prime field regular QC LDPC codes with a variety of block lengths and rates, and without 4-cycles. Experiments show that the constructed codes perform approximate 2dB from Shannon limit and have no error floor down to the bit-error rate of 10−7.

Note that a cycle of length 4 in H = [hi,j]γp×ρp is defined by 4 positions hi,j = 1 such that:
1) two consecutive positions are obtained by changing alternatively of row or column only; and
2) all positions are distinct, except the first and last ones. The cycle of length 4 can be associated with an ordered series of circulant permutation matrices, say
σ(hi,j ), σ(hi,k), σ(hl,k), σ(hl,j ), i = l, j = k
According to theorem 1 and the relationship between H and H, we have
hi,k − hi,j + hl,j − hl,k = 0 mod p,
which is equivalent to
i(k − j) + l(j − k) = 0 mod p,
or phrased slightly differently,
(i − l)(k − j) = 0 mod p.
This implies that either i = l or j = k holds. A contradiction is obtained and the correctness of the theorem follows.
Given 1 ≤ γ < ρ ≤ p, we can further set H (γ, ρ) as the top-left γ × ρ submatrix of H and denote H(γ, ρ) as the corresponding γp × ρp binary matrix with column weight γ and row weight ρ. It is clear that (γ, ρ) regular QC LDPC code defined by the matrix H(γ, ρ) is of length ρp, of code rate 1 − γ/ρ, and of girth at least 6.
IV. CODE PERFORMANCE
In the following experiments, we study the performance of regular QC LDPC codes constructed by our method. We construct (γ, ρ) regular QC LDPC codes, by varying the values of γ and ρ based on different fields (Zp, +, ×). The error performances over AWGN are simulated and we compare those with the Shannon Limit. In the decoding process, we adopt the sum-product algorithm and set the maximum decoding iteration to 10. Through these experiments we will explore the impacts of the characteristics of different fields and different γ and ρ when constructing (γ, ρ) regular QC LDPC codes.

A. Impact of the Field Characteristic
In this experiment, let us consider the construction of regular QC LDPC codes with the same weights of columns and rows but different fields. We set γ = 6, ρ = 37 and consider the three different fields, (Z37, +, ×), (Z61, +, ×) and (Z71, +, ×). So those (γ, ρ) regular QC LDPC codes are with rate of 0.84 and they are relatively short LDPC codes. Based on (Z37, +, ×), (Z61, +, ×) and (Z71, +, ×), we construct three different parity check matrices H31(γ, ρ), H61(γ, ρ) and H71(γ, ρ), with length of 1369,2257 and 2627 respectively. Fig. 2 depicts the performance of those codes over AWGN channel. From Fig. 2 we may see that at the Bit Error Rate(BER) of 10−6, the best code among the three code performs about 2.1dB from the Shannon Limit. Also, codes constructed by the fields of Z61 and Z71 have the comparable performance, but outperform the code based on the field of Z37 apparently.

Secondly, for those regular QC LDPC, codes of large length may not guarantee better error performance. Lastly, values of weights of columns and rows have impact on the code performance, and larger γ and ρ may not guarantee lower error performance when the rate of codes keeps the same.
V. CONCLUSION
The letter proposes a regular QC LDPC construction with girth at least 6 based on the prime field. The encoding operation is very simple. The constructed QC LDPC codes are of a variety of block lengths and rates and have no error floor down to the bit-error rate of 10−7.
