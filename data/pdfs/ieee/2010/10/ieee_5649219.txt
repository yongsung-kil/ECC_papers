Importance Sampling for LDPC Codes Based on Optimal Simulation Probability Density Function

Takakazu SAKAI Dept. of Computer Science Kitami Institute of Technology 165 Koen, Kitami, 090-8507 Japan. Email: sakai@cs.kitami-it.ac.jp

Koji SHIBATA Dept. of Computer Science Kitami Institute of Technology 165 Koen, Kitami, 090-8507 Japan. Email: shibata@cs.kitami-it.ac.jp

Abstract‚ÄĒThis paper shows a fast simulation method of lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes by using importance sampling (IS). In IS, the design of the simulation probability density function (PDF) plays a very important role to reduce the simulation time. We derive the optimal simulation PDF, which can reduce the variance of the estimator to zero. Since the optimal simulation PDF includes true error probability, it can not be utilized in the practical simulation. The simulation PDF which approximates the optimal simulation PDF is proposed. In addition, we show some numerical examples to show the effectiveness of the proposed simulation method.
I. INTRODUCTION Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1], which are constructed by very sparse factor graphs, yield excellent error correction performance by iterative decoding methods using message passing algorithms such as Sum-Product algorithm. Many LDPC codes with moderate block length present an error floor which is almost flattened error performance as increase of signal to noise ratio (SNR) [2]. The error performance of LDPC codes over a memoryless channel is almost approximated by the specific non-codeword called trapping sets at the error floor region. When the block length of LDPC codes is moderate, which is order of hundreds to thousands, a Monte Carlo simulation method can not estimate an error probability because it takes enormous simulation time. This causes difficulties to evaluate such a very low error performance for a certain application of LDPC codes. To evaluate low error probability of LDPC codes, some importance sampling (IS) [3] applications are proposed [4]‚Äď [7]. The envelope method [4] can not directly estimate lower error probability since the evaluation of the probability density function (PDF) of slightly lower SNR region of interests is needed. Dolecek et al. approximated the error probability of LDPC codes by the sum of the probability of fixed representative absorbing sets, which are similar to trapping sets [5]. However, it can not theoretically avoid the underestimate of the error probability because we can not evaluate the whole absorbing sets. The simulation time reduction by the classification of trapping sets [6] can not also give unbiased estimators. Cole et al. presented a three-step frame error rate (FER) evaluation method which is constructed by the trapping set search, the error boundary search of the trapping set, and the IS simulation of the FER [7]. The method by Cole et al.

guarantees unbiased estimators but the simulation time by this method is proportional to the number of the dominant trapping sets used in the simulation.
In this paper, a new estimation method of a low error probability of LDPC codes is proposed. The proposed method is related to the optimal simulation PDF, which can reduce the variance of the estimator to zero. It is impossible to utilize the optimal simulation PDF, since it includes true probabilities of error bit sequences. We approximate a probability of a error bit sequence by using upper bound of the probability of the most effective trapping set. The proposed simulation method also guarantees the unbiased estimator.
The rest of this paper is as follows; Section II shows background of LDPC codes and trapping sets. The concept of IS to speedup the simulation is presented in Sec. III. Section IV presents the three step-simulation method proposed by Cole et al. [7]. We derive the optimal simulation PDF and propose a fast evaluation method of LDPC codes in Sec. V. A new simulation PDF design, which is based on the optimal simulation PDF, is proposed. In Sec. VI, some numerical examples are shown to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. Finally, Section VII concludes this paper.

VI. NUMERICAL EXAMPLES
Some numerical examples are shown to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. As parity-check matrices, following Gallager codes with rate 1/2 were used [12].

Candidates of the dominant trapping sets were selected by [13], which was corresponding to step one in [7]. The decision process of the error boundary of the dominant trapping sets was the same with the second step in [7]. Five hundred trapping sets were selected from ones with smaller squared Euclidean distance between the correct signal and the boundary point calculated in the second step. The total number of simulation runs of the proposed method was 105. On the other hand, the number of simulation runs per trapping set for Cole et al.‚Äôs method was one thousand to make the total simulation runs of the proposed method be the same. VII. CONCLUSION We have shown a fast estimation method of the error performance of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. The simulation probability density function (PDF) of the proposed method is based on the optimal simulation PDF, which can reduce the variance of the estimator to zero. The utilization of the proposed simulation PDF can also avoid the dependency

between the simulation time and the number of the dominant trapping sets used in the simulation. Some numerical examples are shown to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods. The simulation time of the proposed method is reduced to 1/17.5 of that of the Cole et al.‚Äôs when we estimate a BER of 10‚ąí14 of the LDPC code of length 408.
