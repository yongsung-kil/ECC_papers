An Efficient Stopping Criterion of LDPC Decoding for Optical Transmission
Ming Jiang*,вА†, Jinfang DouвА†
*National Mobile Communications Research Laboratory, Southeast University Nanjing Email: jiang_ming@seu.edu.cn
вА†Science and Technology on Space Microwave Laboratory, China Academy of Space Technology Xi'an, P. R. China

Keywords: LDPC codes, Stopping criterion, Decoding.
Abstract
The stopping criterion based on parity-check constraints is an inherent advantage of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. However, the parity-check computation with signs of the posterior information costs additional hardware or decoding delays, which may be a particular problem for the full-parallel LDPC decoding in optical transmission. Furthermore, the performance degradation caused by using simplified stopping criterions should also be considered, where the maximum number of the iterations is usually limited by the very high decoding speed in optical transmission. In this paper, we propose an efficient early stopping criterion with the consideration of the complexity/performance tradeoffs. The new method is based an intelligent combination of check equations and temporary hard decisions, and can be pipelined with check-node updating, thus leading to lower complexity and power consumption, which are desirable for both optical and wireless communications. Simulation results show that the hybrid criterion not only achieves the same performance as the conventional stopping criterions, but also provides a noticeable gain for the other simplified stopping criterions.

Some stopping criterions [2][4][6][9][10][12] were developed to terminate the possible useless iterative decoding early in the low signalto-noise ratio (SNR) region, whereas the others [1][14] were designed to declare the success of iterative decoding as early as possible in the high SNR region. In this paper, we focus on the latter class of stopping criterions and design a criterion for LDPC codes that offers an attractive performance/complexity tradeoffs.
The parity-check matrix of an LDPC code presents itself as a useful tool for designing a stopping criterion. The iterative algorithms can take advantage of the inherent parity-check constraints on the signs of the posterior information (SPI) to verify successful decoding in time. Although straightforward, the SPI criterion requires considerable complexity to compute all parity-check equations in the high-speed decoders [1], for example the full-parallel decoding for optical transmission. Hard decision aided (HDA) criterion [14] is a primary stopping criterion proposed for turbo codes, where the iterative decoding is ceased when the results of two consecutive iterations agree with each other. This criterion is readily extendable to decode LDPC codes with relatively low complexity. However, it often causes miss detections and incurs an unacceptable high probability of undetected errors, therefore resulting in an inevitable error floor.

3 Simulation Results
The performance of BP algorithm and MS algorithm with SPI, HDA, SEI and hybrid stopping criterions are presented. We consider the (6, 32)-regular LDPC code specified for the 10 Gigabit Ethernet standard [11] and irregular LDPC codes specified for the 802.16e [8] and 802.11n [7] standards. For the implementation using VLSI architecture, the LDPC decoder usually adopts the normalized min-sum (NMS) algorithm [3], which uses simplified processing at the checknode update and requires less complexity. In the simulation, whether the decoded bits are not correct or the decoder sends a decoding failure signal, the transmission block will be defined as an error block.

The converging speed of 4-bit quantized NMS decoding is much slower than that of float decoding. Therefore, the parity-check constraints computed based on the posterior or extrinsic information sometimes cannot be all satisfied and sends a false alarm signal, although the information bits have already been correctly decoded. Thus, the 4-bit quantized NMS decoding with the hybrid stopping criterion has the same error performance with the SPI and SEI criterion.

4 Conclusion
In this paper, an efficient stopping criterion based on the SEI and HDA criterion is proposed for optical transmission. The simulation results show that the iterative decoding with the hybrid stopping criterion has nearly the same error performance with that of the standard SPI criterion, when both the regular codes in [11] and irregular codes in [8] are used. Although the convergence speed with SEI criterion is slower than that with SPI criterion, it can be compensated by the HDA criterion, which also can be implemented faster and easier. So the overall decoding complexity with the hybrid criterion is much lower than that of SPI criterion. The decoding performance of some irregular LDPC codes with our proposed hybrid criterion even outperform that with the SPI criterion, where the irregular LDPC codes are considered to achieve further more coding gain in the future high-speed optical transmission. Therefore, the hybrid stopping criterion offers a good tradeoff between the error performance and the decoding complexity.
