Abstract‚ÄĒThe performance of LDPC codes decoded by iterative algorithm depends on the structural properties of their underlying Tanner graphs. For discrete memoryless channels, error patterns dominating the frame error rate (FER) in the error floor region are termed trapping set. We have shown approximate examinations of small trapping sets of LDPC codes using the probabilistic algorithm. In this paper, we propose an efficient method for the detailed evaluation of the FER of LDPC codes in the error floor region. The accuracy of the FER lies on the failure probability of the number of trapping sets determined by our probabilistic algorithm and the FER estimated by the importance sampling.
I. INTRODUCTION Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes exhibit the performance close to the Shannon limit by iterative decoders [1]. The performance depends on the structural properties of their underlying Tanner graphs. On the binary erasure channel (BEC), stopping sets determine the error floor performance, and also dominate the bit error rate (BER) and the frame error rate (FER) of LDPC codes [2]. For discrete memoryless channels, the dominating error patterns have different names motivated by different analytical techniques. The decoding failure at the error floor region is influenced by a certain configuration of the subgraph in the Tanner graph, which is termed trapping sets [3]. Moreover, procedures that the low error rate performance is determined by small trapping sets have been presented in [4]. As for the analysis of performance at the error floor region, we have proposed a probabilistic algorithm for examining small trapping sets of LDPC codes in [6]. It is based on Stern‚Äôs algorithm which finds low-weight codewords of linear block codes with high probability [5]. We have applied this probabilistic algorithm to several LDPC codes and have found a lot of small trapping sets which influence the FER of these codes in the error floor region. In this paper, we propose an efficient method for the detailed evaluation of error floors of LDPC codes. In order to develop this method, first, we give the failure probability of determining the number of trapping sets by our probabilistic algorithm. Then, we evaluate the FER at the error floor region from the trapping sets by using the importance sampling [7]. Combining the probabilistic algorithm and the importance sampling, we can evaluate the low FER of LDPC codes with high accuracy.

Additionally, in the experimental results for several LDPC codes, we show the comparison between the FER performance obtained by our method and that of conventional methods.
