Abstract—In this paper we introduce puncturing techniques to produce

high-rate π-rotation low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. The tech-

niques rely on symmetry considerations to maintain simple description and

to achieve excellent performance. We also extend the bounds on the minimum d2 for the high-rate codes.
Keywords—Lattice, LDPC Codes, Manhattan Metric, Puncturing

VIII. SUMMARY
We have reviewed the structure of the π-rotation LDPC codes
based on the parity-check matrix composed of the dual-diagonal
sub-matrix and a composition of permutation matrices. This
matrix pattern has valuable symmetries that are exploited to de-
termine the minimum weight of codewords when the informa-
tion vector is weight-two. Based on this structure we show how
to efficiently search for π-rotation codes with minimum d2 distance. We then introduce a novel puncturing techniques appli-
cable to π-rotation codes. We can continue to use the estab-
lished π-rotation searching techniques to produce high perfor-
mance π-rotation codes operating at higher rate through punc-
turing. Opportunities exist for further research to discover better
permutation patterns in both the high and low rate domain.
