Reliability-Based Iterative Decoding of LDPC Codes Using Segmented Accumulation of Likelihood Ratio

Dong Zi-jian
School of Electronic Engineering Huaihai Institute of Technology
Lianyungang, China e-mail: zjdong@hhit.edu.cn

Abstract-In this work, we present an improvement on the belief propagation (BP) algorithm concatenated serially with order statistic decoding (OSD) for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. We use the segmented accumulation of log­ likelihood ratio (LLR) of variance nodes as the ordered information metric to reconstruct received sequence. This method can reduce the sensitivity of accumulated parameter selection to the fluctuation of performance, and further improve decoding performance.
Keywords-low-density parity-check codes, reliability-based decoding, segmented accumulated log-likelihood ratio (LLR)

I.

INTRODUCTION

For medium and short length LDPC codes, the performance of belief propagation (BP) decoding, though effective, is regarded as suboptimal, therefore the ordered statistics decoding (OSD) [I] concatenated to BP is proposed [2]. For such a BP-OSD reprocessing strategy, if no valid codeword is returned after BP decoding, the reliability information of codeword bits is outputted to OSD.
The reliability information delivered from the last BP iteration may be inaccurate due to the LLR oscillation phenomenon [3], which may results in a faulty OSD decoding. In [4], the accumulated LLR information is used to reconstruct ordered information sequence, instead of the last BP LLR output, which achieves a noticeable performance
a gain. But the evaluation of parameter in [4] is no rule and
no theoretical basis. In fact, the reason that BP decoding can not reach to the
MLD performance is that the girth of LDPC code is limited. After iterations more than half of girth, the bit decision information may become independent. For this reason, we improve the algorithm in [4] considering the impact of the girth. Simulations show that this method can reduce the
a impact of selection of on FER, and can further improve
decoding performance.

IV. CONCLUSION
In this letter, a modified BP-SOSD algorithm is proposed, which is based on the segmented accumulation of LLR as the ordered metric for OSD. This method can degrade the impact of parameter selection on decoding performance, and further improve the performance.
Simulations show that our proposed method is viable and effective. The decoding performance of segmented accumulation LLR strategy can be further improved by
a carefully selecting the weighting factor for each segment
according to the girth of LDPC codes and the laws of message passing during BP iterations.
