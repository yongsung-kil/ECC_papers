Design of Structured LDPC Codes with Quasi-Cyclic and Rotation Architecture

Qiao Guo-lei Dong Zi-jian
School of Electronic Engineering Huaihai Institute of Technology
Lianyungang, China

Abstract-Quasi-cyclic low density parity check (LOPC) codes
and rotation LOPC codes are investigated in this paper, based on which, a new class of well-structured LOPC codes are proposed. Additionally, for ease of coding, a dual-diagonal structure is applied to the parity-check matrices of those codes, which ensure that parity-check bits can be recursively calculated with linear computational complexity. With the rotating and cyclic shift structure, decoders can be effectively implemented with partially parallel architecture appeared in some literatures on QC-LOPC codes and other structured LOPC codes. Simulation results show that the proposed structured LOPC codes are feasible.
Keywords-quasi-cyclic; rotation structure; low density parity check (LDPq codes

I.

INTRODUCTION

The decoding of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes allows a high degree of parallelism, which makes them very suitable for high data rate applications such as magnetic storage systems and wide-band wireless multimedia communications [1-3]. There are two main classes of LDPC codes suitable for hardware implementation in current standards and literatures, RA(IRA)-LDPC codes used in
DVB-S2, CMMB[4] and QC-LDPC codes[5]. QC-LDPC
codes have effective parallel decoding structure and good performance [6,7], and now are one of the research hotspot. The parity-check matrices of those codes can be grouped, which makes the decoders can be implemented with partial parallel architectures, and therefore achieve a good tradeoff between hardware complexity and decoding throughput.
Regardless of many advantages of decoder of QC-LDPC codes, the encoding problem of those codes maybe an obstacle. Some literatures discuss this issue. A way of efficient encoding by exploiting the sparseness of parity­ check matrix is to use the graph-based message passing decoder as an encoder [8,9], assuming the unknown parity check bits are erased. However, this method does not work well with the case of existing stopping sets in Tanner graph. Myung proposed a special class of QC-LDPC codes [10], whose parity-check matrices are almost lower triangular matrices. Those codes can be encoded by the method proposed by MacKay and Richardson [11], which is based on the approximate low triangulation (ALT) of the parity­ check matrix by row and column permutations. But the computational complexity is still high.
In this correspondence, we present a special class of structured LDPC codes, called rotating quasi-cyclic LDPC

(RQC-LDPC) codes, which can be linearly encoded using parity-check matrix. The part of parity-check matrix corresponding to the parity check bits in codeword is a dual­ diagonal structure, and the part corresponding to the information bits is a quasi-cyclic and rotating structure, therefore the RQC-LDPC code is an irregular LDPC code.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. In section II and III, we review the QC-LDPC codes and the rotation LDPC codes. In Section IV, we propose the RQC­ LDPC codes, and a brief analysis of the encoding and decoding is presented. Simulation results are presented in section V, followed by conclusions in Section VI.
