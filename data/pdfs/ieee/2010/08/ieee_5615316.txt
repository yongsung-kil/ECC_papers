Low Redundancy Overhead Multibit Error Correction in Memory

Ming Zhu, Liyi Xiao, Zheng Sun, Yanjing Zhang, Yuqian Jiang, Shuhao Li
Microelectronics Center Harbin Institute of Technology
Harbin, China zhumingbaby@sina.com; xiaoly@hit.edu.cn

Abstract—As technology scales, memories have become more susceptible to radiation induced multiple bit upsets (MBUs). Multibit error correction codes (MECC) are an effective approach to mitigate MBUs in memories. This paper proposes a new codeword structure to protect memory against MBUs. Euclidean Geometry Low Density Parity Check (EG-LDPC) codes and Hamming codes are combined in the proposed codeword to assure the reliability of memory with low redundancy overhead. Furthermore, the proposed codeword can protect some longer data which is difficult for other MECCs to deal with. By using the proposed codeword, both low redundancy overhead scheme and long data width scheme for multibit error correction are presented. Finally, the proposed schemes have been implemented in Verilog and validated through a wide set of simulations. The experiment results reveal that the proposed method has a superior protection level. Compared with general MECC, it has lower redundancy and performance overheads.
Keywords-component; Memory, Multiple Bit Upsets, Multibit Error Correction Codes
I. INTRODUCTION
When feature size has been reduced into deep submicron (< 0.18 μm), integrated circuits are becoming more prone to transient errors due to alpha-particle and atmospheric neutron. With the increase of cell density, the distance of adjacent cells will decrease. As a result, the probability that memories affected by multiple bit upsets (MBUs) is obviously increased [1].
Single error correction and double error detection (SECDED) Hamming codes are capable of correcting one error and detecting all possible double errors. They are widely used in memories and caches. However, they may not provide adequate protection against MBUs. An effective approach to mitigate MBUs is multibit error correction codes (MECC). The Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) [2] codes, Reed-Solomon (RS) [3] codes and Golay [4] codes can deal with multiple errors in memories. The general drawbacks of these methods are latency and power consumption. Moreover, the encoding and decoding are more complex and require several lookup tables for multiplication in higher order fields.
Nowadays, Euclidean Geometry Low Density Parity Check (EG-LDPC) codes have been widely applied to communication and storage medium fields. EG-LDPC codes have the characteristic of high coding gain and low

undetectable probability. Furthermore, EG-LDPC codes can be decoded by majority logic decoder which can avoid iteration decoding and reduce the correction latency. The authors in [5] early used EG-LDPC codes to mitigate MBUs in memory. However, it is targeted for special NanoMemory rather than usual memory data (e.g., 32bit, 64bit, 128bit). In addition, EG-LDPC codes can’t handle some longer data in memory (e.g., 512bit).
In this paper, we propose a new codeword structure which is called Mix codes. The Mix codes combine EGLDPC codes with Hamming codes to correct multibit errors in memory. Firstly, the layout architecture of a codeword is considered to improve the reliability of memory. Secondly, Mix codes have a characteristic of low redundancy overhead. When the same data bit need to be protected, the Mix codes have significantly lower redundancy overhead compared with EG-LDPC codes used alone. Thirdly, the correcting capability of Mix codes is equal to combined EG-LDPC codes. Finally, Mix codes can protect some data that EGLDPC codes can not deal with. The proposed Mix codes can suit all data bits of ordinary length in memory (e.g., 2n bit).
This paper is divided into the following sections. In section 2, EG-LDPC codes for multibit error correction are introduced. In section 3, Mix codes are proposed, and some details of the proposed schemes are described. In section 4, simulation results and performance are analyzed. Finally, some conclusions are presented in section 5.

B. Low Redundancy Overhead Scheme
Though some data bits can be protected by EG-LDPC codes, it needs very high redundancy overhead to correct errors. In the proposed Mix codes, multibit errors can be corrected with a very low redundancy overhead.
A codeword is defined as (n, k, t), where n is code length, k is information bit, and t is correcting capability. For some data bits (e.g., 64bit, 256bit) usually used in memory, EG-LDPC codes can offer higher correcting capability. The double redundancy bits will bring double area overheads in memory system, which is unacceptable in majority memory design. Furthermore, the high correcting capability (e.g., t=15) is not necessary in usual radiation environment. Therefore, a low redundancy overhead scheme for multibit error correction is presented.

IV. EXPERIMENT AND ANALYSIS
Our proposed schemes have been implemented in Verilog. The design has been simulated with ModelSim and tested for functionality by given various inputs. Synopsys Design Compiler has been used for synthesizing the encoder and decoder circuits targeted to the SMIC 0.18um standard cell library. Fault injection is used for investigating correcting capability of the proposed schemes. The memory reliability is estimated by Mean Time to Failure (MTTF), which is obtained from a reliability model we presented. Then, area and power consumption are estimated by Synopsys tool. Critical path delay is estimated by Hspice.
A. Fault Injection
The fault injection is one of the key methods to estimate the coverage of error detection and correction. Our schemes are validated by randomly injecting errors in words and verifying the output results.

To analyze the performance of the proposed schemes, (71,64,1) Hamming code, (127,64,7) EG-LDPC code and (84,64,2) Mix code have been implemented for a memory with 64-bits word sizes. Hamming code has the lowest hardware overhead, but it can only correct single error and detect double errors. EGLDPC code can correct at least 7-bit errors. However, it needs multiple-step majority logic to decoding, which brings tremendous area, power and delay overheads. Furthermore, the latency of encoder and decoder is so large that it is difficult to apply in high speed memory.

V. CONCLUSION
This paper proposes a new codeword structure which is called Mix codes. Mix codes use the layout architecture of codeword and combine EG-LDPC codes with Hamming codes to correct MBUs in memory. Then, a low redundancy overhead scheme and a long data width scheme for multibit error correction are presented, respectively. The redundancy overheads in the proposed schemes are at least 60% lower than EG-LDPC codes. Furthermore, the proposed Mix codes can suit all memory data width. The results of fault injection and the MTTF show that our proposed schemes have superior protection level. Performance analysis shows that the encoder and decoder of the proposed method have the significantly lower area, power consumption and delay overheads than EG-LDPC codes.
