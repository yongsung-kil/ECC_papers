Efficiently Encodable Irregular QC-LDPC Codes Constructed from
Self-Reciprocal Generator Polynomials of MDS Codes
Norifumi Kamiya, Member, IEEE
AbstractвҖ”This letter presents a class of quasi-cyclic (QC)
low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes which are efficiently
encodable and have better memory efficiency than other ran-
domly constructed QC-LDPC codes. The parity-check matrix
of the codes is obtained from a block circulant matrix that
is generated from a self-reciprocal generator polynomial of a
maximum distance separable (MDS) code by replacing some of
its block entries by all zero matrices. We show by simulations
that the codes can achieve excellent error performance.
Index TermsвҖ”Low-density parity-check codes, maximum dis-
tance separable codes, encoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
Q
UASI-CYCLIC low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC)
codes with a dual-diagonal parity structure have attracted
considerable attention (e.g., [3], [4], [7]) because of their
encoding simplicity as well as outstanding error performance.
Advanced wireless communication standards such as IEEE
802.11n [3] and IEEE 802.16e [4] have recently adopted such
codes as optional channel coding schemes. The parity-check
matrix of the codes is composed of two submatrices: one cor-
responds to systematic information bits and the other to parity
bits. The former submatrix consists of an array of circulant
permutation and zero matrices, which will be chosen randomly
so that the resulting QC-LDPC codes perform well under
iterative decoding algorithms [7], [8]. The latter submatrix is
a block dual-diagonal matrix with a single weight-3 column
block satisfying certain constraints.
In this letter, we present a class of QC-LDPC codes with
a dual-diagonal parity structure. The parity-check matrices of
these codes are constructed from a common block circulant
matrix generated from a self-reciprocal generator polynomial
of a maximum-distance separable (MDS) code. We show that
a dual-diagonal parity structure can be obtained simply by
applying a masking technique [6], [7], [10] to the block
circulant matrix. The codes in this class have simple structures
which allow very low complexity encoding and save memory
space for storage of their parity-check matrix. We also show by
simulations that they can achieve excellent error performance.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this letter, we have presented a method for constructing
QC-LDPC codes with a dual-diagonal parity structure from a
self-reciprocal generator polynomial of an MDS code. The
method will also be applicable to constructing QC-LDPC
codes with such structures as those presented in [2] and [9].
