Enhancing the Performance of IDMA System by Irregular LDPC Codes
Jingxi Zhang1,2, Zhisong Bie1, Weiling Wu1

1. Key Lab of Universal Wireless Communications, Ministry of Education, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Beijing, 100876, People's Republic of China
2. School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, North China Electric Power University, Baoding, Hebei, 071053, P.R.C. Email˖zhjxbupt@eyou.com

Abstract—A method to enhance the performance of IDMA system by means of irregular LDPC codes was studied. Interleavers were used as signature character for user identification and iterative turbo detection and decoding approach was employed with reduced complexity. Gaussian approximation was applied to approximate the message probability density function on factor graph with multiple users. Iterative message update formulas on factor graph were derived. Degree profiles were obtained by using the iterative formula. Simulation result shows that the performance of irregular LDPCCoded IDMA system is better than that of (3,6) regular LDPCCoded IDMA system in terms of BER and performance gain about 0.6dB is achieved at BER 10-5 with 8 users for AWGN multiple access channel.
Keywords- Gaussian approximation; MAC; LDPC; IDMA; Degree profiles
I. INTRODUCTION
The convergence behavior of iterative decoding has been investigated[1][2], and iterative decoding of low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes is a powerful technique for approaching capacity on noisy channels [3]–[7]. In [5] the authors proposed a density evolution algorithm to calculate convergence thresholds for LDPC codes on the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. Density evolution[6][7] was applied to construct LDPC codes with very low thresholds. Gaussian approximation[8] was put forward and applied to calculate the Probability Density Function(PDF) of message passing between nodes on factor graph[9] to reduce computational complexity.
We consider Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) Multiple Access Channel (MAC) in which many users send information simultaneously. In this scenario, interleavers can be employed as character or signature sequence to distinguish signals from different users, and all the users employ a common spreading sequence. As we know, turbo-type iterative multi-user detection (MUD) method[10] has significant effect on mitigating Inter Symbol Interference (lSI) and Multiple Access Interference (MAl) which greatly restricts the performance of multiple access wireless systems. We are interested in the low cost turbo-type iterative MUD. [11] used this method and provided a comprehensive study of the Interleaver-based Multiple Access(IDMA) scheme and derived several low-cost detection algorithms for different channel conditions. In IDMA system powerful error correcting codes can be a tool to enhance the performance. However, in MAC scenario the well

known on the shelf good performance LDPC codes for single user AWGN channel are no longer good codes due to their mismatch to the MAC. Therefore we have to search new degree distribution for MAC channel. In [12] regular (3,6) LDPC codes were applied to IDMA system (called as LDPCCoded IDMA system) and performance comparison between IDMA systems based on LDPC Codes and based on convolution codes is made over different transmitting environments. Simulation results showed the powerful performance of LDPCCoded IDMA system. We inherited the idea of [10] for combating MUD and extend the work of [12] by using irregular LDPC codes and the same system module. We derived the formula for approximating the messages of different types of nodes on factor graph by using Gaussian approximation and searched the optimized degree profile used in LDPC-Coded IDMA system. By using the optimized irregular codes we constructed check matrix randomly and simulated the performance.
The paper is organized as follows: Section II introduces the LDPC-Coded IDMA system scheme. Section III elaborates the method used in searching irregular LDPC code. In Section IV simulations are given showing the performance of the proposed scheme. Section V concludes the paper.

B. Iterative Chip-by-Chip Receiver
The lower part of Figure 1 illustrates the receiver structure. The receiver operation is based on two constraints: the multiple access channel constraint and the LDPC code constraint of C. Figure 1 employs a low-cost chip-by-chip detection strategy which avoids conventional maximum a posteriori(MAP) operations. The receiver consists of an elementary signal estimator (ESE) and K a posteriori probability (APP) decoders (DECs). The two constraints are considered separately in the ESE and in the DECs, only chip extrinsic information (CEI)[13] is transferred, which offers the best performance compared with bit extrinsic information (BEI) and APP. In this way the output of DECs can be refined by using a turbo-type iterative processing, which greatly reduces the complexity involved[11].

In this way we fix the spectrum efficiency of the system to 0.5. We assume that all the users have equal power and equal rate, therefore it is unnecessary to make the degree profiles different between different users.

V. CONCLUSION
We consider AWGN MAC scenario where many users send information simultaneously. We applied the system proposed in[12] by using irregular LDPC codes with an effort to approach MAC channel capacity. Interleavers were employed as signature character for user identification and low complexity chip-by-chip turbo detection was adopted. The method of Gaussian approximation was employed in treating the message from VN, CN and CFN on factor graph. We defined SD and derived the message update formula of VN, CN, and CFN based on Gaussian approximation By differential evolution we obtained degree profiles in the case of 2 and 8 users with spectral efficiency 0.5. We constructed the check

matrix randomly based on the optimized degree profile and made simulations. Simulation results showed the superiority of the proposed method compared with regular (3,6) LDPC code in terms of SNR and performance gain about 0.6dB has been achieved for a system with 8 users at spectral efficiency 0.5 for AWGN MAC channel.
