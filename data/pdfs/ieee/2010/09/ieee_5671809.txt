2010 IEEE 21st International Symposium on Personal Indoor and Mobile Radio Communications

Early Stopping Scheme for Iterative Carrier Synchronization of LDPC Codes

Chengxin Xu, Yongyu Chang, Changhao Zhai, Wenyan Leng and Xin Zhang
Wireless Theories and Technologies Lab (WT&T) Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications
Beijing, China

Abstract—In order to reduce the system computation complexity and delay, we propose a scheme to decrease the iterative time of the carrier synchronization for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. The system will stop iteration if the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) message at the output of decoder satisfies the limitary conditions. Simulation results show that the proposed scheme is efficient and there is little degradation on the system performance. Moreover, the additional complexity is almost negligible.
Keywords-iterative synchronization; LDPC codes; stopping criterion; iterative decoding
I. INTRODUCTION
In order to achieve the desired error correction capability of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, strict carrier synchronization is a crucial prerequisite. It is observed that even a small carrier frequency offset or phase offset in the decoder input can cause a severe effective SNR reduction. In [1-2], the iterative carrier compensations embedded into the iterative decoding process achieve a bit error rate (BER) curve close to that of the ideal recovery. In [3-4], some iterative carrier synchronization schemes are proposed for LDPC codes.
However, the scheme with a pre-set maximum iteration time does not work well. If the maximum iteration time is small, the correction on the carrier offset is few. The system performance is not better than that with enough iteration time. If the maximum iteration time is large, the system does not stop iterative synchronization until successful decoding or achieving the maximum iteration time. In fact, most of the iterations are unnecessary because the system has reached steady state before these stopping conditions. Moreover, an iteration of the synchronization follows with many iterations of the decoding. As a result, the computation and delay are increased, and much power is wasted. Therefore, we propose an early stopping scheme for the iterative carrier synchronization of LDPC codes with the motivation in [5].
In [5] an early stopping criterion named convergence of mean magnitude (CMM) was proposed for the decoding of the LDPC codes. This scheme can efficiently stop iterative decoding by comparing the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) messages at the output of decoder. The proposed scheme also uses the LLR message to decide whether to stop the iterative synchronization. In [6], an effective carrier synchronization scheme for turbo codes and LDPC codes was proposed. We use this carrier synchronization scheme to explain the

proposed early stopping criterion. Because the characters of the LLR messages are almost the same in most of the iterative synchronization schemes, the proposed scheme also suits to those synchronization schemes.
The main contribution of the proposed scheme is that it can stop the iterative carrier synchronization automatically when the output of the decoder has reached steady state. Consequently, the proposed scheme can decrease the system delay and the number of the iterations for decoding, which is important for the power-limited system and the real-time communication system. Moreover, the additional computation is tiny. In additional, if the CMM algorithm combines with the proposed scheme, the computation and delay will be further reduced.
The outline of this paper is as follows. We start by introducing the system model in Section Ⅱ. In Section Ⅲ, more details about the proposed scheme are given. Then, some simulation results are shown in Section Ⅳ. Finally, in Section Ⅴ, we draw a conclusion.

V. CONCLUSION
Iterative carrier synchronization can improve the performance of LDPC codes. However, good synchronization means more computation and delay. Therefore, we propose an early stopping scheme for the iterative carrier synchronization to reduce the computation and the delay. Simulation results show that the proposed scheme can effectively decrease the iterative time of the synchronization. Moreover, the additional complexity of this scheme is negligible compared with the decoding algorithm, and the degradation of the system performance is tiny.
