Modified Progressive Edge-Growth Algorithm for Fast-Encoding LDPC Codes
Xueqin Jiang, Mi Sung Lee and Moon Ho Lee, Department of Electronics and Information Engineering
Chonbuk National University, Korea Email:{jiangxueqin, llmmss, moonho}@jbnu.ac.kr

Abstract—Progressive edge-growth (PEG) algorithm is known to construct low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes at finite code lengths with large girths.A simple variation of the PEG algorithm, linear-encoding PEG (LPEG) algorithm, can be applied to generate linear encodable LDPC codes. This paper presents a modified LPEG algorithm to construct fast encodable LDPC codes with a certain girth constraint. The presented fast encodable LDPC codes facilitate the parallel encoding processes for parity bit generations. Therefore, their encoding time is much shorter than that of LPEG codes.
Index Terms—LDPC codes, encoding, PEG algorithm , girth.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes first proposed in the early 1960’s [1] and re-discovered in 1996 [2], have recently attracted much attention due to their capacityapproaching performance and low decoding complexity. In particular, construction of good LDPC codes at short and intermediate code lengths is of great practical importance. Among the existing methods, one of the most successful approaches for the construction of LDPC codes is progressiveedge-growth (PEG) algorithm proposed in [3]. PEG construction builds up a Tanner graph, or equivalently a parity-check matrix, for an LDPC code in an edge-by-edge manner and by maximizing the local girth at symbol nodes in a greedy algorithm. It is simple and also flexible in that it can be applied to construct codes of arbitrary length and rate. In addition, PEG algorithm can be modified to construct linear encodable LDPC codes. In this paper, we call this modified algorithm linear-encoding PEG (LPEG) algorithm. LDPC codes with m check nodes designed by the LPEG algorithm can be encoded in m recursive steps [3]. Therefore, for a given code rate R, the encoding time grows linearly with the code length n = m/(1 − R).
The motivation of this paper is to reduce the encoding time of the LPEG without performance loss. In this paper we modified the LPEG algorithm and obtained a new PEG algorithm which is called fast-encoding PEG (FPEG) algorithm. The encoding time of the FPEG codes depends only on the maximum degree of symbol nodes, not the code length n. Since dm s ax is much smaller than m, the encoding time of FPEG codes is much faster than that of the LPEG codes. Moreover, we proved that the PEG codes and our codes have similar lower bound of girth .
This paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews the

PEG algorithm and the LPEG algorithm. Section III introduces the FPEG algorithm and the modified FPEG algorithm (MFPEG). Lower bound on the girth of the FPEG algorithm is also derived in this section. Section IV presents simulation results comparing the performance of FPEG codes and MFPEG codes with that of LPEG codes. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.
