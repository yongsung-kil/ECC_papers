Abstract-the LDPC application involves the problem: encoding complexity. Since the generator matrices are dense and with random positions of "Is", the encoder needs to store every "1" of the generator matrices by using huge chip area. To solve problem, we need to design the LDPC codes with block cyclic generator matrices. This paper reveals that the protograph LDPC codes can have lower encoding complexity under the condition of the proposed theorem that the parity check matrices of the LDPC codes are nonsingular and circulate. The circulate property of generator matrix of protograph LDPC codes can be obtained from the quasi-cyclic parity check matrix of protograph LDPC codes. Thus it can reduce the encoding complexity. We give an algorithm of protograph LDPC codes with fast encoding based on the theorem, and it verifies that protograph LDPC codes with circulate property of generator matrices can simplify encoder
hardware implementation.
Keywords- Protograph LDPC codes; cyclic matrices; fast encoding algorithm; BER performance
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-check (LOPC) codes were proposed by Gallager [1] in 1962. This coding technique was largely forgotten for more than 30 years. The mid-1990s were highlighted by the rediscovery of Low-Density Parity-Parity check (LOPC) codes in the field of channel coding [2]. After 1993 there have been many contributions to the design and analysis of LOPC codes.
Recently a flurry of work has been conducted on the design of LDPC codes with imposed sub-structures, starting with the introduction of multi-edge-type codes in [3] and [4]. LOPC codes with protograph structure are a subclass of multi-edge­ type LDPC codes [5]. Protograph LDPC codes have the advantage of high-speed decoding, low error floor and low iterative decoding threshold [6-10]. A protograph is a Tanner graph with a relatively small number of nodes. A "copy-and­ permute" operation [11] can be applied to the protograph to obtain larger derived graphs of various sizes. While, protograph LDPC codes of [6-10] are not sure to have the circulate property of generator matrices, which leads the encoding complexity problem. In order to reduce the encoding complexity, we propose a theorem concerning circulate property of generator matrices of protograph LDPC codes, it

shows that the block cyclic generator matrix of protograph LOPC codes can be obtained from the corresponding quasi­ cyclic parity check matrix. We propose a fast encoding algorithm of protograph LOPC codes based on the theorem; the fast encoding algorithms simplify encoder hardware.

IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, in order to reduce the encoding complexity, we propose a theorem concerning circulate property of nonsingular generator matrix of protograph LDPC codes, it proves that the quasi-cyclic parity check matrix of protograph LDPC codes can obtain block cyclic generator matrix. We propose a fast encoding algorithm of protograph LDPC codes based on the theorem; the fast encoding algorithms simplify encoder hardware. The proposed code has no girth-4, it has a codes' length of 5120; and it is much shorter than the GB20600 LDPC codes at same code rate. The simulation results in AWGN channels show that the BER performance of the proposed codes is better about 0.6 dB than that of existing GB20600 LDPC codes.
