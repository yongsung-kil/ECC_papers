Abstract-This paper shows a fast simulation method of 
low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes over an additive non­
Gaussian noise channel. The evaluation of an error performance 
of LDPC codes is usually executed by using a Monte-Carlo 
simulation since the theoretical calculation method has not been 
reported. It is very difficult to evaluate an error performance of 
LDPC codes with finite block length when the error probability 
is very low. To solve this problem, some fast simulation methods 
by using importance sampling (IS) 
are proposed. However, 
the conventional methods can not reduce the simulation time 
of LDPC codes over an additive non-Gaussian noise channel 
because the simulation probability density function (PDF) used 
in IS is designed for an additive white Gaussian noise. We 
propose a new simulation PDF design related to the Chernoff 
bound to estimate an error performance of LDPC codes over 
an additive non-Gaussian noise channel. In addition, we show 
some numerical examples to demonstrate the effectiveness of the 
proposed simulation method. The simulation time of the proposed 
method is reduced to about 1/2000 of that of the conventional 
method when we estimate the frame error rate of 10-10• 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1], which are 
constructed by very sparse factor graphs, yield excellent error 
correction performance by iterative decoding methods using 
message passing algorithms such as Sum-Product algorithm. 
Many LDPC codes with moderate block length present an 
error floor which is almost flattened error performance with 
increase of signal to noise ratio (SNR) [2]. The error perfor­
mance of LDPC codes over a memoryless channel is almost 
approximated by the specific non-codeword errors called trap­
ping sets at the error floor region. When the block length 
of LDPC codes is moderate, which is order of hundreds to 
thousands, a Monte Carlo simulation method can not evaluate 
an error probability because it takes enormous simulation time. 
To evaluate a low error probability of LDPC codes, some 
importance sampling (IS) [3] applications are proposed [4]­
[8]. The envelope method [4] can not directly estimate lower 
error probability since the evaluation of the probability density 
function (PDF) of slightly lower SNR region of interests is 
needed. The basic process of the other IS applications [5]­
[8] are almost the same, and are constructed by a search of 
dominant trapping sets and an IS simulation of them.

codes over an additive non-Gaussian noise channel, the con-

IV. CONVENTIONAL FAST SIMULATION METHOD 
The three-step method proposed by Cole et al. [7] is 
explained since the evaluation process of the error probability 
in this paper is the same except for the proposed simulation 
PDF. At the first step, low weight trapping sets are searched 
by utilizing the property of the local graph structure. A certain 
number of candidates of trapping sets are picked up from the 
set of low weight trapping sets. The second step decides the 
error boundary of each trapping set by adding deterministic 
noise. The boundary is searched along the line connecting the 
correct signal point on the signal space with the signal point 
corresponding to the given trapping set (Fig. 1). At the third 
step, the frame error rate (FER) is evaluated by summing the 
IS simulation results for each dominant trapping set. The MT 
method [9] is used for constructing the simulation PDF of 
each signal component since an additive white Gaussian noise 
(AWGN) channel is implicitly assumed.

Figures 2 and 3 depict the error performance of C1 and C2. 
In all cases, the error probability of the proposed method is 
slightly larger than that of the MT method, especially at the 
high Eb/NO region (Eb/NO ;:: 5 [dB)). The phenomenon is 
caused by the difference of the design of the simulation PDF. 
The important signal areas are rarely observed by using the 
simulation PDF designed by the MT method. As increase of 
the number of simulation runs, the error performance of the 
MT method approaches to that of the proposed method. At 
Eb/NO 
= 9 [dB], the proposed method detects about sixty 
percent of the transmitted sequences as the target error bit 
sequences. In the case of the MT method, the percentage of the 
detection of the target errors is about forty. The difference is 
one of the reasons of the difference of the error performances.

two methods at high Eb/NO. Since the theoretical calculation

VII. CONCLUSION 
We have proposed a new design method of a simulation 
probability density function (PDF) to evaluate a low error 
probability of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes over an 
additive non-Gaussian noise channel. The proposed simulation 
PDF is based on the concept of the Chernoff bound. Further­
more, it includes the mean translation method as a special case. 
Some numerical examples are presented to demonstrate the 
effectiveness of the proposed method. The proposed method
