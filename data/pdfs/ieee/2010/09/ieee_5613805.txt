Abstract-Cooperative communication is a well known tech­
nique to yield transmit diversity in the case of fading channels 
and to increase the spectral efficiency in the case of Gaussian 
channels. Error-correcting codes have to be carefully designed 
to achieve the promised gains. Good LDPC codes are known for 
fading channels and for Gaussian channels, but an LDPC code 
ensemble that performs well on both channels has not yet been 
presented in the literature. This paper merges two families of 
LDPC codes into a universal LDPC code ensemble for both fading 
channels and Gaussian channels. Furthermore, the new universal 
LDPC code ensemble outperforms previously proposed codes on 
the block fading channel, which is explained through a fading 
space analysis. Simulation of the word error rate performance of 
the new proposed family of LDPC codes shows that it performs 
well on both fading channels and Gaussian channels. 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Cooperative Communication [6] is an alternative to multiple 
antenna channels to yield transmit diversity and increase 
the spectral efficiency in a fading environment. The most 
elementary cooperative network is the relay channel. The task 
of the relay is specified by the protocol. In the case of coded 
cooperation [6], the relay decodes the message received from 
the source, and then transmits to the destination additional 
parity bits related to the message. Coded cooperation yields 
full-diversity if the overall error-correcting code is carefully 
designed [4]. 
The word error rate (WER) of good codes should be inde­
pendent of the block length. The solution is to use capacity­
achieving codes [3], for example LDPC codes. LDPC codes 
designed for block fading (BF) [1] relay channels are Rate­
Compatible RootCheck (RCRC)-LDPC codes [4], [5], exhibit­
ing the two necessary properties for coded cooperation: rate­
compatibility and full-diversity. For Gaussian relay channels, 
bilayer LDPC codes [11] have been proposed to implement the 
binning techniques of decode-and-forward. Then, improved 
multi-edge type bilayer-expurgated LDPC codes have been 
designed in [12]. 
The aim of this paper is to design a universal LDPC code 
ensemble, in the sense that is has good performance on both 
the BF channel and the Gaussian channel, which has not been 
presented yet. Furthermore, this has led to an improved coding 
gain on the BF channel which is explained through an analysis 
in the real space defined by the fading coefficients, which will 
be referred to as fading space in the following.
