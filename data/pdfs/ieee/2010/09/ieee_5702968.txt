A Hybrid Low Complexity Decoding of LDPC Codes

Hua Wang, Guangrong Fan, Jingming Kuang
School of Information and Electronics Beijing Institute of Technology Beijing, China wanghua@bit.edu.cn

Abstract—An efficient early stopping criterion based on the evolution of the number of reliable variable nodes (NRVN) is proposed for low density parity check (LDPC) codes in this paper. This criterion can significantly reduce the average number of iterations (ANI) at low to middle signal to noise ratios (SNRs) with minimal performance degradation. Furthermore, a hybrid decoding method is presented to reduce the decoding complexity of LDPC codes by combining the proposed stopping criterion and forced convergence (FC) decoding algorithm. Compared with the classic belief propagation (BP) decoding, significant computation complexity reduction can be achieved with small computation overhead.
Keywords-ldpc codes; iterative decoding; reduced complexity decoding; early stopping criterion
I. INTRODUCTION
It is well known that LDPC codes [1] with iterative BP decoding algorithm can achieve excellent performance that is very close to the Shannon limit [2]. And now LDPC codes are being considered for use in new communications standards such as DVB-S2, IEEE 802.3an, and 802.16 etc.
The classic BP decoding of LDPC codes is an iterative process, and parity check constraints are tested to determine successful decoding at the end of each iteration. In order to reduce the LDPC decoder power consumption, several stopping criteria [3]-[7] have been proposed. The received block is distinguished into decodable and undecodable block according to the additional criterion, and then the decoding procedure will be stopped as soon as the received block is decided to be a undecodable one. Consequently, power and processing time can be saved due to early terminated decoding. In [3], the presented decision rule is based on the evolution of the variable node reliability (VNR), where VNR is defined as the summation of magnitudes of log-likelihood ratio (LLR) messages along all variable nodes in each decoding iteration. Similarly, a stopping criterion in [4] is also based on the convergence of the mean magnitude (CMM) of LLR messages. These two criteria can detect undecodable blocks effectively. However, additional complex computation is required because of the summation of the magnitudes of LLR messages. A simple decision rule is proposed in [5] based on the monitoring of the number of satisfied parity check (NSPC) constraints. However, it will suffer some performance degradation when inappropriate parameters are chosen. Later, a slightly different stopping criterion is developed in [6]. Unlike the strategy adopted in [5], the presented method accumulates evidence in

favor of successful decoding. This approach can further reduce the ANI compared with the former one but incurs more performance loss. In [7], two metrics (i.e., the variation of the LLR message between successive iterations and the number of unsatisfied parity check constraints) are adopted in the stopping criterion. Although this method is more robust, LLR messages in the previous iteration need to be stored and subtractions of the LLR messages between successive iterations are required.
Besides the stopping criteria, the concept of FC decoding [8] has been studied to reduce the number of node operations in LDPC decoding. It utilizes the fact that some check nodes or variable nodes have already converged to relatively reliable nodes, and need no update operation. In this paper, we propose a reliability decision rule to determine the reliable variable and check nodes per decoding iteration, and then, inspired by [5], a stopping criterion based on the evolution of the NRVN is presented. Furthermore, combining the stopping criterion and FC decoding algorithm, a hybrid low complexity decoding method for LDPC codes is developed.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. In Section II, a new stopping criterion for LDPC codes is proposed based on the variation of NRVN. And then in Section III, we present a hybrid decoding method by combing the proposed stopping criterion and the FC decoding algorithm. Simulation results and discussions are shown in Section IV. And finally conclusions are given in Section V.

Clearly, the essentiality of the early stopping criterion is to identify the decoding behavior, and decide whether continue iterating or stopping. It should be pointed out that there are three possible decision results, i.e., hit, false alarm and miss detection. Hit means that we predict the decoding behavior correctly. False alarm is defined as the case where the received block is wrongly decided as undecodable, while in fact it is decodable. And miss detection is just the opposite case of false alarm. Among the three cases, false alarm can cause performance degradation. Thus, an efficient early stopping criterion should have a lower false alarm rate (FAR), especially at middle to high SNRs.

different iterations, but they are kept constant for the sake of
simplicity. In general, small Tc and Tv will result in
performance degradation, large Tc and Tv can not obtain
significant complexity reduction. However, finding the optimal threshold parameters analytically appears to be a rather difficult task. So, for a given code, we use simulation to manipulate this. Besides, optimal thresholds are also dependent on SNR, hence we normalize the thresholds by the standard deviation of noise, and the simulations below show that the normalized thresholds works well for the concerned SNR region.

V. CONCLUSION
Due to the wide application in practice, there is a strong demand for low complexity decoding of LDPC codes. In this

paper, an efficient early stopping criterion based on the evolution of NRVN has been proposed to reduce the ANI for LDPC codes, and moreover, by combining the proposed stopping criterion and FC decoding algorithm, a hybrid decoding method is presented to further reduce the decoding complexity of LDPC codes. Simulation results have demonstrated that the proposed stopping criterion can significantly reduce the ANI at low to middle SNRs, and besides the proposed hybrid decoding method can significantly reduce the number of node operations. It is also shown that the performance degradation is very little as compared with the BP decoding algorithm, and it can be efficiently implemented with small hardware overhead.
