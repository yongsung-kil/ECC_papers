A-min Algorithm Using TAP Approach
M. Abdelhedi, O. Hamdi, and A. Bouallegue

Abstract- In this paper, we present the decoding algorithms of Low-density parity-check (LDPe) codes using statistical physics arguments. Previous works, have shown that the BeliefPropagation (BP) algorithm, is equivalent to the ThoulessAnderson-Paimer(TAP) [1] approach. In this paper, we develop the log-likelihood ratios-Belief Propagation (LLR-BP) algorithm and its simplification, the A-min algorithm using this method developed in the study of disordered systems. The obtained results by simulation confirm that the A-min algorithm gets closer to the LLR-BP algorithm as A increases.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were first discovered by Gallager [2], in his thesis, in 1962 and have recently been rediscovered by Mackay and Neal [3], [4]. They deliver very good performance when decoded with the beliefpropagation (BP) algorithm [3]. As LDPC codes are being considered in the second Satellite Digital Video Broadcasting normalization (DVB-S2), the search for efficient implementations of decoding algorithms is being pursued intensively.
The implementation of the BP algorithm is difficult. That's why different simplifications of this algorithm were discovered. The BP algorithm can be simplified using the BP-Based algorithm [5] and the A-min algorithm [6].
LDPC codes are based on random construction. Because of this randomness, it is not easy to analyze them with the traditional methods of information theory [7]. N.Sourlas [7] have shown in 1989 that there is a mathematical equivalence of error-correcting codes to some theoretical spin-glass models. So, it is possible to use this equivalence with spin glasses, to study the properties of these codes using the methods of statistical mechanics developed in the study of disordered systems [7]. One of these methods is the Thouless-AndersonPalmer (TAP) [1] approach which is shown equivalent to the BP algorithm by Kabashima and al. [8]. The purpose of our work is to develop the log-likelihood ratios-Belief Propagation (LLR-BP) and the A-min decoding algorithm with the TAP approach.
