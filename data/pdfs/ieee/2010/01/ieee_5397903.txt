An Implementation-Friendly
Binary LDPC Decoding Algorithm
Guosen Yue and Xiaodong Wang
AbstractвҖ”We introduce an implementation-friendly binary
message-passing decoding method for low-density parity-check
(LDPC) codes that does not require the degree information
of variable nodes or degree dependent parameters. For hard-
decision decoding, given its low-complexity, the implementation
cost for variable node degree information is an important
consideration. We develop an estimation method for the extrinsic
error probability (EEP) as well as its analysis. The proposed
method offers similar performance as the existing methods for
time-invariant decoding in most cases, while it facilitates efficient
circuit implementations of the LDPC decoder.
Index TermsвҖ”LDPC codes, iterative binary decoding, time-
invariant decoding, time-variant decoding, code optimization,
circuit implementation.
I. INTRODUCTION
W
HEN Gallager first introduced the low-density parity-
check (LDPC) codes, two binary iterative decoding
algorithms were presented [1], i.e., the Gallager decoding
algorithm A (GA), and the Gallager decoding algorithm B
(GB) as named in [2]. The GA algorithm is a time-invariant
decoding algorithm, i.e., the parameters associated with the
algorithm do not change with the decoding iterations. It
is generalized to a majority-vote-based threshold decoding
algorithm in [3]. The majority-based decoding can be applied
to both regular codes [4], [5], [6] and irregular codes [7], [8].
On the other hand, the GB algorithm is a time-variant decoding
algorithm, where the majority-based decoding rule can still be
applied in every iteration, but the threshold value changes with
the iteration number during variable node process to minimize
the extrinsic error rate.
Although the binary LDPC decoding algorithm has the
performance loss compared to the soft decoding algorithm, it
facilitates very low implementation complexity and very high
decoding throughput in terms of hardware design [6], [9], [10].
However, for irregular codes, the common implementations
of the existing binary decoding algorithms require the degree
information of variable nodes. Such requirement can signif-
icantly increase the circuit level implementation complexity

As shown in [11], the degree information is not required
for the time-variant GB decoding algorithm. Due to the
superiority of time-invariant decoding algorithms over GB
decoding in terms of decoding time and robustness to the
imperfect knowledge of the channel [7], [8], in this paper, we
propose an implementation-friendly LDPC decoding principle
for time-invariant decoding that does not require the degree
information of variable nodes, leading to a significantly sim-
plified circuit implementation compared with existing binary
LDPC decoders. Although the proposed decoding method is
very simple, we show that with this new method, by jointly
optimizing the degree profiles and the flipping threshold, the
resulting codes under time-invariant decoding do not incur any
performance loss for most cases compared with the existing
decoders that require the degree information of the variable
nodes.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. In
Section II, we introduce our proposed decoder and discuss
the estimation and analysis of extrinsic error probability. In
Section III, we present the threshold optimizations for irregular
codes with the proposed time-invariant decoders. Section IV
contains the conclusions.

IV. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we have proposed an implementation-friendly
binary message-passing LDPC decoding method for time-
invariant decoding. In this method, the same flipping thresh-
old is used for decoding all variable nodes. Therefore, it
does not require the degree information during the decoding.
This feature facilitates efficient circuit implementation of the
LDPC decoder. We have also presented the EEP estimation,
EEP analysis, and code optimizations for the proposed time-
invariant decoder. It is seen that the proposed decoding method
for low-complexity circuit implementation does not incur any
loss in code performance.
