Abstract-In this paper, we propose an estimation method of a very low bit error rate (HER) of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. No analytical tool is available to evaluate performance of LDPC codes, and traditional Monte Carlo simulation methods can not estimate low HER of LDPC codes due to the limitation of time. Cole et al, show a fast frame error rate simulation method of LDPC codes by applying importance sampling (IS). At first, we extend Cole et aI.'s method to the HER estimation of LDPC codes. Since the simulation time of the HER evaluation version of the Cole et aI.'s method is proportional to the number of total dominant trapping sets used in the simulation, we should appropriately choose the dominant trapping sets to reduce the simulation time. To avoid the difficulty, we propose another simulation method, which selects a dominant trapping set as proportional to its upper bound. Additionally, we show some numerical examples to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. When we evaluate HER of 10- 16 , the simulation time of the proposed method is reduced to 1/6.6 of that of the HER evaluation version of the Cole et aI.'s method under the condition of the same accuracy of the estimator.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1], which are constructed by very sparse factor graphs, yield excellent error correction performance by iterative decoding methods using message passing algorithms such as Sum-Product algorithm. Many LDPC codes with moderate block lengths present an error floor which is almost flattened error performance as increase of signal to noise ratio (SNR) [2]. At the error floor region, the error performance of LDPC codes over a memoryless channel is almost determined by specific non codeword errors called trapping sets. The finite set of dominant trapping sets can approximate the error performance on the error floor region. When the block length of LDPC codes is moderate, which is order of hundreds to thousands, an error performance evaluation by the ordinary Monte Carlo simulation is almost impossible because of the necessity of enormous simulation time. For a certain application of LDPC codes, this causes difficulties to guarantee such very low error performance.
Some importance sampling (IS) [3] applications are proposed to reduce the evaluation time of LDPC codes [4]-[7]. The envelope method [4] can not directly estimate lower error probability since it needs to estimate the probability density function (PDF) of slightly lower SNR region of interests. In Ref. [5], the probability of fixed representative absorbing sets, which are similar to trapping sets, approximate the error

probability. The utilization of the representative absorbing sets can not theoretically avoid the underestimate of the error probability. The simulation time reduction by the classification of trapping sets [6] can not also give unbiased estimators. Cole et al. present a three-step frame error rate (FER) evaluation method which is constructed by the trapping set search, the error boundary search of the trapping set, and the IS simulation of the FER [7]. It guarantees unbiased estimators by normalizing with the sum of all simulation PDF of the dominant trapping sets.
In this paper, we propose a new fast BER simulation method of LDPC codes. The proposed method can effectively choose dominant trapping sets to prevent the increase of the number of simulation runs. The frequency of the utilization of a dominant trapping set is proportional to its probability, which is related to the upper bound calculated by the squared Euclidean distance between the correct signal and error boundary. The proposed simulation method also guarantees the unbiased estimator.
The rest of this paper is as follows; Section II shows background of LDPC codes and trapping set. The IS method to speedup the simulation is presented in Sec. III. Section IV presents the proposed evaluation method. We extend a fast FER simulation method proposed by Cole et al. [7] to BER estimation. We also propose a new simulation PDF design, which treats more trapping sets than the BER version of the Cole et al.'s method without increasing the simulation time. In Sec. V, some numerical examples are shown to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. Finally, Section VI concludes this paper.

VI. CONCLUSION
We have shown a fast bit error rate (BER) estimation method of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes .
