Attenuated Iterative Reliability-based Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes

Minghua Liu, Lijun Zhang
School of Electronic and Information Engineering Beijing Jiaotong University Beijing, China
Email: minghua0916@hotmail.com

Abstract—An attenuated iterative reliability-based majority-logic (AIML) decoding algorithm for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is proposed. This novel algorithm is devised based on the orthogonal check-sums of the one-step majority-logic decoding algorithm in conjunction with certain of reliability measures of the received symbols. The computation of reliability measure of the syndrome sum is refined by introducing an attenuation factor. Simulation results show that in binary input-additive white Gaussian noise channel, the AIML algorithm outperforms other popular iterative reliability-based majority-logic decoding algorithms with a slight increase in computational complexity. With maximum iterations five and fifty, the AIML algorithm can achieve almost identical error performance for LDPC codes. No error floor effect can be observed for AIML algorithm down to
the bit error rate (BER) of 10−8 , while error floor appears for
sum product algorithm (SPA) around the BER of 10−7 even with
maximum iteration 100. The inherent feature of parallel decoding for AIML algorithm enforces the decoding speed in contrast to those serial decoding schemes, such as weighted bitflipping algorithm.
Keywords- low-density parity-check codes; attenuation factor; reliability-based; iterative; majority-logic
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes are currently the most promising coding technique to achieve Shannon capacity for a wide range of channels. These codes were first discovered by Gallager in the early 1960s [1] and have been rediscovered and generalized [24]. Ever since the rediscovery, decoding of LDPC codes has received much attention in recent decade [5-8]. Decoding algorithms for LDPC codes can be roughly classified into three categories: hard-decision, soft-decision and hybrid decoding schemes including bit flipping (BF) algorithm [1], weighted BF (WBF) algorithm [5,9], sum-product algorithm (SPA) [1], etc. Soft-decision decoder retains all the information provided by the channel. Hard-decision decoder quantizes the received symbols into two levels, ones or zeros, discarding the channel information. It is the simplest decoding among the three categories of decoding for LDPC codes. However, its simplification results in a significant performance loss compared to soft-decision decoding. To bridge the performance gap between soft-decision decoding and harddecision decoding, hybrid decoding was proposed. Hybrid decoding is a reliability-based decoding in which the decoder

processes the quantized received symbols in the hard-decision decoder but also the reliability measures extracted from the received channel information. Performance/complexity tradeoff was achieved for many hybrid message-passing algorithms [5-8].
In this paper, we propose a novel algorithm by introducing an attenuation factor to the computation of reliability measures, called attenuated iterative reliability-based majority-logic (AIML) decoding algorithm. The rest of the paper is organized as follows. System description and OSMLG decoding are introduced in Section Ċ . Section ċ presents our AIML decoding in detail. Simulation results are reported in Section Č . Section čconcludes the paper.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
To verify our AIML decoding algorithm in terms of error performance, convergence speed and error floor, extensive simulations for many LDPC codes, including finite-geometry LDPC codes and Mackay’s randomly constructed LDPC codes are operated [10,14]. Allow for implementation, both weighting factor in IWBF and attenuation factor in AIML equal 0.5. No range limit of the reliability measures is considered for fairness in Chen’s WIML algorithm [6]. The range limit will degrade the decoding performance, though it is simple for implementation.
Performance demonstration of the (255,175) EG-LDPC codes with IMWBF algorithm [8], WIML algorithm [6], IML algorithm [7] and AIML algorithm is shown in Fig. 1. SPA is also included for reference [1]. The maximum iteration number is set to 50. As shown in Fig. 1, the advantage of our AIML algorithm is obvious. Furthermore, the (255,175) EGLDPC code with the WIML algorithm shows an error floor around the bit error rate (BER) of 10−5 , while no error floor
can be observed down to BER of 10−8 for the AIML algorithm. Since wireless communication systems need faster and more accurate LDPC decoders with lower implementation complexity and higher convergence speed, such as the fourth generation of mobile communication, satellite digital video and deep space communication. It is practical to design a decoder obtaining better error performance with less maximum iterations and lower decoding complexity. Fig. 2 shows the convergence speed of AIML algorithm. Maximum iterations 1, 2, 5, 10, 50 are considered. It can be seen that AIML converges very quickly and the error performance of the (255,175) cyclic EG-LDPC code decoded with 5 iterations is almost the same as that with 50 iterations. The error floor effect of Mackay’s (408,204) LDPC code [14] is disclosed in Fig. 3. The maximum iteration is set to 100 to ensure sufficient convergence of SPA. Clearly, the SPA is more susceptible to error floor in high SNR. Error floor arises for SPA below the BER of 10−7 , while no floor emerges till the
BER reaches 10−8 for the AIML algorithm.
V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we explore more accurate computation of reliability measure and present the AIML decoding. The proposed algorithm shows better error performance than currently vogue non-SPA decoding algorithms for LDPC codes with only a slight increase in complexity. Even with maximum iteration 5, the AIML decoding can provide almost the same performance as the one with fifty iterations, which can be attributed to its fast rate of convergence. This feature is very useful in high speed decoder. The inherent characteristic of parallel decoding for the AIML algorithm further enhances the decoding speed in contrast to such serial decoding algorithms as WBF and IMWBF [5,8-10]. In brief, the fast convergence behavior and parallel style of AIML help to find applications in the fields where high speed decoding is indispensable.
