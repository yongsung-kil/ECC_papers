Abstract—In this paper, we present the performance of the Belief Propagation-Based (BP-Based) algorithm as a function of the magnetization. Previous works, have shown that the BP algorithm, is equivalent to the Thouless- Anderson-Palmer(TAP) [1] approach, we develop the log-likelihood ratios-Belief Propagation (LLR-BP) algorithm and its simplification, the BP-Based algorithm using this method.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were first discovered by Gallager [2], in his thesis, in 1962 and have recently been rediscovered by Mackay and Neal [3], [4]. In fact, when LDPC codes have been invented, their decoding was too complicated for the technology, and so they have been forgotten. These codes can achieve very good performance when decoded with the belief-propagation (BP) algorithm [3]. This algorithm can also be simplified to the BP-Based algorithm [5], which greatly reduces the decoding complexity in implementation.
It is known, since the work of Sourlas [6], that there exists a close relationship between the statistical behavior of error correcting codes and the physics of some disordered spin models. Recently the tools developed in statistical physics have been employed in studying LDPC codes [7]. In [8] Kabashima and al. have shown that the BP algorithm is equivalent to Thouless-Anderson-Palmer (TAP) [1] approach.
In this paper, we develop the log-likelihood ratios-Belief Propagation (LLR-BP) and the BP-Based decoding algorithms with the TAP approach. Their performance is evaluated using a statistical physics parameter which is the magnetization.
This paper is organized as follows. Section II corresponds to a general description of the LDPC codes and their decoding algorithm. Section III describes the similarity between the BP algorithm and the TAP approach. In section IV, we develop the LLR-BP algorithm and its simplification the BPBased algorithm with the TAP approach. Finally and before concluding, simulation results are proposed in section V.
II. LDPC CODES AND DECODING ALGORITHM
A. Low-Density Parity Check Codes
Binary LDPC codes, are linear block codes defined by a sparse parity check matrix A(M × N ), where N denotes the codeword length and M the number of parity-check equations.

Using a notation similar to that in [3], [5], let L(μ) = {j/Aμj = 1} denote the set of bit j that participate in check μ. Similarly, we define the set of checks in which bit j participates as M (j) = {μ/Aμj = 1}. We denote a set L(μ) with bit j excluded by L(μ)\j, and a set M (j) with parity check μ excluded by M (j)\μ. Finally, s = (s1, ..., sN ) denotes the transmitted codeword.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have been interested in the decoding of LDPC codes from a statistical physics approach. First, we have examined the correspondence between LDPC codes and Ising spin systems. Then, the relation between the BP algorithm and TAP approach is investigated. Besides, we showed that LLRBP algorithm and its simplification i.e the BP-Based algorithm can be obtained using the TAP approach of Ising spin systems in statistical physics. We finally, showed by simulations that the BP-Based algorithm reduces the complexity for updating extrinsic information but there is a degradation in performance compared to the LLR-BP algorithm at low signal to noise ratio.
As a perspective of our work, we propose to study the replica method. This is a method from the statistical physics applied to find the magnetization. Besides, we propose to simplify the equations of this method by the same approximation in the BP-Based algorithm in order to compare the analytic results with the experimental ones.
