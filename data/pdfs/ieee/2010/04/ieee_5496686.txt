Efficient LDPC Decoder Implementation for DVB-S2 System

Chung-Jin Tsai and Mu-Chung Chen
Product Design Div. V Home Entertainment Business Unit
Sunplus Technology Co., Ltd.

Abstract — Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes have been adopted in many communication standards in recent years due to their Shannon limit approaching performance. More than that, many standards adopt long block length LDPC codes to achieve better performance. Calculation units are, therefore, increased significantly. Traditional simplified algorithm is not suitable for cutting edge transmission of high quality multimedia data because of the serious performance degradation. Better performance with minimum compensation architecture must be realized. This paper is to present the compensation architecture with minimum added circuits.
Index Terms — Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes, LDPC decoder, compensation architecture, normalization, early termination, shrinking.

I. INTRODUCTION
Recent years, sustained growth of demands for multimedia communication causes forward error correction (FEC) become more important in the future in order to provide stable received video stream. LDPC codes are linear block codes originally proposed by Gallager in 1962[1] and rediscovered by MacKay and Neal[2] in 1996, which have introduced remarkable error correcting capabilities near the Shannon’s limit. Until recent years, according to the advanced VLSI techniques, the implementation of LDPC in current application product has become more possible. Nonetheless, existed architectures of LDPC decoder usually occupy most area of a communication receiver due to the usage of huge memory storages. The number of elements need to be stored a LDPC decoder cannot be reduced and that leaves the reduction of calculation units is crucial. The cost of implementation of LDPC decoder with sum-product algorithm is too high due to complex computation. Although Mim-Sum required less hardware overheads, the performance degradation is too much as well. Many reduced complexity decoding algorithms are discussed in [4] and [5].
In our LDPC decoder, we choose min-sum algorithm as our decoding algorithm. The performance is improved with compensation methods and whose performance is approaching to sum-product algorithm. Furthermore, a method for reducing word-length is proposed for hardware implementation.
This paper is organized as follows: Part II, we introduce DVB-S2 LDPC codes. Part III, we briefly discussed the algorithms for decoding LDPC codes. Part IV, background of compensation circuits is provided and discussed. At last, we provide the simulation results and comparison.

B. Proposed Method:
Although applying dynamic normalization factor can significantly compensate the degradation caused by Min-Sum approximation, the costly computation of normalization factor ȕ at each beginning of iteration is not suitable for hardware implementation. Therefore, a more efficient manner for compensation is to fix the normalization factor throughout all iterations. The fixed ȕ can be obtained by averaging the computed ȕ in each iteration or directly using experimental result.
In the proposed method, two fixed normalization factors (ȕm1, ȕm2) is multiplied at the output for compensating two over-estimated by Min-Sum output. The check node output of Min-Sum processing has employed the two smallest input magnitudes, M1 and M2. These smallest two input magnitudes M1 and M2 are compensated by the normalization factors, ȕm1 and ȕm2, respectively. We obtained these two values through massive trials and errors. In Fig. 2, nine different normalization factor pairs are simulated and compared. From the simulation result, the pair of normalization factor, 0.75 and 0.875, is most suitable for code rate (1/2) in the normal frame (n=64800).
Although the fixed normalization factor can significantly reduce implementation cost but it also induces extra performance degradation due to undesired normalization on unsuitable iterations. It is because the normalization factor increases with iteration number and Signal to Noise Ratio (SNR) in the channel. In the other words, as the number of iteration increases, the normalization factor closer to 1. If the same fixed normalization factors are used throughout whole decoding process, at last several iterations the magnitude of CNP output which process by Min-Sum algorithm, might be over-compensated.

C. Hardware architecture:
Based on the normalization and termination method discussed above, the proposed check node processor architecture is shown in Fig. 4. In the sorting block, first and second minimum are obtained. The minimum data and second minimum data are normalized by Scale_m1 and Scale_m2, separately. Normalization process is early terminated with a switch, Scale_en. At the output, normalized output is combined with the outputs from sgn block. That concludes the CNP as desired.

IV. FIXED POINT IMPLEMENTATION ISSUE
In the proposed decoder architecture, the word length of passing message is 6-bits. In Fig. 6, the simulation shows the BER performance gap between floating point and fixed point simulation which is caused by insufficient finite word length. In our observation, the magnitude of passing message grows with number of iteration in iterative decoding. It will cause enormous degradation compared with the performance simulated by floating point.
Since above proposed decoding method adopts the concept of Min-Sum algorithm, it introduces a characteristic which is that in the same iteration all the bit node inputs can be multiplied by a constant without any loss of performance. According to the characteristic of the proposed decoding scheme, clipping problem can be solved by multiplying a shrinking factor at all of check node processor output and initial LLR as shown in Fig. 5. According to the experimental result, the shrinking factor is 0.96875. In Fig. 6, the simulation performance shows that the proposed shrink scheme can effectively conquer clipping problem without adding extra bits and very close to the performance of floating point.

V. SIMULATION RESULTS
The LDPC codes of normal frame (n=64800) defined in DVB-S2 are simulated. All the simulations are simulated more than 1000 frames for each point over AWGN channels. Fig. 7 shows the simulation result of Sum-Product algorithm, Min-Sum algorithm and proposed decoding algorithm for code rate equals to 3/5 and 8 Phase-Shift Keying (8PSK) modulation. The Sum-product algorithm is simulated as the baseline performance which implies the performance bound. The decoding performance of Min-Sum algorithm has tremendous degradation from the performance of Sum-Product. It will not be tolerated in current high quality transmission demands. This paper proposed methods to employ efficient compensation method in order to approach the performance baseline. Fig. 7, t shows that the proposed decoding algorithm can significantly improve the performance of original Min-Sum algorithm about 0.9dB at most in code rate 3/5 8PSK case.

VI. CONCLUSION
The aim of this paper was to complete an efficient LDPC decoding algorithm for hardware implementation. Simulation results have shown the proposed LDPC code decoding algorithm which has effectively achieved significant improvement and left little performance degradation compared to the optimal Sum-Product algorithm. The proposed LDPC decoder architecture combined normalization, early termination and shrinking schemes together to achieve tolerable performance loss at fewest hardware cost.
