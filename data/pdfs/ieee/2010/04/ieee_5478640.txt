2010 17th International Conference on Telecommunications

An Incremental Redundancy Short Length LDPC Codes Based on Random Construction Techniques

Shereen A M. Baghdady, Yasmine A. Fahmy and Madgi M. S. El-Soudani
Electronics and Communication dept. Cairo University Cairo, Egypt

Abstract—In this paper, a powerful class of irregular low-density parity check (LDPC) codes based on random construction techniques is presented. The resultant codes have accurate profile with the additional features of making them suitable for incremental redundancy systems. The proposed codes also have low encoding complexity and can be decoded efficiently. Moreover, these codes show better puncturing performance than irregular LDPC codes obtained by other techniques over a wide range of code rates (0.6~0.9) and become more distinctive at higher rates.
Keywords: LDPC codes; IR-HARQ; Random Construction techniques.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, firstly introduced by Gallager [1], form a subclass of linear block codes. In general, LDPC codes are linear block codes which can be represented by sparse parity check matrices. Due to their capacity-achieving performance, they were recently adopted by different standards such as Digital Video Broadcasting Standard DVBS2[2], WiMAX Standard, IEEE 802.16e[3] and WiFi Standard IEEE 802.11n [4],[5]. In contrary to Turbo codes, they do not have explicit error floor and may have a linear decoding complexity using the so-called Belief Propagation (BP) or sum-product algorithms[6]. However these standards consider the use of link adaptation based on Adaptive Modulation and Coding (ACM) and Hybrid Automatic Repeat reQuest (HARQ). This requires the design of various LDPC code rates and coded block lengths with uniform encoding/decoding structure to be used.
Two steps are generally required to construct Irregular LDPC codes. First, a profile that describes the desired number of columns (rows) of each weight in the parity-check matrix H is calculated, hence the code’s general performance is known. Second, H is constructed to achieve the given profile. Random Construction Techniques (RCT) [7],[8] is based on random sampling from appropriate degree distribution ensemble by adding column satisfying the column degree sequentially in empty matrix taking into consideration certain design constraints such as the avoidance of length -4 cycles.
Unfortunately, in short block length, the desired accurate profile cannot be achieved because the row weights are not controlled. In [9], an Accurate Random Construction Techniques (ARCT) that forced the matrix to have a specific row weights is introduced. This method guarantees the desired

profile. To speed up this process, the authors suggested another technique called Speed-Up Technique or SUT that simply starts the H matrix construction from an initial H to speed up the construction process.
Although this method provides an accurate profile, it can not provide a reasonable encoding complexity, efficient decoding processes and rate compatible properties to be applied in wireless systems.
Efficiently-Encodable Rate-Compatible (E2RC) codes are presented in [10],[11]. They are Rate-compatible punctured codes (RCPC) so the codes with higher rates are embedded in all codes with lower rates. They have a linear-time encoder and good performance under puncturing for a wide range of rates but suffer from the drawback of RCP-LDPC codes that is the slow convergence of the Believe Propagation (BP) decoding compared to un-punctured codes. A larger number of iterations is needed to estimate the punctured bits. A trade-off between the number of iterations and the bit error rate (BER) performance is needed. A Layered BP algorithm is a modified BP algorithm that accelerates the decoding convergence and reduces the required number of iterations for RCP-LDPC codes was introduced in [12].
In this paper, a powerful class of codes based on the SUT having an accurate profile is generated with the additional features of making them suitable for new wireless system demand of HARQ by integrating the SUT with E2RC. This results in a good performance and linear -time encoding codes. Layered BP algorithm is used to maintain an efficient decoding process.
This paper is organized as following, definitions and notations are given in section 2. In section 3, we present the proposed algorithm. In section 4, simulation results are presented. Finally, section 5 concludes the paper.

III. THE PROPOSED ALGORITHM
The proposed construction technique here by named as Random Compatible Random Construction (2RC) algorithm depends on two previous techniques: ARCT introduced in [9] and E2RC codes construction algorithm of [10]. E2RC codes are systematic rate-compatible puncturing codes based on the puncturing algorithm [14], [15] in the following sub-sections the rate-compatible puncturing algorithm will be shortly explained afterward the 2RC algorithm is presented.

B. Rate Compatible Random Construction algorithm
The 2RC algorithm divides the parity check matrix to systematic H1 and non-systematic H2 parts. The non-systematic is constructed by E2RC algorithm. The SUT, with a modified supplier, is applied to the systematic part to get an accurate profile.
After the creation of H2 using the E2RC algorithm, high degrees appear in some rows. To prevent the distribution from being far from the desired one and getting performance degradation, we should re-calculate the new row indexes that can be in the modified supplier u and remove the degree assigned to H2.

Finally, we studied the performance of the obtained parity check matrix in the IRARQ System. Here, an appropriate number of parity bits are sent incrementally by the transmitter depending on the rate requirement. The receiver decodes by treating the parity bits that are not transmitted (called punctured bits) as erasures. Thus, if the receiver fails to decode at a particular rate, it only needs to request additional parity bits from the transmitter [16].
The throughput performance of incremental redundancy (IR) hybrid automatic repeat request (HARQ) systems highly depends on the performance of high-rate codes. V. CONCLUSION
We have proposed a new class of codes based on SUT technique and E2RC algorithm, which has several advantages. The codes have an accurate profile as near as possible resulting in generally good performance, provide a wide range of code rates in a rate-compatible fashion, and have efficient encoder and simple decoder. The proposed codes 2RC show better puncturing performance than irregular LDPC codes obtained by other techniques over a wide range of code rates (0.6~0.9) and become more distinctive at higher rates. For example at a code rate of 0.8 and BER of 10-5 the 2RC achieves more than 2.8 dB gain. We verify also by simulation that the 2RC show better throughput performance in IR-HARQ systems over timevarying channels. These advantages allow LDPC to be used IR-HARQ wireless communication systems.
