A General Class of LDPC Finite Geometry Codes and their Performance
Jun Xu, Heng Tang, Yu Kou, Shu Lin, a n d Khaled Abdel-Ghaffar Dept. Electrical and Computer Engineering, UC Davis Davis, CA 95616, USA
{junxu,hngtang,ykou,shulin,ghaffar)Qece.ucdavis.edu

Abstract - We present a general class of finite geometry LDPC codes which perform well with iterative decoding although their Tanner graphs may contain many cycles of length 4. A hybrid two-stage decoding algorithm is proposed that combines iterative and multi-step majority-logic decodings to achieve good performance with low decoding complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
Interest in low-density-parity-check (LDPC) codes, first introduced by Gallager in 1962 [l],has increased dramatically in the last five years due to their near Shannon-limit performance when decoded iteratively. Recently, Kou, Lin, and Fossorier [2] discovered a class of LDPC codes, based on lines and points of finite geometries. This class of finite geometry LDPC codes performs well with the sum-product algorithm (SPA). Actually, this is the first class of Shannon-limit approaching codes with algebraic, rather than random, construction. More recently, the Kou-Lin-Fossorier construction of LDPC codes has been generalized by Lin, Tang and Kou [4]. This new construction of LDPC codes is based on flats of two consecutive dimensions. The Tanner graphs [5] of these codes do not contain cycles of length 4. It is a general belief that codes with short cycles, and in particular cycles of length 4, perform poorly with SPA. In the presentation of this paper, we demonstrate that finite geometry codes with millions of cycles of length 4 in their Tanner graphs perform surprisingly good with SPA. The construction of these finite geometry codes is based on flats in finite geometries. We also present a hybrid two-stage iterative/hard-decision decoding technique for these codes.

IV. HYBRIDDECODING
With SPA, cycles of length 4 result in decoding correlation only after two iterations. This correlation may be stopped by using a hybrid two-stage decoding. At the first stage, SPA is performed for only two iterations. At the end of the second decoding iteration, hard-decisions of code symbols are made and the resulting sequence is decoded at the second stage using a hard-decision decoding algorithm suitable for the code. For finite geometry codes, multi-step majority-logic decoding is performed at the second stage. Significant reduction in decoding complexity is achieved using hybrid decoding compared to SPA decoding with very little performance loss as shown based on simulation results.
