IV. PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS WITH DENSITYEVOLUTION

Analysis using density evolution involves evaluating the probability density functions (pdfs) of the messages exchanged between the nodes of the factor graph [I], given the pdfs of the initial messages. To apply density evolution, the all-zero codeword is assumed to have been transmitted. This assumption is not restrictive since the fading channel considered herein satisfies the channel symmetry condition f(Zblak = 0) = f(-Zklak = 1). In addition, for the decoding algorithms described in Section 111, the channel constraint node preserves symmetry since a flip in sign of Z k results in a flip in sign of p k at each iteration.
Under the standard assumptions of large girth (compared to the iteration number), the neighborhood of a graph is essentially a tree. In this case, all messages passed in the factor graph are independent and all calculated pdfs are exact. In the practical situation where cycles are present in the graph, it was shown in [ I ] that the averagebehavior ofthe code convergesto the cycle-free case as the length ofthe code increases.
It is observed that for all algorithms (except the QDF algorithm) described in Section 111, the messages from the channel constraint nodes do not change with iterations. Thus, in order to perform density evolution iti'these cases,, it suffices to evaluate the initial message densities and follow the standard pdf transformations described in [I] to trace the pdfs of the messages exchanged in the code portion of the factor graph.
