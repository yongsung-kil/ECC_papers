Motivation ' Various partial response maximum likelihood (PRML) systems and iterative decoding systems have been proposed for perpendicular magnetic recording channel. Long PR target such as PR(1,2,3,2,1)ML increases hardware complexity exponentially as density increases. Dual decision feedback equalization (DDFE) detector has a comparable performance to PR(1,2,3,2,1)ML system with very low complexity at normalized recording density 3 and 4 when a rate 16/17 maximum transition run (3,ll) code is used. Furthermore, we propose advanced version of DDFE (ADDFE) that outperforms PR(1,2,3,2,1)ML system with lower hardware complexity.

REGULAR LATTICE LDPC CODES IN PERPENDICULAR MAGNETIC RECORDING

Bane Vasic, Erozan M. Kurtas * and Alexander V. Kuznetsov *

Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Arizona, Tucson 8.5721
* Seagate Technology, Pittsburgh, PA 15203

It has been well recognized that random Low-Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes perform close to the theoretical limit. However, iterative decoders employed for their decoding have very high complexity and are incapable of operating in the >1 Gbps regime, expected data rates of the next generation of magnetic recording channels. Efficient regular LDPC codes were proposed in [l]. These codes are based on a Balanced Incomplete Block Designs (BIBD), known as Steiner systems, and have reduced complexity of the LDPC encoders. In this paper, using integer lattices we introduce a new class of well structured LDPC codes that have high rates, low hardware complexity, and investigate their performance in the perpendicular magnetic recording channels.

Lattice construction. A BIBD is a pair (V,B), where Vis a set of v points, and B is a collection of b blocks (block is a subset of k points), such that each point of Vis contained in exactly r blocks and any 2-subset of Vis contained in /z blocks.
