Introduction
The knowledge on the domain structure of ferro- as well as antiferromagnetic surfaces is essential for the understanding of many physical effects as,e.g., the exchange-bias effect and exchange-coupling in magnetic multilayers.
However, especially the investigation of antiferromagnets is difficult because of the lack of a macroscopic magnetization. Spin-polarized scanning tunneling microscopy/spectroscopy (SP-STM/STS) USing magnetic probe tips has long been considered as a promising tool in order to study magnetic properties down to the atomic scale.

Introduction

Low-density parity check (LDPC) codes are receiving considerable attention as a next-generation

coding technique for magnetic recording channels[13,because they show a 5dB gain over the con-

ventional PRML method. The gain, however, reduces to 1-1.5dB if the code is combined with an

error correcting code (ECC). After the LDPC code, the ECC is not effective in the sense that its

gain is less than the rate loss. In fact, we found that ECC-less LDPC codes perform better than

LDPC codes with ECC. However, it is still difficult to avoid use of the ECC since the iterative

decoder is unable to correct erasures caused by thermal asperity (TA) or media defects. This paper

presents a method of erasure compensation for the iterative decoder. The method enables the use

of ECC-less LDPC codes, which leads to better performance than using LDPC codes with ECC.
