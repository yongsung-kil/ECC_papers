Abstract - This paper presents a method for constructing low density parity check (LDPC) codes based on a special type of combinatoric designs, known as t h e balanced incomplete block designs (BIBD’s). Several classes of BIBD’s suitable for constructing LDPC codes are presented. Codes constructed based on these classes of BIBD’s perform well with iterative decoding

IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we presented a method for constructing LDPC codes based on BIBD’s with X = 1. The Tanner graphs of these BIBD-LDPC codes do not contain cycles of length 4 and they perform well with the SPA decoding. Furthermore, most of these codes are quasi-cyclic and hence their encoding can be implemented with linear shift registers. New LDPC codes can be constructed by row splitting or column deletion
of their parity check matrices [4] . For a BIBD with X = 2, the
code given by the null space of QT contains cycles of length 4 in its Tanner graph. However, preliminary studies showed that the performance with SPA decoding is good. For example code (876, 804) has X = 2 and performs 1.43 dB from Shannon limit at BER
