Abstract - We investigate the property of iterative decoding for finite length codes’, and give some insights on the suboptimality of iterative decoding due to the cycles in the graph representation of the codes, for several different types of iteratively decodable codes.
I. SUMMARY
It is now known that the properties of decoders used in the iterative decoding for low-density parity-check (LDPC) and turbo-like codes define the associated performance threshold for infinite codeword length. However, the effect of inevitable cycles in the graph representation of finite length codes are not well understood yet. In this paper, we show that the characteristics of constituent decoders significantly affects the trajectory of iterative decoding for finite length codes as well, and eventually defines different nature of suboptimality for each code.
We first apply the reliability-based iterative decoding algorithm [1] to LDPC and turbo-like codes. This approach
utilizes the ordered statistics decoding (OSD) [a] at each it-
eration of iterative decoding, by regarding the a posteriori probabilities as reliability information. The most likely candidate codeword with respect t o the received signal vector is chosen as the final estimate. Due to the reliability-based nature, the additional coding gain obtained is a nice indication on the closeness of the decoding trajectory to the correct codeword. Three rate-1/2 codes of length 504 are examined: a turbo code (PCCC) with 16-state recursive systematic convolutional (RSC) constituent encoders (23/35), a serially concatenated code (SCCC) composed of the four-state RSC outer encoder (7/1) and rate-1 accumulator inner code, and a regular (3,6)-LDPC code of girth six. The word error rate (WER) of iterative decoding and reliability-based iterative decoding are shown in Fig. 1. The number of iterations are selected so that further processing provides little performance improvement. It is first observed that with conventional iterative decoding the PCCC shows the best performance with the least number of iterations while the LDPC code performs a little worse and requires many iterations, the SCCC coming in between. On the other hand, the additional gain obtained by the reliability-based approach is the largest for the LDPC code, the smallest for the PCCC and again the SCCC falls between them. We explain these facts by the input/output behavior of constituent decoders and their interaction. Roughly speaking, the fast convergence on average for the PCCC implies a large typical divergence from the ideal decoding trajectory when iterative decoding fails due t o correlation values introduced by the short cycles; on the other hand, for LDPC codes, the

The unified view given above explains the effect of constituent code choice, code rate, and channel conditions on the potential additional gain, and accordingly the suboptimality of iterative decoding. For PCCC’s or SCCC’s, the use of constituent codes with large free distance in general provides relatively poor performance of iterative decoding. Moreover, the additional coding gain due to the reliability-based iterative decoding for those codes is often small. This observation further stresses the distinction t o be made between “good codes for optimum decoding” and “good codes for iterative decoding”. Further details of this paper are given in [4].
