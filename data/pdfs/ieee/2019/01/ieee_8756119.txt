Optimization of QC-LDPC Codes by Edge Exchange Method Based on ACE
Xin Qin, Chuanchuan Yang , Senior Member, IEEE, Ziyuan Zheng, and Ziyu Wang

Abstract— Long quasi-cyclic low-density parity check (QC-LDPC) codes have been chosen as one of the forward error correction (FEC) schemes in passive optical network (PON) systems benefiting from the excellent performance. About how to get the appropriate LDPC codes, some traditional methods which focus on obtaining large girth or flat approximated cycle extrinsic message degree (ACE) spectrums are proposed for the design of LDPC codes. These methods which may still make the existence of small stopping sets containing short cycles probably are not so useful for long QC-LDPC codes. Recently, the parallel vector message passing (PMP) algorithm oriented-to decreasing the number of short cycles is proved to be an effective method to optimize LDPC codes. Since ignoring short cycles with poor connectivity which can induce error floor, this method cannot guarantee its effectiveness for long QC-LDPC codes. In this letter, an edge exchange optimization method based on the global properties of ACE and girth called EEA method is proposed, which tries to reduce cycles with low ACE in the order of cycle length. The numerical results prove that our EEA method can bring the improvements of error floor up to two orders of magnitude to long QC-LDPC codes and obviously outperforms PMP algorithm.
Index Terms— Quasi-cyclic low-density parity check (QC-LDPC) codes, passive optical network (PON), approximated cycle extrinsic message degree (ACE).
I. INTRODUCTION
T O REACH error-free transmission, forward error correction (FEC) is an unavoidable technique for optical fiber communication systems. To be specific, for passive optical network (PON) systems, the traditional FEC codes employed are mainly the Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) codes and the Reed-Solomon (RS) codes [1]–[3]. Recently, many attentions have been paid to low-density parity check (LDPC) codes which have excellent performance approaching Shannon limit. In particular, quasi-cyclic low-density parity check (QC-LDPC) codes are suitable for PON systems benefiting from the low complexity and simple hardwareimplementation. In order to get appropriate QC-LDPC codes, the optimization method for QC-LDPC codes is a promising area of research. As is known, the length of the shortest cycles in a Tanner graph defined as girth is a simple metric to evaluate the performance of LDPC codes [5]. Because of ignoring the connectivity of Tanner graphs, the methods [6] pursuing large girth are difficult to achieve good performance in optimizing QC-LDPC codes. Since some short cycles with good connectivity which have sufficient impinging message flow to correct errors should be allowed in the Tanner graph while some short cycles with poor connectivity which are vulnerable to channel noise should be precluded. The approximated cycle extrinsic message degree (ACE) spectrum which describes the connectivity of cycles is demonstrated to be a useful metric to evaluate the performance of LDPC codes [7]. Based on this theory, some optimization methods pursuing flat ACE spectrums are proposed in [8]. Nevertheless, the codes optimized by these methods have some small stopping sets containing short cycles which can result in error floor. Recently, the parallel vector message passing (PMP) algorithm oriented to decreasing the number of short cycles or increasing the length of short cycles is proved to be an effective method to optimize QC-LDPC codes [9]. However, short cycles with low ACE values which deteriorate bit-error rate (BER) performance are allowed in PMP algorithm.
In this letter, an edge exchange method called EEA based on girth and properties of ACE is proposed to optimize QC-LDPC codes. The EEA method reduces short cycles with low ACE which can cause error floor. By optimizing girth and global ACE spectrum related parameters in turn, the proposed EEA method can bring better BER performance to long QC-LDPC codes compared with the PMP algorithm.

B. The Proposed EEA Method
There is little leeway to improve girth of long LDPC codes. Therefore, just utilizing the girth metric is difficult to bring an effective optimization to long QC-LDPC codes. Since not all short cycles are equally harmful, selective treatments of more harmful cycles allow us to do cycle conditioning effectively [10]. Some short cycles with low ACE are more possible to deteriorate the performance of QC-LDPC codes. Accordingly, increasing the minimum ACE values of short cycles, produces LDPC codes with lower error floors. The optimization direction of our proposed EEA method is adjusting each wr,b, i.e. exchanging edges to increase girth, the elements in ACE spectrum and decrease the elements in number spectrum of ACE by turn. Particularly, zero elements in W are not adjusted to keep the code parameters such as the code length, code rate and degree distribution unchanged.

III. NUMERICAL RESULTS
The performance improvements of QC-LDPC codes brought by PMP algorithm and our EEA method are compared in this section. The Monte-Carlo simulation is used to estimate BER performance of QC-LDPC codes, assuming binary phase shift keying (BPSK) modulated transmission over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. The high-throughput multicore LDPC decoders with Horizontal Turbo-Decoding Message Passing (TDMP) Min-Sum decoding algorithm proposed in [12] are used in our simulation platform. At least 100 frame errors are obtained for calculation of low BER points and at least 10 frame errors are obtained for calculation of high BER points.
In order to prove the validity of our proposed EEA method, three long QC-LDPC codes with code rates greater than 0.8 which meet the requirement of high code rate for PON systems [2] are optimized with the PMP method and our EEA method respectively. IV. CONCLUSION
In this letter, EEA method based on girth and global ACE properties is proposed to optimize QC-LDPC codes. The target of EEA method is reducing or eliminating the short cycles with low ACE which cause error floor. By optimizing three LDPC codes, it can be proved that the proposed EEA method can bring better BER performance to long QC-LDPC codes compared with the PMP algorithm and make them meet the requirement of post-FEC BER for PON systems.
