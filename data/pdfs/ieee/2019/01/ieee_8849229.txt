Hamming Distance Distribution of the 0-reprocessing Estimate of the Ordered Statistic
Decoder

Chentao Yue, Mahyar Shirvanimoghaddam, Yonghui Li, Branka Vucetic School of Electrical and Information Engineering, The University of Sydney, NSW, Australia
{chentao.yue, mahyar.shm, yonghui.li, branka.vucetic}@sydney.edu.au

Abstract—In this paper, we derive the distribution of the Hamming distance at 0-reprocessing of the ordered statistics decoding (OSD). With the assumption of decoding a random linear block code, we first find the distribution of the number of errors in any partition of the ordered channel output sequence. Then the distribution of the Hamming distance after 0-reprocessing is derived by a mixture model of two random variables. Based on the proposed statistical approach, we outline the design of high-efficiency OSD algorithm. Simulation and numerical results show that our proposed statistical approaches accurately describe the Hamming distance distributions in OSD decoding process.
I. INTRODUCTION
Since 1948 when Shannon introduced the notion of channel capacity [1], researchers have been looking for powerful channel codes that can approach this limit. Low density parity check (LDPC) and Turbo codes have been shown to perform very closely to the Shannon’s limit at large block lengths and widely applied in the 3rd and 4th generations of mobile communications [2]. Polar code proposed by Arikan in 2008 [3] has attracted much attention in the last decade and has been chosen as one of the standard coding schemes for the 5th generation (5G) of mobile communications. Polar codes take advantage of a simple successive cancellation decoder which is optimal in an asymptotically large block length. For short codes, it is however sub-optimal [4].
Short code design and the related decoding algorithms have rekindled a great deal of interest among industry and academia recently [5]–[7]. This is because 5G has put forward the demand for ultra-reliable and low latency communications (URLLC). For URLLC, reducing the latency mandates the use of short block-length codes in bandwidth limited scenarios and conventionally designed moderate/long codes are not suitable [4].
Ordered statistics decoding (OSD) was proposed in 1995 as an approximate maximum likelihood (ML) decoder for block codes [8]. For a linear block code with minimum distance dH , it is proven that an OSD with order m = dH /4−1 is asymptotically optimum [8]. However, the decoding complexity is the main drawback of OSD, as the overall complexity of an order-m decoding can be up to O(Km). Much previous work has focused on improving OSD in terms of efficiency and some remarkable progresses have been achieved [9]–[15]. Recently with the demand of URLLC, OSD has aroused interests again

due to its potential to be an universal decoding algorithm for short block length codes [16]–[18].
Many of these approaches utilize the distance (either Hamming or weighted Hamming distance) in designing various conditions to reduce the decoding complexity. The Box-andMatch algorithm [11] greatly reduces the size of the candidates list by designing the matching technique and a distancebased optimal condition. The distances between sub-test error patterns and received signals are calculated as a reprocessing rule to improve decoding efficiency in [13]. Skipping and stopping rules based on distance were used in [13] and [14] to abandon unpromising candidates. Also, a fast OSD algorithm combining stopping rules from [14] and sufficient conditions from [15] was proposed in [18]. Despite the improvements in decoding complexity, these algorithms lack the full analysis of performance and complexity.
In this paper, we derive the distribution of Hamming distance in the 0-reprocessing of OSD, which can be easily extended to any order i-reprocessing by considering the ordered discrete statistics. Apart from the weighted Hamming distance (WHD) or Euclidean distance (ED), it is reasonable to analyze the distribution of Hamming distance in OSD first, because the distribution of WHD or ED can be derived from the Hamming distance by further combining the channel output reliability. We verify that the distribution of Hamming distance can be described by a mixed model of two random variables representing the number of errors and the code weight enumerator, respectively, and the weight of the mixture is determined by the channel conditions. Furthermore, the proposed statistical approach for Hamming distance can highlight the properties of OSD and pave the path to evidence-based design of efficient decoders. Simulation and numerical results show that our proposed statistical approach can describe the distribution of 0-reprocessing Hamming distance accurately.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II describes preliminaries. In Section III, the statistical approach is described and the distribution of Hamming distance is derived. Simulation results and some discussions are presented in Section IV. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.

C. Decoder Design based on Distance Distribution
Based on the property highlig hted by the distance distribution, here we outline the design of an efficient OSD decoder utilizing distance statistics. Generally, the improvements of reprocessing efficiency can be introduced by applying the sufficient condition (SC) and necessary condition (NC). Once the distribution of distance is clearly obtained, the two conditions can be precisely designed.
Assume that the distribution of the Hamming distance in i-reprocessing is known, and the distribution of WHD is accordingly derived by combining channel output reliabilities. For the NC, the decoder can compare the minimum distance after each re-encoding with the probability mass function and terminate the decoding immediately if the minimum distance is less than a predetermined boundary. The success probability of the NC can be accurately determined from the weights of two random variables. For the SC, given one specific TEP, the distance after re-encoding can be evaluated and accordingly the probability that it is less than the current minimum distance can be calculated. Further decisions of discarding TEPs are made accordingly. We refer to this decoder as the segmentation discarding OSD algorithm. Please refer to [22] for further details.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we investigated the distribution of Hamming distance in the 0-reprocessing of OSD. The distribution can be described by the mixture model of two random variables representing error numbers and code weight spectrum, respectively. Using the proposed statistical approach, the analysis can be easily extended to the case of any order i-reprocessing. Numerical and simulation results show that our approach describes the Hamming distance tightly. Accordingly, we proposed a segmentation-discarding OSD algorithm, which

reduces the complexity of the OSD algorithm without a
noticeable performance degradation.
