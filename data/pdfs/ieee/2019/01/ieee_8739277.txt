The Method of Predicting Retention Threshold Voltage Distribution for NAND Flash Memory Based
on Back-Propagation Neural Network

Kunliang Wang1, Gang Du1,*, Zhiyuan Lun2 and Xiaoyan Liu1,*
1Institute of Microelectronics, Peking University, Beijing 100871, China 2Hisilicon Technologies Co., Ltd., Hangzhou, China E-mails: gangdu@pku.edu.cn; xyliu@ime.pku.edu.cn

Abstract-A new retention threshold voltage (Vth) distributions prediction method for 3-D TLC and QLC NAND flash memories based on back-propagation neural network (BpNN) is proposed. The data pre-processing and post-processing techniques are developed to improve the prediction accuracy of BpNN model. Based on our proposed BpNN model, the predicted Vth distributions after different data retention times (t) and program/erase (P/E) cycles have good agreement with the measurements, which can be used for read voltage optimization (RVO) not only hard-decision decoding algorithm but also softdecision decoding algorithm. Especially, this BpNN model can be embedded into the SSD controller and help in improving the reliability of NAND flash memory.
Keywords-Vth distribution, retention, NAND flash memory, neural network, read voltage optimization
I. INTRODUCTION
Recent years, NAND flash memory has been the prevailing a choice for solid-state drives (SSD) application due to lower cost per bit and higher performance [1]. However, as NAND flash memory density increases, higher bit error rates (BER) are introduced by various device and circuit level noises [2], such as program interference noise, read disturbance noise, P/E cycling noise and data retention noise, which significantly degrade NAND flash reliability. Among all the noise sources, data retention noise is a dominant factor for NAND flash memory errors [3], especially for multi-bit storage technologies, such as: triple-level program cell (TLC) and quad-level program cell (QLC) [4].
In order to improve the reliability of NAND flash memory, read voltage optimization [5-6] based on hard-decision decoding and soft-decision decoding algorithms are adopted in the SSD controller. The accuracy Vth distributions of NAND flash memory are essential to kinds of read voltage optimization method [5-6]. However, it takes a lot of time to obtain the retention Vth distributions based on the experimental results. Modeling the Vth distribution of NAND flash memory can make the process more effective and efficient. But, it should be mentioned that the evolution of Vth distribution during data retention operation results from the effect of multiple variability sources in NAND flash memory and operation modes. It is difficult to separate the effect of different variability sources in NAND flash memory, such as: electron intrinsic fluctuation [7-8], electron numbers fluctuation [9], device parameters fluctuation [10] and data patterns fluctuation [11], and establish corresponding physical models. In addition,

NAND flash memory has multiple operation modes, such as: data retention times (t), operation temperatures (T) and P/E cycles (p). It is an important challenge to predict the retention Vth distribution considering all the variability sources in NAND flash memory and arbitrary operation modes.
To solve this problem, a new retention Vth distribution prediction method based on BpNN is proposed. The data preprocessing and post-processing techniques are developed to improve the prediction accuracy of BpNN model. Based on our proposed BpNN model, the predicted Vth distributions after different data retention times (t) and P/E cycles (p) have good agreements with the experimental results, which can be used for read voltage optimization not only hard-decision decoding algorithm but also soft-decision decoding algorithm. Especially, this BpNN model can be embedded into the SSD controller and help in improving the reliability of 3-D TLC and QLC NAND flash memories.

In order to correct bit errors and improve the lifetime of NAND flash memory, several error correction codes (ECCs), such as BCH (BoseтАУChaudhuriтАУHocquenghem) and LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) are common used in the SSD controller. However, the decoding efficiency strongly depends

on the read voltage position. Therefore, for hard-decision decoding, read voltage control (RVC) [12] and valley tracking technology (VTT) [3] are proposed as shown in Fig. 4 (a). Similarly, for soft-decision decoding, we always want to sense the crossover region between two adjacent states with a higher precision and sense the main component with less sensing operations or even no sensing operations as shown in Fig. 4 (b) no matter voltage entropy (VE) based [5] or maximizing mutual information (MMI) based [6] read voltage optimization methods. Thus, modeling the retention Vth distribution is of great significance, which can provide the optimized read voltage position (Vopt) [5-6], reduce read latency and improve the performance of SSD. Here, a universal Vth distribution prediction method based BpNN is developed.

Fig. 7 shows the retention Vth distribution after 4.8h data retention times at 8K P/E cycles after using data preprocessing. From the figures we can find that the variability is suppressed for small data region, unfortunately, a few ripples inevitably appear due to the inherent property of BpNN. Thus, two filtering algorithms (maximum based and increment based) are adopted to eliminate the effect of ripples. Fig. 8 (a) shows the principle of maximum based boundary detection algorithm in this work. A filter kernel (1 f) is defined, and we make f equal to 10 in this work. First, we shift the filter from left to right (increase r) to cover the output matrix, and find the maximum of them. If the maximum reach a pre-defined threshold (Nth, it is 2 in this work), stop and the left boundary is r at this time. Then, we shift the filter from right to left (not shown here), and we can get the right boundary of the Vth distribution. The final extracted boundaries are shown in Fig. 8 (b).

IV. CONCLUSIONS This work proposes a new retention Vth distributions prediction method for NAND flash memory based on BpNN model. Based on our proposed BpNN model, the predicted Vth distributions after different data retention times (t) and P/E cycles (p) have good agreement with the measurements, which can be used for read voltage optimization.
