Decoding Binary Linear Codes Using Penalty Dual Decomposition Method
Ming-Min Zhao , Qingjiang Shi , Yunlong Cai , Min-Jian Zhao, and Quan Yu

Abstract— In this letter, we utilize the penalty dual decomposition (PDD) framework and develop a novel PDD decoding algorithm for binary linear codes. Instead of relaxing the discrete constraints to continuous ones, we take an alternative by transforming them into equivalent equality constraints. This idea leads to a double-loop parallel algorithm: In the outer loop, we update the dual variables and certain penalty parameters, while in the inner loop, we divide the primal variables into several blocks and employ the block successive upper-bound minimization method to iteratively optimize each block variable in closed form. Every limit point generated by the proposed algorithm is guaranteed to be a stationary point of the maximum likelihood decoding problem. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm shows great error rate performance at both low and high signal-to-noise ratios.
Index Terms— Iterative decoding, binary linear codes, penalty dual decomposition, decomposition method.
I. INTRODUCTION
L INEAR Programming (LP) is an important decoding technique for binary linear codes [1], [2]. This method is based on LP relaxation of the original maximum likelihood (ML) decoding problem and it can provide analytical interpretations on convergence, complexity, and correctness of decoding without limitation on the sparsity of the paritycheck (PC) matrix. Due to these advantages, LP decoding has been intensively studied in the literature especially for decoding low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1]–[3]. However, it also comes with two major drawbacks, i.e., high computational complexity and poor performance at low signalto-noise-ratios (SNRs) compared with the classical belief propagation (BP) decoder. This limits the application of LP decoding in real implementations.
To address the aforementioned drawbacks of LP decoding, the work [4] proposed an alternating direction method of mul-

In [5], a reduced-complexity algorithm was proposed to simplify the Euclidean projection operation in ADMM decoder based on the cut-search algorithm in [6]. Further improvements on decoding complexity can be found in [7] and [8] etc. Moreover, in order to obtain better error correcting performance, the work [9] developed an ADMM-penalized decoder by adding various penalty terms to the objective function. Note that with this technique, the obtained solutions are more likely to be integral and pseudo-codewords would become more costly than true codewords, therefore comparable performance can be achieved with that of BP decoding at low SNRs. In [10], penalty terms were modified by assigning different penalty parameters for difficult variable node degrees in respect of irregular LDPC codes. A quadratic programming (QP) decoder based on ADMM was proposed in [11] with linear complexity and better performance compared with the BP decoder.
In this letter, we aim to improve the error correcting performance for binary linear codes by adopting the penalty dual decomposition (PDD) framework [12] and developing a PDD decoding algorithm. To tackle the difficult PC and discrete constraints, we first transform the original ML problem to its equivalent form by employing the cascaded decomposition method [3]. Next, instead of relaxing the discrete constraints to continuous ones, we take an alternative by transforming them into equivalent equality constraints with the help of auxiliary variables. Then, the PDD decoding algorithm is presented, where coupling constraints are handled by Lagrangian relaxation, block decomposition is performed over the resulting augmented Lagrangian (AL) function, and appropriate penalty parameters are utilized to eventually enforce the equality constraints. We show that the proposed PDD decoding algorithm can be implemented in fully parallel with linear complexity. Furthermore, it also provides guaranteed convergence to stationary solutions. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed PDD decoder provides superior performance compared with existing decoders.1

III. THE PROPOSED PDD DECODER
In this section, we first present the proposed equivalent reformulation of the discrete constraints by introducing auxiliary variables. This idea leads to the proposed PDD decoding algorithm, which mainly consists of two loops. In the inner loop, the AL subproblem is approximately solved, while the outer loop is to update the dual variables and certain penalty parameter. Note that the main novelty here is to introduce a way to handle the nontrivial constraints of the difficult problem (6) and develop a low complexity algorithm accordingly by applying the PDD framework. Complexity analysis is also conducted to show the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm. Throughout this letter, we let k and m denote the inner and outer iteration index, respectively.

V. CONCLUSION
In this work, a novel PDD decoding algorithm for binary linear codes is designed and evaluated, which can be implemented in parallel with linear complexity. The main idea of the proposed algorithm is to transform the original ML decoding problem into its equivalent form by introducing auxiliary variables. Then, the resulting problem with coupling constraints are tackled by the PDD framework. Simulation results validate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.
