Learning to Decode Polar Codes with Quantized LLRs Passing

Email:{jiangao, daijincheng, niukai}@bupt.edu.cn

Abstract—In this paper, a weighted successive cancellation (WSC) algorithm is proposed to improve the decoding performance of polar codes with the quantized loglikelihood ratio (LLR). The weights used in the WSC are automatically learned by a neural network (NN). A novel NN model and its simplified architecture are build to select the optimal weights of the WSC, and allzero codewords can train the NN. Besides, we impose the constraints on weights to direct the learning process. The small number of trainable parameters lead to faster learning without performance loss. Simulation results show that the WSC algorithm is valid to various codewords and the trained weights make it outperform SC algorithm with the same quantization precision. Notably, the WSC with 3-bit quantization precision achieves a near floating point performance for short length.
I. INTRODUCTION
Polar Codes, discovered by Arikan in [1], have recently been adopted as the coding scheme for control channels in the 5th generation (5G) system. Polar codes achieve the symmetric capacity of binary-input discrete memoryless channels (B-DMCs) under low encoding and decoding complexity. SC algorithm was proposed for decoding polar codes [1], but, it suffers from high latency. The simplified SC (SSC) algorithm was introduced as a method to improve the latency of SC without affecting the error-correcting performance in [2]. In the implementation of hardware, polar code decoders that use quantized LLRs suffer significant performance loss compared to floating point decoder. In [3], the authors designed three uniform quantizers for SC decoder based on either optimizing the equivalent channel capacity, cutoff rate or mean-squared error (MSE). Also, they achieve a near floating point performance with 6-bit precision for the (1024,512) code. In [4], the authors discuss the robustness of quantized decoding algorithm without practical methods proposed for the quantization of polar codes.
The concept of applying the neural network to decoding has been proposed in the early nineties [5]– [7]. Recently, T. Gruber et al. [8] revisit the idea of using a deep NN for one-shot decoding of random and polar codes. They elaborate that the most challenge facing applications of deep NN to decoding is the large size of training code space (exponential in the block

length). This problem leads to a poor generalization of the NN to new codewords. The scheme of designing NN with the functionality of a decoding algorithm has been proposed to overcome the drawback. E. Nachmani et al. [9] [10], L. Lugosch and W. J. Gross [11] suggested a deep NN which is modeled on the belief propagation (BP) algorithm to improve the performance of decoding High-Density Parity Check (HDPC) codes. These NNs are constructed to closely mimic the message-updating of BP algorithm on the Tanner graph. The trainable weights and bias are assigned to the edges of the Tanner graph. A remarkable property of the BP algorithm is the independence of the performance from the transmitted codeword and is preserved in these NN decoders. This allows training the parameters on a single codeword. Moreover, B. Vasic et al. [12] utilize such weighted BP decoder to decode LDPC codes with finite-alphabet message passing. The NN decoder with 3-bit precision maintains no loss performance compared to the min-sum algorithm. Based on a similar spirit, W. Xu et al. [13] used the NN to improve BP decoding for polar codes by assign weights to the nodes in the Tanner graph.
SC is a conventional decoding algorithm for polar codes. However, the performance loss caused by the quantized LLRs limits its implementation. In this paper, we propose to apply deep learning to improve the performance of SC with quantized LLRs. The main contributions are summarized as follows:
1) A WSC algorithm is proposed, and the weights used in the WSC are automatically learned by a NN.
2) We build a novel NN model and its simplified architecture based on the SC factor graph to select the optimal weights. The training only requires a set of all-zero codewords.
3) We design a new method to constrain the number of weights. This weights assignment method guides the NN to learn the characteristics of quantized data at a faster rate without performance loss.

B. Neural Network Model
Traditionally, the weights w in the WSC are selected through experimentation and experience. However such an approach is inaccurate and inefficient. Besides, with the increase in the size of the weights, it is difficult to find the optimal weights w by the traditional method. Deep learning and NN provide us with powerful tools to solve this complex problem. We build a NN model based on the SC factor graph. Fig. 4 shows the architecture of the NN, where, each blue rectangle represents one layer. The scheduling of the hidden layers in the NN is the same as in Fig. 3. That means the NN is equivalent to an SC decoder before training if the weights are initialized to 1.

The training of a NN is a process of gradient descent, and the gradient must be alive during the training phase. However, the nonlinear operation XOR of the SC algorithm causes the gradient disappears. Therefore, in this paper, the softened sign function ssign(x) is not only used as the activation function of the output layer (ssign neurons) but also replaces the hard decision operation. The partial sum sˆ is converted form {0,1} domain into {1,-1} domain after the replacement and is calculated by the multiplier instead of XOR operation. For instance, in the NN, sˆ2,1 = uˆ1 · uˆ3 in stead of sˆ2,1 = uˆ1 ⊕ uˆ3.
C. Training and Testing Scheme of the NN
The NN extracts feature from the quantized LLR by training autonomously. Since the NN is build based on the SC factor graph, all the relationships between LLRs in SC are retained, and the training of the NN only requires a set of all-zero codewords. Fig. 6 shows the training and testing scheme of the NN. In the training phase, only one quantizer participates, and the channel LLRs are fed into the NN as training samples after quantized. However, the intermediate LLRs generated during the decoding process are still floating point values. IV. SIMPLIFIED NN MODEL
A. Architecture of the Simplified NN
We introduced in II-C that the SC algorithm has two kinds of computational redundancy. First, the frozen bits are known to both encoder and decoder and usually set to 0. The corresponding LLRs L(ui|i ∈ Ac) does not need

to be calculated. Second, uˆi can be directly calculated by the L(xI ) instead of L(uˆI ) and all calculations related to uˆI can be omitted.
Based on a similar spirit, we cut some neurons in the NN, and the redundant neurons are categorized into two types:
• Type-1 indicates the neurons associated only with the frozen bits;
• Type-2 represents that the neurons embedded in the constituent code with rate 1;
Fig. 7 shows the simplified NN (SNN) architecture of (8,4) polar codes with Ac = {0, 1, 2, 4}, where, the red dotted neurons are associated only with the frozen outputs {uˆ0, uˆ1, uˆ2, uˆ4} and belong to Type-1. The blue dotted neurons are embedded in the constituent code with output {uˆ6, uˆ7} and belong to Type-2.

V. EXPERIMENTS AND NUMERICAL RESULTS
We implemented the NN and SNN on the advanced neural network library Tensorflow and verified the performance using (64,32) and (1024,512) codewords. Since the NN and SNN are build based on the SC factor graph, therefore, they are equivalent to the quantized WSC decoder in the testing phase.
Different codewords: For (64,32) polar codes, batch size is set to 100 with the varying SN Rs = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}dB and 20 samples per SN R. We test the performance of the NN and SNN after every 100 batches. The test set is different from the training set, which is a collection of random codewords in {0,1} domain. The Block Error Rate (BLER) performance of (64,32) polar codes is shown in Fig. 8. We can see from it that the NN and SNN have a similar BLER performance. VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we apply deep learning to improve the performance of the SC algorithm with quantized LLRs and propose the WSC algorithm. A novel NN model and its SNN are build based on the SC factor graph. The NN and SNN only need to training by allzero codewords. We replace the hard decision with the softened sign function, ensuring that the gradient does not disappear during the training phase.
