Performance Analysis and Implementation of LDPC for DOCSIS 3.1 Uplink

Jewon Lee and Joon-Young Jung Media Broadcasting Research Section Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute
Daejeon, Repulbic of Korea jw_lee@etri.re.kr

Abstract—Data over cable service interface specification 3.1 (DOCSIS 3.1) is released to provide multi-gigabit data service in hybrid fiber coaxial (HFC) networks. To support the data rate of DOCSIS 3.1 uplink, the low density parity check (LDPC) decoding processing speed is required as corresponding to the data rate. We implement the LDPC encoder and decoder based on the DOCSIS 3.1 uplink and verify them by the laboratory test. The results of the laboratory test show that the implemented LDPC encoder and decoder conforms to the DOCSIS 3.1 uplink.
Keywords—DOCSIS 3.1 uplink, LDPC, codeword selection algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION In hybrid fiber coaxial (HFC) networks, data over cable service interface specification 3.1 (DOCSIS 3.1) is released to provide services such as 4k, cloud computing, wifi backhaul networks [1]. DOCSIS 3.1 has significant changes in the physical layer compared to previous DOCSISs. The significant changes are OFDM, wide bandwidths with up to 192 MHz per a channel, high order quadrature amplitude modulation (QAM), low density parity check (LDPC) [2]. DOCSIS 3.1 provides data rates of about 2 Gbps in downstream and 1 Gbps in upstream based on maximum bandwidth per a channel. In terms of hardware (HW) implementation, LDPC decoding processing speed is more important than anything to support the maximum data rate of DOCSIS 3.1. In the DOCSIS 3.1 downlink, 8/9 rate LDPC, which one of forward error corrections (FECs) of digital video broadcasting – cable version 2 (DVB-C2) ,is adopted. The size of the parity check matrix of 8/9 rate LDPC is 5 by 45. The parity check matrix is consisted of sub-matrices of which size is 360 by 360. In [3], the authors have proposed the LDPC decoder with 1Gbps processing speed by using three LDPC decoder cores that can process the sub-matrix in parallel in the 100 MHz system clock. In [4], in order to optimize the error correction performance of LDPC, the authors have analyzed the bit error rate (BER) performances depending on the number of bits representing log (LLR) value which is the input of LDPC and the number of iteration decoding. The DOCSIS 3.1 uplink supports up to 96 MHz bandwidth per a channel, 1024 QAM and three LDPC codewords included shortening. In the DOCSIS 3.1 uplink, in order to optimize the data transmission efficiency by reducing redundancy such as parity bits, codeword selection algorithm (CSA) is adopted. Thus, an uplink data is consisted of various codewords by the CSA, which is different the downlink data consisted of only a codeword. Therefore, in terms of HW implementation of LDPC for DOCSIS 3.1, additional considerations are required.

The purpose of this paper is to review the DOCSIS 3.1 uplink and LDPC, and to verify the implemented LDPCs conforming to DOCSIS 3.1 under laboratory test.

(a)
(b) Fig. 5: The structure of the LDPC HW module, (a) encoder (b) decoder
The Xilinx XAUI and XAUI to FEC modules are the media access control(MAC). The MAC receives IP packets and transmits the IP packets to the width converter module. The width converter controls the bit widths of input and output and the clock speed between adjacent modules. The randomizer is a scrambler defined in DOCSIS 3.1. The symbol mapping module receives the LDPC encoder output and generates QAM symbols to transmit.
The MAP2LLR module converts the received QAM symbols into log likelihood ratio (LLR) values. For example, when each bit-wise LLR value is represented by 7 bits in a 1024 QAM, the total number of bits is 70. The LDPC decoder module consists of three full-parallel LDPC decoders which are the structure similar to the proposed structure in [3]. Fig. 6 shows the structure of the LDPC full-parallel decoder. The parallel processing module processes 2520 bits = 360 ൈ 7 bits at a time, and the number of the parallel processing modules is 35. The number of the sub-matrix for the long codeword is maximum 35 except zero matrices based on 1 row.

V. CONCLUSION The DOCSIS 3.1 uplink supports about 1 Gbps data rate with 96 MHz bandwidth and 1024 QAM. The LDPC decoding processing speed should also be about 1 Gbps to achieve its required data rate. We had implemented the LDPC encoder and decoder based on the DOCSIS 3.1 uplink and verified them by the laboratory test. In future work, we process the integration of DOCSIS 3.1 uplink PHY with the implemented LDPC encoder and decoder and verify by performing the laboratory test.
