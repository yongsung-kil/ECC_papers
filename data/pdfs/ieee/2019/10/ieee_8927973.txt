Abstract‚ÄĒThere are many algebraic tools, e.g., finite fields and combinatorial designs, for constructing good quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes. In particular, QC-LDPC codes designed based on two arbitrary subsets of a given finite field perform very well and some well-known algebraic constructions of QC-LDPC codes have been verified as special cases of this method. How to choose these two subsets to further improve the performance of finite field based QC-LDPC codes is of interest. In this paper, we study such codes and analyze their structural properties from isomorphism perspective, and generalize some rules to efficiently determine the non-isomorphic finite field based codes. By comparing the cycle distributions of non-isomorphic codes, we can easily construct the finite field based QC-LDPC codes with larger girth and fewer short cycles. These codes generally perform very well and can be used as the ingredients of some further processings, e.g., masking. Numerical results show that the constructed codes have better performance with the iterative decoding algorithms.
I. INTRODUCTION
Since quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes [1] possess various advantages such as practical iterative decoding based on layered minimum-sum algorithm (MSA) [2], easy hardware implementation of encoder and decoder, and lower error-floor, they have been extensively investigated and successively used in many digital and storage systems [2]‚Äď[11]. In particular, QC-LDPC codes have been accepted as the channel coding scheme for the enhanced mobile broadband data channel of 5G communication [11], [12], and also recommended as the candidate coding scheme for the data channel of ultra-reliable low-latency communications.
In this paper we concentrate on the QC-LDPC codes constructed from two subsets of a given finite field [4]‚Äď[6]. It has been verified [4] that some well-known constructions based on finite fields and combinatorial designs are the special cases of such method. The Tanner graph of resulting codes based on this method is free of length-4 cycles and has a girth at least 6. Such finite field based codes perform very well under the iterative decoding.
Cycle distribution, defined from the graph-theoretic point of view, has important influence on the performance of an LDPC code. To the best of our knowledge, A. Tasdighi et al. [8] and C. Sun et al. [7] first introduced the isomorphism of graph theory into LDPC codes when studying the optimal girth of fully-connected (3, dc)-regular QC-LDPC codes. Then isomorphism is constantly used to design LDPC codes, e.g., short-

block nonbinary LDPC (NB-LDPC) codes [9] and NB-LDPC cycle codes [10]. Theoretically, isomorphic LDPC codes have same structural properties, e.g., girth, cycle distribution, and minimum distance, so they have similar performance when decoded with the iterative algorithms. In [8], the authors focused on the fully-connected 3 √ó dc exponent matrices with arbitrary shift values. Such irregularity of shift values may bring huge search complexity with the increase of exponent matrix size and lifting size. Thus, if we concentrate on some structural exponent matrices constructed by the specific tools, e.g., finite fields, we can make the most of these structural properties and efficiently search the non-isomorphic exponent matrices of flexible size to design QC-LDPC codes.
Motivated by the significant error performance and wonderful structural properties of finite field based QC-LDPC codes constructed based on two subsets of a given field (even though these two subsets are chosen arbitrarily) [4]‚Äď[6], in this paper we will investigate such codes from the isomorphism of general QC-LDPC codes to further improve the performance. From the perspective of isomorphism, we specifically generalize some rules to help efficiently search non-isomorphic finite field based QC-LDPC codes. By enumerating the cycle distributions of non-isomorphic codes, we can easily obtain a series of isomorphic/non-isomorphic finite field based codes with larger girth and fewer short cycles. Numerical results show that the constructed codes have better performance under the iterative decoding.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II presents two classes of finite field based QC-LDPC codes for later discussion. Section III first reviews the isomorphism of general QC-LDPC codes and then generalizes some properties of finite field based QC-LDPC codes from the isomorphism perspective. In Section IV, some search results and the construction of finite field based QC-LDPC codes are presented. Finally, Section V concludes this paper.

The parity-check matrix H of a QC-LDPC code can be rep-

resented as a bipartite graph, termed as Tanner graph [13], and the representation is unique. A closed path that starts from and ends at the same node of a Tanner graph is called a cycle whose length, defined as the number of edges on this path, is even number no less than 4. The girth of a Tanner graph, denoted by g, refers to the length of the shortest cycles. The numbers of cycles of various lengths (g, g + 2, g + 4, . . .) are defined as the cycle distribution of the Tanner graph, denoted by the polynomial C(x) = Cgxg + Cg+2xg+2 + Cg+4xg+4 + ¬∑ ¬∑ ¬∑ . For various cycle distributions, we use notation C‚ąó(x) to denote one that has largest girth and fewer short cycles throughout the paper. In this paper, for a QC-LDPC code Cqc, the cycle distributions of its parity-check matrix H and exponent matrix P both denote the cycle distribution of the Tanner graph associated with H.
The impact of Tanner graph on code performance is complex. In general, short cycles, especially the cycles of length 4, will degrade the error performance of a QC-LDPC code when decoded with the iterative algorithms. The well-known row&column constraint (RC-constraint) [14] can ensure that the Tanner graph of designed QC-LDPC code has no cycles of length 4 and obtains girth at least 6. Finite field based QCLDPC codes can achieve this property and thus have girth at least 6 [4]‚Äď[6].

Above two examples show that the isomorphism of QCLDPC codes and our derived specific properties of finite field based QC-LDPC codes from the isomorphism perspective can help simplify the exhaustive search and provides a series of isomorphic/non-isomorphic finite field based QC-LDPC codes with larger girth and fewer short cycles. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, motivated by the significant performance of QC-LDPC codes whose exponent matrices are constructed based on two subsets of a given field (even though these two subsets are chosen at random) [4]‚Äď[6], we studied such finite field based codes from the isomorphism perspective and generalized some rules to efficiently determine non-isomorphic exponent matrices. After enumerating the cycle distributions of these non-isomorphic matrices, we can easily construct a set of finite field based codes with larger girth and fewer short cycles for a given code length. Numerical results show that the constructed codes can obtain better performance over the AWGN channel under the iterative decoding and are very suitable for the ultra-reliable low-latency communications.
