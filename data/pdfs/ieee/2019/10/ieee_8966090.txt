Abstract—Algebraic constructions of quasi-cyclic (QC) lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes with unequal error protection (UEP) capabilities have recently been proposed, which can provide different error-correcting capabilities for different parts of the coded bits. However, conventional decoding algorithms are not designed for LDPC codes with UEP. In this paper, we propose three decoding schemes for UEP QC-LDPC codes extended from existing scheduling strategies, tailored to the special structures of the parity-check matrices. Simulation results show that the proposed schemes can provide improved error rate performance especially for coded bits on low protection levels. The decoding convergence can also be accelerated, thereby reducing the computational complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density pariy-check (LDPC) codes [1] have found many applications in practical systems due to near-capacity performance. With structured parity-check matrices composed of blocks of circulant matrices, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes [2], [3] have advantages of efficient encoding/decoding and reduced implementation complexity. Error-correcting codes with unequal error protection (UEP) capabilities can provide different parts of coded bits with different error-correcting capabilities. Algebraic constructions of UEP QC-LDPC codes via masking have recently been proposed in [4], which have superior performance over time-sharing schemes and competitive performance against randomly constructed LDPC codes.
The LDPC decoding process is commonly conducted by using iterative message passing based on the sum-product algorithm [5]. The updating order of the check/variable nodes is usually referred to as “scheduling.” Good scheduling strategies [6]–[12] can improve the performance and accelerate the convergence.
Conventional decoding algorithms are not designed for LDPC codes with UEP capabilities and may cause unnecessary computational effort. In this paper, we propose three decoding schemes extended from existing scheduling strategies, with the new subcodeword-update-terminating mechanism and the special updating order of the variable nodes, tailored to the structures of the parity-check matrices of UEP QCLDPC codes. For UEP QC-LDPC codes, as coded bits of different protection levels exhibit different correcting capabilities, the levelk2 subcodeword has a lower bit error rate than the level-k1 subcodeword at a fixed SNR for k2 > k1. Therefore, higherlevel subcodewords are easier to be decoded successfully than lower-level subcodewords. Moreover, the upper-triangular form of the parity-check matrix in (1) facilitates orderly checking whether the subcodeword is correct from the highest level to the lowest level. Thus, when the highest-level subcodeword has been decoded correctly, we can stop decoding that subcodeword and computational power can be assigned to other subcodowrds. For example, consider decoding a 3level UEP QC-LDPC code. When the level-3 subcodeword has been decoded correctly, we can stop decoding that part. Subsequently, other parts will be updated until the level2 subcodeword is decoded successfully. Finally, only the remaining level-1 subcodeword is updated.

IV. DECODING SCHEME BASED ON SHUFFLED SCHEDULING
While flooding scheduling operates concurrently, shuffled scheduling [8] is a serial procedure that updates the variable nodes sequentially in a predetermined order, which has the advantage of faster convergence than that of flooding. Since coded bits in higher protection level has better correcting capabilities than those in lower level, shuffled scheduling is integrated to our decoding scheme in the way that the variable nodes are updated from the current highest protection level to the lowest protection level, together with the subcodewordupdate-terminating mechanism. VII. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose three decoding schemes extended from existing scheduling strategies for UEP QC-LDPC codes. Our schemes, tailored to the special features of UEP QCLDPC codes, not only eliminate the unnecessary waste of computational power but also provide other benefits.
