Optimal Search for Girth-8 Quasi Cyclic and Spatially Coupled Multiple-Edge LDPC Codes
Mohammad-Reza Sadeghi

Abstract— Two essential categories of LDPC codes that are more preferable to other types are quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes and spatially coupled LDPC convolutional codes (SC-LDPC-CCs) because of their excellent performance curves in waterfall and error floor regions. An efficient approach to construct these codes is protograph-based method that is categorized into two classes: single-edge (SE) and multiple-edge (ME) protographs. We, for the first time, provide a necessary and sufficient condition for exponent matrices of these codes with girth-8 and based on the ME-protographs. As a result, a lower bound on the lifting degree of girth-8 ME-QC-LDPC codes and a lower bound on the syndrome former memory order of girth-8 ME-SC-LDPC-CCs are obtained, which are tighter than the existing bounds in the literature.
Index Terms— Multiple-edge protographs, QC-LDPC codes, SC-LDPC-CCs, girth, difference matrices, lifting degree.
I. INTRODUCTION
A Protograph (PTG)-based QC-LDPC code is associated to a base matrix W whose entries determine the type of the PTG. A base matrix with entries 0 and 1 corresponds to a single-edge (SE) PTG. If a base matrix contains Wij ≥ 2, then its corresponding PTG yields a multiple-edge (ME) QC-LDPC code. ME-PTGs have some advantages over SE-PTGs. For example, QC-LDPC codes lifted from ME-PTGs allow larger upper bounds on the minimum distance [1], [2].
LDPC codes are simply described by a Tanner graph (TG). The parameter girth which is the length of the shortest cycles of the TG has a remarkable impact on the code performance. A base matrix containing Wij ≥ 3 results in a girth-6 TG, [1]. In the category of QC-LDPC codes, one of the most interesting subjects among researchers is the construction of QC-LDPC codes with a specific girth and the shortest length. Moreover, many efforts have been put into obtaining a necessary and sufficient condition to reduce the size of the search space to obtain entries of an exponent matrix B corresponding to W . In [3], a necessary and sufficient condition for an exponent matrix of girth-6 ME-QC-LDPC codes were proposed which provides a simplification for checking 4-cycles in B. The obtained analytical lower bound on the lifting degree resulted in girth-6 ME-QC-LDPC codes with shorter lengths compared to their counterparts in the SE-category.
Spatially-coupled LDPC codes (SC-LDPC codes) can be viewed as a type of LDPC convolutional codes [4]. In fact, SC-LDPC codes are constructed by coupling together a series

Small cycles and graphical structures like trapping sets and absorbing sets, which often contains short cycles, are known to have strong influence on the error floor [5]. One of the merits of SC-LDPC codes is that in the coupling process a huge number of absorbing sets in the TG are broken [6]. This is the main reason for a remarkable performance in waterfall and error floor regions of their bit error rate curves. Constructing SCLDPC convolutional codes (SC-LDPC-CCs) with the lowest constraint length and free of short cycles such as 4-cycles is a challenging problem. Regarding to construct SE-SCLDPC-CCs with girth up to 12, using a compact method, and SC-LDPC-CCs with small constraint length we point out to [7]–[9].
In this letter, we use the difference matrices, introduced in [3], to simplify checking 6-cycles in the exponent matrix of ME-QC-LDPC codes. The obtained results contribute to reduce the size of the search space in recognizing the entries of an exponent matrix and yield new lower bounds on the lifting degrees. We also use these results to propose a different representation for Time-Invariant ME-SC-LDPC-CCs. This approach demonstrates a close relation between these two categories of LDPC codes and helps to obtain lower bounds on the syndrome former memory order which are tighter than the ones in [10]. In the sequel, Section II provides basic definitions. Section III and Section IV, propose our main results for ME-QC-LDPC codes and ME-SC-LDPC-CCs, respectively. A summary of our results is in Section V.

V. CONCLUSION In this letter, we considered two approaches to protographbased LDPC codes, QC-LDPC codes and SC-LDPC-CCs. Given an exponent matrix, we obtained the necessary and sufficient conditions to have QC-LDPC codes and SC-LDPC-CCs with girth 8 whose protographs are multiple-edge (ME). These conditions, which are new results for both categories, contribute to simplify constructing exponent matrices and yield lower bounds on the lifting degree of ME-QC-LDPC codes and on the syndrome former memory order of ME-SC-LDPC-CCs.
