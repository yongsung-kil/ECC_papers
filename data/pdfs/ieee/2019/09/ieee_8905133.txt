A Decomposition Mapping based Quantized Belief Propagation Decoding for 5G LDPC Codes

Hangxuan Cui*, Khoa L e ft, Fakhreddine Ghaffarif, David Declercqf, Jun Lin*, and Zhongfeng Wang* *School of Electronic Science and Engineering, Nanjing University, P. R. China
fETIS, UMR-8051, Université Paris Seine, Université de Cergy-Pontoise, ENSEA, CNRS, France ^Faculty of Electrical and Electronics Engineering, Hochiminh City University of Technology, VNU-HCM, Vietnam haxcui@163.com; khoa.le@hcmut.edu.vn; {khoa.letrung, fakhreddine.ghaffari,declercq}@ensea.fr; {jlin,zfwang}@nju.edu.cn

Abstract—Since the degree-1 variable nodes (VNs) in the low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for the 5th generation (5G) mobile communications are very sensitive to be erroneous, the quantized min-sum (QMS) and offset min-sum (QOMS) decodings suffer from poor error-correction performance due to the imprecise estimation of the check-to-variable (C2V) message magnitudes. For this reason, this paper proposes a decomposition mapping based quantized belief propagation (DM-QBP) decoding for 5G LDPC codes. In order to reduce the computation complexity, the check node (CN) update function is realized by look-up tables (LUTs). Furthermore, a decomposition method is presented to eliminate the high memory cost of using large tables without performance loss. Therefore, the CN update function can be implemented based only on simple mappings and fixed-point additions. Simulation results show that, the DM-QBP decoder considerably outperforms the state-of-the-art ones for several 5G LDPC codes. With a small number of quantization bits, its performance is even better than the floating-point OMS decoding in some cases.
Index Terms—5G LDPC decoder, low-density parity-check (LDPC) code, finite alphabet iterative decoding (FAID), look-up table (LUT), quantized belief propagation (QBP).
I. Introduction
Owing to excellent error-correction performance and capac­ ity approaching capability, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have been widely adopted in many communi­ cation standards, such as IEEE802.16e, IEEE802.11ad, and IEEE802.11n/ac/ax. Recently, LDPC codes were adopted in the enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) scenario in the 5th generation mobile networks (5G) [2], [3]. Two base ma­ trices, BG1 and BG2, which are constructed from raptor­ like protographs with quasi-cyclic (QC) structures, have been introduced for 5G to support rate compatible coding and scalable information transmission [4].
Typically, LDPC decoding algorithms take iterative message passing manner, where the decoding messages are iteratively exchanged between variable nodes (VNs) and check nodes (CNs) in each iteration. Among numerous decoding algorithm­ s, the belief-propagation (BP) algorithm could provide the best decoding performance but it is hardly used in practical due to the high computation complexity. By simplifying the CN-update function, min-sum (MS) algorithm significantly reduces the decoding complexity. However, the MS algorithm suffers from a considerable performance loss. Therefore, offset min-sum (OMS) and normalized min-sum (NMS) algorithms

were proposed [5], [6], which greatly reduce the performance loss by involving a correction factor. In the finite-precision decoding, where the messages are presented by a limited number of quantization bits, the MS algorithm and its variants are widely used since they are hardware friendly. However, due to the limited precision, quantized decoders usually perform worse than their floating-point counterparts, especially for 5G LDPC codes.
The 5G LDPC codes are, generally speaking, a concatena­ tion of a high rate LDPC code and a low-density generator matrix (LDGM) code [4]. Therefore, the 5G LDPC codes are dramatically irregular and a large part of VNs are with degree1, which leads to the fact that these VNs are very sensitive to be erroneous since they can only receive a unique extrinsic message. As a result, the quantized min-sum (QMS) and offset min-sum (QOMS) decodings exhibit a poor error-correction performance due to the imprecise estimation of the check-tovariable (C2V) messages [7].
In this paper, a decomposition mapping based quantized belief propagation (DM-QBP) decoding is proposed for 5G LDPC codes. To improve the accuracy of the CN-update function, the C2V messages are computed by BP and realized by look-up tables (LUTs) for simplicity. Moreover, a decom­ position mapping method is introduced to reduce the size of LUTs so as to greatly save memory costs. Our method leads to a hardware-friendly decoder which can be implemented based only on simple mappings and fixed-point additions (including subtractions). Simulation results show that the DMQBP decoder has a much better error-correction performance than the state-of-the-art ones for several 5G LDPC codes with a negligible additional hardware resource.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section Π introduces the structure of the 5G LDPC codes. This section also recalls the QBP and QMS decodings. In Section III, the proposed DM-QBP decoding for 5G LDPC codes is described in detail. Section IV presents the simulation and synthesis results. At last, the conclusion is drawn in Section V.

III. The Proposed DM-QBP Decoding Algorithm
As described in Section II, the QMS and QOMS decodings perform worse on 5G LDPC codes due to the imprecise estimation of the C2V message magnitudes. To reduce the error probability of the degree-1 VNs, the adapted min-sum (AMS) decoding was proposed in [10], where the core and the extension checks are processed by different algorithms. The OMS decoding is applied to the core checks while the traditional MS decoding is used for the extension checks. Though AMS performs much better than MS and OMS decodings, its performance is still not good enough when compared to the BP decoding.
In this section, a decomposition mapping based quantized belief propagation decoding is proposed to further improve the error-correction performance of 5G LDPC codes. Since the degree-1 VNs can only be corrected by the unique extrinsic message, the accuracy of C2V messages has an extremely large impact on these bits. V. C o n c l u sio n
In this paper, we propose a decomposition mapping based quantized belief propagation decoding method to improve the error-correction performance of 5G LDPC codes. In the proposed DM-QBP decoding, the CN-update function is decomposed into two steps. Two mapping functions are intelligently designed and combined that significantly reduce the memory costs without performance degradation. By doing this, the DM-QBP decoder offers a much better decoding performance than the state-of-the-art one with negligible ad­ ditional hardware resource. Simulation results show that the DM-QBP decoder exhibits a performance that approaches the floating-point BP decoder in the waterfall region.
