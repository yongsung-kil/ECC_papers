Design of Protograph-LDPC-Based BICM-ID for Multi-Level-Cell (MLC) NAND Flash Memory
Yingcheng Bu, Yi Fang , Member, IEEE, Guojun Han , Senior Member, IEEE, Shahid Mumtaz , Senior Member, IEEE, and Mohsen Guizani , Fellow, IEEE

Abstract— In this letter, the optimization of protographlow-density parity-check (LDPC)-based bit-interleaved coded modulation with iterative detection and decoding (BICM-ID) with anti-Gray mapping is investigated over multi-level-cell (MLC) NAND flash-memory channels. Since the existing protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) algorithm is not applicable to the BICM-ID MLC flash-memory channels, a voltage-sensing PEXIT (VS-PEXIT) algorithm is proposed to facilitate the threshold analysis of protograph codes. Exploiting the proposed VS-PEXIT algorithm, we find that the optimal AR4JA code over AWGN channels cannot preserve its superiority over BICM-ID MLC flash-memory channels. To tackle this problem, we further propose a design scheme to construct a high-rate protograph code, referred to as optimized accumulate-repeat-accumulate (OARA) code, tailored for such scenarios. Theoretical analyses and simulation results illustrate that the proposed OARA-based BICM-ID appears to be an excellent storage scheme over MLC flash-memory channels.
Index Terms— Protograph-based LDPC codes, BICM-ID, multi-level-cell NAND flash memory, VS-PEXIT algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
T HE NAND flash memory is extensively used in consumer electronic products for its desirable properties, such as low power consumption and high access speed. With the emergence of the multi-level-cell (MLC) technology [1], the number of bits stored in each cell increases from one bit to two bits, which significantly enhances the storage density and capacity. However, it is accompanied by the degradation of the data reliability and the decrease of lifetime. The lifetime of flash

In particular, the repetitive PE cycles wear the flash memory, resulting in the occurrence of errors. Unfortunately, conventional error-correcting codes (ECCs), such as BCH codes, are not able to guarantee the data reliability for flash memory. Thanks to the advantage of low-complexity and capacity-approaching performance, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were introduced for flash memory [2].
MLC flash memory system can be regarded as a 4-pulseamplitude modulation (4PAM) bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) [3] system when LDPC codes are employed. Gray mapping is generally adopted in MLC flash memory. As is well known, incorporating iterative detection (or demodulation) and decoding (ID) [4] into BICM can significantly boost the system performance. Thereby, the BICM-ID framework was applied to flash memory systems in [2]. However, Gray mapping can only achieve trivial gain in BICM-ID-aided flash memory, especially in MLC flash memory. For this reason, anti-Gray mapping is preferred to Gray mapping in such scenarios.
LDPC codes are powerful ECCs that have been successfully applied in various data transmission scenarios. As a subclass of LDPC codes, protograph-based LDPC codes not only enjoy simple structures, but also can be easily designed and implemented [5], [6]. In particular, the AR4JA code is a classic type of protograph code and can achieve nearShannon-limit performance with linear encoding complexity over AWGN channels [5]. In [2], the degree distribution of LDPC codes was optimized for BICM-ID-aided three-level cell (TLC) flash memory by using an asymmetric density evolution (DE). More recently, the rate-adaptive protograph LDPC (RAP-LDPC) codes were introduced to dynamically adapt to different PE cycles over BICM MLC flash-memory channels [7], in which the ID framework was not considered.
To the best of our knowledge, little is known about the specific analysis and design of protograph-based BICM-ID in MLC flash memory. More importantly, due to the asymmetric of MLC flash-memory channels, the conventional optimal protograph codes over AWGN channels may not perform well over MLC flash-memory channels with BICM-ID framework. With the aforementioned motivations, we conduct an in-depth study on the protograph-based BICM-ID with antiGray mapping over the flash-memory channels. II. SYSTEM MODEL AND PRELIMINARY FOUNDATIONS
Fig. 1 describes the block diagram of a protograph-based BICM-ID system over an MLC flash-memory channel. Interleaver is not required when LDPC codes are adopted owing to their self-interleaving feature. The information-bit sequence s is first encoded by a protograph encoder into the coded-bit sequence c = [c0, c1, . . . , cn−1]. Then the coded-bit sequence c is reshaped into a subsequence c = [c0, c1, . . . , cm−1] consisting of m k-tuples, where cp is a k-tuple including k coded bits, k = n/m, p = 0, 1, . . . , m − 1. The subsequence c is mapped into a modulated symbol sequence x = [x0, x1, . . . , xm−1], where xp is a modulated symbol from a constellation χ of size |χ| = 2k. For the convenience of presentation, we denote cpt (t = 0, 1, . . . , k − 1), as the t-th bit in the p-th modulated symbol xp ∈ χ and bt(xp) as the value of t-th bit of xp.
The MLC flash-memory channel model considered in this work contains the programming noise, random telegraph noise, cell-to-cell interference and data retention noise [8]. The distribution of threshold voltage in an MLC flash memory with anti-Gray mapping is described in Fig. 2. The four threshold voltage levels, called Vmin, V1, V2 and Vmax, represent the data symbols of ‘11’, ‘10’, ‘01’ and ‘00’, respectively. Given a PE cycles, we can acquire the read voltage levels by maximizing the mutual information (MI) of the quantized flash-memory channel [9].

III. VOLTAGE-SENSING PEXIT ALGORITHM FOR BICM-ID MLC FLASH MEMORY
In 2007, PEXIT algorithm was proposed by Liva et al. [10] to evaluate the MI of each variable node (VN) and check node (CN) in a protograph and predict its convergence performance. Nevertheless, the PEXIT algorithm cannot be employed to calculate the decoding threshold of protograph-based BICM-ID over MLC flash-memory channels. To address this issue, a VS-PEXIT algorithm, which takes the variation of threshold voltage and ID framework into consideration, is proposed.
According to channel model in [7], [8], the distribution of four threshold voltage levels Vmin, V1, V2 and Vmax in an MLC flash memory can be viewed as Gaussian-distributed random variables (see Fig. 2). Exploiting Monte-Carlo simulations, it can be easily measured that the variances of V1, V2 and Vmax are almost the same. When given a PE cycles N ,

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this work, the performance of protograph-based BICM-ID with anti-Gray mapping has been investigated over MLC flash-memory channels. We have conceived a VS-PEXIT algorithm to facilitate the design and analysis of protograph-based BICM-ID MLC flash-memory systems. Based on the proposed VS-PEXIT algorithm, we have observed that the conventional AR4JA code cannot perform well in this scenario. We have then developed a new design scheme to construct a high-rate protograph code, namely OARA code, which possesses both the lowest decoding threshold and the desirable linear-minimum-distance property. Both theoretical analyses and simulation results have demonstrated that the proposed OARA code is superior to the state-of-theart protograph codes over MLC flash-memory channels with BICM-ID. Furthermore, our design approach can be extended to three-level-cell (TLC) flash memory.
