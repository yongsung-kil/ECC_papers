A simplified LLR of SD-LDPC for probabilistically shaped QAM constellation

Wenjing Zhang1, Shaohua Hu1, Jing Zhang1, Xingwen Yi2, and Kun Qiu1
1Key Lab. of Optical Fiber Sensing and Communications, University of Electronic Science and Technology of China, Chengdu 611731, China
2School of Electronics and Information Technology, Sun Yat-Sen University, Guangzhou 510275, China
zhangjing1983@uestc.edu.cn
Abstract: We present a simplified log likelihood ratio (LLR) for LDPC decoding and compare the postFEC BER performances of PS-16QAM in coherent optical communications systems. The improvement of post-FEC BER is 1.17dB for PS-16QAM. Keywords: Coding and forward error correction for optical communications, Digital signal processing techniques for optical communications

INTRODUCTION
Recently, the probabilistically shaped (PS) modulation formats have been widely used to approach the Shannon limit in both direct detection and coherent optical fiber systems [1,2]. The probabilistically shaped PAM (PS-PAM) based direct detection systems have been demonstrated to meet the bandwidth requirement in data center interconnections (DCIs) [2]. There are many comparisons between PS-QAM and uniform modulation to evaluate the shaping gains. In [3], the maximum reach gains of probabilistically shaped 64QAM range from 15.5% to 34% compared with uniform 16QAM and 32QAM modulation formats in long-haul coherent optical communications system. The gains are based on pre- or post-FEC BER, mutual information (MI) and reach as performance metrics [4]. It is necessary to evaluate the shaping gains at the same net rate because the gains are various for different systems, modulation formats and distributions. The forward error correction coding plays an important role in PS schemes which carry out the shaping (Distribution Matching) before FEC encoding to preserve the desired distribution [1,5]. Besides, distribution dematching at receiver has low tolerance of errors and hence an efficient FEC decoding is necessary in such schemes. The calculation of likelihood ratio (LLR) on coded bits is an important function in soft decoding. However, an exact calculation of LLR (ELLR) is inefficient. Moreover, the probabilities of binaries are unequal in PS based system. So, an efficient LLR calculation scheme for PS systems is required.
In this paper, we present an efficient calculation scheme of log likelihood ratio (LLR) for soft decoded LDPC in PS constellation based coherent optical systems. Since the exponentiation in ELLR enhances the differences of summation terms and one of the summation terms will dominate the sum results, we choose the maximal one to substitute the whole calculation to reduce the complexity. The simulation results of PS-16QAM show that the simplified LLR is an effective approximation and there is almost no performance penalty compared with an exacted LLR. Besides, the approximate LLR with priori probability (ALLR-P) is deduced and demonstrated its effectiveness for probabilistically shaped constellations. Then, we carry out simulations and experiments of PS-QAM at the same net transmission rate to fairly compare the postBER gains. The coherent optical experiment of PS-16QAM with ALLR-P indicates that there is a 1.17 dB gain of postFEC BER compared with uniform 16QAM.

CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we have presented a simplified calculation of log likelihood ratio (LLR) and compared the post-FEC BER performances of PS-16QAM with uniform 16QAM at the same net transmission rate. The simulation of PS-16QAM indicates that the simplified LLR is an efficient approximation with negligible performance degradation. In addition, there is a 1.17 dB gain of post-FEC BER with ALLR-P for PS-16QAM compared with uniform 16QAM in coherent optical communications system.
