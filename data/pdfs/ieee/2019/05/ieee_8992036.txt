Reconfigurable Check Node Unit Design of Dual-Standard LDPC Decoding for 60 GHz Wireless Local Area Network

Hsin-Hao Su, Tang-Syun Chen, and Cheng-Hung Lin Department of Electrical Engineering, Yuan Ze University, Jungli, Taiwan 32003, R.O.C.

Abstract-- With the development of multimedia technology, many products require fast data transmission and good quality, such as 4K high-definition video, Augmented Reality (AR) or Virtual Reality (VR). The recent low-density parity check (LDPC) decoder designs were designed for individual implementations of IEEE 802.11ad and IEEE 802.15.3c. In this paper, the same set of key module of LDPC decoder, i.e. check node unit (CNU), is proposed for both standards. In IEEE 802.15.3c, the hardware usage rate achieves 100% and 90% for the rate-7/8 and rate-1/2 LDPC decoding, respectively, using the proposed CNU design. In IEEE 802.11ad, the hardware usage rate achieves 96% and 90% for the rate-13/16 and rate-1/2 LDPC decoding, respectively.
I. INTRODUCTION
The high parallel implementation and excellent error correction capabilities of low-density parity check (LDPC) codes [1] make them adopted in several modern wireless communication standards, such as IEEE 802.11ad [2] and IEEE 802.15.3c [3]. The both IEEE 802.11ad and IEEE 802.15.3c were proposed for 60-GHz wireless short-range transmissions. They can be employed in applications of ultrahigh-quality video transmission, augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) devices that require high-speed transmission for huge amounts of data.
In order to hold high-quality transmission under the interference of noise, the both IEEE 802.11ad and IEEE 802.15.3c use the LDPC codes as forward error correction (FEC) schemes. However, the two schemes have different parity check matrixes (PCM), code rates, expansion factors, as shown in Table I. Different matrix schemes of LDPC codes will use different decoding methods, which results in different decoder hardware architectures and increases the cost of the hardware implementations.

II. LDPC DECODING FOR DUAL STANDARDS

In this paper, the LDPC codes of the two standards are decoded by layered decoding. In the layered decoding, the information below the same column must be updated by the information in the upper row. In the rate-1/2 PCM of IEEE 802.11ad standard, as shown in Fig. 1, the row weights between the first layer and the second layer are interlaced. This represents that the two layers can be processed at the same time without the concern of data dependency.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a reconfigurable CNU and its hardware-shared sorter design are proposed for the LDPC decoding of the IEEE 802.11ad and the IEEE 802.15.3c standards. Using the block layer decoding, the overall eight matrixes of the two standards are divided into up to four block layers. The reconfigurable CNU is designed based on the IEEE802.15.3c LDPC decoding and supports the IEEE802.11ad LDPC decoding. From the experimental results, the proposed design achieves at least 90% hardware usage of the sorters for both standards.
