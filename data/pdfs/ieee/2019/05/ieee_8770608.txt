Design of a Multi-Core Scheduling Scheme for Tera-bit/s LDPC Decoding

Qiangyi Zhao and Liuguo Yin School of Information Science and Technology, Tsinghua University, Beijing, 100084, China Beijing National Research Center for Information Science and Technology, Beijing, 100084, China Key Laboratory of EDA, Research Institute of Tsinghua University in Shenzhen, Shenzhen, 518057, China
yinlg@tsinghua.edu.cn

Abstract—A scheduling scheme for multi-core Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) decoding is proposed to make full use of logical resources and to maximize the decoding throughput in the high-speed communication systems. In the proposed scheme, consecutive code blocks are processed in different decoding cores according to a variable allocation sequence which is decided by the parity checking result of the decoding sequence. And the scheme is mathematically analyzed with the Gaussian approximation analyzing algorithm and the Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA). It is shown that the proposed scheme can improve the decoding throughput by up to 130% compared to the conventional multicore parallel architectures.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity Check (LDPC) code has become one of the most popular channel codes in recent years after being rediscovered in the 1990s [1]. LDPC code has been chosen to be the channel coding method for the 5th-Generation New Radio mobile communication technology [2] [3]. As the communication speed is increasing rapidly, the ultra-high throughput LDPC decoders are needed. The IEEE 802.3bs standard has been approved, aiming at the Ethernet speed of 400 Gbit/s [4]. The throughput of the optical fiber channel is even higher than 1000 Tbit/s, as shown in [5]. Practical and effective LDPC decoding methods are needed to meet throughput requirements for high-speed communication systems, while taking the limited logical resources into consideration.
Several kinds of architectures have been proposed trying to solve the problem and to meet the requirements of the high throughput. A multi-core parallel architecture is proposed in [6] for the LDPC decoding. With more resources cost, the full-parallel decoding scheme is used in [7], which obtain a throughput of 13.21 Gbit/s. And a kind of unrolled decoding architecture is brought out in [8] for a higher throughput of 160 Gbit/s. Recently, an unrolled full-parallel decoder using a serial message-transfer approach is proposed obtaining a throughput up to 588 Gbit/s [9]. However, the throughputs of these methods are not enough for future generation of ultrahigh speed communication systems.
To maximize the throughput of high-speed communication systems, a scheduling scheme for multi-core LDPC decoding is proposed. Consecutive code blocks are processed in different decoding cores according to a variable allocation sequence which is decided by the parity checking result of the decoding sequence. The proposed scheme is analyzed with

the Gaussian approximation analyzing algorithm [10], and it is proved that the proposed scheme can improve the throughput by up to 130% compared to conventional multi-core parallel architectures.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section II describes the proposed scheme for the multi-core scheduling LDPC decoding. Then, the mathematical analysis for the proposed scheme is presented in Section III. After that, Section IV compares the proposed scheme with existing architectures. Finally, the conclusion is stated in Section V.

V. CONCLUSION

A scheduling scheme for multi-core LDPC decoder is proposed to improve the throughput of LDPC decoders for the high-speed communication systems. The proposed scheme is analyzed using the Gaussian approximation analyzing algorithm. The mathematical models about the distribution of needed iteration numbers and the relationship between the throughput improvement ratio and the Eb/N0 of the information channel are constructed. And the result is obtained that the scheme can improve the decoding throughput by up to 130% compared to conventional architectures. The proposed scheme can also be extended to other decoding algorithms and other kinds of the channel coding. The proposed multi-core LDPC decoder scheme can be widely adopted in the future ultra-high throughput LDPC decoders in FPGAs or ASICs.
