Performance Analysis of Finite-Length LDPC Codes Over Asymmetric Memoryless Channels
Zhen Mei , Kui Cai , Senior Member, IEEE, and Guanghui Song , Member, IEEE
Abstract—In this paper, we analyze the waterfall performance of finitelength low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for asymmetric memoryless channels by considering variations of channel parameters: the observed bit error rate Pobs and mutual information Iobs during the real-time transmission. Moreover, we propose to use the variation of a new channel parameter: the maximal achievable channel coding rate Robs at finite blocklengths to estimate the waterfall performance. Simulation results demonstrate that our estimations can well predict the waterfall performance of LDPC codes. In particular, the estimation based on Robs can fit the waterfall performance for various code lengths. For estimations based on Pobs and Iobs, their accuracy increases as the codeword length increases and no curve fitting is required.
Index Terms—LDPC codes, asymmetric channels, density evolution, finite-length analysis.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density parity-check (LDPC) codes are well known for their capacity-approaching performance, and their performance can be predicted by some asymptotic analysis methods such as density evolution (DE) [1]. However, when the codeword length decreases, there is a large gap between the asymptotic performance and actual performance since the asymptotic analysis assumes that the code length is infinite and the Tanner graph representation is cycle-free. Hence, it is of great importance to analyze the performance of finite-length LDPC codes. In the literature, the waterfall region performance of LDPC codes for the symmetric channels have been well investigated. For example, the performance of finite-length LDPC codes over the binary erasure channel (BEC) was estimated by solving a combinatorial problem with recursions [2]. Moreover, the waterfall region performance of LDPC codes with iterative decoding has been proved to follow a scaling law for BEC [3]. In [4], a simple estimation method based on the decoding threshold was proposed for symmetric channels without any curve fitting parameters. It predicts the block error probability (BLEP) of decoding by considering the probability that the real-time channel quality is worse than the decoding threshold. Recently, a similar method was proposed in [5] which can provide a slightly better estimation of the waterfall performance. However, it requires multiple times of DE which increases the complexity.
On the other hand, most of the published works focus on symmetric channels due to the favorable properties induced by channel symmetry.

The asymmetric channels have not been well investigated although they are also widely used to model various communication and data storage channels. Among different asymmetric channels, the Z-channel and binary asymmetric channel (BAC) are two special cases and have been commonly used to model the asymmetric errors. In addition, the asymmetric Gaussian channel (AGC) has also been widely used to model the optical-fiber channels and non-volatile memory (NVM) channels [6], [7]. In this paper, we will analyze the waterfall region performance of LDPC codes over the asymmetric memoryless channels, including the Z-channel, BAC and AGC.
To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work on the finite-length analysis of LDPC codes over the asymmetric channels. To achieve this, we extend the waterfall region performance analysis presented in [4] from the symmetric channels to the asymmetric channels. Our main contributions are as follows. First, we consider variations of asymmetric channels and derive the statistical characteristics of the real-time channel parameters: the observed bit error rate (BER) Pobs and mutual information (MI) Iobs during the transmission of codewords. Then, the BLEP of LDPC codes can be obtained by calculating the probability that the real-time channel quality is worse than the decoding threshold obtained from DE. Second, we propose, for the first time, a new observed channel parameter Robs, which is the maximal achievable channel coding rate at finite blocklengths [8] to estimate the waterfall region performance of LDPC codes. With this new channel parameter, the finite-length performance of LDPC codes can be very accurately predicted even with short blocklengths.

Considering the real-time channel quality, the BLEP PB(N, λ(x), ρ(x)) of a specific LDPC ensemble can be approximated as the probability that the observed channel behaves worse than the decoding threshold. V. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
In this section, the predicted BER and frame error rate (FER) of LDPC codes are compared with the simulation results. In particular, for Robs based on prediction, the fitting parameter β is also given for each case. We simulate different code ensembles with rate 1/2 for the BAC, Z-channel and AGC, including regular (3, 6) LDPC codes, and an irregular LDPC ensemble λ(x) = 0.4x2 + 0.4x5 + 0.2x8, ρ(x) = x8 chosen from [4]. Note that all LDPC codes in our simulations are constructed randomly with cycles of length 4 removed and only the uniform input distribution is used. The decoding algorithm is the sum-product algorithm and the maximum decoding iterations is 100.
For the BAC with p0 = 0.07, as shown in Fig. 1, the gap between the actual performance and the decoding threshold is large, especially for short-length codes. In terms of finite length analysis, the accuracy of the predictions by Pobs and Iobs improves as the blocklength increases. With N = 1000, the differences between our predictions (Iobs and Pobs) and simulated BER are 0.012 and 0.015, respectively at BER of 10−5. As N increases to 20000, the gap is reduced to only 0.002 and 0.003, respectively. For the prediction by Robs, it accurately predicts the simulation results for all different lengths. Moreover, it can be observed that when a unified fitting parameter β = 0.9 is used, the BER estimation by Robs can still closely approach the simulated BER, except there is a small gap for N = 20000.
Next, we consider a different crossover probability p0 = 0.03. As can been seen in Fig. 2, similar to Fig. 1, the estimation by Pobs and Iobs becomes more accurate as the blocklength increases, while the estimation by Robs can always closely approach the simulation results even when the blocklength is as small as 1000 bits.
In Fig. 3, both FER and BER of regular LDPC codes over the Z-channel are given. Similar to the BAC, the prediction based on the Robs can accurately fit the simulation results, while the estimation by Iobs and Pobs shows 0.007 difference on p1. It also can be observed that with a fixed β = 0.9, the FER and BER can still be predicted accurately by Robs. For the AGC, we consider σ1 = 2σ0 as an example. VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have provided an analytical/semi-analytical method to predict the waterfall region performance of LDPC codes over asymmetric memoryless channels. Simulation results have shown that the estimation based on Robs can fit the simulated BER and FER very well for N ≥ 1000 by tuning a fitting parameter β. It is still an open problem to find the best β analytically. The estimation based on the Pobs and Iobs is slightly less accurate, but requires no additional parameters for tuning. In addition, the gap between the Pobs and Iobs based estimation and the actual performance is very small with long code lengths (e.g. N = 20000).
