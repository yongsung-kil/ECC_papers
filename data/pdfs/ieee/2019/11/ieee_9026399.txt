Abstract-This paper presents an Enhanced Offset Min-Sum 
(EOMS) decoder for Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes 
used in the 5th generation (SG) mobile communications. It is 
observed that a significant part of Variable Nodes (VNs) in the 
SG LDPC codes are with degree-1 and are very sensitive to be 
erroneous, leading to the fact that the decoding performance is 
generally reduced. In the EOMS decoding, the core check nodes 
(CN) and extension CNs are processed with different update 
rules. A new CN-update criterion is also proposed by making 
use of the third minimum value. As a result, the offset factors 
are adaptively selected and the error probability of degree-1 
VNs is significantly reduced. Simulation results show that the 
proposed EOMS decoder offers a much better error-correction 
performance than the state-of-the-art benchmarks for several SG 
LDPC codes with a negligible complexity overhead. 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have received 
increasing attention in the past several decades. Due to the 
capacity-approaching performance, they have been adopted 
in the enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) scenario in the 
5th generation mobile communications (SG) [2], [3]. The 
decoding algorithms of LDPC codes usually take the iterative 
message passing manner. In each iteration, the decoding 
messages are iteratively exchanged between variable nodes 
(VNs) and check nodes (CNs). Though the belief-propagation 
(BP) algorithm [4] shows the optimal decoding performance, 
it requires complex computations involving large hardware 
implementations [5]. For this reason, the min-sum (MS) al­
gorithm was proposed that simplifies the BP algorithm at the 
cost of obvious performance degradation [6]. Many algorithms 
have been proposed in the literature to provide a better tradeoff 
between decoding performance and complexity by modifying 
the MS algorithm [7]-[9]. Among these algorithms, the offset 
MS (OMS) and normalized MS (NMS) algorithms [9] are 
the most widely used. Compared to the MS algorithm, the 
additional complexity is negligible since only an offset factor 
is introduced to the CN-update function. 
In practical, the decoder should be quantized first before 
being implemented [10]. However, the quantized decoders 
perform much worse than their floating-point counterparts 
due to the limited precision. Moreover, the offset factor is 
hard to be optimized in quantized decoders, especially for 
SG LDPC codes, in which the difference of row degree is 
particularly large [11]-[13]. The third generation partnership 
project (3GPP) considers two rate-compatible base graphs, 
BG 1 and BG2, for the channel coding of SG new radio 
(NR) [2]. These two LDPC codes are, strictly speaking, a 
concatenation of an LDPC code and a low-density generator 
matrix (LDGM) code [14]. The row degree of the LDPC part 
is relatively high. Moreover, all VNs in the LDGM part are 
degree-1 VN s, which are more sensitive to be erroneous due 
to lack of check messages. Therefore, since the offset factor 
can not be the optimal one in quantized decoders, the OMS 
decoder performs much worse than MS decoder on SG LDPC 
codes [12], which is contrary to the situation in floating-point, 
where the OMS algorithm always performs better than MS 
algorithm. 
In this work, we propose an enhanced OMS (EOMS) 
decoder for SG LDPC codes. Since the degree-1 VNs only 
connected to a unique CN, the reliability of check-to-variable 
(C2V) message has a substantial influence on these VNs. 
Therefore, an adaptive CN-update criterion is designed for 
extension checks by applying the third minimum value and 
consequently an adaptive offset factor can be obtained. Based 
on this modification, the error-correction performance of the 
SG LDPC codes is much improved, even better than the 
floating-point OMS decoding in the waterfall region. 
This paper is organized as follows. Section II recalls SG 
LDPC codes and quantized LDPC decoders. In Section il, 
the proposed EOMS decoder is described in detail. Section 
IV presents the simulation results, as well as the complexity 
analysis. At last, the conclusion is drawn in Section V.

Ill. THE PROPOSED ENHANCED OMS DECODER 
Though the AMS decoder improves the error-correction 
performance of SG LDPC codes, it is still not effective enough 
since the extension checks are decoded by MS, which is 
far from the optimal solution. To further improve the error­
correction performance of SG LDPC codes, an enhanced OMS 
decoder is proposed in this section. As stated before, only a 
unique CN is connected to degree-1 VNs. Therefore, they are 
more prone to be erroneous than other VNs since they can only 
be corrected by the unique extrinsic message passed from that 
CN, which means that the reliability of C2V message has a 
substantial impact on these VNs. Starting from improving the 
reliability of C2V messages, we propose a new CN-update 
criterion for extension checks by applying the third minimum 
value. 
Since MS and OMS decoders are the simplified versions 
of BP decoder, the C2V messages calculated by BP decoder 
are more reliable and thus the proposed CN-update criterion is 
designed to make the C2V messages approaching those calcu­
lated by the BP decoder. Let min1(s,.,.n) and min2(s,.,.n) 
denote the first and second minimum value in set s,.,.n, 
respectively. Unlike MS, OMS, and AMS decoders where only 
min1(s,.,.n) is used, as shown in Eq. (4), min2(s,.,.n) is also 
applied in the proposed EOMS decoder. Therefore, in addition 
to the first two minimum values, the third minimum value 
among all received V2C messages in a CN is also needed to 
generate the min2(s,.,.n) for each VN.

V. CONCLUSION 
In this paper, we propose an Enhanced Offset Min-Sum 
decoder aiming to improve the error-correction performance 
of SG LDPC codes. In order to reduce the error probability 
of degree-1 VNs, a new CN-update criterion is designed for 
extension checks. By introducing the third minimum value 
to the CN-update function, the offset values are adaptively 
selected and the reliability of the C2V message is significantly 
improved. Simulation results demonstrate that, with a small 
number of quantization bits, the proposed EOMS decoder 
shows a much better error-correction performance than the 
state-of-the-art one at the cost of small additional complexity, 
even can surpass the floating-point OMS decoder in the 
waterfall region.
