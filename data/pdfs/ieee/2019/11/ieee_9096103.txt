AbstractтАФFor the 5G and beyond, the UTW-OFDM has 
been proposed as one of the new-waveforms which can strongly 
suppress out-of-band emission (OOBE) and has high 
compatibility with the CP-OFDM-based system currently used 
for the LTE. In the UTW-OFDM system, interference generated 
by windowing is suppressed by using channel coding, and the 
practicality of UTW-OFDM has been shown for the IEEE 
802.11af system using the convolutional coding and the LTE 
system using the turbo coding Meanwhile, to our best knowledge, 
the UTW-OFDM-based 5G system using low-density parity-
check (LDPC) coding has not been presented. In this paper, the 
authors describe how the UTW-OFDM can be applied to 5G 
new radio (NR). Firstly, the authors point out that common 
calculation methods of the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) in the 
decoding 
process 
of 
the 
LDPC 
coding 
degrade 
the 
communication quality, especially in a high SNR environment. 
Then, an adjustment method of the LLR is proposed to mitigate 
the degradation of the communication quality and demonstrate 
that the proposed method strongly suppresses OOBE with 
maintaining the communication quality when applying the 
UTW-OFDM to the 5G NR.

I. INTRODUCTION  
The fifth-generation mobile communications (5G) system 
supports not only high data rate communications but also 
massive machine-type communications (mMTC) for the 
Internet-of-Things (IoT) applications [1]. Considerable 
spectrum resources will be consumed to accommodate an 
enormous number of devices. Therefore, it is necessary to 
improve the spectrum efficiency. One of the solutions is to 
suppress out-of-band emission (OOBE) of the transmission 
signal [2]. In the existing Long Term Evolution (LTE) and the 
upcoming 5G new radio (NR), the cyclic prefix-based 
orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (CP-OFDM) is 
applied as a transmission scheme. In general, the CP-OFDM 
requires a guard band in the frequency-domain as a margin 
due to its high OOBE caused by the discontinuity between 
successive CP-OFDM symbols. In the case of LTE, 10 % of 
the allocated bandwidth is used as the guard band. Therefore, 
further suppression of OOBE will curtail the unused guard 
band.  
As one of the transmission schemes enabling the 
significant reduction of OOBE, the universal time-domain 
windowed OFDM (UTW-OFDM) has been proposed [3]-[5]. 
By applying a time window with a long transition duration, 
the UTW-OFDM reduces OOBE tremendously. Although the 
time window with a long transition duration induces inter-
symbol interference (ISI) and inter-carrier interference (ICI), 
its negative effects can be alleviated by channel coding. The 
total performance of the UTW-OFDM, in essence, depends on 
physical layer parameters (i.e., numerology) and a channel 
coding scheme of the target system. Some literature reported 
the effectiveness of the UTW-OFDM by comprehensive 
evaluation for IEEE 802.11af using the convolutional coding 
[3] and LTE using the turbo coding [4], [5]. However, there is 
no report applying the UTW-OFDM to the 5G NR 
numerology using the low-density parity-check (LDPC) 
coding. 
In this paper, we apply the UTW-OFDM to the 5G NR to 
reduce OOBE and comprehensively evaluate its performance. 
As above-mentioned, the LDPC coding [6] is adopted as a 
mandatory channel coding scheme in 5G NR [7], and message 
passing (MP) algorithms [8] such as the sum-product 
algorithm [9] by using log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) of 
received bits are generally used for decoding. Firstly, we point 
out how common calculation methods of the LLR in the 
decoding process degrades the communication quality, 
especially in a high signal-to-noise power ratio (SNR) 
environment when UTW-OFDM is applied to 5G NR. Then, 
an adjustment method of LLR is proposed to mitigate the 
degradation of the communication quality. Finally, we 
demonstrate that the proposed method enables the UTW-
OFDM to achieve tremendous OOBE reduction for the 5G NR 
with maintaining the communication quality by the 
comprehensive 
evaluation 
of 
the 
OOBE 
reduction 
performance and block error rate (BLER). 
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. In Section II, 
the overview of the conventional CP-OFDM and the UTW-
OFDM is described. In Section III, the UTW-OFDM is 
applied to the 5G NR, and a novel LLR adjustment scheme is 
proposed. In Section IV, the UTW-OFDM-based 5G NR with 
the proposed LLR adjustment scheme is evaluated by 
computer simulation. The results show that the UTW-OFDM 
has the potential to drastically reduce the OOBE in the 5G NR 
by applying the proposed LLR adjustment scheme. Finally, 
we conclude this paper in Section V. 
II. TRANSMISSION SCHEMES 
A. CP-OFDM 
The CP-OFDM is one of the multi-carrier transmission 
schemes adopted to the existing LTE and the upcoming 5G 
NR. Modulated complex symbols are allocated to orthogonal 
subcarriers in the frequency domain. Then the subcarriers are 
converted to the time-domain OFDM symbol by using the 
inversed discrete Fourier transform (IDFT). The effects of a 
long delay multi-path channel are reduced by copying the end 
part of the OFDM symbol and prefixing it to the OFDM 
symbol. The 5G NR provides a variety of CP lengths and 
subcarrier intervals. While the CP-added OFDM symbol (i.e., 
CP-OFDM symbol) is a continuous signal, each transition 
point between successive CP-OFDM symbols has the 
discontinuity causing high OOBE [2].

III. UTW-OFDM FOR 5G NR WITH LDPC CODING 
In this section, we consider the applicability of the UTW-
OFDM to the 5G NR. First, we outline the LDPC coding (i.e.,  
a channel coding method in the 5G NR) and the sum-product 
decoding algorithm (i.e., a general decoding method for the 
LDPC coding). Then, we describe the problem in the 
application of the UTW-OFDM to the 5G NR. After that, the 
LLR adjustment method is proposed to solve this problem. 
A. LDPC Coding and Sum-Product Algorithm 
The LDPC coding is one of the linear codes that is defined 
by a sparse parity-check matrix (i.e., a small number of non-
zero elements). Notably, it is called the binary LDPC coding 
when the elements of the parity-check matrix are 0 or 1. Also, 
it is called the regular LDPC coding when the parity-check 
matrix has a constant number of non-zero elements in every 
row and every column. In other cases, it is called an irregular 
LDPC coding. In the 5G NR, the irregular binary LDPC 
coding is applied, and the model matrix [10] of the parity-
check matrix is selected according to the transport block size. 
After that, the parity-check matrix is generated using cyclic 
permutation.

B. Problems of Conventional LLR Calculational Method 
According to (13), since an absolute value of LLR is 
inversely proportional to the noise power, the absolute value 
of LLR becomes huge in a high SNR environment. In such 
environment, the possibility that likelihood of transmit bit 
represented by the LLR is incorrect (i.e., the sign of LLR is 
erroneous) is quite low; however, a tremendously large 
absolute value with an opposite sign is induced in rare cases. 
Fig. 2(a) is an example of the distribution of the LLRs 
calculated at the receiver when 16 QAM modulation is applied 
in the AWGN channel with ES / N0 = 3 dB. Here, the absolute 
values of some LLRs exceed 20. In the case that the sign of 
such LLR is incorrect, the errors cannot be corrected by the 
MP algorithms because the sum-product algorithm fixes the 
sign of the left side in (8) and (10). In particular, ISI and ICI 
are induced by a time window with a long transition duration 
when applying the UTW-OFDM. Thus, the probability that 
the sign of LLR is incorrect becomes high, even in a high SNR 
environment. Therefore, the LLR adjustment method is 
required to prevent the degradation of communication quality.

coding such as the LLR clipping method [12] and the LLR 
normalization method [13], [14] have been proposed. Besides, 
the normalized BP-based decoding and the offset BP-based 
decoding to adjust the LLR in (8), and the damping method 
that prevents the value of the message from diverging has been 
proposed [15], [16]. In this paper, we evaluate the 
conventional LLR clipping method and the conventional LLR 
normalization method to improve the communication quality 
since these methods allow relatively easy implementation. In 
the following sub-sections, the details of the conventional

IV. EVALUATION 
In this section, we evaluated the performance of the UTW-
OFDM-based 5G NR by computer simulation from the 
aspects of OOBE reduction and BLER. The simulation 
parameters are shown in Table I. In the evaluation, a simple 
transmission signal that continuously transmits the signal in 
units of one subframe was used. 
A. Out-of-band-Emission (OOBE) 
Fig. 4 shows the averaged relative power spectrum density 
(PSD) of the transmission signal of the UTW-OFDM-based 
5G NR with a resolution bandwidth of 100 kHz. The 
modulation scheme is QPSK, and the oversampling factor is 
four. The horizontal axis represents an offset frequency from 
the center frequency. Comparing PSD at the channel edge (i.e., 
at ┬▒2.5 MHz), the UTW-OFDM can remarkably reduce 
OOBE by increasing the window transition duration NTR. 
When NTR /N = 1, the UTW-OFDM can reduce OOBE by up 
to 69.4 dB compared to the conventional CP-OFDM. Note 
that the same OOBE reduction performance can be obtained 
regardless of the primary modulation scheme. 
B.   BLER 
BLER characteristics of the UTW-OFDM-based 5G NR 
were evaluated in a frequency selective fading environment. 
Channel estimation is performed by linear interpolation with 
the demodulation reference signal (DMRS) in the 5G NR 
allocated to the even-numbered subcarriers of the third and 
twelfth OFDM symbols. According to [17], a tapped delay 
line (TDL)-A model [18] with a delay spread of 30 ns is used 
as the channel model. The center frequency is set to 4.0 GHz, 
and the mobile terminal velocity is 3 km/h. Also, four-branch 
maximum ratio combining (MRC) is applied to the receiving 
antenna diversity. For the evaluations, BLER = 10тАУ3 (i.e., the

1)
Conventional LLR calculation method without 
LLR adjustment: 
Fig. 5 shows the BLER characteristics of the UTW-
OFDM-based 5G NR with QPSK, 16QAM, and 64QAM 
without LLR adjustment method. In the case of QPSK, BLER 
of 10тАУ3 can be achieved with NTR / N < 1/2. Meanwhile, BLER 
is degraded with NTR / N тЙе 1/2 because longer transition 
duration of the time window induces higher ISI and ICI, and 
the generation rate of incorrect LLR with huge absolute value 
becomes higher in a high ES / N0 environment. The same 
tendency was observed in the case of 16 QAM and 64 QAM. 
2)
With LLR clipping method:  
Fig. 6 shows the BLER characteristics of the UTW-
OFDM-based 5G NR with the conventional LLR clipping 
method for QPSK, 16QAM, and 64QAM. In this evaluation, 
the clipping factor k was set to 10 from our preliminary 
simulation. By adjusting LLR with the LLR clipping method, 
the degradation of the communication quality in a high ES / N0 
environment has been eliminated except for the case of 16 
QAM with NTR / N тЙе 1/2. However, the conditions of 16QAM 
with NTR / N = 1 and 64QAM with NTR / N = 1/2 cannot 
achieve BLER of 10тАУ3.

C. Trade-off between OOBE Reduction and BLER 
To confirm the potential of the UTW-OFDM-based 5G 
NR with the proposed LLR normalization method, we discuss 
the trade-off between the OOBE reduction and the BLER. Fig. 
9 shows the relationship between the averaged PSD at the 
channel-edge (i.e., OOBE reduction performance) and the ES 
/ N0 to achieve BLER of 10тАУ3 with various NTR. The proposed 
LLR normalization method was applied to the UTW-OFDM-
based 5G NR in this evaluation. The UTW-OFDM-based 5G 
NR can reduce the PSD at the channel-edge by about 33.7 dB 
in the case of QPSK and about 17.5 dB in the cases of 16 QAM 
and 64 QAM with maintaining almost the same 
communication quality as the conventional CP-OFDM. 
Furthermore, in the cases of QPSK, 16 QAM, and 64 QAM, 
the UTW-OFDM-based 5G NR can reduce OOBE by about 
69.4 dB, 58.1 dB, and 46.1 dB, respectively, when about 0.9 
dB, 1.4 dB, and 0.8 dB degradations of the ES / N0 to achieve 
BLER = 10тАУ3 are acceptable.
