A Reconfigurable Decoder for Standard-Compatible LDPC Codes and Polar Codes
Ting Lin, Shan Cao, Shunqing Zhang, Shugong Xu and Chuan Zhang†
Shanghai Institute for Advanced Communication and Data Science Key Laboratory of Specialty Fiber Optics and Optical Access Networks Joint International Research Laboratory of Specialty Fiber Optics and Advanced Communication
Shanghai University, Shanghai 200444, China †National Mobile Communications Research Laboratory, Southeast University, China
E-mail: {zjzslt, cshan, shunqing, shugong}@shu.edu.cn, †chzhang@seu.edu.cn

Abstract—With the raised requirements by 5G wireless communications, a reconfigurable decoder supporting both LDPC codes (for data plane) and polar codes (for control plane) is required. Due to the inherent structure differences between LDPC codes and polar codes, an straightforward combination of existing decoders is generally difficult. In this paper, we propose a joint decoding algorithm by analyzing the standard-compatible coding structure and re-investigating the merged BP algorithm. Based on that, we implement the joint decoding scheme using a pipelined hardware architecture and show that the proposed approach achieves higher hardware efficiency than traditional schemes. Decoding performance loss is negligible if compared with existing stand-alone LDPC or polar decoder.
Index Terms—LDPC code, polar code, reconfigurable architecture, standard-compatible
I. INTRODUCTION
Aiming to support dense, diversified, and mission-critical wireless traffics, the fifth generation (5G) wireless systems [1] have been identified as one of the key enablers for the information society beyond 2020. Based on the current progress, a hybrid solution to apply low-density parity-check (LDPC) code [2] for data plane and polar code [3] for control plane was agreed in the 3GPP radio access network meeting [4]. Although LDPC and polar codes have been proven to offer high throughput data transmission and reliable control signaling simultaneously, the decoding complexity is usually high, especially at the 5G terminal side. From the hardware resource point of view, a brute-force approach to build LDPC and polar decoders in a separate manner definitely results in hardware redundancy. Therefore, a reconfigurable architecture to support both simultaneously with refined decoding algorithm is expected [5].
Thanks to the high compatibility of belief propagation (BP) algorithm, a merged BP based decoding scheme was proposed in [6] with shared factor graphs. Nevertheless, the conventional reconfigurable architecture can only support limited code length and the concatenation approach to generate large codeword length is proven to be inefficient for LDPC codes and the straightforward extension to standard-compatible decoder is generally difficult. In this paper, we analyze the coding structure as specified in 3GPP document [7] and re-investigate

the BP decoding algorithm accordingly to figure out the common feature. By proposing a joint detection algorithm and pipelined hardware architecture, we are able to show with practical Field-Programmable-Gate-Array (FPGA) implementation that the proposed detection algorithm can support a standard compatible decoding requirements with 26.65 Mbps throughput, less than 36 µs delay and 82.1% hardware reuse efficiency for LDPC decoding, and 21.80 Mbps throughput, less than 24 µs delay and 76.0% hardware reuse efficiency for polar decoding. Meanwhile, our proposed scheme achieves higher hardware efficiency and comparable throughput. This decoder can be used for both LDPC and polar decoding, which serves a suitable architecture for battery limited 5G terminals.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. The joint polar-LDPC decoding is proposed in Section II. The full pipelined decoder is discussed in Section III. In Section IV, we provide the implementation results and comparison with the separated one. Finally, we conclude this paper in Section V.

B. Case Study for Polar Codes
In polar decoding mode, the RDU is reconfigured to 6 polar BCB blocks. Each BCB integrates 3 M units and 3 A units, and is divided to 2 pipeline stages. The data path of RDU in polar mode is highlighted as shown in Fig.2(b), and the grey ALUs and registers are in idle state at the illustrated cycle. BCB 1∼3 works in the A − M manner, while the otherill 3 BCBs works in the M−A manner. A BCB operation has a 2clock-cycle latency, and every two BCBs blocks are grouped to update messages of neighboring bits to avoid memory access conflicts.
In the proposed architecture, the reconfigure control unit is used to re-order the decoding flow, re-group decoding units and reconfigure the data path of each RDU. The memory controller connects the proposed architecture to the system memory, and is used to re-organize the intermediate data and reconfigure the memory for different codes and variable lengths of codeword. The memory organization is designed correspondingly to enable the modified decoding and data flow. There are two memory blocks, one for the LLR messages of LDPC codes, the other one for both the C2V messages of LDPC codes and L and R messages of polar codes.

In this section, we simulate and implement the proposed reconfigurable decoding scheme according to the 3GPP R15 standard [7]. (4992, 960) LDPC and (1024, 512) polar codes are considered and the proposed architectures are implemented and evaluated under Altera Stratix IV series.

As a baseline performance, we assume the 16-bit floatingpoint parameters are applied and the overall BER versus SNR curve is evaluated as the performance metric. The decoding performance and the overall hardware resources for different data widths and data precisions are depicted in Fig. 3. Based on the simulated results, we show that 6-bit data width provides sufficient resolution for both polar and LDPC codes in terms of BER versus SNR performance, and requires relatively small amount of hardware resources in the meantime.

V. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
In this paper, we implement a reconfigurable LDPC/polar decoder with pipelined structure by exploiting same common operations in decoding algorithm for LDPC and polar decoding. The results of FPGA implementation show advantages of hardware utilization over a separate manner. The decoding architecture will be expanded to support all code structures in 3GPP R15 standard. To further improve the throughput of the decoder, a clustered architecture composed of more RDUs and implemented by ASIC is one of our future work.
