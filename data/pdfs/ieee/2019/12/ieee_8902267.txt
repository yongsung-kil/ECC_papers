A Study on Iterative Decoding With LLR Modulator by Neural Network Using Adjacent Track Information in SMR System
M. Nishikawa 1, Y. Nakamura 1, Y. Kanai 2, H. Osawa1, and Y. Okamoto1
1Graduate School of Science and Engineering, Ehime University, Matsuyama 790-8577, Japan 2Department of Engineering, Niigata Institute of Technology, Kashiwazaki 945-1195, Japan

Our previous work focused on a log-likelihood ratio (LLR) computed as the decoding reliability by a posteriori probability (APP) decoder in a two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR) system using shingled magnetic recording (SMR). In this article, we propose a neural network LLR modulator to reduce the influence of pattern dependent medium noise and to perform efficiently iterative decoding using a low-density parity-check (LDPC) code. In this study, we clarify that the performance improvement of iterative decoding can be obtained by considering LLRs of the adjacent track bits affecting the decoding bit as the input of the neural network. Furthermore, we clarify that the application of a hybrid genetic algorithm (HGA) selects LLRs by using the adjacent track LLRs affecting the decoding bit and realizes more effective iterative decoding.
Index Terms— Log-likelihood ratio (LLR), neural network, two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR).

I. INTRODUCTION

V ARIOUS information storage devices coexist for various needs with the information explosion. In particular, hard disk drives (HDDs) are important as a mass-storage device. In recent years, shingled magnetic recording (SMR) [1] assuming two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR) in HDDs has been adopted as a recording method to realize higher recording density. We have previously proposed waveform equalization using a two-dimensional finite impulse response (TD-FIR) filter [2], [3] and the inter-track interference (ITI) canceller [4] as a signal processing method for SMR. Furthermore, we showed that effective iterative decoding can be realized by a neural network log-likelihood ratio (LLR) modulator [5] selecting and modulating likely LLRs correctly decoded by the a posteriori probability (APP) decoder for the decoding target track in low-density paritycheck (LDPC) coding. However, the influence of the ITI from the adjacent tracks remains in the APP decoder output. In this article, we propose a new LLR modulator using a neural network considering LLRs for the adjacent tracks. We also study the selection of LLRs in the adjacent tracks by the neural network learned by hybrid genetic algorithm (GA) (HGA) [6] which is a combined GA and back propagation algorithm (BPA).

The average size of the magnetic grains of the granular medium model, the grain diameter dispersion, and the average nonmagnetic grain boundary width were set to 5.0 nm, 1.0 nm, and 1.0 nm, respectively. We assume that the areal recording density is 4 Tbits/in2. The bit length and the track pitch are also set to 7.3 and 22.1 nm, respectively [4]. Therefore, each channel bit is composed of about five magnetic grains. In the writing process, we assume that the media switching field distribution follows a Gaussian distribution with a mean of 2 kOe and a standard deviation of 5%. The maximum field intensity from the write head was about 16.9 kOe [9], when a writer with an isosceles triangle main pole shown in Fig. 2 was used. In the reading process, the decoding target track and the adjacent tracks were read by the array head with three readers shown in Fig. 2, and different, additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) was added to each waveform to represent system noise. We also calculated the sensitivity function of the reader by micromagnetic simulation using finite element method. The signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) of the system noise at the reading point was defined as SNRS = 20log10(A/σS) [dB], where A is the saturation level of the isolated waveform and σS is the root-mean-square (rms) value of the system noise in bandwidth of the channel bit rate fc. The channel bit response including R/W process on the decoding target track was equalized to the partial response class-I (PR1) target by an equalizer composed of three low-pass filters (LPFs) having a cutoff frequency xh = 0.4 normalized by fc, and a TD-FIR filter having the number of taps/track Nt = 15 [2], [3]. The output waveform from the PR1 channel [10] was iteratively decoded by turbo equalization performed between an APP decoder and an sum-product (SP) decoder [11]. The LLR modulator selected the more likely LLRs in the LLR sequence based on the information obtained from the estimated recording pattern and gains them as the SP decoder works well. Note that the proposed neural network LLR modulator modifies the APP decoder output LLR using the LLRs of adjacent tracks as well as the decoding target track. The SP decoder iteratively decodes for the LDPC code until the maximum iteration number isp is reached. Furthermore, the external LLR sequence obtained by the SP decoding was returned to the APP decoder again, and the turbo equalization for the target track was performed with the maximum iteration number iglobal times. After the given number of iterations, the output data sequence was determined by a hard decision, and RLL decoding. Finally, the BER was calculated by comparing input and output sequences.

V. CONCLUSION
In this study, we have proposed a LLR modulator using a neural network for considering LLRs of adjacent tracks affecting the decoding target bit. We also have studied bit selection in adjacent tracks and simplification of configuration by applying HGA. As a result, the correct LLR increases by using the proposed LLR modulator, and effective iterative decoding is performed by considering the adjacent tracks. The application of HGA and the removal of connection weights smaller than “0.10” also selects the bits in the adjacent tracks that most affect the decoding target bit, and achieves more effective iterative decoding.
