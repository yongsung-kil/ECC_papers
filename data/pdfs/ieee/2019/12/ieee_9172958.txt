Modified Iterative BP-CNN Decoder Under Correlated Noise with Symmetric α-stable Distributions
Senlin Li1,2, Sihui Zheng1,2, Jingwen Zhang3, Xiang Chen1,2*, Zesong Fei3 1School of Electronics and Information Techology, Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou 510006, China 2Key Lab of EDA, Research Institute of Tsinghua University in Shenzhen (RITS), Shenzhen 518075, China
3School of Information and Electronics, Beijing Institute of Technology, Beijing 100081, China *Email: chenxiang@mail.sysu.edu.cn

Recently, an iterative belief propagation-convolutional neural network (BP-CNN) decoder has been proposed to process the lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) decoding under correlated noise. However, in many practical scenarios, correlated noise may have extra special characteristics, such as impulse. Therefore, when used in such scenarios, there will be some space for the performance improvement of the BP-CNN decoder, since its loss functions cannot make full use of the special characteristics of correlated noise. Fortunately, the impulsive property of correlated noise can be effectively described by the symmetric α-stable (SαS) distribution model. Thus, we propose a novel loss function to train a well-behaved CNN model under correlated noise with SαS distributions. In order to take advantage of the features of SαS distributions captured by CNN, the proposed loss function involves a probability density function (PDF) estimation of the residual noise and a similarity detection. The similarity detection uses the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence method to compare the similarity between the estimated PDF and the Gaussian distribution. In the BP-CNN decoder, the residual noise is defined as the difference between the actual noise and the estimated noise. The effectiveness of our modifications for the BP-CNN decoder will be demonstrated with simulation results.
Index Terms—LDPC, BP, CNN, SαS distribution, PDF estimation
I. INTRODUCTION
C HANNEL coding and decoding technologies are widely used in various communication systems as effective methods to ensure reliable transmission of information. At present, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] are able to yield the performance close to the Shannon capacity under AWGN channels with the low complexity belief-propagation (BP) decoding algorithm [2]. However, in practical communication systems, the noise may exhibit correlations. Especially in some special scenarios, correlated noise is non-Gaussian and exhibits an impulsive character [3]. For example, the underwater acoustic noise in sonar applications [3] and the atmospheric noise in radar communications [4]. Many studies have proven that the symmetric α-stable (SαS) distribution which is a nonGaussian distribution with lager tailing is a very successful

LDPC codes will encounter a performance degradation under correlated noise [7]. The most straightforward method to address this issue is to transform correlated noise to white noise [8]. However, this method requires matrix multiplication, of which the complexity will increase with the increasing length of codes. Furthermore, it may change the structure of the coded symbol sequence thus complicates the BP decoding. Fortunately, inspired by recent advances in deep learning, a special belief propagation-convolutional neural network (BPCNN) decoder has been proposed in [8] to help BP decoding effectively for LDPC codes under correlated noise. In the BP-CNN architecture, the BP decoder is used to estimate coded bits and make an inaccurate estimation of channel noise, while CNN is used to remove estimation errors from the BP decoder by learning the correlation of channel noise. However, when channel noise is correlated noise with SαS distributions, it has impulsive characteristics. Then, there will be space of performance improvement for BP-CNN, since it has not taken features of the impulsive characteristic other than the correlation.
In order to solve this problem, we carefully analyze the mechanism of the BP-CNN decoder and find the performance of the BP-CNN decoder is determined by the functioning of CNN. It is common knowledge in deep learning that the performance of a network depends critically on the choice of loss functions for the model training [9]. Therefore, it is reasonable to modify the original loss functions in the BPCNN decoder to improve the decoding performance.
In this paper, we propose a novel loss function based on the characteristics of correlated noise with SαS distributions. This loss function includes the accuracy of the noise estimation and a detection of the similarity degree between the estimated probability density function (PDF) of the residual noise and the Gaussian distribution. The similarity detection we adopt is based on the Kullback-Leibler (KL) Divergence method. This loss function enables CNN to make better use of features of the SαS distribution and to generate a special distribution output which is an available start for the BP decoder. For convenience, we rename BP-CNN with the revised loss function as Modified BP-CNN. Moreover, in our experiments, we find that both the

II. SYSTEM MODEL
In this section, the signal model will be firstly described. Furthermore, the SαS distribution and the complex SαS distribution models will be particularly introduced in detail as an important foundation for our research.

A. Signal Model
At the transmitter, a block of uniform distribution bits x of length K is encoded to a binary codeword u of length N through the LDPC encoder. The codeword u is then mapped to a symbol vector s through the QPSK modulation. Then the symbol vector s will be transmitted with the additive nonGaussian correlated noise. This noise vector, denoted as w of length N , satisfies the complex SαS distribution which will be explained in detail in the next subsection.
The received signal y at the receiver can be written as

y = s + w.

A. Original BP Limitations under Correlated Noise
The BP decoding algorithm is well known to show a good performance in AWGN channels [11]. In the BP decoding algorithm, an important assumption is that all coded bits and check bits are independent respectively. Thus, the probability model of the BP decoding algorithm operation based on the factor graph can be described as [8]

B. BP-CNN Limitations under Correlated Noise with SαS Distributions
The BP-CNN decoder was proposed in [8] to overcome these limitations of BP decoding caused by correlated noise. However, when analyzing the BP-CNN decoder from the perspective of the loss function, only the correlation is used as a learning feature in the CNN training process, so it is a universal decoder for correlated noise. And the performance of the decoder is possible to be further improved when processing for some special correlated noise. For correlated noise with SαS distributions, in addition to the correlation, it also has the impulsive characteristic. This characteristic can be reflected in the (SαS) s PDF which can be expressed with power series expansions and be obtained by feasible methods [4]. Therefore, when the BP-CNN decoder is used to train this noise, the absence of extraction of impulsive features may result in the training model not matching well with the actual noise.

V. SIMULATION RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS
In this section, we present some simulation results to verify the effectiveness of our modifications. We compare the BER performance of the BP-CNN decoder with the loss functions in [8] and the Modified BP-CNN decoder under correlated noise with SαS distributions. Furthermore, when 9 k-bit LDPC codes are adopted, we modify the relevant parameters of CNN and show the BER performance of Deep BP-CNN and Modified deep BP-CNN.
In our simulations, QPSK modulation, (576,144) LDPC codes which are adopted in [8] and (9144,3048) LDPC codes are used, respectively. The experiments are implemented in TensorFlow [17]. The network is initialized using the Xavier initialization [18] method. To better illustrate our modifications for the iterative BP-CNN decoder, we set α = 1.8 to generate noise samples. The value of λ will influence the performance of the revised loss function. Through our experiments, we find that it is valid to set λ = 0.1.
Before training the network, the training data is first generated under different channel conditions set as Eb/N0: {0, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 2.5, 3.5}dB. The data of each channel condition occupies the same proportion. In order to train the coefficients of convolution kernels, 2 million data samples are used as the training set and 0.2 million data samples are used as the test set. The number of iterations between CNN and the BP decoder is set to one.
We use the conventional mini-batch gradient descent method to train the network. We adopt the Adam optimization [19] method to search the optimal network parameters. During the

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we find an effective method to improve the decoding performance under correlated noise with SαS distributions by analyzing the mechanism of the BP-CNN decoder. Therefore, a new loss function for CNN is proposed. In addition, through experiments, we found that when using the BP-CNN decoder, it is ineffective to modify the loss function when the CNN training process fails due to the change of the length of codewords. We need to adjust the number of CNN layers and the size of convolution kernels according to the trend of the length of codewords. We have carried out some simulations to show the effectiveness of modifications for the BP-CNN decoder under correlated noise with SαS distributions.
