Abstract—This paper presents a general decoder architecture for 5G LDPC and polar codes. To achieve the hardware reuse maximization, both LDPC and polar codes are decoded by belief propagation (BP) algorithm on sparse bipartite graphs. However, by counting the number of short cycles on the polar bipartite graphs, we observe that numerous short cycles cause the poor performance than conventional decoding algorithms, like successive cancellation (SC) and BP decoding. To reduce the impact of short cycles, a LDPC-like BP list (BPL) decoding algorithm is proposed for polar codes, which is applied on L different polar bipartite graphs constructed by different permuted polar factor graphs. Finally, the simulation shows that the proposed general decoder for polar codes can obtain beyond SC and BP error performance with lower decoding latency and computational complexity, and even near successive cancellation list (SCL) performance in high signalnoise ratios (SNRs).
Keywords-sparse bipartite graph; general decoder; belief propagation; LDPC codes; polar codes; list decoding
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes [1] which proven near the Shannon limit performance were proposed by Gallager in 1961. Since the complicated iterative decoding algorithm was hard to be implemented, LDPC codes had not received much attention in the next thirty years. In 1996, Mackay and Neal proposed a graph-based BP decoding algorithm which makes LDPC codes practical for implementation [2]. Nowadays, BP has become the mainstream decoding algorithm for LDPC codes.
Polar codes [3] were proposed by Arikan in 2009, which are the first type of channel codes that are proven to achieve channel capacity for binary-input discrete memoryless channels (B-DMCs). Meanwhile, the SC decoding algorithm was also proposed in [3], and then some improved algorithms on SC were proposed, such as SCL [4], CRCaided SCL(CA-SCL) [4], etc. All decoding algorithms mentioned above are serial decoding methods, which cause large decoding latency and hard to meet the requirements of high throughput in hardware implementation. In [5], Arikan

proposed a hardware-friendly BP decoding algorithm. However, the performance of BP decoding is not as good as SCL decoding due to numerous short cycles in factor graphs. Inspired by SCL decoding, the BP List (BPL) decoding was proposed to improve the performance of BP decoding by permuting factor graphs [6-7]. [8] proposed a one-to-one mapping between the permutations on the factor graph layers and the permutations on the codeword positions, which is more suitable for hardware implementation.
LDPC and polar codes are adopted by 3GPP as the channel coding schemes in the 5G eMBB scene by virtue of excellent performance [9]. A general architecture for decoding LDPC and polar codes is very important for a 5G mobile terminal. Simply splicing the decoding algorithms of the two codes would bring a large hardware overhead. Therefore, studying the general decoding architecture by considering hardware reuse maximization could reduce the chip area and power consumption. [10] proposed a general decoding architecture which supports 4 types of channel codes including LDPC and Polar codes, but the soft-in-softout module (SISO) of polar codes is separate from the other three types of codes. [11] proposed a reconfigurable decoder for LDPC and polar codes by sharing min-sum units on BP algorithm. However, because the factor graphs of LDPC and polar codes are different, the BP decoding processes for LDPC and polar codes are different and hard to be scheduled to share computation logic by one SISO.
In this paper, we propose a reconfigurable general decoder architecture for LDPC and polar codes in 5G standards. [12] presents that polar codes can be regarded as LDPC-like codes. Inspired by [12], proposed architecture adopts LDPC BP decoding algorithm for both LDPC and polar codes in one SISO. It is easier to achieve the hardware reuse maximization by using the same decoding algorithm. However, Due to numerous short cycles in bipartite graphs of polar codes, the performance of the method proposed in [12] is not as good as conventional decoding algorithm for polar codes, such as BP and SC. To solve this problem, we propose a LDPC-like BPL decoding algorithm based on different bipartite graphs, and then analyze the error

III. PROPOSED GENERAL DECODER
In this section, a general architecture for 5G LDPC and polar codes is proposed.
A. Layered BP Decoding for 5G LDPC Codes
5G LDPC codes are quasi-cyclic (QC) and suitable to decode by Layered BP algorithm, which is proven to obtain good performance and parallelism [14]. Compared with conventional BP decoding, Layered BP decoding can update CN-to-VN and VN-to-CN more quickly to reduce the LDPC complexity of QC-LDPC decoder, and it is widely used in industry.
B. Proposed LDPC-like BP List Decoding for 5G Polar Codes
Since we adopt BP decoding for LDPC codes, we also adopt BP decoding for polar codes to achieve the hardware reuse maximization and more throughput. Original BP decoding is applied on ݊-stages factor graphs so that the processes of decoding is different from LDPC BP decoding. Therefore, original BP decoding for polar code is hard to share many computation logics by one SISO, and LDPC-like BP decoding based on a sparse bipartite graph is more suitable for polar codes to realize a general decoder architecture.
However, the performance of LDPC-like BP decoding is not as good as conventional decoding algorithms, such as SC and BP. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed a reconfigurable general decoder architecture for 5G LDPC and polar codes. Polar decoder is adopted by LDPC-like BPL algorithm which can be fully shared with LDPC decoder. The simulation results show that the proposed general decoder for polar codes can obtain beyond SC and BP error performance with lower decoding latency and computational complexity, even near SCL performance in the high SNRs. The drawback of the proposed decoder is that The Error performance is not as good as SCL and CA-SCL algorithms. Besides, the permutation of polar factor graphs is randomly generated, so the performance of proposed LDPC-like BPL decoding is not the optimal.
In the future, we will consider the better permutation of polar factor graphs, and FPGA implementation results are given in future works for more hardware-friendly general architecture, which will obtain better performance and complexity.
