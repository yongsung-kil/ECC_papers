An Efficient Encoding Method for Spatially Coupled Low-Density Parity-Check Codes

Zhanji Wu*, Min Zhao, Shuoshuo Wang
Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications BUPT
Beijing, China e-mail: wuzhanji@bupt.edu.cn

Abstract‚ÄîIn this paper, an efficient encoding scheme for spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) codes is proposed. In order to lower the error floor, we optimize the degree distribution of SC-LDPC codes and propose a split variable nodes algorithm constructing sub-matrices with large girth of SC-LDPC codes. The corresponding sliding window decoding is studied as well to fasten the decoding convergence. Simulation results show that on the AWGN channel, SC-LDPC codes can obtain better error performance, lower decoding complexity and faster decoding convergence as compared with the LDPC block codes (LDPC-BC).
Keywords-spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SCLDPC); windowed decoding; degree distribution; split variable nodes algorithm
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were introduced by Gallager, which is a class of linear codes described by sparse parity-check matrices. In 1999, superimposed convolutional structure was applied to LDPC, extending coding schemes. Then, the concept of spatial coupling is promoted into LDPC. Kudekar explains that ‚Äúspatially coupled‚Äù codes perform so well due to the increasing of the belief-propagation (BP) threshold of the new ensemble to its maximum possible value.
Recently, the theoretical research and application of SCLDPC codes have achieved good results. The current researches mainly focus on the design and construction of SC-LDPC codes, decoding algorithms, performance analysis and applications in various fields. A new design algorithm of irregular SC-LDPC codes with non-uniform degree distributions using linear programming (LP) is proposed by [1]. Spatially coupled protographs for window decoding (WD) and arbitrary rate is optimized in [2]. There are theoretical lower bounds on the syndrome former constraint length for the SC-LDPC codes and new code design techniques that approach or achieve such theoretical limits are proposed by [3].
With the semi-infinite matrix structure, traditional blockwise encoding and decoding are no longer suitable for SCLDPC codes. As a result, SC-LDPC codes can exploit shift register for encoding and sliding window (SW) iterative algorithms based on belief propagation (BP) for decoding[4]. Through effective decoding termination, message reuse, and

amplification, an improving windowed decoding of SCLDPC codes is introduced [5].
In addition to the good performance of the SC-LDPC itself, the degree distribution is a significant factor affecting the threshold of the waterfall. On the other hand, another important factor is the well-constructed sub-matrices of SCLDPC codes with large girth. In this paper, we propose a split variable nodes algorithm which can achieve large girth.
The paper is organized as follows. In Section II, the parameter definitions of the SC-LDPC are briefly introduced. We present how SC-LDPC codes encode and propose a split variable nodes algorithm constructing SC-LDPC codes with better degree distribution. In Section III, the process of sliding window decoding is given. In Section IV, the BER performances of the optimized SC-LDPC codes are simulated, compared with LDPC block codes. We also analyze the parameters in the sliding window decoding. Conclusions are drawn in Section V.

V. CONCLUSIONS
An efficient encoding construction method for spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) codes is put forward. The degree distribution of the SC-LDPC codes is optimized, and a split variable nodes algorithm is proposed to achieve the larger girth. With the investigation of different
sliding window decoding algorithms and D parameter, we
find out that the offset Min-sum with the factor 0.4
andD 1has the best error performance. Compared with the
LDPC-BC codes, SC-LDPC codes show the advantages in terms of better error performance, lower decoding complexity and faster decoding convergence. Therefore, the proposed SC-LDPC code is simple, efficient and reliable, thus suitable for the low-error-floor and low-latency communications. We can further study the SC-LDPC codes design with the rate compatibility, hybrid automatic repeat request (HARQ) and higher order modulation.
