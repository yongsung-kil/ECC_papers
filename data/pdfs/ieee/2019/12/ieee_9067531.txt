SEARCHING FOR LARGE GIRTH QUASI-CYCLIC SPATIALLY COUPLED LDPC CODES
ZHAN-JI WU*, SHUO-SHUO WANG, MIN ZHAO
School of Information and Communication Engineering Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Beijing 100876, China
E-MAIL: *wuzhanji@bupt.edu.cn

Abstract: In this paper, we present an approach which searches the
large girth quasi-cyclic (QC) spatially-coupled (SC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes by lifting the coupled protographs matrices. At first, a set of performance measures is employed to facilitate the construction of protographs. Then the QC lifting procedure searches the large girth base matrices for SC-LDPC based on the protographs. A systematic design of SC-LDPC code with stringent structural constraint condition makes the encoder more efficient. Furthermore, it is easy to synchronize the process of encoding and sliding windowed decoding by using grouping period time-varying sub-matrices. We find that the performance the QC-SC-LDPC is superior that of the block QC-LDPC, and the QC-SC-LDPC has lower complexity as well as delay of than block QC-LDPC.
Keywords: Spatially coupled low-density parity-check; Protographs;
Time-varying; Sliding windowed decoding
1. Introduction
SC-LDPC codes have recently captured more attention because of the capacity-approaching ability and become potential candidates for future lightwave transmission systems. SC-LDPC codes are divided into two categoriesпјҢ strongly an weakly coupled SC-LDPC codes. according to the size of the syndrome former memory and the size of the sub-matrices, maximizing the syndrome former memory and constraining the size of the sub-matrices were called strongly coupled SC-LDPC codes [1]. The original work of Thorpe has been recognized that protographs provide an efficient method of constructing LDPC codes [2]. The protographbased construction of code ensembles with good asymptotic properties can be obtained by applying a graph-lifting procedure [5]. The lifting procedure used in protographs can maximize the girth of the Tanner graph and help to the implementation of parallel decoder.
Ensembles of SC-LDPC have the attractive characteristics of thresholds approaching capacity with

belief-propagation (BP) decoding [3]. The windowed decoding scheme can exploit the semi-infinite convolutional structure of the SC-LDPC codes, while maintaining the key strengths of the iterative BP decoder, such as the low complexity and superior performance [7]. Furthermore, using half the number of decoding iterations, the layered decoding applied for SC-LDPC can achieve the same performance as the traditional message passing algorithm [4]. In this paper, we use the sliding windowed decoding scheme based on layered decoding architecture.
The paper is presented as follows. In Section 2, we review the basic concepts of the strongly coupled SC-LDPC codes and give the encoding procedure of the QC-SC-LDPC codes with time-varying stringent structure. In section 3, we show how to construct a good protograph codes and reduce the searching number of states. Simulation results are investigated in section 4. We conclude in section 5.

5. Conclusions
In this paper, we give an efficient encoding algorithm for strongly coupled QC-SC-LDPC by grouping method, packaging the time-varying QC-SC-LDPC into a larger timeinvariant QC-SC-LDPC, which has the advantage of the synchronization of encoding and sliding windowed decoding. The base protomatrix can be constructed more flexible according to the proposed criteria. The QC lifting algorithm makes the strongly coupled QC-SC-LDPC matrix achieve lager girth. When the delay and complexity of d4 SC LDPC are 50% and 67.5% of the d3 BC-LDPC respectively, they have nearly the same performance.
