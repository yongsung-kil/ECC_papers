Linear-Complexity ADMM Updates for Decoding LDPC Codes in Partial Response Channels

Xiaopeng Jiao , Jianjun Mu , Yu-Cheng He , and Weinan Xu

Abstract— This letter investigates the application of alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) in decoding low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes over partial response (PR) channels. Unlike the ADMM decoding in memoryless channels, the ADMM decoding in PR channels becomes involved with the correlation among adjacent code symbols. As the update equations for code symbols constitute the most complicated part of the ADMM decoding in PR channels, this letter focuses on their derivation by making use of the iterative fashion of ADMM and incorporating the use of a penalty function. A notable advantage of the proposed ADMM penalized decoding algorithm is that its complexity increases linearly with the memory length of PR channels. Simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can achieve error performance comparable with that of Turbo equalization (TE) in the waterfall region. It can also outperform TE at high signal-to-noise ratios as iteration grows.
Index Terms— ADMM, complexity, LDPC codes, partial response channel, turbo equalization.
I. INTRODUCTION
A LTERNATING direction method of multipliers (ADMM) provides a decomposition-coordination way to efficiently solve large-scale optimization problems [1]. As an efficient implementation of linear programming (LP) decoding of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes in memoryless channels [2], ADMM decoding has recently received much attention [3]–[12]. When the objective function is adjusted by a penalty term, the so-called ADMM penalized decoding can achieve more coding gains [13], [14].
Partial response (PR) channels are a class of practical channel models for communication systems and magnetic recording systems. To cope with the inter-symbol interference (ISI) in PR channels, Turbo equalization (TE) [15], [16] has been deeply investigated, where the channel detector and the channel decoder operate iteratively by exchanging soft information between them. The BCJR detector usually yields an exponential complexity in the channel memory length.
There also exist numerous results on the decoding of LDPC codes in PR channels based on the optimization methods. In [17], the maximum likelihood decoding in PR channels was relaxed to be a quadratic programming (QP) problem and

II. ADMM PENALIZED DECODING FOR PR CHANNELS
Consider a binary LDPC code with parity-check matrix H = (Hji)M×N . In the Tanner graph, the variable node vi and the check node cj are connected by the edge (vi, cj) if Hji = 1 for i ∈ N {1, 2, . . . , N } and j ∈ M {1, 2, . . . , M }. Let Nv(i) = {j ∈ M : Hji = 1} be the index set of the check nodes connected to vi, and Nc(j) = {i ∈ N : Hji = 1} the index set of the variable nodes connected to cj. Then, the degrees of vi and cj are given by dv,i = |Nv(i)| and dc,j = |Nc(j)|, respectively.
For a finite set S of points {p1, . . . , pk} in Rn, the convex hull of S is defined as conv(S) = {θ1p1 + · · · + θkpk : θ1 + · · · + θk = 1, θi ≥ 0, i = 1, . . . , k} [21]. A polyhedron is defined as the solution set of a finite number of linear equalities and inequalities. A bounded polyhedron is called a polytope [21].
A PR channel of memory D can be expressed as a discrete-time finite impulse response (FIR) sequence (h0, h1, . . . , hD) ∈ RD+1 with additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN). V. CONCLUSION We have proposed an ADMM penalized decoding algorithm for LDPC codes over PR channels. The contribution lies mainly in the derivation of update equations for code symbols, where the difficulty caused by the correlation among adjacent code symbols is circumvented by exploiting the iterative fashion of ADMM.
