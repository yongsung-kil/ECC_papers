Abstract—This paper is concerned with decoding waves for spatially-coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) ensembles on binary memoryless symmetric (BMS) channels. We propose an approach termed as interpolated density evolution (IDE) to predict the velocities of decoding waves. In this approach, we approximate the densities of decoding waves via interpolation and establish two transfer functions for the update of interpolation functions. Comparison results indicate that the proposed IDE approach achieves a good trade-off between computational cost and accuracy as compared with the standard coupled density evolution (CDE) approach and the extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) approach based on Gaussian approximation (GA). This highlights the attractiveness of the proposed approach in the analysis of SC-LDPC ensembles on general BMS channels.
I. INTRODUCTION
Spatially-coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) ensembles [1] are found to have better performance than their underlying ensembles under belief propagation (BP) decoding [2]-[3]. This can be interpreted with the mechanism termed decoding wave propagation [4], which describes how reliable messages are generated at the two boundaries of the coupling chain and propagate inward as the iteration proceeds. Due to this mechanism, the BP thresholds of SC-LDPC ensembles saturate to the maximum-a-posterior (MAP) thresholds of their underlying ensembles universally [5].
The velocity analysis of decoding waves has attracted much attention in recent years. For scalar coupled systems, several wave-like solutions are established in [4], implying that the velocities will converge as the iteration proceeds. Some bounds for the velocities are derived in [4] and [6]. Recently, an approximation formula based on potential functions is derived in [7]. Several numerical examples are presented therein to demonstrate the accuracy of this formula.
The majority of the above studies focus on scalar coupled systems. To the best of our knowledge, very few efforts have been devoted to the decoding waves for SC-LDPC ensembles for transmission on general binary memoryless symmetric

(BMS) channels. The interested reader may refer to the related sections in [4] and [7] for details. The main difficulty arises from the fact that the existing approaches such as standard coupled density evolution (CDE) (see Subsection III-B in [5] for details) and extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) charts (see discussions in Section IV in [4] and Subsection V-B in [7]) are either computationally intensive or inaccurate. Due to this reason, we propose an approximation approach termed as interpolated density evolution (IDE). In specific, we approximate the densities of decoding waves via interpolation between some fixed points of uncoupled density evolution with onedimensional functions, thereby reducing the computational cost compared with the CDE approach. More importantly, the transfer functions in the IDE recursion enable us to predict the velocities more accurately than the EXIT approach based on Gaussian approximation (GA). Numerical results for the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise channel (BIAWGNC) case presented at the end of this paper highlight the advantages of the proposed IDE approach in the asymptotic analysis of SC-LDPC ensembles.

III. A PHENOMENOLOGICAL STUDY ON FPS AND DPS
In this section, we aim to highlight the relationship between FPs and DPs: A DP can be thought of as a path in X parameterized by t, which goes through some FPs in S. More importantly, this DP can be accordingly divided into several transition regions, each of which moves apart from others in a solitonic manner as the iteration proceeds.

B. Two Numerical Examples
In this subsection, we consider two specific irregular degree distributions and set the channel entropy H(c) such that the FP set S contains exactly NS = 3 FPs, i.e., S = {(x0, y0) = (x−, y−) , (x1, y1) , (x2, y2) = (x+, y+)}. In Example 1, the degree distributions are given by
λ1 (x) = 0.15x + 0.35x2 + 0.50x16, ρ1 (x) = x9 (11)
and the channel entropy H(c) = 0.440. From Fig.1, we can easily justify that x(l) indeed goes through x1, and thereby P = {(x0, y0) , (x1, y1) , (x2, y2)} with IP = {0, 1, 2}. As a result, x(l) contains two transition regions, one of which is between x0 and x1 and the other is between x1 and x2. V. NUMERICAL RESULTS
In this section, we give a comparison between the proposed IDE approach and the GA-based EXIT approach for the irregular ensembles defined in Subsection III-B on the BIAWGNC.

Since we limit ourselves to the asymptotic analysis in this
paper, we adopt the CDE approach as the benchmark.
The results for the SC-LDPC(λ1, ρ1) ensemble are depicted in Fig.4, where NQ denotes the number of transition regions predicted by the GA-based EXIT approach. We can find that, for most samples of H(c), we have NP = NQ. This implies that, by using the IDE approach, one can predict the number of transition regions correctly over a wide regime of H(c).
In addition, the gap between the CDE approach (black dots)
and the GA-based EXIT approach (green crosses) is noticeable while the IDE approach gives a good estimate of {vj}. Taking H(c) = 0.455 as an example, the results calculated by the CDE approach show that there exsits NP − 1 = 1 transition region that moves toward the left-hand side (v1 < 0). We can predict this correctly via the IDE approach since NQ − 1 = 1 and vˆ1 < 0. On the other hand, we could be misled by the GAbased EXIT approach which implies NQ − 1 = 2 transition regions, with the upper one moving toward the right-hand side.
