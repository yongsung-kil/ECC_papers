Error Crrection for Read-hot Data in 3D-TLC NAND Flash by Read-disturb Modeled Artificial Neural Network Coupled LDPC ECC

Daiki Kojima, Toshiki Nakamura and Ken Takeuchi
Department of Electrical, Electronic, and Communication Engineering, Chuo University, Tokyo, Japan Email: kojima@takeuchi-lab.org

Abstract — Read-disturb Modeled Artificial Neural Network Coupled LDPC ECC (RDNNLDPC) is proposed to correct errors of read-hot data for 3D-TLC NAND flash. Conventional ANN-LDPC is optimized to correct errors of read-cold data. However, ANN-LDPC does not correct errors of read-hot data. To correct errors of read-hot data, this paper analyzes how input parameter and model change. As a result, measured results of proposed RDNN-LDPC extend acceptable read cycle of 3DTLC NAND flash by 10-times.
I. INTRODUCTION 3D-TLC (triple level cell) NAND flash (Fig. 1(a)) has complicated errors in both read-cold and hot data because of 3D construction that vertically stacks wordline (WL) (Fig. 1(b)). To correct errors in read-cold data where data-retention (D.R.) error is dominant, ANNLDPC has been proposed [1]. However, this ANNLDPC is specialized to correct read-cold data. Therefore, to correct errors in read-hot data where readdisturb (R.D.) error is dominant, this paper proposes Read-disturb Modeled Artificial Neural Network Coupled LDPC ECC (RDNN-LDPC).
II. ANALYSIS OF ERRORS IN 3D-TLC NAND FLASH Figs. 2-4 show comparison of error characteristics between read-cold and hot data. In read-cold data, the D.R. error has mainly two patterns of electron movement, vertical charge de-trap and lateral charge migration [2]. D.R. error causes VTH decrease (Fig. 2(a)). Conversely in read-hot data, the R.D. error causes VTH increase and decrease (Fig. 2(b)). In read-hot data, impact of neighboring cell of VTH-state is similar to that of read-cold data (Fig. 3). Moreover, bit error rate (BER) is different at each WL because the size of hole varies (Fig. 4(a)). Upper WL is impacted by D.R. and lower WL is impacted by R.D. (Fig. 4(b)). As shown in Figs. 2-4, error characteristics of read-cold and hot data are different. Ⅲ. PROPOSED READ-DISTURB MODELED ARTIFICIAL
NEURAL NETWORK COUPLED LDPC ECC Proposed SSD controller is shown in Fig. 5. Lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) ECC corrects errors based on log-likelihood ratio (LLR) which represents reliability of each bit. Accurate LLR is necessary for fast and effective LDPC decoding (Fig. 6) [3]. In NAND flash, BER is available to calculate LLR. Proposed RDNN-LDPC automatically predicts BER of read-hot data to correct R.D. errors. Fig. 7 shows neural network (NN) training. Proposed NN is supervised learning. NN needs information of various parameters from training dataset per 1 bit data in NAND flash. Though ANN-LDPC used read-cold data as training dataset, RDNN-LDPC needs to use read-hot

data to predict R.D. errors (Fig.8). Conventional ANNLDPC has D.R. time as input parameter, but proposed RDNN-LDPC has read cycles instead of D.R. time to correct R.D. errors. Target VTH-state, page type, WL number and WL(n±1) cell VTH-state are binary data as input parameter because these data are discrete value. Whereas, D.R. time, read cycles and write/erase cycles are continuous value. In conventional ANN-LDPC, parameter of D.R. time is used as it is. However, to input large number of such as read cycles and write/erase cycles, these parameters need to be normalized (Fig. 9). This paper assumes to types of normalization such as linear and log scaling. By giving answer data to NN during training, RDNN-LDPC improves predictive accuracy of read-hot data just like ANN-LDPC for readcold data [1]. When RDNN-LDPC predicts BER, it needs input parameter per 1 bit data. RDNN-LDPC after training predict probability of error and not error as output. Then, Probability of error is set as BER (Fig. 10) for calculating LLR.
Ⅳ. Measurement Result Table 1 shows parameter normalization pattern. In conventional ANN-LDPC, D.R. time and write/erase cycles are normalized log fitting. In proposed RDNNLDPC, 4 normalization patterns are evaluated. Fig. 11 shows measured BER and predicted BER per read cycle. Proposed RDNN-LDPC brings predicted BER close to measured BER by 4.3-times higher than conventional ANN-LDPC. Figs. 12 and 13 show BER of per VTH-state read from NAND flash and WL respectively. Proposed RDNN-LDPC precisely predicts BER of each VTH state and WL compared with the measured BER. LDPC decoding results are shown in Fig. 14. In conventional ANN-LDPC, when the read cycles increase, errors cannot be corrected even if small write/erase cycles. Proposed RDNN-LDPC corrects errors in wide range of read cycles and write/erase cycles.
Ⅴ. Conclusion This paper proposes RDNN-LDPC to correct R.D. error. Table 2 summarizes this work. RDNN-LDPC improves acceptable BER by 2.1-times and acceptable read cycle is extended by 10-times compared with conventional ANN-LDPC.
