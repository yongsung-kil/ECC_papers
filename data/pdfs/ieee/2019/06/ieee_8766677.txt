Abstract—This paper presents a novel scheme to construct a large class of high-rate low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes based on dual-diagonal matrix (DDM). After estimating the feasible sizes of the submatrices, we introduce two principles and two important parameters (interval and increment), based on which the parity-check matrices (PCMs) with 4-cycle free are constructed successfully. The designed PCMs can be used to generate high performance LDPC codes. Experiments confirm that the proposed scheme has better performance in waterfall and error floor region than the advanced LDPC coding methods. Our method can construct a large number of PCMs of similar decoding performance, which increases reliability and security during high speed communication.
Index Terms—channel coding, 5G, LDPC code, parity-check matrix, interval, increment
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were initiated by Gallager in 1963 [1]. In information theory, LDPC code is a linear error correcting code, a method of transmitting a message over a noisy transmission channel [2]. It is finding increasing use in many channel coding fields requiring reliable and highly efficient information transfer, e.g. the fifth generation (5G) of digital communication.
Of its relevant versions, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes in [3] [4] [5] achieve a remarkable performance which is close to the Shannon limit [6], but huge computation and storage resources are needed during code construction. To improve its construction efficiency, a method to construct QC-LDPC codes with dual–diagonal matrix (DDM) structure has been explored in [7]. However, cycles of length 4 and 6 exist in parity-check matrix (PCM) can degrade LDPC iterative decoding performance [8]. Although PCM with DDM yield high encoding efficiency, setting the proper size of submatrix and selecting the submatrix offset to eliminate four-cycles remain to be studied. In addition, the key to guarantee security performance is the massive sample space of LDPC codes, which can make it harder to decode. Aiming at the two demands, we present a novel method to construct different PCMs for LDPC codes.
In this paper, a loose sufficient condition under which 4cycle-free state can exist is first developed. After that, two corresponding methods to select parameters are introduced

based on whether the submatrix size is a prime number or not. Furthermore, a method to boost the sample space of LDPC codes is presented. Lastly, we demonstrate the simulation results to confirm the performance of our scheme.
