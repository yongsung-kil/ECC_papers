An Optimized Lifting Method Based on PMP for LDPC in PON System

Jiazhe Hu, Xin Qin and Chuanchuan Yang
The authors are with the State Key Laboratory of Advanced Optical Communication Systems and Networks, Peking University, Beijing 100871, China, email: yangchuanchuan@pku.edu.cn

Abstract: We propose a new lifting method based on parallel vector message passing (PMP) called LPMP to generate quasi-cyclic LDPC codes, which can derive a (BER) performance improvement up to 0.4dB. В©2020TheAuthor(s)

1. Introduction Driven by the continuously increasing demand for large capacity data services, the upgrade for high speed passive optical network (PON) system is of great importance. To meet the stringent optical power budget, current PON standard systems usually use forward error correction (FEC) [1]. As a special type of FEC codes, low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes which were first raised by Gallager [2], have prominent performance close to the Shannon limit in decoding. Moreover, LDPC has been standardized in G.hn, WiMAX, IEEE 802.3an, IEEE 802.11n and so on. As we all know, LDPC codes are divided into two categories: random LDPC and structured LDPC. Quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes [3], which have low error floor over additive white gaussian noise (AWGN) channel and low complexity are the most promising class of all the structured LDPC codes. Recently, IEEE P802.3ca 100GEPON has decided to deploy QC-LDPC codes in upstream and downstream channels for next-generation Ethernet passive optical network (NG-EPON) system [4].
In order to adapt to different requirements of storage space and bit error rate (BER) in various communication systems, we often need codes of different lengths. Lifting is a good method to obtain longer QC-LDPC codes. However, the traditional modulo-lifting method [5], based on decreasing the number of the shortest cycles is not always useful. In this paper we propose an improved approach for lifting QC-LDPC codes based on parallel vector message passing (PMP) algorithm [6], called LPMP which is more effective than the traditional lifting method. In our proposed algorithm, we introduce the metric called Cycles Metric (CM) to evaluate the performance of QCLDPC codes. Applying CM to the LPMP, we can obtain lifted QC-LDPC codes with BER performance improvement up to 0.4dB.

The LPMP method uses the metric CM as the key for estimating the performance of H. Deduced from the definition in Eq. (2), CM can not only characterize the number of short cycles with length 2l , but also utilize the parameter пҒў to ingrate the effects of cycles with different lengths on the overall Tanner graph. In the Tanner graph, the number of edges is constant. Hence, when the number of cycles with length 2l decreases, the number of cycles with other lengths may increase. In our proposed LPMP method, all the cycles of length 2l in the Tanner graph are considered as a whole with the parameter пҒў and they are measured with CM. The CM takes cycles of all lengths into account and finds a balance between the cycles and lengths. Therefore, the new metric CM can better characterize the performance of LDPC codes. The traditional method only considers the number of the shortest cycles and neglects other short cycles which makes QC-LDPC codes canвҖҷt be effectively lifted. In our LPMP method, CM is used to replace the number of the shortest cycles for judging if the performance of H is better. The

Two test QC-LDPC codes with different characteristics are lifted by different times. The Code 1 is the code (648,486) in IEEE 802.11ad with code rate 0.75 [7]. The Code 2 is code (2304, 384) generated in [8]. The parameters of the two codes are shown in Table 1.
