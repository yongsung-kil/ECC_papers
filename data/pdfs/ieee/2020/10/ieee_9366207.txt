An Iterative Bit-Flipping Decoding Algorithm For Binary Reed-Muller Codes∗
Yong-Ting Ni (倪詠庭), Cheng-Yu Pai (白承祐), and Chao-Yu Chen (陳昭羽) Department of Engineering Science National Cheng Kung University Tainan 701, Taiwan, R.O.C.
Email: {n96074104, n98081505, super}@mail.ncku.edu.tw

Abstract—This paper presents an iterative bit-flipping (BF) decoding algorithm for binary Reed-Muller (RM) codes. The BF decoding algorithm is a hard-decision decoding algorithm. According to the updated hard reliability measures, one bit of the received hard-decision sequence is flipped at a time in each iteration. The simulation results show that the proposed BF decoding algorithm outperforms the conventional hard-decision majority decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
The Reed-Muller (RM) code is a class of multiple-errorcorrection codes. In the past, RM codes are used in wireless communications, specially in deep-space communications. RM codes were first discovered by Muller [1] and the conventional decoding scheme of RM code, that is the majority logic decoding, was proposed by Reed in 1954 [2]. Recently, the comparisons of RM codes and polar codes were presented in [3], [4]. Ever since Reed and Muller’s discovery, a number of efficient decoding schemes have been constructed. In 1995, Schnabl and Bossert proposed a generation of Reed-Muller codes by multiple concatenations and provided a decoding procedure using soft-decision information [5]. In 1999, a maximum-likelihood decoder which uses a distance-preserving map and multiple fast Hadamard transforms was presented by Jones [6]. Then, a simple maximum-likelihood decoding for the first-order RM codes with low complexity was developed in [7] whereas a maximum a posterior decoding algorithm for the first-order RM codes was proposed in [8]. In 2000, [9] summarized a number of approaches for decoding these codes and presented soft-decision signal-domain decoding algorithms. In the same year, a new soft-decision majority decoding algorithm was presented by revising the conventional majority decoding [10]. In [11], the authors modified the majority decoding using the sum-product [12] and minsum [13] decoding algorithms from low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [14] at highest-order bits for RM codes. In [15], [16], RM codes were decoded using their parity-check matrices just like LDPC codes. Although various algorithms for decoding these codes have been designed, all are softdecision decoding. In this paper, the hard-decision decoding is taken into consideration with low complexity.

This paper presents a novel hard-decision decoding algorithm for Reed-Muller codes by exploiting the idea of bitflipping decoding for LDPC codes. The proposed algorithm is devised based on the min-sum operation with hard reliability measures of the received sequence. We flip one bit of the received hard-decision sequence at a time in each iteration. Therefore, this proposed algorithm is called an iterative bitflipping decoding algorithm. Through decoding iterations, the hard-decision sequence is updated. Moreover, to reduce the number of iterations and complexity, a termination process is also provided.
The numerical experiment results show that the proposed decoding algorithm provides a better performance than the existing conventional majority decoding over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. In hard-decision decoding, AWGN channel can be regarded as the binary symmetric channel (BSC).

III. PROPOSED BF DECODING ALGORITHM

Based on the conventional majority decoding algorithm, we make the following modifications: 1) Binary additions of conventional algorithm are replaced by min-sum operations; 2) instead of majority voting, the reliability measures of information bits are obtained by the addition of vote information; 3) an iterative process is employed to update the hard-decision sequence in each iteration; 4) in each iteration, it allows flipping one bit of the hard-decision sequence at a time.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS

In this section, we evaluate and compare the block error rate (BLER) performance over the AWGN channel for the majority decoding algorithm [2], hard-decision maximum likelihood (HDML) decoding algorithm, and our proposed BF decoding algorithm. To demonstrate the convergence performance, we set the maximum iteration Imax = 100. The complexity of the BF algorithm is Imax times more compared to the majority decoding algorithm.
Figs. 1 to 3 demonstrate the BLER performances for RM(1, 5), RM(1, 6), and RM(2, 7), respectively. In Fig. 1, the BF algorithm achieves 0.34 dB gain over the majority decoding algorithm at the block error rate of 10−6 and performs 0.29 dB away from the HDML decoding. As it can be seen from Fig. 2, at the block error rate of 10−5, the BF algorithm outperforms the majority decoding algorithm with 0.45 dB gain. The BF algorithm also performs well for secondorder RM codes. In Fig. 3, the BF algorithm can achieve about 0.55 dB performance gain over the majority decoding algorithm at the block error rate of 10−4.

V. CONCLUSION In this paper, we have presented an iterative bit-flipping algorithm for decoding Reed-Muller codes. This algorithm is based on the concept of bit-flipping decoding algorithm for LDPC codes. Using the min-sum operation, the reliability measures of information bits can be obtained. Then, we can flip one received bit at a time in each decoding iteration according to the reliability measures. The proposed BF decoding algorithm is a hard-decision decoding and hence has low computational complexity. The results of experiments show that our proposed BF decoding algorithm has 0.34 to 0.55 dB performance gain over the hard-decision majority decoding algorithm.
