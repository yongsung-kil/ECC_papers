AbstractÔÇöIn recent years, the demand for low latency and high reliability in industrial have been increasing rapidly. To meet these requirements, we propose a new decoding architecture of low-density parity-check convolutional codes (LDPC-CC). The proposed decoding architecture reduces the distance of the neighboring processors by using the rationale of compact pipeline decoder. At the same time, the stopping rule is employed in the process of iteration and the weighting factor is added to reduce the error propagation caused by the update of the parity-check node, thus accelerating the convergence of the decoding. The results show that the proposed algorithm has about 0.3 dB gain compared with the on-demand variable node activation (OVA) algorithm when the bit rate error (BER)
is 10´Çş4 and maximum number of iterations is 10. Meanwhile,
the initial delay and storage requirement are reduced by about 50% and the average number of iterations per decoded bit is also reduced largely.
KeywordsÔÇöLDPC-CC, weighting factor, compact pipeline decoder, initial delay, storage requirement
I. INTRODUCTION
With the development of Industry 4.0, the requirements for high reliability and low delay have become stricter than before, especially in industrial wireless communication [1] [2]. In industrial wireless communication systems, if there are errors at the receiver caused by noise in the transmission, the actuators may cause unpredictable dangers to the operating equipment and reduce production efficiency. For example, the loss of effective information in the alarm system will endanger people's lives and property. Hence, the messages must be transmitted reliably in communication system [3].
To improve the reliability of information transmission, channel coding is introduced into modern communication systems [4][5]. Low-density parity-check convolutional codes (LDPC-CC), a kind of channel codes, were first proposed by Felstr├Âm and Zigangirov in 1999 as the convolutional counterparts of the Low-density parity-check codes[6][7]. Since it is a kind of error correcting code approaching the Shannon limit, it has attracted a lot of attention [8]. The pipeline decoder architecture is proposed in [7] for the special structure of LDPC-CC. This decoder allows for parallelization of the iteration by several processors working in different areas of the Tanner graph. Then the layer decoding and on-demand variable node activation (OVA) algorithm based on the pipeline decoder are given in [9] and [10] respectively to improve the BER performance by changing the rules of message transmission schedule. Furthermore, based on the OVA algorithm, the stopping rule is applied for LDPC-CC to reduce decoding complexity, through reducing the average number of iterations per bit and the compact pipeline decoder reduces

the half of the initial delay and storage requirement without obvious performance loss by using the overlapped architecture [11][12]. However, compared to OVA algorithm, the compact pipeline algorithm has the same large decoding complexity and the stopping rule has not reduced the decoding initial delay and storage.
In order to solve these questions, we propose a new decoding architecture for LDPC-CC in this paper. The new decoding architecture is based on the compact pipeline decoder architecture. In this structure, the stopping rule is employed to decide whether the processor iteration could be stopped or not. Moreover, to improve the decoding performance of LDPC-CC effectively, different weighting factors are simulated and the optimal value of weighting factor is employed to the update of the parity-check node. The BER curve of proposed algorithm and other existing classical decoding algorithms are simulated by using MATLAB. In addition, the storage requirement and initial decoding delay of different decoding algorithms are also given in this paper. The results prove that the proposed algorithm could achieve the better performance than OVA algorithm in the same simulation conditions. Compared to the OVA decoding algorithm, the storage requirement and initial decoding delay can be cut in almost half and the average number of iterations per decoded bit is greatly reduced.
The remainder of this paper is constructed as follows: The related basic knowledge of LDPC-CC is introduced in Section II. Section III is devoted to describe the proposed decoding architecture in detail. On condition of the Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel assumption, Section IV analyzes the BER performance of different decoder architectures through the MATLAB simulation. Meanwhile, the storage requirement and initial decoding delay of different decoder architectures are also given in Section IV. Finally, conclusions are given in Section V.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose a new method to improve the decoding performance of LDPC-CC. Through the MATLAB simulation, the proposed algorithm and other classical decoding algorithms, such as pipeline algorithm and OVA algorithm, are analyzed and compared. A large number of simulation results show that the BER performance of proposed algorithm performs better than the OVA algorithm. In addition, compared to the OVA algorithm, the initial latency and storage requirements were reduced by nearly 50% on account of the using of the overlapping processor architecture. The average number of decoding iterations per bit is also reduced by using the

stopping rule in the decoding process. These results also show that the traditional decoder architecture would be an efficient way to decode LDPC-CC when the improved decoding architecture is employed. Since the low latency plays an important role in the requirements of industrial information transmission, how to further reduce the decoding dalay will be a future work.
