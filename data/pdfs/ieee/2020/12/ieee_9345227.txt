Syndrome-Aided Gradient Descent Bit Flipping Algorithm for LDPC Decoding

Yuang Huang
College of Electronic Information and Optical Engineering, Nankai University Tianjin, China
e-mail: yuanghuang99@yahoo.com

Haiyang Liu
Institute of Microelectronics Chinese Academy of Sciences
Beijing, China e-mail: liuhaiyang@ime.ac.cn

Abstract—Gradient descent bit flipping (GDBF) algorithm is an important low-complexity method for decoding LDPC codes. In particular, the multi-bit GDBF decoding has the best decoding performance among BF algorithms if a suitable threshold is prescribed. However, extensive computer searches are needed to find such a threshold for a specific LDPC code. In this paper, we propose a syndrome-aided GDBF (SAGDBF) algorithm to address the problem. The thresholds in our algorithm are adaptively adjusted according to the syndrome values in the iterative process. Simulation results for different LDPC codes suggest that the error performances of the proposed SAGDBF algorithm are close to and even better than the original multi-bit GDBF algorithm at a relatively small number of iterations. In addition, the complexity increased in our proposed algorithm is negligible compared with the original multi-bit GDBF algorithm. Hence, the proposed algorithm is suitable for practical purposes.
Keywords-LDPC Codes; bit flipping algorithm; gradient descent bit-flipping (GDBF); syndrome
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, devised by Gallager in the 1960s [1], have received much attention in the recent years. It has been demonstrated that a welldesigned long LDPC code can achieve excellent bit error rate (BER) performances under iterative belief propagation (BP) based decoding algorithms [2], [3]. However, the complexity of these decoders is relatively high, which is unsuitable for applications with strict constraint requirements. In contrast, bit-flipping (BF) is a class of low-complexity decoding methods for LDPC codes, whose performance is worse than that of the BP-based algorithms in general. In order to tackle the problem, several methods for improving the performance of BF decoding have been presented [4]-[6].
In 2010, Wadayama et al. proposed a novel BF algorithm based on the optimization theory, referred to as gradient descent bit flipping (GDBF) [7], which can work in the single-bit flipping manner or the multi-bit flipping manner. In particular, a carefully-designed multi-bit GDBF decoder can achieve better BER performances compared with the single-bit GDBF decoder and other known BF decoders. In order to obtain satisfactory performances, the multi-bit GDBF decoder should preset suitable thresholds at different signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) values, which needs extensive simulations for a specific LDPC code. Several adaptive threshold methods [8]-[10] have been proposed to address

the problem. Their performances are close to or even better than the original multi-bit GDBF decoder. However, we still need computer searches in advance to find the near-optimal parameter values used in the methods for a target LDPC code. To avoid the threshold selection, the authors in [11] provided a method for estimating the number of flipped bits without using threshold. However, the method has to search for multiple maximum values, which may cause large decoding latency.
In this paper, we propose a syndrome-aided GDBF decoding algorithm for LDPC codes. Our algorithm does not need extra simulations to determine the thresholds or other parameters. Instead, the thresholds in our algorithm are adaptively adjusted according to the syndrome values in the iterative process. Compared with the original multi-bit GDBF decoder in [7], the complexity increased in our proposed algorithm is negligible, and the extra computations can be easily implemented in practice. Besides, the proposed algorithm can flexibly switch from the multi-bit manner to the single-bit manner or conversely. Additionally, unlike the method in [11], the multi-bit flipping process can be performed in the parallel manner and the decoding latency can be reduced. Simulations on LDPC codes with various code lengths and rates suggest that the performances of the proposed algorithm are almost the same as and even better than the original multi-bit GDBF algorithm at a relatively small number of iterations. All these suggest that the proposed algorithm is amicable for practical purposes.
The paper is organized as follows. A simple review of GDBF algorithm is provided in Section II. The proposed syndrome-aided GDBF decoding algorithm is described and analyzed in Section III. Simulation results are presented in Section IV. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.

We know from the figure that the proposed SAGDBF decoder achieves about 0.5 dB gain over the multi-bit GDBF decoder. Fig. 4 shows the performance of a rate-8/9 LDPC code of length 1908. We notice that the SAGDBF decoder has wonderful performance behavior in high SNR regions, while the performance is inferior than the multi-bit GDBF decoder in low SNR regions. One possible reason is that the threshold T can be further improved for in low SNR regions for high rate codes. This is a direction for our future study.

V. CONCLUSION This paper proposed a syndrome-aided GDBF decoding algorithm for LDPC codes. The proposed algorithm can

calculate the near-optimal inversion threshold θ adaptively, which is general for different LDPC codes. Simulation results show that the performances of the proposed SAGDBF algorithm are almost the same as and even exceed than the original multi-bit GDBF algorithm with less iteration numbers. Furthermore, the complexity increased of the proposed SAGDBF algorithm is insignificant compared with the original multi-bit GDBF algorithm. Hence, the proposed SAGDBF algorithm is attractive from the practical point of view.
