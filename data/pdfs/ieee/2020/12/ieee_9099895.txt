Fully Parallel Circular-Shift Rotation Network for Communication Standards
Hassan Harb , Member, IEEE, and Cyrille Chavet , Senior Member, IEEE

AbstractŌĆöThis brief sheds light on a formal approach to design an innovative class of permutation network called Fully Parallel Circular-shift rotation Network. Thus, the contribution of such a design is the capability to process - in parallel - more than one set of elements with different lengths and permutation constraints. Yet, processing several sets simultaneously, arises from the increasing demand for higher throughput rate for applications such as the incoming 5G codes. Furthermore, the ability to handle several different sets of elements in parallel reduces the number of idled components in the designs (which improves the performances/area ratio). Meanwhile, independent circularshifter network blocks are generated in order to process the different sets simultaneously. Hence, the cost in terms of number of Multiplexers (MUXs) is low compared to the best existing works we know in the state-of-the-art, with the same number of stages.
Index TermsŌĆöCircular-shift rotation network, 5G LDPC codes, parallelism, multiplexers, barrel shifter.
I. INTRODUCTION
T HE BASIS of a fundamental evolution of recent communication protocols and inter-operability connections (e.g., Wireless broadband, IoT, M2M and more), will be constituted by a modern networking criteria such as Cognitive Radio, SDR, 5G, . . . , etc. where the latter [1] collects various radio signals (LTE-A, WiFi/WiMax) in licensed or free bandwidth; via a combined heterogeneous and flexible network; under the adoption of new Error Correction Code (ECC) families which are the Polar codes [2] and Quasi-Cyclic Low Density Parity Check (QC-LDPC) [3]. Consequently, this brief highlights our research work on an interconnection network of a highly parallel QC-LDPC decoder; for efficient and high throughput parallel decoding; which requires a convenient architecture relying on many Processing Elements (PEs) that are concurrently utilized in decoding the received information [4], via being connected with several memory banks (RAMs) through a dedicated Interconnection Network (IN) that transfers data between PEs and RAMs, based on predefined access orders (defined in the standards). Accordingly, several approaches have been proposed ([5], [6], [7], [8] to cite only a few), proving that it is a very complex and time consuming task to design efficient parallel hardware architecture for frame processing. On the other hand, QC-LDPC codes are constructed based on a prototype matrix via subrogating the non-null elements

Consequently, the objective of this brief is to demonstrate interconnection network architecture, called Fully Parallel Circular-shift rotation Network (FPCN), which can handle various frames in parallel with different permutation constraints applied to each of these frames, where such restrictions must be achieved with the minimum architectural cost (i.e., area and power consumption).

II. RELATED WORKS
As described in the introduction, 5G QC-LDPC standard [1] is dependent on the codeŌĆÖs rate and size; with expansion factor sizes of 51 values, ranging between 2 and 384. Therefore, this validates the hardware point of view concerning the inevitable ability of the interconnection network in performing rotations on vectors of variable sizes, along with parallel processing on several heterogeneous concurrent frames for the sake of the throughput enhancement. Among the vast research field on interconnection networks, very few works (as best as we know) target our studied drawback on how to design a lowcost parallel interconnection network architecture (in terms of area and power consumption), with high performances in terms of throughput and flexibility/adaptability. For instance, the authors in [9] suggested a high throughput 5G LDPC encoder based on a Barrel Shifter, but without the capability to deal with different frame lengths and distinct circular shift rotation values because of the classical shifters deployed. Hence, flexibility can be added on the encoder architecture of their work. However, since our demonstrator in this brief is based on the QC-LDPC of the 5G standard, a dedicated Barrel Shifter network [10] could be interesting; although it is dedicated to perform circular shift rotation on constant length vectors, which contradicts with the objective of this brief in terms of taking advantage of the performances potentially offered by 5G standard.
Additionally, considering the targeted standard and for the purpose of high throughput; the authors of [11], presented a parallel Wi-Max LDPC code architecture based on two classical Barrel Shifters of size 96 followed by a merge stage, making their proposal relatively costly compared to our approach (more details later in this brief). III. PRINCIPLES OF OUR MODEL
Our proposed method is derived from the idea of partitioning the interconnection network in several independent blocks that can be combined to interleave one or more sets of elements. Thus, for a given set of elements, interleaved by b blocks, the main obstacle will be to split up this set in b subsets with respect to the modular construction proposed for our network.
For example, let L = {asŌłÆ1, . . . , a0} be a set of vector (or frame) lengths that could or could not be shifted in parallel, where {s, ai} Ōłł {N+}2, i = s ŌłÆ 1, . . . , 0. So, the maximum number of inputs for the interconnection network is LM = max(L) where the design of this LM-Barrel Shifter cannot be fulfilled by [10] or [15], because they could not propose parallel frame processing. Hence, for every vector length (or frame length) in L, we compute its greatest odd divider (g.o.d.) where the resulting set of computed odd values is called Lodd. Then, we select the highest value in Lodd (called aodd). For example, let L = {2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12}, then the g.o.d. of 2 is equal to 1, the g.o.d. of 3 is equal to 3. . . , and the g.o.d. of 12 is equal to 3. Thus, Lodd = {1, 3, 1, 3, 1, 3} and aodd = max(Lodd) = 3.

B. Application for 5G LDPC Codes
The set of lifting sizes of the 5G LDPC codes is L = {2, 3, 4, . . . , 320, 352, 384} [1]. In this case, aodd = 15 and a start with a 15-GBS block. Duplicating 15-GBS then adding a trading stage constitute 30-FPCN and duplicating 30-FPCN then adding a trading stage form 60-FPCN. Hence, this process iterates until a 480-FPCN block is designed. Indeed, a 480-FPCN covers many cases of parallelism for set of vectors having lengths less than or equal to 240 where the order of parallelism differs with the size of inputs. For instance, two sets of lengths 240 can be processed simultaneously while four sets of lengths 120 can be processed simultaneously.

V. CONCLUSION AND PERSPECTIVE
In this brief, an innovative method to design Fully Parallel Circular-shifter Network was introduced via the following procedure: 1) Analysis of the sets of frames to be shifted, in order to identify the aodd value; 2) Design of aodd-GBS core block and 3) Iterative extension of the core block till the desired length and order of parallelism are covered. For a given circular-shift value, the permutation passes in two steps: 1) Trading stages operation and 2) aodd-GBS operation.
