Velocity Analysis of BP Decoding Waves for SC-LDPC Ensembles on BMS Channels: An Interpolation-Based Approach

Zhonghao Zhang , Kexin Zhang, Zhangyou Peng, Dan Zeng, and Mingjun Dai

Abstract— This paper is concerned with the dynamics of spatially-coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) ensembles for transmission on general binary memoryless symmetric (BMS) channels under belief-propagation (BP) decoding. The decoding waves of such ensembles are found to exhibit solitonic behavior, propagating along the Tanner graphs at asymptotically constant velocities. A low-complexity approach termed interpolated density evolution (IDE) is proposed to predict the decoding wave velocities. In this approach, the densities of a decoding wave are approximated by interpolating between some fixed points of an uncoupled DE recursion with one-dimensional functions. Two transfer functions for updating these interpolation functions in the IDE recursion are established and a simple strategy is introduced to deal with the coexistence of multiple transition regions. In addition, a threshold analysis is developed based on two ansatzes, explaining why our approach can achieve a good trade-off between computational cost and accuracy, as illustrated with some numerical examples at the end of this paper.
Index Terms— Density evolution, potential functions, threshold saturation, spatial coupling, low-density parity-check codes.

The velocity analysis of decoding waves has attracted much attention in recent years and has been applied to the design of SC-LDPC ensembles such as degree distribution optimization, the way of coupling, e.g., [8] and [9]. In the binary erasure channel (BEC) case, some wave-like solutions are established in [4], implying that the velocity of a decoding wave will converge as the iteration proceeds. Several bounds for decoding wave velocities are derived in [4] and [10]. Some of them can be calculated based on degree distributions and a fixed point of uncoupled density evolution (DE), thereby eliminating the need of coupled DE (CDE) for SC-LDPC ensembles. By investigating CDE in the continuum limit, the authors of [11] derived a formula for the velocity and applied it to general spatially-coupled systems such as generalized SC-LDPC ensembles.
In some of the above-mentioned works, many efforts have also been devoted to general BMS channels. In such scenarios, one may accurately predict the velocities in the asymptotic limit (in the sense of infinite codeword lengths of the underlying ensembles) via the CDE approach. However, this approach is impractical due to its expensive computational cost, even for a modest length of the coupling chain. Alternatively, one may exploit low-complexity techniques such as extrinsic information transfer (EXIT)-like approaches based on Gaussian approximation (GA), as done in [4] and [11]. Unfortunately, these GA-based approaches will produce a noticeable error. Refer to the examples of threshold calculation in [4] and velocity approximation in [11] for details.
In this paper, we propose an approach termed interpolated density evolution (IDE) based on two ideas. The first idea is to approximate the density of a decoding wave (termed the decoding profile in this paper) via interpolation. This is motivated by an observation that a decoding profile can be thought of as a path that “goes through” some fixed points of uncoupled DE. Motivated by this, we approximate a decoding profile by interpolating densities between uncoupled DE fixed points with one-dimensional functions. As a result, the computational cost of the analysis can be greatly alleviated. The second idea is to establish variable-node (VN) and check-node (CN) transfer functions in the IDE recursion.

This allows us to derive a threshold analysis for the IDE approach, explaining why it is superior to the GA-based EXIT approach, at least for the channel entropy close to the BP threshold of an SC-LDPC ensemble.
Furthermore, we investigate the coexistence of multiple transition regions in a decoding wave. Similar work has been done in [10] in the BEC case, where it was suggested to analyze the velocity of each transition region in a separate manner, but following the same line as in the single transition region case. This idea relies on the assumption that the number of multiple transition regions is known as a priori, which is computationally impractical for general BMS channels. In the proposed IDE approach, we present a low-complexity strategy to predict this number based on the monotonicity of velocities.
To demonstrate the advantages of our approach, at the end of this paper, a comparison of the IDE, the CDE and the GA-based EXIT approaches on the binary symmetric channel (BSC) and the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise channel (BIAWGNC) is presented for regular and irregular SC-LDPC ensembles. Numerical results show that the IDE approach can accurately predict the number of transition regions and estimate their velocities with an acceptable computational cost and outperforms the GA-based EXIT approach. This highlights the attractiveness of our approach in the analysis of SC-LDPC ensembles on general BMS channels.
The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces several preliminary notations. In particular, we focus on a subset of uncoupled DE fixed points (Definition 4 and Remark 3). Section III presents a phenomenological study on the solitonic behavior of decoding profiles. The main purpose of this study is to show that how the solitonic behavior is related to those fixed points described in the previous section (Ansatz 1). This motivates our IDE approach proposed in Section IV, where decoding profiles are approximated via interpolation (Definitions 11-13). Furthermore, a simple strategy based on the monotonicity of velocities is introduced to cope with the coexistence of multiple transition regions (Algorithms 1 and 2). In addition, a threshold analysis for the IDE recursion is presented (Theorem 1). Numerical results are depicted and discussed in Section V. Finally, Section VI concludes the whole paper.

III. A PHENOMENOLOGICAL STUDY ON FPS AND DPS
In this section, we aim to highlight the relationship between FPs and DPs: A DP can be thought of as a path in the density space X parameterized by the chain axis t, which goes through some FPs in S. More importantly, this DP can be accordingly divided into several transition regions, each of which moves apart from others (i.e., at distinct velocities) as the iteration proceeds.
To this end, we present a phenomenological study on the dynamics of DPs via two numerical examples. Our focus is put on x(l) and the extensions to y(l), x˜(l) and y˜(l) are straightforward. In addition, we formulate the observations into an ansatz that motivates the development of the IDE approach in Section IV.

In this paper, a low-complexity approximation approach has been proposed for the velocity analysis of BP decoding waves for SC-LDPC ensembles on BMS channels. Unlike other GA-based approaches, the proposed IDE approach is based on the interpolation between FPs of an uncoupled DE recursion.
