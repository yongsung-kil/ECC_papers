Quadratic Programming Decoder for Binary LDPC Codes via ADMM Technique with Linear Complexity
Jing Bai, Yongchao Wang State Key Laboratory of Integrated Services Networks, Xidian University, China
Email: j.bai@stu.xidian.edu.cn, ychwang@mail.xidian.edu.cn

Abstract‚ÄĒIn this paper, we develop an efficient quadratic programming (QP) decoding algorithm via the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) technique for binary low density parity check (LDPC) codes. Its main content is as follows: first, through transforming the three-variables parity check equation to its equivalent expression, we relax the maximum likelihood decoding problem to a quadratic program. Second, the ADMM technique is exploited to design the solving algorithm of the resulting QP decoding model. Compared with the existing ADMMbased mathematical programming (MP) decoding algorithms, our proposed algorithm eliminates complex Euclidean projection onto the check polytope. Third, we prove that the proposed algorithm satisfies the favorable property of all-zeros assumption. Moreover, by exploiting the inside structure of the QP model, we show that the decoding complexity of our proposed algorithm in each iteration is linear in terms of LDPC code length. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed QP decoder attains better error-correction performance than the sum-product BP decoder and costs the least amount of decoding time amongst the stateof-the-art ADMM-based MP decoding algorithms.
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, mathematical programming (MP) techniques, such as linear programming (LP), have attracted everincreasing attention for decoding low density parity check (LDPC) codes due to their strong theoretical guarantees on decoding performance [1] [2]. However, compared with the classical belief propagation (BP) decoder, MP decoders suffer from two major drawbacks: high decoding complexity and poor error-correction performance in low signal-to-noise (SNR) regions [2]‚Äď[4]. Therefore, intensive studies have focused on improving the error-correction performance and decoding efficiency of MP decoding.
In [5], redundant parity checks (RPCs) based on fractional cycles in the tanner graph were adaptively added into the simple LP problem. These RPCs made it possible to tighten the original feasible region and eliminate unnecessary nonintegral vertices of the fundamental polytope. Subsequently, authors in [6] proposed a cutting-plan algorithm to obtain RPC cuts by column permutations of the parity check matrix. In addition, authors in [7] proposed another cut-generating approach to generate the cut-inducing RPCs by transforming the reordered matrix based on the parity check matrix into a

‚Äúpartial‚ÄĚ reduced row echelon form. However, all of the above decoding algorithms are based on general LP solvers suffering from high computational complexity. Recently, the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) technique [8] was exploited to solve the linear program decoding model [4]. In [9], to improve error-correction performance of LP decoder in low SNR regions, penalty terms are added to the linear objective function of the LP model in [4]. However, the ADMMbased penalized decoders involve check-polytope projection (CPP), which limits their efficiency in practice.
In this paper, we focus on designing a new MP decoder, in comparison with the classical BP decoder, whose computational complexity is comparable, i.e., linear to the code length of the LDPC code, but error-correction performance is superior. The main content of this paper is as follows:
‚ÄĘ Based on equivalent expression of the three-variables parity check equation, we relax the maximum likelihood (ML) decoding problem to a quadratic program (QP).
‚ÄĘ The ADMM technique is customized to solve the resulting QP decoding model. Compared with the state-of-theart ADMM-based MP decoders, our proposed ADMMbased QP decoder does not involve the complex CPP operation and variables in each update step can be calculated in parallel.
‚ÄĘ We show that the proposed QP decoder satisfies the allzeros assumption property. Moreover, through exploiting the inside structure of the QP model, we show that complexity of our proposed algorithm in each iteration scales linearly with LDPC code length.
‚ÄĘ Simulation results demonstrate that our proposed QP decoder attains better error-correction performance than the classical sum-product BP decoder and costs the least amount of decoding time amongst the state-of-the-art ADMM-based MP decoders.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose an efficient QP decoding algorithm via the ADMM technique for binary LDPC codes. We prove that the proposed decoding algorithm satisfies the favorable all-zeros assumption property. Moreover, by exploiting the inside structure of the formulated decoding model, we show that the decoding complexity in each iteration is linear in terms of LDPC code length. Simulation results demonstrate that the FER/BER performance of the proposed decoding algorithm is better than the classical sum-product BP decoding algorithm and costs the least amount of decoding time amongst the state-of-the-art ADMM-based MP decoding algorithms.
