Design and Optimization of Tail-Biting Spatially Coupled Protograph LDPC Codes Under Shuffled Belief-Propagation Decoding

Zhaojie Yang , Yi Fang , Guofa Cai , Guohua Zhang , and Pingping Chen

Abstract— In this letter, we conduct a comprehensive investigation on tail-biting (TB) spatially coupled protograph (SC-P) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes under shuffled beliefpropagation (BP) decoding. We first propose a shuffled extrinsic information transfer (S-EXIT) algorithm tailored for the shuffled decoding. Exploiting the proposed algorithm, we accurately predict the convergence performance (i.e., decoding thresholds) for TB-SC-P codes under such a schedule and demonstrate that their convergence performance can be further improved. Based on the S-EXIT analysis, we develop an optimization method to generate a new type of TB-SC-P codes, called improved TB-SC double-protograph (I-TB-SC-DP) codes, which can inherit the superiorities of two mother protograph codes and possess more desirable decoding thresholds. Theoretical analyses and simulated results illustrate that the proposed I-TB-SC-DP codes exhibit better error performance than the existing counterparts.
Index Terms— Tail-biting spatially coupled protograph codes, extrinsic information transfer, shuffled decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
P ROTOGRAPH low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have attracted much attention due to their outstanding error performance and relatively low-implementation complexity [1]. During the past decade, there is a surge of research interest in developing theoretical-analysis tools and design methods for protograph codes. For instance, the extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) algorithm [2] has been conceived to predict the asymptotic performance of protograph codes and to determine the best candidates for a specific communication system.
Owing to the appealing advantages of protograph codes, a class of protograph-convolutional-like codes, called as spatially coupled protograph (SC-P) codes, has been proposed. From the graph-structure perspective, SC-P codes are generated by interconnecting a series of identical protograph

Compared with the original protograph codes, the SC-P codes not only can inherit all their advantages, but also can possess the convolutional property to achieve better performance. Therefore, the SC-P codes have gained increasing interest in the coding community. In particular, much research effort has been devoted to promoting the theoretical and practical advancement of SC-P codes in different scenarios, such as binary-phase shift-keying (BPSK) modulated systems [4] and high-order modulated systems [5].
However, abundant research works on the design and analysis of protograph/SC-P codes have been developed under the flooding belief-propagation (BP) decoding [6], [7], in which all the variable-node (VN) and check-node (CN) messages are simultaneously updated and propagated along the edges of the Tanner graph [8]. To accelerate the convergence speed of decoding process, two different schedules, referred to as layered schedule and shuffled schedule, have been investigated. Particularly, the layered schedule updates the CN messages sequentially [9], while the shuffled schedule updates the VN messages sequentially [10]. The above two shuffled schedules are capable of updating the message in a sequential order, and thus achieve faster convergence speed and better performance than the flooding schedules [11]. Analytical studies have proved that the shuffled schedule not only can maintain the same complexity as the flooding schedule, but also can converge twice as fast as the flooding schedule [11]. Nevertheless, there appears to be no research works for the design and analysis of SC-P codes under shuffled schedules to date.
With the above motivation, this letter carries out a study on the tail-biting SC-P (TB-SC-P) codes under shuffled BP decoding to avoid the rate-loss issue incurred by the terminating operation. To be specific, we first put forward a shuffled EXIT (S-EXIT) algorithm tailored for the shuffled BP decoding, which can facilitate the asymptotic-performance (e.g., decoding threshold) analysis and structure optimization of TB-SC-P codes in such a scenario. We further develop an optimization method, which consists of a new edge-spreading scheme and a degreedistribution optimization scheme, to construct a novel type of TB-SC-P codes, called improved TB-SC double-protograph (I-TB-SC-DP) codes, based on two different mother protograph codes. Simulation results agree well with the theoretical analyses and show that the I-TB-SC-DP codes are superior to the state-of-the-art protograph codes and TB-SC-P codes.

B. Iterative Decoding Algorithm
The system model considered in this letter is described as follows. First, the information bits are encoded by a/an protograph/SC-P encoder. Then, the binary coded bits vj ∈ {0, 1} (j = 1, 2, . . . , n) are transmitted to the BPSK modulator, whose output is given by xj = (−1)vj ∈ {+1, −1}. After that, the modulated signal will be transmitted through an additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. The channel output is written as yj = xj + nj, where nj represents the Gaussian noise with zero mean and variance σ2 = N0/2, denoted as nj ∼ N (0, σ2).
At the receiver, the signal output from the channel will be received and processed by a soft-input soft-output (SISO) decoder with BP algorithm. The log-likelihood ratio (LLR) corresponding to the channel message of the j-th VN vj is calculated by lvj = log [p(yj|vj = 0)/p(yj|vj = 1)].

B. Decoding-Threshold Analysis
The thresholds of the AR4JA protograph code, TB-SC-DP code, and I-TB-SC-DP code are studied by exploiting two different EXIT (i.e., the conventional EXIT and S-EXIT) algorithms. Referring to Table I, the I-TB-SC-DP code exhibits the smallest decoding threshold, indicating that it can achieve optimal performance in the low-SNR region. Moreover, the decoding thresholds estimated by the proposed S-EXIT algorithm are the same as those of conventional EXIT algorithm.
Fig. 1 shows the evolution of the average a-posteriori MIs of the AR4JA code, TB-SC-DP code, and I-TB-SC-DP code at their corresponding SNR thresholds. As seen, the average a-posteriori MIs of all codes can quickly converge to the value of one with an increasing number of iterations in the S-EXIT algorithm. Furthermore, the MI convergence speed of all codes in the S-EXIT algorithm is at least twice as fast as that of conventional EXIT algorithm, which indicates that the former benefits from lower computational complexity than the latter.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we proposed an S-EXIT algorithm which not only can exactly estimate the decoding thresholds of protograph/SC-P codes under shuffled BP decoding, but also can cost lower computational complexity than the conventional EXIT algorithm. Moreover, a new edge-spreading scheme was conceived to construct a novel type of TB-SC-P codes from two mother protograph codes, called TB-SC-DP codes. Based on the S-EXIT algorithm, we optimized the decoding thresholds of the TB-SC-DP codes to generate a type of improved TB-SC-DP (I-TB-SC-DP) codes. The I-TB-SC-DP codes significantly outperform the TB-SC-DP codes, TB-SC-P codes, and existing protograph codes conditioned on BPSK modulation under both flooding and shuffled schedules. Also, the proposed I-TB-SC-DP codes appear to be a good candidate for high-order modulated communication systems.

It is also shown that the I-TB-SC-DP code exhibits the best performance compared with other four types of codes, which is reasonably consistent with our decoding-threshold analysis.
