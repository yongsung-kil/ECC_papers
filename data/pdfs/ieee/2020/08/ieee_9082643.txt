Two Effective Scheduling Schemes for Layered Belief Propagation of 5G LDPC Codes
Bingbing Wang , Yan Zhu, and Jing Kang

Abstract— Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have been chosen for the fifth-generation (5G) new radio (NR) enhanced Mobile Broad Band (eMBB) data channel. Since NR LDPC codes puncture the first two block columns of the paritycheck matrix, the decoding complexity is reduced, but it slows down the convergence rate. In this letter, we propose a fixed schedule that decodes the layers with the least-punctured edges and those with the highest-degree (LPHD), which has much faster convergence speed than conventional schemes. The fixed scheduling schemes cannot take full advantage of the dynamic changes of decoding messages. As a result, the residual-based layered belief propagation (RB-LBP) is proposed to rearrange the layers dynamically among different iterations. Compared with some informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) strategies, RB-LBP reduces the decoding complexity with slight performance loss.
Index Terms— LDPC codes, 5G, layered belief propagation, scheduling schemes, residual-based LBP.
I. INTRODUCTION
T HE emerging fifth-generation (5G) wireless communication technology can support the interconnection of various types of devices. low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have been chosen as the data channel for the 5G new radio (NR) enhanced Mobile Broad Band (eMBB) by the 3rd Generation Partnership Project [2].
LDPC codes are widely used for its performance is close to the Shannon limit. Many communication standards [3]–[5] chose LDPC as the channel coding codes. Different decoding algorithms can significantly affect the decoding performance. The popular decoding algorithm belief propagation (BP) [6] is the flooding schedule which updates all check nodes and then all variable nodes in one iteration. Flooding shows strong parallelism, but the latest information obtained in the current iteration is not available until the next iteration, which leads to a slow convergence speed. To make new information available as soon as possible, sequential scheduling was proposed. A typical sequential scheduling is layered BP (LBP) [7], in which new information obtained from the upper layer can be used by the lower layer immediately. With appropriate

Compared with conventional LBP which randomly determines the order of the layers, the fixed schedules [8], [9] determine order according to some prior rules. These schedules bring no additional complexity but improve performance. However, these fixed schedules are generic and take no account of the unique nature of NR LDPC. Herein, we propose the least-punctured and highest-degree (LPHD) scheduling that not only considers the degree distribution, but also factors into the number of punctured edges. It will be seen later that this schedule is more effective than conventional scheduling scheme.
A series of informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) strategies can be found in [10]–[12]. Part of these strategies like residual belief propagation (RBP) [10] and node-wise RBP (NW-RBP) [11] have a faster convergence speed, but need mounts of computations. Herein we propose residual-based LBP (RB-LBP), which represents an excellent compromise between additional complexity and performance. According to the simulation results, RB-LBP achieves a much better errorrate performance in comparison to conventional LBP. And despite a small gap compared to NW-RBP, it largely reduced complexity.

The proposed LPHD scheduling and RB-LBP are introduced in section III. Section IV analyzes algorithm complexity and shows the simulation results. Section V concludes this letter.
II. PRELIMINARIES
A. Structure of NR LDPC Codes
The parity-check matrix of NR LDPC is defined by two small base graphs (BG). BG1 is mainly used when information bit length is between 300 and 8448, while the rate between 1/3 and 8/9. BG2 is mainly used when information bit length is between 40 and 3840, and the rate between 1/5 and 2/3. Fig. 1 presents the general structure of the NR LDPC base graph. Submatrix [A B] is relatively dense with non-zero elements, and is called the kernel matrix. Submatrix C is an all-zero matrix and submatrix [D E] corresponds to the extended check bits, where E is the identity matrix. Each element of the BG represents an all-zero matrix or an identity matrix with the cyclic shift. The size of the matrix is Z × Z, where Z is lifting size. III. PROPOSED SCHEDULING STRATEGIES
A. The Proposed LPHD Scheduling
Each check node is connected to the first 2 × Z variable nodes by one or two edges, but these variable nodes are punctured in NR LDPC. The decoder pads zeros at the punctured locations. According to (1), if a check node ci has two punctured variable nodes, the messages transferred from ci to its all neighboring variables are zero. And if a check node has only one punctured variable node, it can be recovered after one update, therefore, we prioritize the update of such check nodes. This schedule denoted as LP.
If the check nodes are connected to more variable nodes, it means that the decoding messages can be spread more widely. Therefore, if the layers have the same number of punctured edges, we update the layers with the highest rowweight first. According to this, the largest column-weight of the neighboring variable nodes in each layer is found, based on which further sorting is performed. This strategy is denoted as HD. The combination of LP and HD is denoted as the LPHD scheduling.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose a fixed scheduling strategy LPHD based on the structure of NR LDPC codes. The layers
with the single punctured variable node are chosen first so
that more decoding messages can be saved in the first few iterations. Then the degree distribution are further considered.
RB-LBP prefers the layers that contribute more to the decoding process and achieves a reasonable balance between
complexity and performance. When these two schedules
are applied to NR LDPC codes, the convergence speed and error-rate performance can be improved significantly
compared to flooding or conventional LBP. Both of the
proposed schedules are supposed to shed light on studies in the field of NR LDPC decoding.
