Design of Protograph-Based Quasi-Cyclic Spatially Coupled LDPC Codes

Shuoshuo Wang, Zhanji Wu*, Qihao Wu
School of Information and Communication Engineering Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications Beijing 100876, China
*Corresponding author: Zhanji Wu, wuzhanji@bupt.edu.cn

Abstract—In this paper, we deal with time-varing quasicyclic (QC) spatially-coupled (SC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes by lifting the coupled protographs. The construction method of girth-6 protographs for any code rate is proposed. We provide a theoretical lower bound on the syndrome former constraint length under condition of the protographs without 4-cycles in their Tanner graphs. Then we use periodic QC lifting method to search the QC-SC-LDPC matrices with larger girth based on the protographs. The minimum dimension size of QC-SC-LDPC codes is given to count the girth of QC-SC-LDPC codes with a semi-infinite binary-check matrix structure. The performance of such constructed codes with lower complexity and delay is superior to the LDPC block codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) codes were first introduced as LDPC convolutional code (LDPC-CC) in 1999 [1]. SC-LDPC codes are divided into two categories according to the size of the syndrome former memory and the size of the sub-matrices: maximizing the syndrome former memory and constraining the size of the sub-matrices was called strongly coupled SC-LDPC codes and vice versa weakly coupled SC-LDPC codes [2]. SCLDPC can be viewed as coupling together a sequence of disjoint LDPC block codes into a single coupled chain. Some works have investigated and studied the time-invarint SCLDPC codes with small syndrome former constraint lengths, and their design approaches usually start from constructing quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check block codes and exploit suitable unwrapping procedures to obtain SC-LDPC codes [3], [4].
Another way to construct the SC-LDPC codes is based on protograph design [5], [6]. The protograph design has been recognized that it can provide an efficient method of constructing LDPC codes [7]. Then the protograph-based construction of SC-LDPC code ensembles with good asymptotic properties can be obtained by applying a graphlifting procedure [8]. We call the SC-LDPC codes with graph-lifting as the QC-SC-LDPC codes. The lifting procedure used in protographs can not only generate the QC ensemble results with a desirable property for practical decoder implementation but also maximize the girth of the Tanner graph. Furthermore, Ensembles of terminated QC-

SC-LDPC have the attractive characteristics of thresholds approaching Shannon’s capacity with belief-propagation (BP) decoding, due to the threshold saturation phenomenon [9].
QC-SC-LDPC codes can also be divided into two types: time-varing and time-invariant [1]. It has been shown that periodically time-varying codes with period smaller than their memory are able to achieve better girth properties than time-invariant codes with the same block length [10]. And the advantages of using the periodic time-varing QC-SCLDPC codes have been demonstrated by designing a hardware model, which shows substantial reduction in memory requirements and a significant improvement in the operating clock frequency of the decoder [11].
Parity-check matrix of QC-SC-LDPC codes exhibits a diagonal band of nonzero entries, giving way to perform the sliding window decoding (SWD) [12]. The windowed decoding scheme can exploit the semi-infinite convolutional structure of the SC-LDPC codes to decode nonterminated codes, while providing the flexibility to change the decoding complexity and latency. By using half the number of decoding iterations, the layered decoding applied for SCLDPC can achieve the same performance as the traditional message passing algorithm [13]. In this paper, we use the sliding windowed decoding scheme based on layered decoding architecture.
The paper is organized as follows. In Section II, we review the basic concepts of the SC-LDPC codes. In Section III, we show how to construct a girth-6 protograph code without code rate limit. In Section IV, by further performing QC lifting of the protograph codes according to the proposed principle, we show that the girth of at least eight can be derived. Simulation results and analysis are in Section V. Finally, the conclusion appears in Section VI.

The performances of regular dv = 4 QC-SCLDPC can achieve 0.13-0.54dB gain for different code rate, compared with regular dv = 3 BC-LDPC at the same equal iteration time Ie = 30 . When the iteration time ( Ie = 20 , Iw = 2 ) and complexity of dv = 4 SC-LDPC are 67% and 89.8% of the dv = 3 BC-LDPC ( Ie = 30 ), respectively, the dv = 4 QC-SC-LDPC still maintain 0.110.34 dB gain compared with the dv = 3 BC-LDPC at the BER of 1e-6. We can find that the regular dv = 4 QC-SCLDPC codes with lower code rate can achieve higher gain compared with higher code rate.
VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, by regrouping the strongly coupled QC-SCLDPC codes, we package the time-varing QC-SC-LDPC into a larger time-invariant QC-SC-LDPC code, which has the advantage of the synchronization of encoding and sliding windowed decoding. The girth-6 protographs can be constructed for any code rate by solving a simple algebraic problem and the theoretical lower bound of the syndrome former constraint length is proposed. Furthermore, with the replicate procedure used in the QC lifting, the integrated coupled property of the matrices can be taken into consideration, so the lager girth strongly coupled QC-SCLDPC matrix can achieve better error performance, particularly in the error floor. Our proposed deigns of QCSC-LDPC codes show improvements on performance with lower delay and complexity compared with BC-LDPC. As a result, QC-SC-LDPC codes are extremely promising to be a candidate for the next-generation intelligent communication systems.
