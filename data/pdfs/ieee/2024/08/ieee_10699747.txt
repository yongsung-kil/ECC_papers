Performance Investigation of OAM-SK Optical Communication System Based on PCNN-NMS
Zhengwei Hou, Chen Wei*, Lixia Yang, Xiaojun Sun Jianghuai Advance Technology Center, Anhui University
Hefei, China Email: tshzw022411@163.com, chenwei1704@126.com, lixiayang@yeah.net, sunxj1122@163.com

AbstractвАФThis paper introduces a PCNN-NMS demodulation method for an OAM-SK optical communication system operating under atmospheric turbulence, by integrating the data-driven probabilities of a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model with the Normalized Min-Sum (NMS) LDPC decoding algorithm. Then we simulated the average bit error rate (ABER). Initially, the transmission of Laguerre-Gaussian (LG) beams through atmospheric turbulence is simulated using the power spectrum inversion method, and a dataset is created for training the CNN network. This training achieves a direct classification accuracy of 97.3% with the CNN demodulator. Subsequently, the classification probabilities from the CNN are incorporated into the NMS algorithm, using the output of the CNN to refine the credibility of channel estimation. The final simulation results show that the PCNN-NMS significantly improves the system's BER performance, offering valuable insights for enhancing the BER performance in free-space optical communication system.
KeywordsвАФOAM-SK; NMS; ABER; CNN; PCNN-NMS
I. INTRODUCTION
Free-space optical communication technology utilizes free space as the channel and light beams as the propagation medium. Owing to its large capacity, high speed, and reliability, it has gained significant attention in recent years. In free-space optical communication systems, signal transmission is typically achieved through the properties of light beams, such as beam amplitude, wavelength, and polarization. Orbital Angular Momentum (OAM) represents a novel fundamental characteristic of light beams with substantial application value [1]. The phase distribution characteristic of beams with OAM properties can be described by exp(imѕЖ), where m is the OAM mode value [2]. Theoretically, an infinite number of OAM mode values can be selected, and beams carrying different OAM mode values are orthogonal to each other. Currently, there are two main approaches to OAM-based communication: one uses OAM beams as carriers for multiplexed transmission, and the other encodes and modulates the OAM mode values. Orbital Angular Momentum Shift Keying Modulation (OAM-SK) is an innovative technique based on the latter approach, which utilizes a large number of discrete, orthogonal Orbital Angular Momentum (OAM) states to modulate data within the spatial domain [3].
However, when OAM beams are transmitted through the atmosphere, the turbulent effects within the atmospheric channel can cause the dispersion of OAM modes, significantly degrading the quality of the beam transmission and greatly

increasing the difficulty of demodulation, leading to the loss of effective information during transmission. Consequently, the quality of the demodulation scheme in an OAM-SK optical communication system is crucial. By integrating channel coding techniques, the BER of free-space optical communication systems can be somewhat reduced. Simultaneously, with the recent development of machine learning technologies, CNN, widely used in image classification [4], are also applied as demodulators in free-space optical communication systems [5]. Therefore, we integrate CNN with the NMS decoding algorithm and introduces the classification probabilities from the CNN to design an adaptive decoder for OAM-SK. This approach enriches the strategies for optimizing the bit error rate performance in free-space optical communication systems.

Recognized outcomes are then input into an LDPC decoder to obtain the final information sequence.

In this paper, we use ReLU as the activation function for the convolutional layers, pooling layers, and fully connected layers. During the network training process, the Adam optimizer and cross-entropy function are used to update the neuron parameter weights.

III. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, we compare demodulation methods' ABER under varying refractive index fluctuation, and transmission distance. Here, the outer scale L0 of atmosphere turbulence is 100m, the inner scale l0 =0.01m, the screen width D=0.6m, and the maximum transmission distance in this study is set to 1000m.The dataset used to train the CNN network consists of 48,000 light intensity images, with 3,000 images collected for each mixed mode, corresponding to the 16 encoded codewords 0000~1111. The CNN network trained using this dataset achieved a direct classification accuracy of 97.3% on the test set.
Fig.3 depicts the light intensity images of 16 superimposed modal LG beams before and after passing through atmospheric turbulence (the distance №Љ аµМ Ќ≥Ќ≤Ќ≤Ќ≤ЁЙ and the turbulence intensity вАЂ№•вАђаѓ°ађґ аµМ Ќ≥ аµИ Ќ≥Ќ≤ађњађµађЈ , which are used as the mapping scheme in the OAM-SK optical communication system, corresponding to 1000~1111. After traversing the atmospheric turbulence, the light intensity undergoes severe distortion, significantly degrading the quality of the beams.
Fig.4 shows the simulation results of the ABER for three demodulation methods: without LDPC (Without LDPC), simple combination of CNN with NMS (CNN-NMS), and integrating the classification probabilities from CNN into NMS (PCNNNMS). The results demonstrate that across various transmission distances and turbulence intensities, PCNN-NMS consistently achieves the lowest BER, followed by CNN-NMS. The NMS algorithm reduces decoding errors by introducing a normalization factor that adjusts the messages transmitted in each iteration, mitigating overfitting and oscillations. Meanwhile, PCNN-NMS leverages the probability outputs from the CNN to refine the LDPC decoding process, providing more accurate error predictionзАЖ and enabling more effective error
correction by the LDPC decoder, thereby significantly lowering the overall BER.

IV. CONCLUSION
This paper utilizes the power spectrum inversion method to construct a random phase screen for atmospheric turbulence, simulating the transmission process of LG beams through an atmospheric turbulence channel. Building on this, a PCNNNMS demodulation method for OAM-SK optical communication systems in turbulent atmospheric conditions is proposed, combining data-driven probabilities predicted by a CNN network model with the NMS-LDPC algorithm. This method optimizes the LDPC decoding process using the probability information output by the CNN, providing more accurate error predictions and enabling the LDPC decoder to perform error correction more effectively. Simulation results of the final bit error rate performance show that the average BER of the PCNN-NMS decoder is significantly better than the decoders using no LDPC and the simple combination of CNN with NMS. Overall, the PCNN-NMS method effectively enhances the BER performance of LDPC decoding by integrating data-driven probabilities predicted by deep learning models with traditional channel coding techniques, significantly improving the reliability of data transmission in environments with severe interference. This work provides valuable insights into optimizing the bit error rate performance of free-space optical communication systems.
