Cost-Efficient Partially-Parallel LDPC Decoder Architecture for 50G-PON Standard

Jeongwon Choe, and Youngjoo Lee Department of Electrical Engineering, POSTECH, Pohang, Republic of Korea
youngjoo.lee@postech.ac.kr

Abstract—In this paper, we present a cost-efficient low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoder architecture tailored to meet the high throughput demands of 50G passive optical network (PON) systems. Building upon a previous layered decoder architecture, the complex multiplexing networks are specifically optimized for the PON LDPC codes, facilitating partially-parallel processing while minimizing congestion overheads. By incorporating advanced low-cost optimization schemes, the prototype PON LDPC decoder achieves a decoding throughput of 2.94 Gb/s, surpassing state-of-the-art approaches by 2.38-fold.

II. PROPOSED PON LDPC DECODER ARCHITECTURE

I. INTRODUCTION
In modern communication standards such as 50G passive optical network (PON) systems, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have emerged as key tools, offering superior error-correcting capabilities at practical implementation costs [1], [2]. With the rising demand for high decoding throughput in recent communication systems, previous LDPC decoder designs have focused on increasing parallelism [3]. However, for the long-length LDPC codewords particularly in the 50GPON standard, the extensive parallelism poses challenges in terms of area complexity and wiring congestion. To address this issue, partially-parallel architectures have been proposed to balance decoding complexity and throughput, thus meeting the stringent requirements of communication systems [2].
In this paper, we present an efficient partially-parallel LDPC decoder architecture designed specifically for the 50G-PON standard. By analyzing the characteristics of the parity check matrix (PCM), we introduce a novel column rearrangement technique that substantially reduces multiplexing overheads for managing nonzero entries in the PCM. Furthermore, we optimize the previous area-consuming rotation network for the proposed partially-parallel architecture by implementing odd/even scheduling, effectively mitigating congestion issues. Integrated with other advanced optimization techniques, our prototype PON LDPC decoder achieves a decoding throughput of 2.94 Gbps on a field-programmable gate array (FPGA) platform, demonstrating a 2.38-fold improvement over the current state-of-the-art solution [2] while maintaining the error-correcting performance.

The PON LDPC code is characterized by a 12×69 exponent matrix, with each element representing either a Z × Z cyclically shifted identity matrix or a zero matrix [2]. To enhance parallelism, our proposed decoder processes Z/2 PCM rows simultaneously. Variable node processing (VNP), check node processing (CNP), and posterior probability (APP) update operations are performed for each PCM row, following a layered scheduling approach [3]. At the beginning of decoding, as depicted in Fig. 1, the APP memory is initialized with loglikelihood ratio (LLR) values received from the channel. In each decoding iteration, the entire APP messages are accessed and reorganized for the current processing rows, leading to substantial congestion overheads for long-length codewords.
To address this challenge, exploiting the fact that the maximum check node degree is 23, the read permutation unit selects the required 23 · Z APP messages from 69 · Z candidates based on the nonzero elements in the PCM. Moreover, we strategically rearrange the PCM columns to alleviate wiring congestion and reduce multiplexer (MUX) utilization within the permutation unit. As illustrated in Fig. 2(a), more specifically, we partition the exponent matrix into several groups such that each group contains one nonzero submatrix for every row. Consequently, for each nonzero entry within a group, the corresponding Z APP messages are selected independently of other groups, across the processing layers. This optimization significantly diminishes congestion overheads and results in a 50% reduction in MUX utilization.
In the layered LDPC decoder architecture, each of Z APP messages is rotated based on the PCM matrix for aligned row processing. In the proposed scheme, to efficiently manage the partially-parallel decoding scheduling of Z/2 rows, the decoder adopts an odd/even scheduling strategy, where the rows with odd indices are processed simultaneously, followed by those with even indices. Each selected set of Z/2 APP messages undergoes rotation in the subsequent rotator based on the shift amounts specified in the exponent matrix. Note that this odd/even scheduling approach can support arbitrary shift amounts in the PCM while concurrently reducing the number of inputs of the following rotators. This optimization results in a 53% reduction in the number of MUXs compared to the baseline barrel shifter network [4].
The organized 23 · (Z/2) APP messages are subtracted by the check-to-variable (C2V) messages constructed from the C2V memories. To alleviate congestion overheads, the C2V memories are distributed for each row as illustrated in Fig. 1. The generated variable-to-check (V2C) messages are then processed in the CNP modules using the offset minsum (OMS) algorithm [5]. The updated C2V messages are combined with V2C messages to update the APP messages, which are then stored back in the APP memory through the write permutation unit. Note that the rotated results are stored in the memory without restoration, where the following read rotator calculates the rotation amounts by reflecting the prerotated results, leading to reduced hardware costs. These decoding processes iterate until reaching the maximum number of iterations, finally yielding the estimated codewords.
III. IMPLEMENTATION RESULTS
The proposed PON LDPC decoder architecture is implemented in the Xilinx FPGA device XCKU19P, with implementation results compared in Table I. For the 50G-PON standard, puncturing the last 384 bits [2], the proposed OMS decoder is applied to (17280, 14592) PON LDPC codewords, utilizing 8-bit quantized LLRs with a maximum of 12 iterations. Occupying 172K flip-flops (FFs) and 311K look-up tables (LUTs), the proposed prototype decoder operates at a clock speed of 87 MHz, achieving a decoding throughput of 2.94 Gb/s, which is 2.38 times enhanced from the state-of-theart work [2]. In comparison to the work from [2], which targets the same code specification but utilizes lower parallelism, our proposed decoder demonstrates superior hardware efficiency when considering throughput ratio, an efficiency metric outlined in [4]. IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have presented a cost-efficient LDPC decoder architecture tailored for PON applications. To overcome the congestion overheads associated with realizing the partially-parallel decoder, the APP read permutation network is designed with low MUX utilization using the column rearrangement scheme. In addition, to alleviate the area-consuming nature of rotators, the odd/even scheduling method is adopted to reduce the number of inputs to the rotation network. The FPGA implementation results demonstrate that the proposed PON LDPC decoder improves decoding throughput by 2.38 times compared to the previous solutions, while maintaining superior error-correcting performance, providing a hardwareefficient LDPC solution for PON applications.
