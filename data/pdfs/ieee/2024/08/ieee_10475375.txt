Protograph LDPC Code and Shaped Index Modulation Design for Multi-Mode OAM Systems

Zhaojie Yang , Yao Ge , Member, IEEE, Yufei Zhao , Yi Fang , Senior Member, IEEE, and Yong Liang Guan , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract— Orbital angular momentum (OAM) is a mode division multiplexing (MDM) technique enabling simpler implementation and higher capacity than conventional multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) over line-of-sight (LoS) channels. This paper studies the joint design of protograph low-density paritycheck (PLDPC) codes and shaping index modulation (IM) in OAM systems. To begin with, we analyze the distribution of extrinsic log-likelihood-ratios (LLRs) for coded bits output from channel detector, and then propose a customized Box-Cox transformation (CBCT) to make the distribution achieve symmetry and Gaussian features. We also devise a CBCT-based protograph extrinsic information transfer (CBCT-PEXIT) algorithm to predict the convergence performance of PLDPC-coded OAM systems. Furthermore, with the aid of such an algorithm, we construct two new types of improved PLDPC codes tailored for OAM systems, including unpunctured codes and rate-compatible punctured codes. In addition, we present a two-step design method, which seamlessly combines the shaping technique and index rule into a novel modulation scheme, called shaping index. Both theoretical analyses and simulation results demonstrate that the proposed PLDPC-coded OAM systems with shaping index significantly outperform state-of-the-art counterparts.
Index Terms— Orbital angular momentum, line-of-sight, index modulation, extrinsic information transfer, protograph lowdensity parity-check codes.

However, conventional MIMO systems require complicated radio-frequency (RF) chains for antenna elements, this will significantly increase the hardware cost and power consumption in actual implementations [1], [2], [3]. Also, complex digital signal processing techniques (e.g., the transmitting precoding and receiving equalization algorithms) are required in MIMO transceivers for alleviating the inter-channel interference (ICI) [3], [4]. Additionally, MIMO is difficult to further increase the capacity without introducing extra resources, and is not fit for the line-of-sight (LoS) scenario since it is with high channel correlation and low rank. LoS channels are considered as a common model for various communication scenarios, such as LoS-dominant intelligent reflecting surface (IRS) [5], unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) [6], satellite [7], and backhaul [8]. To overcome the above weakness without losing the inherent advantages of MIMO, great research effort has been dedicated to exploring an emerging alternative technique, namely orbital angular momentum (OAM)-aided mode division multiplexing (MDM) transmission [9], [10], [11].
OAM is a phenomenon caused by the variation of electromagnetic (EM)-wave phase with respect to the azimuthal angle around propagation axis. For the EM wave carrying OAM, its structure is a helical wave front expressed by an exponential function exp(−jξφ) [9], where j, ξ, and φ are the imaginary number, OAM mode number, and azimuthal angle, respectively. OAM-aided MDM systems are implemented using EM waves with different OAM modes, which have relatively low correlation. Also, the OAM mode multiplexing/demultiplexing is realized using RF analog processing technologies (e.g., phase shift networks), which are beneficial to circumvent ICI and reduce computational overhead. Owing to these appealing characteristics, a flurry of research activities have been conducted to design and optimize the methods for generating OAM EM waves, such as spiral phase plates [12], helicoidal parabolic antennas [13], metasurfaces [14], and uniform circular array (UCA) [15]. In particular, UCA is one of the most competitive alternatives due to its relatively low hardware cost and implementation complexity. Moreover, a lot of research works have demonstrated the feasibility of UCA-based OAM in both theory [10], [11] and practice [16].
The UCA-based OAM systems have been widely investigated in various wireless applications, including multiple access communications and LoS communications. For example, the authors in [17] have studied OAM-aided orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM) systems and

proposed a two-dimensional fast Fourier transform (2-D FFT) algorithm to reduce energy consumption and hardware cost. In [18], the non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA) technique has been combined with OAM for improving the channel capacities in downlink cellular communications. There are also some research works related to the joint design of OAM and OFDM, which aim to realize high-capacity MDM systems in LoS scenarios [9], [19]. Actually, OAM systems usually suffer from severe inter-mode interference due to the misalignment between the transmitting and receiving UCAs. To alleviate the performance degradation, previous researchers have proposed a beam steering approach tailored for transceivers [20]. The authors in [15] have designed an OAM receiver combining both beam steering and amplitude detection. Additionally, the power allocation [21] and precoding [22] schemes have been optimized to eliminate the inter-mode interference. To improve the spectral efficiency, index modulation (IM) was introduced into OAM framework to establish OAM-IM systems [10], [23]. Specifically, the authors in [10] have studied the bit-errorrate (BER) performance of OAM-IM systems with different index schemes and designed a low-complexity detector. The performance of OAM-IM systems with the least-square receiver for different cell distances has also been investigated in [23]. Furthermore, OAM-IM framework has been applied to achieve robust anti-jamming in narrowband communications [24] and attain performance gains in free-space optical (FSO) links suffering from severe atmospheric turbulence [11].
In parallel with index technique, coded modulation is another solution to meet high reliability-and-throughput requirements of modern communications. For instance, multidimensional OAM coded-modulation scheme has been proposed in [25], whose critical idea is to use all available degrees of freedom (e.g., the amplitude, phase and OAM mode) to carry information. In [26], the joint design of OAM and trellis-coded modulation has been presented to improve both performance and spectrum efficiency. Also, the performance of OAM systems with M -ary phase-shift keying (PSK) and quadratic-amplitude modulation (QAM) has been investigated [10]. In addition, low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes have been considered for OAM systems to further enhance data-transmission reliability in different channel environments, such as FSO [27] and LoS [28] channels. However, the LDPC codes are a type of unstructured error-correction-codes (ECCs). These codes are generated randomly based on a degree-distribution pair. They also have a quadratic encoding complexity with the codeword lengths [29]. For their hardware implementation, the encoder/decoder consumes a lot of resources since the memory has to store a large parity-check matrix corresponding to the LDPC code. To circumvent this issue, a type of structured LDPC codes, namely protograph LDPC (PLDPC) codes [30], has emerged as a more promising alternative due to its simple design and easy implementation. PLDPC codes have been used in different transmission scenarios, including wireless communications [31], visible light communications [32], deep-space communications [33], and flash-memory data storage [34]. Accordingly, the theoretical-analysis tool tailored for such codes, namely protograph-based extrinsic information

transfer (PEXIT) algorithm [35], has also been modified to facilitate the optimization of PLDPC-coded systems.
Motivated by the above discussion, the joint design of PLDPC codes, index technique, and OAM framework has great potential to be a feasible multiplexing solution, which can acquire desirable reliability and throughput in LoS scenarios. However, to the best of our knowledge, this is a first attempt to investigate PLDPC-coded OAM-IM systems over LoS channels. The main contributions of this paper are summarized in the following:
1) We analyze the conditional distribution of the extrinsic log-likelihood-ratios (LLRs) for coded bits output from channel detector, and find that it follows a non-Gaussian distribution. Then, we propose a customized Box-Cox transformation (CBCT), which can transform these extrinsic LLRs to attain symmetric Gaussian characteristics for facilitating the low-complexity performance analysis, code design, and index scheme formulation.
2) Inspired by CBCT, we develop a CBCT-based PEXIT (CBCT-PEXIT) algorithm for CBCT-aided OAM-IM systems (i.e., a transformation model). The proposed algorithm can not only possess relatively low computational complexity, but also accurately evaluate the convergence-performance trends of different PLDPC codes in OAM-IM systems with inter-mode interference. Also, we construct a rate-1/2 improved unpunctured PLDPC code as well as two rate-1/2 improved punctured PLDPC codes by means of the proposed CBCT-PEXIT algorithm, and further extend these two punctured codes to be rate compatible.
3) For existing OAM-IM systems, their transceivers ignore the difference in reliabilities between different OAM modes and activate them randomly to transmit data. To make OAM modes with high reliabilities possess high probability of being activated, we re-design the transceiver, in which the shaping technique is intelligently combined with index scheme to formulate a novel modulation method, called shaping index. This is the first work to apply shaping technique to index procedure.
4) We carry out various theoretical analyses and BER simulations to demonstrate the feasibility of our proposed PLDPC-coded OAM systems with shaping index, and its superiority with respect to existing benchmarks over LoS channels.
The remainder of this work is organized as follows. To begin with, we introduce the system model and the proposed CBCT in Section II. Then, we develop the CBCT-PEXIT algorithm and elaborate its validity and benefit in Section III. In Section IV, we construct two types of improved PLDPC codes and conceive a novel shaping index for OAM-IM systems. We give simulation results in Section V and conclusions in Section VI.

B. Proposed CBCT
For certain transmission environments, channel features should be considered to develop capacity-approaching coding schemes. As far as we know, no work has discussed the LLR characteristic of coded bits transmitted over LoS channels with and without inter-mode interference. To fill this gap, we investigate this issue, which is quite useful for the design of theoretical-analysis tool, PLDPC code, and index scheme for OAM-IM systems.

After receiving the LLRs from simulations, we divide the range of values of these LLRs sequentially into several uniform intervals with length lint = 0.1, and compute the occurrence frequencies of these LLRs in different interval. These occurrence frequencies are multiplied by a scale factor 1/lint to yield the LLR histograms h(li|bi = 0) and h(li|bi = 1), which correspond to the PDFs of an appropriately quantized version of LLRs bi = 0 and bi = 1. Additionally, we present a symmetric Gaussian distribution N (u, 2u) (i.e., N (u0, 2u0) and N (u1, 2u1)) for comparison, where u is the mean, 2u is the variance, and u0 (u1) denotes the average extrinsic LLR values for bi = 0 (bi = 1). Referring to Fig. 2(a), an OAM-IM with NA = 1 is considered for θ = 0 (i.e., perfect alignment between the transmitting and receiving UCAs). One can notice that h(li|bi = 0) and h(li|bi = 1) (i.e., scattered points) are consistent with N (u0, 2u0) and N (u1, 2u1) (i.e., solid lines), respectively. An example (θ = 0.8) is given in Fig. 2(b), where h(li|bi = 0) and h(li|bi = 1) follow skewed distributions (see scattered points), but not symmetric Gaussian distributions. It should be noted that these numerical results shown in Figs. 2 and 3 are calculated based on all extrinsic LLRs obtained from simulations. Also, we adopt the discrete point representations, rather than traditional histograms, to make the observation clearer. Thus, the conventional theoretical-analysis tools used for the density evolution analysis of LDPC codes (i.e., EXIT chart [39] and PEXIT algorithm [35]) and coding schemes tailored for channels with symmetric Gaussian characteristics cannot be directly applied to OAM-IM systems with intermode interference.

• The CBCT is tailored for processing the channel information with a non-Gaussian distribution (e.g., a skewed distribution), and thus it will be also applicable to other communication systems that experience the same phenomenon as the OAM-IM systems.
• We define the CBCT-aided OAM-IM system over an LoS channel as a transformation model, which contains the inter-mode interference and a CBCT procedure tailored for the extrinsic LLRs output from channel detector.
• As observed from Figs. 2(b) and 3(b), the proposed CBCT primarily helps shape the long left/right tails of the skewed distributions, which make them approach the symmetric Gaussian distributions. Importantly, referring to (15), both the original and transformed LLRs have the same positive/negative sign. This implies that when a coded bit has a high probability of being decoded as 0/1 in the original model (i.e., the original LLR has a positive/negative sign), it is also likely to be decoded as 0/1 in the transformation model and vice versa. Therefore, the transformation model does not deteriorate any accuracy

of the BP decoding, but only simplifies the EXIT analysis and code design. For the above reason, the transformation model can capture the performance of the PLDPC codes in the original model. • With the aid of the CBCT, one can devise a feasible low-complexity theoretical-analysis tool, which is used to guide the design and optimization of PLDPC codes and index schemes for improving the performance of OAM-IM systems over LoS channels. The CBCT cannot improve the system performance since it is not used in the practical data-transmission process. • By analyzing the flipping probability of bit 0 (bit 1), which can be measured by the integral of its corresponding PDF from negative infinity to zero (from zero to plus infinity), the OAM-based LoS channel is not a typical asymmetric channel. The motivation and principle of using CBCT tailored for receiver is different from the coding techniques tailored for transmitter [43]. • The KLD analysis can only be used for two discrete or continuous distributions. To compute the KLD between the transformed distribution (i.e., a discrete distribution) obtained from the simulation and the continuous Gaussian distribution determined by the given mean and variance, we must transform the latter into a discrete version. Specifically, given a continuous Gaussian distribution, we first divide it sequentially into multiple intervals with the same length, and then compute the integration for different intervals, which produces the corresponding discrete Gaussian distribution. Afterwards, we calculate the KLD between the transformed distribution and the discrete Gaussian distribution.
III. PROPOSED CBCT-PEXIT ALGORITHM FOR PLDPC-CODED OAM-IM SYSTEMS
A. PLDPC Codes
In [30], the author has first introduced PLDPC codes, which are generated by expanding a small Tanner graph, referred to as protograph. Specifically, a protograph can be represented by Gp = (Vp, Cp, Ep), where Vp = {v1, v2, . . . , vnv } is a variablenode (VN) set, Cp = {c1, c2, . . . , cnc } is a check-node (CN) set, Ep = {ei,j} is an edge set and ei,j denotes the edge connecting the i-th CN ci (i = 1, 2, . . . , nc) to the j-th VN vj (j = 1, 2, . . . , nv). The code rate of a protograph is Rp = (nv − nc)/nv. A protograph can be expressed as a base matrix Bp = (bi,j), whose rows and columns correspond to the CNs and VNs, respectively, bi,j denotes the number of edges connecting ci to vj. Given a protograph (i.e., base matrix), one can perform a lifting operation on it and then attain the corresponding derived graph (i.e., paritycheck matrix), which can be used to generate the PLDPC code. In particular, unlike the conventional LDPC codes, multiple edges connecting a CN and a VN (i.e., parallel edges) are permitted in a protograph, but they must be eliminated in a lifting process. A length-ne PLDPC code can be generated from a protograph with nv VNs, where ne = nv × f and f is a lifting factor. In this paper, the lifting operation is implemented using a modified progressive-edge-growth (PEG) algorithm [31].

It is obvious that all PLDPC codes are capable of achieving significant performance gains with the aid of proposed shaped index scheme. Specifically, for QPSK modulation (see Fig. 15(a)), the regular-(3, 6), AR4JA, and proposed IUP, IP1, and IP2 PLDPC codes achieve

Also, similar phenomenon can be observed for IP1 and IP2 PLDPC codes in Figs. 16(b) & 16(c), respectively. Besides, the proposed shaped index scheme is the best alternative for conventional PLDPC codes (e.g., regular-(3, 6) and AR4JA PLDPC codes), and thus can be treated as an appealing technique for boosting system performance with a slight spectral-efficiency loss.
VI. CONCLUSION
This paper has investigated PLDPC-coded OAM systems with shaping index over LoS channels. Specifically, we have first analyzed the characteristic of extrinsic LLRs output from channel detector, and then proposed a CBCT to formulate a transformation model. Subsequently, we have conceived a CBCT-PEXIT algorithm tailored for the transformation model, which can accurately predict the convergence-performance trends of PLDPC codes in OAM systems with relatively low computational complexity. Based on a CBCTPEXIT-aided computer-search method, we have constructed a rate-1/2 improved unpunctured PLDPC code and two rate-compatible improved punctured PLDPC codes, which exhibit desirable decoding thresholds and BERs. Furthermore, we have designed a shaping index for enhancing the performance of OAM systems at the price of a trivial spectralefficiency loss. Simulation results have shown that OAM systems with the use of proposed PLDPC codes and shaping index can significantly outperform existing counterparts.
