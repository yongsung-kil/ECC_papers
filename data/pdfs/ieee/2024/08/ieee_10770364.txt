Abstract—The Euclidean projection onto check polytope is the most complicated and time-consuming operation in the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) decoding for Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes. The check polytope projection penalized algorithm can avoid the tedious work of penalty parameter optimization and simplified the calculation process of check polytope projection. However, the over-punishment of check polytope projection penalized algorithm may lead to poor decoding performance, when combined with the over-relaxation technique. In this paper, an improved check polytope projection penalized algorithm is proposed. In order to reduce the ratio of even-vertex projection, we restrict the points far away from the even-vertex of the check polytope by introducing the penalty constraint condition. Simulation results show that the frame error rate (FER) performance of the proposed algorithm is almost the same as that of the cut search projection algorithm (CSA), and it can save the average decoding time by 22.9%-52%.
Keywords—Alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, penalty terms, check polytope projection
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes, which have the advantages of good error correction performance, simple coding and decoding, and parallel implementation, have been widely used in wireless communication applications. Linear programming (LP) decoding algorithm proposed by Feldman et al. in [1] brings LDPC decoding to the realm of convex optimization, and it also has been generalized to other codes and channels [2], [3], [4]. LP decoding has many desirable attributes that other message passing algorithms do not possess [5]. LP decoding satisfies all-zeros assumption and maximum likelihood certification property, but the research on LP decoding was hampered by the excessive complexity. Therefore, the works [6], [7], [8], [9] were proposed to reduce the complexity of LP decoding model. Nevertheless, its development was limited by the excessive complexity. Recently, the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) with parallel potential was put forward by Boyd et al. [10]. In [11], Barman et al. applied the framework of ADMM to LP decoding algorithm and proposed the alternating direction multiplier method based linear programming (ADMM-LP) decoding algorithm to reduce the complexity of LP decoding algorithm. ADMM-LP decoding algorithm effectively reduced the complexity of the LP decoding algorithm, but the complexity was still high compared with the BP decoding algorithm, and the frame error rate (FER) performance was poor at the low SNRs. Liu et al. [12] proposed an ADMM decoding algorithm based on a penalty function (PF), which greatly improved the FER performance at the low SNRs by punishing the pseudo-

The parameters of the decoder can be optimized using the method of differential evolution [16], [17], but the optimization process is complicated and timeconsuming. Wei et al. [18] proposed a novel ADMM check node (CN) penalized decoding algorithm, which improved the decoding performance at the expense of some increase in the decoding complexity.
The Euclidean projection onto check polytopes is the most complicated operation in the ADMM decoding. In order to reduce the complexity of the Euclidean projection algorithm, X. Zhang et al. [19] proposed the cut search projection algorithm (CSA), which calculates the projection of the vectors to be projected to cutting plane of check polytope using the cut search algorithm. G. Zhang et al. [20] proved that the complex projection onto the check polytope can be transformed into a simple simplex projection. Jiao et al. [21] simplified the calculation of the check polytope projection by establishing a look-up table (LUT) and quantizing the input vectors, resulting in a reduction in the computational complexity of Euclidean projection at the cost of significant memory resources. Subsequently, Jiao et al. [22] proposed a non-uniform quantization method in response to address the issue of excessive memory consumption during the LUT process. This method minimizes the mean squared error of the Euclidean projection result during quantization to save memory costs, achieving similar decoding performance when using smaller memory units compared to the ADMM decoding algorithm based on a LUT. Additionally, Wei et al. [23] proposed an iterative check polytope projection algorithm, but the convergence speed of this algorithm was slow while maintaining decoding performance. Xia et al. [24] attempted to further simplify the complexity of Euclidean projection calculations by using the line segment projection algorithm (LSA), which projects the vector to be projected onto a line segment composed of two even-vertices on the check polytope, to obtain an approximate value for the projection. Liu et al. [25] proposed a check polytope projection penalized ADMM decoding algorithm, which dynamically selects even-vertices in the check polytope closest to the input vector as an approximate projection. III. IMPROVED CHECK POLYTOPE PROJECTION PENALIZED ALGORITHM
The key to simplifying ADMM algorithm is reducing the complexity of Euclidean projection calculation. However, some existing simplification was at the cost of FER performance degradation. In this section, we propose and elaborate an improved check polytope projection penalized algorithm build on Liu et al. [25]. It provides a scheme for ADMM decoding to improve FER performance while maintaining low complexity.

A. Sources of Innovation
Adding penalty term to the objective function of ADMM decoding for LDPC codes can improve FER performance. However, the optimization process of the penalty coefficient is difficult and time-consuming. In addition, the calculation of the Euclidean projection on the check polytope is the most complex operation in ADMM-LP. Recently, Liu et al proposed a simple and novel check polytope projection penalized ADMM decoding algorithm by dynamically selecting the even-vertices of the check polytope that are closest to the vector to be projected as an approximate projection result [25]. This algorithm avoids the fussy parameter optimization work and has good decoding performance. It treats the projection result z of the check polytope as a "copy" of the variable node x, and therefore can improve the performance of the ADMM decoding algorithm by penalizing the check nodes. The even-vertex projection algorithm (EVA) updates the value of the projection result z to 0 or 1, that is, the variable node x is penalized away from the fractional point. In addition, EVA directly takes the even-vertices in the check polytope closest to the projected vector as the approximate projection, that proves the check nodes in this part satisfy the local paritycheck equation. The projection calculation process of the check polytope projection penalized algorithm is as follows: First, calculate the vertex closest to the vector to be projected on the check polytope, and denote it by VT. If the elements of VT have an even number of “1”, the vertex is an evenvertex, which means that the it is on the check polytope, then VT is used as the projection result, called the even-vertex projection. On the contrary, if the vertex VT is an odd-vertex, use LSA or CSA to calculate the projection result. The combination of this algorithm with CSA is called the improved cut search algorithm (I-CSA), and with LSA is called improved line segment algorithm(I-LSA). B. Improved Check Polytope Projection Penalized ADMM decoding Algorithm
The algorithm proposed by Liu et al. [25] can save decoding time compared with the original ADMM-LP decoding algorithm. Over-relaxation can accelerate the algorithm's convergence speed, but simulation results show that when combined with over-relaxation, the algorithm of Liu et al. [25] yielded poor FER performance, which was affected by that the points far away from the even-vertex of the check polytope are excessively penalized. We try to constrain the punishment further to reduce the ratio of evenvertex projection. Based on these, an improved check polytope projection penalized algorithm is proposed. In order to ensure that the improved algorithm has less average decoding time while improving the FER performance, the complexity of the constraint condition needs to be relatively low. Drawing on the idea of check polytope penalty projection, the setting of constraints needs to be related to characteristics of the vector v itself. We calculate the normalized l2-norm distance and normalized l1-norm distance to the nearest even-vertex of the projected vector respectively to carry out constraint test on the projected vector. Experiments show that the results are similar when the constraint conditions are l1-norm distance and l2-norm distance, and l1-norm distance can avoid the calculation of square and square root, the complexity is relatively low, so we adopt it as the constraint conditions. If the value of t is within the scope of constraints, use the even-vertex as the projection result. On the contrary, the CSA is used to calculate the projection results. IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In our simulations, the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel with binary phase shift keying (BPSK) modulation is supposed. We consider the Margulis (2640,1320) rate 1/2 code C1, the irregular (576, 288) rate 1/2 code C2 and the regular (576, 480) code C3 from IEEE 802.16e. The check degree of C1, C2 and C3 are 6, {6, 7} and 20, respectively. The simulation experiment uses the penalty method in the literature [15]. All decoding algorithms use early stopping technology and overrelaxation technology, and the relaxation coefficient =1.9 .
The maximum number of iterations is set to 500, and at least 100,000 frames and 100 error frames are collected at each SNR. The comparative group based on the combination of check polytope projection penalized algorithm and cut search algorithm, which is denoted as ADMM-I-CSA. The improved algorithm we proposed denoted as ADMM-C-ICSA. And the original cut search algorithm is denoted as ADMM-CSA. At the same time, we imported ADMM-LSA and BP for comparison.

V. CONCLUSION
In summary, we proposed an improved check polytope projection penalized ADMM decoding algorithm which can constraint the ratio of even-vertex projection, achieve faster convergence and save the average decoding time. Compared to ADMM-I-CSA, the frame error rate performance of the proposed algorithm is significantly improved. What's more, compared with ADMM-CSA, the average decoding time of the proposed algorithm can be shortened by 22.9% -52.0%, which can guarantee the decoding performance and reduce the decoding time.
