Burst Error Identification Method for LDPC Coded Systems

Lintao Li , Member, IEEE, Jiayi Lv, Yimin Li, Xiaoming Dai , Member, IEEE, and Xiyuan Wang, Member, IEEE

Abstract— In this work, we propose an erasure aware technique to mitigate the detrimental effects of the burst error in a low density parity check(LDPC) coded system. First, we generate the estimated error pattern based on tentative decision over the received symbols and parity check matrix, via computing the total number of unsatisfied check equations for each variable node. Second, we determine the occurrence of burst errors based on the weight of error pattern, and identify the position and length of the burst error using a sliding window search method. Furthermore, the closed-form expressions of the detection probability and false alarm probability for burst error detection was derived. Finally, numerical and simulation results demonstrated that the proposed erasure-aware technique based on estimated or empirically determined threshold achieves significant performance gain over the conventional erasure-agnostic one.
Index Terms— Low density parity check (LDPC) code, burst error, burst erasure.

I. INTRODUCTION
LOW density parity check(LDPC) codes can provide near Shannon limit error correction ability when the iterative belief propagation(BP) algorithm is employed for decoding [1]. They have been widely used in modern communication systems, such as digital video broadcast(DVB), Wi-Fi(802.11n), 5G NR [2], [3], [4]. However, the LDPC code is severely vulnerable to burst errors. A moderate-length burst error can easily lead to significant performance degradation or even a high error floor. Therefore, alleviating the effect of burst errors and improving the error-correction capabilities of LDPC codes has attracted considerable research attention.
Numerous methods have been proposed to improve the burst error tolerance performance in LDPC coded systems [5], [6], [7], [8]. Since recent studies have demonstrated that the LDPC decoding performance can be improved considerably by erasing burst errors (setting the logarithm likelihood ratio of the corresponding variable nodes(VNs) to zero) in the BP decoding process [9]. In [14], authors introduced a pre-decoding processing step that updates the log-likelihood ratio(LLR) reliability information to reflect inferences in the presence of a jammer. In [15], authors proposed Guessing Random Additive Noise Decoding-Erasure Decoding by Gaussian Elimination (GRAND-EDGE) algorithm and its variants to address bit-level errors and erasures simultaneously, over an adversarial channel model. Most of the previous studies did not provide a theoretical analysis for the burst error detection performance, and the burst error location is always based on decoding outputs of LDPC which will surely introduce an extra processing delay.
This work develops a universal method that can identify burst errors in an LDPC code without using the decoding output. We first made a hard decision over the received symbols and calculated the number of unsatisfied check equations for each VN. The burst error was identified based on the weight and distribution of 1s in the estimated error vector.

V. CONCLUSION
This letter proposed an improved decoding method for the LDPC coded system in the presence of both burst and random errors. The proposed burst error identification method is conducted without using the decoding output, which render it suitable for implementation in practical systems. Following the main results presented in this study, a near future work is to extend the proposed burst error detection method to more realistic channel models. In this extension, multiple burst errors within a codeword and of various types will be considered.

B. BER Performance
In this subsection, we study the BER performance of LDPC coded communication system in the presence of both burst and random errors. We carry out simulations for LDPC-only (without burst error erasure), LDPC with perfect burst erasure and the proposed mehod when a single burst error occurs. Also, the modified min-sum decoding algorithm is employed and the maximum iteration number is 20. For the traditional CME (consecutive mean excision) method, JSR (jamming to signal ratio) represents the jamming signal power to signal power.
Experimental results in Fig. 5 show that a high error floor is caused by burst error with a moderate length. However, the proposed burst error identification algorithm followed by a LLR zero forcing method can significantly improve the BER performance of a LDPC coded system. The BER performance when the burst error positions are perfectly known is also studied, which can be seen as a performance bound for the burst erasure method. The proposed method outperforms the LDPC-only method, and induced approximately 1dB performance loss comparing with the performance bound. The BER performance is also compared with the traditional CME method which can detect the jamming signal based on received signal strength. The results show that the proposed method outperforms the traditional CME method in the scenarios of low JSR.
