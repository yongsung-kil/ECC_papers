Accelerating Pro-ADMM-HL Penalized Algorithm for Decoding Binary LDPC Codes

Jing Bai , Weipeng Duan, Yuhao Chi , Senior Member, IEEE, and Chau Yuen , Fellow, IEEE

Abstract— The alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) approach to decoding low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes has garnered lots of attention due to its superior error correction performance in error floor regions. However, improving convergence characteristic of ADMM decoding efficiently remains to be a crucial but challenging issue for practical application. In order to tackle this challenge, in this letter we first propose an efficient Pro-ADMM-based decoding algorithm framework by inserting additional quadratic proximal terms to each iteration of ADMM decoding for stabilizing the variable updates. Second, an message-passing interpretation of Pro-ADMM decoder is considered, and then an improved version based on horizontal layered scheduling, named Pro-ADMM-HL decoder is developed to further enhance the convergence behavior of Pro-ADMM decoding. Additionally, we analyze that the complexity of the proposed Pro-ADMM-HL decoders at each iteration displays linearly to LDPC code length. Finally, the effectiveness of the proposed Pro-ADMM and Pro-ADMM-HL decoding algorithm in terms of error correction performance and convergence characteristic is demonstrated by simulation results.
Index Terms— Low-density parity-check (LDPC), alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), horizontal layered scheduling, convergence characteristic.
I. INTRODUCTION
LINEAR programming (LP) decoding based on alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) iterative algorithm, known as ADMM-LP decoding, has become an attractive alternative approach for decoding low-density parity check (LDPC) codes in recent years due to its solid
mathematical foundation [1]. However, a projection onto a so-called check ploytope, a convex hull of all binary vectors with the same even weight, is requires in ADMM-LP decoding so that its decoding efficiency get reduced. As a result, much effort has been devoted to improving the check-ploytope projection for reducing the complexity of
ADMM-LP decoding [2], [3], [4], [5]. Moreover, Liu et al. introduced an improved ADMM-based
penalized decoder by adding penalty terms to the objective

function of LP decoding formulation [6]. These penalty terms increase the cost of undesirable non-integral solutions and thus improve the error correction performance of ADMM-LP decoding. Subsequently, there exists some work for optimizing ADMM-based penalized decoders by altering the penalizing strategies [7], [8]. Additionally, a parameter-free ℓp-box ADMM decoding algorithm was suggested to circumvente penalty parameter setting in [9].
On the other hand, ADMM-based decoding can be essentially seen as an iterative message-passing algorithm [1]. As a result, a limited amount of previous research has focused on investigating various scheduling technologies to enhance the convergence of ADMM-based decoding. Authors in [10] proposed a node-wise scheduling to lower the average number of decoding iterations for ADMM-based decoding. In [11] and [12], algorithmic formulations based on horizontal layered (HL) scheduling and vertical layered scheduling, respectively, were suggested to make a convergence improvement of ADMM-based decoders.
Furthermore, some improved ADMM versions, such as BADMM decoding [13] and Pro-ADMM decoding [14] were proposed by changing the structure of original ADMM algorithm to accelerate the convergence. However, few studies has been investigated on the underlying message passing interpretation of those improved ADMM algorithms to further enhance decoding performance of ADMM decoding so far. Motivated by this, we propose a fast converging Pro-ADMMHL penalized decoder by merging Pro-ADMM penalized decoding algorithm with HL scheduling strategy for LDPC codes. Besides, theoretical analyses show that the proposed Pro-ADMM-HL decoding algorithm has a linear complexity to code length in each iteration. Finally, the proposed ProADMM-HL penalized decoder is demonstrated to achieve better error correction performance and a faster convergence speed compared with the state-of-the-art ADMM decoders by simulation results.

The index set of nearby variables related to check j ∈ J is denoted by Ncj = {i ∈ I : Hji = 1}, and the degree of the j-th check node is indicated by djc.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, several simulation results are presented to illustrate the superiority of the proposed Pro-ADMM-HL Algorithm 2 in terms of error correction performance and convergence characteristic. Moreover, the state-of-the-art comparative algorithms considered in the simulations involves ADMM-based quadratic programming (ADMM-QP) decoder [5], the ADMM-based variable-node-penalized (ADMMVNP) decoder [6], the ADMM-based ℓp-box (ADMM-ℓpbox) decoder [9], ADMM-based with HL (ADMM-HL) decoder [11], G-BADMM decoder [13], Belief Propagation (BP) decoder [15] and HL-based BP (BP-HL) decoder [16]. In addition, in the simulations we take three LDPC codes from different communication applications into consideration, which are a “Margulis” regular (2640,1320) LDPC code C1 in [17], an irregular (576,288) LDPC code C2 from the IEEE 802.16e standard [18], and a regular (648,432) LDPC code C3 from the IEEE 802.11n standard [19], respectively.
In the simulations, the modulation mode used is binary phrase shift keying (BPSK) and the BIOS channel is based on an additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. V. CONCLUSION The novelty of this letter aims at improving convergence characteristic of ADMM decoding and introducing an efficient decoding algorithm for binary LDPC codes, based on a Pro-ADMM technique. The main idea of the introduced Pro-ADMM penalized decoder is that additional quadratic proximal terms are appended to each original ADMM penalized decoding iteration. Moreover, for further enhancing the convergence of Pro-ADMM penalized decoder, an improved Pro-ADMM decoder based on HL scheduling strategy, called Pro-ADMM-HL decoder, is proposed. Finally, simulation results demonstrate that our proposed Pro-ADMM-HL decoder achieves better error correction performance and convergence behavior than the state-of-the-art ADMM-based decoders.
