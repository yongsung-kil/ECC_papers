A Novel Ultra-Reliable Decoder of Short Rate-Compatible Codes for Mission Critical Communications
Ke Zhang , Member, IEEE, Chunjie Li, Jian Jiao , Senior Member, IEEE, Ye Wang , Member, IEEE,
Xiao Ma , Member, IEEE, and Qinyu Zhang , Senior Member, IEEE
Abstract—In this correspondence, towards the ultra-reliable low-latency requirements of mission critical communications (MCC) in autonomous driving, we design a novel ordered likelihood decoder (OLD) for short ratecompatible (RC) codes. Based on the linear approximation of ordered reliability sequence, we model the probability of test error patterns (TEPs) to the flipping weight (FW), which only corresponds to the flipped positions of most reliable basis (MRB). Then, an integer partitions-aided TEPs generating criterion (GC) is proposed to generate the ordered TEPs in near-optimal. Further, by combining the weighted Hamming distance (WHD) estimation and the proposed TEPs GC, a stopping criterion is given to terminate the decoding when the most likely TEP is found. Comparing to the existing ordered statistics decoders (OSD) for RC coding schemes, simulation results show that the proposed OLD can achieve superior performance in terms of error-rate performance and decoding complexity.
Index Terms—Ordered statistics decoder, rate-compatible (RC), test error patterns probability, flipping weight, error-rate performance.
I. INTRODUCTION
The short block-length channel coding technology, including the strong encoding schemes with bit-level granularity and flexibility of code rates, have rekindled a great deal of interest among mission critical communications [1], [2], [3], and mainly used in status and location update in autonomous driving [4].
To support the mission critical communications (MCC) with various channel conditions in autonomous driving scenario, the novel high-performance decoders for rate-compatible (RC) coding schemes have attracted a great deal of interests [5]. For a linear block code with minimum Hamming distance dmin, the ordered statistics decoder (OSD) [6], [7], [8], [9], [10] with order of dmin/4 − 1 can approach the maximum likelihood (ML) decoding performance, coming in as an

The Fast-OSD [9], which combines the probabilistic necessary condition (PNC) and probabilistic sufficient condition (PSC) together, is proposed to restrict the re-encoding number at high signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs). Moreover, a segmentation-discarding decoding (SDD) algorithm is proposed in [13], which divides the test error patterns (TEPs) sequence into several segments and discards a group of TEPs when a condition is satisfied. In this correspondence, we model the probability of TEPs to the Flipping Weight (FW) by linear approximation of ordered likelihood. Aided by integer partitions, a novel TEPs generating criterion (GC) is proposed with near-optimal order. Furthermore, based on the weighted Hamming distance (WHD) estimation and the proposed TEPs GC, a stopping criterion (SC) is given to terminate decoding after the most likely TEP is processed. The proposed ordered likelihood decoder (OLD) can terminate the decoding adaptively and does not need to specify the decoding order or maximum number of TEPs, and simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can achieve better error-rate performance and lower decoding complexity compared to the existing OSD algorithms, especially for the RC coding schemes.

B. Average WHD Estimation and Stopping Criterion (SC)

C. Decoding Process of OLD
The OLD first enables the 0-order re-encoding by MRB cB and initials the current minimum LRB WHD as DL∗ RB(ce, c) = DLRB(cBG, c). By employing the TEPs GC and the proposed SC, the TEPs are generated by setting likelihood R = [αk/Q], which corresponds to the last bit of MRB be flipped. Then, with the likelihood R increasing, we can get the corresponding TEPs in near-descending order of its probability. The OLD does not need to specify the decoding order or a predetermined maximum number of TEPs, and can be terminated only when the proposed SC is satisfied.
D. Complexity Analysis
Compared to OSD, the additional complexity in OLD is caused by TEPs generating and stopping criterion. In the actual decoding process, the TEPs GC can be done by table look-up. Conversely, the additional complexity caused by SC has a lesser impact than the reduction in the number of re-coding. Due to the space limitation of the paper, we omit the detail computational complexity analysis of OLD, and only reflects the average re-encoding number as a metric of decoding complexity.
IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, we present the simulation results to demonstrate the performance of the proposed OLD algorithm. The compared decoding schemes including conventional OSD [6], Fast-OSD [9], PB-OSD [15] and cyclic redundancy check (CRC)-aided successive cancellation list (CA-SCL) decoding with list size L = 32 for 5G NR polar codes [17]. Besides, the Polyanskiy-Poor-Verdu´ (PPV) normal approximation (NA) bounds [18] are also considered to illustrate the performance limits. All simulations are conducted by assuming BPSK modulation and AWGN channels, we show the decoding performances and complexity of decoders by comparing the block error rate (BLER) and average re-encoding number, respectively.
As shown in Fig. 3, the OLD exhibits the best performance than other algorithms. For example, in decoding the (128, 42) 5G CRC-11 polar code and (128, 64, 22) eBCH code, the OLD with D = 2 has almost the same performance as that of OSD with order 5, and can approach the NA bound.
The same simulation is conducted for two different code lengths of 5G CRC-11 polar codes with k = 64, as shown in Fig. 4(a) and (b).

Fig. 5. Eb/N0 required to achieve BLER = 10−4 for 5G CRC-11 polar codes with fixed message length k = 42.
For code (96, 64), all the variants of OSD present almost the identical BLER performance and outperform CA-SCL. However, the average number of re-encoding for OLD is much smaller than that of existing OSD algorithms. For code (128, 64), the proposed OLD has the best performance with 0.3 ∼ 0.6 dB decoding gain and requires almost the same average number of re-encoding compared to state-of-the-art lowest complexity decoding algorithm (PB-OSD). The above experimental simulation results show that the proposed OLD can achieve better BLER performance with lower complexity compared to the existing decoding algorithms. It is worth to note that the optimal order of OSD is decreased as the code rate increases. For instance, Fig. 4(a) demonstrates that a smaller OSD order (order of 2) achieves the optimal decoding performance. In comparison to the 2-OSD, the performance enhancement of OLD is not substantial, given that for (96, 64) polar codes, the 2-OSD is already capable of approximating the ML decoding performance. However, the decoding complexity of OLD is considerably less than that of the 2-OSD.
As shown in Fig. 5, we depict the required Eb/N0 to achieve BLER of 10−4 for RC 5G CRC-11 polar codes with k = 42 and 54 ≤ n ≤ 124. Fig. 5 shows that the OLD is much closer to PPV NA bound than other decoding algorithms with various code rates, which indicates that OLD has excellent performance at RC coding schemes.
Furthermore, compared with the existing OSD algorithm, the proposed OLD algorithm can be adapted to the RC coding schemes, and cascade the existing iterative decoder, such as neural decoder for RC LDPC codes [19], to realize the error performance improvement. In the future, we will investigate the decoding performance of OLD in realistic channel models based on exact channel estimation [20].

V. CONCLUSION
Towards the ultra-reliable low-latency requirements for MCC in autonomous driving, we proposed the OLD algorithm for short RC codes. Different from the existing OSD algorithms with fixed decoding order or a predetermined maximum number of TEPs, we designed a novel TEPs GC by modeling the TEP probability to the FW, combining the adaptive stopping criterion based on the WHD estimation, the OLD can terminate the decoding with most likely TEP. Simulation results illustrate that the proposed OLD can achieve superior decoding gains with identical complexity compared to the existing PB-OSD for short eBCH and polar coding schemes. Furthermore, for the RC 5G polar codes, the OLD algorithm approach to the PPV NA bound over various code rates.
