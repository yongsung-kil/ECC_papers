An Efficient Ordered Likelihood Decoder for
Rate-Compatible Short LDPC codes
Chunjie Li1,2, Ke Zhang∗,2, Ye Wang2, Jian Jiao2,3, Xiao Ma1 and Qinyu Zhang2,3 1School of Computer Science and Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou 510006, China
2Pengcheng Laboratory, Shenzhen 518055, China 3Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen), Shenzhen 518055, China {lichj, zhangk, wangy02}@pcl.ac.cn, {jiaojian, zqy}@hit.edu.cn, maxiao@mail.sysu.edu.cn

Abstract—This paper proposes a concatenated multi-belief ordered likelihood decoding (MB-OLD) algorithm for ratecompatible (RC) short low-density parity check (LDPC) codes, where the output log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) of belief propagation (BP) are sent to a well-designed bit-flipping decoder, which we called ordered likelihood decoder (OLD). In contrast to conventional ordered statistic decoder (OSD), the test error patterns (TEPs) sequence of OLD is generated from most likely to least likely, where the ordered reliability sequence associated with the most reliable basis (MRB) is approximated as multiple lines, and a stopping criterion (SC) is taken to reduce the decoding complexity. Furthermore, we analyze the LLR behavior of BP decoder in short block-length regimes, and propose an optimal iteration number. Based on these analyses, the output LLRs of BP within the optimal number of iterations are well combined and sent to OLD. Simulation results show that the proposed MB-OLD has the superior decoding performances in terms of error-rate and decoding complexity than its counterparts.
Index Terms—Multi-belief ordered likelihood decoder, ratecompatible, short LDPC codes, stopping criterion, decoding performance.
I. INTRODUCTION
Towards the ultra-Reliable Low-Latency Communication (uRLLC) requirements, the coding schemes require low latency (end-to-end latency less than 1 ms) and various reliability (block error rates as low as 10−4 ∼ 10−9) with maximum efficiency, and can adapt to varying channel conditions [1]–[4]. However, channel coding with strong error performance at short block-lengths is still an open problem due to the conflicting requirements of reliability and low latency. Specifically, powerful codes with long block-lengths offer reliability at the expense of latency while short block-length codes offer low latency but compromise reliability. Recently, limits of coding at short block-lengths have been derived in [5], but the design of efficient short codes and decoders still raise many challenges [6].

LDPC codes are a class of capacity-approaching codes. By adopting the belief propagation (BP) decoding, LDPC codes can achieve excellent error performance and approach to the Shannon channel capacity in sufficiently large blocklength regimes [7]. Owing to their superior error correction abilities and high throughput, LDPC codes have been employed for the 5G enhanced Mobile Broadband user data channel [8]. However, for the short LDPC codes, BP decoding algorithm is sub-optimal due to the short cycles in their Tanner graphs [9]. As mentioned by [10], the error performance of short LDPC codes could be further improved by resorting to maximum likelihood (ML) decoding.
The ordered statistic decoder (OSD) [11] has been proposed for the short block-length codes and can approach the performance of ML decoding with the order of m = dmin/4 − 1 , but the complexity can be as high as O(km), where k is message length and dmin is minimum Hamming distance of the code. Although many works have been proposed to reduce the complexity of OSD [12]–[15], almost all existing OSD variants still have high complexity to achieve ML performance [6]. In the literature, there are some nearML decoding algorithms for short LDPC codes by combining the BP and OSD [16]–[19], where the concatenated OSD can correct the errors that are not corrected by BP, and further improve the error performance of short LDPC codes. A BP-aided OSD algorithm is first proposed in [16], where the OSD is performed over the output LLRs of BP decoding at each iteration. Then, a serial concatenation of BP and OSD is given in [17], where the OSD is performed over the accumulated LLR during iterative decoding. Moreover, a novel modified-BP-OSD is proposed in [18], where a modified BP (mBP) algorithm needs to be performed to enhance the reliability of the received message. However, these researches are insufficient for rate-compatible (RC) coding schemes, where the setting of the BP iteration number and decoding order of OSD are lack of theoretical analysis, and leads to performance degradation at different code rates. In general, the concatenation of BP and OSD is a potential way to achieve great performance with low complexity for short LDPC codes.

III. ORDERED LIKELIHOOD DECODER
The idea of OSD is to find the optimal TEP with the minimum WHD of codeword. For a m-order OSD, the main decoding complexity is related to TEP number, where the potential TEP number of searching can be up to 1 + im=1(ik). However, for the various code rates of RC codes, the optimal order of OSD cannot be predetermined, which leads to the degradation of error performance.
In this section, we first propose a novel ordered likelihood decoder (OLD) for RC linear block codes [20]. In OLD, a TEPs sequence generating method is presented based on the approximation of ordered reliabilities, where the TEPs are generated in optimal order. Then, a stopping criterion (SC) is taken to reduce the complexity, and the decoding is terminated when the most likely TEP is found.

C. Decoding Process of OLD
The initialization of OLD is similar to OSD. However, after the order-0 is processed, the TEPs are generated by setting approximate likelihood R = [αk/Q], which corresponds to the last bit of MRB being flipped. Then, with the likelihood R increasing, we can get the corresponding TEPs in the near-descending order of their probability. The decoding will be terminated when the SC is satisfied. Of course, a predetermined maximum number of TEPs can be set to limit the complexity. The decoding process of the proposed OLD is shown in Algorithm 1.

IV. MULTI-BELIEF ORDERED LIKELIHOOD DECODER
A. MB-OLD for LDPC codes in short block-length regimes
Assume that the girth of an LDPC code is g, which is equal to the length of its shortest cycle. Then, the input messages of each node are independent before g/2th iteration [7]. In other words, the messages of each node are dependent after (g/2 − 1)-th iteration, where the nodes contained in the girth will receive copies of their initial messages. Therefore, the output LLR sequence L(l) is not reliable when iteration number l ≥ g/2.
To enhance the performance of short block-length LDPC codes, we proposed a novel multi-belief OLD (MB-OLD) by considering the impact of short cycles. VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have proposed a novel BP-aided concatenated decoding algorithm (MB-OLD) for RC short LDPC codes by considering the girth of LDPC codes, and the output LLRs of BP within the optimal number of iterations are combined and sent to OLD. Different with the existing OSD algorithms, we have designed a TEPs generating method for OLD to generate the TEPs from most likely to least likely. Combining with the stopping criterion, the decoder can terminate the decoding when the most likely TEP is found. Simulation results show that MB-OLD can achieve better error performance and lower decoding complexity compared to the existing BP and BP-OSD decoders for the short LDPC with RC coding schemes.
