Parallel Network Structure-aided Neural Shuffled Offset Min-Sum Decoder
Liang Lv‚ąó, Yurong Wang‚ąó, Yi Fang‚ąó, and Pingping Chen‚Ä† ‚ąóSchool of Information Engineering, Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou 510006, China
‚Ä†Department of Electronic and Information Engineering, Fuzhou University, Fuzhou 350108, China Email: {lianglv0206@163.com, wangyurong0714@163.com, fangyi@gdut.edu.cn, ppchen.xm@gmail.com}

Abstract‚ÄĒThe paper carries out an in-depth investigation on the neural decoder design for protograph low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes. To be specific, based on the model-driven method, we propose a new type of protograph neural decoder with the parallel network (ParNet) decoding structure, called neural shuffled offset min-sum (NSOMS) decoder. The ParNet uses the model-driven method to unfold shuffled LDPC decoding algorithms into the interpretable network. Furthermore, with the aid of the over-estimation value (OEV), we optimize the training method to accelerate the convergence speed of the proposed decoding network. Theoretical and simulation results indicate that the proposed NSOMS decoder exhibits desirable performance compared to the state-of-the-art counterparts. Hence, the proposed NSOMS decoder can be regarded as a terrific solution for high-reliability communications.
Index Terms‚ÄĒProtograph low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, parallel network (ParNet) structure, neural shuffled offset min-sum (NSOMS) decoder, over-estimation value (OEV).
I. INTRODUCTION
The protograph low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, well-known for their flexible structure and efficient error correction, are widely used in 5G new radio (NR) communications [1], [2]. For decoding, protograph LDPC codes typically adopt the belief propagation (BP) algorithm [3]. As the improved versions, the min-sum (MS) [4], normalized min-sum (NMS) [5], and offset min-sum (OMS) [6] algorithms can reduce the computational complexity. To enhance the convergence performance of the decoding, the layered and shuffled schedules have been proposed to optimize the iterative decoding [7], [8]. Although the above-mentioned traditional decoding algorithms achieve excellent performance in error correction, a well-designed neural decoder can perform more efficient decoding with the rapid development of neural networks.
The neural network can improve the decoding performance of LDPC decoders by learning more optimal message-passing strategies [9]. For instance, the factor graph neural network (FGNN) integrating with the BP algorithm has been proposed to improve the performance of the LDPC decoder [10]. However, the FGNN suffers from high complexity because it requires a massive number of network parameters and

In addition, many neural decoder design-related works that combine the neural network with low-complexity decoding algorithms have been proposed to decrease the complexity and accelerate the convergence performance of decoding. In other words, the model-driven method has been utilized in LDPC decoder design [11]. Specifically, a neural layered decoder combines the layered MS (LMS) algorithm with deep neural network (DNN), called neural LMS (NLMS) decoder [12], and a neural MS decoder with the parameter-sharing mechanism, called neural flooding MS (NFMS) decoder [13], have been proposed. Nevertheless, the existing neural decoders lack generalization capacities and require more hidden layers to ensure the iterative decoding converges. Moreover, the authors have proposed a state-of-theart parallel network (ParNet) structure with network branches to reduce the depth of the network [14]. However, the existing neural decoders do not consider adopting the ParNet structure. Therefore, designing a low-complexity and well-performing neural decoder by combining the advantages of network structures and decoding algorithms is a challenging issue.
Furthermore, the network training method is critical to the neural decoder and directly affects the performance and learning efficiency of the neural decoding network. In particular, a multi-loss training method based on the binarycross-entropy (BCE) loss function has been used to increase the backpropagation gradient [15]. However, the BCE loss function has limited feature learning for log-likelihood ratios (LLRs), resulting in the inadequate update of network parameters. To solve the inefficient parameter update issue, the over-estimation value (OEV) has been utilized to improve the performance of traditional MS decoders [16]. Also, the OEV can be compatible well with the ParNet structure. Thus, inspired by the OEV and the ParNet structure, designing a novel network training method for the parallel decoding network is beneficial to improving the training ability of the neural decoder.

II. PRELIMINARIES
A. Protograph LDPC Codes
The LDPC codes adopted in 5G NR systems are protograph LDPC codes with the quasi-cyclic (QC) structure [2]. To construct a protograph-based QC LDPC code, the lifting operation, i.e., ‚Äúcopy-and-permute‚ÄĚ, is performed on a base graph (BG), i.e., protograph. The BG with code rate R = (nb ‚ąí mb)/(nb ‚ąí 2) corresponds to a base matrix B = (bi,j) with size of mb √ó nb, where mb and nb are the numbers of check nodes (CNs) and variable nodes (VNs), respectively, bi,j ‚ąą {0, 1} is the edge connecting CN ci and VN vj. Subsequently, BG is copied Z times to derive a Tanner graph (i.e., parity-check matrix H) with size of mbZ √ó nbZ. Note that the BG has the same properties as the derived Tanner graph [17]. Then, the edges are permuted by ‚Äúcyclic-shifting‚ÄĚ operations. The base matrix B corresponds to a cyclic shift matrix F = (fi,j) with the same size, where fi,j denotes the cyclic shift index of bi,j. The elements ‚Äú0‚ÄĚ and ‚Äú1‚ÄĚ in a base matrix B are replaced by an all-zero matrix OZ√óZ and an identity matrix IZ√óZ , respectively. After that, each identity matrix IZ√óZ that replaces the element ‚Äú1‚ÄĚ is cyclically shifted to the right pi,j = mod (fi,j, Z) times. Based on the lifting operation, a protograph LDPC code can be generated.

B. Training the Proposed NSOMS Decoder
In neural networks, the loss function is crucial for the training of the network. The suitable loss function can enhance the learning ability and effectiveness of the proposed network. For example, the existing neural decoding networks adopt the BCE loss function [15], [18]. However, the existing neural decoders suffer from the inefficient parameter update, resulting in inferior convergence performance for the decoding network. As a result, designing a novel training method for the proposed ParNet is a worthy topic to be explored.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS
In this section, we provide the performance analysis of the proposed protograph NSOMS decoding systems under the TensorFlow framework to verify the advantages of the proposed NSOMS decoder. Unless otherwise mentioned, the channel coding scheme is 5G BG2 code [20], the number of hidden layers is 10 (i.e., iteration number is 10), g = 2, the batch size is 1, the learning rate is 0.0001, the training SNR range of 0 to 5 dB, and the training batch number is 1600.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a protograph neural decoder, namely the NSOMS decoder, based on the ParNet structure has been developed. For the training and inference stages, we have proposed the two different network structures by the layer arrangement and parameter simplification to improve the pertinence of networks. Furthermore, with the aid of the OEV, we have optimized the training method of the proposed NSOMS decoder. The proposed training method ensures that the decoding network can perform efficient parameter updates adequately. Theoretical and simulation results demonstrate that the proposed NSOMS decoder outperforms the state-ofthe-art neural decoders in terms of error performance and

convergence speed. In addition, the proposed NSOMS decoder
possesses lower computational complexity from hardware
implementation perspective. Thanks to the benefits mentioned
above, the proposed NSOMS decoder stands out as a promis-
ing technique for wireless communication scenarios.
