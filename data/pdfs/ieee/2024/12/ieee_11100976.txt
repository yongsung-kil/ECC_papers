Low-Complexity Layered Decoding for LDPC
Codes
Heping Wan, Joonyoung Cho, Min Jang, Charlie Jianzhong Zhang, Fellow, IEEE
Abstract—This paper presents methods to reduce the com-
plexity of the layered decoding for LDPC codes with negligibly
impacting the error-correcting performance. We accomplish this
by deactivating variable nodes. The LLR updates associated
with the deactivated variable nodes are skipped. To determine
which variable nodes should be deactivated, the most reliable
bit-level channels, derived from the 𝑀−ary input channel, are
identified. Then, from the variable nodes assigned to the most
reliable bit-level channels, we select those with initial LLRs
above a threshold as the deactivated variable nodes. We establish
an analytical relationship between the threshold and a desired
error probability. The threshold can be adjusted accordingly by
specifying the desired error probability. The standard 5G LDPC
codes are simulated. By setting the desired error probability to
10−3, more than 20% of the variable nodes are deactivated while
the error-correcting performance is preserved.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1], due to its
ability to approach channel capacity asymptotically, have
been widely applied to practical communications systems. For
example, the quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes have been
adopted to support throughput up to 20 Gbps in NR-5G data
channel [2].
The belief propagation (BP) decoding is essential for the
successful implementation of LDPC codes. It operates by it-
eratively exchanging the log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) between
check nodes (CNs) and variable nodes (VNs) in a Tanner graph
representing LDPC codes. This process continues until either
the decoding converges or the maximum number of iterations
is reached. The slower the convergence speed, the more
iterations are required to achieve a block error rate (BLER).
Conventionally, the BP decoding utilizes a flooding schedule
where the LLRs for the CNs/VNs are updated simultaneously
in each iteration.
Although benefiting from full parallelism, the flooding
schedule suffers from a slow convergence speed. This issue
can be mitigated by using the layered decoding [3] which
reduces the necessary number of iterations by half. In each
iteration of the layered decoding, the CNs are processed in
a sequential manner, ensuring that the latest messages are
immediately exploited by the succeeding CNs and resulting
in a faster convergence speed. To further boost the throughput
of LDPC decoding, Zimmermann et al. [4] proposed to detect
the reliability of the VNs in each iteration and stop updating
the reliable VNs in the subsequent iterations. The reliability

However, the above methods introduce extra computational
complexity mainly due to the detection of the reliable VNs in
each iteration.
This paper aims to reduce the complexity of the layered
decoding by skipping the LLR updates for a set of VNs.
The set is constructed based on the characteristics of the
initial LLRs produced by the soft demodulators. Therefore,
the construction of the set is completed prior to the layered
decoding and remains unchanged throughout the decoding
iterations. We also develop a method to manage the trade-off
between the complexity and the error-correcting performance,
by analyzing the probability density function (PDF) of the
initial LLRs.
In Section II, the (standard) layered decoding and the
layered decoding incorporating the proposed complexity-
reduction methods are described. Section III details the pro-
posed methods. In Section IV, simulation results are demon-
strated and discussed. Section V concludes the paper.

probability if the absolute value of its AP-LLR is large. For
convenience, when referring to a “large” LLR in the sequel, we
are implying that the absolute value of the LLR is significant.
With the decoding iterations, AP-LLRs increase and even-
tually converge, indicating their stability in the subsequent
iterations and resulting in correct decisions. Therefore, when
the AP-LLR of a VN is large, it can be deactivated (i.e., the
relevant LLR updates are skipped) subsequently, to reduce the
complexity of LDPC decoder. However, the VNs converge
with different speeds, that says some of the AP-LLRs turn out
to be large in a few iterations while others stay small. In [4]–
[9], the deactivated VNs (dVNs) are recognized dynamically in
each iteration, causing additional complexity. In this paper, the
set of the deactivated VNs (dVNs) are constructed solely based
on the initial LLRs. This construction is completed before the
decoding iterations start and unchanged during the decoding
iterations.

The 5G LDPC codes with and without the proposed
complexity-reduction methods are simulated. Fig. 2 illustrates
the structure of the PCM base graph for the 5G LDPC codes. It
is composed of the matrices A, B, C, the all-zero matrix 0 and
the identity matrix I. The systematic bits are generated by the

Throughout simulations, the QAM-256 and the bit inter-

ensure that the most reliable levels carry as many systematic

than half of the systematic bits are allocated to the most
reliable levels. Note that the bits corresponding to the first two

The technique of shortening is employed to support the
rate matching of the 5G LDPC codes. The shortening bits
are specific systematic bits which are set to zeros. At the
receiver side, the initial LLRs of the shortening bits are set
as infinity. Therefore, the shortening VNs are deactivated.
Moreover, updating the AP-LLRs and the C2V LLRs for the
SPC VNs is also redundant because:
• The hard decisions of the SPC bits are unwanted, because
they are not involved in the syndrome check,
• Each SPC VN has one neighboring CN and hence it only
needs to pass the initial LLR to its neighbor in each
iteration.
Since deactivating the shortening VNs and the SPC VNs has
no impact on the BLER of the message bits, it is applied in
all simulations (including the simulations under the standard
layered decoding).

In the decoding iterations, dVNs are prohibited from re-
ceiving intrinsic LLRs to correct the signs of their AP-LLRs,
which may degrade the error-correcting performance of the
layered decoding. Figs. 4c and 4d demonstrate the BLER
performance of the layered decoding with and without dVNs
S. 𝑃𝑑= 10−2 and 𝑃𝑑= 10−3 result in up to 1.6 dB loss
and negligible loss, respectively. The 1.6 dB loss is attributed
to the fact that setting 𝑃𝑑= 10−2 introduces additional hard
decisions based solely on the initial LLRs of small values,
thereby increasing the number of bit errors. In short, the BLER
performance depends on the ratio of the dVNs. This explains
the spikes of the BLER curves. For example, the required SNR
increases from 𝐾= 700 to 𝐾= 800 in Fig. 4c, which is due
to the incremental of the ratio of the dVNs from 𝐾= 700 to
𝐾= 800 in Fig. 4a.
Deactivating VNs accelerates the convergence of the layered
decoding. As shown in Figs. 4e and 4f, setting 𝑃𝑑= 10−2
reduces the average number of iterations (ANI) by up to 1.7
and a slight ANI reduction is also observed for 𝑃𝑑= 10−3.
According to the results shown in Fig. 4, setting 𝑃𝑑= 10−3
results in a good trade-off between the complexity reduction
and the BLER loss. Although setting 𝑃𝑑as 10−2 brings up to
1.7 ANI reduction, it leads to significant BLER loss and trivial
additional reduction of VNs. For different target BLERs, 𝑃𝑑
needs to be designed accordingly to achieve good trade-off.
To make a trade-off between the error-correcting perfor-
mance and the complexity, a lookup table of the thresholds
depending on the coding parameters and SNRs can be con-
structed offline by employing the proposed threshold design.
To highlight the optimal performance of our method, the
simulation results are obtained by implementing the threshold
design on the fly.
V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we reduce the complexity of the layered
decoding for LDPC codes with negligible impact on perfor-
mance. The LLR updates are omitted for the variable nodes
characterized as the deactivated variable nodes. We identify
the most reliable bit-level channels which are derived from
the 𝑀-ary input channel. A variable node is characterized as
the deactivated variable nodes when it is assigned to the most
reliable bit-level channels and its initial LLR is larger than a
threshold. We develop a method to design the threshold on
the basis of the PDF of the initial LLRs. The threshold can be
accordingly generated by adjusting a parameter. The 5G LDPC
codes are evaluated. With a proper selection of the parameter
(i.e., 𝑃𝑑= 10−3), over 20% variable-node reduction is obtained
without sacrificing the BLER performance.
