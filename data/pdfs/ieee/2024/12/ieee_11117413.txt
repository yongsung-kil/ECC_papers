An LDPC Decoding Method Using a Neural Network

as a De-mapper

Shuailong Zhu Department of Computer Technology and Applications
Qinghai University Qinghai Province, 810016, China

Lijun Zhang* Department of Computer Technology and Applications
Qinghai University Qinghai Province, 810016, China *Corresponding author’s e-mail: ys220854040296@qhu.edu.cn

Abstract: In this paper, we propose a novel LDPC decoding model that employs a fully connected neural network as a demapper to achieve excellent decoding performance while maintaining low computational complexity and computational effort. This neural network model does not need to rely on external coding during the training process, thus significantly reducing the required computational resources. In addition, the model can quickly converge to a parameter configuration with excellent performance through a limited number of iterations. To further enhance the decoding results, this paper also combines this neural demapper with a modified version of the confidence propagation (BP) algorithm. The integrated model achieves excellent decoding performance while keeping the computational load low, even under more stringent conditions.
Keywords: neural network; error correction; code; communication; LDPC
I. Introduction
LDPC code is widely used in wireless communication because of its performance close to the Shannon limit. It is mainly used to improve the reliability and efficiency of data transmission in 5G communication, which is particularly hot nowadays. Due to its ability to effectively handle large data blocks and high throughput transmission scenarios and the efficient error correction capability and low decoding complexity of LDPC codes, it plays an important role in 5G NR. The Belief Propagation algorithm is one of the main methods to decode LDPC codes, which is a probabilistic iterative algorithm, and it can be used to decode LDPC codes by adding the variable nodes and checkpoints in the LDPC code’s Tanner graph of LDPC code by exchanging probabilistic information between the variable nodes and the check nodes in the graph. However, the BP decoding algorithm has the disadvantage of high computational effort and computational complexity. A large number of floating point operations are involved in each iteration. In addition, as the code length of the LDPC code increases, the number of iterations to be performed may also increase, which further increases the computational effort and complexity. There are many researchers who have proposed improved algorithms for the BP algorithm , [1][2][3][4][5][7][8][9] which effectively simplify the computational complexity and computation of the BP algorithm. The goal of our research is to achieve decoding results comparable to the BP algorithm while keeping the computational complexity and computational effort low. For this purpose, we adopt two improved algorithms, MinSum [6] as well as Boxplus-Phi, as our decoding strategy, which also reduces the computational effort of our model.

In the current research, decoding methods for low-density parity-check codes mainly focus on the improvement of the traditional confidence propagation Belief Propagation algorithm. The BP algorithm requires complex probabilistic multiplication and division computations in each iteration, which leads to its high computational complexity. In order to reduce this complexity, researchers have proposed a variety of improved algorithms that aim to increase efficiency by reducing the amount of computation or simplifying certain arithmetic steps, while trying to maintain or improve the decoding performance. For example, the Min-Sum and Boxplus algorithms reduce the reliance on complex functions by simplifying the computational process. The Min-Sum algorithm reduces the computational complexity by replacing multiplication and division with the minimization operation. The Boxplus algorithm, on the other hand, tries to improve the decoding performance by more accurate probability propagation while keeping the computational effort low. In addition, the Boxplus-Phi algorithm, an improved version of Boxplus, further optimizes the numerical stability, making the algorithm more efficient for hardware implementation. In this study, we develop an innovative LDPC decoding model called Neuraldemapper_boxplus-phi, which skillfully combines neural network techniques with a simplified version of the confidence propagation algorithm. Through this combination, we not only maintain the efficient performance of LDPC decoding, but also significantly reduce the computational complexity. Specifically, the Neuraldemapper_boxplus-phi model utilizes a neural network as a demapper to work in concert with a simplified BP algorithm to achieve efficient optimization of computational resources without sacrificing decoding effectiveness.
A. Neural demapper
The neural network demapper architecture used in this study consists of a well-designed three-layer neural network, as shown in Figure 1. Since we are using a 64-QAM (Quadrature Amplitude Modulation) constellation diagram, where every six binary bits are modulated as a complex signal, the last layer of neurons of the network outputs the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) value corresponding to the six bits of that complex signal. This design allows the model to accurately process and map signals in higher-order modulation formats.
In this architecture, the first layer after the input layer is equipped with 256 neurons, immediately followed by the second layer reduced to 128 neurons, and finally each complex number is represented by 6 neurons in the output layer.

For the choice of activation function, the ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit) function is chosen for the first two layers of the neural network in order to enhance the nonlinear representation of the network, as well as to promote the convergence speed of the network training. For the last layer, we chose not to use the activation function, which was done to directly output the LLR value so that the subsequent decoding algorithm can handle it more efficiently. Overall, this neural network structure is designed to optimize the accuracy and efficiency of the demapping process, providing a solid foundation for highperformance communication systems.
B. Neuraldemapper_boxplus-phi

This study proposes a neural network model with dualmode operation, including training mode and testing mode. The flowchart of the training pattern is shown in Figure 2. The core objective of the training mode is to improve the performance of the model in the testing phase by optimizing the parameters of the neural network. In order to reduce the computational load and save time during the training process, this study adopts an innovative approach: instead of relying on external coding, equal-length random binary sequences are directly generated as input. These sequences are processed through a neural network and their outputs are compared with the original inputs in a binary cross-entropy loss function to calculate the errors. Subsequently, the model will be iteratively optimized based on these errors and the network parameters will be adjusted until a lower loss value is reached, thus ensuring that the model can show excellent performance in real applications. Through this approach, we are able to effectively improve the training efficiency of the model while maintaining or even improving the decoding accuracy of the model.
In the testing mode, the model is encoded with LDPC and the Boxplus-Phi algorithm, a simplified version of the Belief Propagation (BP) decoding algorithm, is applied to perform the decoding computation. The flowchart of the test pattern is shown in Figure 3. The decoding process employs a hard verdict mechanism to obtain the final decoded result. In this mode, we focus on two key performance metrics: Block Error Rate (BLER) and Bit Error Rate (BER).

To be consistent with the training mode, the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) in the testing mode is similarly set in the range of 4.0 to 8.0 dB with a step of 0.5 dB. In order to effectively reduce the amount of computation, two conditions for early termination of the simulation are set in this study: first, if the number of training error blocks reaches 1,000 at a certain SNR level, the simulation at that SNR level is terminated, and the simulation is transferred to the next SNR level; and second, if no error block occurs throughout the entire simulation at a certain SNR level, the model is considered to have zero error in the simulation at that SNR level and above. The simulation will remain with zero error, thus terminating further simulations of

the model to improve simulation efficiency.
In the field of communications, it is often assumed that if a model exhibits zero BER at a particular SNR, then its BER will also remain zero at higher SNR levels. This assumption is based on the intrinsic link between SNR and BER, an increase in SNR usually improves the performance of a communication system. With these strategies, we aim to optimize the simulation process to ensure an efficient and accurate evaluation of the model performance with limited computational resources. In order to enhance the generalization ability of the neural network demapper, we introduce the concept of stochastic signal-tonoise ratio (SNR) during the training process. Specifically, the

II. Experiments
A. Settings
In this paper, we designed an experiment. Three control groups were set up in the experiment as follows:
1. Baseline: LDPC coding using A Posteriori Probability demapping and minsum decoding as a baseline.
2. NeuralDemapper_minsum: a decoding method combining neural network demapping and minsum decoding.
3. Boxplus-phi: a decoding method combining Posteriori Probability demapping with the boxplus-Phi algorithm.
In the experimental design, we carefully selected three different code length configurations, specifically 300, 600, and 1200 bits, each maintaining a constant coding rate of 0.5, which implies that the corresponding message code lengths are 150, 300, and 600 bits, respectively. In order to optimise the performance of the neural network, a total of 5000 iterations of the training process were performed to ensure that the model parameters achieved a good fit. The channel chosen for the experiment is an additive Gaussian white noise (AWGN) channel, and the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) is set in the range of 4.0 to 8.0 dB with a step size of 0.5 dB. With this experimental design, we can comprehensively evaluate the performance of different decoding methods under the same conditions, thus providing an experimental basis for the optimization of LDPC coding. The experimental results will help us to understand the performance differences of different decoding algorithms under low SNR conditions, as well as the potential and effectiveness of neural network demappers in practical applications.

We use Sionna [10] to build and train our models and conduct entire simulation experiments. Sionna is an open-source library based on TensorFlow for simulating the physical layers of wireless and optical communication systems. Using differentiable layers, gradients can be back-propagated through the entire system, which is key for system optimization and machine learning, especially the integration of neural networks. One of the key advantages of Sionna is that components can be trained or replaced by neural networks, enabling easy and fast simulation of our ideas.

IV. Conclusion
In this paper, we propose an LDPC decoding method using a fully connected neural network as a demapper, which achieves relatively good decoding results under relatively harsh experimental conditions. Although our study provides valuable insights, there are some limitations. First, our sample size limits the generalizability of our results. Second, we only investigated the effectiveness of the Neuraldemapper_boxplus-phi model for long codes and did not conduct experiments for short and medium codes. Future research should consider the effect on short and medium codes and the application of other machine learning methods to error correcting codes.
