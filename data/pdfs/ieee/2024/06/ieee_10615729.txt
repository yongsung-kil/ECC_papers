Abstract‚ÄĒDue to its powerful generative capabilities, denoising
diffusion probabilistic models (DDPM) have found applications in
various domains. In this paper, DDPM is employed in a wireless
channel receiver, where we design a decoder structure incor-
porating DDPM and low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoding
to enhance the error correction capabilities of the receiver. The
LDPC decoding method based on DDPM proposed in this paper
is iterative. The received signal must pass through the DDPM
and demodulation modules first. The output is multiplied with
the LDPC parity check matrix to get the parity information and
then feedback to the DDPM module to assist and carry out the
following iterative denoising output. Compared with the typical
communication system, the LDPC decoding method proposed in
this paper utilizes the diffusion model to assist the channel equal-
ization with the parity check information generated during the
decoding process and realizes the inter-module joint optimization
at the receiver. Also, in order to have a better performance on
processing and predicting sequence data, recurrent neural net-
work (RNN) is a reasonable choice for its unique circular concept
and the long-short-term memory network structure. This paper
proved that the proposed decoding method has BER performance
gains of 0.5dB to 0.2dB over the typical LDPC decoding methods
using normalized min-sum(NMS) algorithm under additive white
Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel and Rayleigh channel.
Index Terms‚ÄĒLDPC code, DDPM, RNN, Iterative Decoding
I. INTRODUCTION
W
ITH the continuous development of communication
systems, reliable communication is becoming increas-
ingly demanding. The emergence of channel coding techniques
has made it possible to support this requirement. As one of
the channel coding methods, low-density parity-check (LDPC)
codes have been extensively investigated and applied.
LDPC codes were first proposed by Gallager [1] in 1961 and
refined by MacKey and Spielman [2] in 1997. Due to its high
performance and low implementation complexity, researchers
have meticulously investigated and analyzed LDPC codes. In
order to maximize the error correction capability of LDPC

After the LDPC was proposed, the research on the decoding
method of LDPC codes has also made significant progress; two
of the essential decoding algorithms, belief propagation (BP)
algorithm and min-sum(MS) algorithm [3], have been proposed
one after another. By using the appropriate decoding algorithm,
the error correction capability of the LDPC codes are very
close to the Shannon channel capacity. Also, to balance decod-
ing performance and complexity, normalized min-sum(NMS)
algorithm were proposed, compensate for the excessive ap-
proximation problem caused by MS through scaling factor [4].
Based on these LDPC decoding algorithms, researchers have
also been continuously improving these algorithms. researchers
in [5]‚Äď[8] used the properties of LDPC decoding algorithms to
optimise the decoding process and these attempts have obtained
significant performance gains.
In recent years, with the popularization of machine learning
concepts and methods, more and more deep learning algorithms
have been applied to traditional physical layer communication
field, such as channel equalization and channel coding. Deep
learning algorithms show great potential in the field of error-
correction codes, algorithms based on deep learning are being
applied to improve the performance of error-correction codes.
Researchers in [9] exploited deep neural networks (DNN) to
reduce the computational complexity and improve the bit error
rate (BER) performance in both LDPC and polar decoding.
More attempts have been made to combine existing LDPC
decoding algorithms (BP, MS, NMS) with deep learning. Re-
searchers in [10] replaced the belief propagating process of the
BP algorithm using convolutional neural networks (CNN), thus
increasing the efficiency of utilizing the parity check informa-
tion and reducing the decoding complexity. In [11], researchers
combined BP algorithm with recurrent neural network (RNN),
improving decoder performance with parallel outputs from
multiple BP-RNN decoders. The same year, researchers used
neural networks to implement and replace part of the structure
of the MS algorithm [12]. In [13] , researchers implemented
both the iterative process as well as the parameter estimation
in the NMS algorithm using lightweight neural networks.

Since denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPM) was
proposed, researchers have also been experimenting with apply-
ing DDPM to channel estimation and equalization of receivers
due to its ability to extract information from noise [14]. In
2022, researchers introduced the DDPM into error-correction
codes [15], simulated the noise process of channel transmission
by using the forward process of the DDPM, and used the
backward approach of the model to perform channel denoising.
Based on this structure, Muah Kim [16] , Mehdi Letafati [17]
and Tong Wu [18] also conducted research analysis on DDPM‚Äôs
noise cancellation capabilities.
In the above studies, although the application of deep
learning algorithms makes it possible to transfer the check bit
information between different modules in the LDPC decoding
process, only few researchers have attempted it. The LDPC
codes used in these researches are not representative, and
research on Rayleigh channels and other modulation methods
has not yet emerged.
Therefore, this paper proposes a novel LDPC decoding
method for joint optimization between different receiver mod-
ules. Combining the benefits of DDPM with RNN, this paper
presents an RNN-DDPM model to achieve the joint optimiza-
tion of channel equalization and LDPC decoding in the receiver.
The decoding method based on RNN-DDPM seeks to fully
utilize the parity check information generated during the LDPC
decoding process under the AWGN and Rayleigh channels.

III. SYSTEM MODEL
In this section, we will introduce the system model with
RNN-DDPM. The complete system model is shown as Fig. 2.
In a typical communication system, we divide the system into
three parts: transmitter, channel and receiver. From a systems
perspective, the transmitter and the channel together form the
forward process in diffusion model. Meanwhile, the receiver
can be categorized as a diffusion model predictor as well as a
loop iterative process.
In decoding process, the received signal eS needs to pass
through the DDPM module and the demodulation module
first, the output ňÜyt is multiplied with the LDPC parity check
matrix to get the parity information and then feedback to
the DDPM module to assist and carry out the subsequent
iterative denoising output eSt‚ąí1. Consequently, this section will
be presented regarding the transmitter and the channel, the
decoding process, and the predictor.
A. Transmitter and Channel
On the transmitter side, the required information bits is
expected as a random binary, so that the information bits
sequence x is generated randomly.
After the information bits sequence x is generated, the
encoded bits sequence y can be obtained by using the generate
matrix G of LDPC code. The relationship between information
bits sequence x and encoded bits sequence y can be denoted
as y = G ¬∑ x.
The encoded bits sequence y needs to be modulated into
symbol sequence s for channel-to-channel transmission. How-
ever, although the forward process of diffusion models can
simulate the channel‚Äôs additive noise in the transmission sym-
bol sequence s, there is still a specific conversion relationship
between the channel noise and the forward process. Under
the Rayleigh channel, the noise process of the channel can

B. Decoding Process
In system model, after receiving the symbol sequence ňÜs,
channel estimation and equalization will be performed first to
minimize the effects of Rayleigh multiplicative noise. In the
system model proposed in this paper, we do not pay attention to
the specific operation process of the channel estimation module,
but simplify it to perfect channel estimation, and directly divide
the received symbol sequence ňÜs by the Rayleigh multiplicative
noise h. The obtained symbol sequence can be defined as

into ŌĶőł, the noise predictor of the diffusion model‚Äôs backward

C. RNN Based Predictor
The predictor in RNN-DDPM is mainly acted by RNN
to predict and calculate the additive noise in equation(21).
Its unique circular concept and the most important structure,
the long-short-term memory network, make it perform well
on the problem of processing and predicting sequence data.
Using RNN for noise prediction can take advantage of the
characteristics of this neural networks.
For the RNN used in reverse process, its structure is shown
as Fig. 3. In order to improve the predictive ability of network,
parameters related to hidden layers are often debugged during
training.
The input of RNN is not the simple splicing of symbol
sequence est and parity information sequence Cqt, it should be
the interweaving and elevation of these two sequences. After
embedding est and Cqt, the input would be a matrix with a size
of ((1+log2M)n‚ąík, dim), where (n, k) represents the shape
of the LDPC code, M represents the order of modulation and
dim represents the dimension of the input after embedding. To
get the best performance of predicting the noise, the size of the
hidden layers in RNN is adjustable and can be represented as a
fixed size iterative matrix Layer(l, m), where l represents the
number of the hidden layers and m represents the dimension
of the hidden layers.
Besides, the size of output should be the same as the symbol
sequence est. Therefore a fully connected layer needs to be
connected to ensure that the output sequence is the right size.

B. BER Performance under AWGN Channel
As shown in Fig. 4 and Fig. 5, compared to the LDPC
decoding method based on the NMS decoding algorithm, RNN-
DDPM (represented in the figure as DM) method has a perfor-

For LDPC codes (354, 264), the decoding method based on
RNN-DDPM has a BER gain of about 0.5 dB under BPSK
modulation. It also has better performance under QPSK modu-
lation, with a BER gain of about 0.3dB to 0.4dB. Meanwhile,
for LDPC codes (576, 432), the decoding method based on
RNN-DDPM has a BER gain of about 0.3 dB under BPSK
modulation and a BER gain of about 0.2 dB to 0.3 dB under
QPSK modulation.
From the description of performance gains above, it can be
noticed that the difference in modulation order affects the BER
performance of the decoding method. QPSK modulation has a
smaller performance gain than BPSK modulation under low
SNR conditions, while under high SNR conditions, both gains
reach similar levels. Also, from low SNR to high SNR condi-
tions, the performance gain of RNN-DDPM has an increasing
and then decreasing trend compared to the decoding method
based on the NMS algorithm.

V. CONCLUSION
This paper proposes a LDPC decoding method based on
RNN-DDPM to jointly optimize two modules, channel equal-
ization and LDPC decoder. By passing the parity information
corresponding to each LDPC code between modules, the pro-
posed LDPC decoding method achieves the joint denoising
process from both the LDPC parity check information as well
as the Markov process of the diffusion model. The proposed
LDPC decoding method shows performance enhancement in
both AWGN channels and Rayleigh channels. This enhance-
ment is inextricably related to the modulation order, the error
correction capability of the LDPC codes, and the SNR condi-
tion. In future work, we intend to implement a full interaction
between the modules in receiver and add channel estimation
module into our LDPC decoding method.
