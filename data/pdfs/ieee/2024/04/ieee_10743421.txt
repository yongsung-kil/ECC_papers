Optimization of LDPC coded PDMA systems with

adaptive overload

Hanqing Ding, Yanyan Jin, Jian Zeng, Jin Xu*, Jiaxue Li, Lichao Mo

College of electronic information

Zhengzhou University of Light Industry

Zhengzhou, China

*Corresponding author: jin.xu@zzuli.edu.cn

Abstract—For low-density parity-check (LDPC) coded pattern division multiple access (PDMA) systems with adaptive overload, the receiver performance is degraded due to the mismatched extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) characteristic between the front-end PDMA-detector and back-end LDPC-decoder when iterative detection and decoding (IDD) is employed. For the overload adaptive system, the PDMA-detector shows differentiated EXIT characteristic for different pattern matrices (PMs), thus a single optimized code is no longer a good choice. In this work, we propose to optimize the corresponding LDPC codes for each submatrix of the nested pattern matrix via a simplified method which uses check-concentrated degree distribution pair. Both EXIT chart-based analysis and bit-error-rate simulation results show a prominent performance gain brought by the proposed method.
Keywords-PDMA; LDPC;EXIT optimization;adaptive overload; nested PM

Since the maximum degree Dc,max of check-node of LDPC
dominates the decoding complexity, reasonable chosen Dc,max
is beneficial for practical applications such as hardware implementation. The check-concentrated property claims that only one or two reasonable chosen check-node degree is able to obtain about the same performance as the relatively large Dc,max
as depicted in theorem 3.96 in [16]. In contrast to [2] using highcomplexity two-stage iterative optimization algorithm, in this work, we proposed to optimize the concatenated LDPC code of the overload-adaptive PDMA systems with a simplified optimized algorithm which employs the check- concentrated degree distribution pair. Linear programming tool are used to match the Extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) curves of the two partitioned modules of the iterative detection and decoding (IDD) based receiver.

I. INTRODUCTION
As a candidate non-orthogonal multiple access scheme, pattern division multiple access (PDMA) employs sparse irregular pattern matrices (PM) to support overloaded users and to reduce the computational complexity of the belief-propagation (BP) based receiver [1]-[7]. In wireless communication system, due to the channel condition, high-overload PDMA will lead to detection failure and thus low system throughput. In order to develop the overload adaption ability of PDMA system, a pattern matrix (PM) with nested structure is proposed in [5], where low overloaded PM is designed as a submatrix of high overloaded one. Bit-error-rate (BER) performance comparisons are given to show that kind of PM has great advantages in terms of computational-complexity and iterative- convergence performance. However, the authors evaluate the BER performance under a single standard low-density parity- check code in (LDPC) in World Interoperability for Microwave. Access (WiMax) protocol with code-length of Nb = 2304 bits
and code-rate RC = 0.5 [8]. The authors in [1] have proposed a
two-stage optimization algorithm to find near-optimal LDPC degree distribution to enhance the PDMA system performance based on extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) tool. In general, a single LDPC code with a specific degree distribution is not always a good choice since different PMs exhibit different EXIT characteristics [9]-[15]. In another word, optimizing the LDPC degree distribution according to different PMs is necessary to achieve their limiting performance.

II. UPLINK PDMA SYSTEM WITH ADAPTIVE OVERLOAD
For uplink PDMA system, the i-th user encodes its information bits via a rate RC LDPC code. After interleaving and binary phase shifting keying (BPSK) or quadrature phase shift keying (QPSK) modulation, the resulting modulation symbols of length N S are denoted by X (i) = [x1i , x2i ,, xNi s ]T for 1 ≤ i ≤ K ,where the superscript denote the index of users and the subscript denote the index of the modulation symbols. For each user, the modulation symbols xij , 1 ≤ i ≤ K and
1 ≤ j ≤ Ns ,is mapping onto N orthogonal-frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM) resource-element (RE) according to useri’s pattern sequence (PS) which is the i-th column of the PM. The PDMA system overload is defined as β = K / N . For
example, the nested PM designed in [5] is shown as Fig.1.
From Fig. 1, two S2,3 PMs are embedded in PM S4,6, and one is scheduled on the first two REs and the other is on the last two REs. It is evidently that both S2,3 and S4,6 have the same BER performance. In addition, the 150% overloaded S4,6 is nested in 200% overloaded PM S4,8. On one hand, generally speaking, the system performance is degraded as the overload factor increasing since more users collide on the limited RE. On the other hand, for different PM, optimizing the concatenated LDPC code is necessary since different PMs exhibit different EXIT characteristics. In this work, belief-propagation (BP) based PDMA-detector is employed, and the description about it is shown in [1],[2] in details, so we omit it.

The BER performance of LDPC mainly depends on its degree distribution. For simplicity and practical consideration, a simplified optimization algorithm that utilizing the check-concentrated property is proposed to obtain the variable-node degree distributions of LDPC codes by means of matching the EXIT curves of the front-end BPdetector and back-end IDPC-decoder. B. BER performance simulations
To further verify the validity of the above method, BER performance comparisons are given in this section. WiMAXLDPC codes with rates 0.5 and 0.75 are constructed as baseline according to their degree distribution. Code 1-Code 6 are constructed with IT++ library according to their degree distribution as aforementioned. And the length of all LDPC codes is 2304 bits [17]. Standard BP algorithm and the number of 30 BP iterations is used for the LDPC decoder. Let Out _ iter denote the number of iterations between the BP-
detectors and the LDPC-decoder while In _ iter denote that of
BP-detector of PDMA. In all simulation settings, it is assume Out _ iter = 2 and In _ iter = 3 for computational-complexity
consideration. BPSK modulation and independent identically distributed (i.i.d) fading channel are used. Furthermore, perfect channel estimation is assumed, i.e., channel information is only known at the receiver but not at the transmitter side. As a result, no power allocation is employed.
Fig. 4 shows the simulation results of the overload-adaptive PDMA with WiMax-LDPC and the optimized LDPC codes (Code-1 - Code-3) respectively under the same condition, where the dashed lines denote the BER curves for WiMax- LDPC coded PDMA systems and the solid lines are those of the optimized-LDPC coded ones. we have the following conclusions,
• As the overload factor increases, the BER performance of all PDMA system degraded. In another word, when the PDMA system fails to work at a high-overload

configuration, it can switch to the low-overload case to obtain better system throughput.
• Using only a single LDPC code such as WiMax-LDPC is not a good choice. As shown in Fig. 4, prominent BER performance gain is obtained for all overloaded cases.
• The proposed codes (from Code-1 to Code-3) have maximum check-node degree Dc,max = 6 which is lower than the Wimax-LDPC whose maximum check-node degree Dc,max = 7 . Since both schemes with the same parameter Out _ iter = 2 and In _ iter = 3 , so the
scheme with the proposed LDPC codes have the lower computation-complexity than the one with a single WiMax-LDPC code.
Fig. 5 shows the simulation results of the overload-adaptive PDMA with WiMax-LDPC and the optimized LDPC codes (Code-4 - Code-6) with code-rate RC = 0.75 and the same conclusion is obtained as above. The Eb N0 in these two tables are evaluated at BER level of 1e-2. VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this work, we have optimized the overload-adaptive PDMA systems with the proposed simplified optimization algorithm which employs the check-concentration property. Both the EXIT chart analysis and numerical simulation results show that the overload-adaptive PDMA system equipped with a fixed LDPC code is not a good choice and the proposed LDPC codes bring prominent performance gain compared with only a

single LDPC code. Furthermore, the proposed codes with slightly lower decoding complexity than the baseline WiMaxLDPC code. In the future, some link-level throughput simulations are needed to further demonstrate the superiority of the proposed scheme.
