A Layered CPA Decoder for Reed-Muller Codes
Jiajie Li and Warren J. Gross Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, McGill University
Montre´al, Que´bec, Canada Emails: jiajie.li@mail.mcgill.ca, warren.gross@mcgill.ca

2024 58th Asilomar Conference on Signals, Systems, and Computers | 979-8-3503-5405-8/24/$31.00 ©2024 IEEE | DOI: 10.1109/IEEECONF60004.2024.10942623

Abstract—When decoding low-rate and short-length ReedMuller (RM) codes, the recently proposed projection-aggregation (PA) decoder yields near maximum-likelihood decoding performance. However, the practicability of the PA decoder’s implementation is nevertheless negatively impacted by its high computational cost. It has been demonstrated that this decoder is closely connected to the belief propagation (BP) decoder based on the parity-check matrix. Techniques inspired by the layered decoding and the broadcast modification for the BP decoder used by the low-density parity-check codes are proposed in this work. The proposed broadcast modification reduces the computational overhead induced by the proposed layered decoding for the collapsed PA (CPA) decoder. The proposed layered and broadcast-based CPA decoder has negligible degradation in decoding performance, and it produces a 47% reduction in the average complexity at Eb/N0 = 2.5 dB when decoding RM(8, 3) codes.
Index Terms—broadcast, iterative decoder, layered decoding, projection-aggregation decoder, Reed-Muller codes

I. INTRODUCTION

Reed-Muller (RM) codes are classical linear codes first

proposed by Muller in [1]. Reed’s majority-vote decoder [2]

is the first RM code decoder and is capable of correcting error

patterns with weights less than half of the minimum distance.

There is an efficient maximum-likelihood (ML) decoder for

order-one RM codes, the fast Hadamard transform (FHT)

decoder [3], [4], which has a complexity of O(n log n). Many

decoders such as the Dumer’s recursive list decoder [5] are

proposed to decode RM codes with an order larger than one.

Given a large enough list size, the Dumer’s list decoder is ex-

perimentally shown to achieve ML decoding performance [5].

A recently proposed recursive projection-aggregation (RPA)

decoder is observed to outperform Dumer’s recursive list de-

coder when decoding low-rate and short-length RM codes [6].

When extending the RPA decoder to a Chase-type list decoder,

the RPA list decoder is observed to achieve ML decoding

performance under various code lengths and rates [6]. The

problem associated with the RPA decoder is the high compu-

tational complexity due to the recursive structure, which grows with the code order r (O (nr log n)) [6].

Methods to reduce the computational complexity of the

RPA decoder have been proposed. A collapsed version, the

collapsed projection-aggregation (CPA) decoder, removes the

recursive structure, which removes repeated subspaces used

by the projection step and reduces the maximum number of

subspaces to

the complexity as well such as the sparse decoder variant [8],

early-stopping [9], [10], a reduced number of iterations in the

recursive projection [11], pruning metric [12], and optimized pruning methods [13].
In this work, the authors propose a layered CPA decoder. To reduce the overhead brought by the layered decoding, the authors also propose a broadcast-based modification to the inter-iteration message update for the CPA decoder. Proposed methods have negligible degradation in decoding performance, and they are observed to have a 47% reduction in the average complexity on top of using early-stopping when decoding RM(8, 3) codes at Eb/N0 = 2.5 dB.
The structure of this work is the following. The necessary background information for proposed techniques is provided in Section II. Proposed techniques are explained in Section III. Experimental results of the proposed methods are shown in Section IV. This work is concluded in Section V.

IV. EXPERIMENTAL DATA AND RESULTS
A. Experimental Setup
The binary phase-shift keying modulation and the additive white Gaussian noise channel are used for the simulations. The adaptive syndrome check and the scheduling scheme used in [10] are also applied to the layered CPA decoders. The frequency δ of performing the syndrome check is determined by the simulations, and the decay factor dSCH used by the scheduling scheme is set to 2. The weight ω is set to α/δ where α is a non-negative real number. We set Nmax = ⌈m/2⌉.
The layered and broadcast-based CPA decoder denoted as Layered is implemented according to Algorithm 1. The CPA decoders with the extrinsic and broadcast update and the flooding schedule are denoted as Extrinsic and Broadcast respectively. The complexity of the decoder is measured by the method proposed in [10]. The operational overhead caused by the layered decoding is set to 4n (three units of operations for multiplication, and one unit of operation for addition) according to the weight table proposed in [10]. The CPA decoders are used for r = 2 RM codes, and the optimized and pruned CPA decoders [10] are used for r ≥ 3 RM codes.

V. CONCLUSION
This work proposes a layered CPA decoder and uses a broadcast modification to minimize the overhead caused by the layered decoding. The layered and the broadcast modification has negligible degradation in the FER, and the average number of operations is reduced. Up to 47% reduction in the average number of operations is observed when decoding RM(8, 3) codes at Eb/N0 = 2.5 dB.
