Design of Spatially Coupled LDPC Codes with Girth at Least 8
Yuxiang Zhou, Jian Fang, Baoming Bai, Min Zhu State Key Lab. of ISN, Xidian University, Xi’an 710071, China
E-mail: bmbai@mail.xidian.edu.cn

Abstract—Spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SCLDPC) codes are a special type of sparse graph codes that have recently attracted great interest due to their capacityapproaching performance. To reduce the performance loss incurred by short cycles, this paper proposes a method based on the greatest common divisor (GCD) full-length row-multiplier (FLRM) matrix to construct girth-8 quasi-cyclic SC-LDPC codes with improved performance and flexible code rates. Besides, we further extend the lower bound of the circulant permutation matrix (CPM) size to be compatible with different code lengths. Numerical results demonstrate that the proposed SC-LDPC code has performance gains of 1.05 dB and 1.0 dB compared with that based on RS structure at an BER of 10−6 over AWGN channels for code rates 0.45 and 0.625, respectively. Additionally, SC-LDPC codes based on the GCD constraint achieve the tradeoff between decoding performance and complexity..
Index Terms—Spatially coupled low-density parity-check codes, greatest common divisor, full-length row-multiplier matrix.
I. INTRODUCTION
Spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) codes are a special type of LDPC codes with a convolutional structure and are constructed by coupling a sequence of block LDPC codes together [1]. SC-LDPC codes are proven to achieve channel capacity universally with an iterative decoding algorithm known as the belief propagation (BP) decoder [2]. And they have attracted much attention due to their threshold saturation, which means their performances under BP decoding approaches the maximum a posteriori (MAP) decoding performance of uncoupled component LDPC codes [3].
SC-LDPC codes can be designed based on algebraic and protograph construction. Algebraic-based construction mainly applies finite fields and combinatorics to design QC-LDPC codes with flexible code length and rates. Graph-theoretic based constructions are expected to avoid short cycles for steep waterfall region and low error floor. It is generally believed that small cycles have a negative impact on the performance of QC-LDPC codes. Therefore, constructions of QC-LDPC codes without small cycles have attracted great interest due to the advantages of competitive performance and relatively low complexity for code implementation [8]. There are many algebraic methods for constructing non-binary (NB) matrices that satisfy the 2 × 2 submatrix (SM) constraint [4], which eliminates cycles of length 4. The most flexible one is the method proposed in [5], which is based on two arbitrary sets of a chosen NB field. Besides, balanced incomplete block design (BIBD) [6] is a special type of combinatorial

structure and can also be applied to construct QC-LDPC codes free of cycles of length 4. In [7], the authors proposed the greatest common divisor (GCD) scheme and gave a simple and flexible approach to construct quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes. Compared with the former constructions, GCD construction satisfies 3 × 3 SM constraint [4], which eliminates cycles of length 6. Therefore, QC-LDPC codes constructed based on the GCD structure are ensure to have a girth of 8 [7].
Plenty of the GCD frameworks have been widely studied [9]–[12] to achieve high reliability. In [13], the author applied the GCD frameworks to construct the globally-coupled (GC) LDPC codes of girth 8 with improved performance. In this paper, we apply them to SC-LDPC codes and propose a novel method for constructing the SC-LDPC code based on the GCD FLRM matrix to achieve empirical performance and flexible code rates. To enhance the code length flexibility, we further extend the lower bound of the CPM size. Simulation results show that the proposed SC-LDPC codes based on GCD outperform that based on Reed-Solomon (RS) structure, achieving the trade-off between performance and decoding complexity.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. The structure of SC-LDPC codes and the construction of GCD constraint are introduced in Section II. The proposed GCD-based SC-LDPC code, the lower bound of CPM size and the corresponding decoding method are presented in Section III. Numerical results are provided in Section IV. Conclusions are drawn in Section V.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
We now investigate the performance of the proposed SCLDPC code with SWD and the maximun iteration number 50 over the binary-input AWGN channel with BPSK modulation. Set W = 4 and s = 2. Code1 and code2 are constructed base on the GCD constraint, and code3 and code4 are constructed based on RS structure. The parameters of these codes are given in TABLE 1, where b and l represent the number of coupling blocks and the coupling length (l = 2 × b), respectively, and Ci (i = 4, 6) represents the number of the cycles of length i. It is obvious that SC-LDPC codes constructed based on GCD constraint and RS structure do not have cycles of length 4. More importantly, code1 and code2 are free of cycles of length 6 to achieve better performance compared with code3 and code4.
The bit error rate (BER) and block error rate (BLER) performance of code1-4 are shown in Fig. 5. For W = 4, the proposed SC-LDPC code has performance gains of 0.45 dB compared with that based on RS structure at an BER of 10−4 for both code rates 0.45 and 0.625, and has performance gains of 0.7 dB compared with that based on RS structure at an BLER of 10−2 for both code rates 0.45 and 0.625, respectively.
