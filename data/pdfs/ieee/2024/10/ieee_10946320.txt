AbstractвҖ”This paper proposes a new explicit construction method for quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes without 4-cycles and 6-cycles with large column weights. Two integer sequences entirely define the exponent matrix of the new method. The first sequence is an arithmetic sequence starting from 0 with a tolerance of 1, and the second sequence is a special sequence composed of integers that meet the constraint of the greatest common divisor (GCD). The existing explicit method can only provide a variety of row weight types with large circulant size. Compared with the recently proposed symmetric structure method based on computer search, the new explicit construction method has better decoding performance, extremely low description complexity, and does not require computer search.
Index TermsвҖ”Cycle, greatest common divisor (GCD), lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) code, quasi-cyclic (QC)
I. INTRODUCTION
The structure of codes determines the performance of LowDensity Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1]. As an essential class of LDPC codes, Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC-LDPC) codes have been widely concerned because the quasi-cyclic structure dramatically reduces the requirement of hardware storage space for encoding and decoding [2].
LDPC codes can be divided into random LDPC codes and structured LDPC codes. Although the random construction method can obtain a larger girth and design LDPC codes with excellent performance, due to the lack of certain structural characteristics, the coding complexity is high, which is not conducive to hardware implementation, and the hardware storage of the check matrix is also more complicated [3]. Although the structured LDPC codes constructed by the algebraic method have cyclic or quasi-cyclic structure and low coding complexity, it is difficult to effectively eliminate short cycles, resulting in the loss of iterative decoding performance of LDPC codes [4]. Compared with the search method, the mathematical formula method can provide more profound theoretical results [5]; under certain parameters, the mathematical formula method can also provide a small circulant size comparable to the search method [6]. Therefore, how to use the mathematical formula method to construct a QC-LDPC code

without 4-cycles and 6-cycles and the size of the circulant size as small as possible is a very challenging theoretical problem.
In reference [7], special QC-LDPC codes called full-length row-multiplier (FLRM) codes are defined by two integer sequences, one of which is an arithmetic sequence with a tolerance of 1 from 0. The other is an integer sequence constructed using the greatest common divisor (GCD) method, which can make the girth of the constructed LDPC code at least 8 [8]. In reference [9], combined with the GCD method and FLRM codes, several kinds of specially constructed codes are proposed, making these codes have no 4-cycles and 6cycles under special row weight L.
This paper proposes a class of QC-LDPC codes with girth of at least 8 for FLRM codes, which can satisfy any row weight when the column weight is 6 [9]. The performance of QC-LDPC codes generated by the new construction method is significantly better than that of the existing computer search methods [10]. Compared with the existing search methods, the new construction method proposed in this paper not only has a low description complexity but also does not require computer search.

The decoding performance of LDPC codes is usually affected by the girth of the QC-LDPC codes, and the existence of short cycles usually leads to incomplete convergence of decoding, which seriously affects the error correction performance of the codes. Thus, when constructing LDPC codes with good error correction performance, one should avoid at least the presence of 4-cycles in the Tanner graph.
The girth of an LDPC code is an even number greater than or equal to 4. The girth is the length of the shortest cycle. Therefore, the girth of a QC-LDPC code without 4-cycles and 6-cycles is at least 8. The situation of 6-cycles in QC-LDPC codes is shown in Fig. 1.

(J, L)-QC-LDPC codes are a special class of LDPC codes, whose parity-check matrix is jointly determined by an exponent matrix E and the circulant size P [14]. All the circulant are circular permutation matrices, where each row is the result of right-shifting the row above it [15]. In this paper, a special class of QC-LDPC codes is studied. The exponent matrix can be defined by two sequences, which can be expressed as E = S2TВ·S1, where S1 = [0, 1, . . . , L-1], and S2 is an integer sequence containing J elements.
According to the GCD method and the equation [9], the following lemma is obviously true:
Lemma 1 [9]: Let x be an arbitrary positive integer. For any circulant size P вүҘ x(LвҲ’1)+1, the Tanner graph corresponding to S2 = [0, x] does not contain 4-cycles.
Lemma 2 [16]: Let S2 = [a0, a1, . . . , aJ-1] be a sequence of J integers in increasing order. If the equation (GCD constraint) (ak вҲ’ai)/gcd(ak вҲ’ai, aj вҲ’ai)>L holds for all triples (ai, aj, ak), then for any circulant size P вүҘ (aJ-1 вҲ’ a0)(L вҲ’ 1) + 1, the exponent matrix E can generate QC-LDPC codes with girth8.
