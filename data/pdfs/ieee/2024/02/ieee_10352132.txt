Optimized Non-Surjective FAIDs for 5G LDPC Codes With Learnable Quantization
Yanchen Lyu , Ming Jiang , Member, IEEE, Yifan Zhang , Chunming Zhao , Member, IEEE, Nan Hu , and Xiaodong Xu

Abstract— This letter proposes a novel approach for designing non-surjective (NS) finite alphabet iterative decoders (FAIDs) for quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, especially 5G LDPC codes. We employ recurrent quantized neural networks to optimize the look-up tables used in NS-FAIDs, the design of which is the kernel of FAIDs. During the optimization of LUTs, the quantized message alphabets and quantization thresholds are jointly designed. To cope with the untrainable problem of quantization thresholds in the existing neural-network-based linear FAIDs, we use softmax distribution to soften the implied one-hot distribution of quantization thresholds, making it trainable in the neural network. The proposed decoders offer enhanced universality compared to existing neural network-based linear FAIDs, making them directly applicable to 5G LDPC codes with support for 2-bit quantization over the additive white Gaussian noise channel. Additionally, they significantly outperform the original NS-FAID in terms of performance.
Index Terms— Non-surjective finite alphabet iterative decoders, low-density parity-check codes, recurrent quantized neural networks.
I. INTRODUCTION
DUE to the excellent error-correcting performance, lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes have gained significant attention in the last three decades. LDPC codes are widely used in various communication systems, such as the latest fifth generation (5G) wireless systems. Although the classical belief propagation (BP) decoding of LDPC codes has capacityapproaching performance, designing an ultra-fast BP-based decoder for high-speed communication systems, such as future 6G and fiber-optic communications, is still challenging. The main reason is that the internal data flow of a soft BP-based decoder is hundreds of times the input data rate [1]. To cope with this, many coarsely quantized LDPC decoders [2], [3],

[4], [5], [6], [7], [8] have been proposed to improve the trade-off between performance and complexity.
Meanwhile, deep learning techniques, particularly neural networks (NNs), have shown great potential in improving the performance of various signal-processing tasks. Deep unfolding [9], in particular, has been proposed as a promising approach to improve the performance of probabilistic generative models, such as recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and deep neural networks (DNNs). Recently, many researchers have turned their attention to developing low-complexity and low-precision decoding algorithms utilizing NNs [10], [11], [12], [13], [14].
Extensive literature shows that non-uniform quantization of check node (CN) and variable node (VN) messages can reduce the performance loss for LDPC decoders with limited precision quantization. In [2], Planjery et al. propose the finite alphabet iterative decoders (FAIDs). Unlike traditional quantized decoders, FAIDs use a finite alphabet of message values and design updating functions using knowledge of potentially harmful subgraphs in the LDPC codes. This enables FAIDs to outperform the conventional BP-based decoders in the error-floor region while requiring much lower complexity. They also provide a methodology for selecting FAIDs for any LDPC codes with a uniform column weight of three. Moving forward, in [3], the proposed non-surjective FAIDs (NS-FAIDs) extend the use of FAIDs to any channel model and both regular and irregular LDPC codes. It provides a unified framework for several designs previously proposed in [15], [16], [17], [18], and [19], making it a promising approach for cost-effective, high-throughput, and low-power decoder designs. These algorithms can be summarized as using a look-up table (LUT) to replace the internal mathematical calculations of VN updates and CN updates in the minsum (MS) algorithm. Recently, Xiao et al. propose a recurrent quantized neural network (RQNN)-based FAID for regular LDPC codes [10], [11], the key of which is to utilize NNs to optimize the LUTs used in FAIDs.
In this letter, we optimize the design of 2-bit and 3-bit NS-FAIDs based on RQNNs, which can be applied to both regular and irregular LDPC codes, such as 5G LDPC codes over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. When training the decoders, we use the softmax distribution [20] to soften the implied one-hot distribution of quantization, which renders the quantization thresholds trainable in the NNs. For both the 2-bit and 3-bit quantization schemes, the simulation results verify that the proposed trainable RQNNbased NS-FAID can significantly outperform the traditional NS-FAID when the 5G LDPC codes are adopted.

III. RQNN FOR NS-FAIDS
A. Model-Driven Structure for NS-FAIDs
In this section, we propose a model-driven RQNN for NS-FAIDs, which is designed to align with the computation process of the NS-FAIDs. To achieve this, the message update functions in NS-FAIDs, namely Φv(·), Φc(·), and Υ(·), are employed as the activation functions between different layers in the RQNN. The RQNN architecture comprises five hidden layers, as depicted in Fig. 1, and encompasses three types of update functions utilized in an NS-FAID.
1) CN update layers: Layer-1 and Layer-3 are used to update the CN messages r through Φc(·).

B. Straight-Through Estimators
The straight-through estimator (STE) is a proxy derivative technique utilized in the training of quantized NNs (QNNs) to address the issue of discontinuity which arises from quantization functions during gradient propagation [21]. In conventional gradient descent algorithms, the calculation of gradients relies on continuous derivatives. However, the quantization functions are typically discontinuous on the input values, resulting in zero derivatives that prevent the gradient from propagating to the parameters of the preceding layer. The STE introduces the concept of an approximate derivative, enabling the gradient to continue propagating.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose a novel approach for designing the NS-FAIDs for LDPC codes over the AWGN channel. Our method utilizes RQNNs to optimize the framing function F (·) of NS-FAIDs. Our approach demonstrates several advantages. Firstly, it can be applied to various types of LDPC codes, including regular and irregular codes, especially 5G LDPC codes. This provides greater flexibility in the design of NS-FAIDs compared to the existing methods. Secondly, we address the issue of untrainable quantization thresholds by introducing a softmax distribution, enabling trainable quantization thresholds within the NNs. By jointly training the quantization values and quantization thresholds, we achieve noticeable performance improvement for 5G LDPC codes with very limited quantization precision.
