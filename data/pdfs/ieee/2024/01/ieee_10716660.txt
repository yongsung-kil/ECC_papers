A Novel EICNet-Based Iterative Receiver for PLDPC-Coded
M -ary DCSK Systems

Yuyi He , Liang Lv , Graduate Student Member, IEEE, Yi Fang , Senior Member, IEEE, Liuguo Yin , Senior Member, IEEE, and Pingping Chen

Abstract— In this letter, we propose a novel neural network-aided receiver, called extrinsic-information chaos network-aided iterative decoding (EICNet-ID) receiver, for protograph low-density parity-check (PLDPC)-coded M -ary differential chaos shift keying (DCSK) systems. Specifically, the EICNet structure captures the correlation features between the chaotic sequences of the received M -ary DCSK symbol to obtain more accurate a-priori log-likelihood ratio (LLR) without requiring the channel state information (CSI). Moreover, the EICNet structure considers both the received symbol and its a-posteriori probabilities, allowing the extrinsic information provided by the PLDPC decoder to update network input and enhance error performance. Both simulation and analysis results demonstrate that the proposed EICNet-ID receiver exhibits desirable performance compared to the existing counterparts.
Index Terms— M -ary differential chaos shift keying (DCSK) systems, protograph low-density parity-check (PLDPC) codes, deep learning, iterative decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
CHAOTIC signals are well suited for spread spectrum communication due to the characteristic of inherent wideband [1]. Nowadays, differential chaos shift keying (DCSK) which has a simple transceiver structure, has become the most popular chaotic modulation scheme [2]. However, traditional DCSK systems merely use half of the energy to transmit the information-bearing signal and the other half for the reference signal, leading to low spectrum and energy efficiencies. To overcome these drawbacks, various enhanced versions of DCSK schemes, e.g., high-efficiency DCSK (HE-DCSK) [3] and short reference DCSK (SR-DCSK) [4], have been proposed. In addition, many researchers have combined the index modulation with DCSK systems to fully utilize the frequency and time slot resources. For instance, the authors have conceived a DCSK scheme with multidimensional index modulation (MIM-DCSK) exploiting the time slot, carrier, and Walsh code as indices [5].
In parallel with the development of the DCSK modulation schemes, protograph low-density parity-check (PLDPC) codes

For instance, the authors have proposed a weighted reference estimation and iterative decoding (WRE-ID) receiver to enhance the error performance of PLDPC-coded reference reused DCSK systems [6]. Moreover, the authors have proposed an iterative receiver for PLDPC-coded M -ary DCSK systems to boost the detection and decoding performance [7]. Among all coded DCSK receiver design-related works, the existing receivers typically employ an energy detection scheme for signal detection, which ignores the correlation features between the chaotic sequences of the received symbol. Moreover, the existing receivers require the fading factor or the average power gain for each transmitting path to calculate log-likelihood ratios (LLRs), which limits the application of the PLDPC-coded DCSK systems.
Recently, deep learning (DL) techniques have been proposed to overcome the shortcomings of DCSK energy detection. In [8], the authors have proposed an intelligent receiver based on long short-term memory deep neural network (LSTM-DNN) to improve reliability of orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing DCSK (OFDM-DCSK) systems. In [9], the authors have proposed an neural network (NN)-based receiver for OFDM-DCSK systems without transmitting the reference signals to improve energy efficiency. Nonetheless, the above-mentioned NN-based receivers do not consider combining NN with coded DSCK systems. In PLDPC-coded M -ary DCSK systems, the accuracy of a-priori LLR significantly affects the performance of the PLDPC decoder. Moreover, the extrinsic information (EI) from the PLDPC decoder can be fed back to the demodulator to further improve system reliability. However, the existing NN-based receivers cannot utilize the EI, which will affect the error performance of receivers. Therefore, it is necessary to design a DL-aided receiver for PLDPC-coded M -ary DCSK systems, which can simultaneously capture the feature of the received M -ary DCSK symbol and utilize the EI of the PLDPC decoder to boost system reliability.
Inspired by the above discussion, we carry out a systematical investigation on DL-aided PLDPC-coded M -ary DCSK systems. Specifically, we propose a novel NN-aided receiver, referred as extrinsic-information chaos network-aided iterative decoding (EICNet-ID) receiver, for PLDPC-coded M -ary DCSK systems. In particular, the traditional demodulation and detection modules are replaced by the EICNet structure in the proposed receiver. The EICNet structure can detect correlation features between chaotic sequences of the received M -ary DCSK symbol to obtain more accurate a-priori LLRs without channel state information (CSI). Additionally, the proposed EICNet structure considers both the received symbol and its corresponding a-posteriori probabilities, allowing the EI to update network input and enhance error performance.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we have proposed a novel EICNet-ID receiver for PLDPC-coded M -ary DCSK systems to enhance system reliability. Specifically, the proposed EICNet structure captures the correlation features between the chaotic sequences of the received M -ary DCSK symbol to generate more accurate a-priori LLRs without the CSI. Moreover, the EICNet structure considers both the received symbol and its a-posteriori probability, and thus can exploit the EI to update network input and enhance error performance. More importantly, the proposed EICNet-ID receiver can obtain superior performance and robustness compared to the existing counterparts over AWGN and multipath Rayleigh fading channels. Thus, the proposed EICNet-ID receiver can be considered as a promising solution to enhance the reliability of PLDPC-coded M -ary DCSK systems.
