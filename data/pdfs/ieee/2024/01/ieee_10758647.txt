ABSTRACT In this paper, we propose the modified cycle-concentrating progressive-edge-growth (CC-PEG) algorithm for lifting protograph-based quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes over the non-uniform additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. By introducing various selection criteria for shift values based on the non-uniform channel characteristics, we improve the decoding performances of lifted LDPC codes. From numerous simulation results, we demonstrate that each selection criterion is effective for the performance improvement and the modified CC-PEG algorithm outperforms the conventional lifting methods over the non-uniform AWGN channel. Furthermore, we analyze these simulation results based on some metrics such as the girth, number of cycles, and approximate cycle extrinsic message degree (ACE).
INDEX TERMS Approximate cycle extrinsic message degree (ACE), cycle-concentrating progressive-edgegrowth (CC-PEG) algorithm, mutual information (MI), non-uniform additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, protograph-based quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes.

I. INTRODUCTION Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes [1], [2] have attracted a significant attention due to the efficiency and convenience of implementation, where the circulant architecture allows QC-LDPC codes to be utilized with the simple hardware structure based on shift registers. Together with the superior error correction capability, QCLDPC codes are adopted in many communication standards such as IEEE 802.11n [3] and DVB-S2 [4]. For a given protograph, QC-LDPC codes are usually constructed from lifting algorithms. The quasi-cyclic progressive-edge-growth (QC-PEG) algorithm [5] is a well-known lifting method for protograph-based QC-LDPC codes, where the local girth is maximized for selecting shift values. The conventional PEG algorithms for QC-LDPC codes usually assume the uniform channel. However, transmitted signals often suffer from the different channel qualities in the practical communication systems and memory devices [8], [9]. Therefore, a new PEG method considering the nonuniform channel has to be studied. Recently, it has been found that concentrating cycles at variable nodes (VNs) with the superior error correction capabilities enhances the decoding performances of lifted codes [10]. In addition, it also does not assume the non-uniform channel as in the conventional PEG algorithms. From various selection criteria related with the different channel qualities, we propose a new PEG algorithm for QC-LDPC codes in the non-uniform channel by improving the conventional CC-PEG algorithm. First, we consider the length of cycles consisting of only VNs with the inferior channel quality in the lifting procedure. Next, we mitigate the greedy operation for selecting shift values by considering multiple candidates, where the number of candidates is adaptively determined from our algorithm. Furthermore, we introduce the weighted ACE to reflect the effects of the non-uniform channel characteristics on the cycle structures. From the numerical analysis, we demonstrate the effectiveness of each selection criterion in the modified CC-PEG algorithm. II. PRELIMINARIES In this section, we introduce some backgrounds and notations utilized in this paper. Specifically, the protograph-based QC-LDPC codes, non-uniform AWGN channel, CC-PEG algorithm, and ACE are introduced to describe the proposed lifting algorithm for protograph-based QC-LDPC codes over the non-uniform AWGN channel.
A. PROTOGRAPH-BASED QC-LDPC CODES In this paper, we focus on lifting methods of a protographbased QC-LDPC code [11] lifted from a protograph Pm×n, where m and n denote the numbers of protograph check nodes (PCNs) and protograph VNs (PVNs), respectively. Then, the protograph can be represented by an m × n base matrix Bm×n = [Bi,j], where Bi,j is the number of edges between the ith PCN and jth PVN in the given protograph. For a lifting factor z, the lifted Tanner graph and mz × nz parity-check matrix (PCM) can be obtained by replacing each nonzero entry (i.e., Bi,j ̸= 0) of Bm×n with Bi,j distinct z × z circulant permutation matrices whereas each zero entry is replaced with a z × z zero matrix.
In general, 2-step lifting is conducted to enhance the minimum distance of lifted codes. Thus, we assume that the

QC-PEG algorithm [5] is employed to eliminate all parallel edges in the protograph with a lifting factor p (p is larger than or equal to the maximum number of parallel edges) in the first lifting step. By adopting a lifting factor q = z/p in the second lifting step, the resulting lifted Tanner graph is represented as mpq×npq. In the second lifting step, each q×q circulant permutation matrix corresponds to a shift value wi,j ∈ {0, 1, . . . , q − 1}, i ∈ {1, . . . , mp}, j ∈ {1, . . . , np} and a q × q zero matrix corresponds to a shift value −1. In the lifted Tanner graph, the sth VN and rth CN are denoted by vs and cr , respectively.
B. NON-UNIFORM AWGN CHANNEL In general, the conventional PEG algorithms are conducted in the uniform channel, where channel qualities (e.g., signal-tonoise ratio (SNR)) are consistent across all transmitted bits. However, practical communication systems often encounter diverse channel conditions, leading to different signal qualities and interference patterns for transmitted bits. In fact, the channel that consists of sub-channels with different qualities is called the non-uniform channel [9]. Since channel qualities are varied in the non-uniform channel, it is necessary to adjust various parameters in the PEG algorithms to accommodate the non-uniform channel characteristics. For instance, transmitted bits with the inferior channel quality may require the stronger graph connection compared to bits with the superior channel quality.
In contrast to the conventional PEG algorithms, we take into account the channel non-uniformity for the proposed PEG algorithm. This paper focuses on a non-uniform channel that comprises two AWGN sub-channels having different Eb/N0’s, where the difference of them is represented as Eb/N0g − Eb/N0b = x dB. Note that Eb/N0g (Eb/N0b) denotes the superior (inferior) channel quality. In this paper, we assume three non-uniform scenarios (i.e., x = 1, 3, 5) that are similar to those in [9].
C. CC-PEG ALGORITHM [10] The CC-PEG algorithm is an efficient lifting method for protograph-based GLDPC codes. In GLDPC codes, VNs connected to generalized constraint (GC) nodes are more likely to receive reliable messages and robust to short cycles compared to undoped VNs. Along with this motivation, the CC-PEG algorithm is designed to concentrate local cycles at doped VNs while creating fewer cycles at undoped VNs. For selecting shift values in the lifting procedure, the CCPEG algorithm first selects shift values that result in the largest undoped girth, where the undoped girth denotes the length of the shortest cycle containing at least one undoped VN. Among the selected candidates, the CC-PEG algorithm again selects one shift value having the lowest modified cycle metric (CM) called the concentrated CM. These two subprocedures are performed using the parallel vector message passing (PMP) algorithm [12] that detects all cycles whose length is less than 2g, where g is the girth of the current graph.

D. ACE FOR EVALUATING EFFECTS OF CYCLES

A metric, called extrinsic message degree (EMD), measures the connectivity of cycles in the Tanner graphs of LDPC codes [13], [14]. Since the graph connectivity is closely related with error floor performances, it is beneficial to avoid cycles with the low EMD when lifting LDPC protographs. However, calculating EMDs for all cycles induces the high computational complexity. In fact, an alternative metric, called ACE [6], is a more preferable option due to its simplicity. The ACE of a cycle is defined as s(ds − 2), where ds is the degree of the sth VN in the cycle. Although the ACE is a crucial metric for lifting protographs, the CC-PEG algorithm does not employ the ACE for selecting shift values. Thus, we can expect the performance improvement by using the ACE in the lifting procedure, which will be discussed in Section III. PROPOSED PEG ALGORITHM FOR NON-UNIFORM CHANNEL AND ANALYSIS OF EACH CRITERION In this section, we first describe the modified CC-PEG algorithm for protograph-based QC-LDPC codes over the non-uniform channel. Then, we analyze the effectiveness of sub-procedures and criteria in the modified CC-PEG algorithm.
A. MODIFIED CC-PEG ALGORITHM FOR PROTOGRAPH-BASED QC-LDPC CODES As mentioned in Section II-C, doped VNs of GLDPC codes in the uniform channel are similar to VNs of LDPC codes
VOLUME 12, 2024

transmitted over the superior sub-channel in terms of the reliability. In the decoding procedure of GLDPC codes, doped VNs receive more reliable messages than undoped VNs because GC nodes represent arbitrary linear codes which consist of multiple single parity check (SPC) nodes. In addition, it is apparent that VNs with the superior channel quality are more reliable than VNs with the inferior channel quality in LDPC codes over the non-uniform channel. Consequently, the CC-PEG algorithm can be modified for protograph-based QC-LDPC codes over the non-uniform channel by using this similarity. Let Vg (Vb) denote the set of VNs with the superior (inferior) channel quality in the lifted Tanner graph. Then, gu of the CC-PEG algorithm is replaced with gb, where gb denotes the length of the shortest cycle containing at least one VN in Vb. Similarly, we replace gU , Vu, and Vd of the CC-PEG algorithm with gB, Vb, and Vg.
By concentrating local cycles at VNs in Vg, the modified CC-PEG algorithm is given in Alg. 1. We employ the same lifting order as in [10], [15], where the lifting order is obtained from 1) sorting VNs in the ascending degree order and 2) locating VNs in Vg later among VNs of the same degree. The modified CC-PEG algorithm consists of four criteria for selecting shift values: gb, gB, Mcc, and the weighted ACE.
1) gb AND gB The conventional CC-PEG algorithm for GLDPC codes considers only gu for selecting shift values because gu is easier to evaluate than gU . However, the most harmful cycles on decoding performances are the cycles that only consist of undoped VNs (VNs in Vb over the non-uniform channel). Therefore, we consider not only gb but also gB for the nonuniform channel. The selected shift values based on gb and gB are represented as T1 and T2, respectively.
2) MCC WITH REDUCED GREEDINESS The conventional CC-PEG algorithm only selects shift values with the lowest Mcc. However, this greedy operation might restrict a possibility of establishing the superior edge connections. Thus, we introduce a new method to alleviate the negative effects of the greedy operation. In specific, we first determine the number of shift value candidates c and then choose the shift values with the c lowest Mcc in T2. In fact, c is adaptively determined as ⌈δk⌉, where δ is a scale parameter and k is the entire number of shift values of which girth properties (gb and gB) are the same (i.e., |T2|). From heuristic experiments in Section III-B, we set δ = 0.1 and then the resulting shift value candidates are represented as T3. In the modified CC-PEG algorithm, we set α = 0.1 and β = 0.01 as in [10].

IV. PERFORMANCE COMPARISON AND DISCUSSION In this section, we compare the proposed modified CC-PEG algorithm with the conventional lifting methods that do not take into account the non-uniform channel characteristics. In specific, we consider 5 types of PEG algorithms: QCPEG [5], QC-PMP [12], ACE-PEG [6], CC-PEG [10], and proposed PEG algorithms. For the brief description of these algorithms, the QC-PEG algorithm selects a shift value creating the largest local girth. The QC-PMP algorithm selects a shift value having the lowest CM, where the CM is closely related with the number of cycles. Among shift values creating the largest local girth, the ACE-PEG algorithm selects a shift value maximizing the minimum ACE of newly created cycles. The conventional CC-PEG and proposed modified CC-PEG algorithms perform lifting procedures described in Sections II-C and III-A, respectively. For the conventional CC-PEG algorithm, we utilize the same α = 0.1 and β = 0.01, where Vu (Vd ) is converted to Vb (Vg). Using the aforementioned lifting methods, we investigate the various metrics and decoding performances of lifted LDPC codes. Note that in this paper, lifting methods are conducted for given protographs rather than utilized for designing LDPC protographs. As mentioned in Section II-D, we compare the average metrics related with the girth, CM, and ACE as well as average decoding performances while excluding the worst 5 out of 50 lifted codes for a fair evaluation.

V. CONCLUSION From the CC-PEG algorithm for GLDPC codes, we proposed the modified CC-PEG algorithm considering the non-uniform channel characteristics. While mapping doped (undoped) VNs into VNs with the superior (inferior) channel quality, we could generalize the CC-PEG algorithm for QCLDPC codes over the non-uniform AWGN channel. Unlike the conventional CC-PEG algorithm using only the undoped girth and concentrated CM as shift value selection criteria, we added other sub-procedures to improve the decoding performances. First, we considered the length of the shortest cycle consisting of only VNs with the inferior channel quality, which has the negative influence on decoding performances. Next, in order to mitigate the greedy operation for selecting shift values, we adaptively determined the number of candidates for Mcc. Finally, we added the weighted ACE as the selection criterion while considering the APP-LLR MI difference of VNs induced by the non-uniform channel.

From numerous FER simulations, we demonstrated that the proposed modified CC-PEG algorithm outperforms the conventional PEG algorithms in the non-uniform AWGN channel. In addition, we analyzed the performance gap between the lifting algorithms using various metrics. Although this paper focused on the non-uniform AWGN channel, it is expected that applying the modified CC-PEG algorithm to arbitrary non-uniform channels (e.g., nonuniform fading channel, non-uniform binary erasure channel (BEC)) can be considered for future work. In addition, this paper assumed that the positions of superior/inferior channels are always fixed. However, it is expected that those positions can be varied and thus we have to study the more realistic non-uniform scenario for future work.
