A Regression-Based Optimal Scaling Factor Scheme for 5G LDPC Codes with MIMO Systems

Abstract— Low-Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes are powerful forward error correction (FEC) codes which achieve near-channel capacity performances. They have been adopted in several technologies, such as WiFi and 5G NR, and there is active research on optimising both their encoding and decoding algorithms. This paper proposes a low-complexity regressionbased optimal scaling factor (OSF) scheme to optimise the Normalized Min-Sum Algorithm (NMSA) performance in 5G NR with different Multiple-Input Multiple-Output (MIMO) configurations. The proposed scheme provides Eb/N0 gains in all tested scenarios, with the maximum achievable Eb/N0 gain being 1 dB when using the 8T2R MIMO configuration in the low BER range.
Keywords—5G, LDPC, NMSA, Scaling Factor, Regression.
I. INTRODUCTION 5G New Radio (NR) is continuously being improved through succeeding 3GPP Releases to achieve its objective of supporting a panoply of vertical industries. The technical specifications of 5G NR, which are defined by the 3rd Generation Partnership Project (3GPP), are based on requirements set by ITU. 5G NR is required to support three main service classes with differing performance requirements: enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB), ultrareliable low-latency communications (URLLC) and massive machine-type communications (mMTC) [1][1].
MIMO is one of the most crucial technologies in 5G NR, allowing for high data rates and capacity. It exploits multipath propagation to support the transmission of multiple signals through a single channel, as shown in Fig 1 [3]. MIMO provides spatial diversity, effectively improving link reliability and reducing interference's impact [4].
Fig. 1. MIMO Transmission.
In [5], a hybrid of orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) and Nonorthogonal Multiple Access (NOMA) is proposed to improve the performance of 5G NR. NOMA is used to multiplex users in the power or the code domain and provides several benefits, such as increasing spectral

efficiency, reducing latency and increasing cell-edge user throughput [6]. The proposed hybrid scheme provides a higher capacity and increased data rates but degrades the BER. An increase of 160 Mbps is observed when employing the proposed scheme compared to Orthogonal Multiple Access OMA and NOMA without OFDM [5] at a signal-tonoise ratio (SNR) of 5 dB.
The authors in [7] proposed a modification to the maximalratio combining (MRC) detection for systems employing LDPC codes, OFDM and massive MIMO to improve their BER performance. The modification consisted of using a derived probability distribution function (PDF) instead of the conventional Gaussian distribution to obtain the Log Likelihood Ratios (LLR) used by the LDPC decoder. The authors used a polynomial equation to approximate the LLRs. Simulation results show that the proposed modification leads to an improved BER, especially in the high Eb/N0 region [7].
In order to improve the MSA's error performance to par with that of the Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA), a modification based on the Wiener and Linear Minimum Mean Square Error (LMMSE) estimators for LDPC codes was suggested in [8]. Their approach involved using the golden section search algorithm to optimise parameter estimate computations to enhance its error performance. With this optimisation, the proposed technique outperformed MSA in simulations where it was found to perform close to SPA. Although its performance is slightly lower than that of SPA, it outperforms MSA at higher SNR settings by 1.75 dB [8].
In this work, polynomial regression models are derived to estimate the OSF, which can be applied in the LDPC NMSA decoder to enhance the bit error rate (BER). Different models are derived for various 5G NR setups, and the performance of the proposed technique is evaluated against a system that implements a fixed scaling factor through MATLAB simulations.
The paper is organised as follows: Section II gives an overview of the 5G NR system. The NMSA is described in Section III. The proposed regression-based OSF scheme is explained in Section IV. Simulation results and their analysis are provided in Section V. Finally, a conclusion is given in Section VI along with recommendations for future works.

IV. REGRESSION-BASED OPTIMAL SCALING FACTOR

The NMSA is designed with an adjustable scaling factor, used in equation (3), necessary for compensating for the higher check node values compared to LBPA [16]. Previous works use techniques such as density evolution to identify optimal scaling factors (OSFs) but the latter is unsuitable for long LDPC codes used for PDSCH transmissions [17]. As OSF was shown to vary with Eb/N0 [18], this work uses polynomial regression to relate the noise estimate, 3, with the OSF. V. SIMULATION RESULTS AND ANALYSIS
The 5G NR system model described in Section II was designed in MATLAB using the 5G Toolbox, which includes functions based on the 3GPP standards [20]. During the simulation, 1000 frames of length 10 ms were transmitted over a Clustered Delay Line (CDL) channel suitable for assessing MIMO transmissions. Hybrid automatic repeat request (HARQ) was enabled and the modulation scheme was set to 16-QAM [21].
Three PDSCH coderates (340/1024, 490/1024 and 658/1024) were tested to observe how the performance varies as the coderate increases. Moreover, 4T2R and 8T2R MIMO configurations were simulated. The BER performance of the conventional 5G NR system with the scaling factor fixed to 1 and the system employing the regression-based OSF scheme were compared.
The BER results for PDSCH code-rate 340/1024 are given in Fig 8.

The 8T2R configuration provides Eb/N0 gains of 0.5 dB in the high BER range of order 10-2 and 0.4 dB in the low BER range of order 10-5.

The results show that the 8T2R configuration provides Eb/N0 gains of up to 3 dB over the 4T2R configuration.
With the 4T2R MIMO configuration, the OSF scheme achieves Eb/N0 gains of 0.5 dB in the high BER range of order 10-1 and 0.2 dB in the low BER range of order 10-4. Compared with the 8T2R MIMO configuration, the OSF scheme provides Eb/N0 gains of 1 dB near the high BER range of order 10-1 and 0.1 dB in the low BER range of order 10-5.
The results obtained using different code-rates have shown similar trends. The 8T2R configuration always provides the best error performance compared to the 4T2R one. The regression-based OSF scheme consistently provides performance gains irrespective of Eb/N0 and code-rate. With the 4T2R and 8T2R configurations, the best Eb/N0 gains obtained were 0.8 dB and 1 dB, respectively.
VI. CONCLUSION This paper proposes a polynomial regression-based OSF scheme that provides the best performance in NMSA. The scheme is tested in a 5G NR system model simulated in MATLAB. The model is designed with processes based on 3GPP technical standards.
Simulation results show that the proposed scheme improved the BER performance for all tested code rates and MIMO configurations. The peak Eb/N0 gain of 1 dB was obtained using an 8T2R MIMO configuration.
As a future work, a computational complexity and latency analysis can be performed to observe the reduction in latency when using the regression-based OSF scheme with NMSA in the 5G NR system compared to the more complex LBPA.
