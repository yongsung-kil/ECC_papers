A Threshold-Based Binary Message Passing Decoder With Memory for Product Codes
Xinwei Zhao , Shancheng Zhao , Qingyong Deng , Senior Member, IEEE, Zhetao Li , Member, IEEE, and Xiaohu Tang , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract— Product codes (PCs) are typically decoded using iterative bounded distance decoding (iBDD) to ensure a low decoding complexity. To obtain further performance gain, a soft-aided decoding algorithm, termed the iBDD with scaled reliability (iBDD-SR), was proposed for PCs. In this paper, we propose an enhanced iBDD-SR by introducing threshold and memory when passing messages between the component decoders. The resulting algorithm is referred to as the thresholdbased binary message passing (TB-BMP) with memory. In the proposed decoding algorithm, the soft reliability of the BDD output at the current half-iteration is a weighted sum of the BDD output, the channel reliability, and the content of the memory unit, where the content of the memory unit at the current half-iteration is related to the selected threshold and the BDD output at last half-iteration. Due to the existence of memory, the Bayesian network is used to model the decoding process of the TB-BMP. Based on the Bayesian network, we derive the density evolution (DE) equations for the TB-BMP under the constraint of extrinsic message passing (EMP). The analytical results of the DE analysis can be used to guide the selection of the parameters of the TB-BMP decoder. Extensive simulation results show that the TB-BMP decoder outperforms the iBDD-SR over the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise (Bi-AWGN) channels. I. INTRODUCTION
I N THE optical transport network, product codes (PCs) [1] are well known for their considerable net coding gains (NCGs) when decoded with low-complexity decoders. A PC is specified by an n×n array where each row and column in the array is protected by a component code of length n. Reed-Solomon (RS) or BCH codes are used as the component codes of PCs such that they can be efficiently decoded with iterative bounded distanced decoding (iBDD). Such a decoding scheme is also referred to as hard decision decoding (HDD) since only hard decisions of the channel outputs are used.
For high throughput applications, the minimum distances of component codes of the PCs are small, which leads to severe performance degradation for iBDD due to miscorrections of the underlying component codes. To tackle this problem, several mitigation techniques based on the HDD results for PCs or product-like codes [2], [3], [4], [5], [6], [7] were proposed in [7], [8], and [9]. These decoders, referred to as anchor-based decoders, exploit the conflicts between different hard decoding results to detect and prevent misccorrections. Extensive simulation results showed that the performances of anchor-based decoders are very close to those of “genieaided” miscorrection-free (MF) iBDD (MF iBDD). However, due to the HDD nature of the anchor-based decoders, their performances cannot be further improved.
To obtain further performance gain, a new class of decoding schemes [10], [11], [12], [13], [14], [15], [16] called soft-aided HDD are introduced for PCs or product-like codes. These decoding algorithms are shown to provide a large performance gain compared to the underlying HDD while keeping a moderate increase in decoding complexity. The idea underlying soft-aided decoding schemes is to assist HDD with soft messages provided by the channel outputs. Among these soft-aided decoding schemes, a low-complexity decoding algorithm, named iBDD with scaled reliability (iBDD-SR) [10], provides a good tradeoff between the performance and the decoding complexity. The main idea of the iBDD-SR is to make a hard decision based on a weighted sum of the output of BDD

Density evolution (DE) analysis of the generalized low-density parity-check code (GLDPC) ensemble which encompasses PCs [17] is used to select the parameters of the iBDD-SR. A refined version of iBDD-SR, called iBDD with combined reliability (iBDD-CR), was presented in [15]. Moreover, several decoding algorithms based on error-and-erasure decoding (EED) were proposed for PCs or product-like codes [18], [19], [20], [21], [22], [23], [24]. In particular, the EED-based dynamic reliability score decoder (DRSD) proposed in [21] achieves a large performance gain such that its performance is very close to that of MF EED.
This paper is intended to enhance the performances of softaided iBDD for PCs. Particularly, we introduce a memory unit and a threshold into the iBDD to obtain the threshold-based binary message passing (TB-BMP) decoder with memory. In the proposed decoder, the soft reliability of the BDD output at the current half-iteration is a weighted sum of the BDD output, the channel reliability, and the content of the memory unit. The introduction of memory for TB-BMP is motivated by the decoding scheme proposed in [25] and [26] for LDPC codes [27]. Intuitively, the previous decoding results may be helpful to the current decoding process. Furthermore, a weak “opposite force” introduced by setting a flipping threshold may allow decoder to escape the local trap. However, the analytical results in [10] and [26] cannot be directly applied to the proposed TB-BMP for the following reason. When compared with iBDD-SR, a memory unit is introduced in TB-BMP. When compared with the decoder with memory in [25] and [26], a threshold is used in TB-BMP to determine the content of the memory unit. The above differences make the independent assumptions used in [10] and [26] unsuitable. To analyze the TB-BMP decoder, we first use a Bayesian network to model its decoding process when used for the decoding of a class of GLDPC ensembles encompassing PCs. We then derive the density evolution equations for TB-BMP under the constraints of a given threshold and extrinsic message passing (EMP). These equations are finally used to compute the decoding threshold of the GLDPC ensemble and then guide the selection of the parameters of the TB-BMP decoder. Extensive simulation results show that the TB-BMP decoder outperforms the iBDD-SR over the Bi-AWGN channels at the expense of only a minor increase in complexity. In particular, for a PC based on a two-error-correcting extended BCH code of length 256, the TB-BMP decoder performs about 0.28 dB better than the iBDD-SR at BER= 10−7.
The paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews the structure of PCs and presents the system model. The proposed TB-BMP is introduced in Section III. In Section IV, the scaling factors of TB-BMP are optimized via the DE analysis of TB-BMP. Numerical results are presented in Section V and Section VI concludes the paper.

C. Bounded Distance Decoding
BDD is used to decode the received sequence for the component code of C. We assume that the transmitted codeword is c = (c1, c2, · · · , cn) and the hard-decision sequence of the channel output is r = (r1, r2, · · · , rn). To correct up to t errors, the minimum Hamming distance dmin of C must satisfy dmin ≥ 2t +1. In other words, the Hamming distance between two arbitrarily chosen codewords c and c′ of C should satisfy dH(c, c′) ≥ 2t + 1. The decoding results of the BDD can be classified as three cases. First, if the weight of the error pattern dH(c, r) is less than or equal to t, then the hard-decision sequence r can be correctly decoded by BDD. Second, if the weight of the error pattern satisfies dH(c, r) > t, there may exist another codeword c′ satisfying dH(c′, r) ≤ t. For this case, the hard-decision sequence r is erroneously decoded as c′, which leads to miscorrection. Third, if dH(c′, r) > t for all c′ ∈ C, the decoder declares failure and the decoding result of BDD is r.
Conventional decoding procedure for PCs performs BDD
for all rows and columns iteratively. Each decoding iteration consists of two half-iterations: row decoding and column
decoding. Following [10], the iterative decoding of PCs based
on BDD of the component codes is referred to as iBDD.

IV. DENSITY EVOLUTION OF TB-BMP
This section aims to optimize the scaling factors and the threshold via the DE analysis of TB-BMP.
The DE analysis is based on the assumption of infinite block length and cycle-free. However, it is difficult for PCs in practical applications to meet the above two assumptions. Thus, as mentioned in [10], instead of minimizing the error probability, the purpose of selecting the scaling factors and the threshold via DE is to avoid resorting to an optimization based on the time-consuming numerical simulation. Furthermore, it is known that EMP is essential for the derivation of DE. For the purpose of DE analysis, similar to [10], we assume that the decoding rules of the extrinsic BDD are employed. In the following, we first introduce the extrinsic BDD in [17] for PCs.
A. Extrinsic Message Passing
PCs can be treated as a member of a special class of GLDPCs whose Tanner graphs consist of n2 degree-2 VNs and 2n degree-n CNs. In the graph, each VN is connected to two different CNs. Recall the conventional decoding of PCs, that is iBDD, the output u¯ri,,j(ℓ) of BDD for the row decoding can be seen as the message passed from CN j to VN i in the ℓ-th iteration. However, the computation of u¯ri,,j(ℓ) should use the input message u¯ci,,j(ℓ−1), which violates the principle of EMP. To make only extrinsic messages pass between VNs and CNs, the input message u¯ci,,j(ℓ−1) is replaced by the channel output ri,j. The EMP algorithm of TB-BMP is shown in Fig. 5. Note that, for a row or a column, the BDD in the conventional iBDD is executed only once, while the extrinsic BDD is required to execute n times.
The decoding schedule of EMP algorithm for PCs involves two half-iterations, where CNs corresponding to the column

B. Bayesian Networks
Under the cycle-free (tree-like) assumption and based on the extrinsic decoding rule, the DE analysis of memoryless algorithms is significantly simplified [29]. However, the DE analysis of decoding algorithms with memory is not as straightforward as it may seem. In [26], the authors employ the Bayesian network to analyze the dependencies between messages and memory contents. In the following, we resort to Bayesian network to analyze TB-BMP for a class of GLDPC ensembles which encompasses PCs. where P(X |Z) denotes the conditional distribution of X given Z. Given the Bayesian network G, the d-separation is a criterion for deciding whether X ⊥⊥ Y|Z holds or not. Hence, some necessary definitions are given as follows.
Definition 1 (undirected path blocked): [26] An undirected path between two distinct nodes is blocked by a set of nodes X if the path includes a node U such that either: (a) U has head-to-head arrows along the path (→ U ←), and neither U nor any of its descendants is in X , or (b) U does not have head-to-head arrows along the path and U is in X .
Definition 2 (d-separation): [26] Given a set of nodes Z, if every undirected path from any node in X to any node in Y is blocked by Z, then X is said to be d-separated from Y by Z.
Based on the definition of d-separation, we have the fact that X ⊥⊥ Y|Z if Z d-separates X from Y [30]. Particularly, the concept of d-separation can be easily understood by the so-called Bayes-Ball algorithm [31], which can be seen as a computational implementation of the d-separation criterion.
C. The Bayesian Network of TB-BMP

V. NUMERICAL RESULTS
In this section, we perform software simulations over BiAWGN channels to show the performance advantages of the proposed TB-BMP based on EMP and IMP in terms of BERs. Furthermore, the decoding thresholds obtained via DE and the decoding complexity for TB-BMP are also discussed in this section. The parameters of BCH codes used in PCs for simulations are shown in Table I.

A. Theoretical Results
We first evaluate the decoding threshold numerically for different T using the DE analysis of TB-BMP based on (8), where the memory truncation is set to δ = 3, the step size of T is 0.20, and the target error probability x(ℓ) of DE is set to 10−15 for 100 half-iteration. The decoding thresholds of PCs with C1, C2, and C3 are shown in Fig. 8. The optimal threshold T derived by DE is denoted as TDE. For the PC with C1, the decoding threshold is Eb/N0 = 3.681 dB with

T = TDE = 2.6. For the PC with C2, the decoding threshold is Eb/N0 = 4.395 dB with T = TDE = 2.6. Similarly, the decoding threshold is Eb/N0 = 4.182 dB with T = TDE = 2.0 for the PC with C3. Note that the decoding threshold of TBBMP with T = 0 is the same as that of iBDD-SR. We point out that, when T = 0, we have β(ℓ) = 0 and the TB-BMP degenerates to the original iBDD-SR.
It can be seen from Fig. 8 that the decoding threshold decreases as T increases until T = TDE. When T > TDE, we observe a degradation of the decoding thresholds when we further increase T . This degradation is partially due to the fact that too many correct bits saved at the memory unit are flipped when T is large.
B. Simulations of EMP Decoding

In the following simulations, the parameters w(ℓ) and β(ℓ) for both TB-BMP-1 and TB-BMP-2 are selected via the DE of TB-BMP-2. Furthermore, both TB-BMP-1 and TB-BMP2 are executed for the first 10 iterations and iBDD are then executed for the last 2 iterations. The reason for the additional 2 iterations of iBDD is to avoid the error floor caused by TB-BMP-1 or TB-BMP-2, which is similar to that for iBDDSR [10] and iBDD-CR [15].

D. Complexity Considerations
In [19], the complexities of most existing decoding schemes for PCs have been discussed. Among these decoding schemes,

the BMP-based decoding schemes, like iBDD-SR, iBDD-CR,
and TB-BMP, only exchange the hard messages between the
decoders to ensure a low decoding data flow that is similar
to that of iBDD. Compare with the iBDD-SR, there is an
additional complexity for TB-BMP due to the combination of
messages provided by the memory unit. Furthermore, in TB-
BMP, additional memory is required due to the memory unit
and the weighting factors. In the following, we compare the
complexity of TB-BMP and iBDD-SR in terms of memory
requirements. Consider a PC based on the (n, k, t) BCH code. Assume that
this PC is decoded with a total of ℓ half-iterations, where ℓ is
even. For both iBDD-SR and TB-BMP, memory is required to store the channel LLRs (L) and scaling factors (w(ℓ)). We denote by q the number of bits for the quantization of the
channel LLRs and scaling factors. The memory requirements for storing L and w(ℓ) for both iBDD-SR and TB-BMP are (ℓ + n2)q bits.
For TB-BMP, additional memory is required to store u(ℓ) and β(ℓ). Since the contents of the memory unit are binary, we only need n2 bits to store u(ℓ). Furthermore, we assume that the scaling factors β(ℓ) are quantized to q′ bits. Then,

In addition to the extra storage overheads, it is also necessary to discuss extra binary-addition operations introduced in TB-BMP. Assume that the scaling factor β(ℓ−1)w(ℓ) is quantized into q bits. Then, to decode a row or a column, qn additional binary-additions are required. As a result, for an half-iteration, n · qn binary-additions are required. Given the maximum half-iteration ℓmax for the decoding of TB-BMP, a total of qn2(ℓmax − 1) additional binary-addition operations are required compared with iBDD-SR. Note that there are no binary-addition operations introduced by the memory contents at the 0-th half-iteration.
VI. CONCLUSION
This paper presented a variant of iBDD-SR by introducing a memory unit and a threshold. The introduced memory was a function of the output of BDD at last half-iteration and the threshold T . We derived the DE for the GLDPC ensemble when decoded with TB-BMP. The analytical results of the DE were used to guide the selection of the parameters of TBBMP. The proposed TB-BMP provided a large performance improvement at the expense of a minor increase in complexity compared to iBDD-SR. Extensive numerical results were presented to show the performance advantages of the proposed TB-BMP. In particular, for a PC with (256,239,2) extended BCH component code, the proposed TB-BMP performed about 0.28 dB better than the iBDD-SR at BER= 10−7. These results confirmed the potential of the TB-BMP in optical fiber communication systems. As a matter of future work it might be of interest to study TB-BMP for other product-like codes, and their use in error-and-erasure decoding. Moreover, it might be a good and challenging work to consider more previous memory contents rather than the memory content at last half-iteration. Also, it is important to explain why using the extrinsic BDD does not provide any gain over the intrinsic BDD in TB-BMP.
