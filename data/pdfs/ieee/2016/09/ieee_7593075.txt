Abstract-This paper proposes a general framework aiming at 
describing and comparing LDPC decoders based on the ADMM 
approach. ADMM is a linear programming decoding method. 
When applied on LDPC codes, it acts as an iterative message 
passing technique and enhances the error correction performance 
compared with the usual belief propagation based decoders. In 
this paper, three ADMM decoding algorithms classified according 
to their scheduling type are described and compared. Simulation 
results show that the layered scheduling is efficient to enhance 
the error correction performance while decreasing the average 
number of decoding iterations for the considered LDPC codes. 
I. 
INTRODUCTION 
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a class of valu­
able linear block codes. They are now chosen by a wide array 
of the latest communication and storage systems as forward 
error correction in many digital communication standards such 
as DVB-S2, WiMAX, WiFi and WRAN. 
Algorithms based on Sum-Product (SPA) or Min-Sum 
(MS) variants have been considered in the usual message 
passing approaches to decode LDPC codes. Recently, Feldman 
et al. in [1] proposed an alternative approach based on Linear 
Programming (LP) for LDPC decoding and proved it to be 
more efficient in terms of error correction performance than 
the usual message passing (MP) LDPC decoding. 
With the advance of the alternating direction method of 
multipliers (ADMM) technique [2], a significant improvement 
towards LP LDPC decoding optimization has been made 
possible. In [3], Barman et al. exploit the geometry of the LP 
constraints and develop a decoding method with a message 
passing algorithmic formulation. Recent works in [4], [5] and 
[6] proposed enhanced ADMM decoders to improve the error 
rate performance and demonstrate that they can perform better 
than both standard LP and SPA decoders. Despite the fact that 
ADMM is distributed and has strong convergence guarantees, 
current implementations of the ADMM-based LP decoders still 
suffer from high computation complexity and huge memory 
[7]. They cannot achieve low decoding latency which is a 
predominant criterion in future standards like 5G. 
In order to reduce the computation complexity of the 
ADMM LDPC decoding algorithm, the convergence speed 
might be considered by scaling down the amount of decoding 
iterations. One way to reach this objective consists in using 
different message passing schedulings. In fact, for usual Min­
Sum based algorithms, the use of the layered scheduling [8], 
[9] left no doubt about its custom scheduling advantages 
with regards to the classical flooding scheduling. For instance, 
the horizontal layered scheduling halves the number of itera­
tions necessary for convergence while keeping the same error

propagation (BP) LDPC decoders. It is incidentally for this 
reason that all hardware implementations of LDPC decoders 
switched from the flooding scheduling [10], [11] to the layered

All the ADMM based LDPC decoders considered so far 
are based on the flooding formulation [3]-[6], [14], [15]. This 
has turned our research to exploring scheduling strategies like 
the ones proposed for traditional LDPC decoding [8], [9]. 
However, since the ADMM decoding introduces a different 
node processing, the advantages and the possible drawbacks 
of the layered schedulings have to be evaluated not only in 
terms of computation complexity reduction but also in terms 
of memory footprint and error correction performance. We first 
evaluated the horizontal layered scheduling (HL) applied on 
the ADMM approach in [16] where the experimental results 
for the studied codes showed that it is efficient to decrease the 
average number of decoding iterations while improving the 
error correction performances. 
This paper draws upon the results published in [16]. It 
provides a novel algorithmic formulation of vertical layered 
scheduling (VL). Further comparisons of the flooding schedul­
ing (FS) with the HL and VL schedulings of the ADMM algo­
rithm are given in terms of computation complexity and mem­
ory complexity. For the studied LDPC codes, we show that 
the layered scheduling decoding enables a fast convergence 
while enhancing the error correction performance. Simulation 
results show that it can be applied to the ADMM LP decoders 
without any error floors. Besides, the VL scheduling seems to 
be a good candidate capable of providing low decoding latency 
if its computation complexity is bypassed. 
The remainder of this article is organized as follows: 
Section II summarizes the ADMM algorithm principles for 
LDPC decoding. Section III describes the different schedul­
ings. Section IV compares the flooding scheduling with the 
layered ones in terms of computation complexity and memory 
cost. Finally, in Section V, some performance results are 
provided and discussed.

III. 
THE ADMM DECODING SCHEDULINGS 
The ADMM-based decoding algorithm with h penalty can 
be expressed in the form of an iterative message passing 
one that bears resemblance to the usual BP algorithm. The 
most prevailing scheduling associated with the BP algorithm 
is the FS where all the variable nodes update their messages 
at the same time then all the check nodes are computed 
simultaneously. Once all the messages are processed, one 
decoding iteration is achieved. Iterations are repeated until the 
algorithm converges or until the maximum number of iterations 
is reached. Hitherto, all the proposed ADMM decoders are 
based on the flooding scheduling [3]-[6], [14], [15] except 
the one that we have recently described in [16] where we 
introduced the HL scheduling ADMM decoder.

B. The Horizontal layered Scheduling 
To schedule the MP computation in the Tanner graph, it 
is possible to process in a "CN centric" way. Indeed, during 
each iteration, the CN nodes are considered one by one. 
For each CN, its internal state is computed according to its 
VN neighbors which are then updated before the next CN 
computation. The fast VN updates enable the decoding process 
to converge earlier. A formalization of the HL scheduling for 
the ADMM 12 penalized method is depicted in [16]. 
This modification of the scheduling impacts the maximum 
computation complexity of the decoding process. It is called 
Chlayer and can be expressed as:

COMPLEXITY ANALYSIS 
The ADMM algorithm is a BP like decoding approach. 
So far, the flooding scheduling has been used for the ADMM 
decoding. In Table I, we present the computation complexity 
in terms of floating-point like operations of two versions of 
the ADMM penalized decoder. The usual flooding-scheduled 
decoder is compared with the one inspired by [15] where the 
useless Euclidean projections in the CN kernel are removed. 
In each CN computation, the sum of the absolute differences 
between the projection vector of the current iteration and the 
one of the previous iteration are evaluated. If the computed 
result is lower than an already fixed floor value, then the 
projection operation is skipped. It shows that in order to com­
pute the projection's skipping condition, the CN complexity 
is increased and so is the total number of memory access. 
Even though this simplification does not impact the error 
correction performance [15], it is still complex compared with 
the ADMM-12.

applying the horizontal layered scheduling to the ADMM 
decoder enables up to 40% decoding iteration savings at 
a comparable computation complexity cost to the flooding 
scheduling. On the other hand, the vertical layered scheduling 
has the highest computation complexity. Nevertheless, it has 
the fastest convergence speed and the best error correction 
performance. Hence, in case the hardware complexity can be 
circumvented, the vertical layered scheduling would provide 
the lowest decoding latency. To sum up, the study shows that 
the layered scheduling, when applied to the iterative ADMM 
approach, improves the error correction performance. Besides, 
it converges valuably faster than the flooding scheduling and 
doesn't exhibit any error floor behavior.
