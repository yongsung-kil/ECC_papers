Construction of High-Rate QC-LDPC Codes With Multi-Weight Circulants
Xiaoning Wu, Ming Jiang, and Chunming Zhao School of Information Science and Engineering Southeast University Nanjing, China 211189
Email: {wuxn1991.jiang_ming.cmzhao}@seu.edu.cn

Abstract-In this paper, we propose a construction method of quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes with multi-weight circulants (MQC-LDPC codes) which is suitable for high-rate LDPC codes with moderate length. MQC-LDPC codes can achieve better degree distributions, Hamming weight distributions, and stopping set weight distributions than conven­ tional QC-LDPC codes with single-weight circulants (SQC-LDPC codes). Within the same framework, we generalize and simplify the error minimization progressive edge growth (EMPEG) algo­ rithm for supporting the structure with multi-weight circulants. Simulation results show that the constructed MQC-LDPC codes offer better performance at both high and low signal to noise ratio (SNR) regions in additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels than the conventional SQC-LDPC codes defined in S02.Had and S02.16e.
I. INT RODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have aroused much attention around the world over the past decade due to their low decoding complexity and near capacity-approaching performance. As the most promising class of LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes are more suitable for hardware implementation thanks to the quasi-cyclic (QC) nature. Hence, QC-LDPC codes have been applied to various digital communications systems, such as 802.11ad [2] and 802.16e [3], and may play an important role in 5G technolo­ gies.
QC-LDPC codes used in the practical communication ap­ plications are usually constructed by circulant permutation matrices (CPM). Since the weight of each row/column in a CPM is one, we call the CPM as the single-weight circulant to then compare with the multi-weight circulant. When QC­ LDPC codes with single-weight circulants (SQC-LDPC codes) are constructed, there is one conunon problem that the max­ imum degree of variable nodes is limited by the number of CPMs per column. This restriction may prevent SQC-LDPC codes from having better degree distributions, especially for high-rate SQC-LDPC codes, where there is a very limited number of CPMs per column. To solve this problem, we prefer to construct QC-LDPC codes with multiple-weight circulants (MQC-LDPC codes). Since the row/column weight of a multi­ weight circulant is no longer limited by one, better degree distributions can be obtained in the MQC-LDPC codes.
Two methods have been proposed to construct MQC-LDPC codes [4] [5], however they mainly focused on maximizing

girth and lacked considering the effects of ACE or EMD [6], which may prevent the MQC-LDPC codes from better error floors [7]. Besides, there are two general categories of struc­ tured constructions of QC-LDPC codes, the pseudorandom constructions [8] [9] [10] [11] and the algebraic/combinatorial constructions [12] [13]. However, the algorithms mentioned above usually are applied on the constructions of SQC-LDPC codes instead of MQC-LDPC codes. Among the pseudoran­ dom constructions, the error minimization progressive edge growth (EMPEG) algorithm [11] efficiently makes use of girth and EMD to optimize the block error probability over binary erasure channels (BEC), which leads to excellent performance in the error floor region.
In this paper, we generalize the EMPEG algorithm to support the construction of MQC-LDPC codes and give some performance analysis and simulations. The analysis results show that, MQC-LDPC codes not only can provide better degree distributions but also achieve better Hamming weight and stopping set weight distributions than SQC-LDPC codes, which is consistent with the discussion in [14]. Furthermore, from the simulated results, we can also see that MQC-LDPC codes obviously outperform SQC-LDPC codes at both high and low signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) regions in additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels.

To verify the performance improvement of the QC structure with multi­ weight circulants, we compare the performance of MQC­ LDPC codes and SQC-LDPC codes given by the two examples in Section V. VII. CONCLUSION In this paper, we proposed a method to construct moderate­ length high-rate MQC-LDPC codes, where a generalized EM­ PEG algorithm is given. For MQC-LDPC codes, the maximum degree of the variable nodes can be further increased and better degree distributions can be achieved, which actually provides a noticeable performance improvement in the waterfall region.

Furthermore, MQC-LDPC codes also have better distributions
of Hamming weight and stopping set weight, which results in
superior performance in the error floor region.
