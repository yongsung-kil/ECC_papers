Construction of Structured LDPC Code Based On Correlation Limitation

Jun Xu1, Dongming Wang1, Jin Xu2, Xiaomei Xu2, LiGuang Li2 and Xiaohu You1, Fellow, IEEE 1National Mobile Comm. Research Lab., Southeast Univ., Nanjing, China; 2Algorithm Dept, Wireless Product R & D Institute, ZTE Corp, Shenzhen, China, xu.jun2@zte.com.cn

Abstract—Channel coding for the next generation communication system needs to support higher data throughput and transmission rate. Due to the inherent parallel feature, low density parity check (LDPC) codes comply with the target. In this paper, we propose a complete channel coding scheme based on structured LDPC codes. In our design, the correlation among different parity check matrices for different code rates is established, meanwhile the correlation between adjacent rows is established, then matrix design should be restricted by these two type defined correlations in order to make great progress in the support of multiple code rates and layered decoding algorithm. Our scheme includes the definition of the four matrices and five constraint features of these matrices, also the benefits of these features are analyzed. Simulation result shows that the performance of the proposed scheme is the same as or a little better than that of 11ad LDPC codes. The proposed LDPC scheme obviously decreases route complexity and increases decoder throughput. So, this scheme is very suitable for high speed decoder with more than 1Gbps throughput in various future communication systems.

Banyan, Correlation Limitation
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes invented by Gallager[1] in the early 1960s have experienced an amazing comeback in recent 10 years. Structured LDPC as a special type of LDPC codes attracts a lot of interests and applications due to good performance, easy design of degree distribution and girth distribution and its facilitating hardware implementation in the communication system. In 2005, complete structured LDPC design based on circularly shifted identity matrices firstly was adopted by WIMAX standard (IEEE 802.16e)， the single common feature of four base matrices of size mb× nb for rate 1/2,2/3,3/4,5/6 is the same column size of nb=24, which was suggested by ZTE. Furthermore the two matrices of rate 3/4B and 5/6 designed by ZTE were adopted [2][3]. In 2006, structured LDPC codes also were proposed for 3GPP LTE by many companies including ZTE [4][5][6][7]. However structured LDPC codes failed to substitute turbo

codes because some companies doubt and argue the routing complexity of structured LDPC codes. Fortunately, in 2009 structured LDPC codes similar with WIMAX design were adopted by IEEE 802.11n. The same common feature of different base matrices of size mb×nb for rate 1/2,2/3,3/4,5/6 also is the same column size of nb=24. What is more, structured LDPC similar with 16e design was also adopted by IEEE 802.11ad. Different base matrices of size mb×nb for rate 1/2,5/8,3/4,13/16 also have the same column size of nb=16. It is convinced that the factor from nb=24 to nb=16 is a good progress, because the complexity of check node update unit can be decreased by the fact that the maximum row weight is smaller than 16. In recent years, many efficient hardware implementation and algorithm optimization are proposed [8][9][10][11].
Normal BP algorithm and layered BP algorithm are two typical forms of BP algorithm as the most typical decoding algorithm. Further, the normal BP is suitable for full parallel implementation of decoder for the LDPC code of one code size and one code rate, while the layered BP is suitable for partial parallel implementation of decoder for the LDPC codes of multiple code rates. In the wireless communication, since multi-rate LDPC codes are widely needed, the layered BP or other layered forms will be more suitable.
In general, two drawbacks of LDPC design in the current standards include: (1) There are different matrices for different rates, and parity check matrices of different rates are basically independent, which leads to a high complexity of multi-rate LDPC decoder since different independent matrices means different routes. (2) Since the matrix elements in each base matrix are completely random, any two non-negative-one elements of two adjacent rows in the same column are independent. If a layered decoding algorithm is applied for high rate LDPC codes, row information updating of the second row shall begin until row information updating of the first row is finished. That is to say, this serial processing feature will prevent efficient parallel pipeline processing and leads to the phenomenon known as “off line”. And this issue will degrade the efficiency of LDPC decoder seriously.
In this paper, a new constructed LDPC codes based on correlation limitation can be used to solve the above problem.

C. The new feature of the construed LDPC matrices
Definition of feature 2.1 – Nested characteristics of short cycles of size 4: At least 50% short cycles of size 4 in base matrices of rate 1/2, 5/8, and 13/16 also occur as the short cycles of size 4 of the base matrix of rate 3/4.
Due to this feature, the scenarios of short cycles of all base matrices are basically consistent, If short cycles of 4 can be avoided in the parity check matrix of rate 3/4 code, then short cycles of 4 can also be avoided in the parity check matrix of other rate codes.
Example: For rate 1/2,5/8,13/16 base matrices above, 30 and 40 in the first column together with 18 and 12 in the third column constitute a short cycle of size 4. The same short cycle of size 4 composed of 30 and 40 in the first column as well as 18 and 12 in the third column also occurs in the base matrix of rate 3/4.
Definition of feature 2.2 – The same set of the same column: All non-negative-one elements in the same column of different base matrices are from the same set composed of at most 4 elements, wherein each base matrix is used for each code rate.
Example: For rate 1/2, 5/8, 3/4 and 13/16 base matrices above, all non-negative-one elements in the first column are from the set {0,8,30,40}, then all non-negative-one elements in the 13-th column are from the set {0,31,34,8}, the similar situation exists in other columns.
Definition of feature 2.3 – column weight matched girth distribution: For base matrices of all code rates, any girth of variable nodes corresponding to systematic bits is smaller than or equal to any girth of variable nodes corresponding to parity bits since any column weight of systematic bits is larger than any column weight of parity bits in the parity check matrix H. Wherein, the girth of one variable node is defined as the size of the shortest cycle among all cycles which go through the variable node in the LDPC bipartite or tanner graph.
Example: For the base matrix of rate 1/2, all girths of all variable nodes except the final z variable nodes are 8, and all girths of the final z variable nodes are infinite. For the base matrices of rate 5/8, 3/4 and 13/16 base matrices, all girths of variable nodes corresponding to systematic bits are 6, and all girths of variable nodes corresponding to parity bits except the final z variable nodes are 8, and all

C. throughput advantage of feature 2.4
Characteristics of the new definition of 2.4 can significantly improve the throughput on condition of no additional cost. One example is given here, and the base matrix of rate 3/4 LDPC codes is used here. Suppose that the reading, processing, and writing time for one check node unit (CNU) are one, two, and one clocks respectively, the basic store unit is a word, both parallelism l and word size are 21, each z=42 bits in the received codeword corresponds to a log-likelihood ratio( LLR) vector. For the whole base matrix, there are nb=16 LLR vectors where each vector corresponds to each column. Two words are used to store one LLR vector of length z, wherein the number of words = z/parallelism, further one word is used to store odd bits in the LLR vector, another word is used to store even bits in the LLR vector.
According to the figure 6, one CNU process to accomplish 21 rows information update includes three steps of reading (R) , processing(P), and writing(W) marked by one solid horizontal line with three arrows. Moreover, the first horizontal solid line with arrows in the vertical order means that the extrinsic information of all odd rows among the first z=42 rows in the parity check matrix H is updated, the second horizontal solid line with arrows means that the information of all even rows among the first z=42 rows in H is updated, the third line denotes that the information of all odd rows among the second z=42 rows in H is updated, the fourth line denotes that the information of all even rows among the second z=42 rows in H is updated, and so on. For normal layered algorithm, the CNU processing depicted by the third solid line can begin only after the writing operation of the second solid line is done fully. However, the writing operation of the second line and the reading operation of the third line can be done at the same time, because the second line and the third line have used different words in our example if very few second type adjacent pairs are not considered, thus one clock can be saved due to the overlap time of writing and reading marked by the first circle. Finally, for normal layered algorithm, 20 clocks are needed to finish a complete iteration, but for each LDPC decoding iteration of our new design 4 clocks can be saved, thus nearly 20% throughput can be improved.

D. Further routing complexity advantage of feature 2.5
Since the first element of none-negative-one element is 0 in all base matrices, the posterior LLR of systematic bits or the whole codeword can be derived once a LDPC decoding iteration is completed. Then, error detection is performed by code block CRC or parity check matrix to decide termination or continuing.
If one cycle shift operation is only done for the subtraction of adjacent two non-negative elements in one column, the inverse cycle shift operation after CNU calculation can be avoided in our design. Compared with the LDPC decoder with the inverse cycle shift network[13], half intra-vector routing complexity can be avoided further, which means that the complexity in the previous table can be decreased half again on condition of any parallelism.
