A Combined Algebraic- and Graph-Based Method for Constructing Structured RC-LDPC Codes
Xijin Mu, Conghui Shen, and Baoming Bai, Member, IEEE

Abstract— This letter presents a new method for constructing rate-compatible low-density parity-check (RC-LDPC) codes, which is based on combining algebraic-based and graph-based construction methods. We first use a graph-based tool, protograph extrinsic information transfer chart, to construct a masking matrix. Then, the parity-check matrices of RC-LDPC codes are determined in accordance with the framework of the algebraic-based method. The proposed codes own a quasi-cyclic structure, and thus have advantages in hardware implementations of encoder and decoder. Simulation results show that the codes can achieve good bit error rate performance in the waterfall region within a wide range of code rates.
Index Terms— RC-LDPC codes, channel coding, finite fields, P-EXIT chart.
I. INTRODUCTION
R ATE-COMPATIBLE LDPC (RC-LDPC) codes are a family of LDPC codes with various rates for a fixed information length. All codes in the family should have acceptable error performance and are capable of sharing a set of encoder and decoder [1]. RC-LDPC codes can provide an efficient realization for incremental redundancy coding systems, e.g., hybrid automatic repeat request, which have been widely used in modern communication systems.
Puncturing is the first way to construct RC-LDPC codes, i.e., starting with a low-rate LDPC mother code and then puncturing some parity bits to get high-rate LDPC codes [1], [2]. But for practical code lengths, the RC-LDPC codes constructed from this method may suffer from large performance degradation at high rates [3]. Shortening is another construction method [4], [5], which is achieved by dropping some (known) information bits, and this method has been extended to construct nonbinary RC-LDPC codes [6]. Extension is also an efficient construction method, which starts with a high-rate LDPC mother code and then produces additional parity bits to yield low-rate codes [7], [8]. In [7], a new ensemble of RC-LDPC codes, named Kite codes, was conducted and shown to perform well within a wide range of code rates. However, Kite codes are random-like and hence difficult for implementation. In [8], a technique for constructing structured RC-LDPC codes by extending a protograph [9] was provided. There are generally two categories to construct LDPC codes: algebraic-based and graph-based methods. Most algebraic methods, e.g., construction based on finite fields [10], combinatorial designs [11] and finite geometries [12], can be included in the framework of superposition construction [13]. Main process of superposition construction consists of designing a base matrix, masking operation and expansion operation [14]. For graph-based methods, the progressive edge-growth (PEG) [15] and protograph-based methods [9] are the most common, and some optimized algorithms based on density evolution [16] have been developed for searching degree distributions or protographs with minimum decoding threshold. There are many similarities between the algebraicbased and graph-based methods if a broader view is taken. More discussion about the connection between these two methods can be found in [17].
In this letter, we put forward a method for constructing RC-LDPC codes by combining algebraic-based and graphbased methods. We first construct a base matrix by using the algebraic-based method introduced in [14]. Then, we design a graph-based optimized algorithm for searching a masking matrix with the lowest decoding threshold. At last, we obtain the parity-check matrices of RC-LDPC codes through masking operation and expansion operation. The resulting matrices are arrays of circulant permutation matrices, and possess quasi-cyclic (QC) structure. LDPC codes with QC structure have advantages in hardware implementations of encoder and decoder [18], [19]. Simulation results show that the proposed RC-LDPC codes can achieve good error performance in the waterfall region within a wide range of code rates.
The rest of the letter is organized as follows. In Section II, we first introduce the algebraic-based methods for constructing LDPC codes. Section III presents the construction method of RC-LDPC codes by combining algebraic-based and graph-based methods. Section IV gives simulation results of proposed codes. Finally, conclusion is drawn in Section V.

A circulant permutation matrix (CPM) is a permutation
matrix where each row is the right cyclic-shift of the row above
it and the first row is the right cyclic-shift of the last row. It is
uniquely specified by the location of the unique 1-component in its top row. Let A(α j ) be a (q − 1) × (q − 1) CPM with the
single 1-component of its top row at the location j . There are totally q−1 distinct CPMs of size (q−1)×(q−1), and they can be used to comprise the superposition set. An M × N array H
of CPMs and/or zero matrices can be formed from Bmask by replacing the (m, n)-th entry αcm,n in Bmask with the CPM A(α j ) if αcm,n = α j , or with a (q − 1) × (q − 1) zero matrix if αcm,n = 0. The above replacement is the expansion operation.
The null space of H defines an LDPC code.
