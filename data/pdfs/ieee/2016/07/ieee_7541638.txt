Abstract-In this paper, we develop a new decoding algorithm of binary linear codes for symbol-pair read channel. The Symbolpair read channel has recently been introduced by Cassuto and Blaum to model channel whose write resolution is higher than read resolution. The proposed decoding algorithm is based on the linear programming (LP). It is proved that the proposed LP decoder has the maximum-likelihood (ML) certificate property, i.e., the output of the decoder is guaranteed to be the ML codeword when it is integral. We also introduce thefractional pair distance dfp of the code which is a lower bound on the minimum pair distance. It is proved that the proposed LP decoder corrects
up to Idfp /2l - 1 errors.
I. INTRODUCTlON
Recent advances in data storage technologies have made it possible to write information to storage devices with high resolution. However, it may cause a problem of the gap between the write resolution and the read resolution. In the case that the resolution of the reader is lower than that of the process used to store the data, it is difficult to successfully read the individual symbols which are stored to the storage device. The symbol-pair read channel proposed by Cassuto and Blaum [1] is the channel model that models the reading process of such storage devices. The outputs of the symbol-pair read channel are overlapping pairs of symbols. We need new error correction framework for this new channel model. In the reading process, symbol-pair errors would occur rather than individual symbol errors, where a symbol-pair error means that at least one of the pair symbols is read erroneously. The main task is to construct error correcting codes and decoding algorithms that can correct many symbol-pair errors. Hereafter, we refer to error correcting codes for the symbol-pair read channel as symbol-pair codes.
Recently, some symbol-pair codes and decoding algorithms have been developed. Cassuto and Blaum [l] built a framework for symbol pair codes. They defined a new metric called pair distance and studied its relation to pair error correction capability. They also provided codes construction methods, decoding algorithms, and abound on the size of the optimal symbol-pair codes. An algebraic construction method of cyclic symbol-pair codes and their property are presented in the paper. Yaakobi et al. [2] proposed efficient decoding algorithms for cycIic symbol-pair codes. Hirotomo et al. [4] proposed a

decoding algorithm for symbol-pair codes based on the newly defined parity-check matrix and syndromes.
On the other hand, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are known to be capacity-approaching codes for a wide range of channel models [5]. Although message passing (MP) algorithms are commonly used as decoding algorithms for LDPC codes, linear programming (LP) decoder proposed by Feldman et al. [6] also provides good performance. The LP decoder has many attractive properties that the MP algorithms do not have. One of the important properties is that the output of the LP decoder is guaranteed to be the maximum-likelihood (ML) codeword when the output is integer valued (ML certificate property) [6]. Besides, LP decoding performs weil for the uItra-low error rate regime [6], [16]. This is important for data storage since it requires uItra-low error rates.
In this paper, we develop an LP decoder for symbol-pair codes. Not only establishing the LP decoder for symbol-pair codes, we also investigate its property. First, we show that the LP decoder has ML certificate property even for the symbolpair read channel. Second, we introduce the fractional pair distance dfp of symbol-pair codes which is a lower bound on the pair distance. It is proved that the proposed LP decoder
can correct up to Idfp /2l pair errors. We examine the average
fractional pair distance of randomly chosen LDPC codes from an ensemble of Gallager [10].
The rest of the paper is organized as folIows. In Section 2, we introduce basic notations and definitions for the symbol pair codes. In Section 3, we establish the LP decoder for symbol-pair codes. In Seetion 4, some properties of the proposed LP decoder are given. We concIude the paper in Seetion 5.

D. Relation between the Fractional Pair Distance and the Fractional Distance oi Nonbinary Codes
Our formulation of the LP decoding for the symbol-pair read channel is similar to that of the LP decoding of nonbinary linear codes for memoryless channels [14]. Authors in [15] showed that the fractional distance has operational significance even for nonbinary codes. They also provided an efficient calculation method of lower bounds on the fractional distance of nonbinary codes. Regarding the pair of symbols (Xi, Xi+l) as a symbol of F 22, one may think that the LP decoding for the symbol-pair read channel is the same as the LP decoding of nonbinary codes. However, there are constraints that the latter symbol of (Xi, Xi+l) equals to the former symbol of (Xi+l' Xi+2), which does not appear in the LP decoding of nonbinary codes. Therefore the fractional pair distance of binary codes and the fractional distance of nonbinary codes are different concepts.
V. CONCLUSION
We have developed an LP based decoding algorithm of binary linear codes for the symbol-pair read channel. We also proved some results on its error-correcting performance. We introduced a notion called the fractional pair distance of a code, which characterize the lower bound of the number of correctable pair errors that the proposed algorithm can correct.
There are some future directions of the work presented here. We assumed that the pair error occurs at each position independently. However, this assumption may not hold for some cases. There are two types of errors that can happen. One is that a stored bit is flipped. Another is that some error happens because of electronic noise in the read-out process. While the memoryless property for the symbol-pair read channel could be justified for the latter, it may inappropriate for the former. The LP decoding for finite-state channels is

proposed in [13] and we can consider the symbol-pair read channel with finite state. To develop the LP decoding algorithm for such channel is a future work.
For memoryless channels, some computationally efficient algorithms which solve the LP of the LP decoder have been developed [8][9]. Since the LP of the proposed LP decoding for the symbol-pair read channels have more variables and constraints than that of the LP decoding for the memoryless channels, development of efficient algorithms for the proposed LP would be important.
