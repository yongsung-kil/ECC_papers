Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check codes based on progressive cycle growth algorithm

Liu Yuan-hua, Zhang Mei-ling
School of Telecommunication and Information Engineering Xi'an University of Posts and Telecommunications Xi'an, China yuanhliu@163.com

Niu Xin-liang
Xi'an Branch of China Academy of Space Technology Xi'an, China

Abstract—Short cycles in the Tanner graph of a given LDPC code has negative effect on the performance of iterative decoding. To improve the performance, we presented an approach for constructing Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC-LDPC) codes whose associated Tanner graph has large girth and a small amount of short cycles. Each column block of the parity check matrix was designed by adding circulant permutation submatrices one after another, provided the current parity check matrix has minimal number of short cycles. The condition for the proposed QC-LDPC codes to have girth at least 6 was also analyzed. Regular and irregular QC-LDPC codes with various lengths and rates can be constructed. Simulations show that the proposed QC-LDPC codes have less short cycles and better performance than existing progressive edge growth based QCLDPC codes.
Keywords—QC-LDPC codes; construction algorithm; PEG algorithm; girth; cycle
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes under iterative decoding, such as Belief Propagation (BP) algorithm, have near capacity-approaching performance, and they have been one of the important research topics in the area of coding theory over the past few decades. There are two major types of methods for constructing LDPC codes: random constructions and structured constructions. The random constructions allow the flexibility to select the code parameters, a class of random LDPC codes with arbitrary length and rate can be easily constructed. The random methods can reduce short cycles from the Tanner graphs, and the designed LDPC codes provide good performance. One effective random construction is Progressive Edge-Growth (PEG) algorithm [1], and for a given degree distribution, it can construct random LDPC codes with large girth. Unfortunately, relatively large memory is required for random LDPC codes to store the parity-check matrices. In actual implementations, structured constructions have been strongly preferred because they result in much less storage space and lower encoding and decoding complexity. One particular class of structured LDPC codes are the Quasi-Cyclic (QC) LDPC codes [2-10], and the recently proposed communication standards [11] [12] adopt QC-LDPC codes as error-correcting codes. Nevertheless, QCLDPC codes have less flexible code parameters, such as code lengths and rates, etc. Consequently, PEG-QC LDPC codes [3] were constructed by adding a constraint into the original PEG

method, and the performance at high code rate is comparable with that of some existing methods. However, it is easier to construct PEG-QC LDPC codes with small girth, and the performance will be affected [4]. To improve the performance of PEG-QC LDPC codes, [4] proposed a modified method to construct Girth-Maximized (GM) PEG-QC LDPC codes. Large girth, however, is not enough to evaluate the performance. The number of short cycles is another important performance influence factor. In GM-PEG-QC LDPC codes, however, the cycles with length equals to girth are not eliminated effectively. In this paper, we propose a Progressive Cycle Growth (PCG) algorithm to construct QC-LDPC codes, which can ensure as few short cycles as possible, while maintaining the large girth.
The outline of this paper is as follows. In section II, the definitions of QC-LDPC codes based on the Circulant Permutation Matrix (CPM) are introduced. The PCG algorithm for designing QC-LDPC codes is described in Section III. Section IV presents the simulation results. Finally, Section V gives conclusions in the paper.

III. PCG METHOD FOR QC-LDPC CODES
It is known that the BP decoding has the optimal performance on the condition that the corresponding Tanner graph is free of cycles. Since the presence of short cycles in the Tanner graph has negative effect on information convergence. To improve the performance, [3] and [4] have proposed the PEG-QC algorithm and GM-PEG-QC algorithm, which can improve the girth property of QC-LDPC codes. It is worth noting that girth and the number of short cycles both play an important role on the performance. To reduce the number of short cycles and increase the girth, we present a progressive cycle growth method to design QC-LDPC code with various lengths and rates. And both regular and irregular QC-LDPC codes can be constructed.
For the QC-LDPC codes with parity-check matrix H shown in (1), all the variable nodes and check nodes need to be grouped, and each group has q nodes. The generation of QCLDPC codes will be sped up by the factor q. We only need to construct a small matrix Hb with dimension m×n, and the matrix H can be obtained through the basic matrix Hb under matrix extension. It can be seen that the proposed PCG method construct the parity check matrix by adding circulant permutation sub-matrices one after another provided the current parity check matrix has minimal short cycles and short cycles can be effectively avoided. QC-LDPC codes with flexible lengths and rates can be constructed under different parameters m, n and q, and irregular QC-LDPC codes can also be constructed under given degree distributions. For irregular codes, it is assumed that all columns corresponding to one group have the same column weight. And the proposed method can be easily extended to design irregular QC-LDPC codes. IV. PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
It is well known that the short cycles in Tanner Graphs of LDPC codes cause the sub-optimality of message-passing iterative decoding algorithms, because a message sent by a node along a cycle propagates back to the node itself after some iterations, which causes dependences among messages and performance degradation. As a result, short cycles should be avoided as far as possible in the code design. The local girth and the number of short cycles of PEG-QC codes [3], GMPEG-QC codes [4] and the presented PCG-QC codes are compared in Table I. The size of CPM is 128×128 and the column weight is 3. The length of information bits is 4096 and the code rates are 0.91, 0.84, 0.74 and 0.64, respectively. It can be seen that the proposed PCG-QC codes show a good local girth property for various code rates, they are comparable to GM-PEG-QC codes and superior to PEG-QC codes. In addition, compared to GM-PEG-QC codes, PCG-QC codes have fewer short cycles with length equal to girth.

V. CONCLUSIONS
A new PCG method for constructing QC-LDPC codes with large girth and small number of short cycles was presented. The proposed method constructed the parity check matrices by adding sub-matrices one after another provided the current matrix has minimal short cycles. The condition for the proposed QC-LDPC codes to have girth at least 6 was also analyzed. Regular and irregular QC-LDPC codes with various lengths and rates can be constructed. Simulations show that the proposed codes have less short cycles and better performance than original and modified PEG-QC LDPC codes.
