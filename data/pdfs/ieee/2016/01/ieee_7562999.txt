A Base Matrix method to construct column weight 3 Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes with high girth

Abhishek Kalsi
Department of Electrical Engineering Indian Institute of Technology Ropar, India abhishekkalsi@iitrpr.ac.in
Abstract— This article presents a simple, less computational
complexity method for constructing exponent matrix 3, K  of
girth 8, 10 and 12 of quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check codes (QC-LDPC) based on generation of base matrix. The construction of code deals with generation of base matrix by a simple algorithm and element wise element method for girth 8, while only element wise element method for girth 10 and 12. These methods are flexible for block-column length K. The simulations are shown in comparison with some existing appreciable work.
Keywords— Channel coding; circulant permutation matrix (CPM); girth;, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a special class of codes which are error correcting codes and are known for their high throughput and good decoding performance. The introduction of LDPC codes was brought into existence in 1962 [1] by Robert Gallager. Initially LDPC codes were ignored for almost three decades because of its high computational complexity for hardware implementation at that time. During 1992 Berrou developed the first code whose performance was very close to Shannon limit or channel capacity known as turbo codes. Its success led to rediscovery of LDPC codes by Mackay and Neal [2]. The rediscovery of LDPC codes leads to its performance competent to turbo codes over an AWGN (additive white Gaussian noise) channel. LDPC codes can also be represented in bipartite graph usually known as Tanner Graph [3]. By bipartite graph we means set of nodes further categorized into two subsets such that each subset is independent and is connected to each other. The two subsets are known as variable nodes representing columns and check nodes representing rows of a parity-check matrix.
Since LDPC codes has high implementation complexity and hence requires huge memory, so a new class of LDPC codes were introduced known as quasi-cyclic LDPC (QCLDPC) codes. These QC-LDPC codes were based on geometry and algebraic theories [4]. QC-LDPC codes are a class of LDPC codes in which there is cyclic connection between rows or columns of a sub matrix [5]. Using shift registers QC-LDPC codes can be encoded efficiently and by a simple mechanism of address generation, so as to have less

1) The first row and the first column of an exponent matrix both are fixed to be a zero vector.
2) It is mandatory that the 2nd row will always be in the ascending order.
3) Repetitions of indices are not allowed, i.e. at different indices we will have different values.

D. Base matrix generation for girth 10 and 12
For the generation of base matrix of girth 10 and 12 we will follow element wise element method with same rules, as in necessary conditions 3.1.1. Let us assume that CPM size is to be P d 30u K . As the first row and the first column is fixed to be a zero vector so we will first generate the 2nd row by element wise element method i.e. 2nd element of 2nd row which leads to a 2u 2 sub matrix. After that we will check its girth and will then move to 3rd element i.e. to 2u3 sub matrix so on up to 2u5 sub matrix, by doing this we will find the 2nd row
for the base matrix of size 3,5 exponent matrix.
After finding 2nd row we will apply the above mentioned procedure for the generation of 3rd row i.e. firstly construct 3u 2 sub matrix and then will check its girth, after that 3u3 sub matrix till 3u5 base matrix. By going through this method we will fix our second row and make all the changes in the third row so as to get the base matrix.
Suppose at any point (after getting 2nd or 3rd index of 3rd row) we are not getting any index in 3rd row by such procedure for desired girth then we will delete that pair of preceding indices and will start the same method from beginning of 3rd row from the original set of indices.
Since in the beginning we have assumed our P size to be P d 30u K , so to get optimized CPM size we will now decrease its value until we get the desire girth of base matrix. It can be seen in example 2 of section IV.
Based on our algorithm, all we have to work is on generation of base matrix for girth 8, 10 and 12. For constructing base matrix we have to follow the necessary conditions, algorithm and element wise element method for girth 8, while for girth 10 and 12 we just have to follow element wise element method with the necessary conditions. Thus we will reduce the computational complexity of the program.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we presented a simple less time consuming construction method for H matrix, having girth 8, 10 and 12. Our proposed method is based on first obtain the base matrix, which leads to generate consecutive block-column sub matrices for desired E(H) size. We obtained a class of CPM-
QC-LPDC codes as explained in our examples in section IV. The performance of proposed QC-LDPC codes is simulated in terms of BER and number of iterations, which is comparable to the existing work. We also compared our obtained CPM size for block-column length up to 12 with well known existing work. The results are helpful in construction of binary regular QC-LDPC codes.
