2016 IEEE International Symposium on Information Theory

Spatially-Coupled Codes Approach Symmetric Information Rate of Finite-State Markov Fading
Channels
Hiroshi Abe and Kenta Kasai Dept. of Communications and Computer Engineering
Tokyo Institute of Technology 2-12-1 Ookayama, Meguroku, Tokyo 152-8550, Japan
Email: {hiroshi,kenta}@comm.ce.titech.ac.jp

Abstract—Fukushima et al. proved that spatially-coupled codes, without pilot symbols and any optimization for the channels, universally achieve the symmetric information rate (SIR) of generalized erasure channels with memory. We expect that the universality is also valid for fading channels. The receiver performs joint iterative channel estimation and decoding where factor-graphs-based BCJR channel estimator for finitestate Markov channels and the LDPC decoder are considered. We demonstrate that the reliable transmission is possible at a rate close to the SIR.
I. INTRODUCTION
This paper deals with error correction for finite state Markov channels (FSMC) modeling flat fading channels. Worthen et al. [1] proposed a unified factor graph framework of FSMC. Eckford et al. [2] proposed optimization of irregular codes for a given FSMC. Arnold et al. [3] developed a scheme to calculate symmetric information rate (SIR) on factor graph of FSMC. Loeliger et al. [4] gave a unified treatment of various algorithms as message passing on factor graphs.
In [5] by Niu et al., channel estimation with Kalman smoothing is performed for Jakes’ simulator [6] based Rayleigh fading channels. The receiver in [5] is based on the application of sum-product algorithm to a factor graph representing the maximum a posteriori (MAP) probability of the transmitted symbols.
Li et al. applied BCJR algorithm [7] for channel estimation on time-invariant first-order Markov modeled fading channels [8]. In both [5] and [8], pilot symbols are used for the estimation. The system with pilots does not achieve channel capacity since there is no dependency on the information in pilots. To achieve the capacity, transmitting data must depend on the information. We therefore consider performing channel estimation without pilots.
The channels represented by factor graphs can be treated as FSMC [9] in a unified manner. Generally LDPC codes optimized for AWGN channels [10] are not optimal for FSMC. Irregular LDPC codes need degree distribution optimization [2] based on the density evolution to receive transmitted data at a rate close to the capacity with vanishing error rate in FSMC. Further, optimized LDPC codes tend to exhibit high error

floors [11], [12]. This makes the codes difficult for practical use.
Symmetric information rate (SIR) is defined as mutual information of independent and uniformly distributed (i.u.d.) inputs and the corresponding outputs. SIR is a lower bound on channel capacity since the capacity is a maximum of SIR over all input distribution. SIR is often used as a benchmark of achievable rate since it is difficult to derive the capacity for general channels with memory.
Fukushima et al. proved that spatially-coupled (SC) MacKay-Neal (MN) codes universally achieve the symmetric information rate (SIR) of any generalized erasure channels (GEC) [13] without pilot symbols [14]. We expect the universality of the SC-MN code is also valid for the general finite-state channels with memory and the SC-regular codes [15] approach the SIR of the channels.
In this paper, we consider SC codes for fading channels without pilot signals. The codes do not need any parameter optimization for various channels. The receiver performs an efficient joint iterative channel estimation and decoding on a factor graph. SIR can be derived by sum-product algorithm on the factor graph. Further, we demonstrate that reliable communications are possible at a rate close to the SIR.

VIII. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we have proposed the iterative receiver for the finite-state-Markov channels without pilot symbols assistant. We demonstrated that SC codes achieve block error rate 10−3 at spectrum efficiency close to the SIR of the fading channels without optimization.
