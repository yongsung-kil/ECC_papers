Efficient Search of Girth-Optimal QC-LDPC Codes
Alireza Tasdighi, Amir H. Banihashemi, Senior Member, IEEE, and Mohammad-Reza Sadeghi

AbstractвҖ” In this paper, we study the cycle structure of quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with the goal of obtaining the shortest code with a given degree distribution and girth. We focus on QC-LDPC codes, whose Tanner graphs are cyclic liftings of fully connected base graphs of size 3 Г— n, n вүҘ 4, and obtain minimal lifting degrees that result in girths 6 and 8. This is performed through an efficient exhaustive search, and as a result, we also find all the possible non-isomorphic codes with the same minimum block length, girth, and degree distribution. The exhaustive search, which is ordinarily a formidable task, is made possible by pruning the search space of many codes that are isomorphic to those previously examined in the search process. Many of the pruning techniques proposed in this paper are also applicable to QC-LDPC codes with base graphs other than the 3 Г— n fully connected ones discussed here, as well as to codes with a larger girth. To further demonstrate the effectiveness of the pruning techniques, we use them to search for QC-LDPC codes with girths 10 and 12, and find a number of such codes that have a shorter block length compared with the best known similar codes in the literature. In addition, motivated by the exhaustive search results, we tighten the lower bound on the block length of QC-LDPC codes of girth 6 constructed from fully connected 3 Г— n base graphs, and construct codes that achieve the lower bound for an arbitrary value of n вүҘ 4.
Index TermsвҖ” Quasi-cyclic low-density paritycheck (QC-LDPC) codes, protograph codes, cyclic liftings, girth, girth-optimal QC-LDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Q UASI-CYCLIC low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes are an important class of LDPC codes that lend themselves to simple implementations, and are thus favorable in practice. The cycle structure of an LDPC codeвҖҷs Tanner graph has long been known to affect its performance [1]. In particular, the length of the shortest cycles in the graph, girth, is identified as an important measure of the codeвҖҷs performance. There is a large body of work dedicated to the design of LDPC codes with large girth. A large portion of such literature is on construction of QC-LDPC codes with large girth, see, e.g., [2]вҖ“[6]. Related to this, lower bounds have been derived on the block length of QC-LDPC codes

One example is the array-based codes [9], which are cyclic liftings of degree n of fully-connected base graphs of size m Г— n, where n is a prime number. The array-based codes have girth 6 and block length n2, which is proved to be the shortest possible for girth 6 [7]. In this work, we focus on QC-LDPC codes that are cyclic liftings of fully-connected base graphs of size 3 Г— n. The parity check matrix of such a code is a 3 Г—n array of circulant permutation matrices (CPM). For the code to have girth 6, we show that the minimum block length is 20, 25, 42, 49, 72, 81, 110, 121, and 156, for n = 4, . . . , 12, respectively. For girth 8, the minimum block length is shown to be 36, 65, 108, 147, 200, and 270, for n = 4, . . . , 9, respectively. These results are proved using an efficient exhaustive search that demonstrates smaller block lengths (lifting degrees) cannot achieve the target girth for the given value of n. Using the proposed exhaustive search, we also find all the non-isomorphic codes with the minimum block length for each value of n and girth. Some of these codes have a larger minimum distance and/or dimension compared to others. Some of the codes with girth 6 that we find are arraybased codes. Some others are the codes that were previously found in the literature but were never proved to be optimal. Our results show that the largest minimum distance of the optimal QC codes with girth 6 discussed above is 6, while there are also some that have minimum distance 4. Some of these codes have the largest minimum distance or dimension compared to codes previously reported in the literature (with the same girth and block length). The largest minimum distance for girth-8 codes discussed above is 6, 10, 10, 10, 8, and 8, for n = 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9, respectively. For each n, there are also other codes with minimum block length and smaller minimum distances.
Given a base graph and a target girth, the problem of finding a cyclic lifting with minimum degree (or equivalently, a QC-LDPC code with minimum block length) with that girth is a hard problem, particularly if the size of the base graph and/or the value of girth is large. To the best of our knowledge, this work is the first attempt in rigorously solving this problem for non-trivial sizes of base graph and girth. To accomplish this, we devise an efficient exhaustive search of all possible cyclic liftings of a given degree. This corresponds to searching among all non-isomorphic QC-LDPC codes of the same block length. To make the search efficient, we reduce the size of the search space by pruning cases that are isomorphic to those previously examined in the search process. We call such a sequence a cycle if it is closed, that is, v0 = vn, and if it has distinct intermediate nodes. The length of the shortest cycle(s) in G is called the girth of G, and a cycle of length k is referred to as a k-cycle. A graph G(V, E) is called bipartite if it is possible to partition the set V into two subsets U and W such that every edge in E connects a node in U to a node in W . Tanner graphs of LDPC codes are bipartite graphs, in which U and W are respectively referred to as variable nodes (or left nodes) and check nodes (or right nodes). A regular Tanner graph is one in which all the nodes in U have the same degree du (known as left-degree), and all the nodes in W have the same degree dw (known as right-degree).

IV. EFFICIENT EXHAUSTIVE SEARCH OF GIRTH-OPTIMAL QC-LDPC CODES
In this section, our goal is to describe an efficient exhaustive search algorithm for finding the minimum lifting degree of a QC-LDPC code with girth 6 or 8, constructed based on a 3 Г— n fully-connected base matrix. Based on Corollary 1, we only need to search among exponent matrices PвҲ— that have an all-zero first row and an all-zero first column, and that their second row is sorted in increasing order of its elements. For an exhaustive search, this corresponds to testing
N 2(nвҲ’1)
exponent matrices, which still becomes prohibitively complex for large values of n and N. We thus use the results presented in the previous section to further reduce the complexity of the search by pruning the search space from the isomorphic classes for which at least one member has already been tested and failed to have the target girth. To start the search, we have two options. We can either use the existing lower bounds on the lifting degree for a given girth, such as those of [7], and work our way up, increasing the lifting degree by one at each step until we can find an exponent matrix that can provide us with the target girth, or alternatively, we can start from a lifting degree (smallest one) that has already been reported in the literature as achieving the target girth, and work our way down, decreasing the lifting degree at each step until the target girth cannot be achieved. In our search, we use the latter approach.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we devised an efficient exhaustive search to find all the non-isomorphic QC-LDPC codes of minimum block length for a given girth, constructed through cyclic liftings of fully connected base graphs. The search space was pruned by eliminating codes that were isomorphic to those previously tested in the search process. Using the proposed search techniques, we found codes that are superior to existing codes of the same girth and block length in terms of minimum distance or dimension. We were also able to find codes of girth 8, 10 and 12, that are shorter than codes previously constructed in the literature from the same base graphs. To the best of our knowledge, this work is the first to find all the non-isomorphic shortest QC-LDPC codes of a given girth for non-trivial values of girth and block length. Motivated by the search results for optimal codes of girth 6, we tightened the existing lower bound on the lifting degree of cyclic liftings of girth 6 resulting from fully connected 3 Г— n base graphs. We also constructed codes that achieved the bound for an arbitrary value of n вүҘ 4.
