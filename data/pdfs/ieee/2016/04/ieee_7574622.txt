System-Level Error Correction by Read-Disturb Error Model of 1Xnm TLC NAND Flash Memory for
Read-Intensive Enterprise Solid-State Drives (SSDs)

Yoshiaki Deguchi, Tsukasa Tokutomi and Ken Takeuchi Department of Electrical, Electronic, and Communication Engineering, Chuo University, Tokyo, Japan
Phone: +81-3-3817-7374 E-mail: deguchi@takeuchi-lab.org

Abstract— Read-disturb Modeled LDPC (RDM-LDPC) ECC is proposed. Conventional Advanced Error-Prediction LDPC (AEP-LDPC) [1] corrects data-retention errors of data-storagepurpose SSDs storing photos, movies, etc. but cannot correct read-disturb errors. For read-intensive computing-purpose enterprise SSDs, this paper analyzes the read-disturb errors, develops the error model of 1Xnm TLC NAND Flash memory and proposes ECC suitable for read-disturb errors. It is experimentally demonstrated that proposed RDM-LDPC extends the read cycle of SSDs by 5000-times.

I. INTRODUCTION As the NAND flash memory is scaled below to 1Xnm, the memory cell reliability is degraded and a stronger errorcorrecting code (ECC) is required [2]. Moreover, in the high capacity triple-level cell (TLC) NAND Flash memory, the narrower VTH margin increases the bit-error rate (BER) of memory cells by over 10-times compared with multi-level cell (MLC) NAND flash [3]. As a result, memory-cell errors of TLC NAND Flash cannot be corrected by traditional BCH ECC. In order to improve the reliability of TLC NAND Flash, low-density parity-check (LDPC) ECC has been proposed [4]. Although LDPC is slower, it has better error-correcting capability than BCH ECC [5]. The LDPC decoding time depends on the log-likelihood ratios (LLRs), which describe the probability of bit-errors among the VTH states. Conventional soft-decoding LDPC for TLC NAND flash estimates LLR with 49 reads (=7 reads/state x 7 states) [6], which is unacceptably long. More practical solutions, such as AEP-LDPC [1] and quick-LDPC [7], estimate LLR by referring to pre-recorded tables and decrease the read time by 2.3-times and 13-times, respectively. Since these approaches are optimized for data-retention errors, the infrequently-read data-storage is suitable. However, in case of the read-intensive enterprise SSDs, the read-disturb error is dominant and is uncorrectable by conventional ECC (Fig. 1). To correct readdisturb errors, this paper proposes RDM-LDPC. By modeling read-disturb errors of 1Xnm TLC NAND Flash, LLR is precisely estimated. The acceptable BER increases to 2.2times and the read cycle is extended by 5000 times.

II. SYSTEM-LEVEL ERROR CORRECTION

A. Model of Read-Disturb Bit-error Rate (BER) Fig. 2 shows error patterns of data-retention and read-
disturb errors. The data-retention error is caused by the stressinduced leakage current from the floating gate [8] or by charge de-trap from the tunnel oxide and decreases the memory-cell VTH, which is corrected by the conventional ECC [1, 7]. The read-disturb error is caused by the electric field-enhanced electron injection into the floating gate and increases the VTH [9]. In the read-intensive applications, both VTH decrease due to the data-retention and VTH increase due to the read-disturb occur simultaneously, which is not corrected by the conventional ECC. Fig. 3 shows the relation between LLR and bit-errors. This relation is represented as follows [1].

B. State-error rate (SER) calculation Figs. 7 and 8 describe a case study of the SER, which is
BER of each VTH state, calculation. Conventionally, dataretention SER is assumed that the VTH decreases by only one state during data-retention [1]. For example, if data is in the “B” state, the 2nd page BER is considered “Large” (10-1~104) because “C” can move to “B” during data-retention. When the data is “C”, the 2nd page BER is considered “Small” (<107) because the probability that “B” shifts to “C” is low. However, the same assumptions cannot be made for enterprise SSDs, because the read-disturb can cause “B” to “C” type errors. Thus, the conventional scheme underestimates the read-disturb BER. In the proposed RDM-LDPC, the VTH shift in both upward and downward directions (i.e., “B” to “C” and “C” to “B”) are also included. As a result, the read-disturb SER can be calculated precisely.

III. MEASUREMENT RESULT
Fig. 9 shows the measured ECC decoding results. In conventional AEP-LDPC, when the read cycles increase, errors cannot be corrected. On the other hand, in the proposed RDM-LDPC, over the wide range of read cycles and write/erase cycles, the read-disturb error is successfully corrected (green “Pass” region of Fig. 9 (a)). Compared with conventional AEP-LDPC, acceptable read cycles are improved by 5000-times (Fig. 9 (b)). Acceptable BER is extended by 2.2-times (Fig. 9 (c)).
IV. CONCLUSION
RDM-LDPC is proposed to correct read-disturb errors for read-intensive enterprise SSDs. By introducing the readdisturb error model of 1Xnm TLC NAND Flash memory, LLR is estimated precisely. As a result, acceptable read cycles are extended by 5000-times compared with conventional AEP-LDPC. Thus, proposed RDM-LDPC is suitable for the enterprise SSD where the read-disturb errors are dominant.
