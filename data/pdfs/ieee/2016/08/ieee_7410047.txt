An Area Time-Efficient Structure to Find the Approximate First Two Minima for
Min-Sum-Based LDPC Decoders
Xi Zhu and Guanghui He, Member, IEEE

Abstract—Aiming at reducing the hardware complexity of lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) decoders based on min-sum algorithms, this brief presents a general structure to find the minimum value and an approximate second minimum value. The proposed structure is proved to obtain the exact second minimum value with high probability in theory, and simulation results demonstrate that only a negligible degradation of error performance is introduced when adopting the proposed structure in LDPC decoders. Furthermore, mixed radix architecture is investigated to improve the area–time efficiency. Implemented in a SMIC 65-nm CMOS technology, the proposed architecture significantly improves the overall area–time efficiency compared with state-of-the-art works.
Index Terms—Approximate first two minimum values generator, low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoder, normalized minsum algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
O WING to excellent error correction and capacity approaching capability, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have been widely adopted in recent standards, such as 10GBSAE-T standard for 10-Gbit Ethernet (IEEE 802.3an), digital satellite broadcasting (DVB-S2), and Worldwide Interoperability for Microwave Access (WiMAX) standard for microwave communications (IEEE 802.15e).
Among numerous decoding algorithms, min-sum algorithm is widely used since it is hardware friendly [2]–[4]. In the design of LDPC decoders based on min-sum algorithms, one of the major problems is to determine the first two minima and the index of the minimum value among many inputs. Previous works [5]–[7] have presented several approaches to generate the first two minima. Compared with a sorting-based approach, tree-structure-based architecture minimizes the path delay at the expense of doubling the area consumption [5]. Recently, a modified tree-based architecture has been introduced to optimize the area–time efficiency with a sacrifice in path delay [6]. However, as the number of inputs grows, both area consumption and critical path delay become considerably high.

To reduce further the hardware complexity of LDPC decoders based on min-sum algorithm, approximations of the first two minima have been introduced in recent works. By partitioning the check matrix H in columns and replacing the global first two minima in each partition with local ones, the splitrow threshold algorithm [3] significantly reduces the hardware complexity. However, this rough approximation suffers from remarkable degradation of error performance [3]. Recent works [8]–[10] have presented several approaches to generate the first minimum value and an approximate second minimum. Moreover, it is shown that these approaches can significantly reduce the hardware complexity with negligible error rate performance degradation. However, these approaches cannot be adapted to all LDPC codes.
In this brief, we present a general structure to determine the minimum value and an approximate second minimum value of many inputs. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that the proposed structure can obtain the second minimum value with high probability. Simulation results show that replacing the second minimum value with an approximate one only introduces negligible degradation of error performance. Combined with a mixed radix architecture, the proposed structure improves the area–time efficiency significantly compared with previous works.

III. PROPOSED STRUCTURE TO FIND MINIMUM VALUE AND APPROXIMATE SECOND MINIMUM VALUE
Here, we present a general structure to generate the approximate second minimum value and the minimum value. The proposed structure is an extension of structures in previous works [9], [10]. Moreover, it is proved that the proposed structure can be applied to any LDPC code since the number of inputs has little effect on the distribution of the generated AMin2.

IV. HARDWARE ARCHITECTURE OF AMVG
Fig. 4 shows how the proposed AMVG is constructed to process n inputs. In Fig. 4, the inputs are divided as G = {G0, G1, . . . , Gm−1}, and each partition has g inputs. To find the minimum value of set G, mg-input minimum value finders (g-MVF) are employed in the first stage, and the output of the first stage is denoted M = {M0, M1, . . . , Mm−1}. At the second stage, a m-MVG is used to determine the exact first two minima of M, which is also the minimum value Min and the
approximate second minimum value AMin2. Note that, in the case m = 4, the probability that AMin2 =
Min2 is about 75% and the probability that AMin2 Min4 is over 98%. The proposed AMVG with a small m can obtain AMin2 that approaches Min2 with high probability. It has been shown that MVGs with a small number of inputs have

V. CONCLUSION
In this brief, a general structure has been presented to generate the minimum value and an approximate second minimum value. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that the approximate second minimum value approaches the exact one with high probability. When applying the proposed structure into the LDPC decoder based on min-sum algorithm, simulation results

show that only a negligible degradation of error performance is introduced. Furthermore, AMVG with mixed radix architecture is adopted to optimize the area–time efficiency. The proposed architecture implemented in a 65-nm SMIC technology demonstrates that it outperforms previous works in terms of area–time efficiency.
