Abstract: We proposed a method which combines finite geometries and optimized searching algorithm to construct quasi-cyclic low-density parity check (QC-LDPC) codes which have large girth for high speed optical communications. Compared with other method, our algorithm has lower complexity and faster speed.
OCIS codes: (060.2330)Fiber optics communications ; (060.2360) Fiber optics links and subsystems

1. Introduction

Recent years, transmission rate of single wavelength channel is getting higher and higher in optical communications, from 10G/40Gbps to 100Gbps, even to 1Tbps. Thus, the higher requirement is put forward to channel coding, which require high coding gain at the BER region lower than 10 for forward error correction (FEC) that easy to be implemented in high speed. QC-LDPC codes are recommended in ITU-T G.975.1, which do not only have the BER performance approaching the Shannon limit, but also have good structural property which makes it easy to implement.
Girth is an important parameter of QC-LDPC codes, which not only determines the lower bound of minimum Hamming distance of LDPC codes, but also effect the performance loss via the iterative decoding procedure. The larger the girth is, the greater the min( ) is and the less the performance loss is. Therefore, it is necessary to construct large girth QC-LDPC codes, which have better BER performance. However, there are some problems for constructing large girth QC-LDPC codes, especially in optical communication which needs high rate and large block length, such as complicated calculation and too much time consumption [1-2].
In order to solve this problem, our method is construct base code with no 4-cycle by use finite geometries and eliminate short cycle in base code by searching method which use local optimization, in this way, we accelerate the searching speed and shorten the time in total.

other word, we will stop when we find a desired length code, no longer consume time to optimize the code, because

as the increase of the cycle length, calculate the cost will be more complex and spend more time.

4. Conclusion
In this paper, we proposed a fast method for constructing large girth QC-LDPC codes. Because of low complexity, the optimized algorithm can find QC-LDPC codes with desired girth faster. Compared with Francis’s method, optimized searching method can reduce iterations over ten thousand times, and obtain large girth QC-LDPC codes that performance are very close to Francis’s.
