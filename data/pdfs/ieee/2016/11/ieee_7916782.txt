Abstract— Low density parity check (LDPC) codes are well known for their good error correction capability near to Shannon’s limit and also due to parallel decoding possibility. The proposed work deals with design and FPGA implementation of flexible LDPC encoder using general and RU methods. LDPC encoder architecture with RU method encodes with propagation delay of 1.256ns less compare to general method. The flexible encoder designed with propagation delay of 5.208ns with 2/5,3/5. rates of code. Flexible Encoder designed can be capable of encoding for any rate and any length of the parity check matrix.
Index Terms— Low-Density-Parity-Check(LDPC)cods, FPGA,Tanner Graph, codeword.
Introduction
As the advancements in the wireless communication over the past few decades always there is a demand for high and efficient data transmission and reception. The forward error detection and correction techniques will ensure the efficient and reliable high data transmission. The low density parity check (LDPC) codes are known as better error correction whose performance is proven to be very close to Shannon’s limit. LDPC codes are discovered by Gallagar in the year 1962[1]. In 1996 LDPC codes are rediscovered by Mackay and R Neal showing LDPC codes posses good coding gain and the implementation cost of codes are very low[2] compare to Gallagar codes. The efficient error correction capability made them to use in many modern communication standards like DVB-S2, satellite transmission ,WiMax,Wi-Fi standards IEEE 802.16e[3]etc. LDPC codes are represented by very sparse parity check matrix (H), the sparseness of the H matrix reduces the decoding complexity and also allows parallel decoding[8]. Encoding and decoding of LDPC codes can be directly can carried out from the parity check matrix (H) and its Tanner graph. In this proposed work we have implemented LDPC encoder with a general and RU method using Xilinx Vertex 5 FPGA board. The implementation results of encoder designed shows improvements in propagation delay with RU method at the cost of utilizing more resources of the device. The proposed work is organized as, Section I deals with LDPC codes construction, section II with Encoding of LDPC codes, section III FPGA implementation of Encoder and section IV with results and discussion followed by section V conclusion

I. LDPC CODE CONSTRUCTION LDPC codes belong to class of linear block codes. LDPC codes are constructed using the very sparse density parity-check matrix and graphically it is denoted by Tanner graph consisting of columns bits as variable nodes and row bits as check nodes as shown in figure1.

A LDPC parity-check matrix is considered as (wc;wr)regular if no of ones in each column is fixed as wc and number of ones in each row is fixed as wr of the H matrix. If the constraints for (wc;wr)-regular are relaxed, then an irregular code can be constructed. For an irregular parity-check matrix, the fraction of columns with weight i is denoted v and the fractions of rows with weight i by h. Collectively v and h is called the degree distribution of the code. Generally irregular codes perform better than regular codes, at the cost of hardware decoding
complexities [4][6]. The parity check matrix H is
constructed using random construction, semi-random construction and also structured construction. Here we have taken irregular and regular (10,3,5),(10,3,6) parity check matrices with random construction for flexible encoding. Parity check matrices generated are very sparse. Here the Mackay Neal construction method is used to construct LDPC codes by avoiding 4-cycle free code by giving input code length n, rate r, and column and row degree distributions v and h.

The flexible encoder is designed for different rates of parity check matrices. The flexible encoder main block consists of controller, memory and encoder core as shown in figure 4.

The encoder generates codeword finally combining information message s with parity bits obtained using equations p1and p2 and output the same. Flexibility of the encoder architecture is supported by counter present in the controller block. Depending on the different rates of the code counter is loaded initially and keep track of the same till the completion of encoding process.

V. CONCLUSIONS
We implemented multiple code rate flexible and efficient encoder using RU method. The propagation delay of encoder with RU method is less compare to general method. RU method encoding technique can be applied to any type of parity check matrices to get efficient encoder with less operational complexity and high speed.
