Abstract‚ÄĒA new type of spatially coupled low density parity check (SCLDPC) codes is introduced. These codes have exponentially increasing nodes as position goes to the middle of the chain. We call the codes spatially ‚ÄúMt. Fuji‚ÄĚ coupled LDPC (SFCLDPC) codes because the shape constructed by the number of nodes looks like ‚ÄúMt. Fuji‚ÄĚ. By this structure, the SFCLDPC codes have the rate loss O(őĪ‚ąíL), while the usual SCLDPC codes have O(L‚ąí1). In addition, the decoding processes for the SFCLDPC codes are accelerated exponentially fast as the iteration progresses. An infinite length analysis for the SFCLDPC codes with the density evolution and a finite length performance comparison between the SFCLDPC codes and the SCLDPC codes with computer simulations are made.

I. INTRODUCTION
Spatially coupled low density parity check (SCLDPC) codes are constructed as a coupled chain of L underlying low density parity check (LDPC) codes whose code lengths are M . It has been proven their belief propagation (BP) threshold coincides with the maximum a posteriori (MAP) threshold of underlying LDPC codes on the binary erasure channel (BEC) [1], and the general binary input memoryless output symmetric (BMS) channel [2] in the limit of large M and L. Further, their design rates simultaneously increase up to the design rate of underlying codes with order O(L‚ąí1). This phenomenon is called ‚Äúthreshold saturation.‚ÄĚ
However, there are some serious problems in practical situations. First, for large L, it is known that it takes many iterations to propagate reliable BP messages from the end of the chain to the middle. Second, if M is small then the propagation of reliable messages to the next position will stop with some probability. Therefore, we are not able to increase L infinitely and we must have the late loss of O(L‚ąí1).
To deal with these problems, various codes have been introduced in [3]‚Äď[6]. Some of these studies [3]‚Äď[5] have described the ways to connect short chains which have small L to accelerate the propagation or to improve the BP threshold. The approaches of these studies may be regarded as to improve decoding performance with some rate loss accepted. The approach of [6] is slightly different from the others. The study [6] has introduced the multi dimensional coupling for robustness against burst erasures. It can be said that the study [6] focused on the problem that the reliable message propagation stops.
In this paper, we address the above problems by introducing a new type of SCLDPC codes which have exponentially increasing nodes as position goes to the middle of the chain and whose rate loss to the underlying codes is O(őĪ‚ąíL), where őĪ is some rational number. This structure which increases the

For the first problem, this structure enables us to make L much smaller than the SCLDPC codes under the same rates. For the second problem, it is enabled to make M larger than the SCLDPC codes in the same code lengths and the same rates by the smaller L. Especially, the number of nodes at the middle of the chain where the reliable messages tend to stop can be made large.
Even though we can use small L with small rate loss O(őĪ‚ąíL), there is no use if the nodes which are exponentially increased with respect to the positions take exponentially many iterations to decode. However, an infinite length analysis with density evolution shows that the reliable message propagation from the end of the chain occurs in the similar manner as SCLDPC codes. Moreover, it is observed that the number of corrected nodes at each iteration is accelerated exponentially fast as iteration progresses. On the other hand, it is also observed that the BP thresholds slightly decrease.
In additive finite length simulations to compare the SFCLDPC codes and the SCLDPC codes, it is shown that the performance of the SFCLDPC codes is improved as compared with SCLDPC codes in both of the bit error rate and the number of iterations despite the decrease of the BP threshold of the SFCLDPC codes which is observed by the density evolution. Therefore, the effect of small L and large M is considered to be stronger than that of the decrease of the BP threshold especially in high rate and finite length situation.

VI. CONCLUSION
The SFCLDPC codes have been introduced. The onesided density evolution analysis has shown their one-sided BP threshold and the exponential acceleration of decoding. The finite length simulations to compare the SFCLDPC codes and the SCLDPC codes have shown the performance improvement of the SFCLDPC codes about both the number of iterations and the bit error rate.
