Abstract—Many researchers have analyzed the error correction capability of LDPC codes for iterative decoding algorithms. However, it is difficult to show the error correction capability of LDPC codes for iterative decoding algorithms based on belief propagation theoretically. Chilappagari et al. reported the error correction capability of column-weight-three regular LDPC codes under the Gallager A algorithm since the behavior of the iterative decoding algorithm is easily analyzed. In this paper, we investigate the error correction capability of irregular LDPC codes. We also show relations between the decoding failures and 2-cycles of irregular LDPC codes under the Gallager A algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are linear codes defined by sparse parity-check matrix [1]. Iterative decoding algorithms for LDPC codes perform message passing on the Tanner graph which is graph representation of paritycheck matrix. The error correction capability depends on the structural properties of the Tanner graph. The decoding failures are characterized by subgraphs of the Tanner graph, termed pseudo codeword, stopping set, trapping set. In [2] and [3], Chilappagari et al. have reported the error correction capability of column-weight-three LDPC codes. In the analysis, it focuses on the relation between correctable error patterns and fixed sets of variable nodes under the Gallager A algorithm. In [4], we have shown roles of calculating messages on the variable and check nodes in the Gallager A algorithm, and have shown a sufficient condition that fixed set of irregular LDPC codes under the Gallager A algorithm.
In this paper, we theoretically investigate the error correction capability of irregular LDPC codes under the Gallager A algorithm. We focus on the message passing on the degree2 variable nodes, and show relations between the decoding failures and 2-cycles. Furthermore, we compare the decoding failure probability given by the theoretical evaluation based on 2-cycle with the frame error rate of decoding simulations.

III. 2-CYCLE
The original value of a variable node is its value in the transmitted codeword. We say a variable node is good if its received value is equal to its original value and bad otherwise. A message is said to be correct if it is equal to the original value of the corresponding variable node and incorrect otherwise. When the channel and the decoder satisfy certain symmetry conditions, we can assume that the all zero codeword is transmitted. Hence, a bad variable node has received value 1 and an incorrect message has a value of 1.
Definition 1: The subgraph in Tanner graph is 2-cycle if the degree is all good variable nodes is 2 and the degree of bad variable nodes is greater than or equal to 2. Also, it is called (k, w) 2-cycle if the length of cycle is k and the number of bad variable nodes is w.
It characterizes to play role of variable nodes of degree 2 under the Gallager A algorithm. Note that this definition is different from 2-cycle referred in [5].

IV. 2-CYCLE AND DECODING FAILURE
In this section, we show that (k, 1) and (k, 2) 2-cycles are dominant failure set of variable nodes under Gallager A algorithm. From the definition of 2-cycle, the degree of good variable nodes in 2-cycle is 2. Thus, the estimate of the variable nodes is 1 if a bad variable node in 2-cycle receives at least one message 1. Furthermore, the estimate of the variable nodes is 1 if a good variable node in 2-cycle receives two messages 1.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have theoretically investigated the error correction capability of irregular LDPC codes under the Gallager A algorithm. we focused on the message passing on the degree-2 variable nodes, and showed relations between 2cycles and the decoding failures. Furthermore, we compared the frame error rate of decoding simulation with the theoretical evaluation based on 2-cycles.
