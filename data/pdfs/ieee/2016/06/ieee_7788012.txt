Construction of Irregular QC LDPC Codes with low error floor for High Speed Optical Communications

Dongdong Wang1, Liqian Wang1, Xue Chen1,*, Aimei Fei2, Chi Chen1, Chen Ju1, Zhirong Wang1, Huitao Wang2
1State Key Lab of Information Photonics and Optical Communications (Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications), P.O. Box 128, #10

Abstract: We propose a construction method of irregular QC LDPC codes for high speed optical
communications, by which the constructed codes not only can achieve high coding gain only through a few iterations but also do not exhibit error floor above the BER of 10-10.
OCIS codes: (060.2330) Fiber optics communications; (060.2360) Fiber optics links and subsystems

1. Introduction
Driven by explosive growth of Internet services, transmission rate in a single wavelength channel has raised from 10G/40Gbps to 100Gbps, and is still developing rapidly towards 400Gbps to 1Tbps. The higher the transmission rate, the higher the bit error rate (BER) requirement. Large girth regular QC LDPC codes with 20% overhead have been verified with high NCG 10.95dB and 11.3dB at BER of 10-12 [1] and 10-15 [2], respectively, which means that girth ›É is an important factor influencing the performance of QC LDPC codes. However, I-TUT G.975.1 standard [3] reveals that a high coding gain with no error floor above BER of 10-16 can also be achieved by smaller ›É ‡µå Õ∏ QC LDPC codes with column weight ›Ä‡Ø© ‡µå Õπ, which means large ›Ä‡Ø© can lead to a good performance at low BER region but may lead to performance loss at high BER region. In addition, irregular LDPC codes can achieve better BER performance than regular LDPC codes, which means the distribution ·àºﬂ©‡Øú·àΩ of column weights ·àº›Ä‡Ø©‡Øú ·àΩ also has a great influence on the performance of LDPC codes, but usually irregular codes do not have structural characteristics that limits its application to high speed systems.
Based on the above analysis, three parameters, including girth ›É , column weight ›Ä‡Ø© and column weights distribution ·àºﬂ©‡Øú·àΩ , are the main factors affecting the QC LDPC code performance. Then we propose a construction method based on block puncturing, by which can not only simultaneously optimize the above three parameters, but also can maintain the quasi-cyclic structural characteristics. The irregular QC LDPC codes constructed by this method do not exhibit error floor above BER of 10-10, and only through a few iterations can achieve high coding gain.

4. Conclusion

We propose a construction method of irregular QC LDPC codes for high speed optical communications, through the
optimized design of the parameters affecting the code performance, the constructed codes not only can achieve high coding gain only through a few iterations but also do not exhibit error floor above the BER of 10-10.
