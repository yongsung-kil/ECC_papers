Time-Invariant Quasi-Cyclic Spatially Coupled LDPC Codes Based on Packings
Mu Zhang, Zulin Wang, Member, IEEE, Qin Huang, Senior Member, IEEE, and Shafei Wang

Abstract— This paper presents two packings derived from balanced incomplete block designs to construct quasi-cyclic spatially coupled LDPC convolutional codes (SC-LDPC-CCs). The construction gives time-invariant codes, since the subblocks corresponding to each time instant of the parity-check matrix are identical. Moreover, it provides flexible design rates and constraint lengths. Simulation results show that the proposed packing-based SC-LDPC-CCs outperform the existing time-invariant codes and perform closely to the time-varying protograph-based codes.
Index Terms— BIBD, CPM, packing, spatially coupled LDPC, time-invariant.
I. INTRODUCTION
S PATIALLY coupled LDPC convolutional codes (SC-LDPC-CCs) [1] have been well known for their good decoding thresholds [2]–[8]. They have great potential for applications such as continuous data transmission [9] and block data transmission in frames of arbitrary size [10], [11].
The convolutional-like spatially coupled construction was first discussed in [12] in 1980s, but not involved in LDPC form. After the rediscovery of LDPC block codes (LDPC-BCs) [13]–[15], it is proposed in [1] to construct the convolutional counterparts of LDPC-BCs by unwrapping their parity-check matrices. Considerable convolutional gain is observed by the unwrapping operation. Later, the above two approaches were unified in [16] with the help of the so-called graph covers. In [17], a voltage graph-based construction was proposed to search for LDPC-BCs and LDPC-CCs with large girths and minimum distances. Another graph-based construction of SC-LDPC-CCs was introduced in [18] by performing graph lifting operations on a protograph. It was proved that the protograph-based codes with large graph lifting have large minimum distances and trapping sets, and good performance [19].

In general, a code without structure is problematic in both encoding and decoding implementations. For LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) structure is an important structure to facilitate their hardware implementations [20]. It is shown in [21] that QC LDPC-BCs based on circulant matrices can be used to construct QC LDPC-CCs with superior performance. Another class of QC LDPC-CC based on arrays was discussed in [22]. Later, it was extended in [23] to optimize the code performance in error floor region. In [24], a class of periodical timevarying SC-LDPC-CCs based on repeated-accumulate codes was proposed to reduce the memory requirement. Recently, a repetition and masking scheme [25] was introduced to obtain SC-LDPC-CCs from QC LDPC-BCs.
Besides QC structure, time-invariant structure is another important structure to greatly simplify hardware implementation. However, there are not many works addressing time-invariant SC-LDPC-CCs [21], [26]–[28]. In [26], timeinvariant SC-LDPC-CCs with large girth were constructed by cycle termination. In [28], a class of time-invariant SC-LDPC-CCs was proposed based on arrays. However, these existing time-invariant SC-LDPC-CC constructions suffer significant performance degradation compared with their timevarying counterparts.
Combinatorics is a powerful tool to construct structured codes. Combinatorial designs, such as Euclidian geometries, partial geometries, block designs, etc, have been widely used to construct structured LDPC-BCs [29]– [36]. For instance, some classes of LDPC-BCs based on balanced incomplete block designs (BIBDs) [37] constructed by Bose [38] perform fairly well as their random counterparts [32], [33]. A block design is an arrangement of objects into blocks. A BIBD requires any two objects arranged in λ blocks. If λ = 1, the incidence matrix of such BIBD satisfies the row-column (RC) constraint [20] of the parity-check matrix of an LDPC code. Moreover, the incidence matrix of BIBD-LDPC-BC can be highly structured, e.g., symmetric and QC. Thus, it is preferred for simple encoding and decoding.
In light of the advantages of BIBD-LDPC-BCs, this paper proposes to construct time-invariant SC-LDPC-CCs from combinatorial designs. However, the constraint of BIBD that every two objects appear in some block is too stringent for SC-LDPC-CCs. Thus, we extend the Bose-BIBDs and derive two types of packings [37] with λ = 1 to construct SC-LDPC-CCs. Packings only require any two objects to arrange in no more than λ blocks. Moreover, the proposed packings are constructed such that only consecutive base blocks have elements in common. Thus, their incidence matrices can be convolutional like and satisfy the RC-constraint. II. BACKGROUND
A. SC-LDPC-CC
An SC-LDPC-CC Csc is defined as the null space of a steplike parity-check matrix consisting of sub-blocks as shown in (1), as shown at the top of this page, [18]. At the time instant t, H has a sub-block [H0(t)H1(t +1) . . . Hms (t +ms)]T. If [H0(t)H1(t + 1) . . . Hms (t + ms)]T is independent of t, Csc is said to be time-invariant. In this case, we denote [H0(t)H1(t + 1) . . . Hms (t + ms)]T by [H0 H1 . . . Hms ]T. Otherwise, it is time-varying. For all time instants, [H0(t)H1(t + 1) . . . Hms (t + ms)]T should be a sparse matrix. They are loosely connected by the overlapped check nodes (CNs) such that message can be passed between the sub-blocks. Since the connection is not global, message passing is locally limited if the number of iterations is finite. Thus, the decoding of SC-LDPC-CCs can be performed in a sliding window [1], [42]. If all Hi (t)’s in (1) have the same size a × c, where 0 ≤ i ≤ ms, the code Csc has design rate R = 1 − a/c, coupling width ms, and constraint length vs = (ms + 1)c. We say that Csc is (γ , ρ)-regular if all submatices [H0(t)H1(t + 1) . . . Hms (t + ms)]T’s for t ≥ 0 have constant column weight γ , and [Hms ( j )Hms−1( j ) . . . H0( j )]’s for j ≥ ms have constant row weight ρ.

A BIBD can also be described by its incidence matrix. The incidence matrix H of a BIBD B is a binary matrix in which, each row corresponds to an object in X, each column corresponds to a block in B, and each 1 indicates that the corresponding object is contained in the corresponding block. If a BIBD satisfies λ = 1, any two rows of its incidence matrix H have exactly one position where they both have nonzero elements. In other words, H satisfies the rowcolumn (RC) constraint, i.e., there is no cycle of length 4 in the Tanner graph of H. Thus, the incidence matrices of BoseBIBDs define the parity-check matrices of LDPC-BCs [32].

C. Definition of Packing
Suppose that X is a set of ν objects. Take a collection B of n blocks (subsets) with γ distinct objects of X. Then, (X, B) is a p-(ν, γ , λ) packing if every p-subset of objects in X occurs in at most λ blocks in B [37]. It follows from the definition of BIBD and packing that every BIBD is a packing, but not vice verse. Thus, BIBD can be considered as a special case of packing. It is shown in [37] that every p-(ν, γ , λ) packing with n blocks is equivalent to a binary nonlinear code of length ν, size n, and constant weight γ . To the best of our knowledge, this design has never been considered to construct LDPC codes.

III. PACKINGS DERIVED FROM BOSE-BIBDS
As described in the above section, a BIBD requires every two rows of its incidence matrix H to have at least one position where they both have nonzero elements. In other words, H is a globally connected matrix, i.e., any two VNs in the whole Tanner graph of H may have CNs in common. However, the parity-check matrix of an SC-LDPC-CC is locally connected as shown in (1), i.e., only two VNs within its constraint length may have CNs in common. In order to obtain SC-LDPC-CC, we need to break the global connections and preserve only the local ones in H. Thus, we use packing instead of BIBD to construct SC-LDPC-CCs.

IV. SC-LDPC-CCs BASED ON PACKINGS
In this section, we demonstrate that the two types of packings can be used to construct good SC-LDPC-CCs with flexible design rates and constraint lengths. In all the examples, packing-based SC-LDPC-CCs (P-SC-LDPC-CCs) are regular, QC and time-invariant. They have girth 6 computed by the cycle counting algorithm in [43]. This section also presents comparison of the proposed codes and existing SC-LDPC-CCs.
The simulations are performed under the AWGN channel with the BPSK modulation. All SC-LDPC-CCs are unterminated and decoded by the pipeline decoding scheme based on the sum-product algorithm with a windowed behaviour for LDPC-CCs in [1]. The default maximum number of iterations is set to 100. The window size is the product of the maximum number of iterations and the constraint length of each code. In each iteration, a whole block of bits corresponding to one time instant of the parity-check matrix are shifted in/out of the window. Since the codes are not terminated, their decoding thresholds are lower bounded by the LDPC-BCs with the same row/column degrees [44]. To evaluate the code performance, the decoding thresholds of the related LDPC-BCs are given as a benchmark as in [28].

C. Comparison and Discussion
In this subsection, we will first compare the P-SC-LDPCCCs with existing SC-LDPC-CCs, including time-invariant [21], [27], [28] ones and time-varying ones [18]. Simulation results show that the proposed codes outperform the existing time-invariant codes and perform closely to the timevarying protograph-based codes. Then we will discuss the impact of convolutional base matrices [18] on the performance of SC-LDPC-CCs. Different code performance is observed for different convolutional base matrices. Last, we will present the construction of time-invariant SC-LDPC-CCs from BIBDs by decomposition and unwrapping. It shows that these SC-LDPC-CCs can be seen as a subclass of the proposed P-SC-LDPC-CCs.

V. CONCLUSION AND REMARKS
In this paper, we designed two types of packings for the construction of time-invariant CPM-QC SC-LDPC-CCs. The proposed codes can have very good performance in both waterfall and error floor region for various design rates and constraint lengths. As far as the authors have investigated, P-SC-LDPC-CCs outperform existing time-invariant SC-LDPC-CCs, either structured or randomly constructed and perform closely to the time-varying protograph-based ones. What’s more, we found that convolutional base matrices of packings produce SC-LDPC-CCs with good performance.
