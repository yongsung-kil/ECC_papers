An Improved Message Passing Schedule of BP-Based Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes

Jingru Dong, Hang Yin, Sijia Wang
Engineering Center of Digital Audio and Video Communication University of China Beijing, China
dongjingru@cuc.edu.cn, yinhang@cuc.edu.cn, wangsijia@cuc.edu.cn

AbstractвЂ”In this paper, we propose an optimized schedule called IRMP (Improved Row Message Passing) schedule, based on analysis of the existing message passing schedules. This type of schedule has changed the message updating order between variable and check nodes. In accordance with this schedule, an improved BP-Based decoding algorithm is proposed. Simulations show that, this algorithm has beaten the performance of BPBased algorithms available in terms of error rate and number of iteration.
Index TermsвЂ”LDPC codes; Message Passing Schedule; BPBased; Iterative Decoding
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC (Low Density Parity Check Codes), first discovered by Gallager in 1962, is a class of codes which can achieve very good performance [1]. However, it was not highly valued until 1990s, when Mackay and Neal rediscovered and proved that it has near Shannon limit properties, due to hardware confinements [2]. BP (belief propagation) decoding algorithm, which was also brought forward in the Gallager thesis, proved to be a powerful tool for iterative decoding, and yet its nonlinear computation in the iteration goes against its hardware implementation. In 1999, Fossorier developed a simplified version, called BP-Based decoding algorithm, in which he used comparison operations to approximate the standard BP decoding procedure, and thereby achieved significant reduction of complexity [3]caused by BP; but this has caused degradation in decoding performance. Thus, to improve its performance, J.Chen came up with two improved versions of BP-Based decoding algorithms, namely the Normalized BP-based and the Offset BP-based algorithms [4], and since then have been widely employed because of their performance-complexity tradeoff [5]. Apart from decoding complexity, another drawback for LDPC is that it requires too many times of iteration. P.Radosavljevic and his coworkers brought forward Row Message Passing Schedule (RMP) [6], those that also applied similar serial scheduling scheme proposed at the same time are Layered BP decoding algorithm [7], Shuffled BP decoding algorithm, and etc. [8]. RMP schedule is in fact exploiting a 'turbo-scheduling', and compared to SMP (Standard Message Passing) schedule, it has accelerated convergence speed and iteration number is down by 50 percent. In this paper, we improve on RMP schedule by introducing a new message exchanging methodology, which

III. OPTIMIZED DECODING ALGORITHM BASED ON MESSAGE PASSING SCHEDULE

A. RMP Scheduling Algorithm
At each iteration of the standard BP algorithm, all the check-to-variable messages and variable-to-check messages are processed and transmitted simultaneously, and variablenode messages from previous iteration are used in updating check-node messages. This message scheduling scheme is called SMP schedule. The major drawback of the LDPC codes versus the turbo-codes is their comparative low convergence speed: 25-30 iterations vs. 8-10 iterations for turbo codes. At each iteration of the RMP algorithm, check-node messages updated during the current decoding iteration are immediately utilized within the same iteration for computation of new variable-node messages.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have close scrutiny at various message passing schedules in LDPC decoding algorithms, such as SMP and RMP, and come up with an improved IRMP schedule. This schedule using dual forward message passing in one row, has further optimized the message updating method in iteration, and thereby improved confidence degree of checknodes in the iterative procedure. In accordance with this schedule, an improved BP-Based decoding algorithm is proposed. Simulations and theoretical analysis show that, compared to the original modified BP-Based decoding algorithm with RMP schedule, when maximum number of iterations is no more than 10 , the average number of iterations is reduced by 25 percent, and the performance results for this schedule is improved by more than 0.1dB. Compared to standard BP decoding algorithm, the performance of the algorithm mentioned in this paper is improved by more than 0.4dB, and the average number of iterations is reduced by

approximately 50 percent. When the maximum number of iteration is restricted, this modified algorithm can significantly lower the average number of iteration, and in the meantime, obtain better BER performance.
