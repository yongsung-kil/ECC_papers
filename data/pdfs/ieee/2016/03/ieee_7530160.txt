Abstract— A hybrid sub-optimal technique has been proposed in this paper for decoding the LDPC codes, which delivers a better performance. The two decoding algorithms namely, the Sum Product Algorithm (SPA) and One Step Majority Logic Decoding Algorithm (OSMLGD) are pooled together to accomplish decoding of LDPC codes. Also the two algorithms are implemented exclusively in order to compare the performance. The error correction capability is compared by plotting the BitError Rate v/s SNR graph and the computational complexity is also compared.
Keywords— Low Density Parity Check Codes; One Step Majority Logic Decoding; Sum Product Algorithm
I. INTRODUCTION
The key idea of Forward Error Correction Code is to augment redundant data and improve the error detection and correction capability. Channel coding helps to enhance the performance of a communication system by facilitating it to withstand the channel impairments. The error correction codes help to decrease the probability of error, though there will be an increase in bandwidth. The Low Density Parity check codes are linear error correction codewords whose parity check matrix are sparse, consisting of fewer number of nonzero entities. LDPC codes are capacity approaching codes and are decoded iteratively. Iterative decoding fundamentally involves message passing between the edges of Tanner graph, which is the graphical representation of LDPC code [1] and [2].
The rest of the paper is systematized as follows: a brief note on encoding has been given on section II. Section III gives an insight on the decoding algorithms namely, SPA, OSMLGD and their hybrid. The results are discussed in the IV Section and finally the article is concluded in Section V.

Thus encoding can be done in LDPC codes by converting the parity check matrix into an approximate lower triangular form by performing row and column permutations only. III. LDPC DECODING
Decoding can be performed in LDPC codes iteratively. The various types of decoding algorithms employed in LDPC codes include Soft decision decoding (SDD), Hard decision decoding (HDD) and Reliability based decoding. In SDD, the decoder will process the value of symbols received as real number and hence the information catered by the channel will be retained. They are basically iterative in nature and involves belief propagation. Sum Product Algorithm is a SDD algorithm that has a higher performance, though it involves a higher decoding complexity. In Hard Decision Decoding, the received symbol is quantized and mapped into elements in same set of transmitted symbol before processing and thus loses some channel information. Hard Decision Decoding is quite simple in terms of complexity, but has a poorer performance. One Step Majority Logic Decoding is a HDD algorithm. The Reliability based decoding provides a better performance by processing quantized symbols as in HDD along with reliability value extracted from unquantized symbol. Thus they have slight increase in the decoding complexity.

Else the reliability value is given to the OSMLGD algorithm and further decoding is carried out. V. CONCLUSION
The LDPC codes are the set of capacity approaching linear error correction codes. They are used in applications that require high reliability and efficient data transmission, which includes DVB S2, IEEE 802.16: WiMAX and IEEE 802.11 : Wi-Fi to name a few. In this paper the decoding algorithm for LDPC namely SPA, OSMLGD and a SPA – OSMLGD Hybrid are implemented and compared for the decoding performance and computational complexity. The SPA provides a better performance though it involves very high computational complexity, while the OSMLGD provides a poorer performance with lesser complexity. Further, it can be remarked that the SPA – OSMLGD Hybrid delivers a better performance with relatively lesser computational complexity.
