Anytime Characteristics of Protograph-Based LDPC Convolutional Codes
Nan Zhang, Md. Noor-A-Rahim, Badri N. Vellambi, Senior Member, IEEE, and Khoa D. Nguyen, Member, IEEE

Abstract— Anytime transmission has been shown to be an effective means of stabilizing an unstable system over noisy channels. A crucial issue in implementing anytime transmission strategies is the design of anytime codes that offer good finite-length performance and asymptotic anytime properties. This paper introduces an efficient anytime code derived from protograph-based low-density parity-check convolutional codes known as extended S-LDPCC (XS-LDPCC) codes. The asymptotic anytime properties of XS-LDPCC codes over both the binary erasure channel (BEC) and the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise (BIAWGN) channel are demonstrated through density evolution. By means of certain observations verified by simulations, we provide accurate estimates of the delay exponents of XS-LDPCC codes over the BEC and the BIAWGN channel. Through simulations, the finite-length performance of XS-LDPCC codes and the effects of different code design parameters are quantified.
Index Terms— Anytime codes, protograph-based codes, LDPC convolutional codes, density evolution, delay exponent.
I. INTRODUCTION
A NYTIME coding is an indispensable component in the design of communication aspects of systems tracking and controlling unstable processes over a noisy channel [1]–[4]. In anytime coding, information symbols are available at the encoder in real time, and the encoder encodes the symbols causally. At each instant, the decoder recovers estimates of the current as well as previously received symbols. The key requirement in anytime coding is that the probability of decoding error should decay exponentially with decoding delay [1].
A significant portion of the anytime literature focuses on random (non-linear) tree codes [4]–[7]. The existence of linear codes with causal encoding that meet anytime requirements over the binary erasure channel (BEC) was proven

in [8] and [9]. However, the achievable codes therein are not sufficiently structured to be deemed practical. Recently, several practical anytime code designs based on low-density paritycheck convolutional (LDPCC) codes have been proposed by Dössel et al. [10], Grosjean et al. [11], Zhang et al. [12], and Noor-A-Rahim et al. [13].
Systematic low-density parity-check convolutional (S-LDPCC) codes are analytically tractable and have low encoding complexity [10], [11]. In the asymptotic regime, anytime reliability is achieved by increasing the check- and variable-node degrees with time. However, these codes exhibit error floors and (symbol) index dependent performance in the finite block-length regime. These issues are partially addressed by detecting and feeding back problematic error patterns to the transmitter. The anytime reliability of the S-LDPCC codes over the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise (BIAWGN) channel was later characterized in [14].
Another class of practical anytime codes based on spatially-coupled LDPC (SC-LDPC) codes was proposed in [12]. Unlike the S-LDPCC codes considered in [10] and [11], the SC-LDPC codes proposed in [12] have fixed check- and variable-node degrees. Anytime characteristics of SC-LDPC codes is obtained by employing an exponential coupling between the check and variable nodes instead of the uniform coupling in the original formulation of SC-LDPC codes [15]. Specifically, in anytime SC-LDPC codes, the possibility of a connection between a check and a variable node decays exponentially with the distance (i.e., difference between indices) between the nodes. In this way, two things can be simultaneously achieved: (a) reliable performance at small delays due to the presence of connections between adjacent nodes, and (b) improved performance at large delays due to presence of connections between distant nodes. A variant of anytime SC-LDPC codes, known as protographbased repeat-accumulate (P-RA) code, was studied in [13], and it was noted that this variant offers better finite-length performance and possesses a simpler code structure compared to SC-LDPC codes.
While each approach has its own advantages, both S-LDPCC and anytime SC-LDPC codes are subject to the tradeoff among finite-delay performance, delay-exponent and error-floor in the finite block-length regime. Consequently, the code parameters need to be fine-tuned for specific applications. This paper concentrates on an extension of S-LDPCC codes that we refer to as XS-LDPCC (Extended S-LDPCC codes), which were initially termed as P-LDPCC codes in [16].

The main design parameter of S-LDPCC codes controls the rate at which check and variable node degrees grow with time, and is limited to natural numbers. This may restrict the practical applicability of S-LDPCC codes, especially in short block-length transmission at rates close to channel capacity. In this paper, we extend the class of S-LDPCC code by introducing an additional code parameter. A major difference between S-LDPCC codes of [10] and Dossel.2014 and XS-LDPCC codes is that the latter are based on protographs with randomized edge construction. This modification combines the advantages of random construction of SC-LDPC codes and the structure of S-LDPCC codes, leading to improved performance compared to existing codes. The complexity of implementing the proposed code is comparable to that of S-LDPCC codes, however its design and analysis are not trivial. XS-LDPCC codes are also different from those in [12] because the variable- and check-node degrees in the XS-LDPCC codes increase linearly with time. Due to this structural difference, the delay-exponent of XS-LDPCC codes is limited by the rate at which variable node degree increases with delay, and the channel quality (similar to the codes in [10] and [11]), whereas the corresponding delay-exponent of the codes in [12] is limited by the variable node degree and the exponential coupling parameter. The overall contributions of the paper are summarized as follows.
• We propose a high performance anytime reliable code by introducing controlled randomness in the S-LDPCC anytime code construction. Through density evolution, we establish the anytime properties of XS-LDPCC codes over the BEC and the BIAWGN channel.
• Using observations substantiated by simulations of density evolution equations, we derive analytical estimates of the delay exponents of XS-LDPCC codes over the BEC and the BIAWGN channel; these analytical estimates corroborate well with those obtained from simulations.
• We show by simulations over the BEC and the BIAWGN channel that finite-length XS-LDPCC codes outperform existing anytime codes proposed in [11] and [12].
• We propose a novel feedback scheme for the BIAWGN channel that effectively alleviates the error floor and index-dependent behavior of XS-LDPCC codes.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section II describes the mathematical model and the requirements for anytime transmission. A brief description of the S-LDPCC anytime code is given in Section III. Section IV presents the XS-LDPCC code construction. Density evolution analysis, simulations of the performance of these codes, and the estimation of the achievable delay exponents over the BEC and the BIAWGN channel are given in Sections V and VI, resp. Finally, concluding remarks are given in Section VII.

VI. THE PERFORMANCE OF XS-LDPCC OVER THE BIAWGN CHANNEL
This section consists of four parts. The first part focuses on the asymptotic anytime property of XS-LDPCC codes over the BIAWGN channel. Density evolution is employed to demonstrate the asymptotic anytime characteristics of XS-LDPCC codes over the BIAWGN channel. Gaussian approximation (see [19]) is employed to simplify the asymptotic analysis, and to approximate the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) messages as Gaussian random variables whose means are twice their variances. With this approximation, we only need to track the mean of the LLRs during the density evolution iterations.
The second part of this section investigates the performance of finite-length XS-LDPCC codes. The third part presents estimates of the delay exponent achieved by XS-LDPCC codes over the BIAWGN channel using density evolution analysis as well as simulations, and finally, we present a feedback mechanism that alleviates the index-dependent performance and error floor issues associated with XS-LDPCC codes.

D. Alleviating Index-Dependency in XS-LDPCC Codes
Even though finite-length XS-LDPCC codes offer attractive features such as log-linear fall in bit-error rates (until error floor is attained), the performance of different symbols is in fact different. More generally, in finite-length codes, the higher the symbol index, the larger the delay it takes to attain loglinear fall in bit-error probability. As an illustration, refer to Fig. 15 where the error floor for bit-error rate of different blocks of a code from an XS-LDPCC ensemble with x = 1 and w = 2 is different. This phenomenon is a consequence of the constant increase of the check-node degree with decoding delay. Note that this issue is by no means specific to only the proposed codes. A similar trend was noticed in other anytime codes proposed in literature, e.g., see [11]. However, this issue has been addressed for the BEC using noiseless feedback from the decoder [11]. Here, we address this issue via a novel decoder feedback strategy.
The presence of either channel or decoder feedback allows for improved performance guarantees in both traditional as well anytime coding frameworks. The improvement in decoder error probability and delay exponent of anytime coding in the presence of noiseless channel feedback has been explored in detail over (i) a composite channel comprising of AWGN and erasure channels; (ii) Markov channels, and (iii) the GilbertEliot channel [20]–[23]. However, there is limited work on the benefits of decoder feedback on practical anytime coding.
In [11] and [24], Grosjean et al. detailed a decoder feedback strategy to improve error floor, expand the log-linear portion of the decoder erasure probability vs delay curve, and mitigate the index-dependency issue in the case of erasure channels. In their strategy, the decoder requests retransmission of the bit with the least index that is not decodable upon termination provided the decoding process terminates at a stopping set whose size is larger than a pre-determined threshold. VII. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have proposed a novel protographbased LDPCC code for anytime point-to-point transmission setup. Through density evolution technique, we have shown that this code asymptotically possesses anytime characteristics over the BEC and the BIAWGN channel. Finite-length XS-LDPCC codes provide a more flexible way to balance anytime property with desired decoding performance, i.e., when compared with existing anytime codes, XS-LDPCC codes offer better trade-offs between reliable anytime decoding and finite-length decoding erasure/error probability.
