Partially-Stopped Probabilistic Min-Sum Algorithm for LDPC Decoding

Chen-Pei Song1, Cheng-Hung Lin1,2, and Shu-Yen Lin1,2
1. Department of Electrical Engineering, 2. Innovation Center for Big Data and Digital Convergence Yuan Ze University
Jungli, Taiwan 32003, R.O.C.

Abstract—Low-density parity check (LDPC) codes have been widely applied to many storage devices because of their good error correcting performance. Howe ver, power consumption and area cost are still the major issues for their hardware implementations. Therefore, power and area reduction techniques are necessary for the implementation of check node unit (CNU) of LDPC decoder. In this paper, a novel partially stopped criterion is proposed for the normalized probabilistic min-sum algorithm to further reduce the power consumption of the check node units. The simulation result shows the proposed CNU achieves 13% power reduction with a 1.8% area overhead and an ignorable BER degradation.
Keywords—low-density parity-check code; low power design;
I. INT RODUCT ION
Flash memories have been widely used in smart house applications. Because low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have excellent error correction performance, flash memories apply the LDPC code as their error correction code in order to increase data reliability. The LDPC codes are constructed by a sparse matrix H. In H, each row is represented as check node (CN) and each column is represented as variable node (VN). The CNs and VNs are connected by 1’s elements in H to exchange the belief messages. The most popular algorithm for exchanging the belief messages between CNs and VNs is minimu m-summation algorith m (MSA) [2] that is known for the hardware efficiency of LDPC decoder.
Because the large power consumption and high area cost of the CN operation, many researches focus on decreasing the hardware complexity of MSA such as single-minimu m algorithm (SMA) [3] and split-row decoding [4]. The SMA is based on the MSA by only searching the minimu m value of a CN. The second min imu m value of the CN is replaced by adding an offset value to the minimu m value. The split-row decoding splits the searching minimu m tree (SMT) of MSA into two parts and the two SMTs search their minimu m values individually. To decrease more power in the CNs, the truthful check node (TTCN) [5] based on split-row decoding was proposed to reduce the CN operation by predicting the minimu m location. Although the SMA, split-row decoding, and TTCN can efficiently reduce the hardware and power of CN operation, the correcting performance is seriously degraded [6]. Therefore, the normalized probabilistic min-sum algorithm (NPMSA) was proposed [6] to reduce hardware and power of CN unit (CNU) with good correcting performance.

To further reduce power consumption with a low hardware cost, a novel partially stopped criterion based on NPMSA [6] is proposed in this paper. The proposed partially-stopped probabilistic min-sum algorith m (PS-PMSA) can discard unnecessary calculations in CNUs with ignorable error correction degradation and a less area overhead. In addition, the proposed PS-PMSA works well under the well-known parity check equation (PCE), one of efficient early termination schemes that terminate the convergent iterations.
II. PROPOSED PART IALLY ST OPPED PROBABILIST IC MIN-
SUM ALGORIT HM
The NPMSA was proposed for reducing the complexity of CNU and its CNU is similar to the CNU of SMA. The CNU of NPMSA uses the 2-to-1 comparators at front stages and 2-to-2 comparators at the final stage. Different to the SMA, the NPMSA has the definitely correct min imu m value and the possibly correct second minimu m value. To provide more data reliability, an approximate algorith m [7] like NPM SA proposed a CNU that uses N-to-2 comparator at the final stage with N larger than 2. According to number of input at the final stage comparator, the SMT can be divided into N groups.
It was revealed in [5] that the minimu m value of CNU would locate at a certain VN on successive iterations. Thus the TTCN checks the location of min imu m value and then only calculates the SMT where the minimu m value appeared in the previous iterations. However, the TTCN might take a wrong minimu m value if the location of minimu m value changes and make the unreliable data in the following iterations . In this paper, the proposed PS-PMSA detects the maximu m output of SMTs and then discards the corresponding unnecessary calculations of the SMT in the following iterations . A demonstration of CNU of the PS-NPMSA is shown in Fig. 1. At the fully-active iteration, the four SMTs in the CNU find four min imu m values and send them to the 4-to-2 co mparator to find out the exact minimu m and second min imu m values. Currently, the 4-to-2 co mparator detects the largest minimu m value among the SMTs and then stores the index. At the partially-stopped iteration, the CNU stops the tagged SMT based on the stored index because this SMT become less possible to contain the min imu m value. On ly several mu ltiplexers are used to find the tagged SMT because the largest minimu m value is easily captured by the of the 4-to-2 comparator when it co mpares each value fro m SMTs. Thus the PS-PMSA can be implemented with a small area overhead.

III. EXPERIMENT AL RESULT S
The setting of stopping iteration is required to apply the proposed algorithm. Based on massive simu lations, the 1st, 3rd, and 5th iterations are set as the fully-active iterations and others are partially-stopped iterations in order to achieve good correcting performance. The simu lations are under a code rate0.89 matrix H (9728, 8704) for NAND flash applications with the row weight of 36, AWGN, and maximu m nu mber of iteration Imax of 8. The NPMSA and PS-PMSA have the same normalized factor of 0.75. The PCE is also employed to terminate the convergent iterations. Fig. 2 shows that the BER performance of PS-PMSA is very close to that of NPMSA with or without the PCE. Fig. 3 shows that the average iterations of PS-PM SA and NPMSA are less than one at high Eb/N0, and it reveals the PS-PMSA works well under the PCE. At low Eb/N0, the PS-PMSA can efficiently reduce the power consumption when the PCE does not work.
Table I shows the silicon result of the distinct CNUs in a TSMC 90-n m process synthesized at 200 MHz with 6 input quantization bits. Co mpared with the NPMSA, the area overhead of PS-PMSA is only 1.8%. Fig. 4 shows the average power ratio of the distinct CNUs. The PS-PMSA achieves 13% power reduction compared with the NPMSA.
IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a partially stopped criterion was proposed for the NPMSA. The PS-PMSA discards the unnecessary messages of VNs to reduce the calculations in the CNU. The

PS-PM SA achieves power reduction with ignorable error correcting degradation and a small area overhead.
