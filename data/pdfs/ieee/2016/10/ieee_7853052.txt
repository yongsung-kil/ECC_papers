An Escaping Scheme for Gradient Descent Bit-Flipping Decoding of LDPC Codes

Hua Li, Hong Ding and Linhua Zheng School of Electrical Science and Engineering National University of Defense Technology
P. R. China, Changsha 410073

Abstract-The gradient descent bit-flipping (GDBF) decoding algorithm offers an attractive tradeoff between performance and complexity compared with the other bit-flipping (BF) decoding algorithms. However, the performance of GDBF decoding is seriously effected by infinite decoding loop, which always results in decoding failure when maximal iteration number is reached. To avoid the decoding loop and improve the performance, an efficient escaping scheme is proposed for the GDBF algorithm in this paper. This scheme firstly introduces syndrome weight to detect the decoding loop. If a decoding loop is detected, the escaping mechanism works, where only the bit which can
decrease the syndrome weight most efficiently is selected to be
flipped. In addition, the excluding set is introduced to further prevent the local maximum. Simulation results show that the GDBF algorithm can obtain about I dB code gain by utilizing this scheme. Compared with noise GDBF, it has O.IdB performance loss as well as lower complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have received significant attentions due to their outstanding performance[2] , which have been employed i n many communication standards. LDPC codes can be efficiently decoded by brief-propagation (BP ) and bit-flipping (BF) decoding[1] , [3] . BP decoding has good performance but suffers complexity increasing. And the BF decoding can be implemented with low complexity at the cost of great performance loss. To achieve a good tradeoff between performance and complexity, various variants of BF algorithm have been presented, such as weight BF (WBF) [4] and its improved versions [5] , [6], [7] , gradient descent BF (GDBF) [8] and its improved versions [9], [10] . These algorithms have the same structure with BF decoding algo­ rithm, but they have better error-rate performance compered to original BF decoding algorithm. The reason is that they introduce the soft channel information to the decoding process. However, in these algorithms, the decoding loop or local maxi­ mum may seriously degrade their performance. To weaken the effect of the loops, several papers [11] , [12] introduced the loop detection and prevention methods proposed by Liu [13] , where the loop was detected by recording each position of flipped bit and then the most unreliable bit was removed into excluding set to prevent the detected loop. This scheme was also modified to adapt to the multi-bit BF algorithm in [14] . For GDBF algorithm, there are a number of schemes proposed to escape from the decoding loop except the scheme in [13] . Wadayama et al. [8] adopted a mode switching strategy, where multi-bit GDBF decoding was used at the beginning of the

decoding process and the single-bit GDBF decoding was fol­ lowed. Subsequently, a noise GDBF (NGDBF) algorithm [10] was proposed by introducing a random perturbation into each symbol metric to escape from decoding loop. This algorithm offers a low-complexity loop-preventing solution. However, it was found that new loop might exist in NGDBF algorithm and they eliminated it by re-decoding with a different perturbation sequence [15] .
In this paper, we propose a novel escaping scheme for GDBF decoding with low complexity and good performance. Firstly, the variation of syndrome weight is used to detect the infinite decoding loop. Since the syndrome weight is easy to get with small complexity increased, the detecting process can be simply implemented. After a decoding loop detected, instead of selecting the flipped bit by the flipping function of GDBF, we select the bit to be flipped which can decrease syndrome weight most efficiently. It is proved that the bit can be selected by the flipping function of original BF decoding algorithm, which almost increase no complexity. In addition, the excluding set is also introduced to further prevent the decoding loop. Simulation results show that the proposed scheme can efficiently improve the performance of GDBF algorithm. And it almost has the same performance compared with NGDBF algorithm.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows: Section II presents some notations of LDPC codes and GDBF decoding algorithm. The proposed escaping scheme is introduced in Section III. Simulation results of the proposed scheme is demonstrated in section IV. Section V concludes the paper.

III. THE PROP OSED ESCAP ING SCHEME
In the GDBF decoding algorithm, the decoding process may fall into an infinite loop, which will lead to a decoding failure when the maximum allowable iteration number is reached. The current solution was proposed in [13], where the loops were detected by recording and comparing the flipped bits and prevented by introducing excluding set. In the scheme, the decoding loop can be successfully detected but it requires additional computation and storage. To find a more efficient scheme, the syndrome weight is employed. Since a successful GDBF based decoding is a decreasing process of the syndrome weight, an increasing of syndrome weight can be treated as a decoding loop. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, an escaping scheme is proposed for GDBF decoding of LDPC codes. This scheme first uses the syn­ drome weight to detect the local maximum or decoding loop. Then the flipping function of original BF algorithm and the excluding set are utilized to prevent the decoding loop. Combined with the escaping scheme, the performance of GDBF algorithm can be greatly improved. Compared with NGDBF algorithm, the proposed scheme has lower complexity and almost the same performance.
