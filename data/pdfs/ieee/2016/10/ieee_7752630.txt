A Self-Normalized Weighted Bit-Flipping Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes

Chunli Wang, Xiaofu Wu, and Wei-Ping Zhu Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Nanjing 210003, CHINA
Emails:xfuwu@ieee.org.{1015010533.andzwp}@njupt.edu.cn

Abstract-A novel self-normalized weighted bit-flipping (SNWBF) decoding algorithm for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is proposed. Compared to the best known weighted bitflipping decoding, the SNWBF algorithm converges significantly faster but with little performance penalty. For decoding of LDPC codes, the proposed SNWBF algorithm considers not only the reliability of the neighboring check nodes, but also the reliability of each variable node. Compared to the IMWBF algorithm, the proposed algorithm does not require multiplicative normalization and performs even better.
Index Terms-bit-flipping (BF) decoding, low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes, quantification.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes was first proposed by Gallager in his pioneer paper [7]. In recent years, iterative decoding of low-density parity-check codes has received much attention [2]-[5]. Bit-flipping (BF) decoding is simple, but it often incurs considerable performance loss compared to other decoding algorithm [2], [5]. To improve the performance of BF, weighted BF (WBF) decoding and its variants were proposed [2], [4]-[6], among which, the improved modified-WBF (IMWBF) decoding performs extremely well [5]. IMWBF decoding algorithm calculates the reliability of variable nodes, where a weighted parameter should be introduced. Often, this multiplicative factor should be determined by extensive simulations. If this weighting factor is not properly chosen, the IMWBF may perform not good. In this paper, we propose a novel SNWBF algorithm, which does not require further normalization, but with performance often better than the IMWBF.

IV. CONCLUSION
We have proposed a novel self-normalized weighted bitflipping decoding algorithm (SNWBF) for LDPC codes. Compared to the WBF decoding, the proposed SNWBF decoding converges much faster. Compared to the IMWBF decoding, the proposed SNWBF decoding does not consider the unfixed weighting factor. For LDPC codes, it was shown that the SNWBF quantized version has lower implementation complexity.
