A Study on Construction of Low-Density Parity-Check Codes Using Nonlinear Feedback Shift Registers

Shotaro Moriyama and Akio Tsuneda Department of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, Kumamoto University
2-39-1 Kurokami, Chuo-ku, Kumamoto 860-8555, Japan E-mail: moriyama@st.cs.kumamoto-u.ac.jp, tsuneda@cs.kumamoto-u.ac.jp

Abstract—Digital communications are daily used in the modern society supported by ICT (Information and Communication Technology). In digital communications, an error-control technology is necessary for correcting/detecting bit errors caused by channel noise. Many kinds of error-correcting/detecting codes have been proposed. Among them, LDPC (Low-Density ParityCheck) codes are known to have error-correcting ability that is near to the Shannon limit. In this paper, we propose the construction of LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) codes based on de Bruijn sequences generated by NFSRs (Nonlinear Feedback Shift Registers). The BER (Bit Error Rate) performance of the proposed codes is also evaluated.
Index Terms—LDPC, de Bruijn sequence, NFSR, bit error rate
I. INTRODUCTION
Our modern society is supported by ICT including digital communications that are widely used in our daily life. In digital communications, the reliability of the transmission is more important than the speed. For correcting/detecting bit errors, error-control techniques must be used in actual communication channels and various error-correcting/detecting codes have been studied [1].
It is known that LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) codes, which were proposed by Gallager in 1962 [2], can have high error-correcting ability near to the Shannon limit and the decoding speed can be improved by using a parallel decoder (e.g., sum-product algorithm) [3], [4]. Therefore, LDPC codes are used in practical communications such as WiFi, WiMax, and DVB-S2 standards [4]. For LDPC codes, the low-density parity-check matrix with mostly 0’s and relatively few 1’s plays an essential role. One of methods to construct row (or column) vectors of such parity-check matrices of LDPC codes is to use random numbers [3]–[5].
In this paper, we propose the construction of low-density parity check matrices for LDPC codes using de Bruijn sequences which are kinds of pseudo-random sequences generated by NFSR (Nonlinear Feedback Shift Register). We evaluate the BER (bit error rate) performances of the proposed LDPC codes by computer simulations. The main purpose is to investigate the dependency of the BER performance on the auto-correlation properties of binary sequences used for lowdensity parity check matrices.

III. BER PERFORMANCES
We perform computer simulations using the proposed LDPC codes, where five de Bruijn sequences with period N = 512 and three de Bruijn sequences with period N = 256 are used. We use the LDPC simulation program available at [7]. The (981, 500) LDPC code (sample data) given at [7] is also used for comparison.
Fig.2 shows the BER characteristics comparing (1024, 512)

codes and (512, 256) codes using three different de Bruijn sequences and three logic functions given by eq.(1). For comparison, the BER of the (981, 500) code given in [7] is also shown. For the S/N ratio less than around 1dB, BER of the (512,256) codes is smaller than the others, but for the S/N ratio greater than around 1dB, BER of the (512,256) codes is larger than the others. Also, for the S/N ratio greater than 2dB, BER of the sample (981, 500) code is smaller than the others.

IV. CONCLUSION
We have constructed LDPC codes by using de Bruijn sequences generated by NFSRs. By computer simulations,

basic BER performances of the proposed LDPC codes have been presented. We find that the BER depends on the autocorrelation properties of the sparse binary sequences. Detailed analyses concerning the auto-correlations and BER performances are left to future study.
