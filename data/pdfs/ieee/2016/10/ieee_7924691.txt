Abstract-The 
alternating 
direction method 
of 
multipliers 
(ADMM) with over-relaxation and the accelerated ADMM are 
two effective methods to improve the convergence rate of 
ADMM. In this paper, an efficient linear programming (LP) 
decoder based on accelerated ADMM for low-density parity­
check (LDPC) codes is proposed. Then it is shown that the 
ADMM update steps for decoding LDPC codes using the 
accelerated ADMM and the over-relaxed ADMM are quite 
similar. Therefore, it is impossible to obtain a more efficient 
ADMM decoder by combining the two improved ADMM 
methods. Simulation results also validate that the average 
number of iterations for LDPC decoding using the accelerated 
ADMM and the over-relaxed ADMM are close to each other.

The alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) 
is an efficient distributed method to solve optimization 
problems [1]. Recently, Barman et al. proposed an efficient 
linear programming (LP) decoding algorithm for low-density 
parity-check (LDPC) codes based on ADMM [2]. Simulation 
results in the literatures show that the ADMM decoder is 
comparable with the classical belief propagation (BP) 
decoding for LDPC codes [2] [3]. 
Later, the computational complexity of ADMM decoder 
is reduced further in several aspects. Firstly, the Euclidean 
projection onto check polytopes, which is the most complex 
part involved in the ADMM decoder, is simplified in [3] and 
[4]. 
Secondly, 
instead 
of simplifying the Euclidean 
projection, [5] considers to decrease the number of Euclidean 
projections during the ADMM decoding. Then it is shown 
that the layered scheduling, when combined with the method 
in [5], leads to significant decoding speed-up for ADMM 
decoders [6]. Thirdly, the over-relaxed ADMM is a general 
method to improve the convergence speed of ADMM while 
retaining convergence guarantees [1]. Simulation results in 
[2] show that the over-relaxed ADMM decoder can save the 
average decoding time effectively. Note that the methods 
shown above in different aspects can be combined with each 
other to obtain a more efficient ADMM decoder. 
Recently, Goldstein et al. proposed a fast ADMM 
method, called accelerated ADMM, which is also a general 
method to improve the convergence rate of ADMM [7]. As

VI. 
CONCLUSION 
In this paper, an accelerated ADMM decoder for LDPC 
codes is proposed fIrst. Then the similarity of the ADMM 
update steps between the over-relaxed ADMM decoder and 
the accelerated ADMM decoder is analyzed for LDPC codes. 
Simulations have also shown that the average number of 
iterations and the average decoding per frame between the 
over-relaxed and the accelerated ADMM decoders are quite 
similar.
