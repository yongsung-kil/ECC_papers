Performance of Improved AR3A code Over EPR4 Channel

SiJie Yang1, Lin Wang1, Yi Fang1, Pingping Chen1 1Dept. of Communication Engineering, Xiamen University, Fujian 361005, P.R.China
Email: wanglin@xmu.edu.cn

Abstract ü High rate low-density parity-check(LDPC) codes have been under intense research in the serial concatenation of inner Extend partial–response Class 4(EPR4) and outer LDPC codes due to their decoding performance close to the Shannon capacity. The Accumulate Repeat-3 and Accumulate (AR3A) codes, viewed as a subclass of protograph LDPC codes, achieve good decoding performance with simple linear encoder structure and flexible code rates via puncturing variable nodes, which makes us consider the potential of serially concatenating the EPR4 channel with AR3A codes. Simulation results show that AR3A codes can’t preserve their performance advantages over regular LDPC codes in the EPR4 channel, although they surpass regular LDPC codes in the AWGN channels. This paper proposes an improved AR3A code where we change the puncturing method, that is, the degree-1 variable nodes are punctured instead of degree-5 ones. The performance curves verify that the improved AR3A code possesses better decoding performance than the regular LDPC code in EPR4 channels and still keeps its original excellent characteristics of low hardware complexity.
Keywords- EPR4 channel; protograph; PEG; the improved AR3A codes; BCJR;
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes were originally proposed by Gallager in 1962 [1], and revisited by Mackay in 1996 [2]. LDPC codes have been widely applied into information transmission and magnetic recording due to their capacity-approaching performances. Since the rediscovery, a large volume of work has been conducted on the design of LDPC codes with imposed substructure, starting with the introduction of multi-edge-type (MET) codes in [3]. One such structured code design is based on protographs, firstly proposed by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) of NASA in [4]. As classes of protograph LDPC codes, repeat and accumulate (RA) codes [5], irregular repeat and accumulate (IRA) codes [6] and accumulate repeat and accumulate (ARA) codes [7] were successively proposed for their simple substructures of encoder and excellent decoding performance. This family of protograph LDPC codes with accumulator can achieve high-speed semi-parallel decoding and possess low encoding and decoding complexity. In 2007, JPL proposed accumulate repeat-4 jagged accumulate (AR4JA) codes to the Consultative Committee for Space Data Systems (CCSDS) of the US as a standard error correcting codes in space data systems [8]. Moreover, protograph-based ARA codes can obtain any desired high code rate close to 1 with good thresholds that stay close to the channel capacity uniformly.
LDPC codes have been a promising error correcting codes (ECC) for high density magnetic recording system. However,

typical hard disk drive (HDD) systems require bit error rates (BERs) as low to 10-12 to 10-15 [9], while most LDPC codes are believed to suffer from error floor. Thus, much research has been dedicated to solving this problem by designing LDPC codes with low floors [10]-[11] or improving the decoder in [12]. Motivated by ARA protograph-based codes with low error floor, we investigate the performances of ARA codes over magnetic recording channels. In this paper, AR3A code is selected for our research. We consider magnetic recording channels as Extended Class-4 Partial Response (EPR4) channels at the presence of AWGN for our research purpose. As noted in [20], irregular LDPC codes of such high rates have been shown to perform slightly worse than regular codes, thus, we consider the performances of regular LDPC codes for comparison. However, our investigation shows that over EPR4 channels AR3A codes can’t maintain their priority over regular LDPC codes as that over AWGN channels. Hence we make improvement on AR3A codes for EPR4 channels. Systematic simulation results show that the performances of the improved ARA codes proposed in this paper is much better than original AR3A codes and regular LDPC codes.
The organization of this paper is as follows. In Section II, we make a brief tutorial of protograph LDPC codes and AR3A codes. Section III shows the system model which adopts turbo equalization. Simulation results including the performances of regular LDPC codes, AR3A codes and the improved AR3A codes over AWGN channels and EPR4 channels are presented in Section III, and some conclusions are drawn in Section Č.
II. PROTOGRAGH, PEG AND AR3A CODES
A. Protographs and Protograph LDPC Codes
LDPC codes can be represented by Tanner graphs which is denoted as a set consisting of variable nodes V, check nodes C and edges E connecting V to C. Protograph LDPC codes were first introduced by J.Thorpe [4]. A protograph can be any Tanner graph, with a relatively small number of nodes. Similar to normal LDPC codes’ Tanner graphs, a protograph G = (V, C, E) consists of a set of variable nodes V, a set of check nodes C, and a set of edges E. Each edge e∈ E connects a variable node ve ∈V to a check node. In
the protographs, parallel edges are allowed, so the mapping e → (ve,ce ) ∈V × C isn’t necessarily one to one.

IV. SIMULATION RESULT
We present in this section performance simulation results of an AR3A code, an improved AR3A code and a (3, 15) regular LDPC code constructed by the original PEG algorithm over AWGN channels and EPR4 channels respectively. All the codes have 4096 information bits and rate 0.8. In simulation, the sum-product algorithm is used for the decoder and the BCJR algorithm is used for the detector. Each simulation point is collected over the statistics of 500 bit errors.
A. Simulation performances over AWGN channels
Fig 8 shows the performance comparison of the forementioned three codes. Solid lines and dotted lines present BER and FER respectively. From Fig 8 we can see that the AR3A code surpasses the regular LDPC code by about 0.3dB at BER=10-4. The improved AR3A code performs close to the regular LDPC code in low Eb/No region but worse than the regular LDPC code in high Eb/No region, suffering from error floor at BER<10-5. However, their performance priority and inferiority over EPR4 channels are totally different with those over AWGN channel, which will be shown in the next subsection.

It has been known that irregular LDPC codes of such high rates perform slightly worse than regular ones under the framework of turbo equalization over EPR4 channels [20], which is the reason why we choose a regular code for comparison. Fig.9 gives the FER and the BER curves of the three forementioned codes over EPR4 channels with different numbers of inner iterations and outer iterations denoted by “x/y” where x and y represent the number of outer iterations and the number of inner iterations respectively. For fairly comparison, we constrain that the product of x and y in all “x/y” is the same. Solid lines and dotted lines present BER and FER respectively.
From Fig.9, it can be seen that the AR3A code which performs the best among the three codes over AWGN channel turns into the worst over EPR4 channel. The improved AR3A code outperforms the regular LDPC code about 0.1dB and AR3A code about 0.4dB at BER=10-5 when outer iteration is set as 3 or 4. Moreover, the improved AR3A code doesn’t suffer from error floor at the BER of 10-7 in the Eb/N0 (dB) region simulated. Though we are not able to theoretically figure out the reason why performance priority and inferiority of these three codes are so different, a conclusion can be drawn that a code with good performances over AWGN channels doesn’t necessarily possess good performances over EPR4 channels. Hence, it is necessary and meaningful to consider the improvement of LDPC-like codes for EPR4 channels.
In addition, we make an investigation on the effect of different outer/inner iterations on system performance. From Fig 9, we can find that keeping the product of the number of inner iterations and the number of outer iterations fixed, increasing outer iterations can provide better performances for all the three codes. In Fig.9, the performances with 4 outer iterations are better than those with 3 outer iterations for all the three codes. This phenomenon predicts that increasing outer iterations may bring more performance

V. CONCLUSION
This paper investigates the performances of AR3A codes over EPR4 channels. It is found that AR3A codes which outperform regular LDPC codes over AWGN channels perform worse than regular LDPC codes over EPR4 channels. Therefore, some improvements are made onto AR3A codes. Simulation results demonstrate that the improved AR3A codes possess better performances than regular LDPC codes on a scheme of turbo equalization over EPR4 channels. In addition, as a special case of ARA codes, the improved AR3A codes can also achieve low complexity encoder and high-speed semi-parallel decoding and its outer iteration number obviously affects its system performance relative to inner ones. Therefore, the improved AR3A codes can be considered for application in real magnetic recording systems, which is exactly our future work.
