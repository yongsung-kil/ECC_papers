I. 
INTRODUCTION 
A Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) code was firstly 
proposed by Gallager in 1962 [1]. Although the code didn't 
draw much attention at the beginning, its later investigation has 
found that such a code holds an impressive good performance 
that can be made close to channel limit over A WGN channel 
[2]. Within the past decade there are tremendous proposals 
mentioning the matrix design methods. In general, methods for 
constructing LDPC codes can be classified into two categories: 
random or pseudorandom constructions [2] and algebraic 
constructions [3]. In the case of LDPC codes with random H 
matrix, a big memory size is unavoidable, in particular for the 
large block length code. Then, it is hard to access the memory 
efficiently in data encoding. To ease the implementation, most 
LDPC code designs are based on permutation matrix in parity 
check matrix construction. For instance, Mackey [2] has done a 
lot of work in random matrix generation. 
The quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) is widely interesting 
for many researchers. This LDPC variation is promising in 
many applications since it has low-complexity encoding and 
decoding algorithms, simplify hardware implementation, and 
high decoding speed and high throughput [6][7]. 
Based on circulant permutation matrices, Sridhara-Fuja­
Tanner [4] has recently proposed a parity check matrix design 
with a class of algebraically structured quasi-cyclic. Their so 
called SFT technique has quite a good performance when 
compared to that of randomized structure. Chun-Ming Huang 
et al. [5] later proposed a method with quadratic congruences

performance is nearly that of the SFT structure. 
In this paper, we further investigate QC-LDPC codes matrix 
construction. We introduce the use of multiplicative ring under 
a specific Galois Field together with cyclic shifting in our 
construction. The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In

are given in brief. In section II, our design methodology is 
mainly outlined. In section IV, we show some obtained results 
of the formulated algorithm. Finally in section V, we conclude 
the paper.

V. 
CONCLUDSION 
In this paper we have proposed an alternative H matrix 
design of a LDPC code. The method combines the technique 
in SFT and cyclic shifting. The main task is to design the first 
row sub-matrices. The subsequent rows are basically the 
shifted version of the previous row. This method can ensure the 
absence of cycle-4. To obtain the best performance the decided 
order of shifting is still unclear to us and we need more 
investigation. One good point we can see is that the systematic 
shifting can ease the decoder implementation. In comparison 
with the SFT method, the proposed method is slightly more 
complicated as random number generation is employed. 
However, the method is slightly simpler than that of QC 
method where both elements a and b are randomly generated. 
Moreover some associated parameters must be defined. Based 
on the simulation on A WGN channel, the proposed method 
yields the similar performance when compared to others.
