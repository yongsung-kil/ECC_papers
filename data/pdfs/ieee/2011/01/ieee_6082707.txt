Robust carrier recovery for DVB-S2 system 
Pansoo Kim, Deock-Gil Oh, and Ho-Jin Lee 
Broadcasting & Communication Convergence Research Division 
ETRI(Electronics and Telecommunication Research Institute) 
Deajeon, Republic of Korea 
 E-mail pskim@etri.re.kr 
(Invited Paper for ICTC 2011)
 
Abstract— This paper suggests the practical approach to 
mitigate phase noise by using joint detection and decoding 
scheme for DVB-S2 system. The proposed scheme illustrates in 
detail to implement robust DVB-S2 receiver compared to the 
previous study and shows the satisfactory performance when the 
specific  LDPC decoding schedules have been utilized in the 
hardware implementation point of view.   
 
Keywords— Carrier recovery, DVB-S2 system, joint 
detection and decoding, phase noise, satellite communications 
 
I. 
 INTRODUCTION  
DVB-S2(Digital Video Broadcasting-2nd Generation) standard 
is well known to provide HD(High Definition) satellite 
broadcasting  and two way satellite communication  service 
like DVB-RCS(Return Channel System via Satellite)[1][2]. 
One key prerequisite to be successfully commercialized 
product in the broadcasting market is to develop low cost 
terminal. To realize the requirement, it should be designed to 
tolerate carrier instability like phase noise caused by low cost 
LNB(Low Noise Block)  or LO(Local Oscillator).  
A lot of study has been carried out to mitigate the phase noise 
in the academic field. Specifically, it's also done in DVB-S2 
systems. [3][4][5]. The so called, CBC[3](Colavolpe, Barbieri, 
and Caire) scheme firstly built the framework based on joint 
detection and decoding approach through BCJR[6]. However, 
Colavolpe et al [3], was not described in detail to implement 
the practical receiver. Also Barbieri et al[4] proposed to handle  
phase noise based on sum product algorithm and discretized 
phase to be applicable, practically. In addition, Barbieri et al[5] 
applied the previous study  to DVB-S2 system, practically and 
obtained the specific performance result. Even if the authors 
referred this scheme can be extended to non-pilot mode for 
DVB-S2, there was no explicit result.  
Therefore, this paper intends to contribute two points unlike 
previous study. First, it formulates the modified CBC for 
practical implementation in DVB-S2 system. It means to 
describe the systematic procedure to be implemented. Second, 
it's combined with the practical LDPC decoder schedule 
scheme to enhance the practical applicability [7][8] . The paper 
is organized as follows. In section II, the system model is 
described and basic conceptual method is introduced.  The 
ML(Maximum Likelihood) approach is explained to facilitate  
the expansion of proposed scheme in section III.  
The section IV presents the proposed solution which has the 
specific clarification with CBC algorithm in DVB-S2 system. 
The performance of algorithm is evaluated in section in section 
V.  Finally, conclusion is drawn.

1) PLL based phase tracking 
PLL(Phase Locked Loop) based phase tracking is the most 
common and the simplest way to exploit channel constraints: 
the phase estimate is updated once per every symbol according 
to the following first order  recursion [9] 
 
θ෠୩ାଵ= θ෠୩+ λx୩                                    (4) 
 
where λ  is the loop gain and x୩ denotes the phase error 
detector output. The LLR(Log Likelihood Ratio) computation 
unit takes on r୩eି୨θ෠ౡ and computes the vector 
 
LLR୳୮= ቀLLR୳୮(b଴), ⋯, LLR୳୮(b୒ିଵ)ቁ              (5) 
 
by marginalizing Pr(r୩eି୨θ෠ౡ= s). LLR୳୮ is the input of the 
LDPC decoder which is operated independently. This 
approach  suffers from cycle slips. 
 
2) Joint iterative detection and decoding scheme 
In a joint iterative detection and decoding scheme, the vector 
LLR୳୮ is recomputed prior to each decoder iteration utilizing 
benefit from more reliable knowledge coming out symbols 
that the decoder should provide. The exploitation of the 
channel constraint can be performed iteratively with a PLL 
phase tracking scheme.  But when a cycle slip occurs during 
the first iteration, it cannot be recover because the extrinsic 
probabilities of the channel symbols produced by the decoder 
are very poor and the iterative process does not produce 
significant improvement. The PLL phase tracker behaves as a 
hard decision device. We derive a practical soft phase detector 
in order to compute APP(A Posteriori symbol Probabilities). 
 
Let P(b, θ|r)  denote the joint a posteriori PDF(Probability 
Density Function) of the codeword and the phase noise given 
the receive vector.  LLR୳୮(b୧)  can be obtained by 
marginalizing P(b, θ|r) with respect to θ and all b୨ for j ≠i. 
This can be done in an approximate but low complexity way by 
means of the sum product algorithm. From the channel 
constraints, we derive  LLR୳୮
(ଵ)(b). It is fed into the LDPC 
decoder to carry out at the first iteration. The decoder output 
LLRୢ୭୵୬
(ଵ)
(b)  which is used by the mapper to calculate 
Prୢ୭୵୬
(ଵ)
(s), the vector of the extrinsic probabilities of the 
channel symbols. By reusing the channel constraints, we 
obtain LLR୳୮
(ଶ)(b). It is fed into the LDPC decoder to carry out 
at second iteration.  This is the general procedure for joint 
iterative detection and decoding.

IV. 
SUB ML-PHASE DETECTION: THE CBC-ALGORITHM 
In [3], based on the framework of BCJR, a new sub ML phase 
tracking algorithm has been derived. While we showed how to 
reduce the trellis length in the previous section, we were 
unable to reduce the number of states. Unfortunately, due to  
many number of state and transition, the BCJR algorithm is 
not suited for direct implementation and it cannot be 
applicable for iterative joint phase tracking and decoding.  The 
CBC have circumvented this problem by the  use of canonical 
distribution, i.e. the PDF given in the BCJR algorithm are 
constrained to belong to a certain family, characterized by a 
few parameters. The BCJR reduced to a forward-backward 
process aimed at estimating a small number of parameters.

choice of the canonical distribution. In [3], the Tikhonov
