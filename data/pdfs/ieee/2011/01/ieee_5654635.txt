Recursive Design of
High Girth (2,𝑘) LDPC Codes from (𝑘,𝑘) LDPC Codes
Xiongfei Tao, Lixin Zheng, Weizhong Liu, and Dongsheng Liu
Abstract—An approach to construct column-weight-2 LDPC
codes with high girth is presented. The approach derives a
column-weight-2 LDPC code from the Tanner graph of a (𝑘,𝑘)
QC-LDPC code. By the construction, the new LDPC codes
double in girth. To construct the (𝑘,𝑘) QC-LDPC codes with
desired girth, a search algorithm is proposed in this paper. The
approach generates an example of a (2,3) LDPC code with a
girth of 36, which is larger than the column-weight-2 LDPC
codes constructed by the previously methods.
Index Terms—LDPC codes, column-weight-2, recursive design,
high girth.
I. INTRODUCTION
L
OW-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] are lin-
ear block codes with near Shannon limit performance.
Gallager[1] proved that minimum distance of LDPC codes
with column weight 𝑗≥3 increases linearly with code length,
while that of LDPC codes with 𝑗=2 increases logarithmically
with code length. Despite this shortage, the column-weight-2
codes have their own merit: they have lower encoding and
decoding complexity than codes with 𝑗≥3, and better block
error statistics properties which is suitable to be applied on
partial response channels[2]. Moreover, (2,𝑘) graphs are used
to design non-binary (NB) LDPC codes that achieve very good
performance[3].
In this paper we are concerned with the approach to the
construction of regular (2,𝑘) LDPC codes. Zhang and Moura
constructed (2,𝑘) LDPC codes with large girth, e.g. 16 and 20,
based on graphical models[4], however, the rate of the codes
with girth of 16 is 1/2 and the codes with girth of 20 have a
code rate of 1/3. M. Esmaeili and M. Gholami[5] improved
the method in[4], and constructed (2,𝑘) codes with flexible
rate but girth at most 24. In [3], a randomized progressive
edge-growth (RandPEG) algorithm was proposed to design
(2,𝑘) LDPC codes with a length as small as possible. In order
to construct structured (2,𝑘) LDPC codes with higher girth,
we propose a recursive method to design (2,𝑘) LDPC codes
derived from (𝑘, 𝑘) quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes.

V. CONCLUSIONS
An approach to the construction of very large girth LDPC
codes with column-weight-2 is presented in this paper. First,
a search algorithm generates (𝑘, 𝑘) QC-LDPC codes with
desired girth 𝑔. Then, by recursive deriving from such codes,
we design (2,𝑘) LDPC codes with girth 2𝑔.
Furthermore, the search algorithm should be used to find
exponents to construct large girth regular QC-LDPC codes
with column weight greater than 2 and irregular QC-LDPC
codes, but the algorithm has a problem to be solved which is
how to optimize the selection of exponents to lower the block
size.
