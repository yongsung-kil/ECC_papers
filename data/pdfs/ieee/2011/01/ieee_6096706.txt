Optimizing bit-interleaved coded modulation for
half-duplex relay based on protograph codes
Sun Xiaojun∗, Ming Jiang∗, Wei Xu∗, Chunming Zhao∗, and Meiling Ding† ∗National Mobile Communications Research Laboratory, Southeast University, Nanjing 210096, CHINA Email: {sunxiaojun, jiang ming, wxu, cmzhao}@seu.edu.cn †ZTE Corporation, Shanghai, CHINA Email: ding meiling@hotmail.com

Abstract—In this paper, we discuss the bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) for the half-duplex decode-and-forward (DF) relay network based on the protograph LDPC codes. The halfduplex relay BICM design problem can be transformed into a problem of rate-compatible BICM design, where the different segments of modulated symbols experience different SNRs. To optimize the BICM based on the rate-compatible protograph structure, we use the protograph extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) to evaluate the thresholds of different mapping patterns between the variable nodes in protographs and the modulated bits. With this tool, we search the efficient mapping pattern of BICM for the half-duplex DF network. Our work can achieve significant gains over the existing work.
Index Terms—protograph codes, LDPC codes, BICM, protograph EXIT, relay, decode-and-forward.
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, the works in [1]-[11] have shown that relay networks can achieve significant cooperative gains via the cooperation among different nodes. To extend coverage or improve quality of service, the half-duplex DF relay network is of more practical interest [1]-[3], especially for LTE-advanced network. In the case of DF protocols, a relay node firstly decodes the received messages from the source, and then reencodes these information and forwards new messages to the destination. The turbo codes and LDPC codes as well as halfduplex DF relay channels have been widely studied in recent works [4]-[11]. While, most works focused on the BPSK modulation [4]-[10]. To the best of our knowledge, there are few works on optimization high-order modulations and LDPC codes in DF relay channels [11].
It is well known that the next-generation mobile communication networks will provide large capacity with highorder modulation. BICM is a bandwidth efficient transmission, which has high transmission efficiency via a combination of high-order modulations and error-correcting codes [12]-[17]. The LDPC coded BICM system can show near-capacity performance with simple and flexible implementations [13][14]. As the LDPC codes defined by simple protographs [18], the protograph codes possess several advantages, such as small memory requirements and high-speed decoding structures [15][16][19]. To optimize the performance, our previous work [16] uses the protograph EXIT [20] to evaluate the thresholds

of different mapping patterns between the variable nodes in a protograph and the modulated bits.
In this paper, we discuss the protograph coded BICM for the half-duplex DF relay channels. The half-duplex relay BICM design problem can be transformed into a problem of ratecompatible BICM design, where the different segments of modulated symbols experience different SNRs. To construct rate-compatible BICM, our work focuses on the design of concatenated protograph codes and check splitted protograph codes, which target at the joint decoding based on the message from source and relay. With the aid of previous work in [16], we search the efficient mapping pattern of the rate-compatible BICM for the half-duplex DF channels. Compared to the prior work [11], which used bilayer LDPC codes [8], our work can achieve significant gains.

B. Protograph coded BICM
After a block of coded bits is interleaved by the interleaver, this interleaved sequence is mapped to symbols chosen from the modulator. As is well known, the Gray labeling outperforms any other labelings in LDPC coded modulation systems. Due to the built-in random interleaver of the LDPC codes, the interleaver/deinterleaver can be omitted.
The protograph can be seen as the Tanner graph with a relatively small size. Protographs of two representative protograph LDPC codes [19] are shown in Figure 1. In Fig. 1(a), there are five variable nodes, {V0, V1, V2, V3, V4}. The coded sequence is divided into five sub-sequences according to the variable nodes. The sub-sequence, which corresponds to V0, is punched. We consider BICM with the Gray-mapped 16QAM which is equivalent to independently map Graymapped 4PAM to the I and Q components. The extension to higher-order modulation is straightforward. For simplicity, we focus on one component. The modulated bit pairs {X1X2} ({00, 01, 11, 10}) are mapped to a symbol of signal constellation {−3, −1, 1, 3} for I component or Q component. The bitplane mutual information I1 > I2 is computed via Monte Carlo simulation. The previous work [16] used the protograph EXIT to optimize the mapping patterns between the variable nodes in a protograph and the modulated bits. A mapping pattern describes connections between the transmitted nodes in a protograph and the modulated bits {X1X2} in the signal constellation. For example, a mapping pattern, {2, 3; 1, 4}, denotes that bits corresponding to V2 and V3 are allocated to X1, and the others are allocated to X2.

follows, we discuss three methods to construct rate-compatible BICM for relay channels, concatenated protograph coded BICM, check-splitted protograph coded BICM and lengthened protograph coded BICM.

C. Bilayer Protograph code for BICM Relay
The bilayer LDPC codes in [8] are discussed in this subsection. To implement the DF protocol, this method designs of two codes at the rates of S-D link and S-R link, respectively. A subclass of bilayer code, expurgated code, has been used for BICM relay channels in [11]. While, in the case of large difference between CSR and CSD, an alternative bilayer lengthened

LDPC code gives a better performance [8][11]. Thus, we will

consider the lengthened LDPC code for comparison purposes

in this work. The implementation of lengthening is illustrated

in Fig 5 (for more details, please refer to [Fig 8, 6]). Different

from above two schemes, the lengthened codes obtain lower

rate codes via removing information bits.

The lengthened protograph coded BICM, is discussed for

relay channels as follows. The lengthened code involves in

design two codewords so that one is a subset of the other. As

shown in Fig 3(a), we start by designing the code for the S-R

link. Considering the capacity of the S-D link, we choose a

sub-code illustrated in Fig 3 (c) that supports transmission in

the S-D link. Assuming that destination successfully decodes

another codeword from relay, destination obtain this sub-code

via removing variable node V4. Thus, destination performs

two sequentially separated decoding processes to decode the

original message from source [8].

A bilayer lengthened protograph code can be regarded as

an embedded rate-compatible code if destination successfully

removes variable nodes. We will consider this case in the

following. A lengthened protograph has the basic matrix in

the form of [10]

HL = [H1 |Hv ]

(7)

where H1 denotes the basic matrix of the lower rate code s which is equivalent to the sub-code shown in Fig 3 (c), HL is the basic matrix of overall code s1 shown in Fig 3 (a), and the variable nodes of the sub-matrix Hv are removed.
During T1, the codeword HL is used. During T2, the bits of Hv are re-encoded and transmitted. Finally, the bits of Hv are removed. In fact, the performance of bilayer LDPC codes,
i.e., H1 and HL, should be capacity achieving at the rate of S-D link and S-R link, respectively [8].

D. Remarks
In this subsection, we next briefly address the difference between the existing work [11] and our work.
Firstly, the rate-compatible code design is different. The bilayer-expurgated LDPC code [8], which is used in [11], performs two sequentially separated decoding processes to decode messages from the source. First, the destination decodes messages from the relay. Second, the destination decodes original messages from the source with the aid of additional syndrome from the relay, provided that these information can be successfully decoded first. Different from [11], our work mainly considers a joint decoding process based on the messages from source and relay. Therefore, the rate-compatible constraint is different from [11]. Moreover, our work focuses on the design of concatenated protograph codes and check splitted protograph codes which target at the joint decoding at destination. In addition, our work also discuss another subclass of bilayer LDPC for BICM, lengthened protograph codes, which give better performance than expurgated codes [8]. Secondly, our work also considers the optimization of the mapping pattern between LDPC codes and the modulated bits. While, the existing work [11] just used the random mapping pattern.

with Gray-mapped 16QAM by both numerical analysis and

simulations. In the simulations, the optimal maximum a pos-

teriori (log-MAP) algorithm with symbol-to-bit metrics is used

to demodulate the 16QAM signals. The protograph codes are

decoded by the standard sum-product algorithm (SP) with the

maximum number of iterations to be 200. All the codes are

constructed based on the method proposed in [15], where the

lengths of information bits are 4000. The path-loss exponent

is β = 2; the normalized time slot is T1 = 2/3. The rate of the codeword s1 is 2, and the rate of total codeword s is 4/3.
