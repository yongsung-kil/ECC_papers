AN IMPROVED LDPC CODING SCHEME WITH MOTION DECISION FOR DISTRIBUTED VIDEO CODEC

Bin Li, Yumei Wang, Yu Liu, Lin Zhang

Key Lab of Universal Wireless Communications, Ministry of Education, Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Beijing 100876, China
muziwenwu1986@gmail.com

Abstract
In conventional distributed video coding (DVC), we exploit a random irregular check matrix to realize LDPC coding. However, for DVC this method does not fully exploit the character that the bits corresponding to this random matrix with different significance can be protected in different degrees. Therefore, in this paper we propose an improved LDPC coding scheme with motion decision to improve the LDPC decoding performance. Considering the movement of the objects in the video sequence, all of blocks in the frame are classified as different motion areas which need the protection with varying degrees. Meanwhile, we exploit the progressive edgegrowth (PEG) algorithm to generate a WeightIncreasing Parity-Check (WIPC) matrix for LDPC coding. Finally, the corresponding bits of the pixels located in the motion areas could be mapped to the variable nodes with different degrees in the generated matrix to proceed decoding. The experimental results show that compared with the conventional LDPC coding scheme in DISCOVER, the PSNR gain for our proposed coding scheme can reach up to 0.6db.
Keywords: motion area; WIPC; LDPC; DVC
1 Introduction
In typical conventional video coding architecture like H.264/AVC, the video encoder explores complex motion estimation and compensation solutions for better rate-distortion (RD) performance. As a result, the computing power of the encoder is more complex than that of the decoder. However, this architecture has being challenged by some emerging applications (e.g. wireless video surveillance, multimedia sensor networks) which need an encoder with significantly lower complexity while permit a decoder with higher complexity. Distributed video coding (DVC), a new video paradigm, allows for shifting the computing complexity from the encoder to the decoder while maintaining the efficient compression. So it can well fit for these applications. However, there is still a significant

compression efficiency gap in practice between the conventional video coding and DVC.
Basically, this gap is due to many specific aspects of DVC, and the design of the coding scheme plays an important role in reducing the gap and improving the overall system performance. In the traditional DVC solutions, the authors propose to convert the quantified pixels of the Wyner-Ziv (WZ) frame into their corresponding binary representations. Then, the same-position bits of the binary representations for the coefficients are used to construct a serial of bitplanes which are from the most significant bitplane (MSB) to the least significant bitplane (LSB). Finally, each bitplane is coded at the same coding rate based on the binary LDPC code [1]. Then, the authors in [2] illustrate that, from the MSB to the LSB, the bitplane has a different significance decreasing gradually, and protecting the more significant bitpalnes would be desirable. Therefore, considering its significance for each bitplane, different coding rates is set for these bitplanes to realize coding. Furthermore, two rate-adaptive LDPC codes in [3] and [4] are introduced for DVC. In the two methods, according to the bit error rate of each bitplane, the encoder can adjust the corresponding coding rate automatically and reach much better compression efficiency. Actually, these LDPC codes realize the unequal error protection (UEP) for different bitplanes. However, these methods just consider the bitplanes with different significance, but neglect the bits in each bitplane also have different significance and should be UEP during the decoding process.
Therefore, in this paper we propose an improved LDPC coding algorithm with motion decision for DVC. Considering the movement of the objects, all of blocks in the frames are classified as three motion areas, i.e. Stationary/Slow Motion (SM), Moderate Motion (MM), and Fast Motion (FM). Then, the progressive edge-growth (PEG) algorithm in [5] is utilized to generate a WeightIncreasing Parity-Check (WIPC) matrix for LDPC coding. Furthermore, for the blocks in these three different motion areas, the corresponding pixels will be mapped to different variable nodes in the

2 Proposed DVC architecture
In this section, a modified DVC architecture is introduced for our improved coding scheme. As shown in Fig.1, we adopt a fixed Group of Pictures (GOP=2) in this architecture. Therefore, the odd frames of the video sequence are referred as key frame and the even frames are called WZ frames. The key frames are intra coded by using a traditional video coding approach like H.264/AVC, while the WZ frames are WZ coded [7].
Figure 1 The modified DVC architecture for ourproposed coding scheme
At the WZ encoder, we adopt the 2M uniform
scalar quantizer to quantify the WZ frames. Then, the quantization coefficients are converted to their binary representations. In addition, we exploit the PEG algorithm to generate a WIPC matrix for LDPC coding. Meanwhile, according to the block motion information from the “Motion decision” unit of the decoder, the same-position bits of the binary representations for the coefficients are used to construct a serial of bitplanes. In other words, for a certain bitplane, if the pixels located in the block belonging to FM, the corresponding bits of the pixels will be mapped to the latter variable

nodes with the larger degrees/weights; if the pixels located in the block belonging to SM, the corresponding binary representations of the pixels will be mapped to the former variable nodes with the smaller weights; otherwise, the remaining binary representations of the pixels will be mapped to the other variable nodes. Finally, in order to realize the UEP for the different bitplanes, a ratecompatible Low Density Parity Check Accumulate (LDPCA) encoder [8] is used to code each bitplane. For every encoded bitplane, the corresponding syndrome together with an 8-bit CRC is stored in the LDPCA encoder and will be transmitted depending on the requirements of the decoder through the feedback channel. With this method, we can transmit as fewer syndrome bits as possible and guarantee the overall compression efficiency.
At the WZ decoder, when we have successfully decoded the adjacent decoded key frames
( X B and X F ), we could adopt the method in [9] to
generate the side information Y and the method in [10] to estimate the residual frame R between the encoded WZ frame and the SI. Subsequently, the residual frame R is sent to the “Motion decision” unit for setting three different motion areas, i.e. SM, MM and FM, depending on the movements of the objects in the video sequences. In our architecture, each WZ frame is partitioned into non-overlapping blocks of size 16 by 16 and all these blocks are classified as one of the three motion areas. If the movement of the objects is complex or nonlinear, such as curving and zooming, the corresponding blocks are classified as the FM; if the objects in the blocks are stationary or the movement of the objects is translational and slow, these blocks pertain to the SM; otherwise, the other blocks correspond to the MM. As a result, for the pixels belonging to the three different motion areas, their corresponding binary representations will be mapped to the variable nodes with different weights. Therefore, the block motion information need be sent to the encoder through the feedback channel. In addition, when the decoder receives the transmitted syndrome bits from the encoder, with the help of the SI and motion information, the LPDCA decoder will proceed iterative decoding. During this process, if the Cyclic Redundancy Check (CRC) sum is incorrect or the Hamming distance between the received syndrome bits and that calculated from the decoded bitplane is not equal to zero, it means that the bitplane can not be decoded successfully. At this time, more syndrome bits from the LDPCA encoder will be requested until there are enough syndrome bits for successful decoding.

5 Conclusions
In this paper, we propose an LDPC-based improved coding scheme with motion decision for DVC. In this scheme, considering the movements of the objects in the video sequences, we divided

all of blocks in the frame into three motion areas.The pixels belonging to different motion areas will be mapped to the variable nodes with different weights in the WIPC matrix for UEP. Compared with the coding scheme in DISCOVER that we base on, the PSNR gain for our proposed coding scheme could reach up to 0.6db improvement.
