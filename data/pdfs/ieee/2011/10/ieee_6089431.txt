Iterative Soft Decoding of Binary Linear Codes Using a Generalized Tanner Graph

Eirik Rosnes Department of Informatics, University of Bergen, N-5020 Bergen, Norway
Email: eirik@ii.uib.no

Abstract‚ÄĒIn this work, we consider iterative soft-decision decoding of binary linear codes using a generalized Tanner graph. A generalized Tanner graph is constructed from the code‚Äôs Tanner graph representation under the objective of mitigating the effect of small cycles. Then, iterative decoding is applied to the generalized Tanner graph using trellis-based soft-input softoutput decoding in the generalized check nodes. When combined with the stochastic shifting algorithm of Jiang and Narayanan (IEEE Commun. Letters, 2004), the proposed decoding method provides both improved error rate performance and reduced average decoding complexity, without increasing the worst-case decoding complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
Inspired by the success of iterative decoding of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, introduced by Gallager [1] and later rediscovered in the mid 1990‚Äôs by MacKay and Neal [2], the idea of iterative decoding has recently been applied to classical algebraically constructed codes in order to achieve low-complexity soft-decision decoding [3]‚Äď[5]. To combat the large amount of short cycles, in particular 4-cycles, in the Tanner graphs of such codes, different modifications of standard belief-propagation (BP) decoding have been suggested. The stochastic shifting algorithm of Jiang and Narayanan [4] uses the quasi-cyclic property of the binary images of ReedSolomon (RS) codes to mitigate the effect of cycles in the Tanner graph. This idea was recently generalized by Halford and Chugg [5] using the automorphism group of the code.
In this work, we propose to combine check nodes in the original Tanner graph to mitigate the effect of short cycles on iterative decoding. The resulting generalized Tanner graph is used with stochastic shifting iterative decoding as outlined in [4]. The idea of combining check nodes into a super node to remove cycles first appeared in [6]. Also, in [7], this approach was proposed to lower the error floor of LDPC codes by targeting problematic trapping sets rather than cycles. Here, however, the focus is on classical linear codes. A greedy algorithm is also presented to construct such a generalized Tanner graph from the original Tanner graph. Finally, a case study of a binary image of the (31, 25) RS code over GF(25) shows a performance improvement of about 0.1 dB (resp. 0.3 dB) on the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) (resp. the independent Rayleigh flat-fading) channel at a frame error rate (FER) of 10‚ąí6 with lower average decoding complexity compared to the stochastic shifting algorithm of Jiang and Narayanan, without increasing the worst-case decoding complexity.

II. PRELIMINARIES

A binary linear code C of length n and dimension k is a k-dimensional subspace of {0, 1}n. The code can be specified

as the null space of a rank (n ‚ąí k), m √ó n binary parity-

check matrix H where m ‚Č• n ‚ąí k. The ith row of H, i =

0, . . . , m ‚ąí 1, will be denoted by the row vector hi.

The Tanner graph T = T (H) of a parity-check matrix H

is a bipartite graph with a left vertex set V = {v0, . . . , vn‚ąí1} consisting of variable nodes, and a right vertex set C =

{c0, . . . , cm‚ąí1} consisting of check nodes. The variable nodes correspond to the columns of H, and the check nodes corre-

spond to the rows of H. There is an edge between variable

B. Decoding

Iterative decoding on a generalized Tanner graph is ac-

complished using soft-input soft-output (SISO) decoding in

the generalized check nodes. In this work, we will consider

trellis-based SISO decoding using a trellis representation of

the binary linear code, or its dual code, corresponding to each

generalized check node. When combining a small number of

check nodes, the resulting binary linear codes are of high rate.

Thus, trellis-based SISO decoding using a minimal bit-level

trellis of the dual code gives a lower computational complexity

than using a minimal bit-level trellis of the code itself. SISO

decoding using a dual code trellis has been considered in

various previous works, and the interested reader is referred

to [10]‚Äď[14] for further details.
