2011 International Conference on Computational and Information Sciences

A Modified Construction Method of Low-Density Parity-Check Codes Based on PEGPC

Jiwei Chen, Zemao Zhao, Jianrong Bao
School of Telecommunication Engineering, Hangzhou Dianzi University Hangzhou 310018, China e-mail: lizhe5x@gmail.com

Abstract—A modified construction method of LDPC (Lowdensity Parity-check) codes based on polynomial of cycles of PEG (Progressive-Edge-Growth) is proposed according to reaction on the performance of LDPC codes by polynomial of cycles based on PEG and connectivity of cycles. It can reduce the number of the shortest cycles, increase average local girth and improve connectivity of cycles while deleting short cycles by considering girth of check matrix, number of the shortest cycles and ACE (Approximate Cycle EMD), so that it can improve performance of decoding. At the same time, a construction method of QC-LDPC (quasi-cyclic-LDPC) codes based on ACE and polynomial of cycles of PEG is also proposed to deal with the complexity of implementation on hardware of unstructured codes. Simulation results show that the performance of this code is better than unstructured Mackay code and it is easier for implementation on hardware.
Keywords- LDPC codes;PEG; ACE;Polynomial of Cycle
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were firstly proposed by Gallager in the early 1960s [1]. But they were not rediscovered until 1990s by Mackay [2], and proved to be good codes for the performance close to Shannon limit. LDPC codes began to be a research hotspot of channel coding from then on. LDPC codes are mainly modified and constructed by graphs and algebra.
Progressive-Edge-Growth (PEG) algorithm was proposed by Xiao-yu Hu based on idea of maximizing girth [3], which maximizes the girth of new added edge by constructing subgraph edge by edge, expanding the tree subgraph of valuable node to its fullest extent, and looking for the farthest check node from this valuable node. This algorithm also has been considered as the optimal method for constructing LDPC codes under the dual finite field. Lei Xiong proposed a PEGPC algorithm [4], which was added the coefficient of polynomial of cycle on the basis of PEG. The method of PEGPC for constructing tree subgraph is as follows: To assign a polynomial of cycle to each node of tree subgraph. The optimal node is the minimum polynomial of check nodes in the maximum level, because minor polynomial leads to minor cycles. Irregular LDPC codes constructed by PEG or modified PEG have good performance, but their check matrices are completely random, which are complex for implementations of encoder and decoder. Structured LDPC codes have excellent

performance, and the complexity of codecs is lower than the unstructured code so that they were concerned by researchers. Quasi-cyclic (quasi-cyclic, QC) LDPC code is one of structured codes. Because the code is semi-structured block matrix structured, it is easy for hardware implementation, and also has excellent error correction performance.
Existing methods for construction, only take elimination of short cycles into account mostly to improve the performance, which often lead to a higher error floor. Only a few methods take Approximate Cycle EMD (ACE) of cycles into account [5]. However, most constructions considering the distribution of ACE only consider the maximization of the distribution of ACE, so finally check matrix could not effectively balance girth and distribution of ACE. This paper draws on these constructions, and we propose a new construction method for LDPC codes. The method is based on PEGPC algorithm, adding the idea of ACE, without affecting the idea of maximum girth, optimizing ACE of check matrix to the greatest extent, to constraint the shortest girths of the check matrix, the average local girth and the distribution of ACE scientifically. And it can construct the structured and unstructured LDPC codes of an arbitrary degree distribution and arbitrary code length.

The concept of stopping sets was proposed by Changyan Di, and they discovered that performance of decoding was mostly limited by the smallest stopping sets under binary erasure channel [7]. Stopping set is a set of variable nodes, in which all the neighbor nodes are connected with it at least twice. And extend it to AWGN channel, stopping sets of smaller dimension impact on the decoding performance at the same. Therefore, increasing the stopping sets of the smallest dimensions can improve decoding performance of LDPC codes, but there is no effective construction method can control stopping sets distribution well. It proposed the concept of ACE in paper [5]. It can reduce small dimension distribution number of stopping sets by increasing the value of ACE to improve expansion of cycle, and then improve the decoding performance. The PEGPC-ACE algorithm is a modified algorithm based on PEGPC, which is added the idea of connectivity of cycle. It adds values of ACE as constraints to lower error floor of decoding when constructing a new edge in Tanner graph. The check matrices constructed by this algorithm satisfy the following conditions:

As Property 2, every girth-2l block cycle formed by shifting coefficient matrix P would lead to girth-2nl cycles (n is positive integer) of check matrix H. Therefore, improving girth of shifting coefficient matrix P can help to expand girth of check matrix correspondingly. The difference between the method proposed in this paper for constructing QC-LDPC codes and others is that it uses PEGPC algorithm to construct basis matrix, and uses ACE algorithm to optimize it. That is because PEGPC forms less cycles when constructing basis matrix, so that the exchange of cycle number would be less when constructing shifting coefficient matrix. Although PEG and PEGPC both take maximum girth as their constructing ideas, but it still would cause short cycles when the dimension of matrix is small. So we optimize the girth of check matrix and remove short cycles by constructing proper shifting coefficient matrix, to make the girths longer than 6 in this paper.
The steps are as follows: ķ Set 0 elements to -1 and 1 elements to 0 of basis
matrix Hb, and then we got the initial shifting coefficient matrix P0; ĸ 0 elements in the first row of P0 remain unchanged, but the 0 elements in the second row are assigned positive integers in order. -1 elements in the these rows remain unchanged; Ĺ Starting from the third row, use Equation (6) to detect if there are girth-6 formed, if no remain unchanged, else generate a random number p (1İp İA) to replace the 0 element, and then use Equation (6) to detect if there are still short cycles (4 and6); ĺ if yes, generate another random number to replace it, till there are no short cycles. If we could not remove short cycles within certain times, then go back to the preceding row, re-select the shifting coefficient. If there are still short cycles, go back to the preceding row above and so on, till we get shifting coefficient matrix P; Ļ get check matrix H from shifting coefficient matrix P. The difference of constructing shifting coefficients between this paper and others is that it retains 0 elements of initial shifting coefficient matrix as many as possible, which is also to retain the large cycles of good performance in basis matrix.

V. SIMULATIONS AND ANALYSIS

LDPC codes of different code length and degrees are constructed in this paper and compared with LDPC codes constructed by PEGPC and other classic random LDPC codes by simulations of BER (bit error rate) performances. Parameters of these simulations are as follows: the channel is a binary input AWGN channel; use belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm as decoding algorithm; the maximum number of iterations is 50. When it reaches 200 decoding errors at each SNR, terminate the simulation, and calculate BER.
Simulation 1: PEGPC-LDPC codes constructed with the same parameters are compared with PEGPC-LDPC codes in paper [4]. The girths of PEGPC-LDPC codes constructed in this paper are 8 and 10, and the average local girth is 8.07, which are basically the same as paper [4]. VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a random construction algorithm of LDPC codes is proposed and applied to construct QC-LDPC codes. Simulation results show that: the performance of random LDPC codes constructed by PEGPC-ACE is better than PEG and PEGPC. Furthermore, QC-LDPC codes constructed in this paper have better performance than random MacKay codes. As a structured encoding method, it is easier for hardware implementations of actual communication systems.
