Construction of protograph LDPC codes with circular generator matrices

Kaiyao Wang1,*, Yang Xiao1, and Kiseon Kim2
1. Institute of Information Science, Beijing Jiaotong University, Beijing 100044, P. R. China; 2. Department of Information and Communications, Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology, Gwangju 500-712, Korea

Abstract: The application of protograph low density parity check (LDPC) codes involves the encoding complexity problem. Since the generator matrices are dense, and if the positions of “1” s are irregularity, the encoder needs to store every “1” of the generator matrices by using huge chip area. In order to solve this problem, we need to design the protograph LDPC codes with circular generator matrices. A theorem concerning the circulating property of generator matrices of nonsingular protograph LDPC codes is proposed. The circulating property of generator matrix of nonsingular protograph LDPC codes can be obtained from the corresponding quasi-cyclic parity check matrix. This paper gives a scheme of constructing protograph LDPC codes with circulating generator matrices, and it reveals that the fast encoding algorithm of protograph LDPC codes has lower encoding complexity under the condition of the proposed theorem. Simulation results in additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels show that the bit error rate (BER) performance of the designed codes based on the proposed theorem is much better than that of GB20600 LDPC codes and Tanner LDPC codes.
Keywords: protograph low density parity check (LDPC) codes, cyclic matrices, fast encoding algorithm, bit error rate (BER) performance.

1. Introduction
Low density parity check (LDPC) codes were proposed by Gallager in 1962 [1]. This coding technique was largely forgotten for more than 30 years. They were highlighted by the rediscovery of LDPC codes in the field of channel coding in the mid-1990s [2,3]. After 1996 there have been many contributions to the design and analysis of LDPC codes [4−12]. LDPC codes have been the standard channel coding in standards: GB20600 [13], digital video broadcasting (DVB)-S2 [14], 802.16e [15], the

consultative committee for space data systems (CCSDS) [16,17].
Recently a flurry of work has been conducted on the design of LDPC codes with imposed substructures, starting with the introduction of multi-edge-type codes in [18]. Thorpe proposed a new class of LDPC codes constructed from the protograph in [19], called protograph LDPC codes. The protograph is a Tanner graph with a relatively small number of nodes. A “copy-and-permute” operation [19] can be applied to the protograph to obtain the larger derived graphs of various sizes. LDPC codes with protograph structure are a subclass of multi-edge-type LDPC codes [20]. Protograph LDPC codes have the advantages of high-speed decoding, low error floor and low iterative decoding threshold [21−28].
While, the protograph LDPC codes in [21−28] are not sure to have the circulating property of generator matrices, and the encoder needs to store every “1” of the generator matrices by using huge chip area, which leads the encoding complexity problem. In order to reduce the encoding complexity, we propose a theorem concerning the circulating property of generator matrices of nonsingular protograph LDPC codes, and it shows that the block cyclic generator matrices of nonsingular protograph LDPC codes can be obtained from the corresponding quasi-cyclic parity check matrix. We give a scheme of constructing protograph LDPC codes with circulating generator matrices. And it verifies that the fast encoding algorithm of protograph LDPC codes under the condition of the proposed theorem can simplify encoder hardware implementation.
2. Protograph LDPC codes
A protograph G = (V, C, E) consists of a set of variable nodes V , a set of check nodes C, and a set of edges E. Each edge e ∈ E connects a variable node ve ∈ V to a check node ce ∈ C. Parallel edges are permitted, so the mapping e → (ve, ce) ∈ V × C is not necessary to be 1:1.

We can obtain a larger graph by a “copy-and-permute” operation, illustrated in Fig. 2 and Fig. 3. In Fig. 2, the protograph has been copied three times. The three copies are overlaid so that same-type vertices are in close proximity, but the overall graph consists of three disconnected sub graphs. In Fig. 3, the endpoints of the three copies of each edge in the protograph have been permuted among the three copies of the corresponding variable and check nodes. After this swapping of endpoints of edges, the three sub graphs are now interconnected. The graph in Fig. 3 is the Tanner graph of an LDPC codes (n=12, k=3). We call this graph to be the derived graph, and the corresponding codes to be protograph LDPC codes.

5. Conclusions
In order to reduce the encoding complexity, we propose a theorem concerning the circulating property of gener-

ator matrix of nonsingular protograph LDPC codes and prove that the quasi-cyclic parity check matrix of protograph LDPC codes can obtain a block cyclic generator matrix if the codes have nonsingular and circulating parity check matrices. We propose a fast encoding algorithm of protograph LDPC codes based on the theorem; the fast encoding algorithm can simplify encoder hardware. Simulation results in AWGN channels show that the BER performance of the protograph LDPC codes are better about 0.5 dB than GB20600 LDPC codes and about 0.6 dB than Tanner LDPC codes.
