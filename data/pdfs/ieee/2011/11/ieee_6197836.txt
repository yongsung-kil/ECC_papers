A Class of (3, k) Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes from Difference Sequences with Girth 8

Bing Li1, Lijun Zhang1 and Lee Lung Cheng2 1School of Electronic and Information Engineering, Beijing Jiaotong University, Beijing, 100044, P. R. China
bjbuptlb@gmail.com, ljzhang@bjtu.edu.cn 2Department of Electronic Engineering, City University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong, P. R. China

Abstractâ€”An approach for constructing a class of (3, k)-regular quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes is proposed, which is based on combinatorial objects termed difference sequences. By an efficient algorithm for searching good difference sequences, codes in this class have girth at least eight. Simulation results show that the codes slightly outperform the counterpart PEG codes and have better performance than the corresponding MacKay codes and array codes.
Keywords-difference sequences; quasi-cyclic low-density paritycheck codes; girth.
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes, first discovered by Gallager [1] and rediscovered by MacKay [2], are currently the most promising coding technique to achieve Shannon capacity for a wide range of channels [3]. As a result, construction of high performance LDPC codes has been a hotspot of research in LDPC codes. A binary LDPC code C is defined by the null space of a sparse parity-check matrix H over GF(2). Most methods for designing good LDPC codes are random construction, but algebraically structured LDPC codes may be needed for practical implementation purposes.
In the case of general LDPC codes of large code length, a significant amount of memory is required to store their paritycheck matrices. As an attractive subclasss of structured LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes, whose parity check matrices consist of circulant matrices or zero matrices, are getting more attention due to their easy implementation and other graceful features [4]. Thus, QC-LDPC codes may be a good candidate to solve the memory problem due to their algebraic structures. Recently, several classes of QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices (CPMs) have been proposed [5]-[10]. These codes perform quite well compared with random LDPC codes.
In this paper, based on a kind of combinatorial design, i.e., difference sequences, we present a new class of (3, k)-regular QC-LDPC codes, called DS-LDPC codes. The girth, which is defined as the length of the shortest cycles in Tanner graph [11] of the codeâ€™s corresponding parity-check matrix H, is one of key factors affecting the performance of an LDPC code. Since a relatively large girth can help to improve the BER performance [7], a searching algorithm aimed at finding good

difference sequences to ensure that the DS-LDPC codes have girth at least eight is proposed.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section II provides the structure of the DS-LDPC codes. Some definitions and a searching algorithm for the DS-LDPC codes to have girth at least eight are given in Section III. Section IV presents some examples and the computer simulation results. Finally, some concluding remarks are given in section V.

V. CONCLUSION
A method for constructing a class of (3, k) QC-LDPC codes based on difference sequences is presented. Taking full advantage of the properties of difference sequences derived from the quadratic polynomials, we can make sure that the associated Tanner graph of the parity check matrix has a girth of at least 6. By the searching algorithm, DS-LDPC codes with girth 8 can be further obtained. Simulation results show that the codes perform well over AWGN channel with iterative

decoding in comparison with the pseudo-random PEG codes, the random MacKay codes and structured array codes. Moreover, the DS-LDPC codes exhibit no sign of error floor down to the BER of 10-8.
