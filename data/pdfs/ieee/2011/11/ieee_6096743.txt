Construction of Irregular Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes
Based on Euclidean Geometries
Xueqin Jiang, Moon Ho Lee , Xiaofei Liao and Ying Guo
Abstract—This paper presents a method to the construction
of irregular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes based on
Euclidean geometries over the Galois field. Codes constructed
by this method are quasi-cyclic (QC) and have large girths.
The proposed irregular LDPC codes having flexible column/row
weights are obtained with a hyperplane decomposing method
in Euclidean geometries. Therefore, the degree distributions of
proposed irregular LDPC codes can be optimized by technologies
like the curve fitting approach in the extrinsic information
transfer (EXIT) charts. Simulation results show that these codes
perform very well with an iterative decoding over the AWGN
channel.
Index Terms—Euclidean geometry, 𝜇-flats, parallel bundles,
column decomposing, LDPC, EXIT chart.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, first proposed in
the early 1960’s by Gallager [1] and re-discovered in 1996 [2],
have recently attracted much attention due to their capacity-
approaching performance and low decoding complexity. As an
attractive class of LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes
are well suitable for certain practical applications since they
can easily be encoded and decoded with linear complexity [3].
Richardson et al. [4] showed that irregular LDPC codes
with carefully chosen degree distributions perform very close
to the Shannon limit on the erasure channel. Empirically,
the same is true for other channels. To find good irregular
LDPC codes, a powerful tool called extrinsic information
transfer (EXIT) chart was introduced in [5] for optimizing the
degree distributions in the Tanner graphs. It is stated in [5]
that, to optimize the check/variable node degree distributions
in the Tanner graph of irregular LDPC codes for a given
code rate, the parity-check matrix should have at least three
different column/row weights. As column/row weight defines
variable/check node degree distribution and variable/check
node degree distribution corresponds to column/row weight,
we use the terms variable/check node degree distribution and
column/row weight interchangeably.
Methods for the construction of regular QC LDPC codes
based on Euclidean geometries have been proposed in [3][6-
9]. By column/row splitting [3][6], these regular QC LDPC
codes can be extended into irregular LDPC codes. However,
the resulting irregular codes have at least two drawbacks: 1)
They are not quasi-cyclic, therefore the encoding and decoding
complexity is high; 2) There are only two different column/row
weights and regular distribution (not adjustable) in parity-
check matrices, therefore, their degree distributions can not
be optimized by tools like EXIT charts. In this paper, by a
hyperplane decomposing method, irregular QC LDPC codes
are obtained. Compared to the geometric irregular codes in
[3][6-8], the proposed irregular LDPC codes have two advan-
tages: 1) They are quasi-cyclic; 2) They have flexible column
and row weight distributions by hyperplane decomposing in
Euclidean geometries. We employ the EXIT chart technique
to find an optimal degree distribution for our irregular QC
LDPC codes. We also demonstrate further that our proposed
codes perform very well over he additive white Gaussian noise
(AWGN) channel with an iterative decoding algorithm.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section
II gives a brief review of Euclidean geometries and introduces
three steps to construct QC LDPC codes. Irregular QC LDPC
codes are constructed in Section III. Section IV introduces an
approach to find an optimization degree distribution for the
proposed irregular QC LDPC codes. Code design examples
and simulation results are given in section V. Section VI
concludes the paper.

III. CONSTRUCTION OF IRREGULAR QC LDPC CODES
In this section, we proposed the column decomposing and
row decomposing methods which are based on the hyperplane
decomposing. By hyperplane decomposing, a base matrix 𝐵of
the non-optimized column and row weight distribution can be
adjusted into an irregular base matrix ¯𝐵of optimized column
and row weight distribution. From ¯𝐵we design the shift
matrix 𝑆and finally obtain an irregular parity-check matrix
𝐻of the optimized column and row weight distribution.
A. Column Decomposing and Row Decomposing
It is easy to see that if 𝜇1 < 𝜇2 −1, any column of 𝐵,
which is corresponding to a 𝜇2-flat, can be replaced with 𝑝𝑠
columns, which are corresponding to a (𝜇2 −1)-flat parallel
bundle, without changing the row weight of 𝐵. Therefore, we
can see that, if 𝜇1 < 𝜇2 −1, each column of column weight
𝑁(𝜇2, 𝜇1, 𝑠, 𝑝) of 𝐵can be replaced with 𝑝𝑠columns of col-
umn weight 𝑁(𝜇2−1, 𝜇1, 𝑠, 𝑝). Likewise, if 𝜇1 < 𝜇2−2, each
column of column weight 𝑁(𝜇2 −1, 𝜇1, 𝑠, 𝑝) can be replaced
with 𝑝𝑠columns of column weight 𝑁(𝜇2 −2, 𝜇1, 𝑠, 𝑝). Since
𝜇2-flats are decomposed into parallel (𝜇2−1)-flats, the weight
of each row is not changed during the column decomposing.
In the similar way, if 0 ≤𝜇1 −1, each 𝜇1-flat can be
decomposed into 𝑝𝑠parallel (𝜇1 −1)-flats. Therefore, each
row can be decomposed into 𝑝𝑠rows. The weight of each
column is not changed during the row decomposing.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a method to the construction of large girth
irregular QC LDPC codes based on Euclidean geometries have
been presented. Unlike the previous geometric irregular LDPC
codes, codes proposed in this paper can be optimized with
the EXIT chart. To get a good error floor performance, we
limit the fraction of degree-2 variable nodes. Due to the quasi-
cyclic structure, our codes have low encoding and decoding
complexities. Simulation results show that our codes perform
very well and have low error floor with the log-likelihood
belief propagation algorithm over the AWGN channel. This
suggests that the new codes have large minimum distances.
Their exact computation is an open issue for future research.
