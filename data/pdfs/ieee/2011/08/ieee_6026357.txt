Abstract-NAND Flash memory controllers need to equip strong 
and high speed error correction blocks as the cell size scales 
down and multi-level cell technology is employed. We have 
developed an LDPC (low-density parity-check) decoder for 
NAND Flash memory error correction, and implemented it 
using a layered min-sum decoding architecture. A shortened 
(69615,66897) regular EG-LDPC code that has the code rate of 
96% is used, which has a good minimum distance and quasi­
cyclic structure. In order to increase the decoding throughput 
and reduce the chip area, the word-length reduction of variable­

I. 
INTRODUCTION 
NAND Flash memory has the characteristics of high 
capacity and low power consumption, and hence is used in 
many hand-held devices such as mobile phones and solid-state 
drives. MLC (multi-level cell) Flash memory architecture 
stores multiple, typically two, bits in a cell to increase the 
storage density, however their cell error characteristic is much 
worse than that of SLC (single-level cell) Flash memory [1]. 
Codes with a small error-correcting capability, such as the 
Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) code, have been used 
for the MLC NAND Flash [2]. However, as the device size 
scales down continuously, strong error correcting codes such 
as low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are now needed in 
MLC NAND Flash [3]. Especially, LDPC based decoding can 
employ soft-input error correction, which usually shows a 
much better error correction performance than hard-input error 
correction. 
This work was supported in part by the Ministry of Education, Science 
and Technology (MEST), Republic of Korea, under the Brain Korea 21 
Project. 
In this paper, we propose an area-efficient and high-speed 
LDPC decoder for NAND Flash memories. The proposed 
decoder employs Euclidean geometry (EG)-LDPC codes 
which have good error-correcting performance and adequate 
structural properties for hardware implementation. The 
proposed decoder applies word-length optimization and 
message compression schemes to reduce the chip area because 
EG-LDPC codes have relatively high degree of variable and 
check nodes, which results in large memory requirement for 
storing extrinsic messages. Furthermore, we develop an 
optimized fixed-point arithmetic scheme for layered decoding. 
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. 
Section II introduces EG-LDPC codes and the decoding 
algorithm of LDPC codes. The architecture of a layered min­
sum LDPC decoder and its optimization methods are present­
ed in Section III. Section IV provides VLSI implementation 
results. Finally Section V concludes this paper.

A. EG-LDPC Codes 
EG-LDPC codes are algebraically generated and form a 
class of geometric LDPC codes. EG-LDPC codes show good 
error performance with iterative decoding and they are 
advantageous for hardware implementation because their 
parity-check matrices have a cyclic or quasi-cyclic form [4]. 
In this work, a (69615, 66897) regular EG-LDPC code is used, 
which consists of 17 4095 x4095 sub-matrices as shown in Fig. 
1. Each sub-matrix has a cyclic structure and its row and 
column weights are both 16, and hence the variable and check 
degrees of the parity-check matrix are 16 and 272, 
respectively. 
Since the message length of the (69615, 66897) EG-LDPC 
code does not match with the 8kB page size of NAND Flash 
memory, appropriate shortening is required. For this purpose,

a result 80 variables in each sub-matrix, while 81 variables for 
the last one, are not processed. Furthermore, since the page

the employed parallel and pipe lined decoder. Due to the nature 
of layered decoding, the path delay is much longer than that of 
the conventional flooding schedule. Therefore, pipe lining is 
essential to reduce the path delay. Furthermore, parallel 
architecture is required to increase the decoding throughput. 
In addition to pipelined parallel architecture, the word­
length reduction and the message compression schemes are 
applied to minimize the size of memory (SRAM). Further­
more, fixed-point arithmetic optimization for node processing 
is applied to mitigate the effect of quantization error.

IV. 
IMPLEMENTATION RESULTS 
A layered min-sum LDPC decoder was implemented with 
130nm 6LM CMOS technology and Synopsys tools. Table I 
shows the implementation results of the serial and the 
proposed architecture. The SRAM area storing extrinsic 
messages is reduced by 81 %, while that of APP memory 
remains unchanged. Due to the parallelization, the area of 
combinational logic such as that of the node processing units 
and the minimum detectors increases by six times, which 
occupies only 10% of the total area. Finally, the total cell area 
is reduced by 38% compared to the serial architecture. The 
critical path delay of the proposed decoder is 5.31ns, which 
gives the maximum clock frequency of 172MHz and the 
maximum decoding throughput of 6.24Gb/s. 
V. 
CONCLUSION 
In this paper, the architecture of a layered min-sum LDPC 
decoder for NAND Flash memories is presented. In order to 
increase the decoding performance and reduce the cell area, 
the word-length reduction, data compression, and fixed-point 
arithmetic optimization techniques are applied besides 
employing the widely used pipe lining and parallelization 
architecture. The proposed layered min-sum decoder achieves 
the maximum decoding throughput of 6.24Gb/s with 48mm2 
cell area using a 0.13um CMOS process and shows good 
error-correcting performance close to the floating-point 
implementation.
