Analysis of Message-Passing Decoding of
Finite-Length Concatenated Codes
Kai Yang and Xiaodong Wang
Abstract—We analyze the performance of message-passing de-
coding of finite-length concatenated codes. We first show that the
message-passing decoder is closely related to a dual optimization
decoder. The connections between these two decoders are further
elucidated by proving that both of them attain the same objective
function value of a generalized linear programming decoder
in the limit as the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) goes to infinity.
Consequently, the framework of pseudo-weight analysis, which
was originally proposed for analyzing the linear programming
decoder, can be extended to analyze the performance of the
message-passing decoder for finite-length codes. We then derive
lower bounds to the pseudo-weights of general concatenated
codes by utilizing the special structure of their parity-check
matrices. We finally present a method to increase the max-
fractional weight by adding redundant parity-check constraints
and thereby improving the decoding performance. Simulation
studies are carried out to assess the performance of the proposed
algorithms and substantiate the theoretic claims.
Index Terms—Pseudo-weight analysis, linear block codes, con-
catenated codes, message-passing algorithm, linear programming
decoding, max-fractional weight, dual optimization.
I. INTRODUCTION
T
HE celebrated message-passing (MP) algorithm [1] was
originally proposed as a simple heuristic method for
decoding low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. It can be
viewed as an iterative algorithm over a sparse bipartite graph,
and it is optimal if the graph has a tree-like structure. Later it
was found that the MP algorithm is closely related to the belief
propagation algorithm, which was developed as a statistical
inference technique over graphical models [2].
Despite its empirical good performance, the MP algorithm
remains difficult to analyze. Important progresses were made
in [3], [4], where tools based on Gaussian approximation,
i.e., the density evolution techniques and extrinsic information
transfer (EXIT) chart were developed to characterize the MP
decoding performance for parallel concatenated codes in the
limit of infinite block length. The key idea of the EXIT chart

number, namely, the extrinsic information, and messages from
different components are independent of each other. However,
this assumption holds only for infinite block-length codes
which renders this technique not applicable to codes with
short to medium block lengths. Consequently, it remains a
significant challenge to develop a rigorous analytical method
that is capable of analyzing the MP decoding performance for

To cope with this long-standing challenge, the pseudo-
codeword analysis has emerged as a promising approach
for the finite-length analysis of MP decoders. Traditionally,
pseudo-codewords are derived from the computation tree of
the MP decoder [5]. However, due to its combinatorial nature,
it is in general difficult to describe all such pseudo-codewords
and so far this method is only applicable to very simple codes.
In the presence of such difficulty, an alternative approach
was proposed in [6] to describe the pseudo-codewords by
a convex set termed as the fundamental polytope. In such
a context, pseudo-codewords correspond to all vertices of
the fundamental polytope. Moreover, it has been shown in
[6] that the weight of a pseudo-codeword, i.e., the pseudo-
weight, offers a useful metric to characterize the MP decoding
performance. At about the same time and independently, the
concept of linear programming decoding appeared in [7],
which essentially presents the same idea as those in [6].
This approach is further extended in [8], [9] where it is
shown that any fundamental polytope can be represented in a
cascaded form, which significantly simplifies the description
of a fundamental polytope.

B. Dual Optimization Decoding
The LP decoder can be viewed as a close relative to the
MP decoder. And it has been shown that the performance
of an LP decoder can offer a good approximation to that of
an MP decoder, especially at high SNR [11]. Characterizing
the performance an LP decoder is tantamount to analyzing of
the vertices (pseudo-codeword) of its fundamental polytope,
i.e., the convex hull 𝑐𝑜𝑛𝑣(Ω1) ∩𝑐𝑜𝑛𝑣(Ω2)...𝑐𝑜𝑛𝑣(Ω𝑀). Most
recent work on pseudo-codeword analysis focused on the
LDPC codes, because the convex hull of the component code,
i.e., the single parity-check code, can be readily obtained [7],
[9]. On the other hand, the MP decoder has been widely used
to decode a number of other concatenated codes such as the
generalized LDPC code. These concatenated codes are built
from more sophisticated component codes and the convex hull
of their component codes can be very difficult to obtain. It is
not immediately clear how the pseudo-codeword analysis can
be extended to analyze the performance of these codes. To this
end, we propose a dual optimization decoding framework. We
show that the solution to this dual optimization problem is in a
one-to-one correspondence with that of a so-called generalized
LP (GLP) decoding problem, which can be viewed as a natural
extension of the LP decoder. The connections between the dual
decomposition decoder, the MP decoder, and the GLP decoder
are further elucidated by proving the asymptotic equivalence
between them in the high SNR limit.

decoder over (𝒰, ℱ) is equal to that of the GLP decoder.
Hence, the above three decoders attain the same objective
function value in the limit as SNR goes to infinity.
Notice that both the LP decoder, dual optimization decoder,
and the MP decoders are approximations or relaxations of the
ML decoder. Hence the solutions obtained by these decoders
are approximate solutions (pseudo-codewords) to the original
ML decoding problem. As widely used in optimization theory,
the objective function value is a useful metric to judge the
quality of an approximate solution. More importantly, the
objective function value is related to an important metric of
the decoding performance, i.e., the fractional distance, which
motivates us to state the above theorem.

We finally compare the performance of an MP decoder
for the (3,4) LDPC code with length 60 operating over
the original parity-check matrix and a parity-check matrix
with 15 redundant constraints generated by Algorithm 4.
The MP decoder operating over a parity-check matrix with
redundant parity-check constraints is termed as the improved
MP decoder. As evident from Fig. 4, such an improved MP
decoder can considerably reduce the BER compared with the
original MP decoder. The complexity of the improved MP
decoder increases only linearly with the number of redundant
constraints. Also, notice that the choice of the redundant
parity-check constraints is crucial to the performance of an
MP decoder. A randomly generated redundant parity check
constrains might deteriorate the decoding performance, since
such a redundant constraint could introduce loops into the
parity-check matrix.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
We have presented a dual optimization decoder for decod-
ing linear block codes. The connections between the dual
optimization decoder, the GLP decoder and the MP decoder
have been revealed by proving the asymptotic equivalence
among them at high SNR. Hence, the performance of these
decoders can be analyzed under the framework of pseudo-
weight analysis. We have derived lower bounds to the max-
fractional weight and pseudo-weights of concatenated codes.
We also have developed a systematic approach to improving
the code performance and reducing error floor by increasing
the max-fractional weight of the parity-check matrix.
