A Simple Method to Construct LDPC Codes Based on Projective Planes

Sergei Kovalev
Department of Information Systems State University on Aerospace Instrumentation

Abstract-We consider a simple description of the incidence matrix of a projective plane over the field GF(q), using multiplication and addition tables of the finite field. Some basic properties of LDPC codes derived from such incidence matrices are discussed. Further, a class of LDPC codes of paper 111, derived from Reed-Solomon (RS) codes with two information
symbols, is considered. By comparing these two classes, we give
a simple equivalence proof for them.

Keywords - finite projective planes; LDPC codes; Reed­ Solomon codes; circulant matrices.

I.

INTRODUCTION

LDPC codes based on projective planes present an

interesting class of LDPC codes with (J, L)-regular structure

(i.e. which are described by parity check matrices having J

ones in any column, and L ones in any row). Such LDPC

codes were earlier considered in many publications (see e.g.

[2] [3D. The resulting codes are found to have interesting

properties and they have capacity-approaching performance.

In particular, some good quasi-cyclic LDPC codes were

derived based on this approach. Another interesting class of

LDPC codes, which is constructed based on RS codes with

two information symbols, was proposed in [1]. This class also

has (J, L)-regular structure and it demonstrates attractive

performance over AWGN. In this paper, we compare these

two classes of codes. By describing the incidence matrix of

projective plane via multiplication and addition tables of fmite

field [4], we prove that both classes are equivalent. Further on,

comparison of our results with closely related results [8],[9] is

provided.

VI. CONCLUSIOINS
In this paper, we propose a new, simple description of
FG(2, q)- based Gallager-type LOPC codes, and we further show their equivalence toLDPC codes constructed from RS
[q - 1,2, q - 2]-codes over GF(q). A nice algebraic structure of sub-matrices in parity-check matrix construction is established. Further, some examples are provided.
After the paper went into review, the reviewers have drawn our attention to papers [7] and [8], both of which establish relations between the RS-based LOPC codes and LOPC codes derived from finite affine planes. Thus, in [7] it is shown that an RS-based LDPC code formed from an
extended RS [q, 2, q - 1] -code over GF(q) , q = pS , is equivalent to a Gallager-type LDPC code formed from
incidence vectors of lines in EG(2, q). In the second paper
[8], the author shows that an EG(2, q)- based Gallager-type
LOPC code is equivalent to a constacyclic MOS [q + 1,2, q]­ code. In his construction, the LOPC parity check matrix is
build from (q + 1) - circulants. In our paper, we show
equivalence between FG(2, q) - based Gallager-type LDPC
codes and RS [q - 1,2, q - 2] -codes over GF(q) . Our approach is different from those described in papers [7]-[8], and it results in a simple description of the parity check
matrix via q -permutation matrices that have nice algebraic structure. Expression (2) also provides a simple description of the full-length parity check matrix ofLDPC code based on
FG(2, q), in which an extra q -bit column band and a single bit colunm extend the full-length RS-based LDPC code. Finally, it is worth to mention that the proposed method could be used as a basis for different generalizations: for example, forLOPC parity check matrix construction we can use addition and multiplication tables of a finite ring, or use a pair of multiplicative and additive groups with respective mappings, instead.
