Abstract—Greedy algorithms are proposed for fast decoding of linear block codes over a binary symmetric channel. They are motivated by matching pursuit schemes developed in compressive sensing. Theoretical guarantees are provided for regular LDPC codes. The algorithms are highly efficient, as they only require vector–matrix multiplications and mostly use binary arithmetic. Their complexity is completely determined and depends on the code’s block length and a sparsity parameter. The experimental results validate the performance of the proposed algorithms.

I. INTRODUCTION

The decoding efficiency of linear block codes employed in

the transmission over noisy communication channels is of high

importance. In the case of a binary symmetric channel (BSC)

with crossover probability p, maximum likelihood decoding is

high data rates. Low–density parity–check (LDPC) codes [5],

and other known families like Bose–Chaudhuri–Hocquenghem

(BCH) and Reed–Solomon (RS) codes, further constrain the

code structure to allow for decoding algorithms of polynomial

complexity (e.g. the message passing and Berlekamp–Massey

algorithms). To this end, we demonstrate that low–complexity

iterative algorithms, based on concepts that have been used by

greedy algorithms in the context of compressed sensing, can

be developed for the efficient decoding of linear block codes,

with favorable decoding performance.

Compressed sensing (CS) is concerned, among others, with

sparse linear representations of vectors in a finite–dimensional

space [3]. Given a dictionary —a finite collection of vectors

spanning the whole space— sparse approximation algorithms

can recover any vector that admits a sparse representation in

terms of the dictionary vectors, provided that the dictionary

is sufficiently incoherent. Matching pursuit (MP), orthogonal

matching pursuit (OMP), and subspace pursuit (SP) are low–

complexity representatives of this class [2], [10], [14], [15]. A

considerable amount of research has been devoted to the study

of good dictionaries that guarantee the performance of greedy

algorithms. Popular examples are random Gaussian and ran-

dom Bernoulli matrices. More general admissible classes are

defined in [15]. Recent attention has been given to dictionaries

associated with linear codes. LDPC codes with their random like behavior is a notable example. It is established in [7], [11], [12] that sparse parity–check matrices of LDPC codes satisfy the restricted isometry property (RIP) and therefore boost the performance of greedy algorithms. Along these lines, expander graphs are employed in [16] and algorithms are developed for compressive sampling and the recovery of sparse signals from a small set of measurements.
In this paper, the ideas underlying MP methods are applied in the context of decoding linear block codes. In particular, we propose two greedy algorithms, the syndrome MP (SMP) and its aggressive version called aSMP, that mostly perform finite field (binary) operations; both algorithms search among the columns of the code’s parity–check matrix to find a good match to the syndrome vector. In this respect the proposed approach differs from the methods of [14], [15]. The approach taken by most authors [7], [12], [16] is to search among the rows of the parity–check matrix, defining a collection of measurements, and find a solution (at each iteration) maximizing the number of measurements satisfied. The algorithms proposed in [11], admit theoretical guarantees similar to the ones derived in this paper, but the dictionary they use (still based on LDPC codes) is the code itself. Although the basic algorithm SMP builds a solution one term at a time using a minimum distance criterion, its aggressive version aSMP applies this criterion only once, therefore having a much lower computational cost. Theoretical performance guarantees are established for both algorithms, in the case of regular LDPC codes, and depend on the weight of the parity–check matrix columns. Their complexity is linear in the signal dimension, and the number of measurements needed is of the same order as that for MP. The performance of SMP, aSMP is compared with the adaptive bit–flipping (BF) and the one–step majority– logic (MLG) algorithms, in hard–decision decoding of regular LDPC codes over the BSC channel. The experimental results demonstrate that SMP and aSMP have decoding performance comparable with, and in many cases better than, that of the BF and MLG algorithms.

III. GREEDY DECODING OF LDPC CODES

In this section, we develop two efficient greedy algorithms for decoding linear block codes, analyze their complexity, and prove theoretical performance guarantees in the case of regular LDPC codes. The theoretical analysis of the algorithms makes extensive use of the fact that no cycles of length 4 are present in the Tanner graph associated with the parity–check matrix H of the code C (i.e. the girth g of the graph satisfies g = 2l, for some l ≥ 3) [8]. Thus, two columns (or rows) of H have at most one 1 in common.
The proposed decoding algorithms work on binary matrices and vectors, and operations are performed modulo 2, with the exception of correlation which is computed over the integers; this is opposed to standard practice (see e.g. [11], [16]), where s, e, H are real or complex valued (frequently, H is defined over {±1}) and algorithms operate with real arithmetic. As a consequence of our assumptions, there is no (straightforward) evidence that the theoretical framework laid down in the work of [14], [15] —which assures the convergence, under certain conditions, of greedy algorithms based on MP— is applicable. Since ties among candidate columns are resolved in a probabilistic manner, a detailed analysis on the probability of selecting a column from Φ should be performed; this is part of ongoing research work.

The complexity of the proposed algorithms rests with computing the correlations of the residual with the columns of H, and seeking for the largest ones. The standard way to compute s, hω requires O(m) operations. No multiplication is needed since all vectors are binary: the calculations reduce to counting the common elements in the supports of s, hω. As γ is equal to the weight of columns, we get a total of O(γn) operations. To locate the columns of maximum correlation, we need O(n) operations —the list of maximally correlated columns is built progressively while computing the correlations. By Theorem 1, we can take ν = O(γ) number of iterations. Hence, the total complexity of SMP, aSMP is O(γ2n) and O(γn) respectively for regular LDPC codes. Since γ is fixed, and usually γ n, we get O(n) for both algorithms.

V. CONCLUSIONS
Two matching pursuit based algorithms are proposed for the efficient decoding of linear block codes over binary symmetric channels. In the case of regular LDPC codes, both algorithms can correct all sparse error patterns with sparsity smaller than half the weight γ of the parity check columns. Block and bit error performance simulations indicate favorable comparison with fast LDPC decoding algorithms, such as the bit–flipping and majority–logic decoding. Their computational complexity depends on the complexity of searching for the optimal local solutions at each iteration.
Experiments indicate that the performance of the proposed decoding schemes is observed in other code families, besides the regular LDPC codes. Further extensions to soft–decision decoding over other channel models, such as the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, offer additional performance gains [6]. Variations incorporating a two–dimensional greedy decoding logic are also considered.
