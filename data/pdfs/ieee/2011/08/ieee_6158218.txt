A Novel Puncturing Scheme for Rate-Compatible 
Low-Density Parity-Check Codes 
Xi yuan Chen, Honglin Zhao, Yongkui Ma, Xiangnan Liu 
Communication Research Center 
Harbin Institute of Technology 
Harbin, China 
cyuanhit@gmail.com 
Abstract-In this paper, we present an effective puncturing scheme 
for rate-compatible low-density parity-check (RC-LDPC) codes. 
Our scheme is based on the criterion of maximum average girth 
which 
is 
an 
important 
performance 
parameter 
for 
the 
construction of RC-LDPC codes. We select a pattern wherein the 
punctured bits are far apart from each other in the Tanner graph 
of the codes by sorting algorithm, and then puncture the bits with 
the maximum average girth. In the process, there is no need to do 
any optimization. By simulations, the proposed codes show good 
performance under puncturing over a wide range of rates. The 
introduced puncturing criterion results in lower bit error rate and 
error floor when compared with random puncturing. Moreover, 
the performance of proposed method is similar to the direct 
structured LDPC codes by progressive edge growth (PEG). But 
the scheme is very simple to implement and lowers the complexity 
of encoding and decoding. 
Keywords-rate-compatible low-density parity-check (RC-LDPC) 
codes; puncturing; maximum average girth 
I. 
INTRODUCTION 
Error control measures are widely used in different states of 
channel using variable code rate, especially in certain time­
varying channels where channel state information (CSI) is 
available. We can not adopt a fixed rate code. Construction of 
flexible rate codes is needed so that one can encode at a higher 
rate when the channel becomes more reliable, while encode at a 
lower rate when the channel becomes worse, respectively. 
Puncturing is the most commonly used in the construction of 
error correcting codes. First design codes with a low code rate, 
and then puncture certain bits to gain a higher rate. We can use a 
pair of encoder/decoder to encode/decode, which lowers the 
complexity of instruments. 
Puncturing is a good technique that has been researched for 
various 
classes 
of 
error 
correcting 
codes 
(in 
specific 
convolutional codes, Turbo codes and BCH codes) [1], [2]. 
Convolutional codes and BCH codes are easy to implement, but 
they can not offer sufficient throughput. Turbo codes have good 
performance but with high complexity of decoding. LOPC codes 
have been extensively studied due to the capacity approaching 
property and the novel graph-based iterative decoding method, 
which have been adopted as an important element in newly

developed industrial standards, such as OVBS2, 3 GPP2, IEEE

this, Li and Narayanan proposed RC-LOPC codes and structured 
a serial of punctured LOPC codes from regular LOPC codes by 
puncturing and extending [5). Yazdani et al. [6] chose a mother 
code at a fixed rate that was in-between the range of required 
rates so that the excessive degradation because of puncturing to 
high rates was minimized. The authors adopted optimization to 
get irregular LOPC codes as mother code, and constructed a 
serial of RC-LOPC codes with the rates of 811 0, 8111,"', 8119 
by puncturing and extending. Oholakia et al. [7] acquired RC­
LOPC codes from array LOPC mother codes by adding 
probability of erasure and decreasing parity check equations, 
which was used in OSL. Ha and McLaughlin [8, 9] studied the 
convergence of punctured LOPC codes in theory and pointed 
out additional probability of erasure in decoding iteration 
process only depended on degree distribution of mother codes 
and erasure. When distribution of erasure was appropriately 
designed, additional probability of erasure converged to zero 
with the increase of decoding iterations. [5] considered the 
problem of designing a good rate-compatible ensemble of LOPC 
codes for a given range of rates. In [10], the authors found a 
positive correlation between the probability of successful 
recovery of a bit and the number of un-punctured nodes in the 
recovery tree of a node. Therefore, in [10], the authors used a 
grouping algorithm to partition the set of bit nodes into various 
groups. Within each group, they identified the order for 
puncturing based on their sorting algorithm. 
The proposed puncturing scheme in this paper adopts the 
idea of sorting algorithm in [10], but does not search for optimal 
nodes during the grouping algorithm. We do not need to 
consider the degree of the survived nodes. We can classifY our 
puncturing scheme that divides the set of bit nodes into groups. 
In the process of partitioning, the order of the selected nodes is 
random. The next round of selecting is based on the pre-round 
and its neighbors. 
The paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces RC­
LOPC codes and some common puncture schemes such as 
random erasure and intentional erasure. Section III presents our 
proposed puncturing scheme in detail. Section IV presents 
computer simulation results to demonstrate the effectiveness of 
the scheme. At last, section V concludes this paper.

V. 
CONCLUSIONS 
In this paper, we present a rate-compatible puncturing 
scheme for low-density parity-check codes. We design a novel 
algorithm, which punctures the bits far from each other in 
Tanner graph and maximizes the number of survived check 
nodes. It simplifies the grouping and sorting scheme in the rules 
of finding nodes and update. The proposed algorithm is simple 
to implement and quite different from previous schemes in the 
optimization on distribution of degree. Base on the maximum 
girth criterion, we provide a series of RC-LOPC codes for a 
wide range of supporting rates. By simulations, it is shown that 
the proposed scheme is superior to random puncture scheme at 
certain rates over A WGN channels. Very good RC-LOPC codes 
can be constructed by the scheme for practical communication 
systems at an affordable computational complexity. In addition, 
we note that the proposed RC LOPC code design may be 
suitable for other channel models.
