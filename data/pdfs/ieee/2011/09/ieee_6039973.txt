Efficient Degree Optimization for High-rate Structured QC-LDPC

Zhaoxia Zheng, Xiong Yin, Yan Hong
Department of Electronic Science and Technology HuaZhong University of Science and Technology
Wuhan, China yinxiong.yx@gmail.com

Yi Dan
Practical Teaching Center Wuhan Technical College of Communications
Wuhan, China

Abstract—In order to efficiently optimize degree distribution of high-rate structured QC-LDPC (SQC-LDPC), based on analysis to the special structure of SQC-LDPC, this paper modifies previous degree distribution optimization method. At first, it proposes a new set of global optimization constraints for Differential Evolution (DE) algorithm. Furthermore, when Gaussian Approximate (GA) is used to compute the threshold of a given degree distribution pair, binary search rather than usually used fixed step search is adopted to speed up degree distribution optimization. Simulation results indicate that proposed degree distribution optimization method can obtain good degree distribution pairs of high-rate SQC-LDPC with less iteration times, and that high-rate SQC-LDPC designed by good degree distribution pairs embraces good performance.
Keywords-degree distribution optimization; structured QCLDPC; Gaussian Approximate (GA); Differential Evolution (DE)
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC has been widely applied in wireless communication filed, e.g. DVB-S2, IEEE802.16e, IEEE802.11n and CCSDS and so forth. Designing LDPC with good performance has been increasingly attracting more and more researchers’ attention. Previous research shows that performance of LDPC is relative closely to three factors: degree distribution, girth characteristic of bipartite graph and their minimum Hamming distance. Among these three factors above, degree distribution plays a key role in determining performance LDPC [1]. Currently, it is practical and efficient to use following method to optimize degree distribution: first, compute threshold value of a given degree distribution pair via GA [2]; and then search the best one in all potential degree distribution pairs by global optimization algorithm, i.e. DE algorithm [3].
Almost all of previous methods to optimize degree distribution only focus on ordinary random LDPC without special requirements to degree distribution [1] [2]. However, as far as LDPC was concerned, all of the implementation-oriented ones are SQC-LDPC holding certain structure and comparative fixed degree distribution for variable nodes with degree 2 and 3 [4] [5]. And therefore previous methods aren’t able to be used to optimize degree distribution for SQC-LDPC, especially the high-rate ones. To solve this problem, this paper proposes a set of new constraints for DE algorithm. In addition, when the maximum degree of variable node is large, optimization

process is extremely time-consuming. In view of this point, binary search is proposed to replace fixed step search, which can save time dramatically.
The outline of this paper is as follows. In section Ⅱ, a class of prevailing structure of SQC-LDPC has been analyzed and its special requirement to degree distribution is presented. In section Ⅲ , we propose a set of new global optimization constraints to meet that special requirement. Moreover, binary search applied to compute threshold also is presented. In section Ⅳ, we list a table of optimized good degree distribution pairs. At the same time, SQC-LDPC based on a couple of good degree distribution pairs are simulated over a BIAWGN channel. Finally, concluding remarks are given in section Ⅴ.

II. SQC-LDPC’S SPECIAL REQUIREMENT TO DEGREE
DISTRIBUTION
QC-LDPC with modified dual diagonal structure, proposed in [5], is one of the most prevailing structures currently due to the fact that it not merely is encoded efficiently but also has good performance. For example, this type of SQC-LDPC is recommended by both IEEE802.16e [6] and IEEE802.11n [7] standard. In this paper, we take this type of SQC-LDPC as an example and mainly focus on it to present our degree distribution optimization method. Other type of SQC-LDPC [4] [8] [9] can also use the method this paper proposes with slightly modification according to parity-check’s structure. To find special requirement of SQC-LDPC to degree distribution, we will analyze the structure of its mother parity-check matrix firstly. The structure is as follows:

III. MODIFIED DEGREE DISTRIBUTION OPTIMIZATION METHOD
Degree distribution optimization’s general approach is as follows: at first, set the maximum degree of variable node dvmax ; second, density evolution is employed to compute the threshold of a given degree distribution pair [1] [2]; finally, with density evolution as cost function, global optimization algorithm DE produces many generations to search the best individual who holds the largest threshold [3]. A generation in DE includes many individuals and every individual denotes a different degree distribution pair. In [1], the precise density evolution spends so much time that it is not practical. Generally, most researchers use GA [2] to compute threshold whose precision is very close to the more precise density evolution and extremely convenient.

B. Binary Search Speeding up Computing Threshold
In degree distribution optimization, most of the time is spent on computing the individual threshold in every generation. Fixed step search, trying to search the maximum threshold by continually increasing σ by fixed step, is used usually. However, there is a drawback that it has to try many times before obtaining the maximum threshold. If speeding up this process, we can save much time. In this case, we proposed binary search replacing former search method to compute threshold. The range of threshold is quantified in an array sigma[middle] . The binary search algorithm is as follows:
Initial low =1, high = length while (high - low > 1) begin
middle = floor( (low + high) 2 ) if GA is convergent when σ = sigma[middle]
high = middle else low= middle end
threshold = sigma[middle]

V. CONCLUSION
Currently, SQC-LDPC, gradually developing from theoretical simulation to hardware implementation, is used wildly in wireless communication field. This paper propose modified degree distribution optimization method that can be applied to search good degree distribution pairs for SQC-LDPC exclusively, especially high-rate ones. Simulation results indicate that this modified method can obtain good degree distribution pairs efficiently and these good degree distribution pairs are close to Shannon limit when dvmax is large. Moreover, the performance of SQC-LDPC based on these good degree distribution pairs is good.
