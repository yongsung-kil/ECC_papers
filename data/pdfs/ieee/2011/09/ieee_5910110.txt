Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes on Cyclic Subgroups of Finite Fields
Li Zhang, Shu Lin, Life Fellow, IEEE, Khaled Abdel-Ghaffar, Member, IEEE,
Zhi Ding, Fellow, IEEE, and Bo Zhou
Abstract—A new class of quasi-cyclic LDPC codes whose
parity-check matrices are arrays of circulant permutation matrices
constructed based on cyclic subgroups of finite fields is presented.
This class of codes contains several known classes of algebraic
quasi-cyclic LDPC codes as subclasses. Experimental results
show that the codes constructed perform very well over the
AWGN channel when decoded with iterative decoding based on
belief propagation. This class of new QC-LDPC codes contains a
subclass of codes which have large minimum distances. Combi-
natorial expressions for the ranks of the parity-check matrices of
a subclass of codes constructed based on fields of characteristic
two are given.
Index Terms—Quasi-cyclic LDPC code, finite field, cyclic sub-
groups, circulant permutation matrix, row-column constrained
matrix, array, rank.
I. INTRODUCTION
T
HE rapid dominance of LDPC codes [1] in applications
requiring error control coding is due to their capacity-
approaching performance. LDPC codes were first discovered
by Gallager in 1962 [1] and then rediscovered in late 1990s
[2], [3]. Ever since their rediscovery, a great deal of research
effort has been expended in design, construction, structural
analysis, encoding, decoding, performance analysis, general-
izations and applications of these codes. Many LDPC codes
have been adopted as the standard codes for various next gen-
erations of communication systems, such as wireless, optical,
10G BASE-T, DVB, and NASA LANDSAT. Applications of
these codes to storage systems, such as hard disk drives and
flash memories, have now been seriously investigated.
This paper is concerned with algebraic construction of
LDPC codes that have quasi-cyclic (QC) structure, called QC-
LDPC codes [4]-[13]. QC-LDPC codes have some major ad-
vantages over other types of LDPC codes in hardware imple-
mentation of encoding and decoding. Encoding of these codes

implementation, well designed algebraic QC-LDPC codes

A binary QC-LDPC code 𝒞𝑞𝑐is given by the null space of
an array H of sparse circulant matrices (or simply circulants)
of the same size over GF(2). If H, viewed as a matrix over
GF(2), has constant column weight 𝛾and constant row weight
𝜌, the QC-LDPC code 𝒞𝑞𝑐given by the null space of H is
said to be (𝛾, 𝜌)-regular. If H has varying column and/or row
weights, its null space gives an irregular code.
In almost all of the proposed constructions of LDPC codes,
the following constraint on the rows and columns of the parity-
check matrix H is imposed: no two rows (or two columns) can
have more than one place where they both have 1-components.
This constraint on the rows and columns of H is referred to as
the row-column (RC)-constraint which was first introduced in
[4]. The RC-constraint ensures that the Tanner graph [18] of
the LDPC code given by the null space of H has a girth
of at least 6 [4], [19]. It also ensures that the minimum
distance of a (𝛾, 𝜌)-regular LDPC code is at least 𝛾+ 1 [4],
[19]. This distance bound is tight for regular LDPC codes
whose parity-check matrices have large column weights, such
as finite geometry cyclic LDPC codes [4] and finite field QC-
LDPC codes constructed in [8]-[12] and this paper. A parity-
check matrix that satisfies the RC-constraint is called an RC-
constrained parity-check matrix.
In this letter, we present a new class of QC-LDPC codes
whose parity check matrices are arrays of circulant permu-
tation matrices (CPMs) of the same size over GF(2). These
arrays of CPMs are constructed based on cyclic subgroups

satisfy the RC-constraint. We show that this new class of QC-
LDPC codes is a large expansion of a class of QC-LDPC codes
constructed in [10] (method-3) and contains the major class
of QC-LDPC codes constructed in [9] (method-1) and a class

examples of [8]-[12] decoded with iterative decoding using
the SPA displayed excellent performance in terms of error-
rate, error-floor and rate of decoding convergence.
III. A CLASS OF RD-CONSTRAINED MATRICES

RD-constrained matrices constructed based on cyclic groups
of finite fields. We show that this class of RD-constrained
matrices contains several major classes of RD-constrained
matrices presented in [9], [10] and [12] as subclasses.

IV. A CLASS OF QC-LDPC CODES
In this section, we present a class of QC-LDPC codes
constructed based on the class of RD-constrained base ma-
trices given in the last section. For a special subclass of
codes constructed based on fields of characteristic two, we
give combinatorial expressions of the ranks of their parity-
check matrices. We also show a subclass of codes with large
minimum distances.

C. A Special Subclass of QC-LDPC Codes with Large Mini-
mum Distances
An RC-constrained (𝛾, 𝜌)-regular LDPC code whose parity-
check matrix has column weight 𝛾is one-step majority-
logic decodable and is capable of correcting ⌊𝛾/2⌋or fewer
errors with one-step majority-logic decoding (OSMLGD) [19],
[21]. OSMLGD is one of the simplest hard-decision decoding
methods which requires only logical operations. For an RC-
constrained (𝛾, 𝜌)-regular LDPC code to be effective with
OSMLGD, its parity-check matrix must have a reasonably
large column weight 𝛾.
For a given field GF(𝑞), let 𝒞𝑞𝑐,𝑓be the QC-LDPC code
generated by the null space of the full RC-constrained array
H obtained by array dispersion of the RD-constrained base
matrix W given by (4). The subscript “𝑓” of 𝒞𝑞𝑐,𝑓stands
for “full array”. Since the column weight of H is 𝑞−2, the
code 𝒞𝑞𝑐,𝑓is capable of correcting ⌊(𝑞−2)/2⌋or fewer errors
with the OSMLGD. For 𝑞= 2𝑚, it follows from the second
expression of (12) (Theorem 3) that the rank of the full array
H is 3𝑚−3. In this case, 𝒞𝑞𝑐,𝑓is an RC-constrained QC-LDPC
code with the following parameters: 1) length (2𝑚−1)2; 2)
number of parity-check symbols 3𝑚−3; 3) minimum distance
at least 2𝑚−1: and 4) OSMLGD error-correction capability
2𝑚−1 −1. Since the number of rows of H is (2𝑚−1)2 and
the rank of H is 3𝑚−3, H has (2𝑚−1)2 −3𝑚+3 redundant
(or linearly dependent) rows. For 𝑚≥3, H has a large row
redundancy.
The code 𝒞𝑞𝑐,𝑓given by the full array H, not only performs
well with iterative decoding using the SPA but also provides
good error performance when decoded using the iterative
binary message-passing decoding algorithm (IBMPDA) pre-
sented in [22] with significant reduction in decoding com-
plexity. The IBMPDA presented in [22] requires only integer
additions and binary logical operations. The number of integer
additions required per iteration in decoding 𝒞𝑞𝑐,𝑓is equal to
the number of 1-entries in H which is (𝑞−2)(𝑞−1)2. It is
shown in [22] that this IBMPDA outperforms all the known
existing weighted bit flipping (WBF) decoding algorithms
described in [21] with much less computational complexity
and performs close to the SPA.

[8], [9], [19]. Masking results in a sparser matrix whose
associated Tanner graph has fewer edges and hence fewer
short cycles than that of the associated Tanner graph of the
original 𝛾× 𝜌subarray H(𝛾, 𝜌). To carry out masking, we
first design a low density 𝛾× 𝜌matrix Z(𝛾, 𝜌) = [𝑧𝑘,𝑙] over

for 𝑧𝑘,𝑙= 0. We call Z(𝛾, 𝜌) the masking matrix, H(𝛾, 𝜌) the
base array and M(𝛾, 𝜌) the masked array, respectively. Since
the base array H(𝛾, 𝜌) satisfies the RC-constraint, the masked
array M(𝛾, 𝜌) also satisfies the RC-constraint, regardless of
the masking matrix. Hence, the associated Tanner graph of the
masked matrix M(𝛾, 𝜌) has a girth at least 6. The null space
of M(𝛾, 𝜌) gives a new binary QC-LDPC code [8], [9], [19].
Masking can be either regular or irregular. Masking subarrays
of H produces many more QC-LDPC codes.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we first presented a large class of arrays of
circulant permutation matrices that are constructed based on
cyclic subgroups of finite fields. Based on this class of arrays
of circulant permutation matrices, we constructed a large class
of new QC-LDPC codes whose Tanner graphs have girth of
at least 6. Then, we presented the combinatorial expressions
for the ranks of the parity-check matrices of codes constructed
based on finite fields of characteristic 2. Experimental results
show that the codes constructed perform well over the binary-
input AWGN channel with iterative decoding using the SPA.
In the paper, we also identified a subclass of constructed QC-
LDPC codes that have large minimum distances. Decoding
of codes in this subclass with the SPA converges very fast.
Furthermore, we showed that, when decoded with the binary
message-passing decoding algorithm recently devised in [22],
codes in this subclass give close to the SPA performance with
significant reduction in decoding complexity.
