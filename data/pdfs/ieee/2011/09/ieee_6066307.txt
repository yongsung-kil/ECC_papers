The Investigation of Improved Extrinsic Information Feedback Algorithm for LDPC Coded BICM-ID

Jin-Fang Dou
Science and Technology on Space Microwave Laboratory, China Academy of Space Technology (Xi’an), Xi’an, China

Ming Jiang
National Mobile Communication Research Laboratory, Southeast University, Nanjing, China

Abstract—LDPC codes are very powerful error correction codes for Bit-interleaved coded modulation with iterative decoding (BICM-ID) scheme. In this paper, an improved extrinsic information feedback algorithm based on Gaussian approximation is investigated for LDPC coded BICM-ID system. This method can alleviate the correlation of extrinsic information during iterative processing and thus make the extrinsic output of decoder more credible. Simulation results show that, compared with the conventional algorithms, the improved extrinsic information feedback algorithm achieves considerable error performance improvement with only modest increase in computation complexity.
Keywords-Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes; Bitinterleaved coded modulation (BICM); BICM with iterative decoding(BICM-ID) ; Extrinsic information
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are state-of-theart codes which can approach Shannon limit at relatively low complexity using iterative decoding algorithm. It was first introduced by Gallager in 1960’ [1] and rediscovered by Mackay [2, 3]. Recently, coded modulation using LDPC codes has been extensively investigated. A popular approach to LDPC coded modulation is the bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) [4, 5], BICM is a bandwidth-efficient coded modulation schemes based on the serial concatenation of binary error-correcting coding, bit-by-bit interleaving and high-order M-ary modulation. Despite its simplicity, BICM has been proven to be a very power-efficient approach provided that LDPC codes are employed.
BICM can increase the time diversity of coded modulation and therefore can improve the performance of coded modulation over the Rayleigh fading channels. However, this improvement is achieved at the expense of reduced free (squared) Euclidean distance, leading to a degradation over the nonfading Gaussian channels. To further improve the system performance, information iterative processing between the demodulator and channel decoder on the receiver is employed, termed as BICM with iterative decoding (BICMID) [6, 7], which can provide excellent performance over both the Gaussian and the fading channels.
The performance of BICM-ID system relies on not only the error correction codes, the mapping strategy and the interleaver

on the transmitter, but also the detection/decoding algorithm on the receiver. The calculation of extrinsic information for iterative detection/decoding is very important [8]. In this paper, an improved extrinsic information feedback algorithm based on the Gaussian approximation (GA) for LDPC coded BICM-ID system is investigated [9]. The extrinsic information delivered by every iterative decoding is multiplied by a factor, and then are used as a prior log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) fed back to the input of the demodulator for the next iteration. This method can alleviate the correlation of extrinsic information during iterative processing and thus can make the extrinsic output of decoder more credible. Simulation results show that, compared with the conventional algorithms [7], this improved algorithm achieves noticeable error performance improvement with only modest increase in computation complexity.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. In section II, the system structure of LDPC-BICM-ID is reviewed, while section III describes extrinsic information feedback algorithm of the LDPC-BICM-ID system. Section IV shows simulation results of extrinsic information feedback algorithm over LDPC-BICM-ID system. Section V concludes the paper for this work.
II. STRUCTURE OF THE LDPC-BICM-ID SYSTEM
Fig. 1 depicts the schematic block diagram of a LDPCBICM-ID system. There is no bit-interleaver needed when employing the LDPC encoder/decoder due to the selfinterleaving characteristic of LDPC codes.
At the transmitter, the information bits are first encoded to generate the codeword vector c. After that, codeword vector c is transformed into a vector consisting of m-tuples ck. Each mtuples ck is mapped to one point sk from a complex valued signal constellation χ with cardinality |χ|=2m=M. The complex symbols sk are transmitted over an additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. It is assumed that the transmission channel is properly normalized so that the received symbols energy-to-noise power spectral density ratio Es/N0=σ-2. The received symbols yk=sk+nk, where nk designates the complex Gaussian noise sample with zero mean and variance σ2.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this sectionˈwe show simulation results for improved extrinsic information feedback algorithm based on the GA described in section III.
FirstlyˈGaussian estimate factor is investigated. In the different SNR, 5.0dB and 6.2dB respectively, the relationship between the Gaussian estimate factor and the number of the statistical frames is depicted in Fig. 2. We may find that, when the number of the frames achieves a certain numbers, the Gaussian estimate factor will tend to be a fixed value. For the (504,252) LDPC code used in this example, the Gaussian estimate factor is approximately 0.6. We also find, with the increase of the SNR, the Gaussian estimate factor accelerates the convergence to the fixed value and the value is always less than one.
The bit error rate (BER) performance of the LDPC-BICMID over AWGN channel is depicted in Fig. 3 to Fig.6, with set partitioning (SP) mapping. The number of inner and outer iteration is both set to ten.
Fig. 3 shows the performance comparison of traditional and improved extrinsic information feedback iterative decoding/demodulation algorithm respectively, with 8PSK modulation. The Code-1 is 1/2 code rate, regular (504, 252) LDPC code. The Code-2 is 1/2 code rate irregular (1056, 528) LDPC code of 802.16E standard. Trand denotes traditional algorithm, and Gauss Aprox denotes improved algorithm. For code-1, Gaussian estimate factor is approximately 0.6, for code-2, Gaussian estimate factor is approximately 0.5. Simulation results of Fig.3 show that, the proposed algorithm can obtain a performance improvement 0.6dB for code-1 and 0.4dB for code-2, respectively.
Fig. 4 to Fig.6 shows the performances of LDPC-BICM-ID in the different Gaussian estimate factor, with 8PSK and 16QAM, respectively. LDPC code of WiFi standard is employed, code length is 1944, code rate is 1/2, 3/4 and 5/6 respectively. GA denotes the Gaussian estimate factor, GA=1

denote traditional algorithm. But how to find the optimal Gaussian estimate factor will be further investigated.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we exploited an improved extrinsic information feedback algorithm based on Gaussian approximation for LDPC coded BICM-ID system. This method can alleviate the correlation of extrinsic information during iterations and thus make the extrinsic output of decoder is more credible. Simulation results show that,

compared with the conventional algorithms, this improved algorithm achieves considerable error performance improvement with only modest increase in computation complexity.
