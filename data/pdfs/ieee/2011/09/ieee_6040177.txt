Two-way normalization of min-sum decoding algorithm for medium and short length low density
parity check codes

Waheed Ullah1, Jiang Tao, Yang Fengfan
College of Electronics and Information Engineering Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics
Nanjing 210016, China 1uet_waheed@yhaoo.com
Abstract—This paper proposes a new technique to improve bit error performance of the min-sum decoding algorithm for low density parity check codes. The proposed technique is based on two way normalization of min-sum LDPC decoding algorithm. The variable message is modified by two normalization factors to achieve the optimum approximation for the magnitude overestimation at the check node processing. This new method does not need exhaustive search for the optimum normalization factor when there is a change in code length and code rate. The performance has been evaluated for medium and short length codes using a small number of iterations. The results show improved performance of the proposed method, which makes it attractive for many practical applications.
Keywords- Digital communication, error correction coding, low density parity check codes, LDPC codes, min-sum algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
After the first radio invention, the question always attracted researchers “how to send the data reliably over the noisy channel?” This question gave birth to error correction codes or channel coding. Among the error correction codes, Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes are one of the most powerful error correction techniques. LDPC codes are a type of linear block codes first proposed by Gallagar [1] and were scarcely considered in the three decades that followed due to thier computational complexity. They were reinvented by Mackay & Neal [2] and have taken considerable attention recently due to its powerful error correcting capabilities and their near Shannon limit performance [3]-[4] with belief propagation decoding algorithm. The name comes from the characteristic of their parity check matrix as the density of ones (1s) is less than the density of zeros (0s). The Belief Propagation (BP) or the Sum-Product decoding algorithm (SPA) is the best performing algorithm with a very high computational complexity and has dependency on the noise variance. The other popular iterative decoding algorithm which offers much lower hardware complexity at the cost of performance degradation is the min-sum algorithm (MSA) [5][6]. It is independent of noise variance as well. Efforts have been made to achieve optimum tradeoff between complexity and bit error performance (BER) of LDPC decoders. Several approaches attempted to keep the performance close to SPA with less hardware complexity for practical applications.

Different methods are used to bring the simplified form of the algorithm close in performance to the original BP or sum product algorithm. The most popular approaches are the normalized min-sum (Normalized MSA) and the offset minsum (Offset MSA) [7] algorithms. To reduce the magnitude of overestimation, the check message is modified during the iteration process which brings these min-sum algorithms close in performance to the standard SPA [8]-[9] and makes them suitable for practical applications and hardware implementation [10]. Moderate length min-sum LDPC decoding algorithm, due to its reduced complexity, has gained popularity in the wide area of wireless communication.

In normalized min-sum, a scaling factor is used which is found by exhaustive search algorithm for better performance. Similarly, in offset min-sum, the offset value is selected using search algorithm. The scaling factor in either of these two variants of min-sum algorithm is not fixed as it depends on code length and code rate. The suitable parameter for scaling factor can only be selected by simulation prior to the implementation.
This paper proposes an improvement to the modified minsum decoding algorithm (MMSA) presented in [11] by using two scaling factors instead of just one. The proposed algorithm offers better BER performance and can be easily implemented in hardware.
The proposed method corrects overestimated magnitude of the variable message during two consecutive iterations. When the signs of the present and the previous message are the same then the present message is scaled and updated with a normalized factor, however when the signs are different then the two messages are added and scaled with a normalized factor, which is different from the first scaling factor. A. Representation of LDPC codes
LDPC codes are a type of linear block codes and are represented by parity check matrix. LDCP code can be denoted in general as (N, dv, dc); where N is the length of the code equal to the number of columns in the parity check matrix, dc is the number of ones in a column of the matrix; dv is the number of ones in a row of the matrix. LDPC codes can be regular or irregular. If the number of ones in each row and column of a parity check matrix are the same then it is a regular code and otherwise it is called irregular code. III. PROPOSED METHOD
Min-sum algorithm (MSA) minimizes the complexity of SPA but suffers from performance degradation and the bit error ratio is significantly higher than SPA. The improvements to MSA attempted to date have been aimed at making it close to SPA in performance. The Offset and the Normalized MSA alter the inaccurate magnitude for the check node update calculated in step 2. The performance improvements by offset values and normalization factor are significant at low hardware complexity. As stated previously, this paper proposes an improvement to the modified min-sum algorithm (MMSA) presented in [11], which is based on the following conditions for any two consecutive iterations.

IV. HARDWARE IMPLEMENTAION
The scaling factors chosen require ‘divide by 2’ and ‘divide by 4’ operations. These can be easily achieved in hardware as ‘right shift operations’ using shift registers as shown below.

Fig.3.Implementation for variable message correction
The first factor sf1 is implemented in shift register-2 and the second factor sf2is implemented in shift register-1. Compared to the algorithm in [11] there is an additional shift register. Hence the hardware complexity has increased only by one additional shift register, and, as will be seen later, it contributes greatly to the error correction performance. Also, the shift register is a fairly simple and fast circuit element and quite easy to implement. The sign comparator decides which input is to be selected for assigning to the current message Vmi n through the multiplexer (Mux) unit. Instead of uniform modification to all the variable messages, the proposed method gives flexibility to update the messages in two ways, which is significant in terms of error performance.

VII. PERFORMANCE COMPARISON
It is clear from the results presented in Figures 7, 8 and 9 that the proposed two-way normalized min-sum algorithm outperforms the one-way normalized min-sum – MMSA [11], and even the SPA, for medium and short length codes. The better BER performance of the proposed new min-sum algorithm compared to the standard sum product algorithms is due to the fact that SPA depends on large sparse parity check matrix while the three parity check matrices selected for the proposed algorithm are of medium and short lengths.

VIII. .CONCLUSION
In this paper, a two-way normalization technique is proposed to improve the performance of the min-sum decoding algorithm for medium and short length codes. The scaling factors used by the proposed technique only require ‘divide by 2’ and ‘divide by 4’ operations, which can be easily achieved by simple shift registers. Therefore this technique can applied to practical systems without much hardware complexity. The proposed method is an efficient way to modify the variable message during the check node process in all iterations and thus the overestimation is corrected optimally. Due to its twoway normalization for correcting the magnitude of the variable message, this method provides better approximation to minsum algorithm and significantly improves the error performance compared to one-way normalization. Simulation results have also confirmed that the proposed two-way normalized min-sum algorithm outperforms the sum-product algorithm for medium and short length codes, which are used in many applications such as wireless communications.
