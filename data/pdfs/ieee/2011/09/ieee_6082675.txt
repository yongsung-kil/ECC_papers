Analysis of Early Stopping Method applied to Horizontal Shuffling Scheduling Scheme of DVB-S2
LDPC code
Gawn-Seok Jang*, In-Ki Lee, Dae-Ig Jang and Deock-Gil Oh
*University of Science and Technology, Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute Daejeon city, Republic of Korea
cksjngns@etri.re.kr, popularity1@etri.re.kr, dchang@etri.re.kr, dgoh@etri.re.kr

Abstract— In this paper, we verify that a horizontal shuffling scheduling (HSS) LDPC decoding scheme makes its LLR value converge faster than a Flooding Scheduling (FS) scheme. Also, we analyze the effects of channel adaptive early termination (CAET) method on HSS decoding scheme. Simulation result shows that combining the CAET method and HSS scheme effectively reduces the average number of iterations for LDPC decoding process with acceptable BER performance
Keywords— Early Stopping, LDPC, Layered Decoding, Horizontal Shuffling Schedule, HSS, DVB-S2.
I. INTRODUCTION
Digital Video Broadcasting via Satellite (DVB-S2) standard has adopted Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) code as its Forward Error Correction (FEC) scheme [1]. The distance of satellite channel is so long that re-transmission of wrongly received information takes too much long time. It results in significant throughput degradation of satellite broadcasting system. Thus, powerful FEC scheme such as LDPC code is strongly needed.
An LDPC code can be decoded in various ways ranging from low to high decoding complexity and from reasonably good error performance to very good performance [2]. Also, decoding of this code has massive computation complexity, hence several low complexity decoding algorithms are proposed such as Min Sum, Offset Min Sum and Normalized Min Sum algorithm [3][11].
Furthermore, a layered decoding scheme which improves convergence speed of Log Likelihood Ratio (LLR) value at the LDPC decoding process has been proposed [4]. There are two different schemes about layered decoding. The one is Vertical Shuffling Scheduling (VSS), and the other one is Horizontal Shuffling Scheduling (HSS) [5]. The HSS scheme makes easier to realize hardware implementation of the layered decoding scheme. In addition, it is also possible to reduce the iteration number of DVB-S2 LDPC decoding algorithm from 40 to 15 [3].
Since Frank Kienle and Norbert Wehn first proposed an

iteration control criterion for LDPC code in 2005 [6], several early stopping methods have been proposed in [7]-[10]. Convergence of Mean Magnitude (CMM) based on the evolution of the variable node and Convergence of the summation of the sign products computed in the check-tovariable messages have been proposed in [7][8]. Also, based on the evolution of the LLR messages of the check node, Check Node Mean Magnitude (CNMM) and Check Node Mean Checksum (CNMC) algorithms have been proposed in [9]. Recently, the CAET method also has been proposed in [10].
To increase decoding speed of the LDPC decoder, combining the HSS scheme and CAET method is best suitable with the view of its trade-off i.e. computational complexity and memory size. However, there have been no researches about combining HSS scheme and CAET method.
In this paper, we verify that the HSS LDPC decoding scheme makes its LLR value converge faster than conventional Flooding Scheme (FS) in Ⅱ. Also, we
analyze the CAET Method on HSS decoding scheme of DVB-S2 LDPC normal and short frame in Ⅲ. Finally, we
show that combining HSS scheme and CAET method efficiently reduces the average number of iterations for LDPC decoding process with acceptable Bit Error Rate (BER) performance in Ⅳ.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS OF COMBINING CAET MECHOD AND HSS DECODING SCHEME
We analyzed CAET method for HSS LDPC decoding scheme so that we selected some parameters for CAET stopping criterion. We have selected 9 as the Ite value for both (64800, 32400) and (16200, 7200) LCPC code, also selected 18% and 5% as a Thr respectively.
Fig.8 and Fig.9 illustrate that the average number of iterations for CAET method on HSS decoding scheme. Due to the differences of SCR shape between Fig. 5 and Fig. 6, the average iteration number has different value of ite as 5 and 7. The reason is that the SCR of (16200, 7200) code is continuously converges to zero, hence the code can be decoded with the number of maximum iteration in EbNo region from 0 to 1dB. However, (64800, 32400) code does not converge to zero in same EbNo region.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have verified that the HSS LDPC decoding scheme reduces the number of iterations, and the CAET method also reduces the average number of iterations. For high speed of LDPC decoder, reducing the number of iterations is a critical issue in decoder design. Combining the early stopping method and HSS decoding scheme has been expected to reduce the number of iterations effectively. The analysis in this paper and simulation results show that this combining scheme reduces the number of iterations effectively with acceptable BER performance.
