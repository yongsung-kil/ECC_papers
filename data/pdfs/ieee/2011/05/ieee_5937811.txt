Sliding Window Method for Stochastic LDPC Decoder

Jienan Chen Student Member, IEEE and Jianhao Hu

Science and Technology on Communications University of Electronic Science and Technology of China
Chengdu, China {neonanme and jhhu}@uestc.edu.cn

Abstract—This paper proposes a Sliding Window Method (SWM) for stochastic Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) decoder designing. The SWM is formulated for solving the latch-up problem in the Variable Nodes (VN) information updating. The bit in the latch-up state is evolved from the information bit in the sliding window. Then, an optimized hardware structure is proposed for SWM. Compared with traditional VN structure, the SWM require about 35% less hardware resources to achieve the same BER performance.
Keywords; Low Density Parity Check (LDPC), Sliding Window Method (SWM), Stochastic decoding, Bit-Error Ratio (BER)
I. INTRODUCTION
Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes have superior coding performance [1], which have been adopted for several practical applications, including DVB-S2 [2], IEEE 802.3an(10GBASE-T) [3] and IEEE 802.11n [4]. LDPC codes allow a high parallel degree for decoding, since Parity check Nodes (PN) and Variable Nodes can be updated concurrently. However, the major challenges of the parallel LDPC decoder are the hardware complexity and interconnection confliction [5].
Recently, a fully parallel stochastic LDPC decoder is proposed by [7], in which the probability message is converted into Bernoulli bit stream. The higher density of bit ‘1’ in the bit stream represents the larger probability, vice versa. The multiplication and division operations in the LDPC decoding algorithm can be implemented with simple gates, which require small hardware area with much low latency. Since the probability message can be transmitted in the form of bit stream with asynchrony mode, the interconnection confliction problem can be solved easily [7].
However, one of the major issues for the stochastic LDPC decoder design is the latch-up phenomenon for VN update processing, which degrades the decoding performance greatly. Edge Memory (EM) is proposed for stochastic LDPC decoder in [6] to solve the latch-up problem. EM is used to provide the VN updating information when the hold state occurs. But the EM needs so many memory blocks that are not suitable for

VLSI implementation. Tracking Forecast Memories (TFM) is proposed in [8], which needs less hardware area than EM to achieve similar decoding performance. TFM is also used as re-randomization unit in [9]. TFM updates and traces the statistic information of the regular states to generate the information for the hold state.
In this paper, we proposed a new re-randomization method to generate the VN updating information for the hold state. First, we propose a more precise probability updating algorithm than TFM. Then, the sliding window method (SWM) is used to update the statistic information of the stream with the regular state bits in the sliding window. Last, we optimize hardware structure for the implementation of SWM.
SWM needs the same memory size with TFM, which is much less than EM. However, the widths of adder and comparator in SWM are much smaller than those in TFM. The 4-bit SWM, which has similar BER performance, can reduce about 35% hardware resource in logic compared with TFM. The method of Majority-based TFM structure in [9] is also used in the proposed decoder to achieve more resource reduction.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows: Section II reviews the stochastic LDPC decoder. The SWM is introduced in section III. The performance analysis is given in section IV. Section V is the conclusion.

A. Bit-Error-Rate Decoding Perforamance
The BER performance is shown in Fig. 5. The EMs-based (32-bit 48-bit and 64-bit), TFMs-based (8-bit fixed-point), SWMs-based (3-bit and 4-bit) stochastic decoder and the logSPA is compared.
As shown in Fig. 5, the SWMs-based LDPC decoder can provide similar performance as TFMs and EMs for stochastic decoders.

B. Hardware Comparision
The structure of SWM is simply, which contain two counters and one comparator. The widths of the counters and comparator are less than those of TFM. We compare the hardware cost based on the Xilinx FPGA, Virtex-4-LX200. From Table I, we know that SWM needs the least hardware resources among these three methods. For example, the 4-bit SWM require 8 Flip-Flops which is less than EMs and equal to 8-bit TFM, and the cost of 4-input LUT is about 35% less than TFM. Moreover, the SWM does not need random number to generate the conservative bit, and the critical path of SWM is shorter than that of TFM.

C. Throughput
We apply the early termination criterion method to reduce decoding cycles. According to the simulation results, the average decoding cycle of SWM is about 70 DCs when Eb / N0 = 4dB . When the system achieve 200MHz clock rate, the throughput of decoder can reach 1.5Gb/s.

D. Applications of SWM
The SWM can be used as re-randomization unit based on the proposed structure. Also, the Majority-Based TFM stochastic LDPC decoder proposed in [9] can be replaced by SWM to achieve better decoding performance and lower hardware cost. The SWM also can be used as the stochastic divider for the other applications.

V.

CONCLUSION

In this paper, a sliding window method based rerandomization unit is proposed for stochastic LDPC decoder. The SWM requires about 35% less area compared to 8-bit TFM. The BER performance of SWM is nearly same to TFM and EM. Also, SWM is easily for ASIC and FPGA implementation. The critical path of SWM is shorter than TFM and EM. This paper showed that the stochastic LDPC decoder can be implemented more efficiently and fast with SWM.
