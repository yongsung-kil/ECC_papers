Low Complexity Piecewise Linear LLR Calculation for MIMO-BICM Systems
Chao Zheng, Raman Yazdani, Masoud Ardakani Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Alberta
Edmonton, Alberta T6G 2V4, Canada

Abstract—Log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) are efficient metrics used in the decoding of modern channel codes such as lowdensity parity-check and turbo codes. LLR calculation, however, can be a complex task since LLRs are usually complicated nonlinear functions of the channel output. In multi-input multioutput (MIMO) channels, the complexity of the calculation increases exponentially with the number of antennas. In this paper, an LLR approximation method is proposed for MIMOBICM systems. In particular, piecewise linear approximation functions are introduced and an accuracy measure is provided to optimize the parameters. Simulations verify that the performance of the optimized approximate LLRs is better than the existing approximation method and quite close to that of true LLRs.
I. INTRODUCTION
Error-correcting codes such as low-density parity-check codes (LDPC) [1] and turbo codes [2] are known to approach the Shannon limit on many channels [3]–[5]. The messages used in the decoding process of these codes are usually loglikelihood ratios (LLRs). However, LLRs are usually nonlinear complicated function of the channel output, especially in multi-input multi-output (MIMO) channels. As a result, it may cost too much time and power in the decoder to calculate exact LLRs. Consequently, accurate approximate LLRs should be used to implement efficient demodulators.
Calculation of approximate LLRs has been considered in recent literature, e.g, see [6]–[9]. The most common approximation method is the max-log approximation. The maxlog approximation is accurate at high signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs), but modern error-correcting codes such as LDPC and turbo codes usually operate at low SNRs [6]. Thus, alternative methods, accurate at also low SNRs, are much needed.
Piecewise linear LLR calculation has been considered in [6] for soft Viterbi decoding of convolutional codes in the HIPERLAN/2 standard. In [8], approximating LLRs by linear functions has been proposed for binary phase-shift keying (BPSK) over symmetric channels and a measure of LLR accuracy has also been introduced. Using this measure, linear LLRs have been designed with almost the same performance as true LLRs. Later, this measure has been generalized to binary asymmetric channels in [9] and applied to non-binary modulations under bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) [10]. Numerical results have also shown that the performance loss of their optimized piecewise linear LLRs based on the generalized measure is quite small.
None of the above mentioned results consider MIMO channels. MIMO technology has attracted much attention in

the past few decades since it can offer high spectral efficiency and reliable wireless communication over a multi-path environment [11]. MIMO-BICM has also received attention because of its lower detection complexity and near-optimal performance [12], [13]. Another benefit of BICM is that it allows LLRs to be computed independently at different bit levels. The piecewise linear approximation method of [9] does not directly apply to the MIMO-BICM case. This is because on a MIMO channel the received signal is a vector and not a single value.
In this work, an efficient LLR approximation method based on piecewise linear functions is proposed for MIMO-BICM systems, and an accuracy measure is also introduced to be used for optimization of the parameters. Numerical results are provided to show the success of the proposed method.
II. BACKGROUND
A. MIMO-BICM system model
The block diagram of a MIMO-BICM system is depicted in Fig.1. Here, a flat slow-fading MIMO channel with Mt transmitting antennas and Mr receiving antennas is considered.
During the transmission, a sequence of binary information bits generated by the binary source is first encoded by an error-correcting code and then interleaved to get the sequence {ci}. After being de-multiplexed into Mt antenna sequences, each group of m bits are mapped to data symbols, xi ∈ X , i = 1, . . . , Mt. Here X denotes the symbol alphabet of size |X | = 2m and unit average power. As a result, at time index t the transmit vector, which carries R0 = m × Mt coded bits, is given by x[t] = (x1[t], x2[t], . . . , xMt [t])T [7], [14].
At the receiver, the received vector is given by
y[t] = γ[t]H[t]x[t] + n[t].
where H[t] is a Mr × Mt channel matrix, the entries of which are all independent, and n[t] is the additive white Gaussian noise vector, (n1[t], n2[t] . . . , nMr [t])T , each component of which is modeled as i.i.d. circularly symmetric complex Gaussian with zero mean and unit variance. Assuming H[t] with normalized entries and unit average power, γ[t] is the average SNR per transmit antenna. We also assume that H[t] changes slowly with time. For ease of notations, the time index t is omitted in the rest of the paper.

V. DISCUSSIONS AND CONCLUSION
LLR calculation is generally complicated especially for
MIMO channels. In this work, an efficient method using
piecewise linear functions for approximating LLRs over flat
slow-fading MIMO channels is studied. The system considered
in this work is MIMO-BICM which allows us to compute
LLRs at bit levels. To this end, an accuracy measure and
approximation functions for MIMO-BICM systems are pro-
posed. We use this measure to optimize the parameters in
the approximation functions. The performance of the proposed
method is investigated in terms of BER and achievable rate.
Our results show that the performance of the optimized
piecewise linear approximation function are superior to the
max-log approximation. In fact, the achieved performance is
very close to that of true LLRs. Compared to max-log, our
method is more complex because it needs the parameters to be
optimized. Thus, our method is more attractive when channel
gains are not subject to rapid change.
