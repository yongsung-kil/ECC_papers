A Universal Efficient Circular-Shift Network for Reconfigurable Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Decoders
Suwen Song , Hangxuan Cui , and Zhongfeng Wang

Abstract— Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes for modern communication standards usually have multiple code rates and block lengths. Therefore, reconfigurable LDPC decoders have received widespread attention, which require circular-shift networks to support various expansion factors. Besides, for inputs smaller than the network size, the circular-shift network is desired to process multiple frames in parallel to maximize hardware utilization efficiency. The increasing demands put severe challenges to low-complexity implementations of shift networks, especially for codes with numerous expansion factors, such as 5G LDPC codes. In this brief, we present a universal design of efficient reconfigurable circular-shift networks. Through an ingenious modification on the order of permutations, the generation of control signals is considerably simplified, leading to a significant reduction of area and critical path. Moreover, a hybrid architecture organically integrating different networks is proposed for further complexity reduction. Implementation results under TSMC 90 nm technology demonstrate that the proposed network can achieve 25% area reduction and 46% areaefficiency (AE) improvement over the state-of-the-art ones.
Index Terms— 5G low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, Banyan network (BN), barrel shifter, circular-shift networks, reconfigurable LDPC decoder.
I. INTRODUCTION Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes [1], owing to their remarkable error-correction performance and inherent parallelism for hardware implementation, have been widely adopted in modern wireless standards, such as IEEE802.11n [2], IEEE802.16e [3], and 5G [4]. In these communication standards, LDPC codes usually have multiple code rates and block lengths. For example, 5G LDPC codes have 51 expansion factors (Z) varying from 2 to 384 to pair with different code rates and lengths. Therefore, a reconfigurable LDPC decoder is desirable to support codes with different parameters. The circular-shift network is a fundamental module in LDPC decoders that performs circular-shift operations between check node units and variable node units according to shift values (SVs). For a reconfigurable LDPC decoder, the circular-shift network is required to support various expansion factors. Moreover, considering part of circuitry will stay idle if the expansion factor is less than the network size, the circular-shift network is preferably capable of processing multiple inputs with different SVs in parallel to maximize the hardware utilization efficiency. An N × N Benes network [5] is composed of 2 log2 N − 1 stages of 2 × 2 switches and could perform any sort of permutations over

In [6], the variation Benes network is proposed where 3 × 3 and 5 × 5 primitive switches are introduced to efficiently handle the case that the input size is not a power of 2. However, the variation Benes network [6] still suffers a long critical path and the generation of control signals is extremely sophisticated. Another alternative is the barrel shifter, in which the SVs are natural control signals thus facilitating controller design. By applying the bypass network to the barrel shifter, the QC-LDPC shift network (QSN) [7] could process input data of variable sizes, but is not able to process multiple frames in parallel. This issue is solved by the extended barrel-shifter (EBS) [8], but it requires a more complex generation of control signals than that for the QSN and the input frames must have the same SV. In [9], a fully parallel circular-shift rotation network is proposed. Based on a generic barrel shifter (GBS) core block, it could handle various frames with different permutation constraints. The main drawback of this design is that it is hard to perform run-time reconfiguration since the control signals need to be generated offline and stored in an extra read-only memory (ROM). The variation Banyan network (VBN) [10] is suitable for parallel processing due to its recursive structure and it is extended in [11] to support 5G standards. Lam et al. [12] also presents a segmented reconfigurable cyclic shifter for 5G LDPC decoders. However, when numerous operating modes need to be considered, their suboptimal controller designs still lead to a long critical path and large area consumption.
In this brief, we aim to develop a universal design of efficient circular-shift networks for reconfigurable LDPC decoders. Major contributions are summarized as follows.
1) Considering the complexity of the controller rises rapidly as the number of operating modes increases and thus deteriorates the throughput and area-efficiency (AE), we develop an improved Banyan network (IBN) by subtly adjusting the order of permutations, which facilitates a noticeable reduction on area consumption and critical path.
2) By analyzing the properties of different networks, a welldesigned hybrid architecture is proposed, which is more flexible and efficient than prior works. Besides, it could provide a wide range of parallelism options covering all required expansion factors.
3) Following the proposed modifications, the reconfigurable circular-shift networks for IEEE802.11n and 5G standards are designed and implemented, which could provide 37% and 46% AE improvement over the existing works, respectively.
II. BACKGROUND
The basic permutation networks could be classified into three categories: the Benes network, the Banyan network (BN), and the barrel shifter. Considering only the circular-shift operation is required in LDPC decoders while the Benes network is able to perform any kind of permutation, it is wasteful to apply the Benes network in LDPC decoders. Therefore, only the other two networks are briefly introduced in the following.

A. Banyan Network Fig. 1 shows the structures of the BN and the VBN. A 2n × 2n BN
is composed of n stages of 2 × 2 switch units. As shown in Fig. 1(a), the construction of the BN follows a recursive manner, so it is suitable for processing multiple frames in parallel. For example, if we set the former i stages of 2 × 2 switches to the bar state (do not switch), 2i frames (Z 2n−i ) could be processed in parallel. However, the BN is not flexible since the network size N could only be a power of 2. Consequently, part of components will be in idle state when the input size IN is not 2n . The VBN is constructed by introducing a stage of 3 × 3 switch units to the conventional BN and it is more efficient to process data with 3 · 2n inputs, such as 5G LDPC codes (Zmax = 384 = 3 × 27). As shown in Fig. 1(b), a 3 · 2n × 3 · 2n VBN consists of an n-stage BN which has 3 · 2n inputs and one stage of 3 × 3 switches.
B. Barrel Shifter Network
The predominant characteristic of the barrel shifter is that the SV is the natural control signal, and thus the hardware overhead of the control unit is negligible. Fig. 2 shows a 7 × 7 barrel shifter. As can be seen, SV[2], SV[1], and SV[0] are the control signals for each stage, which could circularly shift the input vector to left by 4, 2, and 1 times, respectively. Moreover, the network size of the barrel shifter could be an arbitrary value. However, the barrel shifter could only process one frame at a time, thus being inefficient when the input size is much smaller than the network size. EBS [8] could process several frames in parallel, but it requires a more complex generation of control signals and the input frames must have the same SV.
C. Bypass Network
When the input size IN is smaller than the network size N, the outputs of BN and barrel shifters would be ineffective due to the

block problems [7], [10]. Therefore, the bypass network is proposed where two shifters are employed to eliminate the obstruction. One shifts the input data to right by SV times, while the other shifts the input data to left by IN–SV times. At last, a Merge Network extracts the useful part from the left-shift output and the right-shift output to comprise the final result. The applications of bypass network in the BN and barrel shifter could be found in [7]–[9].
III. PROPOSED CIRCULAR-SHIFT NETWORK
For reconfigurable LDPC decoders, a well-designed circular-shift network should have the following characteristics: 1) could perform cyclic shift on input data with arbitrary size no larger than the network size (IN N); 2) could process multiple input frames with different SVs in parallel; and 3) the complexity and critical path of the network are the smaller the better. To meet these purposes, we present an IBN and a hybrid circular-shift architecture in this section and realize the corresponding circular-shift networks for 5G and IEEE802.11n LDPC codes.
A. Improved BN
Let Ck denote the control signals of the kth stage in VBN, where k = 1, 2, . . . , n + 1. Algorithm 1 gives the generation process of Ck when a single frame is processed in the VBN [10] with network size 3 · 2n. Considering the control signals of the first stages of every subnetwork are the same, Ck is shared by 2k−1 Banyan subnetworks. According to Algorithm 1, Ck is determined by SVk which equals SV%(3 · 2n+1−k ). Therefore, n modular operations are introduced to the controller, resulting in a long critical path. When numerous operating modes need to be supported, the number of modular operations increases rapidly. Though a mapping scheme was proposed in [11] to eliminate modular operations, it requires lookup tables (LUTs) for data format conversions, thus inducing a larger area consumption.

B. Hybrid Circular-Shift Network Architecture
Based on the analysis in Section II, it could be concluded that the BN is suitable for parallel processing, the barrel shifter has the lowest complexity and the best flexibility, and the bypass network could be used for supporting variable input sizes. Therefore, to incorporate the advantages of different networks, we propose a hybrid circular-shift network which is more flexible and efficient than existing works.
Fig. 4 illustrates the structure of the proposed hybrid circularshift network, which is constructed by recursively extending the elementary unit (EU) using IBNs. The IBN is used for parallel processing and the bypass network is also applied to handle the case when the input size is not exactly the network/subnetwork size. In the proposed network, EU is the minimum subnetwork, whose size is set as the smallest number among all required expansion factors, which is denoted by p. Since the EU will not be divided anymore, it is implemented by the barrel shifter for the lowest complexity. In summary, the proposed hybrid network could support arbitrary expansion factors included in the corresponding standards and provide a wide range of parallelism options while each input could possess a particular SV.

C. Applications on 5G and IEEE802.11n LDPC Codes
In this subsection, we implement the proposed hybrid network for 5G and IEEE802.11n LDPC codes, as shown in Fig. 6. It should be noted that the proposed circular-shift network is not only effective for these two standards but is universal and could be generalized to other cases. For 5G LDPC codes, the maximum and minimum expansion factors are 384 and 2, respectively. Since p is very small ( p = 2), EUs are integrated into IBNs so the corresponding hybrid network consists of two 3 × 27-input IBNs cascading a Merge Network. As shown in Table II, it could support all expansion factors in 5G standards and has eight parallelism options. At most 192 frames could be processed in parallel. For IEEE802.11n LDPC codes, the maximum and minimum expansion factors are 81 and 27, respectively. Therefore, the corresponding hybrid network consists of 3 × 20-input IBNs, 27-input EUs, and a Merge Network. As shown in Table II, it could process three size-27 frames simultaneously.

V. CONCLUSION
In this brief, we have proposed a universal efficient circular-shift network for reconfigurable LDPC decoders. Following an ingenious modification on the permutation order, the IBN is developed which significantly simplifies the generation of control signals. Moreover, to further improve the hardware efficiency, a hybrid architecture is presented by elaborately integrating barrel shifters and the proposed IBN.
