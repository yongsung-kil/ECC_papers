Evaluation of the Effects of SEUs on Configuration Memories in FPGA Implemented QC-LDPC Decoders

Zhen Gao, Yinghao Cheng School of Electrical and Information Engineering,
Tianjin University, Tianjin 300072, China

Abstract—LDPC codes are widely used in wireless communication systems for reliable data transmission due to their excellent error correction capabilities. SRAM-FPGAs are a popular option for the implementation of LDPC decoders due to their excellent computing capabilities and re-configurability. However, when applied in critical environments, e.g. space platforms, the SRAM-FPGA based LDPC decoders will suffer single-event upsets (SEUs) that can cause failures and disrupt communications. Therefore, analyzing the reliability of LDPC decoders to SEUs on the FPGA is important. This paper first analyzes the effects of SEUs on different parts of the FPGA implemented LDPC decoder based on the module functions, including the influence of the parallelism on the decoder reliability. Then fault injection experiments are performed to validate the conclusions of the analysis. Experiment results show that about 98% of SEUs on the configuration memories can be tolerated by the decoder itself, and the modules with more interconnections are less robust to SEUs. In addition, the reliability of LDPC decoders decreases for lower levels of parallelism due to the larger computation load of each unit. These results will be a valuable input to design efficient SEU protection schemes for LDPC decoders.

There are a few works studying the reliability of LDPC decoders to SEUs. For example, the authors in [11] evaluate the effects of SEUs on the user memories in the FPGA based LDPC decoders, including the input memories, intermediate results and control bits. But this work is based on software simulations, so the errors on configuration memories are not considered. The authors in [12] evaluate the reliability of the LDPC decoders to SEUs on the configuration memories of the FPGA implementation. But this work focuses on the control module of a layered LDPC decoder and did not examine the reliability of the key computing modules.
In this paper, the effects of SEUs on all parts of the configuration memory of an SRAM-FPGA implemented LDPC decoder are evaluated using a hardware fault injection platform. The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In Section II, the principle of the LDPC decoders is explained, including decoder structure and the Min-Sum algorithm. In Section III, the implementation of the LDPC decoder on FPGA is described. In Section IV, the reliability of each module in the LDPC decoder is first analyzed, and then evaluated using fault injection experiments. Finally, conclusions are summarized in Section V.

I. INTRODUCTION
Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes were proposed by Gallarger in 1962, and achieve performance close to the Shannon Limit [1],[2]. In particular, Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QCLDPC) has been widely used in different standards of wireless communications, e.g. DVB-32 [3], IEEE802.16e [4], CCSDS [5], due to its low complexity and good error correction capability. Recently, LDPC codes have also been adopted as the channel decoding scheme in 5G NR to meet the requirements of low latency and high throughput [6].
In recent years, on-board processing (OBP) is getting more and more attention due to its good flexibility [7]. SRAM based FPGAs have abundant logic resources and good reconfigurability, so they are a good option for OBP platforms [8],[9]. However, SRAM-FPGAs are sensitive to cosmic radiation, like Single Event Upsets (SEUs) events [9],[10]. SEUs can corrupt both the configuration memory and the user memories (including flip-flops and Block RAM) of SRAMbased FPGAs [10]. While errors on user memory only corrupt the data stored, errors on the configuration memory can change the circuit implemented by the FPGA and can only be corrected by reconfiguration. So if the LDPC codec, especially the decoder with much higher complexity than the encoder, is implemented with SRAM-FPGAs in OBP platforms, its reliability becomes an important problem.

The structure of a typical LDPC decoder is shown in Fig. 1. If the length of information bits and coded bits are K and N, respectively, there are N Variable Nodes (VNs) and K Check Nodes (CNs) and one Parity Check in the decoder. III. FPGA IMPLEMENTATION OF A LDPC DECODER
A. Structure of the FPGA implemented LDPC Decoder
The QC-LDPC code in the IEEE 802.16e standard is used as a case study in this paper. The code length and coding rate are 576 and 1/2, respectively. If defining the degree of a CN (or VN) as the number of VNs (or CNs) that are connected to it, there are 264 VNs with the degree of 2 (VN2), 192 VNs with the degree of 3 (VN3), 120 VNs with the degree of 6 (VN6), 192 CNs with degree of 6 (CN6) and 96 CNs with the degree of 7 (CN7) in the decoder. A partial parallel structure is applied in our implementation, where a small number of VN Units (VNUs) and CN Units (CNUs) are reused for the processing of all VNs and CNs. Such structure is suitable for QC-LDPC decoders [13], and the parallelism can be adjusted to achieve a trade-off between decoding throughput and hardware complexity.
As shown in Fig. 2, the decoder is composed of control modules (DecCtrl, DecCheck and Ctransport), compute

modules (VNU2/3/6 and CNU6/7), input modules (InputV2/V3 /V6), interconnection modules (IterForC6/C7, IterForV2/V3/ V6, IterLatC6/C7 and IterLatV2/V3/V6) and memories (Qstore, Rstore and Cstore). And IterForC7 and IterForC7 feed inputs for CNU6 and CNU7, respectively. z IterLatV2, IterLatV3 and IterLatV6 get the results from VNU2, VNU3 and VNU6, respectively, during their reuse process, and store the results in Qstore. If denoting the numbers of units for CNU6, CNU7, VNU2, VNU3 and VNU6 as n, m, x, y and z, respectively, the reuse times of them for each iteration are 192/n, 96/m, 264/x, 192/y and 120/z, respectively. In this paper, three parallelisms are applied, including (n, m, x, y, z) of (8, 4, 11, 8, 5), (6, 3, 8, 6, 4) and (4, 2, 6, 4, 3). In the remaining part of this paper, the number of CNU6 (n) is taken to denote the level of parallelism.

IV. RELIABILITY ANALYSIS OF LDPC DECODERS
In this section, we first analyze the reliability of each module in the FPGA implemented LDPC decoder based on their function and properties, and then the influence of parallelism to the decoder reliability. 1) Reliability of control modules
DecCtrl is the top control module of the decoder, so an error on this module will be very likely to corrupt the normal operation of the decoder. The DecCheck is the key module for iteration termination, so an error on it will cause abnormal termination of the decoder, and further affects the decoding results. Ctransport controls the access of the decisions (C), so an error on it will affect the update of Q (according equation (3)) and the value of the decisions, which may directly cause wrong decoding results. 2) Reliability of CNUs and VNUs
CNUs only include comparison & selection operations to get the minimum value from several inputs. So if an error on CNUs does not affect the selection of the minimum value, it will have no influence to the decoding results. On the other hand, VNUs include additions for the update of Q and comparisons for the decoding decisions. So an error on VNUs will directly affect the updated Q and the decision results. In particular, the errors changing the decision results may corrupt the termination condition, so that the decoder cannot terminate as usual. With these properties, it can be concluded that CNUs would be more reliable than VNUs.
In addition, an error on CNU6 may affect the R messages for 6 VNUs, and that on CNU7 will affect the R messages for 7 VNUs, so the reliability of CNU6 should be higher than that of CNU7. Similarly, the reliability of VNUs should follow VNU2 >

VNU3 and VNU6 based on the influence range when an error occurs on them. 3) Reliability of IterForC and IterForV
IterForC modules provide input for CNUs. Since an error on IterForC6 and IterForC7 may affect the updates of at most six and seven R value, respectively, the reliability of IterForC6 would be higher than that of IterForC7. Similarly, the reliability of IterForV modules should follow IterForV2> IterForV6 > IterForV7. 4) Reliability of IterLatC and IterLatV
IterLatC and IterLatV store the outputs of CNUs (R) and VNUs (Q) into the Rstore and Qstore, respectively. An error on them will bring wrong inputs for VNUs and CNUs, respectively. Based on the possible influence range of an error on these modules, their reliability should follow IterLatC6 > IterLatC7 and IterLatV2 > IterLatV3> IterLatV6. 5) Influence of parallelism to the decoder reliability
The function and complexity of DecCtrl, DecCheck and Ctransport is almost independent of the parallelism, so their reliability should also unrelated with the parallelism.
The function and complexity of each CNU and VNU is independent of the parallelism, so the number of essential bits and critical bits for each CNU or VNU will not change. But for the decoder with lower parallelism, the same CNU or VNU will be reused by more times, so an error on the module will affect the updates of more R or Q values, which will decrease the reliability of the decoder.
For lower parallelism, each CNU and VNU will be reused more times, so the modules which provide inputs to them (IterForC, IterForV and InputV) and get results from them (IterLatC and IterLatV) become more complex due to larger number of switches involved for the selection of different inputs and outputs. This will also increase the number of essential bits, and an error on one of these modules may affect the updates of more R or Q values. So the reliability of these interconnection modules would decrease for lower parallelism.

Fault injection platform
To assess the reliability of the LDPC decoders, SEUs have been injected using the fault injection tool in [14] on the Xilinx UtralScale SoC. As shown in Fig. 3, the injection platform consists of the Processing System (PS) and the Programmable Logic (PL). The PS consists of the ARM processor, DDR controller and UART controller. The DDR stores the list of essential bits in the configuration memory and the UART logs results to the PC. The PL part consists of two copies of the LDPC decoder (Golden and Design Under Test (DUT)), a comparator and the Internal Configuration Access Port (ICAP). ICAP allows the ARM to access the configuration memory for the DUT decoder for SEU injection. The mismatches between the Golden and DUT decoders are detected by the comparator.

B. Effects of SEUs on configuration memories of the decoder
The effect of SEUs on the configuration memories of the LDPC decoder can be categorized into three main types. The first is that the error has no influence to the decoding process. The second is that the error affects the decoding process, but does not change the decoding results. This type of effect can be further divided into three cases, less iterations, equal iterations and more iterations. The third is that the decoder outputs wrong results with the maximum number of iterations (decoding failure). For the SEU on each essential bit, we run the decoder for 50 times with different inputs. The SNR for the inputs are 2dB so that there are no error bits in the fault free case.
The values of Qstore and Rstore after the last iteration for each of the inputs in the fault free case are stored as the reference for the determination of critical bits. An essential bit will be identified as a critical bit if the SEU on it changes the final values of Qstore and Rstone for at least one of the 50 inputs. Then the ratio of the number of critical bits over that of essential bits is the key measure of the decoder reliability. In addition, considering that the effect of an SEU on a critical bit may be different for different inputs, the fraction of runs for each type of effect is also measured, which is defined as the ratio of the number of runs producing a type of effect over 50x of the number of critical bits.

As shown in Fig. 5 and the left subfigure of Fig. 6, the numbers of essential bits and the ratios of critical bits for the control modules (DecCtrl, DecCheck, Ctransport) and compute modules (CNUs and VNUs) do not change much for different levels of parallelism. The right subfigure of Fig. 6 shows the portion of affected runs (including all error effects) over the total runs (50x number of essential bits). As we can see, the portion of affected runs for CNUs and VNUs decreases a little bit when the parallelism increases from 4 to 8. This implies that an error on one CNU or VNU has less influence to the decoding process when more units run in parallel due to the smaller computation load of each one. These results are consistent with the analysis in Section IV.
Based on Fig. 7, Fig. 8 and Fig. 9, when the parallelism decreases from 8 to 4, the number of essential bits for the input and interconnection modules (InputV, IterForC, IterForV, IterLatC and IterLatV) increases, and the ratio of critical bits also increases obviously. This means the reliability of these modules decreases for lower levels of parallelism, which is also consistent with the analysis in Section IV.
It should be noted that, since the SEU rate is proportional to the configuration memory bits, the decoder with higher parallelism will suffer more SEUs due to higher resource usage (as shown in Section III.C). So the reliability of the decoder may be worse for higher parallelism even if the impact of a SEU is lower on a single compute or interconnection module.

First, the effects of SEUs on different modules of the decoder are analyzed based on the module function, including the influence of parallelism on the decoder reliability. Then fault injection experiments are performed to evaluate the

reliability of each module with different parallelism. The experimental results are consistent with the analysis, and some important conclusions are obtained: 1) Less than 30% of the essential bits will affect the processing process of the decoder; 2) More than half of the critical bits in control modules will cause wrong decoding results, but this portion decreases to around or less than 10% for compute and interconnection modules; 3) The modules with more connections with others are less robust to SEUs; 4) Decoders with lower levels of parallelism are less robust to SEUs on the configuration memories. These conclusions would be valuable to design efficient fault tolerant implementations of the QC-LDPC decoders which is an interesting topic for future work.
