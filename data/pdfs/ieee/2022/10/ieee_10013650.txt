An Improved Hybrid LDPC Decoder over Rayleigh Fading Channel

Reza Biazaran Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering
The George Washington University Washington, DC, USA rbiazaran@gwu.edu

Hermann J. Helgert Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering
The George Washington University Washington, DC, USA hhelgert@gwu.edu

Abstract—Soft decision decoders applicable to low density parity check codes such as sum product algorithms provide excellent error performance, however, it comes at the expense of computational complexity. Additionally, many iterations may be required of these decoders to achieve desired error performance. The processing delay associated with too many iterations may be a drawback for cases where low latency is a critical requirement. Conversely, performance of hard decision decoders such as bit flipping decoder and its variants, while improved, generally are not on par with that of soft decision decoders. Such decoders also require many iterations to achieve a given error performance. We have proposed a two-stage hybrid decoder with a simplified sum product algorithm (SPA) in the first stage, and an improved noisy gradient decent bit flipping decoder in the second stage. We have shown that our proposed hybrid decoder outperforms the legacy individual decoders, studied in this paper, from error performance point of view as well as required number of iterations that will reduce the overall network latency.
Keywords—LDPC, Bit Flipping Decoder, Rayleigh fading channel,
I. INTRODUCTION
Low density parity check (LDPC) codes [1] have gained popularity due to their efficient decoding and excellent error performance capabilities. Of the few major decoding algorithms applicable to LDPC codes, soft decision decoding such as sum product algorithm (SPA) [2] offers superior performance relative to other decoding algorithms that employ hard decision decoding like bit flipping (BF) and its many variants [1] [3], [4], [5]. The excellent error performance of SPA decoders, however, comes at the expense of computational complexity. There has been many efforts to modify or simplify the computational complexity associated with SPA decoding [6], however, such simplifications have resulted in some degree of performance degradation. On the other hand, hard decision decoders such as BF algorithm is relatively easily implemented, however, their error performance lacks that of SPA’s decoding capability. Efforts to improve BF decoding performance has reduced the performance gap, however, such efforts have inevitably added to the complexity of such decoders. Another problem associated with both types of decoders is the fact that both soft and hard decision decoders applicable to LDPC codes are iterative decoders. To achieve a desired error performance many decoding iterations may be required which will add to the processing delay and will subsequently increase latency. Many modern communications systems require extremely low network latency and can not afford increased delay due to decoding. Authors of [7] initially proposed a hybrid decoder that consists of two individual decoders where each decoder only performs a limited number of iterations to reduce overall latency.

In this paper and inspired by [7], we propose a hybrid decoder that performs decoding in two stages. The first stage decoder is a modified SPA decoder with low complexity to reduce computational complexity. The second stage decoder is an improved noisy gradient decent bit flipping decoder. We show the performance of our proposed hybrid decoder is superior to each individual decoder while maintaining low processing delay. The remainder of this paper is organized as follows: Section II.A provides a brief overview of SPA decoder and its modifications. Section II.B provides a brief overview of the gradient decent bit flipping algorithm, its noisy variant [3], [4], and its improvement proposed by the authors of this paper [11]. Section III provides the results of the simulation where a SPA decoder has been used as the first stage decoder, and an improved noisy gradient decent bit flipping algorithm has been used as the second stage decoder.

We have previously shown that the perturbation coefficients ℎF and RF should be selected such that they resemble the characteristics of a Rayleigh fading channel as well as added receiver noise, respectively [4], [11]. Another assumption we made was that, for practical purposes, ℎF ’s are kept relatively constant across each transmitted frame used in our simulations. We note one application for our proposed hybrid decoder is its use in modern communications systems where relatively short frames are transmitted and received. One such application is vehicle to vehicle communications (V2V) where short frames are transmitted to provide situational awareness to drivers and to prevent crashes. V2V transmit frames are typically modeled after cellular communications such as LTE or beyond (4G and 5G) where channel estimation is typically performed at OFDM symbol level or subframe level [14], [15], [16]. In case of V2V applications, number of demodulation reference symbols (DMRS) are increased from 3 to 4 to better adapt to fast channel variations due to speed of vehicles [17]. Even with the advances in physical layers and channel estimation techniques for mobile applications, changing perturbation coefficients at transmitted bit level is extremely challenging [18]. We believe it is appropriate to keep perturbation coefficients relatively constant across each transmitted and received frame.
Finally, given the V2V communications apply to (moving) vehicles, a Doppler frequency of 1000 Hz was considered that correspond to vehicle speed of 120 mi/h (53.6 m/s). The speed was chosen to simulate a head on collision where two vehicles approach one another with the speed of 60 mi/h relative to ground. The Doppler frequency also represent a fast fading Rayleigh channel assuming the V2V transmit packet is a 10 ms long LTE radio frame.
IV. RESULTS
To assess overall performance of our proposed hybrid decoder, three major elements have to be evaluated. Error performance of the decoder is the first, and probably the most important, element that quantifies the ability of the decoder to detect and correct introduced errors. The second element is the efficiency of the decoder that is quantified by the number of iterations required to achieve certain error performance (probability of error). And the last element is the computational complexity in terms of number of mathematical or logic operations per given iteration.
Our proposed hybrid decoder utilizes a two stage decoding scheme with first stage being a simplified SPA decoder and the second stage being an improved noisy gradient bit flipping decoder described in [11]. Our proposed hybrid decoder outperforms individual decoders used in each stage from error performance and efficiency perspectives as shown in figure 2. Figure 2 shows performance of the proposed hybrid decoder in a simulated fast fading channel with Doppler frequency of 1000 Hz. As can be seen in figure 2, our proposed hybrid decoder provides a minimum of 3 dB improvement over other decoders to achieve the probability of error of 1: 10 m . Additionally, our proposed decoder provides 60% improvement in required number of iterations comparing to the second best decoder (SPA). V. CONCLUSIONS We have proposed a two-stage hybrid decoder using a simplified SPA decoder in first stage and an improved noisy gradient decent bit flipping decoder in the second stage. We have observed a minimum of 3 dB improvement over the second best decoder studied in this paper. Additionally, we have observed approximately 60% improvement in required number of iterations to achieve a given probability of error. The improved error performance of the proposed decoder observed here also agrees with previous works such as [7].
