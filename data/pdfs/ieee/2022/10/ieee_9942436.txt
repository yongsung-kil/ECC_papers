A Fast Decoding Convergence of SISO Repetition – QC-LDPC Codes Concatenated at the Receiver

Amr Tarek Communication Department Military Technical College (MTC)
Cairo, Egypt amrarag@gmail.com

Amr Abdelaziz Communication Department Military Technical College (MTC)
Cairo, Egypt amrashry@mtc.edu.eg

Hisham Al-Dahshan Communication Department Military Technical College (MTC)
Cairo, Egypt hdahshan@mtc.edu.eg

Abstract—Low-Density Parity Check (LDPC) is one of the contemporary error-correcting codes that has shown near Shannon capacity performance; it has been implemented into various standards such as the second-generation Digital Video Broadcasting for Satellite (DVB-S2) and eventually made its way toward the 5G New Radio (NR) for the data channel. In an effort to enhance the LDPC decoding performance without a significant increase in the decoding complexity, in this paper, a receiver-based concatenated decoding approach is proposed, which is composed of the soft-input soft-output (SISO) repetition and offset minsum (OMS) algorithm representing outer and inner decoders, respectively. At the receiver, the received signal is oversampled with an oversampling rate (OSR) faster than multiple integers of the Nyquist rate to produce repeated symbols. Moreover, the repetition employed at the receiver to handle the repeated symbols will not impact the coding rate. Finally, the simulation results revealed that the proposed scheme could accomplish faster-decoding convergence compared to the conventional LDPC decoders and achieve coding gain up to 2.3 dB with OSR = 2. Additionally, the computational complexity does not increase significantly; on the contrary, the achieved gain can be exploited to reduce the number of iterations.
Index Terms—OMS, SISO repetition, Oversampling, Serial Concatenation, Complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
L DPC codes, first introduced by Gallager in 1960 [1] and later reintroduced by MacKay and Neal [2], can achieve efficient error correction performance, rendering them a viable choice for wireless communication systems, such as the DVB-S2 standard and DVB-S2 Extensions (DVB-S2X) in [7], which are the most well-known standards used for satellite communications, the WiGig millimeter wave (mmWave) standard [8]. In addition, LDPC codes have recently been adopted for the data channel in 5G New Radio (NR) [9]. On the other hand, concatenated codes set the foundation for a global code with a high error-correction capability. Moreover, concatenated codes are widely used in satellite communications, such as BCH codes which are serially concatenated with LDPC codes in the DVB-S2 standard. In this context, repetition codes were serially concatenated with an accumulator when employed as a part of repeat-accumulate (RA) codes, and irregular repeat-accumulate (IRA) codes can be introduced as a new class of LDPC codes, where the irregularity arises from a non-uniform construction of repetition

code [11]. Moreover, in [12], both BCH and repetition coding schemes are examined for reducing the atmospheric turbulence effect, and the results show that they can significantly improve the decoding performance when concatenated. Furthermore, oversampling with a rate faster than the Nyquist rate has been investigated in terms of achievable rate and resolution enhancement in [13], [14]. In this regard, oversampling with a 1-bit quantizer at the receiver can benefit from the Independent and identically distributed, i.e., i.i.d, randomness in the channel noise, intrinsic receiver noise, and inter-symbol interference of the band-limited channels. Additionally, in [15], oversampling within the bit decreases the likelihood of a false alarm when only the noise is presented at the receiver’s front end. In an effort to satisfy the strict requirements of the bit error rate (BER) performance and complexity for the Ultra-Reliable and Low-Latency Communication (URLLC) applications [18], the concatenation decoding scheme at the receiver is introduced to improve the BER performance and benefit from the fast decoding convergence in reducing the number of iterations, and the decoding latency. In this sense, the received signal is oversampled with a rate faster than the Nyquist rate within the symbol period; this repeated M-folded data are then processed by a serial concatenation decoding scheme composed of SISO repetition and OMS decoders that represent inner and outer decoders, respectively. Finally, simulation results reveal that the proposed scheme improves the decoding performance, which can be exploited to reduce the decoding latency. This paper is organized as follows. In section II, the iterative decoding process is described briefly for several LDPC decoders such as Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA), Min-Sum (MS), Normalized Min-Sum (NMS), and Offset Min-Sum (OMS). Then, in section III, the proposed scheme is interpreted in detail. Finally, in section IV, the simulation results are discussed.

This paper proposes a receiver-based concatenation scheme to improve the performance of the MS decoding algorithm without significantly affecting the decoding complexity. The simulation results show that the proposed scheme outperforms the well-known LDPC decoders, such as SPA, MS, and OMS algorithms, in the decoding performance. Furthermore, this proposed scheme exhibits a notably faster decoding convergence than conventional LDPC algorithms and achieves coding gain up to 2.3 dB with OSR = 2. In addition, this improvement can be exploited to reduce the number of iterations significantly. Moreover, the considerable coding gain achieved by the proposed scheme makes it attractive for mmWave communications, such as satellite communications.
