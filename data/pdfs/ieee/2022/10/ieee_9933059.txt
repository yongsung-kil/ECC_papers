Abstract—In this paper, read disturb (RD) at various program/erase (P/E) stages has been thoroughly explored, and shortterm lifetime prediction models of RD and endurance are proposed. Based on these, a new short-term warning system (STWS) is proposed, which can extend the lifetime of 3D NAND-based storage by adjusting LDPC codes dynamically. The experimental result shows that the proposed prediction models have high reliabilities, and STWS can effectively prolong the lifetime of NAND flash.

I. INTRODUCTION
Because of the low power consumption, high reliability, and ultra-high storage density, NAND-based solid-state drives (SSDs) are increasingly becoming the mainstream non-volatile storage devices. Thereby, improving SSD performances by co-optimizing raw NAND chips and storage systems turns to be very important. Commonly, the number of P/E cycles before an uncorrectable error occurs is called the lifetime of NAND flash. There have been a large amount of works on predicting the trend of error bits to prolong the lifetime of NAND flash. However, most of the prediction models are built on machine learning techniques that require extensive tests and calculations for constructing models [1], [3], [4], and many parameters are required, such as operating time, voltage threshold (Vth), etc [2]. A simpler prediction model combined with dynamic LDPC codes may both save user data space and extense NAND flash lifetime.
In this paper, we study the read disturb (RD) degradation in different endurance of 3D triple-level cell (TLC) chargetrap (CT) NAND flash memory via the raw NAND chip tester (NplusT). The relationships of fail bit count (FBC) between different read times and the relationships of FBC between different endurances are investigated. Some linear relationships are found between FBC in the early stages of operations and FBC in the middle and late stages of operations in different blocks. Based on the above relationships, we summarize FBC-related formulations for the chip with few blocks, and variables in the formulations are only FBC and the number of target operations. According to (3) and (4), we propose a short-term warning system (STWS) for hot-data storage in 3D TLC CT NAND flash memory. During the use of NAND flash, STWS is activated periodically to predict FBC. The periodic value is decided by the user. STWS firstly predicts FBC after several P/E cycles based on the currently known FBC, then it chooses the most suitable LDPC codes for future encoding and decoding according to the predicted value.

III. CONCLUSION
In this work, we study the short-terming warning system in NAND-based storage systems. Based on the characterizations of CT type TLC 3D NAND chips, it is found that linear relationships exist between the FBC in the early stages and in the middle/late stages during PE cycling operation. Thereby, a reliable short-term prediction model for hot-data storage is verified and a short-term warning system (STWS) is proposed. By evaluating FBC periodically and adjusting LDPC codes dynamically, STWS can save space for user data in the early stage of PE cycling and can prolong the storage lifetime.
