Abstract—This paper is devoted to the design of a decoder
structure for
Quasi-Cyclic
(QC)
Low-Density
Parity-Check
(LDPC) codes of the DVB-S2X standard. The quasi-cyclic form
of the matrix representation is the most convenient for the
hardware implementation of the LDPC decoder, so the matrices
of the DVB-S2X standard are converted to the QC form. The
implementation of an LDPC decoder for the DVB-S2X standard
is a challenging task, especially because of the long code words
and large parity-check matrices. We propose a highly parallel
FPGA implementation of LDPC decoder design based on the
layered architecture and Min-Sum decoding algorithm. The
structure of the decoder consists of 360 parallel cores responsible
for the sequential calculation of variable-to-check (VTC), check-
to-variable (CTV) messages and update posterior probabilities
(APPs). Block RAM memory was used to store intermediate
values of APP and CTVs. The decoder design is applicable to all
code rates and allows to achieve throughput up to 500 Mbps at a
clock frequency of 250 MHz

I. INTRODUCTION
Low Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes were initially
proposed by Gallager in 1963 [1] but they were not used for
almost four decades mainly due to the computational
complexity of the algorithms. LDPC codes were then
rediscovered by Mackay and Neal [2] in late 1990’s whereas
the increasing computing capacity made their usage realistic.
Due to their good error correction performance able to
achieve up to 0.0045 dB of the Shannon’s limit at a BER of
10−6 [3], LDPC codes were adopted in many wireless
standards such as IEEE 802.11n/ad/ay/ax (WiFi) [4], IEEE
802.16e (WiMAX) [5], ETSI 5G [6]. Space standards
including DVB-S2 [7], DVB-S2X [8] and CCSDS [9] are
also selected LDPC codes.
The current trend towards the development of wireless
communication systems for satellites with expected high
throughput
increases
the
need
for
efficient
solutions
providing good performance LDPC codes as well as high
performance hardware decoder architectures.
LDPC codes are decoded by message-passing iterative
algorithms. The Min-Sum algorithm (MSA) is one of them.
The MSA is a simplified Belief Propagation algorithm (BP).
The
key
feature
of
the
MSA
is
the
reduction
of
computational complexity by eliminating and replacing the
operations
of
calculating
hyperbolic
tangents.
Simultaneously, the MSA achieves good performance close
to BP algorithm.
In this paper, we propose an implementation of the LDPC
decoder for the DVB-S2X standard. The implementation of
the decoder for the DVB-S2 standard will differ from the
proposed one in that it will be necessary to consider the
presence of several diagonals in the parity-check matrix. The
solution to this problem was proposed in the articles [10-12].
Also, we focus on the high throughput and parallelism of the
decoder in combination with optimization of consumed
resources. To increase parallelism and save FPGA resources,
memory structures based on block memory (BRAM) have
been proposed.
The parity check matrix of DVB-S2X standard can be
converted to a QC form [13]. The quasi-cyclic form is one of
the most convenient for building decoders with a high
throughput and has been widely used in recent years [14-16].
The remainder of the paper is organized as follows: in
section II, notations and MS algorithm are introduced. Then,
in section III the proposed FPGA architecture of the LDPC
decoder for QC LDPC codes is presented. The hardware
implementation results are presented in section IV. Finally,
section V summarizes the implementation results.
II. DEFINITIONS AND NOTATIONS
A regular (N, K) LDPC code is represented as a matrix H
which includes M ≥N −K rows and N columns. Each
column in H is associated to a bit of the codeword or
Variable Node (VN), and each row corresponds to a parity
check equation or Check Node (CN).
Layered decoding was introduced by Mansour [17] and
Hocevar [18]. Term layered decoding means that the parity
check matrix is considered as a sequence of elementary
matrixes, or layers. Each layer is updated in sequence.

III. PROPOSED DECODER ARCHITECTURE
Decoding of LDPC codes can be two types: hard decision
decoding and soft decision decoding. Our LDPC decoder
uses soft decision at the input and gives hard decision at the
output. The proposed general structure of the decoding core
is shown in Figure 1 [20]. The LDPC decoder core consists
of a VTC, a Min/Submin calculation blocks and a block for
calculation CTV and APP values. The LDPC decoder core
assumes that the APP values and its corresponding CTV
value are received at the input one by one. In turn, the
resulting APP values (APP_NEW) and the corresponding
updated CTV
(CTV_NEW) are sequentially fed to the decoder core output.

The storage of checks-to-variable messages is built on a

cell also stores 4 values of 9 bits each, but the number of
addresses has been increased to 660 (see Figure 4). This
number of addresses is intended for storing CTV messages
for all rows and columns of the parity-check LDPC matrix.

on the Kintex-7 FPGA (XC7k325tfbg900 - 2) development

Mbit/sec.

V. CONCLUSION
Layered min-sum algorithm was discussed and its short
overview was given. This layered approach combined with
the storage organization scheme can significantly reduce
resource consumption and increase throughput.
This paper presented a highly parallel LDPC decoder
architecture. In order to solve the problem of long codewords
and large matrices of the traditional LDPC decoder for DVB-
S2X standard, we propose an efficient memory design. Main
strengths of this architecture are efficient use of FPGA
resources and its high throughput. This implementation can
reach up to 10 Gbps per iteration.
In summary, this architecture is area efficient, high
throughput and can use any LDPC code from DVB-S2X
standard. Implementation can use any block size, code rate
and any matrix from the specified standard.
