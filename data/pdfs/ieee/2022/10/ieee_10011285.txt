Abstract—Hard-decision LDPC decoders are widely used in communication and storage systems due to their simple circuits. However, existing hard decision LDPC decoding circuits lead to slow decoding convergence due to flipping according to the maximum energy value, reducing decoding performance. This paper proposes the Dual-Energy Probabilistic Gradient Descent Bit-Flipping (DPGDBF) hard-decision algorithm. If the energy value of the variable node reaches the threshold, all nodes with the threshold energy will be flipped, and the node with the submaximum threshold energy will be flipped with a certain probability. otherwise, only all variable nodes with the maximum energy value are flipped. The simulation results show that, in a limited iterative range, compared with Tabu-list RandomPenalty Gradient Descent Bit-Flipping (TRGDBF), for (2048, 1024) codes, the average number of decoding iterations of the algorithm is reduced by 12%, and the decoding performance is improved to a certain extent; for (9216,7680) codes, the average number of decoding iterations of the DPGDBF algorithm is reduced by 18%, and the decoding performance is improved by 4 times. In addition, this paper proposes a hardware implementation of the DPGDBF algorithm for (2048, 1024) codes, with a maximum clock frequency of 305.53MHz and a through-put of 19.52Gbps.
Keywords—low-density parity-check codes, Gradient descent bit-flipping, iterative decoding, Double-Energy value
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) is a high-performance channel coding proposed by Gallager in the 1960s [1]. Because its performance is close to the Shannon limit, the decoding is simple, and the structure is flexible. LDPC codes have been applied to communication standards such as WiMax, DVB-S2, IEEE802.16e, IEEE802.11, and the latest 5G air interface.
The LDPC decoder achieves decoding by iteratively exchanging information between the variable nodes and the check nodes. The LDPC decoding algorithm is divided into a soft-decision algorithm and a hard-decision algorithm accord-

ing to the different types of exchanged information. Softdecision algorithms exchange decoding probability information and include Belief Propagation BP [2], Min-Sum MS [3], and Offset Min-Sum OMS [4]. Hard-decision algorithms exchange binary messages, classical algorithms such as the Bit-Flipping BF [5] and the Gallager-B [6] algorithms. The soft-decision algorithm has better performance than the hard-decision algorithm. However, the calculation is complicated due to the large number of messages passed between nodes. The calculation time is long, the hard-decision decoding performance is poor, but only one bit of information is passed between any two nodes, which is easy to implement in hardware. Therefore, the hard-decision algorithm is mostly used to implement LDPC in communication systems. Many variants of BF algorithms have been proposed in academia, such as Weighted Bit-Flipping BF (WBF) algorithm [7], Improved Bit-Flipping BF algorithm (IBF) [8], Gradient Descent Bit-Flipping (GDBF) [9], TRGDBF [10].
GDBF is a hard-decision algorithm proposed by academia in recent years. The algorithm defines a nonlinear objective function, converts the decoding processing into solving the maximum value of this function processing, calculates the energy value of the variable node according to the inversion function, finds the maximum energy value among all variable nodes (VNs), and the VNs with the maximum energy value are flipped. To improve the decoding performance of the GDBF algorithm, The random-penalty term of the TRGDBF algorithm is introduced when calculating the energy value to prevent the decoder from entering the trap set; In addition, the tabu-list is defined by the TRGDBF algorithm to avoid continuously repeated flipping, thereby improving the decoding efficiency. However, these two algorithms both flip according to the maximum energy value, resulting in slow decoding convergence speed and low probability of escaping from trap sets, reducing decoding performance.

III. DUAL-ENERGY PROBABILISTIC GDBF ALGORITHM
In the TRGDBF algorithm, only the VNs with the maximum energy value are flipped. In terms of numerical optimization, to avoid oscillation at the local maximum, the search point is moved towards the local maximum of the objective function with a small step size. However, a smaller step size results in slower convergence.

In order to speed up the convergence speed, this paper proposes a dual-energy value bit-flipping algorithm, which has a faster convergence speed than the single-energy value bitflipping algorithm due to its larger step size. However, suppose the search point is near the local maximum. In that case, the bit-flip algorithm with a larger step size is not suitable for finding the local maximum point, which leads to the oscillation of the dual-energy value bit-flip algorithm. It is necessary to dynamically adjust the larger step size to the smaller one to find the local maximum. Therefore, adjusting the dual-energy value bit dynamically flips to the single-energy value bit flips is necessary.

In addition, to further improve the decoding performance, the probability flip method is adopted. V. HARDWARE ARCHITECTURE
The hardware resource overhead of DPGDBF is unavoidable to reduce the resource overhead, the sequence of the random penalty and flip factor are predetermined and put into the random number register, and the random penalty term and flip factor are generated by shifting the register right. In order to improve the throughput and decoding efficiency, the method of early termination is adopted. Once all the parity checks are satisfied, the decoder stops decoding and starts decoding when the next codeword is pushed.

A. Top-Level Architecture
The top-level architecture of the DPGDBF decoder is shown in Fig 6, which is similar to the TRGDBF structure, the difference is the setting of the random sequence generator of the flip factor. The decoding process is briefly described in the following. The first group of D flip-flops store the codeword r received from the channel, and the second group of D flip-
flops are VN registers that store the estimated codeword v .At
the beginning of the decoder, according to the init signal, the codeword r in the channel is assigned to the variable node registers, and the XOR of the received codeword r and the VNs v are calculated. The check node units (CNUs) calculate the syndrome vector c according to the interconnection between the variable node and the check node to judge whether the estimated codeword is valid. Energy calculation units (ECUs) calculate the energy values of all VNs according to the interconnection of the CNs to the VNs, and express it in a special format called the one-hot format, the details will be shown below. In the inversion indication module, for the variable node that meets the requirements, the related inversion
indication signal I j  1 .otherwise, I j  0 .Finally, the XOR of
the VNs and the related inversion indication signal are calculated, complete the inversion of the VNs, and start the next iteration. If the syndrome vector c are equal to 0, the correct signal becomes 1, the value in the variable node register is output, and the new codeword is input into the first group of registers.
