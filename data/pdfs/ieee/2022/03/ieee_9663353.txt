QC-LDPC Codes With Large Column Weight and Free of Small Size ETSs

Farzane Amirzade , Mohammad-Reza Sadeghi , and Daniel Panario , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract— An approach to improve the performance of QC-LDPC codes is the removal of harmful trapping sets by increasing the girth. However, constructing these LDPC codes with large column weights and girth more than 8 is not easy. We are concerned with protograph-based LDPC codes with large column weights and free of small size trapping sets. We use the edge-coloring technique and some concepts from graph theory such as rainbow cycles to show that for large column weights the removal of all 8-cycles but the ones we call rainbow 8-cycle causes the elimination of several small size trapping sets. We provide a detailed theoretical analysis of these harmful trapping sets. Then, we apply them to array-based LDPC codes to significantly simplify and optimize the necessary and sufficient conditions to eliminate those 8-cycles from the Tanner graph. The given exponent matrices and simulation results show the impact of this simplification and the removal of the above mentioned 8-cycles.
Index Terms— QC-LDPC codes, array-based LDPC codes, girth, tanner graph, trapping sets.
I. INTRODUCTION
P ROTOGRAPH-BASED LDPC codes such as QC-LDPC codes, and algebraic-based LDPC codes such as arraybased LDPC codes [1], which are associated with respective exponent matrices, are a class of LDPC codes in which checking and controlling 2k-cycles and other graphical features of their Tanner graph (TG) are accomplished by means of their exponent matrix.
In order to construct the exponent matrix B of an LDPC code with large column weight m and large girth g, identifying the entries of B is difficult due to the high complexity of the search algorithm. For example, suppose B with girth g ≥ 10 is required. Then, for column weight m = 3, we should avoid 8-cycles in all submatrices of sizes 2 × 2, 2 × 3, 2 × 4, 3 × 2, 3 × 3, 3 × 4 of the exponent matrix. When the column weight increases from 3 to m ≥ 4, then in addition to check those submatrices for each three rows of B we need to consider submatrices with 4 rows, that are 4 × 2, 4 × 3 and 4 × 4 submatrices of B; this causes an increase in the computational complexity. This is the reason for the lack of examples of protograph-based LDPC codes with girth 10 and large m. In fact, most of the existing numerical results for exponent matrices with g = 10, 12 have m = 3, 4 [6]–[8] and for g = 6, 8, there are exponent matrices with larger m = 5, 6 [6].
One of the reasons for increasing the girth is the removal of graphical features such as small size trapping sets which

can have detrimental impact on the performance of an LDPC code. An (a, b) trapping set is an induced subgraph of the TG with a variable-nodes and b check-nodes of odd degrees. If the check-nodes are of degrees 1 and 2, then it is an elementary trapping set (ETS). For a bipartite graph G corresponding to an ETS, a variable node (VN) graph is considered by removing all degree-1 check-nodes, defining variable-nodes of G as its vertices, and degree-2 check-nodes connecting the variable-nodes in G as its edges. Recently, several articles [2]–[5] have been published with the focus on constructing LDPC codes with TGs free of small size trapping sets. The existing results provide us with different methods, including an edge-coloring technique [2], [3], to remove a specific trapping set without increasing the girth that we use here.
An edge coloring of a graph is an assignment of colors (labels) to the edges of the graph so that no two adjacent edges have the same color (label). According to the edgecoloring technique, the necessary condition to the existence of an (a, b) ETS in the TG of a protograph-based LDPC code with an m × n exponent matrix B is that the edges of its VN graph can be labeled by m colors. Indeed from the definition of a VN graph we can conclude that each edge of the VN graph corresponds to a row of B and thus the row indices are used to color the edges of the VN graph. Thus, the VN graph of an 8-cycle from two rows of B is a 4-cycle which is colored by two labels. The VN graph of an 8-cycle from three (four) rows of B is labeled by three (four) colors. A cycle in an edge-colored graph is a rainbow if no color repeats on it. Hence, the VN graph of an 8-cycle from four rows of B is a rainbow 4-cycle.
In [2], we introduced the edge-coloring technique to construct an exponent matrix with a TG free of small size ETSs. We applied it to an algebraic-based QC-LDPC code with girth 6 and m = 3. In [3], we generalized this method to all protograph-based LDPC codes and extended the results for codes with g = 6 (g = 8) and m = 4, 5, 6 (m = 3, 4). In [4], we provided other technique to remove harmful trapping sets containing an 8-cycle with a chord (8-cycle which includes two 6-cycles) or a 12-cycle with a chord (12-cycle including two 8-cycles). All these methods prove that without increasing the girth we can remove harmful features from the TG and then improve the performance of the code.
In this letter, motivated by the above mentioned methods to remove ETSs instead of increasing the girth, we provide a method to remove small size ETSs of LDPC codes with large column weights and girth 8. The merit of our method is that it causes the removal of ETSs which are also removed when raising the girth from 8 to 10.
In our method, we eliminate all 8-cycles from 2 and 3 rows of B. Thus, each three rows of B is free of 8-cycles and the VN graph of the existing 8-cycles is rainbow which we call them rainbow 8-cycles. LDPC codes. We provide necessary and sufficient conditions

condition for the TG of an array-based LDPC code to have 8-cycles{i1,i2} is that ±(j1 − j2) ≡ ±(j3 − j4) (mod p).
Proof: Consider two 2 × 2 submatrices with row indices i1, i2 and column indices j1, j2 for one submatrix and column indices j3, j4 for another submatrix. V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, using the edge coloring technique we provide a method to remove small size trapping sets of a QC-LDPC code with large column weight and we apply our method to array-based LDPC codes.
