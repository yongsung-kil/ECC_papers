A Polygonal Line Min-Sum Decoding Scheme for Low Density Parity Check Codes
Yin Xu , Member, IEEE, Hao Ju , Graduate Student Member, IEEE, Dazhi He , Member, IEEE, Na Gao , Yiyan Wu , Fellow, IEEE, Yihang Huang , Member, IEEE, and Wenjun Zhang , Fellow, IEEE

Abstract—Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are widely used as error correction codes in new generation digital TV standards, such as the second generation of terrestrial digital video broadcasting standard (DVB-T2), Advanced Television Systems Committee (ATSC) 3.0, etc. The nonlinear belief propagation (BP) algorithm has excellent decoding performance for LDPC codes, but is often simplified in hardware implementations by linear min-sum (MS) algorithm due to its high complexity. This simplification also leads to over-estimation problems, which can be corrected by adding factors in conventional algorithms (e.g., normalized min-sum (NMS), offset min-sum (OMS), and variable scaling normalized min-sum (VMS) algorithms). However, the correction factors of these modified MS algorithms cannot adapt to different channels and modulations, and the performance needs further improvement. In this paper, the concepts of over-estimation value (OEV) and over-estimation rate (OER) are introduced to describe the over-estimation problem of the MS algorithm. Then, under the guidance of OEV and OER, a polygonal line min-sum (PMS) algorithm with correction factors adapted to different channels and modulations is proposed according to LLR distribution. Following the properties of OEV and OER, PMS algorithm is further simplified into Simplified PMS (SPMS) algorithm. LDPC codes from ATSC 3.0 are adopted in this paper to evaluate SPMS algorithm in comparison with the conventional algorithms. Extensive simulation results show that the SPMS algorithm for ATSC 3.0 LDPC decoder has at most 1.61dB, 0.24dB and 0.36dB gain over NMS, OMS and VMS algorithms respectively when frame error rate (FER) is at 10−4 level over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel with QPSK modulation. More importantly, the simulation results show that the SPMS algorithm can achieve much better performance than these modified MS algorithms over AWGN and Rayleigh channel with higher-order modulations or under limited maximum iteration number.
Index Terms—OEV, OER, PMS, SPMS, LDPC, decoding, ATSC 3.0.

So far, several types of structured LDPC codes [2], [3], [4] have been adopted in many wireless standards and systems, such as Wi-Fi [5], Wi-Max [6], Digital Video Broadcasting (DVB) [7], [8], ATSC 3.0 [9], [10], [11], and 5G New Radio (NR) [12], [13], [14].
As we all know, the BP algorithm is one of the most important decoding methods for LDPC codes. However, its computational complexity of horizontal step is too high to be widely used in practical hardware implementation. The MS algorithm [15] greatly reduces the complexity of the BP algorithm by introducing an approximate operation during the horizontal step, but this approximate operation brings an overestimation problem and dramatically reduces the decoding performance.
Then, the NMS algorithm [16], [17] and the OMS algorithm [18], [19] were proposed to compensate for the overestimation problem in the MS algorithm by using normalized and offset factors to improve the performance. In [20], the author provided an optimized normalized and offset factor for the ATSC 3.0 LDPC codes of both raptor-like (RL) and irregular repeat accumulate (IRA) structures and the actual SNR performance when FER is at 10−4 level over AWGN channel with QPSK modulation.
To further improve the decoding performance, paper [21], [22] qualitatively analyzed the relationship between the over-estimation problem and the degree distribution and proposed the two-dimensional normalized min-sum (2D-NMS) and two-dimensional offset min-sum (2D-OMS) algorithms after verifying performance by density evolution (DE). In [23], the authors qualitatively explained that the over-estimation problem is closely related to the reliability of the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) value in the iterative decoding process, and the LLR value actually changes with iteration. Based on this, a variable-scaling normalized min-sum (VMS) algorithm in which the normalized factor changes with iteration was proposed. In [24], the author analyzed the VMS algorithm based on generalized mutual information (GMI) [25] method and compared the performance of VMS, NMS, and OMS algorithms. The performance of VMS algorithm is better than NMS algorithm when the correction factors are obtained under AWGN channel with QPSK modulation.

II. LDPC DECODING ALGORITHMS INTRODUCTION
Before introducing LDPC decoding algorithms, it is necessary to introduce its parity check matrix (PCM) first. The PCM Hmn of LDPC code represents that it has m rows and n columns. In this paper, the point (i, j) in PCM Hmn is discussed only when it is ‘1,’ which means the ith check node (CN) is connected with the jth variable node (VN) from the view of Tanner graph. dc and dv represent the CN and VN degree, respectively.
In the iterative decoding of LDPC code, the information (usually LLR) exchanges between VNs and CNs. Uj(,0c)h represents the initial LLR message sending to VN j from channel. Vi(jiter), Ui(jiter) and Vj(iter) represent the LLR message from VN j to CN i, the LLR message from CN i to VN j and the sum of VN j in iteration iter.

III. POLYGONAL LINE MIN-SUM LDPC DECODING ALGORITHM
The modified MS algorithms in [16]-[25] solve the overestimation problem brought by MS algorithm to better approach Shannon limits while maintaining a relatively acceptable complexity. Numerical results have shown that these algorithms have a considerable performance improvement compared with MS algorithm. However, it has been found that these algorithms cannot perform well if the correction factors optimized under AWGN channel with QPSK modulation are directly applied to other channels or modulations.
In this section, a polygonal line min-sum (PMS) algorithm is proposed, which only needs one set of correction factors for variety of channels and modulations. To optimize the PMS algorithm, the quantitative analysis is elaborated as well.

B. PMS Algorithm
As stated in the previous section, the changing of correction factors is corresponding to the rise of average LLR while the number of iterations increases. Basically, LLR distribution is the decisive factor for OEV and OER instead of iteration numbers.
In view of the tanh(|x|) function shown in Fig. 1, the absolute value of LLR can be roughly divided into 3 parts, by two segmentation points P1 and P2. Part 1: [0, LLRP1 ], part 2: (LLRP1 , LLRP2 ] and part 3: (LLRP2 , ∞). From Fig. 1, it can be found that the difference between the variations of tanh(|x|) in these three parts is unmistakable. The value of tanh(|x|) function in part 1 varies roughly linearly with the input LLR value, and the input LLR value is small; the value of tanh(|x|) function in part 3 tends to 1 with slight variation, but the input LLR value is large; the second part is in the middle of the two parts and has less distinctive features. Therefore, this paper determines the segmentation points P1 and P2 by part 1 and part 3.

V. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, the performance of SPMS algorithm is analyzed based on ATSC 3.0 LDPC codes. First, the performance of SPMS algorithm is compared with BP and other modified MS algorithms over AWGN channel with QPSK modulaton. The maximum iteration number is 50, and at least 20 erroneous blocks are attained to ensure the reliability of simulation results at each Es/N0 point. Then, these algorithms are simulated, with same simulation conditions and correction factors, over AWGN and Rayleigh channel with 64QAM modulation. In order to verify that the correction factors of SPMS algorithm can adapt to different channels and modulations. In addition, a performance analysis of the SPMS algorithm is provided when the maximum number of iterations is limited.

In summary, it can be concluded that when using the parameters optimized over AWGN channel under QPSK modulation, the performance of other modified MS algorithms degrades at 64QAM, while the SPMS algorithm can maintain excellent performance. This is because different modulations change the Es/N0 , which makes the LLR input from channel different. The correction factor error of other modified MS algorithms is large. In contrast, the correction factor of the SPMS algorithm is selected based on the LLR distribution, and its characteristics do not change significantly with the modulation, so the correction factor error is small. This verifies that the correction factors of the SPMS algorithm can adapt to different modulations.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, by quantitatively analyzing the over-estimation problem caused when MS algorithm is used to simplify BP algorithm. The PMS algorithm based on the LLR distribution is proposed. To simplify the correction factor selection, PMS algorithm was effectively simplified to SPMS algorithm. Extensive simulation results show that SPMS algorithm for ATSC 3.0 LDPC decoders has at most 1.61dB, 0.24dB and 0.36dB gain over NMS, OMS and VMS algorithms respectively when FER is at 10−4 level over AWGN channel with QPSK modulation. The simulation results show that SPMS algorithm, using parameters of AWGN channel with QPSK modulation, can maintain excellent performance over AWGN or Rayleigh channel with 64QAM modulation. At the same time, it is verified that relying on the parameters obtained under the maximum iteration number of 50, SPMS algorithm can achieve excellent performance when the maximum iteration number is limited. Above all, after obtaining a set of optimal parameters for a certain code through offline operations, it can be directly applied over different channels using variety of modulations without worrying about significant performance loss, which greatly facilitates the practical application of the SPMS algorithm. Consequently, the SPMS algorithm is an excellent choice for decoding schemes while taking into account the decoding complexity and power consumption.
