Parity-Check Coding Transmit Diversity for Wireless Communications With High Mobility
Qi-Yue Yu , Senior Member, IEEE, Tang Li, Hong-Ru Lin, and Feng-Feng Cao

Abstract—High mobility is an important scenario for the 5G wireless communications. However, the high mobility results in serious time-varying fading, bringing challenges to the reliabilities of wireless transmission. Diversity technique is an essential method to overcome the effect of fading. It is noted that an LDPC (lowdensity parity-check) code is a concatenation of single parity-check codes (SPC) and repetition codes (REP) by a random interleaver, and a REP can be viewed as a typical example for achieving time/frequency diversity. Thus, it is appealing to jointly consider LDPC codes and diversity techniques. This paper presents paritycheck coding transmit diversity (PCTD) schemes based on halfrate invertible LDPC codes, which are capable of achieving both diversity and coding gains from the time-varying fading channels. Besides, we investigate two types of PCTD schemes, and take into consideration two transmission modes, which are single-carrier (SC) and orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) transmission modes. A chase combining based message passing algorithm (CC-MPA) is proposed for the proposed schemes, which can take full advantages of signal processing and coding gains. Furthermore, the Shannon limits of time-varying fading channels with BPSK signaling are deduced. Simulation results show that the proposed PCTD with CC-MPA algorithm can provide better BER performances than the classical time-domain diversity scheme in time-varying fading channels, which is appealing for the high mobility communications.
Index Terms—Half-rate invertible code, low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes, message passing algorithm (MPA), timevarying fading channels, transmit diversity.

The appealing solutions are modulation schemes, coding techniques, efficient diversity techniques, and etc [1]–[13]. From the perspective of modulation, [1] proposes a novel transmission scheme named as orthogonal time frequency space (OTFS) modulation, which can significantly improve the bit error rate (BER) performance in the delay-Doppler fast fading channels. In order to detect the OTFS signals, receivers need to know the channel state information (CSI). Then, [2] and [3] propose pilot based channel estimation schemes for OTFS in the fast fading channels. In [4], it focuses on the window designs of OTFS to improve the performances of channel estimation and data detection. The authors of [5] apply OTFS to the downlink multiuser massive multiple-input-multiple-output (MIMO) systems, and presents the precoder and detector with low complexity.
Some works focus on different error control codes to improve the BER performances for high mobility communications [6]– [9]. For example, [6] constructs polar codes in various fast fading channels and derives the upper bound of the error probability. [7] proposes a hash-based cyclic redundancy check (CRC)-aided polar codes. Simulation results show that the hash-concatenated polar codes can provide better BER performances than the classical polar codes in the high-mobility environment. [8] constructs a concatenated code based on an inner irregular low-density parity-check (LDPC) code and an outer CRC code. Simulation results show that the proposed concatenated code provides lower error floor in the fast fading channels. In [9], it constructs a type of quasi-cyclic (QC) non-binary (NB) LDPC codes, and the NB-LDPC based coded modulation system can improve the BER performances in the high-mobility scenarios.
Diversity technique is always viewed as one of the most valuable techniques to overcome the influence of fading. There are various types of diversity methods, i.e., time-domain, frequencydomain, and space-domain diversities. [10] proposes a novel MIMO wireless transmission technique named absolute amplitude differential phase spatial modulation, which is more robust in fast-fading channels. In [11], the authors utilize dual antennas to compensate Doppler shift for an orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) system. The authors of [12] present location-based antenna beamforming technique to overcome the effect of Doppler shift. In [13], a CSI prediction method is proposed for the time-varying fading channels.

It is known that LDPC codes can provide excellent error correction performances, which are widely used in communication and storage systems [17]–[19]. There are many works have been done on this topic [20]– [25], especially, a concatenation of LDPC codes and antenna diversity [24], [25]. In [20], it analyzes the performances of an OFDM-MIMO with LDPC system. [21] presents a virtual multi-antenna system with LDPC code. In [22], it jointly designs NB-LDPC codes and MIMO, and proposes a combined decoder to efficiently compute the LLRs (log likelihood ratio) of an NB-LDPC code, thus reduces the decoding complexity. The authors of [24] present two types of LDPC-MIMO systems, which are based on the transmit antenna selection and orthogonal space-time block code (STBC), and analyze the approximate BER by using Gaussian approximation (GA) approach.
Actually, most of the recent studies simply connect LDPC codes and diversity techniques, without considering the inherent characteristic of LDPC codes. So far, there are many works have been done on constructions of LDPC codes, e.g., finite field, finite geometry and combinatorial mathematics with various lengths and rates [26]–[31]. In general, the construction method of LDPC codes can be classified into two categories, they are graph-theoretic-based LDPC codes, e.g., protograph (PTG) LDPC, and algebraic-based LDPC codes, e.g., superposition (SP) LDPC [32]. In [32], it has been proved that the graphtheoretic-based and algebraic-based approaches can be unified to a general structure of SP-LDPC. It is appealing to construct some special LDPC codes for different applications [33], [34]. Note that an LDPC code can be viewed as a concatenation of single parity-check codes (SPC) and repetition codes (REP) by a random interleaver. Currently, people have tried to extend the SPC part of a LDPC code into some kinds of linear block codes, e.g, BCH code [35], [36]. In contrast, few work focuses on the REP part. Actually, repetition codes are renamed as time/frequency domain transmit diversity, and widely used in fading channels.
Thus, it is appealing to jointly considering LDPC codes and diversity techniques, and provides a update decoding algorithm. Until now, most of the decoding algorithms are investigated to reduce complexity of LDPC codes [37]–[41]. This paper presents parity-check coding transmit diversity (PCTD) schemes with a chase combining based message passing algorithm (CC-MPA), which is a joint consideration of LDPC codes and diversity technique. Moreover, there exhibits a Eb/N0 threshold phenomenon for error-free decoding of LDPC codes that are close to the Shannon limit, and the threshold can be obtained by using density evolution (DE) [42], [43], or Gaussian approximation (GA) [33], [44]. However, the complexities of both DE and GA approaches are extremely high, and not flexible to a general case. Therefore, this paper directly deduces the Shannon limit of a fast fading channel, instead of the threshold of LDPC codes. The main contributions of this paper can be summarized as follows:
1) This paper presents PCTD schemes based on half-rate invertible LDPC codes instead of repetition codes. We mainly introduce two types of PCTD schemes corresponding to the time-domain and the space-time domain

diversities, and investigate the PCTD schemes in both time-varying frequency non-selective and time-varying frequency selective fading channels; 2) We introduce the conception of half-rate invertible codes, including cyclic codes and LDPC codes. Then, a half-rate (3, 6)-regular (1016, 508) QC-LDPC code is presented for further discussion; 3) By jointly utilizing the processed signals and passed messages, a CC-MPA is proposed for the PCTD system, which can be applied to various time-domain diversity cases, providing appealing BER performances; and 4) We deduce the Shannon limits of time-varying fading channels with BPSK signaling, which are utilized as theoretical references. The remained of this paper is outlined as follows. Section II presents the system model. In Section III, we first introduce the conception of half-rate invertible codes, and then propose the PCTD schemes in details. A CC-MPA is investigated in Section IV. In Section V, the Shannon limits of time-varying fading channels with BPSK signaling are presented. Finally, simulations are evaluated in Section VI, and some conclusion remarks are given in Section VII.
II. SYSTEM MODEL
In this paper, let x and x be a scalar and a vector respectively. Define B, N and C by binary, natural and complex fields, respectively. If a vector x is denoted by (x1, x2, . . . , xn, . . . , xN ), define x(a : b) = (xa, xa+1, . . . , xb), where a, b ∈ N and 1 ≤ a < b ≤ N.
Assume the BS is equipped with NT transmit antennas, and the user holds one received antenna. Suppose the information block is u ∈ B1×K , which is sent to the PCTD modular, and the output vector is c ∈ B1×N , where u = (u1, u2, . . . , uK ) and c = (c1, c2, . . . , cN ). By BPSK modulation, c is mapped to a complex mother vector, defined by s ∈ C1×N . Let Nc be a positive integer, and N = NT · Nc. Thus, the 1 × N mother vector s can be divided into NT children vectors, as s = (s1, . . . , snt , . . . , sNT ) with 1 ≤ nt ≤ NT , and the ntth child vector snt is defined by snt = (snt,1, . . . , snt,nc , . . . , snt,Nc ).
This paper considers both time-domain and space-time domain diversities. For the time-domain diversity type, set NT = 1 and NR = 1; meanwhile, for the space-time domain diversity type, let NT = 2 and NR = 1. We will introduce them one-byone.
A. Time-Domain Diversity

Thus, for the DCscheme, the codeword is expressed as c = (u, u, b), which has the same length as that of the RC-scheme case. For this case, the vectors s1(1 : K), s1(K + 1 : 2 K), and s1(2K + 1 : 3 K) are the BPSK modulated signals of u, u and b respectively.

IV. A CHASE COMBINING BASED MESSAGE PASSING DECODING ALGORITHM
Owing to the feature of the half-rate invertible codes, it is known that the information digits u can be obtained by either one of u, b or v. Thus, u and b together can provide diversity gain. Moreover, u and b together form the codeword v = (u, b) that is capable of correcting errors, providing coding gain. The proposed CC-MPA detection algorithm takes into consideration both the diversity gain of signal processing and the coding gain of decoding algorithm. It is known that LDPC codes can be regarded as a concatenation of SPC codes and REP codes. The decoder is interested in computing the LLRs of the codeword and passing them between the variable nodes (VNs) and check nodes (CNs) [48].
In the following parts, the CC-MPA of time-domain PCTD (Ω = 2, 3) will be introduced one by one. It is noted that, the proposed CC-MPA is not necessary for the space-time PCTD case, since the equivalent channel gain is the same for both information and parity-check blocks, indicating there is no more diversity gain.

V. SHANNON LIMIT OF THE PROPOSED SYSTEM IN A FAST FADING CHANNEL
Due to the application of LDPC codes, this section deduces the Shannon limit of the proposed system in a fast fading channel. The Shannon limit is defined by the minimum Eb/N0 that is capable of supporting correct transmission with rate Rc, where Eb represents the bit energy. The computation of Shannon limit involves two steps. The first step is to calculate the channel capacity; and the second step is to solve the lowest Eb/N0 that sustains reliable communications.
With respect to the capacity of fading channels, two concepts are often used, i.e., the ergodic capacity and outage capacity. The ergodic capacity is normally used when the channel varies fast enough with respect to the transmission period of a block, so that all possible channel states may experience. By comparison, the concept of outage capacity is introduced for the scenarios where the channel varies slowly.

VII. CONCLUSION
This paper proposes PCTD schemes that are capable of obtaining both diversity and coding gains from the time-varying fading channels. We mainly introduce two types, one is time-domain diversity, including Ω = 2 and 3, and the other is space-time domain case. For the Ω = 3 time-domain PCTD, RC and DC schemes are discussed in details. We also investigate two transmission modes, they are SC transmission mode and OFDM transmission mode corresponding to the frequency non-selective and frequency-selective fading channels. Half-rate invertible codes are introduced, especially, a half-rate invertible LDPC code. In addition, CC-MPA detection algorithms are presented for the time-domain PCTD, which can take full advantages of the diversity and coding gains.
