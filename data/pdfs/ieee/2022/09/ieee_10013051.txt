Abstract‚ÄĒWe present an enhanced informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) strategy to avoid propagations of unreliable messages in the residual belief-propagation (RBP) algorithm for decoding low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. To reduce the resource needed for updating the degree-one variable nodes (VNs) in an RBP-based algorithm for decoding 5G New Radio (NR) LDPC codes, we further introduce a process consisting of several simple procedures for managing the resource consumption. Simulation results demonstrate that our RBP algorithm based on the proposed IDS strategy outperforms existing RBP-based algorithms at the early decoding iterations. By using our degreeone VN resource managing approach, the performance of both the proposed and the existing decoders is significantly improved in decoding the 5G NR codes.
Index Terms‚ÄĒLDPC codes, belief-propagation decoding, informed dynamic scheduling, decoding resource management, 5G NR.
I. INTRODUCTION
It has been shown that low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes provide capacity-approaching performance when the belief propagation (BP) algorithm is used for decoding. Due to their excellent error-correcting capability, LDPC codes have been considered as the channel codes in several wireless communication standards such as IEEE 802.11ac (WiFi) [2], IEEE 802.16e (WiMAX) [3], and the 3GPP 5G New Radio (NR) specification [4].
For the conventional BP algorithm, all the variable-tocheck (V2C) messages are updated simultaneously, so are all check-to-variable (C2V) messages propagated. Such a fullyparallel updating scheme, which is called flooding scheduling, often need many decoding iterations to achieve the required error-rate performance. To have better decoding efficiency and performance, the standard sequential scheduling (SSS) [11] and informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) strategies have been introduced. It has been shown that some IDS-assisted BP algorithms do yield impressive performance improvement against those using the SSS or flooding scheduling.

The first IDS-aided BP decoder, the residual belief propagation (RBP) algorithm, determines the updating order of the C2V messages based on the C2V residuals, which is defined as the difference between the previous C2V message value and its precomputed update value. It offers impressive performance at the first few iterations but fails to yield satisfactory converged performance. This might due to the fact that the RBP decoding process tends to focus on a small part of nodes/edges of the code graph and thus deprive some erroneous decisions of the opportunities to be corrected. To remedy this greedy behavior in RBP decoding, several related modified decoding strategies have been proposed in the literature [5]‚Äď[10]. Among these improved (or we called RBP-based) algorithms, the silentvariable-node-free (SVNF) RBP algorithm introduced in [6] can not only yield quite nice performance but provide almost best performance-complexity tradeoff.
Most IDS strategies, as just mentioned, aim at reducing the negative effects brought about by the greedy approach and encouraging the propagation of reliable information. On the other hand, we must also pay attention to the cases when erroneous messages were spread. Our extensive computer simulations reveal that sometimes the unreliable messages can be spread via the dynamic scheduling used. It is therefore necessary that an IDS strategy can also prevent or suspend the propagation of unreliable messages.
In each update, the RBP algorithm forwards the C2V message with the maximum residual. A check node (CN) may send messages to its connected variable nodes (VNs) several times but between two consecutive C2V messages sent by the same CN, there might be no new information received by this CN. As no updated V2C message is received, the two C2V messages are generated from the same set of V2C messages. A large C2V residual sometimes implies that the associated VN or the related V2C message might carry unreliable information [9]. Hence, if the C2V message with the maximum residual is sent by the associated CN, this CN should stop delivering

In this paper, we propose what we call the update-beforepropagate RBP (UBPRBP) algorithm. After a CN sends its C2V message in UBPRBP algorithm, this CN will temporarily stay muted until it can receive new information from its connecting VNs; no C2V message can be delivered as the associated CN is set muted. Simulation results show that compared with the RBP algorithm, our algorithm can provide better decoding performance with lower computational complexity.
Furthermore, we found that the LDPC codes used in the 5G NR specification contain many degree-1 VNs. It is observed that the C2V messages associated with the degree-1 VNs are more likely to be updated than the other edges if an IDS decoder is applied. However, this would reduce the decoding efficiency since a degree-1 VN can only provide its intrinsic channel information to the connected CN. As a result, we propose a scheme to remedy this shortcoming. With our method, all degree-1 VN can only be updated at the end of the iteration; when an edge linking to a degree-1 VN is selected, the passing of the C2V message will be performed by one of the other edges linking to the same CN instead. Simulation results show that our degree-1 VN handling process would significantly improve the performance of the RBP-based algorithms when decoding the NR LDPC codes.

III. PROPOSED PROCESS FOR UPDATING DEGREE-ONE VNS FOR IDS STRATEGIES
The LDPC codes specified for the 5G NR have some characteristics which are rarely seen over the LDPC codes used in the other communication standards. One of the characteristics is the appearance of degree-1 VNs on the code graph, which is designed for supporting a higher level of flexibility for the incremental-redundancy hybrid automatic-repeat-request (IRHARQ) schemes [4].
Through our simulations, we found that the degree-1 VNs can be selected and updated more frequently than the other VNs when IDS strategies are applied in decoding. One of the proper explanations for this phenomenon is given as follows. Based on the density evolution for LDPC codes with BP decoding [14], we know that the VN LLR magnitudes will expand as the iteration count increases and so do the V2C messages. For a degree-1 VN, however, the V2C message it sends would always be the channel reliability value (2yn/ŌÉ2). Suppose the degree of VN vn is one and cm is its onlyconnected CN. After the message-passing decoding is performed a few iterations, the magnitudes of the V2C messages Lnv2‚Ä≤‚Üí c m for n‚Ä≤ ‚ąą N (m) \ n might rapidly grow while Lnv2‚Üíc m remains unchanged. Since the magnitude of a C2V message Lcm2‚Üí v a computed by (2) is usually dominated by the smallest magnitude of the V2C messages Lav2‚Ä≤‚Üíc m, a‚Ä≤ ‚ąą N (m) \ a. It could be predicted that the C2V messages‚Äô magnitude (either for the propagated or precomputed versions) Lm c2‚Üí v n‚Ä≤, n‚Ä≤ ‚ąą N (m) \ n would be dominated by |Lnv2‚Üíc m| = |2yn/ŌÉ2| and hence delivery of these C2V messages would be eliminated since the associated residuals can be quite small. By contrast, the residual of Lcm2‚Üí v n might be larger than C2V messages due to the continuous updates of Lnv2‚Ä≤‚Üí c m n‚Ä≤ ‚ąą N (m) \ n. As a result, the C2V message related to a degree-1 VN would have a higher opportunity of being selected and updated than the other C2V messages linked to the same CN. Consuming much decoding resource on updating the degree-1 VNs, however, would result in low decoding efficiency because these nodes can only provide its intrinsic channel information to the connected CN.
For the IDS strategies we introduce a process to reduce the efficiency loss caused by the appearance of the degree-1 VNs. The proposed degree-1 VN (D1VN) processing scheme works based on following guidelines: 1) all degree-1 VNs would be updated only once in each iteration, and the updating is performed at end of each iteration; 2) in one iteration, the C2V messages associated with the degree-1 VNs can participate the message selection once only; 3) while the C2V

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, we compare the proposed algorithms with the original RBP algorithm [5] and the SVNF-RBP algorithm [6], which is one of the best-performed IDS algorithms in the literature. Two codes are adopted in our simulations: one is the (1944,972) code used in IEEE 802.11 (WiFi) standard, and the other is the (1848,616) codes used for 5G NR, which is constructed base on the Base Graph (BG) I [4] with lift size Z = 28. Note that following the instructions in [4], the NR code is a punctured code generated from a length-1904 mother code, and the punctured bits are the VNs corresponding to the first two columns in the BG I. Also, there are 1176 degree-1 VNs in this code. For all algorithms we present, one iteration will be counted after E C2V messages are propagated.
Fig. 1 shows the frame error rate (FER) performance of the RBP, SVNF-RBP, and the UBPRBP algorithms in decoding

the WiFi code. The results of 2 and 5 iterations are both presented in the figure. It can be seen that the UBPRBP decoder achieves about 0.4 dB and 0.2 dB gains against the RBP and SVNF-RBP decoder at FER ‚Čą 10‚ąí1 when the iteration count is 2. At the 5th iteration, our UBPRBP algorithm outperforms the RBP decoder about 0.1 dB at FER ‚Čą 10‚ąí3. What presented in Fig. 2 are the BER and FER convergence performance of the IDS algorithms in decoding the WiFi code at SNR (Eb/N0)=1.75 dB. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we present the UBPRBP algorithm, which adopts a new IDS strategy, for decoding LDPC codes. For the UBPRBP algorithm, we introduce the concept of setting the CNs just delivering C2V messages muted to mitigate the propagations of unreliable messages. Experimental results show that in comparison with the RBP algorithm and the SVNF algorithm, the proposed UBPRBP algorithm yields superior performance at the early stage of decoding. The D1VN processing scheme is also presented in this paper. As the D1VN processing scheme incorporated, the RBP-based algorithms are able to release the decoding resource unreasonably occupied by the degree-1 VNs and hence distribute the resource more effectively while decoding the 5G NR LDPC codes. Simulation results indicate that the RBP-D1VN, SVNF-RBP-D1VN, and UBPRBP-D1VN algorithms would significantly outperform their corresponding original versions in decoding the 5G NR LDPC codes, and the UBPRBPD1VN algorithm is the best-performing one among these three decoders.
