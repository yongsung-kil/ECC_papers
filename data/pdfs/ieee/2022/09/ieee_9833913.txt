Improved Layered Normalized Min-Sum Algorithm for 5G NR LDPC
Beeshanga Abewardana Jayawickrama , Member, IEEE, and Ying He , Member, IEEE

Abstract—Layered Normalised Min-Sum algorithm is a widely used message passing algorithm for decoding Low Density Parity Check codes. The decoding performance heavily depends on the normalisation factor. Firstly, we leverage the bit structure present in 5G transport blocks to develop an improved decoding algorithm for 5G. Secondly, we determine the optimal normalisation factors of the proposed algorithm using a Deep Neural Network. Performance evaluations show an improvement of 0.3-1.9 dB, with no extra cost on multiplier resources on hardware. Hence, the proposed method is highly attractive for practical 5G decoder implementations.
Index Terms—Low density parity check codes, iterative decoding, neural networks.
I. INTRODUCTION
T HE 5G New Radio (NR) uses Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes for channel coding [1]. LDPC codes are capacity achieving in Memoryless Binary Input Output Symmetric (MBIOS) channels. The parity check matrix is sparse. Theoretically, the Maximum Likelihood LDPC decoding in MBIOS channel can be done as a brute-force search among all possible codewords i.e., the problem is NPcomplete. However, that is not practical due to extremely high computational complexity. Therefore, various sub-optimal message passing algorithms are used for achieving capacities near the theoretical bounds. In LDPC message passing algorithms, a bit in a codeword is referred to as a Variable Node (VN). The abstract node that checks the parity of selected VNs is referred to as a Check Node (CN). Each CN checks a subset of VNs indicated by the parity check matrix. Belief Propagation (BP) and Min-Sum (MS) are well-known families of decoding algorithms. BP provides good decoding performance [2], but at a higher computational complexity than MS [3]. Prominent variants of BP and MS decoding algorithms are discussed in Section II.
To meet the high throughput requirements of NR Enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB) and to enable Incremental Redundancy Hybrid Automatic Repeat Requests (IR-HARQ), NR uses protograph based variant of LDPC codes. Encoding is based on a small protograph called a Base Graph (BG). NR uses two BGs depending on the Transport Block Size (TBS) and code rate [1, Sec. 5.3.2]. Due to the presence of many degree 1 VNs in NR BGs, MS performance is noticeably

In [2], authors propose to schedule CNs based on a specific criterion. This entails reordering the parity check matrix to change the order in which CNs are processed in a decoding iteration. In [3], authors present a correction and a scale for selected VN↔CN messages to reduce unreliable VN sign flips. Performance gain is noticeable when the number of iterations is 50, however in practice decoders are limited to about 10 iterations. In [3], [6], authors present an algorithm to erase carefully selected unreliable bits following a BP decoding failure. The erasure algorithm is performed in addition to BP. In [7], author presents the seminal work on the well-known layering technique to improve the decoding performance. In [8], authors use a neural network to optimise the offsets in Offset MS (OMS) decoding. Offsets are set per CN-VN pair. Memory requirement of the decoder is reasonable when TBS is fixed, however NR TBS varies significantly. In [9], authors consider an MS decoding algorithm that is a hybrid of OMS and Normalised MS (NMS). They use a Deep Neural Network (DNN) to find suitable coefficients. However, the error correction probability of the proposed DNN framework could be improved, as it is unable to leverage layering in [7]. In [10], authors proposed a DNN Layered OMS (LOMS) decoding. They use the DNN to find the suitable coefficients, including the scaling factor, offset and relaxation factor. All methods in [2], [3], [6], [8]–[10] yet result in high hardware complexity.
In this letter, we propose a novel approach to LDPC decoding enabled by a DNN. Our foundation is the wellknown layered NMS (LNMS) decoding algorithm. To mitigate the propagation of unreliable VN messages, we modify the LNMS messages construction by leveraging the known structure of the filler bit positions in an NR Transport Block (TB). We build a DNN where a hidden layer represents an iteration of the proposed algorithm. We train this DNN and determine the optimal set of normalisation factors per decoding iteration. Collectively this translates to 0.3-1.9 dB gain over LNMS and 0.3-1.7 dB gain over other DNN decoders [10], in a 3GPP Rel 16 compliant NR Physical Uplink Shared Channel (PUSCH) link. Unlike most literature [2], [3], [6], [9], we achieve this error correction performance improvement with the identical number of multiplication operations as NMS/LNMS. Hence highly attractive for real hardware implementations.
In Section II, we summarise the relevant aspects of NR LDPC codes and existing LDPC decoding methods. In Section III, we present the proposed 5G NR decoding algorithm and the DNN based optimisation.

III. PROPOSED ALGORITHM AND DNN BASED OPTIMISATION
Our key contributions are: 1) Using the known locations of filler bit positions in a
5G NR TB, we propose an improved LNMS decoding algorithm in Section III-A. It reduces the propagation of incorrect VN↔CN message passes, hence leading to lower decoding failures. 2) The per iteration normalisation factor αi has a great impact on the probability of decoding success. We propose a forward propagation DNN in Section III-B and a training method based on typical 5G NR link adaptation operation in Section III-C, to determine optimal αi . In contrast to conventional optimisation or greedy methods that optimise one parameter at one time/iteration, DNN learn and optimise multiple parameters together. Therefore, we are able to optimise multiple values of αi for all iterations simultaneously. The proposed decoding method with trained αi uses the same number of multipliers as LNMS in runtime.

We evaluate the performance of 1 × 2 NR PUSCH link using a 3GPP Rel 16 compliant transmit chain, in an AWGN channel. Aligning with practical decoder implementations, we set Im = 10. Bandwidth is 4 resource blocks, all of which are allocated for PUSCH in each slot. V. CONCLUSION
We present an improvement to the well-known LNMS algorithm. Our contributions are: 1) We leverage the receiver knowledge of the filler bit positions in a 5G NR transport block to present a modified CN→VN message and a hard decision criterion - this reduces propagation of unreliable messages reducing the probability of decoding failure; 2) We present a forward propagation DNN to determine the optimal per iteration normalisation factor αi . During decoding the proposed method uses the same number of multiplication operations as LNMS. 3GPP Rel 16 compliant NR PUSH simulator shows 0.3-1.9 dB better BLER than LNMS. Therefore, the proposed approach achieves significantly better error correction probability than LNMS, at zero extra cost on multiplier resources on a FPGA.

The code rates [308, 679, 822]/1024 correspond to [112, 16, 24] filler bits in the simulated use case. Clearly the gain of 308/1024 is significantly higher than the others, because the number of filler bits is higher. The proposed DNN gives an additional 0.1-0.3 dB improvement for code rates up to 679/1024. At 822/1024 the gain that stems from DNN appears to be marginal. Overall, our proposed approach improves LNMS performance by 0.3-1.9 dB. In fading channels [13], the performance gains appear to be comparable, in the order of 0.25-1.8 dB as shown in Fig. 4.
The numbers of multipliers, summations, comparisons and sign flips of the proposed method are in the same order as LNMS. Memory requirement increases from O(1) to O(Im ), as α becomes αi .
