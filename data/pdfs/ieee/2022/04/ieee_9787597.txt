An Architecture for Efficient encoding of Quasi cyclic 
LDPC codes and its implementation in FPGA 
Sowmya G, Keerthi K, T Lalitkrushna J, Srividhya S, Jothy Soman, Vanitha M 
Payload Data Management Group, 
UR Rao Satellite Centre, Bangalore, INDIA 
Abstract— The Quasi Cyclic LDPC codes are one of the most 
widely used channel coding technique as they offer low complexity 
encoding along with parallelism in decoding. The existing 
architectures used for the encoding of such codes focus either on 
higher throughput or lower hardware resources. The proposed 
architecture optimizes both, especially for the implementation in 
space-qualified FPGAs where the resources are limited, yet higher 
throughput and encoding speed are expected. The proposed 
architecture can be used for any QC-LDPC code where the length 
of the information sequence is even multiple of the code’s 
Circulant size. As an illustration to the proposed architecture, an 
encoder for the CCSDS specified (8176, 7154) QC-LDPC codes is 
implemented in FPGA.  
Keywords: LDPC encoder, SRAA, Quasi-cyclic LDPC, FPGA 
The Paper is divided into four sections. 
Section 1 briefs about Quasi Cyclic LDPC codes, associated 
Parity check and Generator matrices in their systematic 
circulant form. 
Section 2 introduces the proposed architecture along with the 
mention of traditional SRAA circuit. 
Section 3 describes the implementation aspects of (8176, 
7154) QC-LDPC encoder in FPGA along with the 
comparison of performance across various architectures 
Sections 4 presents the concluding remarks. 
1 
INTRODUCTION 
Low Density parity check codes (LDPCC) are first developed 
by Robert G Gallager in 1961. They are also called as Gallager 
codes after him. They were impractical to implement at the time 
of their discovery and didn’t come into limelight. However, the 
advent of high-density VLSI programmable devices and the 
discovery of iterative decoding algorithm, led to the primary 
advance of these codes. They are capacity approaching and they 
have better performance like low error floor in higher code rate 
range when compared to other capacity approaching codes like 
turbo codes. The term low density in LDPC means the parity 
check matrix is sparse or in other words, the ratio of total 
number of ‘1’s to total number of elements in the parity check 
matrix is << 0.5. 
1.1 
Quasi Cyclic LDPC codes 
This Paper discusses the class of LDPCC called Quasi cyclic 
LDPC. It is built using simple shift registers and combinatorial 
logic. They also show very low error floors and fast iterative 
convergence. They are also advantageous with respect to 
implementation of decoder IC due to their cyclic symmetry. 
The Quasi-cyclic codes have a base matrix or parity check 
matrix which is formed by combining different smaller 
circulant matrices. A Quasi-cyclic parity check matrix is 
formed by an array of smaller circulant matrices of same size. 
Consider a parity check matrix H formed by s x t array of 
circulants each of size m x m. where s and t are positive integers 
with s <= t.

The information bit from odd set, loaded into the encoder as uo 
is fed as input to AND gate along with Ro bits and the 
information bit from even set, loaded as ue is Fed as input to 
AND gate along with Re bits. Both the products are summed up 
along with the accumulator value using 3-input XOR gates and 
the result is stored in the accumulator. At the beginning of the 
first cycle, r1,j(0)  and r2,j(0)  is loaded into the shift register Ro and 
Re respectively. The register A is loaded with Zeros and the 
information bits u1 and um+1 (the first bits of sections u1 and u2 
respectively) are loaded as uo and ue into the encoder. The 
registers Ro and Re are right shifted for every clock cycle as the 
next m-1 information bits of the corresponding odd and even 
information sets are loaded in the next m-1 cycles.  At the end 
of the first m clocks, u1R1,j + u2R2,j is computed and the 
Registers Ro and Re  are loaded with r3,j(0)  and r4,j(0) . The 
information bits of the sets u3 and u4 are loaded as uo and ue 
into the encoder for the next m clock cycles and so on. This 
way, at the end of (t-s)/2 * m cycles, all the information bits are 
loaded into the encoder and pj is computed from (6). The 
Register Ro takes in r1,j(0) , r3,j(0)  …… rt-s-1,j(0)  and Re takes in r2,j(0)  
, r4,j(0)  …… rt-s,j(0). 
The 2-bit SRAA structure shown in figure 2, facilitates for 
computation of a single parity section pj. Therefore, depending 
upon the coding rate, for the generation of s parity sections, s 
such encoder circuits should run in parallel, where the same 
two-bit inputs are loaded into all of them. Below is the figure 
showing 2-bit SRAA based QC-LDPC encoder.

Each 2-bit SRAA has m*3 Flipflops, m*2 two input AND gates 
and m three input XOR gates. Therefore, s such SRAAs put 
together, the resource consumption is 3sm Flipflops, 2sm two 
input AND gates and sm three input XOR gates. 
3 IMPLEMENTATION 
3.1 
(8176, 7154) QC-LDPC code 
The CCSDS specifies a 7/8 rate, (8176,7154) LDPC code [1] 
which is optimized for the characteristics and requirements of 
near-earth space missions. For this code, s = 2, t = 16 and m = 
511. The two-bit SRAA is implemented in VHDL for
Shortened (8160, 7136) QC-LDPC code [1]. Since the
spacecraft and ground systems work on 32-bit word size and
neither of 8176, 7154 are multiple of 32, the information bits
are shortened to 7136 and 18 zeros are padded to make it 7154.
These 18 zeros are not transmitted, therefore 7136+1022(parity 
bits) are transmitted which account to 8158, to make it a
multiple of 32, two zeros are appended. Figure 4 shows the
signal flow through all the VHDL modules implemented. Each
rectangle represents a component or an entity. The signals to
the left are inputs to the component and the ones on the right
are the outputs.
Fig4: Block diagram for the VHDL implementation of 2-bit SRAA based 
(8176, 7154) QC LDPC codes

POR: power on reset  
CLK: clock  
data_in:  2-bit input data 
stb3577: Data valid which is high for 3577 clock cycles, to 
accommodate the 7154 bits.  
The outputs are  
data_out:  two-bit output of the encoder  
data_Stb_out: data valid for the output stream. 
The inputs first go to the interleave module, it interleaves the 
data as per the encoder requirements. datatoenc is the

4. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
The proposed architecture takes half the number of clocks and 
doubles the throughput when compared to the serial SRAA with 
nearly 50 % more usage of resources. It has lesser encoding 
speed when compared to parallel SRAA, but it can be 
advantageous because parallel SRAA requires numerous 
resources especially for codes with higher information 
sequence length.  
This architecture can be extended to any QC-LDPC by 
parallelizing more than two SRAA circuits in similar fashion to 
2-bit SRAA, to form 3-bit SRAA, 4-bit SRAA etc. It further
increases the throughput at the cost of more hardware 
utilization. This b-bit SRAA can be used for AR4JA LDPC 
codes that CCSDS proposed for deep space missions, which
