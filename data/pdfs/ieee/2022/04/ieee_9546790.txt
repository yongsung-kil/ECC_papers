A Study on Performance Evaluation With Neural Network Detector in SMR System
M. Nishikawa 1, Y. Nakamura 1, Y. Kanai 2, and Y. Okamoto 1
1Graduate School of Science and Engineering, Ehime University, Matsuyama 790-8577, Japan 2Department of Engineering, Niigata Institute of Technology, Kashiwazaki 945-1195, Japan

We previously applied a neural network detector (NND) to the low-density parity-check (LDPC) coding and iterative decoding system in a shingled magnetic recording (SMR) system. In this study, we evaluate the performance of iterative decoding using a new NND by computer simulation. The conventional NND gets the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) as the reliability of the sequence recorded from the two-dimensional finite impulse response (TD-FIR) filter output and the sum-product (SP) decoder output, but the new NND finds the LLR by adding the SP decoder input in order to consider the difference between the SP decoder input and the decoding result. Furthermore, we clarify that the new NND brings the almost same effect as the a posteriori probability (APP) decoder in the iterative decoding system.
Index Terms— Iterative decoding, neural network, shingled magnetic recording (SMR).

I. INTRODUCTION

T HE COVID-19 has spread all over the world and has not yet converged. In the future, a new life called “New Normal” will be born. For example, telework and remote meeting have become commonplace in our life. In this way, when many people use personal computers, tablets, and smartphones, the opportunities of using the cloud increased to store huge data. Recently, data storage such as hard disk drives (HDDs), flash memories, optical disk drives, and magnetic tapes is used according to their respective uses. Among them, a solid-state drive (SSD), which is lightweight and high-speed accessible flash memory, is used as online storage, and an HDD, which provides large capacity and is inexpensively used as near-line storage for data centers, will support the New Normal.
We have proposed signal processing methods for shingled magnetic recording (SMR) [1] to realize HDDs with an ultrahigh recording density. Among them, we have reported that an application of a neural network which is a type of artificial intelligence is effective for reducing inter-track interference (ITI) and signal-dependent noise [2], [3]. Furthermore, we have focused on iterative decoding applying neural networks [4]–[8]. We previously proposed a neural network detector (NND) to realize the automatic construction of the iterative decoding system. However, it could not realize the equivalently effective iterative decoding as that with a posteriori probability (APP) decoder [6].
In this study, we propose a new NND which outputs loglikelihood ratio (LLR) as the reliability for the recorded sequence from the equalized waveform and a sum-product (SP) decoder input–output (I/O) [9]. Here, the new NND uses the SP decoder I/O to achieve effective iterative decoding by

II. READ/WRITE SYSTEM
Fig. 1 shows the read/write (R/W) system of low-density parity-check (LDPC) encoding [10] and iterative decoding using the NND. The input sequence is encoded by a 128/130 (0, 16/8) run-length limited (RLL) [11] encoder, and a (3, 30)-regular LDPC encoder to obtain the recording sequence. The recording sequence is recorded on a granular medium model which consists of the discrete Voronoi diagram. In this article, we assume the areal recording density of 4 Tbit/in2. Fig. 2 shows the granular medium model [2] with the writer and the array head which has three readers. In the granular medium model, the magnetic grains with the magnetization of “0” or “1” direction are indicated in the white or black, where the average grain size, the grain diameter dispersion, and the average nonmagnetic grain boundary are set to 5.0, 1.0, and 1.0 nm, respectively. The bit length lc and the track pitch ltp are also set to 7.3 and 22.1 nm, respectively. In the reading process, the decoding target track

When the LLR calculated from the NND contains the erroneous LLR with a large absolute value, the SP decoder output may also contain the erroneous output with a larger absolute value as isp increases. Then, since the new LLR calculated by returning the SP decoder output to the NND has the same tendency as the previous LLR, the iterative decoding cannot be performed successfully.
From the results, the NND has performed the most effective iterative decoding at isp = 2, so that we set isp = 2.

On the other hand, the symbols of circle, triangle, square, and invertedtriangle correspond to the recording patterns of “000” and “111,” “001” and “110,” “011” and “011,” and “010” and “101,” respectively. V. CONCLUSION In this study, we have proposed the new NND. It is clarified that the NND system improves 1.0 dB of required SNRS to achieve no errors compared to the conventional NND system and provides the equivalent performance to the APP decoder system. Furthermore, the new NND is simplified to half compared to the conventional NND, while it requires about 90 times more computation than the APP decoder. However, since the NND can process in parallel, we think that the difference in processing time between the NND and the APP decoder is not so large.
