Abstract— It has previously been shown that ensembles of terminated LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CC) have an error performance comparable to that of their counterparts LDPC codes. Also they are capable of achieving capacity approaching performance with iterative message-passing decoding on the AWGN channel. In terms of complexity, throughput and latency of iterative decoding algorithms, various interesting tradeoffs have been compared between the two different types of codes.
In this paper, we review Bit-Flipping (BF) decoding algorithm and its variants. We adapt BF, WBF (Weighted Bit-Flipping), MWBF (Improved Weighted Bit-Flipping) and IRRWBF (Implementation-Efficient Reliability Ratio-based WBF) decoding variants, in order to evaluate the bit error rate (BER) performance of LDPC-CCs.
Extensive computer simulations and comparisons are provided to demonstrate the efficiency of LDPC-CCs and the differences between the considered variants of BF decoding algorithm.
Index Terms—low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CCs), bit-flipping (BF), WBF, IWBF, IRRWBF, Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR).
I. INTRODUCTION
Gallager introduced a family of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, which has attracted a lot of research interest in the information theory field. However, because the construction he described was complicated and the performance he claimed was not particularly outstanding [1], the work was mostly unnoticed for many years. Mackay-Neal rediscovered LDPC codes and demonstrated that they have error correction performance close to Shannon capacity limit [2]. As the years go by, researchers have demonstrated that the convolutional equivalent of LDPC codes named LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CCs) have better code performance than conventional LDPC codes with the same memory capacity constraints in the decoder [3].
Both LDPC and LDPC-CCs can be decoded using various iterative decoding schemes such as hard and soft decisions. Despite of their reach to the near-capacity performance, the complexity of the soft-decisions belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm, sum-product algorithm (SPA) and the popular min-sum algorithm (MSA) is still too high to be implemented efficiently in hardware.

The hard-decision Gallager’s BF decoding algorithm flips a bit if the number of unsatisfied parity check equations exceeds a fixed threshold value. Although the BF algorithm is practical just in applications with a high SNR, the decoding algorithm is simple and fast.
For LDPC codes, a derived weighted BF decoding algorithm (WBF) and its variant algorithms from the BF algorithm were presented to bridge the performance gap between BF and BP decoding algorithms. Based on WBF algorithm [4] that considers both the reliability of the received message as well as the check relationship between those messages, an improved weighted BF algorithm (IWBF) is proposed in [5], [6] that contributes to better decoding performance. The IRRWBF (Implementation-Efficient Reliability Ratio-based WBF) algorithm is then proposed [7] as an efficient compromise between error-correcting performance and decoding complexity.
In this paper, performance evaluation comparison of the BF variant algorithms is suggested for LDPC-CCs under the binary-input AWGN (BI-AWGN) channel, and the paper is then organized as follows. In Section II, we provide an overview of LDPC-CCs and their main parameters for the sparse parity-check matrix and the encoding step. Section III is specified as a main contribution to define the BF variant decoding algorithms for LDPC codes and their use for LDPCCCs using the windowed iterative decoding. In the next section IV, the simulation results and performance evaluation are provided to finally concluding the paper with comparative conclusive remarks between LDPC and LDPC-CCs based on BF variant algorithms.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, performances comparison of the Bit-Flipping algorithm and its variants WBF, IWBF and IRRWBF for LDPC-CCs compared to LDPC codes are presented. Our simulation results demonstrate that LDPC-CCs can achieve a good performance with low complexity than the LDPC codes for BF and its three based decoding algorithms.
The IRRWBF decoding algorithm achieves the best performances among the other variants for both LDPC codes and LDPC-CCs. Hence, the simulation results also highlight the greatest performance improvement for the IRRWBF algorithm for LDPC-CCs with an increased number of iterations that outperforms the LDPC codes, and the error correction speed while reducing the number of iterations for decoding LDPC-CCs.
