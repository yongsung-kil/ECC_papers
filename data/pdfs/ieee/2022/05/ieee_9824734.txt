Abstract—Under the boom of low-orbit satellite Internet constellation construction, an LDPC decoder design method is proposed for the outgoing physical layer of satellite Internet in order to carry out effective monitoring for typical satellite signals with high bandwidth and low time delay. The scheme uses a partially parallel architecture, where the node update step based on the normalized min-sum algorithm omits the resource consumption and compresses the number of clocks consumed by operations such as multi-level comparison of message values, which helps to improve the decoder throughput rate. Based on Virtex-7 series hardware platform, we realize the high rate decoding of long and short frame LDPC codes with different modulation methods in DVB-S2/S2X standard. At 204.8 MHz operating frequency, the decoder can reach 768 Mbps throughput. The decoder implemented in this paper is capable of meeting the rate and performance requirements of typical satellite systems for targeted signal monitoring.
Keywords—LDPC, DVB, NMSA, Satellite Internet, FPGA.
I. INTRODUCTION
In the past decade, the construction of global low-orbit satellite constellations has flourished, not only causing fierce competition for space frequency orbit resources, but also posing potential risks to ground communications and network security. Satellite Internet combines the advantages of wide coverage, high transmission rate and large communication capacity, etc. Hundreds of megabytes of bandwidth and signal time delay within tens of milliseconds put forward higher requirements for signal monitoring and processing. In order to meet the requirements of huge data scale and quality of service for various broadcasting services and mobile services of satellites, Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1] have been continuously used since 2005 to form the physical layer channel modulation coding scheme in a series of standards such as Digital Video Broadcasting (DVB) [2] and Deep Space Communication (CCSDS) [3] in the satellite domain because their long codes show good error correction performance in wireless channels.
Various practical coding schemes are mostly constructed by the quasi-cyclic method for QC-LDPC codes, and the hardware design can significantly improve the decoder throughput by increasing the parallelism. Therefore, hardware design methods

around QC-LDPC codes have been widely studied. [4] uses a partially parallel structure and sets 360 parallelism to achieve high throughput rate performance, the high parallelism and the increased use of barrel shift registers in it also leads to high resource usage. [5] proposes a compressed storage strategy based on the LDPC decoder implementation of the CCSDS standard and explores the throughput rate performance and hardware footprint size of the partially parallel structure. [6] proposes that the parallel structure is significantly faster than the serial structure, while for LDPC codes under the DVB standard, the resource occupation requirement is too demanding. [7] proposes a global integrated computing architecture for Minmax comparison operations in decoding, and completes a VLSI implementation of a trellis-based LDPC decoder. [8] is concerned with the strategy of message quantization in FPGA implementation of non-binary LDPC codes, giving a better scheme. However, there is a deficiency of using a large number of multipliers. Most LDPC decoder solutions are used in standards for CCSDS as well as IEEE 802.16e, while the matrix size of DVB series standard is larger and non-regular accumulation of LDPC codes, which requires a hardware architecture that takes into account the decoder processing rate and resource consumption.
In this paper, based on the previous research on the physical layer architecture of satellite Internet, the design and implementation of LDPC decoder for the physical layer of satellite Internet is carried out based on normalized least-sum decoding algorithm for the processing delay and resource requirements of satellite Internet for signal monitoring system with reference to DVB-S2/S2X and Internet Protocol over Satellite (IPoS) standards.
II. RESEARCH OF LDPC CODE DECODING ALGORITHM
LDPC codes have typical properties of linear packet codes. It is characterized by a parity check matrix H of dimension M×N with sparsity, in which the majority of the elements equal zero and non-zero elements are sparsely distributed [1]. The m rows and n columns of the check matrix H based on the bipartite graph model represent m check bits and n variable bits, respectively, corresponding to the check node (CN) and variable node (VN) in the Tanner graph. A. Construction of Check Matrix
Usually QC-LDPC codes use submatrices of size d × d. A check matrix H consisting of m × n submatrices is obtained, after m or n times cyclic shift expansion operations to the right in both directions. The DVB-S2/S2X and IPoS series standards in the satellite domain, have referred to the use of the irregular cyclic accumulation LDPC code construction method. The main idea is to divide the matrix H into two sub-matrices. H1 is a sparse matrix with dimension m × k, a constructed in a quasicyclic manner for the basis matrix of size q × 360, where the value of q varies with the length of code and rates. H2 is a double diagonal matrix with dimension m × m. The resulting nonregular LDPC code can be implemented by hardware to avoid pre-storing the complete matrix H, which will cause too much memory and greatly operation delay of obtaining the address signal.

B. Comparison of LDPC Decoding Algorithms
The classical LDPC code soft-decision decoding algorithms are the probability measure-based belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm, the log-domain BP-LLR algorithm, the modified minimum-sum algorithm (MSA), the normalized minsum algorithm (NMSA), as well as the offset min-sum algorithm (OMSA)[9]-[12]. Additional [13] draws on differential methods to implement decoding schemes avoiding phase tracking and channel estimation, which is temporarily not adapted to the system applications of the physical layer of the Satellite Internet. The core of the BP decoding algorithm is the transmission process of message values between the connected VNs and CNs in the Tanner graph. During each iteration the message values of the current node is corrected according to the CNs and VNs update steps. Finally, the decision of codes is based on the sum of the message values obtained by the VNs. The decoded sequence obtained by the decision is ĉ. When the termination condition of the iterative loop process is get or the set maximum number of iterations MAX_ITER is reached, the message transmission will be stopped and the decoded sequence get output. III. LDPC DECODER ARCHITECTURE FOR SATELLITE INTERNET
The hardware design of an out-routing physical layer LDPC decoder for satellite Internet systems uses a partial parallel structure based NMSA. The general framework is shown in Fig. 2.

Fig. 2. General architecture of LDPC decoder
Multiple message nodes can be designed to parallelize the frame lengths of 16200, 32400 and 64800 in typical satellite signal regimes. Significantly higher throughput rates and reduced processing latency compared to traditional serial architectures. However, the higher the parallelism, the more registers and LUT hardware resources are needed. The LDPC decoder sets the parallelism to 90. According to the framework, the LDPC decoding process is operated, in which the iteration termination condition is judged to end while the number of the decoding iteration reaches MAX_ITER=20. Then the decoding sequence can be output.
A. Functions of LDPC decoder submodules
1) Top-level control submodule This module is responsible for the transition of the decoder between each state such as the INITIAL state, CNU state, VNU state, and OUTPUT state. It also defines the concatenation of control and address signals, and the determination of iteration termination conditions. There is also a function to determine iteration termination conditions. The arrival of an external data input and a valid decoder enable signal will drive this module to turn on. The generated address signal is also calculated and generated in this module.
2) Node messages cache submodule This module implements the node message storage function, where the node messages are divided into two categories: channel initial messages and node update intermediate message values. The initial channel message LLR value uses two dualport RAMs to process the input data alternately. Under the condition of parallelism 90, each LLR value is quantized to 8 bits, i.e., two sets of bit width 720 and depth 720 are used to store the first type of LLR values fed by the decoder input. The second type of message value is extended to 11 bits after the update calculation, i.e., it needs to occupy a memory group of bit width 990 and depth 720 to achieve storage.
3) CNU Submodule

The core operation of this module is to calculate the message value of the check node in the new iteration using the message value of the variable node that was updated in the cache after the previous iteration and is connected to the current check node. The input signal for each CNU submodule is the updated LLR message value L(l)(Qij) of the variable node. In this module, the most-valued comparison operation uses a parallel multi-level comparison structure. This structure allows message values to be grouped and compared in pairs, which reduces the delay of comparisons by compressing the initial comparison between message values at one clock compared to the classical polled comparison structure. This effectively reduces the time required for simultaneous comparison logic of multiple values.
Fig. 3. Parallel multi-level comparison pipeline
4) VNU Submodule The VNU submodule completes the variable node update operation, in 3 processing steps.
a) The message values of each CN connected to the current VN (excluding the currently updated CN) are summed to obtain the “partial sum” directly.
b) The partial sum is added to the channel message value corresponding to the current node being updated to obtain the update message value.
c) The update message value is truncated and sent to the intermediate message cache.

The control side logically determines whether the iteration termination condition is satisfied based on the iteration count signal output by the toplevel control module and the MAX_ITER signal. After the decoding bits are awarded according to the judgment strategy.

B. BER Performance
Normal and short frames modulated by BPSK and QPSK are fed into the LDPC decoder under the channel conditions with Es/N0 from 0 to 5 dB to test the BER performance under different channel parameters and different modulation methods. The BER performance simulation of a typical satellite physical layer signal demodulated and processed and fed to the physical layer LDPC decoder is given in Fig 4. The BER performance of the BPSK modulated short frame LDPC codes is close to the order of magnitude 10-4 at Es/N0 > 1.25dB and the BER of normal frame at Es/N0 > 2.5dB is less than 10-8. QPSK modulated short frame LDPC codes show BER below 10-8 at Es/N0 >1.5dB and normal frame LDPC codes show BER is less than 10-8 at Es/N0 > 2.8dB. The results show that the decoder implemented can meet the decoding BER performance requirements for the new Satellite Internet physical layer signals with various code lengths and different modulation methods.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper an LDPC decoder with high throughput rate and low resource consumption is implemented based on FPGA. The decoder operates at 204.8MHz with a throughput rate of up to 768 Mbps. The NMSA-based hardware implementation scheme is optimized for the core iteration step and reduces the resource usage of some parallel structures. The implementation report shows that the decoder can be used for signal monitoring systems targeting typical satellite networks. The receiving end of the high-band satellite signals is sensitive to processing accuracy and speed, such as the signal frequency conversion capability of the satellite signal receiving system, the A/D conversion accuracy of analog signals and the demodulation and decoding processing rate. On the other hand, the complex design of the receiver system also places limitations on the decoder resource usage. Therefore, it is of great practical importance to design decoders with reasonable hardware resources and meet the requirements of high bandwidth satellite signal processing rate and accuracy.
