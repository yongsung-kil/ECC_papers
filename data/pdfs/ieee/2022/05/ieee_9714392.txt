Low Complexity Majority-Logic Decoding for LDPC-Coded PNC Systems
Xiaoqian Xie, Pingping Chen , Member, IEEE, Yi Fang , Member, IEEE, and Zhaopeng Xie

Abstract— In this letter, we propose majority-logic decoding (MLGD) algorithms of low complexity for low-density paritycheck (LDPC) coded physical-layer network coding (PNC) in a two-way relay channel (TWRC). In particular, we decode the transmit combination of two-user messages from the superimposed signal and then map the XOR message of two users. By exploiting the superposition rules of PNC, we develop hard decision-based and reliability-based decoding algorithms, i.e., modified one-step MLGD (M-OSMLGD) and iterative soft reliability-based decoding (M-ISRB), to iteratively update the combination of two-user messages. Moreover, we propose an improved M-ISRB (IM-ISRB) decoding to update the external reliability of the combination of two-user messages in each iteration. Simulation results show that the proposed decoding algorithms can achieve effective an trade-off between complexity and decoding performance for LDPC-coded PNC systems.
Index Terms— Physical network coding, relay, low-density parity-check codes, majority logic decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
P HYSICAL-LAYER network coding (PNC), originally proposed in [1], has the potential to significantly boost the throughput of multi-user wireless communication networks. Generally, PNC transmissions in a relay network consist of two stages: multiple access (MAC) stage and broadcast (BC) stage. In the first phase, multiple users transmit signals to a relay simultaneously. The relay aims to decode the overlapped signals into a network-coded (NC) message. The two-way relay channel (TWRC) is a basic setup for PNC, where two users exchange information from each other via an intermediate relay. The simple form for the target NC message in a TWRC is the bit-wise exclusive OR (XOR) operation of the two-user messages [1]–[6]. Moreover, in this letter, we consider decode-and-forward (DF) instead of amplify-andforward (AF) strategy to decode the received signals from two users, since the former has much better performance than the latter one [3].
The channel-coded PNC systems were subsequently proposed to ensure communication reliability [7]–[10], where two users adopt the same linear channel code. In particular, capacity-approaching low-density parity-check (LDPC)

First, the XOR-based channel decoding (XOR-CD) scheme first maps each pair of overlapped channel-coded bits to the XOR bits before channel decoding. Second, the joint channel-decoding and PNC decoding (JCNC) scheme first performs channel decoding on transmit message pairs and then maps them into XOR bits. The belief propagation (BP)-based JCNC suffers from high decoding complexity, since the combination of two-user messages are iteratively updated during decoding [11], [12]. Thus, this letter is concerned with developing low-complexity message-passing decoding algorithms for LDPC-coded PNC systems.
Recall that the simplified hard-decision decoding algorithms for LDPC codes, such as bit-flipping (BF) and majority logic decoding (MLGD) algorithms, can achieve significant complexity reduction with extra performance loss [13]. The reliability-based iterative majority-logic decoding (RBI-MLGD) for LDPC decoding was proposed to provide an efficient trade-off between complexity and performance [14]. Moreover, [15] developed two decoding algorithms for non-binary LDPC codes, i.e, iterative hard reliability-based MLGD (IHRB-MLGD) and iterative soft reliability-based MLGD (ISRB-MLGD).
However, there is still no study on low-complexity decoding algorithm for channel-coded PNC systems. The existing simplified decoding algorithms can be directly applied in PNC system, since the combinations of two-user messages are required to be decoded. Thus, to address this issue, this letter proposes three decoding algorithms, i.e., modified one-step MLGD (M-OSMLGD), modified-ISRB (M-ISRB) and improved modified-ISRB (IM-ISRB), for this system. The M-OSMLGD uses channel probability to make hard decision, while M-ISRB quantizes the probability of the overlapped signal at the relay. In the IM-ISRB, we update the external information of two-user messages in each iteration. Simulation results show that these algorithms offer an effective trade-off between decoding complexity and performance in LDPC-coded PNC systems.
The rest of this letter is organized as follows. Section II briefly reviews the TWRC model. Section III presents M-OSMLGD, M-ISRB and IM-ISRB algorithms for PNC systems. Section IV shows the complexity analysis and the simulation results, and Section V concludes this letter.

for LDPC-coded PNC. In the simulation, we use a regular non-binary LDPC code over GF(22) with length n = 1024. The maximum iteration number Imax = 50 [16]. In the decoding algorithms, the parameters r and w are 0.7 and 5, respectively. The scaling factors λ = 5, ε = 0.5. We select λ according to [17], and the parameters r, ε and w are chosen empirically. Fig. 2 depicts the BER performance of the decoding algorithms with the code rate 1/2 and (hA, hB) = (1.2, 0.8). V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose simplified decoding algorithms for LDPC-coded PNC over power-imbalanced channels. We first decode the four transmit combinations of two user messages and then decide their XOR message. Simulation results show that the proposed IM-ISRB achieves a performance gain of 1.5 dB than the conventional iterative reliability decoding, since it exploits the external reliability of check nodes. In addition, the computational complexity of the decoding schemes shows that the performance gain of IM-ISRB is obtained at the cost of a slightly increased complexity. Thus, the proposed scheme can be seen as a low-complexity decoding solution for PNC-based communication systems.
