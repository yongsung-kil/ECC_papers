A Semi Linear State Space Model for Error Floor Estimation of LDPC codes over the AWGN Channel
Ali Farsiabi and Amir H. Banihashemi Department of Systems and Computer Engineering, Carleton University, Ottawa, Ontario, Canada

Abstract—In this paper, we propose a novel state-space model to represent the behavior of sum-product algorithm (SPA) in the vicinity of a trapping set (TS) of a low-density parity-check (LDPC) code over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel in the error floor region. The proposed model takes into account the non-linear behavior of SPA and dynamically adjusts the operating point of the model in accordance to the statistical properties of TS messages. This is in contrast to the existing linear state-space models which linearly approximate such behavior at around the operating point of zero. Simulation results are provided to demonstrate the higher accuracy of the proposed model in estimating the error floor of LDPC codes compared to the linear state-space model.
I. INTRODUCTION
The error floor estimation/analysis techniques in the literature are generally divided into two main categories: the code-dependent and the code-independent methods. In the first category, the TSs are analysed as part of the Tanner graph of a specific code. In the second category, however, they are studied in isolation. The majority of works within the first category are based on importance sampling (IS) techniques, see, e.g., [1]–[7].
Among the code-independent techniques [11]–[16], the linear state-space model used in [2], [8], [9] is of particular importance due to its analytical nature. The model simplifies the complex dynamics of iterative decoders in the error floor region and yet provides a reasonably accurate estimate of the error floor under a wide range of circumstances. There are, however, scenarios where the linearization of missatisfied (degree-2) check node (CN) operations, used in the model, noticeably degrade its performance [11]. In addition, in the existing state-space models, the linearization is performed around the operating point of zero. This creates large errors in cases where the internal messages of a TS are comparable in size with the extrinsic messages. Both the linearization and the assumption of zero operating point result in an overestimation of the error floor [11]. In [9], to tackle the problem of overestimation, a heuristic damping factor is added to the model.
Finding the right operating point to linearize missatisfied CN operations with minimum error is a challenging problem. In this work, we extend the state-space model of [9] by proposing a technique to dynamically adjust the operating points of the model in each iteration. In addition, we improve the model by incorporating the second order approximation (rather than the first order used in the linear model) at initial iterations. We demonstrate that our proposed code-independent technique can be viewed as a fully-analytical counterpart to the semi-analytical code-dependent method of Richardson [1]. One of the features of the method of [1] is that the calculations at a given signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) can be used to find the estimates at other SNR values by extrapolation, thus reducing the complexity. We demonstrate that our proposed technique can also benefit from extrapolation. We also show that although the extrapolation in some cases can lead to large

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
To examine the accuracy of our proposed method, we have applied it to different LDPC codes, decoded by SPA over the AWGN channel. We report our results for the following LDPC codes: (640, 192) QC code (C1) [4] and Margulis (2640, 1320) code (C2). In the following, we use the notation DSLM for the proposed Semi-Linear Model with Dynamical operating point. In the proposed model, we use s = 4 for all the reported results. This value of s is obtained emperically. Notations LMd and LMnd are used for the linear model of [9] with and without damping factor, respectively, and SA is used for the semi-analytical method of Richardson [1]. Moreover, we use notations DSLME, LMdE and SAE for the cases where extrapolation is used in the estimations.
Fig. 3 shows the MC simulation results along with LMd, DSLM and SA estimation results for C1 for different clipping thresholds. This code has VN degree 5 and an irregular CN degree distribution. The irregularity of the degree distribution is taken into account in our model by applying a weighted average based on the degree distribution of the code within the density evolution (DE) framework. It is known that the most harmful TS of this code is a (5, 5) ETS with multiplicity 64. We thus apply our method to this ETS structure and then multiply the result by 64. The results demonstrate a good match between DSLM and SA estimations as well as the MC simulation results. The LMd estimation results, however, are diverging from the simulation curves, in particular, for higher saturation levels. We have also reported the result of LMnd for Cth = 50. This represents the true performance of the linear state-space model without the addition of the heuristic damping factor. As can be seen, while our semi-linear model accurately estimates the error floor in this scenario, the results of LMnd overestimate the error floor significantly.
The results of Fig. 3 also reveal that by increasing the SNR, since (1 − P ) → 1, the performance of LMd converges to that of LMnd.
