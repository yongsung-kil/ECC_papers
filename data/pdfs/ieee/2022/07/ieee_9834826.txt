Trade-Based LDPC Codes
Farzane Amirzade, Daniel Panario Senior Member, IEEE and Mohammad-Reza Sadeghi

Abstract‚ÄĒLDPC codes based on multi-edge protographs potentially have larger minimum distances compared to their counterparts, single-edge protographs. However, considering different features of their Tanner graph, such as short cycles, girth and other graphical structures, is harder than for Tanner graphs from single-edge protographs. Here, we provide a novel approach to construct the parity-check matrix of an LDPC code which is based on trades obtained from block designs. We employ our method to construct multi-edge quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes.
We use those trade-based matrices to define base matrices of multi-edge protographs. The construction of exponent matrices corresponding to these base matrices has less complexity than the ones proposed in the literature. We prove that these base matrices result in QC-LDPC codes with smaller lower bounds on the lifting degree than existing ones.
Index Terms‚ÄĒLDPC codes, Tanner graph, Girth, Multi-edge QC-LDPC codes, Super-simple directed designs, Trade designs.
I. INTRODUCTION
There are different approaches to construct LDPC codes. One of these construction methods is protograph-based. A protograph is a small size bipartite graph whose adjacency matrix is taken as a base matrix W of protograph-based LDPC codes. Protographs can result in quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes. These codes perform very well under iterative decoding algorithms over the binary-input AWGN channel [1], [2].
QC-LDPC codes are divided into two categories, singleedge [1], [3] and multiple-edge (multi-edge) [4]‚Äď[6]. If the protograph is free of multi-edges, then the QC-LDPC code is single-edge. Otherwise, it is multi-edge. For each protographbased LDPC code, two matrices are considered: a base matrix W and an exponent matrix B. The elements of the base matrix of a single-edge QC-LDPC code are 0, 1. The base matrix of a multi-edge QC-LDPC (ME-QC-LDPC) code contains also elements bigger than 1. According to [4]‚Äď[6], ME-QCLDPC codes are known to potentially have large minimum distances. However, obtaining the entries of exponent matrices of ME-QC-LDPC codes with a certain girth is more complex than those of single-edge QC-LDPC codes. Necessary and sufficient conditions to avoid short cycles such as 4-cycles and 6-cycles from the exponent matrix of a multi-edge protograph are proposed in [5], [6].
Here, we provide a novel approach to construct parity-check matrices of LDPC codes with girth at least 6. Then, we use those matrices to propose base matrices of ME-QC-LDPC

codes. To reach our goal we require special classes of combinatorial designs, such as balanced incomplete block designs (BIBD), super-simple directed designs as well as their related designs known as trades. BIBDs have been employed to construct structured LDPC codes [4], [7]‚Äď[11]. The use of trades to construct codes is one of our contributions.
Our proposed technique to construct a parity-check matrix for an LDPC code with girth at least 6 is based on the concept of trade in directed designs. We call these codes as trade-based LDPC codes. One of the merits of this approach is that instead of checking 2k-cycles in a large size paritycheck matrix we can consider cyclical trades of the underlying block design. Moreover, in contrast with the LDPC codes from BIBDs in [7] which yield regular Tanner graphs (TGs) with girth at most 6, our approach results in both regular and irregular LDPC codes with different girths.
Another merit of our approach is using trades and the obtained trade-based matrices to provide a new method to construct a base matrix for an ME-QC-LDPC code. We call the obtained LDPC code as trade-based multi-edge QC-LDPC code. The main contribution of these base matrices, when compared to other constructions in the literature [5], [6], [12], [13], is that they provide a considerable reduction in the computational complexity of the search algorithm used to identify the elements of the exponent matrix. In fact, we show that because of the non-existence of 2 √ó 2 submatrices of entire nonzero entries in the base matrix and because of their sparsity, by defining the entries of a small size submatrix of the exponent matrix we can identify all elements of the exponent matrix. Moreover, the sparseness property of our proposed base matrix causes a decrease in the number of short cycles, which are at the root of many harmful structures in the TG [14]‚Äď[16]. We also show that applying the method in [5] to our proposed protographs results in a lower bound on the lifting degree which is smaller than the one in [5].
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section II presents basic definitions. Section III provides our technique to construct trade-based LDPC codes. Our techniques are structural and applicable to all super-simple directed designs. Section IV explains how to use the trade-based matrix to construct base matrices of an ME-QC-LDPC code and provides experimental results. In Section V, we summarize our results.
