On Average Number of Cycles in Finite-Length Spatially Coupled LDPC Codes
Sima Naseri, Ali Dehghan and Amir H. Banihashemi Department of Systems and Computer Engineering, Carleton University, Ottawa, Ontario, Canada

AbstractβÄîIn this paper, a probabilistic analysis of the cycle distribution of protograph-based spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC LDPC) codes is presented. In particular, we derive the probability that cycles of specific, but arbitrary, length are broken as a result of spatial coupling with a random spreading matrix in a general edge spreading process. Our results show that the probability of existence of cycles in SC codes is O(1/m), where m is the code memory. This result is independent of the cycle length.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity check (LDPC) block codes are known as good candidates for practical communication systems as they perform well under the low-complexity belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm. However, for these codes, there exists a considerable gap between the optimal maximuma-posteriori (MAP) decoding threshold and the sub-optimal BP threshold. In recent years, it has been mathematically and experimentally shown that spatially-coupled (SC)-LDPC codes are capable of reaching the MAP performance of their underlying block LDPC codes using BP decoding [1]. This property is known as threshold saturation phenomenon [2]. Despite the fact that SC-LDPC codes offer an improvement in their performance compared with their underlying block codes [3], [4], they still face a performance degradation at high signal to noise ratios (SNRs) over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, resulting in an error floor. The error floor is mainly due to the existence of some small structures in the codeβÄôs Tanner graph, known as trapping sets (TSs) [5], [6]. TSs, themselves, in turn, consist of small cycles [8]. One would thus be interested in finding the multiplicity of short cycles in the Tanner graphs of SC-LDPC codes. In related work, the cycle distribution of different ensembles of block LDPC codes was studied in [9]. In [10], the average multiplicity of different categories of TSs in regular and irregular LDPC code ensembles were calculated. Empirical results in [11]βÄ™[14] show that increasing the memory m of a SC-LDPC code reduces the multiplicity of TSs and results in an improved error floor performance. In [15], the authors derived a probability distribution function in terms of m to quantify the probability that a 6-cycle, which exists in the base graph of a block code, is eliminated in the corresponding SC protograph under the assumption that the edge spreading is performed randomly. In this work, motivated by [9], [10], [15] and the empirical results in the literature regarding the cycle/TS distribution in SC codes, we conduct a probabilistic analysis of the cycle/TS distribution in Tanner graphs of finite-

length protograph-based SC-LDPC codes, and the effect of memory m on such distributions. In this paper, we only present the results for cycles. Similar results for TSs are available in [19]. We consider a general edge spreading process and derive the probability of the existence of cycles of different lengths in the resultant SC code, when the entries of spreading matrix are chosen randomly based on a uniform distribution from [0, m]. We prove that such a probability is a monotonically decreasing function of m, and thus, increasing memory reduces the average multiplicity of cycles in an SC code.
