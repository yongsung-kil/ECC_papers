PDD-Based Decoder for LDPC Codes With Model-Driven Neural Networks

Yihao Liu, Ming-Min Zhao , Member, IEEE, Chan Wang, Ming Lei, Member, IEEE, and Min-Jian Zhao

AbstractвҖ” In this work, we develop a double-loop iterative decoding algorithm for low density parity check (LDPC) codes based on the penalty dual decomposition (PDD) framework. We utilize the linear programming (LP) relaxation and the penalty method to handle the discrete constraints and the over-relaxation method is employed to improve convergence. Then, we unfold the proposed PDD decoding algorithm into a model-driven neural network, namely the learnable PDD decoding network (LPDN). We turn the tunable coefficients and parameters in the proposed PDD decoder into layer-dependent trainable parameters which can be optimized by gradient descent-based methods during network training. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed LPDN with well-trained parameters is able to provide superior error-correction performance with much lower computational complexity as compared to the PDD decoder.

I. INTRODUCTION
L INEAR programming (LP) decoding proposed in [1] is an important decoding technique for low density parity check (LDPC) codes, based on LP relaxation of the maximum likelihood (ML) decoding problem. As a classical LDPC decoding algorithm, the belief propagation (BP) algorithm based on message passing across a factor graph is widely used [2], however, it is not guaranteed to converge and often suffers from error floors at high signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs). On the contrary, LP decoding comes with ML certificate [1] and can provide strong theoretical guarantees [3]. Besides, it is able to outperform the classical BP algorithm at high SNRs [4]. However, LP decoding has worse performance at low SNRs and higher computational complexity as compared with the BP decoder.
To tackle the abovementioned difficulties of LP decoding, the authors in [4] proposed an efficient distributed LP decoding algorithm based on alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), which exploits the geometry of the LP constraints and can be implemented with a message-passing interpretation [4]. The works [5] and [6] further simplified the Euclidean projection operation in the ADMM decoder and improved the decoding efficiency. In [7], the ADMM penalized

deep unfolding is a model-based deep learning method that unfolds an iterative algorithm into a learnable network which preserves the internal structure of the iterative algorithm [12]. The work [13] proposed a neural BP decoder by untying the model parameters across BP iterations and demonstrated that the proposed neural decoder is able to improve the decoding performance with relatively low complexity. In [14], the authors proposed an ADMM-based neural decoder which shows better error-correcting performance with similar decoding complexity as compared with the original ADMM decoder.
In this work, we aim to improve the decoding performance of LDPC codes by modifying the PDD decoder in [8] and unfolding the resulting new PDD algorithm into a model-driven neural network. Specifically, to tackle the difficulty caused by the discrete constraints, we employ the LP relaxation technique and the penalty method, which are able to reduce the number of optimization variables and simplify the variable node operation in the proposed PDD decoding algorithm as compared with the one in [8]. Then, to avoid manually finetuning the decoding parameters and to further improve the decoding performance, we transform the tunable parameters in the proposed PDD decoder into a series layer-dependent trainable parameters and proposed a learnable PDD decoding network, namely LPDN. B. Proposed LPDN
It is important to mention that in Algorithm 1, choosing appropriate coefficients and parameters is essential, e.g., a larger Ој0 may produce smaller initial residuals and lead to faster convergence, but meanwhile it may also degrade the decoding performance. The traditional method to find appropriate values of the parameters {Оұ, Ој0, c, ОІ} usually requires extensive numerical simulations to traverse all combinations of these parameters, which may lead to high computational complexity. Therefore, in this subsection, we propose to utilize the deep unfolding technique to improve Algorithm 1, which works by unfolding Algorithm 1 into a layer-wise structure.

IV. EXPERIMENTS AND RESULTS
In this section, we present simulation results to illustrate the decoding performance of the proposed LPDN decoder. The platform used for neural network training is TensorFlow. In the backpropagation stage, the Adam optimizer is employed to optimize the trainable parameters [18]. In all of our simulations, we assume that the codewords are transmitted over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels with binary phase shift keying (BPSK) modulation. In addition, two different LDPC codes are considered, i.e., [128,64] CCSDS LDPC code C1 and [256,128] CCSDS LDPC code C2 [19].
The training data plays an important role in network optimization. Considering that the proposed network may have difficulty learning the decoding pattern from codewords under relatively high or low SNRs, the training SNR is set to 3.5 dB for C1 and 2.5 dB for C2. All-zero codewords are chosen as the transmission codewords and the standard BP decoder is employed to filter out the received codewords that can be easily decoded since such codewords have little effect on network training.The resulting data is divided into a training data set and a validation data set which contain 4 Г— 105 and 105 samples, respectively. The learning rate is set to 10вҲ’3 in the first 10 epochs and then we reduce it to 10вҲ’4 in the latter epochs. In the training stage, the numbers of inner and outer layers are set to 5 and 20, respectively.
In the simulations, the decoding performance of the standard BP decoder, the ADMM L2 penalized decoder [7] and the PDD decoder [8] is also provided for comparison. For the standard BP decoder and the ADMM L2 decoder, the maximum iteration numbers are both set to 2 Г— 104.2 For PDD decoders proposed in [8] and in this letter, the initial penalty parameter Ој0 and control parameter c are set to 0.5 and 1.001, respectively. Besides, for the proposed PDD decoder, the penalty coefficient Оұ and the over-relaxation parameter ОІ are set to 1.2 and 1.5, respectively. The maximum inner

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we proposed a new PDD-based decoding algorithm, which solves the relaxed and penalized LP decoding problem. Moreover, we presented the deep unfolded version of the proposed PDD decoder, known as the LPDN. Simulation results showed that the proposed LPDN with well-trained parameters is able to outperform the original PDD decoder significantly with much lower complexity.

In Fig. 2, we provide the frame-error-rate (FER) performance of the considered decoders for C1 and C2 codes. In all the curves, at least 100 error frames are collected for each SNR point. It can be observed that the proposed LPDN decoder shows similar performance with the proposed PDD decoder and outperforms the other decoders at all SNR regions. Specifically, about 0.2 dB gain can be obtained as compared with the other decoders at FER = 10вҲ’3 for both C1 and C2 codes.
Fig. 3 shows the average inner iteration numbers required by the PDD decoder in [8], the proposed PDD decoder and the LPDN decoder. While for C2 code, the inner iteration number can be reduced by 70% and 35% as compared with the PDD decoder in [8] and the proposed PDD decoder, respectively.
