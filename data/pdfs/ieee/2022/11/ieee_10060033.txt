Abstract— Forward error correction (FEC) codes corrects the error bits in transmission channel. The FEC codes are more suitable for applications like digital video broadcasting (DVB) satellite systems, cloud computing, and social networking applications. This manuscript reviews low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, Bose–Chaudhuri–Hocquenghem (BCH) codes and concatenation of LDPC with BCH codes on field-programmable gate array (FPGA) and also the comparative results are discussed in detail. The FPGA-based architectures provide great flexibility, high-speed parallel architectures with lower cost than the application-specific integrated circuits (ASIC) platform. This manuscript discusses the commonly used recent encoding and decoding mechanisms for communication system on the FPGA platform. The performance analysis of LDPC codes, BCH code, and concatenation of LDPC with BCH codes are discussed. The performance metrics include chip area utilization (Slices and LUTs), frequency, and throughput parameters. Finally, the research findings and future challenges are addressed in detail.
Keywords— FEC, LDPC, BCH, FPGA
I. INTRODUCTION
There is a significant increment in internet traffic by the usage of most of the emerging applications like social networks, digital streaming videos, and cloud computing. So most of the telecom operators increase their data rate.Forward correction codes (FEC) are introduced to improve the transmission rates under random error conditions. The FEC codes provide many features like the electronic end–to–end–processing mechanism, enhancing transparency, reducing the bit error rate (BER), implementation complexity, and many more [1-2]. Block codes and convolutional codes are the most commonly used FEC codes. The available block codes are linear, cyclic, Reed-Solomon (RS), BCH, LDPC, Turbo codes and many more. These block codes improve the transmission quality in channel conditions. In contrast, convolutional codes provide better memory usage with less channel transmission bandwidth. The commonly used convolutional codes are punctured codes, systematic and non-systematic codes [3-4].
For real-time applications usage, hardware-based architectures are essential, especially the FPGA-based architectures. The FPGA provides reconfigurable architectures with easy use and design as per user requirements with scalable and reliable features [5]. Many characteristics have to be incorporated while designing and implementing the FEC codes on FPGA. These characteristics are flexibility, hardware requirements, latency, throughput, energy efficiency, bandwidth efficiency, and transmission energy efficiency [6-7]. The FEC codes are implemented on ASIC based platform, which consumes more time and NonRecurring Engineering (NRE) costs. The integration of LDPC with BCH codes is used in most physical layer security applications [8-9].

ARCHITECTURES
A. LDPC Architectures
Zhang et al. [10] present the LDPC decoder architecture with reconfigurable features using a universal mapping algorithm for message extraction. The Decoder uses the MPA algorithm for block RAM reduction on the decoder side with better BER and throughput. Chen et al. [11] present the integration of a lower triangular-based encoder and min-sum algorithm-based LDPC decoder on the LABVIEW FPGA platform. The MSA decoding provides better BER performance on FPGA and a suitable for communication environment. Purnamasari et al. [12] present the LDPC codes implementation on FPGA for DVB – satellite applications. The bit-flipping decoding with hard decision parallel architecture is used to improve the performance constraints, Broulim et al. [13] explain the irregular LDPC codes implementation on FPGA with detailed architecture and analysis of the BER with different code rates under the AWGN channel. Xie et al. [14] present the Quasi-Cyclic (QC) LDPC decoder with the shared hard decision on parallel FPGA. The Decoder uses stage– pipelined architecture for QC-LDPC decoder to improve the hardware resources on FPGA. Amaricai et al. [15-16] present the flooded LDPC decoder for memory improvement and later hard decision-based QC-LDPC decoder for resource improvements on FPGA. The HDLDPC decoder improves the BER and is suitable for WiMAX applications.
Roh et al. [17] describe the LDPC decoder architecture using a software-defined system on Chip (SD-SoC) on the FPGA- Central Processing Unit (CPU) platform. The Decoder offers better speed than the software-based decoder approach.Hui et al. [18] present the LDPC codec architecture with high throughput on the FPGA platform and analyze the error correction failure probability with different code rates. Johnson et al. [19] present conventional and lower triangular-based LDPC encoder architecture on the FPGA. The lower triangular-based LDPC encoder reduces the hardware complexity than the traditional encoder approach. Nimara et al. [20] present the LDPC decoder architecture with a multi- codeword feature on FPGA. The Decoder improves the system throughput and reduces the BRAM on FPGA even for higher code length. Caglan et al. [21] explain the LDPC based Error correction codes (ECC) on Virtex-7 FPGA with less BRAM utilization and moderate 25.42 Mbps throughput. Zincheko et al. [22] present the FPGA-based LDPC codec for the DVB-S2 standard. The Decoder uses pipelined architecture and

B. BCH Architectures
The BCH decoder with minimum weight features for double error correction on FPGA is presented by Medany et al. [44].The Decoder uses a hard-decision-based minimum weight decoding algorithm to correct two bits and achieve 7Mbps Throughput. Hasan et al. [29] analyze the performance of the BCH codec on Spartan-3 FPGA using a multipath Rayleigh fading channel. The modulation used is Binary phase-shift keying (BPSK) incorporated with BCH codes to analyze the BER performance under the multipath Rayleigh fading channel. Fupeng et al. [30] present the BCH Codec on FPGA for satellite communications system of china. The linear feedback shift register (LFSR) is used inBCHencoderto encode input messages. Decoder uses a cyclic redundancy check (CRC) generator, with correction block and logic gates to decode the corrupted bits. Haroussi et al. [31] present the BCH-based serial in-serial out (SISO) decoder on FPGA to improve the hardware complexity and BER performance.
Mehra et al. [32] explain the BCH encoder module with high-speed architecture for wireless applications on FPGA. The work describes the serial and parallel-based BCH encoder using LFSR modules. Arunkumar et al. [33] present the BCH codec as applicable to Satellites. The design verifies the BER using both software (Matlab) and hardware (FPGA) approaches. Mathew et al. [34] present the BCH codec for Wireless Body Area Network (WBAN) application on FPGA using LFSR and BMA. The design results are verified on FPGA with better improvement on-chip area. Koila et al. [35] explain the BCH codec for Free-space optical (FSO) communications on the FPGA platform.
Chandrasekhar et al. [36] present the BCH decoder architecture for memory applications. The parallel BCH decoder uses syndrome generator (SG), Error pattern generator (EPG), error detector (ED), and error corrector (EC) modules. The BCH decoder is capable of correcting 2bits and works at 268.9MHz on Virtex-5 FPGA.

C. BCH Architectures
Xie et al. [42] present the LDPC with BCH code concatenation for magnetic recording read channel. The work improves the symbol error rate and works at 1 Gbps on the ASIC platform. Gomes et al. [43] present the parallel FEC codec architecture with scalable features on FPGA for satellite applications. The work analyzes the FPGA resources and throughput calculation on the FPGA platform. Muthammal et al. [44] present the LDPC with BCH codec for error correction on FPGA environments. The LDPC codes uses less area and power than BCH codes while implementing individually on FPGA platform. The FEC decoding subsystem-based FPGA implementation for satellite application is presented by Alves et al., [47]. The deinterleaver receives the channel data and passes it to LDPC and BCH decoders to analyze the hard bits. The complete FPGA performance setup is presented and verified with simulation. Nghia et al. [51] illustrate the parallel LDPC with BCH codec for DVB satellite channel applications on FPGA. The work analyzes the power and BER parameters on a TV analyzer in the experimental setup.Lichioui et al. [52] present the different FEC code’s performance analysis on FPGA. The BER and FPGA resources of LDPC and BCH codes are analyzed in detail.
The Lower triangular encoding technique is used widely for LDPC Codes implementation. The Systematic encoding Technique is used widely for BCH Codes. For the implementation of combination of LDPC and BCH codes, the combination of Lower triangular encoding and Systematic encoding technique is used. For the LDPC implementation the various decoding techniques used are Minimum sum algorithm (MSA), Bit flipping algorithm (BFA), Overlapped message-passing algorithm (OMPA), Flooded Minimum sum algorithm and Off-set Min-sum (OMS) algorithm. For the BCH implementation the various decoding techniques used are Berlekamp-Massey algorithm (BMA), LUT approach and Chase-Pyndiah algorithm. For the implementation of concatenation of LDPC and BCH codes, the combinations such as MSA + BMA and Sum product algorithm (SPA) + BMA are used.

IV. RESEARCH GAPS AND FUTURE DIRECTIONS The previous sections discuss the review of recent works on LDPC codec, BCH codec, and concatenation of LDPC with BCH codes on the FPGA platform. A few of the research findings or gaps are addressed. Most of the available concatenation of LDPC with BCH codec is implemented in a software environment and is not suitable Codes and Concatenation of LDPC and BCH codes are tabulated and discussed. Limited number of works is available on hardware implementation of amalgamation of LDPC and BCH codes and in most of the cases stand alone FEC codes implemented on Hardware are mentioned. Future directions of FEC codes implementation are also highlighted.

for real-time application construction. Many research contributions available implemented the LDPC codec or BCH codec. Only few architectures are available in the current works on the concatenation of LDPC with BCH codec for application developments.
There are many scopes to improve the FEC codes for future research and development on the FPGA platform. There are various applications that work on long range communication, where the performance metric such as Time on Air, Received Power and Received Signal strength indicator depends on bandwidth and coding rate. So for higher coding rates a good FEC code is very much essential. Small satellites known as CubeSats are increasingly popular these days because of their small size, cost and power requirement. However CubeSats are required to provide high speed connectivity. In this regard the use of improved FEC codes in the baseband architectures and implementation on reconfigurable platforms such as FPGAs proves to be more desirable. To combat the errors that are introduced in the wireless application such as 5G communication FEC codes known as POLAR codes are used. Introducing reconfigurable computing using Highlevel synthesis (HLS) on FPGA with compact programming models and parallel architecture to improve the computational performance throughput. Usage of flexible decoders to improve the rum-time flexibility and the hardware cost by reducing the hardware complexity. Concentration should be made on low-processing latency and low energy consumption-based architectures on FPGA for use in the complex application environment.
