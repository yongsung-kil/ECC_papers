Irregular Generalized LDPC codes in Practical Communication Scenarios
Irina E. Bocharova, Boris D. Kudryashov, and Sander Mikelsaar
University of Tartu, Estonia Tartu 50409, Estonia
Emails: {irinaboc, boriskud, sander.mikelsaar}@ut.ee

Abstract—High-rate irregular Generalized LDPC (GLDPC) codes with low-complexity constituent codes are studied. The main area of focus is the optimization of the underlying irregular binary LDPC codes and their matching with constituent codes. Finite length random coding bound on the maximum-likelihood decoding error probability of a random ensemble of irregular GLDPC codes is derived and compared with a similar bound for non-binary (NB) LDPC codes over small alphabets.
A new algorithm for assigning columns of the parity-check matrix of a constituent code to nonzero elements of a base matrix of the quasi-cyclic (QC)-GLDPC code is presented. Examples of the constructed QC-GLDPC codes are given. The simulation results for frame error rate (FER) performance of the belief-propagation (BP) decoding for the QC-GLDPC codes are presented and compared with the same performance of NB QC-LDPC codes over small alphabets and binary LDPC codes in the WiFi and 5G standards.
Both GLDPC codes and NB LDPC codes with sufficiently low decoding complexity are shown to outperform their binary LDPC counterparts, while GLDPC codes appear to be more advantageous in terms of decoding complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
Generalized LDPC (GLDPC) codes stem from Tanner’s paper [1]. Originally, GLDPC codes were targeted at the lowrate range since this construction requires additional parity checks to be introduced in the parity-check matrix of the underlying LDPC code. In other words, nonzero elements of the base parity-check matrix are replaced with columns of the parity-check matrix of a constituent code. This idea was extensively studied in [2] and [3], where regular GLDPC codes were introduced. It was shown that Hamming codes are the most promising candidates for constituent codes and maintain a relatively high rate of the resulting GLDPC code construction. The random ensembles of GLDPC codes obtained from Gallager’s ensemble by replacing single parity-check codes with Hamming codes were analyzed. It was proved that there exist such GLDPC codes for which the asymptotic minimum distance of GLDPC codes grows linearly with length. An upper bound on the maximum-likelihood (ML) decoding error probability for the ensemble of GLDPC codes with column Hamming weight J = 2 was derived in [3]. Simulation results presented for the rate R ≈ 1/2 GLDPC codes showed some coding gain over short binary LDPC and turbo codes. However, these GLDPC codes did not achieve the same bit error rate (BER) performance as long turbo codes.

Low-rate irregular GLDPC codes of short length with Hamming constituent codes were designed in [4]. A number of papers have studied both structured and unstructured regular, as well as irregular GLDPC codes of different rates based on different constituent codes (see e.g., [5]–[11] and references therein). It was shown in [6] that extremely long (of length (n > 106)) and extremely low-rate codes are about 0.25 dB from the BICM Shannon limit. In [5], GLDPC codes were studied as candidates for high-speed optical communications. Weight distributions for the ensembles of irregular GLDPC codes were analyzed in [12].
In this paper, we study low-complexity solutions for rate larger than 1/2 irregular GLDPC codes. Our approach to constructing GLDPC codes differs from previous works in this area in that we focus on optimization of underlying binary LDPC codes whose parity-check matrices are used as base matrices in the construction of GLDPC codes, while only using constituent codes with minimum distance of two (number of redundant symbols 3 and 4). This restriction is important, as it limits the computational complexity of MAP decoding of constituent codes. We modify the labeling technique applied in [13] for optimization of NB QC-LDPC codes in order to perform labeling of the underlying LDPC code by the columns of the constituent code. This approach to matching the base LDPC code and the constituent codes significantly improves the BP decoding performance of the GLDPC construction.
The GLDPC codes and NB LDPC codes over GF(2m), m > 1 are two powerful generalizations of Gallager’s binary (J, K)-regular LDPC codes. It was found that constructions other than ‘ultra-sparse’ (see e.g., [14], [15], [16] and references therein) should be used for NB LDPC codes over small alphabets (m < 6). In particular, it was noticed that in this case irregular NB LDPC codes are preferable [17], [18], [19]. Constructions of irregular NB LDPC codes over small alphabets were studied, e.g., in [20], [21], [22]. A finite-length random coding bound on the maximum-likelihood (ML) decoding performance of irregular NB LDPC code ensemble was derived in [22]. In this paper, we compare low-complexity solutions for GLDPC codes and NB LDPC codes.
In order to analyze the irregular GLDPC codes we consider a random ensemble of irregular GLDPC codes based on a generalization of Gallager’s ensemble. As density evolution

of 0.2 - 0.4 dB in the decoding performance. The complexity
per bit of the trellis-based MAP decoding of the constituent code is of order 2rc . Thus, for practical scenarios, GLDPC
codes seem more attractive than NB LDPC codes.

IV. ALGORITHM FOR LABELING A BASE PARITY-CHECK MATRIX WITH COLUMNS OF PARITY-CHECK MATRIX OF
THE CONSTITUENT CODE
Design of GLDPC codes is performed in two steps. First, a base matrix is constructed. Then, columns of a constituent code parity-check matrix are assigned to nonzero elements of the base matrix. In this section, we focus on the second step, assuming that the first step is done as for NB LDPC codes, see e.g., [13].
It is known that the existence of short cycles in the Tanner graph reduces the efficiency of BP decoding due to violating the hypothesis on independence of parity checks. Another interpretation of this phenomenon is that if the error combination covers a cycle, then check nodes involved in a cycle get zero syndrome values and do not help in error correction.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we compared GLDPC codes with lowcomplexity decoded constituent codes and NB LDPC codes over small alphabets. Despite their low decoding complexity codes from both classes achieve lower FER performance compared to their binary counterparts. It was shown that the finite-length random coding bound on the ML decoding error probability for both classes are rather close, but the random GLDPC codes outperform the random NB LDPC codes with the same average column weight of their base parity-check matrices. A new algorithm for labeling the base matrices of QC-GLDPC codes by the columns of a paritycheck matrix of a constituent code is proposed. The presented simulation results confirm that properly optimized codes from both classes provide similar decoding performance in practical scenarios.
