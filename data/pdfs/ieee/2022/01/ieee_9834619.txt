Ternary Message Passing Decoding of RS-SPC Product Codes
Mingyang Zhu1, Ming Jiang1,2, Chunming Zhao1,2 1National Mobile Communications Research Lab., Southeast University, Nanjing 210096, China
2The Purple Mountain Laboratories, Nanjing 211111, China Email: {zhumingyang, jiang ming, cmzhao}@seu.edu.cn

Abstract‚ÄĒThis paper presents a ternary message passing (TMP) decoding algorithm for product codes constructed from binary image Reed-Solomon (RS) codes and single-parity-check (SPC) codes. All exchanged messages among component decoders in TMP decoding take value from a ternary alphabet {‚ąí1, 0, +1}, which gives a potential for designing fast decoders. In particular, intersymbol interference (ISI) channels are considered due to their applications in many high-speed systems. Moreover, we propose the density evolution (DE) analysis for RS-SPC product codes over AWGN and ISI channels. The DE analyses and simulation results show RS-SPC product codes under TMP decoding performs well in various channels.
I. INTRODUCTION
For emerging applications requiring transmission at data rates of several hundreds of Gbps, such as optical systems, high-performance forward error correction (FEC) schemes are required to achieve a large coding gain [1]. Whereas, designing ultra-fast soft-decision decoders is still a challenge by now, since two important indicators, performance and complexity, are difficult to be balanced. For this reason, several binary message passing (BMP) decoders and their variations are proposed to offer excellent performance-complexity tradeoffs, see, e.g., [2], [3], [4]. The key principle of these low-complexity decoders is that the exchanged massages among component decoders take value from a quite small alphabet (e.g., binary, ternary), while exploiting channel soft information (i.e., loglikelihood ratio (LLR)) to improve performance.
In our previous work [5], we investigated a new type of block turbo codes (BTCs), based on binary image ReedSolomon (RS) codes and single-parity-check (SPC) codes. These BTCs can be easily designed with flexible code rates and lengths due to the usage of SPC component codes. Moreover, when large-minimum-distance RS codes are considered, RS component decoders can prevent miscorrections [6, Ch. 7] and then can be early stopped, which lowers the overall decoding complexity and latency. However, the soft-decision decoding proposed in [5] has relatively high complexity, which is not suitable for high-speed communication systems.
In this paper, we propose a ternary message passing (TMP) decoding algorithm for RS-SPC product codes, in which only ternary messages are exchanged between component decoders. In particular, intersymbol interference (ISI) channels are considered due to their applications in many high-speed systems [7], [8], [9]. Furthermore, the density evolution (DE) analysis for TMP decoding is developed for both the additive

white Gaussian noise (AWGN) and ISI channels. The given simulation results verify that RS-SPC product codes under TMP decoding outperform the conventional BTCs under iterative bounded distance decoding (BDD) [3], [12] for some ISI channels.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose a TMP decoding algorithm for RSSPC product codes, which gives good performance-complexity tradeoffs. The corresponding DE analysis is developed for both the AWGN and ISI channels. Moreover, the numerical results show that RS-SPC product codes perform well in many channels under the proposed TMP decoding.
