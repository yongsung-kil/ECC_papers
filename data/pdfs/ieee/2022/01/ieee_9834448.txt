Fully Analog Noise-Resilient Dynamical Systems Storing Binary Sequence
Tadashi Wadayama ∗Nagoya Institute of Technology, Gokiso, Nagoya, Aichi 466-8555, Japan,
wadayama@nitech.ac.jp

Abstract—This paper presents fully analog noise-resilient dynamical systems for storing a binary sequence. The proposed dynamical system is a gradient descent dynamical system based on a potential energy function defined based on a parity check matrix of a binary linear code. We assume that the dynamical system operates with stochastic disturbances such as thermal noises. We formulate the whole system, including stochastic disturbances, using stochastic differential equations (SDE). From a discretized stochastic difference equation, i.e., the EulerMaruyama equation, we can study the covariance evolution of error vectors regarding the random walk of the system state around an equilibrium state. Some numerical evaluations for the (7,4) Hamming code and related codes indicate the robustness of the proposed dynamical system against the stochastic disturbances.
I. INTRODUCTION
This paper studies storage systems composed of fully analog devices such as adders, multipliers, and integrators. Operational amplifiers can realize these analog devices in electronic circuits [1], [2], [3]. In the field of optical computation, optical adders and multipliers are realizable and were used for solving ordinary differential equations [4].
Combining these analog devices can implement nonlinear dynamical systems [5] storing digital information such as a binary sequence. Assume a dynamical system with multiple equilibrium states corresponding to binary sequences. Such a dynamical system can be used as a memory or digital information storage.
It may be natural to assume that such a dynamical system operates with stochastic disturbances such as thermal noises if the noise level in the system is not negligible. Of course, we can use a dynamical system with strong signals, but it may lead to higher energy consumption or violate another physical restriction. When the signal level in the system is comparable to the noise level, a dynamical system should be robust against stochastic disturbances to reliably storing the digital information.
In this study, we propose a noise-resilient dynamical system storing a binary sequence, which can be implemented with fully analog adders, multipliers, and integrators. The proposed system follows gradient descent dynamics minimizing potential energy [5]. The potential energy function is defined based on a parity check matrix of a binary linear code. A bipolar codeword corresponds to a minimum of the potential energy function, and thus a codeword state corresponds to an equilibrium state of the dynamical system.

A similar error correction approach using a potential energy function has been presented by [8] but the potential energy used in [8] is defined based on a generator matrix of a binary linear code. The potential energy function h(x) has been inspired from the non-convex objective function used in the GDBF decoding algorithm [9]. Note also that the studies on fault-tolerant decoding algorithms for LDPC codes [12], [13], [14], [15], [16], [17] deal with circuits with non-negligible system noises. The aim of the studies is similar to ours, but these systems discussed in the above papers are discrete-time systems. We will focus only on continuous-time systems in this paper.
We then formulate the whole system, including stochastic disturbances, using stochastic differential equations (SDE) [10], [11]. From a discretized stochastic difference equation, i.e., the Euler-Maruyama equation, we can study the covariance evolution of error vectors regarding the random walk of the system state around an equilibrium state. The system state’s mean squared error (MSE) can be derived from the covariance matrix. Since the MSE is an indicator of robustness against stochastic disturbances, the theoretical results presented in this paper may provide helpful insight for a robust dynamical system storing binary information.

VI. CONCLUDING SUMMARY
In this paper, a continuous-time dynamical system for storing a binary sequence was discussed. The Euler-Maruyama method was used to examine the statistical properties of the solutions to the stochastic differential equation (5). The statistical properties of the random walks around the codeword were analyzed by using linear approximation of the EulerMaruyama equation.
The numerical experiments showed that the analysis based on the linear approximation explained the experimental results well when the noise level α was small. We believe that these results provide a qualitative and quantitative understanding of the behavior of the proposed dynamical systems when α is small. In addition, the results of numerical calculations and experiments in Section V show that there is a trade-off between the robustness to stochastic disturbances and coding rate. Furthermore, the experimental results show that the robustness to disturbances can be significantly improved by introducing linearly redundant rows into the parity check matrix without changing the code.
