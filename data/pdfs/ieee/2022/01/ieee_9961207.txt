Horizontal Layered Scheduling ADMM Penalized Decoder Based on the Improved Penalty Function for LDPC
BIAO WANG AND ZHONGFEI WANG
School of Mathematics and Information Science, Baoji University of Arts and Sciences, Baoji, Shaanxi 721013, China
Corresponding author: Zhongfei Wang (loverkind0908@163.com) This work was supported by the Natural Science Basic Research Plan in Shaanxi Province of China under Grant 2021JM-515.

ABSTRACT For low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, reducing the number of Euclidean projections, choosing a suitable scheduling strategy, and devising an improved penalty function are three effective ways to increase the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) penalized decoding speed. By combining the above three ways, this paper proposes a horizontal layered scheduling ADMM penalized decoder based on the improved penalty function to speed up decoding. The experimental data indicate that the proposed decoder can obtain better performance and lower average number of iterations than the existing ADMM penalized decoders.

INDEX TERMS Horizontal layered scheduling, alternating direction method of multipliers, penalized decoding, low-density parity-check codes.

I. INTRODUCTION For low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, literature [1] devised a linear programming (LP) decoding method, which is classical while decoding complexity is high. To reduce the LP decoder’s complexity, an iterative LP decoding method is designed based on the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) (called ADMM decoding method) [2]. The ADMM decoder’s complexity is similar to the belief propagation (BP) decoder’s, while its decoding performance is worse at low signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs). Recently, many researchers have studied the decoding complexity or performance of the ADMM decoder.
Euclidean projection, as the most complicated operation of the ADMM decoder, is widely concerned by many researchers. Through the cut search algorithm [3], projecting onto a simplex [4], designing look-up tables [5], removing the sorting operation [6], proposing the line segment projection [7], exploiting the LP model’s orthogonality structure [8], alternately adopting even-vertex and other accurate projection algorithms [9], and developing approximate

projection onto the parity polytope [10], all these methods have reduced the ADMM decoder’s complexity. And without doubt, directly reducing the number of Euclidean projections can also reduce the complexity [11].
Currently, most of the existing ADMM decoders use the flooding scheduling. Choosing a suitable scheduling strategy for the ADMM decoder is another way to increase its convergence speed. For example, the horizontal layered (HL) scheduling [12], vertical layered (VL) scheduling [13], and node-wise scheduling [14] can achieve the above result to varying degrees. By adopting the approximate line segment projection algorithm, Xia et al. designed the modified nodewise scheduling algorithm that can simplify the node-wise scheduling ADMM decoding method [15].
The over-relaxation technique, adding a parameter ρ to the ADMM algorithm, and the early termination rule are also two critical strategies that can improve the ADMM decoding convergence [16].
To improve the ADMM decoder’s performance at low SNRs, the ADMM penalized decoder is designed by introducing a penalty function [17]. An improved ADMM penalized decoder, which significantly improved the performance, is devised based on the weighted penalized factor [18].

With the improved penalty function combined by the l1 and l2 penalty functions, the decoder obtains better performance and faster convergence rate than those with the l1 and l2 penalty functions [19]. By exploiting the quadratic programming (QP) model’s inside structure, Bai et al. developed an efficient QP ADMM decoding algorithm that attains better performance [20]. Taking the check node (CN) as the core, Wei et al. proposed a novel ADMM CNpenalized decoder that improved the ADMM variable node (VN) penalized decoder’s performance [21]. In addition, Wasson et al. presented a hardware-based implementation of ADMM-based LP decoding that attained excellent error-rate performance [22].
We know that reducing the number of Euclidean projections, choosing a suitable scheduling strategy, and devising an improved penalty function are three effective ways to speed up the ADMM penalized decoding. By combining these three methods, literature [23] proposed a fast converging ADMM penalized decoding method that increases the decoding speed.
This paper extends the work by designing a new ADMM penalized decoding method with all of reducing the number of Euclidean projections [11], horizontal layered scheduling [12], and improved penalty function [19]. In addition, an algorithmic complexity comparison of the proposed method and the decoding method in [23] is illustrated in this paper. The experimental data demonstrate that the proposed method can increase the decoding speed, and improve the decoding performance by using the improved penalty function.

A. MOTIVATION

By observing the results of many simulation experiments,

Wei et al. discovered that when the ADMM decoder is about

to obtain the correct solution vector, the proportion of VCs

whose values continue to change is very small, while the

proportion of VCs whose values do not change is particularly

large. This phenomenon shows that in the later iterations

of the ADMM decoder, the input vector between the two

adjacent iterations exhibits little or no change. At this time,

there is no need to continue the time-consuming Euclidean

projection operation. Based on this discovery, to reduce the

ADMM decoder’s complexity, Wei et al. designed a method

by reducing the number of Euclidean projections rather than

simplifying the Euclidean projection operation [11]. In terms

of the ADMM decoder, the HL scheduling [12] and the VL

scheduling [13] can speed up decoding compared with the

flooding scheduling. This result occurs because the iterations

needed to converge to a valid codeword is much lower for the

layered scheduling which leads to a meaningful computation

saving compared with the flooding scheduling. By adopting

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS The LDPC codes C1-(576, 288) rate-0.5 and C2-(1152, 864) rate-0.75 (both chosen from IEEE 802.16e standard) are simulated to compare the frame error rate (FER) performance, average number of iterations (ANIs) and average decoding time (ADT) of the following nine ADMM penalized decoders: the flooding and HL scheduling ADMM penalized decoders with the l1 penalty function and improved penalty function, respectively; the four decoders using reducing the number of Euclidean projections [11]; and the decoder designed in [23]. In particular, all the ADMM penalized decoders employ the over-relaxation technique and early termination rule (HxT = 0).
During the simulation, the maximum number of iterations, error tolerance and over-relaxation parameter are set to 1000, 10−5 and 1.9, respectively; the parameters µ and α are optimized according to the method presented in [2].
