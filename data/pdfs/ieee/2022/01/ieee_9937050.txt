Iterative Multihead Multitrack Detection Scheme for Bit-Patterned
Media Recording

1Institute for Hokmah General Education, Chongshin University, Seoul 06988, South Korea 2Department of Information and Communication Engineering, Soonchunhyang University, Asan 31538, South Korea 3Department of Electronics and Information Engineering, Sejong University, Seoul 05006, South Korea

Abstract—An iterative multihead multitrack detection scheme for bit-patterned media recording is described in this letter. The scheme employs two iterative strategies with multihead, multitrack detection where three tracks are simultaneously processed to accurately estimate the channel with track misregistration (TMR) and effectively detect the data by using intertrack interference (ITI) information with high reliability. The first outer iteration aims to compensate for the TMR effect, and the second inner iteration aims to improve the reliability of the data. In the outer iteration, the TMR effect is compensated by modifying the generalized partial response (GPR) target to a channel that reflects the TMR estimated by a TMR estimator using an expectation and maximization algorithm. In the inner iteration, iterative equalization and decoding (IED) is conducted between the two-dimensional partial response maximum-likelihood detector and the low-density parity check decoder based on the revised GPR target. Since each track has a different channel performance according to the amount of ITI information in the multitrack detection, we design the GPR target and the code rate separately for each track to maximize the overall channel performance. The bit error rate performances of the proposed IED scheme are compared with the conventional IED scheme when the areal density is 2 Tb/in2. Simulation results show that the IED scheme has more than 2 dB gain compared with the conventional IED scheme for 30% TMR.

Index Terms—Information storage, bit-patterned media recording, multitrack detection, intertrack interference, track misregistration.

I. INTRODUCTION
Bit-patterned magnetic recording (BPMR) is a promising technology that is considered to be capable of achieving high-density recording up to 4 Tb/in2 [Karakulak 2010]. In BPMR, the high areal density is enabled by reducing the intersymbol and intertrack distances. Therefore, intertrack interference (ITI) in the cross-track also occurs in addition to the intersymbol interference (ISI) in the down-track. Also, the read head offset, which is known as track misregistration (TMR), can aggravate ITI since the read head senses the islands in the adjacent tracks [Nabavi 2007]. As a result, channel performance has dramatically deteriorated when TMR is dominant. Hence, strong signal processing techniques are essential to mitigate ISI and ITI for BPMR channels with strong TMR.
Various detection schemes have been introduced for high-density BPMR channels [Karakulak 2010, Nabavi 2007, Chang 2010, Myint 2009, 2012]. In Karakulak [2010], Nabavi [2007], and Chang [2010], detection schemes considering both ISI and ITI have been proposed. While these schemes show good channel performance in the presence of strong ITI, the channel performance of these schemes is seriously degraded when TMR is dominant due to the design of their structure regardless of the effect of TMR. Also, iterative equalization and decoding (IED) schemes [Myint 2009, 2012, Nguyen 2017, Jeong 2018] have been proposed to improve the reliability of the detection

Since the generalized partial response (GPR) target is set to the channel without TMR, the reliability of the soft information gets worse as the iteration increases when the effect of TMR is strong. Therefore, the detection scheme should be investigated by estimating TMR and reflecting the effect of TMR into the equalizer in the presence of strong TMR for high-density BPMR channels.
In this letter, we model an iterative multihead multitrack detection scheme for high-density BPMR channels with TMR. When TMR is dominant, the channel response is seriously distorted. Therefore, the equalizer should be designed by reflecting the channel with TMR to detect the data with high reliability. To effectively estimate and compensate for the effect of TMR, we developed a new IED scheme for the multihead multitrack detection where three tracks are simultaneously read and processed. This multihead multitrack detection has an important role in accurately estimating the channel with TMR by using ITI information with high reliability.
For the IED, two iterative strategies are employed. The first outer iteration aims to compensate for the TMR effect, and the second inner iteration aims to improve the reliability of the data. In the outer iteration, the effect of TMR is compensated by modifying the GPR target and filter coefficient of the equalizer to the channels with TMR estimated from a TMR estimator using an expectation and maximization (EM) algorithm. II. ITERATIVE MULTIHEAD MULTITRACK DETECTION
Fig. 1 shows the iterative multihead multitrack detection scheme for high-density BPMR channels with TMR. The detection scheme considers a channel model where three tracks are read and processed simultaneously as in Myint [2012]. We also consider an environment in which electronic noise is the only source of noise and the phases between the three-track input signals are synchronized and aligned. For IED, we introduce two iterative strategies for the multihead multitrack detection to accurately estimate the channel with TMR and to effectively conduct IED by utilizing ITI information with high reliability obtained by the LDPC decoder. First, in the outer iteration, the decision data from the LDPC decoder are transferred to the TMR estimator using the EM algorithm in order to estimate the channel with TMR in an iterative manner. When TMR is strong, the channel performance is seriously degraded since the original channel response is considerably distorted by head offset. Therefore, the channel with TMR should be estimated, and this estimated channel is reflected into the equalizer at the next outer iteration. In the 2-D equalizer, the GPR target and equalizer coefficients are calculated based on the estimated channel with TMR obtained from the TMR estimator. The optimized GPR targets and the equalizer coefficients for the given channel response can be computed as in Chang [2010]. Second, in the inner iteration, IED is performed between the 2-D PRML detector reflecting the estimated channel with TMR and the LDPC decoder to enhance the reliability of the data. To implement the 2-D PRML detector with simple complexity, we adopt the joint-track detection scheme [Karakulak 2010] that used a reduced-state trellis diagram operating on four states and eight branches with a priori probability from the LDPC decoder as in Kong [2012]. However, since the side-tracks partially use the a priori probability, each track has a different channel performance. In IED, the performance imbalance between main-track and side-tracks makes the overall channel performance poor as iteration increases since the exchange of the soft information with low reliability leads to the error

propagation. Hence, we should design the GPR target and code rate separately for each track according to the amount of ITI information to equalize the channel performance for each track and maximize the overall channel performance.

When there is more than 20% TMR, the proposed IED scheme has a much better BER performance than the conventional IED scheme. For 30% TMR, the proposed IED scheme has more than 2 dB gains when compared with the conventional IED scheme. By using the coefficients and GPR target reflecting the estimated TMR for the 2-D equalizer and 2-D PRML detector, the proposed IED scheme showed a significant performance improvement over the conventional IED scheme at high when TMR is strong.
IV. CONCLUSION In this letter, we present an iterative multihead multitrack detection scheme for BPMR channels with strong TMR. By using the two iterative strategies, the TMR effect could be effectively mitigated and the reliability of the data could be improved. Simulation results show that the IED scheme has more than 2 dB gains compared with the conventional IED scheme for 30% TMR. Therefore, the proposed scheme is well suited for high-density BPMR channels with strong TMR.
