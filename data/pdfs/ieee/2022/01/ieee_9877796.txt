1 ABSTRACT In this paper, we propose a new code design technique, called partial doping, for 2 protograph-based generalized low-density parity-check (GLDPC) codes. While the conventional construc3 tion method of protograph-based GLDPC codes is to replace some single parity-check (SPC) nodes with 4 generalized constraint (GC) nodes applying to multiple variable nodes (VNs) that are connected in the 5 protograph, the proposed technique can select any VNs in the protograph to be protected by GC nodes. 6 In other words, the partial doping technique facilitates finer tuning of doping, which in turn enables a 7 sophisticated code optimization with higher degree of freedom. We construct the proposed partially doped 8 GLDPC (PD-GLDPC) codes using the partial doping technique and optimize the PD-GLDPC codes by the 9 protograph extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) analysis. In addition, we propose a condition guaranteeing 10 the linear minimum distance growth of the PD-GLDPC codes and use the condition for the optimization. 11 Experimental results show that the optimized PD-GLDPC codes outperform the conventional GLDPC codes 12 and have competitive performance compared to the state-of-the-art protograph-based LDPC codes without 13 the error floor phenomenon over the binary erasure channel (BEC).

Protograph-based GLDPC 49 codes can be constructed from a small protograph [25] using 50 the so called doping technique [26]. Doping a GC node, 51 defined by a (Вө, Оә) linear code of length Вө and dimension 52 Оә, means the replacement of an SPC node by the GC node 53 with Вө вҲ’ Оә constraints, which causes a rate loss. In the 54 perspective of VNs, Вө VNs are selected to be doped by a 55 GC node, assuming that there are no parallel edges in the 56 protograph. Thus, the smallest unit of doping, also called 57 the doping granularity, is Вө for the conventional protograph 58 doping technique. In other words, the conventional doping 59 technique has two limitations: 1) the degree of the SPC node 60 to be replaced should be Вө, which implies that the doping 61 operation is dependent on the underlying protograph and the 62 parameter Вө of component codes and 2) one cannot choose a 63 finer doping granularity less than Вө and thus the code design 64 cannot be sophisticated. Due to the limited design flexibility, 65 there has been little works on the well-designed optimization 66 for protograph-based GLDPC codes especially for medium 67 to high code rates. 68 In this paper, we propose a new doping technique, called 69 partial doping on the VNs, to minimize the doping granularity 70 and enlarge the code design freedom. In detail, the partial 71 doping involves the following three steps: 1) A VN to be 72 doped is selected in the protograph. 2) The Tanner graph is 73 obtained by the lifting operation [25] from the protograph 74 with a lifting factor N . 3) Additional GC nodes are connected 75 to the lifted N VNs in the Tanner graph after lifting the 76 protograph. The main difference from the conventional pro77 tograph doping technique is that the partial doping operation 78 is conducted on the Tanner graph instead of the protograph 79 domain. Thus, it is possible to partially dope on a single VN 80 in the protograph and the doping granularity becomes one, 81 which is also independent of Вө. In other words, the partial 82 doping enables fine tuning of the code structure regardless of 83 the underlying protograph and the parameter of component 84 codes. Specifically, the selection of VNs to be protected by 85 GC nodes and the rate loss can be adjusted in a more flexible 86 manner. 87 We denote the proposed protograph-based GLDPC codes 88 constructed using the partial doping as partially doped 89 GLDPC (PD-GLDPC) codes. The structural characteristics 90 of the PD-GLDPC codes have several advantages. First, the 91 PD-GLDPC codes are structurally adequate to adopt the 92 puncturing technique that compensates the rate-loss. Since 93 the partially doped VNs are highly and locally protected 94 by GC nodes, the performance loss occurred by punctur95 ing the doped VNs is relatively small while attaining the 96 code rate gain. Second, the asymptotic performance of the 97 PD-GLDPC codes can be analyzed by the low-complexity 98 extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) analysis. PROTOGRAPH LDPC CODE AND BEC 180 Let x = {x1, В· В· В·, xk }, xi вҲҲ {0, 1} be a k-bit binary message 181 vector, which is encoded via an (n, k) linear code, forming an 182 n-bit codeword c = {c1, В· В· В·, cn}, ci вҲҲ {0, 1}. The codeword 183 passes through a memoryless BEC, where each bit is either 184 erased with a probability or correctly received. 185 Protograph LDPC codes [25] are defined by a relatively 186 small bipartite graph G = (V , C, E) representing a proto187 graph, where V = {v1, В· В· В·, vnv } is a set of VNs and C = 188 {c1, В· В· В·, cnc } is a set of check nodes (CNs). Let E be a set of 189 edges e, where e = (v, c) connects a VN v вҲҲ V and a CN 190 c вҲҲ C. The bipartite graph can also be expressed in terms of 191 an nc Г— nv-sized base matrix BncГ—nv = {bi,j}, i вҲҲ [nc], j вҲҲ 192 [nv], where bi,j вҲҲ {0, 1, 2, В· В· В·} and [A] is a set of positive 193 integers less than or equal to a positive integer A. The rows 194 represent the CNs and the columns represent the VNs in the 195 protograph. Each entry bi,j of the base matrix represents the 196 number of edges connected between a VN and a CN. If there 197 are no edges connected between vj and ci, the entry bi,j is zero. 198 The variable (check) node degree deg(vj) (deg(ci)) is defined 199 as the number of edges incident to itself. A protograph LDPC 200 code is constructed by copy-and-permute operation of G. The 201 bipartite graph G is copied by the lifting factor N and copies 202 of each edge e = (v, c) вҲҲ E are permuted among copies 203 of v and c. In general, the large value of N guarantees the 204 sparseness of the code.

264 Now, we briefly explain the decoding process of GLDPC

265 codes over the BEC [13]. The VNs process the conventional

266 message-passing decoding over the BEC by sending correct

267 extrinsic messages to the CNs if any of the incoming bits

268 from their neighborhood is not erased. The SPC nodes send

269 correct extrinsic messages to the VNs if all of their incoming

270 messages are correctly received, and send erasure messages

271 otherwise. In this paper, the decoding of GC nodes is pro-

272 cessed by the maximum likelihood (ML) decoder. For each

273 iteration, a GC node ci with the (ni, ki) component code

274 receives the set of erasure locations {ei} from N (ci). Let

275 HGC be the parity-check matrix (PCM) of the component

276 code and He be the submatrix of HGC indexed with {ei}.

277 The decoder computes the Gaussian-elimination operation 278 of He, making it into a reduced row echelon form Hereduced . 279 If rank(Hereduced )= |{ei}|, the GC node solves all the input 280 erasures and otherwise, the decoder corrects the erasures 281 corresponding to the rows with weight 1 from Hereduced . The 282 decoding complexity can be further reduced if the GC node

283 exploits bounded distance decoding; however, the degrada-

284 tion of asymptotic performance is not negligible, as shown

285 in [7].

452 D. COMPARISON BETWEEN PROPOSED PD-GLDPC 453 CODES AND PROTOGRAPH DOPED GLDPC CODES 454 The main difference between the proposed PD-GLDPC codes 455 and the protograph doped GLDPC codes is the perspective of 456 doping. While the protograph doped GLDPC codes replace 457 an entire row in the protograph, i.e., a protograph CN by the 458 PCM of the component code, the proposed PD-GLDPC codes 459 append some rows incident to the VNs copied from single 460 protograph VN. The focus of the protograph doped GLDPC 461 codes is to choose a certain protograph CN to be replaced, 462 whereas the PD-GLDPC codes focus on choosing which 463 protograph VNs are further protected by partial doping. The 464 constraint for the protograph doped GLDPC code is that 465 the CNs to be replaced should have the degree equal to the 466 component code length, while the constraint for the proposed 467 PD-GLDPC codes is that the lifting size of a protograph 468 should be the multiple of the component code length. 469 Furthermore, compared to the proposed PD-GLDPC 470 codes, the protograph doped GLDPC codes have large doping 471 granularity. By generalizing a single CN by a component 472 code with parameters (Вө, Оә), the Вө protograph VNs are doped 473 assuming that the corresponding base entries are all ones 474 for the protograph check node. Whereas, for every partial 475 doping of ОІ GC nodes in the proposed PD-GLDPC code, 476 VNs copied from single protograph VN are partially doped. 477 In other words, the doping granularity is 1, which is smaller 478 than that of the protograph doped GLDPC codes. Since the 479 doping granularity of protograph doped GLDPC codes is 480 large, construction of the small protograph with capacity 481 approaching performance is very difficult. In Section IV.B, 482 we propose the construction method of PD-GLDPC codes 483 with partial doping of |X | protograph VNs.

789 VI. CONCLUSION 790 We proposed a new class of GLDPC codes called PD-GLDPC 791 codes that has advantages of a finer doping granularity 792 compared to the conventional protograph doped GLDPC 793 codes. Also, we proposed two optimization algorithms for 794 the PD-GLDPC codes: protographs constructed from ran795 dom LDPC code ensembles and protographs for PD-GLDPC 796 code ensembles constructed from genetic algorithms. Fur797 thermore, we proposed the partially doping and puncturing 798 technique. Using the proposed technique, the constructed 799 PD-GLDPC codes have good FER performances compared 800 to the popular protograph LDPC codes. Since it is possible to 801 partially dope the protograph VNs with a granularity one, the 802 rate loss is reduced from partial doping, and thus, GLDPC 803 codes can have capacity approaching performance in the 804 medium to high code rate regime. For future work, use of 805 other component codes and protographs with degree-1 VNs 806 can be studied. Also, constructions of PD-GLDPC codes by 807 generalizing the partial doping process such as doping over 808 multiple protograph VNs or doping only a portion of a proto809 graph VN can be considered. Furthermore, new constructions 810 of PD-GLDPC codes over additive white Gaussian noise 811 channels can be made.
