CRC-Aided Adaptive Belief Propagation Decoding of NR LDPC Codes
Xianwen Zhang1, Ming Jiang1,2, Mingyang Zhu1, Kailin Liu1, Chunming Zhao1,2 1National Mobile Communications Research Laboratory, Southeast University, Nanjing 210096, China
2The Purple Mountain Laboratories, Nanjing, China Email: {zhangxianwen, jiang ming, zhumingyang, lkl seu, cmzhao}@seu.edu.cn

Abstract—In this paper, we focus on how to further improve the performance of short block-length low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, especially the short LDPC codes in 5th generation (5G) New Radio (NR) systems. We propose an enhanced adaptive belief propagation (ABP) decoding algorithm with the assistance of partial cyclic redundancy check (CRC) bits. Meanwhile, the error detection ability can still be guaranteed by the remaining CRC bits and adaptive decoder parameters. Moreover, the proposed partial CRC-aided ABP (CA-ABP) decoding can be combined with the conventional BP decoding to further improve the performance. Simulation results show that compared with the BP decoding our proposed decoding method achieves an improvement in the error-correction performance.
Index Terms—New Radio, LDPC code, CRC-aided, adaptive belief propagation
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have been adopted by the 3GPP release 15 standard as the data channel coding scheme in the enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) scenario of the 5th Generation (5G) New Radio (NR) [1]. Thanks to their excellent error correction ability, a growing number of researchers pay attention to the decoding schemes for LDPC codes, especially for NR LDPC codes [2]–[5]. Although long LDPC codes are able to be designed close to the Shannon limit [6], the NR LDPC codes are considered to support the short length transmission from 100 to hundreds of bits to meet the different requirements of 5G applications. However, there is a significant gap between the practical performance of short LDPC codes and their theoretically optimal bounds [7]. Therefore, designing capacity-approaching coding/decoding schemes in the short block-length regime is still a challenge by now.
Regardless of whether the code itself has error detection capability, the information bits will be pre-coded by a cyclic redundancy check (CRC) code to avoid missed detection in most of practical communication systems, such as 5G NR and 4G LTE. According to the latest 5G specification (release 16), the maximum length of the CRC bits attached to LDPC codes is 24. Although the CRC codes are usually used for error detection, the successive cancellation list decoding of polar codes can be greatly aided by CRC detection (CA-SCL), which can not be successfully integrated into the iterative BP decoding of LDPC codes. Thus, there is a great potential to enhance the performance of short block-length LDPC codes if the CRC code can be ingeniously exploited.

The paper [8] proposes to combine the BP and the ordered statistical decoding (OSD) with the help of partial CRC bits, which considerably improves the performance of LDPC codes. However, the final output of the OSD method completely relies on the normalized Euclidean distance, which is highly dependent on the channel characteristics and is usually not a good indicator. Moreover, due to the characteristics of the OSD algorithm, when all CRC bits are used to enhance decoding, the algorithm will lead to a very high undetected error rate (UER). Thus, there is a difficult trade-off between the error detection and correction for the CRC aided OSD scheme.
Jiang and Narayanan proposed the adaptive belief propagation (ABP) algorithm with the polynomial complexity for Reed-Solomon (RS) codes [9]. The ABP algorithm has the best performance among all known decoding algorithms of RS codes. Its basic idea is to continuously use the Gaussian elimination to adjust the parity-check matrix according to the bit reliabilities. On the one hand, it allows unreliable bits to get more assistance when updating soft information, and on the other hand, it minimizes the error propagation of these less reliable bits in message passing decoding. The ABP algorithm uses a serial Gaussian elimination with high complexity, but it provides quite impressive performance almost close to the maximum likelihood (ML) lower bound, which is not inferior to other exponential-complexity algorithms, such as the KotterVardy algorithm. Although ABP is mainly used for soft decoding of RS codes, in fact, it can be employed for almost all short block-length codes, including LDPC and polar codes [10], [11].
This paper mainly concentrates on the NR LDPC codes with short block lengths and proposes a generalized ABP algorithm as a powerful supplement to BP decoding. Meantime, the ABP algorithm supports the use of partial CRC bits by combining some parity-check constraints of the CRC code and the paritycheck matrix of LDPC code. We compare our proposed scheme with the BP algorithm used in 5G NR. Simulation results show that our proposed scheme achieves a large gain over the conventional BP decoding while still guaranteeing the error detection capability.

III. CRC-AIDED ABP DECODING

To start with the ABP decoding of CRC-LDPC codes, the joint parity-check matrix is initialized by (8) using the combination of the parity-check matrix of LDPC codes and all or partial rows of the matrix HO. Following the conventional iterative BP-based decoding on the sparse matrix HI fails, channel outputs can also provide reliable information for the sorting and Gaussian elimination in ABP scheme. Finally, if the ABP decoding successfully outputs a result, remaining CRC bits are used for further detection to avoid missed detection.
In addition, different from the method of BP+OSD [8], we do not use the iteratively updated soft output of BP decoding for the second stage of ABP decoding. Because simulation results show that for short NR LDPC codes, reliabilities obtained from channel outputs are more accurate and robust than those built from the iterative BP decoding outputs.
If there still exists error bits in the information bits following BP decoding, the ABP scheme is performed, where the decoding error can be detected in a variety of ways, such as parity-check constraints and the CRC checking. The matrix HI with N − K parity-check equations is always used as an early stopping criterion for the LDPC decoding. Meanwhile, decoded information bits of the inner BP decoder will be further verified by the outer detection of the CRC. When the conventional BP decoding fails, the ABP decoding processes the reliability information through the following steps, including the sorting, the Gaussian elimination, and the BP decoding. These key processes are listed in Algorithm 1. Here, the matrix used for Gaussian elimination is the joint parity-check matrix H of CRC-LDPC codes. In this way, the participation of L CRC bits may have considerable effect on the ABP algorithm, especially for short LDPC codes, where the CRC redundancy accounts for a larger proportion, and the advantages of the ABP algorithm will be more obvious.
In the j-th iteration of the ABP algorithm, we perform Gaussian elimination on the reordering matrix H according to the reliability information, the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) rj = (r1j, r2j, ..., rNj ), where the columns associated with the less reliable bits are transformed into identity matrix form. The complexity of the ABP algorithm mainly lies in the Gaussian elimination operations in the iterative process. It is a serial and high-complexity operation. One Gaussian elimination requires N × min{K, (N − K)}2 binary operations. If the maximum number of outer and inner iterations are set to TO and TI , respectively, the complexity of the ABP algorithm is approximately O(TI · TO · N 3).
The algorithm we proposed can be divided into two main parts to calculate the decoding complexity: BP and CA-ABP decodings. As mentioned before, the CA-ABP decoding only works when the BP decoder fails to correct the codeword. Assuming that the FER of the BP algorithm is ζ, the complexity of the proposed decoding scheme is Xp = Xb + ζ · Xc, where Xb and Xc represent the complexity of the BP and the CA-ABP decodings, respectively.

Compared with the complexity of the conventional BP decoding, the complexity of implementation for the ABP decoding is extremely high for long codes. Therefore, for short NR LDPC codes, it is reasonable and practical to apply the ABP decoding on the joint parity-check matrix of a sparse matrix HI and a high-density small matrix HO .
IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
A. The Effect of Damping Factor on UER and FER
Under 5G standards, the UER of decoding should be strictly under 1% of the FER. Although the UER can be well controlled under 1% of the FER performance for moderate and long length of NR LDPC codes, that of short codes should be carefully considered. In the proposed decoding scheme, when CA-ABP decoding uses all CRC bits to assist in error correction, the UER may exceed the threshold requirement. There are two methods to improve the UER performance. One is to decrease the CRC bits used in CA-ABP, and the other is to adjust the damping factor in CA-ABP algorithm.
In a bid to further study the influence of the damping factor on the UER, we simulate different damping factors and give the performance comparison of UER and FER, demonstrating the overall trend of changing damping factor. We set the Eb/N0 = 1 dB, R = 2/3 and length N = 108 in the simulation. BP+CA-ABP(20, 3, 24) represents that the BP algorithm concatenated with our proposed CA-ABP in which the maximum numbers of inner iterations, outer iterations and the length of participating CRC bits are 20, 3, and 24, respectively. The length of the CRC bits is set to 24, which means that all CRC bits are used in CA-ABP. In Fig. 2, the interval between α = 0.4 and α = 0.5 is very meaningful. The CA-ABP decoding with α = 0.5 exhibits better FER performance, but the UER is rather high and unsatisfied.

However, it suggests that decreasing the damping factor results in better UER performance, providing a trade-off possibility between the error correction and detection. Besides, we also present the result of the BP+CA-ABP(20, 3, 21) decoding, implying that remaining three CRC bits are used for checking to lower the UER.
B. FER Performance Comparisons
To the best of our knowledge, the BP+OSD is the optimal algorithm for short LDPC codes. Here, we compare the decoding performances of short CRC-LDPC codes in 5G NR using BP, BP+OSD, and BP+CA-ABP algorithms by simulation. For the purpose of evaluation, the parity-check matrices of NR LDPC codes come from the coding scheme according to the 5G standard [1]. The encoded bits are transmitted over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel using the binary phase shift keying (BPSK) modulation. The receiver chooses iterative BP (sum-product algorithm) decoding as the reference algorithm for LDPC codes, the maximum number of iterations is set to 50, and each Eb/N0 at least 100 decoding errors are counted. The UER performance should also be evaluated since partial CRC bits are taken into error correction, thus we collect at least 50 undetected errors which satisfy all parity-checks of LDPC codes and the CRC checking for the CA-ABP decoding at each Eb/N0 point.
Generally, due to the high complexity of Gaussian elimination, it has no advantage compared to the standard BP algorithm. So, the ABP algorithm is usually configured as a positive enhancement of the BP decoder, after detecting the failure of the first-stage decoding process. Considering the implementation cost and complexity, for the ABP algorithm, we set the maximum numbers of inner iterations and outer iterations to TI = 5, 20 and TO = 3, respectively. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose the CA-ABP algorithm, which can effectively enhance the decoding of short NR LDPC codes in 5G systems. With the assistance of partial CRC bits, our proposed scheme for short LDPC codes provides a significant performance gain compared with the conventional BP decoding. Although the error detection ability is inevitably affected by the reduction of CRC redundant bits, the UER of the proposed scheme can still be well controlled at a sufficiently low level by adjusting the damping factor or reducing the length of the participating CRC bits. The simulation results show that when partial CRC bits are used to improve performance, the codes with higher code rates and shorter code lengths enjoy better decoding gains. Therefore, the CA-ABP algorithm is a more effective supplement to the conventional BP decoding of short LDPC codes, which is beneficial for more-reliable applications and developments beyond 5G.
