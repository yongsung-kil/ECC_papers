Abstract—In this paper, we propose an intelligent coded-bits 
stream recovery scheme for improving reliability performance. In 
this design, a residual learning aided convolutional neural 
network (ResCNN) architecture with BCEwithLogits loss function 
is proposed to achieve the intelligent distortion compensation for 
the received data. In addition, at the online deployment stage, the 
ResCNN will calculate and output the float estimates of coded-bit 
streams, which act as the soft information to be regarded as the 
initial log likelihood ratio (LLR) for the subsequent decoding 
algorithm. Thus no channel state information (CSI) is required, 
then pilots used for the channel condition estimation can be 
removed to improve both the efficiency and the practicality. 
Subsequently, we analyze the computational complex and provide 
simulation results, which demonstrate that the proposed system 
can effectively improve the reliability performance of the receivers 
with no need of the CSI estimates to achieve higher practicality. 
Keywords—correlated fast fading channel, reliability, deep 
learning, residual learning aided convolutional neural network 
(ResCNN) 
I. INTRODUCTION  
Channel encoding and decoding technology has been widely 
adopted in modern communication system to ensure the reliable 
recovery of user information bits at the receiver. For example, 
thanks to the high reliability, low decoding latency and low 
implementation complexity, the low-density parity-check 
(LDPC) codes with belief propagation (BP) decoding 
algorithms has been applied or recommended in 5G enhanced 
mobile broadband [1], digital video broadcasting [2] and 
satellite communication [3]. 
However, the reliability performance will degrade with the 
existence of non ideal channel conditions of transmission 
environments and imperfect signal processing of hardware 
equipments in practical communication system, which will 
introduce additional noise and interferences to distort the 
transmitted signal and damage the coded-bits stream [4]. 
In order to improve the reliability performance, many 
traditional signal processing algorithms have been applied at the 
receiver. For example, the least square and minimal mean square 
error algorithms can achieve the channel estimation and 
equalization at the cost of pilot overhead [5]. Besides, the filter-
based and wavelet transformation-based signal denoising 
algorithms can increase the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) by 
suppressing 
noise 
with 
the 
knowledge 
of 
statistical 
characteristics of signals and noise [6], [7]. 
However, the operating conditions dynamically vary, which 
can hardly be exactly evaluated. To be explicit, the additive 
noise can be Gaussian or non-Gaussian distributed, white or 
correlated, or even random with unknown statistical 
characteristics due to the unstable hardware components [4], [8]. 
The multiplicative fading can also be fast varying and correlated 
due to the multiple paths in transmission environment and the 
poor scattering in propagation paths as well as the mutual 
coupling between antenna elements [9]. 
Recently, deep learning (DL) has been widely applied in 
communication field thanks to its excellent learning ability, 
convenient self-optimization algorithms and superior ability to 
optimize system performance. In order to enhance the 
adaptability of traditional channel equalization algorithms, a DL 
based joint equalizer and decoder [10] is proposed to equalize 
the channel fading utilizing the useful signals instead of pilots 
and to retrieve the information bits intelligently. A double 
convolutional neural network (DCNN) [11] is also proposed to 
respectively obtain the channel state information (CSI) and 
additive noise estimates and improve the decoding performance 
over correlated fast fading channel. Furthermore, a traditional 
wavelet denoising theory based residual shrinkage CNN 
(RSCNN) denoiser is proposed in [12] to improve the denoising 
performance and has demonstrated the significant reliability 
improvement when applied before the decoding. 
However, the complexity of the intelligent decoder 
exponentially increases as the information bit length increases. 
Thus the DL based decoders are mainly used for very short 
codes and has poor practicality [13]. Besides, though the DL 
based signal processing has improved the reliability of 
traditional decoding algorithms, the neural network (NN) design 
still depends on the statistical characteristic of time-varying 
fading and noise, and the traditional decoding performance are 
still influenced by the accuracy of initial log-likelihood ratios 
(LLRs), whose evaluations require the probability distribution 
of additive noise [14]. 
In this paper, we propose a residual learning aided 
convolutional neural network (ResCNN) based intelligent 
coded-bits stream recovery scheme over time-varying channels 
with correlations. Different from the existing research, the

Our motivation is to reliably recover the user data with no 
need of the channel state information (CSI) estimation. To be 
explicit, in our design, the proposed ResCNN is composed of 
batch normalization (BN) layers, convolutional (Conv) layers 
and nonlinear rectified linear unit (ReLU) activation functions. 
Then the residual learning strategy is used to maintain the 
statistical features of coded-bits stream and accelerate the 
convergence of NN. Besides, the binary cross entropy with 
logits (BCEwithLogits) loss function is used to measure the 
difference of each actual output and its expected bit, so as to 
guide the next training direction. At the offline training stage, 
the input of ResCNN is the received data and the expected 
output is the corresponding coded-bits, then the ResCNN can be 
trained, optimized and configured. Afterwards, at the online 
deployment stage, the well-trained ResCNN can compensate the 
distortion of received data and output the soft information as the 
initial LLR for the sequent decoding algorithms to retrieve data 
bits reliably. 
Briefly, the main contributions of this paper include: 
1) We propose a ResCNN based intelligent coded-bits 
stream recovery scheme to compensate the distortion of 
received signals from time-varying channel with 
correlations. 
2) The proposed ResCNN consists of BN layers, Conv 
layers, ReLU activation functions and residual learning 
strategy. Then the BCEwithLogits loss function is used 
to measure the NN convergence. 
3) The ResCNN will calculate and output the soft 
information as initial LLRs for the sequent iterative 
decoding algorithm, thus no CSI estimates are required 
in the bit recovery to improve both practicality and 
robustness performance.

Section III presents the proposed ResCNN based coded-bits 
stream recovery scheme in detail. Next Section IV analyses the 
computational complexity of ResCNN, and provide the 
simulation results for performance comparisons with benchmark 
schemes to validate the superiority of our proposal. Finally, 
Section V summarizes this paper.

B. Reliability Performance 
Subsequently, the reliability performance in terms of the bit 
error rate (BER) of the proposed ResCNN based coded-bits 
stream recovery aided channel decoding is analyzed and 
compared with that of benchmarks over the correlated fast 
fading channel. Note that the number of decoding iteration is set 
as 25. 
Firstly, Fig. 3 presents the reliability performance over the 
correlated fast fading channel with the fixed 𝜃𝑛 and different 𝜃ℎ 
respectively being 0.0, 0.8, 0.9. It can be seen that the BER 
performance of the proposed ResCNN based communication 
system is similar to that of benchmark [11], [12] and is worse 
than the upper bound. However, as the correlation of 
multiplicative fading 𝜃ℎ increases, the performance gap 
between the proposal and the upper bound becomes narrower. 
Then, we fix the 𝜃ℎ to 0.9 and change the 𝜃𝑛 to 0.5, 0.8, 0.9 
to analyse the BER performance over the correlated fast fading 
channel. As shown in Fig. 4, when compared with the traditional 
decoding with pCSI, the proposed ResCNN based decoding can 
achieve the similar BER performance when 𝜃ℎ = 0.9 and 𝜃𝑛= 
0.5, and can obtain the significant BER performance gain when 
𝜃ℎ= 0.9 and 𝜃𝑛 = 0.8, 0.9. Therefore, thanks to the intelligent 
coded-bits stream recovery by the proposed ResCNN, the 
communication 
system 
can 
achieve 
better 
reliability 
performance than the traditional upper bound. Besides, when 
compared with other two intelligent distortion compensation 
based decoding scheme [11], [12], our proposed ResCNN based 
decoding scheme can achieve the similar and even better BER 
performance. 
Last but not the least, it is worth pointing out that the DCNN 
based decoding scheme [11] needs to evaluate the distribution 
of residual noise to calculate the initial LLRs whenever the NN 
configuration and channel condition changes. Additionally, the 
RSCNN based decoding scheme [12] requires the CSI 
estimation and the knowledge of the noise distribution to adjust 
the loss function and conduct the data preprocessing. By contrast, 
our proposed ResCNN can achieve the coded-bits recovery with 
no requirement of channel state, thus the practicality is enhanced 
while maintaining the similar and even better reliability 
performance with acceptable complexity. 
V. CONCLUSION 
In this paper, we propose an intelligent coded-bits stream 
recovery design for reliable information transmissions over the 
correlated fast fading channel, which removes the requirement 
of the CSI estimation and the practicality is improved. We 
propose to construct the ResCNN with BN layers, Conv layers, 
ReLU function, BCEwithLogits loss function and residual 
learning strategy to compensate the distortion and recover the 
coded-bits. After the offline training, the configured  ResCNN 
can calculate and output float estimates of coded-bits as the 
initial soft information to be directly sent to the subsequent 
decoding module, which removes the complicated LLRs 
evaluation of channel conditions including the CSI and noise 
distribution. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed 
system can achieve the similar and even better reliability 
performance with acceptable complexity when compared to 
benchmarks. Hence the proposed design provides an intelligent 
and reliable solution for practical transceivers which can hardly 
obtain perfect CSI.
