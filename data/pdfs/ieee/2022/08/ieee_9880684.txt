An Improved 5G NR LDPC Decoding Algorithm Based On Box-Plus Operation

Liming Lua, Yi Lia, Li Fangb aKey Laboratory of Universal Wireless Communications, Ministry of Education, Beijing University
of Posts and Telecommunications, Beijing, China bBeijing Laboratory of Advanced Information Networks, Beijing University of Posts and

Corresponding author: liyi@bupt.edu.cn

Abstract—Iterative box-plus operation is an equivalent function of BP algorithm used in fifth generation(5G) new radio Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) decoders, which has gathered significant interest in the past few years. However, it is very challenging to approximate iterative Box-Plus operation effectively since it contains continuous logarithmic and exponential operation. In this paper, the problem is solved gracefully by proposing a simple yet efficient approximate function. The corresponding optimal parameters are given by density evolution and Monte Carlo simulation, which could greatly reduce the unreliability of check-to-variable messages. The extensive experiments in error-correction performance have show the feasibility of the proposed method, compared to existing NMS and OMS algorithms, which makes it more attractive in practical 5G LDPC decoding applications.
Index Terms—5G, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, decoding, iterative Box-plus operation
I. INTRODUCTION
One type of linear block codes with performance that is on the verge of the channel capacity limit is low-density parity-check (LDPC) code [1], [2]. The fifth-generation (5G) new radio (NR) data channel coding scheme in the enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) scenario [3] has adopted QC-LDPC as a very potential code due to its exceptional data throughput and superior error correction performance. It has also been adopted by several industrial standards, including IEEE 802.11n WLAN [4], IEEE 802.16e Wi-MAX, power line ITU-T systems [5], the second generation satellite digital video broadcast DVB-S2, and Advanced Television System Co (ATSC) [6].
Gallager initially devised the Iterative Belief Propagation algorithm (BP), commonly known as the sum-product algorithm (SPA). In the last decade, several applications and algorithmic studies of the LDPC code have arisen, which all evolved from the BP algorithm. When LDPC code and BP decoding algorithm work together, it has been shown that they can approach the Shannon limit. The check node (CN) update process comprises a high number of inverse hyperbolic tangent function operations, making it very difficult to implement the BP method in hardware design. Min-Sum (MS) [7] algorithm is one of the earliest techniques developed to minimize the complexity of BP algorithm. It approximates the nonlinear update equation of check nodes in BP algorithm

by addition and minimization operations. It can greatly simplify computation, but at the expense of check-to-verifiable message correctness. By incorporating coefficients into the MS method, the Normalized-Min-Sum (NMS) and OffsetMin-Sum (OMS) [8] algorithms strike a compromise between complexity and performance loss.
In order to reduce bit error rates, several researchers have recently merged the BP algorithm with neural networks in their investigations of LDPC codes. The traditional BP decoding approach may be combined with model-driven deep learning [9] or gated neural networks [10] to gain better performance, however this comes at the cost of thousands of dynamic parameters. Due to the processing capability of the chip, the aforementioned algorithms (including BP) will not be implemented in hardware at this time. NMS and OMS algorithms are still often used as decoding modules in FPGA development [11]–[13]. The optimized Box-Plus operation, which replaces the continuously multiply operations in the BP algorithm with two applications of Jacobian logarithmic identity [14], was the most potential remedy for this fragmentation, even though a few optimal factors for various scenarios recently introduced to the MS algorithm could offer a balance between implement complexity and accuracy [15]–[17]. Additionally, the computation of the two least trustworthy outgoing magnitudes in check nodes simplified the Box-Plus operation [18]. However, as a dense area denotes a high row degree, this approach can only be used to the base matrix’s core check area.
The main contributions of our work are summarized as follows:
• 1. Analyze and list the drawbacks of the aforementioned algorithms, noting that the inaccuracy between them and the BP algorithm grows with row degree.
• 2. Propose a revolutionary low complexity LDPC decoding technique, using Density Evolution and Monte Carlo simulation providing the necessary parameters.
• 3. To demonstrate the validity of the proposed algorithm, numerous simulations are performed; the results reveal a 0.2dB increase in comparison to existing optimum algorithms.

V. CONCLUSION
This paper proposes a revolutionary Improved Box-Plus Algorithm to significantly enhance the error-correction performance of the 5G NR LDPC decoder. It was demonstrated that two factors could be used to approximate the iterative BoxPlus operation. The simulation results reveal that the proposed IBA algorithm achieved superior BER and FER performance compared to existing NMS and OMS decoders despite a modest increase in computational complexity. In practical LDPC decoder designs, this is an advantageous trade-off between necessary number of operations and performance. Considering that the proposed algorithm performs better than BP at low bit rates, our next research will focus on implementing the IBA algorithm in IR-HARQ.
