Optimized LDPC Code Concatenated with Trellis Shaping for PAPR Reduction in SISO-OFDM Systems

Abstract—Different techniques have been used for the peak-toaverage power ratio (PAPR) reduction of orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) systems. Among these, trellis shaping provides state of the art performance in terms of PAPR reduction as well as mean power reduction with moderate computational complexity. Therefore, this manuscript addresses the effect of using shaping codes with higher trellis depths for PAPR reduction of the OFDM systems. Using shaping codes of higher depths improve the PAPR reduction as well as bit error rate (BER) of the system. Moreover, we concatenate an optimized low-density parity-check (LDPC) code with Trellis shaping to further improve the performance of the OFDM system in terms of BER. For the LDPC code, the variable node degree distribution is optimized based on the error probabilities of the individual bits inside a quadrature amplitude modulation (QAM) constellation taking into consideration the error probabilities of the shaping codes used. Simulation results show that the proposed technique has better PAPR reduction as well as better BER as compared to the already existing systems using Trellis shaping with a shaping code of 4 states concatenated with an optimized LDPC code.
Index Terms—OFDM, Trellis shaping, PAPR, irregular LDPC codes, shaping convolutional codes, constellations

I. INTRODUCTION

In wireless communication, OFDM is a widely used communication technology which provides high spectral efficiency, robustness against channel impairments, and simplicity in implementation. However, a major disadvantage of OFDM is its high peak to average power ratio (PAPR), defined as

Hence, there is a dire need to address the high PAPR issue of the OFDM systems [1]. In order to lower down the high PAPR of the OFDM transmit signal, different approaches were put forward in the past. A detail of the proposed techniques can be found in [2]. The proposed PAPR reduction strategies can mainly be categorized as signal distortion, signal scrambling, and probabilistic techniques [10]. The simplest signal distortion technique is Clipping in which the transmit signal is clipped if its amplitude crosses a predefined threshold [3]–[7]. Authors in [5] proposed a peak windowing technique, which is an alternative approach for the hard clipping, whereas, [6], [7] presents the peak cancellation technique, where a peak cancellation waveform is generated for the PAPR reduction, after the IFFT block. The scaling, shifting, and subtraction of this waveform from the OFDM signal at points where the signal comprises high peaks reduces the PAPR. Signal scrambling techniques work in two steps; firstly, a set of tones is reserved to spawn a corrective function used for PAPR reduction and secondly, the corrective function is iteratively added to the transmit signal in time domain for PAPR reduction. The modifications to the structure of the OFDM signal are made to reduce the PAPR of the transmit signals. Prominent signal scrambling techniques used for PAPR reduction are Tone Reservation (TR) [10] and Tone Injection (TI) [10]. Multiple signaling and probabilistic techniques are based on generating multiple samples or frames of the original data and later choosing one with minimum PAPR for transmission. Well known probabilistic techniques are Selected Mapping (SLM) [8] and partial Transmit Sequence (PTS) [9]. The computational complexity increases in the case of the multiple signal techniques for PAPR reduction, especially at the OFDM transmitter. A detailed analysis of all the PAPR reduction techniques is presented in [1].
Trellis shaping (TS) was initially put forward for the reduction of the average power by G.D Forney in [1]. However, it was later extended to PAPR reduction by Werner et. al. in [11]. In [11], the authors proposed two metrics, one for the time domain and another for the frequency domain, as a branch metric for the trellis of the shaping code used. It was observed that the time domain metric performs better than the frequency domain metric with added computational complexity as multiple DFT/IDFT pairs were used. TS is known to be the most flexible and appropriate PAPR reduction technique which can also be used to reduce the average power by controlling transitions of the signals and exploiting the trellis codes structure. No side information transmission is required and it is easily applicable in almost all systems. Unlike other PAPR reduction techniques, TS does not introduce nonlinear distortions. For the application of TS for PAPR reduction, a technique with minimum complexity was developed that used auto-correlation of the side lobes of the transmit signals in frequency domain by Ochiai in [12]. Through out the literature, the authors have used shaping codes with a small constraint length, i.e., usually using a 4-state shaping code. In [1], Forney has shown that the shaping gain increases with an increase in the trellis depth. Moreover, it has also been found in the literature that the BER performance of the convolutional codes improves as the constraint length increases (higher states).
Herein, we consider Trellis shaping among all the available techniques for PAPR reduction because of its high efficiency and reduced complexity. Moreover, for TS, we consider different shaping codes of higher constraint length (trellis depths). This results in performance improvement in terms of PAPR reduction. Moreover, we also evaluate the BER results of the shaping codes used. For further improvement of the system performance in term of BER, we concatenate an optimized irregular LDPC code by taking into consideration the error probabilities of different shaping codes used. This improvement in PAPR reduction makes the proposed OFDM system as a promising digital communication technology in the upcoming generation of wireless networks.

V. CONCLUSION
For PAPR reduction using trellis shaping, we have used shaping codes of various depths. The simulation curves makes it evident that system performance in terms of PAPR can be improved using shaping codes of higher depths. However, greater PAPR reduction is obtained at the cost of a slight increase in the system’s computational complexity. Moreover, BER curves are evaluated for different shaping codes, which shows that the BER penalty for obtaining the gain is marginal, i.e., 0.4 dB for shaping code with 16 states trellis depth.
