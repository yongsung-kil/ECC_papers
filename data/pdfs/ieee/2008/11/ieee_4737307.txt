Soft-Quantized Demodulation for BPSK over Discrete-Time Memoryless Fading Channel
Qiuliang Xie, Kewu Peng, Jian Song, Changyong Pan and Zhixing Yang Tsinghua National Laboratory for Information Science and Technology (TNList) Department of Electronic Engineering, Tsinghua University, Beijing 100084, China
Email: xql06@mails.tsinghua.edu.cn

Abstract— The demapping method that simply multiplies the received symbols with Channel State Information (CSI) coefficients is proved optimal in this paper for any BPSK modulated Discrete-time Memoryless Fading Channel (DMFC) from the viewpoint of channel capacity, when CSI is available at the receiver. A generic method to calculate the channel capacity of BPSK modulated DMFC subjected to uniform soft quantization is proposed, which offers a powerful criterion for optimal quantizer design. Numeric results of the Rayleigh fading case are presented, showing that 6-bit uniform quantization offers effective demodulation compared with unquantized demodulation, losing less than 0.1 dB for both with and without CSI cases.
I. INTRODUCTION
Soft-decision Maximum Likelihood Decoding (MLD) has about 3 dB of coding gain over hard-decision decoding in Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel and more gains in fading channels [1]. Therefore, soft-decision decoder is increasingly employed in practical broadcasting and communication systems, especially after the introducing of Turbo codes and the rediscovery of the Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes, which offer unprecedented coding gains with soft decoding iterative schemes.
At the receiver, it is highly likely that the soft-decision decoder should be implemented with fixed point arithmetic. Namely, the received signal should be demapped and quantized before sending to the soft decoder. The questions are “what is the optimal demapping arithmetic and how many quantization bits are enough?” Tosato [2] pointed out that the optimal BPSK demapping arithmetic is simple by multiplying the received symbols with the CSI coefficients, from the LogLikelihood Ratio (LLR) point of view, while we also show that this arithmetic is optimal from the viewpoint of channel capacity in this paper. Dasgupta [3] discussed the quantization effect to turbo decoder under AWGN channel and pointed out that 3 or 4 bits quantized decoder offers indistinguishable performance from unquantized one. Ohhashi [4] and Jeong [5] derived that 6-bit uniform quantizing in Rayleigh fading channel approaches optimal bit error rate (BER) with proper choice of the clipping level for LDPC and Turbo decoders, respectively. The quantization effect to the soft decoder also draws very much attention recently, i.e., Oh [6] discussed the quantization effect to Min-Sum LDPC decoder.
On the other side, analytical tools are also proposed in many literatures. Cutoff-rate criterion is used in [5] to design an optimal channel quantizer for Turbo decoders and

it works well, although the performance of Turbo codes often exceeds the theoretical limit determined by cutoff-rate. Minimum Mean-Squared Error (MMSE) is proposed in [7] as another criterion option. Besides, Wilson [8] showed that 3-bit quantizing provides essentially the same efficiency as unquantized demodulation over AWGN channel with BPSK modulation, losing perhaps 0.25 dB, from the channel capacity point of view. Liveris [9], [10] compared these three criterions for BPSK modulated AWGN channel quantizer design: cutoff rate, MMSE and capacity, and found that the capacity criterion yields slightly better than cutoff-rate criterion, and MMSE is not a good criterion for quantizer design.
Motivated by the pioneers’ work, in this paper, we extend channel capacity as a general criterion for optimal quantizer design to BPSK modulated Discrete-time Memoryless Fading Channel (DMFC), and propose a method for capacity calculation after quantization. Unlike in AWGN channel, both demapping and quantization should be considered together in DMFC. The most relevant work to our’s was done by Binshtok [11], where they also considered DMFC and the channel capacity criterion, and pointed out that 4-bit optimal nonuniform quantization offers less than 0.2 dB loss in Rayleigh fading case with CSI available at the receiver. However, uniform quantization is preferred because the quantization output should be passed to a soft-decoder, and not only the capacity loss should be minimized but also the LLR metric of the output should be held.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II presents the system model. Following the system model offered in [12], only real channel is considered throughout this paper. Therefore, no phase information is needed and CSI only consists of magnitude information. Section III gives the channel capacity of BPSK modulated DMFC with CSI, and presents capacities of Rayleigh fading case both with and without CSI. Section IV discusses the demapping arithmetic and the optimal uniform quantization for BPSK modulated DMFC with numeric computation. Finally Section V concludes this paper with some remarks.

V. CONCLUSION
The channel capacity of BPSK modulated DMFC with perfect CSI at the receiver is derived, and those of BPSK modulated discrete-time memoryless Rayleigh fading channel with and without CSI are also obtained. Numeric results show that the capacity of BPSK modulated Rayleigh fading channel with perfect CSI is about 1 dB better than that without CSI.
The BPSK demapping method, that simply multiplies the received symbols with CSI coefficients, is optimal for any DMFC from the channel capacity point of view, when CSI is available at the receiver.
The channel capacity after uniform quantization over fading channel is discussed, and a generic capacity computation method is proposed. Numeric results for the Rayleigh fading
