Good Encodable Irregular Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes

Yang Xiao
Institute of Information Science Beijing Jiaotong University Beijing 100044, China E-mail: yxiao@center.njtu.edu.cn

Kiseon Kim
Dept. of Information and Communications Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology Gwangju 500-712, Korea, E-mail: kskim@gist.ac.kr

Abstract—The existing encodable irregular LDPC codes may have a degraded BER performance when it is derived from classical regular QC LDPC codes since both girth property and weight property are not carefully given attention to two or more thing, which lead to some codes with large girths have small minimum weights and poor BER performance. To solve the problem, we propose a new approach to construct the encodable irregular QC codes, which can not produce new short girths and can keep the large minimum weight and minimum distance of the codes. The theorems of girth 4 and girth 6 are provided to test the girth variance of the obtained irregular QC codes. Simulations verify the construction of the QC LDPC codes to be valid, since better BER performance than that of existing encodable irregular LDPC codes is obtained. Keywords-Channel coding, circulant matrices, low-density paritycheck codes, encodable quasi-cyclic codes.

I. INTRODUCTION

Classical QC LDPC codes have algebraic structure, for

some cases, the short girths can be avoided [1-5]. However,

QC LDPC codes have the so-called encoding problem: it is

impossible to derive their generator matrices from their parity-

check matrix, since there are at least j-1 dependent rows in the

parity-check matrix. To find these dependent rows of a parity-

check matrix is a NP problem, because these dependent rows

are the linear combinations of independent rows [4].

III. THE TEST ALGORITHMS OF CONSTRUCTED CODES

This section tests the properties of the irregular QC codes constructed in Section 2. Especially, the structure of the constraint graphs, the minimum distance of the codes, and encoding techniques are described.

III.1 Girth Test

The BP decoding algorithm converges to the maximum a

posteriori (MAP) solution for LDPC graphs to have no girths;

for graphs with girths, there is no such optimality [1-4]. The

girth of a graph is the length of the shortest cycle in the graph.

To evaluate the proposed QC LDPC codes, we need to

consider the girth problems. We have developed a theorem to

test the girth 4 [18].

Definition 1: If there exist two columns or two rows of parity

check matrix H to have 1 at same position, which forms a

quadrangle, we call it 4-girth of the LDPC code.

Theorem 1(test the 4-girths) [18]: The parity check matrix

H has no girth of 4, if and only if the entries of the test matrix

O all are 1s or 0s except the diagonal line, where

III.2 Minimum Weight Test
The other important properties of encodable irregular QC codes are the minimum weight and the minimum distance. At high SNRs, the maximum-likelihood decoding performance of an error-correcting code is dominated by the code’s minimum weight and minimum distance.
Definition 3: The weight of binary vector cm m ∈{1,...,2N } is the number of 1’s in cm , expressed by w(cm ) . Definition 4: The minimum weight of binary vectors cm m ∈{1,...,2N } is the minimum number of 1’s in code set
{cm} , expressed by wmin .

Definition 5: The distance of two binary vectors cm and cn m, n ∈{1,..., M }is the number of 1’s in different positions of
them, expressed by d (cm , cn ) . Definition 6: The minimum distance of a binary vector set c is the minimum number among the distances {d (cm ,cn )} , and
expressed by dmin . In Definition 6, if cn is zero code, then dmin = wmin .
It is easy to know that the row vectors of generator matrix
G belong to the code set {cm} [16], and it is necessary for
LDPC codes to have large minimum weight of row vectors of
G . Many QC codes without 4-girths, but the minimum weight of row vectors of G is lower, which lead to them not obtain
good BER property [16]. It is easy to know that the row vectors of generator matrix
G belong to the code set {cm} [16], and it is necessary for
LDPC codes to have large minimum weight of row vectors of
G . Many QC codes without 4-girths, but the minimum weight of row vectors of G is lower, which lead to them not obtain
good BER property [12]. The algorithm of estimating the minimum weight of LDPC
codes is as following [20].
Step 1: Given the parity-check matrix H , obtain generator matrix G from (9), and calculate the minimum weight of row vectors of G , and it is wmin (g) .
Step 2: Construct a decoder of Set K to be a larger integer.
Step 3: Resulting in a code cn of the parity-check matrix H ;
Step 4: From i=1 to wmin (g) ,
input the decoder a sequence with continuous “1s” of number i and other to be zeros;
if the decoder solves the sequence as a all zero code then i=i+1, else the tested codes have an estimated minimum weight of 2(i-1)+1, which is a tight upper bound the real minimum weight. If in step 4, we have not found the estimated minimum weight
of 2(i-1)+1, the estimated minimum weight will be wmin (g) .
The details of the algorithm and related theorem can be found in [20].

V. CONCLUSIONS
A code construction based on circulant matrices for encodable irregular QC LDPC codes has been proposed. The good properties of girth, minimum weight and BER of the constructed LDPC codes can be ensured by the proposed test theorems and algorithms. Simulations show that the constructed codes perform better BER than those of existing encodable irregular QC LDPC codes of same block length and code rate.
