A Necessary and Sufficient Condition for Determining the Girth of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes
Xiaofu Wu, Xiaohu You, and Chunming Zhao

Abstract—The parity-check matrix of a quasi-cyclic lowdensity parity-check (QC-LDPC) code can be compactly represented by a polynomial parity-check matrix. By using this compact representation, we derive a necessary and sufficient condition for determining the girth of QC-LDPC codes in a systematic way. The new condition avoids an explicit enumeration of cycles for determining the girth of codes, and thus can be well employed to generate QC-LDPC codes with large girth.
Index Terms—Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, quasicyclic codes, girth.
I. INTRODUCTION
Q UASI-CYCLIC low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes are a special class of LDPC codes whose paritycheck matrices consist of circulant matrices. Particular examples of circulant matrices include circulant permutation matrices, sums of two or more circulant permutation matrices, and also the zero matrix. If the parity-check matrix of a QC-LDPC code consists of circulant matrices of the same size, namely, L × L, the required memory for storing the parity check matrix can be reduced by a factor of about 1/L, as compared to randomly structured LDPC codes. The encoding of QC-LDPC codes can be efficiently implemented by simple shift-register circuits. Compared to general LDPC codes, the design of a decoder for QC-LDPC codes can also be significantly simplified due to simplified routing of messages from memory to computation units [1]. Therefore, QC-LDPC codes are favorable for hardware implementation.
A (J, K)-regular LDPC code is defined by a parity-check matrix H in which each column has weight J and each row has weight K. In [2]–[4], the authors considered a special class of regular QC-LDPC codes whose circulant matrices are circulant permutation matrices. It has been shown in [2], [4] that any (J, K)-regular QC-LDPC code of this class has minimum distance always upper bounded by (J + 1)!, and girth upper bounded by 12.

By composing a parity-check matrix not only with circulant

permutation matrices but also with sums of two circulant per-

mutation matrices and with zero matrices, one can increase the

minimum distance while keeping the same regularity. Further

allowing the sums of three or more circulant permutation

matrices leads to six-cycles, thus are often not of interest

for iterative sum-product decoding. This new class of QC-

LDPC codes are called type-II QC-LDPC codes in [5], while

the codes whose parity-check matrices are composed of only

circulant permuation matrices and the zero matrix are of type

I. Specifically, for (3, 4)-regular codes in the type-I class, the

minimum distance bound is 24 , while the minimum distance

bound for (3, 4)-regular codes in the type-II class is 32 [5].

part of performance loss is usually attributed to small values

of girth. A necessary and sufficient condition for a type-

[2]. Although this condition takes a simple form, one has to

introduction of sums of two circulant permutation matrices,

IV. CONCLUSION
We have derived a necessary and sufficient condition for determining the girth of QC-LDPC codes. Compared to [2, Theorem 2.1], the main advantage of Theorem 4 is that it avoids an explicit enumeration of cycles for type-I QC-LDPC codes, and can also be used to determine the girth of general QC-LDPC codes. Hence, this condition can be well employed in search of good QC-LDPC codes with large girth.
