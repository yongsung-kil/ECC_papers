Design of Low-Complexity Well-Structured LDPC Codes Based on Iterative-Filled Approach

Wei Zhang, Guangxi Zhu, Li Peng and Qiongxia Shen Department of Electronics and Information Engineering,
Huazhong University of Science and Technology, Optical-Electronic National Laboratory, Wuhan, 430074, P.R.China
E-mail:stonez1011@163.com

Abstract
Low density parity check codes are a popular class of linear block codes for forward error correction in communication channels. Recent years have seen a lot of work towards the hardware-oriented constructions for LDPC codes which have comparable errorcorrecting performance to random construction codes. This paper proposes a novel method called Iterativefilled (IF) to construct LDPC codes and a new kind of LDPC codes named IF-LDPC codes. Based on the properties of the method proved in this paper, we present a design of regular and quasi-regular IFLDPC encoders which have linear encoding complexity, simple construction, low memory requirement and are suitable for partly parallel decoding. These advantages make them easy to hardware implementation. The IF-LDPC codes exhibit good performance in computer simulations and some even better than MacKay codes1.
1. Introduction
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are one of the best performing channel codes known today which are to be used for forward error correction in a vast number of upcoming standards like DVBS2[1], WiMax (IEEE802.16e), and wireless LAN (IEEE802.11n). Beside their impressive errorcorrecting performances that can be quite close to Shannon’s limit, LDPC codes show an intrinsic parallel structure suited to reach remarkable decoding throughputs[2][3], which is difficult for turbo decoders. These peculiarities have significantly contributed to make LDPC codes among one of the most promising

To make LDPC codes come to practical applications, there has recently been much work done on the study of constructions with advantages in hardware implementation. High complexity is the primary difficulty of implementation. To reduce encoding complexity requires the H-matrix in systematic form which can avoid Gaussian elimination and make a linear time encoding possible. For decoding, as fully parallel decoders are too complex to implement practically[4], partly parallel structures[5] become mandatory. In the current literature, most LDPC code designs rely on random construction of the parity check matrix and the resulting lack of structure makes the codes hard to implement in partly parallel structures.
Therefore, we can see that the key to a practical LDPC code system is to design hardware-oriented Hmatrix constructions. Based on the methods of construction, LDPC codes can be classified into two general categories: 1) random (or random-like) codes[6][7][8]; and 2) structured codes[9][10]. However, due to their random structure, random (or random-like) codes are hard to use partly parallel structures. As opposed to random construction of LDPC codes, the group-structured quasi-cyclic LDPC codes are proposed[11][12]. This particular type of codes is of interest because they provide good performance and are hardware friendly.
In this paper, we propose an original method named Iterative-filled (IF) to construct LDPC codes and a new ensemble of LDPC codes called IF-LDPC codes. IFLDPC codes provide freedom in the selection of code parameters, which makes them compatible with the existed standards. Furthermore, IF-LDPC codes encode directly by parity check matrix H rather than generator matrix G, and the encoding complexity can be made linear in message length. The group-divided structure

This contradicts the precondition that there are no cycles of length 4 in the Father Matrix. Thus the supposition is not true. In a similar way we can prove the cases of cycles of length 6, cycles of length 8 and others.
Theorem 2: In the manipulation of Matrix Filling, if there is a cycle of length 4 in the Father Matrix and the CFMs fall into the same COS, it concludes that there are no cycles of length 4 in the Son Matrix by avoiding the same CFM be filled into the four “1”s of the cycle.
Proof. For COS is an orthogonal set, the “1”s of the matrixes in the COS do not overlap. This property guarantees that the Son Matrix has no cycles of length 4 if the CFMs filled in the four “1”s of the cycle are not the same.

4.3. Encoding Complexity of quasi-regular IFLDPC codes

Equation (4)(5) show that each parity bit pi is the sum of pi-1 and K products. Therefore the calculating complexity of each parity bit is K products and K + 1 adds. In fact, as there are only t “1”s in every row of Hd , the actual calculating complexity is 2t+1, including t products and t+1 adds. There are M parity bits to be calculated in encoding processes, Thus the encoding complexity is (2t+1)M. For H-matrix is sparse, that’s, t<<M, the complexity is linear with the information bits length M, O(M) namely.

Fig. 1 compares the regular IF-LDPC codes with different Expanding Factors (twice Iterative-filled). These codes are of block length 6144, column weight 3, row weight 6 and girth 6. In such conditions, the Expanding Factors n1 and n2 have to satisfy the following restrictions: n1·n2 = 1024 and n1 > 15. The restrictions allow for 6 kinds of IF-LDPC codes shown in Fig.1. We also find that the regular IF-LDPC codes’ redundancies and decoding iterations are determined by n1. The larger n1 is, the less the redundancies are and the more the iterations are. In Fig.1, the code of n1 = n2 = 32 has the best performance of 4.28dB at 10-5 BER and the lowest error floor. With increment of the absolute difference between n1 and n2 (|n1-n2|), the performance of these codes decrease and the code of n1 = 256 n2 = 32 has the worst performance of 4.61dB. Generally speaking, the difference between these codes is not obvious, even almost the same below 4dB. There is another curve of QC-LDPC code (n1 = 1024 n2 = 0) which does not use the Iterative-filled method. Compared with the QC-LDPC code, IF-LDPC codes win decoding gain of 0.5~1.1dB and the decoding iterations decrease obviously. The result comes from the fact that the Iterative-filled method increases the randomicity of H-matrix. The Expanding Factors have effect both on H-matrix’s randomicity and CFM’s repetition probability: the larger the Expanding Factor is, the more randomicity is, which makes performance better; the less the Expanding Factor is, the higher repetition probability is, which makes worse performance. According to this, n1 and n2 should be as large as possible for good performance. While the product of n1 and n2 is a constant, it gets the best performance when the absolute difference between n1 and n2 is minimum, which is the best balance point between randomicity and repetition probability.

6. Conclusion and future work
In this work a family of structured LDPC codes called IF-LDPC codes has been proposed. The matrixes constructed by the Iterative-filled method have the following advantages: 1) Constructed easily. For there are no additions and multiplications to generate H-matrix, it can be implemented easily by shift-registers; 2) No need to store the whole matrix and use CFVs instead; 3) Encode directly with Hmatrix avoiding calculating G-matrix; 4) Groupdivided structure is compatible with partly parallel structure. These advantages make IF-LDPC CODEC implemented efficiently in hardware. And the simulation results exhibits remarkable error-correcting performance of IF-LDPC code.
There are still some future works to study the Iterative-filled method more deeply. In this paper, we have only discussed the codes generated by twice Iterative-filled and with the CFM of identity matrixes. The effects of more times’ Iterative-filled or other kinds of CFMs, such as Q-matrix[14], D-matrixes[15] etc, need to research by more simulation experiments. What’s more, the hardware implementation of IFLDPC code CODEC is another research direction. This work is sure to play an important role in many communication fields.
