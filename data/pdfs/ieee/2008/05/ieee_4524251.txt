Generalized Combining Method for Design of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes
Yuanhua Liu, Xinmei Wang, Ruwei Chen, and Yucheng He

Abstract— A generalization of the Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT) combining method is proposed to design much more and better quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes when the parity check matrices of the component codes are given. It can design a much larger class of QC-LDPC codes with similar performance by loosening the condition for determining the intermediate parameters. By permuting the block rows of the parity check matrices of the component codes, a lot of QC-LDPC codes with much less 6-cycles and better performance can be designed. At a BER of 10−6 some QC-LDPC codes designed by the generalized combining method outperform those designed by the CRT combining method by 0.5 dB.
Index Terms— Low-density parity-check codes, quasi-cyclic, circulant permutation matrix.
I. Introduction
L OW-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, first proposed in the early 1960s [1] and re-discovered in 1996 [2], have recently attracted much attention due to their capacityapproaching performance and low decoding complexity. As an attractive class of LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes are well suitable for certain practical applications since they can easily be encoded using simple shift-registers with linear complexity [3]. In addition, well-designed QC-LDPC codes of moderate length can perform as well as random LDPC codes.
A Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT) combining method was proposed in [4] in order to design QC-LDPC codes of larger length by combining QC-LDPC codes of smaller length. Since the intermediate parameters are uniquely determined by a constraint condition, only one QC-LDPC code can be designed once the parity check matrices of the component codes are given. Due to the similar structural property of the component codes e.g., array codes, there exists a lot of 6-cycles in the Tanner graph of the designed code and the performance is not good enough with iterative decoding. The main contribution of this paper is to generalize and improve the CRT combining method. By loosening the condition for determining the intermediate parameters, a much larger class of QC-LDPC codes with similar performance can be designed. By permuting the block rows of the parity check matrices of component codes, a lot of 6-cycles in the Tanner graph of the designed code can be eliminated and a large number of QC LDPC codes with better performance can be designed.

IV. Performance results
In this section, performance of several QC-LDPC codes designed using the generalized combining method and the CRT combining method is compared over an AWGN channel with BPSK modulation. In both cases, the sum-product algorithm (SPA) is used with a maximum of 50 decoding iterations.
Fig. 1 presents the shortened QC-LDPC codes (539, 310) and (924, 695) designed based on array codes C(3, 7) and C(3, 11) by these two methods. In the generalized combining method, A1 = 1, A2 = 6 and the following permutation scheme is used: S (H1) remains unchanged; the first and the second row of S (H2) permutes with each other. By using the method proposed in [6], the number of 6-cycles is counted. The code (539, 310) designed by the CRT combining method has 1386 6-cycles and only 154 6-cycles exist after permuting the rows of S (H2). For code (924, 695), 3696 6-cycles are eliminated by permuting the rows of S (H2).
Array codes C(3, 11) and C(3, 13) are used to design the shortened QC-LDPC codes (1573, 1146) and (2860, 2433) presented in Fig. 2. In the generalized combining method, A1 = 1, A2 = 8 and the same permutation scheme is used. There exists 7150 and 858 6-cycles in the Tanner graph of codes (1573, 1146) designed by the CRT and the generalized combining method, respectively. For codes (2860, 2433), 25740 6cycles are produced by the CRT combining method and 20878 6-cycles are eliminated by the generalized combining method.
As shown in these figures, the QC-LDPC codes designed using the generalized combining method perform much better. At a BER of 10−6 the QC-LDPC codes designed by the generalized combining method outperform those designed by the CRT combining method by 0.5 dB.
