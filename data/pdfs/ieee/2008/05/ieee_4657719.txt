Abstract-In this paper, we proposed a new method for constructing quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC- LDPC) codes based on circulant permutation matrices via the onecoincidence sequences. The main advantage is that QC-LDPC codes with a variety of block lengths and rates can be easily constructed with no cycles of length four or less. Simulation results show that the proposed QC-LDPC codes perform slight better than the random regular LDPC codes for short to moderate block lengths and have almost the same performance as Sridara- Fuja-Tanner codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were originally invented by Gallager [1] in the early 1960s and have recently attracted wide attention in the coding community because they can achieve near Shannon limit performance with iterative message-passage decoding. A binary (j, k)-regular LDPC code is defined as the null space of a sparse paritycheck matrix Hover GF(2), and satisfies the following properties: 1) each column has weight j; 2) each row has weight k; 3) no two rows (or two columns) have more than one I-component in common; 4) both j and k are small compared to the code length. Most methods for designing good LDPC codes are based on random construction, but the lack of structure makes the encoding process complicated and the memory in the decoder required to store the paritycheck matrices may be prohibitive in practical application.
Recently, several quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes are proposed to overcome these problems, and they can further be separated into two categories: one is based on finite geometries [2], and the other is based on circulant permutation matrices [3], [4]. Both of them can produce medium length codes with good minimum distance. Encoding can be done efficiently by using shift registers as far as hardware implementation is concerned.
Both Sridhara-Fuja-Tanner (SFT) codes [3] and Fossorier codes [4] are QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices and have been widely investigated. The results show that these codes perform quite well compared to random LDPC codes at moderate block lengths. Furthermore, the required memory for storing these QC-LDPC codes can

be reduced by a factor lip, while p x p circulant permutation matrices are employed.
In this paper, one new method to construct the QC-LDPC codes based on the circulant matrices is proposed, and these circulant matrices are permuted according to the onecoincidence sequences (OCSs). Here OCSs are defined as the multilevel sequences with the properties that any two different ones have at most one element in common [5, 6]. This simple method can be used to construct the (j, k)-regular QC-LDPC codes in variety of block lengths and rates with no 4-cycles.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the construction of OCS-based QCLDPC codes. The system performance analysis is examined in Section III, and Section IV summarizes the results and concludes the paper.

III. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, we present the simulation results of some LOPC codes with different block lengths and rates constructed by the proposed method. These results are compared with competitive codes [3], [7] of similar rates and block lengths for a binary phase-shift keying (BPSK) modulated AWGN channel with SNR Eb/No. In all cases, the iterative sum-product algorithm (SPA) was used for decoding, and the maximum number of decoding iterations is 50. The SPA decoder stops when either a valid codeword is found or the maximum number of decoding iterations is reached.
Figure 1 shows the bit error plots for the proposed (3, 5)regular QC-LOPC codes constructed by these three DCS families. These LOPC codes all have code rate r ~ 0.4 but different block lengths. The performance of LOPC codes constructed randomly but explicitly avoiding cycles of length four is also shown. As shown in Fig. 1, the proposed QCLOPC codes perform slightly better than the randomly constructed LOPC codes for short to moderate block lengths, and it is obvious to see that the performance improves as the block length increases. However, the performance of the proposed QC-LOPC codes is not as good as randomly constructed LOPC codes at longer block length. This may be caused by their limited minimum distance. (For a (j, k)-

As shown in Fig. 2, the proposed QC-LOPC codes perform slightly better than the randomly constructed LDPC codes and have similar performance as the SFT codes. Figure 3 compares the performance of r ~ 0.8239, (3, 17)regular QC-LDPC codes constructed by the proposed method with that of the SFT codes. It can be seen that the performance of the proposed QC-LOPC codes almost matches that of the SFT codes. Moreover, these simulation results show that the QC-LDPC codes constructed by the DCS families have similar performance and the performance can be improved by increasing the block lengths.
IV. CONCLUSION In this paper, we presented a new method for constructing QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices by using the OCS families. The simulation results show that the proposed QC-LDPC codes perform as well as the random constructed LDPC codes at short to moderate block lengths and have almost the same performance as the SFT codes for different block lengths and rates. Moreover, the quasi-cyclic structure of the codes makes them suitable for practical applications.
