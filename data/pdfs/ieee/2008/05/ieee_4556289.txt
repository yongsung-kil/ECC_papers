Impact of Roundoff errors in LDPC decoding

Abstract—In this paper the impact of roundoff mechanisms on the performance of message-passing LDPC decoding is studied. It is shown that finite word length introduces error by means of two mechanisms, each of which is analyzed. The impact and behavior of the two mechanisms are clarified by experimental results.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity check (LDPC) codes [1] have recently become popular and have already been adopted in a variety of standards ranging from satellite video (DVB-S2) to 10GBase-T ethernet (802.3an).
Decoding LDPC codes by using message-passing algorithms is a common technique. As their name indicates, message-passing algorithms exchange messages between computation units, which correspond to operations modeled by nodes of a bipartite code graph.
Several custom VLSI architectures have been proposed for implementing the decoding of LDPC encoded messages, either for general codes, or for codes of special structure [2][3].
The determination of the various word lengths required to represent data is a tedious and time consuming procedure, which requires extensive simulations. Targeting at the objective of reducing the corresponding design time, analytical models that relate word lengths to decoding performance are sought. In this paper, two mechanisms that contribute to the total roundoff error, are identified and experimentally studied. The presented analysis can serve as the first step to the sought error model.
The reminder of the paper is organized as follows. Section II describes the log Sum-Product (SP) decoding algorithm. Section III analyzes the quantization error which appears due to the application of two different mechanisms, also analyzed. Section IV presents some simulation results and Section V concludes the paper.

III. EFFECT OF Φ(x) QUANTIZATION
As Φ(x) is transcendental, approximation error and finite word length effects affect its computation. A trade off arises between the accuracy in the computation of Φ(x) and the complexity of the hardware implementation. Reduction in accuracy reduces complexity, but only at the cost of reduced decoding performance of the algorithm. Various schemes have been proposed in order to efficiently realize Φ onto hardware, such as piece-wise linear approximation [6] or non-uniform quantization [7]. A way to compute function Φ(x) is to store it in a lookup table using a uniform quantization scheme [8]. Such a scheme effects the performance of the algorithm in two ways; i.e., there are two mechanisms that degrade the performance by introducing quantization error. Let Φˆ (x) denote the quantized Φ(x) expressed using f fractional and i integral bits.

V. CONCLUSIONS
This paper investigates the ways in which the finite word length, used to represent Φ(x), imposes roundoff error. There are two mechanisms, each one of them introduces error and affects decision by its own way. The first one, named Roundoff Mechanism, introduces error of small value. This kind of error has the characteristics commonly encountered when uniform

Fig. 10. Histogram of Quantization Error for i = 5, f = 4.
quantization schemes are used, namely follows a normal distribution. The second mechanism, namely Saturation Mechanism, can introduce error of larger values and different distribution.
This work is the first step in developing a theoretical model that describes algorithm’s decision changes of a code for different values of parameters i, f and SN R, aiming at the objective of limiting the need for extensive time-consuming simulations.
