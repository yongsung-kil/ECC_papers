A Modification to Weighted Bit-Flipping Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes based on Reliability Adjustment

Dajun Qian, Ming Jiang, Chunming Zhao and Xiaofu Wu National Mobile Commun. Research Lab.
Southeast University, Nanjing 210096, China Email: {qiandj 2004, jiang ming, cmzhao2}@seu.edu.cn, xfuwu@ieee.org

Abstract— In this paper, a modification to the weighted bitflipping (WBF) decoding algorithm for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is proposed. This modification, based on the adjustment in reliabilities of the check sums during iterative decoding, can be applied to various WBF algorithms. Our studies show that the modification can be achieved with a small increase in complexity, but leads to an appealing improvement in error performance.
I. I
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, first proposed by Gallager [1], [2], and recently brought into prominence by Mackay [3], [4], are now attracting a great deal of research interest. LDPC codes can be decoded with various decoding methods, including hard-decision, soft-decision, and hybrid decoding schemes, such as bit-flipping (BF) decoding [1], weighted bit-flipping (WBF) decoding [5], and belief propagation (BP) decoding [3], in order of complexity. Among these algorithms, WBF preserves the speed advantage of BF and bridges the performance gap between the BF and BP decoding, resulting in an effective trade off between performance and complexity.
The WBF algorithm flips one bit per iteration, according to the criterion known as the flipping function. The flipping function includes both the number of unsatisfied check sums calculated from the received sequence, and the check-sum reliabilities. Various modification schemes to WBF have been suggested. In [6], Fossorier introduced a modified weighted bit-flipping (MWBF) algorithm, considering the reliability information from both the check sums and the received symbols. Improved modified weighted bit-flipping (IMWBF) algorithm, presented in [7], is an improvement of MWBF. In [8], Liu and Pados proposed LP-WBF algorithm as another improved WBF scheme. The modified LP-WBF, called weighted-sum weighted bit-flipping (WS-WBF) algorithm was obtained in [9].
In this paper, we introduce a modification scheme to the WBF decoding algorithm, named check-sum reliability adjustment. This adjustment is based on the observation that, the check sums involving an error bit would become more reliable after this error is flipped during iterative decoding. Therefore, the idea is to update the reliability values of these check sums in each iteration. In our study, the modification

can be implemented on the MWBF, IMWBF, and WS-WBF algorithms according to each decoding structure. Furthermore, this modification provides an attractive improvement in error performance without much complexity increase.
The remainder of the paper is organized as follows: In section II, various WBF algorithms are briefly reviewed. The main principle of the check-sum reliability adjustment and the modified algorithms are depicted in Section III. Simulation results of the modified algorithms are presented and discussed in Section IV. Finally, conclusions are drawn in Section V.

A. Principle of the modification
In this section, a modification scheme called check-sum reliability adjustment is introduced. The idea of the modification is based on the observation that, during iterative decoding, the check sums involving an error bit would become more reliable after this bit is flipped. Therefore, in each iteration, we can examine the check sums involving the bit to be flipped and increase their reliability values.
In essence, among the check sums involving the to-beflipped bit, only the unsatisfied ones can be considered for adjustment. As for a certain satisfied check sum, it possibly contains another error bit besides the flipped one. Therefore, this kind of satisfied check sums still cannot be measured of higher reliabilities in the next iteration. In summary, the modification scheme simply increases the reliability values of the unsatisfied check sums involving the to-be-flipped bit at the end of each iteration.
The proposed check-sum reliability adjustment can be used in various WBF decoding algorithms. In this study, we apply this modification to MWBF, IMWBF, and WS-WBF, respectively.

IV. S R
A. Choice of modification factor δ
When applying this modification to the MWBF and IMWBF algorithms, we have to determine the modification factor δ in order to achieve the best possible performance. Since a theoretic approach to get the optimal δ seems difficult, we resort to simulations. Fig. 1 shows the effect of δ on the frame error rate (FER) of the (1023, 781) finite geometry (FG)-LDPC code [5] decoded by the modified MWBF. It can be observed that the error performance is not very sensitive to the value of δ at low SNRs. The simulation results also reveal that the optimal value of δ nearly remains the same at different SNRs. Hence, when decoding a given LDPC code, factor δ can be fixed at a constant value for any SNR. In our study, the optimal value of δ used in Fig. 2 and Fig. 3 are determined through simulations, where the codeword is transmitted over an AWGN channel, using BPSK signaling with unit energy. It is important to note that the optimal δ is dependent of the signaling scheme and the channel condition.
B. Performance of the modified MWBF and IMWBF algorithms
The error performances of the modified MWBF and IMWBF algorithms, which incorporate the check-sum reliability adjustment (CRA) are depicted in Fig. 2 and Fig. 3, in comparison with the standard algorithms. Simulation results demonstrate that the modification schemes are effective of both Gallager codes [4] and FG codes.

It is also observed that, the modification results in a 0.1dB improvement with the IMWBF.

C. Performance of the modified WS-WBF algorithm
Fig. 4 compares the error performances of the (255, 175) FG-LDPC code with the WS-WBF, WS-WBF with CRA, and BP algorithms. The maximum number of iterations is set to 100. For WS-WBF and its modified algorithm, the thresholds Tk and Th are fixed at 6 and 0.55, respectively [9]. We find that, at the FER of 10−5, the WS-WBF with CRA achieves an extra 0.2dB improvement and is only 0.4dB away from the BP decoding.
