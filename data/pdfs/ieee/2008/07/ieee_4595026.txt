Abstract‚ÄĒWe consider communication over the binary erasure channel (BEC) using low-density parity-check (LDPC) code and belief propagation (BP) decoding. Furthermore, a gap between the bit error probability after ŇŅnite number of iterations for ŇŅnite block length n and that for inŇŅnite block length is asymptotically őĪ/n, where őĪ denotes a speciŇŅc constant determined by a degree distribution, a number of iterations and erasure probability. Our main result is to derive an efŇŅcient algorithm for calculating őĪ for regular ensembles.

I. INTRODUCTION

In this paper, we consider communication over the bi-
nary erasure channel (BEC) using low-density parity-check (LDPC) code [1] and belief propagation (BP) decoding. Let Pb(n, , t) denote the bit error probability of LDPC codes with block length n over the BEC with erasure probability after t iterations of BP decoding. For inŇŅnite block length, Pb(‚ąě, , t) := limn‚Üí‚ąě Pb(n, , t) is calculated easily by the density evolution [2]. Furthermore, there exists a threshold BP such that limt‚Üí‚ąě Pb(‚ąě, , t) = 0 for < BP and limt‚Üí‚ąě Pb(‚ąě, , t) > 0 for > BP. Although the analysis for inŇŅnite block length is easy, ŇŅnite-length
analysis is complicated. For inŇŅnite number of iterations, Pb(n, , ‚ąě) := limt‚Üí‚ąě Pb(n, , t) is calculated exactly by stopping sets analysis [3]. Furthermore, for ŇŅnite number of iterations, Pb(n, , t) is also calculated exactly in a combinatorial way [4]. However, these exact ŇŅnite-length analyses are
computationally expensive as the block length increases.

V. NUMERICAL CALCULATIONS AND SIMULATIONS
The speed of convergence of n(Pb(n, , t) ‚ąí Pb(‚ąě, , t)) to őĪ( , t) as n gets large is investigated via numerical simulations. In the proof in Section III, we have counted only the error probability due to cycle-free neighborhood graphs and single-cycle neighborhood graphs. Hence, it is expected that simulation results are close to the limit őĪ( , t) only for large block length where the probability of multicycle neighborhood graphs is sufŇŅciently small. In particular, {(l ‚ąí1)(r‚ąí1)}t < n is necessary for (l, r)-regular ensemble.
Contrary to the expectation, the convergence for (3, 6)regular ensemble seems to be fast for large in Fig. 2. Furthermore, simulation results for (2, 3)-regular ensemble are close to the limits őĪ( , t) already at small block length for any in Fig. 3. Simulation results are close to őĪ( , t) at a block length 801 which is so small for 20 iterations that the probability of cycle-free and single-cycle neighborhood graphs is zero. Fast convergences of other (2, r)-regular ensembles are conŇŅrmed via numerical simulations.
For (3, 6)-regular ensemble, |őĪ( , t)| is very small for small since there do not exist single-cycle stopping sets which do not include variable nodes of degree 2. Hence, contribution of multicycle stopping sets becomes larger than that of singlecycle stopping sets, and therefore the convergence is slow for small . Furthermore, the convergence is expected to be faster if multicycle stopping sets are expurgated from the ensemble.
