Abstract—This paper investigates error control at the physical layer of a multicast network using low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. Packets for transmission are encoded into LDPC codewords. A joint iterative message-passing scheme for decoding LDPC codewords at a receive node in the network is proposed to improve error performance. Also proposed is a split-codeword transmission to provide equal error protection for all transmitted packets. Density evolution analysis and some simulation results are also presented.

I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, linear network coding has emerged as an effective method to increase the throughput of multicast networks [1]. In practice, network coding must be designed to cope with noise, interferences and distortions in communication environments. As a result, error control coding is needed in network communications to provide reliable information transmission. LDPC codes [2] is currently the most promising error control coding technique to achieve Shannon capacity [3] over a wide range of communication channels.
The use of LDPC codes and joint network-channel decoding in a multiple access wireless relay channel has been studied in [4], where the joint decoding is performed on a single Tanner graph. In this paper, LDPC codes are incorporated into the well-known butterfly multicast network model [1], and a new joint iterative decoding scheme on two interactive Tanner graphs is proposed for a good error performance. To provide equal error protection for all the received words at a sink node, a split-codeword transmission scheme is also proposed. With properly selected LDPC codes, these two schemes provide significant coding gain over the non-joint iterative decoding. These coding schemes can be generalized to more complex multicast networks.

VI. DENSITY EVOLUTION AND SIMULATION RESULTS

Density evolution (DE) was introduced in [7] to predict the

asymptotic performance of an ensemble of LDPC codes with

a given degree distribution under message passing decoding,

and it has been shown to be a powerful tool in the analysis

and optimization of LDPC codes in many applications. DE

can also be used to evaluate the asymptotic performance of

the proposed joint decoding schemes.

It is proved in [7] that when a certain set of symmetry condi-

tions in the channel and the decoder are satisfied, the message

passing decoder’s probability of error will not depend on the

transmitted codeword, hence an all-one (over ) codeword p

can be assumed in the performance analysis. Similarly, we

have proved that the error performance of our proposed joint

decoding scheme is independent of the transmitted codewords

, under the same symmetry conditions. Therefore all-one

&

I

codewords can be used in the performance analysis, and the

density evolution for a single SPA decoder can be readily

modified for the joint decoding.

VII. CONCLUSION
In this paper, an error control coding scheme at the physical layer of a multicast network is presented. In this error control scheme, LDPC codes with a joint iterative message-passing scheme are integrated with network coding. To provide equal error protection to transmitted codewords, a split-codeword transmission scheme is also proposed. Experimental results show that the proposed error control scheme works very well with the butterfly model of multicast network in [1]. In more complex network coding models, basic operations at sink nodes are similar to those in the butterfly model. Hence the proposed joint decoding and split-codeword transmission can be similarly extended and applied in future research.
