Rate-Compatible Irregular Repeat-Accumulate Codes for DVB-SH Applications

David Benmayor∗†, Stylianos Papaharalabos†, P. Takis Mathiopoulos†, Georgia Tsiropoula† and Philip Constantinou∗ †Institute for Space Applications and Remote Sensing, National Observatory of Athens, Palea Penteli, Athens, Greece
Email: {benmayor, spapaha, mathio, georgia}@space.noa.gr ∗School Electrical and Computer Engineering, National Technical University of Athens, Athens, Greece
Email: fkonst@mobile.ntua.gr

Abstract—This paper presents a systematic rate-compatible (RC) irregular repeat-accumulate (IRA) low-density parity check (LDPC) family of codes for Digital Video Broadcasting-Satellite to Handheld (DVB-SH) applications. Puncturing and extending of an optimized IRA mother code of rate 1/3 are used to obtain the full range of code rates. The main contribution of this paper is a simple, yet memory-efficient and modular, extending algorithm to obtain the lower code rates, based on a truncated Vandermonde matrix. In contrast to previously published extending methods, the one introduced in this paper does not use density evolution analysis, but instead achieves lower rate codes up to 1/5, by employing solely circulants and identity matrices in a deterministic structure. Compared to the standardized 3rd Generation Partnership Project 2 (3GPP2) turbo codes, the proposed RC-IRA codes feature lower complexity decoding, that is beneficial from processing, power and memory constrained handheld devices. The performance of the proposed family of codes is evaluated for the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel with information block length k = 12282 bits. Computer simulation results demonstrate that, for a wide range of rates, the proposed RC-IRA family of codes performs very close to the 3GPP2 turbo codes, and outperforms the 3GPP2 turbo codes when the latter exhibit an error-floor at FER below 10−3 and at at BER below 10−5, apart from the case of rate R=1/5.
I. INTRODUCTION
Digital Video Broadcasting - Satellite to Handheld (DVBSH) is a transmission system standard designed to deliver multimedia and data services to small handheld devices, such as mobile telephones and personal digital assistants (PDAs), using S-band frequencies [1]. A hybrid satellite/terrestrial architecture is employed using a satellite to achieve wide region of coverage and subordinate terrestrial gap fillers in areas with inadequate satellite signal reception, as shown in Fig. 1 on the next page. The system and waveform specifications have been published as European Telecommunications Standards Institute (ETSI) standards in [1], [2] and the first guidelines for setting up networks and services using the DVB-SH specifications have been given in [3].
This paper is part of the 03ED554 research project, implemented within the framework of the ”Reinforcement Programme of Human Research Manpower” (PENED) and co-financed by National and Community Funds (20% from the Greek Ministry of Development-General Secretariat of Research and Technology and 80% from E.U.- European Social Fund). It has also been supported by the SatNEx-II project (IST-027393) funded by the European Commission under the FP6 program.

DVB-SH systems are subject to time-varying channel conditions that need to be compensated for, in order to enhance the signal robustness. Therefore, the 3rd Generation Partnership Project 2 (3GPP2) turbo code combined with a highly flexible channel interleaver that offers time diversity has been selected for forward error correction (FEC) [4].
Since DVB-SH has been designed for delivering internet protocol (IP) based media content and data to handheld terminals, adaptive channel coding with unequal error protection (UEP) and low complexity decoding would be very beneficial considering the fact that handheld devices have limited processing, power and memory capabilities. Motivated by the need for an alternative family of codes featuring the aforementioned characteristics, we propose a rate-compatible (RC) irregular repeat-accumulate (IRA) low-density paritycheck (LDPC) family of codes for DVB-SH applications.
RC codes are defined as a family of nested error correction codes, where the coded bits of the higher rate codes are embedded in the lower rate codes. RC codes are usually applied to communication systems that operate under a wide range of signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs), but need to maintain a single encoder/decoder architecture, to achieve high throughput. LDPC [5], [6] codes have attracted considerable attention owing to their capacity-approaching performance in various types of channels and iterative message-passing (MP) decoding [7]–[9]. Furthermore, LDPC codes feature lower decoding complexity and better performance than turbo codes [10], especially for large coded block lengths. Puncturing has been used to achieve rate compatibility in the context of LDPC coding [11]. Several density evolution models for punctured LDPC codes in the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel have been developed so far [12]–[14]. These models propose different techniques to acquire an optimal puncturing distribution which, firstly, does not disturb the optimality of the base code distribution and, secondly, results in well performing punctured LDPC codes. Although puncturing is the easiest way to obtain higher rate codes, it cannot provide a sequence of capacity-approaching LDPC codes over a wide range of rates. As explained in [15], [16] puncturing should be combined with extending to achieve rate compatibility. However, the family of codes introduced in [15], [16], by random puncturing and extending, is limited to a range of code

The proposed extending method is based on obtaining the optimized proportions of extending nodes through density evolution analysis. Yet, this extending method has increased design complexity and it is not memory efficient. Consequently, it is not suitable for applications that demand limited hardware, memory and power resources. Motivated by the above, our contribution is the design of an RC-IRA family of codes for DVB-SH applications with low decoding complexity, wide range of code rates and high memory, power and processing efficiency. Furthermore, a simple and deterministic extending method to obtain lower rate codes is introduced, as opposed to the design via density evolution in [17]. The proposed extending method extends the work in [16] by broadening the obtained range of code rates up to R=1/5.
II. DVB-SH CHANNEL CODING
As shown in Fig. 1, signals are broadcasted to terminals using a direct path through a satellite, if available, or alternatively, an indirect path through one or more terrestrial repeaters.
Orthogonal frequency division multiplex (OFDM) (for terrestrial or satellite transmission mode) and time division multiplex (TDM) (for satellite mode) are deployed to ensure an efficient transmission scheme and commonality with the Digital Video Broadcasting-Terrestrial (DVB-T), Digital Video Broadcasting-Handheld (DVB-H) and the 2nd version of Digital Video Broadcasting-Satellite (DVB-S2) standards. Based on the above, two reference architectures are specified [1]:
• DVB-SH A: for OFDM-based terrestrial and satellite transmission;
• DVB-SH B: for OFDM-based terrestrial transmission and TDM-based satellite transmission.

III. LDPC AND IRA CODES
An LDPC code is a linear block code defined by a sparse parity-check matrix H having m rows and n columns (the designed code rate R = 1 (m/n)). It is assumed that parity-check matrices are defined over Galois Field (2) (GF(2)). That is, the element Hi,j at the intersection of row i and column j in H is either 1 or 0. IV. CONSTRUCTION OF RC-IRA CODES

The parity puncturing portion of this scheme leverages the work of [17] to obtain the higher rate RC-IRA codes, while the extending part of the approach introduces a novel technique based on a truncated Vandermonde matrix. Rate-compatibility is achieved by placing identity matrices at the lower right part of the extension matrices, whereas the entries at the upper right part are all zero. As it underlined in [17], degree-1 nodes play an important role in designing low rate codes. The optimal proportion of degree-2 and degree-1 nodes has been investigated by means of computer simulations. The results show that the optimal proportion is π∗ = 1.0, indicating that the extended nodes at the lower right part of the parity-check matrix should be all degree-1 nodes.
To obtain the higher code rates 2/5, 1/2, 2/3, parity bit puncturing is used. This technique can be applied either randomly, as in [15], [16], or in a deterministic manner [12]– [14]. Even though higher rate codes can easily be obtained by random puncturing, it is not recommended since it causes considerable performance loss. The proposed family of codes adopts the puncturing algorithm introduced in [17] for finite length RC-IRA LDPC codes. This algorithm is based on minimizing the maximal recoverable step of the punctured nodes, and it is used to puncture the degree-2 variable nodes that are deterministically placed and connected to the check nodes in a zigzag shape.
V. SIMULATION RESULTS
The performance of the proposed family of codes is evaluated in a DVB-SH system assuming an input frame size of 12282 bits, binary phase-shift keying (BPSK) modulation, and an AWGN channel. A single pair of encoder and decoder is used, i.e. the lowest code rate (1/5), and the optimal sum-product algorithm (SPA) performs at most 80 decoding iterations.
