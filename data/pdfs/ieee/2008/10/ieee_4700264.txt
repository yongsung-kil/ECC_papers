Abstract-In this paper we investigate the performance enhancement due to the implementation of low density parity check (LDPC) codes on IEEE 802.11n system under channel model B TGn and its Application Specific Integrated Circuit design.
Simulation result shows that LDPC codes gives 6 dB better
performance compared to binary convolutional codes. Logic synthesis on 0.13 pm CMOS technology with low-power standard cell library shows that the proposed Min-sum algorithm-based LDPC decoder is suitable for portable devices.
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, multimedia data becomes huge and need a high quality system to communicate it on wireless system. Orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) is a promising technique to support such communication due to its ability in providing high throughput communication system.
One class of wireless local area network (WLAN) which is based on OFDM system with single antenna at both transmitter and receiver had been standardized by IEEE 802.1 la/b/g. This system offers maximum throughput of 54 Mbps. However to communicate bigger amount of multimedia data in a convenient way, one should uses higher grade system. The WLAN standard of IEEE 802.1 In enhances the previous system with multiple antennas at both side named multiple-inputmultiple-output (MIMO)-OFDM. This combination promises high performance and and high throughput (HT) communication system. It offers throughput of 260 Mbps at physical layer for 20 MHz of bandwidth, which can be achieved by using modulation coding scheme (MCS) index 30 and 31 with 4 spatial streams [1].
To enhance the error performance one should involve error correcting codes (ECC) in his system. Turbo codes and LDPC codes are well known as the most powerful ECC. Both
of them can give performance close to Shannon limit on binary symetric channel (BSC) [2] Both of them have some
similarities. They are kind of sparce matrices and may use sum-product algorithm as decoding algorithm. However LDPC

codes offer lower decoding complexity [3]. Further more, with
a long code length and a large number of decoding iteration, LDPC codes can achieve performance as close as 0.0045 dB to Shannon limit on BSC with aditive white Gaussian noise
(AWGN). [4]. Two types of ECC technique are adopted in IEEE 802.1 In
WLAN standard for forward error correction (FEC) encoder
scheme. They are binary convolutional codes (BCC) and
LDPC codes. The BCC are mandatory for all MCS configura-
tion and LDPC codes are used optionally as high-performance
ECC technique in HT system [1]. In this paper we report our investigation about performance
enhancement by using LDPC codes for IEEE 802.11n instead of BCC. In our simulator, system with BCC as FEC encoder are decoded using soft-decision Viterbi algorithm. While system with LDPC are decoded by min-sum algorithm. We compare the performance of both systems on AWGN channel and to get closer to real situation, we compare them on small-office channel model of channel model B of task group IEEE 802.1 In (B TGn). Information about channel model B TGn can be found in [5]. In Application Specific Integrated Circuit (ASIC) design, we synthesized our LDPC decoder using Synopsys Design Compiler on 0.13 pm CMOS technology with low-power standard cell library.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. LDPC
codes for IEEE 802.11n is briefly reviewed in Section II.
Computer simulation results for both system performance are
displayed in Section III. ASIC design of LDPC decoder and
logic synthesis results are presented in Section IV and some conclusion are drawn in Section V.

LDPC decoder. To implement an LDPC decoder based on the Min-sum algorithm, we employed partially-parallel architecture [6], [7] and to represent internal data in the decoder we used 16-bits fixed point representation which consists of a sign bit, 11 bits integral part and 4 bits fractional part. Horizontal Processing Units (HPUs) and Vertical Processing Units (VPUs). Brief explanation of these units are as follow.
A. MUL unit
The MUL unit is a 16-bits multiplier which calculates the
log likelihood ratio (LLR) Aand 2yt /2, wher2eyo and
represent the received word and the variance of the channel noise, respectively
To reduce the delay time of the multiplier, we employed a four-stage pipelined structure which is composed of a threestage carry save adder (CSA) and a carry propagation adder
(CPA).
B. IBUF, X-Memory and OBUF units
Our LDPC decoder works simultaneously on three consecutive received words. While one received word is being iteratively decoded, the next received word is loaded into the decoder and the tentative LDPC code word of the previous received word is read out from the decoder.
The X-Memory unit stores LLRs which are used for LDPC decoding and the IBUF unit buffers LLRs for the next received word. The OBUF unit buffers the tentative LDPC code word.
