A Channel Decoder Implemented by CMOS Analog Circuits in Digital Communication System

Shuhui Yang, Xuehua Li and Yafei Wang College of Photoelectricity Information and Communication Engineering
Beijing Information Science and Technology University huishuyang@sina.com

Abstract
To realize low power channel decoder in digital communication system, basing on the a-posteriori probability algorithm, the paper fabricates an analog probability decoder of trellis code by using CMOS analog circuits. The decoding performance is given. When the SNR is over 4.8dB, for 950KHz input signal, the analog decoder’s BER is zero. If the input signal is 6MHz,the BER will be about 10-4.The highest speed of the decoder can be up to 20MHz. Simulation results also show that the analog decoder decreases at least one order of magnitude in power consumption and chip area at the same rate compared with the digital decoder. The design method is also suitable for implementing the analog decoders of Turbo code and LDPC code.
1. Introduction
Error control codes are generally used in digital communication system to overcome the channel noise. Usually, the channel decoder is implemented by digital circuits. While the digital circuits are suitable for algebra decoding not fit for the probability decoding. In fact, fabricating the Viterbi decoder requires more chip area than that of BCH decoder at the same bit rate. This situation becomes more serious in decoding for the high-performance codes, such as, Turbo code and LDPC code, which will help the communication system approach the Shannon limit. For these codes, the decoding algorithms commonly contain probability calculating and iterative process, which need much computing power. If we still use the traditional digital circuits to realize the decoding process, the decoder will be very complex. So, it is necessary to research new design technique for fabricating the channel decoders of Turbo code and LDPC code.
Utilizing analog circuits to implement the channel decoder is one of the new research methods in recent ______________________________________ 978-1-4244-2179-4/08/$25.00 ©2008 IEEE

years. Compared with the digital decoder, the analog decoder consumes much less power and needs smaller chip area [1][2].
Basing on the a-posterior probability algorithm (APP), this paper designs an analog probability decoder of (5,2,3) trellis code by using 0.6ȝm analog CMOS technique. The decoder mainly includes three parts: two-level pipeline input interface circuits, probability computing network circuits and output interface circuits. The decoding performance is given. When SNR is over 4.8dB, for 950KHz input signal, there is no error in the output of the decoder. When the input signal is 6MHz, the BER is about 10-4.The maximum speed of the decoder can be up to 20MHz. Simulation results also show that, compared with the decoder designed by digital circuits, the analog decoder reduces at least one order of magnitude in power consumption and chip area for the same speed.
2. Analog probability XOR gate circuit
CMOS circuit commonly exhibits low power
characteristic, and its work current is in microampere
level. While, if the gate-source voltage is lower than the
threshold voltage, the MOS transistor will not turn off
immediately, but get into subthreshold mode, and the
work current will decrease to under microampere.
Meanwhile, there will be an exponential relationship
between drain current and gate-source voltage of the
MOS transistor, like that in BJT. Employing the
exponential characteristic, we can fabricate analog
current mode multiplier. This paper changes the
traditional multiplier and presents an analog probability
XOR gate circuit, as depicted in Figure 1.

4. System structure of the Analog Decoder

Before decoding, it is necessary for the traditional channel decoder implemented by digital circuits to convert the analog signal to the digital form by hard decision circuits. And there will be ADC and DAC in the interface circuits. The system structure of the analog decoder is plotted in Figure 4, which utilizes some probability gate circuits similar as the analog probability XOR gate. The analog decoder consists of three parts: input interface circuits, analog probability decoding circuit and output interface circuits. There are no ADC and DAC in the interface circuits, and the decoding circuit does not contain logic gates and digital memory cells. The output of the analog decoder is digital signal which can be directly sent to the subsequent digital circuits.

5. Decoding Performance

The modulation signal with encoded digital information will be disturbed, if it is transported in the high AWGN channel, which makes the demodulated “soft-bit” information yield error bits. The “soft-bit” information is analog signal. When the “soft-bit” signal is sent into the channel decoder, its form is not impulse square wave but the signal with cosine forming. Using HSPICE, this paper makes the simulation examination for the analog decoder. The a-posterior probabilities of the two effective information sequences which are output of the analog decoding circuit are shown in Figure 5 and Figure 6, respectively.

After the probability decoding output signals are

sent to and decided by the current comparators, the two

effective information sequences can be correctly

decoded: x1=110101 and x3=010011. More simulation results show that when the SNR is higher than 4.8dB,

for the 950kHz input signal, the output will have no

error. If the input signal is 6MHz, the BER will be about 10-4. With 5V work voltage, for the 5MHz input

signal, the total power consumption of the analog

decoder is 2.957mW. The maximum speed of the

analog decoder will be up to 20MHz.

If we use digital circuits to realize the a-posterior

probability decoder, it will consume much more power

and occupy much more chip area than that in the

analog decoder. In reality, we can use the 5-bit adder

and multiplier which have low precision and high

speed to realize the probability decoding circuit. A gate

circuit can be defined as the basic cell in digital circuits.

Thus, finishing a 5-bit full adder needs approximately

30 gates and a multiplier needs about 110 gates.

Assuming the delay of each gate is same and every

node load capacitor is 0.1pF, then the power

dissipation of the CMOS circuits can be calculated by

the following formula:

¦ p = Ci ×Vi2 × fi

Where, Ci is the node load capacitor, Vi is the voltage swing of the node, and fi is the node turning frequency.

When the work voltage is 5V and the input data rate is 5Mbit/s, the adder will need about 0.375mW to finish one time addition and the multiplier need approximately 1.375mW. To finish the a-posterior probability decoding of (5,2,3) trellis code needs 32 times multiplication and 4 times addition, then, the total dissipation of the probability decoding comes to:
1.375mW × 32 + 0.375mW × 4 =45.5mW. If we
consider the input and output interface circuits and the storage cells, the dissipation will increase by 2 times. Hence, from the theory, for same speed, the power consumption of the analog decoder will decrease at least one order degree than that of the digital decoder.
Moreover, the probability decoding process requires the multiplication and addition be made at the same time, which needs 32 multipliers and 4 adders. Hence, the probability computing circuits of digital decoder will occupy at least one order degree chip area more than that of analog decoder.
6. Conclusion
This paper designs the decoder of (5,2,3) trellis code by CMOS analog circuits and gives the test performance. The simulation results show that, compared with the digital decoder, the analog decoder reduces at least one order degree in respects of power dissipation and chip area. The design method also can be used to implement the analog decoders of Turbo code and LDPC code.
