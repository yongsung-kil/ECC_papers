I. INTRODUCTION
The computational complexity of encoding for Low density parity check codes is focused on communications and channel coding. Recently, the standards of WiFi, DVB-S2 and WiMax have referred LOPC codes. An efficient encoding by the parity check matrices is proposed by Richardson and Urbanke[l]. They were success to make codeword without the generate matrices. Richardson's encoding matrix decomposes six sub-
matrices. The complexity of matrix D is focused
on many works in [4][6] which has remark ¢ = I

Richardson's encoding is O(n+ g2) . Recently,
[5] targets on the matrix T and its inverse in order
to reduce complexity. In this paper, our goals are to

approach low complexity encoding of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Code by using Circulant Permutation Matrices(CPM) [2] for high-rate and fast communication systems. We propose two encoding
scheme. First approaches T = E =1 and restricts
D consisting of dual diagonal matrices and second
makes T = ¢ = 1 without cycle-4, so we achieve
reducing the complexity to O(n) . This paper is
organized as follows. We simply review QC-LOPC Codes and efficient encoding scheme in section 2. In section 3 and section 4, we proposed our low complexity encoding scheme I and II respectively. In section 5, we showed numerical results and compared proposed encoding with [5] and various LOPC. Last section presented remarks and conclusions.
