NEW SIMPLIFIED SUM-PRODUCT ALGORITHM FOR LOW COMPLEXITY LDPC DECODING

Myung Hun Lee, Jae Hee Han and Myung Hoon Sunwoo

School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Ajou University San 5, Wonchun-Dong, Yeungtong-Gu Suwon, 443-749 Korea Tel : 82-31-219-2390 Fax : 82-31-212-9531 e-mail : sunwoo@ajou.ac.kr

ABSTRACT
This paper proposes new simplified sum-product (SSP) decoding algorithm to improve BER performance for lowdensity parity-check codes. The proposed SSP algorithm can replace multiplications and divisions with additions and subtractions without extra computations. In addition, the proposed SSP algorithm can simplify both the ln[tanh(x)] and exp[tanh-1(x)] by using two quantization tables which can reduce tremendous computational complexity. Moreover, the simulation results show that the proposed SSP algorithm can improve about 0.3~0.8 dB of BER performance compared with the existing modified sumproduct algorithm.
Index Terms — iterative decoding, sum-product algorithm, forward error correction, LDPC, IEEE 802.11n standards
1. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes were proposed by Gallager in the early 1960s [1], however, they were forgotten until the 1990s. LDPC codes were rediscovered by MacKay and Neal in 1996 [2]. LDPC codes achieving close to the Shannon limit performance among the forward error correction (FEC) systems [2] have been adopted by the advanced standards, such as Digital Video Broadcasting – Satellite – Second Generation (DVB-S2) [3], WLAN (IEEE 802.11n) [4] and WiMAX (IEEE 802.16e). In DVB-S2, the LDPC code is incorporated with adaptive coding and modulation (ACM) and variable coding and modulation (VCM) techniques and it is optimized high order modulation schemes for satellite signals, such as the amplitude phase shift keying (APSK). The result typically shows a 30% capacity increase over Digital Video Broadcasting – Satellite (DVB-S) under the same transmission conditions [3].

Research works on LDPC decoding algorithms have focused on reducing computational complexity, decreasing the number of iterations and achieving better BER performance. The sum-product (SP) algorithm, also known as the belief propagation algorithm, presents the highest BER performance [5]. In contrast, the min-sum (MS) algorithm aims for reducing computational complexity [6].
In this paper, we propose a simplified sum-product (SSP) decoding algorithm that can concurrently simplify both ln[tanh(x)] and exp[tanh-1(x)] functions. The proposed algorithm can minimize the performance degradation compared with the existing approximation algorithm [7]. Moreover, the proposed algorithm can replace multiplications and divisions with additions and subtractions, and thus, it can dramatically reduce computational complexity compared with the existing SP algorithms [5, 7].
This paper is organized as follows. Section 2 explains the SP algorithm and the modified SP algorithm. Section 3 describes the proposed SSP algorithm. Section 4 presents performance evaluations. Finally, Section 5 concludes the paper.

Both the SP algorithm [5] and the modified SP algorithm [7] require multiplications and divisions to update the check nodes. In contrast, the proposed SSP algorithm requires additions and subtractions instead of multiplications and divisions for the check node updates. In general, in terms of hardware complexity and delay, both multiplication and division are much more complicated and longer than addition and subtraction.

We have verified the proposed SSP algorithm and the existing SP algorithms [5, 7] to all of 12 LDPC codes in the WLAN (IEEE 802.11n) standard. 5. CONCLUSIONS

In this paper, we proposed new SSP decoding algorithm for LDPC codes. The proposed algorithm can replace both multiplications and divisions by additions and subtractions adding the logarithm and exponential functions in the check node updates. Two quantization tables which consist of six regions and six quantization values have been selected to compute the ln[tanh(x)] and exp[tanh-1(x)], respectively. Therefore, the proposed SSP algorithm can update the check nodes without computing the ln[tanh(x)] and exp[tanh-1(x)] functions. In addition, the proposed SSP algorithm can significantly reduce the computational complexity compared with the existing SP algorithms [5, 7] and it achieves less BER performance degradation compared with the existing modified SP algorithm [7]. The simulation results show that the proposed SSP algorithm improves maximum of 0.8 dB BER performance compared with the modified SP algorithm [7].
