A Novel Construction Scheme with Linear Encoding Complexity for LDPC Codes

Dan Xia, Huan He, Youyun Xu, Yueming Cai
Department of Wireless Communication Institute of Communications Engineering PLAUST
Nanjing China morseding@yahoo.com.cn

Abstract—Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes can achieve better performance and lower decoding complexity than Turbo codes and other error control codes. But it is difficult for LDPC codes to be commercial because of their high encoding complexity and consequent encoding delay . Using an efficient encoding method proposed by Richardson and Urbanke, we can efficiently reduce the LDPC encoding complexity apparently to the linear complexity, but the encoding delay becomes much longer because of the forward substition operation which can only be performed serially. In this paper, we consider the LDPC encoding method combined with the construction of H matrix to construct the LDPC codes with linear encoding complexity, which we named as LEC-LDPC(linear encoding complexity LDPC) codes. The encoding complexity of the LEC-LDPC codes is exactly linear due to the inverse operation been omitted, and we can remove the forward substition operation to greatly decrease the encoding latency.
Keywords-linear encoding complexity, encoding latency, forward substition operation, inverse operation

I. INTRODUCTION

LDPC Codes are a class of linear error-correcting codes, and they can be denoted by the parity check matrix H [1] [2]. Generally, it is necessary to encode with a generator matrix G which is transformed form the parity check matrix H . As the generator matrix G isn’t as sparse as the parity check matrix H , the complexity of encoding is o(n2 ) , and n denotes

the block length. At present, the block length n = 104 ~ 105 is

applied in general. Thus, the complexity of coding with

generator matrix G is too high. The way of coding that used

mostly at present is encoding with a specified parity check

matrix H with a low triangular shape which was proposed by

Richardson and UrBanke(named as RU algortithm)[3].

According to their method, they transformed the parity check

matrix H to a low triangular shape as figure 1, and partitioned

it into 6 parts. Suppose that the vector of message block is s ,

and the vector of check bits is p1 , p2 , so the vector of the codeword after encoding is

u = (s, p1, p2 )

According to the RU algorithm:

p1T = −Φ−1(−ET −1 A + C)sT

Where Φ = −ET −1B + D . And we can encode the message

blocks by the parity check matrix H directly. After some

rational step, we can reduce the complexity of encoding

to o(n + g 2 ) , where g is the size of the submatrix.

When g << n , the complexity of encoding with RU
algorithm is o(n) approximately. However, it’s always
independent between construction and encoding of LDPC codes, and it’s difficult to pay attention to the size of g while
constructing the parity check matrix H . Especially for the regular LDPC codes, too much consideration to the size of g
will influence the performance of the LDPC codes constructed. Based on the analyses above, we propose a novel
construction scheme with liner complexity for LDPC codes by considering the construction and encode of the LDPC codes together. This scheme is suit for the matrix with odd weight of column. By utilizing the character of the permutation matrix, we can construct the parity-check matrix novelly, and make the complexity of encoding independent to the size of g , which is
always o(n) .

III. THE CONSTRUCTION OF CHECK MATRIX
According to equation (2) and (3) we know that most of the complexity and delaying of encoding is due to the computation of Φ−1 and T −1 . As Φ = −ET −1B + D , so the construction of submatrix E, B, D, and T have a great effect on the complexity of encoding. Therefore, we divide the check matrix
H to two parts, namely H = [ Ha Hb ] . Suppose there are m
rows and n columns in the check matrix H , and the weight of each row is b , the weight of each column is a(b > a) . The
size of Hb is m × (n − m) , the weight of each row is b1 = b − a , the weight of each column is a . We will investigate the construction of submatrix Hb mainly.

A. The construction of matrix Hb

According to the RU algorithm, they preprocessed

submatrix T to a low triangle form. By way of reducing the

size of the dense matrix Φ−1 , they enlarged submatrix T as

much as possible. And for the sake of reducing computation

complexity, they replaced computation of T −1 by the forward

substitution operation. However, the forward substitution

operation can only function with single thread processing,

which obviously increased the delaying of encoding.

Therefore, we set a permutation matrix to the submatrix T , so

T −1 = T T , then it is no need to preprocess submatrix T to a

low triangle form, and there will be no delaying from forward

substitution operation neither. This is propitious to process with

multithread processing.

For Φ−1 , we can treat it like T −1 . And for the sake of

convenience, we will construct Φ to an identity matrix I with

a special way. Since Φ = −ET −1B + D , and T −1 = T T , thus

B. The procedure of constructing Hb
According to the analysis above, the entire steps of constructing Hb are as follow:
Step 1: Based on the weight of column a , construct a permutation matrices with size p × p randomly, which are treated as submatrix T and Ei (1 ≤ i ≤ (a −1)) respectively. And make sure that Ei (1 ≤ i ≤ (a −1)) accord with constraint condition 2. And the value of q is lie on the size of check matrix.
Step 2: Construct Bj (1 ≤ j ≤ (a −1)) using equation (11). Step 3: Construct Dij (1 ≤ i ≤ a −1,1 ≤ j ≤ a −1) using equation (10). Following the three steps above, we obtain the matrix Hb2 , ts size is ap × ap , the weight of columns and rows are a , meet the equation T −1 = T T , Φ = I , avoid computing the inverse of matrices and the forward substitution operation. Thus the complexity of encoding is reduced.

V. CONCLUSIONS
This paper proposes a noval construction scheme with liner complexity for LDPC codes. By exploiting the characteristics of the permutation matrices, the encoding computation and complexity can be reduced to a larger extent since the inverse operation and the forward substitution operation in the encoding which can shorten the encoding latency can be avoided.
