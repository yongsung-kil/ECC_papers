Two Algebraic Methods for Constructing Efficiently Encodable Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes

Keke Liu, Zesong Fei, Jingming Kuang
RCDCT, Modern Communication Lab, Dept. of E.E. Beijing Institute of Technology Beijing, P. R. China
liukeke498.student@sina.com, feizesong@bit.edu.cn

Abstract—In this paper, we present two algebraic methods for constructing Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes. The first method is based
on the quadratic residues modulo p (where p is a prime), the
second method is based on the multiplicative inverses in finite fields. The codes constructed based on these methods are efficiently encodable and have a girth at least 6. Simulation results show that with SPA decoding, the constructed codes based on our methods perform very well over the AWGN channel compared to some other types of famous codes, such as random Mackay code or extended EG-LDPC code recommended by NASA.
Keywords-Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes; Quadratic Residues; multiplicative inverses in finite fields
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes, first discovered by Gallager [1] in 1962 and rediscovered by Mackay in 1990’s [2,3], have caught the attention of Channel Coding Field due to its near-capacity performance, and the constructions of LDPC codes has been a focal point of research in LDPC codes. Recently, constructions of Quasi-Cyclic(QC) LDPC codes has caused wide concern because QC-LDPC codes enable linear encoding complexity[4] as opposed to the quadratic complexity in computer-generated random LDPC codes [5].
A large amount of research has shown that algebraic tools are very effective in constructing QC-LDPC codes with excellent performances. The greatest advantage of constructions of LDPC codes using algebraic tools is that the short cycles in the Tanner graphs [6], especially cycles of length 4 which have the most harmful effect on the code performance with iterative decoding, can be naturally avoided, so that the tanner graph of constructed code has a girth at least 6. Many algebraic tools have been successfully applied to construct QC-LDPC codes, such as the points, lines, or hyperplanes of finite geometries [7,8], and the additive subgroups, multiplicative subgroups, or the primitive elements of finite fields, and so forth [9,10,11].
In this paper, we present two new algebraic methods for constructing Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes. The first method is based on the quadratic residues modulo p (where p is a prime), the second method is based on the multiplicative

inverses in finite fields. The codes constructed based on these
methods are efficiently encodable and have a girth at least 6. It is shown that with SPA decoding [3], the codes based on these
methods given in examples perform very well compared to some types of well-known codes, such as randomly
constructed Mackay codes and extended EG-LDPC code recommended by NASA for high-speed satellite
communications. The comparison made between our code and corresponding random Mackay code shows that our code
given in the example has a lower error floor than corresponding Mackay code.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II is a general description of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices and gives a necessary condition for existence of cycles of length 4 in QC-LDPC codes, which is very important for eliminating cycles of length 4. In Section III and Section IV, two new algebraic methods for constructing QC-LDPC codes are presented, respectively. Section V concludes this paper.

III. A CLASS OF QUASI-CYCLIC LDPC CODES BASED ON QUADRATIC RESIDUES MODULO A PRIME

A. Quadratic Residues
For a given integer q , if there exists an integer x , such that x2 = q(mod p) , we call q a quadratic residue modulo p , where p is a positive integer. If p is a prime, we can define quadratic residue over the Galois Field GF ( p) . It is known that for a prime p , the set of integers, {0,1,..., p −1} , forms a field GF ( p) under modulo- p addition and multiplication. Consequently, for a given element q in GF ( p) , if there exists an element x such that x2 = q ,where the power is

defined over GF ( p) , we call q a quadratic residue in
GF ( p) .
B. Construction of QC-LDPC Codes base on Quadratic Residues modulo a prime
Let p be a prime, then consider the p × p matrix P(1) over GF ( p) , shown in (4) at the bottom of this page, where all the operations are defined over GF ( p) .The matrix P(1) has the following structural properties: 1) All the elements in P(1) are quadratic residues in GF ( p) ; 2) Due to the fact that there are only ( p + 1) / 2 distinct quadratic residues in all, not all the elements in a row, or a column of P(1) are distinct. Replacing each element pi, j in P(1) by its corresponding circulant permutation matrix I ( pi, j ) with size p × p , we obtain a p × p array of p × p circulant permutation matrices, denoted by H(1) . The tanner graph of H(1) has a girth at least 6, which is guaranteed by the following theorem.
Theorem 2: The tanner graph of H(1) is free of cycles of length 4.
Proof: In this proof, all the operation are defined over
