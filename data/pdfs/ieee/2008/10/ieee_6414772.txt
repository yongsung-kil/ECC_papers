Construction of Good Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes Based on the row vectors of generator matrix

Lingjun Kong Yang Xiao

Institute ofinformation Science, Beijing Jiaotong University Beijing 100044, P. R. China E-mail: kljangeI401@163.com

Abstract: The existing construction of Quasi-Cyclic low-density parity-check (short for QC-LDPC) codes has not considered the problems of small stopping sets and small girth and small minimum code weight together, while their existences will lead to the BER performance of QC-LOPC codes to be much poorer than that of randomly constructed LOPC codes even decoding failure. To solve the problem, some theorems of the QC-LOPC codes without small stopping sets and small girth were proposed first. Then good QC-LOPC codes without small stopping sets and small girth were designed based on the row vectors of generator matrix. The small minimum code weight also can be prevented due to the new algorithm based on the row vectors of generator matrix, which is the new way to choose the shifted factors to construct the good QC-LOPC codes for the given prime. The effectiveness and the practicability of the algorithm are demonstrated by the simulation results. It is significant for us to analyze and optimize the design of LOPC codes. Key words: Quasi-Cyclic low-density parity-check codes, stopping set, stopping distance, girth, minimum code weight
I. INTRODUCTIONS
Low-density parity-check (LOPC) codes [1] have attracted considerable attention, because they can achieve near-capacity performance with iterative message-passing decoding on the codes' Tanner graph [2-3] and sufiiciently long block sizes. QC-LOPC codes are a class of important structured LOPC codes and manifest excellent performance under iterative decoding. The algebraic structure of the codes makes them particularly suitable for high-speed V LSI implementation [4-10].
It is well-known [11] that the performance of LOPC codes over the BEC channel is determined by certain combinatorial objects called stopping sets which also play the important role over the AWGN channel because of the cycles they have. With iterative decoding, the bad performance of the codes is due primarily to the small stopping sets that LOPC codes have [12-13].Although the stopping sets and girth are studied in many papers [9-10],[14], there is no efiicient algorithm to construct QC-LOPC codes without small stopping sets and small girth.
To find the minimum weight computation of LOPC codes is a NP problem, so we do not consider the LOPC codes which have the small minimum weight when designed them. whose existences will lead to the BER performance of QC-LOPC codes to be much poorer.*
To solve the above problems, this paper proposes some theorems of the QC-LOPC codes without small stopping sets and small girth. Then good QC-LOPC codes were designed based on the row vectors of generator matrix. For the given
prime m, how to choose the shifted factors a and b to

VI. CONCLUSIONS
According to those proposed theorems and corollaries, we can design good QC-LDPC codes without small stopping sets and small gilih. The small minimum code weight also can be prevented due to the new algorithm based on the row vectors of generator matrix, which is the new way to choose the shifted factors to construct the good QC-LDPC codes for the given prime. The simulation results show that the QC-LDPC codes can effectively avoid specified stopping sets and small girth and prevent the small minimum code weight due to the proposed algorithm.
