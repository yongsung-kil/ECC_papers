Constructing Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes For Efficient Encoding Based on Fast Gauss Elimination

Yuan Jinsha, Chen Zhixiong
School of Electrical & Electronic Engineering North China Electric Power University Baoding, Hebei, P.R.China, 071003
yuanjinsha@126.com, chenzx1983@sohu.com

Abstract—In this paper, a fast gauss eliminating algorithm for parity-check matrices of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes is proposed firstly. Enlightened by the gauss elimination, some Quasi-Cyclic square matrices with full rank are constructed. By proper matrix extension based on the full rank square matrices, we construct a kind of parity-check matrices whose column weights are either two or three. It is suitable for efficient encoding procedures in [7] with a linear complexity. Simulation results show a better performance compared to array based LDPC codes and LDPC codes in 802.11n standard.
Keywords- Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes; encoding; gauss elimination; rank
I. INTRODUCTION
Because of their capacity-achieving performance and capability of parallel decoding structure, LDPC codes which were introduced by Gallager [1] and rediscovered by Mackey[2] have recently attracted an ever increasing amount of attention, considered as next-generation error-correcting codes for telecommunication. They have been recently adopted in several standards: Wireless Local Area Network (IEEE 802.11n)[3] and Metropolitan Area Network (802.16e)[4] etc.
Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes[5,6], which is a kind of structured LDPC codes, are easy to implement because of their block and cyclic interconnections. As generating matrices are also Quasi-Cyclic[7], the required memory to store QC-LDPC codes for both encoders and decoders, can be reduced by a factor 1/L, when L×L circulant permutation matrices are employed. Moreover, QC-LDPC codes have encoding advantages over pseudo-random LDPC codes as they can be encoded using simple shift-registers, with probable complexity linearly proportional to their code length[7][8].
A fast encoding algorithm with linear complexity is proposed in [8], but it only suits a special class of QC-LDPC codes with dual diagonal structure of their parity-check matrices H. In [7], more general efficient encoding methods for QC-LDPC codes are presented, but for given parity-check matrices H, it is difficult to find their rank or search full rank square matrices in H. In this paper, we firstly proposed a fast gauss elimination for QC-LDPC codes. Then a novel method to construct parity-check matrices for QC- LDPC codes with

full rank is proposed, which adapts to the encoding algorithm in [7]. The outline of the paper is as follows.
In Section Τ , basic concepts of QC- LDPC codes are reviewed. In Section Υ, a fast gauss eliminating algorithm based on grouping cyclic shift and block elimination is proposed. In Section Φ, we present a method to construct systematic QC-LDPC codes. The performance of new QCLDPC codes is investigated by simulation in SectionΧ and concluding remarks are given in Section Ψ.

III. FAST GAUSS ELIMINATION BASED ON GCS
The Gauss elimination is used to eliminate a parity-check matrix of LDPC codes into an upper trigonal form based on module two operations. Then by proper matrix transposition, the systematic generating matrix for the sake of linear systematic encoding is get. For LDPC codes with a paritycheck matrix H(L,m,n) that has full rank, (L×m) times of module 2 operations are needed in order to get the generating matrix by gauss elimination.

The parity-check matrix of QC-LDPC codes is made up of
CP- matrices whose row element is the cyclic shift of the other
row element. It is difficult to ensure the full rank property of parity-check matrices. For example, if m is even, the rank of H(L,m, n) for array LDPC codes [10] is not full affirmatively. Because if the other rows of H(L,m, n) are added up to the first row, then the first row is transformed into a zero vector. Besides, if the rank of H(L,m, n) is full, to find a Quasi-Cyclic square matrix with full rank by gauss elimination is
complicated

In this section, we introduce a fast gauss elimination
algorithm which is suitable for QC-LDPC codes. For simple notation, we introduce some definitions and notation. The S
bits row grouping cyclic shift (GCS, for short) is defined as the upwards cyclic shift of the ith row grouping in H for S bits. VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, a fast gauss elimination based on row GCS and block elimination for parity-check of QC-LDPC codes are proposed. Then a quasi-cyclic square matrix with full rank for systematic encoding is constructed utilizing the novel gauss elimination. By proper matrix extension and exponent locating algorithm, we construct linearly encodable QC-LDPC codes whose column weight is near three, which adapt to the encoding procedure in [7]. Compared to array based LDPC codes and LDPC codes in standard 802.11n, the proposed QCLDPC codes perform a better performance.
