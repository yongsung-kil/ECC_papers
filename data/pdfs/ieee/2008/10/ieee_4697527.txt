Abstract
This paper reveals that the current LDPC codes in DVB-S2 are not good, since they have the codes of minimum weight of 5, which leads to their BER can not satisfy the requirements of DVB-S2. This paper develops an algorithm to estimate the minimum weight of LDPC codes based on generator matrices. Using it, we can find the minimum weight problem of the current LDPC codes in DVB-S2. To enhance the minimum weight problem of the LDPC codes, we propose a new approach to construct the encodable irregular QC codes, which can not produce new short girths and can have much larger minimum weight and minimum distance of the codes. Simulations verify the construction of alternative QC LDPC codes for DVB-S2 to be valid, since better BER performance than that of existing irregular LDPC codes of DVB-S2 is obtained. Index Terms—DVB-S2, channel coding, circulant matrices, low-density parity-check codes, encodable quasi-cyclic codes.

The LDPC codes are standardized with considering both performance and encoding complexity. In general, encoding complexity of LDPC codes is high [4-7]. To solve the problem, DVB-S2 adopts a staircase lower triangular submatrix in their parity check matrices to let encoding to be simple. However, this paper shows that it is the staircase lower triangular submatrix to result in DVB-S2’s codes of minimum weight of 5 or 6, which leads the poor BER performance of the codes. Thus, it is impossible to reach the specifications of FER of DVBS2 for given code rate and SNR. This paper develops a tool for the minimum weight test of LDPC codes. To solve the poor BER needs to enhance the minimum weight test of LDPC codes. This paper proposes two new constructions of encodable irregular LDPC codes to have large minimum weights, one refers QC LDPC codes, the other refers good random LDPC codes, and the proposed LDPC codes can replace those in DVB-S2.
2. The minimum weightproblem of DVD-S2 LDPC codes

1. Introduction
DVB-S2 is the second-generation specification for satellite broadband applications, such as TV and sound broadcasting, internet access, and professional services like TV contribution links and digital satellite news gathering [1-3]. The first-generation specification, DVB-S, uses QPSK modulation and concatenated convolutional and Reed-Solomon (RS) codes. DVB-S2 employs LDPC codes that try to realize 35% throughput increase compared to DVB-S. In broadcasting like DVB-S2, ARQ is not a practical solution. Thus, generally, error correcting codes with large code length are used. The LDPC codes standardized in DVB-S2 are tens of thousands bits long.

2.2 Minimum Weight Test

The other important properties of encodable irregular QC codes are the minimum weight and the minimum distance. At high SNRs, the maximumlikelihood decoding performance of an error-correcting code is dominated by the code’s minimum weight and minimum distance. Ref. [5] shows that the row vectors
of generator matrix G belong to the code set {cm} , and
it is necessary for LDPC codes to have large minimum
weight of row vectors of G . Many QC codes without 4girths, but the minimum weight of row vectors of G is
lower, which lead to them not obtain good BER property. The algorithm of estimating the minimum weight of
LDPC codes is as following [10].
Step 1: Given the parity-check matrix H , obtain generator matrix G from (9), and calculate the minimum
weight of row vectors of G , and it is wmin (g) .
Step 2: Construct a decoder of Set K to be a larger integer.
Step 3: Resulting in a code cn of the parity-check
matrix H ; Step 4: From i=1 to wmin (g) , input the decoder a
sequence with continuous “1s” of number i and the other to be zeros; if the decoder solves the sequence as a all zero code then i=i+1, else the tested codes have an estimated minimum weight of 2(i-1)+1, which is a tight upper bound the real minimum weight.
If in step 4, we have not found the estimated minimum weight of 2(i-1)+1, the estimated minimum
weight will be wmin (g) .
The details of the algorithm and related theorem can be found in [10].

4. Conclusions
Two code constructions based on circulant matrices and random structure for the DVB-S2 LDPC codes has been proposed. The good properties of girth, minimum weight and BER of the constructed LDPC codes can be ensured by the proposed test theorem and algorithm. Simulations show that the proposed codes perform much better BER than those of existing DVB-S2 LDPC codes of same block length and code rate.
