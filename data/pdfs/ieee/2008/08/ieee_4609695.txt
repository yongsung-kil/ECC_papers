2008 ISECS International Colloquium on Computing, Communication, Control, and Management

A Construction of Linearly Encodable QC-LDPC Codes by Grouping Cyclic Shift and Block Elimination
Chen Zhixiong, Yuan Jinsha School of Electrical & Electronic Engineering North China Electric Power University，Baoding, Hebei, 071003, China chenzx1983@sohu.com yuanjinsha@126.com

Abstract
In this paper, the girth and rank property based on grouping cyclic shift of a parity-check matrix is proved firstly. Then a Quasi-Cyclic square matrix with full rank is constructed by grouping cyclic shift and block elimination. By proper matrix extension, we construct a parity-check matrix based on the square matrix whose column weight is close or equal to three, which is suitable for efficient encoding procedure in [3][4][11] with a near linear complexity. Simulation results show a better performance compared to array based LDPC codes and LDPC codes in 802.11n standard.
1. Introduction
Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes[1], kinds of structured LDPC codes, are easy to implement because of their block and cyclic interconnections while their performance is almost the same as general LDPC codes[2]. As generating matrices are also QuasiCyclic[3], the required memory to store QC-LDPC codes for both encoders and decoders, can be reduced by a factor 1/L, when L×L circulant permutation matrices are employed.
Moreover, QC-LDPC codes have encoding advantages over pseudo-random LDPC codes as they can be encoded using simple shift-registers, with probable complexity linearly proportional to their code length [3][4][10].The LDPC parity-check matrices in standards such as IEEE 802.11n[5] and IEEE 802.16e[6] are based on QC- LDPC codes whose parity-check matrices have dual diagonal structure with a single weight-3 column. The dual diagonal structure enables an efficient encoding of LDPC codes but may cause performance degradation due to their column weight restriction [7][8].
In this paper we propose a novel method to construct parity-check matrices for QC- LDPC codes with full rank and attractive column weight distribution. By proper matrix extension, we get a regular QC- LDPC codes with weight-3 columns which can be encoded efficiently. The outline of the paper is as follows.
In SectionⅡ, basic concepts of QC- LDPC codes are reviewed. In Section Ⅲ, cycle and rank properties based

grouping cyclic shift are analyzed. In Section Ⅳ , we present a method to construct linearly encodable QCLDPC codes. The performance of new QC- LDPC codes is investigated by simulation in SectionⅤand concluding remarks are given in Section Ⅵ.

4.2. Construction of matrix without small length cycles
Secondly, we construct a matrix H2 to form a whole parity-check matrix H for QC-LDPC codes that contains no length-four cycle but a grouping of L length-6 cycles which are brought by H1.The steps to construct
(n − m)column groupings are shown as follow:
Step 1）Initialization: Set E(H)=[E(H1) E(Q)], where
Q denotes a mL × (n − m)L zero matrix. Replace all the
exponents “∞”which denote zero CP-matrices in E(H) by a large number such as 99999. Initialize the variable j as (m+1);
Step 2) Replace three exponents “99999” in the jth column of E(H) by random three positive integers that are less than L;
Step 3) Among the front j column groupings of H, find all 4-block-cycle and 6-block-cycle that connect any block corresponding to the exponent in the jth column grouping of E(H). Whether the 4-block-cycle and 6block-cycle form any length-4 cycle or any length-6 cycle is checked. If contains, replace the exponent in the jth column grouping of E(H) that causes length-4 cycle or any length-6 cycle by other positive integer less than L and return to step 3). If not, go to step 4);
Step 4) Set j=j+1. If j<n+1, return to step 2). If not, save the matrix E(H). Thus the matrix extended by the last (n-m) column groupings of E(H) is H2.
4.3. Efficient encoding of the QC-LDPC codes proposed
The parity-check matrices of QC-LDPC codes are get by extending the exponents of E(H) into CP-matrices. Note that the girth of the QC-LDPC codes is six. In fact if we replace the CP-matrix in the second row grouping and the fourth column grouping by a zero matrix of the same size, then the girth is eight. Because the column weights are all three except a column grouping are two, the QCLDPC codes proposed is irregular.
Note that there is a square matrix with full rank, the QC-LDPC codes can be encoded by the method introduced in the third part of paper [3] with a linear encoding complexity proportional to the number of parity bits of the code for serial encoding, and to the length of the code for high-speed parallel encoding.

Then the code lengths are L×n and the code rates are not less than (1-m/n). 6. Conclusions
In this paper, a novel construction of QC-LDPC codes for linear encoding are proposed. We have proved that row or column GCS doesn’t change the girth property of QC-LDPC codes. A fast Gauss elimination based on row GCS and block elimination is demonstrated, which keeps the rank of a matrix. Then a quasi-cyclic square matrix with full rank for systematic encoding is constructed utilizing row GCS and the fast block elimination. By proper matrix extension and exponent locating algorithm, we construct linearly encodable QC-LDPC codes whose column weight is near three, which adapt to the encoding procedure in [3][4][11]. Compared to array based LDPC codes and LDPC codes in standard 802.11n, the proposed QC-LDPC codes perform a better performance.
