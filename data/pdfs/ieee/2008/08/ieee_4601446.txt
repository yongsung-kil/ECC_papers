Stability Analysis of an Improved Min-Sum Decoder
Mahdi Ramezani, Student Member, IEEE, Raman Yazdani, Student Member, IEEE, and Masoud Ardakani, Member, IEEE

Abstract‚ÄĒIt has been shown that under min-sum (MS) decoding, scaling the messages at the output of check nodes can improve the performance of regular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. However, for irregular codes designed for the sum-product decoder, linear scaling can hinder the performance. The problem of code design for MS and linear scaling min-sum (LSMS) decoders have been recently investigated. It is shown that the gap to the capacity for LSMS codes is better than MS codes, but compared to sum-product codes the gap is still considerable. In this letter, a modified MS decoding is proposed and studied. We use the stability analysis of density evolution to show that the proposed method allows for a larger fraction of edges connected to degree-2 variable nodes than LSMS codes. Finally, by designing codes based on the modified method, we show that compared to MS and LSMS codes, a smaller gap to the capacity can indeed be achieved while the complexity of decoding remains essentially the same.
Index Terms‚ÄĒLow-density parity-check (LDPC) code, linear scaling min-sum decoder, stability condition, irregular code.

L OW-density parity-check (LDPC) codes under the sumproduct (SP) decoding exhibit strong error correcting capabilities [1]. There are many other decoding algorithms proposed for LDPC codes among which min-sum (MS) is one of the most attractive ones. Compared to a SP decoder, a MS decoder has substantially lower complexity at the expense of some performance degradation. Therefore, improving the performance of MS decoder attracted much attention. As a case in point, it has been shown that for regular LDPC codes under MS decoding, if the messages are scaled down properly, the performance in terms of the gap to the capacity is improved [2], [3]. However, for irregular LDPC codes designed for SP decoder, which have very small gap to the capacity, scaling the messages may deteriorate the performance even worse than the MS decoder.
Authors in [4] design LDPC codes based on MS and linear scaling min-sum (LSMS) decoders. They show that LSMS codes exhibit smaller gaps to the capacity than MS codes, however, the gap is still large compared to SP codes.
