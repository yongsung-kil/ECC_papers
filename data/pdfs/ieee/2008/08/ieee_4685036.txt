Hybrid Decoding for One Class of Low-density Parity-check Codes
Guangwen Li, Xuelan Zou, Xin Wang School of Telecommunications and Information Engineering
Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications Nanjing, Jiangsu of China

Abstract- For high rate LDPC codes with light column weight and few redundant checks in their parity check matrix, the belief propagation (BP) decodings yield good error performance at the cost of high complexity. Aiming at seeking a effective decoder with good performance versus complexity tradeoff for this kind of LDPC codes, we present a new decoding framework which cascades a bit flipping (BF) variant with the ordered statistic decoding (OSD). During the iterative decoding of BF variant, the flipping value for each codeword bit is summed. The summation for each bit will be utilized as the reliability input for the OSD postprocessing, if no valid codeword is found after the maximum number of iterations for the BF variant is reached. With respect to the BP, simulation results show that the proposed BF-OSD achieves comparable performance with much less complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, invented originally by Gallager [1], has attracted great attention since the 1990s, after forgotten by more than 30 years. There are many variants of decoding algorithms designed for LDPC codes, among which are the hard-decision decoding [1] [2], the soft-decision decodings[3] and the hybrid decoding [4] [5] [6] [7]. The various decoding schemes offer different performance versus complexity tradeoffs. In practice, it is regarded that the belief propagation (BP) methods, belonging to the soft-decision decoding, yield the near most likelihood (ML) performance at the cost of high computational complexity. Unfortunately, in some applications where low complexity is at the highest priority, the hard-decision or hybrid decoding, such as bit flipping (BF) or its variants, is preferred to the soft-decision decoding, allowed for performance degradation to some extent.
Interestingly, for one class of LDPC codes, known as finite geometry (FG) LDPC codes, whose parity-check matrix has large column weight and many redundant rows, they are advocated for the feasibility of being encoded in linear time with feedback shift registers, which attributes to the cyclic or quasi-cyclic property of their parity check matrix. Moreover, the parallel BF variants, such as those mentioned in [8] [9] [10], could provide near performance as the BP decoding. However, for those high rate LDPC codes with light column weight and few redundant checks in their parity check matrix,

To fill such a performance gap, one way, as indicated throughout the paper, is to postprocess the decoding failures of the BF variant after extracting the most information from the iterative BF process.
In [6], the logarithmic likelihood ratio (LLR) value for each codeword bit is accumulated during the iterative BP decoding, then the OSD [11] [12] is invoked only for those BP decoding failures, with the accumulative value of each bit as the input to it. Enlightened by it, in our framework, the BF variant [10] is firstly applied on the received sequence. In the case that no valid codeword is found after the maximum number of iteration is reached, the accumulative flipping value for each bit during the iterative BF process is utilized as the input to the syndrome decoder [13] for postprocessing. Such configuration guarantees that the OSD is solicited once at most per sequence, the same with [6]. Therefore, the reduced complexity BF variant, when in cascade connection with low
complexity order-p OSD, assuming p :S 2, achieves a new

For ease of analysis, regular (dv, de) LDPC codes are assumed herein, where dv, de are column weight and row weight, respectively. For the PWBF, after ignoring the preprocessing operations required initially and a few integer operations involved in each iteration, the dominant cost is

IV. CONCLUSIONS
We put forward a BF-OSD cascade connection for one class of high rate short or medium length LDPC codes, with light column weight and few redundant check sums in their parity matrix, attempting to fill the performance gap between the BF and the BP decodings. The accumulative reliability of each codeword bit is measured by the sum of flipping value obtained in each iteration of the BF variant. Then the reliability information is employed in the order-p OSD. The validity of such a configuration has been justified by the simulation results.
