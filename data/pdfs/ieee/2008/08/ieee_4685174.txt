A decoding algorithm for low-density paritycheck codes in impulse noise environment

Zhijiang Xu , Limin Meng, Li Gang
College of Information Technology Zhejiang University of Technology
Hangzhou, China

KangWang
Basic Courses Department Zhejiang Police College Hangzhou, China

Abstract- The iterative message passing algorithm (MPA) for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is effective in the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels. However, the impulse interference is not negligible in many communication channels, such as power line channels, where the impulse interference greatly deteriorates the performance of MPA based on Gauss optimization. It is necessary to restudy the decoding for LDPC codes in impulse environment. We introduce symmetric alpha-stable (SaS) noise into a statistical model of impulse noise environment, and consider that interference in the receiver is a mixture of AWGN and SaS noise. Based on this noise model, we analyze the probability density function (PDF) of the mixed noise through the numerical calculation method, and propose a robust MPA (RMPA) to account for the impulse environment. The simulations show that the bit error rate (BER) performance of the proposed RMPA is robust.
Keywords - LDPC codes, symmetric alpha-stable, RMPA
I. INTRODUCTION
In many communication systems, LDPC codes proposed by Gallager [1] are widely used as channel coding. Many iterative decoding algorithms for LDPC codes, such as message-passing algorithm (MPA), and sum-product algorithm (SPA), are available by exchanging messages (probabilistic quantities) between check nodes and variable nodes in the Tanner Graph [2]. The performance of LDPC codes depends
not only on the check matrix H , but also on the
decoding algorithms. In the AWGN channels, the performance of the decoding algorithm equals to maximum a posteriori (MAP) approximately. The MPA in the AWGN channels is called the standard MPA (SMPA) in this paper.
In many communication channels, such as wireless indoor channels, acoustic channels, and power line channels, the observed noise exhibits Gaussian channel as well as impulsive characteristics, i.e., the receiving signal is corrupted by both additive Gaussian noise and impulsive noise.

Research shows that in such channels, although we use the pulse noise is not dominant, but if used SMPA algorithm directly, the performance deteriorated rapidly, prone to widely error floor [6].
Heavy-tailed distributions, which are wide used to model impulsive signals, assign relatively high probabilities to the occurrence of large deviations from the median. It has been suggested that the family of alpha-stable random variables provides useful models for impulsive phenomena [4].
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In section 2, a review of LDPC decoding algorithms in impulsive environment is provided. A robust decoding algorithm is proposed in section 3. Section 4 gives the results of simulations. Conclusions are drawn in the last part.

In the SuS noise with the same
characteristic index, if the ratio f increases, i.e. the
divergence ra increases, the number of big noise
increases too. On the one hand, if the sign of the big noise is same to that of BPSK signals, then it is helpful to decode correctly and low the BER. On the other hand, if the number of big noise increases, then the rest is the smaller noises due to the fact that the total power of the mixed noise is a constant. Variables nodes corrupted by those smaller noises can be decoded correctly with high probability. According to the decoding rules in the Tanner Graph, those variable nodes decoded correctly contribute to correct the variable nodes corrupted by big noises. V. CONCLUSION

In this paper the PDF of the mixture interference is derived via numerical method due to that the CF and the PDF of r. v. is a pair of Fourier transforms. Then a function is used to approximate the PDF of the real mixture interference. Based on the approximate PDF a modified robust MPA (RMPA) is proposed. Simulations show a good and robust performance under different mixture interference environments. However, our RMPA do need to know the GSNR as prior knowledge. Hence, how to estimate the GSNR in impulsive environment or a new algorithm without prior information is our further research.
