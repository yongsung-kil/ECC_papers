Construction of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes from Quadratic Congruences
Chun-Ming Huang, Jen-Fa Huang, and Chao-Chin Yang

Abstract— In this paper, we proposed a novel method for constructing quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes based on circulant permutation matrices via a simple quadratic congruential equation. The main advantage is that QC-LDPC codes with a variety of block lengths and rates can be easily constructed with no cycles of length four or less. Simulation results show that the proposed QC-LDPC codes perform slight better than the random regular LDPC codes for short to moderate block lengths and have almost the same performance as Sridara-Fuja-Tanner codes.
Index Terms— Circulant permutation matrices, quasi-cyclic (QC), low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes were originally invented by Gallager [1] in the early 1960s and have recently attracted wide attention in the coding community because they can achieve near Shannon limit performance with iterative message-passage decoding. A binary (j, k)regular LDPC code is defined as the null space of a sparse parity-check matrix H over GF(2), and satisfies the following properties: 1) each column has weight j; 2) each row has weight k; 3) no two rows (or two columns) have more than one 1-component in common; 4) both j and k are small compared to the code length. Most methods for designing good LDPC codes are based on random construction, but the lack of structure makes the encoding process complicated and the memory in the decoder required to store the parity-check matrices may be prohibitive in practical application.
Recently, several quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes are proposed to overcome these problems, and they can further be separated into two categories: one is based on finite geometries [2], and the other is based on circulant permutation matrices [3], [4]. Both of them can produce medium length codes with good minimum distance. Encoding can be done efficiently by using shift registers as far as hardware implementation is concerned. Both Sridhara-Fuja-Tanner (SFT) codes [3] and Fossorier codes [4] are QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices and have been widely investigated. In this paper, one new method to construct the QC-LDPC codes based on the circulant matrices is proposed, and these circulant matrices are permuted according to the one-coincidence sequences (OCSs). Here OCSs are defined as the multilevel sequences with the properties that any two different ones have at most one element in common [5, 6]. This simple method can be used to construct the (j, k)-regular QC-LDPC codes in variety of block lengths and rates with no 4-cycles. Since the QC-LDPC codes constructed by using these OCSs have similar performances, the OCSs obtained by a simple quadratic congruential equation in [5] are used as an exemplification in this paper.

IV. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we presented a new method for constructing QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices by using a simple quadratic congruential equation. The simulation results show that the proposed QC-LDPC codes perform as well as the random constructed LDPC codes at short to moderate block lengths and have almost the same performance as the SFT codes for different block lengths and rates. Moreover, the quasi-cyclic structure of the codes makes them suitable for practical applications.
