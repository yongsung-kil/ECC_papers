2008 5th International Symposium on Turbo Codes and Related Topics
Reduced-complexity Serial Min-sum Decoding
Hironori Uchikawa, Kohsuke Harada and Yasuhiko Tanabe
Research and Development Center, Toshiba Corp. Email: hironori.uchikawa at-mark toshiba.co.jp

Abstract—We propose a complexity-reducing method for serial min-sum decoding that reduces the number of check nodes to process during an iteration. The check nodes to skip are chosen based on the reliability, a syndrome and a log-likelihood-ratio (LLR) value, of the incoming messages. The proposed method is evaluated by computer simulations and shown to reduce the decoding complexity up to 70 % compared with conventional serial min-sum decoding with small fractional decibel degradation in error correction performance.
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes [1] have received significant attention recently. For many communications standards, it has been decided to use LDPC codes for forward error correction (FEC). As a result, it is important to reduce the complexity of LDPC decoding in order to implement it with low-cost hardware. Three main approaches to reducing the complexity have been studied: replacement of costly computations by simpler functions such as the min-sum algorithm [2], [3]; utilizing a scheduling technique, which changes a message updating order, with fast convergence (there are two main schedules involving a serial update of variable node’s [4] or check node’s messages [5]); and reducing the number of processed variable and check nodes per iteration [6], [7], [8]. Each approach has been investigated individually except for [9], in which a hybrid of the two last-mentioned approaches was studied. Although studies with the two last-mentioned approaches, including [9], have employed the costly sum-product algorithm as the check node computation, no attention has been paid to an algorithm for using all three approaches.
We propose a reduced-complexity decoding algorithm, which employs all three approaches. The concept of the algorithm, namely, updating a subset serially, is similar to the lazy scheduling in [9]. The proposed algorithm can, however, reduce memory requirements significantly by using the minsum algorithm, owing to its serial update of check node’s messages, as opposed to the lazy scheduling involving that of variable node’s messages. The proposed algorithm employs both the minimum magnitude log-likelihood-ratio (LLR) message and the syndrome of a check node, whereas LLR messages of variable nodes are utilized in the lazy scheduling.
The paper is organized as follows. Section II includes background on LDPC codes and their decoding. Section III introduces the proposed reduced-complexity decoding algorithm. Then simulation results for two different LDPC codes are presented in Section IV. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.

II. LDPC CODES AND THEIR DECODING A bi-partite factor graph with M check nodes and N variable nodes describes a binary (N, N −M) (dv, dc) LDPC code and is also represented by parity-check matrix H = [Hmn]. Let dv and dc denote the maximum numbers of ones in each column and row, and be called the degree of a variable node and a check node, respectively. In particular, every variable node has degree dv and every check node has degree dc in a (dv, dc)-regular LDPC code. A one in the parity-check matrix represents an edge between a variable node and a check node in the bi-partite graph. We denote the set of variable nodes that connect check node m by N (m) = {n : Hmn = 1}, and the set of check nodes that connect variable node n by M(n) = {m : Hmn = 1} . A belief propagation (BP) algorithm exchanging LLR messages along the edges is used for LDPC codes. The sumproduct algorithm, one of the BP algorithms, was described as a decoding algorithm for LDPC codes in Gallager’s thesis [1]. The min-sum algorithm approximates the computation at the check nodes with a simple minimum operation. Although the performance of the min-sum algorithm is slightly worse than that of the sum-product algorithm, the min-sum algorithm is hardware efficient. III. REDUCED-COMPLEXITY SERIAL MIN-SUM DECODING A. The observations of syndrome LLRs
A reliability of check node m is measured by a syndrome LLR representing the products of a syndrome S and the first minimum magnitude L1m into check node m in the proposed decoding algorithm, as opposed to the forced convergence method [7] that utilizes only magnitudes.
Fig. 1 demonstrates evolution of syndrome LLRs in the decoding of a (1008,504)(3,6)-regular LDPC code [12], where 10 syndrome LLRs of each frame are plotted. The simulation employed BPSK modulation over an AWGN channel of Eb/N0 = 1.5 dB. It can be seen that syndrome LLRs fluctuate during the first few iterations, and the LLRs of the decodable frames, 1 and 2, increase continually without bound when they surpass an arbitrary positive value. On the other hand, the LLRs of the undecodable frame 3 fluctuate and change polarity forever. The result allows us to skip updating the check nodes satisfying the condition that a syndrome LLR is positive and the magnitude of the syndrome LLR is large.

D. Decoder Architecture A decoder architecture with the reduced-complexity serial
min-sum decoding is depicted in Fig. 2. The decoder processes each check node serially. However the proposed algorithm can also be easily incorporated into a parallel type decoder. The Ln messages related to check node m are fetched from the Ln memory and Rmn messages obtained in the previous iteration are subtracted from the Ln messages to obtain the Qmn messages. The Qmn messages are transferred to the CNFU1, triplet values (Lm 1 , L2m, nL1 ) and sign S are obtained. The computations of the CNFUm1 are described at line 15 and 16 in Algorithm 1. The triplet (L1m, Lm 2 , nL1 ) and sign φmn obtained from S and sign(Qmn) are transmferred to CNFU2 to obtain the Rmn messages. The computation of the CNFU2 is also described at line 12 and 19 in Algorithm 1. In addition, update flag Um is obtained from L1m and the sign S for check node m according to lines from 12 to 14 in Algorithm 2. If the update flag Um is false, Ln messages related to the check node m are not fetched and proceeds the processing of check node m + 1.
This architecture requires saving (N + 3M + dc) values for the Ln, Lm 1 , Lm 2 , nL1 , and Qmn messages, and (E + M ) bits for the φmn and Ummdata. Note that E is the number of edges in a parity-check matrix. Additional memory provided by the proposed algorithm is only Um.
