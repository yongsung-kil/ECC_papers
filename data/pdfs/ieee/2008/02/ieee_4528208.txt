Large Girth Low-Density Parity-Check Codes for LongHaul High-Speed Optical Communications

Ivan B. Djordjevic1, Lei Xu2, Ting Wang2, and Milorad Cvijetic3
1University of Arizona, Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, 1230 E. Speedway Blvd., Tucson, AZ 85721, USA 2NEC Laboratories America, Princeton, NJ 08540, USA 3NEC Corporation America, Herndon, VA 20171, USA

Abstract: An FEC scheme based on large girth LDPC codes is proposed. A girth-10 block-
circulant LDPC code of rate 0.8 outperforms its turbo-product code counterpart by 0.95dB at BER of 10-10, on an AWGN channel. It provides the net effective coding gain of 10.95dB at BER of 1012. In long-haul optical transmission at 40-Gb/s it provides at least 1.47dB improvement over
corresponding turbo-product code.

1. Introduction The state-of-the-art optical communication systems standardized by the ITU employ different concatenated BoseChaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH)/Reed-Solomon (RS) codes [1],[2]. The iteratively decodable codes, such as turbo codes [1],[2] and low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [3]-[8], have generated significant research attention, recently. Turbo codes based on recursive systematic convolutional (RSC) codes exhibit the error floor phenomena at bit-error ratio (BER) around 10-4 as shown in [10], and as such are not suitable for high-speed optical transmission. Instead, turbo product codes (TPCs) with BCH components codes are proposed for use in optical communications [1],[2]. On the other hand, it has been shown in [3],[4],[6] that TPCs can be matched and outperformed by LDPC codes in terms of coding gain and decoding complexity.
The random LDPC codes [5] provide excellent error-correcting capabilities, but are difficult to implement because of the lack of nice mathematical structure in their parity-check matrices. To overcome this shortcoming, we propose the structured LDPC codes [3],[4],[6]-[8] instead, which are easy to implement at the expense of certain performance loss. To improve the performance loss of structured LDPC codes we can use the girth (the shortest cycle in corresponding bipartite graph representation of a parity-check matrix) as the optimization parameter.
In this paper we are interested in designing the structured LDPC codes of girth at least 8 with parity-check matrices represented in a block-circulant (array) fashion [6]-[8], to facilitate the implementation at high-speed. It has been shown in [8] that block-circulant (BC) codes cannot have the girth larger than 12. We have noticed that the codes with girth 12 require unacceptable long code words if the code rate is to be kept reasonable high (≥0.8), so that we restrict our attention to girth-8 and girth-10 LDPC codes. We have shown that girth-10 BC LDPC(24015,19212) code of code rate 0.8 outperforms longer BCH(128,113)xBCH(256,239) TPC of code rate 0.82 by 0.95 dB at BER of 10-10 on an AWGN channel model. It provides the net effective coding of 10.95 dB at BER of 10-12 on the same channel. The proposed LDPC codes were tested on a realistic simulation model that takes into account all major transmission impairments, inlcuding Kerr nonlinearities, stimulated Raman scattering, dispersion effects, ASE noise, filtering effects, intersymbol interference, and linear crosstalk effects (see [4] for more details), and even more impressive improvement was found. For example, the LDPC(16935,13550) code outperforms BCH(128,113)xBCH(256,239) TPC by 1.47 dB at BER of 10-7.

We are turning our attention to the evaluation of proposed LDPC codes using: (i) an AWGN channel model,

and (ii) a realistic fiber-optic communication system model based on the nonlinear Schrödinger equation which was

solved using the split-step Fourier method. The model (ii), as already mentioned in Introduction, takes into account

Kerr nonlinearities, stimulated Raman scattering, dispersion effects, ASE noise, filtering effects, intersymbol

interference, and linear crosstalk effects (for more details an interested reader is referred to [4]). The dispersion map

and parameters of D+ and D- fibers, used in simulations, are given in [4]. The span length is set to L=120 km, and each span consists of 2L/3 km of D+ fiber followed by L/3 km of D- fiber. Pre-compensation of -1600 ps/nm and corresponding post-compensation are also applied. RZ modulation format of a duty cycle of 33% is observed, the

extinction ratio is 14 dB, and the launched power is set to 0 dBm. EDFAs with noise figure of 6 dB are deployed

after every fiber section, the bandwidth of optical filter is set to 3Rl and the bandwidth of electrical filter to 0.7Rl, with Rl being the line rate (the bit rate (40 Gb/s) divided by code rate).

3. Conclusion We proposed an FEC scheme based on large girth (g=8 or g=10) block-circulant LDPC codes as an attractive option for high-speed optical transmission. The girth-10 block-circulant LDPC(24015,19212) code of rate 0.8 outperforms its turbo-product code counterpart BCH(128,113)xBCH(256,239) by 0.95 dB at BER of 10-10 (on AWGN channel model), and provides the net effective coding gain of 10.95 dB at BER of 10-12. The simulations results on a realistic channel model at 40 Gb/s indicate that proposed LDPC codes performs significantly better in the presence of simultaneous random errors and burst errors due to intrachannel nonlinearities. The LDPC(16935,13550) code provides at least 1.47 dB improvement over corresponding TPC in the presence of random errors and burst errors due to fiber nonlinearities. The proposed LDPC codes are shorter than TPC, the encoders can be implemented based on shift registers and modulo-2 adders, and decoder complexity, based on min-sum-with-correction-term algorithm, is low because column weigh is low (3 or 4).
