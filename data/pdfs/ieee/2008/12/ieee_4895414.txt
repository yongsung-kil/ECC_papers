Abstract
In this paper, we present an efficient systematic encoding algorithm for quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes which are related to cyclic maximum-distance separable (MDS) codes. The algorithm has linear time complexity, and it can be easily implemented by using polynomial multiplication and division circuits. We show that the division polynomials can be completely characterized by its zeros and that the sum of the numbers of the zeros is equal to the parity-length of the codes.
1. Introduction
Recently, several constructions of LDPC codes with the help of classical algebraic coding theory have been presented (cf. [1, 4]). In particular, it has been shown in [1] that q-ary Reed-Solomon codes of dimension two can be used to construct LDPC and QC-LDPC codes with good error-performance. In [2], good high-rate QC-LDPC codes which contain finite geometry codes as subcodes have been presented, and the relationship between the codes and q-ary MDS codes of length q + 1 has been clarified. These algebraic QC-LDPC codes are expected to have advantages in terms of ease of implementation over other types of LDPC codes.
This paper addresses the issue of efficient encoding of the QC-LDPC codes related to cyclic MDS codes. We show that the algebraic nature of these codes allows to determine explicitly their dimension and to design a systematic encoding algorithm with linear time complexity. The encoding algorithm can be easily implemented by using polynomial multiplication and division circuits. The division polynomials can be completely characterized by its zeros, and the sum of the numbers of the zeros is equal to the parity-length of the codes.
This paper is organized in the following way. In Section 2, we present and investigate a class of QCLDPC codes obtained from a matrix related to cyclic MDS codes of length q + 1. The codes are equiva-

lent to those presented previously in [2] in the sense that one can be obtained from the other by applying a fixed permutation. In Section 3, we present an efficient encoding algorithm for these codes. In Section 4, we consider a class of QC-LDPC codes related to a cyclic MDS code of length q ‚ąí 1, which were originally presented by Djurdjevic, et al. [1]. We show that the same encoding procedure for the codes presented in Section 2 can be applied.

5. Conclusion
In this paper, we have investigated a class of binary QC-LDPC codes obtained from cyclic MDS codes. The parity-check matrices of these codes are not fullrank, contrary to randomly constructed block-circulant parity-check matrices, which tend to be either fullrank or almost full-rank. We have shown that their parity-check matrices can be put into a triangular form and that it yields an efficient encoding implementation. The encoding is systematic and has linear time complexity. A special case of the encoding implementation is reported for a microwave radio system in [3]. The codes employed there are of length 4095 and dimensions 4023 ‚ąí 42r, r = 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, which are equivalent to Cr(65), r = 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, as noted in Section 2. In the microwave radio system, they are used in conjunction with a high-order modulation scheme. The system exhibits good bit error rate performance.
