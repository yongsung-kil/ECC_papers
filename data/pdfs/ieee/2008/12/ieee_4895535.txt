Abstract
We propose Variable-to-Check Residual Belief Propagation (VCRBP) for LDPC decoding for faster convergence, better error correcting performance and lower complexity. It is similar to Residual Belief Propagation (RBP) that was recently applied to LDPC decoding by Vila Casado et al. because it is also a dynamic scheduling belief propagation using residuals, but it is different because its residuals are computed from variable-tocheck message. Simulation shows that it out-performs with only maximum 8 iterations by about 0.3 dB compared with RBP at an FER of 10ŌłÆ4. Also, we propose node-wise VCRBP (N-VCRBP) for reducing complexity of VCRBP. N-VCRBP also exhibits the performance very close to VCRBP but with significantly lower decoding complexity.
1. INTRODUCTION
Next generation communication systems require faster and more accurate channel decoders due to very high data transmission rate, e.g. 4G wireless communications. A lot of decoding algorithms have been developed to cope with the requirements. Particularly in Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes, Belief Propagation (BP) algorithm have been extensively studied for faster decoding convergence of LDPC codes. A typical scheme of BP is the serial scheduling decoding such as the shuffled decoding in [1] and layered decoding in [2]. It was addressed that, in [1, 2], the convergence of the decoding by these algorithms is twice faster than conventional BP algorithms.
Recently, more effective serial scheduling scheme of BP algorithm based on informed dynamic scheduling strategies, called Residual BP (RBP), was introduced in [3]. RBP for LDPC codes in [4], [5] can accelerate the convergence of the decoding faster than the shuffled/layered serial decoding. However, RBP has some shortcomings both in the error correcting performance

(because of its greediness [4], [5]) and in the decoding complexity yet.
In this paper, we propose the advanced versions of informed dynamic scheduling strategy that we call Variable-to-Check Residual BP (VCRBP) and Nodewise VCRBP (N-VCRBP). VCRBP solves the greediness problem of RBP pointed out in [4]. We show, based on our extensive experimental results, that the error correcting performance by our VCRBP is better than the one by RBP in [4] about by 0.3dB (at FER 10ŌłÆ4). Furthermore, VCRBP also significantly improves the decoding complexity, compared to RBP, in two perspectives: It removes all unnecessary recomputations of messages, and also, it effectively reduces the number of ordering process of residuals. For more reducing decoding complexity, we propose NVCRBP. We provide experimental evidences that NVCRBP can improve the decoding complexity, without loosing the error correcting performance, that is significantly better than that of both the VCRBP and RBP.
The rest of this paper consists of as follows. In Section II, we briefly review RBP for LDPC codes. In Section III, we introduce VCRBP for LDPC codes in two schemes, VCRBP and node-wise VCRBP (Algorithm A and Algorithm B, respectively). In Section IV, we present our experimental results with the VCRBP, N-VCRBP, and RBP and show clear advantages of using the VCRBP and N-VCRBP by comparing the error correcting performances and decoding complexities. Finally, we summarize this paper in Section V.

However, RBP for LDPC codes in [4], [5] has some margin of improvement both in the error correcting performance and the decoding complexity. Aspect of the error correcting performance, RBP generates and propagates new errors which do not appear in non-dynamic scheduled decoding because of its greediness [4], [5]. Aspect of the decoding complexity, We specifically try to concentrate on Lines 5 and 10 in Algorithm 1. When check-to-variable message is updated, mcŌåÆv is unnecessarily recomputed because it has been already calculated when r(mcŌåÆv) is determined in the previous step. Conversely, mcŌåÆv has to be computed additionally only for residual. Moreover, in Line 10, Q is re-ordered whenever residual value of each edge is calculated. Here, Q denotes a set containing the information on the magnitude of all the remaining residual values.
3. VARIABLE-TO-CHECK RESIDUAL BELIEF PROPAGATION FOR LDPC CODES
3.1. VCRBP for LDPC Codes
VCRBP is a less greedy algorithm for overcoming the negative effect caused by the greediness of RBP. This method has not only better performance but also lower complexity than Residual Belief Propagation (RBP) in [4] and [5]. Main difference between VCRBP and RBP is in the calculation of residuals: VCRBP calculates the residual from the difference of variableto-check message values before and after update, in other words, it sequentially updates check node corresponding to the chosen edge. On the other hand, RBP calculates the residual from the difference of checkto-variable message values before and after update, in other words, it sequentially updates variable node corresponding to the chosen edge.

In error correcting performance aspect, VCRBP guarantees better performance than RBP. RBP tends to propagate the message to the less reliable variable nodes because the message which has the largest residual based on only one check equation. This greediness of RBP can generate new errors that need a large number of message updates to be corrected. However, VCRBP propagates first the message which has the largest residual based on all the check equations (except only one). So, VCRBP is a less greedy algorithm because it uses more reliable message for the residual than RBP. Moreover, VCRBP solves the trapping set

more effectively by updating the check node (based on the largest residual) and then simultaneously all the variable nodes representing the check equation. These facts result in much faster convergence as well as better performance than RBP.
3.2. Node-wise VCRBP for LDPC Codes
As a complexity-reduced version of VCRBP, we propose Node-wise Variable-to-Check RBP (N-VCRBP) for LDPC codes. N-VCRBP is similar to SBP. The main difference between SBP and N-VCRBP is the following: SBP updates variable nodes sequentially according to a predetermined schedule while N-VCRBP selects the next variable node to be updated based on the residual value. 5. CONCLUSION
We propose VCRBP for LDPC codes. Simulation shows that (1) VCRBP makes decoding of LDPC codes converge very fast in terms of the number of iterations; and (2) it performs similarly better after sufficiently many iterations. The complexity reduction can be seen easily from the comparison of Algorithm 1 and Algorithm A.
For further reduction of complexity, we propose N-VCRBP. N-VCRBP has very close performance to VCRBP with significantly lower decoding complexity.
