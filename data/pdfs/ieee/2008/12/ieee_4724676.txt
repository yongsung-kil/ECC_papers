Modified BP Decoding Algorithms Combined with GA for Low-Density Parity Check Codes

Zerong Deng, Xingcheng Liu, Man Teng Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering
Sun Yat-Sen University Guangzhou, 510275, China
isslxc@mail.sysu.edu.cn

Abstract
In this paper we propose algorithms which combine Belief-propagation (BP) decoding algorithm with Genetic Algorithm (GA). The main idea is to improve the performance of traditional BP decoding algorithm by efficiently using the belief of variable nodes passing to check nodes. By considering the belief as genes, the proposed algorithms mainly employ the mutation operation in GA, and change the genes’ amplitudes (either increase or decrease) when updating the check nodes’ information. First, we explore that the marriage of the GA and BP decoding algorithm is reasonable. Three algorithms are proposed accordingly. Proposed algorithm I mutates chosen genes by amplitude enhancement, while proposed algorithm II by amplitude reduction. Finally, proposed algorithm III is a mixture of the former two. From computer simulation results, we show that for short and middle length LDPC codes, our proposed algorithms can improve both BER (Bit Error Rate) and FER (Frame Error Rate) performance over traditional BP decoding algorithm, with slight modification of traditional BP decoding algorithm and little increase of computation complexity.

Decoded by the Belief Propagation (BP) decoding algorithm [2], LDPC codes can achieve very good performance. Because of their good performance, they have gained intensive academic attention. 2.2. Genetic algorithm

Genetic algorithm (GA) is based on the Darwinian principle of biological evolution: the survival of the fittest [7], [11]. Every genetic algorithm starts with an initial population, and evolves to an optimal solution by repeatedly modifying the individuals in each generation of the population. Each individual has a fitness measurement. Fitness shows the ability of the individual’s surviving to the environment. During each generation some individuals survive due to their high fitness, some are eliminated because of their poor survival performance. And a chosen part of individuals are supposed to change to generate their children into a new generation.
Operations are executed from generation to generation by using specific genetic operators such as reproduction, crossover, and mutation. Reproduction copies the selected individuals, which have high fitness, into the next generation without any changes. The operations of crossover and mutation require changes to generate the offspring. Crossover exchanges part of their genes in two selected individuals to generate their children. As to mutation, the process changes part of an individual’s genes to form its descendant. The purpose of these operations is to keep the fitter individuals or produce fitter ones in the population. And after a number of evolutions, the population is apt to the fittest one to the environment. All these three genetic operations are on the basis of their fitness. In the next section, we explore how GA and BP decoding algorithm marry to each other and define necessary parameters.
3. Proposed algorithms

From the process of genetic algorithm, we can see that an initial population is needed. We initialize a population by the receiving message from the channel. And we take all the variable nodes as a population and every variable node vi as an individual, and the message L(qijk) passing to the check nodes as genes. In this way the solution population and all the GA operations are separated in two spaces (individual

space and gene space) naturally. That fits to the BP decoding algorithm. GA changes the individuals’ genes in gene space and this lead to the related individuals’ improvement in individual space. In this way, GA gets the whole population fitter and fitter by evolution. Take Fig. 1 for example, the population contains ten individuals and each individual has three genes.
In Tanner graph, message of different individuals can not be exchanged directly, because the genes are highly related to individuals, so the genetic operation crossover can not be applied here. In order to take the advantage of GA, we use reproduction and mutation to form a new generation during each BP iteration, that is, one generation is formed in each BP iteration.

| L(cˆik ) | is, the more likely cˆi is to be 1 or 0. So measurement of individual surviving to the environment (i.e. fitness) is:

4. Simulation and analysis

Figs. 2-5 show the BER and FER performance of traditional BP algorithm and the proposed algorithms over AWGN channel with BPSK modulation. Fig. 6 shows the relationship between the key parameterλ
and the performance both in BER and FER of (504, 252) regular LDPC codes with Eb/N0=2.5dB, comparing with the traditional BP decoding algorithm. For the three proposed algorithms we set the parameters as follows, λ=50 and Δλ=10. Figs. 2, 3
show the BER and FER of (504,252) regular LDPC codes respectively, and Figs. 4, 5 show the performance of (1008, 502) regular LDPC codes. For all the decoding algorithms in the simulation, the maximum iteration number is set to 200.
We can see that our proposed algorithms achieve better BER and FER than those of the traditional BP decoding algorithm. From operation (6) the enhancement modification occurs on the high reliable genes that are chosen. But in this situation, from (2) we can obtain that the magnitude of the L(rjik) changes little when βij increases. This can clearly be seen from the simulation results by comparing the traditional BP decoding algorithm with proposed algorithm I. Operation (7) cut the magnitudes of the genes from the less reliable individuals. In this way we let other much stronger individuals’ genes to affect on the individuals who have poor gene performance. This algorithm is

more efficient than traditional BP decoding algorithm and proposed algorithm I, because during the transmission only a very small number of the bits are polluted. We minimize the magnitude of these possible polluted individuals’ genes to let the whole population to get fitter. From the proposed algorithm III, we can obtain that the key factor to improve the decoding performance lies on cutting tumor genes.

5. Conclusion

In this paper, we proposed three modified BP decoding algorithms combined with genetic algorithm (GA). In the proposed algorithms, we mainly use mutation operation on the individuals (i.e. variable nodes) during each generation, either by enhancing or degrading the chosen genes or using both. From computer simulation, we showed that our proposed algorithms can improve the traditional BP decoding algorithm over BER and FER. Particularly, the proposed algorithm II gains a much better improvement with the least increase of computing complexity and using fewer parameters.
