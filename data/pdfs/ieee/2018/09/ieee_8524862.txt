Abstract
The given paper presents several probabilistic statements for the methods related to constructing a QC-LDPC code of shorter length by length adaption from a given QCLDPC code of maximal length. Several probabilistic statements concerning a theoretical improvement of the generalization of floor lifting method with respect to the number of small cycles are proved. Simulation results show that the generalization of floor lifting method under base graph 2 (BG2) allow to achieves from 0.1 to 0.3 dB coding gain.
1. Introduction
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were first discovered by Gallager [1], generalized by Tanner [2], Wibberg [3] and rediscovered by MacKay et al.[4, 5] and Sipser et al. [6].
Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes are of great interest to researchers [7, 9–11] since they can be encoded and decoded with low complexity and allow to reach high throughput using barrel shifter [12–14].
QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices (CPM) allow to analyze their code and graph properties easier than in the case of random LDPC codes. The performance of LDPC codes depends on the its graph properties: girth [15–16], trapping sets [17–18], and code properties, e.g., the Hamming distance of the code [19, 20] and the ensemble weight enumerator [21].
The main contribution of the paper are prove of several

probabilistic statements for different lifting methods to construct QC-LDPC code of short length by recalculating the specifications of given maximum length code. Performing some calculations of the parameters it is possible to construct a sequence of codes with different sizes of the circulant from the exponent matrix of the QC-LDPC code of maximum length. The only additional parameter we store in memory is constant scaling value in the lifting procedure. The modular lifting, floor lifting and generalization of floor lifting method was introduced in [22, 23, 26].
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section 2, we introduce some basic definitions and notations for our presentation. In Section 2, we review state-of-art lifting methods for QC-LDPC codes. Also assuming some natural assumption we prove some probabilistic statements with respect to cycles of length 4 and provide a comparison between lifting procedures. In Section 4, we present our floor scale modulo lifting method for QC-LDPC codes and prove several probabilistic statements concerning theoretical improvement of the method with respect to the number of small cycles. The performance of QC-LDPC codes obtained by the floor scale modulo lifting method is investigated by simulations in Section 5.

The trapping set (a, b) is a subgraph formed by cycles in Tanner graph or their overlap with a variable nodes and b odd degree checks. For example, Trapping sets TS(5,3) formed by overlap of three 8-cycles, and TS(4,4) formed by cycle 8 in Tanner graph, [17]. The harmfulness of a trapping set is defined as the number of variable nodes, error in which can cause suboptimal iterative decoder to fail on this TS. Under equal probability of error, TS(5,3) more harmful, because 3 errors in odd degree check nodes provide 5

errors in variable nodes. The TS(4,4) produces 4 errors after 4 errors in odd degree check nodes, and can be consider as a pseudocodeword with weight 4. Broken of the most harmful cycles improve pseudocodewords weight spectrum which lower probability of error-floor. It’s harmfulness are strongly related to decoder parameters. Parameters modification can change probability of decoder fail due to TS pseudocodewords, [24].

5. Simulation Results

QC-LDPC codes of smaller lengths can be obtained by lifting exponent matrix of QC-LDPC codes of maximal length. Their performance under sum-product decoder with 50 iterations over an AWGN channel with QPSK modulation was analyzed by computer simulations. Figure 1 shows the SNR value required to achieve 10−5 FER performance for information lengths k with granularity 16 bits k < 1024 and 32 bit k > 1024 for QC-LDPC codes with rate 1/3. Code A is the QC-LDPC with base graph 2 (BG2) from 5G EMBB standard, [25]. BG2 standard lifting shows catastrophic performance for some lengths caused by error-floor. Code B is BG2 lifted using the proposed floor scale modular lifting, [26].

6. Conclusion

The probabilistic statements concerning a theoretical improvement of proposed lifting method with respect to the number of small cycles are proved. This approach improves cycle properties of QC-LDPC codes, decreases number of harmful Trapping sets and shows from 0.1 to 0.3 dB gain compared to EMBB lifting.
