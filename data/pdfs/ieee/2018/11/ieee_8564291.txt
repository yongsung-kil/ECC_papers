Girth Analysis of Tanner (5,11) Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes

Hengzhou Xu, Hai Zhu, Mengmeng Xu, Bo Zhang School of Network Engineering Zhoukou Normal University Zhoukou, China

Abstract—Motivated by the works on the girth of Tanner (3,5), (3,7), (3,11), and (5,7) quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes, we in this paper study the girth of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes. We first analyze the cycles of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes, and obtain the conditions for the existence of cycles of length less than 12 in Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes of length 11p where p is a prime number and p ≡ 1 (mod 55). Notice that the condition is represented by the polynomial equations in a 55th root of unity of the prime field Fp. By checking the existence of solutions for these equations over Fp, the girths of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes are obtained.
Keywords-LDPC code; quasi-cyclic; girth; prime field

I. INTRODUCTION

Because of easy implementation in hardware [1], [2],

[3], fast convergence of iterative decoding [4], and good

performance in the waterfall and error-floor regions [5],

quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes [6] have been widely studied

by industry and researchers recently. The performance of an

LDPC code is determined by several structural properties

collectively: minimum distance, girth, cycle distribution,

variable node connectivity (or structure), row redundancy,

trapping set, degree distributions, and some other unknown

structures. If all of these structural properties are considered

together, it is not easy to design/construct an LDPC code

with good performance.

However, research results show that there are some rela-

tionships among these structures. Tanner graph defined by

the parity-check matrix H of an LDPC code is free of 4-

cycles if a constraint is imposed on H: no two rows (or

two columns) of H have more than one nonzero component

in common. This constraint on H is well known as the

row-column (RC) constraint (or RC-constraint) [1]. The RC-

constraint also affects the minimum distance dmin of a (J, L)-regular LDPC code generated by the null space of

H, i.e., dmin ≥ (J + 1), where J is the minimum column weight of H. The minimum distance dmin can also be lower bounded by [7]

where g is the girth of the (J, L)-regular LDPC code. For short to moderate lengths, the minimum distance of an LDPC code affects its performance in the error-floor region if the variable node degrees are small. Hence, girth plays an important role in designing LDPC codes, and it is of interest to construct QC-LDPC codes with good girth values.
In 2001, R. M. Tanner presented a class of (J, L)-regular QC-LDPC codes [8], called Tanner (J, L) QC-LDPC codes in this paper. In 2006, S. Kim et al. studied the girth of Tanner (3,5) QC-LDPC codes of length 5p where p is a prime of the form 15i+1 [9], and their girth values were provided. When p equals 31, Tanner (3,5) QC-LDPC code has girth of 8, and when p belongs to {61, 151}, the girth of the code is 10. For the other values p, i.e., p ∈ {P15\{31, 61, 151}}, Tanner (3,5) QC-LDPC code achieves the maximum girth value 12. Notice that P15 stands for the set of the primes with the form 15i+1. This result shows the promising girth property of Tanner (J, L) QCLDPC codes. Recently, the girth problem of Tanner (3,7), (3,11), and (5,7) QC-LDPC codes have been solved [10], [11], [12]. Similar to the result of Tanner (3,5) QC-LDPC codes, these three classes of Tanner QC-LDPC codes have a limited number of codes with girth 6, 8, 10, and most of Tanner QC-LDPC codes have girth 12. So far, the girths of the remaining Tanner (J, L) QC-LDPC codes are unknown.
In this paper, we study the girth of Tanner (5,11) QCLDPC codes of length 11p, where p is a prime of the form 55i+1. We first analyze the cycles of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes, and give the definition of the equivalent relation between cycles with same lengths. According to the equivalent relation, we classify the cycles of lengths 4, 6, 8, and 10 into sixteen equivalent classes, and then present the sufficient and necessary conditions for the existence of cycles in Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes. According to these conditions, we can obtain some polynomial equations in a 55th root of unity of the prime field Fp. By checking the existence of solutions for these equations over Fp, the candidate girth values of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes are obtained. By summarizing these results, the girth of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes is proposed in the conclusion.

6) Class (0,1,0,2,4): The basic equation for 10-cycles in the equivalent class (0,1,0,2,4), i.e., a block sequence, (0,0); (1,0); (1,t); (0,t); (0,t+u); (2,t+u); (2,t+u+v);(4,t+u+v); (4,t + u + v + w); (0,t + u + v + w); (0,0), t + u + v + w = 0 (mod 11). The modified basic equation is
1 − α5t + (1 + α11)α5(t+u) + (1 + α11)α5(t+u+v)+22 +α5(t+u+v+w)+44 = 0 (mod p).
7) Class (0,1,0,3,4): The equivalent class (0,1,0,3,4) corresponds to a block sequence, i.e., (0,0); (1,0); (1,t); (0,t); (0,t+u); (3,t+u); (3,t+u+v);(4,t+u+v); (4,t+u+v+w); (0,t + u + v + w); (0,0), where t + u + v + w = 0 (mod 11). The modified basic equation is
1 − α5t + (1 + α11 + α22)α5(t+u) + α5(t+u+v)+33 +α5(t+u+v+w)+44 = 0 (mod p).
8) Class (0,1,2,0,1): A block sequence, i.e., (0,0); (1,0); (1,t); (2,t); (2,t + u);(0,t + u); (0,t + u + v); (1,t + u + v); (1,t+u+v+w); (0,t+u+v+w); (0,0), where t+u+v+w = 0 (mod 11), stands for the equivalent class (0,1,2,0,1). The modified basic equation is given as:
1 − α11)(1 + α5t+11 − (1 + α11)α5(t+u) + α5(t+u+v) −α5(t+u+v+w) = 0 (mod p).
9) Class (0,1,3,0,1): For 10-cycles in the equivalent class (0,1,3,0,1), i.e., a sequence of blocks, (0,0); (1,0); (1,t); (3,t); (3,t+u); (0,t+u); (0,t+u+v); (1,t+u+v); (1,t+u+v+w); (0,t + u + v + w); (0,0), t + u + v + w = 0 (mod 11). The modified basic equation is given as:
1 + (1 + α11)α5t+11 − (1 + α11 + α22)α5(t+u)+ α5(t+u+v) − α5(t+u+v+w) = 0 (mod p).
III. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we analyzed the cycles of length less than 12 of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes, and divided them into sixteen equivalent classes. For the cycles in each equivalent class, we derived the basic equation in a primitive 55th root of unity in Fp, where p is a prime with the form 55i+1. By checking the solution of these equation, we obtained the girth of Tanner (5,11) QC-LDPC codes. When p is 331, 7591, or 21011, the girth is 6.

When p ∈ {661, 881, 991, . . .}, the girth is 8. When p ∈ {2861, 2971, 3301, . . .}, the girth is 10. For the remaining values p, i.e., p ∈ {37951, 45541, . . .}, the girth is 12.
