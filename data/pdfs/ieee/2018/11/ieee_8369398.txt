A Study on Iterative Decoding With LLR Modulator by Parity Check Information in SMR System
M. Nishikawa 1, Y. Nakamura 1, H. Osawa1, Y. Okamoto1, Y. Kanai2, and H. Muraoka3
1Graduate School of Science and Engineering, Ehime University, Matsuyama 790-8577, Japan 2Department of Information and Electronics Engineering, Niigata Institute of Technology, Kashiwazaki 945-1103, Japan
3Research Institute of Electrical Communication, Tohoku University, Sendai 980-8577, Japan

In recent years, shingled magnetic recording (SMR) has attracted attention as a new recording system of the hard disk drives. Although SMR can realize narrow track by overwriting data track like tile roofing, it has remarkable performance deterioration due to the influence of inter-track interference. Therefore, the performance improvement of the low-density parity-check (LDPC) coding and iterative decoding system using the sum-product decoding is required. In this paper, we propose the LDPC encoding and iterative decoding system with the log-likelihood ratio modulator and show the error floor is eliminated and improvement of signal-to-noise ratio to achieve the error free is obtained in the SMR under an areal recording density of 4 Tbits/in2 specification for the channel bit sequence coded by run-length limited and LDPC encoders by computer simulation. Therefore, the channel bit is composed bit about five magnetic grains.
Index Terms— Log-likelihood ratio (LLR), parity check information, shingled magnetic recording (SMR).

I. INTRODUCTION
S HINGLED magnetic recording (SMR) has attracted attention as a recording system for further increasing the recording density of hard disk drives [1]. However, the intertrack interference (ITI) is caused by overwriting on the previous adjacent track as well as the sensitivity extension to the adjacent tracks in the SMR [2], [3]. Therefore, it is desirable to develop a signal processing system which has a tolerance for the ITI to achieve higher track densities by the iterative decoding as an error correction method. In this paper, we propose a scheme to improve the performance of iterative decoding by adjusting the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) by LLR modulator which calculated by the a posteriori probability (APP) decoder for the sum-product (SP) decoding using the constraint of the low-density parity-check (LDPC) code in the SMR [4], and evaluate the performance under the areal recording density of 4 Tbits/in2 by computer simulation.

In the reading process, we also assume that the additive white Gaussian noise as a system noise is added the waveform from each reader at the reading point. The signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) of the system noise is defined as SNRS = 20 log 10 ( A/σS) (decibel), where A is the saturation level of the isolated waveform and σS is the effective value of the system noise in bandwidth of the channel bit rate fc. Then, the reproducing waveform from the intended or central track is equalized to partial response class-I (PR1) [8] characteristic by a low-pass filter with a cutoff frequency xh = 0.4 normalized by fc and a 2-D finite impulse response (2-D-FIR) filter having the number of taps/track Nt = 15 [9], [10] with the reproducing waveforms from the adjacent readers. The 2-D-FIR filter output sequence is converted into the decoding reliability LLR by an APP decoder for the PR1 channel with 1-D response based on the max-log-MAP algorithm in an APP decoder [11]. The LLR modulator adjusts the LLR sequence from the APP decoder output sequence by the parity check information [12] and threshold determination. The SP decoder performs iteration of up to isp using the constraint of LDPC code. Furthermore, the turbo equalization is executed between the SP decoder and the APP decoder up to iglobal. Afterward, the output sequence is obtained by the hard decision unit and the RLL decoder. Finally, a comparison of the RLL decoder output and encoder input sequences provides the bit error rate (BER).

The first submatrix in the parity check matrix has a run of “1” according to Gallager’s construction method [13]; however, the first submatrix is also randomized by row permutation in order to avoid the influence of inter-symbol interference in PR channel as well as the second and the third submatrices. The modulator decides the APP decoder output LLRs by their signs, and performs a parity check for the hard decision results as shown in Fig. 3, and each row has the parity check result. Afterward, the results for “1” in the parity check matrix are summed in the column direction by the channel bit, and the parity check information sequence composed of “0,” “1,” “2,” and “3” is obtained. Thus, the modulator regards that the LLR with the parity check information “0” or “1” is a high possibility of true, and selects as a candidate to modulate.
Fig. 4 shows the BER performance for the LLR modulating weight, where the only LLRs with the parity check information “0” are gained by the weight. The simulation parameters are set to SNRS = 21.5 dB, iglobal = 30, and isp = 15. As can be seen from Fig. 4, the BER is at a minimum weight of 3.0.

IV. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
Fig. 7 shows the LLR distributions of the 29th sector among 30 sectors at the SP decoder input and output without the LLR modulator. The simulation parameters are set to SNRS = 21.5 dB and isp = 15. In Fig. 7(a) and (b), the iglobal is set to 0, and in Fig. 7(c) and (d), it is set to 20. The green dot and red cross symbols show the correct and erroneous LLR, respectively. Here, we focused on the LLR of the 29th sector under 33 280 channel bits/sector with error floor even iglobal = 20. As can be seen from Fig. 7, the LLR distributions and the number of errors are not much difference between (a) and (b) or (c) and (d). The results show that the iterative decoding does not work well. Fig. 8 shows the LLR distributions of the 29th sector among 30 sectors at the SP decoder input and output with LLR modulator. The simulation parameters and symbols are the same as Fig. 7. As can be seen from Fig. 7, the iterative decoding works well by adopting the LLR modulator, and the system achieves no errors at iglobal = 20.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed LDPC coding and iterative decoding system with the LLR modulator and examined performance improvement by SMR simulation using granular medium model.
In the results, it became clearly that the saturation tendency of the BER performance can be discharged at SNRS = 21.5 dB by the iterative decoding with the LLR modulator. In addition, the LLR modulator improves the SNRS by 0.5 dB to achieve no errors. Therefore, we consider that modulating LLRs using the parity check information provides an effective iterative decoding.
