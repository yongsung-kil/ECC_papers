A Novel Jointed Detection Scheme for MIMO-LDPC Systems

Guiwu Yang*, Jianhao Hu*, Guoqiang Yao, Jienan Chen, Wei Cao
National key Lab. Of Communications University of Electronic Science and Technology of China, UESTC
Chengdu, China E-mail*:{ guiwuyang_uestc@163.com and jhhu@uestc.edu.cn}

Abstract—High performance and high efficiency detection scheme is one of the key issues for MIMO-LDPC system designs. In this paper, we proposed a hybrid jointed detection scheme for MIMO-LDPC detection, in which the expectation propagation (EP) algorithm is used in MIMO detection and the belief propagation (BP) algorithm is adopted for the low density parity check code (LDPC) decoding. In the proposed scheme, we provide a jointed detection algorithm (EP-BP JDD) with the integrated factor graph for MIMO-LDPC systems. Compared with conventional MMSE-PIC IDD and BP-BP JDD, the proposed method can achieve low computational complexity with high performance. The complexity and performance gains become more significant with the number of antennas increasing.
Keywords-MIMO-LDPC; expectation propagation; belief prop-
agation; Joint Detection/Decoding
I. INTRODUCTION
Multiple-input Multiple-output (MIMO) wireless system can achieve high spectral efficiency as well as link reliability by transmitting multiple spatial streams concurrently within a frequency band [1-2]. The optimal MIMO detector is Maximum Likelihood (ML) detector whose computational complexity increases exponentially with the number of transmission antennas and the orders of modulation. It restricts the practical application of ML. The low complexity MIMO detectors include linear methods such as Minimum Mean Square Error (MMSE), MMSE-PIC etc, and nonlinear methods such as Sphere Decoding (SD). However, these methods still have a high computational complexity for large scale MIMO, since they all need matrix inversions or nonlinear operations. In recent years, the Belief Propagation (BP) [3] and Expectation Propagation (EP) [4-5] method based on factor graph are proposed for massive MIMO detection. Compared with MMSE, they can achieve the better performances for large scale MIMO. Low-density Parity Check Code (LDPC) is an approaching Shannon Limit encoding scheme, which is widely used in many communication systems. The most commonly used decoding methods of LDPC are Belief Propagation (BP), likelihood ratio BP (LLR-BP) and Min-Sum algorithms.
High performance and high efficiency detection scheme is one of the key issues for MIMO-LDPC system designs. In [6], a receiver performs MIMO detection and convolutional code

decoding in a pipeline mode. This scheme is simple for application, but the performance is limited. In [7], an iterative receiver has been proposed, in which the message propagates Iteratively between Detector and Decoder (IDD). However, IDD is not practical because of its extremely high computational complexity. To solve this problem, a Jointed Detection/Decoding (JDD) method based on the integrated factor graph (FG) is proposed by [8-9]. This method integrates messages passing between BP-MIMO Detector and BP-LDPC Decoder (BP-BP JDD) into a jointed factor graph. Simulation results show that the BP-BP JDD method approximates the best performance of IDD with much low complexity. However, the complexity of BP method in MIMO detection is still not satisfactory.
In this paper, we propose a hybrid jointed method based on the integrated FG, which adopts Joint EP in MIMO Detection and BP in LDPC Decoding (EP-BP JDD). The proposed method divides the FG into Observed Nodes (ON), Check Nodes (CN) and Jointed Variable Nodes (JVN). The messages of detection and decoding are propagated over the three nodes in iterative mode. We also provide the message transmission scheme between EP domain and BP domain in this paper. The simulation results show that the proposed method can reach the same performance as conventional BP-BP JDD over Bit Error Rate (BER), meanwhile, this method has much lower complexity. When the scale of antennas is 1212 , the proposed method has only 52% complexity of BP-BP-JDD with only 0.005dB performance loss. What’s more, it has 43% complexity of MMSE-PIC IDD with 4 iterations and achieves 1.1 dB performance gain.
The paper is organized as follows. In Section II, we show the system model. In Section III, we show the details of proposed method. In Section IV, we provide the performance simulation results and complexity analysis. We conclude this paper in Section V.

IV. PERFORMANCE AND COMPLEXITY ANALYSIS In this Section, we compare proposed hybrid joint EP-BP JDD scheme with the conventional BP-BP JDD and MMSEPIC IDD over the BER performance and computational complexity .
4.1 Performance In our simulation study, we adopt a 4-QAM modulation
under Quasi-static Rayleigh fading channel. Channel code is the standard LDPC of 802.11aj with code length and code rate are 672 bits and 1/2, respectively. Both the maximum iteration time of the proposed EP-BP JDD scheme and conventional BP-BP JDD scheme are 50. For the IDD scheme detector is MMSE-PIC and decoder is BP, we pay attention to the performance when the outer iteration of IDD is 1, 2 and 4 and the iteration times of BP decoder are 50.
Figure 3 and Fig.4 show the results of two kind of simulation conditions. From Fig. 3 and Fig.4 we know that the EP-BP JDD has almost the same BER performance as the conventional BP-BP JDD for different antenna scales, while it outperforms the MMSE-PIC IDD scheme a lot. When

antennas scale is 8  8 , the EP-BP JDD has 1.7dB at BER= 3.5105 performance gain, compared with IDD. When antennas scale is 12 12 , the performance gain is 1.1dB at BER= 7 105 .

V. ACKNOWLEDGMENT This work was supported by the National Nature Science Foundation of China under Grant No.61571083 and 61501084.

VI. CONCLUSION In this paper, we provide jointed detection algorithm (EPBP JDD) with the integrated factor graph for MIMO-LDPC systems. Compared with the conventional MMSE-PIC IDD and BP-BP JDD, the proposed method can achieve low computational complexity with high performance. The complexity and performance gains become more significant with the number of antennas increasing.
