Construction of Reed-Solomon Based Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes Based on Protograph
Inseon Kim and Hong-Yeop Song School of Electrical & Electronic Engineering
Yonsei University, Seoul, South Korea email: {is.kim, hysong}@yonsei.ac.kr

Abstractâ€”In this paper, we propose construction of ReedSolomon(RS) based Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check(QCLDPC) codes using protograph combining two existing QCLDPC codes construction. One is the construction RS based QCLDPC codes whose girth is at least 8 and another is protograph based QC-LDPC codes to increase the upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance. We construct the protographs to increase the upper bound of minimum Hamming distance for Proto-RS-QCLDPC codes and simulate some experiment to show coding gain in sense of BER compare to existing QC-LDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-Cyclic low-density parity-check(QC-LDPC) codes [1] has been getting attention by many researchers because its simple representation make them attractive for encoding and decoding implementation and analysis. The decoding performance of this codes family can reach the Shannon limits if it is well designed algebraically [2]. For this reason, QCLDPC codes have been chosen to be standard in several communication system including next 5G communication system [3]â€“[5].
The performance of QC-LDPC codes under iterative message passing decoding algorithm depends on the number of short cycle in their Tanner graph. The short cycles play a important role in the error floor performance of QCLDPC codes since they cause stopping set or trapping set. Therefore, it is important to avoid short cycles in Tanner graph when constructing QC-LDPC codes. Finding QC-LDPC codes with large girth has been issue to constructing good performed codes. Xiao et al. proposed the construction of Reed-Solomon(RS) based QC-LDPC Codes which guarantee that the length of the shortest cycles is at least 8 by choosing RS codes with specific conditions [6].
Another aspect of constructing structured LDPC codes is by using protograph based LDPC codes. Protograph based QCLDPC codes can be constructed by copying multiple times the small Tanner graph with more than 2 edges between some pair of a check node and a variable node and lifting the edges to copied graphs. In [8], the authors proposed the upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance of protograph based QC-LDPC codes and construction of protograph based QC-LDPC codes to increase the upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance.
In this paper, we proposed construction of RS based QCLDPC codes using protograph basically combining two existing constructions. The codes obtained by the construction

show better performance than existing RS based QC-LDPC codes in sense of bit error rate(BER). In Section II, we introduce how to construct RS based QC-LDPC codes and protograph based QC-LDPC codes to increase the minimum Hamming distance. In Section III, we provide a construction of RS based QC-LDPC codes based on protograph and some error performance simulations. Finally, Section IV concludes the paper.

B. Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes based on Protograph

For parity-check matrix of LDPC codes, Tanner graphs of LDPC codes are bipartite graphs and can be generated in following way: for every column of matrix, we draw a variable node, for every row, we draw a check node, and we connect a variable node and a check node by an edge if the corresponding entry in is nonzero.
A protograph G = (V, C, E) is a bipartite graph with a set V = {v0, v1, آ· آ· آ· , vIâˆ’1} of variable nodes, a set C = {c0, c1, آ· آ· آ· , cJâˆ’1} of check nodes and a set E =
e0, e1, آ· آ· آ· , e|E|âˆ’1 , respectively. In protograph, we can connect one variable node and one check node with more than 2 edges, called parallel edges. To obtain a larger graph from protograph, we use copy-and-permute procedure. The protograph is copied N times and the edge copies are reconnected between the variable node copies and the check node copies. IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose construction of RS based QCLDPC codes using protograph combining two existing QCLDPC codes construction. Since we select the protographs to increase the upper bound of minimum Hamming distance for Proto-RS-QC-LDPC codes, there are coding gain in sense of BER/BLER compare to RS-QC-LDPC codes. Increasing the minimum Hamming distance can positively affect the improving performance when the RS-QC-LDPC codes have high rate. To attain more coding gain, we will focus on the construction strategy for protographs with their upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance.
