Design and Analysis of Spatially Coupled Protograph LDPC Codes
for Two-Dimensional Magnetic Recording Systems
Zhaojie Yang1, Yi Fang1, Lingjun Kong2, Guohua Zhang3, Guojun Han1
1Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou 510006, China, 2Nanjing University of Posts and Communication, Nanjing 210044, China
3China Academy of Space Technology (Xi’an), Xi’an 710100, China

Abstract— In this paper, a turbo-like extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) algorithm is proposed for two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR) systems. Based on the proposed turbo-like EXIT algorithm, we not only analyze the decoding threshold of spatially coupled protograph (SC-P) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes over TDMR channels under turbo detection, but also conceive a design methodology to construct capacity-approaching terminated SC-P codes. Compared with the original protograph codes, the terminated SC-P codes possess desirable performance gains in both low and high signal-to-noiseratio (SNR) regions.
Index Terms – Extrinsic information transfer, Turbo Detection,
spatially coupled protograph codes, magnetic recording channels.
I. INTRODUCTION
Protograph low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a promising choice for error-correction coding (ECC) schemes in two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR) systems [1]. In recent years, a variety of protograph code construction schemes have been introduced to improve the data-storage reliability of TDMR systems. As a type of protographconvolutional-like codes, spatially coupled protograph (SC-P) codes inherit the benefits of both protograph and convolutional codes, and thus can significantly outperform them [2]. Although the performance of SC LDPC codes have been studied over one-dimensional (OD) MR channels in [3], the corresponding codes were constructed based on conventional Tanner graphs rather than protographs. To the best of our knowledge, there has been no prior work on SC-P code design for MR (especially TDMR) systems in the literature. With the above motivation, this paper considers the analysis and optimization of SC-P code over TDMR channels. To be specific, we first propose a turbo-like extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) algorithm for TDMR systems with a turbo detector. Afterwards, we compare the decoding thresholds of terminated SC-P codes and conventional protograph codes utilizing the proposed EXIT algorithm. We finally construct a new family of terminated SC-P codes that can exhibit excellent performance over TDMR channels.

III. TURBO-LIKE EXIT ALGORITHM
In this section, a turbo-like EXIT algorithm is proposed for TDMR systems, which can be used to facilitate the design and optimization of SC-P codes.
In the proposed turbo-like EXIT algorithm, an initial Eb/N0 and an a-priori mutual information (MI) IAd are first assumed, which serve as the input parameters for the BCJR detector. Based on the Monte-Carlo simulation, one can obtain the extrinsic log-likelihood-ratios (LLRs) of the coded bits output from the BCJR detector. Then, an extrinsic MI IEd can be computed to as the initial decoding message of the variable nodes in the SC-P decoder. Exploiting IEd and an initial apriori MI IAv of each variable node, one can calculate its corresponding extrinsic MI IEv. Similarly, with an a-priori MI IAc of each check node, one can also calculate its corresponding extrinsic MI IEc. Finally, one can use the extrinsic MI IEc to calculate the average MI transferring from the LDPC decoder to the BCJR detector, which serves as an updated a-priori MI IAd. Furthermore, the a-posteriori MI IAPP of each variable node can be measured. The evaluation process is terminated when IAPP = 1 for all variable nodes or the maximum number of turbo iteration is reached.

V. SIMULATION RESULTS
In simulations, the codeword length of the SC-P code and protograph codes is assumed as 4800. Moreover, the maximum number of turbo iterations between the BCJR detector and the LDPC decoder is set to 10, while the maximum number of BP iterations for SC-P/protograph codes is set to 30. Fig. 1 shows the BER curves of the (3, 6) terminated SC-P, (3, 6) protograph, NP2 protograph, and ODMR protograph codes over a TDMR channel with a recording density of 4 Tb/in2. Referring to the figure, the (3, 6) terminated SC-P code is the second-best performing code among the four types of codes. At the BER of 4 u 10-5, the (3, 6) terminated SC-P code achieves 0.1 dB and 0.15 dB gains over the NP2 protograph code and the (3, 6) protograph code. In addition, the trend of the BER curves is reasonably
consistent with the decoding threshold, which verifies the
accuracy of the turbo-like EXIT algorithm.
VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have proposed a turbo-like EXIT algorithm for TDMR systems and investigated the error performance of terminated SC-P codes. Both theoretical analyses and simulation results have indicated that the terminated SC-P codes have better performance than the original protograph codes. In the forthcoming full paper, we will further propose a SC-P code construction method tailored for TDMR system.
