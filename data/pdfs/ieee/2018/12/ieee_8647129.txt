A Modified Two-bit Finite-Alphabet Iterative Decoder for LDPC Codes
Keke Liu, Yang Han, Shaohua Yang, Bruce Wilson, Xuebin Wu and Wu Chang Broadcom Inc.
1320 Ridder Park Dr San Jose, CA, 95131, U.S.A. Email: keke.liu@broadcom.com

Abstract—A modified two-bit finite-alphabet iterative decoder (FAID) is proposed in this paper. As distinct from similar existing two-bit FAIDs, this proposed decoder is probabilistic and incorporates the idea of using single a-posteriori message to replace the conventional variable-to-check (v2c) messages. This brings significant variable-node unit (VNU) complexity reduction. For high-rate regular LDPC codes, it is demonstrated that the proposed decoder, in waterfall region, substantially outperforms other simplified decoders reported in the literature.
I. INTRODUCTION
Due to the capacity-approaching performance, Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1] have been widely adopted as error correction schemes in various communication and storage systems. Many systems, including optical communiation system and flash memories, employ detectors with hard decision output. For such systems, extensive research efforts have been made to devise simple decoders with much less complexity than the traditional belief-progataion (BP) decoders [2] in order to enable low-power implementations at very high throughput. One important category of such simplified decoders is the type of bit-flipping (BF) decoders. The conventional BF decoder [3] originally proposed by Gallager can be treated as voting systems, where each coded bit receives a vote for or against its current value from each parity check sum containing this bit. A bit is flipped if the number of votes against its current value exceeds a certain threshold. Many improvements on Gallager’s original decoder have been proposed (see, for example the list in [4]). One notable improvement of the conventional BF decoder is the gradient-descent bit-flipping (GDBF) decoder [5], which takes into account the agreement between the current bit value and the initial value received from channel when determining whether to flip a bit or not.
Recently, two-bit decoders, which employ two bits for each message inside the decoders, have also received a lot of attention. Sassatelli et.al proposed a two-bit decoder [6], [7], which shows better performance than the Gallager-B decoder [1] for column-weight-four regular LDPC codes. This two-bit decoder is later generalized into the type of finitealphabet iterative decoders (FAIDs) [8]. Also, Nguyen and Vasic proposed a two-bit BF decoder [9] that outperforms the Gallager’s BF decoder for column-weight-three regular LDPC

codes. This two-bit BF decoder is lately further improved by Oh and Ha [10], who incorporate the idea of weighted bitflipping [11] into the two-bit BF decoder. In these works, twobit decoders are shown to offer attractive trade-offs between performance and complexity.
In addition, the introduction of randomization (or probablism) into decoders has become a focal point of research. It has been well-established that a probabilistic BF decoder can significantly improve performance compared to its determinisitic counterpart. Miladinovic and Fossorier first proposed the concept of a probabilistic bit-flipping decoder [12], which makes a randomized decision to flip each bit with some probability. This randomized behavior improves performance compared to the conventional BF decoder. Sundararajan et.al proposed the noisy-GDBF (N-GDBF) decoder [13] by introducing some random perturbation into the GDBF decoder, and demonstrated the greatly improved performance for the additive-white-Gaussian-noise (AWGN) channel. Rasheed et.al combined the concept proposed in [12] with the GDBF decoder to propose the probabilistic GDBF (P-GDBF) decoder [14] for the binary symmetric channel (BSC). The P-GDBF considerably enhances decoder performance compared to the GDBF decoder, and closes the performance gap between the BF decoders and the standard BP decoder. After that, Le et.al proposed a probablisitic parallel BF (PPBF) decoder [15] that avoids the global sorting of the energy functions of all the bits and increases throughput, while maintaining performance comparable to a P-GDBF decoder.
Despite the advantages mentioned above, the P-GDBF and PPBF decoders still have some drawbacks. The first issue is that decoder convergence is still very slow compared to the BP decoder. Also, they usually require a careful optimization of the probability of flipping bits, and realizing a Bernoulli random variable with the optimum probability values (such as 0.7 [14]) usually incurs a relatively large hardware cost. In addition, randomization has also been introduced into FAID decoders by evaluating FAID decoders that have faulty hardware [16], but so far no performance improvement compared to the determinisitic FAIDs has been reported in the literature.

II. REVIEW OF TWO-BIT FAIDS
An (n, k) LDPC code is defined by the null space of a sparse parity-check matrix H = [ht,j]0≤t<m,0≤j<n. Let G(CG, VG, EG) denote the Tanner graph [20] associated with the parity-check matrix H, where CG, VG and EG denote the sets of check nodes (CNs), variable nodes (VNs) and edges in G, respectively. Suppose a codeword v = (v0, v1, ..., vn−1) is transmitted over the BSC and let r = (r0, r1, ..., rn−1) denote the output of the BSC corresponding to v, where vi, ri ∈ {0, 1} for 0 ≤ i < n. Let y = (y0, y1, ..., yn−1) be the input to a message-passing iterative decoder for decoding of v, where yi = (−1)ri C with 0 ≤ i < n, and C is an arbitary positive number.

IV. EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS
We demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed MPTBFAID by simulation results. In order to show the necessity and advantage of our MPTB-FAID, we use the following two other FAIDs as reference decoders for comparison. One reference FAID is the TB-FAID described in Section II, and the other FAID, formulated in the Appendix A, is also a probabilistic two-bit (PTB) FAID but does not replace the v2c messages by a-posteriori messages, and is referred to as PTB-FAID. For our MPTB-FAID and the PTB-FAID, we only consider the cases that the Bernoulli random variable parameters for all the VNs and all the iterations are identical.
Apparently, the choice of parameters C, S and W have significant impact on the performance of all FAIDs. In order to provide a fair comparison, we search the combination of C, S, W (and also the Bernoulli random variable parameter for PTB-FAID) resulting in the best performance for each reference decoder. This further justifies the advantage of our MPTB-FAID over the best possible reference FAIDs. In our search, C is normalized to 1 for simplicity without loss of generality, and both S and W are set to multiples of 0.125 with 0 < S ≤ W ≤ 2. Also, the random variable parameter p for the PTB-FAID is set to multiples of 0.125 with 0 ≤ p ≤ 1.
Example 1: In this example, we simulate a quasi-cyclic [22] (QC) LDPC code Cqc, whose parity-check matrix has regular column weight of 4 and is composed of a 19 × 144 array of 256 × 256 submatrices. Each submatrix is either a circulant permutation matrix or a zero matrix. In addition to the two-bit FAIDs mentioned above, GDBF, P-GDBF and two 7-bit layered MS decoders [23] (with and without check-tovariable (c2v) message scaling factors [21]) are also simulated as references. For the layered MS decoders, the maximum number of iterations is set to 20 and the scaling factor for the

scaled layered-MS decoder is set to 0.75, the value which optimizes decoder performance. The maximum number of iterations is set to 300 for all the other deocders.
It follows from Fig. 1 that the proposed MPTB-FAID has 1-2 orders of performance gain over both TB-FAID and PTB-FAID. This shows the benefit and necessity of our proposed modification, especially the replacement of v2c messages by total a-posteriori messages. Compared to PGDBF, the proposed MPTB-FAID achieves nearly 3 orders of performance gain. Despite the two-bit nature, our MPTBFAID has Bernoulli random variable parameter of 0.5, as shown in Table I, which is much easier to implement than the value of 0.95 that optimizes the performance for the P-GDBF. In addition, our MPTB-FAID does not need to compute the flipping function as required by P-GDBF (eq. (3) in [14]), which needs 4 bits of storage per VN for a column-weight4 code. We also see that our MPTB-FAID outperforms the MS-decoder without c2v message scaling factor, and performs within 2 orders of the scaled MS-decoder for rBER ≥ 0.009. The average number of iterations for various decoders are included in Fig. 2, where we see that compared to TB-FAID and PTB-FAID, our MPTB-FAID can reduce the average number of iterations for rBER ≥ 0.009, and has an only slightly higher average iteration count in the lower rBER region. This is especially interesting if we prefer a faster decoder at a higher rBER region with better performance. Such a decoder can be used as an effective initial decoding attempt to successfully decode most of the codewords. We also see that compared to the P-GDBF, our MPTB-FAID requires a much smaller average number of iterations, at least for higher rBER region.

V. CONCLUSION AND REMARKS
In this paper, we presented a modified probabilistic twobit finite-alphabet iterative decoder. Besides introducing randomization, we also combine the idea of using a-posteriori message to replace the conventional variable-to-check (v2c) messages, into the FAIDs. The proposed decoder outperforms other known simplified decoders for high-rate regular LDPC

codes, with a very hardware-friendly implementation complexity.
It is worth mentioning that the new MPTB-FAID is studied in a very heuristic way. Therefore, the systematic guideline of finding the optimized parameters related with the MPTBFAID and analyzing how the parameters affect the performance of the MPTB-FAID are interesting research topics. Also, a theoretically interesting work is to evaluate the best possible performance for the MPTB-FAID without requiring hardware-friendly parameters. Moreover, in terms of hardware implementation, it is also worth studying the most efficient approach to generation of the randomized sequence used in the MPTB-FAID.
It is well-established that irregular LDPC codes with optimized degree distribution have significant performance advantage over regular codes with BP-based decoders. Consequently, the generalization of the MPTB-FAID to irregular LDPC codes deserves further investigation.
