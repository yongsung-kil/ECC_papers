Performance analysis of a reduced complexity decoding algorithm based on an improved offset
min-sum approximation

Abstract— The phenomenal growth in the field of wireless communication has increased the need for secure data transmission and reception over noisy channel conditions. In this context, many soft decision based LDPC decoding algorithms are considered as a prime front runner for meeting the requirements of good error rate performance with minimal decoding complexity. This paper presents a reduced complexity decoding algorithm based on an improved offset approximation. The results of this work is analyzed through numerical simulations in terms of error rate performance and convergence speed. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed scheme attains enhanced decoding stability with a faster rate of convergence without enhancing the computational complexity.
Keywords— Computational Complexity; Convergence rate; LDPC Codes; Offset approximation; Wireless Communication.
I. INTRODUCTION
In recent times, the advanced contemporary communication systems have evolved to a greater extent and so as the demand for error free communication of reliable information over the noisy environment. However, since their renascence, the Low-density Parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] is widely embodied as the most sought after error correcting code due to their characteristic nature of capacity approaching performance. Many research works have demonstrated the practical importance of irregular LDPC codes in the field of digital, wireless, quantum and optical communications. However, for the low complexity hardware realization of irregular LDPC codes, an efficient decoding algorithm which can provide a reliable balance between error rate performance and complexity is still persists as an open research challenge. In general, the problem of decoding irregular LDPC codes [2] using iterative decoding scheme is two-fold: i) computational complexity and ii) error correcting capability. Many research works have come up with many innovative solutions to meet the constraints of this trade-off problem. The most prominent iterative scheme widely adopted for irregular LDPC codes is the Min-Sum decoding algorithm (MSA) with multiple error correction factors [3]. These algorithms have demonstrated that they can achieve good error correcting performance with moderate decoding convergence. However, on the bleak side, these algorithms have increased memory accessing bits per iteration. This in turn introduces an additional decoding iterations leading to numerical instabilities and significantly higher error floors.
Many researchers have proven that the decoding schemes for irregular LDPC codes with multiple error correction factors can perform better than the conventional ones.

Moreover, the evolution in the iterative decoding process has proven that the error correction factors can be employed simultaneously to both the nodes during message updating process. However, the main problem due to the utilization of multiple error correction factor is that if the message updating is not processed uniformly in accordance the variable node degree distribution, it directly affects the convergence speed of the decoding process. Furthermore, it may lead to magnitude overestimation or non-negligible hardware overhead.

II. FRAMEWORK
In this work, an adaptive threshold based offset min-sum scheme is proposed to enhance the error correcting performance at low SNRs without introducing any error floors. The proposed scheme averts the magnitude over the estimation issue by adopting scaling and offset factors at the check node and variable node update process. This fastens the convergence rate by lowering the iteration count. For analyzing the performance of the proposed scheme, a (N, K) LDPC code C is considered with an M × N parity check matrix H. Based on the threshold factor, the outgoing bits are classified as stable and non-stable by measuring the overall reliability checksum of the unsatisfied (non-converged) check nodes. If more non-converging bits are processed to the next level of the error correcting process, the iteration count is increased. Then the overall memory accessing bits per iteration also increases, which may lead to numerical instabilities and increased computational complexity. To mitigate this effect a stifling factor is employed along with the conventional offset error correction factor.

III. PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS AND RESULTS
To evaluate the decoding performance of this scheme , a Wi-MAX irregular rate 1/2 LDPC code of block length 2304 is employed. In Fig.1, the illustration of the Bit Error Rate (BER) performance of different decoding schemes is depicted. The decoding iteration limit is fixed at 12. The experimental results shown in Fig. 1 illustrates that the this scheme achieves better coding gain when compared to as of the algorithms such as RCMSA [5], DOMSA [6] and I-POMSA [7]. At BER of 10-6, the coding gain achieved by proposed scheme with respect to DOMSA is 0.16 dB. Similarly, at a BER of 10-6 the proposed scheme has improved coding gain of 0.1 dB and 0.08 dB when compared with I-POMSA and RCMSA. Moreover, the proposed scheme is only 0.02 dB away from BPA [8].
On the other hand, good results in terms of decoding convergence are achieved in the waterfall region which is evident from Fig.2. In the light of results evident from Fig.2, it is apparent that this methedology and BPA have a lower iteration count compared to other schemes [5-7]. Basically the BPA has the fastest convergence speed among the other algorithms, but it also has huge time complexity constraints for error correction. This is avoided in our proposed scheme by utilization of multiple error correction factors using heuristic simulations [9-10]. Which in turn influences the error correcting performance. However, the enhanced rate of convergence achieved by the proposed scheme marginally increases the computational complexity [11].

IV. CONCLUSION
This work presents a reduced complexity decoding algorithm based on an improved offset min-sum approximation. The aim of this work is to attain a better stability between error rate performance and computational complexity. The validity of the proposed scheme when tested on irregular Wi-MAX standard LDPC codes are shown to outperform conventional algorithms in terms of BER. Meanwhile, the numerical simulations illustrate that the proposed algorithm has a better decoding stability and enhanced decoding acceleration. This results in a faster rate of convergence with minimized error propagation. Therefore, based on the numerical results, the proposed scheme is found more suitable for high speed communication systems which requires a better decoding balance in accordance with implementation complexity.
