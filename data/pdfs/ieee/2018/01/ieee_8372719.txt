Early Termination Belief Propagation Decoder with Parity Check Matrix for Polar Codes

Hsin-Fu Yu Huang-Chang Lee
Dept. of Electrical Engineering. Chang Gung University, Taoyuan City, Taiwan, R.O.C. barneyfishyu@gmail.com hclee@mail.cgu.edu.tw

AbstractтАФIn this paper, a belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm with early termination is proposed for polar code. The early termination is accomplished using the syndrome generated by the parity-check matrix, which can be transferred from the generator matrix. With the assistance of the early termination, the BP decoder can provide not only a better bit error rate performance but also significantly reduce the decoding complexity. For (1024, 512) polar codes, the average numbers of iterations can be reduced from 80 to 23 when Eb/N0=3 dB. The reduction in the computation complexity is about 70%.
Keyword - Polar codes, belief propagation, parity check matrix, early termination
I. INTRODUCTION
Polar code has been adopted in upcoming 5G standard. There are two commonly used approaches for decoding: successive cancellation (SC) [2] and belief propagation (BP) [3] algorithms. Although SC algorithm has been proved to provide capacity-achieving error correcting capability when it is applied to an infinite-length code, BP algorithm is more suitable for high-throughput application since its decoding process can be processed in parallel computation. In order to achieve a satisfactory error-rate performance, a large number of iterations are required for the BP decoding algorithm. In order to reduce the decoding complexity of polar code, early termination technique is essential.
Early termination have been used in many iterative decoding algorithms, such as the SPA for low density parity check (LDPC) codes and BCJR algorithm for turbo codes. For linear block codes, syndrome can be generated from the parity-check matrix and can be used to confirm the legal codewords. However, polar codes are defined by the generator matrices rather than the parity-check matrices. Using the predetermined values (all zero) for frozen bits, the paritycheck matrices can be transferred from the generator matrices. Then the syndrome can also be generated by the parity-check matrices for polar codes, and can be used to check the decoding results. An all zero syndrome means that the outcome is a legal codeword and further iterations are not required, i.e., the decoding process can be early terminated.

The benefit of early termination is not limited to reducing the number of iteration. When the BP decoding is applied to polar code without early termination, the decoding process must keep going until the number of iterations reaches the predetermined maximum value. In some case, the decoding result of the final iteration is not correct, but a correct decoding result may be achieved before the maximum number of iteration is reached. In other words, the decoding once converges to the correct codeword but has been misled to an error due to the following iterations. Therefore, it is possible to improve the error-rate performance using early termination.
On the other hand, the all-zero syndrome can only guarantee that the outcome is a legal codeword, but cannot ensure it is the transmitted one, then the undetectable error occurs. According to the simulation results, the probability of undetectable error is much lower than that of the decoding process been misled to an error. Therefore, the performance of BP decoding with parity check matrix is better than original BP decoding.

V. DISCUSSION
For the polar codes adopted in the upcoming 5G standard, the early termination technique has been proposed for the highthroughput BP decoding algorithm. The parity-check matrix transferred from the generator matrix is used to generate the syndrome, where an all zero syndrome denotes a legal codeword and triggers the early termination. With the assistance early termination, the BP algorithm can effectively reduce the number of iteration and also improves let the errorrate performance.
