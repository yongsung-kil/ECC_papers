Error Characterization and ECC Usage Relaxation beyond 20nm Floating Gate NAND Flash Memory

S.H. Ku*, T. W. Lin*, C.H. Cheng*, C.W. Lee*,Ti-Wen Chen*, Wen-Jer Tsai*, T.C. Lu*, W.P. Lu*, K.C. Chen*, Tahui Wang†,*, and Chih-Yuan Lu*

Abstract
Endurance of floating gate flash memories at 19nm node and beyond is studied comprehensively. Experiments reveal that the random telegraph noise (RTN) would degrade the read margin with a tail, which quickly reshapes into a symmetric Gaussian form in a lightly-stressed state. After heavy stress, the lower part of tail would spread further while the upper part keeps roughly overlapped with that during fresh. This unique behavior, which was firstly measured by the self-established Budget Product Tester (BPT), can be explained by stressinduced hole trap creation. To investigate the impact of RTN on operation window, a novel algorithm of Multi-TimesVerify accompanied with the optimal Read-Retry (MTVR2) is proposed and validated by BPT. The advantage of MTVR2 to reduce the requirement of Error-Correcting Code (ECC) bit is demonstrated. Finally, the improvement of bit error rate (BER) in TLC operation with MTVR2 is also evaluated.

Introduction
NAND flash memory is an ubiquitous storage media for many electronic products, such as smart phones and solid-state drives [1,2]. To meet great demands for lower bit cost as increasing storage capacity, multi-level cell (MLC) or even triple-level cell (TLC) has become the dominate technique [3]. In NAND flash memory, the logical value is determined by the cell's Vt. More bits in a cell denote more segment of Vt packet. Each packet should be as small as possible to get sufficient margin. Increase of cell-to-cell interference with smaller dimension of advanced technology also consumes the read margin. RTN, moreover, has made further scaling impractical [4]. Many innovated designs either in algorithm or architecture have been demonstrated to overcome interference [5-7] while only Error Correction Code (ECC) is an effective strategy of RTN mitigation [8,9]. Conventionally, a Bose–Chaudhuri– Hocquenghem (BCH) ECC is often adopted to correct bit errors of NAND flash memory [10]. Beyond 20nm node, however, realistic BCH ECC can no longer suppress BER under a few percent (%) [9,11] and low-density parity-check (LDPC) emerges as the most promising ECC candidate [12-14]. With LDPC, data are decoded iteratively according to additional soft information from extra reads. Complex, bulky, and powerhungry circuitry for intensive calculation is necessary to achieve acceptable error correction. [15]. It would become terribly worse with reliability concern at high endurance when BER increases exponentially with P/E cycle number [16-18]. Recent research has suggested that novel multi-times program verification helps to simplify process development and to reduce ECC requirement, potentially extending the feasibility of BCH beyond 20nm node [19]. Another correction mechanism, Read-Retry [20] or optimal read reference voltage (VREF) [21], is also introduced to improve BER.
In this work, endurance of 1x nm NAND flash memory applying both MTV as well as Read-Retry is characterized. Chip-level data are easily evaluated and analyzed with the turnkey equipment packages consisting of the BPT and rigorous statistical techniques [19,22]. Experiment and Instrument System
MLC FG NAND products with 32Gb density in 19nm technology were performed in this experiment. The chip is divided into 1064 blocks, each consists of 256 pages. Generally, two-step FN programming scheme is utilized in MLC application to alleviate the severe interference. In order to collect reliable and comprehensive information during characterization, a low-cost system, integrated with FPGA and a variety of electronic components, was built as demonstrated in Fig. 2(a) and (b). User interface built upon Excel helps to achieve one-click operation from test-flow creation, automatic control, to data analysis, and is able to extract the whole block Vt of 4MB density within 1.5 minutes steadily. To address endurance performance, randomized data programming is often adopted to neglect the worst data pattern statistically [23] and its sequence generated by BPT is shown in Fig. 2(c).

In the case of lightly-stressed condition under #P/E=1, the tail would quickly re-distributes as Gaussian one at other readings [19]. Previously stated that positive oxide trap creation is dominant around thousands of P/E cycling number [26,27]. The wellknown existence of donor-like bulk oxide traps appears to be electrically neutral if occupied by electrons [28]. Information of stress-induced traps can only be received during the escape of occupied electrons, which responses to a spreading LBVt. To further depict the tail, RTN amplitude among the whole page, the lower tail and the upper tail are compared in Fig. 5(a) and Fig. 5(b). As expected, a symmetric and a negligibly influence on the main part of RTN is shown in the whole page data. But the shapes are distorted in the two tails. Since the lower (upper) tail’s Vts are “born” to be lower (higher) than that of the 2nd reading, more data tend to be accumulated in the positive (negative) axis of RTN amplitude, thus resulting in the distortion in Fig. 5(b).

(2) Integration of Multi-Times Verify and Read-Retry (MTVR2)

Normally, re-distribution of the lower tail would still follows the principle of FN operation, in which the amount of Vt shift almost equals to the program step pulse voltage in FG flash memories owing to its ISPP rate of 1 [29]. As reported in ref. 18, elimination of the tail spreading by MTV tightens the Vt window. Since the extra verification is applied, the program time variation in MTV scheme must be given special consideration. The program throughput (PTP) with the overhead of extra verification and additional program shot can be accessed as below:

Page size PTP=
(tpgm+tPV×level)×#shot + (tpgm+tPV×#MTV)×level

where tpgm is the average one-shot program time, level represents for the numbers of reference voltage (MLC=3 and TLC=7), #shot is the shot number, tPV is the PV time and #MTV is the applying number of MTV, individually. At first glance, throughput seems to be degraded but other factors should be considered in further. Once MTV is implemented, tiny ISPP step to compact the Vt distribution is unnecessary. In other words, less #shot is sufficient to preserved the same operation window. In Fig. 6, the PTP is calculated under various #MTV and is normalized to its value without applying MTV. Comparable or even better throughput can be realized in the early stage of #MTV.
In past technology node, typical ECC deals with the read error at a fixed VREF but it lacks strength as cell downscaling. Experimentally, the window between “E” and “A” states is most severely affected after P/E stress because of the worst interference and read disturbance. In Fig. 7, the distorted Vt

Conclusion
Characterization of error induced by RTN in 19nm FG flash memories is studied by our self-built budget product tester. For a lightly-stressed condition, the RTN tail would transform into to a symmetric shape after sequential readings. Then, the tail re-spreads further as an asymmetric one in a heavilystressed condition. Both different shapes found to have common upper part, which can be elucidated by stress-induced hole trap creation. To mitigate the RTN broadening caused Vt window loss, the proposed MTVR2 scheme is adopted to reduce the BER at least by 60% for block with 3k P/E cycles. In addition, MTVR2 shows the enlargement of reserved margin and the relaxation of ECC’s burden, especially in nextgeneration technology node or TLC operation.
