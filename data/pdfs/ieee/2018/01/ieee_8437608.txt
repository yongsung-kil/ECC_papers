High-rate Spatially Coupled Codes for Channels with Synchronous Errors
Ryohei Goto and Kenta Kasai Tokyo Institute of Technology, 2-12-1, Ookayama, Meguroku, Tokyo, 152-8550, Japan.

Abstract—In this paper, we deal with coding for synchronous errors. In [1], we introduced a synchronously erroneous finite state Markov channel model whose SIR is computable. Numerical experiments demonstrated spatially-coupled codes approach the SIR of the channel. However, at high-rate region, there is a gap to the SIR even if very long code length was used. In this paper, for the channel, we apply density evolution analysis [2] and the extended version for FSMC [3]. The results show that the threshold of protograph coupled codes is degraded at highrate region, while random coupled codes approach the SIR even in the high-rate region.
I. INTRODUCTION
Levenshtein introduced single insertion/deletion/substitution error correcting codes [4]. To correct multiple insertion/deletion/substitution errors, Davey et al. [5] introduced a concatenation of low-density parity check (LDPC) codes and watermark codes. Wang et al. [6] and Inoue et al. [7] used periodic markers instead of watermark codes. These coding schemes could correct some synchronization errors, however additional periodic markers or watermark codes lead to rate loss. Periodic markers are generated independently from data bits. This prevents us to approach the theoretical limit of coding rate.
In [1], the authors developed coding techniques without periodic markers for synchronization errors with bounded position deviation. Furthermore, we proposed a coding scheme using LDPC codes and spatially-coupled (SC) codes and a decoding scheme using factor graph. Numerical experiment shows that SC codes approach symmetric information rate (SIR) at moderate rate region. However, at high-rate region, there is a gap to the SIR even if very long code length was used.
In this paper, for the channel, we apply density evolution analysis [2] and the extended version for FSMC [3]. The results show that the threshold of protograph coupled codes is degraded at high-rate region, while random coupled codes approach the SIR even in the high-rate region.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we apply Kavcic’s method to the channels with synchronous errors and calculate density evolution for two types of spatially coupled codes. From the results, we know that, high-rate protograph codes do not approach SIR while random coupled codes approach to SIR for smaller blocklength than protograph codes.
