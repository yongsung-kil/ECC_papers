A Distributed CRC Early Termination Scheme for High Throughput QC-LDPC Codes
Hao Wu1,2, Fang Wang1, Yuqing Yuan1 1Department of Wireless Product R&D Institute, ZTE Corporation 2State Key Laboratory of Mobile Netwok and Mobile Multimedia Technology, ZTE Corporation
Shenzhen, China wu.hao19@zte.com.cn

Abstract—In order to avoid unnecessary computation and increase system throughput, various early termination schemes are adopted in quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) decoders. Cyclic redundancy check (CRC) based early termination scheme can be used when the information bits are concatenated with the CRC. The CRC is usually checked at the end of an iteration to verify the integrity of information bits. The latency of the CRC calculation results in an unnecessary decoding process. In this paper, we propose a distributed CRC early termination scheme for high throughput QC-LDPC codes. The proposed scheme distributes the CRC calculation in each layer of the iteration to minimize unnecessary computation and increase system throughput.
Index Terms—CRC, QC-LDPC, early termination
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-cyclic low-density party-check (QC-LDPC) is the capacity approaching code and is developed into many wireless communication standards, such as the 3rd generation partnership project new radio (3GPP NR) and the advanced television systems committee 3.0 (ATSC 3.0) [1]-[3]. The power consumption and the decoding delay grow linearly with the number of iterations. Various early terminations (such as the maximum iteration number (MIN) based scheme, the parity check equations (PCE) based scheme, the cyclic redundancy check (CRC) based scheme, etc.) are used in the QC-LDPC decoders to improve energy efficiency and increase system throughput.
The MIN based scheme stops the decoding process when the maximum number of iterations is reached. This scheme is rarely used alone. The PCE based scheme stops the decoding process when the signs of the log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) of the information bits and the parity bits satisfy all of the parity check equations. All of the parity check equations are satisfied only when the information bits and the parity bits are correctly decoded with high probability. The degree of information nodes is usually much larger than the degree of parity nodes. Because of the wave effect [4], the information bits tend to correctly decoded more rapidly than the parity bits. Unnecessary iterations are needed to decode the remaining uncorrected parity bits. The CRC based scheme stops the decoding process when the integrity of information bits is verified. The parity bits need not to be correct when the decoding process is stopped.

The CRC is usually checked at the end of an iteration to verify the integrity of information bits [5]-[6]. When the information bits are correctly decoded at the end of an iteration, the latency of the CRC calculation results in an unnecessary decoding process. In this paper, a distributed CRC early termination scheme is proposed for high throughput QC-LDPC codes. The information bits are divided into nonoverlapping segments. The CRC of overall information bits are obtained by combing the CRC of these non-overlapping segments. Based on the structure of the QC-LDPC codes, the integrity of information bits can be partial verified before the end of an iteration. This reduces the unnecessary computation. Thus the energy efficiency and the system throughput are improved compared to the traditional schemes.
There are a few works ([7]-[9]) in the context of distributing CRC to reduce the decoding latency. The focus of [7] is on the turbo code. In [7], the code block is divided into a number of subblocks depending on the decoding parallelism. The CRC calculation units concurrently process a portion of these subblocks on every clock cycle. The focus of [8]-[9] is on the polar code. In [8]-[9], the CRC bits are distributed throughout the information block via an interleaver to reduce the decoding latency. In this paper, we focus on the QC-LDPC code. And the detailed process of the CRC is different from [7]-[9].
This paper is structured as follows: Section II illustrates the system model of the QC-LDPC encoder and decoder. Section III derives three CRC properties to simplify the implementation of the decoder. Section IV gives the distributed CRC early termination scheme. Section V presents the simulation results to show the advantage of the proposed scheme. And the conclusion is given in Section VI.

V. SIMULATION RESULTS

Simulation is carried out over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel to compare the proposed scheme and the traditional schemes. The QC-LDPC code of the NR standard is used [1]. Two base matrices, whose sizes are 5×27 and 46 × 68 respectively, are chosen in the simulation. The modulation types are quadrature phase-shift keying (QPSK) and 64 quadrature amplitude modulation (64QAM). The cyclic generator polynomials is

G(x) = x24 + x23 + x6 + x5 + x + 1

(19)

This ensures that the probability of undetected error is sufficient small and has a negligible impact on the performance of higher layer protocols. The QC-LDPC code is decoded with the belief propagation decoder. The minimal number of iterations and the maximal number of iterations are 2 and 12 respectively. The other simulation parameters are: L = 8448, Z = 384 and R = 96.
The block error ratio (BLER) performance as a function of signal to noise ratio (SNR) is plotted in Fig. 3. The BLER performances of the proposed scheme and the traditional schemes are almost the same. The strategy of the early termination scheme has little effect on the BLER performance.

The average number of iterations as a function of BLER is plotted in Fig. 4. The PCE based scheme is implemented as in [11]. We see that the average number of iterations of the proposed scheme is smaller than that of the traditional schemes. If the modulation type is 64QAM and the H size is 5 × 27, the average iteration number of the proposed scheme is 14.5% and 4.5% lower than that of the PCE-based scheme and the traditional CRC-based scheme when BLER is equal to 1.2 × 10−3. If the modulation type is QPSK and the H size is 46 × 68, the average iteration number of the proposed scheme is 11.2% and 1.4% lower than that of the PCE-based scheme and the traditional CRC-based scheme when BLER is equal to 1.3 × 10−3. Since the throughput of the QC-LDPC decoder is inverse proportional to the average iteration number, the throughput of the proposed scheme is improved as compared to that of the traditional schemes.
VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a distributed CRC early termination scheme for high throughput QC-LDPC codes is proposed. The CRC calculation of the proposed scheme is distributed in each layer of the iterations. Compared to the traditional schemes, the proposed scheme minimizes unnecessary computation and increases system throughput.
