Dynamic-Reference-Voltage-based Detection Algorithm for LDPC-Coded NAND Flash Memory
Wenjie Liu1, Guojun Han1, Ruiquan He1, Yi Fang1,2, and Guofa Cai1 1School of Information Engineering, Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou 510006, China 2National Mobile Communications Research Laboratory, Southeast University, Nanjing 210096, China Email: wenjie liu@yeah.net; { gjhan, fangyi }@gdut.edu.cn; 1535387791@qq.com; caiguofa2006@126.com

Abstract—In flash memory, the overlap region between the threshold-voltage distributions of two adjacent storage states becomes larger with the program/erase (P/E) cycles and retention time, which dramatically degrades the storage reliability. To address the above problem, we propose a novel dynamic-referencevoltage-based detection algorithm for LDPC-coded NAND flash memory with both cell-to-cell interference (CCI) and retention noise. Specifically, we first employ Monte-Carlo simulations to analyze the threshold voltages of each storage state, and find that these threshold voltages can be approximately characterized by a Gaussian distribution. According to such a distribution, we further develop a dynamic modification scheme for the reference voltages, which can efficiently update the initial likelihood ratio (LLR) for the LDPC decoder. Experimental results illustrate that the proposed detection algorithm not only can achieve significant performance gain over other existing counterparts, but also can remarkably increase the overall P/E cycles and retention time. Thanks to the aforementioned superiorities, the proposed detection algorithm appears to be an excellent alternative to other counterparts for future high-density flash memory.
Index Terms—NAND flash memory, detection algorithms, LDPC codes, cell-to-cell interference, retention noise.
I. INTRODUCTION
With the continual increase of storage density, NAND flash memory cells become more vulnerable to channel noise, which significantly degrades the storage reliability. In such scenarios, conventional algebraic error-correcting codes (ECCs), such as Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) and Reed-Solomon (RS) codes [1], can no longer satisfy the high-reliability requirement for the next-generation flash memory. To address the challenging problem, more powerful random ECCs, such as the low-density parity check (LDPC) codes [2], have been considered as one preferable choice for ultra-high-density flash memory systems. Different from algebraic ECCs, LDPC codes can be decoded by belief propagation (BP) algorithm [3], [4], in which soft information (e.g., log-likelihood-ratio (LLR) information) is updated iteratively so as to achieve superior error performance in flash memory. Therefore, the decoding performance of LDPC codes is highly dependent on the LLRs output from flash-memory channels.
In order to obtain accurate channel initial LLR information, it is crucial to estimate the distribution of threshold voltages. Nevertheless, the threshold-voltage probability density functions (PDFs) corresponding to two adjacent storage states are severely overlapped as the program-erase (P/E) cycles

and retention time increase, which results in lower storage reliability. It has been demonstrated in [5] that the threshold voltages can be approximately modeled by a Gaussian mixture distribution with the increasing of P/E cycles. Following this work, an expectation-maximization (EM) algorithm has been developed to estimate the mean and variance of such Gaussian mixture distribution [6]. Based on the estimated mean and variance, a new LLR calculation method has also been proposed to improve the performance of LDPC-coded flash memory. On the other hand, a two-step decoding algorithm has been devised to enhance robustness against retention noise in flash memory [7], [8]. In [9], [10], the authors have analyzed the effect of retention noise on the threshold-voltage distribution and then developed an exhaustive-search-based optimization scheme for the reference voltages in flash memory. However, the channel initial LLRs have not been optimized in that work.
To overcome the aforementioned drawbacks, we propose a dynamic-reference-voltage-based detection algorithm for LDPC-coded flash memory, which suffers from both P/E cycles and retention time. In such a scenario, the performance of flash memory is not only related to CCI, but also dependent on the retention noise. Taking these two channel imperfections into consideration, we analyze the PDF of threshold voltages corresponding to each storage state and find that it approximately follows a Gaussian distribution. As a further insight, we derive the intersection of the threshold-voltage PDFs corresponding to two adjacent storage states, which can be utilized to adjust the reference voltages. Finally, we exploit the adjusted reference voltages to enhance the reliability of channel initial LLR, and hence improve the overall performance of LDPC-coded flash memory systems. Simulations show that the proposed detection algorithm not only exhibits better error performance and convergence speed with respect to other existing counterparts, but possesses more powerful resistance to P/E cycles and retention time as well.

III. DYNAMIC-REFERENCE-VOLTAGE-BASED DETECTION ALGORITHM
Based on the channel model in Section II, a dynamicreference-voltage-based detection algorithm is proposed for the LDPC-coded flash-memory systems suffering from both CCI and retention noise. The proposed detection algorithm comprises three steps: i) Estimating the threshold-voltage distribution; ii) Adjusting the reference voltages that determine the storage states; and iii) Optimizing the channel initial LLRs.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
We employ a rate-0.9014 (448, 4544) quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC code, 15-level non-uniform sensing scheme and minsum decoding algorithm to validate the performance of the proposed detection algorithm. In our simulations, the parameters of flash memory channel are set as follows: cell-tocell coupling strength factor is 1.4, μe = 1.4, σe = 0.35, ΔVpp = 0.2, Ks = 0.333, x0 = 1.4, At = 3.5 × 10−5, Bt = 2.35 × 10−4, ai = 0.62, ao = 0.3, Vp = 2.6, 3.2, and 3.8. In simulations, “Proposed DET”, “Proposed Simp. DET”, and “Proposed DET/no-LLR-Opt” represent the proposed dynamic-reference-voltage-based detection algorithms with the integration-form LLR optimization (8), (9), with the simplified-from LLR optimization (19)-(21), and without any LLR optimization. For comparison, we also consider Cai’s detection algorithm in [10] and the conventional detection without any adjustment of reference voltages and LLRs [13].
Fig. 3 shows the FER curves of proposed detection algorithm, conventional detection algorithm and Cai’s detection algorithm. It can be seen that the proposed detection algorithm with P/E cycles = 30K has better FER performance than the conventional detection algorithm with P/E cycles = 20K. Furthermore, for a given P/E cycles, our proposed detection algorithm benefits from a stronger resistance to retention time with respect to both Cai’s and conventional detection algorithms. For example, to achieve an FER of 1 × 10−3 at P/E cycles = 30K, the proposed detection algorithms with integration-form LLR optimization, simplified-form LLR optimization, and without LLR optimization possess retention times of 116, 000, 32, 400, and 8, 300 hours, respectively; while Cai’s and conventional detection algorithms only have retention times of 6, 800 and 2.8 hours, respectively.
Fig. 4 plots the FER curves versus P/E cycles of three types of detection algorithms over a flash memory channel. As can be seen, Given a fixed retention time, the proposed detection algorithm has more powerful robustness against P/E cycles than conventional and Cai’s detection algorithms.
As a further insight, we compare the average iteration numbers required to decode each codeword for three types of detection algorithms in Fig. 5. Referring to this figure, the proposed detection algorithm has a faster convergence speed than Cai’s and conventional detection algorithms. In this paper, we conducted an insightful study on the performance of LDPC-coded flash memory in the presence of CCI and retention noise. We put forward a novel detection algorithm for flash-memory channel in order to improve the decoding performance of LDPC codes.
