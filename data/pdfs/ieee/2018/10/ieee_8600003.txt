An Improved Variable-Node-Based BP Decoding Algorithm for NAND Flash Memory

Guojun Yang, Xingcheng Liu, Xuechen Chen
School of Electronics and Information Technology Sun Yat-sen University Guangzhou, China
e-mail: isslxc@mail.sysu.edu.cn

Zhongfeng Wang
School of Electronic Science and Engineering Nanjing University Nanjing, China
e-mail: zfwang@gmail.com

Abstract—To solve the problems of the data reliability for NAND flash storages, a variable-node-based belief-propagation with message pre-processing (VNBP-MP) decoding algorithm for binary low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is proposed. The major feature is that, by making use of the characteristics of the NAND flash channel, the proposed algorithm performs the message pre-processing (MP) scheme to effectively prevent the propagation of unreliable messages and speed up the propagation of reliable messages. To further speed up the decoding convergence, the treatment for oscillating variable nodes (VNs) is considered after the MP scheme being employed. Simulation results show that the proposed VNBP-MP algorithm has a noticeable improvement in convergence speed without compromising the error-correction performance, compared with the existing algorithms.
Keywords-NAND flash memory; LDPC code; message preprocessing; belief-propagation decoding; error-correction performance
I. INTRODUCTION
NAND flash memory is a non-volatile storage technology which has been widely used in data storage systems and consumer electronics such as solid-state disks (SSDs) because of its noticeable advantages of high performance, small physical size, large storage capacity and low power consumption [1]. However, aggressive scaling down of the cell size makes each memory cell store fewer electrons, and narrows the voltage margin between the adjacent storage states. Hence, severe noises are introduced [2] [3], and the data reliability is largely degraded. Moreover, the operational lifetime which can be characterized by the number of program and erase (PE) cycles and retention ages, is shortened.
To overcome the above reliability issues, it is essential to employ the technology of error-correction-codes (ECCs). However, as the cell size becomes smaller and smaller, the raw bit error rate (RBER) grows so rapidly that traditional ECCs, such as Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) codes, are becoming inadequate to correct a great number of bit errors [4]. In this situation, ECCs with outstanding errorcorrection capability are urgently demanded. Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, which have been considered as a major candidate of ECCs for future NAND flash storage systems, are adopted to correct the errors induced by the noises.

LDPC codes are usually decoded using iterative softdecision belief-propagation (BP) decoding algorithms, which have the promising error-correction performance. BP scheduling strategies can be divided into three types, flooding scheduling (FS) [5], standard serial scheduling (SSS) [6] and informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) [7], respectively. SSS provides convergence speed twice as fast as FS [8]. IDS has an obvious advantage in the error-correction performance, compared with FS and SSS. However, the IDS algorithms have the extremely high decoding complexity in the computation of residuals.
To meet the requirements of the low decoding latency in the applications of NAND flash storage systems, numerous FS-based and SSS-based algorithms for the NAND flash memory have been proposed. For example, to improve the LDPC decoding throughput, the intracell data dependence aware min-sum decoding scheme was proposed [9]. The retention-aware belief-propagation (RA-BP) decoding scheme was employed to recover the retention errors without additional memory sensing operations [10]. In addition, the retention error aware LDPC decoding (REAL) scheme [4] and cooperative error correction (CooECC) scheme [11] were both developed to improve the NAND flash read performance. The low-complexity quantization-aware beliefpropagation (QABP) decoding scheme with the unequal allocation of computational resources, was presented to improve the convergence speed [12].
However, the algorithms described above do not consider the propagation of unreliable messages and the effect of the oscillating variable nodes (VNs). To solve above problems, a large number of simulations are executed, and thus the special characteristics of the NAND flash channel are found that the part of VNs getting into overlapping regions are prone to be erroneous. Motivated by the channel characteristics, the message pre-processing (MP) scheme is proposed to prevent the unreliable messages from propagating to reliable VNs, and computational resources are preferentially allocated to update unreliable VNs with reliable messages in the initial stage of iterations. Therefore, the decoding process can continue in the right direction. Besides, considering the oscillation phenomenon, an oscillation-based alternate-selection-scheduling (OB-ASS) scheme is proposed. Combining the MP scheme with the OB-ASS scheme, a variable-node-based belief-propagation with message pre-processing (VNBP-MP) decoding algorithm for binary LDPC codes is proposed to achieve

II. PRELIMINARY AND MOTIVATION

A. MLC NAND Flash Memory Channel Model
Through injecting a certain amount of charge into a floating gate through Fowler-Nordheim tunneling by applying a relatively large voltage to the control gate [2], the storage state of each cell can be determined. However, affected by the channel noises, the actual voltage value is shifted to an uncertain value because the amount of charge stored in a floating gate varies with different PE cycles and retention ages. After the voltage value shifts, errors are induced as a result of the storage state difference before and after noise interference.
An MLC (multi-level cell) NAND flash memory cell stores two bits, which are configured to four different storage states for representing symbols ‘11’, ‘10’, ‘00’ and ‘01’. In order to simulate the actual MLC NAND flash memory, programming noise, cell-to-cell interference (CCI), random telegraph noise (RTN) and data retention noise [3] [13] [14] are considered to establish the MLC NAND flash channel model. As shown in Fig. 1, four black curves represent the threshold voltage distributions of four storage states respectively, while R1, R2, R3, R4, R5 and R6, represent the read reference voltages, realized by the non-uniform memory sensing strategy [14]. The configuration of read reference voltages is based on the voltage entropy function [13]. The specified read reference voltages divide the threshold voltage range into 7 regions, which incorporate 4 data regions (D1, D2, D3, D4) and 3 overlapping regions (E1, E2, E3).

III. VARIABLE-NODE-BASED BELIEF-PROPAGATION DECODING WITH MESSAGE PRE-PROCESSING
For binary LDPC codes, a VN corresponds to one bit. Therefore, as the above regular pattern hinted, the VNs can be divided into two types, blurry VNs and non-blurry VNs. The blurry VNs, which correspond to the LSBs in the regions E1 and E3, and the MSBs in the region E2, have high error probabilities, and are considered to be unreliable. The non-blurry VNs, corresponding to the rest of bits, are considered to be reliable with the relatively large LLR magnitudes.

Before the iteration, each V2C value is initialized with the LLR value of the corresponding VN. If the C2V message is only updated with V2C messages from non-blurry VNs, it is considered to be reliable and has comparatively large magnitude according to (3). Otherwise, once any unreliable V2C message generated from the blurry VN participates in the updating, the updated C2V message will become unreliable because of its indeterminate sign. Conventional SBP algorithm [6] serially updates all VNs and leads to the generation of unreliable C2V messages. To stop the propagation of unreliable messages in the initial stage of decoding, the MP scheme is proposed. By the scheme, all the computational sources are allocated to update blurry VNs with the V2C messages from non-blurry VNs.
Denote a blurry set, V, to store the blurry VNs. Besides, T0, T1 and Tk (k ≥ 2) denote various types of CNs, respectively. For example, a T1 connects only one blurry VN. The specific procedure of the MP scheme is divided into three steps, described as follows
1) Step 1: Put all the blurry VNs into V, and circularly search them in sequence. The blurry VN, v1j, connected with at least two T1, will be chosen to be updated. As mentioned previously, the updated mTk v1j is unreliable, since Tk
connects at least one blurry VN other than v1j. To prevent the propagation of the unreliable messages, the computation of
each mTk v1 j is ignored. Except for v1j, the rest of VNs
connected with each T1, are all non-blurry. Hence, the updated mT1v1j which is considered to be reliable, has a
comparatively large magnitude to execute the domination of the LLR value and V2C messages. The domination is defined below:
Domination: If the signs of the LLR value and V2C messages are wrong, the updated C2V message can change their signs. Otherwise, keep and strengthen their signs.
Therefore, updated by (4) and (5), the LLR value and V2C messages of v1j can be considered to be reliable and v1j becomes non-blurry. Each CN connected to v1j changes from Tk (or T1) to Tk-1 (or T0). At present, reliable V2C messages of v1j can be propagated for the updating of the rest blurry VNs. In this way, each v1j is updated one by one and removed from V. Step 1 ends until v1j cannot be found.
2) Step 2: The rest of blurry VNs in V, are circularly searched in sequence. Each v2j, connected with at least one T1, will be updated. Generally, if v2j is connected with only one T1, it can only receive one reliable mT1v2 j , and the
computation of each mTk v2 j is ignored. Based on the
domination, the updated LLR value and V2C messages of v2j are considered to be reliable, except for mv2 j T1 which
cannot receive any new C2V message according to (4). To limit the propagation of the unreliable V2C messages, each updated VN as stated above, is put into a set, V1. If v2j connected with two or more T1 is updated, no unreliable message is generated as described in Step 1. With the changes of CN types, all VNs in V have chances to be updated and become non-blurry. Moreover, each updated

Let vbj represent a type of blurry VNs without connecting any T1. In SSS, each vbj is updated without receiving any reliable C2V message. Hence, it is likely that each vbj cannot be corrected, and the error-correction performance and convergence performance of SSS are restricted. However, with the MP scheme, the generated messages with high reliabilities can be widely propagated, and the decoding process can take a step towards the right direction, without being affected by the unreliable messages from blurry VNs. Compared with the conventional SSS, the MP scheme can achieve faster convergence speed.

With the increase of the number of iterations, the VNs staying in the oscillating state, are error-prone [16]. The oscillation reason is related to the structure of the parity check matrix. Hence, the oscillation phenomenon appears in nearly all the BP algorithms [17]. To further speed up the convergence, the oscillating VNs should be treated. Firstly, define the oscillating set, O, given below
Oscillating set O: In the iterative decoding, a VN is oscillating if its LLR sign in the current updating is different from that in the last updating. The oscillating VNs are judged and classified as the oscillating set, O.
A minority of non-blurry VNs has wrong LLR information. In the MP scheme, the updating of non-blurry VNs is ignored, which causes a problem that the erroneous non-blurry VNs have no chance to be updated. Hence, a minority of blurry VNs receives the corresponding erroneous messages and cannot be corrected. To solve above problem, an OB-ASS scheme is proposed, described as follows:

1) After the MP scheme, all VNs are processed according to SSS.
2) The oscillation decision for each VN is executed, and the entire set, O is formed.
3) Each oscillating VN in O is updated in sequence. When the updating finishes, O will be cleared.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
To analyze the error-correction performance and convergence speed of the proposed VNBP-MP algorithm, the simulations are performed over the MLC NAND flash channel. Log-likelihood ratios BP (LLRBP) [5], shuffled BP (SBP) [6] and quantization-aware BP (QABP) [12] algorithms are used for comparison with the proposed one. The simulated regular LDPC codes are constructed with the progressive edge-growth (PEG) algorithm [18]. Furthermore, to fulfill the requirement of the large storage capacity in the MLC NAND flash memory, the LDPC codes with high code rate and long block length are applied to the simulated channel model. The maximum number of iterations, Imax is set to 5 and t is set to 1.
Figures 2 and 3 show the frame error rate (FER) performance of the algorithms for (3780, 3402) and (8000, 7360) binary LDPC codes. It can be shown that the proposed algorithm obtains the best FER performance among four algorithms within the range of simulated PE cycles. The main reason is that the MP scheme exploits reliable messages to update unreliable blurry VNs and forbids the

In Fig. 3, when FER is 3×10-4, the proposed algorithm can get about 800 PE cycles gain, compared with the SBP algorithm. It demonstrated that the proposed algorithm can adapt to worse channel conditions and prolong the lifetime of the NAND flash memory. From Figs. 2 and 3, we can observe that the performance gain of the proposed algorithm becomes increasingly outstanding with the increase of the block-length of LDPC codes.

To investigate the convergence speed of the algorithms, the performance of average number of iterations vs. PE cycles for (8000, 7360) LDPC codes at Imax = 5 is presented in Fig. 4. It is shown that the proposed algorithm converges faster than any other algorithms within simulated PE cycles. Note that in the subsequent simulations with the varied Imax, we can observe that the proposed algorithm has better error-correction performance with fewer iterations. After getting convergent, the proposed algorithm has the best FER performance among all the simulated algorithms. Also, the simulations about the throughput which represents the number of frames successfully decoded per unit time are performed, and demonstrate that the proposed algorithm has the lowest decoding latency.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, the MP scheme which makes use of the flash channel characteristics is introduced. In the new scheme, the propagation of unreliable messages associated with the blurry VNs is forbidden, while reliable messages associated with non-blurry VNs can be preferentially propagated. In addition, the OB-ASS scheme is proposed to process the oscillating VNs. Based on the MP and OB-ASS schemes, the VNBP-MP algorithm is presented. Simulation results demonstrate that, the proposed algorithm outperforms the other algorithms in terms of the FER performance. Furthermore, the proposed algorithm has faster convergence speed as well.
