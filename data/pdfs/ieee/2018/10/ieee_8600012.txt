An Algebraic Construction of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes Based on the Conjugates of Primitive Elements over Finite Fields
Muhammad Asif 1,*, Wuyang Zhou 1,*, Juma Saidi Ally 1, Nauman Ali Khan 1, Zain ul Abiden Akhtar 2 1 Key Laboratoryof Wireless-Optical Communications, Chinese Academy of Sciences University of Science and Technology of China, Hefei, Anhui , 230017 , China 2 Department of Telecommunication Engineering, The Islamia University of Bahawalpur Bahawalpur, Pakistan
e-mail: {masif.jeiside.knomix} @mail.ustc.edu.cn.wyzhou@ustc.edu.cn.engr.zain@iub.edu.pk

Abstract-Recently, there have been major developments in utilizing the finite fields to construct Low-density Parity-check (LDPC) codes. In this correspondence, an algebraic approach based on the conjugates of primitive elements over finite fields to construct Quasi-Cyclic (QC) Low-Density ParityCheck codes is presented. Proposed QC-LDPC codes provide an excellent error performance with Belief Propagation (BP) decoding over an Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel. Based on numerical results, the performance analysis shows that the proposed QC-LDPC codes perform as well as the randomly constructed Progressive edge growth (PEG) LDPC codes and algebraic QC-LDPC in the lower signal-tonoise ratio (SNR) region but outperform their counterparts in the higher SNR region. Also, the codes constructed are QC in nature, so the encoding can be done with shift register circuits having linear complexity.
Keywords-low-density parity-check codes; quasi-cyclic; primitive elements; conjugates; finite fields
I. INTRODUCTION
Owing to achieve an excellent performance approaching to Shannon capacity [I], LDPC codes [2] have attracted tremendous attention since its rediscovery in middle 1990s [3]. Compared to other error correction codes, LDPC codes provide many advantages, e.g., very low error floor, high-speed encoder and decoder and more varieties in code construction. Therefore, LDPC codes have become an ideal choice for many communication systems , such as Wi-Fi (802.lln/ac/ad) [4]-[6] and WiMAX (802.16e)[7].
An LDPC code is defined by an M x N parity-check matrix H with 1\11 check nodes in one class and N variable nodes in other class. The parity-check matrix gives a regular LDPC code if all rows and columns of H have same row-weight ui; and same column-weight W e; otherwise it will give an irregular LDPC code. An important constraint on parity-check matrix that any two rows or columns of H can agree in at most one position, called Row-Column (RC) constraint. The RC-con straint

guarantees that the Tanner graph of an LDPC code has no length-4 cycles. If the parity-check matrix consi sts of an array of circulant permutation matrices, then the matrix H gives a q-ary QC-LDPC code over a finite field GF(q) . The performance of an LDPC codes is greatly affected by the cycles of length-4. If the Tanner graph of an LDPC code has length-4 cycles , the messages will correlate after two iterations and the code suffers from performance degradation. Therefore, the short cycles of length-4 must be excluded in the construction of LDPC codes.
Based on the method of construction, LDPC codes are categorized into two classes: A) Random-like LDPC codes are designed based on computer search , the most well known random-like constructions are based on PEG [8] and protograph-based methods [9]-[11] ; B) Structured LDPC codes are constructed based on algebraic techniques such as finite fields [12]-[23], finite geometries[24],[25] , and combinatorial structures[26],[27]. Compared to structured LDPC codes, computer based random-like codes of large length perform very close to the Shannon capacity. But, well designed structured LDPC codes of moderate length perform as the random-like LDPC codes. For storage and digital communication systems, well designed structured LDPC codes provide lower error floor over AWGN and binary erasure channel (BEC). For construction point of view, the construction of structured LDPC codes with large minimum distance is more convenient as compared to random-like LDPC codes. Furthermore, compared to random-like codes with quadratic encoding complexity, structured LDPC can be encoded with shift register circuits having linear complexity. Recently, there have been major developments in utilizing the finite fields to construct QC-LDPC codes . In this manuscript, two classes of binary and nonbinary QC-LDPC codes based on the conjugates of primitive elements over finite fields are constructed. The proposed QC-LDPC codes provide an excellent bit-error rate (BER) performance with iterative decoding over AWGN channel.
