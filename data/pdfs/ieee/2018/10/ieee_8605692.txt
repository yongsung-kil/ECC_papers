B. MCMC Method
One of the superb methods for MIMO detection is the MCMC, which is a method based on probability. The main
idea of this method is to establish an equilibrium distributed Markov chain to obtain a sample of the same distribution. It
is often used for numerical approximations. Detection systems can achieve the optimal solution through such method with a
long enough calculation time. Enlightened by this method, we adopt it in LDPC decod-
ing. Drawing on the form used in MIMO detection [7], the
decoding process can be expressed as follows: • Initialize x(10), . . . , xK(0); • For j = 1, . . . , m x draw sample x1(j) from (x1(j)|x(2j−1), . . . , xK(j−1), y, G); draw sample x(2j) from (x(2j)|x(1j), . . . , xK(j−1), y, G); ... draw sample x(Kj) from (x(Kj) | x(1j), . . . , xK(j)−1, y, G);
where x(j) represents the -th entry in x(j) and x(j) is the j-th iteration result of x.
Usually, random values are used for initialization. But for
the systematic code, the solution obtained by hard-decision through message bits of the received vector can be used instead to accelerate the decoding convergence speed. m x is the total iteration number, and x1(max), . . . , x(Kmax) is supposed to be the convergence result of the message. The key point now is how to draw sample x(j) exactly.

E. Complexity Analysis
Observing specific Algorithms 1, 2 and 3, owing to the existence of multiplication, addition, comparison and sorting, the complexity of the proposed methods are higher than that of BP. Nevertheless, these methods are designed for short codes, so the increase in computation will have minor impact regarding the benefits brought by them. Here, we focus on

According to Algorithm 3, the complexity comes mainly from calculating 2L Euclidean distances in each iteration. As L Euclidean distances are the previous results, the actual amount of calculation added every time is 2L − L = L. In order to simplify the computation, we use binary addition to calculate Gxj. The detailed process can be found in Section IV. With such simplification, the complexity of distance calculation for ( , K) LDPC codes is O( × K × L) in binary addition, O( × L) in decimal addition and multiplication. Meanwhile, the sorting fuction can be implemented in a complexity of O(L × log L). Although the complexity load is relatively heavy, it is acceptable when choosing a right value for L and applying in short codes appropriately.
