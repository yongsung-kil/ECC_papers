Performance of Reed-Solomon Based Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes Based on Protograph

Inseon Kim School of Electrical & Electronic Engineering
Yonsei University, Seoul, South Korea email: is.kim@yonsei.ac.kr

Hong-Yeop Song School of Electrical & Electronic Engineering
Yonsei University, Seoul, South Korea email: hysong@yonsei.ac.kr

AbstractŌĆöIn this paper, we exploit construction of ReedSolomon(RS) based Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check(QCLDPC) codes using protograph combining two existing QCLDPC codes construction and experiment the performance of the codes. Two constructions have benefits: One is the construction RS based QC-LDPC codes whose girth is at least 8 and another is protograph based QC-LDPC codes to increase the upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance. We construct the protographs with three strategies and two of them have coding gain in sense of bit error rate compare to existing Reed Solomon based QCLDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-Cyclic low-density parity-check(QC-LDPC) codes [1] has been getting attention by many researchers because its simple encoding implementation and decoding analysis make them attractive. For this reason, QC-LDPC codes have been adopted in several communication system standards [2]ŌĆō[4].
The performance of QC-LDPC codes under iterative message passing decoding algorithm depends on the number of short cycle in their Tanner graph. The short cycles which cause the trapping set are important matter to lead to the error floor performance of QC-LDPC codes. Therefore, it is important to avoid short cycles in Tanner graph when constructing QCLDPC codes. Finding QC-LDPC codes with large girth has been issue to constructing good performed codes. Xiao et al. proposed the construction of Reed-Solomon(RS) based QCLDPC Codes which guarantee that the length of the shortest cycles is at least 8 by choosing RS codes with specific conditions [5].
Another aspect of constructing structured LDPC codes is by using protograph based LDPC codes. Protograph based QCLDPC codes can be constructed by copying multiple times the small Tanner graph with more than 2 edges between some pair of a check node and a variable node and lifting the edges to copied graphs. In [7], the authors proposed the upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance of protograph based QC-LDPC codes and construction of protograph based QC-LDPC codes to increase the upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance.
In this paper, we exploit construction of RS based QCLDPC codes using protograph basically combining two existing constructions. The codes obtained by the construction show better performance than existing RS based QC-LDPC codes in sense of bit error rate(BER). In Section II, we introduce how to construct RS based QC-LDPC codes and protograph based QC-LDPC codes to increase the minimum

Hamming distance. In Section III, we introduce a construction of RS based QC-LDPC codes based on protograph and some error performance simulations. In Section IV we show some experiment of codeŌĆÖs performance. Finally, in Section V, we concludes the paper.

B. Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes based on Protograph

For parity-check matrix of LDPC codes, Tanner graphs

of LDPC codes are bipartite graphs consisting of variable

nodes associated with column of the matrix and check nodes

associated with row of the matrix. We connect a variable node

and a check node by an edge if the corresponding entry in is

nonzero.

can connect one variable node and one check node with more

than 2 edges, called parallel edges. To obtain a larger tanner

graph from protograph, we use copy-and-permute procedure.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we utilize construction of RS based QC-LDPC codes using protograph combining two existing QC-LDPC codes construction. Since we select the protographs to increase the upper bound of minimum Hamming distance for ProtoRS-QC-LDPC codes, there are coding gain in sense of BER compare to RS-QC-LDPC codes. To attain more coding gain, we will focus on the construction strategy for protographs with their upper bounds of minimum Hamming distance.
