Anti-burst PEG Algorithm for P-LDPC Codes with Short-to-Medium Length

Li Deng1,2, Zhiping Shi1, Yanxia Li1, Rui Tang1
1 National Key Lab of Sci. and Tech. on Communications, UESTC, 611731, Chengdu, China 2 CETC Key Laboratory of Aerospace Information Applications, 050002, Shijiazhuang, China
e-mail: szp@uestc.edu.cn

Abstract—Protograph low-density parity-check (P-LDPC) codes are widely researched in communication systems due to the simple encoder structure and high-speed iterative decoding implementation. The key factors to affect the performance of P-LDPC codes are the base matrix and the lifting method. This paper focuses on the lifting method of P-LDPC codes with short-to-medium length over burst-error channels. An improved anti-burst protograph progressive edge-growth (IPPEG) algorithm based on edge constraints is proposed to improve the bit error rate (BER) performance. Simulation results show that the proposed IPPEG has better burst-resistance performance than the traditional PEG based lifting algorithm (PPEG) with or without random interleaver; additionally, on the premising of correctly decoding, more coding gains of IPPEG can be achieved with condition of larger channel transition probability, shorter code length or smaller coding rate.
Keywords-burst-error channels; P-LDPC codes; PEG algorithm; short-to-medium length
I. INTRODUCTION
Short-to-medium length LDPC codes are often used in military ultra-low-speed wireless communication systems, space transmission control systems and command information transmission systems, where both random and burst errors exist in the hybrid channel. In order to meet the uplink transmission requirements, it is necessary to construct short-to-medium length LDPC codes with low bit error rate (BER). Compared with LDPC codes, protograph LDPC codes (P-LDPC) have lower complexity, less memory space and better error floor performance [1, 2]; and the AR4JA codes with outstanding error performance have been adopted in the deep space communication [3]. In this paper, we focus on the anti-burst lifting methods for base matrix of short-to-medium length P-LDPC codes over burst-error channels.
Improving the performance of LDPC codes over burst-error channels has always been a hot research topic. In addition to the automatic repeat-request protocol, the optimization of channel coding is the mainstream solution. For example, authors in [4] and [5] optimized the structure of binary and non-binary LDPC codes to improve the resistance to burst-erasure errors, respectively; authors in [6,7] designed coding methods for short LDPC codes in burst-error channels; and authors in [8] proposed a joint tactical information distribution system (JTIDS) based LDPC codes over GF(q) for strong burst-error channel; the

asymmetric spatially coupled LDPC codes [9] and the Reed-solomon based non-binary globally coupled LDPC codes [10] are also studied over burst-error channels.
However, these construction methods of LDPC codes are not suitable for P-LDPC codes; and the code length are relative longer. The key factors to affect the performance of P-LDPC codes are the base matrix and the lifting method. Authors in [11] proposed two class of high rate P-LDPC codes suitable for binary erasure channels; thereinto, the proposed ARJA codes with linear minimum stopping set size are suitable for short blocks at low error rates. On the other hand, there are two main kinds of lifting methods for P-LDPC codes; PEG based one-step lifting method (PPEG) is used to construct random P-LDPC codes [12]; and PEG-ACE based two-steps lifting method is for quasi-cyclic P-LDPC codes [1, 13]. Considering the short-to-medium code length studied in this paper, the former lifting method is adopted.
This paper aims to further improve the anti-burst performance of good P-LDPC codes such as AR4JA codes by improving the lifting method. An improved PEG based lifting algorithm (IPPEG) is proposed which constraints the edge permuting process to ensure the local largest sparsity of each row in the parity check matrix. Simulation results show that the proposed IPPEG algorithm can obtain better burst-resistance performance than traditional PPEG algorithm for short-to-medium codewords.
This paper is organized as follows. The system model is presented in Section II. The proposed method is arranged in Section III. In Section IV, simulation and discussion are provided. The last part gives the summary.
II. SYSTEM MODEL
A. The Encoding Structure
P-LDPC codes can be encoded quickly and efficiently according to the protograph. Fig. 1 shows the protograph of 1/2 rate AR4JA codes; and B1_2 is the corresponding base matrix [1]. The black circles in Fig. 1 represent the variable nodes (VNs) of base matrix; the blank circle indicates the punctured variable nodes; and the blank circles with cross denote the check nodes (CNs). The entry values in base matrix correspond to the relevant numbers of muti-edges in Fig. 1. Assuming that the number of VNs in the protograph is nv, the number of CNs is nc, and the number of punctured VNs is np, the coding rate can be calculated as R=(nv nc)/(nv-np).

PEG algorithm is a common method for constructing the parity-check matrix H of LDPC codes with large girth [12]. The main idea of PEG is to set the variable nodes as vertexes, and expand the tanner graph as the form of trees; then find the CNs which satisfy the demand of local maximum girth, and add edges between these CNs. For P-LDPC codes, in order to inherit the characteristics of the protograph, permutations can only be processed between the K copies of the same node. Therefore, the PEG based lifting method for P-LDPC codes (PPEG) can be described as follows:
(1) Copy the protograph (or base matrix) K times; (2) For each CN with multiple edges, permute one of the multiple edges to the relevant copy node with smallest row weight which also satisfies the girth constraint of PEG algorithm; (3) Repeat step 2 until all the multiple edges have been replaced. For a source sequence s, the codewords c is defined as c  s G , where G is the corresponding generator matrix of H.
After BPSK modulation, the transmitted sequence is denoted as x=1-2c.
B. The Channel Model

In the actual channels, burst noises generally do not exist alone, which emerge in the form of instantaneous high power with additive white Gaussian noise as the background. In order to make the channel model conform to the actual communication systems, the burst-error channel model is established by combining the single burst erasure (SBE) channel with the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel [4]. The burst errors in SBE channel are generally continuous, and the location of errors is random.
Fig. 3 shows the simplified model of burst-error channel. In a modulated sequence c with length of N bits, there are Ne bits burst errors. The channel transition probability is p=Ne/N. The starting point i of burst errors occurs randomly at any bit of sequence c. The remaining N-Ne bits are only affected by white Gaussian noise. If the starting point i>N-Ne, we set the remaining errors at the first Ne-N+i bits of c (see Fig. 3(b)).

For long LDPC codes, due to the good sparsity of H matrix, the average distances of non-zero elements are generally large. Therefore, long LDPC codes have a certain capacity of anti-burst errors. However, for short-to-medium length LDPC codes, due to the small size of H matrix, the random selecting strategy of PEG algorithm would make the distances between some non-zero elements too short to overcome burst errors with relative longer length. Based on the above analysis, we propose an improved anti-burst PEG algorithm for short-to-medium length P-LDPC codes. The details are described as follows:
Initialization：Set the size of base matrix B is [M, N], get
the initial H matrix by copying the base matrix K times, then the size of H is [KM, KN]; the initial row number is i=1, the initial column number is j=1, and the multiplier counter is s=1.
(1) If i  M, then i=i+1 and turn step (2); if i=M and s  K, then i=1, s=s+1 and turn to step (2); if i=M and s=K, then i=1, j=j+1, s=1 and turn to step (2); if i=M, j=N, s=K, then turn to step (6);
(2) Let b=B(i, j), if b  0, then turn to step (3), otherwise turn to step (1);
(3) Select a node from CN(i-1)K+1 to CNiK which has the lowest row weight, and connect it with VN(j-1) K+s; then turn to step (4);
(4) b=b-1; if b>0, then turn to step (5), otherwise turn to step (1);
(5) Expand the tanner graph from VN(j-1) K+s; (i) If the number of CNs in the expanded tree stops increasing, then stop expansion. From CN(i-1)K+1 to CNiK which are not added into the expanded tree and have the lowest row weight, connect CNx with VN(j-1)K+s if H(x,:) has the largest average distance; (ii) If CN(i-1)K+1 to CNiK are already included in the expanded tree, then stop expansion. From CN(i-1)K+1 to CNiK which have the lowest row weight, connect CNx with VN(j-1)K+s if H(x,:) has the largest average distance; Here the average distance of H(x,:) is defined as the average value of distances between the element H(x,(j-1)K+s) and each non-zero element in H(x,:). If there are more than

one CN that meet the above two conditions, select one randomly and then turn to step (4);
(6) Output H matrix.

Fig. 8 and Fig. 9 show the BER performance of PPEG and IPPEG with different code lengths of N={300, 500, 700} and fixed parameters of R=1/2 and p=0.2. It can be seen from Fig. 8 that IPPEG performs better than PPEG with various short-to-medium code lengths. Especially, when the length is relative shorter, the lifting method of IPPEG can effectively improve the sparsity of H matrix. However, due to the sparsity of LDPC codes with longer length, the coding gains of IPPEG are not obvious. In Fig. 9, when the random interleaver is added, the BER performance of shorter code length at high SNR region improves about 2 to 3dB; and the coding gains of IPPEG also decrease as Fig. 7.
Fig. 10 and Fig. 11 show the BER performance of PPEG and IPPEG with different coding rates of R={1/2, 2/3, 3/4} and fixed parameters of p=0.1 and N=300. As seen in Fig. 10, IPPEG performs better for codes with lower coding rate. When the random interleaver is added in Fig. 11, the BER improvements of IPPEG and PPEG are not remarkable. The probable reason is that the channel transition probabilities p is moderate, both the effects of constraints in IPPEG and random interleaver are not obvious.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, an improved anti-burst PEG algorithm for short-to-medium length P-LDPC codes was proposed. It ensures the local maximum average distance of non-zero elements at each row while lifting the base matrix. The short-to-medium length P-LDPC codes constructed by IPPEG algorithm have better anti-burst performance than PPEG method over burst-error channels with and without random interleaver. Moreover, on the premising of correctly decoding, more coding gains can be obtained under the condition of larger channel transition probability, shorter code length or smaller coding rate; and the coding gains of IPPEG are relative smaller with random interleaver.
