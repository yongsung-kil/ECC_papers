A Unified Deep Learning Based Polar-LDPC Decoder for 5G Communication Systems
Yaohan Wang†, Zhichao Zhang†, Shunqing Zhang†, Shan Cao† and Shugong Xu† † Shanghai Institute for Advanced Communication and Data Science, Key laboratory of Specialty Fiber Optics and Optical Access Networks,
Joint International Research Laboratory of Specialty Fiber Optics and Advanced Communication, Shanghai University, Shanghai, 200444, China
Email:{wangyaohan1, zhichaozhang, shunqing, cshan, shugong}@shu.edu.cn

Abstract—In the 5G communication systems, a hybrid approach to support polar codes for control plane and LDPC codes for data plane has been identified as the channel coding solution for enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) scenario. One of the major challenges to implement this approach is to design powerful decoders at the terminal side. Inspired from a useful machine learning based polar decoder, we proposed a deep learning based unified polar-LDPC by concatenating an indicator section. Through numerical experiments, we show that the proposed deep neural network (DNN) based decoding architecture can achieve the similar decoding performance compared with the traditional BP-based decoding algorithm. Meanwhile, the proposed unified approach shares the same network architecture and parameters with isolated approaches, which saves significant implementation resources consequently.
Index Terms—polar codes, LDPC codes, unified architecture, deep learning, deep neural networks
I. INTRODUCTION
Polar codes, as the first provable capacity achieving codes for symmetric binary-input memoryless channels, were first proposed by E. Arıkan in his ground-breaking paper [1]. With the discovery of channel polarization phenomenon, the similar concept has been widely extended to various applications, including source coding, data compression, as well as other specific applications with modified polar codes structure [2]. Due to the potential impacts to change the modern coding schemes, polar codes have attracted significant research attentions in both academia and industry [3]. In the meantime, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [4], proposed by R. Gallager 50 years ago, have been proved to be a suitable channel coding scheme for high throughput transmission and adopted as a compulsory channel coding scheme for IEEE 802.11ax system [5]. In the fifth generation (5G) communication systems, since we require reliable control signaling and high throughput data transmission simultaneously for enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) scenario, a hybrid solution to apply polar codes for control plane and LDPC codes for data plane transmissions was agreed in the Third Generation Partnership Project (3GPP) Radio Access Network (RAN) meeting held in Reno [6].
One of the major challenges to apply polar and LDPC codes in 5G systems is to implement powerful decoders at the terminal side. From the hardware resource point of view, a brute-force approach to build polar and LDPC decoders in

a separate manner is inefficient and a reconfigurable architecture to support them simultaneously with smart decoding algorithm is therefore desirable [7]. Thanks to the high compatibility of belief propagation (BP) algorithm, a reconfigurable merged BP-based decoding scheme was proposed in [7] with shared factor graphs. However, this approach rely on a careful design of the decoding structure and the generalization to different coding rates will be challenging.
To solve the above issue, a machine learning based decoding structure becomes popular in recent years. For example, to decode the convolutional codes or turbo codes [8], a recurrent neural network (RNN) architecture was proposed in [9] to exploit the correlation among different encoded bits. To decode polar codes, a scalable deep neural network (DNN) based decoding scheme was proposed in [10], where a near maximum a posterior (MAP) performance could be achieved. As far as we aware, whether a machine learning based decoding structure can be applied to support multiple coding schemes is still open. In this paper, we shall tackle the above problem through a deep learning based decoding structure. By inserting an indicator section to identify coding types, the proposed unified approach exploits the similarity between neural networks and merged BP decoding algorithms. We then optimize the neural network configurations by choosing different network classes and other related parameters. The corresponding performance is also verified under different signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) in order to obtain a reliable result. Through some numerical experiments, we are able to show that more or less the same decoding performance (e.g. less than 0.5 dB SNR gap) can be achieved, if compared with the traditional detection schemes. Meanwhile, the proposed neural network based decoding scheme shares the same network architecture and parameter for both polar and LDPC detection schemes, which saves significant resource overhead, if compared with conventional isolated decoding approaches such as [11].

A. Network Classes
One of the most important approaches to optimize the network configurations is to choose the suitable network class, where we consider commonly used DNN [15], convolutional neural network (CNN) [16], and long-short term memory (LSTM) [17] in this paper1. DNN is a computationally powerful parallel neural network, which can be used for joint channel equalization and decoding [14]. CNN is often used for feature extraction and widely applied to deal with correlated noise [16]. LSTM is a type of recurrent neural network suitable for sequential signal processings such as decoding the convolutional codes [9]. In order to provide a fair comparison, we design the corresponding networks with similar number of training parameters and training samples (epochs). The detailed values of network configurations and parameters are listed in Table I. In Fig. 6, we plot BER versus SNR relation for (16, 8) polar and LDPC codes. Through this experiment, we can show that DNN network architecture outperforms the other two. This partially because DNN architecture has strong ability to deal with independent one dimension data as reported in [15].

B. Size of Indicator Section
Another dimension that we plan to optimize is the size of indicator section. By intuition, the size of indicator section provides possibility to balance the scalability of network (e.g. to decode more types of channel coding) and the network complexity (e.g. to introduce more parameters for training). BER versus SNR relation under different size of indicator section is shown in Fig. 7. As we can see from this figure, the indicator section with only one bit provides sufficient BER versus SNR performance for both LDPC and polar cases. Meanwhile, if we plan to support more types of channel coding, a minimum size of indicator section to identify different coding schemes will be sufficient.

V. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, we compare the proposed DNN-based unified polar-LDPC decoder with traditional BP decoders and isolated schemes. The proposed architecture’s settings are summarized as follows. First, a 3-layer DNN architecture with size (512, 256, 128) is adopted to decode the unified (16,8) polar and LDPC codes, where the generation matrices are elaborated in Section II-A. Second, we choose ‘1’ for ‘polar codes’ and ‘−1’ for ‘LDPC codes’ in the indicator section. Last but not least, we choose the number of training epoch2 to be 5×104. At the test stage, the BER performance is evaluated under 4×105 samples, or equivalently, 3.2×106 bits. All the simulations are performed using a standard desktop3 with operating system Ubuntu 16.04 and the software environment is Python v3.5.2. The detail parameters are listed in Table III.
The comparison results of BER versus SNR performance are shown in Fig. 9. In the polar decoding case, our proposed DNN-based unified polar-LDPC decoder achieves more or less the same decoding performance (less than 0.2 dB gap at BER equals to 10−2) with the conventional polar BP detection [18]. This small gap may be due to the size of the network or the small performance gap caused by the introduction of the indicator section. While in the LDPC decoding case, it outperforms the traditional LDPC BP decoding scheme [16] by 0.8 dB4 at BER equals to 10−2. From the above results, we can see that the proposed unified decoder can approach or surpass the traditional BP decoding performance.
In Table IV, we compare the decoding efficiency of different decoding schemes, including the proposed DNNbased unified polar-LDPC decoder, the isolated DNN-based decoders for polar and LDPC, as well as the conventional BP-based polar and LDPC decoders. From the throughput point of view, the DNN-based approaches provides six orders of magnitude improvement if compared with the traditional BP-based decoding schemes. Meanwhile, compared with the traditional isolated DNN-based polar or LDPC decoders, the unified approach provides nearly the same throughput (less than 0.4% loss) with marginal network overhead (e.g. less than 0.3% overhead in terms of total parameters) from the indicator section.
VI. CONCLUSION
In summary, we propose a unified polar-LDPC decoder using deep learning approach. By integrating the indicator section with observed symbols, we are able to build the unified DNN architecture to support both polar and LDPC decoding simultaneously.
