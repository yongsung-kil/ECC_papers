Low-Rate PBRL-LDPC Codes for URLLC in 5G
Xiaoning Wu , Student Member, IEEE, Ming Jiang, Member, IEEE, Chunming Zhao, Member, IEEE, Liang Ma, and Yuejun Wei

Abstract—In this letter, we propose a class of rate-compatible raptor-like protographs, which is aimed at the design of low-rate low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with short length and fast convergence for ultra-reliable and low-latency communications in 5G. In the proposed protographs, the connection degrees can be progressively optimized by the protograph-based extrinsic information transfer analysis with a limited number of iterations. Following the protograph optimization, we construct the rate-compatible LDPC codes by a simplified error minimization progressive edge-growth algorithm and carry out simulations for performance comparisons. The simulation results show that our proposed LDPC codes have noticeable advantages over the existing low-rate LDPC codes with short length and limited numbers of decoding iterations.
Index Terms—LDPC codes, low rate, protograph, short length, limited numbers of iterations.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) coding technology, which has been adopted in 5G services, is a promising candidate for ultra-reliable and low-latency communications (URLLC). However, existing LDPC codes do not perfectly satisfy new requirements of URLLC. A general URLLC requirement for one transmission of a packet is that the reliability should be 1 − 10−5 success probability for 32 bytes (256 bits) with a user plane latency of 1ms [1], [2]. Due to the low latency requirements, it is necessary to design coding schemes with short length for fast convergence and low complexity in physical layer processing [3], [4]. Besides, ratecompatible structures with good performance, especially for low-rate codes, should be carefully considered for the ultrareliable scenarios, where the error floor should be controlled below the block error rate (BLER) of 10−5.
For some specific applications, such as fiber optical communications and storage systems, it is appropriate to use the algebraic quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes [5], which can guarantee high throughput with a very low error floor.

But, the algebraic codes, which usually can perform close to the Shannon limit at high code rates, do not well support the rate-compatible constructions dedicated to low-rate applications in wireless communications. A low-rate coding scheme with an optimized multi-edge structure is given in [6], which can achieve capacity-approaching performance with very long length. Following that, Divsalar et al. [7] revisit the design of the low-rate structure and introduce more practical protographs for low-rate LDPC codes of short length. Yet, the lack of a rate-compatible structure is still a major bottleneck in application of these protograph codes for URLLC. Moreover, there are always some punctured nodes in these protographs for better iterative decoding thresholds [8], which is harmful to fast convergence [9]. More related works of LDPC codes in [10] and [11] focus on faster decoding convergence and lower implementation complexity. Also, some important construction methods [12], [13] are developed for the rate-compatible LDPC codes of short length. However, according to these existing solutions, there should be a further exploration about an extension to low-rate codes.
In this letter, we generalize the Richardson-Urbanke (RU) protograph in [7] to a class of low-rate protographs. These protographs have a sparser high-rate core matrix and a simple diagonal extension for low-rate encoding, which support rate-compatible constructions and lead to better performance for short length. In these protographs, the connection degrees are determined by a systematic and incremental approach, where we use the protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) analysis with a limited number of iterations [8], [10], [11] as a screening tool to obtain the optimized protographs with fast convergence. We limit the maximum degree of each connection to decrease the search space for the protograph optimization, which also contributes to the low complexity implementations of encoding and decoding. Simulation results show that the new proposed LDPC codes of short length have significant performance advantages over the original RU codes [6], [7], the AR4A codes [7], and the Qualcomm low family (QLF) codes in 5G proposal [14], especially in the condition of very low rates and limited numbers of iterations.
II. RATE-COMPATIBLE LOW-RATE PROTOGRAPHS
A protograph-based LDPC (PB-LDPC) code can be described by a Tanner graph, which is constructed by lifting from a small protograph [15]. The protograph is a small bipartite graph containing some variable nodes and check nodes that are interconnected by multiple edges. III. OPTIMIZATION AND CONSTRUCTION
Given the row number M and the vector length N, we can optimize the vectors a and b by the threshold analysis and determine the final protomatrix. The ranges of the integer variables am and bn are limited to [1, u] and [0, v], respectively, which is important to restrict the space of the optimization and the edge density of the protomatrix. The optimization process is carried out row by row in the matrix PIRC, where we always keep Q updated protomatrices with the lowest thresholds before the optimization of the next row. IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, we compare our low-rate GRU codes with the AR4A codes, the QLF codes, and the original RU codes. The simulations use standard belief propagation (BP) decoding with BPSK modulation on AWGN channels.
Compared with the original RU codes, our codes have better performance for short length as shown in Fig. 2, where the maximum number of iterations is set to I = 20 for BP decoding. In Fig. 2, two groups of rate-1/10 LDPC codes with different information lengths, K = 256 and 10240, are compared. When the codes of the longer length K = 10240 are selected, the original RU code with the better decoding threshold presents a slightly better performance compared with our GRU code. However, for the LDPC codes of the short length K = 256, the GRU code obviously outperforms the original RU one, although the iterative decoding threshold of the GRU code is higher. From the protograph structure, it can be seen that the original RU codes have more short cycles compared with the GRU codes, which may result in poor performance for the original RU codes of short length.
Fig. 3 shows performance comparisons of our GRU codes, the AR4A codes, and the QLF codes with BP decoding, I = 20. Only the codes of the low rates, R = 1/3, 1/6, and 1/12, are considered, and the information lengths are all set to K = 256 according to the simulation assumptions in [3]. Since the protograph of the rate-1/12 AR4A code is not available in [7], we do not compare the corresponding code in Fig. 3 and Fig. 4. With the limited number of iterations, our codes outperform the other codes across all code rates, where our codes are about 0.2 dB better than the QLF codes at the BLER of 10−4. V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose a class of low-rate protographs with a raptor-like structure, which can be seen as a generalized extension to the protograph of the original RU codes. The protograph is designed to support a rate-compatible structure ranging from 1/3 to 1/12 for short length and optimized by the PEXIT analysis with a limited number of iterations. Using an optimized protograph, we construct a class of lowrate LDPC codes, by the two-step lifting and the simplified EMPEG algorithm. These codes have a quasi-cyclic structure and an excellent performance in the limited number of iterations compared with the low-rate AR4A codes, the QLF codes, and the original RU codes. Besides the faster decoding convergence, the proposed codes can also achieve satisfactory performance when the number of iterations is large enough, which is very important for the URLLC applications.
