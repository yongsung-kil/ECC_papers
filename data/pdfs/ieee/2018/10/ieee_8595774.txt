AbstractвҖ”We propose a masking algorithm to optimize the girth and the number of short cycles in LDPC codes of superposition construction. Simulation results show the codes can improve 0.3 dB performance without the implementation cost.
KeywordsвҖ”LDPC codes, making, girth, short cycles, superposition construction
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes [1] have the advantages of BER performance approaching the Shannon Limit while using soft-input and soft-output iterative decoding and easy hardware implementation [2]. Therefore, a varity of methods originating from graphtheorry and algebraic-theory have been proposed for the construction [3]-[6], among which the methods based on algebraic-theory could be included into the framework of superposition construction [7]. The superposition construction is consisted by base matrix design, masking matrix design and matrix dispersion.
A QC-LDPC code C can be defined by the null space of the parity-check matrix H over GF(q), which consists of an array of circulant matrices [8]. C can also be described by the Tanner Graph(TG), which has a one-to-one relationship with the parity-check matrix H. If TG is cycle-free, the sumproduct algorithm (SPA) will be optimal in the decoding process. Actually, such TG cannot be obtained in practical application. What we needed in application is the finite code length, which inevitably leads to the existence of cycles in TG. The presence of cycles will degrade the performance of SPA. Thus, reducing the short cycles and possibly enlarging girth g is an effective method to improve the performance of LDPC codes. Masking technique is one of the important tool to optimize the two indicators, which can drastically reduce the short cycles and possibly enlarge girth g.
In this paper, we focus on the construction of QC-LDPC codes based on array dispersion and arbitrary degree distribution masking. Firstly, a base matrix B that satisfies the RC constraint is randomly generated based on two arbitrary subsets of elements from a given finite field [9]and the cyclic shift coefficient matrix C of B is obtained. IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we focus on the construction of quasicyclic(QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes by jointly optimizing the girth and the number of short cycles. A method named the two arbitrary subsets of elements from a given finite field in the GF(q) domain is introduced to construct the base matrix, then we propose an arbitrary degree distribution masking algorithm to find the masking matrix satisfying the degree distribution. By this way, the constructed QC-LDPC codes drastically reduced the short cycles and possibly enlarged girth g. Simulation results shows that the constructed codes do not only achieve 0.3 dB
performance improvement at the WER of 2 пӮҙ10пҖӯ7 over the
AWGN channel, but also do not exhibit error floor above the
WER of 10пҖӯ7 .
