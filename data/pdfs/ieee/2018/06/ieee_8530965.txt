A New Efficient Adaptive Normalized Min-Sum Decoder for Irregular LDPC Codes in OFDM

Abstract: In this letter a new decoding algorithm based on the adaptive normalized min-sum algorithm and the joint decoding algorithm is proposed for irregular low density parity check codes in OFOM system . The new proposed algorithm changes the update rule of the normalized min-sum algorithm to improve its performance. Also, instead of having separate horizontal and vertical proce ssing phase s in decoding algorithm, the vertical phase is merged with the horizontal phase. The proposed decoder improves the performance in waterfall and error floor regions. The convergence speed of the proposed algorithm is much faster. Therefore, the proposed decoder is able to converge to its final bit error rate in fewer iterations.
Keywords: IrregularLDPC, Error Floor, Min-sum decoding.
I. Introduction
Among the error correction codes, Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) code , a class of linear block codes , is one of the most efficient codes because of its near-capacity performance decoding with low-complexity iterative decoders. The LDPC codes have been adopted by many advanced standards, such as 10 Gigabit Ethernet and DVB-T2.
The graph of a LDPC code 's error rate performance versus the signal to noise ratio (SNR) is divided into waterfall and error floor regions . In the waterfall region, error rate drops rapidly as SNR improves whereas in the error floor region, the error rate declines gradually. The existence of error floor has limited the adoption of LDPC codes in some applications. For example, in the DVB- T2 standard, an outer BCH code is concatenated before an inner LDPC code to lower error floor.
LDPC codes are represented by bipartite graphs in which one type of nodes, the variable nodes, corresponds to bits of the codeword and the other type of nodes, the check nodes, corresponds to the set of parity-check constraints which define the code .
The LDPC codes can be categorized to regular and irregular forms. In the regular LDPC codes , the number of ones in each row and in each column of parity check matrix is constant, while in the irregular LDPC codes, the

Among different iterative decoding algorithms of the LDPC codes, Sum Product (SP) algorithm achieves good decoding performance. However, its hardware complexity is high. To reduce the complexity of SP, MinSum (MS) algorithm has been proposed. But, the performance of MS is worse than the SP. For compensating the performance loss of the MS decoding, several correction methods , such as normalized min-sum (NMS) decoder and adaptive normalized min-sum (ANMS) decoder [I], have been proposed. Also, joint row-column decoding algorithm has been proposed in [2] to lower the error floor of regular LDPC codes. In this paper, we propose a new decoding algorithm based on these algorithms for the irregular LDPC codes ; this new algorithm improves the decoding performance. We call it "Joint Adaptive Normalized Min-Sum (JANMS) decoding algorithm".
This paper is organized as follows . Section II describes min-sum, normalized min-sum and adaptive min-sum decoding algorithm. Also, a brief review of joint rowcolumn algorithm is presented. In section III, we describe our new proposed decoding algorithm. Section IV presents the simulation results and analysis. Finally , conclusions are presented.

CONCLUSIONS
In this paper , we presented a new min-sum decoding algorithm which improves the BER and FER performance of irregul ar LDP C codes both in waterfall and error floor region s in an OFDM multicarrier system. Furthermore, we showed that by reordering the rows of the parity check matrix based on their weights , the performance of the proposed decoder improve s even more. This property is very useful for decoding quasicyclic irregular codes in which their rows are ordered degree-decreasing naturally.

In fact, in the error floor region, the FER performance of the JANMS algorithm is approximately the same as the sum-product algorithm.
For further performance improvement of our decoder, we use the inherent characteristic of the irregular LDPC codes , i.e. having rows with different weights. As a matter of fact, we reordered the rows of parity check matrix based on their weight s, in a degree-decreasing manner from the first row to the last row. Thus , first the check nodes with higher weight s are processed, and then they provide more reliable information for the check nodes with lower weights. Encoding was performed by Guassian elimination, so the row weight s did not change in encoding procedure. Figure 5 shows the result for our propo sed algorithm and the ANMS algorithm before and after rearranging H . It is obvious that, after reordering parity matrix , the performance of our proposed JANMS has improved with respect to ANMS algorithm.
