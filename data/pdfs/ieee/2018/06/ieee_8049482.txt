Construction of QC-LDPC Codes Based on Pre-Masking and Local Optimal Searching

Dongdong Wang , Liqian Wang, Xue Chen, Aimei Fei, Chen Ju, and Zhirong Wang

Abstract— In this letter, we consider the construction of quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes by jointly optimizing the girth g and the number of g-cycles. We first propose a sub-array detection method to accelerate the detection of cycles, and by detecting the non-repeated automatic enumerative cycles in the frame of sub-arrays, cycle detection time can be greatly reduced. Furthermore, a local optimal searching algorithm combined with pre-masking is presented, by orderly searching the local optimal p j,l for each circulant permutation matrix (CPM), a class of QC-LDPC codes with possibly enlarged g, less number of g-cycles, and arbitrary attainable degree distribution could be constructed. Simulation results show that the codes constructed by the proposed method perform well in error floor region.
Index Terms— LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes, cycle detection, girth, masking.
I. INTRODUCTION
Q UASI-CYCLIC low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes have the advantages of BER performance approaching the Shannon Limit and easy hardware implementation [1]. Graph- and algebraic-theory are often used to construct QC-LDPC codes. For graph-theoretic-based methods, progressive edge growth (PEG) [2] and protograph-based methods [3] are the most common. Algebraic-based methods are mainly based on finite geometries [4], [5], finite fields [6], [7] and combinational designs [8], most of which could be included into the framework of superposition construction [9]. The main process of superposition construction consists of base matrix design, masking matrix design and matrixdispersion [10].
A QC-LDPC code C can be represented by the null space of the parity check matrix H , which consists of an array of circulant matrices [11]. C can also be described by its unique Tanner Graph (T G) [12], which is usually used to explain the iterative decoding process. The sum-product algorithm (S P A) will be optimal, if the T G is cycle-free. However, limited by the finite code length, the cycle-free T G cannot be achieved in practical application. Short cycles in T G will degrade the performance of S P A. Therefore, enlarging the girth g and

In this letter, we consider the construction of QC-LDPC codes based on pre-masking and local optimal searching. Different from [15] and [16], the masking matrix M is generated in advance, which is needed to satisfy the only constraint condition of degree distribution. Thus, the design of M becomes very simple. Then, we orderly search the best shift value p j,l of the pre-masked exponential base matrix BM in the local searching manner, which must make its corresponding C P M has the least passing number of gcycles. Finally, the null space of H , which is obtained by operating the matrix-dispersion for BM , gives the QC-LDPC code with possibly enlarged girth g and less number of gcycles. Simulation results show that the constructed codes perform well in error floor region with S P A over the AWGN channel.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose a sub-array cycle detection method to accelerate the detection of 2i -cycles. Then, a local optimal searching algorithm combined with pre-masking is presented to construct QC-LDPC codes with possibly enlarged girth g and less number of g-cycles. Simulation results show that the constructed codes do not only achieve better BER and WER performance over the AWGN channel under the S P A, but also do not exhibit error floor above the BER of 10−9.
