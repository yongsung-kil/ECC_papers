Abstract—This paper proposes to construct a class of multipleburst-correction quasi-cyclic (QC) codes via matrix transformations. Due to the diagonal structure of the transformed paritycheck matrix of a QC code, multiple bursts can be corrected in the transform domain. By well designing the diagonal submatrices, the constructed QC codes are able to achieve optimal or asymptotically optimal multiple-burst-correction capability. In particular, a subclass of our constructed QC codes are QC lowdensity parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes which also perform very well over random channels. Simulation results show that our QC-LDPC codes outperform the existing QC-LDPC codes over burst channels.
Index Terms—Quasi-cyclic, LDPC codes, matrix transformations, multiple-burst-correction.
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-cyclic (QC) codes [1]–[3] are an important class of linear codes which can asymptotically approach the GilbertVarshamov bound. So far, most of constructions for QC codes focus on a wide range of random channels. Well-designed QC codes, especially QC low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [4]–[7], can effectively correct random errors or erasures over random channels, e.g., a binary input additive white Gaussian noise (BI-AWGN) channel. However, many communication and storage channels in practice, such as wireless, magnetic recording and flash channels, are affected by noise disturbance in the form of multiple bursts [2]. Hence, further study of multiple-burst-correction QC codes is very important both in theory and practice. In [8], Fan et al. constructed QC codes based on disjoint difference sets to correct multiple bursts. In [4], Lin et al. introduced algebraic constructions to design QC-LDPC codes for correcting single burst erasure.
Recently, a matrix transformation approach [5], [9]–[11] has been presented to investigate and study the structure of QCLDPC codes in the transform domain rather than the symbol domain. By the Galois Fourier transform (GFT) and row and column permutations, a QC matrix is specified by a block diagonal matrix. Thus, QC-LDPC codes can be effectively analyzed, constructed and fast encoded based on the diagonal structure of their transformed parity-check matrices.

This paper proposes to analyze and construct a class of QC codes for correcting multiple bursts via the matrix transformation approach [10], [11]. It is demonstrated that the multipleburst-correction capability of a QC code is determined by the diagonal structure of its transformed parity-check matrix. A burst occurring in the symbol domain is transformed into several random noise patterns in the transform domain by the GFT and permutations. Hence, multiple bursts can be effectively corrected based on the diagonal sub-matrices of smaller sizes in the transformed parity-check matrix rather than the whole parity-check matrix. For non-binary QC codes, the parity-check matrices of various maximum distance separable (MDS) codes [2], [5], [12], [13] are introduced to construct the diagonal sub-matrices in the transformed parity-check matrices. The proposed non-binary QC codes are capable of reaching the Reiger bound [2] for correcting multiple bursts.
Moreover, we analyze the multiple-burst-correction capability of binary QC codes. For binary QC codes, all diagonal submatrices can be divided into several conjugacy classes [10], so that it only requires to analyze and design the representative of each conjugacy class. Furthermore, we derive a pre-coding to eliminate the impact of several diagonal sub-matrices whose null spaces define poor codes. The proposed binary QC codes can achieve asymptotically optimal multiple-burst-correction capability. Interestingly, a subclass of the proposed binary QC codes are QC-LDPC codes. Simulation results show that the constructed binary QC-LDPC codes have good random correction performance, and outperform existing QC-LDPC codes [4], [9] over the burst channels.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews the matrix transformations for QC codes. In Section III and IV, we analyze and construct QC and QC-LDPC codes for correcting multiple bursts. Section V concludes this paper.

IV. CONSTRUCTION OF MULTIPLE-BURST-CORRECTION QC CODES
Following Theorem 1, we propose to construct a class of multiple-burst-correction QC codes by well designing the submatrices of the transformed parity-check matrix HF,π. The proposed codes can achieve the Reiger bound, and have the MBE and MBEr of 1, respectively.

A. Non-binary multiple-burst-correction QC codes

For an (ns, K) QC code over GF(q), q = 2r, its

parity-check matrix H is an m × n array of s × s circulant matrices over GF(q). All sub-matrices in HF,π =

diag[B0, B1, . . . , Bs−1] are over GF(q) and are independent to each other. Hence, all sub-matrices in HF,π can be constructed

by the m × n parity-check matrices of any (n, n − m) MDS

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have constructed optimal or asymptotically
optimal burst-correction QC codes by designing the diagonal
structure of transformed parity-check matrices. In particular, a
subclass of the proposed QC codes are LDPC codes in fact,
which have good performance over the BI-AWGN channel.
