Analytical Lower Bound on the Lifting Degree of Multiple-Edge QC-LDPC Codes With Girth 6
Mohammad-Reza Sadeghi and Farzane Amirzade

Abstract— Multiple-edge protographs have some advantages over single-edge protographs, such as potentially having larger minimum Hamming distance. However, most of results in the literature are related to the construction of single-edge quasicyclic low-density parity-check codes (QC-LDPC) codes and little research has been done for the construction of multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes. In this letter, for the first time, necessary and sufficient conditions for the exponent matrices to have multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes with girth 6 are provided. As a consequence of this letter, a lower bound on the lifting degree of regular and irregular multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes with girth 6 is derived. We also present QC-LDPC codes whose lifting degrees meet our proposed lower bound. These codes have shorter lengths compared with single-edge QC-LDPC codes. Another contribution of this letter is presenting a technique to reduce the size of the search space to find these codes.
Index Terms— Multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes, protographs, girth, difference matrices, lifting degree.
I. INTRODUCTION
Q Uasi-cyclic low-density parity-check codes (QC-LDPC codes) are an essential category of LDPC codes that are preferred to other types of LDPC codes because of their practical and simple implementations. One of the main approaches for constructing QC-LDPC codes is protographbased method which can be divided into two classes, singleedge and multiple-edge protographs. Compared to single-edge protographs, multiple-edge protographs have benefits such that QC-LDPC codes lifted from them can potentially have larger minimum Hamming distance, [1]. However, little research has been done to construct multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes.
Tanner graph is an important representation of codes. The length of the shortest cycles of the Tanner graph, girth, has been known to influence the code performance. Obtaining necessary and sufficient conditions for an exponent matrix to construct short-length QC-LDPC codes with a specific girth and the minimum lifting degree has been an interesting subject among researchers. Fossorier [2] presented the necessary and sufficient condition for the Tanner graph of fully connected (m, n)-regular single-edge QC-LDPC codes, where m is the column weight and n is the row weight. As a result of the study of necessary and sufficient condition for exponent matrices, lower bounds on the required lifting degree for having

There are also numerous numerical lower bounds on the lifting degree of single-edge regular QC-LDPC codes, which are obtained by search based. For example, O’Sullivan [5] presented lower bounds on the lifting degree of regular single-edge QC-LDPC codes with girths up to 12. However, the search based lower bounds on the lifting degrees are much higher than the existing analytical lower bound for many cases. In general, according to [6], there are very few cases for which these bounds have been proved to be tight. Also, in [4], [6], and [7], for m = 3, 4, exponent matrices of (m, n)-regular single-edge QC-LDPC codes with girths 6 to 12, the minimum lifting degree and short lengths were presented.
In this letter, we obtain the necessary and sufficient conditions for exponent matrices of multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes with girth 6 which simplify considering 4-cycles in an exponent matrix. As a result of this study, an analytical lower bound on the lifting degree is derived which is applicable to both regular and irregular multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes with girth 6. To the best of our knowledge, in this category this lower bound is proposed for the first time. We also provide QC-LDPC codes whose lifting degrees meet our proposed lower bound and in comparison with single-edge QC-LDPC codes, the presented codes with minimum lifting degrees have shorter lengths. In addition, we propose a method to reduce the size of the search space to obtain exponent matrices.

IV. CONCLUSION
In this letter, the necessary and sufficient conditions for the exponent matrices to have regular and irregular multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes with girth 6 were proposed which is a new result in the category of multiple-edge protographs. As a result of this study, constructing exponent matrices of multiple-edge codes with girth 6 has been simplified and a lower bound on the lifting degree of such QC-LDPC codes was obtained. We presented multiple-edge QC-LDPC codes for which our proposed lifting degrees are tight and they have shorter lengths in comparison with single-edge QC-LDPC codes. A technique was also provided to reduce the size of the search space for finding an exponent matrix.
