Piggybacking Belief Propagation Decoding for
Rateless Codes Based on RA Structure
Rong Sun∗†, Meng Zhao∗, Jingwei Liu∗, Jingyuan Sun‡, Hao Guan‡, Zhuyan Zhao‡ ∗State Key Laboratory of ISN, Xidian University, Xi’an, 710071, P.R. China
†Xiamen Key Laboratory of Mobile Multimedia Communications(Huaqiao University), Xiamen, Fujian 361021, China ‡Nokia Beijing Bell Lab, Bejing, P.R. China Email: rsun@mail.xidian.edu.cn

Abstract—In this paper, a new Belief Propagation (BP) decoding algorithm named Piggybacking Belief Propagation (PBP) decoding algorithm is proposed for rateless codes based on repeat-accumulate (RA) structure over AWGN channel. The “piggybacking” characteristic of the proposed algorithm is transmitting the Log-Likelihood Ratio (LLR) results calculated from the previous decode attempt to the new decoding process after receiving more information from the channel. Moreover, the proposed algorithm introduces a new stopping criterion to stop the decoding attempt when it is almost impossible to succeed. The stopping criterion is based on the weight change ratio of syndrome. The simulation results show that, compared with the traditional BP decoding algorithm, the proposed algorithm has lower decoding overhead and the a reduced decoding delay.
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, rateless codes have been widely concerned in communication areas on account of the rateless properties that the code redundancy can be adaptively adjusted to different channel conditions. LT codes [1] and Raptor codes [2] are two kinds of popular rateless codes. LT codes are the first implementation of rateless codes with simple encoding structure and decoding algorithm [1]. Raptor codes are an extension of LT codes, obtaining a linear time encoder and decoder by a concatenation of high-rate pre-codes with LT codes [2]. The RA-structure rateless codes are a new type of rateless codes [3]-[5]. Unlike the LT codes and Raptor codes which are obtained directly from a generator matrix, the RA-structure rateless codes are constructed by a paritycheck matrix. The advantages of RA-structure rateless codes is that the complexity of encoding and decoding is linear in the code length and capacity-approaching performance can be achieved.
Iterative BP decoding scheme is one of the most popular decoding algorithms for rateless codes over AWGN channel [6]-[8]. However, how to reduce the computational complexity of BP decoding scheme is still a significant issue which must be considered. Generally, the complexity of the decoding algorithm is decided by some factors, i.e., check nodes updating algorithm and the number of iterations. By simplifying the formula of check nodes updating, many low-complexity algorithms have been proposed [9][10]. Besides, many other algorithms are put forward aiming at decreasing the number of iterations. In [11], a method of incremental decoding (ID) is proposed, in which a new decoding attempt is initialized with

the results of the previous attempt. Because the convergence can be accelerated if the decoding schedules are not fixed, the informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) strategy originally for LDPC codes is presented in [12]. In order to stop the decoding process early to avoid unnecessary message updating, a method named incremental decoding with informed dynamic scheduling (IDIDS) is proposed for the rateless codes in [13]. In [14], flooding-assisted IDS is proposed to avoid a possible loss of IDIDS in BER over AWGN channel. All of the above studies are for the decoding algorithm of LDPC codes and classic rateless codes. However, these existing decoding algorithms can not be adapted well to the RA-structure rateless codes. So it is of great significance to study a low-complexity decoding algorithm of RA Structure rateless codes.
In this paper, a Piggybacking Belief Propagation (PBP) decoding algorithm for RA-structure rateless codes over AWGN channel is proposed. When the decoding attempt is unsuccessful, the decoder of the RA-structure rateless codes needs to receive more encoded packages from the channel. In addition, the previous decoding attempt will transmit the decoding results to the new decoding attempt. Instead of setting the information of the existing symbols according to the channel information, the new decoding attempt reserves the results of the previous decoding attempt. This method can substantially reduce the iteration number in decoding. Moreover, by studying the convergence of log likelihood ratio (LLR) in the decoding process, it is found that the change rate of the weight of the syndrome is closely related to the success of the decoding process. A simple and effective stopping criterion is proposed in this paper. Compared with the traditional BP algorithm, the proposed PBP algorithm can greatly reduce the iterative operations.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. We start with a description of the RA-structure rateless codes in Section II. The PBP decoding algorithm is introduced in Section III. Section IV presents the simulation results and their analysis. Concluding remarks are summarized in Section V.

B. Piggybacking the results of decoding

In the iterative decoding process, the belief information of the variable nodes obtained from decoding will gradually approach to correct results as soon as the signal-to-noise ratio exceeds the coding threshold. The results of failed decoding attempts are more reliable than those directly obtained from the channel. If the current decoding process fails, for the rateless codes, the decoder will need to receive more encoded packets again to continue the decoding.

SR-Kite codes can effectively lower the error floor and decoding efficiency.

C. Early stopping criterion
In the iterative BP decoding, the amount of iterations will influence the decoding delay. To our experience, no matter what the channel condition is, the LLRs of all bits fleetly converge to some constants after the initial fluctuations and do not change any more as the iteration number increases.
Based on the related work in [16] [17] that the output LLRs of LDPC-like codes initially fluctuate or enter right away a strictly monotonic behavior, it is necessary to append a stopping criterion in the iterative decoding to obtain lower decoding delay.
s is the syndrome and c is the decoded codeword obtained from the Eq.(9).

As shown in Fig. 3, we adopt Gaussian Fitting to describe the variation of the rw. When the decoding attempt fails, rw fluctuates at 0 at the initial 2 to 6 iterations and then quickly converges to zero. Otherwise, when the decoding attempt succeeds, rw shows a upward curve after the initial fluctuations by 0.
Thus, we propose a novel early stopping criterion for the PBP decoding, which adopts the metric, rw, to stop a decoding attempt. A threshold γ is used to judge whether rw is large enough to guarantee the effectiveness of the decoding attempt. When rw < γ and the number of iterations t is larger than Fn, the current decoding attempt stops and some new packets are received by the decoder to start a new decoding attempt. Fn is the defined initial iteration number in one decoding attempt, which is used to tolerate the unstable fluctuation of rw in the first few iterations.
Some important parameters are introduced as follows. The maximum number of iterations is represented by Imax. The code length of the receiver for decoding is n. The length of the newly extra received encoded bits at the receiver is e. The PBP decoding algorithm is described in Algorithm 1.

For Imax as small as 10, at low Es/N0, the total iteration numbers of piggybacking algorithms are less than a half of the iteration numbers for the traditional algorithms, while at high Es/N0, the total iteration numbers of piggybacking algorithms are slightly smaller than the numbers for the traditional algorithms. At large Imax, the iteration numbers of traditional algorithms are much larger compared with the piggybacking algorithms whose iteration numbers remain unchanged with the increasing Imax, see Fig. 5. For example, when Es/N0 = 1.5dB, the decoding overhead for PSPA and SPA is almost the same while the total iteration numbers for PSPA and SPA are 84 and 302 respectively at Imax = 30. Larger iteration number means larger decoding delay and decoding complexity. Hence, the proposed piggybacking algorithms reduce the decoding complexity and decoding delay drastically.
V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose Piggybacking Belief Propagation (PBP) decoding algorithm to decode RA-structure rateless codes over AWGN channel. The proposed algorithm availably utilizes the LLR results of the unsuccessful decoding attempt and introduces the weight change ratio of syndrome as threshold to control the iterative decoding process avoiding unnecessary iterative decoding attempts. Simulation results show that compared with the traditional BP algorithms, the proposed PBP decoding algorithms can obtain good decoding overhead performance while reducing the decoding complexity and decoding delay drastically.
