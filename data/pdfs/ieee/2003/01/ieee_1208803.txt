Abstract- In this paper we introduce multi-rate PAA
(Parity Accumulator Accumulator) codes obtained from replacing single parity cheek codes with multi-rate cycle
free codes in the conventional PAA codes. They can be
easily decoded by applying the sum-product algorithm to
their factor graph. Using the Gaussian approximation, we
investigate the noise thresholds of multi-rate PAA codes
on the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel.
Through simulation results, it is shown that the performance of PAA codes with multiple code rates is close to
the Shannon limit over the AWGN channel.
I. Introduction
Since Shannon's work in 1948, coding theorists have attempted to design error-correcting codes with performance close t o the Shannon limit. Recently, a spectacnlar progress toward capacity-achieving coding schemes has been made by using the codes that are naturally defined on graphs, including turbo codes, low density parity check (LDPC) codes and repeat accumulate (RA)
codes [U,PI, [SI.
Remarkably good results have also been achieved with parity accumulator accumulator (PAA) codes for high rate applications such as storage devices [lo]. PAA codes are composed of a simple single parity check code, two pseudo-random interleavers, and two trivial rat+l two-state 'accumulator' codes.
Since the code rate of PAA codes is determined by the single parity check code, it is not applicable to systems with various code rates. In this paper we propose multi-rate PAA codes obtained by replacing single parity check codes with multi-rate cycle-free codes in the conventional PAA codes. The multi-rate cycle-free codes are obtained from single parity check codc using the EtZion method [4]. In order to analyze their asymptotic performance, we investigate noise thresholds of multirate PAA codes over the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel by using the Gaussian approximation [Z]. The noise threshold is defined as the maximum noise level such that the probability of bit errors tends to zero as the number of iterations goes to infinity. Simulation results show that the performance of PAA codes with

multiple code rates is close to the Shannon limit over the AWGN channel.
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section 11, we describe the construction method and decoding algorithm of multi-rate PAA codes. In Section 111, we derive simple degree distributions of multi-rate PAA codes and analyze their asymptotic performance by using a Gaussian approximation. Finally, some numerical results and concluding remarks are given in Section IV.
