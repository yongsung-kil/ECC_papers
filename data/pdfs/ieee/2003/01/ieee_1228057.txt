I. INTRODUCTION
In recent years, codes defined by a low density parity check matrix (LDPC codes) have been found to perform remarkably well with the implementation of belief propagation algorithms for decoding (see [2], for example). Simulations often reveal the introduction of an error floor at very low bit error probability rates, and this is often attributed to the presence of low weight codewords. In particular, as linear codes are distance invariant, a low weight word often indicates a poor distance distribution. This is not the case, in general, for non-linear codes, and many of the best codes are non-linear. In the following discussion we show that, defined in the right way, a non-linear code can be encoded and iteratively decoded with complexity comparable with an LDPC code of the same information rate.
