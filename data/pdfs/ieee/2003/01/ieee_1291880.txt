I. INTRODUCTION
Lorn-Density Panty-Check (LDPC) codes not only give Shannon-limit approaching performance. but also enjoy the advantages of lower decoding complexity and inherent parallelism in implementation [I]. Moreover, the built-in interleaving mechanism in LDPC eliminates the extra interleaverldeinterleaver othenvise needed in communication systems [2]. Combined with high spectral efficient modulation schemes, LDPC coded M-ary modulation is an ideal candidate for many high speed broadband applications. For example, the DVB-S2 has recently chosen LDPC coded SPSK as its channel coding and modulation scheme.
Efficient hardware implementation of the LDPC'decoder has become a topic of increasing interest. The finite precision effect on decoding performance is an important issue in hard-
ware design of LDPC decoders, since the word length has a significant impact on both hardware complexity and decoding performance. This is especially true in the case of LDPC coded M-ary system, where the initial input to the decoder has a very large dynamic range. However most of the studies on finite precision implementation of LDPC decoder, e.g. [3]
141, are restricted to AWGN channel with BPSK modulation.
The quantification effects on the code performance with higher order modulation scheme and wireless fading channels are yet to be evaluated.
This paper addresses the important issue of the finite precision effects on FPGA implementation of a LDPC decoder core for wireless mobile communications. The possible tradeoffs between hardware complexity and decoding performance are

In this section, we analyze the finite word length effect on the performance of LDPC decoder with M-ary modulation over fading channels. We first consider the quantization of the input LLR, and then compare the quantization effect on the extrinsic LLR with several possible implementations of the sum product algorithm. Uniform quantizer is used throughout the discussion. For a uniform quantizer, let ( q : f ) denotes the quantization scheme in which a total number of q bits are used, of which J bits are used for the fractional part of the value. Under the same q. the precision that can be maintained is proportional to the value of f . The dynamic range on the
other hand, is in inverse relation to f. To meet the hardware
complexity requirement, the maximum quantification bits q is usually limited to a moderate number, e.g. 7.
