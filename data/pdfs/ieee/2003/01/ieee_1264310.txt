Abstract- 
In this paper, we consider the design of simple 
irregular Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes with semi- 
random parity-check matrix for good performance and fast 
encoding in practical applications with finite-length. We limit the 
tium her of degree-two variable nodes to satisfy the semi-random 
construction and then we optimize a simple irregular structure 
for the random part of the parity-check matrix with similar 
technique presented in 111. The procedure is general for any rate 
and the results are illustrated for finite-length and low rate to 
high rate applications. The presented code with rate 118 is 
suitable for CDMA application and is slightly better than the best 
result in 11 1 in addition to fast encoding capability. The high rate 
code (8/9) is also has a gain about 0.25dB with respect to one of 
the best LDPC code used in magnetic recording channel, in 
addition to fast encoding structure.

INTRODUCTION 
A linear block code with the parity-check matrix N,,, 
can 
be presented on a bipartite graph with N vuriable nodes 
corresponding to the elements of codeword and A4 check nodes 
corresponding to the parity-check constraints. Low-Density 
Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are a sub-group of the linear 
block codes with a very sparse parity-check matrix. If both 
degrees of the variable nodes (dv) and check nodes (dc) are 
constant the corresponding graph is regular and the resulting 
code I S  called (dv, dc)-regzdur LDPC codes. Regular LDPC 
codes were introduced by Gallager 121, and rediscovered in 
[3]. LDPC codes have very good distance properties [I], [4] 
and a near Shannon-litnit performance for very large lengths 
[;I. They also can be decoded with a highly parallelizable 
iterative decoding 151, [6] based on the Belief Propagation 
(BP) algorithm [7]. 
The sparse parity check matrix of the LDPC codes results in 
a dense generator matrix for them. The dense generator matrix 
makes the encoding process of these codes complex. General 
consideration for efficient encoding of these codes is 
presented in [SI. A group of LDPC codes with simple 
encoding procedure are designed based on the Jinite- 
geoinetl-ier [9]. However, the structure of the parity-check 
matrix of this group of codes is not completely random, and it 
is not a simple task to design LDPC codes with any length and 
any rate by using this srructure. To reduce the complexity of

If the degree of variable andlor check nodes in a bipartite

V. 
CONCLUSION 
In this paper we introduced simple irregular semi-random 
LDPC codes for finite-length applications. We used asymptotic 
optimization for finding a good check node degree (j) and a 
good dominant degree for variable node (i). As discussed in 
[I], the 
asymptotic threshold 
cannot be a dominant 
optimization factor for finite-length. Therefore, we focused on 
the third parameter ( k )  which has a good effect on the finite- 
length BER performance. The other key-point in our design 
was based on the girth control process [ 11 for constructing the 
semi-random parity-check matrix. 
The design procedure can be applied on any rate. We 
applied the design procedure to construct three simple irregular 
semi-random codes with different rates. The simulation results 
showed the superiority of our designed codes with respect to 
previously reported ones. In addition to having better 
performance, the encoding complexity of the proposed LDPC 
codes is very low. 
The simple irregular semi-random LDPC codes have some 
advantages. These are: simple optimization technique; good 
performance due to the irregularity in their structures; fast- 
encoding capability because of the semi-random parity-check 
structure The above-mentioned advantages of the proposed 
codes make them a suitable candidate for a variety of finite- 
length applications.
