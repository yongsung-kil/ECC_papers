I. INTRODUCTION
Excellent empirical performance of turbo codes and LDPC codes has inspired researchers to investigate decoding algorithms not only for memoryless channels, but also for channels with memory. In this paper, we discuss a channel model with Gibbs distributed noise. The Gibbs distribution which is an exponential-type distribution appears in many fields such as statistical physics, image analysis, neural network etc.. In particular, we here focus on the channels with 2-dimensional correlated noise represented by a Markov random field(MRF). It is known that a joint distribution of any MRF is a Gibbs distribution. 11. PROPOSEDDECODING ALGORITHM
The proposed iterative decoding algorithm includes two inference algorithms: the sum-product algorithm and the mean field approximation MAP(MFA-MAP) algorithm. The mean field approximation is a technique to handle a Gibbs distribution for evaluating expectation and marginal probabilities related to the Gibbs distribution. The MFA-MAP algorithm evaluates approximate posterior distributions of the channel’s state (hidden variables).

111. SUMMARY
In order to evaluate the decoding performance of the proposed decoding algorithm, some simulations have been performed. For some channel parameters, it has been shown that the proposed decoding algorithm can correct errors which cannot be corrected by the conventional sum-product algorithm. In such a case, the MFA-MAP algorithm matched to the channel model improves the decoding perforformance.
