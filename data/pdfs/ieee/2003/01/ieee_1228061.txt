I. INTRODUCTION
After the success of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, LDPC codes have also been proposed for magnetic recording as outer codes in a turbo equalization scheme, where the partial response channel plays the role of the “inner code”. At low and medium linear densities, the performance of such iterative detection/decoding schemes is close to the informationtheoretical limits as given by the Shamai-Laroia (SL) bound for channel capacity, i.e., the performance of high-rate LDPC codes of moderate lengths is only a t about 1.5 dB from the capacity bound [l].However, for recording channels at high linear densities that are predominantly corrupted by electronics noise, this gap considerably increases.

11. CHANNEL MODELAND SYSTEM PARAMETERS
We will use the widely adopted Lorentzian model for the magnetic recording channel with AWGN. Thus, we will assume that the read-back pulse of a single transition is well approximated by a Lorentzian pulse s ( t ) of width PW50 at half of its amplitude. The signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) is defined as SNR = Es/(No/2), where E, denotes the energy of the Lorentzian pulse s ( t ) and N0/2 is the two-sided spectral density of the AWGN. The unitless parameter D , = PWso/T is called the channel normalized linear density, where T denotes the symbol duration. The detection scheme is based on noise-predictive maximum likelihood (NPML) detection, which uses an optimized detector target with spectral nulls at dc and optionally also at the Nyquist frequency. For the coding scheme, we have constructed an array-code-based LDPC code of dimension K=4131 and length N=4652. It has column weight j = 4 and row weights k=35 and 36.
