On Quantization of Low-Density Parity-Check Coded Channel Measurements
Angelos D. Liveris and Costas N. Georghiades
Electrical Engineering Department, Texas A&M University, College Station, TX 77843

Abstract‚Äî We study the effects of quantization at the output of a binary-input additive white Gaussian noise (BIAWGN) channel on the performance of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. The LDPC decoder works with unlimited precision. The quantizer design is based on either optimizing the equivalent channel capacity, cutoff-rate, mean-squared error (MSE) or the LDPC code convergence. All the approaches are compared in search of the most appropriate design criterion.
I. INTRODUCTION
The outstanding performance of turbo codes resulted in their use for practical applications, such as the third generation wireless communication systems. When applying them in such systems, more implementation issues need to be studied, with quantization being one of the most important among them. Significant work has already been done in this field considering quantization of either the received channel values alone [1], [2] or both the received channel values and the internal values in the decoder [3], [4], [5], [6], [7].
More recently, the reinvention of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with performance superior to that of turbo codes led to similar work on quantization for LDPC codes [8], [9]. However, the effect of quantizing only the channel output values with unlimited decoder precision has not been addressed so far. This is the motivation for our work.
We focus on the case of the binary input additive white Gaussian noise (BIAWGN) channel with quantized output values. First, we argue that capacity maximization seems to be the most important criterion for the quantizer design in the case of capacity-approaching codes. It is surprising that so far in the literature this has only recently been suggested [10], not considering any specific channel codes though. Before that, the closest design criterion used has been the cutoff rate [1], [11], [12], which was argued to be better than the minimum meansquared error (MMSE) criterion as it minimizes the bit error rate (BER) for large signal-to-noise ratios [1].
We design several MSE-minimizing, capacity-maximizing and cutoff-rate-maximizing quantizers and compare them in terms of MSE, capacity and cutoff-rate. Then these quantizers are compared by examining the convergence of LDPC codes over the equivalent quantized channels along with codespecific convergence-optimizing quantizers. Both regular and irregular LDPC codes optimized for the unquantized BIAWGN channel are considered. The convergence results are further confirmed with simulations. The purpose of this work is to ascertain which criterion is most appropriate for the design of channel output quantizers in the case of LDPC codes that were designed for the unquantized BIAWGN channel.

V. LDPC-CODE-SPECIFIC QUANTIZATION
In this section, we consider codes designed for the unquantized BIAWGN channel [16] and try to determine the optimum quantizer in terms of faster convergence. The density evolution is used to determine the rate of convergence for each quantizer at a specific Es/N0. Faster convergence in this context means either smaller number of iterations for convergence or lower probability of error for the same number of iterations.
It is straightforward to see that the only difference in the message-passing decoding algorithm when the output of the channel is quantized while the decoder can operate at infinite precision, lies in the channel log-likelihood ratios (LLRs).
