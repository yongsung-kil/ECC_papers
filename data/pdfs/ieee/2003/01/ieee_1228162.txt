Abstract - In this paper, we empirically show the effectiveness of double-loop algorithm based on the concave-convex procedure in decoding linear codes.

I. INTRODUCTION

Recently, Yedidia et al. 111 clarified that if belief propagation (BP) converges, there exists one-to-one correspondence between fixed points of BP and stationary points of the Bethe free energy. Afterwards, Yuille [2] introduced a convergent algorithm called the concave-convex procedure (CCCP) t o find a minimum of the Bethe free energy. Hence by applying CCCP t o decoding of linear codes including LDPC codes, it is expected t o establish a decoding algorithm which exhibits better error performance than the conventional BP-based one. In this paper, we empirically show the effectiveness of CCCPbased decoding algorithm and investigate how the number of iterations of inner loop affects the error performance.
