It is well-known [2] that the performance of LDPC codes over the binary erasure channel (BEC) is determined by certain combinatorial objects called stopping sets. A stopping set S in an LDPC code is a subset of the variable nodes of
the associated Tanner graph, such that all neighbors of S are connected to S at least twice. The size of the smallest stop-
ping set in an LDPC code plays a role in understanding its performance under iterative decoding over the BEC akin to the role played by the minimum distance of a code in understanding its performance under maximum likelihood decoding over a binary symmetric channel. In particular, a good LDPC code must have few, or better yet, no “small” stopping sets.
In this paper, we study stopping sets in LDPC codes arising from 2-designs1of which finite-geometry codes are a subset.
