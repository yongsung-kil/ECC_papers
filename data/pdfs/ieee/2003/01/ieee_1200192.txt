The Extended Irregular -Rotation LDPC Codes
Rich Echard, Member, IEEE, and Shih-Chun Chang, Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—In this letter, we extend the class of deterministic -rotation low-density parity-check codes to a more flexible irregular pattern. The extended codes maintain the advantages of the classic -rotation pattern with easy implementation based on a single vector while providing superb performance.
Index Terms—Code division multi-access, error correction coding.

The extended encoding is easily accomplished following the

classical encoding as shown by the dotted path in Fig. 2. To begin

encoding the extended codeword, compressed projections are

made by replicating the last

bits of the final information

sub-vector in each of the four registers. The projected bits

are combined in the four registers to produce extended

parity bits. If these bits are not punctured, they are sent to

the output buffer. They are also rotated around the perimeter

registers thus enabling additional projections to produce all

the parity bits [3], [5]. The flexibility of this scheme allows

selective column height and puncturing adjustments to match

the configurations discussed in Section II.

IV. CONCLUSIONS
In this letter, we extend the -rotation LDPC codes to include an irregular column stack in the parity check matrix. The performance of these codes is very good for many configurations—less than 1.0 dB from the Shannon limit. The virtues of simple encoding circuitry, superb performance and easily defined code structure make this coding scheme very attractive for future wireless communication applications.
