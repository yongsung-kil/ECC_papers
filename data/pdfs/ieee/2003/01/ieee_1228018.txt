I. INTRODUCTION
Low density parity check (LDPC) convolutional codes are the convolutional counterparts of LDPC block codes. As with traditional convolutional codes, LDPC convolutional codes can be continuously decoded, a potential advantage in some applications. Prior techniques for constructing LDPC convolutional codes [l][2] have resulted in mildly irregular constraint graphs. It has been shown that significantly better threshold performance can be obtained with LDPC block codes by designing code constraint graphs with irregular degrees (31. Following this idea a simple construction technique for obtaining irregular LDPC convolutional codes is proposed.
11. CODECONSTRUCTION
The LDPC convolutional codes constructed herein are timeinvariant and are described by their parity check matrix H(D),.k. ( D is the usual delay operator in convolutional codes.) We first design a binary j x k matrix P . The parity check matrix H(D)j.k of the LDPC convolutional code is then obtained by replacing a ’1’ entry in P by a monomial Da,a 2 0, and leaving the ’0’ entries unaltered. 111. OPTIMIZATION
A particular choice of P completely dictates the possible edge connections of the variable and constraint nodes of the graph. We consider the ensemble of codes obtained for a particular choice of P by randomly choosing the monomial entries of H ( D ) . The threshold is the limiting performance of the decoder when the maximum power of D in H ( D )tends t o infinity. P is chosen so as to obtain a code with a good threshold. We note that if P contains a 2 x 3 or a 3 x 2 sub-matrix with no zero entries, then irrespective of the choice of monomials D”, the constraint graph always has a 12 cycle. Thus, a good choice for the monomial entries would be those that yield a constraint graph with the largest possible girth of 12.
