Design of Scaled BP-Based Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes on Flat Rayleigh Fading SI Channel Using EXIT

He Zheng, Xiaolei Jin, and Hanying Hu
Department of Communications Engineering, Information Engineering University, Zhengzhou 450002, China
E-mail: hezngghotmail.com

Abstract-By means of extrinsic information transfer (EXIT), we propose a novel method for designing the scaled belief propagation (BP)-based algorithm for decoding of lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes on the uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading channel with side information (SI). Using this method, numerical calculations on convergence thresholds for several rate-1/2 regular LDPC codes are provided. The simulation results for the (3,6) regular LDPC code of length 8000 on the Rayleigh fading channel show that the scaled BP-based algorithm with the proposed factor by the EXIT method can achieve the performance very close to that of the BP algorithm.
Keywords-LDPC codes, EXIT, scaled BP-based algorithm, convergence thresholds.
I. INTRODUCTION
It is well known that the belief-propagation (BP) algorithm [7] provides a powerful tool for iterative decoding of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes proposed by Gallager [6]. Recently, various iterative decoding algorithms based on the BP algorithm have been presented. The uniformly most powerful (UMP) BP-based algorithm [5] needs only real additions and is universal as it does not need any a priori infonnation about the channel state. The UMP BP-based algorithm reduces the decoding complexity of BP significantly, but degrades the decoding performance as well. Nevertheless, an improved version named scaled BP-based algorithml was introduced [3][4].
In order to achieve a better performance, Chen and Fossorier [4] used the density-evolution method to obtain the factor of the scaled BP-based algorithm for long LDPC codes on the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. In [8], Ohhashi et al. applied the densityevolution method to the design of scaled BP-based algorithm on the fast Rayleigh fading channel with no side information (NSI). In this paper, we deal with the uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading SI channel, i.e., the channel with perfect SI. However, our proposed method for designing the scaled BP-based algorithm is by using extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) (1112], rather than by the density evolution. In [10], we name it EXIT-trajectory method.
This paper is organized as follows. Section II briefly reviews the scaled BP-based algorithm. In Section III, we

give the EXIT analysis and show the design of the scaled BP-based algorithm on the flat Rayleigh fading SI channel. Experimental simulations are provided in Section IV. Section V concludes the paper.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
On the BPSK modulated uncorrelated Rayleigh fading SI channel, the simulation results of the scaled BP-based algorithm with a=0.75 for the (3,6) regular LDPC code of length 8000 are shown in Fig. 3, where the simulation perfonnance for the BP and UMP BP-based algorithms are also included for comparison. In order to show the validity of our proposed method we also include the performance of the algorithm with a=0.65 and a=0.90 in this figure. For all the simulations, the maximum number of iterations is set to be 100.
From the figure, the scaled BP-based algorithm with the factor we proposed outperfonns the UMP BP-based algorithm significantly, and also are better than the algorithm with scale factors 0.65 and 0.90. At a decoded bit error rate
(BER) of 10-5, the EINo required for the scaled BP-based algorithm with the proposed factor is superior to that of UMP BP-based algorithm over 0.75dB, outperforms that of the algorithm with factor 0.65 (resp., 0.90) by 0.30 dB
(resp., 0.05dB), and has the gap to that of BP algorithm within 0. 15 dB.
V. CONCLUSIONS
On the uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading SI channel, we proposed a novel method for designing the scaled BPbased algorithm for decoding of LDPC codes by using EXIT. Compared with the density-evolution method, our proposed method is more convenient. The simulation results for the (3,6) regular LDPC code of length 8000 on the Rayleigh fading channel show that the scaled BP-based algorithm with the proposed factor by the EXIT-trajectory method can achieve the performance very close to that of the BP algorithm.
