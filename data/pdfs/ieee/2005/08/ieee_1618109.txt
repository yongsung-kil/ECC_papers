Abstract-Rate-compatible coding is appropriate for communication systems that may experience a range of operating SNRs but that seek to adhere to a single underlying codec structure. This paper proposes two special and efficient approaches: extending and information-nulling, to build low rate codes from high rate LDPC codes (mother codes) of moderate length (103<A<104). Both the two proposed approaches result in better performances than punctured RC-LDPC codes at low code-rates. Extending leads to good performance at rate about 0.4-0.6, but which has not so good performance at high rates (about 0.60.8). The threshold of extended code rates is pointed out for the first time, which rests with the code-rate of the mother code. Information-nulling, which results in acceptable performance at high rates, and also good performance at low rates without any threshold of code rates, is confirmed to have a wider rate range and lower computation complexity than extending.
Keywords-low density parity check codes, rate compatible, extending, information nulling
1. Introduction Flexible rate is always desired in the design of practical error control systems. Rate-compatible (RC) codes are a family of nested codes where the codeword bits from the higher-rate codes are embedded in the lower-rate codes and, hence, can be encoded and decoded using a single encoder/decoder pair. They are of particular interest both in packet data systems that allow for retransmission request and in adaptive modulation and coding (AMC) schemes for tiding over time

variant fading interference in wireless channels. This paper focuses on low density parity check
(LDPC) codes [2] which have been shown to provide performance comparable to turbo codes, yet with lower decoding complexity. Basing on the conventional approach of building high rate RC-LDPC codes by puncturing parities of low rate
mother codes [3], two approaches of building low
rate codes from a high rate one: extending and information nuiling, are proposed in this paper. Compared to the bad performance of punctured RC-LDPC codes at low rates [3], both which are efficient to construct good RC-LDPC codes at low rates. Analysis on the extending structure reveals that there is a threshold of extended code rates,
which relies on the code rate of mother code (Ro). Information nulling, which has no such a threshold at low rates and results in much better performance at high rates, has a wider range of code-rate and
lower computation complexity than extending and
puncturing to get the same RC-LDPC c,odes.
This paper is organized as follows. Section 2 discusses the construction of efficient RC-LDPC
codes using extending and information nulling.
Section 3 shows the simulation results and analysis the performance of the above two approaches. Section 4 provides conclusions.
