A CLASS OF TURBO-LIKE LDPC CODES AND THEIR
DECODING BASED ON NEURAL NETWORK
Zijian Zhao and Xiaojuan Wu, Jun Yang School of Information Science and Engineering, Shandong University
E-mail: zhaozi.ian@sdu.edu.cn, xiaoiwu(i)sdu.edu.cn
Abstract Turbo codes and LDPC codes are both superior to other error correcting codes. These two families of codes have some similarities. It seems a good idea to design new codes combining these two codes. Through the analysis of turbo codes, we can consider turbo codes as block codes and find their matrix H. In this paper, we give a new class of turbo-like LDPC codes combining the advantages of turbo and LDPC codes and a decoding method based on neural network. A lot of simulations have been made and the results reveal that these codes have robuster performance than turbo codes. Key words Turbo Codes; LDPC Codes; Sum-product Algorithm; Turbo-like Codes; Message Passing; Sparse Matrix; Self-loop; Hopfield Network;

1. INTRODUCTION Recently, Low-density parity-check
(LDPC) codes have got a lot of attention in the community of error correcting codes. LDPC codes were introduced by Gallager in 1961 [1] with the concept of iterative decoding (message passing algorithm). These codes, which had been forgotten for about 35 years, were recovered, thanks to the work of MacKay and Neal. LDPC codes were also extended in [2] to include the irregular LDPC codes yielding BER (bit-error rate), which has better performance than that of turbo codes theoretically. LDPC codes have been applied to BPSK and 8PSK, and their fundamental performances have been evaluated on additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel and Rayleigh fading channel. In practice the performance of turbo codes is better than that of LDPC codes under some conditions. However, Turbo codes and LDPC codes are both superior to other error correcting codes. We have noted some similarities between them. They can be both defined in terms of sparse graphs, which define the constraints satisfied by codewords. Both families of codes

are decoded using a local probability propagation algorithm. Since these two families of codes are both good in performance, it will be a good idea to design new codes combining these two codes families.
In this paper, the author gives a new class of turbo-like LDPC codes and makes the performance analysis of the turbo-like LDPC codes based on neural network decoding. After a lot of computer simulations, the results show that the new codes are comparable with turbo codes on both AWGN channel and Rayleigh fading channel, using BPSK.

A turbo code (n,k) is defined by a number of constituent encoders (often 2) and an equal number of interleavers. The constituent encoders are often convolutional codes. Feeding them into each constituent encoder in the order defined by the associated interleaver, and transmitting the bits that come out of each constituent encoder encode a string of k source bits. The transmitted codeword then consists of k source bits followed by ml parity bits generated by the first convolutional coder, and m2 parity bits by the second (ml + m2= n -
k). The mx n parity check matrix of a regular
LDPC codes defines m trellises. We can think of a turbo code as a trellis constrained code in which there are 2 trellises. Therefore, it is noted that for the standard constraint length 4
convolutional codes, the profile of the turbo code's parity check matrix has roughly k columns of weight about 4, the remaining columns of weight about 5, and the row weight about 7 for all rows.
The behavioral difference between turbo codes and LPDC codes does not stem from their decoding strategies, but it will be a good idea that we use the "extrinsic information" passing of turbo bit decoding in LDPC codeword decoding, which has been proven by simulation results.

Considering the advantages of turbo codes and LDPC codes, a new method of encoding and decoding is given in this paper. There is a detailed introduction as following.

5. SUMMARY From Fig.2 and Fig.3, it can be seen that
this kind of turbo-like LDPC codes have robuster performance than turbo codes' on Rayleigh fading channel and AWGN channel, which is owing to self-loops. They will be well used in wireless communication systems for their better performance on Rayleigh fading channel. In the domain of high SNR (signal-noise ratio), the turbo-like LDPC codes are not better than turbo codes, although this kind of codes combine LDPC codes with turbo codes. In my opinion, the message passing of the decoding algorithm of turbo codes is more efficient than that of turbo-like LDPC codes. Turbo codes only send message all the way along trellises, so the within-trellis messages are correct. We can predict that some good error correcting codes combining LDPC codes with turbo codes will appear in the future.
