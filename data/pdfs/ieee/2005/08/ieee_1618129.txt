Abstract
In this paper, we propose a novel and simple construction of regular LDPC codes using sparse binary sequences. The distribution matrix of ones in a paritycheck matrix is introduced into this new construction. The cyclic cross correlation function and the cyclic auto correlation function are employed to ensure that the girth of Tanner graph is at least eight. The new codes perform well using the sum-product algorithm for iterative decoding. Low encoding complexity can also be achieved due to the inherent quasi-cyclic structure of the LDPC codes.
1 Introduction
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were first presented by Gallager [1] [2] in 1960's. Several years ago, LDPC codes were rediscovered by MacKay and Neal [3], Sipser and Spielman [4] and Wiberg [5]. Since then, intensive research has been dedicated to the study of these codes. In recent years, various design schemes of LDPC codes have been proposed by some researchers.
Since the sum-product decoding algorithm will provide the optimal decoding if the Tanner graph of the code is free of cycles, it is natural to try to minimize the effects of the cycles when there are cycles in the Tanner graphs [5]. Some improvements have been made to Gallager's original random construction method to avoid short cycles. MacKay [6] gave a random method to get some LDPC codes and their parity check matrices are listed in [7]. The progressive edge growth (PEG) construction [8] hasbeen proved to produce-the best known Gallager codes [7]. These codes with different random structures can often achieve improved error-correction performance.
Yu Kou et al[9] proposed a geometric approach to

the construction of LDPC codes and the construction is based on the lines and points of a finite geometry.
It can get quasi-cyclic LDPC codes with low encoding complexity.
In this paper we present an effective algorithm which can generate LDPC codes with performance slightly better than that of the PEG codes, while maintaining fairly low encoding complexity. In the next section, we describe LDPC codes from sparse binary sequences, their structures and their girth distribution. Section

Sparse binary sequences are those binary sequences that contain a small fixed number of ones and a large number of zeros. These sequences and their shifted sequences by specified positions constitute the paritycheck matrix of an LDPC code such that each sequence corresponds to a row in this matrix.
