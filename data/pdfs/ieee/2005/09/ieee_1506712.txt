An Improvement on the Modified Weighted Bit Flipping Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes
Ming Jiang, Chunming Zhao, Member, IEEE, Zhihua Shi, and Yu Chen

Abstract— We present a major improvement to the modified weighted bit flipping (MWBF) decoding algorithm for Low Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes. We improve the flipping criterion by introducing a more efficient method for computing the reliability of the parity checks. We derive an approximate theoretical expression for the weighting factor and demonstrate its consistency with simulation results. The improved MWBF algorithm achieves a noticeable gain over the MWBF algorithm with only a modest increase in computational complexity.
Index Terms— LDPC, MWBF, iterative decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW Density Parity-Check codes was first proposed by Gallager in his pioneer paper [1] in 1962. They were recently brought back into prominence by MacKay [2] and shown to form a class of Shannon limit approaching codes decoded by iterative algorithms.
LDPC codes can be decoded by several well known decoding algorithms including: hard-decision, soft-decision and hybrid schemes, such as Bit Flipping (BF) algorithm, Weighted Bit Flipping (WBF) algorithm, and Belief Propagation (BP) algorithm, in order of complexity. BF or WBF algorithm flips one bit (or a group of bits) per iteration, based on a certain criterion known as the flipping function, until all the parity checksums are satisfied. The flipping function of BF algorithm simply counts the number of unsatisfied checksums in which every received bit participates. WBF algorithm includes the reliability information of syndromes calculated from the received or flipped received sequence and the LDPC parity check matrix. In [3], Liu and Pados suggested a new LP-WBF method, to improve the computation of syndrome reliability. It was shown that when decoding some Finite Geometric (FG) LDPC codes, LP-WBF can achieve performance within 1dB of iterative BP decoding performance at the BER of 10−6. Another flipping scheme, MWBF, considering the reliability information of received symbol was introduced in [4], in which a modified WBF algorithm is presented as a further improvement of WBF algorithm at the expense of sizable computational increase. In this paper, we present a new approach to improve the performance of MWBF with measurable increase in computational cost.

IV. ANALYSIS OF THE WEIGHING FACTOR
To achieve the best decoding performance for a given code, the weighting factor α should be optimized. In [4], there are some suggestions on the choices of α by simulations. First, the optimal value of α decreases slowly as the SNR increases and the degradation of the performance is relatively small with different α at the low SNR. Second, at a given SNR, the optimal value of α increases with the column weight of LDPC codes, but is independent of the code length.

It is observed that the optimal values of α by BER simulation results given in [4] and by Monte Carlo simulations according to (11) are very close. Furthermore, for LDPC codes with length over 1023, the optimal value of α can be easily derived which would be otherwise very difficult to determined through simulations.
B. Performance of improved MWBF algorithm

In this paper, an improvement of MWBF algorithm for regular LDPC codes has been proposed together with an approximate analysis of the weighting factor α. This algorithm and the computation of factor α are effective for both Gallager codes and FG codes. It offers a good tradeoff between approaching the error performance of SISO iterative BP decoding and the need to maintain low decoding complexity of the hard-decision BF or WBF algorithm.
