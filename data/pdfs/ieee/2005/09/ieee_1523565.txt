Design of Irregular Concatenated Zigzag Codes

Song-Nam Hong
Advanced Research Lab Telecommunication R&D Center Samsung Electronics Co., Suwon, Korea Email: sn7955.hong@samsung.com

Dong-Joon Shin
Coding & Communications Research Lab Dept. of Electronics and Com. Eng. Hanyang University, Seoul, Korea Email: djshin@hanyang.ac.kr

Abstract— Capacity-approaching codes using iterative decoding have been the main research subject of coding area during past decade. In this paper, a new channel coding scheme called irregular concatenated zigzag (ICZZ) code is proposed. ICZZ codes can be viewed as a special case of irregular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with linear-time encodable structure. They are different from concatenated zigzag (CZZ) codes in the sense that the number of information bits entering into each zigzag encoder can be different and different zigzag codes can be used as component codes. A simple method to design ICZZ codes is proposed and by using an example, it is shown that ICZZ code of rate 1/3 has better performance than the optimal CZZ code and Turbo code adopted in 3GPP.

I. INTRODUCTION

Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were introduced by Gallager [1] and rediscovered by MacKay and Neal [2]. Richardson, Shokrollahi and Urbanke showed that irregular LDPC codes could have better performance on the AWGN channel than Turbo codes, which was nearly close to the Shannon limit. Also, the decoding complexity of LDPC codes is less than that of Turbo codes since LDPC decoding is based on sum-product algorithm. However, the encoding complexity of LDPC code is quadratic in the block length, which results in slow encoding. Therefore, efficient encoding algorithms have been studied [3] and various fast encodable codes such as irregular repeat accumulate (IRA) codes, concatenated tree (CT) codes and concatenated zigzag (CZZ) codes were proposed [4], [5], [6].
Especially, CZZ codes, in addition to simplicity, have reasonably good performance with iterative decoding. CZZ codes have the fixed degree for information nodes since the same information bits enter into each zigzag encoder after being interleaved. The performance of CZZ codes can be improved by varying the degrees of information nodes similarly as irregular LDPC and IRA codes. In this paper, we propose a new class of irregular LDPC codes, called irregular concatenated zigzag (ICZZ) codes. ICZZ codes are different from CZZ code in the sense that the number of information bits entering into each zigzag encoder can be different and different zigzag codes can be used as component codes. Therefore, various degrees of information and check nodes are possible. Also, a simple method to design ICZZ codes is proposed and it is shown that ICZZ code of rate 1/3 has better performance than the optimal CZZ code and Turbo code adopted in 3GPP [7].

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we proposed a new class of fast-encodable irregular LDPC codes called irregular concatenated zigzag (ICZZ) codes. By applying density evolution technique based on Gaussian approximation to ICZZ codes, we showed that their threshold values are better than those of the optimal CZZ codes and similar to those of ARA codes [12]. Also, a simple method to design good ICZZ codes is proposed and it is verified that this method results in ICZZ codes which are better than the optimal CZZ code and Turbo code.
