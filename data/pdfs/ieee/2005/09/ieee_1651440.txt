A Study on Symbol and Sub-Carrier Mapping Techniques for MC-CDMA based on LDPC Code with Progressively Increased Column-Weight

Noriaki MIYAZAKI, Chen ZHENG, Toshinori SUZUKI and Hideyuki SHINONAGA KDDI R&D Laboratories, 7-1 Hikarinooka, Yokosuka-shi, Kanagawa, 239-0847 Japan
e-mail: no-miya z aki@ kddi 1abs . j p

Abstract-This paper proposes a symbol mapping scheme on sub-carriers in use of MC-CDMA (Multi Carrier-Code Division Multiple Access) and LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) codes. Aiming at high spectrum efficiency, 16QAM modulation and LDPC codes with the PICW (Progressively Increased Column-Weight) are used here. The PICW-LDPC code is proposed in our previous studies and is proved to have better decoding performance as rate-compatible LDPC codes. By adopting the proposed symbol mapping method, the FER (Frame Error Rate) performance of the PICW-LDPC code is improved, the required EJN0 is reduced by 0.12 dB in an AWGN channel. In order to improve FER performance in the multi-path channel, the proposed sub-carrier mapping is further
applied, and the required EJNO can also be reduced by 2.32 dB. Keywords-LDPC, PICW, MC-CDMA, OFDM, Symbol
mapping, Sub-carrier mapping
I. INTRODUCTION
Broadband transmission and spectrum efficiency higher than that of 3G are indispensable for meeting physical layer performance required by 4G systems or systems beyond 3G. Multi-carrier transmission is regarded as the most likely 4G transmission scheme because broadband transmission causes high multi-path interference in single carrier transmissions. Thus, much research has been performed on multi-carrier transmission such as in [l] to [3]. This paper focuses on MC-CDMA (Multi Carrier-Code Division Multiple Access) with MMSE (Minimum Mean Square Error) equalizer because MC-CDMA is deemed suitable for cellular systems due to its frequency diversity gain and uniform inter-cell interference.
On the other hand, besides multi-level modulation such as 16QAM, FEC (Forward Error Correction) with higher error correction capability than Turbo codes is required in order to increase the spectrum efficiency. In recent years, LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) codes [4] have been the focus of attention of many researchers. LDPC performance with the Sum-Product decoding algorithm is very powerful. For example, the evaluation in [5] reports that the Eb / No required
for error-free transmission on an AWGN channel can almost
attain the Shannon limit by 0.0045 dB with LDPC coding. We have already proposed LDPC codes with the Progressively Increased Column-Weight (PICW) [6], [7] as rate-compatible
LDPC codes. Based on the above background, and assuming the use of
MC-CDMA with multi-level modulation, this paper investigates symbol and sub-carrier mapping techniques for maximizing decoding performance ofthe PICW-LDPC code. This paper is organized as follows. This paper firstly explains the system model of MC-CDMA followed by briefdiscussion of the PICW-LDPC code. Then, the proposed symbol mapping based on 1 6QAM modulation and sub-carrier mapping in MC-CDMA are described, our conclusions are finally given.

III. PICW-LDPC CODE [6], [7]
The construction of LDPC codes is based on a very sparse
parity check matrix. In a parity check matrix including zeros and ones, the numbers of ones in a row and the numbers of ones in a column are defined as a row-weight and a column -weight, respectively. Consequently, the row- and column-weights are very small compared to each matrix dimension. The number of rows corresponds to the number of parity bits and the number of columns corresponds to the length ofa code word. LDPC codes are classified into regular and irregular LDPC codes, and the regular LDPC codes have uniform row- and column-weights. By constructing regular LDPC codes, the positions ofzeros and ones are firstly chosen randomly. However, since decoding performance is degraded when there are a large number of short cycles in the matrix, short cycles should be removed as possible.
On the other hand, irregular LDPC codes have non-uniform row- and column-weights. The columns ofthe irregular LDPC code are divided into different groups, each of which shows the same column-weight. Although there are different algorithms for obtaining a good weight distribution, this paper adopts the density evolution method [8] with a search tool from [9] because it does not use any approximation to optimize the weight distribution. However, since the density evolution is based on infinite-length codes, the finite-length codes cannot avoid having short cycles in the parity check matrix. In the irregular LDPC code, the cycle length is related to the Hamming distance, and columns with a low weight have a much greater impact on the minimum Hamming distance than columns with a high weight [10]. Therefore, in order to optimize the finite-length code, it is important to avoid short cycles generated by columns with a low weight.
Thus, by constructing irregular LDPC codes, this paper starts constructing the lowest column-weight group to guarantee that it will have large cycles. After the above construction, the same construction is performed on the

B. Proposed Weighted Sub-Carrier Mapping for PICW-LDPC Code
In many 3G standards, FDD is employed as a duplex method for up- and downlinks. However, FDD seems to be difficult to predict the propagation channel characteristics because the channel state information transmitted in the opposite link cannot follow up the channel fluctuation. On the other hand, in 4G system, the transmitter can complete each frame transmission earlier than that of 3G by broadband transmission. Therefore, the channel prediction in 4G becomes more accurate than that of 3G. Furthermore, TDD performs much more accurate channel prediction due to the channel reciprocity between the up- and downlinks.
The following discussion is based on the above assumption Ifthe transmitter can predict channel fluctuation, by arranging modulation symbols filled with information bits on the high power sub-carriers and by arranging modulation symbols filled with parity bits on the low power sub-carriers, decoding performance of the PICW-LDPC code might be improved. reduced by 2.21 dB. Since almost the same performance improvements are obtained for the LDPC and Turbo codes, it seems that the weighted sub-carrier mapping leads to the
above E /IN, reduction with another factor except enlarging
the SINR of the information symbols. The discussion in [3] reveals that MC-CDMA with MMSE equalization suffers from inter-code interference due to the loss of the spreading code orthogonality. This loss is caused by channel fluctuation in the spreading code bandwidth. However, by using the proposed sub-carrier mapping, the channel fluctuation becomes nearly constant, so that the inter-code interference power might be decreased and the above performance improvements are realized. V. CONCLUSIONS
This paper presents the weighted symbol and sub-carrier mapping techniques for MC-CDMA with multi-level modulation in order to maximize decoding performance ofthe PICW-LDPC code. Firstly, this paper proposes a weighted symbol mapping method that allocates the information bit onto the noise-tolerant bit position in a 16QAM symbol. In an AWGN channel, the proposed symbol mapping method improves the FER performance ofthe PICW-LDPC code, the required Ez I No can be reduced by 0.12 dB, while the required
E IN, reduction of the regular LDPC code is 0.04 dB.
Moreover, in the multi-path channel, the required E, /No' of
the PICW-LDPC code can be reduced by 0.15 dB, so that it is obvious that the proposed symbol mapping is effective even in the multi-path channel. Moreover, in order to improve the FER performance in the multi-path channel, this paper proposes the sub-carrier mapping that arranges information symbols on the high power sub-carriers. The FER evaluation shows that the reduction of the required E, / No is 2.32 dB for
the PICW-LDPC code. Since the proposed sub-carrier mapping improves both the PICW- and regular LDPC code performance, suppressing the loss of the spreading code orthogonality contributes in the above performance improvement. Examination of the sub-carrier mapping techniques to obtain further performance improvement for the PICW-LDPC code requires further study.
