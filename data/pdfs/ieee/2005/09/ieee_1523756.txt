AbstractвҖ” In this paper, we propose a class of regular LDPC codes from a cyclic difference family, which is a kind of combinatorial design. These LDPC codes have no 4-cycles, i.e., cycles of length 4. We clarify the conditions on which these codes with column weight 3 have no 6-cycles and discuss their minimum distance. Finally, we show the performance of the proposed codes with high rates and moderate lengths.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low density parity check (LDPC) codes, which were introduced by Gallager [1] in 1962, have been shown to achieve good performance close to the Shannon limit when iteratively decoded using the sum-product algorithm with linear complexity. From the practical point of view, it is important to construct the parity check matrix having simple structure which allows us to implement a decoder with lower complexity. Some constructions [2], [3], [4], [5], [6], [7] based on combinatorial design which is determined by a small set of parameters yield such simple parity check matrices. In particular, LDPC codes with cyclic or quasi-cyclic parity check matrices have low complexity of encoding.
In this paper we propose a new method of constructing quasi-cyclic regular LDPC codes from a cyclic difference family which is a kind of combinatorial design. These codes have no 4-cycle, i.e. cycles of length 4, or in other words, their girth is at least 6. We clarify some conditions on which these codes defined by the parity check matrix with column weight 3 have no 6-cycles and discuss their minimum distance. Furthermore, we show performance of the proposed codes, in particular, with high rate (вүҘ 0.8) and moderate codelength (< 10000) which are required in such applications as magnetic recording and optical communications with high speed.

V. CONCLUSIONS
We have proposed a new construction method of quasicyclic regular LDPC codes from a cyclic difference family. Furthermore, we have shown that the proposed codes with column weight 3 have minimum distance 10 вүӨ dmin вүӨ 12, and showed performance of those codes with high rates and moderate lengths.
