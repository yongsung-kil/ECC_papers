I. INTRODUCTION

LOW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes were firstly presented by GalIager [l] in 1962. Since the rediscovery of LDPC codes by Mackay and Neal 121, there has been renewed interest in LDPC codes because their bit error rate performance approaches asymptotically the Shannon limit [3]. Many research are devoted to characterizing the performance of LDPC codes and designing LDPC codes that have good performance L4-71. Based on those methods, we can achieve many LDPC codes with good structure and
excellent performance. However, as showed in Fig 1, for a
given code, given iterative decoding algorithm and given channel, codes have considerable variations in performance
[SI.As Tanner graph (TG) of LDPC codes parameters such
as the girths, degrees and cycles of the nodes have uneven distribution, performance of LDPC codes decoding was influenced differently by different noise distribution patterns although channel has the same signal-to-noise ratios (SNRs). Motivated by this question, we propose a structure protection (SP) algorithm, which is different from the scheme introduced in [5-71.Instead of changing the TG of the code, it finds the noise sensitive nodes of TG and tills given information. By this means, the' probability o f bad noise distribution patterns that destroy the performance of LDPC codes is reduced; and at the same time the probability of good noise distribution patterns is improved.
