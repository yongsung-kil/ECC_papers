AbstractŌĆö In this paper, an algorithm for Low-Density ParityCheck (LDPC) codes with reduced complexity is presented. The complexity reduction is achieved by calculating a linear function for updating the check nodes in each iteration, instead of an exponential and logarithmic function. The parameters of the linear function are optimized by density evolution. Simulation results show that there is nearly no loss in the performance by using this approximation compared to the exact calculation of the belief propagation decoding algorithm, even for very large block lengths.
Keywords ŌĆō LDPC codes, iterative decoding, belief propagation, density evolution, complexity reduction
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes were originally invented by Gallager in 1963 [1]. He showed that LDPC codes are capable of reaching a performance close to the channel capacity at low complexity, when they are decoded by an iterative decoding algorithm, the so-called belief propagation or sum-product algorithm. However, concatenated codes were preferred for practical reasons and LDPC codes were mainly forgotten for more than 30 years. They were rediscovered in 1996 by MacKay and Neal [2] and also by Wiberg [3]. Because of their good performance and their comparably low decoding complexity, they became serious competitors to Turbo Codes.
Gallager defined LDPC codes as codes that are specified by a sparse parity-check matrix, i.e., by a parity-check matrix that contains a low number of non-zero elements. He only considered regular LDPC codes, i.e., codes that are represented by a parity-check matrix with a constant number of ones in each row and in each column. Later, it was shown [4], [5], [6] that the performance of LDPC codes in the waterfall region can be improved by using irregular LDPC codes. To optimize irregular codes for any memoryless channel, a method was introduced in [5], called density evolution. In [7], an irregular LDPC code of rate 1/2 was designed that can perform within 0.0045 dB of the Shannon limit by transmission over an Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel.
Since implementing the belief propagation decoding algorithm for LDPC codes with the exact calculation in a hardware

In this paper, we modify the density evolution to design the optimum parameters of this linear approximation for the decoding of different regular and irregular LDPC codes. We show by simulation results that the use of the optimized linear approximation gives virtually the same performance as the exact calculation.

The variable nodes correspond to the columns of the parity-check matrix H and the check nodes represent the rows of the parity-check matrix. However, for high Eb/N0 all different decoding algorithms results in approximately the same FER. When the codeword length n increases, the influence of the different decoding approximations for the performance gets larger. The threshold of the sig-min approximation is about 0.91 dB worse than the threshold of the exact calculation.
VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, the parameters of a linear approximation for the decoding of LDPC codes were optimized by discretized density evolution. It was shown for several codes, that the threshold of the belief propagation algorithm with the exact calculation is only slightly higher than with the optimized linear approximation. Furthermore, simulation results show that there is nearly no degradation in the performance by using the optimized linear approximation compared to the exact calculation, even for large codeword lengths.
