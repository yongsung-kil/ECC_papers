Abstract- In this paper, we present an algebraic method for constructing regular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes based on narrow-sense-primitive BCH codes. The construction method results in a class of high rate LDPC codes in Gallager’s original form. Codes in this class are free of cycles of length 4 in their Tanner graph and have good minimum distances. They can perform well with the iterative decoding. Also, proposed algebra LDPC codes can be designed for a new class of irregular codes based on a semi-algebraic structure for various code rates. It is shown that, with the proposed construction algorithms, fast construction time and reduced memory without the performance degradation can be achieved.
Index Terms-Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, BCH codes, irregular LDPC codes and sum product algorithm.

I. INTRODUCTION

Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were originally

discovered by Gallager in his doctoral thesis [1]. After being

overlooked for almost 35 years, this class of codes has

received the much attention owing to their possibility of

providing the near Shannon-limit performance and relative

simple decoding structure [2]. These LDPC codes with the

iterative decoding, such as sum-product algorithm [2], can

perform amazingly well. However, main tasks of designing

LDPC codes are how to construct the parity check matrix and

deduce the encoding complexity.

Over recent years, the algebraic construction and efficient

encoding methods of LDPC codes have become a focal point

of research [3]. In [4], Fan proposed a class of algebraically

constructed LDPC codes from a family of array codes, which

is shown in (1). Let k be a prime and j ≤ k an integer.
LetC( j,k)be the binary code of length k 2 , whose parity check

For high rate and moderate length (say up to about 5000),

these codes perform as well as the best comparable randomly

constructed regular LDPC codes given in [2]. Ping presented the semi-random structure, which is one of efficient encoding methods in [5]. Recently, this approach has been extended in
[6] [7] [8] such as designs of π -rotation codes and semi-
algebraic codes. However, these LDPC codes include some correlations (cycle-4) between the left part and the right part although they have a good performance.
In this paper, an algebraic method for constructing binary regular LDPC codes is presented. The construction method is based on binary narrow-sense-primitive BCH codes. The constructed LDPC codes are free of cycles of length 4 and have at least cycle-6. These codes are simple in structure and have good minimum distances. Considering efficient encoding methods, proposed LDPC codes are designed for a new class of irregular codes based on semi-algebra structure for desired code rates. They can achieve the fast construction time and reduced memory without the performance degradation with the iterative decoding algorithm.
This paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the narrow-sense and primitive BCH codes briefly. Section III designs and analyzes algebraic LDPC codes based on BCH codes. Also, the proposed construction is extended to irregular LDPC codes with the semi-algebra structure. Some regular BCH-based LDPC codes and irregular codes are presented in section IV. Section V concludes the paper.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a simple algebraic method for LDPC code construction based on narrow-sense-primitive BCH codes have been presented. The designed codes have girth at least 6 and large minimum distance. They can be easily extended to a new class of irregular LDPC codes based on a semi-algebra structure. Also they can achieve a large coding gain with the sum product algorithm.
