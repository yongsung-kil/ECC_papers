A Dynamic Normalization Technique for Decoding LDPC Codes
Yen-Chin Liao, Chien-Ching Lin, Chih-Wei Liu, and Hsie-Chia Chang Department of Electronics Engineering National Chiao Tung University Hsinchu, Taiwan 300, R.O.C. Email: julia@twins.ee.nctu.edu.tw

Abstract— In this paper, a dynamic normalization technique is proposed to approximate the nonlinear operation in decoding LDPC codes. The criterion in determining the normalization factor is also presented with theoretical analysis. The proposed method improves the approximation accuracy as well as the error performance of min-sum algorithm. Furthermore, the hardware implementation benefits from a simplified normalization scheme, leading to reductions in complexity and implementation loss.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity check (LDPC) codes, proposed by Gallager [1] in the early 1960s and rediscovered by Mackay and Neal[2][3] in the late 1990s, had been proved approaching the Shannon capacity under additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel [1]-[4]. The iterative belief-propagation (BP) algorithm [1] enables efficient decoding for block codes of large block size. Because of the powerful error correction capability and low decoding complexity, LDPC codes have been applied to many applications [5].
An LDPC code can be characterized by a parity check matrix H. The matrix H is often described as a Tanner graph [6]. Fig. 1 is an example of a parity check matrix and its corresponding Tanner graph. The degree d of a node on the graph is referred to the d edges connected to it. The dc bit nodes connected to the m-th check node in the graph correspond to the dc 1’s on the m-th row of H. The object of decoding is to search for the most likely codeword c which satisfies Hc = 0. During decoding, every node updates and sends the messages to its neighbors. The messages will be conveyed from bit nodes to check nodes, and vice versa. Through such message passing, each node on the graph exchanges the knowledge about the codeword; as a result, the reliability of codeword estimation can be increased after collecting these messages. The decoding process proceeds iteratively until a valid codeword is found.
Several reduced-complexity algorithms with different levels of performance degradation have been proposed in [7] and [8]. The normalization approach in [8] approximates the nonlinear operation by a normalization factor in computing check node messages. In this paper, a dynamic normalization strategy depending on the minimum magnitude among the check node’s inputs is presented and shown to have better error performance. The proposed technique can improve the accuracy of the approximation and reduce the implementation loss due to finite-precision representation. It is shown in the simulation

that dynamically selecting from two normalization factors have given good approximation. About 1.0 dB SNR improvement in the fixed-point simulation is obtained in decoding the LDPC codes in [5].
The organization of this paper is as follows. In section II, the algorithms for decoding LDPC codes are reviewed. The normalization factor will be analyzed theoretically in section III. In section IV, the simulated error performance of the LDPC codes in DVB-S2 [5] with our proposed method will be shown. Finally, a conclusion of this work will be given in section V.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
Based on the method proposed in section III, 1000 blocks of the 64800-bit LDPC codes were simulated for the DVBS2 application [5]. The parameters of all coding rates can be derived through the following steps:
1) Given a bit-width, the data is represented in the format of {S, Nint, Nfrc}, where S denotes the sign bit, Nint is the number of the integer bits , and Nfrc is the number of fractional bits.
2) List all available values of β, denoted as {β}. For lowcomplexity design the number of elements in {β} is small.
3) Select one element from {β} that satisfies (15) and results in the best error performance when β1 = β2. Let this β denoted by βfix.
4) Select a pair of distinct elements from {β} that have values close to βfix for B1 and B2.
5) Determine the threshold K for the B1 and B2 chosen in the previous step.
6) There are also few pairs of candidates of B1 and B2 in 4), repeat 4) and 5) and choose the ones corresponding to the smallest probability of bit error by simulation.

After simulating different combination of bit-widths, a set of parameters is obtained for best error performance. The simulation results of two coding rates, 3/5 and 9/10, are presented. These parameters for both coding rates are listed in TABLE I. B1 and B2 for both codes are chosen as 0.5 (1/2) and 0.75 (3/4), which have denominators in 2x form. The choice of these normalization factors can further reduce the hardware complexity, only shifters and adders are needed. The performances affected by the threshold K of both LDPC codes are shown in Fig. 6 and Fig. 7, note that at most 50 iterations was performed to decode a codeword. As the results shown, the best threshold K for the rate-3/5 and rate-9/10 codes are 1.125 (9 × 0.125) and 1 (4 × 0.25).
According to the parameters in TABLE I, the error performance is shown in Fig. 8 and Fig. 9. The ”B=0.75” and ”B=1” cases corresponding to the min-sum algorithm with a fixed normalization factor 0.75 and 1 are also simulated for reference. In the lower SNR case, there is a larger probability that the input magnitude becomes small, which will introduce more errors in (5). About 1.0 dB SNR improvement of the min-sum algorithm is observed in decoding the rate-3/5 LDPC code as shown in Fig. 8. In Fig. 9, the improvement in BER is less significant. The reason for less performance loss for all cases in decoding the rate-9/10 code is due to the higher SNR condition. Thus the normalization factor is always important for the min-sum algorithm at low SNR condition.
