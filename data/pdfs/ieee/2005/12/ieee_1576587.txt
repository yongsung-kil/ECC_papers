Construction of Low-Density Parity-Check Convolutional Codes through Progressive Edge-Growth
Zhengang Chen and Stephen Bates

Abstract— In this letter, we propose a novel approach of constructing low-density parity-check convolutional codes (LDPCCCs), based on the progressive edge-growth (PEG) algorithm. LDPC-CC related modifications are made upon the original PEG algorithm to generate the time-varying parity-check matrix. The proposed method can generate LDPC-CCs with large girth, and is flexible in handling codes with different parameters. Simulation results show that such codes have good performance.
Index Terms— Error correction coding, convolutional codes, block codes.

Simulation results show that LDPC-CCs constructed through PEG have good performance. The proposed method is more flexible than previous LDPC-CC constructions. It can handle both regular and irregular codes. With given code memory, it can generate codes with different periods, which is useful in hardware implementation.
II. PARITY-CHECK MATRIX OF LDPC-CCS

I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check convolutional codes (LDPCCCs), proposed in [1], can be considered as convolutional counterparts of the well-known LDPC block codes (LDPC-BCs). LDPC-CCs are coming into attention of many researchers mainly due to their ability to deal with arbitrary lengths of data and comparable performance to LDPC-BCs [1]. While many methods have been proposed for LDPC-BC construction, the construction methods of LDPC-CCs remain limited. In [1], Jimenez and Zigangirov obtained the periodic parity-check matrix of an LDPC-CC by expanding and reorganizing low-density matrices. Lentmaier etc. constructed regular LDPC-CCs from block parity-check matrices in [2]. In [3], an algebraic construction was proposed to generate quasi-cyclic LDPC-CCs, which are confined to certain degree and size parameters.
In this paper, we propose a construction method for LDPCCCs based on progressive edge-growth (PEG). The PEG algorithm was introduced in [4] with the intention of generating Tanner graphs of large girth. It places the edges between variable nodes and check nodes progressively, trying to maximize the local girth in each step. Although only local optimization is applied, the PEG algorithm can generate LDPC-BCs with very good performance at short to medium block lengths. The PEG algorithm is very flexible in that it can generate LDPC-BCs of any length and any rate. Since LDPCCCs are decoded using the same BP decoding algorithm, maximizing the girth is also a major concern in designing LDPC-CCs. As suggested in section III-A, we can take T = nT as the period and construct the H p with memory M and period T (> M) using the modified PEG algorithm. In this case, however, additional constraints must be applied to guarantee that the LDPC-CC has period T (< T ). Compared to the case T > M, this will narrow the available choices in edge-growth, leading to limited performance.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
We simulate rate-1/2 regular LDPC-CCs constructed through PEG. The proposed method can be used to design a memory M LDPC-CC with different T . Comparison between the LDPC-CC with (M = 128, T = 129) and the LDPC-CC with (M = 128, T = 257) (Fig. 2) shows that increasing T can improve the performance. However, the benefit of increasing T is minor compared to the improvement by increasing M. Larger T implies more phases in the time-varying matrix, but the free distance of the code is mainly dependent on M instead of T . And the decoding complexity is also determined by M. However, making T slightly larger than M can be helpful in synchronizing processors in hardware implementation [5]. Figure 2 shows the bit error rate curves of PEG generated LDPC-CCs with different memory and period parameters. They are better than the performance of the LDPC-CCs constructed by the Jimenez-Zigangirov method [1], and are 0.1-0.2 dB worse than those constructed in [2].

V. CONCLUSIONS
LDPC-CCs can achieve good performance with relatively short memory length. In this paper we have proposed a novel method of constructing LDPC-CCs based on the PEG algorithm. Compared with existing methods of LDPC-CC construction, the proposed approach focuses on girth maximization and can obtain codes with good performance. It is able to generate LDPC-CCs with different rate, memory and period parameters. Both regular and irregular codes can be constructed.
