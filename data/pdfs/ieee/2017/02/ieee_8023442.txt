Reed-Solomon Based Globally Coupled Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes

Juane Li1, Keke Liu2, Shu Lin3, and Khaled Abdel-Ghaffar3
1AppoTech USA Inc., Sunnyvale, CA 94085, USA 2Broadcom Limited, San Jose, CA 95131, USA 3University of California, Davis, CA 95616, USA

AbstractвҖ”This paper presents a special type of LDPC codes constructed based on the conventional parity-check matrices of Reed-Solomon (RS) codes. LDPC codes of this type are referred to as globally coupled RS-LDPC codes. These codes are designed for correcting random errors, random short phased bursts of erasures and long bursts of erasures. The Tanner graph of a globally coupled RS-LDPC is composed of a number of disjoint copies of the Tanner graph of a local RS-LDPC code which are connected together by a group of overall check-nodes (CNs), called global CNs. A globally coupled RS-LDPC code can be decoded with a two-phase local/global iterative scheme which allows correction of local random errors and phased bursts of erasures in the local phase and global random errors and a single long burst of erasures in the global phase.
I. INTRODUCTION
Globally coupled (GC) LDPC codes were first introduced in [1], [2]. The Tanner graph of a GC-LDPC is composed of a number of disjoint copies of the Tanner graph of a local LDPC code which are connected together by a group of global CNs. In this paper, we present methods for constructing two types of these codes with quasi-cyclic (QC) structure based on the conventional parity-check matrices of RS codes. We call these codes CN-based GC-RS-QC-LDPC codes, denoted by CN-GC-RS-QC-LDPC codes. A CN-GC-RS-QC-LDPC code is capable of correcting random errors, short phased bursts of erasures and long bursts of erasures. Using a two-phase local/global iterative decoding scheme, a CN-GC-RS-QCLDPC code can correct local random errors and erasures with a local decoder, and global random errors and a single long burst of erasures with a global decoder.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section II gives definitions, basic concepts, and some earlier results on LDPC codes. Section III presents a specific method for constructing QC-LDPC codes based on the conventional parity-check matrices of RS codes over finite fields of characteristic 2, under certain constraints on the roots of their generator polynomials. Sections IV and V present methods for constructing two types of CN-GC-QC-RS-LDPC codes and show their capabilities of correcting errors and erasures. Section VI concludes the paper with some remarks.
II. PRELIMINARIES
Let GF(2s) be a finite field of characteristic 2 with 2s elements and let Оұ be a primitive element of GF(2s). Then,

the powers of Оұ, namely Оұ0 = 1, Оұ, Оұ2, . . ., Оұ2sвҲ’2, give all the nonzero elements of GF(2s). Note that Оұ2sвҲ’1 = 1.
Let ОІ be a nonzero element in GF(2s) of order k, where k is a proper factor of 2s вҲ’ 1. Suppose 2s вҲ’ 1 = ck. Then, ОІ = Оұc. The k elements ОІ0 = 1, ОІ1, ОІ2, . . ., ОІkвҲ’1 form a cyclic subgroup S of the multiplicative group of GF(2s). The element ОІ is called the generator of S. For 0 вүӨ j вүӨ k вҲ’ 1, we can represent the element ОІj by a binary circulant permutation matrix (CPM) A(ОІj) of size k Г— k whose generator (the top row) has вҖң1вҖқ (the unit element of GF(2s)) as its single nonzero component at position j. This matrix representation of ОІj is referred to as the CPM-dispersion of ОІj. Clearly, the mapping between ОІj and A(ОІj) for 0 вүӨ j вүӨ k вҲ’ 1 is one-to-one with respect to S. If we choose k = 2sвҲ’1, then the CPM-dispersion A(ОІj) of ОІj is a CPM of size (2s вҲ’ 1) Г— (2s вҲ’ 1).
Let B(m, n) = [bi,j ]0вүӨi<m,0вүӨj<n be an m Г— n matrix whose nonzero entries are elements in S. We form an m Г— n array H(m, n) of CPMs and/or zero matrices (ZMs) of size k Г— k from B(m, n) as follows: (1) if bi,j = 0, we replace bi,j with its CPM-dispersion A(bi,j) of size k Г— k; and (2) if bi,j = 0, we replace bi,j by a ZM of size k Г— k. The array H(m, n) is called the binary CPM-dispersion of the matrix B(m, n). If k is large enough, H(m, n) is a sparse matrix. Since H(m, n) is an array of CPMs and/or ZMs, its null space gives a binary QC-LDPC code Cqc [3], [4]. The matrix B(m, n) is called the base matrix of the QC-LDPC code Cqc (or the parity-check array H(m, n)). If no two rows (or two columns) of H(m, n) have more than one location
where they both have nonzero entries, then the Tanner graph of Cqc has girth at least 6. Such a structural property of H(m, n) is referred to as the row-column (RC) constraint [3], [4].
The above CPM-dispersion construction of a binary QCLDPC code Cqc reduces to the construction of the base matrix B(m, n) over a field GF(2s). For such a QC-LDPC code, the
girth of its Tanner graph is related to the choice of the base matrix B(m, n). The following theorem gives the necessary and sufficient condition on the base matrix B(m, n) for the
Tanner graph of a QC-LDPC code constructed by the CPMdispersion of B(m, n) to have a girth of at least 6. The theorem
was proved in [5] and we rephrase it as follows.

III. RS-BASED QC-LDPC CODES

constraint. However, under a certain constraint, parity-check matrices of RS codes over GF(2s) can be constructed to satisfy the 2 Г— 2 SM-constraint, and hence, they (or their shortened
versions) can be used as base matrices to construct QC-LDPC
codes whose Tanner graphs have girths of at least 6, using
the CPM-dispersion construction method. QC-LDPC codes
constructed based on these RS parity-check matrices, called
RS-QC-LDPC codes, perform well over the additive Gaussian

D. Two-Phase Local/Global Decoding
Even though a CN-GC-RS-QC-LDPC code CRS,cn,gc,1 can be decoded iteratively based on its entire parity-check matrix HRS,cn,gc,1(d1, d2, О», О»p) (or HRS,cn,gc,1(d1, d2, p, О»p)), using its local/global structure, a two-phase local/global iterative decoding scheme can be devised to correct local and global errors based on its local and global parity-check matrices, respectively.
Suppose a codeword v = (v0, v1, . . . , vpвҲ’1) is transmitted. Let r = (r0, r1, . . . , rpвҲ’1) be the received word. The decoding starts with a local phase of decoding, where each received section rj, 0 вүӨ j < p, is decoded using a local iterative decoder based on the local code CRS,local with a fixed number of iterations. If all the sections are successfully decoded, the p decoded sections form a locally decoded codeword vвӢҶ = (v0вӢҶ, v1вӢҶ, . . . , vpвӢҶвҲ’1). Then, we check whether this locally decoded codeword satisfies the d2О»p parity-check constraints imposed by the d2О»p global CNs. If it does, the locally decoded codeword vвӢҶ is a codeword in the CN-GC-RS-QCLDPC code CRS,cn,gc,1 and is delivered to the user.
If the locally decoded codeword vвӢҶ does not satisfy the d2О»p global CN-constraints, a global decoder based on the entire parity-check matrix HRS,cn,gc,1(d1, d2, О», О»p) of the global code CRS,cn,gc,1 is activated to decode the received vector r with symbol reliability measures provided by the local decoders and the channel information. The local phase of decoding is a local error correction process which allows for correction of random errors (or CPM-phased bursts of erasures) scattered among p sections of a received word, each containing a small number of random errors (or a single CPMphased erasure-burst). If the local decoders fail to decode the received word r into a codeword in CRS,cn,gc,1, the error pattern contained in r is regarded as a global error pattern, which the global decoder attempts to correct. The local and the global phases of decoding can be carried out iteratively in a turbo-manner.

The null space over GF(2s) of the connection base matrix BRS,conn(d2, n2, n1) of BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2) gives an interleaved code of length n1n2, denoted by CRS,2,interl, which is obtained by interleaving the symbols of n1 codewords in the RS code CRS,2 [3]. We call CRS,2,interl the interleaved code of CRS,2 with an interleaving degree of n1. The subscripts вҖңcascвҖқ in CRS,1,casc and вҖңinterlвҖқ in CRS,2,interl stand for вҖңcascadeвҖқ and вҖңinterleaveвҖқ, respectively.
From (12) and (13), we see that the global base matrix
BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2) consists of n2 sections, each consisting of a local base matrix BRS,1(d1, n1) and a submatrix B2,j , 0 вүӨ j < n2, of BRS,conn(d2, n2, n1). The null space over GF(2s) of BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2) gives a 2sary (n1n2, (n1 вҲ’ d1)(n2 вҲ’ d2)) code, denoted by CRS,prod. From the composition and the structure of the base matrix BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2), it follows that the 2s-ary code CRS,prod is simply the direct product [3] of the two RS codes, CRS,1 and CRS,2, given by the null spaces of BRS,1(d1, n1) and BRS,2(d2, n2), respectively. Hence, the minimum distance of CRS,prod is (d1 + 1)(d2 + 1). The subscript вҖңprodвҖқ in CRS,prod stands for вҖңproductвҖқ.
Based on the structures of B2,j, 0 вүӨ j < n2, BRS,conn (d2, n2, n1), and BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2), it is easy to prove that BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2) satisfies the 2 Г— 2 SM-constraint. Consider a 2 Г— 2 submatrix B(2, 2) in BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2). If the four entries of B(2, 2) are nonzero, then B(2, 2) must reside either in a copy of BRS,1(d1, n1) or in a copy of BRS,2(d2, n2). In this case, since both BRS,1(d1, n1) and BRS,2(d2, n2) satisfy the 2 Г— 2 SM-constraint, B(2, 2) must be nonsingular. If the four entries of B(2, 2) are not from a single copy of BRS,1(d1, n1) or BRS,2(d2, n2), then at least one of the four entries in B(2, 2) is zero. If follows from Theorem 1 that the global base matrix
BRS,cn,gc,2(d1, d2, n1, n2) satisfies the 2 Г— 2 SM-constraint.

VI. CONCLUSION AND REMARKS
In this paper, two types of globally coupled RS-based QCLDPC codes were presented. These codes not only are capable of correcting random errors but also are capable of correcting bursts of erasures, random phased or un-phased. It was shown that a type-2 globally coupled QC-LDPC code is capable of recovering a single erased local codeword.
It seems that the globally coupled LDPC codes may find applications in distributed storage systems. Several aspects of these codes may deserve further investigation which include: (1) devising an effective two-phase local/global iterative decoding scheme for correcting local and global random errors and/or bursts of erasures; (2) designing globally coupled LDPC codes which are capable of recovering multiple erased local codewords (or files in a distributed storage system); (3) developing an effective turbo type scheme for decoding two local LDPC codes in a type-2 globally coupled LDPC code; and (4) investigating the trapping set structures of globally coupled LDPC codes of both types.
