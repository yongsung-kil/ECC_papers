Optimization of LDPC Codes for PIN-Based OOK FSO Communication Systems
Jun Ao, Jiwei Liang, Chunbo Ma, Guixing Cao, Cong Li, and Yufei Shen

Abstract— For middle/short-range terrestrial high-speed freespace optical (FSO) communication systems employing ON–OFF keying (OOK) and PIN photodiode receivers, the noise distribution is symbol-dependent, and the symmetry of the underlying communication channel cannot be assumed. Therefore, this letter employs the asymmetric density evolution technique and an optimization scheme combining four different search algorithms to optimize low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for these FSO systems. Compared with the conventional LDPC codes that are designed assuming symbols-independent symmetric channels the optimized codes are much closer to the Shannon limit for OOK modulated binary non-symmetric channels.

I. INTRODUCTION
S IGNIFICANT efforts have been made to apply on-off keying (OOK) and PIN photodiode receivers to middle/ short-range terrestrial high-speed free-space optical (FSO) communication systems due to the abundant spectrum and low cost of PIN photodiode receivers. However, system performance can be affected severely by various noise sources such as background optical, shot, and thermal noise. Consequently, error correction coding (ECC) is applied to improve the performance of FSO communication systems. Low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes are considered the best ECC known today and approach the Shannon limit very closely with acceptable encoding complexity [1].
For FSO communication systems employing OOK and PIN photodiode receivers, the noise distribution is symboldependent. As a result, the symmetry of the underlying communication channel, which is fundamental for conventional LDPC code design, cannot be assumed. Therefore, this letter considers the optimization of LDPC codes for FSO communication systems employing OOK and PIN photodiode receivers, where the symbol-dependent noise distribution is addressed by adopting asymmetric density evolution [2] for modelling of OOK signal behaviour with belief-propagation decoding

IV. OPTIMIZATION SCHEME
Four search algorithms, including differential evolution (DE), genetic algorithm (GA), particle swarm optimization (PSO) and simulated annealing (SA), were combined to search for optimized degree distribution pairs that yield the minimum possible signal to noise ratio (SNR) threshold for a given bit error rate (BER) [5]. And SNR is defined as S N R = (m1 − m0)2 /σ02 [6]. The search process begins with p0x corresponding to an initial SNR threshold.
