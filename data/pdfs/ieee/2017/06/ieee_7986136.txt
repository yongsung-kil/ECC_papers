Improving LDPC Decoding Performance for ATSC 3.0 LDM profiles

C. Regueiro, J. Barrueco, J. Montalban, I. Eizmendi and M. Velez

Dept. of Communications Engineering, University of the Basque Country (UPV/EHU), Alda. Urquijo s/n, 48013 Bilbao, Spain.
Email: {cristina.regueiro, jon.barrueco, jon.montalban, inaki.eizmendi, manuel.velez}@ehu.eus

Abstract— ATSC 3.0 includes Layered Division Multiplexing (LDM) in addition to the traditional Time Division Multiplexing (TDM) technique in order to offer simultaneous stationary and mobile services. At the receiver side, Log-likelihood ratio (LLR) probability density functions are considered in the decoding process. The main objective of this paper is to analyze the performance of using different LLR PDF formulas for LDM signals in order to find the best trade-off between performance and implementation requirements, measuring the performance gain over TDM.
Index Terms— ATSC 3.0, LDM, LLR, Performance, Simulations, TDM
I. INTRODUCTION
T elevision services consumption is constantly changing due to the fast evolution of big screens and portable devices such as mobile phones and tablets. Viewers demand more video quality as well as novel reception scenarios to satisfy the modern user requests and market rules [1]. In this context, Ultra High Definition (UHD) services must be provided in home devices while High Definition (HD) quality is desired for portable devices in indoor and mobile scenarios.
Advanced Television Systems Committee (ATSC) has just developed the next generation digital terrestrial television standard called ATSC 3.0 [2]. It includes several cutting-edge technologies to fulfill these new requirements. On the one hand, the use of novel very robust Low Density Parity Check Codes (LDPC) [3] which make use of reliability measures in the receiver, such as Log-likelihood ratio (LLR), have been shown to be very efficient [4]. On the other hand, Time Division Multiplexing (TDM) and Layered Division Multiplexing (LDM) [5] have been included as ways to make a flexible use of the spectrum being able to simultaneously transmit several services with different capacity and robustness. In fact, the simultaneous delivery of UHDTV to rooftop antennas and HD services to handheld devices is a top priority of the new generation broadcasting systems. By this way, indoor and mobile novel scenarios are also targeted.
In the literature, LDM, which is slightly more complex than TDM due to the simultaneous transmission of an Upper Layer (UL) and a Lower Layer (LL), has been theoretically proved to offer a notable gain in terms of robustness and capacity when compared with TDM [6]. Furthermore, this performance gain has been also checked through computer simulations and laboratory trials [7].
What is more, it has been checked through computer

simulations that these gains can be even higher when multilayer optimized Probability Density Functions (PDF) are considered for the LLR reliability measures in the UL LDPC decoding process [8]. In return, as the new PDF formula depends on the specific LL constellation points, the UL receiver should keep the whole constellation points although they are not needed with the classical LLR PDF. This is a problem in terms of practical implementation in mobile receivers.
The main objective of this paper is to analyze the gain in terms of performance of LDM over TDM when different LLR PDFs are considered. By this way, the best trade-off between robustness or capacity improvement and practical implementation is presented. In addition, different channel conditions are analyzed by means of computer simulations under several channel models.
The paper is organized as follows. A theoretical analysis of the best alternatives to provide HD indoor and mobile and UHD services is included in Section II. The computer simulations have been described in Section III while the obtained results are included in Section IV. Finally, Section V presents the main conclusions of the work.
II. THEORETICAL STUDY
A. Service Types and Bitrates requirements
In this study, UHD and HD quality services must be provided. The most recommended format for HD services is 1920x1080 progressive scanning (1080p) with 50 or 60 Hz of frame rate [9]. In case of UHD quality, the preferred format for home devices is 3840×2160 progressive scanning (2160p). Besides, more improvements for enhanced quality are also gathered in the BT.2020 recommendation for UHDTV [10]. It includes Higher Frame Rates (HFR) of up to 120 Hz, Wider Color Gamut (WCG) covering 75% of CIE 1931 color space and Higher Dynamic Range (HDR) with more natural and true-to-life colors closer to those in real life. All these enhanced features can be applied in return to an increment in the necessary bitrate of about 8 Mbps [11]-[14].
ATSC 3.0 uses High Efficiency Video Coding (HEVC) [15], whose required output bitrate is still a matter of discussion depending mainly on the perceptual quality. What is more, the Scalable HEVC version (SHVC) [16] even reduces the necessary bitrate when the same information has to be transmitted in two different qualities, as often happens with TDM and LDM signals. III. COMPUTER SIMULATIONS
A. Simulation Platform
A complete ATSC 3.0 simulation platform [20] is used in order to evaluate the system performance under different scenarios. This platform includes a complete transmission and reception chain with all the features described in [2], and in addition, it includes different channel conditions. In the receiver, perfect time and frequency synchronization and ideal channel estimation are assumed. Besides, the platform includes several quality measurements procedures at different points of the receiver chain. In particular, Bit Error Rate (BER) and SNR can be calculated.
What is more, the platform allows any modification to the standard, enabling the possibility of carrying out performance tests of different ATSC 3.0 parameters. In this case, the implementation of the classical [4], LDM optimized [8] and suggested semi-optimal LDM LLR PDFs (considering lower LL constellation orders) for the UL decoding process are implemented.
B. Simulation Procedure
These simulations are carried out with the purpose of evaluating the performance of the delivery of UHD services to

rooftop antennas and HD services in indoor and mobile scenarios. For this purpose, the SNR threshold for correct reception is measured for several channel models to emulate all kind of reception conditions.
In addition to the traditionally considered stationary channel models (AWGN, DVBT-F and DVBT-P) [22], the UHD service is tested in Ray6, Brazil-E & C, CRC-41, 2 & 3, while the robust HD service is also analyzed under indoor (PI, PA, PB) and mobile (PO, VA, VB, TU6) channel conditions. All the selected channel models are defined in the ATSC Proposed Recommended Practice document [21].
The reception is considered error free when the BER value at the outer coder output is lower than 10-6 [22] during 500 msec for stationary channels and during 2 secs for indoor and mobile channel conditions. In order to reach this situation, increasing AWGN in steps of 0.1 dB is added at the receiver input until the first errors appear in the receiver (BER higher than 10-6). The SNR threshold is obtained as the relation between the mean signal power level and the mean external AWGN power level.
This procedure is repeated for the different ATSC 3.0 considered configurations and for all the considered channel models. In addition, different combinations are carried out considering different LLR PDF formulas are in LDM cases in order to measure the performance differences.

B. Optimized vs Semi-optimized UL LDM LLR PDF formulas
Figure 1 shows the SNR thresholds for LDM #3 configuration for different stationary channel models considering different decoding approaches.

Under stationary channel model conditions, combinations 3, 4 and 16 (and sometimes 13, 14 and 15) show a similar performance to the classical approach (combination 1) whereas the rest combinations show a similar performance

than the optimized PDF (combination 2). However, the semioptimized LLR PDF never means degradation in comparison to the classical approach. In addition, the most of the modulation and code-rate combinations have a similar performance to the optimized approach.
The situation follows the same tendency under indoor and mobile channel conditions for the three LDM measured configurations, as it can be seen in Figure 2 for LDM #3.
Figure 2. SNR Threshold for LDM #3 for different LLR PDF approaches under indoor and mobile channel model conditions
The use of combinations 3, 4 and 16 (and sometimes 13, 14, 15, 24 and 25) shows slightly worse performance than the rest of the combinations. However, in this case, the performance of the worst combinations is much better than the classical approach, with degradations of up to 0.4 dB in comparison to the optimized approach.
The small differences in performance for the different combinations can be due to the high robustness of the selected UL, which targets indoor and mobile reception. In these cases, the threshold situation is reached for high noise power levels and, consequently, the influence of the specific LLR PDF is lower.
V. CONCLUSIONS This paper includes a comparative study between TDM and LDM in order to offer HD indoor/mobile services as well as UHD stationary services. In addition, different LDPC decoding PDF have been considered: the classical approach, the LDM optimized approach described in [8] and several semi-optimal approaches in order to reduce the implementation complexity in the receiver. Some computer simulations under stationary, indoor and mobile channel models have been presented showing that LDM is always more robust than TDM in at least 0.9 dB. However, if the optimized formula is considered, there is an extra gain of up to 0.9 dB in stationary reception and 1.5 dB in mobility. The semi-optimal approaches, considering lower LL constellations than the existing one, show very promising results. The most of the options show a similar performance than when using the optimized approach. There are only a few cases with a degradation of up to 0.4 dB in comparison with the optimized approach.
