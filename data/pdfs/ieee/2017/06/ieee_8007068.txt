Abstract—Absorbing sets (ASs) are combinatorially defined objects existing in the Tanner graph of a low-density paritycheck (LDPC) code that have been shown to cause failures in the iterative message-passing decoder when transmission occurs over the additive white Gaussian noise channel. In this paper, we propose an edge spreading approach to construct high rate arraybased spatially-coupled LDPC codes by jointly optimizing the AS spectrum and the minimum distance. By considering general edge spreadings and by considering a larger memory, we show that strictly better codes can be constructed, both in terms of achievable minimum distance for small-to-moderate block lengths and in terms of the number of small ASs.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, originally introduced by Gallager in [1], form a powerful class of capacityapproaching codes with low-complexity decoder implementations based on iterative belief propagation decoding. It is well-known that small graphical sub-structures existing in the Tanner graph of LDPC codes can cause iterative decoding algorithms to get trapped in certain error patterns. These weaknesses contribute significantly to the performance of the code in the so-called error floor, or high signal-to-noise ratio, region of the bit error rate (BER) performance curve. For the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, these error-prone sub-structures are named trapping sets [2]. In that paper, trapping sets were shown to have a strong influence on both the position and slope of the error floor. Previously, such weaknesses of LDPC codes on the binary erasure channel had been defined as stopping sets. In [3], the authors proposed an important subclass of trapping sets called absorbing sets (ASs) that were shown to account for nearly all decoding errors in the error floor of certain LDPC codes. An (a, b) AS (see Section II) is defined as a decoder-independent combinatorial object that can be studied analytically.
Array-based LDPC codes were originally proposed by Fan in [4] and are a construction of quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes with block length p2 for odd prime p. The related array-based spatially-coupled LDPC codes (SC-LDPCCs) were recently studied from the perspective of ASs [5], [6] and from the perspective of minimum distance dmin [7]. Spatial coupling was first introduced in the coding theory literature by Felstro¨m and Zigangirov in [8], where they proposed convolutional LDPC codes or SC-LDPCCs. The number of harmful ASs in the SC-LDPCC depends heavily on the edge spreading method used to couple copies of the underlying block code. In [9], an edge spreading algorithm was presented that was designed to remove harmful trapping sets (with respect to a windowed decoder) from the resulting SC-LDPCC. In addition to the small (dominant) ASs, our results have shown that (6, 0) ASs (codewords with weight 6) cause a significant number of failures of array codes with the min-sum algorithm. This

motivates a construction method that reduces the multiplicity of both the small ASs and the minimum distance codewords.
In this paper, we extend the diagonal cut approach of [5] in two different ways: 1) by considering arbitrary edge spreadings, and 2) by considering a larger memory. We show that both methods give strictly better codes in terms of achievable minimum distance for small-to-moderate values of p, and also better codes in terms of the number of (3, 3) and (4, 2) ASs. Furthermore, we propose a joint AS and minimum distance approach to construct good array-based SCLDPCCs, investigating the trade-off of reducing the number of (3, 3) and (4, 2) ASs versus improving the minimum distance (and associated multiplicity). We remark that the SC-LDPCCs designed in this way can be decoded with a sliding window decoder [10] covering W blocks and, for moderate-to-large p, will have high rate. Here, after a fixed initial delay of W p2, the decoder produces decoded blocks of size p2 in a continuous fashion, a nice feature for streaming applications.

IV. AN EDGE SPREADING APPROACH TO CONSTRUCT ARRAY-BASED SC-LDPCCS

In this section, we present a general edge spreading or
unwrapping procedure to construct array-based SC-LDPCCs
from uncoupled array-based LDPC codes. The edge spreading procedure is defined by an (m + 1)-ary γ × p spreading matrix B, where m denotes the memory of the resulting SC-LDPCC. The purpose of the spreading matrix B is to generate m + 1 component parity-check matrices H0, H1, . . . , Hm, each of size γp × p2 and initially filled with zeros, as follows. Remark 1. There are several ways to ‘break’ an AS over the m + 1 component matrices. Our approach is to characterize these possibilities, and sequentially break the sets existing in the original graph by selectively moving permutation matrices to other component matrices. We note several interesting observations: 1) although we apply our approach for an array-based construction, the approach extends to general QC protograph-based SC-LDPCCs; 2) it is not necessary to keep the p × p matrices intact, i.e., edge spreading could be performed on the lifted graph rather than the protograph, this would give an extra degree of freedom in the design, however, the graph would lose the QC features and associated implementation advantages; and 3) minimizing (or even eliminating) the number of minimal ASs can have a negative effect on the minimum distance, and vice versa (see Section VII).
It was proven in [3] that every minimal (3, 3) AS in G(3, p) was not fully absorbing, i.e., there exists a variable node from V \D that connects to at least two of the three check nodes in O(D). In fact, each pair of the three check nodes in O(D) connects to a unique variable node outside of D [3]. This is not necessarily the case for the (3, 3) ASs in array-based SCLDPCCs where, depending on B, a pair of nodes in O(D) may or may not connect to a unique variable node in V \D.
It is known that the dominant effect in the error floor of array-based LDPC block codes with γ = 3 is (4, 2) FASs [3]. The only possible graphical configuration of the (4, 2) (fully) AS (see [3]) is shown in Fig. 3. Note that this (4, 2) set is obtained by combining two (not fully) ASs of size (3, 3). This can be seen from Fig. 3 by removing either one of the variable nodes connected to a check node from O(D) [3].

B. Numerical Results
We now further reduce notation by (interchangeably) representing the spreading matrix B by a vector b of size 1 × p, where B can be obtained by replacing each entry with the associated base-(m + 1) column vector. For example, the ternary spreading matrix in (2) with m = 2 can be represented by the vector b = [25, 21, 2, 20, 6]. As already noted, this edge spreading eliminates all (3, 3) and (4, 2) ASs from G(3, 5, 2, L, B) for arbitrary L (a result that is not possible following the cutting vector approach of [5], [6]).
Table I shows the number of (3, 3) ASs in array-based SCLDPCCs with memory m = 2 for several example primes p and spreading vectors b. We note that the search space is too large to perform an exhaustive search, so we have performed a heuristic search based on spreading vectors that are guaranteed to break certain cycles. The number of (3, 3) ASs is obtained using (4). In order to compare the number of sets from different codes, we define A(a,b) as the number of ASs of size (a, b) in a code normalized by the code length, i.e., it is the average number of (a, b) ASs that a variable node participates in. For SC-LDPCCs, A(a,b) is computed for L → ∞ which is an upper bound on A(a,b) for finite L.
Fig. 4 shows the minimum A(3,3) obtained for several constructions. The values presented were obtained by sampling 109 candidates from a “guided random search,” where the search space was reduced by selecting the column entries of b from {1, 2, 3, 4, 5} for m = 1 and {5, 7, 11, 15, 19} for m = 2. These values were chosen so that the permutation matrices are “balanced” column-wise over the component matrices. First, we note that spatial coupling has the effect of significantly reducing the multiplicity compared to the LDPC block codes, which have A(3,3) = p − 1. Moreover, the general edge spreading approach presented in this paper gives an extra degree of freedom in the design; as such, we observe that A(3,3) can be further reduced by increasing the memory m. In fact, (3, 3) and (4, 2) ASs can be eliminated completely from SC-LDPCCs with p < 7 for m = 1, and p < 17 for m = 2. Computer simulation results [6] confirmed that reducing the multiplicity of the smallest ASs by a cut significantly lowers the error floor of the SC-LDPCC, so further gains are expected from general edge spreadings.

VIII. CONCLUDING REMARKS
In this paper, we proposed an edge spreading construction
of high rate array-based SC-LDPCCs by considering both ASs
and minimum distance. We showed that the approach results
in strictly better results than existing constructions. Moreover,
both the size and multiplicity of the target structures reduce
by considering larger memories. Combined, these features
promise extremely low error floors, which is confirmed by our
preliminary simulations. Future work involves an extension to
