Modified Weighted Bit Flipping Algorithm Based on Intrinsic Information for LDPC Codes

Velmurugan M, Pargunarajan K,
Department of Electronics & Communication Engineering, Amrita School of Engineering, Coimbatore,
Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham, Amrita University, India E-mail: velumurugan890@gmail.com, k_pargunarajan@cb.amrita.edu

Abstract—This paper proposes a modified bit flipping algorithm and its weighted variants based on reliability derived from intrinsic information. Compared to other existing algorithms where extrinsic information based reliability was used in the flipping function, here intrinsic information based reliability is also equally considered. This algorithm only uses additions and subtractions to modify reliability as compared to other reliability based BF algorithms, which use multiplications and divisions. Hence, it offers good performance with a minimal increase in computational complexity.
Keywords—Intrinsic reliability,GDBF,bit flipping,LDPC,BF
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes were first proposed by Gallager. It was then later revamped by Tanner & MacKay and gained lots of focus in the recent years. Various researches have been carried out to improve the performance of LDPC codes and the two main areas are, methods to construct efficient parity check matrix and methods for efficient decoding. Our focus is on the design of efficient decoding methods. Sum Product Algorithm(SPA) [4][7] is the best-known algorithm but owing to its high complexity implementation, many modified BF algorithms were developed to achieve good performance using very less complexity. Classical Bit Flipping Algorithm(BFA) [1] is simple because only hard decision values are used but performance loss is high as it just uses the count of unsatisfied checksums in making decisions and does not care about the reliability of checksums. To resolve this Weighted Bit Flipping Algorithm(WBFA) [2][3] was proposed which takes into account an approximate estimate of bit reliability from check nodes also called extrinsic information. As a further improvement, Modified Weighted Bit Flipping Algorithm (MWBFA) [2][3] was introduced which excludes the bit information, for which extrinsic information is calculated. Improved Modified Weighted Bit Flipping Algorithm (IMWBFA) [2][3], was developed to take into account the reliability derived from intrinsic information but required a lot of preprocessing to find out the factor α. To eliminate preprocessing and improve performance the Self Normalized Weighted Bit Flipping Algorithm (SNWBFA) [8]was developed. Though there was an improvement in performance, it required complex division operations to take into account the reliability derived from intrinsic information. To eliminate high complexity an intrinsic reliability adjustment based on intrinsic information is proposed which uses simple additions and

subtractions to modify reliability expressions offering considerably good performance.

D. Computational Complexity
Compared to WBFA, the proposed method is slightly more complex as it involves an extra additive factor. However, it is less complex than the SNWBFA which needs extra divisions per iteration when compared with WBFA. The proposed method requires on average extra additions or in worst-case 2 ∗ additions. The exact estimate cannot be made as the number of additions required is based on syndrome value in the ‘E’ expression and the exact value of syndromes varies for each case. In addition, this method does not require any extra storage as it uses already stored | | values for addition/subtraction.

Overall the proposed algorithms require only a mild increase in additive complexity and offer performance much better than the best performing GDBF[5][6]and SNWBFA. This shows the importance of reliability derived from intrinsic information in making decisions, which were usually ignored in other methods. The proposed expressions help the decoder to decide the flipping bit more accurately. Major confusion occurs in the decoder when a large number of bits are in the same level of reliability that is, they lie in the same range of ‘E’ values with very little difference. In this case, other decoders blindly choose the (N (6) which may or may not be the right bit to be flipped. To resolve this confusion intrinsic reliability expressions are introduced, which revises the ‘E’ values so that now the decoder is more clear in choosing the most likely unreliable bit.
IV. CONCLUSION
The above analysis shows the importance of the intrinsic part of bit-reliability metric in decision-making. Intrinsic reliability adjustment carrried out here showed good performance with very little additive complexity. SNWBFA and GDBF were the best performing as compared to WBFA, MWBFA, and IMWBFA. The proposed method offers more performance than SNWBFA and GDBF at much less complexity.
