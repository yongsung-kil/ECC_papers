Low Complexity ADMM-LP based Decoding Strategy for LDPC Convolutional Codes
Hayfa BEN THAMEUR1, Bertrand LE GAL2, Nadia KHOUJA1, Fethi TLILI1, Christophe JEGO2 1 SUPCOM, GRESCOM Lab, University of Carthage, Tunisia.
2 Bordeaux INP, CNRS IMS, UMR 5218, Bordeaux University, France.

Abstract—A node-wise (NS) schedule has been recently proposed for decoding LDPC codes with the linear programming (LP) decoding approach, based on the alternate direction method of multipliers (ADMM). It improves the error correction performances as well as the convergence speed of the ADMM-LP decoder. However, it suffers from a high computational complexity resulted from residuals calculation. In this paper, a new formulation of the ADMM-LP algorithm with a modified computational scheduling is proposed for decoding LDPC convolutional codes. Simulations show that it reduces the computational complexity while improving the error correction performances and speeding up the decoding process. The decoding complexity is further reduced when considering the NS-ADMM approximation which allows savings up to 70% of the residuals calculation.
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CC) provide interesting features such as scalability, low latency decoding and low encoding complexity. Thanks to these features, LDPC-CCs were chosen in many recent applications for wireless (IEEE 802.16m [1]), power-line (IEEE 1901 [2]) and optical communication systems [3]. The application of the LDPC-CC decoding scheme can also cover emergent application scenarios such as MIMO broadband communications [4] and body area networks (IEEE 802.15.4g and 802.15.6 [5]) which are highly recommended for medical health monitoring applications. The interest of LDPC-CCs stems essentially from their flexibility to transmit packet data of arbitrary sizes with the same code structure. Moreover, it has been shown that they can achieve the Maximum-a-posteriori (MAP) decoding threshold with the Belief Propagation (BP) message passing (MP) algorithm over various channel types [6].
An LP based decoding algorithm for LDPC codes was introduced in [7]. It shows a comparable error correction performances to a BP based decoder. A more relaxed version of the original LP problem was defined in [8]. This novel approach relies on the ADMM technique. The main advantage of the ADMM-LP is its capability to behave as an MP algorithm. This is the key motivation of the proposed work that first demonstrates the applicability of the ADMM decoding technique to LDPC-CCs (ADMM-CC) and then proposes different schedulings to reduce its computational complexity.
Generally, the decoding complexity is a critical challenging constraint for MP decoders and so for ADMM-LP decoders. In the literature, two main strategies for LDPC block codes (LDPC-BC) were reported to address this issue. First, multiple algorithmic optimizations were suggested for the Euclidean

projection function that is used during the decoding process [8], [9]. In fact, the Euclidean projection is a highly time and computations consuming operation. Second, alternative scheduling techniques to the conventional flooding (FL) one [8], such as the Horizontal layered (HL) [10] and the NodeWise (NS) [11] schedules, were assessed for ADMM-LP decoders. Indeed, it was observed that the NS-ADMM decoder provides better error correction performances and convergence speed than both the HL and FL [11]. However, extra computations are necessary to calculate residuals. To mitigate this drawback, an approximation of residuals estimation was detailed in [11]. It results in a reduced complexity NS schedule, denoted as ANS. Indeed, the ANS approach aims to remove projection operations needed only for updating residuals. Yet, the ADMM-LP computational complexity is still high.
In order to obtain a competitive global decoding complexity of an ADMM-CC decoder, a less greedy scheduling technique can be applied. In this paper, an efficient mixed scheduling strategy is proposed. It consists in the association of a HL schedule with a NS processing (LNS). The motives behind coupling the NS with the HL are: (a) unlike the FL, both schedules are CN centric, (b) contrary to the NS, no residuals computation is required by the HL, (c) the HL is faster than the FL while providing better error correction performances [10], [11], (d) the NS decoding would be based on more reliable messages than the initially received ones from channel. Thus, it would converge faster than the original NS technique. Typically, it would lead to a relevant lower ADMM-CC decoder complexity. Importantly, the complexity can be further minimized when combining the HL with the ANS (LANS).
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows: in Section II, LDPC-CCs are presented and the ADMM-CC LP decoding is described. In Section III, the novel schedule is detailed and a complexity analysis is provided. Ultimately, experimental results are reported in Section IV.

III. LOW COMPLEXITY NS BASED ADMM-CC DECODING
A. Possible Decoding Complexity Reduction Strategies
1) At the Algorithm Level: In the literature, it was observed that the decoding complexity of an ADMM-LP decoder depends mainly on the complex and time-consuming Euclidean projection operation (about 60% of the CN execution time [16]). Therefore, several algorithmic optimizations of the projection function were introduced [8], [9]. The most efficient

hardware compatible projection formulation was recently developed in [9]. Thus, it will be considered in this paper. In the other hand, Wei et al. [14] suggested to remove the useless Euclidean projections which enables time savings at the cost of extra computations.
2) At the Scheduling Level: As MP decoders performances are sensitive to the order of messages updates, a variety of scheduling techniques have been proposed to address the conventional FL based ADMM-LP decoders’ complexity challenges [8], namely the HL [10] and NS [11] approaches. Both schedulings yield in higher computational complexity than the FL one. Nevertheless, this drawback can be partially sidestepped by the accelerated convergence speed of the ADMM-LP decoder [10], [11]. Later, we propose a new technique that aims to further reduce the resulted additional complexity while improving the decoding performances of the NS and the HL schedules.

C. Low Complexity NS based ADMM-CC Algorithm
Unlike the NS-ADMM-CC schedule, residuals computation is not required by the HL-ADMM-CC. Indeed, both NS and HL are CN centric algorithms. Contrary to the HL, where CNs are processed sequentially in a pre-determined order (Algorithm 2, lines 6 : 11), the CN that has to be updated is chosen dynamically according to r (Algorithm 1, lines 8 : 16). It means that the computational complexity per iteration of the HL is considerably lower than of the NS/ANS [11]. Taking advantage of this property, the new decoding flow is conducted as follows: first, the HL algorithm is performed for a maximum of Ihl iterations (Algorithm 2, Step 2). Then, the NS processing step is executed for a maximum of Ins iterations where Ins = Im − Ihl (Algorithm 2, Step 3). Since the residuals calculation is based on the most recent CN LLRs (Algorithm 2, line 14), the NS algorithm is performed based on more reliable information than the received channel LLRs (Algorithm 1, line 4). Recall that the NS converges after performing a few number of messages updates [17]. Hence, the discussed schedule (LNS) would improve the convergence speed of the ADMM-CC decoder while reducing its overall computational complexity and improving its error correction performances. Furthermore, the complexity of the LNS-ADMM-CC decoder can be further alleviated when teaming up the HL with the ANS schedule (LANS-ADMM-CC).

B. Comparison in terms of Convergence Speed
The average number of decoding iterations (ANI) of the studied ADMM-CC schedules are shown in Fig. 2. Im is set to 20. It can be seen that both FL and HL approaches are slower than the NS/ANS and LNS/LANS ones. Also, the NS and LNS techniques exhibit almost the same ANI as their approximate versions (ANS and LANS). Although the LNS-1 yield the same ANI as the NS, the ANI of different LANS schedulings is raised as Ihl increases. In fact, the total ANI of a LANS is hugely dominated by the resulted ANI from performing first the HL processing. Indeed, it can be deduced from Fig. 3, that about 90% of the ANI of LANS algorithms is produced by the HL, at SNRs= 3.2dB and 3.5dB. This observation can justify the fact that the ANI of LANS schedules of Ihl higher than 12 are closer to the ADMM-CC HL schedule as SNRs raise.
To sum up, the convergence speed in terms of ANI of LNS/LANS schedulings is at most 3.6 times slower than the NS/ANS ones meanwhile it valuably improves the error correction performances. C. Comparison in terms of Average Decoding Time
The speed-up factor (SPF) is investigated. It is the average decoding execution time of one decoding iteration (ATI) for different schedulings with respect to the ATI of the ANS approach with Im equals 20. Resulting accelerating factors are given in Fig. 4. An SPF of about 7 is observed for the FL and HL, making them the fastest ADMM-CC schedules among studied ones. This is due to the fact that they do not need for residuals calculation when performing CN processing. Furthermore, It can be seen that the residuals computation simplification saves decoding time as no projections are required. For instance, ANS and LANS-4 are up to 5 and 3.5 times faster than the NS and LNS-4, respectively. In addition, all of LANS variants are at least 1.5 times faster than the ANS. The accelerating factor can reach 4.5 when Ihl = 16 at high SNRs. Meanwhile, they show close accelerating factors at low SNRs. Moreover, regarding LANS schedules, it can be noticed that, contrary to their ANI evolution, as Ihl increases, the ATI is shorter. Two essential reasons can explain this observation. First, the HL is 7 times faster than the ANS (Fig. 4). Second, for LANS, 90% of the total ANI comes from the ANI related to the HL (Fig. 3). Consequently, the LANS leads to a better decoding acceleration with respect to the ANS.
