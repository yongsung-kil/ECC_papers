Early Stopping Criterion for LDPC

Janak Sodha
Department of Computer Science, Mathematics and Physics University of the W est Indies Bridgetown, Barbados

AbstractŌĆöA log-likelihood ratio (LLR) test on the single M th

check node of an LDPC decoder is utilised to develop an

improved low complexity stopping criterion. The average

number of iterations required at low SNRs is significantly

reduced without sacrificing the BER performance. At high

SNRs, unlike typical stopping criteria which suffer a penalty in

the BER performance because the decoder inadvertently stops

the decoder too early, the proposed algorithm adaptively

switches off at high SNRs. Simulation results are presented for

the rate

1 2

(288, 576) WiMAX 802.16e LDPC code digits using

binary phase shift keying (BPSK) over an AWGN channel.

Keywords-LDPC codes; stopping criterion; decoding failure; iterative decoding

I. INTRODUCTION
For a Low density parity check (LDPC) [1] decoder, the predefined limit on the maximum numbers of iterations the
decoder is allowed to iterate, Lmax is much larger than the
average number of iterations L required to establish a valid
code word, particularly at high SNRs. In this paper, a low complexity stopping criterion is presented that out performs other known stopping criteria [2], [3], [4], [5], [6] without any penalty in the BER at high SNRs.

V I. CONCLUSIONS
Using the short-length W iMAX 802.16e rate ┬Į M = 288, N = 576 LDPC code, it was shown that the proposed early stopping criterion does not adversely effect the BER at high
SNRs with an average number of iterations L below 10 at
the low SNR of 0 dB. This is because the algorithm disables
the stopping criterion at high SNRs, where L is inherently
very low. The stopping criterion proposed reduces the time taken to decode, with a much lower complexity than existing algorithms, and hence the decoding speed is unaffected, with
only the specification of Lmin , T and Tstop required for a
given LDPC code. Future research will investigate the performance of this algorithm for other LDPC codes.
