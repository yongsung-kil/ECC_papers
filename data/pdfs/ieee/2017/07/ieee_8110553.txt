Abstract— Puncturing is a good way to achieve RateCompatible (RC) family codes with high rate. In this paper, a simple yet efficient puncturing strategy for Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes with diagonal structure is proposed and analyzed. In order to minimize the recovery step of punctured nodes, all the parity nodes (PNs) are assumed being punctured at first and then recovered step by step. The algorithm is named as Progressive Node Recovery (PNR) and acquires puncturing order reversely from recovery order. The diagonal structure of mother code provides great convenience during the procedure. Extensive experimental results show that the PNR algorithm can reduce the performance loss caused by puncturing and thus guarantees the good error correction performance of RC family codes with high code rates.
Keywords— Low Density Parity Check Codes; Diagonal Structure; Puncturing; Progressive Node Recovery.
I. INTRODUCTION
With the spring up of modern wireless communication, the demand for high throughput and good communication quality are increasing while the spectrum resources are decreasing at the same time. In traditional communication systems, fixed-rate scheme is adopted universally, whose parameters and modes are set according to the worst situation since the reliability of communication is not dispensable. However, this method leads to the waste of spectrum and channel resources. This motives the research of RateCompatible (RC) scheme, whose code rate varies with channel station adaptively, increasing the throughput without additional channel resources.
Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) code [1] is deemed as a good kind of error correction code. It is ubiquitous in many areas of knowledge as deep space communication, hardware storage, to name a few. Thus RC-LDPC codes draw the attention of researchers and become a hot issue. Generally, RC scheme for LDPC codes is realized by both puncturing [2-8] and extending [6-7]. The former aims at higher rate codes while the latter focuses on lower rate codes. The merging of puncturing and extending has proved to be fruitful. Either of them is unable to provide a set of RC family codes with large rate range and good performance. Full attention will be paid to puncturing strategy in this paper.
The research of puncturing has been studied extensively, starting with RC convolutional codes [10]. Then progress is

made according to special code structure when it comes to LDPC codes. In 2004, J.Ha etc. analyzed the degree optimization of puncturing based on density revolutionary (DE) theory, and provided a puncturing method for codes with infinite length [2]. Further improvements were made for codes with medium and short length. Different code rates are obtained through grouping and sorting during puncturing procedure. In addition, conceptions like one step recoverable (1-SR) node, k step recoverable (k-SR) node, survived check node (SCN), dead check node (DCN) and recovery tree are proposed. It not only reduces the complexity for the selection of punctured nodes, but also provides theoretical foundation for later researches. Since it is not designed for a specific kind of mother code, its universality is at the cost of complexity to a certain extend. Hereafter, puncturing strategies and analysis are usually presented based on LDPC codes with special code structure, such as efficiently encodable LDPC (E2-LDPC) codes[3], block type LDPC codes[4], repeat accumulate (RA) codes[5], irregular repeat accumulate (IRA) codes[6-7] , protograph codes[8-9] and so on.
In this paper, a puncturing strategy tailored for LDPC codes with diagonal structure, such as RA codes, IRA codes, IRA-like codes[11] and QC-IRA-d codes[12], is proposed. It is named as Progressive Node Recovery (PNR) algorithm. With the principle of minimizing recovery step and structural benefits of mother code, the PNR algorithm achieves good performance and practicability due to its efficiency and simplicity.
This paper proceeds as follows. The concepts of recoverable nodes and recovery tree are presented in Section II. In Section III, the PNR algorithm is proposed with detailed analysis. Simulation results are provided in Section IV and Section V concludes the paper at last.
II. RECOVERABLE NODES AND RECOVERY TREE
The concepts of recoverable node and recovery tree, which lay the foundation of puncturing strategy, are proposed by J.Ha [2]. At the receiver, the initialization of likelihood ratio (LLR) of a punctured node is set to 0, since its probability of “1” and “0” is equal. Then the message of the punctured node will be updated during iteration decoding. Once a punctured node receives none-zero LLR messages from its neighboring check nodes, it can be regarded as a recovered node.

III. PUNCTURING STRATEGY WITH MINIMUM RECOVERY STEP
While doing the research of puncturing strategies for RCLDPC codes, several significant discoveries have been made by J.Ha [2]. First, the more neighboring check nodes a punctured node has, the more likely it will be recovered. Second, check nodes with less punctured nodes are easier to recover. Third, the more steps a punctured node takes when it is recovered, the less reliable it will be in iteration decoding. According to these points, requisitions below have to be satisfied to the best when puncturing strategy is designed. Firstly, the punctured nodes have enough neighboring check nodes. Secondly, the recovery step t for a punctured node is

small, and punctured nodes with low recovery step exist as many as possible. Considering these requisitions, the goal for designing a well-performed puncturing strategy for LDPC codes with diagonal structure is set. The variable nodes of LDPC codes with diagonal structure can be divided into two catalogs: information node (IN) and parity node (PN).And generally the variable nodes selected to puncture are all PNs. For the diagonal structure, all the PNs are degree-2 nodes (except for one degree-1 node which can be neglected). Namely, they all have two neighboring check nodes. Thus every PN is equal when selected to puncture and thus the first requisition can be ignored. Then all endeavors are made to reduce recovery step, which is the basic principle for the design of puncturing strategy.
The diagonal structure provides significant convenience for the analysis of puncturing procedure. If there is at least one PN un-punctured, all punctured PNs can be recovered through finite iteration decoding. Suppose there is a LDPC code with diagonal structure whose Tanner Graph (TG) is pictured in Fig.3, and it has k INs and m PNs. The black nodes represent those punctured PNs while the only white one represents the remained un-punctured one. Two 1-SR nodes next to the remained one are recovered after the first iteration, and two 2SR nodes are recovered after the second iteration, and so on , until all the punctured nodes are recovered. The decoding iteration is smaller than m for certain since the distance between any PNs is smaller than m. Thus the highest rate of RC family codes is k/(k+1). For the sake of simplicity, the relationship between INs and CNs in TG can be omitted, since it has nothing to do with the analysis. The notation in Fig.3 will be adopted in the following.

As aforementioned, endeavors should be made to minimize the recovery step for punctured nodes. Aiming at that, a novel puncturing strategy called PNR is proposed. All PNs are assumed being punctured at first and then recovered one by one. Then the puncturing order is exactly the reverse of recovery order. According to the principle of minimizing recovery step k, the punctured node with highest recovery step is chosen to be recovered in every procedure. A simple example, which is presented in Fig.4 and Tab.1, will suffice to explain the principle above. Define punctured PN group as all punctured PNs between two neighbored recovered PNs, and the structures with odd number and even number are given separately in Fig.4 (a) and (b). Then one PN will be selected to recover next. Different choice leads to different recovery step for remaining PNs, and the results are summarized in Tab.1, which proves the selection of PN with highest recovery step is efficient and rational.

IV. SIMULATIONS AND RESULTS
An IRA-like code with code length N=2048 and rate R=0.5 is adopted as mother code in simulation. The code rates of RC family codes vary in set {8 /16,8 /15,!,8 / 9} . All the
simulations proceed under same situations: Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel and Binary Phrase Shift Keying (BPSK) modulation. As for decoding, the traditional Brief Propagation (BP) decoding algorithm is employed and the max iteration time is set 100. The Bit Error Rate (BER) and Frame Error Rate (FER) performance of RC-LDPC codes acquired by PNR algorithm are presented in Fig.6.
For comparison, we also provides simulation results of random puncturing in Fig.7. The superiority of PNR algorithm is more embossed in comparison with random puncturing. When code rates are close to that of mother code, the performance gap between them can be neglected, since most punctured PNs are of low recovery step. However, as the code rates raise and more punctured PNs with high recovery step are produced, the gap is enlarged. Besides, there exists no trends of error floor of PNR algorithm when BER<10-6, which appears in random puncturing at BER=10-4, .
Moreover, more detailed comparison with puncturing algorithm in [7] and random puncturing at FER=10-2 and BER=10-4 are presented in Tab.2. At FER=10-2, the PNR algorithm performs as well as puncturing algorithm in [7] when code rate is raised to 8/9. Through the simulated codes have same parameters, they starts from mother code with different performance, which indicates that the performance loss of PNR algorithm is smaller. As for random puncturing, the performance loss of RC codes with rate 8/9 at BER=10-4 is 6.25 dB, whilst that of PNR algorithm is 5.32 dB, which proves the effectiveness of PNR algorithm.
V. CONCLUSIONS
The impressive efficiency brought by RC codes generates a wave of enthusiasm for puncturing strategy. In this paper, a new puncturing scheme named as PNR algorithm is proposed. Based on the special structure of mother code, the algorithm acquires the puncturing order from recovery order reversely. That is to say, all PNs are punctured at first, and then recovered progressively under the principle of minimizing the recovery step, which leading to the increase of error correction performance. There are three main advantages of PNR algorithm: 1) universality for all LDPC codes with diagonal structure; 2) simplicity and efficiency in realization; 3) small performance loss during puncturing. All these properties guarantee the practicability of PNR algorithm in wireless communication systems.
