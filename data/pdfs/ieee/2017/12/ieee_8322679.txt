A Data Recovering Algorithm for LDPC Codes in Highly Mobile Satellite Communications

Wang Yaowen
PLA Army Engineering University PLA AEU
Nanjing, China e-mail: dimaria07@qq.com

Guo Daoxing
PLA Army Engineering University PLAAEU
Nanjing, China e-mail: xyzgfg@163.com

Abstract—In highly mobile satellite communication systems, the rapid motion of terminals, such as airplanes and missiles, will lead to the interruption of the communication and data loss, which includes burst data loss and packet loss. For the burst data loss, we will improve the decoding algorithm of the LDPC codes, which set the initial channel information corresponding to the lost data 0. This method can exhibit good data recovering performance within a certain range. While for the packet loss, we propose the group coding method to turn the packet recovering into the erasure correcting in the BEC, and we also compare the performance and complexity between iterative decoding and maximum likelihood decoding. The simulation results suggest that, the proposed algorithm can recover the lost data with relatively low complexity, which will be well suited in satellite communication systems under high mobility environments.
Keywords-high mobility, satellite communication, low density parity check code, message passing, maximum likelihood
I. INTRODUCTION
In recent years, reliable communications in high mobility environments have drawn considerable attentions. The current communications are mostly designed for the low and medium mobility environments, and will be limited in high mobility environments. The current researches are mainly focused on fast time-varying fading channel estimation, Doppler diversity, carrier synchronization, handover etc [1]. And these are mainly based on land mobile communications, including high-speed train communications and highway communications. While, it may differ in satellite communications in high mobility environments, especially for terminals like airplanes and missiles. In satellite communications, the terminals will travel faster than trains and cars, which may cause relative high frequency offset and offset variation, besides, the terminals may change the direction and speed more frequently. In 2010, Paolo Chini et al. presented some basic challenges in mobile satellite systems(MSS), such as antenna size, shadowing fading, synchronization and demodulation under low signal-to-noise ratio conditions[2]. However, detection and demodulation of signal and precise carrier synchronization are very complex, and may be unrealizable in some circumstances. Thus, there will exist data loss in high mobility satellite communications, which is usually inevitable. It is significant to find a practical way to recover the lost data due to the high mobility, which

can ensure the reliable communication in high mobility environments. Due to the long time delay of satellite channel, it is not practical to adopt the automatic repeat request (ARQ) [3] method. LDPC codes [4] have extraordinary performance over a wide range of communication channels, especially in additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel and binary erasure channel (BEC). The good error and erasure correcting performance of LDPC codes can be well-suited in satellite communications in high mobility environments. And the data loss can be roughly divided into two types, the burst data loss within a frame, and the whole packet loss.
If received sequence is with burst data loss, which means that there are both errors and erasures in received sequence. Before recovering the lost data, we firstly need to correct the errors which is caused by the AWGN. In order to separate the erasures from the errors, Khaled et al. propose a method, which separate parity-check matrices by deleting the erasured symbols and decoding the resulting vector with respect to a punctured code [5]. However, the proposed algorithm may not work well if there are many erasured symbols, at the same time the puncturing method will lower the weight of the codeword.
While for the packet loss, there are kinds of erasure correcting codes, such as Tornado code[6], fountain codes, in addition to LDPC codes. And the most well-known code for packet recovering is fountain codes, including LT code [7] and Raptor code [8], which have extraordinary packet correcting performances. The biggest advantage of fountain codes is the rateless characteristic, can have been well applied in network transmission. However, the parity-check matrices of the fountain codes are generated randomly with respect the degree distribution, and its performance may have worse performance than LDPC codes, whose paritycheck matrices have already optimized by density evolution method. Besides, the performance of fountain codes will significantly degrade if the channel erasure probability is pretty high. Finally, in satellite channels there exists noise, though fountain codes can also be applied in AWGN channel, its performance is poor compared with LDPC codes. So we need to take full advantage of LDPC codes to correct both errors and erasures, by improving the encoding and decoding algorithm. Hence, the packet recovering can be carried out by two steps, firstly we need try to decode the received sequence, if successfully, then the results will be accepted by the receiver, otherwise the whole packet is discarded as

will cause large Doppler frequency offset and offset variations in the received signal, besides the instability of the frequency oscillator, so the synchronization will be more difficult. When the channel condition changes abruptly, the synchronization may not maintain during the demodulation process, which will cause data loss. We can assume that the timing and frame synchronization is accurate. So if the data loss occurs, the receiver can identify the location of the burst data, including the starting point and the lost, depicted in fig. 1. If the length of the lost data exceeds the ability of the codeword, then it can be assumed that the whole frame are lost.

B. Decoding algorithm in erasure channel
If packet loss occurs, then the receiver needs to recombine the sub-blocks. Now, we have already transformed the packet issues into data recovering over binary erasure channel. Now, the most common decoding algorithms for LDPC codes over the binary erasure channel are iterative decoding (IT) and maximum likelihood (ML) decoding

algorithm. Next, we will briefly introduce the IT and ML algorithm.
i. Iterative Decoding Iterative decoding, which is also called message passing
algorithm [6], corrects the erasures through the iterative message passing between the check nodes and the variable nodes. And the algorithm can be divided into three parts:
1) initialization, all variable bits are set 0, 1 or ‘E’ according to the value they received from the channel, while the check nodes are all set 0;
2) check nodes updating, for every check node,

denoted by ci , if any variable nodes of ci ’s
neighbor nodes is an erasure, then sum up the residual variable nodes module 2, and the calculated value is used to update the check nodes. While if

there are at least two erasures in ci ’s neighbor

nodes, and then the check node ci will not be
updated. 3) variable nodes updating, every erasured bit updates
its value according to the message passing from the check nodes it connects. So, it can be seen that, the biggest advantage of the IT is its low complexity. Namely, it just need xor operation and can run in linear time proportional to the code length. However, it cannot avoid the stopping sets[11]. The existence of stopping set will significantly degrade the decoding performance, while it can be tackled by the ML decoding.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we propose a novel data recovering algorithm for LDPC codes in highly mobile satellite communications. We firstly modify the decoding algorithm of LDPC codes over the AWGN channel to recover the burst lost data within a frame. Then we apply precoding and channel codding method to recover packets, which can effectively recover the lost packets. At last, we compare the performance of IT and ML decoding and analyze the maximum recoverable packet. The proposed algorithm can be well applied in satellite communication in high mobility environments for its low complexity and good performance.
