Design of Protograph-Based LDPC Codes with Limited Decoding Complexity

Chengjun Tang , Ming Jiang, Chunming Zhao, and Hong Shen

Abstract— In this letter, we present a design method to find good protograph-based low-density parity-check codes with the numbers of nonzero elements in the parity-check matrix and decoding iterations both strictly limited. We utilize the protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) analysis to evaluate the error performance and employ the differential evolution algorithm to devise a well-performed coding scheme. In particular, the number of iterations in the PEXIT analysis is set to a small value due to the limited iterations in the decoding process. Both numerical analyses and simulations verify that the resulting codes exhibit better performance than conventional code designs under the limitation of decoding complexity.
Index Terms— EXIT, protograph-based LDPC codes, limited iterations, decoding complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
T HE design of high-performance low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes has been studied extensively in the literature [1]–[3]. As mentioned in [1], the maximum check degrees of LDPC codes should tend to infinity to achieve the capacity of the binary symmetric channel; this implies that the number of nonzero elements in the parity-check matrix should be set to a large value to achieve good performance. From the recently proposed LDPC codes of [3]–[6], we can discover that the numbers of nonzero elements in the paritycheck matrices keep growing, which results in high decoding complexity. Meanwhile, a large number of iterations may be required in the iterative decoding to achieve the theoretical capacity of a conventionally optimized LDPC code; this can lead to high latency and power consumption.
As for the aspect of code design with fast convergence, Richardson et al. [1] proposed a numerical design method to find the irregular LDPC codes that require the least decoding iterations on symmetric channels. In [7] and [8], approximate formulas for the number of required iterations for successful decoding were presented for irregular LDPC codes on binary erasure channels and additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels. Moreover, Smith et al. [8] intended to make a tradeoff between the error performance and decoding complexity, and defined a measure of complexity as T K −1 E, where the variables T, E, and K denote the number of required iterations, the number of nonzero elements in the parity-check matrix, and the length of information block, respectively.

Besides, Mulholland et al. have recently proposed some specially designed irregular LDPC codes and protographbased LDPC (P-LDPC) codes with fast convergence properties in [9] and [10].
Most related works focus on the design of irregular LDPC codes and there is little knowledge about the design of P-LDPC codes that exhibit good performance with limited decoding complexity. The P-LDPC codes, which have been widely used in industry standards, can have various protographs with identical degree distributions. Thus, the analysis of P-LDPC codes is usually carried out by the protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) method [11] or the density evolution with reciprocal channel approximation [12]. These two methods both take into account the protograph structure instead of merely the degree distributions. Therefore, the related studies that optimize the degree distributions of irregular LDPC codes for fast convergence are not applicable to the P-LDPC codes.
Although [10] optimized P-LDPC codes for fast convergence on binary erasure channels, some practical limitations were ignored like the maximum node degrees for implementation convenience and the maximum number of nonzero entries in the parity-check matrix for decoding complexity reduction. The number of nonzero entries was considered in [8] when discussing decoding complexity. Moreover, this factor was regarded as an important issue during the discussions on decoding complexity in 3GPP meetings for 5G standards. In fact, the decoding complexity was shown to increase approximately linearly with the number of nonzero entries [5].
In this letter, we propose a design approach of P-LDPC codes over AWGN channels with the numbers of nonzero entries in the parity-check matrix and decoding iterations both strictly limited. We utilize the differential evolution (DE) algorithm [13] to find good protographs. In the search process of the DE algorithm, the objective function returns the threshold value calculated by the PEXIT analysis. In particular, we set the number of iterations in the PEXIT analysis to a small value owing to the limited iterations in the decoding process. Besides, to handle the constraint on the number of nonzero entries, we apply the direct constraint handling strategy [13]. Lastly, analytical and simulated results show the superiority of the newly designed code and validate our design method.
The rest of this letter is organized as follows. Section II describes the proposed code design strategy, where we also give an example and present the numerical results. Simulation results are provided in Section III and the conclusions are drawn in Section IV.
II. PROTOGRAPH DESIGN AND EXEMPLIFICATION
The parity-check matrices of P-LDPC codes in this study are constructed according to the method in [2]. At first, the protograph is copied and permuted [14] to generate an intermediate matrix which has no entries greater than one.

Then, the entries 1 and 0 in the intermediate matrix are replaced, respectively, by a cyclic permutation matrix and a zero matrix to generate the parity-check matrix. The methods in [15] and [16] can be adopted to optimize the permutation and cyclically shifting parameters. From the construction process, we note that the protograph structure is like a scaleddown version of the parity-check matrix and thus has a crucial effect on the error performance. In this research, our work focuses on the protograph design. For convenience of description, we represent a protograph by a protomatrix whose entries can be greater than one and indicate the number of edges connecting the respective variable and check nodes.
We carry out numerical analysis of error performance by means of the PEXIT method [11]. This method takes into account the protograph structures of P-LDPC codes, analyzes the decoding behavior, and thus provides an accurate performance prediction. III. SIMULATION RESULTS
We carry out Monte Carlo simulations to evaluate the bit-error rate (BER) of various rate-1/2 codes with information block length set to 4096. We employ binary phase shift keying (BPSK) modulation and belief-propagation (BP) decoding algorithm [19]. The maximum number of BP decoding iterations tBP,max is set to 20 or 100 unless otherwise specified.
The BER curves of various rate-1/2 codes are depicted in Fig. 2. The solid curves and dashed curves correspond to the results with tBP,max set to 20 and 100, respectively. We need to note that, to ensure a fair comparison, we consider only the codes that satisfy the constraint on the number of nonzero elements. Thus, some other codes proposed in [3], [5], [6], and [8]–[10] are not considered here.
From Fig. 2, we can see that our proposed code N1/2 outperforms the other codes by more than 0.20 dB at the BER of 10−5 with tBP,max set to 20. Furthermore, in Fig. 2, we present the BER curve of the AR4A code with tBP,max set to 26, and find that the BER performance of the AR4A code with tBP,max set to 26 is slightly worse than that of

IV. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we have presented a design method to find good P-LDPC codes under the limitations on the numbers of nonzero elements and decoding iterations. Different from conventional design methods, we specially set the number of iterations in the PEXIT analysis to a small value in the proposed strategy in order to design codes with fast convergence properties. The performance advantage of our proposed codes has been verified by both numerical analyses and simulation results.
