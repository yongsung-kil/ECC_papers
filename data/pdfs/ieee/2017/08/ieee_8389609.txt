Abstract-For the last 20 years intense research has been carried
out on Low Density Parity Check(LDPC) decoders for supporting various transmission technologies. There are various proven LDPC decoder algorithms such as Log domain sum product algorithm(LDSPA), Min sum decoder(MSD), Single scan min sum decoder(SSMSD) and normalized single scan min sum decoder(NSSMSD) which are currently in use. Each of these algorithm have its own distinct pros and cons from implementation point of view that comes to support various transmission standards. In this paper four LDPC decoding algorithms are implemented to test and support CDTMB standard for its three code rates viz. 0.4, 0.6 and 0.8. The performance evaluation of all four decoder algorithms mentioned above are carried out with Additive White Gaussian Noise(AWGN) channel with BPSK modulation scheme. The software implementation of these algorithms show that min sum decoder algorithm reduces complexity when compared to LDSPA. Also SSMSD and NSSMSD contributes to conservation of memory to 23% and take at least 35% less computation time compared to LDSPA and MSD. LDSPA is preferred for low data rate applications for its good BER performance, but it has the drawback of increase in hardware complexity due to the presence of tanh and log functions. SSMSD and NSSMSD is preferable for high data rate applications as this showed low memory requirement and faster performance.
Keywords: BPSK, CDTMB, parity check matrix, Log Likelihood Ratio ( LLR), Low Density Parity Check(LDPC)
I. INTRODUCTION
The need of error free digital data transmission is of prime
importance . During 1962, Gallager [1] introduced LDPC
codes and various iterative decoding algorithms for a near
error free digital data transmission. But for almost three
decades from then LDPC codes were not renowned and later
by the research of MacKay and Neal [2] LDPC codes were
again back in limelight. Presently these codes are intensely
studied and practiced in various data transmission standards
like DVB-S2, WiMax, WiFi, CDTMB and also for storage
technologies, since they have very good error correcting
capacities very close to the Shannon limit[3].

Yingjian Xue et al has discussed the behavior of finite length LDPC codes used over AWGN channel and found that irregular LDPC codes having variable node and check nodes degree distribution optimized for infinite-length codes are not efficient for finite-length LDPC codes[4]. Yan Ying et al has published a LDPC decoding algorithm with a turbo decoding principle that is based on min sum decoder, and it has shown good performance with reduced complexity[5]. Jun Tang, Tejas Bhattt, et al. discussed a solution of power conservative modified min sum algorithm for ultra wide band(UWB) communication systems[6]. Xiaofei Huang reported about merging the check node and variable node update cycle into a single cycle to benefit memory consumption, known as single scan min sum algorithm[7]. Zhiqiang Cui reported on layered decoding of LDPC codes which increases throughput and reduces computation latency[8]. Researcher Nathan Axvig et al proposed a method of outputting average values of min sum vectors over finite set of iterations resulting in reduced bit rate properties[9]. A paper by Botao Zhang on DVB-S2 has been reported on a row message passing min sum decoder which has no performance loss but has reduced complexity benefit[10].
It is seen from literature review that, researchers have researched on independent decoding algorithms to analyze their performance for different broadcasting standards. So we propose to implement and analyze the suitability study and comparison of various metrics that considers memory conservation and complexity as a performance parameter of four LDPC decoder algorithms viz. (LDSPA), MSD, SSMSD, NSSMSD implemented for CDTMB standard in AWGN channel model with BPSK modulation.

II. THEORETICAL BACKGROUND FOR LDPC DECODER
LDPC codes are one of the classes of linear block codes in which a set of linear parity check constraints are contended by a parity check matrix of m rows and n columns, where m indicates each of the m constraints and n indicates the length of a codeword. The parity check matrix of LDPC codes consist of large number of zeros and less number of one's as shown in sample Fig 1.a. Bipartite graph is useful for easy understanding of decoding process. There are two types of nodes of bipartite graph namely variable nodes and check nodes as shown in Fig 1.b. When every element hij of H matrix is other than zero then check node i is connected to variable node j.

V. CONCLUSION In this paper detailed comparative analysis of four

LDPC decoder algorithms viz. LDSPA, MSD, SSMSD,

NSSMSD for CDTMB standard is presented. It is verified

that for low data rate applications LDSPA is suitable as it

has good performance with respect to BER but at the cost of

increased hardware complexity. Whereas for applications

with less complexity and low memory requirement SSMSD

and NSSMSD would be the preferred choice.
