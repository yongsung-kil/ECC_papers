A Merged BP Decoding Algorithm for Polar-LDPC Concatenated Codes

Jiaai Liu1,2, Shusen Jing1,2, Xiaohu You2, and Chuan Zhang1,2,∗ 1Lab of Efficient Architectures for Digital-communication and Signal-processing (LEADS) 2National Mobile Communications Research Laboratory, Southeast University, Nanjing, China
Email: {jaliu, shs.jing, xhyu, chzhang}@seu.edu.cn

Abstract—Recently, both polar codes and low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes have been adopted by 3GPP eMBB scenario. Since both codes exist in one system, it is natural to consider the concatenation scheme of them. In this paper, a merged belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm for the concatenated codes of polar and LDPC codes is proposed. By jointing factor graphs, this merged algorithm is designed by serially executing the LDPC and polar decoding. According to numerical results, the bit error rate (BER) of this proposed algorithm can achieve a notable improvement for different code rates compared with the conventional separated approach. For (1024, 512) polar code, the performance gain of the proposed scheme is at least 0.5 dB even at a low BER of 1 × 10−2. Finally, further refinements of computational complexity and hardware implementation are also provided in this paper.
Keywords—Polar codes, LDPC codes, concatenated codes, BP decoding, merged decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
Polar codes [1], proposed by Arıkan in 2009, are the first channel codes that can provably achieve the channel capacity for symmetric binary-input discrete memoryless channels (BDMCs). Two well-known decoding methodologies are successive cancellation (SC) and belief propagation (BP) algorithms. The SC decoding has a complexity as low as O(N log2 N ), but suffers from the high decoding latency. Apart from the SC decoding, the BP decoding is an iterative and highly parallel algorithm. Its property of inherent parallelism makes it suitable for low latency and high throughput applications, and attracts extensive research attentions [2–5].
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [6], proposed by Gallager in 1962, outperform other codes for the capacity approaching capability and high decoding throughput. Though ignored for decades, the LDPC codes are being universally studied recently from various angles [7]. The BP decoding algorithm of LDPC codes has led to plenty of research for its great error correcting performance.
In 1966, Forney first proposed the conception of concatenated codes. Since then, a large amount of research on concatenated designs of different kinds of codes such as LDPC and Reed-Solomon (RS) codes in [8] have been carried out. Many conclusions have shown that better performance of concatenated schemes is possible, encouraging more and more researchers to take deeper discussion. The concatenation of polar and LDPC codes has been studied for different purposes [9, 10]. However, former researchers failed to utilize the message-passing similarity of inner and outer decoders. In this

paper, a novel merged decoding scheme is proposed based on the similarity of polar and LDPC BP decoders.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. In Section II, a mathematical model of polar-LDPC concatenated code system and a general introduction of BP decoding algorithm are concisely introduced. In Section III, the proposed decoding algorithm for concatenated codes is demonstrated through graphs and equations elaborately. The simulation and complexity analysis are also shown in the same section. Section IV presents hardware architecture of the merged decoding method. Finally, Section V concludes the entire paper.

B. BP Decoding for LDPC Codes
The BP decoding of LDPC codes is based on factor graphs as shown in Fig. 2. Each pair of nodes linked by these edges propagates messages mutually, and computes the probability values of each bit iteratively.

B. Joint Factor Graph and Algorithm
As mentioned above, polar codes and LDPC codes are both based on factor graphs. Therefore, the decoding algorithm can be conducted on a joint factor graph that connects two graphs in view of their similarity. On account of this idea, this merged decoding algorithm is proposed.
In the conventional decoding scheme for polar-LDPC concatenated codes, the message updating within two graphs are independent with each other. Thus the channel observation messages are used only in decoding of inner LDPC codes. Similarly, the left-to-right messages of the first stage are used only in polar decoder. However, in this merged decoding scheme, as shown in Fig. 6, input messages of LDPC factor graph is the output of polar factor graph, and vice versa. In this way, the efficiency of information utilization is increased. For a simpler representation, another form of polar factor graph which is different from Fig. 3 is applied here. Fig. 6 shows the connection relationship of nodes. At the joint of this graph, all the acquired messages are collected as they can revise each other and approach to a more precise value. On the basis of these results, this merged decoding scheme has an apparent improvement on BER performance over the conventional one, and it is efficient in both low and high code rate situation. In addition, the BER performance will not change much when the value of parameter a is below 0.6. In application, factor a can be set at 0.5 because multiplication of 0.5 can be implemented by shifting one bit.
D. Complexity Analysis
In Fig. 6, the joint graph is proposed just by piecing two factor graphs together. Neither a variable node nor a check node is eliminated or added in this graph. Moreover, only the sequences of messages passing are changed and a parameter is set to adjust the proportion of different messages in joining them.

The detailed analysis of computational complexity is listed in Table I. There is a comparison of the numbers of comparison (CMP), addition (ADD), and multiplication (MUL) per iteration between the two independent BP decoding algorithms and the merged decoding algorithm. In the fourth column of the table, the second and the third columns are added together to get the total numbers of CMPs, ADDs, and MULs per iteration of the separated decoding scheme, and the last two columns are considered comparable.
In the table, Ki and Kj denote the numbers of “1”s of the ith row and jth column respectively in the check matrix H which has m rows and M columns. The outer polar code length is N (N = 2n).
As is shown in the table, the numbers of CMPs are the same between the two different decoding schemes. However, the proposed merged decoding scheme has a decrease on the number of ADDs while adding N multiplications to the original decoding scheme. Despite all that, the MULs can’t be eliminated because they are the keys (the multiplication of factor a) of the performance improvement. In general, an improvement in simulation performance can be reached without much change in computational complexity.

A. Hardware Architecture
The general hardware architecture of the proposed merged BP decoding scheme is shown in Fig. 9. This graph is the example of a decoder of simple 6-bit concatenated codes.
Inputs of this decoder are vector y, R1,js, and factor a. The multiplication of channel observation vector y with −4/N0 stands for the function f (x) of initialization operation. B. Schedule for Merged Decoding Scheme
To improve the time efficiency of the merged decoding scheme, a parallel schedule of the algorithm is proposed here. As is shown in Fig. 10, the N +1th iteration of LDPC decoding starts executing after the Nth iteration of polar decoding is finished in the previous schedule, which causes the time efficiency less than 50%. To address this problem, we design a parallel schedule to execute the N + 1th iteration of LDPC decoding and the Nth iteration of polar decoding at the same time. Though there is still some time blank due to different iteration durations, the efficiency is highly improved.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a merged BP decoding scheme is proposed for polar-LDPC concatenated codes. This new scheme is based on a joint graph and its corresponding algorithm is also given in detail. Numerical results have shown its performance advantage over the state-of-the-art designs. In addition, the computational complexity analysis proves that the complexity

overhead is low. In the end, a detailed hardware architecture and a schedule optimisation of this proposed decoding scheme are given. Further works will be directed towards the VLSI implementation of this approach and its cooperation with other baseband blocks in our 5G cloud platform.
