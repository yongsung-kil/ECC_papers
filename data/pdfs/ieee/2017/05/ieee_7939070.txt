AEP-LDPC ECC with Error Dispersion Coding for Burst Error Reduction of 2D and 3D NAND Flash Memories
Toshiki Nakamura, Yoshiaki Deguchi and Ken Takeuchi
Department of Electrical, Electronic, and Communication Engineering, Chuo University, Tokyo, Japan
E-mail : toshiki.nakamura@takeuchi-lab.org

Abstract— To improve the reliability of Triple-Level Cell (TLC) NAND flash memory, Advanced Error Prediction (AEP) low-density parity-check (LDPC) ECC with Error Dispersion Coding is proposed. In the conventional LDPC, error-correction capability is degraded due to burst errors [1]. To reduce burst errors and improve the error-correction capabilities of LDPC, this paper proposes Error Dispersion Coding (EDC) which reduces burst errors, decreases the worst bit-error rate (BER) of Upper/Middle/Lower pages, and finally extends the data-retention time of TLC NAND flash memory. By applying proposed EDC, in 3D-TLC NAND flash memory, the burst errors and the worst BER are decreased by 87% and 40%, respectively. As a result, the acceptable data-retention time is extended by 1.8 and 2.6 times in 2D and 3D-TLC NAND flash memory, respectively.
Index Terms—Low-density parity-check (LDPC), triple-level cell (TLC) NAND flash memory, reliability, solid-state drive (SSD)
I. INTRODUCTION
The capacity of NAND flash memory has been increased by increasing the number of bit per cells. Currently, TLC NAND flash memory (3-bit/cell) has the high capacity and the low cost. On the other hand, the reliability of TLC NAND flash is degraded due to the narrower VTH margins between states. To improve the reliability of TLC NAND flash, LDPC ECC which can efficiently correct errors close to the Shannon limit is applied [2]. LDPC ECC has higher error-correction capability than traditional BCH ECC [3]. However, error-correction capability of LDPC ECC is degraded due to burst errors [1]. Moreover, error-correction fails in the worst page having the worst BER among Upper/Middle/Lower pages at the short dataretention time. Even if the error-correction of the other pages are successful, the block is judged as bad if the worst page with the worst BER fails.
The data assignments and measured BER of Upper/Middle/Lower pages of the conventional TLC NAND flash are shown in Fig. 1 [4]. Since the higher VTH-states have more data-retention errors, BER of Upper page which has the read reference voltage (VRef) between “F” and “G”-states becomes higher. Thus, in TLC NAND flash, Upper page has the worst BER. The number of ECC decoding failed pages increases with the data-retention time in Fig. 2. Since Upper page has higher BER, the acceptable data-retention time of Upper page is shorter than those of Middle and Lower pages. Even if only the worst Upper page contain errors over the error-correction capability of ECC, the whole block of Upper/Middle/Lower pages is judged as bad. Fig. 3 shows the measured LDPC errorcorrection results as a function of the LDPC iteration cycles. Although random errors are corrected by LDPC in a few iteration cycles, burst errors are left uncorrected even when the iteration cycle is 30. Fig. 4 shows the relation between acceptable BER of LDPC ECC and the average burst length at code rate = 9/10. The burst length is defined as the number of consecutive errors. In Fig. 4, the average burst length is changed based on the Gilbert-model [5]. As the burst length increases,

acceptable BER gradually decreases. Thus, reducing burst errors improves the error-correction capability of LDPC ECC.
To reduce the worst BER of Upper/Middle/Lower pages, a page interleaving technique which can average BERs among Upper/Middle/Lower pages has been introduced in TLC NAND flash memory [6]. Although this technique reduces the worst BER, burst errors are not reduced, which still restricts the errorcorrection capabilities of LDPC ECC. To reduce burst errors, this paper proposes Error Dispersion Coding (EDC) which changes the storing data order and reduces burst errors and the worst BER of Upper/Middle/Lower pages simultaneously. Proposed EDC can be realized with the simple logic circuit and thus can be implemented in the SSD controller without circuit area penalty or access time overhead.

II. OBSERVATION OF BURST ERRORS
Burst errors occur frequently with the conventional data order as shown in Fig. 5, when 1-state errors occur in consecutive data in a page. 1-state errors are, for example, dataretention errors from “G” to “F”-state. 2-state errors are dataretention errors from “G” to “E”-state. Fig. 6 shows the measured number of 1-7 state errors in 2D and 3D-TLC NAND flash with the conventional data order. The number of 1-state errors is 7.7×102 and 5.4×104-times larger than 2-state errors in 2D and 3D-TLC NAND flash, respectively. Because of the large number of 1-state errors, burst errors frequently occur in the conventional data order. To reduce burst errors, burst error masking (BEM) has been proposed [1]. BEM first records the burst error addresses in a table. In the next read cycle, data in burst errors are corrected by referring to the table of burst errors. Although BEM can effectively decrease burst errors, the table is required. On the other hand, in proposed EDC, burst errors are decreased just by changing storing data order without additional table.
III. PROPOSED ERROR DISPERSION CODING (EDC)
The concept of proposed Error Dispersion Coding (EDC) is shown in Fig. 7. Proposed EDC has two advantages; the burst error reduction and the worst BER reduction of Upper/Middle/Lower pages in TLC NAND flash memories. With proposed EDC, the acceptable data-retention time can be extended.
Fig. 8(a) shows proposed EDC architecture with Advanced Error-Prediction LDPC (AEP-LDPC) w/o upper/lower cells [7]. Proposed EDC decreases the worst BER by averaging BERs of Upper/Middle/Lower pages in TLC NAND flash. During the data write, data from the host are first encoded by the LDPC encoder, and then data order is changed by proposed EDC.

During the data read, data from TLC NAND flash is first rearranged to reproduce the original data and then errors are corrected by the LDPC decoder. With proposed EDC, the data order is changed as Fig. 8(b). In the conventional data order, consecutive data are written only to the same page such as Lower page. On the other hand, in proposed EDC, consecutive data are written to all of Lower, Middle, and Upper pages in each cell. Proposed EDC changes conditions where burst errors occur, and averages BERs of Upper/Middle/Lower pages when data are read. Although in the conventional data order burst errors are caused by 1-state errors, in proposed EDC, burst errors also happen by 2 or more-state errors. As a result, burst errors infrequently occur with proposed EDC as shown in Fig. 8(c) because 2 or more-state errors rarely happen (Fig. 6).

In Case1 (burst length = 1), both conventional data order and proposed EDC have random errors caused by 1-state errors in “E”-state. As explained in Fig. 3, these random errors can be correcred by LDPC ECC with a few iteration cycles. In Case2 (burst length = 2), in the conventional data order, burst errors are caused by 1-state errors in “D” and “E”-states in Middle page. On the other hand, in proposed EDC, burst errors are caused by 2-state error or 1-state errors. If 2-state errors occur in “E”-state, they become burst errors when the data order is converted to the original data during the read. In addition, when 1-state errors occur in “E” and “C”-states, they also become burst errors. However, because these burst errors happen only when two 1-state errors occur in Upper page and Lower page in the neighboring two cells, “3 and 4” or “6 and 7” and so on shown in Fig. 8(b), its possibility is very low. IV. MEASUREMENT RESULTS
Fig. 10 shows the measured errors with the burst length = 1-5. Proposed EDC reduces burst errors by 87% with the burst length = 2. Moreover, burst errors with the burst length = 3 or more are completely eliminated in proposed EDC. As shown in Fig. 11, the worst BERs of 2D and 3D-TLC NAND flash are reduced by proposed EDC. At the 19-day date-retention, proposed EDC reduces the worst BERs of Upper/Middle/Lower pages by 23% and 40% in 2D and 3D-TLC NAND flash, respectively. Fig. 12 shows measured LDPC ECC decoding results of the conventional data order w/ burst error masking (BEM) (bit distance between errors, Noffset = {1,2}) [1] and proposed EDC. The green “Pass” regions, in which errors are successfully corrected, are increased by proposed EDC. Proposed EDC also reduces the number of LDPC decoding iterations and achieves the fast read as shown in Fig. 13. With 9/10 LDPC code rate and 300 W/E cycles, proposed EDC extends the acceptable dataretention time by 1.8 and 2.6 times in 2D and 3D-TLC NAND flash, respectively, compared with the conventional data order w/ BEM (Noffset = {1,2}) [1].
V. CONCLUSION This paper proposes EDC which reduces burst errors and averages BERs of Upper/Middle/Lower pages of TLC NAND flash memory.
