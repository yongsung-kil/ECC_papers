Abstractâ€”A random-like framework of the IRA-LDPC codes based on the identity column vectors (ICV) was defined and created. A new family of the IRA-LDPC codes under the constraint of the framework was investigated using algebraic and random methods. Its structural features is that the large-sized sparse parity check matrix Hd corresponding to the information bits is extruded into two smaller-sized easy-to -compute matrices, respectively called base matrix and position matrix. The minimum decomposable unit of the parity-check matrix H is the ICV which can provide a wider selection range of code rates than the most extensively studied QC-LDPC codes. Some conditions for the IRA-LDPC code not to have any 4-girth were considered.

Keywords-irregular repeat-accumulate (IRA) codes; lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes; girth; balanced incomplete block design (BIBD); permutation array; N-queen searching algorithm

The construction of position matrix AE needs to satisfy Cases 1, 2 and 3 in order to guarantee that there is no 4-girth in H [HdHp] . Some algebraic and random methods are used to create AE .
A. The Algebraic Method of AE
One and/or several permutations generated by a random method were used to construct Hd matrix in [7]. Here, we use a set of permutations generated by the algebraic method [12] to design AE matrix corresponding to our Hd matrix.
A permutation array (PA) of length n , cardinality P and minimum Hamming distance d , denoted as (n,P,d) -PA, is defined as a subset of symmetric group Sn with the property that the number of differences (the Hamming distance) between any two distinct permutations in the (n,P,d) -PA is at least d . Here, Sn is a set of all n! permutations of n elements on positive integer field Zn . In our previous work [12], an (n,n 1,n 1) -PA was investigated. The following theorem provides its construction.
