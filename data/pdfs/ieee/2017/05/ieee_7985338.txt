Abstract-In this paper, we present a family of irregular Low­
Density Parity-Check (LDPC) code ensemble, which is designed for 
Binary Symmetric Channel (BSC) with maximum achievable rate. 
Our proposed method is based on solving the optimization problem 
of maximum achievable rate problem based on the related code­
design conditions. In the proposed method, approximation is not 
used and optimization variables does not relaxed, which are used 
typically. Simulation results show some good codes close to the 
capacity. 
Keywords-component; irregular LDPC code, Binary Symmetric 
Channel, Rate optimization problem, Degree distribution of LDPC 
codes 
I. 
INTRODUCTION 
One of the most important and powerful error-correcting 
codes is Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes based on 
Gallager's work [1]. LDPC codes and its relevant iterative 
message-passing algorithm decoder were introduced in 1960 
and over the years was completely forgotten until MacKay 
regained it in 1996 [2]. After this rediscovery, searching about 
good LDPC codes with optimal rate and minimum error 
properties shows that irregular LDPC codes can be better than 
regular one [3]. 
Binary Symmetric Channel (BSC) is one simple discrete 
symmetric memory-less channel model, which can be used to 
model real world and practical channels for transmitting data. 
Less attention to BSC in compare with Binary Erasure Channel 
(BEC) is notable. 
Approaching and achieving to the capacity in BEC studied 
in many articles such as [4] but unfortunately it is not study for 
BSC, until now. Simplicity of BEC comes from Density 
Evolution (DE) constraint, which is introduced in [5] and 
extended in [3] and [6]. In compare of BSC case and BEC case, 
DE constraint is more complicated for BSC case. DE constraint 
is an inequality, which comes from probabilistic analyzing of 
iterative decoding of LDPC codes in finite number of iteration 
decoding process and it shows the behavior of message

CONCLUSION 
In this paper, we utilize the SDP approach to solve the rate 
optimization problem by using our reformulation. We also 
demonstrate a semi-definite problem with no simplification or
