An Algebraic Approach to Design Low Rate Low Density Parity Check Code
Zhe Zhang, Liang Zhou, Junyi Du and Shenglong Peng National Key Lab on Communication, University of Electronic Science and Technology of China, Chengdu, P. R. China
Email:zzhang@std.uestc.edu.cn, lzhou@uestc.edu.cn, jydu@std.uestc.edu.cn, slpeng@std.uestc.edu.cn

Abstract—In this paper, we propose an algebraic approach to design extremely low rate low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes by employing the primitive polynomials. We first investigate the requirements for the polynomials to construct 4-cycle free LDPC codes and then propose an algebraic approach to design the parity check matrix. To achieve a better decoding performance in the extremely low rate region, we propose an extension method to construct more check nodes and virtual variable nodes to obtain more constraints. Our designed LDPC codes have simple encoding and simple decoding features. Simulation results show that our proposed rate-0.03 LDPC code can achieve 2.2 dB gain, compared to the LDPC code designed by the progressive edge growth algorithm with the optimized edge degree distributions described in [7].
I. INTRODUCTION
Since the rediscovery [1] of LDPC code [2], it has attracted increasing interests for the reason of its approaching Shannon limit capability [5]. The existing work [3]–[7] focuses on designing LDPC codes for high code rate region applications. In contrast, the design of LDPC codes in extremely low code rate region is less well investigated. It is still a challenge to design an outstanding LDPC code to meet the urgent communication requirements in an extremely low SNR region.
The application of the dilution signal power, like deep space communication [8], and the communication scenario where a strong obstruction exists, like communication under electronic countermeasure [9], call for the use of extremely low rate codes.
According to the information theory, extremely low SNR channel has low channel capacity. This calls for the extremely low-rate codes. The authors in [10] pointed that, when the rate is extremely low, the extrinsic information increases slowly with the rise of the signal-to-noise (SNR). As a result, the variable nodes need more edges to obtain more extrinsic information. However, adding more edges to the variable nodes will surely increase the density of the parity check matrix and may introduce more short cycles. The extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart [11] and density evolution [12] are derived based on the assumption that the Tanner graph has no cycles, this may make them inaccurate in the optimization process of degree distributions. Consequently, the LDPC code designed by the conventional progressive edge growth (PEG) algorithm [7] may not perform well in the low code rate region.
To achieve a better performance, the authors in [13] designed a turbo-like code by using Hadamard arrays. The

authors in [10] replace the single parity check (SPC) constraints with the Hadamard constraints and construct the super check nodes. Since Hadamard constraints are stronger than SPC constraints, the variable nodes receive more extrinsic information from the supper check nodes. Therefore, their designed codes [10] have excellent performance in the low rate region. Since they replace the parity check constraint with a more complex constraint, this obviously increases the complexity of the decoding process of the check nodes.
In this paper, We propose a new algebraic approach to design the low rate LDPC codes by employing the primitive polynomials. We introduce the primitive polynomial constraints to construct a basic check matrix. Correspondingly, the codeword is a segment of the m-sequence generated by the primitive polynomial. To get more degrees of the variable nodes, we sample the m-sequence to obtain the sample sequence. Then, we investigate the constraints of the sample sequence to construct more check nodes in the check matrix. As a result, the variable nodes of the extended check matrix have higher degrees. Furthermore, the extended check matrix is 4-cycle free if the factors of the primitive polynomial satisfy some conditions that are derived in this paper. To make full use of the primitive polynomial constraints, we propose another extension method for the variable nodes. The extended variable nodes are virtual nodes with no channel information. They act as a bridge to transfer information between real variable nodes.
Our designed LDPC code has simple encoding and simple decoding features. As the codewords of our designed LDPC codes are the segment of an m-sequence, the encoding can be realized by a linear feedback shift register (LFSR) with the information bits as the initial state. In addition, the decoding algorithm is the conventional belief propagation (BP) algorithm.
The paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the code construction approach including the extension method to check matrix and virtual nodes. Section III shows the numerical results. Finally, some conclusions are drawn in Section IV.
