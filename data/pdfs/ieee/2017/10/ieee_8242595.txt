Evaluating the performance of Turbo Equalization with ADMM decoding

Weinan Xu, Xiaopeng Jiao, Jianjun Mu
School of Computer Science and Technology Xidian University XiвАЩan, China
xuweinan2016@163.com, jiaozi1216@126.com, jjmu@xidian.edu.cn

AbstractвАФThe decoding of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) over discrete memoryless channels is a promising method proposed recently. However, it is not clear how the ADMM decoding performs over the inter-symbol interference (ISI) channels. In this paper, we present the details of turbo equalization with ADMM decoding and evaluate the error-rate performances of the proposed scheme over classical magnetic recording channels. Moreover, we also compare the decoding performances of the proposed method to the interior point decoding method. Simulation results show that the proposed scheme performs better than both the turbo equalization with BP decoding and the interior point decoding in terms of error-rate performances.

I. INTRODUCTION
Decoding by optimization method can be traced back to 1990s [1]. It becomes an active research area since Feldman et al. proposed a linear programming (LP) decoding method for binary linear codes [2]. Later, the LP decoding is extended to non-binary linear block codes, such as non-binary low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [3]. A lot of research works that focus on the theoretical analysis of LP decoding, the error-rate performance improvement, as well as the decoding complexity reduction, have been published. For a comprehensive survey of LP decoding, please refer to [4]. Moreover, the LP decoding for error-correcting codes is also extended to the joint detection and decoding problem over inter-symbol interference (ISI) channels [5-7]. However, LP decoding was considered only as a theoretical research problem since its large computational complexity from the point view of implementation.
Recently, Barman et al. proposed an efficient LP decoding method for LDPC codes based on the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) [8]. Generally speaking, the ADMM method provides a distributed framework for solving optimization problems. The distributed feature of ADMM makes it more efficient to solve optimization problems, especially for large-scale problems [9]. More recently, the ADMM decoding of LDPC codes has been improved further in various ways. In terms of poor performances of ADMM decoding at the waterfall region, the penalty terms added to the

objective functions are proposed that can improve the waterfall performances significantly [10-11]. In the ADMM decoding, the most complex part is the Euclidean projection onto check polytopes [12]. To lower the decoding complexity, the Euclidean projection operation is simplified [13-15] or the number of Euclidean projections performed during the ADMM decoding is reduced [16]. In addition, the convergence rate of ADMM decoding is also accelerated by using proper scheduling of the updated messages [17-18]. Simulation results show that both the error-rate performance and the computational complexity of the ADMM penalized decoding are comparable with that of the classical belief propagation (BP) decoding [10][13].
At present, the ADMM framework is only considered for decoding LDPC codes over discrete memoryless channels such as binary symmetric channel and additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. However, how ADMM decoding performs over an ISI channel is not known. Inspired by the advantages of ADMM decoding for LDPC codes, in this paper we make an initial investigation on the use of ADMM decoding over ISI channels. In particular, we consider the turbo equalization [19] with ADMM decoding and compare it with the interior point decoding method [20] for classical ISI channels such as the magnetic recording channels.

C. BER performance Comparisons for PR Channels
In this subsection, the BER performances of the turbo equalization with ADMM decoding, the turbo equalization with BP decoding and the interior point decoding are compared over the aforementioned three PR channels.
In Fig. 5, the BER performances for the three decoding methods are compared over PR4 channel. To make a relatively fair comparison, the maximum number of outer iterations is set to 5 and 10, respectively, for the turbo equalization with both BP and ADMM decoding. We do not choose №±аѓ†аѓФаѓЂ and вАЂ№ЂвАђаѓ†аѓФаѓЂ with optimal BER performance as shown in the last subsection. While for the inner iterations, we do not choose the same number of iterations for the BP and ADMM decoding. This selection is acceptable since the complexity of inner iteration is less than that of the BCJR algorithm. From the figure we can see that the performance gains for turbo equalization with ADMM decoding is about 0.4dB at BER=10-5 when compared to the BP decoding. In addition, turbo equalization with both ADMM and BP decoding performs much better than the interior point decoding.
Fig. 6 compares the BER performances for the three decoding methods over EPR4 channel. It can be seen that the turbo equalization with both ADMM and BP decoding perform significant better than the interior point decoding. For the same number of outer iterations, turbo equalization with ADMM decoding performs slightly better than BP decoding. In particular, when №±аѓ†аѓФаѓЂ аµМ ЌЈ and BER=10-5, there is 0.1dB gains for the turbo equalization with ADMM decoding when compared to the turbo equalization with BP decoding.
Finally, the BER performances for the three decoding methods over modified EPR4 channel are compared in Fig. 7. From the figure we can see that the BER performances for the turbo equalization with BP decoding and the interior point decoding are similar. However, the turbo equalization with ADMM decoding performs much better than the other two methods. For example, the performance gain of the ADMM decoding is 0.5dB at BER=10-5 when compared to the BP decoding.
We also do some simulations for the proposed method over PR channels for two longer LDPC codes with block length 504 and 1008. Simulation results show that turbo equalization with ADMM decoding exhibits the best BER performances among the three decoding methods over the PR4, EPR4 and modified EPR4 channels.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have investigated the turbo equalization with ADMM decoding for PR channels. The details of the information exchange for the proposed scheme are presented. The error-rate performances are provided and compared with the interior point decoding and the turbo equalization with BP decoding. Simulation results show that the proposed scheme performs better than the other two methods. In the future, we will investigate the use of ADMM framework as a whole to solve the ISI channel decoding problem and the BCJR algorithm will not be used as a detector. This may present some advantages since the ADMM method is efficient and fully parallel while the BCJR algorithm is serial in nature.
