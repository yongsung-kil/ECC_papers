Abstract‚ÄĒLinear programming (LP) technique was demonstrated as efficient for binary and non-binary LDPC block decoding. Indeed, it improves the error correction performance of codes and provides the Maximum-likelihood (ML) certificate. In this paper, the LP algorithm is applied for decoding LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CC). The proposed algorithm is built on the decomposition method based on the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM). Motivated by the strong convergence guarantees of an ADMM based LDPC block code (LDPC-BC) decoder and its comparable complexity to a Belief Propagation (BP) based LDPC-BC decoder, the efficiency of decoding LDPC-CCs using the ADMM algorithm is empirically analyzed in this paper. Simulations are carried over LDPCCCs derived from LDPC-BCs and the LDPC-CC proposed by the IEEE 1901 standard. Experimental results show that the LP decoding approach is a promising technique for improving LDPC-CC error correction performance.
Index Terms‚ÄĒLDPC Convolutional Codes, Message-passing decoding, BP decoding; LP decoding, ADMM, complexity analysis, convergence speed analysis.
I. INTRODUCTION
Since their discovery in 1999 [1], LDPC-CCs have gained great attention in the error-correcting area. The interest of LDPC-CCs stems from their encoding simplicity and their reasonable decoding complexity [2]‚Äď[4]. Furthermore, compared to LDPC-BCs [5], their channel universality property, their strong potential to support versatile application scenarios and their flexibility to code packetized data of different lengths using the same code structure [3] also make them attractive. In fact, they have been considered for wireless (IEEE 802.16m [6]), power-line (IEEE 1901 [7]), MIMO broadband communications [8], body area networks (IEEE 802.15.4g and 802.15.6 [9]) and optical communication systems [10].
Recently, a novel approach based on a linear programming (LP) optimization was proposed for decoding binary LDPCBCs by Feldman et al in [11]. Indeed, it has been shown that the error correction performance of the LP-based LDPC-BC decoders is comparable to and often better than the conventional BP algorithm and its Min-Sum (MS) suboptimal variants [11]. The key feature of the LP is its ML certificate which entails in strong theoretical convergence guarantees. Indeed, it was stated that if the LP decoding algorithm outputs an integer solution, it is guaranteed to be an ML codeword [11]. To the best of author‚Äôs knowledge, such decoding technique

was theoretically assessed for terminated LDPC-CCs over the binary symmetric channel in [12]. In this paper, the applicability and the efficiency of the LP strategy for decoding binary terminated LDPC-CCs over the Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel are algorithmically and empirically investigated. Besides, computational and memory complexities are provided and compared with the conventional message passing decoding approaches of LDPC-CCs.
During the last decade, the relaxation of the LP problem was intensively studied in the forward error correction context. The most attractive formulation was stated by Barman et al in [13]. Indeed, a distributed algorithm relying on the ADMM method [14] was introduced for solving the LP problem of decoding binary LDPC-BCs. The fact that it reduces the LP complexity issues, along with other convenient numerical properties of the ADMM approach, motivate our work. First, it has rigorous convergence guarantees [14]. Second, thanks to ADMM, it is possible to interpret the LP decoding as a message passing algorithm that can be iteratively processed similar to a classic BP [13]. Previous works proposed multiple optimizations of the ADMM-LP based LDPC-BC decoders. They aim to improve the error correction performance [15]‚Äď[17] and alleviate its computational complexity and its execution time constraints [13], [18]‚Äď[22]. Due to these advantageous numerical characteristics, the ADMM is considered as an interesting competitor to the BP algorithm for decoding LDPC-BCs. The same assertion is empirically proven for LDPC-CCs in this paper.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows: Section II presents LDPC-CC codes. The ADMM formulation for decoding LDPC-CC codes (ADMM-CC) is described in Section III. In Section IV, the computational complexity and the data memory requirements of the ADMM-CC based decoders are provided and compared with the MS and BP algorithms. Experimental results are reported in Section V.

B. Conventional BP-based Decoding of LDPC-CCs
The potential of LDPC-CCs has been widely assessed using the BP decoding algorithm. The BP is based on an iterative exchange of messages between VNs and CNs over the Tanner graph edges. During a decoding iteration, each CN (VN) sends only one message to every VN (CN) neighbor. Mainly, the decoding process is composed of four steps: Initialization, Check Node Update, Variable Node Update and Decision [1]. Notably, both the Check Node Update and the Variable Node Update computation units are executed sequentially [1].

Fig. 1. An example of HT and its related Tanner Graph [7].

Currently, the BP is the most efficient decoding algorithm for LDPC-CCs. However, it exhibits high computational complexity due to the costly tanh operations. Although many MS based approximations to the BP were proposed [23], they negatively impact the error correction performances. In the next section, a novel approach for decoding T-LDPC-CCs based on the LP algorithm is detailed.

A. Error Correction Comparison
The bit error ratio (BER) performances exhibited by all studied MP algorithms are shown in Fig. 2. The first aspect to note is that the ADMM-l2 provides the same BER performance as the BP algorithm for C1 and C2 codes in the waterfall region. Yet, for these two codes, at BER lower than 10‚ąí5 a coding gain up to 0.4 dB (C1) and 0.9 dB (C2) is observed for the ADMMl2 compared with the BP. However, for the C3 T-LDPCCC decoder, the waterfall of the ADMM-l2 decoder initiates slightly later than the BP (about 0.6 dB later). Moreover, for codes C3 and C4, the BP decoding outperforms the ADMM-l2 until the SNR value 1.9 dB. This is due to the considered penalty coefficients. Indeed, in [16], a parameter generator was introduced to improve the performance of the penalized ADMM LDPC decoders in the waterfall region. Its adaptation to LDPC-CCs is currently under investigation.
The second aspect to note is that an error floor appears for all codes when decoded using the BP algorithm which is not the case for the ADMM-l2 based C1 and C2 T-LDPCCC decoders. Even when an error floor is observed for TLDPC-CC codes (C3, C4), the gap between ADMM-l2 and BP

decoders is roughly constant (1.2 dB for C3 and 0.2 dB for C4). It means that the ADMM-l2 does not generate additional error floor. Meanwhile, the error floor for the ADMM-l2 technique initiates at higher SNR value than that of the BP technique (about 0.4 (0.2) dB for C3 (C4)). For all considered codes, the BER slope of ADMM-l2 is steeper than that of BP decoding. It is similar to that of MS with about 0.6 dB SNR gap.
The third aspect to note is that, regarding the ADMM-LP performance, same observations found for LDPC-BCs are also deduced for LDPC-CCs [15]. Indeed, an improvement up to 1.2 dB (C3), 0.8 dB (C1, C2) and 0.5 dB (C4) is clearly noticed for ADMM-l2 decoders compared with ADMM-LP ones. This is due to the addition of the penalty term. Nonetheless, unlike the ADMM-l2 and BP techniques, studied ADMM-LP decoders do not suffer from error floors.

Obviously, the conventional MP techniques (MS, BP) outperform ADMM-CC algorithms when the MNI is lower than 20 decoding iterations. Nevertheless, at medium values of MNI, the ADMM-CC approaches exhibit better error correction performances. Furthermore, they do not give rise to any error floor behavior.
Ultimately, the ADMM-CC decoding algorithms enable a perceptible error correction improvements and competitive convergence speed compared to classic MP algorithms. Thus, they can be considered as prominent alternatives to traditional MP approaches for decoding T-LDPC-CCs.
VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, the applicability of the ADMM algorithm for decoding LDPC-CCs and its efficiency over the AWGN channel are discussed from an algorithmic and complexity standpoints for the first time. The computational complexity as well as the memory requirements were analyzed and compared with the MS and BP decoding algorithms. Based on experimental results, we can assert that the ADMM-CC technique is a strong competitor to conventional MP methods. Such acknowledgment paves the way to deeply study the performances of ADMM based LDPC-CC decoders.
