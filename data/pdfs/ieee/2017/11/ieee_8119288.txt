CooECC: A Cooperative Error Correction Scheme to Reduce LDPC Decoding Latency in NAND Flash

Abstract—The storage capacity of NAND Flash has increased by scaling down to smaller cell size and using multi-level storage technology, but data reliability is degraded by severer retention errors. To ensure data reliability, error correction codes (ECC) are adopted, such as BCH and low-density parity check (LDPC) codes. However, BCH codes are insufficient when raw bit error rates (RBER) caused by retention errors are high. As a result, BCH codes are inevitably replaced with LDPC codes with stronger error correction capability. Traditional LDPC codes are used to independently correct bit errors in the LSB and MSB pages. Unfortunately, decoding latency in such two pages is significantly unbalanced, MSB pages take much higher latency due to higher RBER, leading to suboptimal flash read performance. This paper proposes a cooperative error correction scheme, called CooECC, to reduce LDPC decoding latency of the MSB page in NAND Flash. By exploiting data error characteristics introduced by retention errors, CooECC integrates the decoding result of the LSB page into the initial information of LDPC decoding for the MSB page, making it more accurate. This in turn enables decoding to converge at a higher rate. Simulation results show that for LDPC schemes with information lengths of 2KB and 4KB, the decoding latency can be reduced by up to 87% and 84%, respectively, when RBER is as high as 8.0 × 10−3.
I. INTRODUCTION
NAND Flash is extensively used in computer storage systems and consumer electronics products because of its large capacity, low power consumption, and non-volatility [16, 17]. The storage capacity of NAND Flash has drastically increased by scaling down to smaller cell size and using multi-level storage technology, i.e., the cell size is scaled down from 6xnm to 1x-nm [5] and each cell stores more than one bit, such as multi-level cells (MLC) [7] with 2 bits, and triple-level cells (TLC) [31] with 3 bits. However, data reliability is sacrificed due to much higher raw bit error rates (RBER) introduced by retention errors [27, 28]. Error correction codes (ECC), such BCH codes and low-density parity check (LDPC) codes [9, 11, 12, 13], are adopted to guarantee data reliability. Unfortunately, when retention errors result in RBER to gradually increase beyond the current error correction capability of BCH codes,

In MLC NAND Flash, two bits in each cell are mapped to two different logical pages, called the least significant bit (LSB) page and the most significant bit (MSB) page [26], respectively. The LSB page is formed by collecting all left bits and the MSB page is formed by collecting all right bits. Traditional LDPC codes are used to independently correct bit errors in the LSB and MSB pages. Unfortunately, decoding latency is seriously unbalanced due to the asymmetrical RBER in such two pages. Decoding latency in MSB pages is much higher due to higher RBER than that of LSB pages, which induces suboptimal flash read performance.
There are several works on improving LDPC performance. Qiao et al. [21] developed a joint decoding strategy relying on retention error characteristics of MLC NAND Flash to reduce the time consumed in the process of non-binary LDPC decoding. Aslam et al. [22] proposed a retentionaware belief propagation scheme, called RA-BP, to correct decoding errors introduced by retention noises. Li et al. [30] proposed a smart sensing level placement scheme based on retention errors to reduce the use of soft sensing levels and thus improving LDPC performance. These existing solutions only consider the effect of retention error characteristics on LDPC performance, without fully extracting and exploiting the large correlation between the LSB page and the MSB page to help LDPC decoding. In contrast, this paper proposes CooECC, a cooperative error correction scheme to reduce LDPC decoding latency of the MSB page thus shortening decoding latency gap between MSB and LSB pages. By exploiting data error characteristics introduced by retention errors, CooECC integrates the decoding result of the LSB page into the initial information of LDPC decoding for the MSB page, thus making it more accurate. By assisting error correction for MSB pages, CooECC can achieve better LDPC decoding convergence with fewer decoding latency. Concretely, when flash performs sequential read operations, data in the LSB page is first read out, then transferred to the LDPC decoder to recover data. Retention errors appear with the increases of retention time and program/erase cycles, which are seen as the most dominant errors [24]. Retention errors are caused by the leakage of electrons stored in the floating gate [19], which causes the cell threshold voltage window to overlap and shift left with different probabilities, as shown in Fig. 1. Crossing the adjacent state to other states has very low percentage. These data error characteristics can be used to design effective ECC. These error transition percentages can be changed into useful log-likelihood ratio (LLR) information required to facilitate LDPC decoding.

This section first presents CooECC overview and explains why CooECC can achieve a reduction in decoding latency, then gives a detailed description of CooECC.

A. CooECC Overview

bit decision can be greatly improved so that decoding latency

is able to be significantly reduced. Traditional LDPC codes

only rely on the information propagation between check nodes

IV. SIMULATION AND RESULTS
This section evaluates CooECC. It first describes the evaluation methodology, and then analyzes simulation results. Finally, this sections analyzes sensitivity and overheads, and gives a brief discussion.
A. Evaluation Methodology
This section gives an evaluation of CooECC using Matlab simulation, compared with the conventional LDPC codes using the more popular NMS decoding algorithm in NAND Flash [12], which does not exploit data error characteristics introduced by retention errors and the decoding result of the LSB page to optimize the decoding initial information of the MSB page. Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes [14], appropriate for NAND flash storage systems, are preferred to be considered, and they have information lengths of 2KB and 4KB, the column weight of 4, the row weight of 36, and the code rate of 8/9. Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel is adopted to simulate NAND flash channel, which has been proven to be approximate to the NAND flash channel [4, 6]. The encoded information sequences are modulated by 4-Pulse Amplitude Modulation (4-PAM) [13] to simulate 2-bit MLC NAND flash cells and then sent to the AWGN channel. The decoding iterations and latency are counted.
B. Results and Analysis

1) Asymmetric RBER: Under the same signal to noise ratio (S/N), RBER counted is different in the LSB and MSB pages, as shown in Table II. RBER in MSB pages is approximately two times higher than that of LSB pages. In terms of retention errors, both S3 to S2 shift and S1 to S0 shift lead to MSB errors, while only S2 to S1 shift results in LSB errors. As a result, MSB pages are more prone to errors and RBER shows the asymmetric characteristic.
2) Decoding Iterations: This section sets the maximum number of LDPC decoding iterations as 50. Average decoding iterations, consumed by LSB pages and MSB pages without CooECC, and by MSB pages with CooECC, are counted under different RBERs of MLC NAND Flash. Simulation results show that decoding iterations increase as RBER increases and have an extremely unbalanced characteristic between the MSB and LSB pages, as shown in Table II. It requires fewer iterations for LSB pages with information lengths of 2KB to correct bit errors, such as less than 10 iterations at an approximate RBER of 1.0×10−2. However, the decoding iteration for MSB pages without CooECC gets to the maximum value of 50, iterations with no guarantee of bit errors being corrected, leading to decoding failure and poor decoding performance. When using CooECC to correct bit errors in MSB pages, much fewer decoding iterations are needed. The decoding iterations are reduced by up to 84% at an approximate RBER of 8.0×10−3 and 80% at an approximate RBER of 1.0×10−2, compared with MSB pages without CooECC. The decoding iterations increase with the increase of RBER, but bit errors can be still corrected, thus significantly improving decoding performance. When RBER is as high as 2.0 × 10−2, the decoding iterations are decreased by 10% under information length of 2KB with 3-bit precision quantization. Getting to such a high RBER needs to experience long retention time or high program/erase cycles. Unbalanced decoding iterations that happen in LSB pages and MSB pages stem from the asymmetric RBER. It costs more iterations for MSB pages with higher RBER to correct bit errors. CooECC can reduce the decoding iterations to a large extent and narrow the gap of decoding iterations between the MSB and LSB pages, as CooECC fully exploits the combination of decoding results and data error characteristics introduced by retention errors, assisting error correction for MSB pages and thus speeding up the decoding converge to the correct codeword.

This section first selects different information lengths and bit quantization precisions to analyze the sensitivity of decoding iterations and latency to different parameters, then analyzes the overhead caused by CooECC. This proposed scheme is also well applied to information length of 4KB. Decoding iterations are reduced with the decrease of RBER, as shown in Table II. In addition, decoding time is reduced by up to 84% at an approximate RBER of 8.0 × 10−3 and 12% at an approximate RBER of 2.0 × 10−2. The longer the codeword is, the higher the decoding complexity and the more the decoding time is. The decoding time smoothly grows with the increase of RBER in Fig. 7. After reaching to RBER of 1.4 × 10−2, decoding time begins to increase rapidly due to the fact that much higher RBER leads to higher decoding complexity. When CooECC is not used for MSB pages with information length of 4KB, the decoding time ratio to LSB pages decreases unsteadily, because the decoding time randomly fluctuates in a small range. The changing trend of decoding time consumed by 4KB is similar to the decoding time cost by 2KB. The proposed scheme is less sensitive to different parameter selections.
This paper also counts average iterations under different S/Ns without using CooECC and 4-bit precision quantization for MSB pages, compared with using CooECC and 3-bit precision quantization for MSB pages with information lengths of 2KB and 4KB. Simulation results show that the decoding iterations with using CooECC and 3-bit precision quantization are close to the decoding iterations without using CooECC and 4-bit precision quantization, as shown in Fig. 8 (a) and Fig. 8 (b). Although using 4-bit precision to quantify the received signal has much better decoding performance, it requires more time, introducing much higher read latency. When using CooECC and 3-bit precision quantization for MSB pages, it slightly decreases the decoding performance but greatly reduces the read latency, compared with 4-bit precision quantization and without using CooECC for MSB pages.

Finally, this paper analyzes the overheads including time and space overheads. For the time overhead, it requires less time to optimize the initial information of the MSB page, which only needs to implement simple operations and is negligible. Although both the LSB page and the MSB page are involved during decoding, the performance loss can be compensated by performing sequential read operations, which has been proven to deliver better system performance [32]. For space overhead, it needs to add a buffer whose size is equal to a flash page size to store the decoding result of the LSB page. For example, if the page size is 2KB, the buffer size is also 2KB, which is negligible for NAND flash of large size.
D. Discussion
Overall, CooECC can reduce the decoding iterations and latency in MSB pages. However, it first needs to acquire the decoding result of the LSB page. CooECC is considered to fit well to the case of sequential read and can significantly improve sequential read performance. Besides, CooECC can also be extended to 2D TLC NAND flash, 3D MLC, and TLC NAND flash, because RBER introduced by retention errors will become more serious and data error characteristics will be more distinct in these NAND flash types.
V. RELATED WORK
This section introduces some previous works, focusing on schemes proposed to decrease effects of retention errors and recently ECC schemes developed based on error characteristics. Cai et al. [1] explored retention errors based on the FPGA platform, and proposed flash correct-and-refresh (FCR) techniques [26] to solve the problem of retention errors. Park et al. [25] proposed the incremental redundancy (IR) scheme to extend data retention time. Wang et al. [27] designed the programming initial step only (PISO) scheme to lower retention error rates. Cai et al. [24] proposed retention optimized reading (ROR) and retention failure recovery (RFR) techniques. Luo et al. [28] proposed WARM, a write-hotness aware retention management policy, to improve flash lifetime. According to retention error characteristics of MLC NAND Flash, Qiao et al. [21] proposed a joint decoding scheme, reducing the decoding time of non-binary LDPC. Zhang et al. [19] proposed a retention error aware LDPC scheme to reduce decoding latency. Tankamaru et al. [40] proposed error-prediction LDPC

and error-recovery schemes, lowering RBER and extending data retention time. Zhao et al. [2] proposed three techniques to guarantee data reliability by utilizing the progressive LDPC decoding approach. Aslam et al. [22] developed a retentionaware belief propagation (RA-BP) decoding algorithm to correct decoding errors caused by retention noises. Zhao et al. [4] developed two techniques to improve LDPC decoding throughput. [22] and [19] exploit retention error characteristics to improve LDPC decoding performance, without considering the correlation between the decoding process of the LSB page and the MSB page. [4] leverages bit error characteristics and data dependency to improve LDPC decoding throughput, without considering the effects of retention noises on bit errors. The previous work [1] characterized data error characteristics introduced by retention errors. These observed data error characteristics inspire us to propose the CooECC scheme in this paper. Specially, this paper fully explores the impacts of decoding correlation between the LSB page and the MSB page and data error characteristics caused by retention errors on LDPC decoding performance, and maximizes the potential of improving decoding performance. It proposes the CooECC scheme that integrates the decoding result of the LSB page and data error characteristics introduced by retention errors into the process of decoding the MSB page to assist the error correction for the page, thus improving decoding performance and shortening latency.
VI. CONCLUSIONS
Retention errors lead to higher RBER, affecting data reliability. LDPC codes with soft decision decoding are adopted to ensure data reliability. When decoding, decoding iterations show extremely unbalanced characteristics between MSB pages and LSB pages. It requires more decoding iterations to correct bit errors in MSB pages due to higher RBER. Such unbalanced characteristics result in serious read latency. To reduce decoding latency in MSB pages, this paper proposes CooECC, associating the decoding result of the LSB page with data error characteristics caused by retention errors to make decoding initial information of MSB pages more accurate. As a result, decoding latency in the MSB page is significantly decreased. Simulation results show that decoding latency is reduced by up to 87% and 84%, respectively, compared with traditional LDPC scheme with information lengths of 2KB and 4KB at the approximate RBER of 8.0 × 10−3.
