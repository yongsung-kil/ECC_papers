Spatially-Coupled LDPC Codes for Two Dimensional Erasure Channel
Gou Hosoya and Hiroyuki Yashima Department of Information and Computer Technology, Faculty of Engineering, Tokyo University of Science, Tokyo, Japan
hosoya@rs.tus.ac.jp

Abstract—In this study, we developed spatially coupled LDPC (SC-LDPC) codes on the two-dimensional array erasure (2DAE) channel. We propose a method of generating new SC-LDPC codes with the restriction on the check node constraint. We evaluate by density evolution analysis that the improvement of the threshold for the proposed two-dimensional SC-LDPC codes over the onedimensional SC-LDPC codes. Moreover we verify that the BP threshold of the proposed codes can approach the corresponding maximum a posteriori (MAP) threshold of the original residual graph on the 2DAE channel.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes and the belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm [1] have received a lot of attention because of their high performance [4]. Furthermore, the theoretical analysis of this coding scheme— density evolution [2]—has shown its promising capability. The performances of irregular LDPC block codes [3] are very close to the Shannon limit. But performance gap between the BP decoding and the maximum a posteriori (MAP) decoding [5], [6], [9] still exists. It is observed that the BP performance of spatially coupled LDPC (SC-LDPC) codes [7], [8] can improve dramatically towards the MAP performance of the corresponding LDPC block codes. This concept, called threshold saturation, has been proven in [8].
Recently, Cassuto et al. have proposed the two-dimensional array LDPC (2D-LDPC) codes in place of the two-dimensional array erasure (2DAE) channels [11], [12]. The studies of array codes have also been carried out in [13], [14]. Array codes are used as a central element of redundant arrays of inexpensive disks (RAID) storage systems [15]. Cassuto et al. have also presented probabilistic analysis for 2D-LDPC codes on the 2DAE channels. The analysis is based on density evolution for the induced graph of the 2D-LDPC codes. By applying the restriction on the check node constraint, the thresholds of 2D-LDPC codes over the 2DAE channels are higher than those of the one-dimensional LDPC codes over the binary erasure channels (BEC) of the same capacity.
To apply SC-LDPC codes on the 2DAE channels, we have to take into account the bit assignment on the two-dimensional array. If the erasures arise, like long burst erasures, the SCLDPC codes will fail to decode. Some related works can be found in [16]–[21]. The works in [17], [18], [19], [20] focused on the block fading channel which is related to 2DA channel. These works presented for a small number of storage devices with low rate code. In [19], [20], spatial coupling using

root-LDPC code were considered. By applying root-LDPC codes for spatial coupling yields high performance. However root-LDPC code has two problems, i.e. it can decode only information bits and it cannot decode the parity bits. Moreover it is difficult to construct code for various rates.
To overcome these problems, we propose two-dimensional array SC-LDPC (2D-SC-LDPC) codes for the 2DAE channels. We propose a method of generating new SC-LDPC codes with the restriction on the check node constraint. We additionally apply this restriction for the conventional SC-LDPC code construction as in [10]. Therefore the proposed code can be constructed for various rates. We evaluate by density evolution analysis that the improvement of the threshold for the proposed two-dimensional SC-LDPC codes over the one-dimensional SC-LDPC codes. Moreover we verify that the BP threshold of the proposed codes can approach the corresponding MAP threshold of the original residual graph as introduced in [11] on the 2DAE channel.

B. Two-Dimensional LDPC Block Codes

An LDPC code is represented by the Tanner graph, which

consists of N variable nodes and Me check nodes with randomly generated edges. Cassuto et al. presented the construc-

tion of the two-dimensional array LDPC block (2D-LDPC-

BC) codes on the 2DAE channels where N = bZ variable

nodes are assigned in an array with the restriction that each

variable node belongs to a distinct column in an array. Figure

1 (b) depicts this restriction called the restriction on the check

node constraint. From this property, each check constraint has

codeword bits (variable nodes) from different columns in an

array. This is an advantage of the erasure decoding over the

2DAE channels since one column failure does not produce

multiple erasures in each constraint.

We compared four LDPC ensembles, LDPC-BC (1D and 2D) of rate 0.5, and SC-LDPC (1D and 2D) of rate 0.49. We set w = 3 for (4,8) SC-LDPC ensembles and w = 4 for (5,10) SC-LDPC ensembles, respectively, and set B0 = B1 = B2 = B3 = B4 = [1, 1]. The thresholds of the 1D-LDPC ensembles on the 2DAE channel are identical to those on the BEC. For example, the threshold of (3,6) LDPC ensemble on the BEC is 0.4294, so its corresponding 2DAE threshold when r = 6 and Z = 12 takes 0.8588(= 0.4294Z/r). The thresholds of 2D-SC-LDPC ensemble are larger than those of the 1D-SCLDPC ensemble whereas those of 2D-LDPC-BC ensemble are usually smaller than those of the 1D-LDPC-BC ensemble. It is well known that as (c, d) gets large, thresholds of SC-LDPC ensemble become large [7]. However, the gap between 1D and 2D SC-LDPC ensembles on the 2DAE channel takes small as (c, d) gets large. From these results, small (c, d) for the SCLDPC codes is a good choice on the 2DAE channel.
Figure 4 shows thresholds comparison for various LDPC ensemble on the (Z = 2r, r) 2DAE channel when r = 3, 4, . . . , 10 (the ratio between Z and r is fixed). For fixed the ratio of Z and r, thresholds of 1D-LDPC ensembles take constant values where as those of 2D-LDPC ensembles become small. However, it is verified that thresholds of 2D-LDPC ensembles are larger than those of 1D-LDPC ensembles.
We also confirmed different protographs comparison for both 1D/2D SC-LDPC ensembles with the same parameters (c, d, L). For a limitation of pages, we omit the results, but we verified performance improvement over the 1D-SC-LDPC ensembles.

V. CONCLUSION
In this study, we developed the SC-LDPC codes on the 2DAE channels. We took into account the bit assignment on the two-dimensional array to facilitate the BP decoding for the SC-LDPC codes. Moreover, we developed the codes with the restriction on the check node constraint and verified the improvement of the threshold over the 1D-SC-LDPC codes. We verified that the BP threshold of the proposed codes can approach the corresponding MAP threshold of the original residual graph on the 2DAE channel.
