Abstract‚ÄĒDue to many small cycles in factor graphs, detection and decoding over two-dimensional inter-symbol interference (2D-ISI) channels has been considered difficult. It is reported that generalized belief propagation (GBP) detection for 2D-ISI performs much better than BP. However, conventional studies for GBP for 2D-ISI were restricted to the separate uses of detection and decoding. Joint usage of detection and decoding were proposed in [1] but hard-decisions are inserted between detector and decoder. This makes a gap to the optimal performance.
In this paper, we study full-joint use of GBP for detector and decoder for 2D-ISI, i.e., we use only soft-messages. Furthermore, we clarify a necessary condition with respect to the region graph that messages converge to a GBP fixed point. We found most of regions graph proposed in the literature do not satisfy the convergence condition. This prevents the full-joint use of GBP for 2D-ISI.
Moreover, we propose a method to construct region graphs which satisfy the condition. Numerical experiments show that the proposed joint detector and decoder perform better than the conventional separate detector and decoder [2] in a simple 2D-ISI channels.
I. INTRODUCTION
Factor graphs corresponding to the two-dimensional ISI channel have many small loops [3]. Such loops degrade the performance of belief propagation (BP) algorithm. Shental et al. [4] employed generalized belief propagation (GBP) proposed by [5] for 2D-ISI detection. Khatami et al. [1] considered concatenation of GBP detector and BP decoder. The decoder‚Äôs hard-decision results are fed back to the detector. However, the decoder‚Äôs soft-decision results are not fed back to the detector.
Matcha et al. [2] used GBP to obtain LLR of each symbol and input the LLR to a BP decoder. However, Matcha et al. [2] did not study soft information feedback from BP decoder to GBP detector.
Since GBP is a generalized version of BP, one can unify a detector and a decoder . This unification means turbo equalization that messages in the decoder return to the detector. However, no study is reported about such unification. Therefore, we tried to unify a detector and a decoder. We found

a problem of GBP detector for unification. Moreover, we found out a resolution of the problem. Additionally, we constructed a unified GBP decoder and evaluated its error performance.
