Pilot-Tone Assisted Log-Likelihood Ratio for LDPC Coded CO-OFDM System
Shengjiao Cao, Pooi-Yuen Kam, Fellow, IEEE, Changyuan Yu, Member, IEEE, and Xiaofei Cheng

Abstract— Pilot-tone assisted log-likelihood ratio (PT-LLR) is derived for low-density parity-check coded, coherent optical orthogonal frequency division multiplexing systems in the presence of linear phase noise. The knowledge of common phase error (CPE) obtained from the pilot-tone is incorporated into the new LLR metric, which eliminates the need for prior CPE estimation and compensation. We compare our metric with the conventional LLR (C-LLR) through extensive simulation using their approximate versions (APT-LLR, AC-LLR). APT-LLR has the same order of complexity as AC-LLR while it outperforms AC-LLR for higher order modulation formats (16-QAM, 64-QAM) at smaller pilot-tone-to-signal power ratios. In addition, we show that with the help of time-domain blind intercarrier interference mitigation, both metrics perform better in the presence of larger laser linewidth.
Index Terms— Pilot-tone assisted, log-likelihood ratio, linear phase noise, common phase error, LDPC coded OFDM.

I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) coded orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) is a suitable coded modulation technique for long-haul optical communication [1]. Compared to single carrier, OFDM is more robust to chromatic dispersion (CD) and polarization mode dispersion (PMD). Recently, there have been quite a few experimental demonstrations using LDPC-coded, coherent optical OFDM (CO-OFDM) for high speed long-haul transmission [2], [3]. However, CO-OFDM is prone to linear phase noise caused by both transmitter laser and receiver local oscillator. LPN will cause both common phase error (CPE) and intercarrier interference (ICI) in the frequency domain. ICI can be approximated as additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) and is negligible when the laser linewidth is small. Pilot subcarriers [4], [5] or pilot-tone [6] have been proposed for CPE estimation.
The performance of the LDPC decoder depends on the calculation of the decoding metric, i.e., the log-likelihood

We propose to incorporate the knowledge of CPE obtained from the pilottone into the calculation of the bit LLR. The derived bit LLR, defined as pilot-tone assisted LLR (PT-LLR), is evaluated from the likelihood function given the received signal that carries that bit and the pilot-tone that carries the knowledge of unknown CPE. The phase noise term is included into the decoding metric and thus the need of prior phase compensation is eliminated. We compare APT-LLR and AC-LLR in simulation, both of which have the same performance as their non-approximate versions. Moreover, APT-LLR outperforms AC-LLR for higher-order modulation formats (16-QAM, 64-QAM) at smaller pilot-tone to signal power ratios (PSR) with the same order of complexity.
When the laser linewidth becomes too large, tackling only CPE is no longer sufficient and ICI needs to be dealt with. In [11], we have proposed a time-domain blind ICI (BL-ICI) mitigation algorithm for non-constant amplitude format. In this letter, we propose to employ the BL-ICI mitigation algorithm prior to the LLR calculation when ICI is no longer negligible. The performance of both metrics under prior ICI compensation is investigated through simulation. With the help of BL-ICI mitigation, the performance of both metrics has improved in the presence of larger laser linewidth. In this case, APT-LLR still outperforms C-LLR at smaller PSR for 16-QAM and 64-QAM.

IV. SIMULATION STUDY
In the simulation, a simple rate-half, regular (204, 102) binary LDPC code [12] is used for demonstration purpose with IDFT/DFT size of 256 and data subcarriers of 204, resulting in an oversampling ratio of about 1.25. Different modulation formats including 4-QAM (QPSK), 16-QAM and 64-QAM with gray coding are employed. The noise variance N0 is assumed to be known at the receiver side. We model the linear phase noise as Wiener process with the phase increment being a Gaussian variable with variance of σ 2=2πνt, where ν is the combined laser linewidth and t is the time difference between two samples. Note that σ 2 = 8 × 10−4rad2 or 2 × 10−4rad2 or 5 × 10−5rad2 corresponds to ν = 1.28 MHz or 320 kHz or 80 kHz for a 10-Gsample/s system.
It is observed in all the following simulation settings that PT-LLR (or C-LLR) and APT-LLR (or AC-LLR) have almost identical BER performance. In terms of computational complexity, PT-LLR involves an extra integration and ln(.) term compared to APT-LLR. Thus, we will employ APT-LLR (or AC-LLR) instead of PT-LLR (or C-LLR) in the simulation.
In the first case, we investigate our metric without any compensation of ICI. Fig. 2 shows the total required Eb/N0 versus PSR with different phase noise variances using AC-LLR or APT-LLR for pilot-tone assisted (a) QPSK, (b) 16-QAM and (c) 64-QAM. For higher-order modulation formats (16-QAM, 64-QAM), our metric (APT-LLR) outperforms the conventional one (AC-LLR) at smaller PSR values. For example, at −16-dB PSR, APT-LLR has more than 0.3-dB (or 0.2-dB) improvement over AC-LLR with different phase noise variances for 16-QAM (or 64-QAM). Our metric performs as good as the conventional one for the rest situations. Since APT-LLR has the same order of complexity as AC-LLR, avoids the extra step of CPE compensation and outperforms AC-LLR in certain cases, we conclude that it (APT-LLR) would be a better alternative of the conventional metric.
When the laser linewidth becomes too large and the ICI term becomes non-negligible, CPE compensation alone is no longer sufficient. In the second case, we investigate the performance of our metric when time-domain BL-ICI mitigation [11] is employed prior to LLR calculation. V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we derived a pilot-tone assisted log-likelihood metric (PT-LLR) based on the received signal as well as the pilot-tone, with consideration of linear phase noise for LDPC-coded CO-OFDM system. Firstly, we compare the performance of our new metric with the conventional one through simulation. The approximate versions (APT-LLR, AC-LLR) are adopted due to their lower complexity with identical performance compared to non-approximate versions. It is demonstrated that APT-LLR outperforms AC-LLR for 16-QAM and 64-QAM at smaller PSR, with the same order of complexity, yet APT-LLR eliminates the need of prior CPE compensation. Secondly, we investigate the performance of both metrics under prior time-domain blind ICI mitigation. With the help of time-domain BL-ICI mitigation algorithm, both metrics gain improvement for larger laser linewidth.

The improvement of our metric compared to AC-LLR at PSR=−16dB vanishes for very large laser linewidth (e.g., 1 MHz) without ICI compensation while the improvement is restored with ICI compensation. APT-LLR requires about 0.3-dB (or 0.2-dB) less SNRb (BER=10−4) than AC-LLR for v = 500 kHz (or 1 MHz) with ICI compensation.
