I. INTRODUCTION 
In modern high speed communication systems, Forward 
Error Correcting Codes (FECs) are widely used [1]. Soft 
decision FECs has high net coding gain but larger circuit 
complexity. In order to achieve high net coding gain along 
with reduced circuit complexity, iterative soft decision FECs 
are employed. One such soft decision based FEC is Low 
Density Parity Check (LDPC) Code. Low-Density Parity­
Check (LDPC) codes first discovered by Gallager have a 
remarkable performance with iterative decoding. Most 
methods for designing good LDPC codes are based on random 
construction, but algebraically structured LDPC codes may be 
needed for hardware implementation. In the case of random 
LDPC codes of large code length, a significant amount of 
memory is needed to pile up their parity-check matrices. Also, 
it is difficult to access the memory and encode data efficiently. 
Quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes may be a good 
candidate to solve the memory problem due to their structural 
regularity. Recently, several coding theorists proposed some 
classes of QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation 
matrices and analyzed their properties. These codes perform 
quite well compared to random LDPC codes. In this paper,

Algorithm (SPA). Although SPA gives superior BER 
performance, it has moderate computational complexity and

it is required to select decoding algorithm with low

Algorithm (MSA). The other two decoding algorithms in 
literature are Modified Min Sum (MMS) and Min Max 
algorithms which are the advanced versions of MSA. The best 
suitable algorithm which has a compromise between error

convergence speed of various decoding algorithms, the theory

thresholding. Here an attempt is made to reduce the latency of 
the system without much sacrifice in error performance.

II. LDPC CODES 
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a class of 
linear block codes having excellent error correcting capability. 
They are characterized by the sparse parity-check matrix 
which contains only a few 1 's in comparison to the amount of 
O's. Their main advantage is that they provide a performance 
which is very near to Shannon limit. Furthermore they are well 
suited for parallel implementations. LDPC codes were first 
introduced by Gallager in his PhD thesis in 1960 [2]. But due 
to the computational complexity in coding and decoding, they 
were mostly ignored for a long time. Later, MacKay 
rediscovered LDPC codes which showed the way to rigorous 
research in related areas.

IV. DENSITY EVOLUTION 
It is possible to resolve how an ensemble of Tanner graphs 
is likely to perform, if the Tanner graphs are all cycle free and 
channel is memory less [12]. For this purpose, the evolution of 
probability density functions is traced out through the 
message-passing algorithm, which is called as Density­
Evolution. Assume that the expected fraction of incorrect 
messages which are passed in the kth iteration be Pe"(k). For 
any €>O , the probability of incorrect messages that are passed 
in the kID iteration tends to zero exponentially as the block 
length n increases [13]. Furthermore, there exists a channel 
noise variance threshold a', with the following property: if cr 
< a' then Iiml-->OO' Pe"(k)=O; if a> a', then there exists a 
constant a(a»O such that Pe"(k)) > a(a) for all k 2:1. Thus 
density evolution greatly reduces the number of iterations 
required for achieving better performance of an error 
correction code or it is possible to find the exact threshold at 
which the algorithm converges.

VI. CONCLUSION 
In this paper, we have done a comparative analysis for 
various decoding algorithms of LDPC code for the design of 
less complex and high speed channel coder. The BER 
performance of a QC LDPC code is evaluated through 
simulation with four decoding algorithms. Among them, SPA 
shows superior error performance. But as it is quite complex, 
other three decoding algorithms are considered. MMS 
algorithm shows low error and it is less complex compared to 
SPA. By proper selection of threshold, MMS can approach 
SPA in BER performance. Also we estimated the Density 
Evolution charts of the four decoding algorithms. MMS 
algorithm converges faster than the other three decoding 
algorithms. With thresholding, we again plotted the BER 
performance of all the four algorithms. Our results show that 
the error performances in both cases are ahnost same. It is 
proved that thresholding with density evolution charts clearly 
improve speed of performance.
