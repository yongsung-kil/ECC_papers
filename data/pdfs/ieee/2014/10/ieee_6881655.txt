Large Girth Column-Weight Two and Three LDPC Codes
Mohammad Gholami and Ghaffar Raeisi

Abstract—In this paper, the concept of broken diagonal pair in the chess-like square board is used to define some well-structured block designs whose incidence matrices can be considered as the parity-check matrices of some high rate cycle codes with girth 12. Interestingly, the constructed regular cycle codes with row-weights t, 3 ≤ t ≤ 20, t = 7, 15, 16, have the minimum lengths among the known regular girth-12 cycle codes. In addition, the proposed cycle codes can be easily extended to some high rate column weight-3 LDPC codes with girth 6. Simulation results show that the constructed non-binary cycle codes and binary QC LDPC codes have good performances over AWGN channel.
Index Terms—LDPC code, Tanner graph, girth.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY PARITY-CHECK (LDPC) codes [1] are the most promising class of linear codes which based on methods of construction can be divided into two categories: random codes [2] and structured codes [7]–[11]. Although randomly constructed LDPC codes of large length provide excellent bit-error rate (BER) performance [2], the memory required to specify the nonzero elements of such a random matrix can be a major challenge for hardware implementation. Structured LDPC codes can lead to much simpler implementations for decoding and encoding.
Each LDPC code C is specified by a sparse parity check matrix H and its corresponding Tanner graph TG(H) [3]. The Tanner graph TG(H) collects check nodes and variable nodes associated to the rows and columns of H, respectively, and each edge connects a check node to a variable node if the intersection of the corresponding row and column of H has a nonzero entry. Cycle codes are a class of LDPC codes with paritycheck matrices having fixed column weight-2 and have shown potential in some applications such as partial response channels [6]. A method to design regular nonbinary cycle codes with both good waterfall and error floor properties is presented in [5]. The parity check matrix H of a cycle code can be considered as the incidence matrix of a cycle graph [6] whose vertices and edges are corresponding to check nodes and variable nodes in TG(H), respectively.

Cycles, especially short cycles, in TG(H) degrade the performance of LDPC decoders, because they affect the independence of the extrinsic information exchanged in the iterative decoding [2]. Accordingly, the design of LDPC codes with large girth is of great interest. Different approaches have been studied to construct LDPC codes with large girth. Among the random-like approach constructions, the progressive edge growth (PEG) [12] is one of the most successful approaches for the construction of LDPC codes with large girth. In [14], the authors proposed some modifications in the PEG algorithm to improve its achievable girth. This modified algorithm is used to design some regular cycle codes with minimum lengths whose associated graphs are cages in some cases.
Except the PEG algorithm and its evolved construction algorithms, some algebraic constructions of large girth LDPC codes have been investigated. For instance, in [10], cage graphs were used to construct cycle codes over a wide range of girths and rates. However, the problem of constructing cage graphs is very challenging and there is no deterministic approach to constructing arbitrary cages. In [11] the authors used Singer perfect difference sets to construct some non-binary cycle codes with girth 12 and regularity t = q + 1, q prime power, which achieved the Gallager bound. Subsequently, some girth-8 cycle codes were constructed in [4], whose parity-check matrices used as the mother matrices of some quasi cyclic (QC) cycle codes with girth 24. Moreover, some short non-binary irregular repeataccumulate (IRA) codes based on well-known Hamiltonian and Hypohamiltonian graphs are presented in [15]. Also, a subclass of low-rate LDPC codes, accumulate repeat jagged accumulate (ARJA) and accumulate-repeat-accumulate code with repetition-4 (AR4A), are proposed in [16] which have very fast encoder structure and near-capacity performance.
The aim of this paper is to construct some column-weight two and three LDPC codes such that the constructed cycle codes have girth 12 and for row-weights t, 3 ≤ t ≤ 20, t = 7, 15, 16, the constructed cods have the best known lengths among known regular girth-12 cycle codes [13]. Our construction provides benefits in terms of memory efficiency in storing the parity-check matrices of the constructed codes in the decoder.
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section II, we set out the mathematical preliminaries that help to describe the construction. In Section III, we extend the constructed cycle codes to some high rate column-weight three LDPC codes with girth 6. Finally in Section IV, we examine the performance of the constructed codes. As simulation results show, the constructed non-binary cycle codes with large girth outperform the non-binary codes based on PEG, RA and Hypohamiltonian graphs [15]. Moreover, the constructed column-weight three QC LDPC codes outperform integer lattice, PEG, ARJA, and AR4A codes over AWGN channel.

Let Mm(v) denote the incidence matrix of Bm(v). It is easy to see that Mm(v) can be considered as the paritycheck matrix of a column-weight 3 LDPC code with girth 6, because for each i = j,Lm(vi) ∩ Lm(vj) = ∅ and the new points added to Lm(vi) and Lm(vj) are distinct. Clearly the first m rows of Mm(v) have the same regularity as Hm(v) and the new t added rows have regularity m/2 .
