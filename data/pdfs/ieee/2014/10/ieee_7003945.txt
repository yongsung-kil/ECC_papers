Fei Wang, Peng Zhang

Abstract-A serial-input serial-output encoder based on pipelined rotate-left-accumulator (RLA) circuits is designed for multi-rate Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC-LDPC) codes of Chinese digital terrestrial/television multimedia broadcasting (DTMB) standard. The RLA circuit can make the area usage economical, and the pipelined architecture can simplify the memory structure. The encoder is implemented on FPGA. Simulation results demonstrate that the design meets the requirement of DTMB standard with lower energy consumption and fewer hardware resources.

I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes were invented by R. Gallager in 1962 [1]. Although LDPC codes have completeness in theory, they could not be directly used in practical applications. So LDPC codes were ignored by researchers for a long period. As the technology developing rapidly, LDPC codes were rediscovered [2] and were shown to achieve error-correction performance very close to the Shannon limit [3]. Subsequently, LDPC codes have been adopted by several standards such as Chinese digital terrestrial/television multimedia broadcasting (DTMB) standard [4]. Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes are an important subclass of LDPC codes. DTMB standard takes irregular QC-LDPC codes as a part of its forward error correction coding.
The implementation of the QC-LDPC encoders in the communication system is mostly based on Shift Register Adder Accumulator (SRAA) circuits and generator matrixes [5-6]. Then some improved methods were proposed [7-8] to design the QC-LDPC encoder for DTMB standard, such as multistage parallel SRAA circuits and partial parallel SRAA circuits which were used in the encoders. But there still remain some problems. Firstly, the content of the shift registers in SRAA circuit refreshes once per clock cycle, which causes much more energy consumption. Secondly, circulant generators occupy multiple ROM, resulting in memory resource waste and high cost. A Rotate-Left-Accumulator (RLA) circuit, proposed in

[9], can solve the first problem. In RLA circuit, the outputs of the look-up table (LUT) are directly connected to the inputs of the AND gates without having to use the shift registers that is indispensible in SRAA circuit. In this way, the area of the circuit is saved and the energy consumption is largely reduced. Furthermore, serial pipelined architecture can simplify the memory structure, and all the circulant generators are stored in a single LUT. These overcome the disadvantages of multiple ROM. As a result, plenty of memory resources are saved and the cost is reduced. Hence a multi-rate QC-LDPC encoder based on serial pipelined architecture for DTMB standard is designed in this paper, and this encoder is successfully implemented on FPGA.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II is a brief introduction of QC-LDPC codes for DTMB standard. Section III presents a design of multi-rate QC-LDPC encoder for DTMB standard. Then section IV is about FPGA implementation and results. Finally, conclusions are made in Section V.
II. QC-LDPC CODES FOR DTMB STANDARD
DTMB standard was announced officially in August 2006, of which the forward error correction coding FEC is performed by an outer code (BCH code) and an inner code (QC-LDPC code). The performances of QC-LDPC codes are almost the same as computer-generated random LDPC codes. Moreover, the complexity of the implementation of QC-LDPC codes is closed to cyclic LDPC codes. The generator matrix of a QCLDPC code is composed of several circulants. In a circulant, each row is the right cyclic shift of the row above it. This architecture makes the encoder easy to implement on hardware.

According to Table I, when Ș=0.4, 35 RLA circuits are needed in the encoder. When Ș=0.6, 23 RLA circuits are needed. When Ș=0.8, 11 RLA circuits are needed. Hence the encoder includes 35 RLA circuits to meet the requirement of multi-rate QC-LDPC codes for DTMB standard.
For Ș=0.4, the procedure of QC-LDPC encoder is as follows. The information sequence m is feed serially. In the first clock cycle, information bit m1 is stored in the register d1. RLA_1 reads the circulant generator g1,1 from the LUT through the data bus, and computes with the data in d1. In the second clock cycle, m1 is shifted into register d2 while m2 is stored in the register d1. RLA_2 reads the circulant generator g1,2 from the LUT through the data bus, and computes with the data in d2. Meanwhile, RLA_1 computes with the data in d1. Similarly, when m3048 is stored in the register d35 and calculated with RLA_35, a QC-LDPC code has been completed and the parity-check bits are stored in each register A of RLA circuits.
For Ș=0.6 and 0.8, the last 12 and 24 RLA circuits are not involved in calculating, respectively.
The multi-rate QC-LDPC encoder designed in this paper is successfully implemented on FPGA, and each module is described as shown below.
Matrix_Cell module: This module is the implementation of the RLA circuit. It has 1bit shift input and 1bit shift output that used to output the parity-check bits serially. As shown in Fig.3, the shift output of RLA_1 circuit is connected to the encoder output.

This pipelined architecture, using a single LUT to store all the circulant generators, can simplify the memory structure and save storage space greatly. V. CONCLUSIONS
A serial-input serial-output multi-rate QC-LDPC encoder for DTMB standard, based on pipelined RLA circuits, is designed in this paper. The RLA-based encoder utilizes fewer hardware resources than the SRAA-based encoder. Particularly, the amount of the registers of RLA circuits is almost half that of SRAA circuits. It not only saves the circuit area, but also greatly reduces the energy consumption. Compared with the traditional QC-LDPC encoder, this pipelined architecture, using a single LUT to store all the circulant generators, can simplify the memory structure, save the storage space and reduce the cost. The whole design is successfully implemented on Cyclone IV E device. The results show that the design satisfies the requirement of multi-rate QC-LDPC codes for DTMB standard. Moreover, this encoder is applicable for not only DTMB standard, but also other standards with QC-LDPC codes as their channel coding schemes.
