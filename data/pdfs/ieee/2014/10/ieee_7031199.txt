Ultra-Low Complexity Early Termination Scheme for Layered LDPC Decoding

Yuan-Syun Wu1, Cheng-Hung Lin1,2 and Shu-Yen Lin1,2
1. Department of Electrical Engineering, 2. Innovation Center for Big Data and Digital Convergence Yuan Ze University
Jungli, Taiwan 32003, R.O.C.

Abstract—Energy consumption is a major issue for LDPC decoding. To save LDPC iterative process energy, early terminations are commonly used. Traditional parity check equation is used but has a growing complexity as the block size increases. This requires a large overhead in term of area. This paper proposes an ultra-low complexity early termination (ET) scheme with a small loss of bit error rate performance based on the layer stopping (LS) criterion. The proposed ET scheme is performed by checking the number of convergent layers stopped by the LS criterion with a preset threshold. Compared with other ET schemes, the proposed ET scheme has a negligible hardware overhead and an acceptable bit error rate performance.
Keywords—low-density parity-check; early termination; NAND Flash
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were invented by Robert G. Gallager [1] in 1962. LDPC codes have attracted tremendous attention due to their near-capacity performance and potential in parallel implementation of decoders. Hence, LDPC codes are widely adopted by several communication standards such as IEEE 802.11n, 802.15.3c, and 802.16e.
LDPC codes are defined as a sparse parity check matrix H. The × parity check matrix H can be expressed by a Tanner graph, where check node and bit node is connected by an edge if and only if the matrix element equals to 1. In the matrix H, each 1’s element in the bit nodes of the row is referred to as row weight , and each 1’s element in the check nodes of the column is referred to as column weight .
Because predominant features of NAND Flash devices are high capacity, fast access speed, and low power consumption, NAND Flash devices have been the fastest growing product in mobile devices, such as cellular phones, digital cameras, and smart-pads. In order to provide higher data transmission reliability, some NAND Flash devices adopted the LDPC codes. However, the energy supplements are finite and crucial in the mobile devices. In order to allow the finite energy devices working for longer time, it is necessary to reduce the power consumption of the LDPC decoding. An intuitive idea is that reducing the power consumption is equivalent to reduce the number of computation. Hence many early termination (ET) schemes have been introduced in literature, such as harddecision aided (HDA) [4], soft-decision aided (SDA) [5] and parity check equation (PCE) criteria. However, those ET schemes are companioned with high computational complexity

in large block size LDPC codes. Hence we propose an ultralow complexity ET scheme for layered the decoding on NAND Flash memory to reduce the overall number of computations. The proposed ET scheme based on layer stopping (LS) criterion [6] and called as layer stopping number termination (LSNT) scheme. The LSNT is a low complexity ET scheme has a negligible hardware overhead and an acceptable bit error rate (BER) performance compared with several common ET schemes.
II. PROPOSED EARLY TERMINATION ALGORITHM
The LS criterion was proposed to reduce the convergent operations within decoding iteration for the layered decoding algorithm [7]. The idea is to detect the convergent layer by comparing the a posteriori values of bit nodes with a preset threshold in each layer. In the following iteration, the record of convergent layer is then able to stop the node computations. Without an ET scheme, the decoder still operates before reaching the preset maximum number of iterations even if lots of convergent layers have been detected by the LS criterion in the following iterative process. By using the method of LS criterion, the LSNT scheme is proposed to check the number of convergent layers with a threshold value. If the number of convergent layers is over the threshold value, the iterative layered LDPC decoding is stopped by the proposed LSNT

In the LSNT scheme, if each a posteriori of bit node is larger than the in one layer, the operation of the layer will be stopped in the following iteration. Simultaneously, if one layer has been stopped during iteration process, the counter of the LSNT scheme will add one to record the number of convergent layers. When the value of counter is over the preset threshold , the entire layered LDPC decoding process is terminated before reaching the preset maximum number of iteration. Thus the LSNT scheme is only composed by one counter and one comparator to perform the ET of the layered LDPC decoding. Compared to other ET schemes, the proposed LSNT scheme has a good feature of low hardware complexity.
III. SIMULATION
The simulations are performed to characterize the threshold values for the LS criterion and LSNT scheme. The simulation environment is under a rate-0.91 QC-LDPC code for a NAND Flash with a block size of 8960, AWGN channel, and layered LDPC decoding with a maximum number of iteration of 8. The appropriate threshold value = 19 is first achieved for the LS criterion via numerous simulations to obtain the same BER performance of layered LDPC decoding with respect to the min-sum algorithm (MSA). We then obtain the appropriate threshold value = 300 by referencing to the widely-used ET scheme, HAD. V. CONCLUSION

In this paper, we propose an ultra-low complexity ET scheme to reduce the number of computations availably. We estimated the area cost of several ET schemes by using TSMC 90nm CMOS process. The LSNT has a high area efficiency compared to other ET schemes based on LS criterion. The LSNT scheme has a negligible hardware overhead with acceptable BER performance compared to the HDA.
