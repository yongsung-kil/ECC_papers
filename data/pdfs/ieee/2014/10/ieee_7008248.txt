IEEE/CIC ICCC 2014 Symposium on Communication and Control Theory
A Non-Linear LLR Approximation for LDPC Decoding over Impulsive Noise Channels

Yi Hou, Rongke Liu, Ling Zhao
School of Electronic and Information Engineering Beihang University Beijing, China
mokyy@ee.buaa.edu.cn, rongke_liu@buaa.edu.cn, zhaoling@ee.buaa.edu.cn

Abstract—The performance of Low-Density ParityCheck codes over symmetric alpha-stable impulsive noise channels is considered in this paper. Due to the lack of closed-form expressions for the probability density functions of almost all the alpha-stable distributions, an efficient non-linear approximation method for channel log-likelihood ratio is presented to achieve high decoding performance covering the range of characteristic exponent from 1 to 2 and avoid the use of complicated numerical integrations. The simulations for both long and short codes show that the parameter selection of the proposed method is not sensitive to the noise level. Even fixed parameter setting for the proposed non-linear approximation can obtain a satisfying decoding performance.
Keywords-LDPC codes; impulsive noise; symmetric alpha-stable distribution; non-linear approximation
I. INTRODUCTION
Gaussian distribution has always been used for representing the noise in communications according to the central limit theorem. However, in many channels, the observation noise exhibits impulsive characteristics, such as the co-channel interference in wireless networks [1], the high frequency ambient noise in shallow water communications [2] and the noise caused by switching transients in powerline networks [3]. Symmetric alphastable (SĮS) distributions have been suggested as a powerful tool for modeling impulsive noises under those above-mentioned circumstances.
For transmission reliability in communications, good channel codes such as Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes are adopted. Due to the characteristic of soft-decision decoding, the accuracy of channel loglikelihood ratios (LLRs) under impulsive noise is

crucial to the decoding performance. Unfortunately, the lack of closed-form expressions for probability density function (pdf) of SĮS distributions makes it difficult to calculate LLRs except for Gaussian and Cauchy cases. The numerical integration method [4] could be used for accurate calculation of LLRs, however, it is extremely time consuming. Several computationally efficient LLR approximation methods for LDPC decoders over impulsive noise channels have been proposed. Logistic penalty functions are applied in [5] to prevent the formation of overly strong priors in decoders under nonGaussian noise. [6] and [7] proposed a linear approximation method to calculate LLR values under alpha-stable noise by setting appropriate slope and threshold parameters. [6] also mentioned the Cauchy approximation as a good calculation method for LLRs when α ≠ 2 . A piecewise approximation method was presented in [8] to simplify the non-linear calculation of LLRs. However, Gaussian and Cauchy LLR approximation methods suffer performance degradation when α parameters deviate from their optimum values. Linear approximation, though much simpler, remains more than 1dB performance degradation as compared to the numerical integration method when α close to 1.
In this paper, an efficient non-linear LLR approximation method is proposed. The selection of parameters for approximation function is considered. According to actual conditions, the impulsive SĮS noise considered in this paper only for 1 ≤ α < 2 cases.

Since the noise characteristic exponent α is only determined by path loss exponent according to the environment [11], it is assumed static during the communication procedure and could be easily estimated at the receiver in this paper. Whereas the noise scale parameter γ is a function of both path loss exponent
and the fading/shadowing statistics, consequently, γ
varies over time and causes GSNR changing rapidly in communications. Fig. 2 and Fig. 3 present the variation of parameter a and b with GSNRs under certain value of noise characteristic exponent α . Both a and b change slightly over a large range of GSNRs. Therefore, it is possible for proposed non-linear LLR approximation method to exploit a more robust way to choose its parameters. Considering that waterfall region of BP decoder for most practical LDPC codes just covers small-scale SNRs, as a result, parameter a and b could be chosen only depend on the noise characteristic exponent α . This will simplify the calculation of approximated LLR values.
The non-linear function (10) in the LDPC decoder can be implemented efficiently through a fixed point pipeline divider in VLSI. Thanks to the recent advances in GPU technology, the proposed method can also be operated in a high-speed and high-precision floating point manner on GPU platform.

IV. SIMULATIONS
In this section, the performance of LDPC decoders that use the proposed non-linear LLR approximation method is evaluated by simulations. The simulation

results compare the linear approximation based on density evolution [7], the Cauchy approximation [6], the piecewise approximation [8], the proposed non-linear approximation and the numerical integration methods for LLR calculation in terms of BER and FER. Two LDPC codes of long and short lengths are chosen for the simulation, Code 1 is (8192, 4096) LDPC code from the CCSDS telemetry standard [12], code 2 is (576, 288) LDPC code from the WiMAX standard [13]. The channels are with SĮS noise and characteristic exponent values of α = 1.9, 1.5 and 1.2 . These values are set to
represent the channel environments under weak, moderate and strong impulsive behaviors respectively.
From the simulation results, it is observed that the performance of both the linear approximation and the Cauchy approximation degrade when impulsive behaviors of noise deviate from their optimal conditions. The performance gap between the linear approximation and the numerical integration method is almost 1dB for code 1 and 1.5dB for code 2 when α = 1.2 (Fig. 4(c) and Fig. 5(c)). For code 2, the linear approximation method suffers from performance loss at high Eb / N0 region even when the impulsive behaviors are very weak (Fig. 5(a)). That is mainly because the parameters P and H which determined by density evolution in [7] may not work well for short codeword length. The performance gap between the proposed method and the numerical integration method is less than 0.5 dB for all of these cases. The BER and FER of the proposed method are only slightly worse than the linear approximation method when channels are near Gaussian (Fig. 4(a)) and much better than the linear approximation when the impulsive noise becomes strong (Fig. 4(b), Fig. 4(c), Fig. 5(b) and Fig. 5(c)). The proposed one outperforms the Cauchy approximation method when the impulsive behaviors are not very strong (Fig. 4(a), Fig. 4(b), Fig. 5(a) and Fig. 5(b)). In all the simulation cases except very heavy impulsive noise condition (Fig. 4(c)), the performance of proposed method is slightly better than the piecewise approximation method. This is possibly due to that the connected region around the point between linear part and asymptotic part in the piecewise approximation function would cause performance degradation especially when the impulsive noise is weak. It should be noted that for both the Cauchy approximation method and the piecewise approximation method in this simulation, instantaneous noise scale parameter γ is assumed to be known exactly at the
decoder, whereas the proposed method uses only fixed parameters for all Eb / N0 conditions under certain α
values. Thus it can be seen that the parameter selection

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, an efficient non-linear LLR approximation for LDPC decoding over impulsive noise channels has been proposed. Simulation results of both long and short codeword length LDPC codes have

shown the proposed method outperform the linear approximation method when α = 1.2 and 1.5, and the Cauchy approximation method when α = 1.5 and 1.9.
The proposed method is slightly better than the piecewise approximation method in almost all the simulation cases. The performance gap between the proposed method and the complicated numerical integration method is less than 0.5 dB. Furthermore, the parameter selection of the proposed method is not sensitive to noise variation. Fixed parameters under certain α conditions could achieve satisfying performance. For practical applications, the proposed method can be implemented efficiently through pipeline dividers in VLSI, and its high performance is very suitable for future software defined radio receivers which work in impulsive noise environments.
