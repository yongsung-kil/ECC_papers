Low Density Parity Check Codes with Quasi-Cyclic Structure and Zigzag Pattern
Wenwen Li, Bin Chen, Jing Lei, Er-bao Li College of Electronic Science and Engineering
National University of Defense Technology Changsha, China
liwenwennudt@outlook.com, lierbency@126.com

ABSTRACT
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes with linear time encoding complexity and low hardware storage are a hot issue recently. We propose a general method for constructing LDPC codes with quasi-cyclic (QC) structure and improved zigzag pattern to obtain both linear time encoding and low hardware storage. We call it irregular repeat accumulate like (IRA-like) codes for its similarity with irregular repeat accumulate (IRA) codes. Through the optimization-constraint of QC-LDPC codes and the modification of dual diagonal structure we have acquired IRA-like codes with reduced number of low-weight codewords and short cycles. Extensive simulation results show that the IRA-like codes not only achieve better performance than some kinds of classical LDPC codes but also have much better practicability. Compared with the LDPC code applied in China Mobile Multimedia Broadcasting (CMMB), IRA-like code significantly outperforms the CMMB-LDPC code about 0.18dB at BER=10-5.
Index Terms— Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) structure, zigzag pattern, linear time encoding, irregular repeat accumulate like (IRA-like) codes

Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1] are linear block codes which have capacity approaching performance and linear time iterative decoding. But LDPC codes have an obvious disadvantage that their encoding procedures are complex owing to the lack of structure. The encoding complexity of LDPC codes generally scales as the square of their block length n. Many good LDPC codes are random generated by the means of computer [2, 3], and the construction with low encoding complexity remains to be a hot issue. Irregular repeat accumulate (IRA) codes [4] exploit the sparseness of parity check matrix H with a zigzag pattern, which indeed makes H an almost triangular [5] parity-check matrix facilitate linear time encoding. Performance comparison of IRA-like codes, PEGLDPC codes and other codes constructed in [11] are presented in Fig. 7 and Fig. 8. Their code rates and block lengths are all the same. Xiao-Yu Hu PEG-LDPC code, MacKay-LDPC code and Random-LDPC code in Fig. 7 and Fig. 8 have uniform VN degree of 3, i.e., dv=3. In order to maintain a good comparability between different codes, we select proper degree distribution for IRA-like codes to compensate the effect of zigzag pattern with almost degree 2 VN. And we should keep the parity check matrix H as sparse as possible. The VN degree distributions of IRA-like code in Fig. 7 and Fig. 8 are λ(x)=0.5x2+0.25x3+0.25x6 and λ(x)=0.5x2+0.5x4, where the only one VN in the zigzag pattern of degree 1 is omitted in the VN degree distribution expression.

5. CONCLUSIONS
The contribution of this paper is that a general method for constructing LDPC codes with QC structure and zigzag pattern to obtain both linear time encoding and low hardware storage has been proposed. It results in the reduction of low-weight code-words as more as possible while increasing the girth. IRA-like codes have the advantages of IRA codes, PEG-LDPC codes and QC structure. Simulation results show that good designed IRAlike codes can not only achieve better performance, but also have much lower encoding complexity.
