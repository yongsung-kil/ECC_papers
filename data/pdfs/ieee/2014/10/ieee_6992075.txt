On the Design of MLC-LDPC-OFDM in DRM MF and HF Channels

Yuan Liang Shulin Zhang Pinbiao Gu Lenan Wu
School of Information Science and Engineering Southeast University
Nanjing, 210096, China Email: 220120740@seu.edu.cn, slzhang@seu.edu.cn, 541272158@qq.com, wuln@seu.edu.cn

Abstract—Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing (OFDM), aided by proper channel coding schemes, can realize very dependable transmission in fading channels. For the purpose of improving the performance of the OFDM based DRM digital AM broadcasting in MF and HF channels, this paper designs MLC-LDPC-OFDM transmission scheme that is suitable for MF and HF channels. The equivalent channel capacities of MLC-LDPC-OFDM with two different decoding strategies, namely IMSD and PDL, are analyzed, basing on which this paper redesigns the code rates of MLC. In addition, the calculation of likelihood ratio in LDPC decoding is modified according to channel models. The simulation results show that the proposed schemes have better performance than the existing ones in MF and HF channels.
Keywords—OFDM; Digital Radio Mondiale; low density parity check codes; multilevel coding; equivalent channel capacity
I. INTRODUCTION
Among the wireless communication standards that employ Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing (OFDM), Digital Radio Mondiale (DRM) is one representative. DRM is one of the most prevailing digital broadcasting standards worldwide today. It employs OFDM plus Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM), combined with Multilevel Coding (MLC) plus Rate Compatible Punctured Convolutional (RCPC) codes, making DRM robust enough to resist interference and fading in all of the frequency bands below 30MHz [1].
Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes are recognized as one of the coding schemes which approach Shannon capacity mostly [2][3]. Many communication standards, such as DVBS2 and CMMB, employ LDPC codes on account of their flexible architectures and the fast parallel decoding algorithms. Because of the outstanding performance of LDPC codes, some scholars have replaced the RCPC in DRM with LDPC, improving the performance on bit error rate (BER) [4].
In order to enhance the performance of DRM in fading channels, this paper redesigns the code rates of MLC and modifies the decoding algorithm of LDPC according to the characteristics of MF and HF channels. The simulation results show that, compared with original DRM standard, the proposed scheme achieves a 1.5~2 dB gain at the same bit error level.

III. THE DESIGN OF MLC-LDPC
In an M-ary mapping scheme, the Euclidean distances between the subsets of digit 1 and 0, which are obtained by binary partitioning of the mapping symbols, are different at different partitioning levels. This results in different bit error rates at different bit levels. The idea of MLC is to encode the bits at different levels independently with different code rates in order to reduce the overall error bits [5]. Because LDPC codes can be constructed randomly by algorithms such as PEG [6], the MLC-LDPC has a more flexible code rates distribution than MLC-RCPC in DRM standard, making it possible to redesign the code rates according to the characteristics of channels. There are 3 different decoding strategies of MLC: Multistage Decoding (MSD), Iterative Multistage Decoding (IMSD) [7], and Parallel Decoding on Levels (PDL) [8]. DRM standard employs IMSD, which has the best performance among the three strategies. In IMSD, the decisions made by decoder at each level serve as a priori information for decoders at other levels, and such decoding process works iteratively. In MLC-LDPC, since LDPC decoding is based on soft information rather hard decisions, the exchanged information among the decoders are a posterior probabilities. In another decoding strategy, PDL, the decoder at each level only decodes once independently without any feedback from other levels, thus it has the highest efficiency. So this paper designs both the best IMSD and fastest PDL. The block diagram of MLC-LDPC is shown in Fig. 2.
Washsmann et al. [9] have introduced the capacity rule which assigns the code rate of each level equal to the equivalent channel capacity of that level. Take 8ASK, the one quadrature component in 64QAM scheme, for instance. Its model in AWGN channel can be represented as

R=S+N,

The equation indicates that the capacities of level 1 and 2 are based on the correct decisions of previous decoding stages. So the equation is only suitable for decoding strategies of IMSD and MSD other than PDL and we analyze the code rates of IMSD and PDL separately.

V. SIMULATIONS
This section verifies the BER performances of the MLCLDPC coding schemes with two different decoding strategies designed in this paper by simulation. In addition to the proposed schemes, the BER performances of other 2 schemes, namely the MLC-LDPC IMSD that still uses the code rates defined in the DRM standard and the existing MLC-RCPC IMSD, are also simulated and compared.
The simulation parameters are listed in TABLE I. Detailed parameters are defined by robust mode [1], and sync is ideal. The LDPC codes used are irregular binary codes constructed by PEG algorithm, and the decoding algorithm is BP with 30 times internal iterations. The simulations are performed in the MF channel, namely Rice with delay, and the HF channels, namely US Consortium, CCIR Poor, and channel no.5, which are all defined in DRM standards. The parameters of these channels are listed in TABLE II. In each path, the time variant attenuation can be generated by filtering WGN.
Fig. 12 displays the BER performances of the 4 schemes in MF channel. The proposed IMSD scheme achieves a 1.5 dB gain over the standard MLC-RCPC at BER level of 10-4, and

only has about one tenth error bits of the LDPC scheme using DRM code rates on the same SNR. The proposed PDL also achieves a 1 dB gain over MLC-RCPC but it is inferior to the IMSD scheme with DRM code rates and of course, to the IMSD proposed in MF channel.
The BER curves of the 4 schemes in HF channels are shown in Fig. 13~15. The proposed IMSD scheme achieves 2 dB gains over MLC-RCPC in US Consortium and CCIR Poor and a 1.5 dB gain in channel no.5. And compared with the LDPC scheme with DRM code rates, it also reduces half the error bits on the same SNR. Surprisingly, the PDL scheme proposed outperforms the IMSD with DRM code rates. This is because: (1) due to the relativity of time-variant channel attenuation in HF channel, most errors occur when the fading is severe, and the number of error bits, in most cases, exceeds the extent to which channel coding can correct. Without the correct decoding results from other levels, the gain achieved by IMSD is little. In this case, Gray mapping is more helpful in reducing error bits. (2) The equivalent channel capacity of each coding level varies a lot from AWGN channel, so the schemes designed for AWGN degrade in HF channel. In addition, since PDL has a simpler architecture and enjoys a fast and parallel decoding process, PDL is more preferable in HF channel.

VI. CONCLUSION
This paper designs the MLC-LDPC code rates in DRM according to the characteristics of MF and HF channels and modifies the calculation of likelihood ratio. The simulation results show that, compared with the existing scheme, the proposed one which has the same decoding complexity obtains an obvious reduction on BER, and the proposed one with a simpler decoding architecture also performs better in HF channel. Suitable schemes can be chosen in practice basing on the channel conditions, and this contributes to the application of DRM and other OFDM based transmission standards.
