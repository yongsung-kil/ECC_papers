The Improved Turbo-decoding Message-passing Algorithm and Corresponding Decoder for LDPC
Based on LTE
Lingling Lao1, Lixin Li2, Meng Zhu3 and Huisheng Zhang4
School of Electronics and Information Northwestern Polytechnical University
Xi'an, China E-mail: llling@mail.nwpu.edu.cn1 lilixin@nwpu.edu.cn2 smile1989zm@163.com3 zhanghuisheng@nwpu.edu.cn4

Abstract—The Turbo-decoding Message-passing (TDMP) algorithm of Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes has a faster convergence speed, lower hardware implementation complexity, and less storage units than the Belief-Passing (BP) algorithms. However, because of the integer quantization operations for initial information, restriction of storage units will cause the problem of decoding information overflowing. This paper gives the reason of overflow errors, proposes the improved decoding algorithm which can partially eliminate overflow errors, and designs the corresponding decoder which only requires some extra subtractors and shift operations. We analyze the computation complexity and compute decoder throughput. Finally, we simulate the error performances of the LDPC codes over the LTE channel, and simulation results show that the proposed algorithm effectively suppresses the overflow errors with a low decoder complexity and a high throughput.
Index Terms—Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC), Turbodecoding Message-passing (TDMP), decoder architecture
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1], for the high error correction performance close to the Shannon limit[2], are widely used in the next generation communication systems. Compared to Turbo codes, the decoding algorithm of LDPC codes is much simpler, with high throughput and powerful decoding performance, and the hardware design can be completely accomplished by parallel operations with high decoding speed. Turbo codes have been used as channel coding in the 3GPP-LTE standards. This paper works on LDPC codes in the 3GPP-LTE system. Many researchers study on defining the low complexity and high throughput encoder and the decoder architectures of LDPC codes. Some of them present a novel architecture that achieves a fair compromise in chip area and decoding speed to support LDPC and turbo decoding for different standards such as WiMAX, LTE, Wi-Fi, and DVBRCS. In paper [3], a quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) code is proposed, which makes it possible for lower complexity encoding implementation. With the QCLDPC code proposed, the partially parallel hardware structure

of QC-LDPC codes with quasi-cyclic characteristics is raised and widely used in high speed communication applications [45]. Partially parallel architectures, combining the serial and parallel processing operations, allow the tradeoff between the decoding speed and hardware resource consumptions.
On this basis, Mansour and Shanbhag presented the layered decoding (Turbo-decoding Message-passing, TDMP) algorithm of LDPC codes, and the corresponding decoder architectures [6-8]. Compared with the Belief-Passing (BP) algorithms, the TDMP algorithm has a faster convergence speed, lower hardware implementation complexity, and less storage units. However, because of quantization processing for initial information, the decoding performance of the algorithm is greatly reduced. To solve these problems, many layered decoding algorithms based on integer quantization were proposed [9-10]. But when the layered decoding algorithm for LDPC codes is performing integer quantization operation, restriction of storage units will cause the problem of decoding information overflowing, which can cause serious decoding errors. This paper analyses the reason, then proposes an improved decoding algorithm and corresponding decoder.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the TDMP algorithm based on uniform quantization, and analyses the reason for overflow errors in finite precision integer quantization operation, then presents the improved decoding algorithm to solve this problem. Section III proposes the corresponding decoder architecture in the 3GPP-LTE and presents complexity comparisons. The throughput and simulation results are given in Section IV. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we have recalled the TDMP algorithm of LDPC and analyzed the reason why quantization operations will cause overflow errors. We have presented the improved decoding algorithms, PEES and PEES-Modified, and designed the corresponding decoders. We have computed the decoding complexity and throughput of decoders. We have simulated the complete communication system in the FPGA-based prototype involved ASIPs over the LTE channel. The proposed algorithms avoid overflow errors and achieve the performance degradations which is acceptable with low computation complexity and high throughput. LDPC (756, 3, 9) codes achieve a throughput of 254.5 Mbps when operating at a clock frequency of 500 MHz.
