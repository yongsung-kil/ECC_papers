Abstract—In this paper, we propose a simplified architecture based on overlapped message passing (OMP) for QC-LDPC decoders with Normalized Minimum Sum (NMS) algorithm, aiming to reduce decoding latency with as less additional resources as possible. According to the OMP architecture, the two stages of NMS, namely check node process and variable node process, could be overlapped. Hence, overall decoding latency is reduced and hardware utilization efficiency (HUE) is improved. Based on the proposed OMP architecture, two irregular QC-LDPC decoders are implemented in serial and partly-parallel styles in FPGA respectively. Experimental results show that, achieving the same decoding performance, the serial QC-LDPC decoder with proposed OMP architecture can reduce latency by 39.1% compared with that of the decoder with TPMP(Two Stages Message Passing) architecture, and the latency reduction of partly-parallel decoder varies with degrees of parallelism.

ⅡFPGA IMPLEMENTATION AND RESULTS

We implement QC-LDPC decoders in XC5VLX110T chip of Xilinx, and set system clock100 MHz. Random codewords, coded and BPSK modulated by the transmitter (PC), are passed to the receiver through an Additive White Gaussian Noise channel. The receiver(FPGA board) quantizes received messages, and decodes them with decoder designed in this paper. The two QC-LDPC codes in TableⅠare employed to implement serial and partly-parallel decoders with NMS algorithm. To fully improve hardware utility efficiency and reduce more latency, pipelining is employed. We consider the pipelined access scheme like this: when the CNP unit writes updated messages of the mth row back to one port of the
dual-port RAM, messages for update of the (m 1)th row are

read from the other port, so does the VNP unit.

Ⅲ CONCLUSION
To reduce decoding latency of QC-LDPC codes in resource-limited circumstance, this paper proposes a simplified overlapped message passing architecture, analyzes the passing messages between VNP and CNP units and implements it on serial and partly-parallel decoder in FPGA. The experimental results show that, the serial decoder with simplified OMP architecture in this paper can reduce latency by 39.1% compared with that of the decoder with TPMP architecture, at the expense of 3 additional 36KBRAMs, and partly-parallel decoder with some parallelism degrees can reduce latency, such as decoder with parallelism of (2,4) can reduce 24.3% with almost the same amount of hardware resources.
