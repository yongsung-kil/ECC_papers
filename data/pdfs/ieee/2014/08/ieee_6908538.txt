Abstract-In this work, we investigate the problem of estimat­ ing time-varying noise distribution parameter on a factor graph. A new message passing scheme is proposed by incorporating
the variational Bayes (VB) into the belief propagation algorithm
for estimating of time-varying noise distribution parameter in a
low-density parity-check decoder. The scheme can also be used
for the estimation of the correlation noise model parameter in distributed video coding. A Bayesian estimator is used to estimate this parameter by obtaining its posterior distribution given the
channel output. The VB algorithm is employed to approximate
the complex form of the posterior distribution with a simple
distribution. Finally, this distribution is used to derive a c1osed­
form expression for the messages on the augmented factor graph
for online parameter estimation and decoding process at the same
time.
I. INTRODUCTION
In order to have the best performance for low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoder, the knowledge of the noise distribution parameter is essential [1]. This parameter is usually considered to be known before decoding process takes place. As the noise distribution parameter usually varies with time, getting the exact knowledge of this parameter is infeasible in reality. Even tough, this parameter can be obtained using a feedback channel or pilot signal under different channel states, taking advantage of channel state information can lead to communication overhead especially in a fast time-varying channel [2]. LDPC coding is also exploited in some other applications such as distributed video coding (DVC) [3]. In a DVC problem, a virtual channel is defined between the current frame in the encoder and an estimation of this frame (side information) in the decoder. In this channel, the distribution of difference between these two frames in the pixel domain or transform domain, called the correlation noise distribution, is usually considered to be Gaussian or Laplacian. An accurate estimation of correlation noise distribution parameter can lead to improvement in the LDPC decoding and consequently a better rate-distortion (RD) performance and coding efficiency for DVC [4]. It is a challenging problem, since the noise statistics varies within each frame and is non-stationary. Also, as there is no actual channel in DVC, most of the methods for estimating the noise parameter in a regular channel coding problem, e.g. using pilot signal, can not be used. In [5],

a parallel LDPC decoding is used to decode and estimate the correlation noise parameters on a factor graph. In this algorithm just one parameter is estimated for each DCT band. It means that non-stationary characteristic of the correlation noise is not taken into account. In [6] and [7], a particle filter based message passing algorithm for decoding and adaptive estimation of the correlation noise parameters have been pro­ posed for the DVC problem. As a stochastic method is used for message passing, a large number of iterations are required to converge and consequently it is very slow. In this paper, a new general mathematical framework and an algorithm are proposed based on variational Bayes (VB) to derive a closed­ form expression for messages on the augmented factor graph for online and joint estimation of noise parameter and decoding process simultaneously. To study the validity of the proposed algorithm, LDPC codes over an AWGN channel with time­ varying variance is considered. The simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can estimate time-varying noise parameter during the decoding process.

III. MESSAGE PASSING USING VARIATIONAL BAYES
Sampling or particle methods such as Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) are frequently used for the approximation of posterior distribution [7]. They are called stochastic approxi­ mation methods [8]. These methods have high computational costs. Also, their results vary after every run for the same data set. In contrast to the stochastic methods, deterministic methods are much faster with steady results. The main idea behind the deterministic methods is to find a distribution function q that is close to the true posterior as much as possible. Variational Bayes (VB) is a well-known deterministic

approximation method that imposes restriction on the distri­
bution function q and approximates it to the true posterior
distribution at the same time [9], [10]. The objective is to
find an approximation for the posterior distribution P(ZIX),
where X is the observation and Z consists of all the unknown
parameters and hidden variables. Log marginal probability
of X can be decomposed as LnP(X) = l(q) + KL(qllp), where l(q) is the lower bound and KL(qllp) is the Kullback­
Leibler divergence used to quantify the similarity between
the distributions p(ZIX) and q(Z). Then, q(Z) is found to
minimize the KL divergence. It is equivalent to maximizing
the lower bound l(q). In fact, we just try to find some simple
and tractable distributions that are flexible enough to pro­
vide a proper approximation to the true posterior distribution
P(ZIX). The distribution over all the elements Z, q(Z), can be factorized with respect to q(Zi), where Zi is a disjoint subset of Z for i= 1,2, ..., N.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a new algorithm based on variational Bayes to derive a closed-form expressions for the messages on the factor graph has been proposed. Then, joint estimation of time-varying noise distribution and LDPC decoding process has been carried out using the message passing algorithm on the factor graph. To study the validity of the proposed algorithm, LDPC codes over an AWGN channel with time­ varying variance has been examined. The simulation results have confirmed the expected performance of the proposed algorithm. In our future work, this algorithm will be applied for dynamic correlation noise parameter estimation in a DVC problem.
