Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes Based on the Improved Combining Method

Yuan-hua Liu, Mei-ling Zhang School of Telecommunication and Information Engineering
Xi’an University of Posts and Telecommunications Xian, China

Abstract—An improved combining method based on the Chinese Remainder Theorem is proposed for constructing much better quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes. By carefully permuting the block columns of the parity check matrices of the component codes, a lot of QC-LDPC codes with much less short cycles and better performance can be designed. Simulation results show that at a BER of 10−6, the constructed QC-LDPC codes outperform those designed by the existing combining methods by about 1.2 dB.
I. Introduction
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are considered as a class of codes with near-optimum performance under belief-propagation (BP) decoding, and a great deal of research effort has been expended in construction, structural analysis, encoding, decoding, performance analysis and applications of these codes. As an attractive class of LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes [1]- [2] are well suitable for certain practical applications since they have some major advantages over other types of LDPC codes in hardware implementation of encoding and decoding. Encoding of these codes can be efficiently implemented using simple shift-registers with linear complexity, and their QC-structure simplifies wire routing for message exchanges and allows partially parallel decoding which offers a trade-off between complexity and speed. Additionally, welldesigned QC-LDPC codes of moderate length can perform as well as or even better than random LDPC codes.
A combining method to design QC-LDPC codes based on Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT) was proposed in [3]. Since the cycles of short length may degrade the performance of LDPC codes, the letters [4] and [5] presented two modified combining methods to improve the performance by reducing the number of short cycles and enlarging the girth of the code, respectively. When the parity check matrices of the component codes are given, the generalized CRT (GCRT) combining method [4] eliminated a lot of 6-cycles by permuting the blockrows of the parity check matrices of component codes. It can be seen that the number of block-rows is very small if the column weight is small, and the optional permutations of block-rows are not enough to design good LDPC codes with small number of short cycles. In this letter, we propose an improved method to increase the optional permutations and construct better QCLDPC codes with much less short cycles. Simulation results show that the codes constructed with the proposed method have much less short cycles and perform much better than the original codes.

The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II presents some definitions related to QC-LDPC codes. The improved combining method is proposed in Section III, simulation results of several QC-LDPC codes designed by the improved method and the existing CRT methods are compared in Section IV. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.

III. Improved combining method
Let L1, L2, · · · , Ls be s relatively prime integers, i.e., gcd(Ll, Lk) = 1 for 1 ≤ l, k ≤ s (l k) and L = L1L2 · · · Ls. Note that if there exists 6-cycle in Hk for all 1 ≤ k ≤ s in the same position, H will have 6-cycle in that position [4]. It is known that the iterative decoding of LDPC codes converges to the optimal solution provided that the Tanner graph of the code is free of cycles. Since the existence of cycles prevents an exact error-probability analysis of iterative decoding procedures, and the shorter the cycles in the graph, the sooner the analysis breaks down. To improve the performance of iterative decoding, the GCRT combining method [4] eliminated a lot of 6-cycles by selecting proper block-row permutation of Hk. It can be seen that the number of block-rows is very small if the column weight of H is small, and the optional blockrow permutations of Hk are not enough to design good LDPC codes. Note that compared with the column weight of H, the row weight is much larger, i.e., the number of block-columns is larger than that of block-rows, and much more permutations can be utilized by permuting the block-columns of Hk. For

a QC-LDPC codes based on circulant permutation matrices with rate 0.5, the column weight of H is about half of the row weight. And if the code rate is 2/3, the column weight of H is about one third of the row weight. The higher the code rate, the lower the column weight of H, compared with the row weight. So we propose an improved CRT combining method, which can design better LDPC codes with much less short cycles by permuting the block-columns of the parity check matrices of component codes.
Let ρ be the row weight of H, (ρ!)s−1 QC-LDPC codes with girth of at least 6 can be constructed by permuting the block-columns of (s − 1) component parity check matrices and maintaining the parity check matrix of one component code unchanged. Since the cycles of small length will degrade the performance of LDPC codes, we can select one QC-LDPC code with smallest number of short cycles from these (ρ!)s−1 QC-LDPC codes. The number (ρ!)s−1 is very large if ρ and s are a little large, then we can choose a large positive integer T as a bound and construct T parity check matrices by randomly choosing T permutations from these (ρ!)s−1 permutations and select one with the smallest number of short cycles. Since the shorter the cycles, the larger the negative influence on the iterative decoding, we only need consider short cycles with length 6 and 8. The proper combination of permutations can be chosen as follows.
Firstly, count the number of 6-cycles for the T permutations, then count the number of 8-cycles for the permutations corresponding to the minimum 6-cycles and select the permutation corresponding to the minimum 8-cycles. If there exists more than one permutation corresponding to the minimum 8cycles, then select one randomly. In the following, we use the examples in [4] to illustrate the improved CRT combining method.
IV. Performance results
In this section, performances of several QC-LDPC codes designed using the improved CRT combining method and the existing CRT combining methods are compared over an AWGN channel with BPSK modulation. And the BP decoding algorithm is used with a maximum of 50 decoding iterations.
Choose the same parameters as the example in [4]: L1 = 7, L2 = 11, b1 = 1 and b2 = 6. C1 is an array code with girth 6 whose parity-check matrix H1 is a 3 × 7 array of 7 × 7 circulant permutation matrices and C2 is a shortened array code whose parity-check matrix H2 is a 3 × 7 array of 11 × 11 circulant permutation matrices. 7! = 5040 QC-LDPC codes with girth of at least 6 can be constructed by permuting the block-columns of H2, and a QC-LDPC code (539, 310) with girth 8 is constructed by utilizing the improved CRT method in about 3.5 seconds. Fig.1 shows the bit error rate (BER) performance of this constructed code. For comparison purpose, the BER performances of the codes designed by the GCRT [4] and CRT [3] methods are also shown. The code (539, 310) designed by the GCRT method has 154 6-cycles, and no 6cycles exist in the improved CRT code. Compared with the GCRT method, the improved CRT method eliminates 30800 8-cycles.

V. Conclusion
An improved combining method based on CRT for the design of QC-LDPC codes with less short cycles has been proposed. Simulation results show that, compared with the existing CRT combining methods, the proposed method can design QC-LDPC codes with much less short cycles and better performance.
