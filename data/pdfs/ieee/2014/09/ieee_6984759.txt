Construction Methods of S-QC-LDPC Based on 0-1 Programming Algorithm

Jun Shi
School of Electronic and Information Engineering, Xi’an Jiaotong University Xi’an, China Email:sj-shijun@163.com

Xia Wang, Yuanshuang Wang
School of Electronic and Information Engineering, Xi’an Jiaotong University Xi’an, China

Abstract—This paper analyzes the design of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) without length-4 circle firstly. Because of some limits in structures of QC-LDPC such as other rows or
columns will be determined with a given row or column and m
short circles will appear with a given short circle in the parity check matrix where m is the order of sub matrix, we deal with the problems with the proposed similar S-QC-LDPC (S-QC-LDPC) based on 0-1 programming. The main idea is looking for constraint conditions without length-4 circles. Moreover, in designing the S-QC-LDPC, we find its particular matrix reflecting the check matrix similar to circulant permutation matrix of QC-LDPC. The simulation results show the proposed S-QC-LDPC can significantly improve the BER performance compared with QC-LDPC.
Keywords—QC-LDPC;0-1 Programming; length-4 circle; SQC-LDPC
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density Parity-Check Codes (LDPC) [1], first discovered by Gallager in 1962, had not been paid much attention until Neal and Mackay found that LDPC had the same perfect performance as Turbo Codes [2]. Recently, LDPC are showing more and more advantages in reliable communication and digital check system.
In Belief Propagation (BP) decoding algorithm [3], the main factor affecting the performance is the length of circle in Tanner graph. The shorter the circle is, the worse the performance will be. Therefore, we need to avoid short circles in designing LDPC. As we know, structured and random construction methods are two primary methods to construct LDPC. Generally, random method is superior to structured method, but the former method will lead to more high encoding complexity and decoding difficulty than structured method. Hence, structured method is widely applied. Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) use circulant permutation matrices to construct check matrix without short circles by the appropriate choice [4]-[7]. [4] - [7] propose the unique method of designing QC-LDPC respectively, but they all have two limits in check matrix: one is that other rows and columns in sub matrix can be determined with a given row or column; the other is m short circles will emerge with a given circle (where m is the order of

sub matrix).
In this paper, construction methods of S-QC-LDPC based on 0-1 programming algorithm are proposed for solving the problems. The reason calling it S-QC-LDPC lies in its similarity to QC-LDPC. They are both based on sub matrices, even if there are some differences between the constitutions of basis matrices. Since the 0-1 programming algorithm can avoid a group of short circles, hence it’s more random than QCLDPC. So its performance is better than QC-LDPC under the same decoding algorithm.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews QC-LDPC and constructs LDPC without length-4 circle. The 0-1 programming is given in Section III. We elaborate how the algorithm is used in designing check matrix without length-4 circle. The performance of codes are compared and presented in Section IV, and conclusions are given in Section IV.

We call circulant matrix A “circulant permutation matrix” when its row and column weight are one [9].

In Fig.2, with the increase of signal-noise ratio (SNR) the performance of class I and class II become better than others. When BER 104 , compared with class III and QC-LDPC, class I and class II respectively acquire about 0.35dB and 1dB enhancements. The reason lies in that each row and column are relatively independent in S-QC-LDPC, increasing doubtlessly some bit information in BP decoding algorithm, thereby leading to better performance. We can also find with the increase of SNR, class II is gradually better than class I. That’s because class I may suffer the situation that some rows and columns are all 0, destroying the structure of check matrix. Class II keeps robust. In addition, the performance of the length-4 cyclic S-QC-LDPC is even better than QC-LDPC without length-4 circle.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, a new method of designing LDPC based on 01 programming algorithm is proposed. It solves the problems of QC-LDPC that as long as we know a row or a column, other rows or columns will be determined in a sub matrix and when

finding a length-4 circle, we also can make sure there are at least m length-4 circles. Simulation results show the performance of S-QC-LDPC in this paper is better than QCLDPC. It can be regarded as an alternative scheme of encoding in future wireless communications.
