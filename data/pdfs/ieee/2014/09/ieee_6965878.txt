Analysis of Decoding Failures of DVB-S2 LDPC Codes
J.-C. Sibel, M. Crussie`re and J.-F. He´lard UEB, IETR
INSA de Rennes, CNRS UMR 6164 Rennes, France
jean-christophe.sibel@insa-rennes.fr

Abstract—The DVB-S2 standard has been revealed a reliable standard for data transmission thanks to the LDPC codes. The waterfall of the bit-error rate is very abrupt to reach low values of order 10−10 in the error floor. In this paper, we propose a novel investigation on the iterative decoding of these codes by the Belief Propagation algorithm on Gaussian channels. Introducing definitions of persistent and created errors, we analyze them according to the degrees of the Tanner graph nodes and the noise power of the channel. We show that the two-degree bits used to accelerate the encoding process of the DVB-S2 are responsible for most decoding errors. This result could help design DVB-S2 codes in a more appropriate way, for instance avoiding the use of BCH codes to tackle the error floor.
Index Terms—DVB-S2, LDPC codes, iterative decoder, decoding failures.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes invented by Gallager [1] were revealed to be very powerful in hardware applications in the 90’s [2], such that they could compete with Turbo codes. The clear advantage of LDPC codes is the low computation complexity of their iterative decoding [3]. These days, well known standards use LDPC codes to make information robust against channels noise, e.g. the wireless connections Wi-fi, Wimax and the Digital Video Broadcast by Satellite of second generation (DVB-S2). In the DVB-S2 specifications [4], several LDPC codes of different rates have been designed to face various situations (climatic changes, SNR falls) given that they are structured on a common shape.
LDPC codes are usually decoded by the Pearl’s Belief Propagation (BP) algorithm [5]. An important aspect largely studied in the literature of DVB-S2 is the decoding complexity issue of BP. As the length of DVB-S2 codes reaches a large magnitude of 64800 bits, the BP decoder indeed introduces a non negligible computation delay. A strong effort has then been made to reduce this complexity by modifying the update equations of BP [6], and to accelerate the decoding process using dedicated architectures [7] and implementation devices as Graphics Processing Units (GPU) devices [8].
The BP decoder is also known to provide suboptimal performance in terms of Bit-Error Rate (BER) due to the presence of loopy structures, as the trapping sets [9], in the Tanner graph of the codes. The main evidence of this sub-optimality is the error floor, brought out by Richardson [10] for LDPC codes, that is an abrupt degradation of

the BER slope for high SNRs. A colossal effort has been made since this discovery to predict [11] and defeat the error floor according to several strategies: post-processing procedures [12], optimization of the degree distributions [13], construction of well-suited codes without trapping sets [14], design of iterative decoders [15] able to combat trapping sets such as multi-level decoders [16] or Generalized BP [17]. In the DVB-S2 standard, the error floor appears obvious for BER around 10−10, and to deal with it a post-processing is introduced in the form of a BCH code concatenated with the LDPC code [18]. As a consequence, the DVB-S2 standard satisfies the quasi error-free constraint defined as a frame error rate below 10−7 (i.e. a BER approximately below 10−11), but at the expense of an extra computation time. In [19] one of the first studies on the decoding issue of DVB-S2 codes is presented, indicating that the minimum distance dmin is too low to reach the quasi-error free without post-processing. Authors consequently propose a new design of codes that helps increasing dmin. However, computing dmin still remains a NP problem considering the optimal Maximum Likelihood Decoder (MLD). Other methods based on iterative decoders offer relevant approximations, as searching for near codewords in [20], but they still present a hard challenge for large LDPC codes.
In this paper, we propose a novel study on the decoding issue of DVB-S2 codes by focusing on specific experimental results of the BP decoder. By analyzing the connectivity property of the Tanner graph nodes of DVB-S2 codes, we design a protocol to explore the connections between the decoding failures and the different degrees of the bits. This method distinguishes between harmless and harmful bits depending on the noise power of the channel, exhibiting two kinds of errors: persistent and created errors. This describes the behavior of BP, which can then help construct typical error events of DVB-S2. The experimental results show that the design of DVB-S2 codes could then be improved concerning the parity bits added by a lower triangle stair-case matrix. In all our simulations, we consider an Additive White Gaussian Channel (AWGNC) with an 8-PSK modulation and the LDPC code of rate R = 3/4. To reach low BER values, we opt for the use of many cores GPUs (Nvidia GTX 660 and Nvidia GTX 670) that offer a massive parallel implementation of BP.

B. DVB-S2 codes
The DVB-S2 code of rate R = 3/4 is a strongly structured code of length N = 64800 and dimension K = 48600. The parity-check matrix H is shared into three parts, each one with bit nodes of a constant degree dv, where dv(i) = |Fi| for any Xi. IV. DECODING FAILURES OF THE BP
Here, we present experimental results about the BP algorithm used to decode DVB-S2 codes. We first have a look at the different kinds of decoding errors that BP may produce. We then study how they share between the different bit degrees.
A. BP forced LLRs mode
A good way to analyze the BP decoder is to submit it to extreme LLRs. In this way, we voluntary make wrong some bits and right the others, which allows us to observe strong reactions of the decoder.
We assume that the all-zero codeword was transmitted through the channel. The channel is accordingly harmless if all the LLRs are positive. The protocol consists in assigning

V. CONCLUSION
In this work, we have focused on the decoding errors of the DVB-S2 LDPC code R = 3/4. We have first brought up the strong links between bit nodes, indicating that twodegree and three-degree bit nodes were the most connected bit nodes. Afterwards, we have introduced a forced LLRs method to observe different origins in the decoding errors of the BP decoder. We have seen that persistent errors were more numerous than created errors, both being clearly dependent on low degree bit nodes.
We have then presented results about the influence of the bit degree on the decoding results and we observed that threedegree bit nodes dominate before the waterfall. It has appeared that inside the waterfall an exchange occurred between the two-degree bit nodes and three-degree bit nodes whereas the error floor region was completely prevailed by two-degree errors. This work shows that the stair-case matrix used to facilitate the encoding process is responsible for the degradation of the BER at high Es/N0, making the quasi-error free constraint unsatisfied without BCH codes.
Consequently, this work could be used to re-design DVB-S2 codes without the intervention of BCH codes, i.e. by avoiding two-degree bit nodes. In addition, the analysis of decoding errors can help build a decoding error model that maps to any Es/N0 a distribution of typical bit-errors.
