On The Construction of Frame Length Specific LDPC Codes For Next Generation Low Power Communication

Ashish Goswami Department of E&CE NIT Hamirpur, India 177005 Email: goswami.ashish@rediffmail.com

Rakesh Sharma Department of E&CE NIT Hamirpur, India 177005 Email: rakesh.kuk@gmail.com

Abstract—In this paper, a new method is proposed for constructing frame length specific LDPC codes which consume low power in encoding and decoding processes. A seed matrix is used and extended to form the parity check matrix having frame lengths divisible by 32 and 64. This method can be used to construct the LDPC codes with frame lengths divisible by even higher power of 2. The memory requirements for encoding and decoding processes of the proposed codes is discussed in this paper. For typical configuration the memory requirements to store the parity check matrix for the encoding and decoding processes are ≤ 1.04% and ≤ 33.33% respectively in comparison to the memory requirements in the traditional encoding and decoding processes. Hence consume low power in their encoding and decoding processes. The different configurations of the proposed codes are simulated and it is found that the codes give desirable error performance over AWGN channel.
I. INTRODUCTION
In todays scenario of low power Communication, the error correcting codes are not only designed to give near shannon limit performance but should be less complex, require low memory and less encoding and decoding processing power. Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are first proposed by Gallager [1] in 1963. Richardson et al. [2] constructed irregular LDPC codes that easily beat the best known turbo codes when the block length of the code is large. This shows that LDPC codes - originally invented by Gallager [1], forgotten for decades, rediscovered by many [3], [4] - can now outperform powerful turbo codes [5] if designed properly. Various methods [6] were developed since last three - four decades to design efficient LDPC codes so as to give near shannon limit performance. Chung et al. [7] developed improved algorithms to construct good low-density parity-check codes that approach the Shannon limit very closely.
In this paper, a new method is proposed that uses a seed matrix to construct the LDPC Codes. The paper is focused on two important constraints of designed LDPC codes i.e its frame (message) length and the memory requirement for their encoding and decoding processes. Less is the memory requirement less is the processing power consumption. The values of frame length that can be currently found in the CCSDS recommendations [8] are k = 1784; 3568; 7136; and 8920 bits. These classical frame length values are divisible by eight, but not by 16. Since the data frame is now usually generated by a microprocessor whose typical word length is

16, 32 or 64 bits, the frame length for the new codes should be divisible at least by 16, with possible extension to divisibility by 32 & 64. In accordance with these new requirements, the values of frame length 1024, 4096, 7136, and 16 384 bits have been considered [9], [10]. The values of code rates for the next generation channel codes are 1/2, 2/3, and 4/5 [9], [10]. The method proposed here is to construct the LDPC codes having frame length of the form 2s, where s be any positive integer preferably ≥ 6 such that the frame length is divisible by 64 or even some higher power of 2. The matrix extension method used here converts the seed matrix to a parity check matrix of size such that the frame length would be of the form 2s. This paper also discusses the memory requirements in encoding as well as decoding processes. The decoding Gallager Sum Product Algorithm (SPA) [6] is modified as per the proposed codes. It is shown that the memory requirement to store the parity check matrix for encoding and decoding processes for the designed codes are less. For typical codes, it is found that for the encoding the memory required is as less as 1.04% and for decoding process it is approx. 33.33%. Since the memory requirement are less, the code requires low power.
The frame length specific LDPC codes designed have desirable error performance and best in terms of he memory requirement. The paper is organized as follows. In Section II, the design for the construction of the parity check matrix of LDPC codes is proposed. In Section III, the encoder design is discussed. In Section IV, the decoding process is discussed, the Gallager Sum Product Algorithm (SPA) is modified for the the LDPC codes constructed from the proposed method. Section V, presents the simulation results and the performance of the simulated codes is analyzed. Section VI concludes the paper followed by references.

V. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
The various configurations of proposed codes are generated and analyzed for the BER performance on AWGN channel. The codes generated are of message lengths 1024, 4096 and 16384. All the simulations are performed in MATLAB. The arbitrary message was LDPC encoded, BPSK modulated and then passed through the AWGN channel. The received message was demodulated and decoded by Logarithmic Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA) [6] with maximum number of 20 iterations and a hard decision at the end. The process is repeated for range of SNR (0 − 6 dB) and averaged to large number of simulations. The BER verses Eb/N0 performance plot for LDPC codes generated from Type-I and Type-II are shown in Fig. 1. The codes prefix by t1 in the legends are Type-I codes while the codes prefix by t2 are Type-II codes. The suffix rXY shows the code rate r = X/Y . The figure shows that the constructed codes gave desirable performance on AWGN channel giving BER of order 10−5 for Eb/N0 < 6 dB for Type-II and Eb/N0 < 5 dB for Type-I codes. Again the performance will depend on the seed matrix taken whose extension can be used to design the codes. For Type-I codes, circulant matrices are used as seed matrices while in Type-II codes arbitrary sparse matrix free of cycle four are used. The code rates of the proposed codes are 2/3 & 4/5. In Fig. 1 the legend t1cir1024r23 means Type-I code having circular matrix as seed matrix with frame length 1024 and code rate is 2/3 while the legend t2sp1024l5r45 means Type-II code having sparse matrix as seed matrix with frame length 1024, level-5 extension and code rate is 4/5. VI. CONCLUSION
This paper has presented a new method for constructing LDPC codes. The paper also discussed two important constraints for designing the LDPC Codes, i.e. the frame length and the memory requirements in encoding and decoding processes. The constructed LDPC codes have message lengths

The method can also construct the LDPC codes with frame lengths divisible by higher power of 2. From the discussions in Section III and IV, it is clear that the memory requirements to store the parity check matrix for encoding and decoding processes are very less for the proposed codes as compared to the traditional encoding and decoding processes. For typical configuration, the encoding and decoding process memory requirements are ≤ 1.04% and ≤ 33.33% respectively to store the parity check matrix as compared to the traditional encoding and decoding processes and hence consume less power in their encoding and decoding processes. A number of codes have been simulated and it is found that the codes give desirable error performance over AWGN channel.
