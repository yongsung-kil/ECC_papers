Abstract—New magnetic recording technologies such as bitpatterned media recording (BPMR), heat (or microwave) assisted magnetic recording (HAMR) and shingled writing (SW)/twodimensional magnetic recording (TDMR) have been proposed to push the storage density of future hard disks beyond 1Tb/in2. However, at such high recording density, many new challenges from the magnetic recording medium, read/write head as well as writing and reading channels arise. These challenges hinder the realization of Tb/in2 hard disks and should be addressed. In this paper, we review these major challenges from a communication theory perspective, and propose ways to mitigate the channel impairments by using low-complexity two-dimensional (2D) channel detection, new low-density parity-check (LDPC) code design paradigm, as well as re-synchronizable coding schemes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Bit-patterned media recording (BPMR), heat (or microwave) assisted magnetic recording (HAMR) and shingled writing (SW)/two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR) have been primed to further increase the areal density of magnetic recording beyond 1Tb/in2 [1]. However, with the reduction of the track pitch, inter-track interference (ITI) becomes a major impairment for these magnetic recording systems. ITI combined with the conventional (down-track) inter-symbol interference (ISI) forms a two-dimensional (2D) interference which severely degrades the performance of data detection in these systems. Although the symbol-based Bahl-CockeJelinek-Raviv (BCJR) 2D channel detector [2] [3]- hereinafter called full 2D channel detector (equalizer) - can provide optimal detection over such 2D-ISI channels, it has prohibitive complexity for the typical 2D data size. Suboptimal 2D channel detectors with lower complexity have been proposed in [3]-[6], but their computational burden remains a bottleneck of the receiver. In [7], the well known suboptimal 2D channel detector, iterative row-column soft decision feedback algorithm (IRCSDFA) [5], has been further simplified using Gaussian approximation, the resultant simplified 2D detector is referred as “GA-IRCSDFA”. It suffers performance loss compared to the original IRCSDFA (as expected), but the loss is minimized by careful decoding-detection scheduling design.

Besides 2D channel detection (equalization), coding also provides a means to combat 2D-ISI. In [8], new low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes optimized for 2D-ISI channels have been designed, and are shown to achieve better performance than LDPC optimized for additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels. However, the LDPC codes in [8] were designed for use with the full 2D channel detector, which is highly complex. As the first contribution of this paper, we design new LDPC codes optimized for 2D-ISI channels detected using GA-IRCSDFA 2D detector instead of the highly complex full 2D channel detector. In so doing, we show that the performance loss incurred by the simplified 2D detector can be almost fully recovered by LDPC re-design, while retaining the benefit of low complexity in the 2D channel detector.
For BPMR, a different key challenge is about how to keep good synchronization between the write head and the magnetic islands on the fast-spinning disc during the writing process. Mis-synchronization due to random island position jitter, variations of magnetic switching field among BPMR magnetic dots, as well as accumulative frequency and/or phase drift caused by spindle speed variation and other mechanical vibration, may result in written-in errors [9]. These written-in errors include not only substitution errors, but also insertion and deletion errors, and greatly degrade the read channel performance. In the literature, papers investigating the written-in errors typically do not consider the 2D-ISI, and vice versa. As the second contribution of this paper, we present a new channel model incorporating both written-in (substitution, insertion, deletion) errors and ISI (W-ISI) to model the real BPMR channel more accurately. Then, the embedded marker code scheme (EMCS) [10] and a newly improved synchronization algorithm are used to correct errors over the W-ISI channels.

II. WRITING/READING CHANNELS AND RELATED MAJOR IMPAIRMENTS
A. Writing Channels
During the writing process, the write head generates the write-signal according to the encoded information bits and magnetizes the recording media with the generated writesignal. In this way, information bits are recorded on magnetic media. As SWR/TDMR uses the conventional recoding media, it has low written-in error rate. However, for HAMR and BPMR, its new write head design and new patterned recording media will introduce some extra written-in errors, thus, it is important to consider such written-in errors during the design of codes and detectors. In particular, for BPMR, the written-in errors include not only substitutions, but also insertions and deletions. Fig. 1 illustrates the insertion and deletion errors caused by the drift of writing clock phase, where T0 is the ideal writing clock while T is the real writing clock. If T < T0, insertion errors will occur, if T < T0, deletion errors will occur. Thus, the writing channel of a BPMR system can be modeled as a binary symmetric channel (BSC) concatenated with an insertion-deletion channel, which is shown in Fig. 2, where Ps is the writing-in substitution probability, Pi is the insertion probability, Pd is the deletion probability and Pt = 1 − Pi − Pd is the transmission probability.

IV. LDPC OPTIMIZATION WITH GA-IRCSDFA DETECTOR
Table I shows that the dominant complexity order of the GA-IRCSDFA channel detector is 2LM +2LN , which is a big improvement from 22LM LN for the original IRCSDFA and 2MLN for the optimal 2D detector. However, as expected, this complexity advantage comes with a cost, i.e. performance loss, see [7]. In this section, we show that the performance loss due to GA simplification can be almost fully recovered by re-designing the LDPC codes to match the 2D-ISI channel detected with the GA-IRCSDFA detector.
A. LDPC Optimization with GA-IRCSDFA Detector
The block diagram of a LDPC-coded 2D-ISI channel with joint detection and decoding is depicted in Fig.4, where the dashed box corresponds to the joint GA-IRCSDFA detector and LDPC decoding scheme. In this case, the iterationscheduling scheme used is the combined detector (CD) in [7]. The EXIT curve of the GA-IRCSDFA detector cannot be expressed in closed form, so we evaluate it by Monte Carlo

simulation, statistically averaged over a large number of GAIRCSDFA simulations.
Fig.5 gives the EXIT curves of the GA-IRCSDFA detector in a 2D-ISI channel with recording density of 4Tb/in2. We observe that all the curves resemble straight lines, therefore, they can well be approximated by a third order polynomial to ease the code optimization process [8]. Fig 5 also shows that the GA detector has a small mutual information (MI) loss compared to the optimal detector, it is this MI loss that we aim to recoup using LDPC re-design.
Following the code design procedure described in [8], the EXIT curves of the GA-IRCSDFA detector in Fig. 5 and the EXIT curves of sum-product decoder can be used to design new LDPC codes optimized for 2D-ISI channels. B. Simulation Results
In the following simulations, we compare the BER performance of the LDPC codes which are optimized for 2D-ISI channels based on different 2D detectors. In the simulations, we set the maximum number of iterations between the GAIRCSDFA detector and the LDPC belief-propagation (BP) decoder to 10, and the maximum number of BP iterations in LDPC decoder to 30, all codes have rate of 8/9. The number of iterations between two component detectors in GA-IRCSDFA detector is set to 3.
In Fig.6, we compare the BER performance of three combinations of code design and channel detector listed in Table II for three different code lengths in a 2D-ISI channel with recording density of 4Tb/in2, where the degree sequences of the ISI-optimized and AWGN-optimized LDPC codes are given in Table III.
Fig.6 shows that the GA-IRCSDFA detector of Scheme 2 (black dashed lines) has a performance degradation of about 0.55 dB over the optimal symbol-based BCJR algorithm of Scheme 1 (blue dashed lines) at BER of 10−5 for all three different code lengths (Nc = 4608, 9216, 13824) in a 2DISI channel with recording density of 4Tb/in2. This is the price to pay for using the low-complexity GA-IRCSDFA detector. Interestingly, this performance loss is almost fully recovered by the newly designed LDPC code (red solid lines). This illustrates that ISI-optimized LDPC code design for 2D-ISI magnetic recording channels is able to make up for the performance loss of a reduced-complexity 2D detector employed in a coded hard disk.

V. CODING AND DETECTION FOR CHANNELS WITH WRITTEN-IN ERRORS AND ISI
A. W-ISI Channel Model and Coding Scheme
In this section, we consider magnetic recording channels with written-in errors and 2D-ISI,, and assume that the read channels are equalized by a 2D equalizer to a 1D target. Thus, we obtain a W-ISI channel model which is shown in Fig. 7, where the detailed writing channel model is shown in Fig. 2 and characterised with parameters Ps, Pi, Pd and Pt. This proposed W-ISI channel model is closer to the real BPMR channels.
Because the EMCS [4] is flexible to adjust the error correction capability of outer LDPC codes and has better error performance than the conventional marker code scheme, we adopt the EMCS in this study. To perform ISI channel detection, although the joint MAP detection is optimal, its complexity is very high. Thus we use separate detection. The proposed coding and detection scheme is shown in Fig. 8. At the transmitter, the binary information sequence m appended with the binary pinning bit sequence p is first encoded into a binary sequence c by the outer LDPC encoder, then these pinning bits, which have pre-selected values and their positions in the transmitted sequence are known to the receiver, in the sequence c are re-arranged according to the parameters of the EMCS to form the transmitted binary sequence x. At the receiver, a BCJR detector is first used to perform channel detection, and then the inner decoder uses the soft output information r from the BCJR detector to correct synchronization errors and provides the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) v for the outer LDPC decoder, finally, the outer LDPC decoder uses both v and pinning bit information to correct the residual substitution errors. processing, we propose to perform channel detection using reduced-complexity 2D detector, and to recover the resultant performance loss by coding re-design. Based on modified EXIT charts which incorporate 2D channel detection, new LDPC codes have been optimized for 2D-ISI channels detected using low complexity GA-IRCSDFA detector. Monte Carlo simulations demonstrated that the new LDPC codes used in conjunction with the low-complexity GA-IRCSDFA detector can perform very close to AWGN-optimized LDPC codes used in conjunction with the highly-complex optimal 2D channel detector. Hence the system performance is maintained while the receiver complexity has been drastically reduced. Next, to deal with synchronization impairments such as insertion, deletion and written-in substitution errors encountered in BPMR systems which also suffer 2D-ISI, we proposed to employ EMCS for coding and the newly proposed SA-WSD for re-synchronization and detection. To our best knowledge, this is the first system capable of dealing with written-in substitution errors, insertion and deletion errors, as well as 2D-ISI simultaneously, while maintaining high code rates.
