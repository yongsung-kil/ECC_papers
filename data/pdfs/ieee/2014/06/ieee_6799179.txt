A Serial Layered Scheduling Algorithm for Factor Graph Equalization
Jianjun Zhang, Yiming Lei, Member, IEEE, Mingke Dong, and Ye Jin

AbstractŌĆöThis letter proposes a novel factor graph equalization based on a serial layered scheduling (SLS) algorithm of belief propagation, where the channel convolutional matrix is decomposed according to the distribution of its non-zero elements. In the SLS algorithm based factor graph equalization, factor nodes are classified into multiple layers referring to the decomposition of channel convolutional matrix, and the a posteriori probabilities message is updated based on a serial layer-by-layer mechanism. This proposed SLS algorithm clearly decreases the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) threshold and also increases the convergence speed of equalization process, which results in a higher reliability and a lower latency of equalization process. Compared with current parallel flooding scheduling (PFS) algorithm, the convergence speed of SLS algorithm increases almost by twice, and its SNR threshold reduces by 7 dB over the Proakis-C channel.
Index TermsŌĆöFactor graph equalization, serial layered scheduling (SLS), signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) threshold, convergence speed.
I. INTRODUCTION
E QUALIZATION has a clear influence on channel capacity and the latency of receiver signal processing. Iterative equalization shows excellent performance in eliminating intersymbol interferences (ISI) over multi-path channels [1], [2], where Turbo and factor graph techniques are frequently used iterative equalization [2], [3]. Compared with turbo equalization, factor graph equalization is welcomed for its much lower complexity of calculation procedure [4].
In the factor graph of equalization, an appropriate scheduling of belief propagation has to be determined for updating messages. A very general selection of scheduling is the parallel flooding scheduling (PFS) algorithm [5], where the messages are passed in each direction over all edges at each clock tick, [6]. While, PFS slows the belief propagation speed and increases the latency of equalization process, because the updated extrinsic messages cannot be used in current iteration [7]. Moreover, the extrinsic messages are passed to itself after two steps of equalization processes in PFS due to the short loops, and this results in an increased SNR threshold.

In this SLS algorithm, H is decomposed into several sub-matrices (Hi, 1 < i < M ), and the number of non-zero element in each column of the submatrices is no more than one. The factor nodes in factor graph are classified into multiple layers corresponding to Hi and the equalization factor-node process is serially performed layer by layer. Each layer contains all variable nodes, and each variable node is connected with one factor node only in each layer. The previous layer provides extrinsic messages to the next layer as priori information, and the priori information can be obtained through the updated a posteriori probability messages. All a posteriori probabilities messages of symbols are concurrently updated in each layer, and the updated messages then are immediately used by the next layer. The extrinsic messages are passed to itself after M steps of equalization factor-node operation, and this reduces the message autocorrelation and SNR threshold. Both PFS and this proposed SLS are examples of the sum-product algorithm (SPA), but, as addressed above, their scheduling mechanisms are different. As expected, SLS algorithm increases the channel capacity and also reduces the processing latency. Compared with current FPS algorithm, the convergence speed of SLS algorithm increases almost by twice, and its SNR threshold reduces by 7 dB over the Proakis-C channel. The principle of SLS algorithm can be also expanded for the iterative receiver of MIMO and OFDM systems [8], [9].
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. The system model and PFS are presented in Section II. Section III introduces the proposed SLS algorithm, and its performance is analyzed and validated in Section IV. Finally, the conclusion is given in Section V.

the factor nodes into several layers and serially updates the

messages layer by layer. As reflected by its logic, this proposed

SLS algorithm can increase the channel capacity and reduce

the equalization processing latency. As shown in simulation

experiments, the convergence speed of SLS algorithm is almost

twice as fast as the FPS is. The SLS algorithm also obtains
