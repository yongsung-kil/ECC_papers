Architectures for Polar BP Decoders using Folding

Bo Yuan and Keshab K. Parhi, Fellow, IEEE
Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering University of Minnesota, Twin Cities Minneapolis, MN, USA {yuan0103, parhi}@umn.edu

Abstract—Capacity-achieving polar codes have received significant attention in past few years. These codes can be decoded using either the successive-cancellation (SC) approach or the belief propagation (BP) approach. Several VLSI architectures of SC polar decoders have been reported in the literature. However, SC decoders suffer from long latency and low throughput due to their sequential decoding nature. On the other hand, although the BP decoders can be operated in an inherently parallel manner with high throughput, the functional units in these decoders are underutilized. In this paper, we exploit various architecture transformation techniques to further improve hardware performance of polar BP decoders. First, we propose an overlapped-scheduling approach at iteration level to reduce the overall decoding latency. Second, we propose codeword-level overlap to further improve hardware utilization efficiency. Third, we show that the above two overlapping approaches can be unified into a general framework into a joint overlapping approach. Fourth, we exploit the folding technique to design low-complexity polar BP decoders, and present two types of folded architectures. Synthesis results show that the proposed two (1024, 512) polar BP decoder designs can achieve 1.50 and 2.43 times reduction in hardware complexity, respectively. In addition, the proposed two designs can also achieve 7.4 and 2.5 times improvement in hardware efficiency, respectively.
Keywords—polar codes, VLSI, belief propagation (BP), overlapping, folding
I. INTRODUCTION
Polar codes [1] have become one of the most favorable forward error correction (FEC) codes in recent years due to their unique capacity-achieving property. With the ultra-regular coding structure [1] and potential beyond-LDPC error-correcting capability [2], polar codes could be the potential candidates for the FEC codes in the next generation communication and storage systems.
In general polar codes can be decoded with either successive cancellation (SC) algorithm [1] or belief propagation (BP) algorithm [3]. In [4-6], several VLSI designs of polar SC decoder have been reported. However, these SC decoders suffer from long latency and low throughput due to the serial nature of SC algorithm. On the other hand, polar BP decoders [7] have unique advantages with respect to parallel processing. Therefore, compared with their SC counterparts, the polar BP decoders are more attractive for high-speed applications. However, one of the main disadvantages of polar BP decoders is their poor decoding performance. In our prior work [8], we had proposed scaled minsum approximation to improve the error-correcting performance of BP decoders to be comparable to that of SC decoders. However, due to the underutilization of the functional units, the hardware performance of polar BP decoder in [8] is still less competitive.

In this paper we exploit several architecture transformation techniques to improve the hardware performance of polar BP decoders. First, we propose an overlapped-scheduling approach at iteration level that can reduce the overall decoding latency significantly. Second, we propose an overlapping strategy at codeword level to improve hardware utilization efficiency. Third, we combine the two overlapping approaches into a general framework. Fourth, we apply folding technique to develop lowcomplexity architectures. With the use of the proposed approaches, we develop two VLSI architectures of polar BP decoders. Synthesis results show that the proposed two polar BP decoders can achieve 1.50 and 2.43 times reduction in hardware complexity, respectively. In addition, the hardware efficiency is also improved by 7.4 and 2.5 times, respectively.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews polar codes and BP algorithm. The proposed optimization approaches are presented in Section III. Section IV presents and analyzes the hardware performance of proposed polar BP decoders. The conclusions are drawn in Section V.
II. REVIEW OF POLAR CODES
A. Polar Codes
Polar codes were proposed based on the phenomenon of channel polarization. As discovered in [1], with recursive encoding procedure, the post-decoding reliability of decoded bits can be polarized according to their locations at the codeword. As a result, a polar code can be constructed by assigning required information bits and “0” bits at the more reliable positions and more unreliable positions, respectively. In general, these information bits and “0” bits are referred as free bits and frozen bits, respectively. For more details of polar encoding procedure and how to determine reliable positions, the readers are referred to [1].
B. BP algorithm for polar codes decoding
As indicated in [3], similar to LDPC codes, polar codes can be decoded by applying belief propagation (BP) algorithm over their factor graphs. For an (n, k) polar code (n=2m), its factor graph is an m-stage network consisting of n*(m+1) nodes, where each node is associated with a right-to-left and a left-to-right likelihood message. During the whole BP decoding procedure, these messages are propagated and updated among adjacent nodes. Fig. 1(a) shows an example 3-stage factor graph for n=8 polar codes. Here each stage consists of n/2=4 processing elements (PEs) (see Fig. 1(b)), which are used to update the message that will be propagated to the nearby nodes.
In general the updates of the propagating messages are computed in logarithmic domain with min-sum approximation [7]. III. ARCHITECTURE OPTIMIZATIONS OF BP DECODERS

A. Low hardware utilization of original polar BP decoder

As discussed in Section II, the BP algorithm for (n, k) polar

code is processed over an m-stage factor graph (see Fig. 1(a)).

Therefore, a systolic (n, k) polar BP decoder can be developed in

a straightforward manner based on the corresponding factor graph.

Fig. 2 shows the overall architecture of such systolic polar BP

Considering m is usually larger than 10 for practical use, the latency can be reduced by approximately 10/2=5 times.

C. Overlapping at codeword level
Section III-B shows that iteration-level overlapped scheduling method can greatly improve the hardware utilization. However, the schedule in Fig. 4 cannot achieve 100% hardware utilization since some stages still remain idle. In general, due to the data dependency between successive iterations, the maximum hardware utilization rate of iteration-level overlapped scheme is limited to be less than 50%.
Different from iteration-level overlapping, codeword-level overlapping, as a common technique used in SC decoder designs [5], can achieve hardware utilization close to 100%. In this paper, we apply this technique for BP decoder optimization. Fig. 5 shows an example of a 4-level-codeword-overlapping scheme. Here different colors represent propagating messages belonging to different received codewords. Compared with the original scheme with iteration-level overlapping (see Fig. 3 and Fig. 4), the codeword-level overlapping scheme fully utilizes those idle stages to process multiple independent received codewords. As a result, the hardware utilization rate approaches 100%. In general, for an (n, k) polar BP decoder, maximum m independent received codewords can be input to the decoder for overlapped processing. As a result, the processing throughput increases by approximately m times at the expense of an extra m-1 clock cycles, and (m-1) times more memory for storing the codewords than that in Fig. 3.

E. Low-complexity folded architecture Section III-C and III-D present two methods that can increase
hardware utilization of polar BP decoders to 100%. However, both these methods need linear increase of memory. In this subsection, we propose to use folding technique to improve hardware utilization without increase of hardware.
Recall that in Fig. 3 m-1 stages remain idle in each cycle. Since the inner architecture of PEs in different stages is exactly the same, we can use folding technique [9] with fewer stages of PEs. Fig. 7 illustrates a folded decoding scheme with folding factor of 4. In cycles-2,6,10, the propagating messages that were previously processed by stage 2, are now routed to be processed by stage 1. Similarly, in cycles-3,7,11 and cycles-4,8,12, the propagating messages that were processed by stage 3 and stage 4 are now processed by stage 1 as well. As a result, the hardware utilization is 100% and the hardware complexity is reduced by a factor of 4. However, the penalty of this method is the extra routing network for routing the messages to the corresponding PEs.

IV. HARDWARE PERFORMANCE ANALYAIS Based on the approaches proposed in Section III, we present folded and interleaved hardware architectures of polar BP decoders. In addition, the targeted polar code is (1024, 512) and decoding algorithm is scaled min-sum with max_iter=60. The RTL model is developed by Verilog HDL with 7-bit quantization scheme and synthesized by Synopsis Design Complier with FreePDK 45nm CMOS standard cell library. Table 1 lists the implementation results of the proposed designs. It can be seen that compared with the original systolic design, the proposed folded iteration-level overlapping and folded architectures can achieve 1.50 and 2.43 times reduction in gate counts, respectively. In addition, because the folded iterationlevel overlapping architecture can achieve 4.68 times reduction in

latency, the proposed two designs can achieve 7.4 and 2.5 times increase in hardware efficiency, respectively.

V. CONCLUSION
This paper has presented several approaches to optimize the hardware efficiency of polar BP decoders. Synthesis results show that the proposed designs can achieve significant reduction in hardware complexity and improvement in hardware efficiency.
