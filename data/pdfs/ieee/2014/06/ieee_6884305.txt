Efficient Check-Node-Stopped LDPC Decoder Design

Tzu-Hsuan Huang
Department of Electrical Engineering Yuan Ze University
Jungli, Taiwan 32003, R.O.C.

Cheng-Hung Lin and Shu-Yen Lin
Department of Electrical Engineering and Innovation Center for Big Data and Digital Convergence Yuan Ze University Jungli, Taiwan 32003, R.O.C.

Abstract--In this paper, we propose a reduced check node operation design for the LDPC decoder. The proposed Check Node Stopping criterion (CNS) reduces the operations of convergent check nodes when the reliability of check node messages that depends on the magnitude of check node messages is larger than a threshold. Thus, the proposed LDPC decoder can can efficiently terminate the redundant check node calculations in the following iterations. From the simulations under the rate1/2 WiMAX LDPC decoding, the operation of check nodes can be reduced by about 12% at Eb/N0 of 3.6 dB with a small coding coding gain degradation. A 2.85mm2 LDPC decoder with CNS is implemented in a 90nm CMOS process. The area overhead of the the CNS is about 0.7% of the total area. The proposed LDPC decoder can decrease 4% power consumption for the stopped check node.
I. INTRODUCTION
The Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes, which are one kind of linear block codes that were proposed by Gallager in 1962 [1][2]. The LDPC codes have been proved to perform very closely to the Shannon limit and are capable of parallel implementation for high data rate [3]. As a result, LDPC codes have been adopted by many recent communication standards, such as digital video broadcasting (DVB-S2), IEEE 802.11n, and IEEE 802.16e.
In [3], LDPC codes are defined as a very sparse m×n parity-check matrix H, where most elements are 0’s and only sparse elements are 1’s. LDPC codes are prevalently decoded by a two-phase message passing algorithm [3] that decodes a codeword v by updating the message between check nodes and variable nodes. The LDPC codeword can be iteratively decoded to reach the better decoding performance. There are two ways used in LDPC decoders to determine the decoding terminations. One is to check the parity check equation (PCE) H ⋅ vT until it equals to zero and the other presets a maximum number of decoding iterations. In each iteration, a large number of messages transmitted between check nodes and variable nodes with complicated operations performed in every node. Due to this characteristic, the LDPC decoding process will consume a large amount of computations. Many techniques are introduced to lower the complexity of LDPC

In this paper, we propose a LDPC decoder with a check node stopping criterion (CNS). If a convergent, check node message is detected by the CNS, the operations can be stopped in the following iterations. The CNS efficiently reduces the power of LDPC decoder with a negligible area overhead and a small BER degradation.

II. PROPOSED ALGORITHM
In LDPC decoding, the decoding process exchanging messages between check nodes and variable nodes needs a lot of memory and complex calculations. Besides, the check node updating is the most complex computational part of the LDPC decoding process. The magnitudes of the check node message will be higher as iterations increases. According to the simulations, the hard decisions become stable with a high absolute value of check node message as iteration increases.
