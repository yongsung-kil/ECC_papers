A Low Power and Ultra High Reliability LDPC Error Correction Engine with 
Digital Signal Processing for Embedded NAND Flash Controller in 40nm COMS 
Wei Linl,2, Shao-Wei Yenl, Yu-Cheng HSUI, 2, Yu-Hsiang Linl, Li-Chun Liangl, Tien-Ching Wang I , 
Pei-Yu Shihl, Kuo-Hsin Lail, Kuo-Yi Chengl, and Chun-Yen Chang2

Abstract 
A multi-mode Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) error 
correction engine with a Digital Signal Processing (DSP) 
module is presented for low power and ultra high reliability 
NAND Flash memory controllers. The DSP module improves 
the reliability of the storage systems via calculating the 
adaptive reliability information and translating the information 
into Log-Likelihood Ratio (LLR) for soft bit decoding. 
According to the experiment results on sub-20nm Triple Level 
per Cell (TLC) NAND Flash memory, the retention ability of 
LDPC with DSP is a 20 times improvement over BCH code 
and 2 to 5 times improvement over conventional LDPC. 
Moreover, the proposed decoder reaches a throughput over 
400MB/s as well as a power conswnption of 21.8m Wunder 
40nm CMOS technology at 45 bit errors. (keywords: LDPC, 
DSP and NAND Flash Controller) 
Introduction 
LDPC codes have attracted more and more attention due to 
its powerful correction capability, as compared to BCH [1] and 
RS code, for the degraded reliability of NAND Flash memory 
[2]. To fully exploit the maximal correction capability of 
LDPC, accurate reliability information for decoder is required 
in the form of LLRs. According to the conventional decoding 
flow of LDPC code as shown in Fig.l (a), a limited number of 
LLR tables are provided by NAND Flash vendor to fit the 
actual reliability information. However, each LLR table is 
given under an assumption of a channel status, which varies 
with the amount of use of the NAND Flash. Hence, the 
optimized level tracking is carried out to re-extract the soft 
information with minimal error probability in order to fit the 
limited number of LLR tables. Nevertheless, a large amount of 
read operations is required for optimize level tracking and 
re-extracting the soft information, which results in intensive 
read disturb and a degraded throughput. A novel decoding flow 
and DSP module are presented to solve the above mentioned 
problems and improve the correction capability of the LDPC 
engine. 
Hardware Architecture 
Figure 2 and table 1 show the architecture and the supported 
mode of the proposed LDPC engine with DSP module. It 
consists of three novel modules: the Dynamic Information 
Scaling (DIS), LDPC decoder with low power engine, and the 
DSP. DIS characterizes the high reliable error based on the

According to the estimated information, DIS translates the 
hard bit into the corresponding pseudo soft bit information. Fig. 
3 shows that DIS can increase the convergence rate of the 
decoder and further enhance the decoding throughput by up to 
53% as compared to pure hard bit information. In the LDPC 
decoder, the low power engine can be activated to correct 
within 60 errors while most of the module can be shut off to 
reduce power consumption. As shown in Figure 4, only

under 40nm CMOS technology while 45 error bits are inserted. 
At the algorithm level, the scheduling decoding algorithm is 
used to reduce the nwnber of iterations by half. At the 
architecture level, the check node unit and variable node unit 
adopt partial-parallel technique to reach an optimwn between 
throughput and area. In addition, the computation unit can be 
partially gated depending on different LDPC modes to 
decrease power consumption. The performance of presented 
LDPC engine is presented in Fig. 5. The hard bit decoding 
capability of the LDPC engine is comparable to BCH code, 
and the soft decoding is capable of correcting twice than BCH 
code. The DSP module is employed to optimize the decoding 
flow and improve the correction strength of the soft bit 
decoding. The soft information from NAND Flash memory is 
re-constructed and translated into channel information. This is 
followed by on-the-fly noise cancellation and reliability 
information scaling with the DSP module. Consequently, the 
adaptive LLR table is calculated by the DSP module for a 
superior correction capability. As illustrated in Fig. 1 (b), the 
optimized level tracking and the LLR retry can be omitted and 
the extra latency and read disturb can be eliminated in the 
proposed decoding flow.
