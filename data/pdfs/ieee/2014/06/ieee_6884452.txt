Abstract— This paper presents a low power and area-efficient check node operation circuit for LDPC decoders based on MinSum algorithm. By improving a heavily used comparator circuit, our proposed check node unit reduces area and power consumption by 8% and 13%, respectively, without decoding speed degradation compared to conventional LDPC decoders.

I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are one of the most promising error correction codes because of their near capacity performance and parallel nature of iterative decoding algorithm [1]. These codes have been widely adopted in many applications, such as communication and storage systems [2-4]. In these applications, target data rates range from a few hundred megabits even to gigabits per second, and achieving such high decoding speeds require a number of computation units which significantly increase hardware costs and power consumption of the decoder. Among various LDPC decoding algorithms, Min-Sum algorithm [5] and improved versions of this algorithm [6] are commonly employed in LDPC decoders due to their simplicity. In this paper, a low power and areaefficient check node operation design for LDPC decoders based on the Min-Sum algorithm is proposed.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. We explain a common structure of LDPC decoders, and analyze the structure to show which part of the implementation is important. Next, we address the proposed design. Experimental results and conclusion will follow.
A. Implementation of LDPC Decoders
LDPC decoding process consists of three operations: variable (bit) node operation, check node operation, and parity check operation. Many researchers have proposed various LDPC decoders to enhance the decoding speed with moderate increase in hardware costs. II. AN IMPROVED COMPARATOR FOR CHECK NODE UNIT The check node units were implemented based on [7]. (2k+1-3) comparators for k-inputs to find first two minimum values are needed. There are several ways to implement the comparator which compares the magnitude of two inputs.
Fig. 2. Magnitude comparators; (a) mux-based (b) 2 bit-wise (c) TTL-7485 (d) proposed.
In this paper, three conventional magnitude comparators were implemented, and the proposed design was compared with them. The gate-level circuits in Fig. 2 describe just two-bit magnitude comparators to show structural differences of each design, and these circuits are extended to a seven bit or a four bit magnitude comparator. The circuits (a) and (b) in Fig. 2 are the comparators included in the cell libraries for FPGA designs, and (c) is a TTL-7485 magnitude comparator circuit. The “out” signals in the original versions of (a), (b), and (c) are ‘greater than’, ‘less than’, and ‘equal’ signals. However, the minimum finding circuit does not require all the three signals. Thus we modified all comparators to generate only the ‘greater than’ signal in this paper. Using this modification, we reduced the area of each comparator on average by 41%.
III. EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS Table II shows the synthesis results of each implementation using Synopsys Design Compiler with a 180-nm standard cell library. The proposed design occupies the smallest cell area. Compared to (a), the proposed comparator with a seven bit precision is smaller by 28% and dissipates less power by 38.5%. The latency of the proposed design is reduced by 24.7% compared to (c). With a four bit precision implementation, the proposed circuit is still advantageous over the others. IV. CONCLUSION
This paper presents a low power and area-efficient check node operation unit for LDPC decoders based on Min-Sum algorithm. From analysis of conventional LDPC decoders, we learned that the check node unit is the most crucial in the LDPC decoder. To improve the check node unit, we proposed a magnitude comparator circuit which reduces area, power, and latency up to by 28%, 38.5%, and 24.7% respectively compared to conventional comparators. The synthesis results after adopting the proposed circuit to a check node unit show that our proposed magnitude comparator circuit is the most suitable for area-efficient, high throughput, and low power LDPC decoders.
