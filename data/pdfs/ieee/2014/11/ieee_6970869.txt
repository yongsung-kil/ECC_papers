Abstract—From Sidon sequence over ZP , two classes of type-1 (J = 3, L) QC-LDPC codes are explicitly proposed with block length L2P and with girth at least ten. For the first method, any Sidon sequence over ZP (P odd) with cardinality no smaller than L (L odd) corresponds to a class of type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes with girth at least ten. For the second one, any Sidon sequence over ZP (P prime) with cardinality no smaller than L (L arbitrary) corresponds to a class of type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes with girth at least ten. Compared with the method from 3D lattices, which can also generate type-1 QC-LDPC codes with girth at least ten, the main advantage of the new methods is that the construction process is much simpler, and L is not necessarily a prime integer. Simulation results show that the new type-1 QCLDPC codes outperform the girth-8 QC-LDPC codes constructed by Sidon sequence or the earliest sequence, and perform almost as well as Bocharova’s shortest girth-12 QC-LDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with large girth have recently attracted an increasing attention [1-12], since they have low encoding complexity and generally outperform random LDPC codes for short to moderate block lengths. A (J, L) LDPC code is defined by a parity-check matrix (PCM) in which each column has weight J and each row has weight L. Let Gg+ denote a girth at least g and Gg a girth exactly g. Strictly speaking, QC-LDPC codes are a type of LDPC codes whose parity-check matrices consist of circulant permutation matrices (CPMs) of the same size. If a parity-check matrix is an array of CPMs and zero matrices (ZMs), then it corresponds to the so-called type-1 QC-LDPC code. Compared with (J, L) QC-LDPC codes, type-1 (J, L) QC-LDPC codes usually have a larger upper bound in terms of the minimum distance [10], which enable a larger minimum distance for a well-designed code.
For G10+ (type-1) QC-LDPC codes, most of the methods available [1-4] are based on combination of some algebraic techniques and computer search methods. In contrast to these semi-random methods, Tanner’s G12 (3, 5) QC-LDPC codes [5] and Zhang’s G10+ type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes from 3-D lattices [6], are explicit in the sense that no computer search is needed in the construction procedure. However, in the two methods, the row weight of L must be 5 and a prime integer, respectively. In this paper, based on Sidon sequences [12-15], we present two new constructions for G10+ type-1

(3, L) QC-LDPC codes, where L is an arbitrary odd integer for the first method, and an arbitrary integer for the second one.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces Sidon sequences and a type of PCM with a special structure. Sections III and IV present two classes of G10+ type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes. Numerical results are given in Section V. Section VI concludes the paper.
II. PRELIMINARY
A. Sidon Sequence and Its Construction
Let P be a positive integer. Denote the set {0, 1, 2, · · · , P − 1} by ZP .
Definition 1 [12,13]: A Sidon sequence over ZP is a sequence of CS nonnegative integers {s0, s1, · · · , sCS−1} with the property that the sums si +sj(i ≤ j) (mod P ) are distinct.
It should be noted that, Sidon sequences over ZP and modular Golomb rulers (mod P ) [13,14] are equivalent in mathematics. Throughout this paper, we use the term, Sidon sequences over ZP .
The cardinality of a Sidon sequence over ZP , CS, is upper bounded by the inequality: CS(CS − 1) + 1 ≤ P [12,13]. For P = q2 + q + 1, P = q2 − 1 or P = p2 − p (p prime and q a prime power), Sidon sequences over ZP with maximum cardinality (q + 1, q and p − 1) can be obtained by Singer construction, Bose construction and Rusza construction, respectively [14]. For other values of P distinct from the above three forms, one can use computer-aided methods, such as the method in [12], to search for the qualified Sidon sequences. For the case of P = q2 + q + 1, some Sidon sequences over ZP obtained by Singer construction are tabulated in [15].
Remark 1: Sidon sequences are first used to construct G12 (2, L) QC-LDPC codes in [7]. Later, Sidon sequences are further employed to construct G8+ (3, L) QC-LDPC codes in [12]. In this paper, two explicit constructions for G10+ type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes are successfully developed based on Sidon sequences.

Based on these Sidon sequences, three G10 type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes are constructed with length L2P , using Construction II. For comparison purpose, the three shortest G12 QC-LDPC codes with L = 4, 5 and 6 in [2] are selected. Their block lengths are of the form LP , where P = 73, 163 and 306, respectively.
Example 4: Let L = 6. From Table 1 in [15], {0, 1, 3, 8, 12, 18} is selected as the Sidon sequence over Z31. VI. CONCLUSION
Two classes of G10+ type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes are explicitly proposed based on Sidon sequence over ZP . Given any Sidon sequence over ZP (P odd) with cardinality no smaller than L (L odd), the first method algebraically generate a class of G10+ type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes. Given any Sidon sequence over ZP (P prime) with cardinality no smaller than L (L arbitrary), the second method also algebraically generate a class of G10+ type-1 (3, L) QC-LDPC codes. The main advantage of the new methods over Zhang’s method based on 3 − D lattices, is its simple construction and flexible choice of row weight L. Numerical simulation results show that the new type-1 QC-LDPC codes perform very well for

moderate code rates and moderate block lengths. The further work includes: (1) doing more analysis and
simulations to show that the new codes work well even in
the error floor region, (2) comparing the codes constructed based on Constructions I and II (for L odd and P prime),
(3) simulating the performance of the new codes with high
rate, (4) considering whether there is a chance with the new constructions to obtain G12+ type-1 QC-LDPC codes, and (5) comparing the CPM size P of the new constructions with the
bound in [8].
