A 1-100Mb/s 0.5-9.9mW LDPC Convolutional Code

Decoder for Body Area Network

Chih-Lung Chen, Sheng-Jhan Wu, Hsie-Chia Chang, Chen-Yi Lee Department of Electronics Engineering and Institute of Electronics National Chiao Tung University, Hsinchu, Taiwan Email: {lung, sjwu, hcchang, cylee}@si2lab.org

Abstract—A low power LDPC convolutional code decoder is implemented in 90nm CMOS technology. The proposal demonstrates a novel FEC candidate based on shift/shared memory architecture for the IEEE 802.15.4g and 802.15.6 body area network applications. Measurement shows the decoder achieves (1) 1∼100Mb/s with power consumption of 0.5∼9.9mW under 0.6V supply voltage (2) better error correcting performance compared with Viterbi decoder under same silicon area.

I. INTRODUCTION
For the data transmission quality, the forward error correction (FEC) scheme emerged from the communication system is a necessity. Some applications require the superior performance; while the others may consider the power consumption as the most critical issue such as wearable devices and near body area communications. The standards of low-rate wireless personal area networks (LR-WPANs) and the wireless body area network (WBAN), defined in IEEE 802.15.4g [1] and 802.15.6 [2] respectively, are two examples. Conventionally, in the lower power usage scenarios, the convolutional codes decoded by Viterbi algorithm are considered as the first choice for FEC. In this paper, a novel FEC candidate, namely lowdensity parity-check convolutional code (LDPC-CC) [3] is presented to show competitive features on both error correcting capability and low power consumption.
To demonstrate the promising potential for low power, a (23,3,6) code-rate 0.5 LDPC-CC is designed and implemented, where 23 is the memory length, and 3 and 6 are the column weight and row weight of the parity check matrix of the LDPCCC. This chip features: 1) competitive performance with convolutional codes, 2) multiple power islands separated by voltage level shifters for aggressive power scaling technique, 3) shared memory among decoding kernels to reduce the amount of the memory banks, 4) 5-stage pipeline to preserve additional timing margin for voltage scaling, and 5) adaptive channel value addressing with permutation network to save 25% storage requirement.
The chip is realized in UMC 90nm 1P9M CMOS technology. From measurement result, the maximum clock frequency is 285MHz, and the corresponding data rate is 285Mb/s. The energy efficiency 19.8pJ/bit/proc is better than state-of-theart chip designs of LDPC-CC decoder and Viterbi decoder. And the decoder power 9.9mW measured at 0.6v supply voltage and 100Mb/s data rate can satisfy the system link budget for body area network. The remainder of this article is organized as follows. Section II reviews the performance requirements of FEC design in system perspective. In Section III, the proposed code construction of LDPC-CC as well as its decoder architecture are presented. II. SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS
Body area network (BAN), which introduces the applications such as medical health monitoring and near field multimedia data transmission, has attracted lots of attentions recently [4]–[6]. Among various emerging transmission techniques, body channel communication (BCC) has the properties of high conductivity, low external RF interface, and low antenna size, making BCC an adorable approach in BAN and body sensor network (BSN) applications [7]. According to the frame error rates of state-of-the-art transceivers reported in [5], [6], the BER is about 10−2 at SNR 10dB under QPSK modulation. However, if the data rate requirement is elevated to more than 100Mb/s, higher order modulation such as 64QAM will be adopted, leading to the lowered raw BER about 10−1 ∼ 10−2. In order to guarantee the transmission quality, the FEC scheme should be able to suppress the decoded BER to be lower than 10−4.
On the other hand, power consumption is another critical constraint for BCC system. Since the BCC system is proposed for application of near human body data transmission and the health care monitoring, the portable devices might be implanted within human body for the intensive medical care, or be carried by user to control the heart rate, oxygen content within blood, and lactic acid within muscle to perform a generic safe exercise. Hence, the designed codec of BCC system as well as FEC scheme must possess the low power capability and, moreover, by the link budget, the designed FEC decoder must consume power smaller than 10mW at the data rate 100Mb/s and be capable of suppressing the error rate from

For these reasons, the low power LDPC-CC codec is imperative for BAN applications. Fig. 1 demonstrates one of the possible application scenarios for the proposed LDPCCC decoder. And for the ultra-low power mode, sub-mW power consumption is demanded when the data-rate is 1Mb/s.

In the beginning, the CN to VN log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) are read from the memory banks MEM-I1∼MEM-I3 and MEM-P1∼MEM-P3 for information and parities, respectively. Through the inter-processor permutation network, these LLRs are distributed into the corresponding processors. Then VN units (VNUs) within each processor sum up the LLRs to generate the VN to CN messages. After that, the VN units and CN units will perform the well-known normalized minsum decoding algorithm with the sorted out min and secmin LLRs. One pipelining stage is inserted inside CNU to balance the critical path. Finally, the updated messages are reversely permuted and stored back to the memories for the next decoding iteration. Due to the permutation network, the out-of-date LLRs are discarded, resulting in a storage reduction by 25%.

IV. IMPLEMENTATION RESULTS
Fabricated in 90nm 1P9M CMOS process, our test chip integrates the OVA scheduling with adaptive memories access addressing, inter-processor permutation network for propagating the LLRs, and 5 stages pipelining. Key features of test chip and performance comparison are given in Table I. The decoder occupies 0.46mm2 area with 12.96Kb two-port SRAMs. Measurement results show that the decoder draws 0.5mW∼9.9mW under 0.6V supply voltage when operating at 1∼100MHz to achieve the information data rate 1∼100Mb/s.
Fig. 7 shows the measurement result and the power consumption of the proposed chip. The Shmoo plot in Fig. 7(a) shows that at 100MHz the supply voltage is scaled down to 0.6V, and the maximum frequency is 285MHz. And for the power and data rate comparison histogram in Fig. 7(b), the group 1 is measured at the full speed and 0.85V. Group 2 and group 3 are measured at the data rate 100Mb/s and 1Mb/s, where the supply voltage is scaled down to 0.6V, and the resulting powers are only 9.9mW and 0.5mW, respectively. And the line chart in Fig. 7(c) shows the designed chip is proposed for serving the low to high rate applications according to the scenarios with conservative power consumption.

V. CONCLUSION
A low power LDPC-CC codec is proposed for body channel communication of BAN application. The BCC requires the performance of the FEC to be BER=10−4 under 64QAM at SNR=10dB, and the decoder power smaller than 10mW when the data rate is 100Mb/s. A (23,3,6) LDPC-CC is specifically designed to satisfy the performance requirement. An aggressive power scaling by multiple power islands is presented for power saving. From measurement results, the chip realized in UMC-90nm 1P9M CMOS technology can be operated at the maximum clock frequency 285MHz, and the corresponding data rate is 285Mb/s. And the decoder power 9.9mW measured at supply voltage 0.6v data rate 100Mb/s satisfies the BCC system link budget, where the energy efficiency 19.8pJ/bit/proc is better than state-of-the-art LDPC-CC and convolutional code Viterbi decoder chip designs.
