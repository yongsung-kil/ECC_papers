Iterative Receiver for Qc-LDPC Coded Underwater Acoustic Communication Systems

Liang Zhao* * Xi’an Research Institute of Navigation Technology, Xi’an, P.R.China
xdyjs204@hotmail.com

Abstract - Quasi Cyclic-Low Density Parity Check (QCLDPC) codes are easy to construct and provide the considerable coding gain, which is suitable for underwater acoustic communication (UWAC). Single-carrier (SC) transmission with frequency-domain equalization (FDE) is today recognized as an attractive alternative to orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM) for communication application with the inter-symbol interference (ISI) caused by multi-path propagation, especially in shallow water channel. In this paper, the turbo theory is applied on the QC-LDPC codes and minimum mean square error (MMSE) decision feedback equalizer (DFE) to design iterative data processing for underwater acoustic communication system. In the proposed iterative structure, the MMSE based FD-DFE and QC-LDPC decoder exchange soft information through an iterative manner so that the performance of underwater acoustic communication system can be improved greatly. Based on sound speed profiles (SSP) measured in the lake and finiteelement ray tracking method, the shallow water channel is constructed to verify the validity of the proposed system structure.
I. INTRODUCTION
The major cause of performance degrade in underwater acoustic communication system is the inter-symbol interference (ISI) introduced by multi-path propagation [1]. Multi-channel adaptive decision feedback equalizer (ADFE), as in [2-4], has been certified as effective approach to eliminate the ISI for the underwater acoustic multi-path channel. Meanwhile, channel coding is an effective technology to ensure reliable data transmission. Low-density parity-check (LDPC) code, firstly presented by Gallager [5], is the one most close to the Shannon limit of a class of error-correcting codes. From the point of view of modern coding theory, Mackay [6-7] proved that LDPC codes are capable of performance close to the Shannon limit. LDPC code has become the hot point in current error-correcting code field. Quasi Cyclic-Low Density Parity Check (QC-LDPC) codes [8] exhibit excellent decoding performance, simple encoding structure, lower encoding complexity and easily to implement with hardware.
For channels with severe delay spread, such as underwater acoustic channel, frequency domain equalization (FDE) is computationally simpler than corresponding time domain equalization because an efficient FFT operation is involved. A single carrier (SC) system with FDE (SC-FDE) has essentially the same performance and low complexity as an orthogonal

frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) system when combined with FFT processes and the use of a cyclic prefix (CP) or zero padding (ZP) [9-10].
But, single technique, such as equalization or coding technique, is difficult to obtain satisfied data transmission because of the complexity of UWA channel, especially in shallow water channel. So, in this paper, an iterative receiver is developed combining MMSE based FD-DFE and Qc-LDPC decoder. In the proposed iterative receiver, the Qc-LDPC decoder and FD-DFE iteratively exchange soft information. And thus, the performance of equalizer can be improved utilizing decoding gain provided by decoder such that the performance of communication system is improved.

III. ITIRATIVE RECEIVER
The block diagram of the proposed iterative receiver is illustrated in Fig.1. In its lth iteration, the frequency domain received vector feeds to the FD-DFE. This produces the vector feeding to a Qc-LDPC decoder producing a posteriori probability (APPs) of data, which are exploited to generate the extrinsic information. This is then processed to produce a soft estimate of the ISI in the received signal samples, and feedback to the feedback filter of FD-DFE for the next iteration.

V. CONLUSION
In this paper, according to the characteristics of underwater acoustic channel, an iterative receiver based on frequency domain decision feedback equalization (FD-DFE) and QcLDPC decoder is investigated to overcome Inter-symbol interference (ISI) caused by multi-path propagation. Based on SSP measured in the lake and finite-element ray tracking model, the shallow water channel is constructed to evaluate the performance of the proposed iterative receiver. The results suggest that the proposed iterative receiver can significantly

improve the performance utilizing decoding gain provided by decoder and obtain better data transmission. Moreover, the proposed FD-DFE algorithm is developed for multi-channel receiver. So, it can easily extend to MIMO system.
