The Design and Verification of a Novel LDPC Decoder with High-efficiency

Dan Yang, Guoyi Yu*, Xuecheng Zou, Yelei Deng, Jianfu Zhong
School of Optical and Electronic Information, Huazhong University of Science and Technology Wuhan, China
*yuguoyi@189.cn

Abstract—A novel high-performance low density parity check codes (LDPC) decoder with small die size for IEEE802.16e criteria is presented in this paper. It utilizes the decoding technique called “Turbo Decoding Message Passing (TDMP)” and a new hardware architecture based on the Ping-Pong operation. Under the generic digital logic process of UMC, when the working frequency is 67MHz, the decoder presented in this paper has cell area of 7.19mm2, layout size of 10.72mm2, and maximum throughput of 1.1Gbps. The simulation results show that under AWGN channel with SNR of 3dB, the frame error rate of random code words is as low as 10-2.5. Compared with other papers, the LDPC decoder designed in this paper has a good error rate efficiency, higher throughput and smaller die size.
Keywords—LDPC;decoder;ASIC;TDMP;IEEE802.16e
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC code was first proposed in 1962 by the Galallager [1], and was rediscovered in 1996 [2], then became a hot topic in the field of channel coding. The error tolerance performance of LDPC code is strong and its decoding algorithm is highly parallel which make it be adopted in a plurality of communication standards in recent years. Compared to the Turbo codes, the study of LDPC’s theoretical is more comprehensive and the analytical methods are more specific. It is believed that LDPC codes will replace Turbo and win the favor of the industrial sector gradually in the future. The current bottleneck facing LDPC code is that it needs too complicated code decoder compared to Turbo codes, requires too much chip area and its throughput is also needed to be further improved. Furthermore, there is no specific method to achieve the LDPC compiling and decoding and a high efficiency circuit is the main challenge for the application of LDPC. Because of the highly complex interconnected circuit inside the decoder, the parallel decoding features of LDPC codes cannot be fully utilized.
II. DESIGN OF DECODER ARCHITECTURE WITH HIGH EFFICIENCY
To improve the efficiency of the hardware and the throughput of the decoder 㧘 there has been proposed a technique called phase-overlapping [3]. The so-called phase overlap means to break the traditional two-phase decoding algorithm to make the calculation of information node and check node can be performed simultaneously and the two phases are no longer be separated but overlapped. The original

LDPC code should be rearranged [4] to achieve the overlap of the phases which can make the contact of information does not exist between the two upper and lower adjacent sub-codes. The experiment result indicates that the decoder’s efficiency of the structure improved 42% [5] compared to the traditional architecture of LDPC decoder based on TDMP technology. But for the LDPC code with 5/6 rate, overlapping technique is helpless.
This paper presents a novel decoder architecture. It can be known that the flaw of this architecture of LDPC decoder for IEEE802.16e is that no matter there is data dependency between parity check matrix layers or not, the calculation result of the upper check node should be saved back to SUM. If we know the next sub-code will use the current calibration results in advance, we can send the current calibration results to the next sub-code directly without writing them back to SUM. According to this idea, the novel decoder structure is shown in Fig. 1. And the configuration of the net parameter Pn is shown in Fig. 2.The figure shows three groups of configuration parameters which are function in each of the three stages of the decoding process, namely the stage of SUM memory initialization, the stage of parity information calculation, the stage of the decoding results output. The update of SUM also changes depending on the different situation: at the stage of initialization, the initialization is processed by the channel value through the net; at the stage of information parity, the corresponding SUM memory will be updated only when the calculation results of current sub-code would not be used by the next one immediately.
The decoding process of the structure in Fig. 1 can be described as followed:

y After the initialization, reading the posterior probability information from the SUM firstly, and then sending it to the subtractor through Pn. At first, the CFU0 works. It calculates the calibration information after obtaining the message of information node from the subtractor. Then CFU0 starts to write the results back to SUM and CFU1 begin to work in which there is three conditions will appear: Firstly, the calculation results of CFU0 will

If it needs to make a choice among the three cases in the second step above based on the parity check matrix in real time, a considerable amount of hardware resources will be consumed. Fortunately, the parity check matrix is known in advance, so that just a little handle on the check matrix can get a bunch of two-bit-wide sequence which contains the control information of a round of iterative calculation process. Defining a bit sequence of which each element is constituted of two bits [b1 b0] and each element corresponds to a submatrix in the parity check matrix. If the current sub-matrix is non-null, then b0 is 1.Or b0 is 0. If the previous sub-matrix in the same row is non-null, then b1 is 1.Or b1 is 0. The matrix in Fig. 3 can be got and a sequence can be got by extracting the each element in each non-null unit. Saving this sequence into ROM, then the control module of the decoder can complete the control of the decoding process by reading out the contents in ROM sequentially. For example, if “11”is read out, it means sending the calculation results of CFU0 (CFU1) to CFU1 (CFU0) directly.

V. CONCLUSION
The synthesized area of the LDPC decoder in this paper is 7.19mm2 by UMC 0.18um process, the layout area is 10.72mm2 and it supports all the LDPC code modes in IEEE802.16e. As a result of utilizing ping-pong operation, the coding efficiency of the decoder structure proposed in this paper is higher. When the operating frequency is 67MH and the number of decoding iteration is 10, the throughput can reach 134.8M.
