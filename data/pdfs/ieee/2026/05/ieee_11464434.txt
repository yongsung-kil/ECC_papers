ON THE ROLE OF EXTRINSIC VALUE EXCHANGE IN EXPECTATION PROPAGATION FOR CODED MIMO SYSTEMS
Fuga Kobayashi1, Takumi Takahashi1, and Hideki Ochiai1
1Graduate School of Engineering, The University of Osaka, Japan

ABSTRACT
This paper investigates the statistical properties of loglikelihood ratios (LLRs) at the output of an iterative signal detector based on expectation propagation (EP), highlighting the role of moment matching (MM)-based extrinsic value exchange in coded systems. Prior studies have mainly examined convergence behavior in asymptotic regimes; however, EP’s advantage is not always evident in practical finite-size uncoded multi-input multi-output (MIMO) detection, where probabilistic data association (PDA), which lacks extrinsic value exchange, can perform comparably to or even better than EP. In contrast, when channel coding is applied, EP consistently outperforms PDA. To explain this, we analyze the statistical properties of demodulator output LLRs and show that extrinsic value exchange not only ensures inter-iteration independence but also enhances the Gaussianity of LLRs. We quantify this effect using negentropy and demonstrate its benefit in improving the decoder’s error-correcting capability, thereby establishing the superiority of EP in coded systems.
Index Terms— Expectation propagation, LLRs, negentropy, channel decoder, coded MIMO systems
1. INTRODUCTION
Expectation propagation (EP) [1, 2, 3] is an approximate inference algorithm that achieves asymptotic Bayes-optimal performance under right-unitarily invariant observations. Its core principle is to approximate the true posterior distribution with a tractable distribution (e.g., Gaussian) and iteratively update its moments to minimize the Kullback-Leibler divergence (KLD) from the true posterior. This moment matching (MM) operation effectively cancels self-feedback components and thereby decouples inter-iteration correlation [2, 3].
It is well known that EP without the extrinsic value generation mechanism reduces to probabilistic data association (PDA) [4, 5, 6, 7]. In underdetermined systems such as compressed sensing, where the number of unknowns exceeds the observation dimension, EP outperforms PDA [2, 3]. In contrast, under overdetermined conditions typical of conventional multi-input multi-output (MIMO) detection—where the observation dimension is much larger—both algorithms perform similarly, and PDA can even surpass EP. Thus, in uncoded MIMO detection with low spatial load, the advantage of extrinsic value exchange may appear limited.
However, in practical coded MIMO systems, the extrinsic value generation mechanism of EP becomes crucial. This is because it significantly enhances the Gaussianity of the final detector output. Soft-decision decoders are fundamentally

designed for additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels and assume Gaussian-distributed input log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) [8, 9, 10]. When the input distribution deviates from the Gaussian distribution, model mismatch degrades the decoder’s error-correcting performance [11]. Therefore, in iterative detection, the statistical properties of the demodulator output LLRs, determined by the final detector output, directly govern post-decoding error rates; however, this perspective has been insufficiently explored in prior works1.
Motivated by this gap, this paper investigates how extrinsic value exchange shapes the statistical properties of demodulator output LLRs and clarifies its role in coded systems. By comparing EP and PDA, we analyze detector output statistics in both the symbol and LLR domains and quantify the impact of extrinsic value generation using negentropy, while accounting for bit-to-symbol nonlinearity. The key contribution of this work lies in a shift in focus from the internal convergence behavior studied in prior works [15, 16] to the output LLR statistics critical in coded systems, thereby revealing the unique role of extrinsic value exchange in EP detectors.

4. STATISTICAL PROPERTIES OF DETECTOR OUTPUT INFORMATION
We analyze a single stream within one channel realization from the previous 16QAM experiment. The detector output at the final iteration is evaluated from two perspectives: (i) the Gaussianity of the Module A output beliefs (symbol estimates), and (ii) the Gaussianity of the demodulator output LLRs provided to the soft-decision decoder. For simplicity, we analyze the real part of the symbol estimates ℜ[xm,k,A→B], exploiting the orthogonality of the real and imaginary components in the QAM constellation. Note that similar results hold for the imaginary part ℑ[xm,k,A→B].

We first examine the distribution of equivalent noise components in the symbol domain, which offers an intuitive perspective. 5. CONCLUSION

This paper highlighted an additional role of extrinsic value generation via MM in EP-based multi-user detection (MUD) for coded MIMO systems—beyond its well-known role in

suppressing self-noise and decoupling inter-iteration dependence. Specifically, the MM mechanism governs the statisti-

cal properties of the final demodulator-output LLRs. By improving their Gaussianity, quantified by negentropy, extrinsic

value exchange via MM directly enhances the effectiveness of subsequent soft-decision decoding. Consequently, although

EP and PDA perform comparably in uncoded systems, EP’s superior performance in coded systems is firmly rooted in this

mechanism. These results underscore the need to evaluate iterative detectors not only by uncoded detection accuracy but

also by the statistical quality of the information delivered to the decoder. Future work includes joint design of detector and demodulator to further improve LLR Gaussianity.
