Design of Protograph-Based LDPC Codes for Rate-Diverse Multiple Access
Jingru Xue , Lichao Zhou, Peng Kang , Xiangming Cai , Member, IEEE,
Pingping Chen , Senior Member, IEEE, and Yi Fang , Senior Member, IEEE
Abstract—In this paper, we investigate the performance of protographbased low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for rate-diverse two-user Gaussian multiple access channel (GMAC). Considering the characteristic of rate-diverse GMAC, we first analyze the message passing model of the coding system. Then, we derive a rate-diverse joint protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (RDJP-EXIT) chart to analyze the rate-diverse joint user messages decoding (RDJD) behavior. Guided by the RDJP-EXIT, we design the protograph base matrices of different channel conditions for two users, respectively. Simulation results show that the proposed protograph-based LDPC codes of low decoding threshold significantly outperform the conventional AR4JA codes and irregular LDPC codes in GMAC channels, which are also close to optimal in terms of sum rate.
Index Terms—Two-user GMAC, LDPC codes, protograph codes, joint decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
Gaussian Multiple Access Channel (GMAC) is a multiuser channel that has received much attention in the 5G and future communication technologies [1]. The capacity region of the two-user GMAC was characterized in [1] and [2], where the corner points of the region can be achieved by single user decoding, and other points can be achieved by time-sharing, rate-splitting, or joint decoding. Channel coding can enhance the performance of GMAC [3]. [4] and [5] used extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart analysis to optimize irregular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for two-user GMAC. More recently, protograph-based LDPC codes were designed for GMAC due to superior performance and low complexity of structured coding [6].
Physical layer network coding (PNC) improves GMAC performance by converting received superimposed signals into useful network coded (NC) messages [7]. Network-coded multiple-access (NCMA) employs XOR-based channel decoding (XOR-CD) and multiuser decoding (MUD) to decode the NC message and then the two user messages [8]. The decoding for compute-and-forward multiple-access (CFMA) is similar to that of NCMA [9]. The achievable rate of XOR-CD for both schemes is limited by the poor user channel. Thus, [10] developed a set of encoding and rate-diverse joint user messages decoding (RDJD). It

II. BACKGROUND
In this paper, vectors are represented by bold symbols, i.e., x, and their elements are indicated by the italic ones, i.e., x.

A. Two-User GMAC Model

The GMAC model consists of two users that need to simultaneously
transmit information to a common receiver. The source message vector u[k] of length Fk for user k is encoded into v[k] of length L, k = 1, 2. Let x[k] be the BPSK modulated codewords of v[k]. At the receiver,
the output signal of the GMAC channel is given by

y = P1x[1] + P2x[2] + z,

(1)

where Pk = E[|hk|2] denotes the power gains, which are the expecta-
tion of channel coefficients hk for user k, and z contains additive white Gaussian noise with zero mean and variance σ2.

B. Rate-Diverse Joint Decoding for GMAC

In the rate-diverse GMAC, channel codes are assigned to users based
on their channel quality, with a high code rate for the better channel and
a low code rate for the inferior one [10]. We assume that strong user 1 adopts a code v[1] of rate R1 with a parity-check matrix H1, while weak user 2 adopts a code v[2] of rate R2 with a parity-check matrix H2. As given in [10], we have the format of the matrices H1 and H2 as

H2 =

H1 He

.

(2)

We see that H2 is composed by adding a residual matrix He to the end
of H1. Let Hc denote the common matrix of H1 and H2, i.e., Hc = H1. We define matrix v[1,2] = [v[1]; v[2]] as the joint codeword of v[1] and v[2], where the j-th element contains two coded bits vj[1,2] = [vj[1]; vj[2]], vj[1,2] ∈ {0, 1} × {0, 1}, j = 1, 2, . . . , L. Under this structure, we have

v[1,2]HcT = 0 for users 1 and 2,

v[2]HeT = 0 for user 2.

(3)

Therefore, in RDJD, we use a common joint user decoder (JUD) within
Hc to decode joint codeword v[1,2], while a residual user decoder (RUD) associated with He decodes v[2]. As shown in Fig. 1, the JUD first uses channel information y and the posterior information of v[2] from RUD to decode (v[1], v[2]), and then the RUD uses the marginal information of v[2] from (vˆ[1], vˆ[2]) to estimate v[2]. This process continues iteratively until the decoding results satisfy vˆ[1]H1T = 0 and vˆ[2]HT2 = 0, or the maximum number of iterations is reached.

In this work, we consider the block fading channel. The proposed design can be extended to time-varying channels, i.e, multi-block fading channel and fast fading channel via block-wise equalization [18], [19]. Note that for the codeword with limited length spans a multiple fading sub-blocks, root-LDPC codes can be designed to achieve full diversity and perform close to the outage limit [20], which we leave for future work.

C. The Design Principles
Our target is to design base matrices B[c] = (bi,j )M1×N and B[e] = (bi,j )(M2−M1)×N for JU and RU, respectively. The desirable base matrices should possess a low decoding threshold, which is the minimum channel quality required for reliable decoding [21]. Based on this criterion, the target rate pair is selected to achieve the best decoding threshold while maximizing the sum rate. In the rate-diverse system, the design principles for the protograph codes are as follows.

1) Determine the numbers of check nodes and variable nodes given the code rate.
2) To achieve a low decoding threshold, the protograph codes contain a certain portion of degree-2 variable nodes, and one variable node has a large degree to speed the converge. Note that we also consider degree-one variable node.
3) The degrees of the remaining variable nodes are set to no less than 3 to satisfy the linear minimum distance growth property.
4) Among all the base matrices that satisfy the constraints, select the one with the lowest decoding threshold.

V. CONCLUSION
This paper proposes a design method for protograph-based LDPC codes for the rate-diverse GMAC. Considering the serial concatenated decoder at the receiver, we first analyze the message model of the system and propose the RDJP-EXIT analysis to evaluate the decoding behavior of the joint user and the residual user. Then, guided by the RDJPEXIT analysis, the protograph matrices are designed for two users with low decoding thresholds for different channel qualities, respectively. Simulation results show that the proposed protograph codes can achieve better performance than the conventional AR4JA codes and irregular codes over different channels and approach the GMAC capacity region.
