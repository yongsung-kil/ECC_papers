Partially-Coupled Staircase LDPC Codes for High-Speed Inter-Satellite Communications

Yaosheng Zhang , Graduate Student Member, IEEE, Jian Jiao , Senior Member, IEEE, Ke Zhang , Member, IEEE, Jiayin Xue , Ye Wang , Member, IEEE, and Qinyu Zhang , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—In this paper, we propose a rate-compatible partially-coupled staircase low density parity check (PS-LDPC) coding scheme for high speed inter-satellite communications. First, we introduce the encoding process and sliding window decoding (SWD) algorithm of PS-LDPC codes, and we investigate the error floor of component codes, which validate that the PS-LDPC codes with short block-length component code can maintain the reliability, and significantly reduce the decoding latency. Then, we analyze the density evolution (DE) of PSLDPC codes based on the multi-edge type (MET)- LDPC framework under the Gaussian approximation, and derive its decoding thresholds of SWD. Further, we propose an optimized coupling pattern (OCP) encoding algorithm that achieves the optimal coupling patterns with the minimized threshold by introducing two-stage column permutations, and modify the message exchanges in SWD algorithm according to this encoding algorithm. Moreover, we design a new decoding algorithm, named cascaded SWD (C-SWD) algorithm, which reduces the error floor and enhances decoding performance by pre-decoding, reliability enhancement, and cascading belief propagation (BP) decoder or ordered likelihood decoder (OLD) due to the error floor. Simulation results demonstrate that our PS-LDPC coding scheme outperforms the existing rate-compatible spatially coupled LDPC (SC-LDPC) coding schemes in terms of bit error rate and complexity.
Index Terms—Inter-satellite communications, staircase LDPC codes, density evolution, sliding window decoding, ratecompatible SC-LDPC codes, error-rate performance.

Nevertheless, the realization of reliable high speed inter-satellite communications faces several challenges, including long free space path loss [6], [7], limited onboard transmission power [8], satellite motion, pointing errors [9], background light, and various cosmic rays [10], [11]. Therefore, rate-compatible forward error correction (FEC) schemes are essential, allowing adaptive coding to ensure both reliability and efficiency under dynamic link conditions [12], [13].
Staircase codes [14] are adopted in the ITU-T G.709.2/ Y.1331.2 standard [15], and have been studied in recent studies for inter-satellite and deep space communication systems [16], [17]. Constructed with Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) component codes [18], [19], [20] to form ladder structures, staircase codes support efficient parallel encoding and decoding, making them well-suited for high speed applications. In fiber-optical communication systems, staircase codes cascading with Hamming codes have been employed to achieve throughput designs of 400 Gbps [21] and even 21.77 Tbps [22]. However, existing schemes struggle to satisfy the stringent reliability demands of inter-satellite communications, and the limited flexibility of BCH code parameters [23] restricts dynamic adjustment of code rates and block-lengths.
Hence, other block codes such as low density parity check (LDPC) [24], [25], [26] and polar codes [27], [28] have been explored as potential component codes for staircase codes. Staircase LDPC codes have demonstrated performance gains of 0.5-1 dB over staircase BCH codes when using the belief propagation (BP)-based sliding window decoding (SWD) algorithm with bit-flipping [24]. Specifically, various rate-compatible spatially coupled LDPC (SC-LDPC) codes have been proposed [29], [30], [31], [32]. These codes offer encoding efficiencies comparable to their component codes and adapt SWD with tunable latency, which makes them attractive for high speed inter-satellite communications. The authors in [30] propose partially information-coupled LDPC (PIC-LDPC) codes and corresponding feed-forward feed-back (FF-FB) decoding algorithm, where the code rate can be adjusted by a simple coupled information bits. The authors in [31] propose systematic convolutional low density generator matrix (SysConv-LDGM) codes and corresponding iterative SWD (ISWD) algorithm, where the code rate can be adjusted by the encoding memory. The authors in [32] propose partial spatially coupled LDPC (PSC-LDPC) codes, where the code rate can be adjusted by modifying the proportion between block LDPC protographs and SC-LDPC protographs, and a large parity check matrix is constructed via the progressive edge-growth algorithm [33]. Therefore, an alternative coding scheme is need to balance performance and complexity.

A preliminary study on rate-compatible staircase LDPC codes is presented in [25], but it lacks detailed theoretical analysis and decoding algorithm. On one hand, for spatially coupled codes, different coupling patterns can lead to distinct performance. The coupling pattern of PIC-LDPC codes [30] is optimized using the extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart [34]. The decoding threshold of sub-block rearranged staircase codes [19] under a fixed coupling pattern is analyzed via density evolution (DE) [35], [36], [37], [38], which is effectively optimized the threshold even with small length component codes, and the conditions for achieving better threshold are derived. However, there is still a lack of research on analyzing the threshold of partially-coupled staircase LDPC (PS-LDPC) codes and optimizing the coupling patterns. On the other hand, unlike schemes that protect encoded bits by multiple component codes [39], PS-LDPC codes contain uncoupled bits protected by only one component codeword, which may degrade overall performance. Recently, the authors in [40] propose the ordered likelihood decoder (OLD) for short rate-compatible codes, which re-encodes the most reliable basis (MRB) to correct errors based on an integer partitionsaided test error patterns (TEPs) generating criterion. This decoder is particularly applicable to PS-LDPC codes, as it takes advantage of the high reliability of coupled bits to correct the more error-prone uncoupled bits. Motivated by the need for both high performance and flexibility in inter-satellite communications, we further investigate PS-LDPC codes with improved algorithms in this paper.
In this paper, we propose a rate-compatible PS-LDPC codes and investigate coding algorithms to better support high-speed inter-satellite communications, aiming to meet the reliability requirements of bit error rate (BER) = 10−5 [41] or BER = 10−6 [11], [42] at lower signal to noise ratio (SNR). Further, we analyze the complexity of our PS-LDPC coding scheme, and compare to the existing schemes by counting the number of binary additions, real additions, multiplications, comparisons, and table look-up operations. Simulation results demonstrate that our PS-LDPC coding scheme outperforms the related rate-compatible SC-LDPC coding schemes in terms of BER and complexity.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the fundamentals of PS-LDPC codes, including the encoding process, SWD algorithm, and preliminary simulation results. In Section III, the PS-LDPC code ensemble is characterized, followed by DE approximate analysis and corresponding threshold results. Section IV of this paper describes the proposed algorithm optimization, including the OCP encoding algorithm and C-SWD algorithm, along with a comprehensive complexity analysis. Simulation results are presented in Section V, where we compare the BER and complexity of our proposed scheme with existing schemes, and separately evaluate the performance improvements from the OCP encoding algorithm and C-SWD algorithm. Finally, Section VI concludes the paper.
II. PS-LDPC CODES
In this section, we first introduce the encoding process of PS-LDPC codes. Next, we describe the dual message-passing iteration process for the corresponding SWD algorithm. Finally, we analyze the performance under short block-length component LDPC codes, which provides critical insights for optimizing decoding algorithm to lower the error floor.

• To meet the Gbps+ throughput requirement of intersatellite communications under varying channel conditions, we propose the PS-LDPC codes, which support flexible code rates by adjusting the coupling depth. Then, we analyze the error floors of short block-length component codes under SWD algorithm, and approximate the block error rate (BLER) lower bound of component codes with ideal a priori information. This analysis validates the short block-length component codes can maintain the reliability and significantly reduce the decoding latency, which gives a practical guideline for subsequent optimization.
• Then, we characterize the PS-LDPC code ensemble using the multi-edge type (MET)-LDPC framework [37] with three edge-types, and define the node-perspective polynomials of variable nodes (VNs) and check nodes (CNs). Under the Gaussian approximation, we describe the DE process of SWD algorithm by tracking the mean and variance of messages, and provide the threshold result. Furthermore, by introducing the two-stage column permutations to adjust the edge-types connected to each VN, we design an optimized coupling pattern (OCP) encoding algorithm that achieves the optimal coupling patterns with the minimized threshold, and modify the message exchange of SWD algorithm according to this encoding algorithm.

A. Encoding Process
The proposed PS-LDPC codes are a class of spatially coupled product-like codes [14]. Each staircase block is constructed by jointly and in parallel encoding a portion of the previous block together with a new information block. The resulting parity check block, along with the information block, forms a complete new staircase block.
The encoding structure of PS-LDPC codes is shown in Fig. 1. First, we select a systematic LDPC code C(N, K) as the component code, with the codeword length of N , the message bits length of K, the parity check bits length of M = N − K, and the generator matrix G. Let B1, B2, · · · , Bi, · · · , BL, Bt1, Bt2, · · · , BtΥ denote the matrices of size m × (N − m), where m is the coupling depth, Bi for 1 ≤ i ≤ L are the ith message blocks, and Bt1, Bt2, · · · , BtΥ are Υ termination blocks. For each Bi = [Bi,info , Bi,parity ] = [Bi,L , Bi,R ], Bi,info is the submatrix of information bits with size m × (K − m), and Bi,parity is the submatrix of parity check bits with size m × M .
Then, the encoding process of PS-LDPC codes starts from B1 and proceeds sequentially in a forward order. As shown in Fig. 1, the matrix C0 is initialized to an all-zero matrix known to the receiver, and does not send over the channel. B. DE Approximation Analysis

Due to the high complexity of exact DE, Gaussian approx-

imation [43] is generally adopted in practical analysis.

Generally, the iterative messages of BP decoding satisfy the symmetry condition [43]: h(x) = exh(−x), where h(x) is the

PDF of variable x. This condition implies that the variance

of the iterative messages is as twice of its mean. Therefore,

the evolution of PDF of iterative messages can be described

by tracking the changes of the mean, which is the so-called

single-parameter Gaussian approximation. However, in SWD

algorithm, ε leads to the symmetry condition no longer being

satisfied. Thus, the single-parameter Gaussian approximation

is not applicable to SWD algorithm of PS-LDPC codes.

IV. ALGORITHM OPTIMIZATION
In this section, we first optimize the coupling patterns of PS-LDPC codes by designing the OCP encoding algorithm. Then, we propose the C-SWD algorithm to improve decoding performance and lower the error floor. Finally, a comprehensive computational complexity analysis is provided for our PS-LDPC coding scheme and compare to the related coding schemes [30], [31], [32].
A. Coupling Patterns Optimization
Our analysis shows that even with identical component LDPC codes, different coupled bits positions (i.e., coupling patterns) results in distinct PS-LDPC code ensembles and affect performance. To optimize the coupling patterns, we employ the DE approximation framework presented in Section III. This allows us to evaluate the decoding thresholds for various coupling patterns, and identify the optimal configuration that minimizes the threshold.
The proposed encoding algorithm is shown in Algorithm 1, where the column permutations Π1 and Π2 are determined based on DE approximation analysis to ensure the optimal coupling patterns. The first column permutation Π1 is used to select m VNs from the first K VNs of component code as the first group of VNs in PS-LDPC code. The second column permutation Π2 is applied after the first group of VNs is determined, and it divides the remaining N − K VNs of component code into the second and third groups of VNs in PS-LDPC code. The resulting coupling does not affect the inherent properties of the component code itself. Therefore, using quasi-cyclic (QC)-LDPC codes as component codes is suitable for high-speed signal processing. Exhaustively searching for the optimal coupling pattern is computationally

B. C-SWD Algorithm
In this subsection, we propose the C-SWD algorithm by modifying the SWD algorithm to accommodate PS-LDPC codes. The algorithm primarily improves performance by optimizing the decoding process of the initial block, enhancing the reliability of coupled bits based on the syndrome matrices, and cascading OLD or BP decoders, primarily to correct errors in non-coupled bits.
Firstly, we introduce a pre-decoding step to enhance performance by increasing the decoding success probability of the initial blocks. The pre-decoding is an additional decoding step performed for B1 before the SWD process, which has the window size of w − 1. Then, B1 is decoded by the SWD with the window size of w again. Specifically, it decodes the window comprising C0, B1, B2, . . . , Bw−2 until J = J or S1 = S2 = 0M×m. The extrinsic information of each block obtained from this pre-decoding step is used for initialization process of SWD algorithm. The performance of SWD algorithm with pre-decoding is shown in Fig. 7. It can be observed that the BER of B2 in SWD algorithm is significantly higher than that of the other blocks for Eb/N0 = 2.04 dB. This is due to the early termination of iterations during the decoding of B1, which prevents sufficient iteration to update a priori information. The introduction of pre-decoding effectively alleviates this issue and improves overall decoding performance.
Secondly, after outputting BV , φVc and φVe,,JV∗ are modified based on S1 and S2. Let L = |L| · sign(L), where |L| and

The key of OLD lies in its TEP generation criterion [40], [44], where the reliability sequence related to MRB is approximated with multiple lines of segment number D ≥ 1. Specifically, the reliability sequence is firstly computed as α = |l|. Next, sort α in descending order and obtain the permutation Π3. Apply Π3 to G to get G = Π3(G). Moreover, we compare the BLER performance and complexity between the SWD and C-SWD algorithms with different cascaded decoders in Fig. 11. Fig. 11(a) illustrates that while the cascaded OLD underperforms the cascaded BP decoder at low Eb/N0, it offers a significantly reduced

error floor at high Eb/N0. Although the OLD has a relatively high complexity [48], Fig. 11(b) shows that cascaded OLD does not significantly increase the overall complexity of the C-SWD algorithm. This is primarily because the OLD is activated with a very low probability. For instance, the OLD is activated only about once per entire decoding process for N = 384, K = 256, m = 140 at Eb/N0 = 1.7 dB. Additionally, for m = 70 and Eb/N0 = 2 dB, the C-SWD algorithm achieves slightly lower decoding complexity, which can be attributed to the reduced average number of outer iterations.
Finally, Fig. 12 shows the decoding performance of C-SWD with cascaded OLD under different m for the optimal coupling pattern, where ε = 0.2 for m = 176 and m = 160, ε = 0.1 for m = 144 and m = 128. We can observe that by varying m, the code rate can be adjusted to accommodate different inter-satellite channel conditions. Meanwhile, a larger m yields better performance but also imposes higher parallel processing demands, which presents a design trade-off. Based on our simulation results of Fig. 10 and Fig. 12, we have identified two key characteristics of a well-performing coupling pattern for PS-LDPC codes. 1) The first group of VNs tend to exhibit the highest average node degree among the three groups. This is because these nodes generally possess more reliable a priori information, and VNs of higher degree exert greater influence during iterative message passing. However, the optimal coupling pattern does not simply maximize the degree of the first group of VNs. We observe that allocating some high degree nodes to the third group of VNs leads to further performance gains. 2) CNs tend to minimize the average number of edges connected from the second group of VNs. The information from uncoupled nodes is typically the least reliable. When multiple unreliable VNs connect to the same CN, this structure increases the likelihood that the parity-check equation may still be satisfied even if these nodes are in error, making the errors difficult to correct.

Based on the analysis, we have proposed the OCP encoding algorithm to achieve an optimal coupling pattern by adjusting the edge types connected to VNs. Furthermore, we have designed the C-SWD algorithm, which improves decoding performance and reduces the error floor through pre-decoding, reliability enhancement, and cascading decoders.
