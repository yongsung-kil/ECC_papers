Abstract—In 5G and beyond communication, the combination of deep learning (DL) and digital twin (DT) channel model makes the system improve efficiency. By using the generative model of DL, the specified statistical distribution, the channel feature as channel impulse response is generated from the measurement data. This paper compares the schemes of Generative adversarial networks (GAN), Variational Auto-encoder GAN (VAE-GAN) and Wasserstein GAN with gradient penalty (WGAN-GP) in acquiring the BERs of SISO/MIMO system with different delays from the real data. From the simulation results, the channel characters are re-edified exactly referred to as the original channel, especially in terms of WGAN-GP. Moreover, of the LDPC decoding algorithms as Linear Approximation Min-Sum (LAMS) and Belief Propagation (BP), the performance of LAMSLDPC is the best in case of employing WGAN-GP in MIMOOFDM system. If the SNR is increased (SNR > 20dB), the better BERs of the WGAN-GP are. Therefore, we propose to exploit LDPC-LAMS combined to WGAN-GP in our coded MIMOOFDM system.
Index Terms—LDPC, MIMO-OFDM, 5G channel modeling, Generative Adversarial Network, GAN, WGAN-GP, VAE-GAN.
I. INTRODUCTION
Multiple-Input Multiple-Output (MIMO) combined with Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiplexing (OFDM) has become a fundamental technology in modern broadband wireless communication systems. By transmitting multiple data streams across several antennas and orthogonal subcarriers, MIMO-OFDM systems can achieve high spectral efficiency and strong robustness against multipath fading [1]. To further enhance reliability, LDPC codes have been widely adopted in wireless standards such as IEEE 802.11n, WiMAX, and 5G New Radio (NR) due to their near-Shannon limit errorcorrection capability [2]. Among LDPC decoding algorithms [3] as 3GPP standard, the BP algorithm is considered the most

accurate approach, as it performs iterative message passing between variable and check nodes to achieve optimal error correction. However, this accuracy comes at the cost of high computational complexity and decoding latency, which make BP less suitable for real-time or hardware-constrained systems. To overcome these limitations, simplified algorithms such as the LAMS method have been proposed [4]- [7]. LAMS modifies the message-passing process to reduce computational overhead and enable faster convergence while maintaining BER performance close to that of BP decoding. Therefore, analyzing the trade-off between decoding complexity and error-rate performance of BP and LAMS is a key step in designing efficient LDPC-coded systems.
The DL techniques in advanced 5G/6G are trending with the real data distribution in the case of how to deal with distributions supported by low dimensional manifolds. As consequences, the virtually generative models illustrate a system with noise in high bandwidth are transpires as the solution in wireless communication channel. Accurate channel modeling is another crucial factor that influences the evaluation of the performance in MIMO-OFDM systems. Conventional analytical models such as Rayleigh or Rician fading describe statistical channel behavior but fail to capture the spatial, temporal, and non-linear dynamics of real-world wireless propagation. To address this limitation, data-driven modeling techniques have emerged, particularly GANs, which can learn complex distributions from measured data and generate synthetic channel datasets that preserve real-world characteristics [9]. GANbased channel modeling has shown potential in reproducing key statistical properties such as path loss, shadow fading, and channel impulse response (CIR), enabling more realistic and scalable simulations [10]- [15]. To deploy the GAN techniques with huge training data proccess, the DT [16] can function as virtual environment that replicating the physical channel

In this work, three generative models—standard GAN, WGAN-GP, and VAE-GAN are used to synthesize channel data that mimic real measured environments. Second, a performancelevel comparison investigates the decoding behavior of BP and LAMS algorithms under both measured and synthetic channel datasets. The BER graphs reveal how decoding performances change when substituting real data with GAN-based data.
The simulation results show that GAN-generated data, particularly from the WGAN-GP model, achieve high fidelity with the measured dataset, producing BER curves that closely follow the real-data baseline. Moreover, while the BP decoder consistently achieves the lowest BER, the LAMS algorithm demonstrates a highly favorable trade-off between decoding accuracy and computational efficiency, achieving similar BER performance at significantly lower decoding complexity. These findings confirm that GAN-based channel data can be effectively used for performance evaluation, reducing the dependency on extensive measurement campaigns while maintaining the reliability of simulation results. III. GENERATIVE ADVERSARIAL NETWORK FOR WIRELESS CHANNEL MODELING
A. Generative Adversarial Network
The GAN model [9], [16] - [18] has recently been employed in wireless communications to model and regenerate complex channel characteristics to capture the conventional statistical models. In a MIMO-OFDM system, the CIR can be represented as a three-dimensional tensor of: h ∈ CNr×Nt×D; where Nr and Ntis the number of receive and antennas, respectively; D denotes the number of delay taps. The goal of the GAN is to learn the empirical distribution pdata(h) of real CIR samples and generate synthetic channel responses hˆ that share similar spatial-temporal characteristics.
The GAN consists of two neural networks: a generator G and a discriminator D. The generator G(z; θG) maps a random latent vector z ∼ pz(z), typically Gaussian noise, into a synthetic CIR sample hˆ = G(z). The discriminator D(h; θD) receives either real or generated CIR samples and outputs the likelihood that the sample originates from real measurements. Both networks are trained in a minimax game defined by:

At convergence, the generated distribution pg(h) approximates the real distribution pdata(h), allowing realistic modeling of MIMO propagation channels.

To address training instability and mode collapse in conventional GANs, the WGAN [10] - [12] replaces the Jensen–Shannon divergence with the Wasserstein-1 (Earth Mover’s) distance, providing smoother gradients during optimization. The WGAN objective is expressed as:

L = Eh∼pdata(h)[D(h)] − Ez∼pz(z)[D(G(z))], (4)
where D (also referred to as a critic) outputs real-valued scores instead of probabilities and must satisfy the 1-Lipschitz constraint. To enforce this constraint, the WGAN with Gradient Penalty (WGAN-GP) [16] adds a regularization term:

where hˆ denotes samples linearly interpolated between real and generated CIRs, and λ is a penalty coefficient. This method stabilizes the learning process and enables the model to capture realistic spatial-temporal dependencies in MIMO fading channels.

C. Variational Autoencoder GAN (VAE-GAN)
To enhance the stability and generalization capability of GAN-based channel models, the VAE-GAN [13]- [15] combines the latent representation learning of Variational Autoencoders (VAE) with the adversarial loss of GANs. The VAE optimizes the evidence lower bound (ELBO) as following:

LV AE = Eq(z|h)[log p(h|z)] − DKL q(z|h)∥p(z) , (6)
where q(z|h) is the encoder that maps the real CIR to a latent variable z, p(h|z) is the decoder reconstructing the CIR, and DKL denotes the Kullback–Leibler divergence. In the VAEGAN framework, the decoder functions as the generator G in the adversarial setting, with an additional loss component:

LV AE−GAN = LV AE + λadv Eh∼pdata [log D(h)] (7)

where λadv balances the reconstruction and adversarial terms. This hybrid architecture leverages the robust encoding ability of the VAE, and further improves the realism of the generated CIRs.

IV. SIMULATION AND RESULTS
In this section, we describe the real measurement dataset and the training configuration used for all above GANbased models, followed by an evaluation of their effectiveness compared to experimentally measured CIRs reported in [3]. The measurement campaign was conducted over the 55– 65 GHz band using a vector network analyzer and open-ended waveguide antennas in indoor environments in both LOS and NLOS conditions. A total 160 channel transfer function (CTF) snapshots were captured. Each CTF is converted into a CIR using the IEEE 802.11ad channel estimation field, resulting in 128 delay-domain samples per CIR. To focus on the dominant multipath components and to match the 802.11ad

signal bandwidth, each CIR is down-sampled and only the first 32 delay taps are retained. These 160 CIR realizations are used as the real data distribution for training the GANbased models. The generated CIR sets are then fed into a 4 × 4 channel-coded MIMO-OFDM communication system implemented in MATLAB to examine the BER performance.

V. CONCLUSION
In this study, we proposed and implemented a digital twin channel modeling approach based on deep generative networks, including GAN, WGAN-GP, and VAE-GAN. The models were trained on real measured CIR data under SISO or 4×4 MIMO configurations, aiming to generate artificial channel data that closely match the statistical properties of realworld measurements. Our experimental results demonstrate that WGAN-GP outperforms to other models in replicating critical channel characteristics such as the Power Delay Profile (PDP), Path Loss, and Shadow Fading. Evaluations using distribution plots, as CDF, PDF and system-level BER simulations confirm that the WGAN-GP generated data exhibits the highest similarity to real data. Among the GAN-based schemes, we propose to use the WGAN-GP due to the most effective in our MIMO-OFDM using LDPC Channel coding. In our BER simulation results, the LAMS decoding is more effective than the BP’s.
