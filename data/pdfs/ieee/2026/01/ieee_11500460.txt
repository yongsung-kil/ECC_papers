Learning to Decode: Graph Attention Network-Gated Recurrent Unit as Channel Decoder
Lixin Qian, Yang Lu , Senior Member, IEEE, Xuefeng Wang , Wei Chen , Senior Member, IEEE, and Bo Ai , Fellow, IEEE

Abstract— Training deep learning (DL) models to address channel coding tasks has drawn increasing attention, a paradigm referred to as “learning to decode”. By constructing Tanner graphs based on parity-check matrices, graph neural networks (GNNs) have shown great promise, yet their performance can be further enhanced with more sophisticated neural architectures. This letter proposes a novel GNN-based decoder that enhances the traditional message-passing framework through the integration of a graph attention network (GAT) and a gated recurrent unit (GRU), termed GAT-GRU. Extensive experiments validate that the proposed GAT-GRU decoder outperforms conventional belief propagation (BP), weighted BP (WBP), and GNN decoders across Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH), regular low-density parity-check (LDPC), and 5G NR LDPC codes. Furthermore, GAT-GRU exhibits robust scalability to unseen code lengths and code rates, particularly for LDPC codes.
Index Terms— Learning to decode, GNNs, channel decoding, BP, BCH, LDPC.
I. INTRODUCTION
CHANNEL coding is a core technology of modern wireless communication systems, designed to reliably recover transmitted information with minimal error rates and latency over noisy channels. Prominent error-correcting codes, such as low-density parity-check (LDPC) [1] and BoseChaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) codes, have been integrated into numerous standards, including 5G New Radio (NR) standards, 3GPP TS 38.212. These codes are commonly represented by Tanner graphs [2], upon which the most representative decoding algorithm is the belief propagation (BP) algorithm. As demonstrated in [3], BP is able to achieve maximum a posteriori (MAP) optimality on cycle-free graphs. However, Tanner graphs of practical codes inevitably contain cycles, particularly for short-to-moderate block lengths. These cycles violate the statistical independence assumption underlying BP, leading to message correlation and oscillation. This results in suboptimal convergence and prevents the decoder from approaching the maximum likelihood (ML) performance bound [4], thereby limiting the error correction capability.

Extensive research efforts have focused on improving the BP algorithm. The min-sum (MS) algorithm reduced decoding complexity at the cost of a minor performance trade-off, while the offset min-sum (OMS) and normalized min-sum (NMS) algorithms achieved favorable decoding performance with low complexity [5]. Further, deep learning (DL) has been exploited to boost wireless intelligence [6]. Regarding channel decoding, DL gives rise to an innovative paradigm, termed “learning to decode”. Specifically, there are two mainstream DL frameworks, namely end-to-end frameworks and BP-iteration frameworks. For instance, the authors in [7] and [8] employed the multi-layer perceptron (MLP) and the recurrent neural network (RNN) to develop end-to-end decoders, respectively, which achieved MAP-level bit error ratio (BER) performance for short-length codes, but they were limited by the curse of dimensionality and the inability to adapt to variant code lengths. Additionally, the authors in [9] proposed a Transformer-based end-to-end decoder, which however, slightly outperformed the single-iteration BP decoder. On the other hand, the authors in [10] adopted the BP-iteration framework by assigning learnable weights to the edges of the Tanner graph, demonstrating improved performance over BP for various high-density parity-check codes. To exploit the graph structure inherent in codes, the authors in [11] proposed to replace BP iterations with graph neural networks (GNNs) [12] on linear binary block codes, which achieved competitive performance with BP and exhibited scalability to varying code parameters.
Nevertheless, existing DL-based decoders demonstrate limited generalization capability. This letter proposes an augmented GNN-based decoder that integrates two powerful components: a graph attention network (GAT) [13] and a gated recurrent unit (GRU) [14], hence termed GAT-GRU. Specifically, we focus on a classic channel coding task involving a single-antenna transceiver over a noisy channel, with the BER adopted as the performance metric. Following the BP-iteration framework, we redesign the initialization step and propose to replace BP iterations with GAT-GRU iterations. The GAT-GRU decoder is trained using a supervised loss function that accounts for the outputs of all iterations. We conduct extensive experiments over BCH, regular LDPC, and 5G NR LDPC codes, yielding three key observations: 1) GAT-GRU generally outperforms BP [3], WBP [10], and GNN [11]; 2) GAT-GRU exhibits robust scalability to unseen code lengths and code rates for LDPC codes; 3) the inference speed of GNN-based decoders remains a bottleneck requiring further improvement. Additionally, ablation studies confirm the effectiveness of both GAT and GRU in enhancing decoding performance.

IV. NUMERICAL RESULTS
This section evaluates the proposed GAT-GRU decoder in terms of BER performance.

A. Experimental Setting
1) Datasets: We consider three types of coding schemes, i.e., BCH, regular LDPC, and 5G NR LDPC. For BCH codes, H is loaded directly from the NVIDIA Sionna [15]. For regular LDPC codes, H is randomly generated based on a specified VN degree of v = 3, CN degree of c = 6, and code length n = 100 [11]. For 5G NR LDPC codes, we utilize the NVIDIA Sionna to generate H by lifting and pruning one base graph defined in the 3GPP 5G NR standard, according to the code length and rate. Then, the training and test datasets are generated via the NVIDIA Sionna.
2) Baselines: Three BP-based decoders from existing works are adopted as baselines: BP [3], WBP [10], and GNN [11].
To quantitatively evaluate decoding performance, we measure the required energy per bit to noise power spectral density ratio (Eb/N0) to achieve a target BER of 10−4.
3) Implementation Details: All models are implemented in Python 3.8 with TensorFlow 2.8 and trained on a computer equipped with a single NVIDIA Tesla T4 GPU, managed by the NVIDIA driver version 470.223.02 with CUDA 11.4. The embedding dimensions for nodes and edges are set to dh = dm = 20 for BCH codes and dh = dm = 16 for LDPC codes, respectively. The weights of the node and message update functions are shared across all nodes and all iterations. For each training batch, the SNR is sampled uniformly in accordance with the parameters in Table I.
For BCH codes, we select layer normalization in (16). For LDPC codes, we use the trainable scalar scaling in (16). For 5G NR LDPC codes, rate-matching is inactivated during the training phase, while activated during the test phase.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we have proposed a GAT-GRU decoder that enhances the BP-iteration framework for binary linear codes. Two modules, i.e., GAT and GRU, were leveraged to enhance message passing over Tanner graphs constructed based on binary parity-check matrices. Numerical results have demonstrated that GAT-GRU achieves significant BER performance improvements over conventional BP-based decoders. A key finding is that GAT-GRU exhibits strong scalability for LDPC codes with respect to varying code lengths and rates. Future work will focus on striking a better balance between decoding performance and computational complexity, with the aim of accelerating the practical deployment of DL-based channel decoders.
