Conditional Neural Decoding for Multi-SNR 
Scenarios: A Lightweight Framework with Parity-
Check Consistency Regularization 
 
Tianyu Shan* 
College of Electronic and Information Engineering 
Tongji University 
Shanghai, China 
*932367092@qq.com 
 
 
Abstract— We propose a lightweight conditional neural 
decoder for short linear block codes that remains reliable over a 
wide SNR range using a single model. For Hamming (7,4) over 
BPSK-AWGN, the decoder takes soft channel observations as 
input and injects Eb/N0 as a condition via feature-wise linear 
modulation (FiLM) to achieve robust multi-SNR inference 
without training separate decoders. To explicitly exploit code 
structure, we introduce a parity-check consistency regularizer 
that penalizes violations of parity constraints and steers 
predictions toward the valid codeword space. Experiments over 
Eb/N0 ∈ [−1, 6] dB show clear BER gains over hard-decision 
syndrome decoding and near-ML performance at medium-to-
high SNR. Under the same SNR range and channel conditions, 
comparisons with representative state-of-the-art neural decoders 
(neural BP and transformer-based decoders) further demonstrate 
a favorable performance–complexity trade-off of the proposed 
FiLM-augmented and regularization-enhanced framework. 
Keywords-channel decoding; Deep learning; Structural prior 
fusion; multi-SNR robustness; AWGN channel 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Channel coding and decoding technology [1] is a key 
component of digital communication systems, and its 
performance directly affects the reliability of data transmission 
in noisy environments. Researchers have proposed various 
classical decoding algorithms for different channel conditions, 
such as the syndrome decoding of Hamming codes and BCH 
codes [2], Viterbi decoding of convolutional codes, and Belief 
Propagation (BP) decoding of low-density parity check (LDPC) 
[3] codes. This type of method usually relies on strict 
mathematical derivation and manually designed algorithm flow, 
which can achieve near optimal decoding performance under 
medium to high SNR conditions, and has been widely applied 
in engineering practice. However, with the increasing 
complexity of communication systems and the diversification 
of application scenarios, traditional decoding methods have 
gradually exposed certain limitations in terms of algorithm 
flexibility, multi-SNR adaptability, and end-to-end joint 
optimization. 
In recent years, deep learning technology has made 
significant progress in fields such as computer vision, speech 
recognition, and natural language processing [4], and has 
gradually been introduced into channel decoding problems. In 
existing research, good results have been achieved, but the 
network structure is usually complex, the training cost is high, 
and the model is often designed for specific SNRs or code 
parameters, which is not conducive to deployment in practical 
systems. However, for small code long line block codes (such 
as Hamming codes), the decoding problem has a relatively 
simple structure, but its application scenarios are still 
representative, especially suitable for verifying new learning 
decoding ideas and structural prior fusion methods. Under the 
condition of small code length, maximum-likelihood (ML) 
decoding [5] can achieve optimal performance by enumerating 
codewords, but its computational complexity increases 
exponentially with code length, making it difficult to generalize 
to more general situations. On the other hand, actual 
communication systems typically operate under a wide range of 
SNR conditions, and most learning decoding methods are often 
trained on a single or a few SNR points, resulting in unstable 
performance of the model when the SNR changes, and even 
requiring the training of multiple models for different SNRs. 
This not only increases training and storage costs, but also 
limits the engineering practicality of learning decoding 
methods. 
Based on the above issues, this paper takes the decoding 
task of Hamming (7,4) linear block code [6] in additive white 
Gaussian noise [7] (AWGN) channels as the research object, 
and proposes a conditional neural decoding method for multi-
SNR scenarios. In this method, the lightweight feedforward 
neural network is used to decode the soft decision symbols at 
the receiver directly, and the SNR information is input into the 
network as a condition through the feature wise linear 
modulation (FiLM) mechanism, thus realizing the adaptive 
decoding of a single model under different SNR conditions. In 
addition, to address the issue of neural network outputs 
potentially deviating from the legitimate codeword space, this 
paper proposes a parity check consistency regularization term 
based on the parity check constraint of linear block codes. This 
term is used to measure the probability of network outputs 
violating the check equation and is introduced into the training 
loss function to explicitly incorporate the code structure prior 
into the end-to-end learning process.

II. RELATED WORK 
A. Traditional channel decoding methods 
Channel coding and decoding are one of the core 
technologies in digital communication systems. For linear 
block codes, especially short codes such as Hamming and BCH, 
traditional decoding methods mainly rely on manually designed 
parsing algorithms. For example, Hamming (7,4) codes usually 
use hard-decision syndrome decoding, which completes single 
bit error correction by calculating the synthesis of the received 
sequence and looking up a table. This method has a clear 
structure and simple implementation, but its performance is 
limited under low SNR conditions. For longer code lengths or 
more complex encoding structures, researchers have proposed 
iterative decoding methods such as Viterbi algorithm [8] and 
Belief Propagation (BP) [9], and have achieved significant 
success in modern channel coding such as LDP [10] and Turbo. 
However, such methods often rely on precise channel model 
assumptions and carefully designed iteration rules, and their 
performance improvement space is limited under low code 
length or non ideal conditions. 
B. Research on Neural Decoding Based on Deep Learning 
In recent years, deep learning methods have been 
introduced into the field of channel decoding, forming the 
research direction of "Neural Decoding" [11]. Early work 
typically viewed decoding problems as an end-to-end 
classification or regression task, directly utilizing multi-layer 
perceptrons [12] (MLP) or convolutional neural networks [13] 
(CNN) to recover the transmitted bits from the received soft 
information. This type of method exhibits certain performance 
advantages in small code length scenarios, but often lacks 
explicit 
utilization 
of 
encoding 
structures, 
limiting 
generalization ability and interpretability. To overcome the 
above problems, some studies have attempted to "neural" or 
"weighted" the traditional BP algorithm, such as introducing 
trainable weights on the Tanner graph structure [14], in order to 
improve performance while maintaining the original decoding 
structure. This type of method has achieved good results in 
structured encoding such as LDPC, but the model structure is 
relatively complex and the training and deployment costs are 
high. There are also studies that focus on the combination of 
short codes and lightweight neural networks [15], using small-
scale networks to learn approximate ML decision rules in a 
limited codeword space. This approach is particularly attractive 
under small code length settings such as Hamming (7,4) and 
BCH, 
and 
is 
suitable 
for 
embedded 
or 
low-power 
communication systems. However, existing methods mostly 
target fixed SNR training, and the robustness of the model 
under multiple SNR conditions still needs to be improved. 
C. Application of structural priors and conditional learning in 
neural decoding 
Introducing encoding structure priors into neural networks 
is an important research direction for improving the 
performance and stability of neural decoding. Some works 
guide the network output to be closer to the set of valid 
codewords by explicitly embedding checksum relationships at 
the input or output of the network, or adding structural 
constraints to the loss function. This type of method to some 
extent alleviates the problem of "illegal codewords" easily 
generated by pure data-driven models, especially under low 
SNR conditions, and has significant advantages. At the same 
time, by introducing channel state information such as signal to 
noise ratio (SNR) as condition variables, a single model can be 
adapted to different channel conditions, avoiding training 
models separately for each SNR. Conditional mechanisms such 
as FiLM (Feature wise Linear Modulation) have been proven 
effective in computer vision and multi task learning, but their 
application in channel decoding scenarios is still relatively 
limited. Overall, there is still room for further exploration in the 
utilization of structural priors, conditional modeling, and model 
lightweighting in existing research. Especially in small code 
length scenarios, how to achieve a decoder that approaches ML 
performance with a minimalist network structure while 
integrating SNR condition information and parity constraints 
remains a valuable research question. 
III. METHOD 
A. System Model and Problem Description 
This paper investigates the decoding problem of Hamming 
(7,4) linear block codes in additive white Gaussian noise 
channels. The sending end of the system first randomly 
generates 4 information bits, and completes encoding through a 
system form generation matrix to obtain a 7-bit codeword 
sequence. Subsequently, the codeword is modulated into a 
symbol sequence using binary phase shift keying (BPSK), 
where 0 corresponds to positive amplitude symbols and 1 
corresponds to negative amplitude symbols. The modulated 
symbols are subject to Gaussian noise interference during 
transmission, and the receiving end obtains a soft decision 
signal with noise. The goal of the decoding task is to use soft 
symbol information at the receiving end to recover the original 
codeword, thereby minimizing the bit error rate as much as 
possible. Traditional Hamming decoding usually adopts a hard-
decision syndrome decoding method, which is simple to 
implement but does not fully utilize soft decision information, 
and its performance is limited under low SNR conditions. This 
article proposes a lightweight decoding method based on neural 
networks, which directly uses received soft symbols for end-to-
end decoding, and improves decoding performance by 
introducing channel condition information and code structure 
constraints.

B. SNR Conditional Neural Decoding Network 
In order to make the single model adapt to different channel 
SNR environments, this paper introduces a conditional 
mechanism based on feature level linear modulation. This 
mechanism takes the current SNR as an auxiliary input to 
dynamically adjust the feature representation of the neural 
network's hidden layer. Its expression is: 
h′ = γ(SNR) ⊙h + β(SNR)                      (2) 
Among them, ℎ represents the hidden layer feature vector, 
γ(⋅) and β(⋅) are generated by the learnable network, and the 
symbol ⊙ represents element wise multiplication operation. 
Through this modulation method, the network can adaptively 
adjust the feature distribution according to different channel 
conditions, thereby improving the decoding stability in multi-
SNR scenarios. Based on the above ideas, this article constructs 
a multi-layer fully connected neural decoding model. The 
network input includes receiving soft symbols and normalized 
SNR 
information, 
outputting 
probability 
estimates 
corresponding to each bit, and obtaining decoding results 
through threshold judgment. 
C. Parity check consistency constraint design 
Linear block codes have strict parity check structure 
constraints. If relying solely on data-driven training, neural 
networks may produce prediction results that do not conform to 
the code structure. To fully utilize the encoding prior 
information, 
this 
paper 
introduces 
parity 
consistency 
constraints during the training phase and uses them as 
additional optimization objectives. The final model training 
objective consists of decoding error loss and check consistency 
loss, and their joint loss function is defined as: 
ℒ= ℒBCE + λℒparity                       (3) 
Among them, ℒBCE represents the binary cross entropy loss 
between the predicted codeword and the real codeword, ℒparity 
represents the parity consistency loss, and λ is the weight 
coefficient. By introducing this regularization term, the model 
can be guided to output results that are more in line with code 
structure constraints while learning decoding mapping 
relationships, thereby improving decoding accuracy and 
robustness. 
D. Model Training Strategy 
This article uses random data generation to construct 
training samples. Firstly, randomly generate information bits 
and complete encoding and modulation processing. Then, 
Gaussian noise is randomly added within the set SNR range to 
simulate different channel environments. To enhance the 
model's generalization ability, multiple SNR intervals are 
covered during the training phase, and the SNR is normalized 
before being input into the network. The model is trained using 
end-to-end supervised learning and the model parameters are 
updated using adaptive optimization algorithms. After training, 
the final decoding result is obtained through probability 
threshold judgment. The model is implemented in PyTorch and 
trained using the Adam optimizer with an initial learning rate 
of 0.001. The batch size is set to 512, and training is conducted 
for 100 epochs. Early stopping is applied based on validation 
BER. The training dataset consists of 2 × 10⁶ randomly 
generated codewords covering uniformly sampled SNR values 
in the range of −1 dB to 6 dB. During training, logits are 
directly used for binary cross-entropy computation without 
sigmoid activation for numerical stability. All experiments are 
conducted on a single NVIDIA RTX-series GPU. 
IV. EXPERIMENT 
A. Experimental setup 
This article validates the proposed neural decoding method 
in the Hamming (7,4) linear block code+AWGN channel 
scenario. The information bit length is k=4, the codeword 
length is n=7, and the encoding adopts a systematic generation 
matrix. The modulation method is BPSK, where bit 0 is 
mapped to+1 and bit 1 is mapped to -1. The channel model is 
additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN), and the SNR is 
expressed as Eb/N0 (dB). The input of the neural decoder is a 
7-dimensional soft symbol vector from the receiving end, and 
the output is logits corresponding to 7 bits. The model adopts a 
two-layer fully connected network and introduces FiLM 
mechanism for SNR conditioning after each hidden layer. 
During the training, Eb/N0 samples uniformly within the range 
of [-1,6] dB, so that a single model can adapt to multiple SNR 
scenarios. The loss function consists of a bit by bit binary cross 
entropy loss and a weighted parity check consistency 
regularization term, where the regularization coefficient is set 
to λ=0.2.

V. CONCLUSION 
This paper proposes a lightweight neural decoding 
framework that integrates SNR conditioning and parity-check 
consistency regularization to address the limited multi-SNR 
adaptability of traditional decoding methods and the 
insufficient exploitation of coding structure priors in existing 
deep learning–based decoders. Focusing on the Hamming (7,4) 
linear block code over an AWGN channel, we design and 
experimentally validate a conditional neural decoder that 
maintains robust performance across a wide SNR range. The 
main contribution lies in constructing a multi-SNR conditional 
architecture in which SNR information is embedded through a 
feature-wise linear modulation (FiLM) mechanism, allowing 
channel quality to serve as a learnable condition so that the 
network can dynamically adjust internal feature representations 
under different noise levels. This enables a single model to 
generalize across varying SNR environments, eliminating the 
need to train separate models for different SNR points and 
improving deployment efficiency. To further incorporate 
structural knowledge, a parity-check consistency regularization 
term derived from linear block code constraints is introduced 
into the training objective together with the cross-entropy loss, 
guiding the network toward the valid codeword space, reducing 
illegal predictions, and enhancing robustness especially under 
low-SNR conditions. Simulation results over AWGN channels 
demonstrate 
that 
the 
proposed 
decoder 
consistently 
outperforms hard-decision syndrome decoding across multiple 
SNR 
intervals 
and 
approaches 
maximum-likelihood 
performance 
in 
medium-to-high 
SNR 
regions, 
while 
maintaining a lightweight architecture with a small parameter 
scale, fast convergence, and low inference complexity. Overall, 
the results confirm that jointly leveraging channel condition 
information and coding structure priors can significantly 
improve the stability and reliability of neural decoding for short 
block codes, and the proposed conditional framework provides 
a practical and extensible solution that can be further expanded 
to longer codes, more complex structures, and hardware-
oriented implementations in future intelligent communication 
systems.
