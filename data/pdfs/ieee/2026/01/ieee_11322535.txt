PEXIT Analysis and Design of Multilevel Coded Modulation With SPC-Aided SC-LDPC Codes
Hongmei Kang , Student Member, IEEE, Yidi Zhang , Yimeng Liu, and Ming Jiang , Member, IEEE

Abstract— In this letter, we propose a multilevel coded modulation (MLCM) scheme based on single parity check (SPC)aided spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC) codes to enhance spectral efficiency. A two-level MLCM framework is designed, in which the SC-LDPC component codes are compatible with the fifth-generation new radio (5G-NR) LDPC codes, and the message length allocations are optimized through the protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) analysis. Besides, an SPC constraint of message bits is introduced between different SC-LDPC codes to further lower the error floor. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed MLCM scheme achieves significant gains over the conventional 5G-NR LDPC codes using bit-interleaved coded modulation, while also notably reducing decoding complexity.
Index Terms— Multilevel coded modulation, spatially-coupled low-density parity-check codes, protograph-based extrinsic information transfer, single parity check.
I. INTRODUCTION
WITH the rapid evolution towards the sixth-generation wireless communication systems, physical layer technologies are anticipated to undergo significant changes to meet the ever increasing demands for higher spectral efficiency and enhanced transmission reliability [1]. However, the bit-interleaved coded modulation (BICM) schemes employed in the fifth-generation new radio (5G-NR) systems with lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes, face critical challenges in balancing implementation complexity and ultra-high throughput, especially under high-order modulation scenarios [2].
Multilevel coded modulation (MLCM) addresses these challenges by decomposing the message into multiple bit levels and optimizing code protection per level [3]. Recent works have introduced probabilistic and geometric shaping techniques to further exploit shaping gain [4], [5], [6], as well as spatially-coupled (SC) codes to improve decoding thresholds and reduce the power consumption of channel decoding with high-order quadratic-amplitude modulation (QAM) [7]. In addition, several hierarchical and multi-rate coded modulation systems have explored bit-level rate trade-offs [8], [9], [10]. In [8], a source-constrained hierarchical modulation

In this letter, we focus on the design of a two-level MLCM scheme based on single parity check (SPC)-aided SC-LDPC (SSC-LDPC) codes. Specifically, we employ the existing simplified error minimization progressive edge-growth (EMPEG) algorithm [11] to construct the SC-LDPC codes under heterogeneous lifting factors. Moreover, inspired by the inter-block cooperative coding framework in [12], an SPC constraint is introduced across different message blocks to reduce the error floor and enhance decoding performance [13]. Additionally, we utilize the protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) analysis [14] to optimize the message length allocations of the SC-LDPC component codes, which represent the code rate allocations across sub-blocks for performance enhancement. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed scheme significantly outperforms the conventional 5G-NR BICM system in both decoding performance and computational complexity.

This enables the optimization of the message length allocations, i.e., the ratio K0/K1, thereby improving the decoding threshold.

IV. THRESHOLD ANALYSIS AND SIMULATION RESULTS
A. PEXIT-Based Threshold Analysis for MLCM Schemes Fig. 2 presents the decoding thresholds (Eb/N0)∗ obtained
through PEXIT analysis for different (q0, q1) and modulation schemes versus K0 under Q-QAM. For each case, since (Eb/N0)∗ varies with K0 in a monotonic or convex manner, we only mark the minimum, maximum, and optimal points, while the intermediate points are also plotted on the curve without additional markers to avoid clutter, which is sufficient to illustrate the overall trend and identify the optimal K0/K1 ratio. The message length of each CB is fixed to K = 8448, with K1 = K − K0. For clarity, each inset shows the optimal thresholds and corresponding K0 values, while also annotating the performance gains relative to the thresholds of 5G-NR LDPC coded BICM systems.
In Fig. 2(a), for 64-QAM (RSC = 2/3), we consider two cases with (q0, q1) = (2, 4) and (4, 2), respectively. The “LB-aided HB” scheme with (q0, q1) = (4, 2) achieves the lowest threshold of 7.7 dB at K0 = 4224, i.e., K0/K1 = 1.0, which is 0.3 dB lower than that of the BICM system. In Fig. 2(b), for 256-QAM (RSC = 1/2), it is shown that when (q0, q1) = (6, 2), (Eb/N0)∗ = 8.3 dB is minimal, achieved at K0 = 4224, i.e., K0/K1 = 1.0, for the “LBaided HB” scheme, which is 0.5 dB lower than that of the BICM system. Besides, in Fig. 2(c), for 1024-QAM (RSC = 2/3), the “LB-aided HB” schemes with (q0, q1) = (6, 4) at K0 = 3380 and (q0, q1) = (8, 2) at K0 = 5914 achieve thresholds that are 0.4 dB and 0.1 dB lower than that of the BICM system, respectively. These results further confirm

that the “LB-aided HB” schemes consistently achieve superior threshold performances across various (q0, q1).

B. Simulation Results of Different MLCM Schemes
In this subsection, we compare BLER performance per CB (excluding the (T − 1)-th CB containing only parity bits) of the MLCM schemes under different (q0, q1) and the optimal K0/K1 ratios obtained in Section IV-A. Besides, we examine the impact of introducing the SPC constraints across CBs on the decoding performance. The 5G-NR LDPC coded BICM system is included as a benchmark with the same code rate and block length. Simulation parameters are fixed as K = 8448 and T = 100, and the maximum number of decoding iterations is set to 10. The vertical dashed lines indicate the numerical thresholds of different schemes.
Across all modulation orders Q and code rates RSSC, the “LB-aided HB” schemes consistently yield superior performance. In Fig. 3(a), for 64-QAM, the configuration (q0, q1) = (4, 2) achieves about 0.2 dB gain over BICM system at BLER = 10−3 when the SPC constraints are applied. Moreover, the SPC constraints significantly improve performance in the high-SNR region (Eb/N0 > 8.5 dB). In Fig. 3(b), the 256-QAM case using (q0, q1) = (6, 2) with the SPC constraints shows approximately 0.5 dB gain compared to the BICM system. In Fig. 3(c), the “LB-aided HB” schemes with (q0, q1) = (6, 4) and (8, 2) outperform the BICM system by 0.5 dB and 0.2 dB, respectively. These results verify the accuracy of the PEXIT-guided optimization and the benefit of SSC-LDPC codes in lowering the error floor, albeit with slight performance degradation at low SNRs due to code rate loss.

V. CONCLUSION This letter has proposed a two-level MLCM scheme based on SSC-LDPC codes under high-order modulation, which can be readily extended to multilevel scenarios. A modified PEXIT algorithm, tailored to the MLCM framework, is employed to optimize the message length allocations of the SC-LDPC component codes, thereby minimizing the decoding threshold. In addition, an SPC constraint of message bits is introduced to lower the error floor. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed scheme outperforms the 5G-NR BICM system in both decoding performance and computational complexity.
