I. INTRODUCTION
Low Density Parity Check (IDPC) codes with iterative decoding based on the sum-product algorithm achieve astonishing error performance [I], [2]. In general, LDPC codes constructed randomly have a drawback that the encoding is done via matrix-vector multiplication with complexity of quadratic in the code length or O(n2).However, some LDPC codes based on Finite Geometry construction presented in [3] can be simply implemented with linear feedback shift register and still possess remarkable error performance.
Reversible LDPC codes can be encoded using the iterative Jacobi method which can be viewed as message passing on a bipartite graph (41. This provides the reuse of the decoder architecture for iterative encoding with O(n) complexity. Furthermore, the sum-product decoder architecture reuse reduces a chip area due to the performing of encoding by the decoder circuit nodes and routing network already in place for calculation. Much less encoding time than the decoding time also provides a full duplex codec. A (3. h)-regular LDPC codes generated from a circulant matrix specified the first row
by the polynomial p(x)=l+x+x'has been designed in [4]. It was shown that the performance of the reversible (3, 6)regular LDPC codes compare reasonably well up to about the AER of 1U5 with that of randomly constructed codes. However, a limitation of this design is the wealth of the block size.
In this paper, we investigate the performance of reversible LDPC codes constructed from the circulant matrix in the region of BER smaller than 10~'.We also propose and investigate the possibility lo a p l y other circulant matrices selected from some primitive polynomials over GF(2). We

expect to offer another choice of block length and in some cases better error performance for reversible LDPC codes.
