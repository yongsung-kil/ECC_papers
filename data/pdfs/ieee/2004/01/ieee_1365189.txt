Puncturing for Finite Length Low-Density Parity-Check Codes
Jeongseok Ha, Jaehong Kim, and Steven W. McLaughlin School of Electrical and Computer Engineering Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, U.S.A. {jsha, onil, swm}@ece.gatech.edu

Abstract — In this paper we study and propose an algorithm to puncture finite length low density parity check (LDPC) codes [1]. The introduced puncturing criterion results in good performance (for 1024 and 4096 bits) when compared with both random puncturing and dedicated LDPC codes, i.e. unpunctured codes designed for a given rate. The comparison also shows that the proposed punctured LDPC codes have better block-error rates than the dedicated codes because of longer effective block lengths of the high-rate puncturing. Although we apply the idea for regular LDPC codes, we can easily modify the idea for irregular LDPC codes.
