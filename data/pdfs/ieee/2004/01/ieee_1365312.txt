I. Introduction
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are one of the most promising next generation error correcting codes and many LDPC codes suitable for hardware implementation have been investigated so far. Although randomly constructed LDPC codes are usually encoded by using generator matrix, this method requires quadratic time complexity and is not easy to implement. In this paper we show that array-type LDPC codes [1, 2] and a special class of Sridhara-Fuja-Tanner (SFT) codes [3] can be encoded by division circuits as cyclic codes, which are very easy to implement. Throughout the paper p denotes an odd prime number.
