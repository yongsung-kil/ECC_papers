I. INTRODUCTION 
Recently, Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes have 
gained a lot of interests. LDPC codes were proposed by Gal- 
lager in 1960s [I] and later rediscovered by MacKay [2] [3]. 
LDPC codes can be decoded in various ways, namely the Bit- 
Flipping (BF) algorithm, the Weighted BF (WBF) algorithm [4], 
the Log-Likelihood Ratio Belief Propagation (LLR-BP) algo- 
rithm [5], and so on. These algorithms provide a wide range 
of tradeoffs among decoding complexity, decoding speed, and 
error rate performance. The BF algorithm is a hard decision 
decoding algorithm and can he easily implemented. The WBF 
algorithm improves the error rate performance of the BF algo- 
rithm by including some kind of reliability information (soft 
information) of the received symbols in the decoding process. 
Thus. the WBF algorithm is between a hard decision and a soft 
decision algorithms. The LLR-BP algorithm is a soft decision 
decoding algorithm. It requires extensive decoding computation 
hut it can achieve near Shannon limit error rate performance. 
Recently, the Bootstrapped WBF (BWBF) algorithm has been 
proposed to improve the error rate performance and the decod- 
ing complexity of the WBF algorithm [6]. The Bootstrap step 
is based on identifying the unreliable received symbols (i.e., the 
received symbols with absolute values smaller than the prede- 
termined threshold CY) and estimating their values and corre- 
sponding reliabilities by passing information to them from more 
reliable hits and through “reliable” check nodes. However, it 
has not been clarified what the optimal threshold (Y depends on. 
Also, the Bootstrap step can he applied to various algorithms, 
however, the effects of the Bootstrap step on the algorithms 
other than the WBF algorithm have not been clarified. In ad-
