Minimum Distance Bounds of Irregular QC-LDPC Codes and Their Applications
Min-Ho Shin, Joon-Sung Kim, and Hong-Yeop Song Dept. of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Yonsei University, Seoul, 120-749, Korea
{mh.shin, js.kim, hy.song}@coding.yonsei.ac.kr

Abstract — We consider a design of quasi-cyclic LDPC codes which have irregular column weights. We derive bit-oriented bound and parity-oriented bound on the minimum distances of irregular quasicyclic codes using a similar technique to Tanner’s simple lower bound.

I. Minimum distance bounds of QC-Codes

A block code is said to be quasi-cyclic(QC) if a cyclic shift of any codeword by p places, for a given constant p, is still a codeword. The block length n of a QC-code is a multiple of p, i.e. n = mp. We consider a QC-code with rate (p − 1)/p constructed from p matrices with parity check matrix, H = [H1, H2, . . . , Hp], where, H1, H2, . . . , Hp are m × m circulant matrices. Each circulant matrix Hi is completely described by the associated polynomial hi(x) corresponding to the top row of Hi. More precisely, we have hi(x) = jm=−01(Hi)0j xj .
Tanner presented [1] two simple lower bounds on the minimum distance d of a code with an m × n regular parity check matrix H. The bounds are expressed in terms of the eigenvalues of HHT and imply good expansion of a graph gives good distance property for the associated code. We derive minimum distance bounds for irregular quasi-cyclic codes using a similar technique in [1].
