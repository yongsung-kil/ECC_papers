I. Introduction
In this paper, the design of Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes for Binary Erasure Channels (BEC) with less number of decoding iterations in the parallel message-passing decoding [3] is considered. We only consider the cases where the capacity is approached with sufficiently low parity-check matrix density [2, 1, 4].

A direct approach to finding the degree distributions of a fixed rate with the minimal decoding convergence time is to solve a constrained optimization problem with the objective function being TќЈ. However, this optimization problem is difficult since the objective function TќЈ is not differentiable and the calculation of TќЈ involves the recursive calculation of Pe(l) which is prone to numerical errors. All existing optimization algorithms converge slowly and stop at suboptimal solutions. To get around these difficulties, at this point, we invoke Theorem II.2 and replace TќЈ by F (ќї, ѕБ, ќЈ). This yields an approximate optimization problem, which has nice properties. We show that both the objective function and constraint set are convex. Therefore, efficient optimization algorithms such as the deepest descent algorithm can be applied. The approximate optimization runs much faster and gives better codes than the suboptimal solutions by the direct optimization.
