Construction of Good LDPC Codes using Dilation Matrices
Marcus Greferath, Michael E. O’Sullivan, and Roxana Smarandache Dept. of Mathematics
San Diego State University 5500 Campanile Drive
San Diego, CA 92182-7720 {greferath, mosulliv, rsmarand}@math.sdsu.edu

Abstract — A new method is given to construct lowdensity parity check codes the graphs of which are of designed girth. We give examples to illustrate the new method, and also present performance diagrams that suggest that these codes are as good as random codes in low SNR, and preferable to random codes at higher SNR.

I. Details of the Construction
Let G be a finite abelian group. For each b ∈ G and α ∈ Aut(G) we call the map g → gα + b a dilation of G . We will assume that α is in the center of Aut(G) .
To construct a bipartite graph we proceed as follows: Let R , S and T be finite sets, and let ρ : T −→ R and σ : T −→ S be mappings. Furthermore, for each t ∈ T fix a dilation θt of G . We write v ∼ w if vertices v, w ∈ V are connected by an edge in the underlying graph, and define a bipartite graph (V, E) with

V := R × G ∪ S × G, and
E := {(ρ(t), g) ∼ (σ(t), θt(g)) | t ∈ T, g ∈ G}.
We focus on the particular case in which G = Zp × Zq with each θt a translation on the first factor and a dilation on the second; θt(x, y) = (x + ct, aty + bt) with at coprime to q . In earlier work (cf. [2]), we chose R a singleton, |T | = 3|S| and σ so that the graph is (3|S|, 3) -regular. We presented methods to select at, bt and ct to ensure that the bipartite graph has either girth 6 or girth 8 .
