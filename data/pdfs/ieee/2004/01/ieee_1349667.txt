The min-sum algorithm is generalized t o group codes of different alphabet sizes and is used t o decode lattices. Bounds on the decoding complexity are provided. The analysis of decoding complexity confirms the usefulness of LDPC codes for the lattice construction. The exact number of computations per iteration for LDPC lattices, along with bounds, are derived. It is shown that the decoding complexity grows linearly with the dimension, exponentially with the label group sizes and is polynomial time in terms of coding gain of the lattice.
