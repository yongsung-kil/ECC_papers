Abstract - A highly-parallel LDPC decoder architecture for 10Gbase-T applications is designed in this paper. Firstly, we reduce the routing complexity and corresponding power consumption by the proposed decoder architecture based on single routing networks. Secondly, the proposed architecture is designed with pipelined layered scheduling and multi-block parallel decoding, which improves operation speed and removes pipeline stalls in conventional highly-parallel layered scheduling. Thirdly, we trade off between hardware cost and throughput by a digit-serial data-path. Fourthly, an efficient early-termination circuit suitable for layered decoding is designed. The decoder is implemented in 130nm 1P8M CMOS process. The core area is 18.4mm2 with 14% reduction, and the decoding throughput is 9.48Gbps operating at 278MHz and 5 iterations. The tested power consumption is 774mW at 1.2V and 80MHz.

II. Design Techniques
The routing networks of LDPC decoder can be designed based on: (a) CN-to-BN and BN-to-CN routing networks [8] [12], or (b) bi-directional global routing networks [9]. This paper reduces the complexity of random routing for (2048, 1723) LDPC code by the proposed SRN-based architecture. This design optimizes the networks based on routing posterior messages. An example of merging 8×8 random backward and forward routing pattern into an SRN pattern is given in Fig. 2.
By six-layer scheduling, the forward and backward routing for (2048, 1723) LDPC code need 2048 6-to-1 q-bit multiplexers, respectively, which results in 207,768 equivalent-gates at q=6 and 300MHz in 130nm CMOS. After optimization, the SRN needs 2048 7-to-1 q-bit multiplexers, which results in 122,435 equivalent-gates. The optimization leads to 41.1% reduction of logic, and reduces 41.67% global random wires.
The proposed SRN for (2048, 1723) LDPC code consists of two stages. The first stage treats 2048 q-bit messages as 32 groups, each containing 64 q-bit messages, and permutes 64×32 messages to 64 groups of 32 messages with fixed interconnect. IV. Conclusions
A highly-parallel (2048, 1723) LDPC decoder for 10Gbase-T application is presented in this paper. Several design techniques are detailed, including single routing networks, pipelined layered scheduling with multi-block processing, digit-serial data-path, and an early-termination circuit. Implementation results show the proposed decoder attains high throughput and small area with high utilization.

This design adopts layered scheduling with pipelined data-path to improve clock speed, removing the pipeline stalls by means of multi-block parallel processing. The pipeline stages in the SISO are allocated as follows: one stage for the subtraction, three stages for the update, and one stage for the addition. Read/write operation of BN-MEM and message routing also need pipeline cycles. To remove pipeline stalls, multi-block (7 blocks in all) processing is adopted. The intermediate messages are stored in memory, implemented as high-density register-file, including BN-MEM and extrinsic memory. The total memory requirement is 206,976-b.
The fixed word-length representation of posterior messages and extrinsic messages are chosen as 6-bit (q=6) and 5-bit, respectively. To further reduce interconnect and complexity, the architecture adopts q/2-bit digit-serial data-path i.e., to decode each layer, 2048 6-bit posterior messages are read from BN-MEM with 3-bit MSBs first and 3-bit LSBs next, then permuted by SRN, updated by SISO array, and finally written back to BN-MEM. Thus, the first stage of SRN contains 2048×3=6144 wires, and the second stage need 6144 7-to-1 multiplexers. By this configuration, the SISO array contains only 32 SISO units with digit-serial/digit-parallel conversion at the module input/output. It needs two cycles to process a layer, and twelve cycles to process once of iteration.
