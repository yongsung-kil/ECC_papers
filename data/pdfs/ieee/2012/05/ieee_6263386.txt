Design and Application of Dynamic Coding in Shallow Water Acoustic Communications

Yougan Chen1,2, Xiaomei Xu1, Lan Zhang1 , and Zheguang Zou1
1. Key Lab of Underwater Acoustic Communication and Marine Information Technology (Xiamen University), Ministry of Education, Xiamen 361005, P.R.C
2. Dept. of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Connecticut, Storrs, CT 06269, USA xmxu@xmu.edu.cn

Abstract—In fast temporal variations shallow water acoustic (SWA) channels, flexible channel coding rate should be desired in the design of practical error control SWA communication system. Herein, as one of the dynamic coding schemes, rate compatible LDPC (RC-LDPC) codes combing OFDM technique are proposed to improve the system reliability for SWA communications. Based on the automatic repeat request (ARQ) mechanism, the proposed SWA OFDM system consists of three important preprocessing: channel state information (CSI) estimator, signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) estimator and RC-LDPC pattern. The design of RC-LDPC codes and SWA channel profile are described. A look-up table (LUT) of RC-LDPC performances for ARQ-SWA system is built via statistical simulation test. For the proposed scheme, we define and derive the sensitivity to channel time variations and the effects of different performance target BER threshold, which are further confirmed via numerical results. It is shown that RC-LDPC codes have good performances with wide range of rates in SWA channels. Finally, coding rate distributions of RC-LDPC codes in different SNR at BER below 10-4 for Xiamen harbor SWA channel are investigated.
Keywords- RC-LDPC codes; Rate compatible; shallow water acoustic channel; underwater acoustic communications
I. INTRODUCTION
The shallow water acoustic (SWA) channel, a typical high bit error rates (BER) channel characterized by poor communication quality and high propagation delay, is one of the most challenging environments for the transmission of digital information [1]. To increase the SWA communication performances, a lot of independent or joint techniques including equalization [2], spatial processing [3], channel coding [4], time reversal techniques [5] and MIMO-OFDM techniques [6] have been adopted in SWA communication design. Because of the coding gain, channel coding techniques have been viewed as one of the necessary elements in different SWA communication system design. Some effective channel coding schemes, such as Turbo codes [7], LDPC codes [8] and RA codes [9], have been investigated in SWA communication recently. However, the fast temporal variations SWA channel [1] needs the temporal variations channel coding scheme at the acceptable cost. Therefore, flexible channel coding rate, adjusted according to different ocean channel characteristics, should be desired in the design of practical error control SWA communication system.

Considering the flexible rate design, rate compatible (RC) codes should be one of the good schemes. RC codes are a nested sequence of codes where the parity bits of higher rate codes are embedded in those of lower rate codes [10]. One of the main advantages of using RC schemes is that all the codes in the sequence can be encoded and decoded using a single encoder/decoder pair [11], which matches the fast temporal variations channel and provides a good choice for SWA channel coding scheme. Recently, the design of RC low density parity check (RC-LDPC) codes using puncturing or extending has obtained much attention [12]. In [12], RC-LDPC codes show good performances with wide range of rates by both punching and extending.
In this paper, we propose the RC-LDPC codes as the SWA dynamic channel coding scheme. The main contributions of this paper are summarized in the following: 1) A ARQ-SWA system using RC-LDPC codes based on OFDM is built; 2) For our proposed scheme, the sensitivity to channel time variations and the effects of BER threshold are discussed; 3) The performances of RC-LDPC codes in the SWA channel are analyzed, and a look-up table (LUT) is built via statistical simulation test; 4) Coding rate distributions of RC-LDPC codes in different SNR at BER below 10-4 for Xiamen harbor SWA channel are given.
II. SHALLOW WATER ACOUSTIC COMMUNICATION SYSTEM USING RC-LDPC CODES
A. SWA Communication System Using RC-LDPC Codes
As shown in Fig. 1, the system will perform the channel state information (CSI) estimation via forward SWA channel (FW-SWA) firstly, and then RC-LDPC pattern can be obtained, which shows the performances of RC-LDPC codes over different SNR level. In the meanwhile, the SNR estimator is working to get a SNR value for encoding. The appropriate coding rate can be used for encoding base on these preprocessing. At the receiver, coding performance evaluation can be readily carried out by comparing BER. If the performances can not reach the BER threshold, an automatic repeat request (ARQ) operating mechanism will work via feedback SWA channel (FB-SWA). The ARQ command includes changing encoding rate from RC-LDPC pattern and adjusting CSI and/or SNR estimation.

III. CONSTRUCTION OF RC-LDPC CODES
A regular LDPC code has parameters (N, K, t, s), which denote the codeword length, information data size, column weight and row weight of the parity check matrix, respectively. A sequential design, the bit filling method, is used to obtain t=3 regular LDPC codes as the mother code for RC-LDPC code family. Usually, two approaches are used to construct simple and efficient RC-LDPC codes: puncturing and extending [10]. In this paper, we will investigate extending approach for SWA channel.

Opposite to puncturing, extending builds RC-LDPC codes from high rates to low rates by adding more parities, which means extending allows bits to be generated only as needed, thus avoiding unnecessary computations at the encoder and the decoder [11]. In that sense, extending is more suitable for the resource constrained SWA channel. We will use the similar expending construction scheme as [11] for SWA channel. The brief description of extending RC-LDPC codes is as bellow [11].
Fig. 3 (a) presents the extending RC-LDPC code structure. The parity check matrix of the highest rate code has M0=N0-K rows and N0 columns with column weight t≥3 (upper left part in Fig.3 (a)). V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we proposed RC-LDPC codes as the dynamic channel coding strategy for SWA communication system to overcome the fast temporal variations channel. The proposed ARQ-SWA system consists of three important preprocessing: CSI estimator, SNR estimator and RC-LDPC pattern. By defining and deriving the sensitivity to channel time variations and the retransmission rate with different BER threshold based on OFDM, performances were analyzed via simulation. Then the design of RC-LDPC codes and the setup of SWA channels were presented. Furthermore, the look-up table was built to fit the feedback mechanism. It was shown that, RC-LDPC codes

constructed using extending demonstrates good performances with wide range of rates in SWA channels. Finally, we investigated the coding rate distribution in different SNR at a given BER level in the sea test SWA channels.
Further research will address several topics, including the hardware implementation, encoding and decoding optimal construction of RC-LDPC codes for SWA channels.
