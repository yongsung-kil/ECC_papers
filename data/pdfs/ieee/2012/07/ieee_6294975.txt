An Efficient Bit-Flipping Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes

Tso-Cho Chen
Department of Avionics China University of Science and Technology
HsinChu, Taiwan chentc@cc.cust.edu.tw

Abstract—In this paper, a new method for improving harddecision bit-flipping (BF) decoding is proposed for low-density parity-check codes. The flipping criterion is derived theoretically from the soft-decision belief-propagation decoding algorithm. The proposed BF decoding algorithm is channel independent and is improved by introducing a more efficient method for computing the reliability of the parity checks. Extensive simulations are provided to demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed algorithm. Simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can achieve about 1.5dB coding gain improvement at
BER = 5 × 10−6 while reducing up to 48% iterations for decoding and maintaining low decoding complexity, compared with the conventional weighted BF algorithm.
Keywords- low-density parity-check codes; bit-flipping decoding; belief-propagation decoding; channel independent
I. INTRODUCTION
The family of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes proposed by Gallager [1] has attracted substantial research interest in the information theory community. LDPC codes can obtain near Shannon limit performance with iterative decoding [2, 3] and has positioned themselves as part of many emerging digital communication systems, such as DVB-S2, WiMAX (IEEE 802.16e), WLAN (802.11.n), and 10GBaseT (802.3an).. They can be decoded using various decoding schemes [1-4] such as hard-decisions, soft-decisions and hybrid decoding schemes. The soft-decisions belief-propagation (BP) decoding algorithm, sum-product algorithm (SPA), was shown to achieve a near-capacity performance [3]. Due to its high complexity, it is impractical for applications. In comparison, the popular min-sum algorithm (MSA) presented in [4] has lower complexity, with comparable performance to SPA. Still, its complexity is too high to be efficiently realized in hardware implementation. The hard-decision weighted bit-flipping (WBF) algorithm [5] strikes a good trade-off between the associated decoding complexity and the achievable performance. The attractive property of the WBF algorithm is that during the each iteration the weighted sum of the same values is computed, resulting in a significantly lower decoding complexity in comparison to the SPA and MSA. A modified WBF (MWBF) algorithm [6], considering the reliability information of received symbol, is presented as a further improvement of WBF algorithm at the expense of sizable

computational increase. An improvement of MWBF algorithm for regular LDPC codes is proposed together with a theoretical expression for the weighting factor α and achieves a noticeable gain over the MWBF algorithm with only a modest increase in computational complexity [7].
The WBF, MWBF and IMWBF algorithms attribute the violation of a particular parity check to only the least reliable bit. In addition, the weighting factor α is usually found by computer simulation and the optimum choice of α varies slightly with the SNR [6, 7]. If the weighting factor α utilized in the MWBF and IMWBF algorithms is not optimum, the error performance may be significantly degraded. Thus in this contribution, the performances are further improved by using the proposed BF decoding algorithm, while avoiding any preprocessing such as finding the optimal weighting factor α of the MWBF and IMWBF algorithms. In addition, the bitflipping criterion of the proposed algorithm is channel independent which is very important in practical situations.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In Section II, The proposed channel independent weighted bit-flipping (CIWBF) decoding algorithm is developed. The simulation results and performance evaluation are provided in Section III. Finally, our conclusion is offered in Section IV.

We notice that the above reliability measure wi, j of the syndrome sum si checking on the jth received bit cˆ j is
different from the reliability measure of the same syndrome sum that is defined by WBF [5], MWBF [6] and IMWBF [7].
The reliability measure wi, j of the syndrome sum si is sums of the reliabilities of all the received bits participating in si
excluding the reliability measure y j of the jth received bit
cˆ j that is checked by si , while reliability measure of si that
is used by WBF, MWBF and IMWBF is only defined by the
least reliable bit. The first part of ej is the sum part of (9),
which contains the reliability information coming from all the
syndrome sums orthogonal on the jth received bit cˆ j but not

including the reliability information of cˆ j . Then ej gives a
weighted reliability measure of the hard-decision received bit
cˆ j . From (9), we see that the more negative the weighted
reliability measure ej , the more syndrome sums orthogonal on cˆ j satisfy the zero parity-check-sum constraint and hence the more reliable cˆ j is. In this case, cˆ j is less likely to be
different from the transmitted code bit c j and hence is most
likely to be error-free. Conversely, the more positive the
weighted reliability measure ej , the more syndrome sums orthogonal on cˆ j fail to satisfy the zero-parity-checksum constraint and hence the less reliable cˆ j is. In this case, cˆ j is
most likely erroneous and should be flipped. Form over the real-number system the n-tuple

whose components give the reliability measures of the bits in
the hard-decision received sequence cˆ = (cˆ1, cˆ2 ,..., cˆn ) . The
received bit in cˆ , say cˆ j , that has the largest ej in the
weighted reliability profile e of cˆ is the most unreliable bit in cˆ and should be flipped in a BF decoding algorithm. From (9), we see that the proposed flipping function is channel independent. Therefore, the proposed BF algorithm is referred to as the channel independent weighted BF (CIWBF) decoding algorithm. The detailed steps of the CIWBF decoding algorithm are as follows:

CIWBF decoding algorithm
Initialization: Set k = 0, cˆ(0) = cˆ , and the maximum number of iterations to kmax . Compute and store wi, j by using (10) for 1 ≤ i ≤ m . 1. Compute the syndrome s(k ) = cˆ(k )HT of cˆ(k ) . If
s(k ) = 0 , stop decoding and output cˆ(k ) as the decoded
codeword; otherwise go to Step 2.
2. Compute the reliability profile e(k ) of cˆ(k ) on the basis
of (9). Go to Step 3.
3. Identify the bit position j for which ej is largest. Go to
Step 4.
4. Flip the jth received bit of cˆ(k ) to obtain a modified received sequence cˆ(k+1) . Go to Step 5.
5. k ← k +1 . If k > kmax , stop decoding; otherwise, go
to Step 1.

IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a channel independent weighted BF decoding algorithm to improve the performance of the WBF algorithm

and its variants for decoding LDPC codes has been presented. The flipping criterion which is derived theoretically from the soft-decision probabilistic BP decoding algorithm need not any preprocessing such as finding the optimal weighting factor α of the MWBF and IMWBF algorithms. Numerical results show that the proposed CIWBF algorithm outperforms the conventional WBF algorithm and its variants, where gains in both performance and average decoding time, and maintains low decoding complexity of the hard-decision BF algorithm.
