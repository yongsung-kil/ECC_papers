Two-Step Construction Method of Protograph-Based AR4JA Codes in Deep Space Communication

Gu Lv, Mingchuan Yang*, Member, IEEE, Hui Li, Ming Li, Xiaofeng Liu
Communication Research Center Harbin Institute of Technology Harbin, Heilongjiang Province, 150001, China

Abstract—This paper discusses the construction of the Accumulate-Repeat-4-Jagged-Accumulate(AR4JA) codes which is suitable for the deep-space communication based on the protograph. Two-step construction method of designing the AR4JA codes whose minimum distance grows linearly with block size, is proposed. Two-step expansion improves the traditional PEG with adding the ACE algorithm for placing edges in a protograph-based code, which brings down the error floor. Compared to the traditional random expansion and the one step expansion like Progressive Edge-Growth(PEG), the two-step construction algorithm aims at constructing the AR4JA codes with high performance and low complexity. Verified by the simulation, the BER performance of the two-step expansion method is similar to the ideal AR4JA codes in the deep space channel condition. With the same Eb/N0, the two-step construction method shows the lower BER than random construction method and one-step extension method through the simulation results.
Keywords-Deep space communication; Protograph codes; AR4JA codes, two-step expansion
I. INTRODUCTION
The characteristics of the deep space communication system are far distance, large transmission delay, serious signal attenuation and the relatively wide channel bandwidth. The main approach is to use the highly efficient channel coding techniques. Therefore, the use of the channel coding is the inevitable choice of the deep space communications. There has been 2 class code words closed to the Shannon limit, namely, Turbo codes and Low Density Parity Check(LDPC)codes[1]. With development of channel coding technology, Turbo codes and LDPC codes are widely used in deep space communication. It is proved that LDPC codes have the excellent performance with the long code length[2]. Considering that the code with long length is suitable for the deep space communication, in recent years, LDPC codes are widely used in the deep space communication.
LDPC codes can show very low SNR thresholds in the error rate performance. However, there exists a tradeoff between SNR threshold and error floor performance. Codes ensembles based on the protograph have been shown to have the properties a minimum distance linearly with block length, which is a powerful feature for floor performance[3]. What is more, the balance between excellent performance and implement complexity is also dilemma. So, there exist some problems that the encoding of LDPC codes with good

performance is complex and the implement of the decoding is difficult.
Accumulate-Repeat-4-Jagged-Accumulate(AR4JA) code is a subclass of LDPC codes, which can be regarded as the special kind of LDPC codes. Compared to the traditional LDPC codes, AR4JA codes is high structural, which is more suitable for high-speed hardware implement. The construction of the check matrix can be summarized as a series, so the encoding and decoding of different code rate can be achieved by an encoder and a decoder. The iterative decoding threshold changed little when the code rate increases. High punch rate and pro-code makes the iterative decoding threshold of AR4JA codes lower. For these reasons, the Consultative Committee for Space Data Systems (CCSDS) selects a family of AR4JA codes for inclusion in the channel coding standard for deep space applications[4]. Even though CCSDS gives the ideal curves of the AR4JA codes, the construction of the AR4JA codes is variable. The existing construction methods are random expansion, PEG expansion and density evolution theory. The random construction has good performance but it is not structural, which is difficult for implement. One step expansion like PEG algorithm and density evolution theory has good structure for implement with the cost of BER performance. Thus, it is worthy of studying the construction method of the AR4JA codes with excellent BER performance and low encoding complexity.
This paper discusses the construction of the AR4JA codes based on protograph. In Section II, we introduce the definition and the representation of protograph-based codes and the evolution of the AR4JA codes. In Section III, we summarize the family of AR4JA codes and introduce a new construction method, two-step expansion. In Section IV, the comparison of different channel codes and different construction methods of the AR4JA codes with the deep space channel condition are given.

The construction of the protograph AR4JA codes can be summarized: At first, a basic model of protograph should be designed according to the performance requirement of AR4JA codes, and then the protograph is copied N times, and finally the sides connecting the same type nodes should be replaced in a certain way. Seen from the construction of the derived graph, the code length of the derived graph is N times than the original graph. In addition, the different ways of expansion of the protograph would not only affect the performance of the AR4JA codes, but also impact the complexity of hardware implementation. So, the expansion method of protograph is worthy of study.

III. TWO-STEP EXPANSION CONSTRUCTS AR4JA CODES
According to the construction process of the protograph AR4JA codes, only the design of protograph is not enough to get the AR4JA codes with superior performance. In order to design an appropriate length of AR4JA code, the protograph need to be extended. The expansion algorithm will affect the AR4JA gain and error code platforms. In this paper, the protograph AR4JA code will be expanded in two steps. At first, the main idea is to maximize the girth of the AR4JA code, to reduce the decoding performance degradation because of short loop in the Tanner graph. In the second step is to make sure that the expanded AR4JA codes have a certain rules of

Based on the protograph, the construction of the AR4JA code can use the PEG algorithm[10] and the ACE algorithm[11]. The PEG algorithm is used to map the protograph’s first expansion. Expand each node in the protograph with a small factor(4 or 8). The first step has a strong random, which just aims at limiting the protograph’s girth as large as possible. The second expansion is to use the cyclic shift matrix with unit matrix and zero matrix to instead the ‘1’ and ‘0’ in the base matrix after the first expansion, which can use the improved ACE algorithm.
A. First expansion
In the H matrix, the existence of short loop will affect the performance of the AR4JA decoding largely. Many coding algorithms are based on the idea of making the loop of AR4JA codes as large as possible. The PEG algorithm, which is one of the most classic algorithm, can construct a AR4JA code with particular degree distribution and large girth, having good BER performance. Of course, the PEG algorithm also has some inadequate that the PEG algorithm can only be used to construct a random code, which cannot be decoded in parallel structure. In order to compensate for this shortcoming, this paper proposes a algorithm based on the improved PEG method, so that the H matrix not only has a big girth, but also has a structure of quasi-cyclic[12].
The classic PEG algorithm has the following deficiencies: First, the H matrix constructed in PEG algorithm does not take in account the degree constraint of the check nodes. Even if the matrix can meet the degree distribution constraints of the bit node, still can not meet degree distribution constraints of check node. Second, the H matrix constructed in the classic PEG algorithm is random, which is mainly used for theoretical research. Third, the traditional PEG algorithm is based on the traditional Tanner to construct H matrix, which can not be applied to the expansion of protgraph.
The traditional PEG algorithm is improved to meet the expansion of protograph. In the case of the same signal to noise ratio, compared to the PEG expansion and the random construction, the bit rate of

the AR4JA code constructed in the two-step expansion is the smallest in the Fig. 7. The main reason is that the short loop (4 or 6) can not be avoided in the random method, which would affect the performance of AR4JA codes. Although the short loops can be eliminated effectively in the PEG expansion, the connectivity between each loop can not be guaranteed. The PEG algorithm in the two-step expansion can extend the girth effectively, and the ACE algorithm can ensure the connectivity between the loops. Finally, the H matrix not only has the quasicyclic nature, but also has a less complexity in hardware implementation than the random construction method.
V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, the design idea and the construction methods of the AR4JA code based on the protograph are introduced, and the two-step expansion based on the protograph is proposed in detail. As a subclass of AR4JA code, the protograph code, AR4JA codes, are shown to have excellent performance. Twostep expansion method is used to extend the protograph to get he quasi-cyclic AR4JA codes, which has the low complexity in hardware implementation. The ACE algorithm used in the second expansion has a relatively low value of EMD, which can be enhanced for the further improvement. The BER performance of the best, compared to the PEG algorithm and the random expansion.
