Optimal Design of a Gallager B Noisy Decoder for Irregular LDPC Codes
S. M. Sadegh Tabatabaei Yazdi, Chu-Hsiang Huang, and Lara Dolecek

Abstract—In this letter, we study the performance of a noisy Gallager B decoder used to decode irregular low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes. We derive the final bit error rate (BER) as a function of both the transmission noise and processing errors. We allow different components of the decoder associated with certain computational units (i.e., bit and check nodes of varying degrees) to have different processing errors. We formulate an optimization problem to distribute available processing resources across different components of a noisy decoder to achieve minimal BER. Simulations demonstrate that the optimal resource allocation derived from our analysis outperforms uninformed (random) resource assignment.
Index Terms—LDPC decoder, Gallager B algorithm, noisy hardware, irregular codes, density evolution.
I. INTRODUCTION
I T has now been widely accepted that due to demands for high integration rate, miniature area, and low power consumption, emerging nano-devices will be inherently unreliable. Characterizing the reliability of systems built out of unreliable hardware components has become a particularly important issue in implementing modern data processing algorithms [1]. The study of circuits made out of faulty gates dates back to von Neumann’s model of transient errors [2] Stochastic computing [3] cleverly interprets unreliable devices as noisy communication channels/networks to harness the analytical toolbox of communications theory.
Recent exciting works have already investigated the use of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes and their decoders implemented on noisy hardware. Fault tolerance of faulty memories was recently improved with a novel use of LDPC codes in [4]. A combinatorial characterization of a one step majority logic LDPC decoder was presented in [5]. The capacity and certain concentration results for a noisy LDPC message passing decoder were computed in [6]. Most recently, LDPC coded schemes and their extensions using space-time coding were proposed in [7] and [8] to improve reliability of faulty circuits. Our preliminary results on regular LDPC codes implemented on noisy hardware were reported in [9].
In this letter, following the works of [4] – [6], we study the performance of irregular LDPC codes on a noisy Gallager B decoder. The processors with different processing error rates are assigned to different computational units (i.e., variable nodes and check nodes) of a noisy Gallager B decoder. V. CONCLUSION
We derived a simple approximation of the final BER of a Gallager B faulty decoder and showed that the optimal assignment of processors is the solution of a linear programming. Simulation results support the analysis. Our results may serve as a starting point in future investigation of optimal resource allocation for data processing algorithms running on noisy hardware characterized by upcoming fabrication technologies.

IV. SIMULATION
We performed MATLAB simulations to show the effective-
ness of our decoder design. While the analysis so far was for
the infinite block length case, benefits of informed resource
allocation can already be seen in the finite-length case. We used two irregular codes from [10]. Code 1 has 9972 variable nodes of which 9141 nodes have degree j1 = 3 and 831 nodes have degree j2 = 9. The code has 4986 check nodes all with degree 7. Code 2 has 1920 variable nodes of which 640 nodes have degree j1 = 14 and 1280 nodes have degree j2 = 18. This code has 5760 check nodes in total, 1280 nodes with degree 4 and 4480 nodes with degree 6. We ran the codes
on a noisy LDPC decoder with two different processing error parameters, q1 = 10−5 and q2 = 10−4. Channel error was p0 = 10−4.
