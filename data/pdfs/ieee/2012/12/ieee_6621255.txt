Improved Min-Sum-Plus-Correction-Factor Algorithm using Stopping Node for LDPC Decoding
Gang Wang!, IEEE Member, Nguyen Thi Dieu Linhl, Georgia Rugumira2 'Communication Research Center, Harbin Institute of Technology, 150001 Harbin, China
2 Dar es Salaam Institute of Technology, P.O. Box 2958, Dar es Salaam, Tanzania E-mail: Dieulinh79@gmail.com;Gwang51@hit.edu.cn and Grugumira@gmail.com.

Abstract- Low-Density-Parity-Check Code (LDPC) codes have received great attention recently because of the excellent error­ correcting capability and optional error correct code. A new method base on Min-Sum-Plus-Correction-Factor Algorithm using stopping node to reduce computing complexity on Low-Density-Parity-Check Code decoding algorithm is presented in this paper. The improved method shows how to make early decisions to reduce computation complexity of LDPC decoding algorithm in the next iteration under QPSK systems. Simulation results show that computational complexity of LDPC decoding using Stopping node criterion can reduce up to 6 times with maintaining quality level.
Keywords- Low-density-Parity-check Codes, Min-Sum Algorithm, Stopping node, Iterative decoding, Min-Sum-Plus­ Correction-Factor Algorithm
I. INTRODUCTION
One of the many tools available for achieving reliable data transmission in communication system is coding for error correction. Low-Density-Parity-Check (LDPC) Codes are a class of linear block codes which provide near-capacity performance on a large collection of data transmission and storage channels while simultaneously admitting implementable decoder. Originally invented by Gallager in 1962[1], but due to the complexity of Decoding Algorithms that requires large volume of calculations and because of the technology that was not mature enough for their practical implementation, they were forgotten for about 30 years. Until the 1990s, along with the introduction of Turbo codes in 1993[2], the study of LDPC codes were resurrected with the work of Mackay and Neal in 1996[3]. The problem of LDPC codes had been solved with the development of computing technology in processing and data storage. LDPC codes can achieve very low bit error rates for low signal to noise ratio (SNR) application, so they can achieve close to the Shannon limit performance. 30% capacity increases over Digital Video Broadcasting-Satellite (DVB-S) [4] in identical transmission conditions is one of the advantages of using LDPC codes when integrated with adaptive coding modulation (ACM) and variable coding modulation (VCM) methods. It has been optimized by advanced order modulation systems for satellite signals for instance the amplitude phase shift keying (APSK). The applications of LDPC codes are not restricted to Digital Video Broadcasting Satellite second Generation (DVB-S2) [4], but they are used in digital communications and adopted by the higher standards such as 10 Gigabit Ethernet (10 GBASE-T) [5], [6],

WLAN, WIMAX. With a low density check matrix, LDPC codes can be
decoded by an iterative decoding algorithm as Belief Propagation Algorithm or so call Message Passing Algorithm in which the calculations are done in probability domain. How to reduce the complexity of the decoder in actual application has become the focus of research in the field of channel coding. To reduce computational complexity and achieve the better BER performance by decreasing the number of iterations, Sum Product Algorithm and Min Sum Algorithm were introduced and they are done in log domain. Iterative decoding of binary LDPC codes using the Sum Product Algorithm has recently been shown to approach the capacity of the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel within 0.0045dB.
Where there is a high SNR, the Min-Sum-Plus Decoding Algorithm will suffer a greater degree of decoding performance decline, so in this paper, the Min-Sum-Plus­ Correction-Factor (MSPCF) decoding algorithm is adopted.
In "Sleeping node decoder" method presented by Bresnan [7], the reached nodes can make early decisions in the next iteration to reduce the number of calculations. The Elimination Node method to make node stops according to
Log likelihood had presented by Lin C Y et af. [8] and Bresnan R et al. [9]. Considering modifications using Early
Stopping (ES) based on Cross Entropy (CE) coefficient were
derived by Hagenauer J et al. [10]. Shao R Y et al. [11]
developed a method based on Hard Decision Aided (RDA), and follow Sign-Change-Ratio (SCR) for Turbo codes have also been proposed to be applied to LDPC decoder.
In this paper, an improved Min-Sum-Plus-Correction­ Factor decoding algorithm for decoding LDPC codes including Stopping Node Algorithm is proposed. This method significantly speeds up decoding process, simplifies the decoding scheme and reduces the times of iterations.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows: Section 2 introduces the LDPC codes. Section 3 describes decoding algorithms for LDPC codes and the proposed Min-Sum Plus­ Correction-Factor Stopping Node algorithm. Section 4 presents simulation results. Finally, Section 5 concludes the paper.

3. MIN-SUM-PLUS-CORRECTION-FACTOR STOPPING NODE ALGORITHM

In this paper, we take a different proposed to improve the performance and decrease processing time of MSPCF algorithm over an AWGN channel under QPSK scheme. The main idea of our proposed is to consider the sign of Log Likelihood Ratio on the bit codes.
During the decoding, with a defmite code, the quality attained depends on the number of iterations executed. The decoding processing time and the quality increase as the number of iterations raises, nevertheless, this relationship is not linear. When performing building code in random or structure mode, it cannot avoid the appearance of finite cycles. This means that, while decoding on the graph, node inter-message transmission rely exclusively on the time taken by message depending nodes, however updating information at any node depends on the node itself. The error floor appears when the quality graph of the code is in regression.
However, the decoding quality of LDPC iterative decoding algorithm has finite length is restricted with the short cycles on bilateral Tanner graph. During iterative decoding algorithms, when transmitting messages from bit node to check node and contrary, frequently error handling appears within short period. So when developing codes, for the case of short cycles must be avoided, particularly in 4 cycle's case. Therefore, this can improve the quality of LDPC decoder iteration through the removal of the effect of short cycles when stopping node method is used. The new method can be explained as follows.

V. CONCLUSIONS
A low complexity approach by means of stopping node criterion is projected to decrease the number of computation of LDPC decoder that was introduced in this article. Comparing performances simulated under QPSK system showed that the computation time is reduced significantly, nearly 6 times than conventional algorithms with maintaining processing quality where losses are insignificant and acceptable. The proposed algorithm can save power, reduce transmission delay and processing cost.
