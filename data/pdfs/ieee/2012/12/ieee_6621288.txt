Abstract- In this paper, we designed fast structure generator 
and parity check matrices of Low Density Parity Check 
(LDPC) codes using a random degree distribution termed as 
the standard stationary random degree distribution. 
The 
achievable Bit Error Ratio (BER) performance of designed 
codes is improved with the aid of the novel designed matrices. 
For example, the designed 1/3-rate LDPC(1200,3600) code 
attains BER  10-5 in excess of an Eb /No value of 1.5dB for 
transmission over the A WGN channel and above 3.5 dB over 
the uncorrelated Rayleigh channel when using Quadrature 
Phase-Shift Keying (QPSK) modulation and a maximum of 
1=20 decoding iterations.

parity bits are recovered with lower probability in 
comparison 
with the information bits having 
higher 
representing frequency in parity bits, when transmission

performance figure. To avoid achieving the error floor, we 
should use the uniform distribution for information bits of 
LDPC code words. We wi11 discus this issue as follows:

CONCLUSIONS 
Our novel LDPC codes designed in this paper can achieve 
the same low BER < 10-5 at lower EblNo in comparison to 
the regular-LDPC designed by Richardson and Yang 
methods. Moreover, our designed LDPC codes having a 
PCM matrix derived directly from GM matrix without using 
the high complex calculation operations as proposed in [11], 
[12]. For the future works, we will continue to optimize our 
LDPC codes by using the integrated channel code systems, 
in which the LDPC codes will be designed to decode 
iteratively with other channel codes such as BICM-ID, turbo 
codes or the soft demodulators,
