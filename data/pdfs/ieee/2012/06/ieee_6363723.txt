Design of Bilayer QC-LDPC Codes for Decode-and Forward based Cooperative Relaying Communication

Lingjun Kong , Kyeong Jin Kim , Kyung Sup Kwak
UWB Wireless Communications Research Center, Inha University, Incheon, Korea Email:ljk.kong@gmail.com,kyeong.j.kim@hotmail.com, kskwak@inha.ac.kr

AbstractРђћIn this paper, we study the problem of devising distributed bilayer quasi-cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC-LDPC) codes for cooperative relaying communications. One theorem in designing the bilayer QC-LDPC code without small stopping sets and small girth is proposed, which significantly simplifies the bilayer QC-LDPC codes design. Based on the proposed theorem and the two-hop decode-and-forward (DF) relaying protocol, in which the relay node is used to assist the source to transmit extra parity bits to the destination, a new design method for a distributed irregular bilayer QC-LDPC is developed. It is demonstrated that the overall performance can be improved by using the proposed approach in DF-based cooperative relaying communications. Finally, it is shown that the proposed construction method also leads bilayer QC-LDPC codes to have a bigger minimum distance.
Index Terms---Decode-and-forward protocol, bilayer quasi-cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC-LDPC) codes, stopping set.
I. INTRODUCTION
Relay network has attracted significant attention due to the inherent diversity gain of the user cooperation diversity [1-2] or cooperative diversity [3-5], which contributes to higher capacity and more reliable communication systems. It becomes more attractive in the LTE-Advanced [6-7] and WiMAX [8] standard development. In the cooperative relaying system, where a relay node is used to assist the source to transmit information symbols to destination, there are two fundamental relaying strategies: amplify-and-forward (AF) and decode-and-forward (DF) [9-10].
Recently, the design of distributed channel codes which can further provide a significant diversity and coding gain has attracted considerable attention in cooperative relaying systems. Previous research works on distributed coding/decoding schemes for relay channels can be found in [11-18].In [11-13], coded codeword is used in the transmission, which increases the bandwidth requirements linearly with the number of codewords. In [11], two codewords are generated from two separate channel encoders, and then decoded by the separate channel decoders. A resulting new single codeword is generated by combining these two decoded sequences. In addition, puncturing, product codes, or concatenations are usually used. A technique in designing bilayer LDPC codes are proposed in [14-15], which generally contains upper and lower check nodes in the corresponding Tanner graph. The

extra parity bits generated by relay node are also transmitted to the destination, such that the bilayer LDPC code can be formed at the destination by combining the codeword from the source. However, the overall optimization algorithm remains complicated to implement since the authors evaluate only the error density probability function at each decoding step of the belief propagation (BP) algorithm which is a multidimensional problem [16].
The distributed coding schemes must be jointly considered to make the channel codes perform well for both of the source-relay and relay-destination links. To solve these challenging problems, a distributed irregular bilayer QC-LDPC scheme is developed in the cooperative relaying system, which requires much less design complexity by using only shift registers.
In this paper, we restrict our attention to DF-based cooperative relaying systems with half-duplex relaying protocol. Inspired by the bilayer LDPC codes structures [15-17], one theorem on the bilayer QC-LDPC codes is provided. Based on the bilayer Tanner graph, it can be verified that the parity check matrix can have no small girth and small stopping sets. This theorem makes possible not only a simple code design method, but also outstanding performance improvement. The main contributions of this paper are as follows: (1) A distributed irregular bilayer QC-LDPC scheme based on the proposed theorem is developed jointly, which performs well in DF-based cooperative relaying communications. Since this distributed design scheme prevents the small stopping sets and small girth, occurrence of a smaller minimum distance can be reduced. From these properties, our construction method also leads bilayer QC-LDPC codes to have a bigger minimum distance. (2) Simulation results confirm the bit error rate (BER) performance of the proposed structured irregular bilayer QC-LDPC code.
The paper is organized as follows. We first present the bilayer Tanner graph and DF-based cooperative relay system model in Section II. We propose a new theoretical design method based on the bilayer Tanner graph in Section III. In Section IV, the simulation results are provided. Finally, conclusions are drawn in Section V.

V. CONCLUSIONS
According to the provided theorem and corollaries, a distributed irregular bilayer QC-LDPC scheme for DF-based cooperative relaying communications has been proposed. Since the proposed design method prevents the small stopping sets

and small girth, it generates a bigger minimum distance. Thus, the proposed construction method enables bilayer QC-LDPC codes to have a bigger minimum distance. The simulation results have verified that the resulting bilayer QC-LDPC codes can effectively avoid specified stopping sets and small girth and achieve an improved BER performance.
