Anytime Reliability of Systematic LDPC Convolutional Codes
Leefke Do¨ssel, Lars K. Rasmussen, Ragnar Thobaben, and Mikael Skoglund School of Electrical Engineering and the ACCESS Linnaeus Center KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm, Sweden {leefke, lkra, ragnart, skoglund}@kth.se

Abstract—We propose a LDPC Convolutional Code ensemble together with an expanding-window message-passing decoder that asymptotically have anytime properties when used for streaming transmission on the binary erasure channel. We show analytically that the decoding erasure probability of these codes decays exponentially over decoding delay and determine the corresponding anytime exponents.
I. INTRODUCTION
For delay-sensitive real-time applications, e.g., control-overnetworks, communication reliability requirements are often in conflict with corresponding delay constraints. Such applications typically require interactive and causal communication where information/observations are periodically reported to a receiver/controller over a noisy channel; and in response, actions are determined and executed. The reliability of previously received blocks may influence the accuracy of the receiver/controller action at the current time instance. Therefore the encoding and decoding processes necessarily involve the entire current streams of causal information blocks and received blocks, respectively. The communication problem is thus fundamentally different to classic point-to-point transmission.
The concept of anytime information theory was developed by Sahai [1], [2] to provide a fundamental framework of such reliable causal communication of bit streams through noisy channels. Within this framework anytime reliability plays a vital role. If the decoding erasure probability of block j decays exponentially with the decoding delay d(t, j) = t − j to the present block at time t, then the channel code is anytime reliable. As the decoding delay increases, the reliability improves.
Since the stream of information is causally encoded anytime codes are necessarily tree codes. A class of nonlinear anytime tree codes for interactive communications were proved to exist in [3]. Similar constructions were further developed in [4], and also used in [1], [2] to prove theoretical results. More recently it was shown in [5] that random linear tree codes are anytime reliable. A more structured class of anytime random linear codes was proposed in [6]. It was shown that suitably constrained random linear causal codes are anytime reliable

with high probability. The codes are time-invariant, and exhibit Toeplitz generator and parity check matrices.
In this paper we investigate anytime reliability of LDPC Convolutional Codes (LDPC-CCs) with message-passing decoding. LDPC-CCs are the convolutional counterpart of LDPC block codes and were first introduced in [7]. Recently a sliding-window decoder was proposed providing efficient trade-offs between decoding latency and error rate for terminated LDPC-CCs [8]. Moreover the decoding latency can be set and changed on-the-fly as required [9].
In contrast, we consider non-terminated LDPC-CCs with increasing memory to enable causal anytime tree codes. We propose an ensemble of protograph-based LDPC-CCs that together with an expanding-window message-passing decoder ensure anytime reliability in the asymptotic case. We formally show anytime properties of systematic LDPC-CCs with message-passing decoding over the binary erasure channel (BEC) through density evolution and determine the corresponding anytime exponents.
The paper is organized as follows. In Section II we introduce anytime channel coding over the BEC, and in Section III we discuss LDPC-CCs and their protograph description. The construction of our proposed protograph-based systematic LDPCCCs is described in Section IV, while the asymptotic analysis of its anytime reliability properties is detailed in Section V. The accuracy of the determined anytime exponents is assessed through numerical examples in Section VI.

V. ASYMPTOTIC ANALYSIS
A. P-EXIT analysis of the expanding window scheme
To show anytime reliability of our proposed code structure, we now analyze the average performance of the protograph ensemble with expanding-window message-passing in the limit of infinite block lengths (M → ∞) and an infinite number of decoder iterations. The decoding erasure performance over time is evaluated using the P-EXIT algorithm [11] working on the matrices IAv,t and IEv,t containing the mutual information of the messages sent between variable nodes and check nodes. Note that for the BEC the P-EXIT analysis is equivalent to multi-edge density evolution.

The entries of IEv,t and IAv,t in position (i, j) at time t when k iterations are performed are denoted as IEk v,t(i, j) and IAk v,t(i, j). The vector of a-posteriori decoding erasure probabilities at time t for a code of rate 1/2 is given as PAPP,t = [PAPP,t(1), ..., PAPP,t(2t)] where PAPP,t(j) denotes the decoding erasure probability of variable node j for the protograph at time t. For the BEC PAPP,t = 1 − IAPP,t. The behavior of the decoding erasure probability of one particular
variable node (index j) over time is described with the vector PAjPP,[1,t] = [PAPP,1(j), PAPP,2(j), ..., PAPP,t(j)].
In every time step t the decoding window can be described by its underlying protograph B[1,t]. Through P-EXIT analysis a vector PAPP,t of a-posteriori decoding erasure probabilities is obtained. From these vectors we can determine the a-posteriori
decoding erasure probability of one particular variable node over time as PAjPP,[1,t].

VI. NUMERICAL EXAMPLES
To validate the derivation of the anytime exponent, we compare the slopes of the asymptotic performance of the proposed protograph structure obtained by P-EXIT analysis with the slope determined by the anytime exponent. Fig. 3 shows the asymptotic performance, obtained by P-EXIT analysis, as a function of the delay for the first three code blocks encoded with the proposed protograph for x = 1 starting from the point in time where the code block appears at the decoder for the first time. It is clear from Fig. 3 that the slope of the decoding erasure probability obtained with P-EXIT analysis corresponds perfectly with the theoretically determined anytime exponent − log( ). Moreover the decoding erasure probability of the different blocks is identical, confirming Eqn. VII. CONCLUSION
We have proposed an LDPC convolutional code ensemble where the decoding erasure probability of an expandingwindow message-passing decoder is shown to decay exponentially with decoding delay in the asymptotic case of infinite block length and number of iterations. The corresponding anytime exponents are determined, thus demonstrating the code’s anytime reliability under message-passing decoding. Since the design takes important aspects of finite-length codes into account the proposed code structures is also suitable for practical implementation. This is further verified in an upcoming paper.
