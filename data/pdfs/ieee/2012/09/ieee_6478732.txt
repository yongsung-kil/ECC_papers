Two Efficient Algorithms Based on Majority-Logic and Min-Sum Algorithms for LDPC Codes

Zuotao Zhang, Yibo Fang，Guanghui Liu
School of Electronic Engineering University of Electronic and Technology of China
Chengdu, China zhangzt12@126.com

Abstract—Recently, an iterative soft information reliability-based majority-logic (ISRB-MLG) decoding algorithm was proposed for Euclidean geometry LDPC (EG-LDPC) and Projective geometry LDPC (PG-LDPC) codes. This paper first presents an iterative soft information reliability-based min-sum (ISRB-MS) decoding algorithm based on ISRB-MLG and traditional minsum decoding algorithm. The performance of ISRB-MS algorithm is greatly improved with a little more complexity than ISRB-MLG algorithm, it mainly needs integer additions and logical operations. Different from traditional min-sum decoding algorithm, the extrinsic information transfer of ISRB-MS algorithm is simplified, we use the full information that all check nodes pass rather than the extrinsic information to update the bit nodes information. Then a modified iterative soft information reliability-based min-sum (MISRB-MS) decoding algorithm is proposed. Compared to (ISRB-MS) algorithm, MISRB-MS algorithm multiplies a scaling factor, so it needs some extra multiplication, but it achieves better performance. Simulation results show that the two algorithms achieve excellent performance and fast convergence rate, they only needs about 10 iterations, if an appropriate scaling factor is chosen, the performance of MISRB-MS algorithm very closes to that of sumproduct algorithm (SPA).
Keywords-EG-LDPC; PG-LDPC; min-sum decoding; iterative soft reliability-based; majority-logic algorithm
I. INTRODUCTION (HEADING 1)
LDPC codes with excellent performance and low complexity were first proposed in 1962 by Gallager [1]. However, they were not attracted attention in that time due to technical limitations until they were rediscovered in the late 1990s [2]-[7]. Due to their perfect performance and low complexity, they become a hot topic for researchers. In codeword construction, encoding, decoding and applications, researchers have made a great deal of effort. Now many efficient LDPC codes have been constructed and a variety of decoding algorithms also have been proposed. These different decoding algorithms make good trade-offs between decoding performance and complexity.
The decoding algorithms of LDPC codes include harddecoding and soft-decoding algorithms. Hard-decoding algorithms main include majority-logical (MLG) decoding algorithm [8], [9], bit-flipping (BF) decoding algorithm [1], weighted MLG and BF decoding algorithms [10] and theirs

variants [11]-[13]. These algorithms are easy to implement, but usually their performance is poor. Compared to harddecoding algorithms, soft-decoding algorithms can achieve better error performance with higher complexity. Currently the best soft-decoding algorithm of LDPC codes is sum-product algorithm (SPA), which is also called belief propagation (BP) algorithm [14]. In order to reduce the complexity, several variants of BP algorithms such as min-sum [15], offset or normalized BP-Based [16] algorithms have been proposed. These variants greatly reduce complexity with a little or no performance degradations.
There are two types finite geometry codes called Euclidean geometry LDPC (EG-LDPC) and Projective geometry LDPC (PG-LDPC) constructed by Yu Kou [10].Their parity-check matrix has a loop or quasi-cyclic structure, so their encoding is very simple and they are a class of one-step majority-logical decoding codes. Recently, a novel algorithm named iterative soft reliability-based majority-logic (ISRB-MLG) [17] decoding algorithm has been proposed based on MLG decoding algorithm for EG-LDPC and PG-LDPC codes. The performance of ISRB-MLG decoding algorithm is very good for the two LDPC codes. In this paper we first propose an iterative soft reliability-based min-sum (ISRB-MS) decoding algorithms based on ISRB-MLG and traditional min-sum decoding algorithm. Then a modified ISRB-MS (MISRB-MS) decoding algorithm is presented.
This paper is arranged as follows. We first review and analysis the ISRB-MLG decoding algorithm in section II. In section III, we propose ISRB-MS and MISRB-MS decoding algorithms. Section IV presents the simulation results and analysis the complexity and the memory requirements. In last section, we make a conclusion

The simulation results show that the novel algorithms perform very well. ISRS-MS decoding algorithm achieves 0.5-0.8 dB improvement than ISRB-MLG algorithm with slightly increased complexity. The performances of MISRBMS algorithm very closes to SPA , it is only worse about 0.1dB than SPA and its convergence rate is also very fast, compared to more than 40 iterations of SPA, it only needs about 10 iterations. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper,the two novel algorithms that are proposed based on ISRB-MLG algorithm for finite geometry LDPC

ISRB-MLG algorithm is based on MLG decoding algorithm,so ISRB-MS and MISRBMS algorithm both achieve excellent performance for onestep majority-logical decoding codes but may not be suitable for other types of LDPC codes. Compared to min-sum or offset BP-Based decoding algorithm, we use the full messages rather the extrinsic messages to update the bit nodes messages,which greatly reduce the complexity with slightly degradations in performance. Simulation results show the error performance is affected by scaling factor , but it will be no significant degradations within a certain range . The optimal scaling factor can be confirmed by simulations or discrete density evolution.
