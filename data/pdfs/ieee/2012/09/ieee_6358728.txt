A 45 GS/s Optical Soft-Decision Front-end
M. N. Sakib, M. Moayedi and O. Liboiron-Ladouceur
Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, McGill University, Montreal, Quebec, Canada. e-mail address: meer.sakib@mail.mcgill.ca

Abstract- A low complexity 45 GS/s soft-decision optical front-end is demonstrated for low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoders. A net coding gain of 7.06 dB at 10-7 is experimentally achieved for 45 Gb/s NRZ-OOK optical signal.
I. INTRODUCTION
The exponential growth of internet video, social media , and the general deployment of broadband access networks is driving network service providers to upgrade their systems to more spectrally efficient data communication to 100 Gb/s per channel, and beyond. Migrating to higher transmission rates comes along with challenges such as signal degradation due to fiber dispersion and nonlinearity. The deployment of advanced error-correction technologies in optical links is considered to be a cost-effective method in combating system impairments [1]. Conventionally hard-decision decoders for directdetection are used, where a limiting amplifier digitizes the analog signal converted by the photodiode followed by a transimpedance amplifier. A single bit of information is provided corresponding to the signal level susceptible to system noise and other signal impairments. Recently, softdecision low density parity check (LDPC) codes have been investigated because of their near Shannon limit performance [1-5]. However, research activities related to LDPC algorithms for optical communication focus on simulation studies. Indeed, few demonstrations of soft-decision front-end circuits have been achieved due to their complex design requirements and their considerable power consumption [2, 3]. To alleviate the challenges of conventional electrical front-ends for decoders, optical front-ends have been suggested [4, 6, 7].
Based on a proof-of-concept we have introduced in [4], we investigate the architectural implementation at sampling rates more conformed to today’s network capabilities using a simplified approached in the datapath enabling an increase in bandwidth. The proposed optical front-end has an advantage over other proposed optical front-ends in [6, 7] as the harddecision datapath is maintained with minimum signal-to-noise (SNR) degradation while a separate soft-decision datapath provides addition bits building up the confidence level of the hard-decision bit.

In the soft-decision datapath, the electrical signal from the power splitter is fed into two limiting amplifiers used as comparators with different threshold levels providing the upper and lower confidence levels, respectively. The threshold values VTh1 and VTh0 are tuned with precision power supplies with a tuning resolution in the order of 1 mV. Threshold levels VTh1 and VTh0 are respectively placed at the lower tail of distribution of the “ones” and upper tail of the “zeros” as shown in the probability distribution graph on the left side of Table 1. The outputs of the comparators are then fed to the XNOR gate which provides the confidence level by encoding the 2-bit binary vector to a single bit indicating the confidence of the received bit (i.e. high or low).
The output eye of the soft-decision segment at point B is shown in Fig. 1. A good eye opening of 341mVp-p is measured for an input signal to the front-end of 24 mVp-p (point A, Fig. 1). The delay between the hard and soft decision datapaths is compensated using a tunable delay line (SHF2000DEL) and data is captured using a sampling oscilloscope with a 80-GHz optical module. To demonstrate the performance of the optical front-end, a high coding gain long block length code (LDPC(32768,26803)) is adopted [5].
III. EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS
The considered system setup for the performance evaluation of the LDPC(32768,26803) optical transmission system consists of 60 km of single-mode fiber (SMF-28) with dispersion compensating fiber. Three channels at 1533.47, 1534.25 and 1535.04 nm are used and the performance is evaluated for the middle channel. The LDPC block codes are loaded onto a programmable pulse pattern generator which is used to modulate a Mach-Zehnder modulator at 45 Gb/s (NRZ-OOK). The frames are captured after transmission using the high speed sampling oscilloscope with 4 samples per bit. In total, the number of captured bits is close to 4.4108 bits. After capturing the frames, they are processed offline in MATLAB using soft-decision algorithm for decoding LDPC codes [4]. The experimental and simulated results for 45 Gb/s NRZ-OOK signal are shown in Fig. 2. The pre-FEC Q-factor in the horizontal axis represents the signal quality calculated from the hard decision datapath of the optical front-end prior to soft-decision error correction. The post-FEC BER indicates the bit error rate (BER) after error correction using the confidence information from the soft-decision datapath. The solid line in Fig. 2 corresponding to the simulated results for LDPC(32768,26803) using 2-bit soft decision. The squares show the measured values from the experimental setup. The achieved net coding gain (NCG) is 7.06 and 9.62 dB for postFEC BER of 10-7 and 10-12 (extrapolated), respectively. The coding gain is 1.9 dB when compared to hard-decision decoding. In addition, the coding gain over conventional RS(255,239) code is 2.96 and 4 dB for post-FEC BER of 10-7 and 10-12 (extrapolated), respectively. IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, a low complexity implementation and potentially more power efficient of a 45 GS/s soft-decision circuit is proposed for the decoding of low density parity check (LDPC) codes. The soft-decision circuit from off-theshelf components is experimentally demonstrated and the performance is evaluated in terms of post forward error correction (FEC) bit error rate (BER) versus pre-FEC Qfactor. It is found that the net coding gain (NCG) at 45 GS/s is 7.06 and 9.62 dB for post-FEC BER of 10-7 and 10-12, respectively, for long block length LDPC(32768,26803) code.
