Cell-to-Cell Interference Compensation Schemes Using Reduced Symbol Pattern of Interfering Cells for MLC NAND Flash Memory

G. Kong, Student Member, IEEE, T. Kim, W. Xi, Senior Member, IEEE, and S. Choi, Member, IEEE

Cell-to-cell interference compensation schemes using reduced symbol pattern of interfering cells for multi-level cell (MLC) NAND flash memory are proposed in this paper. The proposed schemes contain three signal-processing techniques, estimating cell-to-cell interference, compensating cell-to-cell interference, and generating log-likelihood ratio (LLR). Firstly, reduced symbol pattern of interfering cells is used to easily estimate cell-to-cell interference by setting threshold voltage shift to be only two values in the programming state. Based on this estimation, cell-to-cell interference is compensated by modifying the read voltage in the proposed scheme 1 and by subtracting the estimated cell-to-cell interference from the sensed voltage in the proposed scheme 2. Finally, after conducting compensation, LLR is calculated for low-density parity check codes in the assumption of free cell-to-cell interference since interference between cells is mitigated by the compensation procedure. By using these techniques, the interference can be relaxed with a simpler structure and a higher reliability compared to the conventional methods for MLC NAND flash memory.

Index Terms—Cell-to-Cell interference, interference compensation, low-density parity check (LDPC), MLC NAND flash memory.

I. INTRODUCTION
In the recent years, multi-level cell (MLC) NAND flash memory has become the main issue in the flash memory market [1]. In alignment with the market’s fast growth, techniques realizing 8-level and 16-level MLC have been strenuously studied [2], [3]. However, as the number of level increases, correspondingly rising cellto-cell interference becomes the major challenge for further NAND flash memory technology scaling and more aggressive use of MLC. Therefore, it is important to develop techniques that can minimize or compensate cell-to-cell interference [4].
Many prior researches have been focusing on minimizing cell-to-cell interference through device/circuit techniques [3], [5]. However, these are accompanied by high operational complexity. Signal-processing based cell-to-cell compensation methods have been proposed in [4], [6]. In [4], post-compensation and pre-distortion schemes were suggested to tolerate cell-to-cell interference in MLC NAND flash memory. These schemes first estimate the degree of interference between neighboring cells and then the estimation is used to compensate cell-to-cell interference through postcompensation and pre-distortion schemes. However, since there are various interference patterns in MLC NAND flash memory, a considerable amount of complexity is accompanied. Additionally, as the level increases, the number of interference patterns rises exponentially. Therefore, regardless of the number of levels in MLC NAND flash memory, a simplified cellto-cell interference sensing method should be examined to reduce latency of MLC NAND flash memory. In [6], calculating the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) based on a mathematical model for low-density parity check

(LDPC) codes was proposed in the presence of cell-tocell interference. While the LLR contains cell-to-cell interference data, this scheme does not possess high error-correction capability since cell-to-cell interference leads to a lowered reliability of the LLR. Therefore, efficient ways to complement between cell-to-cell interference mitigation with error correction codes (ECCs) should also be studied to compensate cell-to-cell interference more effectively.
In this paper, we propose two schemes including cellto-cell compensation and ECCs with a simplified cell-tocell interference sensing method for MLC NAND flash memory. Reduced symbol pattern of interfering cells is used to easily estimate cell-to-cell interference by setting threshold voltage shift to be only two values in the programming state. Based on the estimation, cell-to-cell interference can be effectively compensated. In the proposed scheme 1, the read voltage is shifted based on the estimated cell-to-cell interference. With the modified read voltage, the threshold detection for the target cell can be performed accurately. In the proposed scheme 2, interference between cells is compensated by subtracting the estimated cell-to-cell interference from the sensed voltage. In order to provide sufficient accuracy to compensate cell-to-cell interference and to generate the LLR, fine-grained cell-threshold-voltage sensing is used. Additionally, the LLR calculation method for LDPC codes is proposed based on the threshold voltage distribution after conducting cell-to-cell interference compensation. Since the LLR calculation is performed in the assumption of free cell-to-cell interference, it is easy to calculate and exploit the LLR with high reliability. By using these schemes, we can alleviate cell-to-cell interference with a simpler structure and a higher reliability compared to the conventional methods.

II. MLC NAND FLASH MEMORY CHANNEL MODEL

A. Cell-to-Cell Interference

MLC NAND flash memory channel model was employed as described in [4]. The threshold-voltage shift of a floating-gate transistor can affect the threshold voltage of its neighboring floating-gate transistors through parasitic capacitance-coupling effect called as cell-to-cell interference. As the level increases in MLC NAND flash memory, cell-to-cell interference is more severe. Since multipage programming has better immunity to cell-to-cell interference [4], we consider the multipage programming strategy under the even/odd bitline (BL) structure aimed to reduce hardware complexity [4] in this paper. As shown in Fig. 1, in case of 8-level MLC, the multipage programming which sequentially programmed can map the symbol to the cell with small voltage change at the third page [4]. When even/odd BL structure is used, the cells on the even BL are programmed first along each word-line (WL), and then the cells on the odd BL are programmed. III. PROPOSED SCHEME TO COMPENSATE CELL-TO-CELL INTERFERENCE
In this section, we will present the proposed cell-tocell interference compensation schemes. First, a simple cell-to-cell interference estimation method is introduced in the even BL. Then, two cell-to-cell interference compensation schemes based on the estimated cell-tocell interference are described. Lastly, the calculation of the LLR values for LDPC Codes is explained.

B. Cell-to-Cell Interference Compensation Schemes
Based on the estimation, cell-to-cell interference can be effectively compensated. In this paper, we propose two cell-to-cell compensation schemes for MLC NAND flash memory. In the proposed scheme 1, the read voltage is shifted by the estimated cell-to-cell interference. With the modified read voltage, we can perform the threshold detection for the target cell more correctly. In the proposed scheme 2, cell-to-cell interference is compensated by subtracting the estimated cell-to-cell interference from the sensed voltage. In order to provide sufficient accuracy for compensating cell-tocell interference and for generating the LLR, finegrained cell-threshold-voltage sensing is used.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed two schemes including cell-to-cell compensation and ECCs with a simplified cell-to-cell interference sensing method. By using a reduced symbol pattern of neighboring interfering cells, cell-to-cell interference is easily estimated and compensated regardless of the number of levels in MLC NAND flash memory. Also, the LLR can be obtained with high reliability by the simple method. Therefore, the proposed schemes are highly practical in reducing the latency as well as in increasing the reliability for the future MLC NAND flash memory.
