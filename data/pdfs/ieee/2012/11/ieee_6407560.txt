I. INTRODUCTION 
In this paper, we propose a novel graph-based 
iterative receiver over low-density parity-check 
(LDPC) coded two-dimensional (2-d) intersymbol 
interference (lSI) channels, which reduces the 
computational complexity of the channel check 
node operation to O(2Q+de-Q), where Q is an 
arbitrary integer with O-s.Q-s.de, and de is the channel 
check node degree. Simulation results show that 
the proposed scheme provides an adjustable 
performance/complexity tradeoff and performs 
very close to the full-complexity approaches in [1] 
and [2].

IV. PERFORMANCE RESULTS 
In Fig. 2, we present the performance of the 
proposed reduced-complexity receiver for LDPC 
coded 2-d lSI channels in terms of bit-error-rates 
(BER) and frame-error-rates (FER). As in [1] and 
[2], the LDPC code is a rate-O.S length-10,000 
regular (3, 6) random LDPC code and the same 
2x2 channel matrix h is used in the simulation. The 
maximum number of iterations for all receivers is 
set to be SO. For comparison purposes, the 
performance of the full-complexity graph-based 
receiver [2] is included in the figure as well as the 
iterative row/column BCJR receiver [1] and the 
performance of LDPC decoding over A WGN 
channels, respectively. Fig. 2 shows that the value 
of Q determines performance and complexities. 
The proposed receiver with Q=l uses S binary 
vectors per check node and has a performance gap 
of 1 dB from the full-complexity receiver at the 
BER of 10-6• Increasing to Q=2 (corresponding to 
6 vectors per check node) narrows the performance 
loss within 0.2 dB and reduces the complexity by 
1-6/16=62.S%. Fig. 2 also shows that the proposed 
receiver with Q=3, which forms 9 vectors per 
check node, performs almost the same as the full­
complexity receiver that considers all 24=16 
vectors throughout the simulated SNR range.

required for various receivers. At low-to-medium 
SNR, the proposed receiver with Q=3 requires
