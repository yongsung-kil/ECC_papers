Design of irregular LDPC codes for nonparametric channels

W. Proß, M. Otesteanu Faculty of Electronics and Telecommunications Politehnica University of Timisoara, Romania

Abstract—In this paper we propose a design-method for irregular LDPC codes in the context of channels, that can not sufficiently be described with the known parametric channelmodels. We introduce a very flexible histogram-based channelmodel. Furthermore a design-method based on the downhill simplex optimization is explained for the design of irregular LDPC codes in the context of histogram-based channel-models. We also propose an extension for soft-decision based LDPC decoders and explain the simulation of LDPC codes considering histogram-based channels-models. Following results based on a 2-state Markov-model prove the effectiveness of our designmethod, the description of the channel and the decoding based on histograms.
Keywords-Irregular LDPC codes, nonparametric channels.
I. INTRODUCTION
Channel-coding plays a major role in nearly all communications systems of todays information society. A big range of channel-codes are available in order to increase the reliability of a communications system. Low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes are one class of linear block codes, that are more and more applied and thereby often replace former coding schemes. LDPC codes have already been introduced in 1962 by R. Gallager [1]. After more than 30 years MacKay and Neal rediscovered them in 1995 [2]. The choice of a channel-code mostly depends on the application and the desired functionality. A fundamental criterion is thereby the respective channel that stands for everything that happens to the codeword in between the transmitter and the channeldecoder. For that a channel-model is chosen that should describe the real channel and is used for different purposes, like the choice of a channel-code and an appropriate decoder as well as following simulations to investigate the choice. In case of a class of LDPC codes called irregular LDPC codes, the properties of the code are designed based on the chosen channel-model. Lots of channel-models are available in order to describe the distributions of received values that represent the binary value of a bit. In most cases the available channel-models are appropriate to describe the real channels. Contrary to that, situations are possible where the application of a known channel-model would be an approximation that is too rough. In this paper we present a design method for irregular LDPC codes, by which it is possible to design these

codes considering channels that are not properly described by available channel-models. Furthermore we propose an extension for LDPC decoders in order to adapt the decoder to the respective channel.

II. IRREGULAR LDPC CODES
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are based on a sparse parity-check matrix (PCM). The m rows of the PCM stand for m parity-check equations. The n symbols of a codeword are represented by the n columns of the PCM. The one-entries in a PCM specify which symbols are involved in which parity-check equation. LDPC codes are alternatively described based on a Tanner graph [3] that comprises of symbol-nodes and check-nodes representing the columns and rows of the PCM, respectively. The edges that connect the two types of nodes stand for the non-zero entries in the PCM. In contrast to regular LDPC codes, irregular LDPC codes exhibit several row weights and column weights and thus several numbers of adjacent edges to the check-nodes and symbolnodes of the Tanner graph, respectively. This is described by means of polynomials. The following polynomial is used to specify the symbol-node degree distribution (SNDD).

IV. DESIGN FOR A HISTOGRAM-BASED CHANNEL-MODEL
The problem with histogram-based channel-models is, that it is not possible to derive a closed-form expression on which the density evolution (DE) analysis is based on. So a design that operates by means of DE ([4], [5]) is not possible. Because of that we choose the downhill simplex (DHS) based design method, that we developed for the design of short irregular LDPC codes [6]. In [7], the authors also propose a DHS-based design, but in contrast to our method their algorithm is based on a simplified version of the DHS algorithm. Our DHS optimization is based on a simplex S = {λ1, λ2, · · · , λN , λN+1} with each vertex λi representing a unique SNDD and thus consists of N values {λi,j}Nj=1. During the optimization process the vertices are constantly sorted according to their function evaluations so that f (λ1) ≤ f (λ2) ≤ · · · ≤ f (λN ) ≤ f (λN+1). VI. RESULTS
Based on the optimization method explained in Section IV and the histogram-based 2-state Markov channel of Section III we designed a rate r = 0.61 irregular LDPC code of length n = 576 with dN = 10. For the histograms of the channel-model we used H = 41 bins in the range of L(hs1i,xi ) = −1.025 and U (hHsi,xi ) = 1.025 which yields δh = 0.05. A simulation was conducted where the channelmodel was applied according to Section V-A and the received data words were decoded as described in Section V-B. VII. CONCLUSION
In this paper we propose to utilize a nonparametric histogram-based channel-model if no channel-model can be found to sufficiently describe a channel. We also provide a design-method for irregular LDPC codes based on the proposed channel-model. We explained a simulation including the extended LDPC-decoding with a histogram-based channelmodel. In the context of our application we constructed a 2-state Markov-model based on two histogram-based subchannel-models. A simulation with an irregular LDPC code designed by means of the proposed optimization method proved the effectiveness of our design-method, the histogrambased channel-model and the extended LDPC-decoding. Despite of a very short block length n = 576, the designed LDPC code showed a superior decoding performance compared to a regular LDPC code.
