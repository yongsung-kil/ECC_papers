A Construction Method of QC-LDPC Codes without Short Cycles

Jianwu Zhang, Chengxia Li, Jianrong Bao
College of Communication Engineering Hangzhou Dianzi University
Hangzhou Zhejiang, China, 310018 zhangjianwu2001@163.com, lcx1988fei@163.com, ibaojr@zju.edu.cn

Abstract-Low-density Parity-check (LDPC) coding is a class of advanced channel coding techniques. To improve the performance of LDPC codes, a simple and effective method to construct QC-LDPC codes without short cycles (cycles of length four and six) is proposed. The approach is especially effective when the parity-check matrix of QC-LDPC codes is composed of circulant permutation matrices. Firstly, we analyze the shapes of the cycles in the exponent matrix, to get the required conditions of no four-cycles and six-cycles. Then we show how to construct the parity-check matrix without four-cycles and six-cycles by filling rows of the exponent matrix with arithmetic sequences. Simulation results show that the proposed codes have better performance than array codes. Though the performance of the proposed codes is not as well as random codes at higher Eb/N0, they can be applied to practical communications because of being encoded in linear time with shift registers.

Keywords- Low-density Parity-check; circulant permutation matrices; cycles; arithmetic sequences

I.

INTRODUCTION

Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were first discovered by Gallagher [1], which is a class of linear block code with performance close to the Shannon limit. The structure of a code determines the code performance to a great extent. Therefore many coding researchers make much effort to construct parity-check matrix of LDPC codes.
Construction methods usually may be decomposed into two main classes: random constructions and structured constructions. Despite of near-capacity performance for

long code lengths, random constructions [2-5] are hard to access memory and encode data efficiently. However, structured LDPC codes with quasi-cyclic property can overcome these problems. The required memory for storing
QC-LDPC codes can be reduced by a factor 1 / L , while L × L circulant permutation matrices are employed.
Cycles and girth are important factors of affecting the performance of LDPC codes. For structured constructions, there have been many methods to construct QC-LDPC codes with no short cycles and large girth, such as constructions based on finite geometries [6], based on BIBD [7] and based on permutation matrices [8-11].
In this paper, a method based on circulant permutation matrices to construct regular QC-LDPC codes without short cycles is proposed. By analyzing the shapes of the cycles of the Tanner graph [12] in the exponent matrix, every row of exponent matrix of QC-LDPC codes is filled with arithmetic sequences to avoid four cycles and six cycles.

III. THE PROPOSED METHOD

A. Analysis of Cycles In Tanner graph, the path that starts from a node v and
ends in node v is called a cycle. The length of the smallest cycle in the Tanner graph is called the girth. A cycle in Tanner graph of a QC-LDPC code can be considered as a sequence of the corresponding cyclic shift value in the exponent matrix. Cycles, especially short cycles, affect the independence of the extrinsic information exchanged in the iterative decoding, resulting in the degradation of the performance of LDPC codes. So short cycles should be avoided, when constructing the exponent matrix. The following proposition was proposed first by Fossorier in [9].

V.

CONCLUSION

This paper introduces regular QC-LDPC codes of column weight three with the proposed method. The constructed codes have no four-cycles and six-cycles. According to simulation results, the performance of codes constructed with proposed method is better than array codes, though is not as well as random codes at higher Eb/N0. Furthermore, short cycle is an important rather than exclusive factor affecting the performance of LDPC codes. The performance of the proposed codes is further improved through the analysis of other factors such as the minimum distance, the trapping sets and so on.
