Serially Concatenated Codes with Euclidean Geometry LDPC and Convolutional Codes
Sina Vafi, Huu Dung Pham School of Engineering and Information Technology
Charles Darwin University, Darwin, Australia {sina.vafi,huudung.pham}@cdu.edu.au

Abstract—This paper presents a new scheme of serially concatenated codes. It is formed by a combination of Euclidean Geometry (EG) Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) and Recursive Systematic Convolutional (RSC) codes linked by the row-column interleaver. A modification is proposed on the interleaver, which improves performance of concatenated codes. This is conducted by recognition of patterns producing low-weights for both LDPC and RSC codes. Simulation results confirm that with similar rates and less decoding complexity, newly designed codes outperform product codes constituted by two cyclic EG codes.

Index Terms—Cyclic codes, Euclidean Geometry (EG) codes, LDPC codes, RSC codes.

I. INTRODUCTION
Rediscovery of Low-Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes has broadened research on Forward Error Correction (FEC) codes in the past two decades. There are several types of LDPC codes implemented for different applications, which are basically classified as either random or structured codes depending on the formation of their parity check matrices [1].
High performance and low-decoding complexity of LDPC codes represent their applications in concatenated codes. They are mainly combined with another type of block codes that aims to mitigate effects of random and burst errors. Two methods proposed codes constituted by LDPC and Bose RayChaudhuri Hocquenghem (BCH) codes as suitable for the magnetic storage and optical transmission systems [2] [3]. As non-binary codes, they are concatenated with Reed-Solomon (RS) codes to outperform single LDPC code [4].
The main similarity between the proposed techniques, is utilizing a random-type LDPC code, whose encoders are designed with high complexity. In contrast, structured-type LDPC codes have more flexibility in their design as well as better rate adaptability compared to random-type LDPC codes [5]. Euclidean Geometry (EG) and Projective Geometry (PG) codes are two of the most well-known structured-type LDPC codes, which are categorized as cyclic codes. The cyclic structure of these codes allows their encoders to be simply implemented by shift registers [1]. In addition, error protection performance remains high due to their high minimum weight and the orthogonality, which prohibits existence of low-cycles in their Tanner graph.
Two cyclic EG-LDPC codes can be concatenated with each other constructing a high-performance product code [6]. Due to interleaving accomplished on combination of constituent

Alternatively, concatenated codes are implemented by LDPC and convolutional codes. These codes do not use any interleaver in their structures and are designed with a random-type LDPC code [7] [8]. Codes outperform a single LDPC code. In addition, their error floors occur at lower Bit Error Rates (BERs) compared to parallel concatenated Recursive Systematic Convolutional (RSC) codes (turbo codes). Graph-based concatenated codes were also proposed, whose constituents are random-type LDPC and RSC codes. High performance of these codes is achieved by convolutional codes having relatively high constraint lengths and bistreams with long length [9].
A considerable advantage of structured-type LDPC codes is their ability to be combined with a convolutional code through an interleaver to form new scheme of concatenated codes having low complexity and high flexibility in their design. This paper introduces serially concatenated codes implemented by an outer EG-LDPC and an inner RSC code, which are linked by the row-column interleaver. Indeed, concatenated codes can be introduced as product codes, when one of the constituent block codes is replaced by RSC code.

IV. CONCLUSIONS
This paper presented new schemes of concatenated codes constituted by cyclic EG-LDPC and RSC codes. They applied advantages of the structured-type LDPC and convolutional codes to form reliable, low complexity and high performance concatenated codes. A modification on row-column interleavers was proposed, which improved performance of these

codes. This was achieved based on recognition of patterns
that produce low weights for LDPC and RSC codes. Sim-
ulation results confirmed that with similar code rates, the
newly proposed codes outperform product codes constructed
by two cyclic EG-LDPC codes. Work on suitable puncturing
technique for these codes will be followed to represent them
as reliable unequal error protection codes.
