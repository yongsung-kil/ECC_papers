Abstract—In this paper, approximate calculation of channel log likelihood ratio (LLR) for wireless channels using Pade´ approximation is devised. Due to the lack of knowledge of the channel state information of a wireless fading channel, such as uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading channels, calculation of exact LLR for these channels is quite complicated for a practical implementation. The previous work, LLR calculation using the Taylor approximation, quickly becomes inaccurate as the channel output leaves some derivative point. To overcome this problem, we devise a new LLR approximation using Pade´ approximation, which expresses the original function by rational form of two polynomials with the same total number of coefficients of the Taylor series, accelerates the Taylor approximation, is devised. By applying the proposed approximation to the iterative decoding and the low-density parity-check codes, we show the effectiveness of the proposed methods by simulation results and analysis based on the density evolution.
Index Terms—Log-likelihood ratio, LLR approximation, pade´ approximation, uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading channel, LDPC code, BP decoding
I. INTRODUCTION
In recent years, iterative decoding techniques based on message passing algorithm such as turbo decoding [2] or belief-propagation (BP) decoding [5], [13], [17] have been attracted by their significant performance which attain close to the Shannon limit. The BP decoding algorithm, a wellknown iterative decoding algorithm for LDPC codes [5], [13], has been widely studied for the binary erasure channel or the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel [5], [12], [13], [15]–[17]. The algorithms firstly derive channel log-likelihood ratios (LLR) where the messages in the decoder are initialized to these LLR values. To exhibit good performance with BP decoding, these channel LLR should be obtained with high accuracy but it becomes complicated for some channel models such as wireless fading channel [14].
In this study, we focus on calculation of channel LLR over the uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading channels with a fading gain r, an additive white Gaussian noise z ∼ N (0, σz2), and a binary phase shift keying (BPSK) modulation where σz2 denotes variance of Gaussian noise. If r at each received bit position is known to the receiver, we call this case known channel state information (CSI). If r at each received bit position is unknown to the receiver, we call this case unknown

CSI. For known CSI, the channel LLR can be easily calculated using the channel outputs y, σz2, and r. However, for the unknown CSI which is more practical than known CSI and is our main interest, calculation of the channel LLR is rather complex due to the integration of r.
The studies of wireless fading channels with the LDPC codes or turbo codes were presented in [1], [6]–[11], [18], [19] with several modulation schemes [4], [14]. In [7], Hou et al. have studied designing irregular LDPC codes [12] using density evolution [15], [16] and have shown that these codes approached the Shannon limit nearly. It is well-known that the Taylor approximation quickly becomes inaccurate as the variable y leaves the derivative point. To overcome this problem, we devise a new LLR approximation using Pade´ approximation [3] on the uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading channels with unknown CSI. Pade´ approximation, which expresses the original function by rational form of two polynomials with the same total number of coefficients of the Taylor series, accelerates the Taylor approximation. Generally Pade´ approximation is accurate not only at the derivative point but also at the other regions of variables. We show by simulation results and analysis based on the density evolution that our method can approximate LLR function with high accuracy and can yield almost the same decoding performance as the true LLR. The Pade´ approximation is a generalization of the Taylor series of approximation, the proposed method exhibits slightly better performance than the method using Taylor approximation [1]. Moreover we design irregular LDPC codes based on our LLR approximation function. It would be emphasized that previous works [1], [7], [18] did not compare the true LLR function with unknown CSI in the analysis of density evolution. The difference of performance between true LLR and approximation methods under the density evolution will be cleared in this study.

Numerical results are shown in Section IV, and Section V concludes the paper.

V. CONCLUSION

In this study, we apply the Pade´ approximation, which is a generalization of the Taylor series of approximation, to the LLR function on the uncorrelated flat Rayleigh fading channels with unknown CSI. Using Pade´ approximation, we can accelerate the accuracy of the LLR function that it is accurate not only around the derivative point but also at the other intervals of the variable. From the simulation results and analysis based on the density evolution, our method can yield almost the same decoding performance as the true LLR function and is slightly better than the conventional approximation

method (Taylor approximation of order n = 3). Moreover
we devise some irregular LDPC code profiles whose iterative
thresholds are close to the Shannon limit and are better than
by using previous methods. Though the improvement by our
method is not so large. We believe that our approach can also
be applied to the other modulations such as 16-QAM where
it exists a room for further improvement.
