Necessary Conditions for Avoiding Cycles of
Length 4 or 6 in Regular quasi-cyclic LDPC Codes
Kyung-Joong Kim, Jin-Ho Chung, and Kyeongcheol Yang,
The Department of Electrical Engineering
Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH),
Pohang, Gyungbuk 790-784, Korea

Abstract‚ÄĒIn this paper we investigate the cycle properties of
regular quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes.
Using the sequence representation, we analyze the conditions
for short cycles to exist in regular QC-LDPC codes and then
give necessary conditions for avoiding short cycles in terms of
their parameters. Numerical results show that our bounds on
the number of rows in the parity-check matrices for regular
QC-LDPC codes without cycles of length 4 or 6 are tighter than
any other known bounds.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, first introduced by
Gallager [1], have attracted considerable attention due to their
Shannon-limit-approaching performance [2]. As a result, there
have been many developments in the construction of LDPC
codes. In particular, Richardson et al. introduced density
evolution to design an ensemble of LDPC codes that perform
extremely close to the Shannon capacity when the code length
goes to infinity [3]. However, it does not predict well their
finite-length performance due to their heavy dependence on
cycles, stopping sets, and trapping sets. For example, short
cycles in the Tanner graph [4] of an LDPC code may induce
a severe performance degradation. For these reasons, several
researchers made many efforts to obtain LDPC codes without
short cycles [5]-[11].
An interesting class of LDPC codes is quasi-cyclic LDPC
(QC-LDPC) codes based on circulant permutation matrices
(CPMs) [12], [13]. They require a smaller amount of memory
for storing their parity-check matrices as well as their algebraic
properties are easier to analyze than those of random LDPC
codes. Therefore, it is natural to take an interest in constructing
QC-LDPC codes without short cycles.
QC-LDPC codes without short cycles have been extensively
studied [5]-[10]. The methods to construct QC-LDPC codes
without short cycles based on an algebraic approach or a
finite geometry are presented in [5]-[7]. However, they do
not give any meaningful bounds on the code parameters
such as code rate and code length. Fossorier [12] presented
some conditions for avoiding cycles of length 4 or 6 in QC-
LDPC codes, although he considered only QC-LDPC codes
whose parity-check matrices consist of nonzero subblocks.
Several researchers presented simple algorithms based on
the progressive edge-growth (PEG) algorithm [8], [9] or an
extension algorithm using lifting methods [10] for constructing
QC-LDPC codes whose parity-check matrices do not contain
only CPMs, but also zero matrices. However, they do not give
any constructive bounds on the code parameters in order to
construct QC-LDPC codes without short cycles.
In this paper we give necessary conditions for avoiding
cycles of length 4 or 6 in regular QC-LDPC codes whose
parity-check matrices consist of zero matrices or CPMs in
terms of their parameters such as the rate and length. A
necessary condition means that no regular QC-LDPC codes
without short cycles can be constructed if their parameters do
not satisfy it. The necessary conditions derived in this paper
are tighter than those derived in [11] and [12] for regular
LDPC codes without cycles of length 4 or 6. Furthermore,
our results can be applied to any regular QC-LDPC codes,
unlike those in [11], [12].
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section II,
we describe a sequence representation of QC-LDPC codes.
Section III analyzes the conditions for cycles of length 4 or 6
to exist in regular QC-LDPC codes. In Sections IV, we derive
necessary conditions to avoiding them in regular QC-LDPC
codes. Finally, we give concluding remarks in Section V.
II. SEQUENCE REPRESENTATION OF REGULAR QC-LDPC
CODES
An LDPC code defined by a sparse parity-check matrix H
of size M √óN may be represented as a Tanner graph with M
check nodes and N variable nodes [4]. The code length is N
and the code rate is larger than or equal to (N ‚ąíM)/N. The
edges in the Tanner graph correspond to the nonzero entries in
H, i.e., an edge is connected between the i-th check node and
the j-th variable node if and only if the (i, j)-th entry of H
is 1. When H has column weight dv and row weight dc, the
corresponding LDPC code is called a (dv, dc)-regular LDPC
code.

V. CONCLUSION
We derived necessary conditions for avoiding 4-cycles and
6-cycles in regular QC-LDPC codes. Numerical results show
that our results give tighter bounds than the previously known
results. Furthermore, our results can be applied to more general
regular QC-LDPC codes without specifying m or L. Our
analysis gave a guideline to construct regular QC-LDPC codes
with large girth.
