Abstract‚ÄĒLinear programming (LP) decoding for low density parity check (LDPC) codes proposed by Feldman et al. has attracted considerable attention in recent years. Despite having theoretical guarantees in some regimes, at low SNRs LP decoding is observed to have worse error performance than belief propagation (BP) decoding. In this paper, we present a novel decoding algorithm obtained by trying to solve a non-convex optimization problem using the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM). This non-convex problem is constructed by adding an 1 penalty term to the LP decoding objective. The goal of the penalty term is to make ‚Äúpseudocodewords‚ÄĚ, which are the non-integer vertices of the LP relaxation to which the LP decoder fails, more costly. We name this the ‚Äú 1 penalized decoder‚ÄĚ. We also develop a reweighted LP decoding algorithm base on this 1 penalized objective. We show that this reweighted LP has an improved theoretical recovery threshold compared to the original LP. In addition, simulations show that, in comparison to LP decoding, these two decoders both achieve significantly lower error rates and are not observed to have an ‚Äúerror floor‚ÄĚ. In particular, the 1 penalized decoder meets or outperforms the BP decoder at all SNRs.
I. INTRODUCTION
Linear Programming (LP) decoding for low density parity check (LDPC) codes was proposed by Feldman et al. in [1]. LP decoder is better than message passing decoders in many aspects. First, the LP decoder has an ‚ÄúML certificate‚ÄĚ property. That is, if the solution of the LP is an integral solution, it is the maximum likelihood (ML) solution. Second, the LP decoder provides better theoretical guarantees. Message passing algorithms such as belief propagation (BP) are not guaranteed to converge and are observed to suffer from an ‚Äúerror floor‚ÄĚ. The LP decoding, on the other hand, is guaranteed to correct some fraction of errors in bit flipping channels (see e.g. [2]‚Äď [4]) and, empirically, has not been observed to suffer from an error floor. Third, adding redundant parity checks improves error performance of the LP decoder because it tightens the LP relaxation. Doing this would degrade the performance of BP decoders.
Despite having these advantages, LP decoder is not efficient using generic LP solvers. This crucial problem was addressed by Barman et al. in [5]. The authors in [5] develop an efficient distributed algorithm for LP decoding. With this improvement, computational complexity is no longer a bottleneck for implementing LP decoding in practical applications. However the simulation results therein show

that the LP decoder has worse error performance compared to BP at low SNRs. This suggests that an ideal decoder should perform like BP at low SNRs and have no error floor effects at high SNRs.
We note that some prior works have sought to improve the error performance of LP decoding. These works can roughly be classified into two categories: (a) change the constraint set of LP decoding by tightening the relaxation, e.g. [6], [7] or (b) change the objective functions, e.g. [8]. Our work is partially inspired by [8], where the authors propose solving a second reweighted LP if the LP decoder fails. This reweighted LP is constructed using the fractional output of the LP decoder, i.e., the pseudocodeword solution. However this technique only applies to binary symmetric channel (BSC). We seek a method that applies to other channels such as the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. To achieve this goal, we introduce an 1 penalty in this work. Namely, we add an 1 penalty term to the linear objective to penalize fractional solutions. The result is that the output of the problem is more likely to be an integral solution. Base on the 1 penalized objective, we develop a new reweighted LP algorithm that meets our goal. Our reweighted LP algorithm has two advantages compared to the algorithm in [8]. First, our algorithm applies to both BSC and AWGN channel. Second, our algorithm allows multiple rounds of reweighting and thus has the potential to achieve better error performance. In addition, similar to [8] we show that the two-round reweighted LP decoding has an improved theoretical recovery threshold compared to the LP decoding of Feldman et al.
The paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the background of the LP decoding using the ADMM. Section III introduces decoding algorithm that incorporates 1 penalty term. Section IV describe reweighted LP algorithm and provide theoretical proof for the two-round reweighted LP decoding. Simulation results are presented in Section V. Conclusions are drawn in Section VI.

III. USING 1 PENALTY FUNCTION FOR DECODING
LP decoding has an ‚ÄúML certificate‚ÄĚ, thus the LP decoding fails whenever it outputs a fractional solution. This solution is called a pseudocodeword. Since these pseudocodewords are directly related to the fractional vertices of fundamental polytope P, it is not surprising that error performance can be improved by tightening the relaxation of P. ‚ÄúCutting plane‚ÄĚ and ‚Äúbranch-and-bound‚ÄĚ are two types of techniques that are used to improve error performance of LP decoding [11].
In this work, we seek for a different angle to improve LP decoding. We are partially inspired by [8] because the authors improve LP decoding not by tightening the constraints, but by properly changing the objective. Following this observation, we propose a method that penalize pseudocodewords using the 1 norm. Namely, we add an 1 penalty to the linear objective of LP decoding, which makes integral solutions more favorable than fractional solutions. This approach is similar to the well known Lasso scheme [12]: the 1 penalty in our approach can be considered as a regularizer. However there is one critical difference: the objective in our approach is non-convex. We show in Sec. IV that we can approximate this non-convex problem using reweighted LP which has theoretical guarantees. We also show in Sec. V that this nonconvex problem performs well in simulations.

To address theoretical issues, we approximate the nonconvex 1 penalized objective using reweighted LPs. The reweighted LP decoder itself is another new decoder constructed by successive LPs. We prove that the two-round reweighted LP decoding has stronger recovery threshold compared to the original LP. That is, it corrects a higher fraction of errors compared to original LP for bit-flipping channels. These theoretical results are conservative due to the adversarial model. However, empirical simulation results show that reweighted LP decoding achieves much better error performance when compared to LP decoding. Although more time consuming, the reweighted LP decoder is less sensitive to the choice of parameter settings than the 1 penalized decoder due to its convex objective nature.
