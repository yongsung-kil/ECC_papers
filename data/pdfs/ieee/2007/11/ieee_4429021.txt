Efficient Check Node Update Implementation for Normalized Min-Sum Algorithm

Lu Xin, Liang Yongsheng Shenzhen Institute of Information Technology, Shenzhen, P.R.China
Yesmoses@hotmail.com Xu Jun
Baseband Algorithm Department, Mobile Division, ZTE Corporation xu.jun2@zte.com.cn

Abstract-This paper has presented various Min-Sum related LDPC decoding algorithms and their typical hardware architectures of check node update in the scenario of parallel implementation. The m-to-2m decoder has been introduced to generate more efficient new hardware implementations of check node update, which can obviously reduce the number of multiplication operations for normalized Min-Sum algorithm for high rate LDPC codes. Simulations have claimed the performance of normalized Min-Sum is nearly the same as that of Log-BP, namely the optimal algorithm. In general, this paper has proved that Normalized Min-Sum is good choices as LDPC decoding methods.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are one special kind of linear block codes, whose parity-check matrix H has a low density of 1’s. LDPCC can be considered powerful competitors to turbo codes in terms of performance and complexity. They are based on a similar philosophy with turbo codes: constrained random code ensembles and iterative decoding algorithms. LDPC coding can play a key role in the base band processing，which can be applied to correct errors in the data transfer.
Since originally invented by Gallager[1] in the early 1960's, they have experienced an amazing comeback in recent years. Many new decoding methods had been brought forward, including standard BP (belief propagation) algorithm, BP-Based algorithm, APP-based algorithm, MaxLog-MAP algorithm, Min-Sum algorithm, Normalized MinSum algorithm[2][3] etc.
In section 2, various typical decoding algorithms will be briefly introduced. Section 3 presents normal parallel implementation of CNU for Min-Sum related algorithms. And Section 4 has shown new more efficient parallel implementation of CNU for Min-Sum related algorithms. Simulation results and performance analysis have been displayed in section 5. Finally, section 6 presents our conclusion.

VI. CONCLUSION
Several typical Min-Sum related LDPC decoding algorithms and normal CNU parallel implementations have been introduced and discussed in this document. Compared with normal CNU implementation, The combinatorial Logics ,such as m-to-2m decoder, 2-to-1 MUX and dc AND gates has been introduced to generate more efficient new hardware implementations of check node update, which can obviously reduce the number of multiplication operations for normalized Min-Sum algorithm for high rate LDPC codes.
According to simulation results, the performance of normalized Min-Sum is nearly the same as that of Log-BP, namely the optimal algorithm. In general, this paper proposes that Normalized Min-Sum is good choices as LDPC decoding applications.
