On the Construction of Quasi-Systematic Block-Circulant LDPC Codes
Ying Xu, Student Member, IEEE, and Guo Wei

Abstract— A class of Quasi-Systematic Block-Circulant LDPC (QSBC-LDPC) Codes is proposed. The parity-check matrix of a QSBC-LDPC code is a sparse block-circulant matrix with a quasi-systematic structure. Due to the special structure of the parity-check matrix, only limited memories, XOR computations and cyclic-shifting operations are needed in the recursive encoding process of the QSBC-LDPC codes. Simulations show that the QSBC-LDPC codes provide remarkable performance improvement with low encoding complexity.

I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes, originally introduced by Gallager in 1960’s [1], have attracted great research interests.
A specific set of LDPC codes have been studied recently. Their parity-check matrices are composed by some smaller matrices which are usually cyclic-shifting results of a simple matrix. These LDPC codes are called ”block-circulant LDPC codes”. The parity-check matrix of a common block-circulant LDPC code is sparse and block-circulant, which is very useful in decoder design [2]-[3]. Li Zongwang had proposed a generic and effective encoding scheme for general blockcirculant LDPC codes [4], and the encoding complexities are much lower than that of the common LDPC codes. In order to further decrease the encoding complexities, Seho Myung constructed a special class of block-circulant LDPC codes [5]. The parity-check matrices proposed in [5] are blockcirculant and designed to be almost lower triangular matrices with additional constraints. The performances of these LDPC codes are good, and the encoding complexities are lower than general block-circulant LDPC codes. According to the introductions above, it is possible and valuable to construct other special block-circulant LDPC codes with further lower implementation complexities.
In this paper, another class of block-circulant LDPC codes with triple-diagonal parity-check matrices is proposed. The parity-check matrices of these LDPC codes are composed by zero matrices and cyclic-shifting results of an identity matrix, and thus the memories needed for encoding is limited. Also, the parity-check matrices are designed to be quasisystematic. Therefore, the encoding process can be completed in a simple recursive manner. Simulation results show that the QSBC-LDPC codes can provide remarkable improvement of performance.

D. Recursive Encoding of QSBC-LDPC Codes

It is clear that a complete recursive encoding process
is given in (3), and the depth of the recursion is 2. The
information bits are encoded with size of Q, and the checkparity bits are also calculated with size of Q. Since elements in HM represent the time of cyclic-shifting of an identity

matrix, all the multiply operations in (3) can be mapped into circulating registers in practical designs.
For a given QSBC-LDPC codes with parameters Q, R, C, M +[(R +3)RC −3]× log2 Q bits are needed to store HM , where represents the integral ceiling function; 3×M −3× Q bits are needed for circulating registers; and M × (R − 1) − 3 × Q times of XOR computations are needed for one coded frame. Obviously, only limited XOR computations and cyclicshifting operations are needed for QSBC-LDPC encoders.
III. SIMULATION RESULTS
The performances of QSBC-LDPC codes are compared with that of Mackay’s LDPC codes [7], with the same coding length and coding rate. Simulations are performed in AWGN channels with BPSK modulation. Two QSBC-LDPC codes with different coding length and rate are chosen for simulations: i) M = 675, N = 1800, r = 0.625 and ii) M = 1995, N = 6650, r = 0.7. The minimum distances of these two QSBC-LDPC codes are respectively 16 and 18, estimated by the modified Error Impulse (EI) methods [8].
IV. CONCLUSION
Well designed parity-check matrices of block-circulant LDPC Codes contribute greatly to decrease the implementation complexities. Quasi-Systematic Block-Circulant LDPC (QSBC-LDPC) codes are a class of special designed blockcirculant LDPC codes. The parity-check matrices of QSBCLDPC codes are quasi-systematic (with triple-diagonal structure) and composed by zero matrices and cyclic-shifting results of an identity matrix. The encoding process of QSBCLDPC codes can be achieved in a recursive way with limited memories and few calculations. The encoding complexity of QSBC-LDPC codes is significantly low, while the performances are very encouraging. These advantage makes QSBCLDPC codes very competitive for practical systems.
