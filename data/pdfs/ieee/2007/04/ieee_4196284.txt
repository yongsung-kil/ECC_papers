Weighted IS Method of Estimating FER of LDPC Codes in High SNR Region
Guangwen Li, Guangzeng Feng School of Telecommunications and Information Engineering
Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications Nanjing, Jiangsu of China
Email: lgwa@sdu.edu.cn, gzfeng@njupt.edu.cn

Abstract— In this paper, one improved frame error rate (FER) estimation approach in high Signal Noise Ratio (SNR) region for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is reported. Firstly dominant trapping sets (TSs), accounting for most of FER in high SNR region, are picked out as decoding result, with input of decoder consisting of a group of biased bits. And how those biased bits are chosen is reduced to finding solution to an binary integer program. Then decoder termination conditions are slightly modified so that history of the tentative decoding could facilitate action of searching for dominant TSs effectively. Without no prior knowledge of shape of error regions for specific LDPC code, we implement weighted mean translation (MT) of importance sampling (IS) method to compute FER in high SNR region. Simulation results demonstrate estimated FER curve conforms well with that of standard monte carlo (MC) simulation in overlapped SNR region.
I. INTRODUCTION
Great attention has been attracted to field of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes since its rediscovery[1]. For iterative decoding scheme of LDPC codes, its performance curve is known as ”waterfall” in low to medium SNR region. However, in high SNR region, phenomenon of error floor is observed [2] which shows that slope of the performance curve is slowing down. For applications such as storage and deep-space communication systems, it is required that bit error rate (BER) has to be below 10−15[3], so performance evaluation in high SNR region is significant to those LDPC codes. On the other hand however, standard MC simulation is infeasible in such situation for the reason of prohibitive simulation time needed. Upon that, fast frame error rate (FER) or BER simulation technique such as IS was applied to LDPC codes, and it has achieved limited success.
Recently, several IS approaches have been proposed to address the performance evaluation of LDPC codes in high SNR region[2], [4], [5], [6]. In seminal paper [2], the concept of trapping set (TS) was born and one computer-based search strategy was formulated to secure dominant TSs. In [4] and [5], a three-step idea of evaluating performance was presented. The inconvenience lies in many parameters in the process have to be decided by experiments. In [6], it adopted biased bits as impulse to probe dominant TSs, then IS method was employed on those TSs to estimate performance. Our simulations show the impulse in this method is often overbiased so that the found dominant TSs are often blended with

trivial TSs. Consequently accuracy of performance evaluation may suffer.
Inspired by the idea in [6], we report an improved approach based on framework of it. Since impulse with the biased bits plays an important role in finding dominant TSs, we propose an scheme of deciding the impulse by reducing it to a problem of binary integer program, and conquer the problem of over-biasing. For the decoding process, if no valid codeword is decoded, we could track back the history of tentative decoding to pick out one near-codeword which appears most frequently, with the constraint that the syndrome weight of this near-codeword is less than a small integer. The benefit of such modification, without compromising characteristic of ”waterfall” in low to medium SNR region, is demonstrated in the step of dominant TSs searching by making those TSs stand out among others. Weighted mean translation (MT) aims to cope with the fact that one error event lands in each error region with different probability. Simulations results of our method, at cost of much less resources, agree well with that of standard MC in overlapped SNR region, thus increasing our confidence in the validity of estimation in high SNR region.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section II explains principle of weighted IS and discusses influence of modification to decoder. Section III details the steps of identifying dominant TSs and weighted IS deploying, among which problem of how to locate biased bits for impulse is emphasized. Simulation results as well as analysis are presented in section IV and section V concludes our work. Lastly acknowledgment is included to end this paper.

C. Modification to Decoding Termination Condition
It is well known selection of dominant TSs for one LDPC code is closely related to its code structure defined by parity check matrix Hm×n, as well as specific decoder implementation[9]. Since different decoder implementation may show various TS set for same LDPC code, thus impacting performance evaluation of LDPC code in high SNR region, our discussion in this work will be limited to one standard sum-product (SP) decoder implementation of [10] .
The termination condition of standard SP decoder[10] is when either an valid codeword is found which satisfies all check equations, or the result is set to tentative decoding result when maximum number of iterations has been reached. In contrast, when no valid codeword could be decoded, similar to [9], the idea of our modification is to select one tentative decoding among decoding process which appears most frequently because of decoding oscillation, with check value less than designated integer at same time. The motive of making such modification consists in that in high SNR region, the dominant error events occur in form of mainly elementary TSs[3] which has maximum unsatisfied checks up to an small integer. T modification pays off in that dominant TSs are picked out accurately and effectively. Evidently, FER performance of LDPC codes will not degrades in any SNR region for such modification.

IV. SIMULATION AND DISCUSSION
Table-1 is MT simulation result with modified decoder. For each row, three counters are used to record number of correct decoding, incorrect decoding caused by specific TS as well as failure of decoding for other reason respectively. Notion of C/T /O denoting the three counters is used for brevity in header of Table-1 . When 5000 all-zero frames are sent through the BI-AWGN channel for testing with standard deviation of σ = 0.4, the near perfect trapping error performance shown in Table-1 justifies our selection of MT density center and modification to decoding termination condition. For instance, in row of TS (5,1) {1 21 32 55 67}, it appears 1178 times incorrect decoding attributed to this TS is captured while only one decoding failure is caused for other reason. In contrast to it, figures of counterparts for standard SP are 178 and 575 separately. The distribution difference of decoding failures between two decoders implies that succeeded weighted IS could be applied more effectively and accurately with result of the former than that of the latter.
Noticeably the expected MT density center of each Ni term is shifted to midpoint of line segment between origin and TS, instead of point of TS. This is understandable since the ideal density center should be adjacent to decision boundary, not interior of error region. Simulations certify that no-error or allerror events happen accordingly when MT shifts the density to the point where origin or TS lie respectively .
As far as computational complexity of searching for TSs is concerned, the method presented in this paper needs at least lblock × n decodings, where n is codeword length, lblock the number of blocks needed to acquire statistic data. Computation load with binary linear programming is usually not heavy, and that of weighted IS depends closely on how many dominant TSs we have gotten, large number of dominant TSs always means heavy computation load but accurate estimation result.
Fig. 1 and Fig. 2 separately are for above (96, 48) code and (2000, 1000) LDPC code which is generated randomly, where M CMD means monte carlo simulation with modified decoder. As seen from those performance curves, both standard MC and our weighted IS method show similar curves in the overlapped SNR region, but when SNR is furthermore lowered, our weighted IS will underestimate the error events compared with standard MC, it is because most of the error events at that moment are out of scope of TSs, and such omitting by weighted IS then causes non-negligible error. The advantage of IS over MC is expressed by the parameter λ, which is defined in (7) of section II , and abundant literature touched this subject with detailed measurement expressions and data[7].
V. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
In this paper we have presented one weighted IS approach to predict FER of LDPC codes, based on the conjecture that dominant TSs account for most of FER in high SNR region. So how to identify all dominant TSs accurately directly determines the validity of final estimation result. In our method, the dominant TSs are sought by imposing a group of biased bits on all-zero frame. Then how to select biased bits is addressed by
