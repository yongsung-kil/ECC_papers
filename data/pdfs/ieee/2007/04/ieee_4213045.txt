A New Wireless Access Scheme: Novel Punctured LDPC Coded Ultra-wideband System

Fangni Chen, Shiju Li, Yanbo Wang
Dept. of ISEE, Zhejiang University Hangzhou, China
gracechen@zju.edu.cn, leesj@cise.zju.edu.cn, happyper@163.com

AbstractŌĆöUltra-wideband (UWB) technology has attracted much attention in high speed indoor wireless communications. UWB communications have transmitted power limitations according to FCC regulation. To face this drawback, while still maintaining significant error correction capabilities, we propose a novel punctured low-density parity-check (LDPC) coding scheme for multiband OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing) UWB systems in this paper. Differently from the randomly puncture coding, we puncture the variable nodes on their degrees. We fix the target SNR threshold of the punctured code and optimize (maximize) the puncturing fraction for that threshold. From our analysis and numerical results, we find this new scheme give a significant performance gain compared with the randomly puncture scheme.

I. INTRODUCTION
Ultra-wideband (UWB) is foreseen as a possible solution to future wireless short-range and high-speed indoor applications. It is defined as any radio transmission that occupies a bandwidth of more than 20% of its center frequency or nominally more than 500MHz. In 2002, the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) in the USA essentially unleashed huge ŌĆ£new bandwidthŌĆØ (3.1-10.6GHz) at the noise floor, where UWB radios overlaying coexistent RF systems. From then on, UWB radios became a fast developing technology in wireless communications, networking, radar, imaging, and positioning systems with uniquely attractive features as well as low cost, low power consumption, low probability of detection , high capability of penetrate though obstacles and ultra high precision ranging at the centimeter level [1][2]. Depending on how the available bandwidth is utilized, UWB can be divided into two groups: UWB impulse radio (UWB-IR) scheme and multi-band OFDM (MB-OFDM) scheme. In this paper, we emphasize on the latter one.
Generally, reliable communications need a proper Forward Error Correction (FEC) scheme in order to avoid the performance degradation due to adverse channel propagation conditions. As an error correcting code, LDPC code was first discovered by Gallager [3] in 1962, they havenŌĆÖt received enough recognition for thirty years. But MacKay and Neal [4] rediscovered the excellent LDPC codes in 1996. As a channel coding scheme, LDPC code has attractive bit error

performance but the added parity bits bring a great amount of redundancy. UWB communications have transmitted power limitations according to FCC regulation. To face this drawback, while still maintaining significant error correction capabilities, puncture solutions could be provided. Puncturing of the code can be performed either randomly or according to intelligently chosen puncturing locations, optimized for a given parity check matrix or Tanner graph. By smartly choosing sequences of bits to be punctured, we can significantly improve the codeŌĆÖs performance at a wide range of rates compared to random puncturing. Jeongseok Ha present a rate-compatible puncture scheme [5] which get an excellent performance but with a high complexity. In this paper, we propose a novel puncturing LDPC scheme for MBOFDM UWB system. Differently from the randomly and Jeongseok Ha puncture schemes, we puncture the variable nodes on their degrees. We fix the target SNR threshold of the punctured code and optimize (maximize) the puncturing fraction for that threshold. Our simulation shows that the proposed punctured LDPC coded UWB system can improve the BER over IEEE 802.15.3a recommended UWB multi-path faded channel.

The multiband OFDM UWB system and the channel model are illustrated in Section II; The LDPC code and the proposed scheme are described in Section III; Section IV gives the computer simulation and the results; Finally, concluding remarks are given in Section V.
II. MB-OFDM UWB SYSTEM
In multi-band UWB radio, UWB frequency band is divided into several subbands. Each subband occupies a bandwidth of at least 500MHz. By interleaving the symbols across subbands, multi-band UWB can maintain the transmit power as if the large GHz bandwidth is utilized. The advantage is that it allows the information to be processed over a much smaller bandwidth, thereby reducing overall design complexity as well as improving spectral flexibility. To efficiently capture the multi-path energy, orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) technique has been used to modulate the information in each subband. The usage of OFDM for each subband has a great significance in implementation as it comes to capturing energy in a dense multi-path environment as well as adding benefit of comparatively low complexity through the use of FFT algorithms.

As a wireless indoor communication, UWB usually face with severe propagation limit due to multi-path fading and the Multiple Access Interference (MAI) which, in practice, introduce burst of errors. As a consequence, a proper channel coding scheme is to be applied.

LDPC code can be decoded with iterative soft decision

decoding algorithms called message-passing algorithms. The

most powerful of these algorithms is known as Belief

Propagation (BP) algorithm. The studies of LDPC for OFDM

systems have been conducted in [7] [8]. The results showed

To achieve a better spectral efficiency without a dramatic QoS

degradation, a common approach relies on puncturing.

Punctured coding scheme is applied for the sake of getting a

tradeoff between these opposite requirements.
