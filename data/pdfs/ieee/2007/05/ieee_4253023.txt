The decoding of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes allows a high degree of parallelism, which makes it very suitable for high data rate applications such as wide-band wireless multimedia communications and magnetic storage systems [1][3]. Recently, many efforts have attempted to simplify the VLSI implementation of the related decoders called “Block-LDPC codes” [4] or “Architecture-Aware LDPC codes” [5]. Based on these design approaches, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes have received significant attention due to their efficient hardware implementation and good performances [6][7]. A. OVERLAPPED MESSAGE DECODING SCHEME
Due to the cyclic characteristics of the parity-check matrix H in QC LDPC codes, the updating computations in the CNUs and VNUs could be overlapped instead of one after another computation assuming dual-port memories are available as shown in Fig. 2. Instead of waiting for the completion of updating computations in the CNUs, the updating computations in the VNUs can begin only after w clock cycles of the CNUs computations, where w is referred to as the waiting time, 0 < w ≤ m. Likewise, the CNUs in the subsequent iterations can start after (m − w) clock cycles of the VNUs computations. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have proposed an efficient highly-parallel decoder architecture for QC LDPC codes using partially overlapped decoding scheme which leads to both the reduced hardware complexity and less power consumption. For the practical implementation of QC LDPC codes, the highly-parallel decoder architectures are necessary to increase the decoding throughput without memory access conflicts. However, the conventional overlapped decoder architectures cannot be easily combined with the highly-parallel decoding scheme. For (3, 5)-regular QC LDPC codes, our proposed highly-parallel decoder architecture could reduce the hardware complexity by approximately 33% for the CNU and 20% for the VNU using the block overlapping scheme compared to a straightforward highly-parallel structure without any performance degradation.
