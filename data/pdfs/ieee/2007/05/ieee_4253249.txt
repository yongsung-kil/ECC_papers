Performance of Quantized Min-Sum Decoding Algorithms for
Irregular LDPC Codes
Daesun Oh and Keshab K. Parhi Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering University of Minnesota, Minneapolis, MN 55455 USA
Email: {dsoh, parhi}@ece.umn.edu

Abstract— In this paper, we analyze the performance of quantized minsum decoding algorithms for irregular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. For regular LDPC codes, it is known that the normalized or offset min-sum decoding algorithm with quantization bits less than 6 bits achieves good performances over wide range of signal-to-noise ratios (SNR). However, finite precision effects in decoding irregular LDPC codes are different from that in decoding regular LDPC codes which is caused by the difference of convergence speeds between low degree nodes and high degree nodes. This paper proposes a novel method to improve the performance of the conventional normalized or offset min-sum decoding algorithm when it is approximated with finite precision for hardware implementations. The proposed method applies down-scaling factors to intrinsic information which has effects on increasing the reliability of extrinsic information at variable nodes and compensating the quantization errors caused by finite precision. Computer simulation results for irregular LDPC codes show that our proposed normalized and offset min-sum decoding algorithms achieve much better performances at high SNR compared to the conventional normalized and offset min-sum algorithms under (6:2) quantization scheme.

In this paper, we discuss the finite precision effects of the normalized and offset min-sum algorithms in decoding irregular LDPC codes, which show different decoding performances from those in decoding regular LDPC codes. We propose an improved method for the normalized or offset min-sum algorithm which can achieve better performances on the finite precision implementations. Our proposed normalized and offset min-sum algorithms quantized by 6 bits finite precision achieve similar performances to floating point ones over BPSK modulations and AWGN channels.
This paper is organized as follows. Section II discusses the finite precision effects of the normalized and offset min-sum algorithms in decoding irregular LDPC codes. In Section III, we propose a novel method for the normalized or offset min-sum algorithm which causes much less performance degradation with respect to the finite precision effects. Section IV presents the performance results simulated by our proposed method. Finally, Section V concludes this paper.

The overall normalized or offset min-sum algorithm consists of computing the Rcv and Lvc iteratively. In other words, in the first half-iteration all the check nodes receive and update their messages Rcv and then, in the next half-iteration all the variable nodes update their messages Lvc. For the offset min-sum algorithm, although the decoding performance under floating point is very good, it is still not good enough at specially high SNR areas when applying the (6:2) quantization scheme. III. NOVEL NORMALIZED AND OFFSET MIN-SUM ALGORITHM

In this section, we will propose an additional modification for the normalized and the offset min-sum algorithms which can achieve robust performances even with finite precision in irregular LDPC codes.
The most irregular LDPC codes have a large portion of variable nodes of low degrees. Since these nodes of degree 2 or 3 need more iterations to get the reliability information, they lead to more error propagation under the fixed number of iterations. In other words, in a floating point decoding, both high and low degree nodes would converge fast and send out more reliable probability ratio to help other nodes. However, the reliability information of low degree nodes in the finite precision decoding would converge slower than that in the floating point one due to quantization effects since different degree nodes have different dynamic ranges on computing the probability ratio. Also, quantization schemes would restrict the dynamic ranges of the updated extrinsic messages and reduce the magnitude of extrinsic information since the extrinsic messages are rapidly saturated to the limited maximum value.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have analyzed the finite precision effects on decoding performance of the normalized and offset min-sum algorithms for irregular LDPC codes. Even though irregular LDPC codes have good performance with floating point, their decoding performance with finite precision would be very poor. They have different behaviors from those of regular LDPC codes due to the difference of convergence speeds between low degree nodes and high degree nodes.
To achieve better decoding performance with the limited quantization scheme, the novel method using down-scaled intrinsic information is proposed for both normalized and offset min-sum algorithms. By increasing the convergence speed within the restricted maximum iteration, our proposed methods achieve very good decoding performances with the finite precision implementation.
