Belief Propagation over SISO/MIMO Frequency Selective Fading Channels
Mustafa N. Kaynak, Member, IEEE, Tolga M. Duman, Senior Member, IEEE, and Erozan M. Kurtas, Member, IEEE

Abstract— In this letter, we propose an iterative belief propagation (BP) channel detector (equalizer) over single-input singleoutput (SISO) and multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) frequency selective fading channels as an alternative to the typically used maximum a-posteriori (MAP) or maximum likelihood (ML) detectors. The proposed detector has a parallel structure, resulting in fast hardware implementations. Moreover, BP detector is less complex than the MAP detector and it has a short decoding delay. We analyze the bit error rate and the mutual information and show that, over frequency selective fading channels, the proposed BP detector achieves a near-optimal performance, even in the presence of the length 4 cycles in the corresponding channel factor graph.
Index Terms— Belief propagation, channel detection, frequency selective fading channels, MIMO systems, mutual information.
I. INTRODUCTION
R ECENTLY, it has been shown that using multiple antennas at the transmitter and the receiver improves the capacity of wireless channels dramatically [1], [2]. Specifically, if the channel is Rayleigh flat fading (with independent fading coefficients between different sub-channels), the capacity is linear in the minimum of the number of transmit and the receive antennas. Motivated by this information theoretical result, space-time code design and spatial multiplexing techniques have received significant attention [3], [4]. In addition to the flat fading wireless channel models, if the symbol durations are decreased further in an effort to increase the data rates, or the multipath spread of the channel is larger, we encounter frequency selective multiple input multiple output channels.
To combat the frequency selectivity of the channel, channel equalization is necessary. Trellis based MAP and ML algorithms as well as sub-optimal linear and decision feedback equalizers are proposed for channel detection [5]. However, computational complexity of trellis based detectors increases exponentially with the multi-path delay spread of the channel, the number of transmit antennas and the constellation size.

Therefore, decoding complexity of optimal detection would be prohibitively high over long channels, especially when multiple transmit antennas are employed, resulting in a need for reduced complexity, soft-input soft-output channel detectors.
In a recent work, iterative BP detectors have been proposed for static ISI channels, and it has been shown that, for the uncoded system, BP achieves the optimal MAP performance over such channels if the channel factor graph is cycle free [6], and a near optimal performance if the channel factor graph is length 4 cycle free [7].
In [8], we extended belief propagation detectors to SISO frequency selective fading channels and showed that length 4 cycles do not pose a significant problem for frequency selective fading channels unlike the case of fixed ISI channels and for most of the channel realizations, BP detector performs well resulting in a very good average error rate.
In this letter, we take a further step and extend the use of belief propagation detector to MIMO systems over frequency selective fading channels. We show that, the BP detector achieves a near optimal performance over MIMO systems as in the case of SISO systems, despite the fact there are many length 4 cycles in corresponding factor graphs. Using the mutual information analysis, we also demonstrate that BP detector converges to the MAP performance for most of the channel realizations.
In an independent parallel work [9], BP detectors were also applied to MIMO frequency selective channels and the authors concluded that BP detector suffers performance loss when there are cycles in the corresponding factor graph. Here we further observe that over frequency selective fading channels, the detector yields near optimal performance even in the presence of length 4 cycles as a result of the randomness of the channel coefficients.

Simulation results are reported in Section IV, and the letter is concluded in Section V.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this letter, we proposed the use of iterative belief propagation channel detector for SISO/MIMO systems over

frequency selective fading channels. Through BER and mutual information analysis, we showed that unlike the fixed ISI channels, belief propagation detector does not suffer from the presence of length 4 cycles over frequency selective fading channels. In addition, BP detector can be implemented in parallel. Therefore, it is very useful for practical implementation purposes reducing the computation time considerably. Latency of the proposed BP detector is shorter compared to the optimal MAP detector as well. We can thus conclude that, the BP
detector is a legitimate alternative of the optimal MAP detector in terms of both complexity and performance.
