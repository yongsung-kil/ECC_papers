On the minimum weight of simple full-length array
LDPC codes
Kenji SUGIYAMA* and Yuichi KAJI* * Nara Institute of Science and Technology 8916-5 Takayama, Ikoma, Nara 630-0192, JAPAN
Email: {kenji-su, kaji} @is.naistjp

Abstract- Simple and full-length array LDPC codes (SFALDPC codes) is a class of LDPC codes which are algebraically constructed from a family of array codes. The minimum weight of SFA-LDPC codes has been investigated in literatures, but exact minimum weight of the code is not known except for some small parameters. In this paper it is shown that the class of
SFA-LDPC codes which are denoted by CA (p, 4) in this paper
contains a codeword whose minimum weight is 10 or less, if p is a prime number greater than 7. Combined with the Yang's
lower bound on the minimum weight of CA (p, 4), this implies that the minimum weight of CA (p, 4) is exactly 10 for any prime
p with p > 7.
I. INTRODUCTION
This paper is to investigate the exact minimum weight of certain subclasses of array LDPC codes. There are a number of factors which affect the performance of LDPC codes. For example, it is widely considered that the girth of an LDPC code significantly affects the performance of the code under a message-passing decoding algorithm. Among many factors, the minimum weight (the minimum distance) is still one of the most fundamental and the most significant parameter for LDPC codes. However, unfortunately, it is difficult in general to analyze the exact minimum weight of long practical LDPC codes. Tanner discussed in [10] a certain bound on the minimum weight of LDPC codes by utilizing the Tanner Graph. Hu and Fossorier proposed a probabilistic procedure to compute the minimum weight of LDPC codes by using a decoding algorithm for LDPC codes[6], and Hirotomo et al. proposed another probabilistic procedure[5] which utilizes the Stern's algorithm. To the authors' knowledge, no efficient algorithm is known for computing the exact minimum weight of arbitrary LDPC codes.
On the other hand, there are some results on the minimum weight of LDPC codes which have certain mathematical structures. MacKay showed that the minimum weight of regularQuasi-Cyclic LDPC codes with column weight j is less than
or equal to (j + 1)![7], and Fossorier discusses the minimum
distance of Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes from circulant permutation matrices[4]. Mittelholzer has derived in [8] some upperbound limits of the minimum weights of certain array LDPC
codes[2]. Array LDPC codes are a class of LDPC codes which are algebraically constructed from a family of array codes[l],
[3], and the array LDPC codes investigated by Mittelholzer is a subclass of (general) array LDPC codes. Thus we call the LDPC codes in the class simple full-length array LDPC

codes (SFA-LDPC codes) in this paper. Mittelholzer showed
that the minimum weight of the SFA-LDPC code CA (p, 4)
(see the next section for the definition and the notation of
the code) is 12 or less[8], which significantly improves the upper bound limit (4 + 1)! = 120 given by MacKay [7] for
general regular case. The result of Mittelholzer is extended
by Yang in [11]. Yang has computed exact minimum weights of some SFA-LDPC codes with small parameters, and also
showed that the minimum weight of CA (p, 4) is 10 or more
if p is a prime number greater than 7. Since the hamming
weight of codewords of CA (p, 4) must be even, this implies that the minimum weight of CA (p, 4) is either 10 or 12 for
every prime number p with p > 7.
Recently the authors have developed an algorithm for computing the minimum weight of SFA-LDPC codes[9]1, and
discovered that the minimum weight of CA (p, 4) is exactly
10 for all prime numbers from 11 to 79. This experimental
results suggest that the minimum weight of CA (p, 4) is 10 for
all prime numbers greater than 7. We give in this manuscript
a proof for this conjecture. Precisely, we show that CA (p, 4)
always contain a codeword with weight 10 or less if p > 7. Besides, for p = 5 and p = 7, the minimum weights of
CA (5, 4) and CA (7, 4) are both known to be 8[9], [11]. This
result, combined with the Yang's bound, implies that the
minimum weight of CA (p, 4) is 10 for all prime numbers
greater than 7. Thus there is little hope that we can obtain
good SFA-LDPC codes by just increasing p.

C. Conditions on support matrices
To investigate the conjecture, we briefly review conditions and properties of the check and support matrices of SFALDPC codes.
Yang has shown that CA (p, j) is closed by doubly transitive group of affine permutations[ll , Lem2]. Using this property, we can show that if CA (p, j) contains a codeword whose weight is w, then there exists a codeword whose weight is w and its first symbol is nonzero. Let v E CA (P, j) be a codeword whose weight is w and its first symbol is
nonzero, and let Sv = [s,,i] be the support matrix of v. From
the definition of HA (p, j), we can assume without loss of generality that the first column of Sv is an all-zero column vector, that is, s, =... = sj,i = 0. (To make the following discussion simpler, we let the indices of s,,i start from 1.)
From Lemma 2.2 and the fact that the first column of Sv consists of zeros only, each column vector of Sv (excluding the the first column) contains at most one zero (we refer this property as PI). On the other hand, Corollary 2.4 assures that
Sv must satisfy the cancel-out condition and therefore each
row of Sv must contain even number of zeros. Because the first column of Sv consists of zeros, each row must contain at least one zero between the second to the w-th column positions to "cancel" the zero on the first column (we refer this property as P2). Note that satisfying the both properties PI and P2 is a necessary condition for v to be a correct codeword.

IV. CONCLUDING REMARKS
Mittelholzer first investigated the minimum weight of SFALDPC codes in 2002. SFA-LDPC codes have quite simple structures, though, the exact value of d(p, 4) was not known unfortunately. We have shown in this paper that d(p, 4) < 10 for any prime number p with p > 7. Together with the lower bound given by Yang, we now know that d(p, 4) = 10 for any prime number p with p > 7.
In [7], Mackay gave the result that the minimum weight
d(p, j) is upper bounded by (j±+)!. This bound is independent
from the parameter p, and thus, the growth of the minimum distance stops at a certain point even if we increase the code length by increasing the parameter p. However, the bound is so loose ((j + 1)! = 120 for j = 4) that the Mackay's result

says little about d(p, j) for small p. Our result clarified that
the growth of d(p, j) stops very soon at p = 7. Consequently,
C(p, 4) with p > 7 has little importance from the practical
viewpoints, since their code length is longer than C(7, 4) while
their minimum distance is the same as C(7, 4).
We conjecture that similar discussion holds for the case
d(p, j) with j > 4. Indeed, our experimental results suggest that d(p, 5) = 12 for p = 13, 17 and 19. We once tried to apply the same approach to show that d(p, 5) < 12, but
failed to obtain meaningful results because the conditions on
the matrix parameters become too complicated. For the j = 4
case, the choice of parameters ((1) to (10)) comes from careful
observation of our experimental result. For j = 5 and more,
further careful observation will be needed.
