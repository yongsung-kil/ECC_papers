Tight Bounds of Minimum Distance Distributions
of Irregular LDPC Code Ensembles

Shinya Miyamoto*, Kenta Kasai*, Tomoharu Shibuyat and Kohichi Sakaniwa*
*Dept. of Communications and Integrated Systems, Tokyo Institute of Technology, Tokyo 152-8552, Japan Email: {itsui, kenta, sakaniwa} @comm.ss.titech.ac.jp
tR & D Department, National Institute of Multimedia Education, Chiba 261-0014, Japan Email: tshibuya@nime.ac.jp

Abstract- Upper bounds of minimum distance distributions of Gallger codes and irregular LDPC codes were derived by Gallage and Di, respectively. Di's bounds are tight for irregular LDPC codes which have variable nodes of degree two, however, it is not tight for irregular LDPC codes which do not. In this paper, we derive tight lower and upper bounds of minimum distance distributions of irregular LDPC code ensembles without variable nodes of degree two.
I. INTRODUCTION
Minimum distance of linear codes is known as important characteristic which bounds ML (maximum likelihood) decoding performance of the codes. In this paper, the average minimum distance distribution of code ensembles, is of our interest. Gallager[2], Di et al. [6] and Orlitsky et al. [4] gave upper bounds of minimum distributions of Gallager ensembles and irregular LDPC code ensembles, respectively. Irregular LDPC codes with variable nodes of degree two show has good threshods but suffer from higher error floors. On the other hand, irregular LDPC codes with no variable nodes of degree two, which we investigate in this paper have deep error floors. In this paper, we give tighter upper and lower bounds of minimum distance distributions of irregular LDPC code ensembles with no variable nodes of degree two. From Theorem 2 and Theorem 3, our upper and lower bounds of minimum distribution of irregular LDPC code ensemble agree each other in a sense of order of n. And our upper bound is tighter than Di's bound when the code ensemble has no variable nodes of degree two.

Minimum distance Dc of a linear code C is defined by

where WH(x) is hamming weight of x. We say a code ensemble g is equi-probable if a probability is assigned to every code in g. Let D be a random variable of minimum distance of a code in g. Then, we have
Pr(D < w) = #{C e g Dc < w} ~~#5

That is to say, we define minimum distance distribution of ensemble g by the fraction of codes with minimum distance which is not greater than w, among ensemble g. In this paper, our subject is to derive upper and lower bounds of minimum distance distribution of (n, A(x), p(x))-irregular LDPC code
ensembles.

V. CONCLUSIONS
We derived tight lower and upper bounds of minimum distance distribution of irregular LDPC code ensembles with no variable nodes of degree two. The derived bounds are tight in the terms that the lower and upper bounds vanish in the same order.
