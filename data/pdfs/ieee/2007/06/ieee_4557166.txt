Improving the Performance of Protograph LDPC Codes by Using Different Transmission Energies
Gerd Richter and Martin Bossert
Ulm University Institute of Telecommunications and Applied Information Theory
Albert-Einstein-Allee 43 D-89081 Ulm, Germany
{gerd.richter,martin.bossert}@uni-ulm.de

Abstract- Irregular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes constructed from small protographs are one of the most powerful LDPC codes. In this paper, we show that the performance of LDPC codes based on small protographs can be further improved by using different transmission energies for every variable node of the protograph. Thus, a protograph is now described by a set of variable nodes, a set of check nodes, edges connecting variable nodes and check nodes, and the transmission energy used for every variable node, which is called the energy distribution. We optimize the energy distribution of protographs by choosing the energy distribution with the highest threshold calculated with a generalization of the discretized density evolution. Furthermore, we show by simulations that the performance of long LDPC codes based on protographs can be improved as expected from the thresholds.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check codes introduced by Gallager [1] more than 40 years ago, have been subject of many relatively recent research activities. The interest in these codes stems from their performance near the Shannon limit, their simple description and implementation, and their low decoding complexity. Because of this, LDPC codes became serious competitors to Turbo codes [2] and multiple Turbo codes [3].
Gallager considered only regular LDPC codes, i.e., codes that are represented by a sparse parity-check matrix with a constant number of ones in each row and in each column. Later, it was shown [4], [5], [6] that the performance of LDPC codes in the waterfall region can be improved by using irregular LDPC codes.
To analyze LDPC codes, density evolution, an analytic tool to calculate the thresholds of these codes, is introduced in [5], which can effectively be implemented as a discretized density evolution [7]. This tool is generalized [8], [9], [10], [11] to determine the thresholds of irregular LDPC codes with a special structure. In [10], LDPC codes are introduced that can be described by small protographs. These protographs can be designed such that they have thresholds very close to the Shannon limit. Since the tree-like structure of the LDPC codes constructed from protographs is exactly predetermined, the performance of these LDPC codes is exactly as expected from the thresholds.
Unfortunately, the protograph optimized in [10] has a high error floor in its bit error rate (BER) performance due to the small minimum distance of the resulting LDPC code.

Therefore, LDPC codes based on protographs are designed [12], whose minimum distances grow linearly with the length. Thus, LDPC codes based on protographs can be constructed that have a very good performance in the error floor region or a very good performance in the waterfall region. It is still an open problem to construct LDPC codes that have very low BERs in both regions.
In [13], asymmetric multiple Turbo codes [14], where in each branch a different convolutional encoder is used, are designed, which have a threshold very close to the Shannon limit. Therefore, the parity bits generated by the different encoders are sent with different energies such that the overall energy used remains the same.
In this paper, we show that also the thresholds of LDPC codes constructed from protographs can be further improved by using different transmission energies for the bits corresponding to the different variable nodes of the protographs. We optimize the energy distribution for LDPC codes based on protographs and we show by simulations that the BERs can be lowered by using different transmission energies for the different bits compared to using the same transmission energy for each bit.
The paper is organized as follows: In Section II, some fundamentals of LDPC codes based on protographs are given and the energy distribution for protographs is introduced. After describing the optimization algorithm for the energy distributions and calculating the thresholds for LDPC codes with different energy distributions in Section III, we show some simulation results in Section IV. Finally, we conclude our paper in Section V.

It is shown [9], that introducing punctured variable nodes, i.e., bits corresponding to variable nodes that are not transmitted, can improve the asymptotic thresholds v* of LDPC codes. The length and the rate of the punctured LDPC codes are denoted
by Np and by Rp = KINp, respectively, where Rp > R.
In [10], a method is described how to construct irregular LDPC codes with very good thresholds and very good performances. This method is based on small protographs and it is recalled here.
A protograph is a Tanner graph with a relatively small number of nodes and edges. The number of variable nodes of the protograph is denoted by n, the number of unpunctured
variable nodes by np, the number of check nodes by m, and
the dimension by k = n-m. The number of edges between a variable node and a check node can be larger than one, i.e., parallel edges are not forbidden. A larger graph can be obtained by a combination of several protographs and by permuting the edges such that the variable nodes of one protograph are connected with check nodes of other protographs. Connections are only allowed between variable nodes and check nodes of the same types as in the small protograph.
