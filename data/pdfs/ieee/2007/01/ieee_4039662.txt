Modeling Bit Flipping Decoding Based on Nonorthogonal Check Sums With Application to Iterative Decoding Attack
of McEliece Cryptosystem
Marc P. C. Fossorier, Fellow, IEEE, Kazukuni Kobara, and Hideki Imai, Fellow, IEEE
Abstract—In this correspondence, iteration-1 of bit flipping decoding based on a set of nonorthogonal check sums is analyzed for both regular and irregular models. In particular, the tradeoff between the Hamming weight (and overlapping) of the check sums and the number of redundant check sums required to start converging under iterative decoding is investigated. The model is then applied to an iterative attack of McEliece public-key cryptosystem since a successful attack of this system can be achieved by algebraic bounded distance decoding of a random code. Based on this model, the attack can be decomposed into two phases: a
preprocessing phase which, for one particular key , consists of finding a sufficiently large set S of check sums up to a certain Hamming weight, and a bit flipping decoding phase which uses the set S for each message encrypted with the key .
Index Terms—Bit-flipping decoding, iterative decoding, linear block codes, McEliece cryptosystem, public key cryptography.
I. INTRODUCTION
Iterative decoding methods have revealed very powerful recently. However they are believed to work well for codes whose Tanner bipartite graph representation [1] has few short cycles, which is indeed not the case for many families of good codes such as short to medium length Bose–Chaudhuri–Hocqunghem (BCH) codes or Goppa codes. Iterative decoding algorithms have been initially proposed in [2], [3] in conjunction with the design of a suitable family of codes. This approach was later recognized as an instance of Pearl algorithm [4], which is optimum for cycle-free graphs. For this reason, its successful application to graphs with a small number of short cycles in decoding algorithms was initially viewed as quite surprising [5]–[7].
The good error performances obtained with iterative decoding schemes also downplayed the importance of minimum distance in designing good families of codes. This is mostly due to the ability of iterative decoding schemes to achieve near-maximum-likelihood decoding (MLD), so that their application to bounded distance decoding (BDD) had no additional motivation. As a result, the effectiveness of iterative decoding schemes for BDD of codes with many cycles in their graph representation has been overlooked. If such applications raise little interest for coding schemes, this is not necessarily the case

for cryptosystems such as McEliece public key cryptosystem (PKC) [8], which highly depend on the hardness of realizing BDD and whose security level does not necessarily require error-free decoding with very high percentage.
Recently, it was shown in [9] that for the binary-symmetric channel (BSC), Gallager’s bit-flipping (BF) Algorithm-B (possibly slightly refined) was able to achieve near MLD for codes with many short cycles in their graph representation. To overcome the suboptimality of the decoding due to short cycles, a very redundant representation of the code parity-check matrix was used. This approach does not extend directly to the decoding of random codes or general families of codes since only minimum-weight codewords of the dual codes are used, and finding such a redundant set of codewords is generally as hard as to solve that the decoding problem [10], [11]. On the other hand, it can be observed from the results of [9] that achieving t-BDD (i.e., BDD up to the designed error correcting capability t of the code) is much easier than achieving MLD, suggesting that the use of minimum-weight codewords of the dual codes to perform the decoding can be relaxed for t-BDD.
Since its introduction in [8], McEliece PKC has been subject to numerous attacks but, as opposed to most other coding-based cryptosystems, this system has successfully resisted them. A good updated coverage of these attacks can be found in [12]. Motivated by this resilience, a signature scheme based on McEliece coding approach has been recently proposed in [13]. Although Goppa codes of much higher rates than that of McEliece PKC are required for this scheme, it was shown to be both efficient and secure with respect to the best known BDD algorithms [11].
In this correspondence, we model and analyze iteration-1 of bit-flipping decoding based on a redundant set of non necessarily orthogonal check sums. Motivated by an iterative attack of McEliece PKC, we are interested in check sums of low weight, but not necessarily of weight close to the minimum distance of the dual code. Furthermore, we implicitly assume that the dual codes considered have a weight profile close to that obtained from a binomial distribution for the check sum weights used in the decoding.
The correspondence is organized as follows. Iterative decoding based on a set of nonorthogonal check sums is presented in Section II for a regular model. The extension to irregular models and the resulting advantages for iterative attacks of cryptosystems are discussed in Section III. Simulation results are given in Section IV. The formulation of the approach to Niederreiter cryptosystem [14] is derived in Section V and concluding remarks are made in Section VI.

VI. CONCLUSION

In this correspondence, a general framework to evaluate the min-

imum number of redundant nonorthogonal check sums of given

weight(s) necessary for iterative bit-flipping decoding to start con-

verging has been developed. Both regular and irregular models have

been considered.

In the context of iterative decoding of linear block codes, this frame-

work provides a necessary condition for convergence of bit-flipping

decoding. However, due to the correlation introduced from the first it-

eration, it cannot be used in a recursive way to evaluate the error per-

formance of the iterative decoding, as done in [2], [3] for orthogonal

a tight performance analysis and in some cases, low-weight codewords

can be readily found based on the code structure.

In the context of cryptography, this framework becomes relevant to

analyze the likelihood of success of a new iterative attack of McEliece

PKC. The complexity of this attack depends on several time–memory

tradeoffs, and the irregular model provides additional refinements

based on error trapping. Although promising, this attack depends on

the difficulty of finding a sufficiently large set of redundant check

sums of small enough weight. Hence, these results motivate the inves-

tigation of new search methods for low-weight codewords: while the

existing searches are rather qualitative (finding few codewords of very

small (minimum) weight) [10], [11], more quantitative approaches

(finding many codewords of small enough weight) would better fit this

framework.
