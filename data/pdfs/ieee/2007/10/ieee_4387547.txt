Studies on Practical Low Complexity Decoding of Low-Density Parity-Check Codes

Zhiqiang Cui and Zhongfeng Wang School of EECS, Oregon State University,
Corvallis, OR 97331, USA Email: {cuizh, zwang} @eecs.oregonstate.edu

Abstract-This paper studies practical low complexity decoding of Low Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes. We first investigate VLSI implementation issues of two state-ofthe-art Weighted Bit Flipping (WBF) based decoding algorithms that were recently proposed in the literature. Then we present an optimized 2-bit soft decoding approach. It is shown that the proposed approach has comparable
hardware complexity with either of the two WBF-based algorithms while it has significantly better decoding performance.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes [1] have been and continue to be found more and more applications in practical communication systems due to their near Shannon limit performance and inherently parallelizable decoding scheme. In general, LDPC codes achieve outstanding performance with large codeword lengths (e.g., N>1000 bits), which lead to a linear increase of the size of memory used to store all the soft messages in LDPC decoding. Thus, the memory usually consumes a
significant portion of hardware in an entire LDPC decoder.
In the industrial standards of next generation communication systems such as WLAN (802.11.n) WiMAX (802.16e) and IOGBaseT (802.3an), the target
data rates range from a few hundred megabits per second to several gigabits per second. To achieve those very high decoding speeds, a large number of computation units are required, which will significantly increase the hardware cost and power consumption of the decoder [11].
Therefore, the research for low complexity decoding schemes and efficient VLSI implementation has become increasingly important to facilitate practical applications of LDPC codes. In general, the Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA) [1-2] has the best decoding performance for LDPC codes. To reduce the decoding complexity of SPA, various approximations of SPA were proposed [3-5].
On the other hand, a significant amount of effort has been made on hard decoding based approaches in order to seek very low complexity decoding. In the early 1960s, the Bit-Flipping (BF) algorithm [1] was introduced. It has very low decoding complexity since only simple logical operations are needed. However, it suffers from significant performance loss. To improve the decoding performance, a Weighted Bit-Flipping (WBF) algorithm [6] was proposed, in which reliability information was

incorporated. To further reduce the performance loss, several modifications on the WBF algorithm were presented recently. In [7], a modified WBF (MWBF) algorithm was proposed by incorporating both the check constraint messages and the intrinsic message for each bit. Further improvements were presented in [8] and [9]. The improved modified WBF (IMWBF) algorithm discussed
in [9] has the best decoding performance in general and largest complexity among these three modifications.

In this paper, we have studied VLSI implementation issues for WBF-based LDPC decoding algorithms. We presented a 2-bit soft decoding scheme. The proposed decoding scheme significantly outperforms the state-ofthe-art WBF-based decoding algorithms for the considered LDPC codes while maintaining comparable hardware complexity to WBF-based algorithms. In brief, the proposed 2-bit soft decoding scheme is more suited for practical low complexity VLSI implementation of LDPC decoders.
