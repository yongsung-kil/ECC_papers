Constructing Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes Using a Search Algorithm
Gabofetswe Malema Department of Computer Science
University of Botswana P/Bag 0022 Gaborone
Botswana malemag @mopipi.ub.bw

Abstract- This article presents a search algorithm for constructing regular and irregular quasi-cyclic LDPC codes. Code rows and columns are divided into j (column weight) and k (row-weight) or more groups respectively. Rows or columns in a group are connected to the same group and in order of appearance. Grouping of rows and columns forms sub-matrices. The sequential row-column connections order creates a cyclic structure in sub-matrices. The row-column constraint is observed to avoid four-cycles. The proposed algorithm is flexible compared
to other methods. It obtains codes over a wide range of girths,
rates and lengths. Bit-error rate simulations show that obtained codes have good performance with randomly searched codes performing better that sequentially searched codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have been shown to have good error correcting capabilities coming closer to the Shannon limit[l]. However, constructing LDPC codes that meet hardware and decoding performance requirements is not easy. Construction of LDPC codes is not unique and several parameters have to be considered. Flexible constructions are important especially at short lengths to meet practical constraints. Random search algorithms such as bitfilling (BF)[2] and progressive-edge growth (PEG)[3] have been developed to construct short LDPC codes with optimized girth, rate and performance. Though these methods are good at finding short length performing codes, obtained codes are not easy to implement because of the random rowcolumn interconnect[4]. Quasi-cyclic LDPC codes are one structured codes that are easy to implement because of their block and cyclic interconnections[5][6]. Although, there are several methods for constructing QC-LDPC codes, they do not allow arbitrary sizes and rates[7]. They also do not allow arbitrary sub-matrix configurations. In this paper we present a search algorithm that is more flexibility (code size, matrix configuration etc) in code construction compared to existing
methods. The algorithm which is based or similar to BF
and PEG algorithms obtains both regular and irregular quasi-
cyclic codes. The proposed algorithm was first introduced in
[8] for constructing column-weight two quasi-cyclic LDPC codes based on distance graph representation. We extend the algorithm to construct quasi-cyclic codes with column-weights higher than two by directly constructing a Tanner graph. This paper is organized as follows. Section II presents the

proposed algorithm with some obtained codes sizes, girths and rates. Section III evaluates bit-error rate performances
