Coded SC-FDE System over Impulsive Noise Channels
Qi Ying-hao, Wang bo, Huang Pei-wei, Cai Wen-hao
Department of Electronic Engineering, Shanghai Jiaotong University, Shanghai, 200030, P.R.China Email: {Qiyhao@sjtu.edu.cn.boby_wb@sjtu.edu.cn.Pwhuang@sjtu.edu.cn.Whcai@sjtu.edu.cn}

Keywords: cyclic prefix, impulsive noise, LDPC codes, SCFDE.
Abstract
Single carrier transmission with cyclic prefix (SC-CP or SCFDE), has lower PAPR (peak to average power ratio) problem and requires smaller backoff of amplifier than orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) technique. In order to combat impulsive noise under power limited channels, we apply regular LDPC (Low Density Parity Check) codes and propose a simplified decoding method. Also We compare the system with and without CP combining under uncoded and coded case. Simulation results show that the proposed method is effective under impulsive noise channels.
I Introduction
Orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (OFDM) with large number of subcarriers has an inherent drawback of high peak to average power ratio (PAPR) of the transmitted signals, comparing with a single carrier case. Large PAPR will greatly restrict the application for the difficulty in system implementation with an extremely large dynamic range required for high power amplifier (HPA) [1].
In 1994 Sari invented a single carrier system with cyclic prefix (SC-CP), known as SC-FDE, which has the similar spectral efficiency and immunity to multipath interference to OFDM system [2]. In addition, it has lower PAPR and sensitivity to nonlinear signal distortion, and significantly alleviates the carrier synchronization problems of OFDM pointed out in [3]. It has recently been proposed or adopted in some wireless applications as an alternative technique to OFDM [4, 5]. SC-FDE is suitable for power limited channels, where clipping may be harmful for OFDM system. In order to eliminate errors in noisy channels, both OFDM and SC-FDE system need powerful error control codes (e.g., Turbo code, LDPC code).
Low density parity check (LDPC) code, first investigated by Gallager [6] in 1962 and rediscoverd by Mackay and Neal [7] in 1996, shows good performance under AWGN and Rayleigh channels and attracts significant interest in recent years. We introduce a simple decoding method for LDPC codes under power limited channel with impulsive noise as simple as the one proposed in [8] and shows good performance close to it. We use the Middleton Class A noise model, which is applied in man-made impulsive noise channels such as wireless channels and power line channels.

Generally CP is removed at the receiver in traditional SCFDE system. In this paper, we compare the system with and without CP combining under uncoded and coded case.
The paper is organized as follows: Section II introduces the system model and analyzes the performance of the uncoded case. Section III presents the decoding method and CP combining under coded case. Section IV shows the simulation results. Section V concludes the paper.

V Conclusion
In this paper, we propose a simplified decoding method for regular LDPC codes in SC-FDE system over impulsive noise channel. CP combining in uncoded and coded systems is studied.
Under both coded and uncoded case, system with shorter CP is more close to the no CP system than that with longer CP. Also longer CP length results in more SNR loss in both cases. Under the uncoded case, system with CP combining can achieve a little gain at high SNR compared with system having no CP combining. The longer CP it is, the more gain it is obtained. However, system with CP combining performs better at low SNR and a little worse at high SNR under coded case.
The proposed method in this paper is suitable for SC-FDE system to combat impulsive noise with only low computation complexity. Because the power spectrum density of impulsive noise is flat in frequency, it is not easy to eliminate its effect with CP combining. Though CP can be combined for better decoding, SNR loss introduced by CP is not compensated yet.
