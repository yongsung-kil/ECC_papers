Abstract—Due to their excellent bit-error-rate performance, Low Density Parity Check codes (LDPC) have been adopted by the recent Digital Video Satellite Broadcast Standard (DVB-S2). In order to simplify the encoding procedure, Irregular Repeat and Accumulate (IRA) LDPC codes have been chosen.
This paper proposes an efficient, low delay and high throughput encoder architecture shared by all DVB-S2 LDPC-IRA codes. The architecture explores the periodic structure of the adopted codes by performing on the fly partial-parallel computation of the parity check bits.
The architecture implementation on a XC2VP30 Virtex2P Xilinx FPGA (@131.7MHz) shows a minimum throughput of 5.93 Gb/s in worst case conditions. Synthesis results are also presented.
Index Terms—Low-density parity-check codes; DVB-S2; encoder algorithm; parallel VLSI architecture; high throughput.
I. INTRODUCTION
The recent Digital Video Satellite Broadcast Standard (DVB-S2) [1], [2] has adopted a powerful forward error correcting (FEC) scheme based on the serial concatenation of BCH and Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes. This new FEC structure, combined with the adoption of high order modulations (QPSK, 8PSK, 16APSK and 32APSK), is able to provide capacity gains of about 30% over the previous DVB-S2 standard [3], with the LDPC codes playing a fundamental role in the improvement in performance.
LDPC codes are linear block codes defined by sparse parity-check matrices H and, usually, represented by Tanner graphs [4]. They are decoded using low complexity iterative belief propagation algorithms operating over the Tanner graph description. However, a major drawback is their high encoding complexity caused by the fact that the generator matrix, G, is in general not sparse. In order to overcome this problem, DVB-S2 standard has adopted a special class of LDPC codes, with linear encoding complexity, known by Irregular Repeat-Accumulate (IRA) [5], [6].
Another important issue in the design of LDPC encoder and decoder architectures for DVB-S2 is the fact that the standard supports two different frame lengths (16200 bits for low delay applications and 64800 bits otherwise) and a set of different code rates (1 4 ,1 3 , 2 5 ,1 2 , 3 5 , 2 3 , 3 4 , 4 5 , 5 6 , 8 9 and 9 10 ) for both frame lengths and different modulation

schemes. For each mode of operation is defined a different LDPC code and, although they share a similar structure and properties, this still poses an enormous challenge on the development of an encoder and a decoder fully compliant with all operating modes.
In this paper we propose a low complexity vectorized hardware architecture capable of encoding all LDPC codes defined in the DVB-S2 standard. The proposed architecture explores the properties of the adopted codes by a compacted memory mapping of the parity check equations and by performing partial-parallel computation of the parity check bits, providing a huge encoder throughput. Also, the partialparallel computation of the parity bits is performed on the fly according to the incoming flux of information bits, providing a negligible delay.
After the conclusion of this work, the authors became aware of a similar work [7]. However, the proposed architecture is more powerful and flexible and follows a different approach.
In the following sections we will describe in further detail the architecture of DVB-S2 LDPC encoder fully compliant with all operation modes. In section 2 we will make a short description of the LDPC-IRA codes adopted by the DVB-S2 standard. In section 3 we develop the mathematic algorithm behind our encoder architecture which we present in section 4. Finally, in section 4 are also presented synthesis results of the proposed architecture using the XC2VP30 Virtex2P Xilinx FPGA family and an estimate of the attainable throughputs which are one order of magnitude higher than the standard requirements.
II. LDPC-IRA CODES
The new DVB-S2 [1] standard adopted a special class of LDPC codes known by IRA codes [9] as the main solution for the FEC system. LDPC-IRA codes ally to the powerful error correction capabilities of the LDPC codes, with linear encoding complexity. In fact, although the parity check matrix H of an LDPC code is sparse, the generator matrix, G , needed for encoding usually is not sparse, leading to storage and encoding complexity problems.

A. H Periodicity

The choice of the wl CN’s that are connected to the first IN of group l is pseudo-random.

IV. ENCODER ARCHITECTURE
Based on the encoder algorithm described in the previous section, we proposed the architecture shown in Figure 2.
The architecture is composed by three major modules which are responsible for computing the Sr values (Task 1), performing the checksum of all the columns of the S memory (Task 2) where the Sr values are stored and, finally, computing the parity check bits (Task 3), according to (10), (11) and (14), respectively.
As it can be seen, the proposed architecture is independent of the chosen DVB-S2 LDPC-IRA code. In fact, we only need to design the S RAM for the worst case, i.e., q = 135 . Based on the code rate and frame length, we select the appropriate row of the control ROM, where the encoder control parameters are stored (the number of IN’s groups of weight w ) for each DVB-S2 LDPC code. The corresponding ROM of shifts and addresses is read sequentially in order to perform Task 1, according to (10) and (8). The addresses and shift values stored in the ROM’s can be easily obtained from the annexes B and C of DVB-S2 standard tables [1] and computed as shown in (10).
Task 2 is performed simultaneously with Task 1. In fact, we don’t need to wait for the end of Sr computation in order to obtain the checksum of all the columns of the S memory, which would imply a long delay. Together with the Sr computation (8), we just need to perform the GF(2) addition of the current shifted information vector with the previous accumulated result.
After Step 1, formed by tasks 1 and 2, the first 359 values of the Total GF(2) Sum register are processed by a simple combinatorial module which produces (13). Once computed, this value is written into the processed GF(2) sum register and Task 3 is realized, computing 360 parity bits per clock cycle according to (14).
The correctness of the encoder outputs were fully verified against known encoded data.

V. CONCLUSIONS
A highly efficient hardware implementation of an encoder for LDPC-IRA codes adopted for the recent DVB-S2 standard was described in this paper. By exploring periodicity

properties of the adopted LDPC-IRA codes, negligible delay and extremely high throughputs are achieved for all code lengths and rates. Synthesis results show a small amount of hardware resources required, which makes this architecture a very attractive solution for an efficient encoder, with a throughput more than one order of magnitude superior to the standard requirements.
