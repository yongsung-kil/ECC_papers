Lowering the Error Floor of LDPC Codes by a Two- stage Hybrid Decoding Algorithm
Yueguang Bian,Youzheng Wang,and Jing Wang

Abstract- In this paper, a hybrid decoding scheme is proposed to lower the error floor of low-density parity-check codes. With the observation that some error bits' LLR values oscillate throughout iterative decoding procedure, a "feedback BP" (FBP) decoding algorithm is presented as second-stage decoding cell to reduce the phenomena of oscillations. The hybrid decoding scheme, which consists LLR-BP decoding algorithm and FBP decoding algorithm, detects errors in the codewords obtained by using the parity check equations of LDPC codes. Simulation results show that the new decoding scheme exhibits a lower error floor than that of belief propagation decoding algorithm in the moderate and high SNR region.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) code, originally introduced by Gallager [1], rediscovered by Mackey [2], have attracted much attention for their excellent performance. In addition to their capacity-approaching performance, LDPC codes are also well suited for low-density iterative decoding [3], which makes them strong candidate for use in many modem communication and storage systems. Nevertheless, there exists one significant limiting factor for the successful employment of LDPC codes, which is the presence of error floors in their error-rate curves. The error floor represents a sudden change in the slope of the error curve that appears at moderate to high SNR. This change in slope makes the error rate of the codes decay very slowly, therefore reducing their effectiveness in systems which require extremely reliable communication. It is now well understood that the BP decoding failure in the error floor domain is due to the existence of pseudocodewords and trapping set [4]. Removing the obstacles and thus improving the BP decoding while keeping a reasonable computational complexity is on a great demand for high performance applications, e.g. optical communications and data storage, where error floor is a serious handicap.
Some general purpose BP-improvement strategies were already discussed in the literature [5], [6]. In [5], probabilistic schedule scheme is presented for message passing between variable nodes and check nodes. Although its computational complexity is mostly the same as those of standard BP, the implementation of schedule to control the flow of message is the extra complexity. And M. R. Yazdani suggested two modifications of BP algorithm in [6], referred to as "nor-
malized BP" and "offset BP", which are based on reducing
the reliability of messages throughout the iteration by using a multiplicative factor and an additive factor, respectively. These methods suggest a way to account for correlations (many loops) on all scales.

The influence of trapping sets on the onset of error floors in LDPC codes can attributed to the following phenomena, related both to the properties of the code graph and decoding algorithm, as well as to realization of certain special channel noise configurations. And the LLR values of error bits in the trapping set don't usually converge but oscillate during
the decoding procedure [7]. Therefore, if we introduce some
previous iteration results into each iteration, we can break the oscillation of bit probability to some extent, and eliminating some trapping sets from a LDPC decoding graph. A modified version of BP decoding algorithm, named "feedback BP" (FBP), by introducing feedback into the iterative process, is proposed in [8]. In [8], FBP algorithm works very well for finite geometry LDPC codes because of its good structure, but for some irregular LDPC codes or some other type LDPC codes, the performance of the FBP algorithm is also the same as the BP algorithm.
In this paper, to reduce the trapping sets under the BP algorithm for various LDPC codes, we propose a new decoding scheme, which combine BP decoding algorithm with FBP algorithm. In the proposed decoding scheme, the received codewords are first decoded by LLR-BP algorithm which yields a temporal estimate. Only if this temporal estimates satisfies LDPC parity check equation, we regard the temporal estimate as the final output and halt the decoding. If the temporal estimate does not satisfy the LDPC parity check equation, the received codewords are sent to the FBP decoder and results are given as final output.
The paper is organized as follows. The proposed two-stage hybrid decoding algorithm for LDPC codes is introduced in Section II. Simulation results of the proposed algorithm are presented in Section III, while some discussions are made in Section IV. Finally, conclusions are drawn in Section V.

IV. DISCUSSION
In [2], it is proposed to check whether the hard decision decoding of the a posteriori probabilities delivered at each iteration defines a codeword by checking whether the corresponding syndrome is zero. This stopping criterion is used as the algorithm of detecting errors in our combined decoding scheme. From Tables shown in Section III, it can be seen that some undetectable errors are found in various kind of LDPC codes. The percentage of undetectable errors can be much less than decoding errors, such as (155,64) and (1057,813) LDPC codes, while the percentage can't be ignored in other LDPC codes, such as (273,191) LDPC codes. In General, we can conclude that undetectable errors are mainly

affected by rate and code length. First, short block length codes usually have smaller minimum Hamming distance, which cause undetected errors increase. Second, high rate code means that more bits are required in each parity check equation and the effectiveness of each bit for parity check equation becomes smaller, consequently, the probability of undetectable errors will increase. And so for high rate and short length code, parity check equation isn't suitable as stopping criterion. Some other stop criterion, such as CRC algorithm, can be considerable.
This combined approach can also be extended in several ways: 1) the BP decoding algorithm can be replaced by suboptimum iterative decoding methods as those discussed in [9] ; 2) the feedback system can be designed for suboptimum decoding method, which can be used as second-stage decoder for combined scheme. ; and 3) some other algorithm of detecting error can be used together with parity check equations to reduce the undetectable errors.
V. CONCLUSION
This paper proposed a new decoding decoding scheme which combines BP decoding with the FBP decoding algorithm. This approach allows to lower the error floor of LDPC codes. Simulation results shows that the proposed can reduce about 40°0 errors with low incremental complexity at middleto-high SNR.
