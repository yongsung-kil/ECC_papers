Lu Xin1, Jun Xu2 1Shenzhen Institute of Information Technology, China 2Baseband Algorithm Department, ZTE Corporation

Abstract
This paper has presented various Min-Sum related LDPC decoding algorithms and their typical hardware architectures of check node update in the scenario of parallel implementation. For one check node update of Normalized Min-Sum algorithm, if
the current row weight is dc , dc multiplications are needed. If dc is large, dc multiplications are
needed, which leads to high complexity. In this article, one innovative method for check node update has been found, which can obviously reduce the number of comparison/selection operations for the Row Weight Matched Min-Sum Algorithm of high rate LDPC codes. Simulations have claimed the performance of Row Weight Matched Min-Sum is nearly the same as that of Log-BP, namely the optimal algorithm, which has shown that Row Weight Matched Min-Sum are good choices for LDPC decoding. Keywords: LDPC, CNU, BP, Min-Sum
1. Introduction
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are one special kind of linear block codes, whose parity-
check matrix H has a low density of 1’s. LDPCC
can be considered a powerful competitor to turbo codes in terms of performance and complexity. They are based on a similar philosophy with turbo codes: constrained random code ensembles and iterative decoding algorithms. LDPC coding can play a key role in the base band processing, which can be applied to correct errors in the data transfer.
Since originally invented by Gallager[1] in the early 1960's, they have experienced an amazing comeback in recent years. Furthermore many decoding methods had been brought forward, including standard BP (belief propagation) algorithm, BP-Based algorithm, APP-based algorithm, Max-Log-MAP algorithm, Min-Sum algorithm, Normalized Min-Sum algorithm[2][3] etc.
For Normalized Min-Sum algorithm, if the
current row weight is dc , dc multiplication shall
be needed. For rate 3/4 codes in IEEE802.16e [5][6],

the row weights of H are either dc = 14 or
dc = 15 , which means 14 or 15 multiplication
operations are need for one check node update. For rate 5/6 codes in IEEE802.16e[7], the row weights of
H are dc = 20 , which indicates 20 multiplication
operations are need for one check node update. For high rate codes, the hardware complexity of LDPC
decoder is high due to dc multiplications. In this
case, we would like to suggest an effective algorithm to dramatically reduce the number of multiplications under the condition that the decoding delay will not be increased.
In section 2, several typical decoding algorithms will be briefly introduced. Section 3 presents normal parallel implementation of CNU for Min-Sum related algorithms. And Section 4 has shown new more efficient parallel implementation of CNU for Min-Sum related algorithms. Simulation results and performance analysis have been displayed in section 5. Finally, section 6 presents our conclusion.

6. Conclusion
Several typical Min-Sum related LDPC decoding algorithms and normal CNU parallel implementations have been introduced and discussed in this document. Compared with normal CNU implementation, The combinatorial Logics ,such as m-to-2m decode, 2-to-1 MUX and dc AND gates has been introduced to generate more efficient new hardware implementations of check node update, which can obviously reduce the number of comparison/selection operations for Row Weight Matched Min-Sum algorithm for high rate LDPC codes.
According to simulation results, the performance of Row Weight Matched Min-Sum is nearly the same as that of Log-BP, namely the optimal algorithm. In general, this paper proposes that Row Weight Matched Min-Sum algorithms are good choices as LDPC decoding applications.
