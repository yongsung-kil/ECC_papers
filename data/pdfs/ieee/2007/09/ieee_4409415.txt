Abstract-The provision of broadband satellite services
depends on the utilisation of efficient and reliable radio interface, which has the ability to adapt to variable propagation channel conditions. In order to use the available spectrum more efficiently and to maximise the link reliability this paper presents the analysis of possible utilisation of LDPC decoder parameters to improve the performance of the DVB-S2 standard adaptive
coding and modulation (ACM) procedures. A switching
algorithm is proposed for selecting between the adjacent transmission schemes that is assisted by the information on the
number of iterations and the number of violated parity checks in previous frames obtained from the iterative LDPC decoder. The simulation results are given in terms of BER curves and the belonging LDPC decoder's average number of iterations and average number of violated parity checks. These results, in particular the LDPC decoder's probability density function of the number of required iterations, are further compared with results obtained with a SNR estimation algorithm proposed in the literature for the use in DVB-S2. The comparison confirms that the LDPC decoder information can be efficiently utilised in ACM
procedures.

In this paper we are focusing only on the ACM procedures, whose main goal is to maximise the link throughput at a given targeted bit-error-rate (BER). In principle we distinguish two types of ACM procedures. The first are the so called pre-estimated ACM procedures, where the transmission parameters are assigned at a call setup and do not adapt to the variations of the channel during the connection.

In the latter various criteria can be used, with the most common approach being based on the receiver's feed-back on the estimated SNR values [2], [4]. Besides controlling the ACM procedures, SNR estimation as an important measure of channel quality, is in contemporary communication systems also used in e.g. power control, BER estimation, iterative decoding, and softdemodulation.
SNR estimation algorithms are in general classified as dataaided (DA) and non-data-aided (NDA) algorithms, with the SNR estimates always derived from observing and averaging
the properties of the received signal. The DA estimators assume the knowledge of the received symbols, while the NDA estimators do not. Those estimators thus have an advantage of 'in-service' operation, without occupying any of the channel's resources. In contrast, the DA estimators require the knowledge of the transmitted symbols which consequently reduces the
system throughput, but are therefore expected to perform
better. Of course no additional penalty in throughput is
incur.ed by the DA estimators employing known data already
presented by training sequences provided e.g. for synchronization and equalization.
The DA estimators can further be divided into estimators
having perfect knowledge of the received sequence, and the DA estimators using an estimate of the transmitted sequence
from the receiver decisions. The first are often denoted by TxDA, while the second are denoted by RXDA [5].

The LDPC codes are typically presented either by the parity-
check matrices or Tanner graphs. Their decoding algorithms are iterative, with the message-passing (MP) algorithm, sometimes also denoted as sum-product algorithm or belief propagation algorithm, implemented in our model. The MP algorithm [7] is based on LDPC code's Tanner graph representation, with the initial APP values obtained from the soft-demodulator. The values are in the iterative decoding process updated in each iteration, until all the parity check constraints are verified, or the maximum number of iterations
