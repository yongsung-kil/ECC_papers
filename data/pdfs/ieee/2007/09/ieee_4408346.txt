1. Introduction
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are one special kind of linear block codes, whose parity-check matrix
H has a low density of l's. LDPCC can be considered a powerful competitor to turbo codes in terms of performance and complexity. They are based on a similar philosophy
with turbo codes: constrained random code ensembles and iterative decoding algorithms. LDPC coding can play a key
role in the base band processing, which can be applied to
correct errors in the data transfer.
Since originally invented by Gallager"' in the early
1960's, they have experienced an amazing comeback in recent years. Furthermore many decoding methods had
been brought forward, including standard BP (belief propagation) algorithm, BP-Based algorithm, APP-based algorithm, Max-Log-MAP algorithm, Min-Sum algorithm, Normalized Min-Sum algorithm[2] [3] etc.
