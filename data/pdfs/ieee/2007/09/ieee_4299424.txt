Geometric Interpretation and Design Strategy for π-Rotation LDPC Codes
Rich Echard, Member, IEEE, and Shih-Chun Chang, Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract— In this Letter we present a geometric interpretation of the π-rotation LDPC codes. Following this interpretation we develop efficient design strategies to search for the minimum d2 distance and the short loop count of a given code.
Index Terms— Lattice, LDPC codes, Manhattan Metric.

I. INTRODUCTION
T HE π-rotation low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were introduced in [1] as a class of deterministic LDPC codes with easy description, simple circuitry and superb performance. The π-rotation pattern traces back to the semirandom matrix introduced in [2] and is similar to the repeataccumulate codes of [3]. There have been studies on the structure, implementation and performance of the π-rotation codes [4], [5], [6], [7], [8], [9]. One of the main advantages of the π-rotation structure is the use of a single permutation matrix to define the entire code. The geometric symmetry in this structure allows very significant reductions in the complexity of selecting the proper permutation to provide good performance. In this Letter we explain how the symmetries apply to the practical aspects of constructing good π-rotation codes.
