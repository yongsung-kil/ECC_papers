Construction of LDPC Codes with Cycles Hold in Tanner Graph

Binbin Liu and Shunliang Mei
Department of Electronic Engineering Tsinghua University Beijing, China
Email: lbb03, meisl@mails.tsinghua.edu.cn

Dong Bai
Department of Electronics Peking University Beijing, China

Abstract—This paper presents a algebraic method for constructing LDPC codes. It uses a parity-check matrix of a short LDPC code with given degree distribution as mother matrix, upon which a long LDPC code is constructed by circulant permutation matrices. The number of cycles of given length in the Tanner graph of constructed codes is equal to or less than that of the short codes. Simulation results show that the error floor of constructed LDPC codes can be suppressed to a very low level.
Keywords-LDPC codes; cycles; circulant permutation matrices
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were first discovered by Gallager in early 1960s [1] and rediscovered by MacKay et al for their near Shannon limit performance under Belief Propagation decoding [2][3]. LDPC codes possess many advantages including parallelizable decoding, selferror-correct capability by syndrome check, and lower decoding complexity than turbo codes.
Methods for constructing good LDPC codes can be mainly separated into two classes. One is random or pseudorandom codes which based on computer search [4]. Another is algebraic constructed codes which based on finite geometries, combinatory design or circulant permutation matrices [5][6][7]. Random and pseudorandom constructions draw their strengths from long code length in conjunction with the Shannon’s noisy channel coding theorem, and the performance is evaluated based on asymptotical properties which become loosely bounded as the code length decreases. The performance of LDPC codes of finite length may be affected by other factors such as cycle property and minimum distance. The algebraic constructed LDPC codes guarantees better minimum distance properties for medium lengths.
Recently, LDPC codes constructed by circulant permutation matrices received increased interest [7]-[10], since their quasi-cyclic structure enables encoding by simple shift registers with the encoding complexity linearly proportional to their code length [11]. Another advantage of these codes is the significant reduction of memory needed to store their parity-check matrices since the special structure of them.

In this paper, we presented an algebraic method for constructing LDPC codes by circulant permutation matrices and constructed a class of LDPC codes named Cycle-Hold LDPC codes. By choosing the circulant permutation matrices appropriately, the number of cycles of given length in the Tanner graph of constructed codes is equal to or less than that of the short codes. The constructed LDPC codes can be either regular or irregular.
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section II, we review the LDPC codes constructed by circulant permutation matrices and their cycle properties. In Section III, we present the construction of CH-LDPC codes. We verify their performance by simulations in Section IV and finally give the concluding remarks in Section V.

However, when we wish to construct a good LDPC code, it seems very hard to find a parity-check matrix having large girth (the minimum length of cycles in Tanner graph). Consequently, in order to design a CH-LDPC code, we
can first find a mother matrix with good degree distribution
by density evolution [4][15], and then replace the 1’s in the mother matrix by circulant permutation matrices P(k,r) . So the constructed parity-check matrix preserves the degree distribution of the mother matrix. The parameters (k,r) for each circulant permutation matrices P(k,r) can be randomly chosen. But for P(k,r) in block-cycles of relatively short length, (k,r) is chosen with restriction α ≠ 1 . Thus every short block-cycle in mother matrix produces only one cycle of
the same length in the constructed parity-check matrix. We
say that the constructed codes have the Cycle-Hold property.

More strictly, if we choose (k,r) with restriction α = 1 and β ≠ 0 , these short cycles can even be eliminated from the constructed parity-check matrix. In other words, we can construct LDPC codes with the number of cycles of given length never increased.

IV. SIMULATION RESULT
Based on the method proposed above, we constructed three regular LDPC codes with length N = 6016, rate R = 1/2 (3,6), 5/8 (3,8), 3/4 (3,12), and compared them with random LDPC codes of the same parameter. For random codes, the cycles with length 2l ≤ 8 are eliminated. While for constructed codes, only the cycles with length 2l ≤ 6 are eliminated, the cycles with length 2l ≤ 10 are hold.
Figure 1 shows the bit error rate (BER) performance of the three constructed LDPC codes compared with random LDPC codes under AWGN channel. Legend (q, m, n) in figures denotes (size of circulant permutation matrices, number of rows in mother matrix, number of columns in mother matrix). Note that the error floor problem of constructed codes is not seen even the BER reaches 10-7 while the error floor of random codes comes much earlier. This is due to the fact that the error floor of constructed codes occurs at a very low SNR. For code with rate R = 1/2, 5/8, 3/4, the BER reaches 10-6 when Eb/N0 equals to 1.7 dB, 2.2 dB, 2.8 dB.
We also constructed three regular LDPC codes with rate R = 1/2 (3,6), length N =1020, 5100, 15300. Similarly, the cycles with length 2l ≤ 6 are eliminated, the cycles with length 2l ≤ 10 are hold. Figure 2 shows the BER performance of the three constructed LDPC codes under AWGN channel. It also can be observed that the constructed codes have very low error floor. As the code length becomes longer, the code performance gets better. For code with length N =1020, 5100, 15300, the BER reaches 10-6 when Eb/N0 equals to 1.5 dB, 1.8 dB, 2.7 dB.

V. CONCLUTION
In this paper, we presented an algebraic method for constructing LDPC codes and constructed a class of LDPC codes named CH-LDPC codes. By using a parity-check matrix of a short LDPC codes as mother matrix, a long LDPC code is constructed by circulant permutation matrices. We proved that by choosing the circulant permutation matrices with some restriction, the number of cycles of given length in the Tanner graph of constructed codes could be equal to or less than that of the short codes. Both regular and irregular LDPC codes can be constructed based on the degree distribution of mother matrix, which can be obtained by density evolution. Simulation results show that the error floor of constructed LDPC codes can be suppressed to a very low level.
