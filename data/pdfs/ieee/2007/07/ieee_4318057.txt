To construct a degree-m cover of T(H), denoted by
T(r)(H), we first make m identical copies of T(H). Any
permutation of the edges of the m copies of the Tanner graph

T(H) such that any edge e with left vertex v = v(e) and right

vertex c = c(e) before the permutation, is incident on the left

to one distinct vertex of the m copies of v, and on the right to

one distinct vertex of the m copies of c after the permutation,

will give a valid cover of T(H). The corresponding cover code

is denoted by C(m) and is a function of both H and the edge

permutation.

A stopping set is a subset of the codeword positions

such that the subgraph of the Tanner graph induced by the

corresponding bit vertices has no check nodes of degree one.

It is well-known that on the BEC iterative BP decoding is

successful if and only if the set of erased positions does not

contain a stopping set [4]. Recently, it has been argued that

stopping sets also influence on the performance of iterative

decoding on other binary-input memoryless channels [7], [10].
