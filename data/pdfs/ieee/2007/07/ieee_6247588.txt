Multi-Rate LDPC Decoder Implementation for China Digital Television Terrestrial Broadcasting Standard

Dimin Niu, Kewu Peng, Jian Song, Changyong Pan and Zhixing Yang
Tsinghua National Laboratory for Information Science and Technology Tsinghua University
Beijing 100084, China E-mail: ndm@mails.thu.edu.cn

Abstract-The China national standard of digital television terrestrial broadcasting employs Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes of three different rates as the inner Forward Error Correction (FEC) Codes. In this paper, according to the structure of the quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) code, an implementation of multi-rate LDPC decoder is proposed using semi-parallel structure, achieving the appropriate balance between decoding throughput and the utilization of hardware resource. Meanwhile, a novel multiplex model of storage space and processing units is proposed to increase the resource efficiency of this decoder, which is much better than that of simple combination of three single-rate decoders. Simulation results demonstrate that this multi-rate decoder's performance under AWGN channel is the same as that of single rate decoders, while it has higher resource utilization efficiency. Therefore it could help reduce the chip size of the decoder greatly. In addition, the multi-rate strategy can also be applied to other QC-LDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes was fIrst discovered by Gallager in 1960's [1] and has been overlooked since then for a long time. It was rediscovered by MacKay in 1990's [2]. The well-designed LDPC codes are Shannon-limit approaching codes, and their performance can outperform the Turbo codes in many situations. An LDPC code is defIned and described by a sparse parity-check matrix Hand LDPC code has many advantages with this property. Recently, the LDPC codes have been widely used in wireless and satellite communications. For this reason, the China national standard of digital television terrestrial broadcasting [3] employs LDPC codes of three different rates as inner Forward Error Correction (FEC) Codes. As these codes are quasi-cyclic LDPC codes and a semi-parallel decoder is applicable to these codes, therefore, semi-parallel architecture for LDPC decoding in [4] can be applied.
In order to construct LDPC decoder at higher effIciency, hardware reuse is absolutely necessary. An implementation of multi-decoder is proposed by Lei Yang, Hui Liu and C. 1. Richard Shi in [5]. As the hardware utilization effIciency for

the multi-rate LDPC decoder highly depends on the code structure, it is desirable to optimize the multi-rate decoder design based on the LDPC codes used in the system.
Our work follows the general idea of [5], but offers more advantages in the design of multi-rate LDPC decoder for China Digital Television Terrestrial Broadcasting Standard. First, we modifIed LDPC decoding procedure and make it more suitable for the decoder with semi-parallel architecture. Second, based on the similarity among LDPC codes of three different rates in that standard, a multi-rate decoder structure is proposed for FPGA implementation. Finally the multi-rate LDPC decoder is carefully optimized based on the special property of the LDPC codes in [3] and results in higher resource utilization effIciency.
The outline of this paper is as follows. In section II, we introduce the quasi-cyclic LDPC codes, a class of special LDPC codes. The decoding algorithm is presented in Section III. A novel FPGA implementation of multi-rate is proposed in Section IV. The performance of the decoder is verifIed by experimental measurement in Section V before conclusions are given in the Section VI.
II. QUASI-C YCLIC LDPC CODES
The parity-check matrix Hof an LDPC code is used to fully characterize the property of the code. The degree of a column is the number of 1's in the column, and the degree of a row is the number of 1's in this row accordingly. A quasi­ cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) [6] code of the index t is a particular linear code, whose parity-check matrix is made up of many square blocks, and each block is small and has same size of t. There are two different blocks in one parity-check matrix: all-zero matrix and circulant permutation matrix. A circulant permutation matrix with the parameter n is generated by shifting an identify matrix to the right by n times. III. DECODING METHOD
The decoding algorithm has been developed for many years. Nowadays, belief propagation (BP) algorithm and improved BP algorithm [2][8][9] is widely accepted as an efficient decoding algorithm of LDPC codes. However, the algorithm complexity makes it difficult for implementation, hence a simplified algorithm, namely min-sum (MS)[8][1O] algorithm, is proposed. Messages of MS algorithm are log­ likelihood ratios. Compared with BP algorithm, the MS algorithm brings a little loss in decoding error performance but leads to a much simple hardware structure. In addition, as described in [10], MS is more robust against the quantization error.

IV. MULTI-RATE DECODER
The LDPC codes used in the China national standard of digital television terrestrial broadcasting have three different rates of 24/59(0.4), 36/59(0.6) and 48/59(0.8) [3][11]. The parity-check matrixes H of these three codes are characterized in Table I.
From Table I, it is clear that the square blocks of each parity-check matrix Hare of same size and the degrees of columns are similar in each H. Besides, there are only two row degrees in each H. Based on these observations, a semi­ parallel architecture can be used in the decoding process [4]. The advantages of this particular design are quite obvious. In the semi-parallel decoder, the functional units can be reused to increase the resource utilization efficiency. By employing the semi-parallel architecture, one can make a trade-off between the hardware complexity and decoding throughput. The degree of the semi-parallel decoder in our decoder is 127. The semi-parallel decoder structure is simplified for single­ rate decoder, then modified for multi-rate decoder.

A, Architecture O/The Single and Multi-Rate Decoder
The multi-rate decoder is derived from the single-rate decoder architecture. A single-rate decoder consists of a controller unit, a number of memory units and processing units. In the multi-rate decoder, the primary structure is also based on the same three parts of the single-rate ones. In general, the architecture of our new multi-rate decoder follows the ideas described in [5], but has some unique features.
In the single-rate decoder, according to the decoding algorithm, the decoding process is show in the Fig. 1. As the figure shows, a complete decoding process contains four steps of initialization (Initial), horizontal update (Horizontal), vertical update (Vertical) and tentative decoding (Decision). Seen from (5), the operation of vertical update is partially similar to that of tentative decoding step. Concretely, the computing results of vertical update can be used in the tentative decoding. So it is possible to combine these two steps. Furthermore, the initial step of the decoding algorithm can also be combined into the vertical update.

B. Controller Unit
In the decoder, the controller unit operated as the core of decoder. The controller unit sends instructions to memory and processing units to let them work appropriately in different steps.
Operating with the processing units, the controller unit sends the rate-choice command to switch the multifunctional units to perform the operations accordingly. In addition, the controller unit also sends instructions to the memory units. The purpose of enable signals, which are sent from the controller unit, is to control the reading/writing operations of the memory. Besides, the controller also receives signals from other units, indicating the working conditions of the decoders, to decide the operation for the next steps.
C. Memory Units
One disadvantage of choosing LDPC codes is the considerable hardware resource (both logic elements and memory units) utilized in the decoding process. In order to save the memory of the FPGA chip, the reuse of memory units is necessary.
The block characters of three LDPC codes are shown in Table II. Seen from the Table, one can fmd the three LDPC codes have same block size and the block numbers are close to each other. This important observation helps achieve a high reuse rate of memory resources. In the multi-rate decoder, only 296 blocks for each processing units is needed, the highest number of memory blocks among all three code rates.
D. Processing Units and Routing Units
The processing units use significant portion of hardware resource in decoder implementation. In the single-rate decoder with code rate of 0.8, for example, the hardware resource (Logic Cells in FPGA) of the processing units is nearly 90 percent of that of the decoder. Consequently, it is desirable to improve the resource utilization efficiency of the processing units to greatly reduce the resource utilization rate. In the semi-parallel architecture of the decoder, VPU can be reused many times. The reuse parameter of our decoder is 127, so one VPU in the decoder can operate 127 times in one vertical update. Therefore less VPUs are needed in the LDPC decoder. Then look at Table I, one can fmd that the number of columns in the parity-check matrixes of the three LDPC codes is identical (59 X 127). So all three decoders need the same number of VPUs. As mentioned before, the degree of columns in all three parity-check matrixes can have only five values: 3,4,7,11, and 16. One VPU operates on one column of each Hat a time, so there are only five types of VPU needed in the decoder: VPU3, VPU4, VPU7, VPUll, and VPUI6.
As expressed in equation (5), VPU performs the additive operation on all input data and get the sum. The output of each VPU unit can be obtained by subtracting its input from the sum. Hence a multi-functional structure is proposed for the reuse of VPU, and each VPU unit uses the maximal possible number of inputs. Fig. 3 shows how an improved VPU can be reused for units of VPU3 andVPU4.
This VPU has six input ports, including a rate-chosen switch, a fixed input (Pn), which is the soft-decision received sequence, and four input data from the RRAM (datal-data4). The output contains a decoding result (Qn), which is only 1 bit, and four vertical update results. The fourth input data (data4) is controlled by the switch. When the switch is set to 1, the VPU operates as unit of VPU3 with the fourth output set to 0, otherwise the VPU operates as unit of VPU4. Besides, as we referred before, vertical update and tentative decoding are combined into the vertical steps. So the improved VPU also performs the tentative decoding operations and the one-bit decoding result shares the memory units with vertical update output.
Consequently this construction requires extra memory units, whose number increased from 296 to 332. But, as referred before, two routing units are saved.

Different from the column degree, the row degree of the parity-check matrix H in all three LDPC codes shares no similarity. For code rate of 0.4, the degree of row has two values of 7 and 8, and for rate of 0.6, the numbers are 12 and 13, while for rate 0.8, they change to 26 and 27. Therefore, there are six different HPUs in our multi-rate decoder and they are HPU7, HPU8, HPU12, HPUl3, HPU26, HPU27. Considering the horizontal update given in (4), the main functions of HPU are: minimum operation and sign operation, ideal for serial structure. Consequently, another reuse is proposed in the HPu. We can combine three HPU7 (or HPU8), two HPUl2 (or HPUI3) and one HPU26 (or HPU27) to form a joint HPu. Fig. 4 shows a J-HPU, which can function as either HPU8-HPU8-HPU8, or HPUI3HPUl3 or HPU26.
As shown in Fig. 4, the J-HPU has a rate-chosen switch and 27 data input ports from QRAMs. The first level of the J-HPU consists of 4 basic HPU units, including two HPU8 and two HPU5. The output of HPU5 can be directly used as the input of HPU8. Similarly, the second level has two HPUI3. Each HPU13 takes output from a HPU5 and a HPU8 of the first level as the input. Finally the third level functions as HPU26.
V. EXPERIMENTAL RESUL
The multi-rate decoder is implemented on an ALTERA STRATICXII-EP2S90 FPGA. This design is materialized in Verilog HDL, analyzed, synthesized, placed and routed by Quartus II 5.0.
In the China national standard of digital television terrestrial broadcasting, the baseband symbol rate is 7.56Mbps. In this system, an OFDM frame contains a frame body (FB) and a frame header (FH). The length of FB is 3780 symbols, and the length of FH can be 420, 595 or 945 symbols. Take 420 for example, a single frame have 4200 symbols and therefore lasts about 555.6us (420017.56 us). In one second, there are 1800 OFDM frames. Taking the highest modulation (64QAM) for example, each symbol is demapped into six bits. Then the minimum gross throughput of the system is 40.824MHz (l800x3780x6).

VI. CONCLUSION
This paper proposed a novel implementation of multi-rate decoder, which is specially optimized to support the Chinese Digital Television Terrestrial Broadcasting Standard. This multi-rate decoder can significantly improve hardware utilization efficiency, therefore, can reduce the implementation cost as well as increase the reliability of the hardware. Experiment result shows the error performance of the proposed design is equivalent to that from the single-rate decoder, validating the effectiveness of our proposed design.

The gross throughput of the decoder is 60.8MHz, which is adequate to the demand of the China national standard of digital television terrestrial broadcasting.
