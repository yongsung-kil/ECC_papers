Efficient Encoding of Low-Density Parity-Checl( Codes in Chinese Digital Terrestrial Television Broadcasting Standard
Jingli Lin, Longjiang Jing, Weile Zhu, Jijun Zheng
School of Electronic Engineering University of Electronic Science and Technology of China
Chengdu, Sichuan, China Linjingli77@163.com

Abstract-Since Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes have many advantages for fast encoding, they are adopted in channel coding of the Chinese Digital Terrestrial Television Broadcasting (DTTB) Standard. The construction of QC­ LDPC codes in Chinese DTTB standard, which is based on the structure of RS codes with two information symbols, is described. Also, three fast encoding circuits are presented, which encode in serial, parallel and partial parallel, respectively. They have speeds and complexities that differ with each other. According to the requirements of the system, one of them can be chosen carefully.
I. INTRODUCTION
Over recent years, low density parity check (LOPC) codes [I] have received much attention owing to their near Shannon-limit performance and relatively simple decoding algorithm. Therefore, the LDPC codes have been used in some broadcasting standards in which the Chinese Oigital Terrestrial Television Broadcasting (OTIB) Standard [2][3][4] is included.
The channel coding of the Chinese OTIB standard uses concatenated coding architectures where BCH codes are as outer codes and Quasi-Cyclic LDPC codes as inner codes. In view of sufficient research and development of encoding of BCH code, this paper is emphasis on fast encoding of LOPC codes involved in this standard.
This paper is organized as follows: Section II describes the construction of these LOPC codes briefly. Section III presents the fast encoding circuit of these LDPC codes in three ways, and Section VI concludes this paper.

IV. CONCLUSION
The QC-LDPC codes involved in Chinese DTTB have many advantages for encoding. Three circuits for fast encoding presented in this paper have respective speeds and complexities, which are listed in Table.I. The hardware can be designed according to tradeoffs between them. As for FPGA, the number of used flip-flops should be take into account carefully. Therefore, the first and the third circuits are better choices.
