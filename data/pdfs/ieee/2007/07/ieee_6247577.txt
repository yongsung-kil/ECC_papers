Design of LDPC Coded Recursive MSK Systems 
Jianzhong Huang, Shuling Che, Baoming Bai and Xinmei Wang 
State Key Lab of Integrated Service Networks 
Xidian University, Xi'an, China. 
hjz_xidian@yahoo.com.cn 
Abstract-T his paper investigates a low-density parity-check 
(LDPC) coded recursive minimum shift keying (MSK) scheme 
when the receiver employs iterative decoding and demodulation. 
Since the recursive continuous phase encoder (RCPE) of MSK 
can be expressed by a bipartite graph, the overall system can be 
viewed as a concatenation of two Tanner graphs. By analyzing 
the error correlation in the MSK demodulator and the overall 
loop in the joint Tanner graph, we show that the error correlation 
has a great impact on the performance of the iterative system 
when the LDPC codes are not well designed and interleavers are 
not employed. We develop a new design rule of LDPC codes 
to reduce the impact of the error correlation on the system 
performance while without interleavers. Simulation results show 
that the proposed design rule can reduce the impact of the error 
correlation in a simple and effective way. 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Minimum shift keying (MSK) is widely used over radio 
channels due to its good spectral properties and ability of 
employing nonlinear amplifiers to operate in saturation [1]. It 
is well known that an MSK modulator can be decomposed into 
a rate-1I2 binary convolutional encoder, namely, continuous 
phase encoder (CPE) and a memory less mapper [2]. Therefore, 
MSK itself can be viewed as a coded modulation scheme. 
The (CPE) in the MSK modulator can be realized either 
in recursive mode (RCPE MSK) or in non-recursive mode 
(NRCPE MSK). Several coding techniques such as trellis 
codes and LDPC codes have been designed for MSK to 
compose coded modulation systems with iterative decoding 
and demodulation [3][4][5]. One of the main ideas in these 
systems is to exploit the inherent memory characteristic of 
the continuous phase modulation. 
The soft output information of MSK demodulator calculated 
by the BCJR algorithm [6] has inherent correlation because of 
the CPE in the MSK modulator. Therefore, an interleaver is 
placed between the outer code and CPE in most concatenated 
systems to achieve an interleaver gain [3][5][7]. Due to the 
inherent interleaving property of the LDPC codes, we hope 
that the interleaver can be omitted in the system. However, 
without the interleaver between the LDPC codes and the RCPE 
MSK, the structure of the LDPC codes should be carefully 
designed. 
The remainder of the paper is organized as follows. 
Section II briefly describes the system model, recursive and 
non-recursive mode of MSK modulator. Section III discusses 
the correlation of the soft output information in the MSK 
demodulator, which facilitates us to understand the design 
rule for LDPC codes proposed in this paper. Section IV deals

III. THE ERROR CORRELATION OF MSK 
Now we study the error patterns of the MSK demodulator 
which will facilitate us to understand the design rule. A 
typical assumption in most LDPC coded modulation systems 
is that channel seen by the LDPC codes is independent, but 
this condition may be unreasonable in LDPC coded recursive 
MSK systems when RCPE form is used. We need to consider 
temporal correlation of the MSK demodulator which will 
determine whether the interleaver is needed or not in an LDPC 
coded modulation system. 
Assume the decision sequence demodulated by the MSK 
demodulator is X' = (xv, xw, ... x'tv). If the two consecutive 
bits (xx, Xx+l) are in error at the same time while Xx_l 
and Xx+2 are correct, we call such error pattern as two 
consecutive errors. Independent error, three consecutive errors, 
four consecutive errors and so on can be defined in a similar 
way. 
As shown in Fig. 2, the NRCPE form of MSK can be 
actually seen as a repetition code. When the channel model is 
AWGN, the BER performance is the same as the interleaved

= (Xl, X2,' " XN) with BPSK modulation with the 
same Eb/NO. Therefore, the equivalent channel seen by the 
LDPC decoder is a memoryless channel when the NRCPE 
MSK acts as an inner code in the system. Table. 1 shows the 
error patterns distribution after NRCPE MSK demodulator ver¬≠
sus Eb/No. The independent error dominates the error patterns 
in NRCPE MSK demodulator. The iterative demodulation does 
not improve the system performance when the NRCPE form 
of MSK is used and codes that are optimal for BPSK are also 
optimal in this case [3]. 
Since the RCPE form of MSK modulator has memory, the 
soft information of the current bit is correlated with previous 
transmitted bits, which will result in error correlation. The 
error correlation will degrade the performance of the system 
greatly which must be considered when we design LDPC 
codes for RCPE MSK.

the extrinsic information generated by MSK demodulator has 
correlation due to the fact that RCPE is a convolutional code. 
Therefore, in an LDPC coded recursive MSK system, the 
check equations in a parity check matrix H have to take the 
error correlation into consideration. In many LDPC coded 
modulation systems, an interleaver is not needed because the 
LDPC codes own a random embedded interleaver. Of course, 
an external interleaver can distribute the error correlation of 
RCPE MSK demodulator, so does the channel interleaver 
used in many communication systems. However, we hope 
that the external interleaver can be omitted in the system. In 
the next section, we will discuss the new rule on the design 
of LDPC codes for RCPE MSK system. 
IV. A DESIGN RULE OF LDPC CODES FOR RCPE MSK 
When the recursive CPE is used, codes optimal for BPSK 
with AWGN are not optimal [3]. The error correlation should 
be considered seriously when we try to design good LDPC 
codes matched to the recursive CPE and the iterative decoding 
algorithm. We begin with the joint graph structure of MSK and 
the LDPC codes. 
Consider the recursive CPE shown in Fig. 3, the two states 
RCPE can be expressed in terms of a bipartite graph similar 
to that of an LDPC code. Therefore, the overall LDPC codes 
and the RCPE can together be expressed as the concatenation 
of two graphs shown in Fig. 4. 
First, we assume that there is no interleaver introduced 
between the RCPE MSK and LDPC codes. The message 
passed from RCPE MSK demodulator to LDPC decoder is 
no longer memory less but acts as a "virtual channel" with 
temporal correlation according to the simulation results shown 
in Table. 2. 
Second, we consider the overall loop in the joint Tanner 
graph. The joint graph can be separated into two parts: one is 
the Tanner graph of LDPC codes and the other is the bipartite 
graph of RCPE MSK. The bipartite graph of RCPE MSK has 
no loops because of its special structure. Although the loop of 
LDPC codes can be quite large, the overall loop in the joint 
Tanner graph may be determined by the joint structure of the

C. Simulation results 
All the simulation results are presented for rate-l/2 codes 
on AWGN channel. We only show the simulation results 
for RCPE MSK in RA and M _RA structures. The degree 
of variable nodes in randomly constructed part is 3. Fig. 8 
shows the BER performance for RCPE MSK in RA mode 
with interleaver or not. It can be seen that the interleaver 
can improve the perfonnance significantly due to the fact 
that the error correlation can be disturbed by the interleaver. 
The perfonnance of M_RA structure with minimum variable 
nodes distance 3 and 11 are compared with the performance 
of RA mode with interleaver in Fig. 9. The M_RA structure 
even outperform the interleaver mode at low Eb/NO which 
indicates the M_RA structure can reduce the error correlation 
effectively. However, the error floor of the M_RA is higher 
than interleaver mode. 
V. CONCLUSIONS 
In this paper, we proposed a new rule on the design of LDPC 
coded recursive MSK systems based on the error correlation 
of the MSK demodulator and the overall loop in the joint 
Tanner graph. Simulation results show that the two consecutive 
errors and four consecutive errors dominate the error patterns 
of the RCPE MSK. The M _RA structure can reduce the error 
correlation to some extent with low complexity while keeping 
the linear encoding complexity of the RA codes because 
no external interleaver is needed in the system. However, 
this method results in higher error floor than the interleaver 
structure. Better results are possible to be achieved by using 
more complex degree profiles which can be found by using 
the density evolution [11] .
