AbstractвҖ”Sparse code multiple access (SCMA) has been considered as a competitive candidate multiple access technology to address the challenge of high spectrum efficiency and massive connectivity for 6G communications. However, the use of uniform quantization with limited accuracy may lead to considerable BER performance degradation for the message passing algorithm (MPA) in SCMA detection. In practical communication systems, SCMA operates with channel coding to provide low-latency and reliable communication, and low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are employed in this work. To address this issue, the quasiuniform quantizer is proposed, where the uniform quantization strategy is adopted when the probability information value is small, otherwise, the non-uniform quantization is used, which improves the dynamic range of message exchanging in MPA and minimum sum algorithms. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed quasi-uniform quantizer achieves superior BER performance even with lower quantization precision compared to the uniform quantizer, and subsequently, the computation cost for hardware implementation can be reduced.

I. INTRODUCTION
Sparse code multiple access (SCMA) [1] is an attractive multiple access technology to address the challenge of massive connectivity and low latency for the increasing demands of Internet-enabled sophisticated applications in 6G communications [2], [3]. Different from Power domain Non-orthogonal multiple access (PD-NOMA) [4], where different power levels are pre-allocated to multiple users before transmission, in SCMA, a promising code domain NOMA technique, the message bits of each user are directly mapped to complex multi-dimensional sparse codewords selected from a predesigned unique codebook. At the receive end, in PD-NOMA, a successive interference cancellation is usually used to detect the transmitted bits, while the factor graph based message passing algorithm (MPA) is widely considered to perform multiuser detection in SCMA.
Generally, SCMA can achieve better performance than PDNOMA in terms of bit error ratio (BER) and sum rate at the cost of high computational complexity [5], [6]. In this way,

most recent studies focus on the reduction of computational cost of the MPA detector, and the codebook design to achieve better BER performance in diversity channels. Since the minimum Euclidean distance between superimposed codewords is a key performance indicator in SCMA systems, the authors in [7] propose a novel SCMA codebook design for the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel with large minimum Euclidean distance in the heavily-overloaded manner. By combining frequency and spatial diversity, the authors in [8] present a hybrid minimum product distance based codebook design over Rayleigh fading channels under the pairwise error probability minimization. To improve the BER performance of SCMA systems in the presence of phase noise, a novel minimum phase noise design metric has been proposed in [9] by analyzing its pair-wise error probability. On the other hand, the computation cost reduction of SCMA detection has gained attention in recent years. A sphere decoding based MPA detection scheme has been proposed in [10] by reducing the number of believable superposed constellation points involved in the exchanging of messages. The authors in [11] propose a threshold-based edge selection criterion for the MPA detection, which reduces the number of neighboring edges involved in the message exchanging. Moreover, by dynamically applying partial gaussian approximation on interference, the computational complexity of the above modified MPA can be further decreased. An improved approximate MPA detection has been proposed for the low earth orbit satellite SCMA systems in [12], where a modified version of this MPA detector has also been provided for the imperfect ground-space channels.
In wireless communication systems, the integration of SCMA and channel coding is practical to enhance its stability and reliability. A high-throughput low density parity check code (LDPC) coded multi-mode SCMA detector has been provided in [13] to construct its low-complexity realization by adopting the minimum mean-square error with parallel interference cancellation detection. The conventional MPA detection for LDPC coded SCMA system suffers from high complexity and large decoding latency when it supports more users. Another practical issue for the hardware implementation of SCMA system is the design of quantizer in the baseband signal processing. The pioneer works in [15] and [16] only adopt the uniform quantizer, which leads to high complexity and performance degradation in the practical hardware design and implementation.
To address this issue, a new quasi-uniform quantization method is proposed for the LDPC coded SCMA system. Specially, the traditional uniform quantization is adopted when the value of probability is small; otherwise, the non-uniform quantization is potentially used. By increasing the dynamic range of probabilities in the iterations, the BER performance of this LDPC coded SCMA system outperforms that of the conventional uniform quantizer with identical quantization bits. Or equivalently, the non-uniform quantizers can achieve comparable BER performance to uniform one with less quantization bits.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this work, we investigate the superiority of the proposed quasi-uniform quantizer over the conventional uniform one in terms of the LDPC coded SCMA systems. To achieve this, the uniform quantized MPA for SCMA detection and min-sum LDPC decoder are firstly introduced, where the conversion criterions of LLRs between MPA and min-sum algorithm are formulated during message exchanging. To reduce the complexity of hardware implementation, the quasi-uniform quantization based iterative detection and decoding for LDPC coded SCMA systems is proposed. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed quasi-uniform quantization can achieve better BER than the uniform quantizer under the same quantization precision. The proposed quasi-uniform quantization provides a more efficient option in the sense of quantization strategy for LDPC coded SCMA systems.
