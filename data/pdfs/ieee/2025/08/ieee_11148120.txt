Construction of QC-LDPC Codes with Product-like Exponent Matrices Based on Rectangular Area
Jian Fang, Yuxiang Zhou, Min Zhu, and Baoming Bai State Key Laboratory of ISN, Xidian University, Xi’an 710071, China E-mail: fangjian@stu.xidian.edu.cn, 23011210536@stu.xidian.edu.cn,
mzhu@xidian.edu.cn, bmbai@mail.xidian.edu.cn

Abstract—This paper proposes a novel algorithm for constructing girth-8 quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes with product-like (PL) exponent matrices, termed PL-QC-LDPC codes. To simplify the analysis of cycles in the Tanner graph of PL-QC-LDPC codes, we introduce the rectangular area (RA) and present an efficient semi-search RA algorithm to determine the minimum size of circulant permutation matrices (CPMs) in the exponent matrix. Moreover, search depth is employed to balance the trade-off between search complexity and optimization of the minimum CPM-size. Numerical results demonstrate that our proposed method, combined with masking method, can achieve smaller CPM-size and lower error floor than the greatest common divisor (GCD) method.
Index Terms—QC-LDPC codes, large girth, product-like structure.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have attracted significant attention due to their capacity-approaching performance and low-complexity iterative decoding. Quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes [2] are a special class of LDPC codes whose parity-check matrices are composed of an array of circulants, such as circulant permutation matrices (CPMs). With the help of this structure, QC-LDPC codes enable efficient parallel hardware implementation and wide application in several communication standards [2]–[4]. As the volume and variety of mission-critical machine-type communication (MTC) grow, ultra-reliable low-latency communication (URLLC) modes will become increasingly important [5]. Short codes with ultrahigh reliability remain to be studied. For iterative decoding of QC-LDPC codes, the performance is significantly impacted by the minimum cycle length (the so-called girth) and degree distribution of the Tanner graph [6]. Besides, QC-LDPC codes with larger girth typically achieve a larger minimum distance [17]. For a given code length and rate, it is challenging to construct QC-LDPC codes with large girth and small CPM size.
The construction of QC-LDPC codes can be roughly divided into computer search-based and algebraic-based methods. Random-like QC-LDPC codes constructed by the exhaustive search generally require high search complexity but achieve larger girth than algebraic-based QC-LDPC codes for the same code length. In contrast, the algebraic-based method provides an explicit construction with scalable structure and predictable girth. As for computer search methods, there are a total of Zm×n exponent matrices P = [pi,j ]0≤i<m,0≤j<n with the

CPM size Z for the full-connected QC-LDPC codes. To reduce the search complexity, nonisomorphic classes were proposed for m = 3 and girths 6, 8, 10, and 12 [7]. To accelerate the calculation of girths, the product of the parity-check matrix with its transpose was employed for n = 2, 3, 4 and girths 6 and 8 [8]. When it comes to algebraic construction, symmetrical constraints [9] and integer ring [10] were employed for constructing QC-LDPC codes with large girth. The powerlike parity-check matrix satisfying pi,j = αiβj mod Z was presented in [11], which was further investigated in [12]–[14] for girths 6, 8, and 10, where α and β are non-zero integers from the field GF(Z). However, the CPM size Z and the block length are limited by the selection of the field size. To improve the flexibility of the CPM size, the product-like parity-check matrix was presented in [15], such as pi,j = ij mod Z. Moreover, a general product-like structure pi,j = aij mod Z with distinct integers ai was investigated, e.g., shortened array codes [16] and full-length row multiplier (FLRM) codes based on the greatest common divisor (GCD) [17]–[20] for regular QC-LDPC codes. However, these existing QC-LDPC codes with large girth primarily focus on the full-connected QCLDPC codes, whereas regular and irregular QC-LDPC codes are directly obtained by masking method [21]. Particularly, construction based on masking matrix instead of all-one matrix would have a smaller CPM size.
In this paper, we extend the QC-LDPC codes with productlike (PL) exponent matrices, termed PL-QC-LDPC codes, such as pi,j = aibj mod Z for distinct integers ai and bj. Recognizing that the product of two integers can be regarded as the rectangular area (RA), we adopt the RA to analyze the condition for cycles. Based on the area constraint, we propose a semi-search RA algorithm for constructing girth8 PL-QC-LDPC codes. Moreover, we introduce search depth to improve the search efficiency of constructing PL-QC-LDPC codes. Finally, we analyze the minimum CPM sizes of QCLDPC codes with different degree distributions. Numerical results show that our proposed RA method can achieve smaller CPM size, lower error floor, and better compatibility with masking method than GCD method.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we investigate the construction of girth-8 PLQC-LDPC codes to minimize the CPM-size for a given code length and rate. With the help of the PL structure, we adopt the concept of RA to simplify the analysis of cycles and propose an efficient semi-search RA algorithm. To balance complexity and optimization of the minimum CPM-size, search depth is introduced. Moreover, the proposed algorithm exhibits favorable compatibility with masking matrices. Numerical results demonstrate that our proposed RA method can achieve smaller CPM-size and lower error floor than the GCD method.
