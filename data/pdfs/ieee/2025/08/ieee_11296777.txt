An Error-Threshold Based Joint Phase Estimation
and Decoding Method for Non-Pilot-Aided Systems
Jiawen Chen∗, Xuhui Ding∗,†, Xianchao Zhang‡, Jianguo Li∗, Xiangyuan Bu§, and Kai Yang§ ∗School of Cyberspace Science and Technology, Beijing Institute of Technology, Beijing, China †Advanced Technology Research Institute, Beijing Institute of Technology, Jinan, China
‡Provincial Key Laboratory of Multimodal Perceiving and Intelligent Systems, Jiaxing University, Jiaxing, China §School of Information and Electronics, Beijing Institute of Technology, Beijing, China Emails: chenjiawen@bit.edu.cn, dingxuhui@bit.edu.cn, zhangxianchao@zjxu.edu.cn, jianguoli@bit.edu.cn, bxy@bit.edu.cn, yangkai@bit.edu.cn

Abstract—In 5G/6G communication environments, synchronization technologies encounter challenges including excessive pilot overhead, poor reliability at low signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), and limited estimation accuracy. This paper proposes a joint carrier recovery and iterative decoding algorithm based on weighted and normalized syndrome satisfaction probability (WNSSP). Our architecture implements parallel phase compensation through a multi-layer factor graph model. Within this framework, WNSSP dynamically weights and fuses belief provided by the soft lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes decoder to construct a maximum likelihood estimation function that simultaneously achieves carrier recovery and information reconstruction. To address the single-metric evaluation limitation, code-modified mean square error (CM-MSE) is established according to the residual phase error threshold, which enables concurrent assessment of synchronization accuracy and demodulation reliability. Simulations demonstrate that while effectively eliminating redundancy caused by pilot sequence, the proposed algorithm achieves 0.5–0.8 dB phase estimation gains and 0.15–0.5 dB bit error rate (BER) improvements compared with existing methods.
Index Terms—Iterative receiver, phase estimation, channel coding.

The extensive application and ongoing research into 5G/6G have intensified the demand for high-speed data transmission and massive connectivity, imposing the challenges of spectral efficiency and synchronization accuracy in wireless systems. These requirements expose critical limitations in existing cascaded synchronization-demodulation architectures, particularly in scenarios with constrained spectral resources and dynamic channel conditions. Consequently, ensuring efficient and reliable information synchronization and demodulation is crucial for next-generation wireless communication systems.
Regarding the carrier recovery problem within synchronization modules, existing methods can be primarily categorized into two classes, data-aided (DA) and non-data-aided (NDA) methods [1]–[3]. DA methods typically employ pilot signals for correlation accumulation to achieve channel parameter estimation, but this approach introduces additional bandwidth and power overhead. In contrast, traditional NDA methods directly utilize the nonlinear transformation of received data for carrier estimation. However, due to the lack of prior information,

Distinguished by near-Shannon-limit error correction capabilities, turbo codes, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, and polar codes have become the primary channel coding schemes adopted in 5G/6G technologies [4]. To overcome the limitations of conventional synchronizers, the receiver architectures have been developed to jointly proceed with decoding and carrier estimation processes based on iterative messagepassing algorithms (iMPAs). Existing literature presents a range of methodologies as shown in TABLE I. In the harddecision domain, [5] and [6] employ hard decision outputs of the iterative decoder to execute the maximum likelihood (ML) estimation of phase offset. However, this feedback mechanism relies on the results from the previous iteration, leading to high computational complexity. While [7] proposes a simplified two-step estimation approach, its estimation accuracy is susceptible to the pre-defined candidate phases in the first step. Most research focuses on constructing cost functions based on the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) information to enhance phase estimation accuracy. In [8], the soft-in soft-out (SISO)

Conversely, [9] achieves a trade-off between performance and complexity by leveraging the feedback of the extrinsic information, thereby circumventing soft value reconstruction. However, this method still exhibits limitations in estimation accuracy and range. [10] derives an approximate expression for phase ML estimation by integrating channel prior information with posterior probabilities of decoding outputs. In the probability domain, the relevant literature is relatively scarce. In [14], a sum-product and the expectationmaximization (SP-EM) framework is first developed to design a message-passing phase estimator, but it necessitates gradient computations that introduce significant computational complexity. Our previous work devises a joint synchronization and decoding architecture employing normalized syndrome satisfaction probability (NSSP) from LDPC decoding as a fundamental cost function [15]. Additionally, most existing works analyze synchronization and decoding performance separately using conventional metrics like bit error rate (BER) and mean squared error (MSE), lacking a comprehensive evaluation of their joint performance.
This paper proposes a novel decoding algorithm with integrated carrier phase estimation. We adopt a weighted and normalized syndrome satisfaction probability (WNSSP) and establish residual phase offset tolerance thresholds, enabling joint analysis of synchronization and decoding performance. Furthermore, we conduct theoretical simulations on codemodified mean squared error (CM-MSE) and BER.

C. Code-Modified Mean Squared Error
To evaluate phase estimation accuracy and decoding performance jointly, we further propose a CM-MSE performance metric as

0,

ε (φˆ) ≥ th σ2 ,

CM − MSE (φˆ) = |φˆ − ∆φ|2, ε (φˆ) < th σ2 , (12)

where th σ2 is the error threshold for NSSP to enable integration of decoding belief with phase estimation accuracy. Specifically, when NSSP reaches higher belief than th σ2 , the phase-synchronized decoding result is regarded as valid, and CM-MSE is set to 0. When the NSSP value falls below this threshold, the metric is equal to MSE.

The MSE/CM-MSE performance comparison of different phase estimation algorithms is presented in Fig. 5. For the DA algorithm, the estimation performance improves as the pilot length increases from 5 bits to 11 bits. However, the enhancement of estimation accuracy indicates a saturation trend when the pilot length increases to 13 bits. By introducing coding gain, the LDPC code-aided algorithms demonstrate marked performance advantages in Eb/N0 ≥ 1.1 dB. The existing cost functions GAMMA, EL, CSVP, and NSSP proposed in [11]–[13], [15] are selected for comparison. The simulations show that in low-to-medium SNR regions, the proposed method achieves optimal estimation performance, outperforming the three existing cost functions by 0.5–0.8 dB. In higher SNR regions, the CM-MSE performance of the four code-aided algorithms converges closely because the influence of noise on phase estimation diminishes.
