Random Constructions of Girth-Eight QC-LDPC Codes with Structural Shaping
Songyan Xue and Guohua Zhang

Abstract—In order to construct girth-8 quasi-cyclic (QC) lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes with low encoding complexity, this paper introduces structural shaping techniques to generate QC-LDPC codes with two different structures. These two structures are QC-LDPC codes constructed with vertical symmetric structure and reverse structure without 4-cycles and 6-cycles. Novel constructions assume that the structures of the parity check matrix (PCM) are symmetric and reverse, respectively, and the greedy exhaustive search algorithm is used to find the exponent matrix with the smallest possible size P of the circulant permutation matrix (CPM). Due to its symmetrical and reverse structural characteristics, the representation of the new codes is more compact than other codes. The simulation results show that the proposed codes have certain competitiveness compared to existing QC-LDPC codes.
Index Terms—low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, quasicyclic (QC), girth, structural shaping, exponent matrix
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a type of widely employed error correction coding techniques, characterized by its performance close to the Shannon limit and low complexity. As a special type of LDPC codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes have quasi-cyclic structure characteristics, which means that their parity-check matrices(PCM) composed of multiple identical or similar submatrices are usually obtained through cyclic shift operations [1]. The existence of short cycles can degrade the performance of LDPC codes, therefore, how to construct girth-8 QC-LDPC codes has long been a focus of researchers’ attention. Recent studies have shown that introducing different structures into the paritycheck matrix can more effectively achieve the construction of QC-LDPC codes. Therefore, the study of QC-LDPC codes with different structures will be a direction for future research.
Based on structural shaping techniques, this paper proposes two types of structures for girth-8 QC-LDPC codes within a certain range of row weights. Due to its symmetrical and reverse structural characteristics, the representation of the new codes is more compact than other codes. The simulation results show that the proposed codes have certain competitiveness compared to existing QC-LDPC codes.

II. PRELIMINARIES

The most common graphical representation method for LDPC codes is Tanner graph. The Tanner graph represents the code’s structure by connecting variable nodes and check nodes. The cycle exist in the Tanner graph have a significant impact on the performance of the code. Girth(g) refers to the length of the shortest cycle in the Tanner graph. Among the Tanner graph, short cycles will reduce the accuracy and convergence speed of decoding. Therefore, compromises on girth are sometimes necessary in LDPC code construction to enhance performance. The PCM of a QC-LDPC code is composed of identically sized circulant blocks [2], [3], [7]. In this paper, these circulant blocks are circulant permutation matrices (CPMs). That is, its PCM can be collectively described by a J × L exponent matrix E and the circulant size P [4]–[6]. Its block length is L × P, and the code rate is R ⩾ 1 − J/L [8]–[10]. A P × P binary CPM is denoted by QP (n) and the only non-zero element in the first row is located in the n-th column where n ∈ {0, · · ·, P − 1}. Let P=3 be an example:

B. Search Algorithm
During the search process, we focus exclusively on the top half of the matrix. The lower portion is derived through symmetry. The search proceeds greedily row by row. Starting from the top row, the algorithm processes elements left to right. For each position, values from the set {0, · · ·, P − 1} are tested sequentially.
Consider the case (J=3, L=4, P=9) as an example. The initial matrix has three rows: the first row is fixed as ⃗0 , and the third row is determined by symmetry. Thus, only the second row requires searching. The algorithm then checks for 4-cycles and 6-cycles. If cycles are detected, t increments to t+1 and the check repeats. This continues until either the cycle-free condition is satisfied or t reaches P-1. If satisfied, t resets to 0 for the next element. If t reaches P-1 without success, the current and symmetric positions are marked invalid (inf ), and the algorithm backtracks to modify the previous element until all elements in the upper half of the exponent matrix are assigned, or no feasible solution is found.
This universal algorithm applies to any structure with appropriate matrix-generation modifications. The upper-half search process is identical for both structures proposed here, differing only in lower-half position and value determination. For instance, in a J-even vertical symmetric structure, an upper-half assignment E(r, c) = t corresponds to E (J/2 + r, c) = mod (−t, P ) in the lower half.

VI. CONCLUSION
This work generates girth-8 QC-LDPC codes by constructing exponent matrices with novel structures. The proposed codes feature low implementation complexity, requiring only partial matrix searches for synthesis. Although this sacrifices some degree of design freedom, it significantly improves search efficiency, as only half of the matrix needs to be determined. Concurrently, numerical simulations confirm that

these girth-8 QC-LDPC codes achieve equivalent or superior performance compared to existing structured codes. The construction demonstrates that explicit construction can avoid the immense time required for searching. However, the RDS structure is limited to J=4, and both the explicit vertical symmetric construction and the explicit reverse construction exhibit significant disadvantages in terms of code length. While the circulant size P identified in this study is theoretically smaller than that of explicit constructions, further research is required to determine the minimum possible P. Exploring additional QC-LDPC code structures represents another promising research direction.
