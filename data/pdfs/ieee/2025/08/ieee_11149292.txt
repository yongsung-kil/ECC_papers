Adaptive Normalized Min-Sum Decoding Algorithm For LDPC Codes in Flash Memory
Min Yu, Xiongfei Zhai, Mengxin Hu, Shunyou Tao, Yi Fang, and Guojun Han School of Information Engineering, Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou 510006, China
Email: 844535734@qq.com, zhaixiongfei@gdut.edu.cn, 850916216@qq.com, 201977654@qq.com, fangyi@gdut.edu.cn, gjhan@gdut.edu.cn

Abstract—For globally coupled low density parity check (GCLDPC) codes, the normalized Min-Sum (NMS) algorithm is a widely-used decoding algorithm but suffers from the degraded performance and the slow convergence speed. To address this issue, in this paper, by analyzing the relation between the bit error ratio (BER) and the degree of polarization of log-likelihood ratio (LLR) information, we propose an adaptive NMS decoding algorithm. In particular, the normalized factor is dynamically adjusted based on the average LLR information in the initial and the current iterations. Besides, the two-level decoding scheme is considered. Simulation results show that, in the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels and the flash memory channels, as compared with the NMS algorithm, our algorithm significantly improves the decoding performance and the convergence speed.
Index Terms—GC-LDPC codes, NMS algorithm, adaptive normalized factor, two-level decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, with the development of artificial intelligence and 5G communication, the amount of data increases explosively. To address the issue of storage, flash memory [1] has been widely used in various areas due to its high-density storage, low power consumption, and non-volatility. However, the increasing storage density leads to the reduced reliability, where the traditional Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) codes cannot deal with the high raw bit error rates (RBERs). To address this, it is found that the low density parity check (LDPC) codes can serve as one of promising candidates for the error correction codes in flash memory [2], [3].
The LDPC code was proposed by Gallager in 1962 which can approach the Shannon limit. Due to the excessively high complexity of decoding, the LDPC codes did not draw the worldwide attention. It was rediscovered by MacKay and Neal in 1996 [4]. In addition, empirical studies showed that LDPC may have some advantages over Turbo codes, for example, in terms of error floor performance and parallel decoding. Therefore, LDPC codes are widely exploited in various areas, such as the very large scale integration circuits, the optical fiber communication, the deep space communication, the 5G communication, and the flash memory.
The effectiveness and efficiency of LDPC decoder depend on the design of parity check matrix. Specifically, the globally coupled LDPC (GC-LDPC) codes in [5] shows a flexible structure and can take full advantage of the local codes in

In the literature, the LDPC decoding algorithms can be divided into two categories: hard decision decoding and soft decision decoding. Bit Flipping (BF) [6] is one of the most considered hard decision decoding algorithms. Although the hard decision decoding algorithms achieve the low complexity, their error correction performance is not satisfactory. To address this issue, the soft decision decoding algorithms based on iteratively updating the information of log-likelihood ratio (LLR), e.g., the Belief Propagation (BP) decoding algorithm [7], were considered. However, due to the extremely high complexity of the BP decoding algorithm, it is difficult for hardware implementation. To reduce the complexity, the authors in [8] converted the complex multiplication operation of the BP decoding algorithm into the addition operation over logarithm domain and developed the logarithmic field BP decoding (LLR-BP) algorithm. In order to further reduce the computational complexity, the update of the check nodes (CNs) was optimized at the cost of degraded performance, the Min-Sum (MS) decoding algorithm was proposed in [9], and the MS decoding algorithm was analyzed and improved in [10], [11]. To achieve both good error correction performance and low complexity, the normalized factor and the offset factor were considered, which lead to the normalized MS (NMS) and offset MS (OMS) decoding algorithms. However, the fixed normalization factors in the above algorithms lead to performance degradation as compared with the LLR-BP algorithm. To address this issue, various algorithms based on factor optimization were proposed in [12]–[14]. However, most works focused on the short codes or the exploitation of neural networks, which may not satisfy the requirement of flash memory.
To address the above issues, this paper proposes an adaptive NMS (ANMS) decoding algorithm based on a dynamical normalized factor, which is adjusted according to the ratio of the mean LLR information in the initial and current iterations. The simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can speed up the convergence, and improve the decoding performance.

For the ease of presentation, we denote r, Rmax, LLRcjh, and LLRj(r) as the iteration number, the maximum iteration number, the initial LLR information received from the channels, and the posterior probability information of the j-th bit in the r-th iteration. Ni and Mj are the set of all VNs connected to the i-th CN and the set of all CNs connected to the j-th VN, while Ni\j (Mj\i) is the subset of Ni (Mj) by excluding the j-th VN (the i-th CN). Besides, V Ii(,rj) and CIi(,rj) represent the exchanged information from the j-th VN to the i-th CN and from the i-th CN to the j-th VN. Due to the special structure of HGC , the implementation of decoding algorithm can be very flexible. In the literature, two types of algorithms for implementation are mainly considered, namely the two-level decoding algorithm (TLA) and the twophase decoding algorithm (TPA). In TLA and TPA, the BP algorithm can be used in both the local and global updates.

As we can see, the updates of CNs contribute most of complexity of the BP algorithm. To address this issue, the MS algorithm is proposed by considering the following approximation for the update of CNs.

The decoding algorithm is an iterative probabilistic decoding algorithm, which iteratively updates the posterior information of CNs and VNs to correct the errors. V. CONCLUSION
There exist the drawbacks of degraded performance and slow convergence in the NMS algorithm due to the fixed normalized factor. To address this issue, this work analyzes the relation between the BER and the degree of polarization of LLR information. Based on the average LLR information in the initial and the current iterations, we propose to adjust the normalized factor dynamically and develop an ANMS algorithm.
