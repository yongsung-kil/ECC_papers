Abstract ‚Äî In this paper, we propose an algorithm to transform a generator matrix of a linear block code into a minimum weight staircase generator matrix (MWSGM). This allows us to apply the representative ordered statistics decoding with local constraints (LC-ROSD) algorithm to cyclic redundancy check (CRC) aided polar (CA-polar) codes. Distinguished from the conventional ordered statistics decoding (OSD), the LC-ROSD implements parallel Gaussian elimination (GE) for MWSGM instead of serial GE for a general matrix, potentially reducing the decoding latency. Numerical results show that the performance of 5G CA-polar codes under LC-ROSD is better than that of CRC aided successive cancellation list (CA-SCL) decoding and can approach the corresponding maximum likelihood (ML) lower bounds at different code rates. The numerical results also show that the LC-ROSD with MWSGM has lower decoding complexity than the CA-SCL decoding in the high signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) region. Keywords ‚Äî Cyclic redundancy check aided polar (CA-polar) codes, Ordered statistics decoding (OSD), Parallel Gaussian elimination, Staircase generator matrix codes (SGMCs). I. Introduction
Channel coding, a fundamental technique for ensuring reliable data transmission over noisy channels, is widely employed in applications, such as Internet of things [1], DNA storage [2], blockchain [3], satellite communication [4], short-packet communication [5], streaming communication [6], and so on. Polar codes, proposed by Arƒ±kan [7], can achieve the Shannon capacity of the binary-input output-symmetric discrete memoryless channel at infinite length with successive cancellation (SC) decoding. They have a wide range of applications in various fields, such as underwater acoustic communication [8], quantum key distribution [9], and coded modulation [10]. However, short polar codes do not perform as well as turbo codes or low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes. To enhance performance, successive cancellation list (SCL) decoding was introduced in [11]. As the list size L increases, the performance of SCL decoding can approach that of maximum likelihood (ML) decoding. However, the ML performance of polar codes is usually inferior due to their small minimum distance. Consequently, the

cyclic redundancy check (CRC) aided polar (CA-polar) codes were proposed to improve the weight spectrum [12], [13]. The CA-polar codes have better performance at short lengths, as compared to other common capacityapproaching codes, such as turbo codes and LDPC codes, and hence have been adopted as the standard coding scheme for control channels in 5G [14]. However, because of the inherent serial nature of SC, the SCL decoding suffers from long latency and poses difficulties in parallel implementation.
Alternatively, the most reliable basis (MRB) reprocessing type algorithms can be applied to CA-polar codes, based on the idea that errors tend to occur less frequently in reliable positions compared to the less reliable positions. The MRB was first introduced by Dorsch [15], where the test error patterns (TEPs) are searched in the order of the increasing soft weight. It has been proved that, as a guessing codeword decoding (GCD) algorithm [16], Dorsch‚Äôs algorithm terminated with the trivial criterion is an ML decoding algorithm [16], [17]. In contrast, the ordered statistics decoding (OSD) investi-

gated by Fossorier and Lin [18] searches TEPs in a predetermined order (with increasing Hamming weight), resulting in a near-ML decoding algorithm. However, the conventional OSD faces two main challenges. The first issue is the requirement of generating and re-encoding a large number of TEPs to achieve near ML performance. The second issue is the Gaussian elimination (GE) process with a complexity of order O(n min{k, n ‚àí k}2) for codes with code length n and dimension k. Even worse, the GE is a serial algorithm without efficient parallel implementation for general matrices, resulting in extra decoding latency.
Various techniques have been proposed to reduce the average number of TEPs [19]‚Äì[22]. Recently, the OSD with local constraints (LC-OSD) has been proposed in [17], [23], [24], which selects k + Œ¥ bits as the extended MRB. These extra Œ¥ bits introduce local constraints on the TEPs, allowing the OSD to skip many invalid TEPs. Armed with tailored termination criteria, the average number of TEPs can be reduced significantly in the high signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) region. In this case, the overall complexity of OSD is dominated by GE. To mitigate the issue caused by GE, the representative ordered statistic decoding (ROSD) was proposed for the staircase generator matrix codes (SGMCs), enabling parallel implementation of GE to reduce decoding latency [25], [26].
Recently, several OSD algorithms have been proposed for polar codes, such as the CRC-aided OSD algorithm [27], the hybrid OSD-SC decoding algorithm [28], the pre-configured error pattern OSD algorithm [29]. Different from these aforementioned algorithms which employ OSD or hybrid OSD to decode polar codes, we propose an algorithm to transform a generator matrix of a linear block code into a minimum weight staircase generator matrix (MWSGM). These MWSGMs allow us to apply the representative OSD with local constraints (LCROSD) to CA-polar codes, where the local constraints with tailored termination criteria can reduce the average number of TEPs and the ROSD can utilize the parallel GE implementation. The numerical results show that the LC-ROSD has a better performance (compared with CASCL) and can approach the corresponding ML lower bounds. The numerical results also show that, by these MWSGMs, the LC-ROSD has lower decoding complexity than the CA-SCL decoding in the high SNR region.

By employing list decoding (say, LC-OSD [23], [24]), we can identify non-zero codewords that are closest to the all-zero codeword in terms of Hamming distance, which corresponds to the 0-th row. The search for the subsequent rows follows a similar approach to the NNCS, but the non-zero positions in the preceding rows are regarded as erasures. As the number of searched rows increases, the search for the next linearly independent codeword can become time-consuming. To avoid excessive search times, one approach is to remove the linearly dependent codewords already searched from the search space. This can be achieved by deleting a specific row in the generator matrix that is linearly dependent on the already searched codewords and using the updated generator matrix to continue the search for new codewords. V. Conclusion and Future Work
In this paper, we have constructed relatively balanced staircase generator matrices, known as minimum weight staircase generator matrices, for CA-polar codes. These staircase generator matrices admit us to apply the LCROSD to CA-polar codes, where the local constraints with tailored termination criteria can reduce the average number of TEPs and the ROSD can utilize the parallel GE. The simulation results showed that: 1) The construction method can construct a staircase generator matrix with relatively balanced staircases for the CA-polar codes, leading to improved performance under LC-ROSD compared to CA-polar codes with trivial staircase gener-

1118
ator matrices; 2) The LC-ROSD outperforms the CASCL and the complexity of LC-ROSD is lower than that of CA-SCL in the high SNR region; 3) The performance of CA-polar codes under LC-ROSD can approach the corresponding ML lower bounds and the RCU bounds at different code rates.
As a topic for future work in this research, it involves providing theoretical proofs for the design principle of MWSGM and exploring its applications in decoding. Another topic to explore is the application of this construction algorithm and decoding for other linear block codes, with a focus on optimizing decoding strategies tailored specifically to those codes.
