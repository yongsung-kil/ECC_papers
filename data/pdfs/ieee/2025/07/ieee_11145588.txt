I. INTRODUCTION
Data transmission in high-capacity and high-speed fiber optic networks is facing serious security challenges due to the transparency of fiber optic cables [1]. Physical Layer Secure Key Distribution (PLSKD) has been proposed and applied as a simple and economical security scheme [2]. Information reconciliation (IR) is the key link in the post-processing of key distribution. Tu introduced IR into PLSKD and constructed a complete post-processing protocol for the physical layer of fiber optic networks [3], while Hajomer further improved the protocol framework [4]. Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes become the key error correction codes for IR due to their error correction capability close to the Shannon limit. To address the resource redundancy problem caused by the fixed number of iterations in LDPC decoding algorithms, Gu¨mu¨s¸ restores the stagnation criterion based on five consecutive bits [5], but suffers from the convergence delay defect, and Zhang proposed a termination mechanism based on syndrome variation patterns [6], however, its performance exhibits high parameter sensitivity, and suboptimal parameter selection results in degraded reconciliation efficacy. Attempting to solve the problem of the narrow error correction range of LDPC, a new reconciliation efficiency metric is defined for timevarying channels, enabling an adaptive scheme [7]. However, its applicability is limited, and performance under-performs theoretical predictions.
The iteration of LDPC decoding in the existing IR lacks the awareness of the system environment transformation, and there is the problem of redundancy in the number of decoding iterations and waste of computational resources. Moreover, for different structures of LDPC code word matrices, there is a lack of adaptive error correction capability for information flow. In this paper, we propose an adaptive IR scheme based on a feedback mechanism and Check Node Correlation Coefficient(CNCC) to realize the Early Termination (ET) of

the number of LDPC decoding iterations. The proposed ET effectively reduces the iterative decoding of the redundancy of LDPC in IR by 76.8% of the number of iterations and realizes the adaptive IR of legal parties by sensing the channel changes, and the Frame Error Rate (FER) of the high Initial Bit Error Rate (IBER) region is reduced by 74.2%.

II. EARLY TERMINATION SCHEME
The Log-Likelihood Ratio-Belief Propagation (LLR-BP) algorithm, a widely adopted decoding method for LDPC codes, iteratively performs check node updates, variable node updates, and hard-decision operations through a message-passing mechanism over Tanner graphs. Upon successful decoding, the CNCC exhibits monotonic growth due to reduced uncertainty in the parity constraints, whereas decoding failure triggers oscillatory behavior in CNCC stemming from conflicting check subsets. Once CNCC enters a plateau phase, redundant iterations not only waste computational resources but also yield no further error-correction gains, necessitating adaptive termination strategies to optimize decoding efficiency.
We propose an ET scheme based on the CNCC, where a flag records the count of ineffective iterations during the decoding process, and a breakpoint serves as the early termination threshold. The iterative decoding is prematurely halted when the condition f lag > breakpoint is satisfied. IV. RESULTS

In order to verify the feasibility and performance of the proposed scheme, the key distribution data were collected from a 120 kilometers coherent fiber optic transmission system experimental platform. For IR, we implemented the LDPC parity-check matrix H and generator matrix G specified in the CCSDS 131.1-O-1 standard. Under the conventional IR framework, the LDPC code rate was configured as R = 2/3 with a maximum iteration limit IterMAX = 100. For the ET scheme, the breakpoint threshold was set to breakpoint = 6 . Fig. 3 compares the FER performance of three schemes under varying IBER conditions. The results demonstrate that the ET-based IR scheme achieves comparable performance to the original protocol, with an average △F ER < 2 × 10−2, confirming preserved FER reliability after ET integration. In low-IBER regimes, all schemes maintain FER below 2.4%, whereas in high-IBER regimes, the adaptive ET scheme reduces the average FER by 74.2% through real-time feedbackdriven bit adaptation, significantly outperforming the typical LDPC IR. This performance divergence highlights the scheme’s capability to dynamically optimize reconciliation efficiency across heterogeneous channel conditions.

V. CONCLUSION
To address the redundant computational resource consumption inherent in conventional LDPC-based IR protocols, this work proposes an ET scheme based on dynamic monitoring of the CNCC. By real-time tracking of CNCC variations to terminate ineffective iterations, the proposed scheme reduces the average iteration count by 76.8% compared to the typical traditional LDPC protocol. Furthermore, targeting the narrowband IR limitations caused by LDPC codes’ finite errorcorrection capability, an adaptive IR scheme is developed to dynamically adjust information bits through real time feedback mechanisms. Experimental results demonstrate a 74.2% reduction in FER and effectively extends the correctable error range, significantly outperforms conventional LDPC-based IR protocols in high IBER regimes.
