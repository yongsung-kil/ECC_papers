Abstract—The transition of air traffic control (ATC) communication networks from traditional circuit-switched systems to modern Internet protocol (IP)-based architectures marks a significant evolution in aviation safety and efficiency. A critical component of this migration is the adoption of very small aperture terminal (VSAT) satellite communications, which enables connectivity in the areas where fiber build-out is lacking or can provide a backup to fiber connectivity. VSAT links have to overcome signal interference issues and utilize modern error correction code (ECC) techniques to minimize packet loss. The implementation of international civil aviation organization (ICAO)-recommended systems for improving traffic flow and safety modernization are built upon an IP network. However, IP protocol is built upon the central concept of low bit errors transmissions and a single bit error results in the loss of an entire IP packet. This paper investigates the integration of lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) decoding methods, enhanced by convolutional neural network (CNN)-based models, into the fullmesh, hubless adhoc VSATPlus® platform, and examines the potential benefits of a more efficient and robust alternative to conventional algorithm-based channel coding schemes that do not leverage CNN models. The proposed CNN-based LDPC decoder, inspired by the belief propagation (BP)-CNN framework, refines noise estimation and error correction through iterative interactions between an LDPC decoder and a CNN module, effectively exploiting residual noise features inherent to satellite communication environments. Through CNN-based LDPC decoding, the system achieves enhanced error correction performance, reduced computational complexity, and improved adaptability to non-Gaussian noise scenarios, ensuring robust operation under multi-frequency time division multiple access (MF-TDMA) schemes. Furthermore, this work explores the practical deployment of the CNN-based LDPC decoder on Xilinx field-programmable gate array (FPGA) platforms using the Vitis artificial intelligence (AI) development framework. The hardware-aware design ensures resource-efficient implementation and real-time feasibility on the Zynq UltraScale+ multiprocessor system-on-chip (MPSoC). Simulation results further demonstrate the scalability and practical applicability of the proposed solution in real-world hubless full-mesh satellite networks by achieving up to 4 dB signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) gain over conventional ECC decoding at a bit error rate (BER) = 10−5. This enhancement suggests significantly improved transmission quality while reducing antenna size requirements for VSATPlus terminals. The findings validate the feasibility of deep learning-assisted LDPC decoding for next-generation hubless VSATPlus networks and pave the way for further research into adaptive neural-assisted error correction techniques.

I. INTRODUCTION
Hubless full-mesh very small aperture terminal (VSAT) communication systems have gained increasing attention for air traffic control (ATC) networks due to their ability to support no single point of failure, full-mesh communications, and enhanced security by separating the network from the internet, providing a private network. Unlike traditional star-configured VSAT networks, where all communication is routed through a central hub station, hubless full-mesh VSAT systems enable direct VSAT-to-VSAT communication [1]. This approach eliminates the need for a hub station and its associated single point of failure and allows for direct remote-to-remote communications which is key for supporting extended-range very high frequency (VHF) communications between the air traffic controller and the pilot. In satellite communication, a forward error correction (FEC) scheme is fundamental to ensuring robust data transmission, especially in environments characterized by noisy channel. Conventional implementations rely on turbo product codes (TPCs) [2], convolutional codes [3]– [6], Reed–Solomon (RS) [4], [5], [7], and low-density paritycheck (LDPC) [4], [8] codes. Among these, LDPC codes [9] have been widely adopted in modern communication systems, such as WiMAX [10] and the fifth generation (5G) new radio (NR) [8] due to their exceptional error-correction performance and computationally efficient decoding processes [11], [12]. Nonetheless, the evolving demands of future communication scenarios, particularly within VSAT networks, highlight the need for advancements in LDPC coding techniques to address the diverse and dynamic challenges posed by modern communication environments.
The performance degradation of LDPC decoders by lowfrequency noise in VSAT systems, resulting from atmospheric and ionospheric effects is a significant challenge, which necessitates the development of advanced FEC techniques. Although conventional approaches, such as noise whitening, have been proposed [13], [14], their significant computational demands and increased decoding complexity make them impractical for systems with long code lengths and limited resources [15].

Recent advancements in data-driven methods, particularly deep learning, have demonstrated exceptional flexibility and accuracy in addressing complex tasks, thereby leading to the development of innovative approaches to enhance LDPC decoding performance in noise-prone environments. The iterative convolutional neural network (CNN)-based approaches introduced in [15] and [16] integrate a CNN module with an LDPC decoder, iteratively processing received symbols to progressively improve the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR). Expanding on this concept, this paper investigates the integration of CNN-based LDPC decoding methods into hubless fullmesh VSATPlus® architecture. The proposed approach seeks to drive unprecedented improvements to traditional error correction code (ECC) methods in multi-frequency time division multiple access (MF-TDMA) systems. It is observed that the CNN architecture in [15], designed for an LDPC block size of 576 with a code rate of 3/4, achieves a performance improvement of almost 2.5 dB at a bit error rate (BER) = 10−4 when extended to an LDPC block length of 1056, which corresponds to the block size used in VSATPlus. To mitigate the limitations in the previous research investigation, the methodology in [15] is extended, introducing a modified CNN architecture optimized to enhance performance by leveraging the larger block size capability of VSATPlus. In addition, this work explores available Xilinx technologies, including field-programmable gate array (FPGA) platforms, intellectual property (IP) cores, and the Vitis artificial intelligence (AI) development environment, to enable practical realization.

In Section V, the FPGA-based deployment of the CNN-LDPC decoder is presented, leveraging the Xilinx Vitis AI development framework. Section VI evaluates the performance of the proposed decoder based on simulation results. Finally, Section VIII concludes the paper by summarizing key findings and discussing future research directions.
II. RELATED WORK
LDPC codes are widely deployed across various standards including the 5G-NR [8], IEEE 802.16e (WiMAX) [10], IEEE 802.11 (WiFi) [17], and DVB-S2 [4]. In parallel, various decoding methods have been developed for LDPC codes to improve the system performance. While the min-sum (MS) approximation [18], [19] and its modified versions, such as the normalized min-sum (NMS) and offset min-sum (OMS) methods [20], [21], have been introduced to mitigate the trade-off between complexity and performance, the belief propagation (BP), commonly known as sum-product algorithm (SPA) [22], remains the foundational and most widely adopted approach, renowned for its proven effectiveness in achieving exceptional decoding performance.

Building on these advancements, the rise of AI has introduced transformative opportunities for further enhancing LDPC decoding. Several studies have demonstrated the potential of neural networks to enhance LDPC decoding by addressing computational challenges and improving performance. In [23]–[25], BP and MS decoders enhanced with deep neural networks (DNNs) are shown to improve the decoding performance of short-length linear block codes. Neural MS decoders proposed in [26] optimize the decoding of general protograph-based (PB) LDPC codes, while the linear approximated MS (LAMS) decoder in [27] improves the decoding of 5G-NR LDPC codes using machine learning (ML). A multilayer perceptron neural network (MLPNN) decoder introduced in [28] enhances the decoding of LDPC codes under finitealphabet message passing (MP). Adaptive neural layered MS (ANLMS) and filtered neural layered MS (FNLMS) decoders, which integrate DNN components to optimize offset and normalization parameters for 5G-NR LDPC codes, are proposed in [29]. Spiking neural networks (SNNs) are introduced into BP decoding for LDPC codes in [30], demonstrating performance improvement at high SNRs. In [31], a beliefpropagation recurrent neural network (BP-RNN) decoder is proposed for short LDPC codes to enhance waterfall-region performance. While most proposed algorithms primarily aim to optimize decoder efficacy through the application of AI, the emphasis in VSATPlus [32] systems is to retain the BP LDPC decoder as the foundational decoding framework, while leveraging AI to enhance its performance under low-frequency noise. Although RNNs are appealing for sequence modelling, they are less suitable in this context because colored noise in an LDPC block length of 1056 appears as local correlations among adjacent bits, which 1-D CNNs capture directly in parallel [16]. Moreover, on an FPGA, convolutional layers map efficiently onto parallel compute resources whereas RNNs incur higher latency and consume more resources through sequential state updates [33], [34]. Consequently, among the various methodologies considered for VSATPlus, we were particularly drawn to the integration of CNN with BP decoders proposed in [15]. This approach present highly promising solutions for improving LDPC decoding performance in noisy environments, further underscoring the need for robust noise handling mechanisms.
III. VSATPLUS SYSTEM OVERVIEW
The VSATPlus [32] system provides full-mesh, single-hop connectivity within a satellite network, leveraging MF-TDMA technology. Each terminal buffers user data and transmits it in short, high-speed bursts scheduled to avoid overlap, ensuring efficient satellite use without requiring a hub to schedule these transmissions. The mesh topology and typical applications of the VSATPlus system in ATC are illustrated in Fig. 1, showing direct IP connectivity between area control centers (ACCs) and airports across regions. The system supports radar, very high frequency (VHF) radio, automatic dependent surveillance–broadcast (ADS-B), and essential ATC services such as messaging, weather updates, and air traffic coordination.

IV. CNN-BASED LDPC DECODER FOR VSATPLUS®

Within the framework of CNN-based LDPC decoders, this paper investigates the application of the algorithm proposed in [15] to the VSATPlus system. The methodology involves adapting the integrated BP decoding and CNN-based noise estimation model to align with the specific requirements of VSATPlus, such as the LDPC block size and unique noise characteristics.
Leveraging the iterative BP-CNN architecture, the system dynamically refines noise estimates and adapts to the residual noise characteristics of the environment [15]. This iterative process is illustrated in Fig. 3, where at the transmitter, bits are encoded using an LDPC encoder, modulated with binary phase-shift keying (BPSK), and transmitted through a channel characterized by colored noise. At the receiver, belief propagation is employed to estimate symbols and channel noise, which are then refined iteratively by the CNN-based decoder to enhance error correction performance.

The CNN designed for LDPC codes with a block length of 576 follows the architecture proposed in [15], achieving a performance improvement of approximately 3.5 dB at BER = 10−4 in the strongly correlated noise with high correlation factor C = 0.8. This design consists of four convolutional layers, defined as {4; 9, 3, 3, 15; 64, 32, 16, 1}, where the first set represents filter sizes and the second denotes feature maps per layer.
To extend this improvement to LDPC codes with a block length of 1056, a new CNN architecture is developed, consisting of five fully convolutional layers, defined as {5; 15, 9, 3, 9, 15; 128, 64, 32, 16, 1}. The additional layer and modified filter configurations are optimized to maintain a comparable performance gain while adapting to the increased block length. Each layer applies 1-D learnable kernels with ReLU (·) activation, except for the final layer, which outputs the processed representation. The weights of the network are initialized using the Xavier, also known as Glorot initialization. Training is performed using mini-batch stochastic gradient descent with Adam optimization, with a learning rate of 0.001. The mini-batch size is set to 1400 blocks of data.

V. CNN-BASED LDPC DECODER DEPLOYMENT SCHEME
The structured deployment flow for implementing a CNN model on the Xilinx Zynq UltraScale+ multiprocessor systemon-chip (MPSoC), a heterogeneous multiprocessing platform that combines a processing system (PS) and userprogrammable logic (PL) [36], is illustrated in Fig. 5. The process is divided into three main phases: model building, hardware building, and deployment. In the model building phase, a trained CNN model using TensorFlow is first quantized with the Vitis AI Quantizer [37], reducing the precision of weights and activations while maintaining accuracy. This quantization step supports both post-training quantization and quantization-aware training (QAT). The Vitis AI Compiler then converts the optimized model into an FPGA-compatible format with the .xmodel extension, ensuring efficient execution on the target hardware. The hardware building phase focuses on generating the necessary FPGA overlays, which define the hardware configuration for running the deep learning model. A deep-learning processing unit (DPU) configuration file (.vh) is used to specify parameters such as memory usage and digital signal processing (DSP) utilization. Using the DPU with python productivity for zynq (DPU-PYNQ) [38] environment and the Xilinx Vivado design suite, the system generates essential overlay files, including the bitstream (.bit), hardware wrapper (.hwh), and binary executable (.xclbin). Additionally, a fingerprint file (arch.json) is created to store the hardware configuration, which is later required for model compilation. This phase must be completed first, as the generated files are essential for subsequent steps. In the deployment phase, the compiled model and generated DPU overlays are loaded onto the FPGA within the Zynq UltraScale+ MPSoC. A Python testing script initializes the FPGA by loading the DPU overlay and executing inference using the compiled model. The processing is managed through the PYNQ framework, which interacts with the FPGA hardware via the Xilinx runtime (XRT). Once deployed, the system evaluates inference performance by measuring latency and throughput.
The proposed CNN architecture with an input size of 1056 adheres to the DPUCZDX8G [39] constraint, in which input channels must not exceed 2048×channel parallel. Given that the lowest supported channel parallel value in the B1152 configuration is 12, the maximum allowable input channel count is 2048 × 12 = 24, 576, which comfortably accommodates our design. Furthermore, the convolutional layers, with kernel sizes {15, 9, 3, 9, 15} and output channels {128, 64, 32, 16, 1}, comply with the DPU’s specifications, which support arbitrary input sizes, kernel dimensions between 1 and 256, and a stride from 1 to 256. The padding constraints also align, as the DPU allows padding up to min(kernel size − 1, 15), which is sufficient for our configuration.

Fig. 5: The implementation scheme [40]. Additionally, the absence of pooling layers simplifies the hardware implementation by eliminating constraints related to max or average pooling operations. Given these considerations, the proposed CNN model is fully compatible with the DPUCZDX8G architecture and can be successfully deployed using the Vitis AI toolchain on the Zynq UltraScale+ MPSoC.

The results indicate that LDPC codes generally outperform their TPC counterparts. Specifically, at BER = 10−5, LDPC (n=1056, k=704) achieves almost a 2 dB coding gain over TPC (32×26)(32×26). However, for LDPC (n=576, k=432) with a smaller block length, the performance gain over TPC (32 × 26)(16 × 11) remains marginal, suggesting that the benefits of LDPC become increasingly significant at larger block sizes. Despite these advantages, LDPC decoding generally requires more iterations than TPC decoding [41]. Nonetheless, its lower computational complexity per iteration offsets this drawback, and the iteration gap decreases at higher SNRs, making LDPC a more practical choice for implementation in VSATPlus.
Fig. 7 presents a comparative analysis of two CNN-based LDPC decoder architectures for LDPC (n = 1056, k = 704) under C = 0.8 and ρ = 0.1. The performance of BP, BPCNN-BP [15], and BP-Modified-CNN-BP demonstrate that the CNN-based decoder employing the structure depicted in Fig. 4, achieves superior error correction. At BER = 10−5, conventional BP requires 2 dB, while BP-CNN-BP and BPModified-CNN-BP attain the same BER at −0.2 dB and −1 dB, respectively, yielding coding gains of 2.2 dB and 3 dB. The steeper BER slope of the modified CNN-based architecture underscores its enhanced capability to mitigate correlated noise, making it more effective in low-SNR VSATPlus applications.

When the correlation coefficient is high, C = 0.8, the CNN can better extract noise correlation features, leading to significant improvements in decoding performance. In contrast, when the noise correlation is weak (C = 0.1) or absent (C = 0) the CNN module has minimal effect, and the decoder operates similarly to a conventional BP decoder. Furthermore, the choice of ρ also impacts decoding performance by affecting the residual noise distribution and its Gaussian approximation.

VIII. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
Inspired by the recently proposed BP-CNN architecture in [15], this paper adapts CNN-based LDPC decoding for the hubless full-mesh VSATPlus system, addressing the limitations of conventional TPC in MF-TDMA configurations. To enhance the applicability of CNN-based LDPC decoding within VSATPlus, a novel CNN architecture is developed and optimized for longer LDPC block lengths. Additionally, a practical deployment strategy leveraging Xilinx FPGA platforms and the Vitis AI development framework is explored, demonstrating the feasibility of real-time implementation. Extensive simulations confirm the effectiveness of the proposed CNN-based LDPC decoder, achieving significant performance gains that align with the requirements of VSATPlus. The findings further highlight the decoder’s potential to enhance link reliability by improving SNR, particularly in correlated noise environments where an SNR gain of over 4 dB is observed. This improvement not only strengthens error correction but also directly impacts antenna design requirements.
Further research could investigate how the proposed CNNbased LDPC decoder enhances performance when integrated with simpler decoding techniques, such as OMS or NMS, as implemented in the Xilinx LDPC encoder/decoder IP core [43]. Moreover, incorporating an adaptive offset or normalization factor could facilitate dynamic parameter tuning.
An additional direction is to explore a 1-D denoising autoencoder as a front-end noise mitigation stage. In this context, colored noise introduces local correlations among adjacent bits, which the autoencoder must preserve through its latent representation. Consequently, the dimensionality of the bottleneck layer should be sufficiently large to model

these correlated fluctuations without compressing them into an excessively compact representation. If the bottleneck is undersized, subtle perturbations arising from closely spaced noise spikes may be lost, thereby impairing the decoder’s capacity to discriminate genuine bit errors from residual interference.
Another promising direction involves optimizing the loss function to better align with system-level performance metrics. As a next step, CNN-based adaptive modulation schemes warrant exploration to maximize spectral efficiency under varying channel conditions. Finally, a critical direction for future research is the development of techniques to mitigate the impact of the noise floor in LDPC decoding at higher SNRs when employing a CNN-based decoder. It is noteworthy that, as part of our ongoing research, we aim to implement and evaluate the proposed CNN-based decoding approach within the operational framework of the VSATPlus system.
