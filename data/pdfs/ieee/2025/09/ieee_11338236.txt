A Rate Compatible QC-Hadamard-LDPC Scheme for Extremely Low SNR Regimes
Lei Xiao†, Zhen Liu†, and Junyi Du‡ † School of Automation Engineering, University of Electronic Science and Technology of China, Chengdu, China
‡ National Key Laboratory of Complex Aviation Systems Simulation, Southwest China Institute of Electronic Technology, Chengdu, China
Email: xiaolei0715@foxmail.com

Abstract—The Hadamard-LDPC code is proved to be a type of low rate code with ultimate-Shannon-limit approaching performance. In this paper, we consider the implementation issues of this code. We try to design a type of Hadamard-LDPC codes with both quasi-cyclic and dual-diagonal structure to decrease the encoding process. To this end, we first optimize the degree distribution in terms of decoding threshold by modifying the extrinsic information transfer chart. We construct the raptor-like parity-check matrix to support multiple code rates and ensure a linear encoding complexity. Furthermore, we try to design a layered normalized decoding algorithm with the max-logMAP rule to decrease the decoding complexity. We optimize the normalized factor to achieve a slight performance degradation. The entire system is implemented on an FPGA board. A bit error rate of 10−5 can be achieved at Eb/N0 = 0.35 with a moderate message bits length 1024 and a throughput of 276 Mbps.
Index Terms—QC-Hadamard-LDPC code; extremely low SNR regimes; raptor-like structure; layered normalized decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
6G is envisaged to provide all-inclusive support to meet the needs of every type of user via the new invention of the space-air-ground integrated network. It aims to inculcate UAVs, ground station and satellite communications to the next higher level. This requires that 6G should be more resilient to complex electromagnetic environment and severe channel conditions. Designing a practical encoding and decoding scheme for extremely low signal-to-noise (SNR) regimes is of importance.
The low-density parity-check (LDPC) code is shown to asymptotically achieve the Shannon limit [1], [2], [3], [4]. An LDPC code can be represented by a bipartite Tanner graph, where variable nodes (VNs) and check nodes (CNs) are connected by single parity check (SPC) constraints. Employing iterative decoding algorithm, such as belief propagation or min-sum algorithm, extrinsic information are exchanged and enhanced between VNs and CNs. However, in a low SNR regime, the traditional design methods may not work well. For a CN, the SPC constraint is weak and it cannot correct even bit errors. When SNR is low, the channel input information is not reliable and bit errors are conveyed to the CN. There is a large probability for a decoding failure. From the aspect of extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart [5], the check node decoder (CND) extrinsic information grows slowly when the a priori information is low. As a result, the decoding process cannot converge in the low SNR regime.

Replacing the weak SPC constraint by other powerful constraints, such as Hamming codes [6] and Hadamard codes [7], is a straightforward idea. This new type of code, called the generalized LDPC code, has advantages of strong correction ability in a very noisy channel, fast convergence speed and low error floor. For Hadamard-LDPC codes, authors in [8] present a notion of super Hadamard CN (HCN) and design codes with an ultimate Shannon-limit approaching performance. However, this work ignores the implementation issues and fails to introduce quasi-cyclic (QC) structure. Authors in [9] solve this problem. They modify the protograph EXIT functions and design QC-Hadamard-LDPC codes. However, they fail to introduce special code structures, like the double-dual structure, to further reduce the encoding complexity. Their proposed encoding process may introduce matrix multiplication and is not easy for implementation. In addition, their designed QCHadamard-LDPC code has a fixed rate. In practical uses, it is more valuable to design multi rates compatible LDPC codes sharing the same parity-check matrix (PCM).
For the decoding of the Hadamard-LDPC codes, authors in [8], [9], [10] propose a symbol-by-symbol maximum posteriori probability (MAP) decoding algorithm by introducing the fast Hadamard transform (FHT). The authors in [11] propose a layered decoding algorithm to further accelerate the convergence speed. These decoding algorithms can indeed improve the decoding performance in the low SNR regime. However, They all ignore the implementation complexity and introduce many nonlinear computations, e.g. the exponential and logarithm functions. These complex functions are not friendly to practical implementation, especially for field programmable gate array (FPGA) programming. It is uesful to design a decoding algorithm with only linear computations at a sacrifice of a slight performance degradation.
In this paper, we aim to design a practical rate compatible QC-Hadamard-LDPC scheme for extremely low SNR regimes. Specifically, we modify the EXIT functions and optimize the degree distribution with a resolution for the introduction of the QC structure. We employ the progressive edge growth (PEG) algorithm to design multiple rates compatible QCHadamard-LDPC codes sharing the same PCM. In addition, we introduce a dual-diagonal structure to ensure a linear encoding complexity. B. Code Design
In this section, we aim to design rate compatible QCHadamard-LDPC codes with a fast encoding structure. The PCM of our constructed codes is presented in Fig. 4. The sizes of the matrices A and C are J1 × (K − J1) and J2 × K, respectively. They are composed of Z × Z zero matrices and CPMs. The matrices B and D are dual-diagonal matrices with sizes of J1 × J1 and J2 × J2. The last matrix E is a J1 × J2 zero matrix. For the ease of description, we denote the length of the higher rate code and the lower rate code as n1 and n2, where n2 = n1 + J2Z.
For the larger rate, rate-r1, its PCM [A, B] has the structure as presented in Fig. 5. With the dual-diagonal structure, the encoding computational complexity increases linearly with the growth of the code length. This is different from the other existing construction methods in the previous literature [8], [9]. To design the QC-Hadamard-LDPC code ensemble, we modify our proposed multi-edge metric-constrained quasicyclic progressive edge growth (MM-QC-PEG) algorithm [4]. We first present the construction process of the rate-r1 code. The VNs are divided into K blocks and each block has Z VNs. 2. For the i-th block, select its first VN vj as the representive node, where j = Z × i, go through Steps 3-6.
3. Compute the number of edges needed to be established as d = di − ρi, go through Steps 4-6.

V. IMPLEMENTATION RESULTS AND ANALYSIS
In this section, we evaluate the BER performances of our designed LDPC codes and the proposed decoding algorithm. Specifically, we implement the entire system on a FPGA board and test its ultimate-shannon-limit approaching ability. The parameters are listed as follows.
• Length of the information bits 1024 • Code rate ranges from 0.05 to 0.1 • Code Length ranges from 10176 to 20736 • Lifting factor Z = 64 • Order of Hadamard code is r = 4 • Iteration number for decoding is I = 30 • Channel LLRs are quantized by Nch = 8 bits • LLRs in the decoder are quantized by Ndec = 8 bits • Operating frequency fc = 200 MHz • FPGA board Xilinx Virtex UltraScale+ VCU118
According to the defined parameters, we utilize the proposed EXIT functions to optimize the degree distribution of rate1/10 code. Based on the optimized distribution, we then optimize the degree distribution of the rate-1/20 code. The results are presented in Table II. The decoding thresholds of the rate-1/10 code and the rate-1/20 code are −0.799 dB and −1.426 dB, respectively. In Fig. 7, the VND curves and HCND curves are perfectly matched.
To construct the PCM of the codes, we first construct the matrix A for the rate-1/10 code with a predesigned dual-diagonal matrix B using the proposed MM-QC-PEG algorithm. After constructing the PCM HJ1,K = [A, B], we continue to construct C based on HJ1,K with another dualdiagonal matrix D. Based on the parameters we used, the PCM size of the rate-1/10 code is 13 × 19 and that of the rate 1/20 code is 28 × 44.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, a practical code design and simple decoding algorithm are proposed for extremely low SNR regimes. The extrinsic information transfer functions are derived and a modified MM-QC-PEG algorithm is proposed to design the PCM with a fast dual-diagonal encoding structure. In addition, a layered normalized decoding algorithm is proposed to reduce the computational complexity significantly with a slight decoding degradation. The entire system is implemented on an FPGA broad. Experiment results verify the resilient ability of the scheme in the extremely low SNR regimes.
