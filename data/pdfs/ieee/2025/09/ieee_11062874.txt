Efficient ADMM Decoder for Binary LDPC Codes in Low-Iteration Regime
Yuxin Cheng, Yujie Ma, Qinglin Zhang, and Qiaoqiao Xia

Abstract— The alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) algorithm for decoding low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes has attracted significant attention because of its superior performance. The convergence characteristic has been regarded as a crucial element to enhance its advantage in practice. In this letter, an efficient ADMM decoder for binary LDPC codes is proposed by adjusting the position of the Euclidean projection to enhance the convergence behavior. The simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can improve the FER performance by 1.1dB as well as save average decoding iterations by 13% compared with the existing CSA when the maximum number of iterations is set to 20. The proposed algorithm can be suitable for use at low-iteration regime in high-throughput scenarios.
Index Terms— Low-density parity check (LDPC) codes, alternating direction method of multipliers(ADMM), check polytope projection, approximate parity polytope projection.
I. INTRODUCTION
LOW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes have been shown to close to Shannon capacity under belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithms after being first discovered by Gallager [1] in 1960s. The linear programming (LP) decoding method for LDPC codes was proposed and improved by Feldman et al. [2]. Their research showed that LP had all-zeros assumption and maximum-likelihood (ML) certificate property. However, the computational complexity of the original LP formulation can be prohibitively high, and its development was still limited.
To tackle the problem, Barman et al. [3] subsequently proposed an efficient LP decoding algorithm that integrates the benefits of linear programming with the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM-LP). This novel approach resulted in an algorithm with a message-passing structure similar to BP and min-sum, which greatly improves the decoding efficiency. Although the ADMM-based LP decoder provided a feasible solution, the frame error rate (FER) was higher than that of BP, primarily due to the non-integer vertices that resulted from the LP relaxation. To enhance the performance, Liu and Draper [4] suggested that penalty functions rendered the optimization problem non-convex and there was a substantial boost in decoding performance. Jiao et al. [5] introduced a novel ADMM-based penalized decoder to further enhance the FER performance of irregular LDPC codes

by adjusting the penalty term in the objective function and assigning distinct penalty parameters to different variable node degrees. Similarly, Wei and Banihashemi [6] proposed incorporating a penalty related to check node degrees in decoding, showing its effectiveness in improving decoding performance. The penalized ADMM-LP decoder was also computationally competitive with BP. However, it still exhibited high complexity, prompting numerous studies aimed at reducing this computational burden.
The most complex part involved in the ADMM-LP is the Euclidean projection on the d-dimensional parity-polytope. Zhang et al. proposed the Cut Search Algorithm (CSA) [7] to identify the cut plane of the parity-check polytope, which simplifies the projection compared to the original method, though it still requires sorting when solving piecewise linear optimization problems. Subsequently, G.Zhang [8] showed that the projection onto the parity polytope can be transformed to a projection onto a simplex. Wei and Banihashemi [9] introduced an iterative algorithm for projection onto the parity-check polytope that avoids sorting operations. However, the convergence speed of the algorithm is slow when keeping the FER performance. Lin et al. [10] proposed a bisection method iterative algorithm (BMIA) for Euclidean projections onto the parity check polytope, which avoids sorting and achieves linear complexity, with convergence speed up to 10 times faster than existing methods in high-dimensional cases.
Recently Xia [11] have tried to further simplify the Euclidean projection with line segment projection algorithm (LSA), which improves upon even-vertex algorithm by projecting onto the line segment consisting of two closest-vertices. However, it may also lose their accuracy in high-throughput applications like optical communications with limited iterations. To address this issue, A. Asadzadeh et al. introduce the SAPA [12], [13], which projects onto the affine hull of a small number of vertices of the polytope and does not require a water-filling process, maintains high performance even when used in conjunction with low-iteration ADMM-LP decoders.
Although the ADMM decoding algorithm has made significant progress in FER performance and complexity, its slow convergence rate remains a major bottleneck that limits its practical application. In this letter, we proposed an efficient ADMM decoder for binary LDPC codes by adjusting the position of the Euclidean projection to improve the convergence behavior to tackle the challenges in scenarios of low-iteration regime. In particular, the proposed algorithm can improve the FER performance by 1.1dB and reduce the average decoding iterations by 13% when the maximum number of iterations is set to 20, compared to the original CSA. Its fast convergence speed makes it more suitable for high-throughput scenarios with limited iterations.

A. Sources of Innovation
The main focus of research in the ADMM based decoding algorithm has been on reducing the complexity of the ADMM-based decoders and enhancing the error correction performance by incorporating penalty terms into the objective function of the LP decoding formulation. However, the issue of accelerating the convergence of ADMM-based decoding algorithms has not been thoroughly addressed in existing research. ADMM decoding algorithms can perform better than BP decoding algorithm at high iteration regime, but perform significantly worse than BP algorithm at lower iteration regime. We will concentrate on enhancing the convergence behavior of ADMM-based decoding, especially in the lower iteration regime.
ADMM penalized decoding algorithms added penalty terms to the LP model’s objective function to penalize the pseudocodeword. The convergence speed and the FER performance are shown to be improved. ADMM penalized decoding algorithms penalize the invalid solutions from the aspect of variable nodes and that the difference between the different ADMM penalized decoding algorithms is only the update rules of variable nodes.
It is worth mentioning that zj can be seen as the copy of x due to Pjx = zj, and Pj is the selecting matrix. Therefore, the problem we concern is whether by changing the update rule of z can be alternately used to obtain a more effective algorithm to improve convergence speed and the FER performance. The results in section IV reveal that changing the update rule of z by adjusting the position of the Euclidean projection can enhance the convergence behavior in the low iteration regime and the simulation results show that the proposed algorithm can improve the FER performance by 1.1dB when the maximum number of iterations is set to 20.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In our simulations, we consider an additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel with binary phase shift keying (BPSK) modulation. Meanwhile, we consider three different codes for simulation, the irregular (576, 384) rate 2/3 code (C1), the irregular (576, 432) rate 3/4 code (C2) from IEEE 802.16e standard and regular (2640, 1320) rate 1/2 Margulis code (C3).

The check node degrees of C1 − C3 are {10, 11}, {14, 15} and 6, respectively.
The l1 penalty function g(x) = −α|x − 0.5| is used for all the ADMM-LP based decoder through the simulations. The penalty parameter µ and the parameter α for a particular code are determined by performing an exhaustive search to achieve the best FER. In the simulations, random codewords are adopted. Besides, the over-relaxation technique is adopted and the over-relaxation parameter is set to 1.9. The early termination criterion is set to HxT = 0, and a unified flooding schedule is employed. The maximum number of iterations is set to 20 in order to compare the performance of BP decoders, ℓp-box decoder [14] and ADMM-LP decoders using LSA, SAPA, HPA [15], CSA, and ICSA in the low-iteration regime. We focus on improving ADMM-LP convergence under limited iterations, mainly comparing ADMM-LP based decoders, with others included for reference. The points plotted in all FER curves are obtained by generating at least 100 error frames and the SNR is measured per bit.

Prior to evaluating the performance of ICSA, it is crucial to investigate the impact of varying values of β on the performance of different codes. Fig. 1 illustrates the FER performance of the proposed ADMM-ICSA decoder for three codes with different values of β. The maximum number of iterations is set to 20, and the value of the coefficient β is varied from 0.98 to 1.12. The proposed ADMM-ICSA achieves optimal FER performance when β = 1.07 for C1. For C2, the optimal parameter is β = 1.08, whereas for C3, it is β = 1.05. Furthermore, as anticipated, when the parameter falls outside the expected range, the performance deteriorates significantly.
Fig.2 shows the FER performance of the different decoders with ICSA employing the optimal β for each code. The maximum number of iterations is set to 20 and 100, respectively. We can observe from Fig.2 that the FER performance of the proposed ICSA significantly outperforms that of other ADMM-LP based methods when Itersmax = 20. For example, for C1, at FER = 1 × 10−5, the performance gains of the proposed ICSA over LSA and SAPA are approximately 1.1dB and 0.9dB. Additionally, compared to CSA, ICSA achieves a gain of about 0.8dB. For C2, at FER = 1 × 10−5, the proposed ICSA achieves a significant performance gain of approximately 1.1 dB compared to CSA. Moreover, a similar trend is observed for C3, where the proposed algorithm demonstrates superior performance. Remarkably, in the case of C1 − C2 the proposed ICSA has only a small performance loss compared to the BP (SPA) decoding algorithm within 20 iterations, highlighting its efficiency and effectiveness in enhancing decoding performance. As expected, the improved method provides better FER at low-iteration by enhancing convergence. Moreover, when the maximum number of iterations is set to 100, the FER performance of various algorithms is evaluated, as shown in Fig.2. It is evident that as the number of iterations increases to 100, the performance gap between the methods diminishes, which is consistent with the underlying algorithmic principles.
The Fig.3 presents a comparison of the average decoding iterations for different codes using various algorithms, with data points obtained by generating at least 100,000 frames for each. It can be observed that the proposed ICSA significantly reduces the number of iterations compared to other ADMMLP algorithms. For example, for C2 at SNR = 4.0dB, the proposed ICSA reduces the number of iterations by approximately 13% compared to CSA and around 19% compared to LSA. It is widely acknowledged that LSA requires the highest number of iterations due to its relatively lower accuracy, necessitating more iterations to achieve the correct projection. In contrast, ICSA does not exhibit this drawback, leading to a lower iteration count. This further supports the claim that the proposed ICSA improves convergence speed more effectively than other ADMM-LP algorithms.
Fig. 4 illustrates the average decoding time for each algorithm, with data points generated from over 100,000 frames. It is shown that, for all three codes, the proposed ICSA exhibits a shorter average decoding time compared to the original CSA. This reduction in decoding time is not due to simplification of the projection calculation, but rather results from the reduced number of iterations. Furthermore,

in terms of average decoding time, ICSA is comparable to the approximate projection algorithms. Specifically, for C2, at SNR = 4.0dB, the average decoding time of ICSA is reduced by 8% compared to CSA, and is comparable to that of LSA and SAPA.
V. CONCLUSION
Considering that the current ADMM-LP decoders are insufficient in meeting the practical requirements related to a restricted number of iterations, this letter proposes an enhanced ADMM decoder algorithm to surmount this obstacle. Compare to the original CSA, the proposed ICSA demonstrates significant improvements in convergence speed, achieving a performance gain of 1.1 dB at 20 iterations and even has only a small performance loss compared to the BP for certain codes. These results highlight the suitability of ICSA for applications in low-iteration regimes. Moreover, though not included in the letter due to the limited space, the proposed ICSA decoder has also been verified to work effectively with 5G LDPC codes.
