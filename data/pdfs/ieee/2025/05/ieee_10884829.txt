Random Staircase Generator Matrix Codes: Coding Theorem, Performance Analysis, and Code Design
Qianfan Wang , Member, IEEE, Yiwen Wang , Yixin Wang, Jifan Liang , and Xiao Ma , Member, IEEE

Abstract—In this paper, we present a class of codes, referred to as random staircase generator matrix codes (SGMCs), which have staircase-like generator matrices. In the infinite-length region, we prove that the random SGMC is capacity-achieving over binary-input output-symmetric (BIOS) channels. In the finite-length region, we propose the generalized representative ordered statistics decoding with local constraints (LC-ROSD) algorithm for the SGMCs. The most distinguished feature of the SGMCs with LC-ROSD is that the staircase-like matrices enable parallel implementation of the Gaussian elimination (GE), avoiding the serial GE of conventional OSD and supporting a potential low decoding latency, as implied from simulations. To analyze the performance of random SGMCs in the finitelength region, we derive the ensemble weight spectrum and invoke the conventional union bound. We also derive a partially random coding union (RCU) bound, which is tighter than the conventional one and is used as a criterion to design the SGMCs. Staircase-like generator matrices allow us to derive a series of (tighter and tighter) lower bounds based on the second-order Bonferroni inequality with the incremental number of codewords. The numerical results show that the decoding performance can match well with the proposed partially RCU bound for different code rates and different profiles. The numerical results also show that the tailored SGMCs with the LC-ROSD algorithm can approach the finite-length performance bound, outperforming the 5G low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, 5G polar codes, and Reed-Muller (RM) codes.
Index Terms—Capacity achieving, ordered statistics decoding (OSD), parallel Gaussian elimination, partial error exponent, staircase generator matrix codes (SGMCs), representative OSD (ROSD).

This issue can be circumvented in some cases by precalculating and storing multiple systematic generator matrices [17] or by invoking specific decoding conditions to skip the GE process [18]. If the constraint on the MRB is relaxed, the GE can be replaced by the polynomial interpolations to form an enlarged basis over an extension field for BCH codes [19] or a near reliable basis [20] for binary images of RS codes.
In contrast to most existing works that aim to simplify OSD for general codes, this paper focuses on random staircase generator matrix codes (SGMCs) [21] that are specifically tailored for efficient decoding using a low-complexity OSD-like algorithm. The considered random SGMCs have staircaselike random generator matrices, which are lower triangular matrices with ones on the staircase, zeros on the upper right to the staircase, and random elements on the lower left to the staircase. Since the concept of SGMCs was introduced in [21], we have made the following achievements, which consist of the main distinct contributions of this paper.
1) Coding Theorem: In the infinite-length region, we prove that the random SGMCs are capacity-achieving over the binary-input output-symmetric (BIOS) channels. This is a new result compared to [21], and the proof is non-trivial, which relies on the recently developed patrial error exponent technique [22].
2) Performance Analysis: To analyze the performance of random SGMCs in the finite-length region, we derive a closed-form formula of the ensemble weight spectrum, instead of the recursive formula of the ensemble weight spectrum in [21]. More distinguishedly, in addition to the conventional UB that was also presented in [21], we derive a partially RCU bound. Different from the conventional RCU (also referred to as totally RCU in this paper) bound [23], which applies to totally random codes, the partially RCU bound can apply to semi-random codes like SGMCs. Notably, the proposed partially RCU bound is tighter than the conventional UB and well-matched to the simulation results. We also derive a series of (tighter and tighter) lower bounds based on the second-order Bonferroni inequality since an incremental number of low-weight codewords (belonging to low-dimensional sub-codes) can be generated from the staircase-like generator matrices.
3) Construction Rule: Distinguished from the heuristic construction in [21], which is derived from the RM profile, we propose an explicit rule based on the proposed partially RCU bound to design the SGMCs for a given target FER, where the profile is optimized to achieve a trade-off between performance and decoding complexity.
4) Decoding Algorithm: For practical use, we turn to the representative OSD with local constraints (LC-ROSD) algorithm for the SGMCs, where the staircase-like matrices enable parallel implementation of the GE and the local constraints are imposed to skip many invalid TEPs. As a further achievement, we propose a generalized LC-ROSD with a parallel factor m to provide a flexible

way to trade off the parallelism (in GE) and the complexity (closely related to the quality of the encoding basis and hence the number of TEPs for re-encoding). When m = 1, the generalized LC-ROSD is the same as the LC-OSD in [15], and when m = k, the generalized LC-ROSD is reduced to the original LC-ROSD in [21]. Particularly, for 1 < m < k, the proposed generalized LC-ROSD is expected to adapt to different SNR regions by adjusting the parameter m, as confirmed by simulations. 5) Numerical Results: The numerical results show that the random SGMCs with the LC-ROSD algorithm can match well with the proposed partially RCU bound for different code rates and different profiles. The numerical results also show that the tailored SGMCs with the LC-ROSD algorithm can approach the finite-length performance bound, outperforming the 5G LDPC codes, 5G polar codes, and Reed-Muller (RM) codes.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. In Section II, we present the system model and the definition of the SGMCs. We also prove that the random SGMC is capacityachieving in the infinite-length region by using the derived error exponent in this section. In Section III, we focus on the finite-length random SGMCs. We propose the generalized LCROSD algorithm of the SGMCs for practical use. The SGMCs are analyzed by deriving the ensemble weight spectrum, the partially RCU bounds, and the second-order Bonferroni lower bounds. The code design for the profile and the performance comparison are presented in Section IV. Section V concludes this paper.

II. STAIRCASE GENERATOR MATRIX CODES
A. System Model
Let F2 = {0, 1} be the binary field. Let C [n, k] be a binary linear block code of length n and dimension k, whose generator matrix and parity-check matrix are denoted by G and H, respectively. At the transmitter, the information sequence Uk ∈ Fk2 is firstly encoded as Xn ∈ Fn2 by C [n, k], i.e., Xn = UkG. Then, the codeword Xn is transmitted through a BIOS memoryless channel, resulting in Yn. At the receiver, the decoder of C [n, k] takes as input the receiving sequence Yn and delivers as output the estimated information sequence Uˆ k. The performance of the system model is measured by FER, which is defined as FER = Pr{Uˆ k Uk}.

III. DECODING ALGORITHM AND PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS

A. Generalized LC-ROSD
The original OSD [5] suffers from two main issues. The first issue is the use of the GE, which is inevitable in determining the MRB. Even worse, for a general matrix, only serial (column-by-column) implementation of GE is available, leading to an additional decoding latency. The second issue is the sub-optimality of the search order on the basis of the Hamming weights of the TEPs, without exploiting the soft information. Actually, for the seconde issue, with an optimized search order, several tailored early stopping criteria can be used to reduce the number of searches. The second issue has been resolved by the LC-OSD [14], [15] that selects an extended MRB of size k + δ and utilizes the SLVA [28] or the two-way FPT algorithm [29] by taking into the local constraints introduced by the extended MRB. The LC-OSD skips many invalid TEPs and hence reduces the number of searches down to less than ten from hundreds of thousands in the high SNR region. The first issue can be mitigated for the SGMC by the use of representative OSD with local constraints (LC-ROSD) [21], which selects at least one bit from each staircase to form a locally reliable basis, resulting in a lower triangular sub-matrix with ones on the diagonal. This lower triangular sub-matrix enables parallel implementation of the GE process, supporting a potentially low decoding latency. We first review the original LC-ROSD algorithm [21] as follows.

IV. CODE DESIGN AND PERFORMANCE COMPARISON
A. Code Design
The performance is closely related to the profile, as evidenced by the ensemble weight spectrum and the partially RCU bound. Intuitively, a good SGMC should compromise the following factors. F1: Uniform staircase: For the LC-ROSD, the locally reli-
able basis is formed by selecting representative bits from the corresponding staircase. Hence, heuristically, we may widen the narrowest staircase to improve the reliability of the basis. This can be achieved by setting all staircases as uniformly as possible. F2: Large w0 for performance: The minimum Hamming weight is upper bounded by the row weights of the generator matrix. So, w0 should be large enough to guarantee the minimum Hamming weight as well as the performance.

Authorized licensed use limited to: Yonsei Univ. Downloaded on June 24,2026 at 07:22:51 UTC from IEEE Xplore. Restrictions apply.

F3: Small w0 for complexity: Increasing w0 narrows the other staircases, limiting the choice and reliability of the local MRB and consequently increasing the average number of TEPs (resulting in a higher complexity). So, w0 should be small to ensure low complexity.

Taking into the above three points, we propose the following nearly uniform (NU) profile, where w0 is first determined and the other staircases wi for 1 ≤ i ≤ k − 1 are allocated as uniformly as possible.
Definition 3 (Nearly Uniform Profile): For any given w0 ≥ n/k , a profile w = (w0, w1, · · · , wk−1) is referred to as the nearly uniform profile if w1 = w2 = · · · = w j = (n − w0)/(k − 1) , and w j+1 = · · · = wk−1 = (n − w0)/(k − 1) , for j satisfying j (n − w0)/(k − 1) + (k − 1 − j) (n − w0)/(k − 1) = n − w0.
A key question arises: how to determine w0? In [21], w0 is simply selected based on the RM profile and evaluated by the conventional UB. Since the conventional UB is loose, the efficacy of the optimization can not be predicted. In this paper, we utilize the tight partially RCU
bound, which matches well with simulations, to choose a minimum w0 that ensures performance while maintaining low complexity. The specific strategy for selecting w0 is as follows.
A code design recipe: For a target FER and a tolerant gap, minimize w0 such that the gap between the corresponding partially RCU bound and the totally RCU bound is tolerant at
the target FER. Example 7: Consider SGMCs [128, 64] with RM profile

V. CONCLUSION
This paper has investigated the random SGMCs, which have staircase-like generator matrices and enable parallel GE, resulting in a low-complexity LC-ROSD algorithm. The random SGMCs have been theoretically proven to achieve capacity over BIOS channels in the infinite-length regime and shown to approach capacity in the finite-length regime through the proposed tight bounds and the demonstrated decoding performance. The numerical results show that the proposed SGMCs with LC-ROSD match well with the proposed bounds. The numerical results also show that the proposed random SGMCs outperform the 5G codes and RM codes.
