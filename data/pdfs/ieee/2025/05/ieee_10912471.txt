Ternary Message Passing Decoding of High-Rate Regular LDPC Codes

Qiushi Xu , Ming Jiang , Member, IEEE, Fan Ding, and Yi Wang

Abstract— In this letter, we present an improved ternary message passing (TMP) decoding scheme designed for the high-rate regular low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, which are widely used in storage systems. The decoders in this approach transmit information internally by only three states, and compared to traditional normalized min-sum decoding, it retains only sign multiplication operations, resulting in lower complexity. Meanwhile, we propose a method for optimizing decoder parameters using the density evolution (DE) analysis, which allows the TMP decoders to achieve performance close to that of the belief propagation decoders. Furthermore, in the case of hard decision inputs, we introduce a quantized DE analysis to optimize and compute decoder parameters, enabling the TMP decoders to even outperform the normalized min-sum decoders.
Index Terms— Ternary message passing, low-density paritycheck codes, density evolution, high-rate, hard-decision.
I. INTRODUCTION
I N RECENT years, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have been widely applied in various fields such as optical networks and information storage due to their excellent performance. When LDPC codes were first introduced along with the belief propagation (BP) decoding [1]. In order to reduce the computational complexity of BP decoding, many algorithms such as normalized min-sum (NMS) decoding were introduced. However, these decoders still face difficulties in meeting the transmission requirements for ultra high throughput due to the use of high-precision internal computations and storage of soft information [2]. Additionally, the widely used NMS decoding involves a large number of comparison operations, posing new challenges when handling high-rate LDPC codes with high row weights in various high-throughput transmission applications [3].
To further reduce complexity, many researchers have focused on transforming the way to update node information in decoders, leading to the development of techniques such as finite alphabet iterative decoders [4], [5], [6], [7] and binary message passing decoders [8]. However, these decoders struggle to strike a balance between decoding complexity and error correction performance, especially when decoding high-rate LDPC codes. As a solution, ternary message passing (TMP) decoding was first introduced in [9] and [10]. In TMP

Compared to NMS decoders, TMP decoders involve only sign multiplications without the need for comparisons, resulting in lower computational complexity. Despite sacrificing some decoding performance, TMP decoders are well-suited for highspeed decoding of high rate LDPC codes with large row weights.
Relative to previous work [9], we change the node update mechanism in TMP decoders, reducing the computational complexity of the decoding process. Additionally, we propose a method for optimizing decoder parameters using the density evolution (DE) analysis to enhance the performance of the TMP decoders for the high-rate regular LDPC codes with large column weights. The optimized TMP decoders demonstrate only a marginal performance gap compared to traditional high-complexity decoders, such as the BP decoders. Thus, we propose the quantized DE analysis corresponding to hard-decision inputs and low precision computations. After optimizing the decoder parameters, the performance of the TMP decoders with hard-decision inputs shows a significant improvement, even surpassing that of the NMS decoders.
II. TMP DECODERS OF LDPC CODES
The basic principle of TMP decoders is similar to the BP decoding of LDPC codes, which involves the soft information, propagating and updating between check nodes (CNs) and variable nodes (VNs). In the sets of VNs and CNs, the number of elements are denoted as n and m, respectively, which correspond to the code length and the number of parity-check equations in a LDPC code. To facilitate the description of the decoding process, the following definitions are used. Let vj and ci denote the j-th VN and i-th CN, respectively. N (vj) and N (ci) respectively denote the set of all CNs connected to vj and the neighboring node set of ci in the Tanner graph. For regular LDPC codes, the number of elements in N (vj) for all VNs is equal to that in N (ci) for all CNs, and they are defined as dv and dc, which represent the degrees of the VNs and CNs, respectively.
When analyzing the performance of TMP decoders, we use the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel with binary phase-shift keying (BPSK) modulation. The received signal vector at the receiving end is represented as y = (1 − 2z) + s, where z is the n-length encoded vector, and s is a noise sequence consisting of independen Gaussian distributed variables with zero mean and variance σ2. What needs to be input into the decoder is a logarithm likelihood ratio (LLR) vector ξ = {ξ1, ξ2, . . . , ξn}, which represents the soft information after channel transmission, where ξj = ln [P (yj|xj = 0) /P (yj|xj = 1)] = 2yj/σ2.

B. The Process of Quantized DE Analysis

In communication systems with stringent requirements for low power consumption and low latency, the inputs of LDPC decoders are typically quantized to low precision [11], [12]. When the bit-width of the inputs is reduced to 1 bit, i.e., when the channel outputs are directly fed into the decoders through hard-decision, the information propagated and updated within TMP decoders becomes discrete. In such cases, the floating-point DE analysis fails to accurately estimate the probability distribution of the edge messages lit→j. V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose a method to reduce the computational complexity of TMP decoders for high-rate regular LDPC codes, along with an optimization approach for the decoder parameters. Additionally, considering the use of hard-decision inputs, we present the corresponding quantized DE analysis. The final simulation results indicate that this TMP application in the decoding of three types of high-rate regular LDPC codes performs commendably, with only slight performance gaps when compared to the other decoders such as BP. Additionally, The quantized DE analysis and the parameter optimization method can significantly improve the performance of the TMP decoders. The demonstrated excellence of TMP decoders in decoding high-rate regular LDPC codes positions them

as promising candidates for use in various communication
systems, including optical fiber networks and new storage
systems.
