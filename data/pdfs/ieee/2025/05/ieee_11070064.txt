I. 
INTRODUCTION 
The need for laser communications, which provide greater 
bandwidth (several gigabits per sec), decreased weight, 
increased suppleness, and improved security, is being driven by 
the approaching bandwidth limit of traditional radio frequency 
(RF) 
communications 
[1]. 
However, 
environmental 
considerations including atmospheric attenuation, loss of free 
space, and refraction index turbulence make the laser 
communications propagation channel less than ideal, leading to 
channel fading [2]. Channel coding is therefore necessary to 
shield the transmit signal from the consequences of 
transmission channel limitations [3]. Additionally, a specific 
physical layer design is needed for laser communications in 
order to achieve high data speeds [4]. Nevertheless, traditional 
channel coding sometimes uses a lot of storage and processing 
power, which is a barrier to reaching ultra-high throughput [5]. 
Innovative, fast, and low-complexity codecs are therefore 
desperately needed, especially for the onboard side where 
embedded limitations that are consistent with a space location 
are quite challenging [6]. The performance of LDPC codes is 
near the Shannon limit, which makes them popular. LDPC was 
selected 
as 
the 
channel 
coding 
code 
by 
numerous 
communication standards, like as DVB-S2, CCSDS, 5G, and 
others [7]. For this application space, the effectiveness of pairs 
of lower-density parity-check codes with decoders has 
previously been demonstrated currently [8, 9]. The CCSDS 
optical working group (SLS-OPT) has been presented with a 
novel forward error correction (FEC) network as a competitive 
substitute for the existing standardised solutions for radio 
frequency telemetry downlinks, including SCCC, DVB-S2 with 
ARJA/C2 LDPC codes [10]. 
The challenge is to provide a low-complexity, high-
throughput, universal LDPC decoder for laser communications. 
Existing approaches frequently have to choose between 
hardware complexity and decoding efficiency. Conventional 
decoders, such as sum-product algorithms or belief propagation, 
need a lot of computing, and simplified methods don't work as 
well with high-speed laser communication systems. 
Effective decoding methods are required due to the growing 
need for high data rates in laser communications as well as 
propagation channel impairments. Using an IFPP-IFP scheme 
with UAMP and VPPSOA, this work introduces a lower-
complexity, higher-throughput LDPC decoder with the goal of 
improving performance in laser communiqu├й systems while 
reducing latency and resource consumption. This motivates us 
to do this work.

high-throughput LDPC decoder especially made for laser 
communications, which tackles the difficulties caused by 
propagation channel impairments. The UAMP method and the

described. Initially, the channel buffer is used to route quantised 
channel log-likelihood ratio (LLRs) of Q bits. 2nd-frame

in CMB is guaranteed by UAMP and VPPSOA methods. 
Updates are synchronised using data aligners. Alongside CNU, 
PCU does parity-check computations. Repeat for the first-frame 
check nodes and second-frame variable nodes. In the 
termination, repeat until the iterations approach Imax or the 
symptoms are zero. The output HDMB is used to read decoded 
hard choices. The description about the LDPC-LC-VPPSOA is

F. Data Alignment Unit and Massage Packing 
Several messages are processed simultaneously and condensed 
into a single RAMM word to increase throughput. An 
optimised low-complexity data aligner that precomputes offsets 
for data alignment prevents memory conflicts. With just two Q-
bit registers and additional clock latency, this technique 
streamlines multiplexers and lowers decoding latency. 
Hard choices and extrinsic messages are packed during the 
VNU phase to minimise memory usage; Q-bit extrinsic 
messages are saved in RAMM, whereas 1-bit hard decisions are 
kept in RAMM. This eliminates the need for further address 
generation and enables Check Node Units and parity-check 
units to function in parallel, sharing address information. 
To avoid memory conflicts while obtaining final hard decisions, 
ЁЭСЛ├Ч ЁЭСЫ hard decisions are recorded to RAMC during the VNU 
phase, while RAMM is written to the outputs. 
By using this method, the necessary number of RAMC memory 
banks is decreased from n to 1.

C. Discussion 
In this section, the proposed LDPC-LC-VPPSOA approach is 
discussed. Decoders attain highest throughput at the rate of 
highest source expenditure. Using a integration of five 
decoders, the proposed decoder achieves a 50% reduction in 
resource usage and a throughput that is 1.3 times higher than 
that of a single decoder. Even with a single decoder, this 
architecture delivers more than double the throughput 
compared to designs from existing approaches, while 
maintaining the same level of resource usage. The proposed 
decoder leverages ASIP structure to minimize hardware 
difficulty while enhancing throughput decoding. Notably, the 
decoder makes efficient use of flip-flop (FF) resources, which 
remain relatively low, but it exhibits a higher reliance on 
lookup tables (LUTs). Implementation comparisons further 
demonstrate that achieving lower critical path delays is 
facilitated by a well-optimized allocation of hardware 
resources. The FPGA frequency is constrained to 260 MHz 
due to the critical path. However, the proposed decoder makes 
a more balanced use of both LUT and FF resources, which 
could result in higher decoding throughput and reduced critical 
path latency. Comparisons reveal that the proposed decoder 
maintains 
low 
hardware 
complexity 
while 
delivering 
impressive throughput. 
V. 
CONCLUSION 
This research presents a Lower-Density Parity-Check decoder 
used for future laser transportation, assisted through the 
advanced Inter-Frame Pipeline and Intra-Frame Parallel 
architecture. The decoder generates efficient IFPP by 
maximizing the use of processing units through the VPPSO 
algorithm and the UAMP scheme. To achieve IFP, it also 
employs low-complexity data alignment units and integrates 
efficient message-packing strategies. Xilinx XCKU060 FPGA 
is utilized to implement the proposed approach. The LDPC-
LC-VPPSOA approach attains 18.34%, 16.23%, 19.56% 
higher decoding throughput18.36%, 20.45% and 21.67% lower 
Bit error rate when analyzed with the existing methods like 
UHTLC-LDPCD-LC, SHTLL-LDPCD-SIMD and HRSS-
LDPC-KC respectively. The future work involves, extending 
the 
proposed 
LDPC 
decoder 
for 
more 
extensive 
communication systems, like satellite and underwater optical 
communications, improving resilience to various channel 
impairments, and investigating integration with quantum error 
correction for next-generation networks.
