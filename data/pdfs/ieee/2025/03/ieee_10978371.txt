Abstract—To assist the design and optimization of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes via density evolution (DE) on binary input additive white Gaussian noise (BIAWGN) channels, we propose a novel mixed Gamma approximation (MGA) scheme to obtain more accurate distribution of messages updated and output by the check nodes during DE iterations. Firstly, we highlight the inaccuracy of existing Gaussian approximation (GA) methods in approximating the distribution of check node output messages, especially when the messages from variable nodes are small with high probability (i.e. low signal-to-noise ratio), and the check nodes have a large degree, which leads to inexact results in GA methods. Then, we establish the MGA scheme by utilizing the statistical properties of Gamma distribution and combine it with GA, which outperforms the existing GA methods in the metrics of error of output mean and Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence of output distribution for a wide range of parameters. Simulation and analysis validate that our MGA scheme has the potential for the design and optimization of LDPC codes, which can provide adequately accurate estimation of check node outputs with moderate complexity for a variety of approximation methods, such as Gaussian capacity approximation, and significantly reduce the computational complexity by sacrificing minor accuracy.
Index Terms—Low-density parity-check codes, density evolution, Gaussian approximation, Gamma distribution.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low earth orbit (LEO) satellite network is considered as crucial technology for delivering ubiquitous access and Internet of things (IoT) services in the sixth generation (6G) mobile networks [1]–[3]. Equipped with free-space optical (FSO) technology, it is possible to achieve global coverage and provide high-speed data services [4]. However, due to the Doppler effect induced by fast-moving satellites and the instability of inter-satellite links [5], simple and efficient coding techniques are required to guarantee the communication reliability.
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, especially spatially coupled LPDC (SC-LDPC) codes, are considered as potential coding techniques for inter-satellite FSO links and widely researched in recent years [6]–[8]. Combined with the belief propagation (BP) algorithm, LDPC codes are able to approach

Density evolution (DE) is a commonly used tool for performance analysis and optimization of LDPC codes under BP decoding, but direct implementation of DE suffers from high computational complexity, as it has to numerically compute the probability density function (PDF) of messages during BP process via Fourier transform [9]. In [10], the authors proposed the Gaussian approximation (GA) to simplify the design of regular and irregular LDPC codes, assuming that the messages passed between nodes obey the so called “symmetric Gaussian distribution”, and most GA-based methods use only a single parameter to represent the distribution of messages, such as the mean of messages [10], the output bit error rate [11], and the output signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) [12]. In recent years, there have also been studies on the implementation of GA on SC-LDPC codes and its superset multi-edge-type LDPC (METLDPC) codes [13], [14].
However, as mentioned in [14], [15], when the degree of the check node is large or the input messages are small with high probability, the distribution of output message of check node tends to deviate from the Gaussian distribution, so that GA cannot give an accurate estimation of the distribution and its parameters, end up producing suboptimal designs. At the same time, targeted studies to improve GA by amending its distributional approximation are scarce.
In this paper, we propose a direct check node output distribution approximation scheme based on the Gamma distribution and GA, without using the Fourier transform or Monte Carlo simulations, referred to as mixed Gamma approximation (MGA) scheme. In contrast to GA which uses a single parameter, our MGA scheme employs multiple easy-to-compute parameters to more accurately describe the distribution of messages from check nodes, providing more precise estimation of parameters with moderate complexity to improve the accuracy of approximated code design techniques, and is applicable under a wider range of parameters. We also point out that the distribution of the check node output messages provided by MGA can be adopted to reduce the computational complexity of some GA-based methods, such as Gaussian capacity

The GA assumes the messages output by the nodes to be symmetric Gaussians with twice the variance of the mean, so that its probability distribution is completely determined by a single parameter, the mean, variance, etc.. By making this assumption, GA is able to easily obtain various parameters of the output message by numerical integration. Therefore,

many GA-based approximation methods tend to use a single parameter to represent the message passed between the nodes, transforming the complex numerical computation of the PDF into a simple parametric computation, thus greatly reducing the computational complexity of DE.

B. Combination with Gaussian Approximation

However, when the input message is large with high probability and the degree of the check node is small, the distribution of S has a spiky shape as shown in Fig. 1(c). In this case, the main feature of the distribution exists in how heavy the tail of its PDF is and its skewness, thus a direct approximation of its distribution using the mean and variance introduces large errors as shown in Fig. 1(d). V. SIMULATION RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS
In this section, we perform numerical simulations to demonstrate the effectiveness of MGA scheme for approximating the output distribution of the check nodes.
To obtain the approximation performance of proposed MGA scheme under different codeword conditions, we set the maximum variable node degree dv,m = 10 and randomly sample the variable node degree distribution in each round of simulation. In order to reflect the updates of the messages at variable nodes, we generate a random channel LLR u0 between [1, 4], and a random number between [0, 1] as the check node message u updated in the previous iteration, and the sequence of variable node messages is obtained by vi = u0+(i−1)u, i = 1, ..., dv,m. Finally, the degree dc of the check node is taken randomly in [6, 20]. The number of samples is 500000 and the exponent r in Eq. (16) is 2.
We compare the output means of the MGA and the original GA proposed in [10] with the output mean obtained from the simulations, and the KL divergence between the approximate and simulated distributions. VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we highlighted the shortcomings of existing GA-based methods in approximating the distribution of check node output messages in the process of DE analysis of LDPC codes, and by analyzing the structural characteristics of the update rule at the check nodes, we proposed the MGA scheme combing both desirable statistical properties of the Gamma distribution and the advantages of GA. Through simulation and analysis, we found that our MGA scheme outperforms GA in the metrics of KL divergence and error of the mean with respect to the simulated results for a wide range of parameters, and can be adopted to greatly reduce the computational complexity of a variety of approximation methods like GCA, without much loss of accuracy. In future work, our MGA scheme would be utilized to derive more accurate moderate-complexity approximation schemes based on or beyond GA, to assist the design of LDPC and SC-LDPC codes for inter-satellite FSO links.
