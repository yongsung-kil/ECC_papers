VN-Based Tail-Biting Globally-Coupled LDPC Codes

Hao Liu, Graduate Student Member, IEEE, and Qi-Yue Yu , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—In this paper, we propose a new class of globallycoupled low-density parity-check (GC-LDPC) codes, referred to as variable node (VN) based tail-biting (TB) GC-LDPC codes. Unlike conventional check node (CN) based GC-LDPC codes, which rely on the global CNs for coupling, the proposed codes employ global VNs for the coupling with a TB structure. The innovative coupling structure not only eliminates the rate loss problem inherent in CN-based GC-LDPC codes but also provides greater flexibility in adjusting the degree distribution. Moreover, the TB structure can significantly reduce the hardware complexity of the encoder and decoder of the VN-based TB-GCLDPC code, making them more practical for implementation. To construct the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, we present two methods based on optimizing girth and degree distributions, respectively. In addition, we introduce the low-complexity encoding scheme and decoding scheme tailored to the special structure of the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. Specifically, the proposed merry-go-round (MGR) component-code-based (CCB) decoding scheme can achieve the global coding gain of the time-invariant VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed codes not only outperform existing CN-based GC-LDPC codes but also achieve comparable performance to advanced LDPC codes, such as 5G-LDPC codes, while requiring lower hardware implementation complexity.
Index Terms—LDPC codes, variable nodes, tail-biting, globally-coupled, construction, encoding, decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
R ECENTLY, globally-coupled (GC) low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes, which were proposed by Li et al in [1], have become an attractive type of coupled LDPC codes. Unlike the spatially-coupled (SC) LDPC codes [2], [3], GCLDPC codes connect a group of disjoint local LDPC codes by using the global check nodes (CNs) or variable nodes (VNs), thereby cascading the group of local codes into a long LDPC code. When the local LDPC codes are coupled by global CNs or VNs, GC-LDPC codes are further referred to as the CN-based or VN-based GC-LDPC codes, respectively [4]. The local and global characteristics of GC-LDPC codes have brought more insights in the designing of LDPC codes as

well as the encoding and decoding schemes, attracting many researchers for further exploration.
Most of the current studies primarily focus on the construction methods of CN-based GC-LDPC codes. In [1] and [4], the authors propose several algebraic methods for constructing CN-based GC-LDPC codes and show that CN-based GCLDPC codes can perform well both on the additive white Gaussian noise channel (AWGNC) and the binary erasure channel (BEC). In [5], the finite geometry GC-LDPC codes are proposed, where the local section consists of the finite geometry LDPC codes and the global section is formed by a finite field LDPC code. For the non-binary (NB) case, a type of CN-based NB-GC-LDPC codes are constructed based on the Reed-Solomon codes [6], which has shown good performance over AWGNC as well as the correction of random errors and bursts of erasures. The authors in [7] construct a special type of CN-based GC-LDPC codes by introducing the “tailbiting” (TB) structure to the global section, which can reduce the degrees of global CNs to obtain large girth. In [8], the authors propose the interleaved-type and product-type CNbased GC-LDPC codes. In [9], a type of CN-based GC-LDPC with a nested structure called Matryoshka GC-LDPC codes is proposed. The authors in [10] design protograph-based GCLDPC codes of near-capacity performances over Gaussian channel with burst erasures.
In addition, some literature investigates the encoding and decoding schemes, theoretical thresholds and relevant application scenarios of CN-based GC-LDPC codes. For example, a two-phase local/global iterative scheme is proposed in [1], which divides the decoding into local and global phases based on the structure of CN-based GC-LDPC codes. The authors in [11] give a theoretical analysis framework of CN-based GCLDPC codes based on the 2D density evolution. In [7], the authors analyze the trade-off between decoding latency and performance. In [12], the global parity-check bits are designed to be transmitted by free-ride coding [13], [14] without extra transmission energy and bandwidth. In fact, many researchers have applied GC-LDPC codes in flash memory [15], multipleinput-multiple-output communication [16], [17], and coding modulation systems [18].
Although the CN-based GC-LDPC code is promising, it has some inherent issues. First, the addition of global CNs introduces global parity-check bits, which results in a reduction of the code rate of the CN-based GC-LDPC codes relative to their local LDPC codes. Second, the connections of global CNs are expected to increase the degree of all VNs, which typically

leads to the degradation of the threshold of CN-based GCLDPC codes compared with the local LDPC codes. Moreover, GC-LDPC codes usually have large block lengths, which demand considerable hardware resources for implementation.
To address these issues, this paper proposes a type of GCLDPC codes, referred to as VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code. Unlike conventional CN-based GC-LDPC codes, VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes establish connections through global VNs, which introduces fundamental changes. First, the use of global VNs naturally addresses the issue of rate loss, as no additional parity-check bits are introduced. Second, the proposed local and global structure offers greater flexibility in the degree distribution design for VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes than CN-based GC-LDPC codes, thereby enhancing the potential for performance optimization. Specifically, since the VNs degree distribution of local and global sections in VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes do not influence each other. According to [19], a good threshold for the LDPC code typically involves a combination of high-degree VNs and a large number of low-degree VNs. Thus, we can jointly design the VNs degree distributions for the local and global sections of VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, making it easier to optimize the threshold. More importantly, the TB structure allows the introduction of block-cyclic structures into both the local and global sections of VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. This enables the simplification of the implementation of both the encoder and decoder for VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes without any performance loss. This indicates that when VNbased TB-GC-LDPC codes achieve performance comparable to other LDPC codes, their encoders and decoders can have lower hardware implementation complexity. The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows.
1) We give the definition of VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes and classify them into time-invariant and timevariant types, where the parity-check matrix of the time-invariant type possesses section-wise block-cyclic property. Then, due to the introduction of global VNs, the local section of the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code is no longer independent. Accordingly, a new local structure, referred to as the component code, is defined to facilitate the encoding and decoding of VN-based TBGC-LDPC codes.
2) Two efficient methods are proposed for constructing VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. The first one, referred to as the standard orthogonal basis (SOB)-based unwrapping method, transforms a small matrix into the large base matrix of a VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code while preserving the girth. The second method employs the masking technique, where a masking matrix is specifically designed to align with the structure of VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. This approach offers greater flexibility in adjusting the degree distribution.
3) To efficiently encode VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, we introduce the component-code-based (CCB) encoding scheme, which reformulates the encoding of the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code as the encoding of multiple component codes. This scheme not only reduces computational complexity but also allows time-invariant

VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes to be encoded using a single component encoder. In order to take full advantage of the section-wise block-cyclic property, we propose the merry-go-round (MGR) CCB decoding scheme, which can achieve the global coding gain with only a single component decoder, thereby reducing the required hardware resources without sacrificing performance.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. Section II gives the definition of VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes and illustrates the structure of the Tanner graph. Section III proposes two methods to construct VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. Section IV presents the CCB encoding scheme. In Section V, the MGR-CCB decoding scheme are proposed. Section VI investigates the performance of the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes over AWGNC and BEC. Section VII concludes this paper.

Compared to the local LDPC codes of CN-based GC-LDPC codes, we can observe that the conventional local LDPC codes of the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code can no longer be directly used for local decoding due to their connection through global VNs. Thus, the component codes defined here are intended to represent the “local codes” of VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. However, the component codes are composed of substructures of local and global sections of the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, whereas the local codes are constructed only from the local section of the CN-based GC-LDPC code. This distinction leads to differences in the design of their corresponding encoders and decoders. For VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, the component codes can be directly utilized for encoding the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code, and the local codes cannot directly compute the entire codeword of the CN-based GCLDPC code. Additionally, using only one component code is capable of achieving the global coding gain, whereas the local code of the CN-based GC-LDPC code only achieve the local coding gain. This will be further explained in the decoding section.

IV. A CCB ENCODING SCHEME FOR VN-BASED TB-GC-LDPC CODES
In Section II, we demonstrated that the VN-based TB-GCLDPC code can be decomposed into M component codes for the purpose of encoding. Building upon this property, this section first introduces a general CCB encoding scheme, which calculates the codeword of the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code by encoding the M component codes. Then, a specific CCB encoding scheme is presented based on the approximate triangular form (ATF) [24].

Based on the abovementioned notations, the CCB encoding scheme has the following four steps. First, each component encoding calculates the corresponding component parity-check vector. Second, using the component parity-check vectors and component local information vectors, we form the local section vl . Third, using the component global information vectors to form the global section vg . Finally, we obtain the codeword vgc = [vl , vg ]. Therefore, the essence of the CCB encoding scheme is to transform a large matrix Hgc into multiple smaller matrices Hc(0), H(c1), . . . , Hc(M−1) for encoding, which can reduce the computational complexity in many cases.
Moreover, for the time-invariant type of particular interest, the M component encoders are identical. Hence, it is sufficient to multiplex a single component encoder for encoding all component codes. This feature facilitates a reduction in hardware resource requirements, which is advantageous for practical implementation.
Additionally, the CCB encoding scheme is well-suited to support the uplink multi-user transmission system. Specifically, let the m-th user utilize the m-th component code Cc(m). If the global section vg is set as a fixed sequence, each user can independently encode the component codeword for transmission. At the receiver, the received sequences are assembled into a form suitable for decoding with a VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code, thereby leveraging the performance gain offered by the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC code. Since the uplink multi-user transmission scheme fixes the global information bits, it leads to rate loss. Therefore, how to achieve a better trade-off is an interesting question.

V. AN MGR-CCB DECODING SCHEME FOR TIME-INVARIANT VN-BASED TB-GC-LDPC CODES
Inspired by the component code structure and the simplified decoding algorithms proposed in [25], [26], we propose an MGR-CCB decoding algorithm specifically tailored for timeinvariant VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. By fully exploiting the section-wise block-cyclic structure, the proposed scheme enables the decomposition of the overall decoding task into multiple component decodings. B. Comparison With Two-Phase Decoding of CN-Based GC-LDPC Codes
A key feature of the CN-based GC-LDPC codes is that their structure supports two-phase decoding, where the local section is used for local decoding and the entire parity-check matrix is employed for global decoding. For time-invariant VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, two-phase decoding can also be performed. However, unlike CN-based GC-LDPC codes, the local section cannot be directly used for local decoding for VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes. In a general sense, local decoding involves using a small portion of the entire parity-check matrix for decoding, while global decoding uses the entire parity-check matrix for decoding. The MGR-CCB decoding scheme uses only a single component decoder, and its hardware overhead is comparable to that of a single local decoder in CN-based GC-LDPC codes. Therefore, it can be viewed as a form of local decoding for time-invariant VNbased TB-GC-LDPC codes.
It is worth noting that, unlike the local decoding of CNbased GC-LDPC codes, which can only achieve local coding gains, the MGR-CCB decoding scheme can directly obtain global coding gains. Therefore, for time-invariant VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, global decoding is not required, since a single component decoder is sufficient to achieve global coding gains. This represents one of the advantages over CNbased GC-LDPC codes, as it can achieve better performance with lower complexity.

VII. CONCLUSION
This paper presents the VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes, whose coupling architecture is realized through global VNs with the TB structure. This design not only mitigates the rate loss observed in CN-based GC-LDPC codes but also facilitates flexible adjustments to the degree distribution for performance optimization. Simulation results show that VN-based TBGC-LDPC codes outperform the corresponding CN-based GC-LDPC codes and can be further design to achieve the performance comparable to other advanced LDPC codes, such as 5G-LDPC codes. Furthermore, by leveraging the sectionwise block-cyclic property, a single component encoder and decoder can be employed to process the entire VN-based TBGC-LDPC code without performance degradation. This can reduce hardware resource requirements, making these codes particularly appealing for implementation in practical systems with constrained resources. For future work, further improving the performance of VN-based TB-GC-LDPC codes at lowto-moderate rates is an interesting issue. For example, by incorporating the approximated cycle extrinsic message degree [31] to optimize the selection of CPMs. Additionally, reducing the computational complexity of the MGR-CCB decoding scheme or investigating its VLSI implementation also presents a promising research topic.
