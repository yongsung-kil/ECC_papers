Early Decoding with Globally Coupled LDPC Codes in Heterogeneous NOMA
Tai-Hsun Chen†, Min Qiu♮, Yu-Chih Huang†, and Jinhong Yuan∗
†Institute of Communications Engineering, National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University, Hsinchu, Taiwan ♮Global College, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, Shanghai, China
∗School of Electrical Engineering and Telecommunications, University of New South Wales, Sydney, Australia E-mail: {kevin410513003.ee10, jerryhuang}@nycu.edu.tw, min qiu@sjtu.edu.cn , j.yuan@unsw.edu.au

Abstract—In this paper, we propose a new coding scheme and decoding algorithms tailored for the coexistence of heterogeneous services with different blocklength and latency requirements in downlink. In such scenarios, early decoding—allowing the receiver to decode messages without receiving the entire transmission frame—is leveraged to perform low-latency multiuser interference cancellation. To facilitate such a decoding scheme with practical channel codes, we design partially globally coupled low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes that enable early decoding of local codes while maintaining connections among them, allowing local decoding processes to support each other for improved overall reliability. In addition, we put forth early decoding based successive interference cancellation (SIC), where the proposed code only requires a portion of the parity-check matrix for decoding. Simulation results show that our proposed design can achieve a 2.4 dB performance gain over 5G NR LDPC codes when using less than one-quarter of the coded symbols for SIC, highlighting its effectiveness in early decoding scenarios. This offers a practical and flexible solution for supporting heterogeneous service coexistence in future wireless systems.
I. INTRODUCTION
The coexistence of heterogeneous services such as enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB) and Ultra-Reliable LowLatency Communications (URLLC) has become one of the important communication scenarios in the evolution of wireless communication systems [1]. It is expected that the future mobile networks should accommodate a wide range of services while meeting their vastly different requirements. This trend is reflected in the IMT-2030 framework [2], which expands the original three 5G usage scenarios into six, highlighting the increasing diversity and complexity of future networks.
Among these scenarios, URLLC is particularly critical, demanding extremely stringent reliability and latency guarantees [3]. To meet these strict requirements, URLLC traffic is often prioritized in transmission and decoding, which can lead to the interruption or degradation of other services such as eMBB. While this ensures URLLC performance, it results in suboptimal spectrum and energy efficiency. As a result, how to support the coexistence of heterogeneous services with differing latency and reliability constraints has emerged as a critical research challenge.
One natural approach to the coexistence problem is to use non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA) to support multiple heterogeneous services [4], [5]. However, conventional

NOMA based on superposition coding and successive interference cancellation (SIC) decoding does not fully address the heterogeneity in latency and error probability requirements. For example, an URLLC user has to wait for the reception of the whole transmission frame in order to perform interference cancellation, leading to significant reception delay. On the other hand, the performance without SIC can be severally degraded due to interference. To address this issue, [6] introduced a hybrid TDMA-NOMA scheme, where the codeword of the user with a longer blocklength is formed by two independent subcodewords. Specifically, the first codeword is superimposed with the symbols of the latency-constrained user, while the second is transmitted in a separate time slot. This structure enables the user with a better channel condition to perform SIC without receiving the whole transmission frame. However, such a design may not scale efficiently to scenarios involving more than two users. Another line of research focuses utilize early decoding [7] based SIC for NOMA with heterogeneous constraints, which we refer to as heterogeneous NOMA. In essence, early decoding allows the decoding of a long codeword based on its partially received coded symbols. This capability is particularly important in low-latency or heterogeneous service scenarios. [8] first investigated the feasibility of SIC with early decoding for the two-user Gaussian broadcast channel (GBC) under heterogeneous blocklength constraints and derived the secondorder achievable rate region assuming Gaussian signaling. Further, [9] refined the analysis in [8] by using shell codes to derive a larger achievable rate region. Complementary to this, [10] proposed a simple scheme based on discrete signaling and treating interference as noise (TIN) decoding for the K-user GBC with heterogeneous blocklength constraints and error probability requirements.
Despite the above progress, practical code design for heterogeneous NOMA is lacking. In particular, the early decoding in [8], [9] is derived under ideal assumptions such as Gaussian signaling. It is unclear whether such a decoding scheme is realizable with practical channel coding and modulations. Motivated by this gap, this paper proposes a new coding scheme and decoding algorithm that enables efficient early decoding while maintaining practical implementability.
In this work, we design new globally coupled Low-Density Parity-Check (GC-LDPC) codes for heterogeneous NOMA

Its Tanner graph consists of several disjoint local LDPC codes, which are interconnected through a set of additional global check nodes (CNs). The local codes are arranged in a cascaded manner, forming a longer code, which can be represented as a blockdiagonal structure in the parity-check matrix. In conventional GC-LDPC codes, when early decoding occurs, some local codes may not be completely received, rendering the globally connecting part’s check nodes ineffective. As a result, each local code can only be decoded independently, leading to a decline in overall decoding capability. To address this issue, we introduce a partial global connecting framework, where partial global check nodes are established between adjacent local codes. timely and efficient error correction is critical. B. Proposed Early decoding based SIC

In this section, we introduce the proposed SIC early

decoders. Specifically, we consider three decoding methods

under the early decoding scenario. The first method, TIN,

serves as a baseline and assumes that interference can be

modeled as additive noise, thereby simplifying the decoding

process. While this approach reduces complexity, it may lead

to performance degradation in some high-interference scenar-

ios. The second method, early decode hard-decision based

SIC (E-H-SIC), improves upon TIN by decoding and subtract-

ing interfering signals, thereby enhancing overall decoding

accuracy. Finally, we introduce an early decode iterative soft-

decision based SIC (E-S-SIC) method that exchanges extrinsic

information between the decoder for the desired signal and the

decoder for the interference signal, thereby further reducing

the error rate.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
For illustrative purposes, we consider a two-user downlink broadcast channel with |h1|2 = 10 dB and |h2|2 = 0 dB. To demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed coding and decoding schemes, user 1 uses 5G NR LDPC codes [15], whereas user 2 employs either the 5G NR LDPC code or the proposed LDPC code. The code rate for both user 1 and user 2 is set to R = 5/11, based on base matrices of size 12 × 22. All LDPC codes are decoded using the min-sum decoding algorithm, with the maximum number of iterations for a single decoder set to 20. User 1 utilizes different decoding strategies, including TIN, E-H-SIC, and E-S-SIC. When using the proposed GC-LDPC code with E-H-SIC and E-S-SIC, the simulation incorporates the PC matrix mechanism. For the E-S-SIC scheme, the maximum number of outer iterations between the two decoders is set to 6. V. CONCLUSION
In this work, we proposed a novel LDPC-based matrix construction method tailored for early decoding scenarios under heterogeneous block lengths. We also examined common decoding strategies, including TIN and E-H-SIC, and introduced a partial parity-check matrix mechanism specifically designed for E-H-SIC based on our proposed base matrix structure. Furthermore, we explored the application of E-S-SIC decoding in heterogeneous blocklength scenarios, achieving significant performance gains.
