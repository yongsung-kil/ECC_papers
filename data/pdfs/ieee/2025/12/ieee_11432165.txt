Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes from Generalized Behrend Sequences for Space Applications
Suresh Kumar Venkataramanappa, Pavan Kumar, Deepak Mishra and Shayan Srinivasa Garani

Abstract—We present a novel construction of quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with arbitrary column weight and girth at least eight. By carefully designing the parity-check matrix, our approach ensures both structural flexibility and improved error-correction capabilities. We evaluate the performance of these codes under the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed QC-LDPC codes outperform the protograph LDPC codes included in the Consultative Committee for Space Data Systems (CCSDS) telecommand standards. Additionally, we benchmark our proposed codes against existing short-length codes with girth 8, observing improvements in the decoding performance while ensuring suitability for hardware implementation. The findings of this work contribute to the ongoing development of efficient LDPC codes suitable for ultra-reliable low-latency communications and area-efficient architecture designs for the Internet of Things (IoT), machine-to-machine (M2M), and satellite communications.
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes, introduced by Gallager in the 1960s [1], are known for their near-optimal performance [2] along with efficient decoding algorithms. Further, structured LDPC codes are amenable for hardware implementation, making them an integral part of high-performance communication systems [3].
LDPC codes have several subfamilies, and we focus on quasi-cyclic (QC) subfamily of LDPC codes, mindful of hardware implementations. The parity-check matrix of the QC-LDPC codes follows a cyclic structure, enabling highly efficient encoding [4] and decoding structures [3], [5] with the needed parallelism, while maintaining strong error correction capabilities, typical of LDPC codes. Due to their scalability and hardware-friendly properties, QC-LDPC codes have been widely adopted in modern communication standards, including digital video broadcasting (DVB-S2) [6], wireless systems [7] and CCSDS standards [8], as well as in data storage systems [9].
Small satellite constellations, such as CubeSats, are crucial for applications such as Earth observation and global connectivity. In spacecraft emergency, low-latency telecommands face

Their versatility also makes them ideal for highly reliable low-latency communications (URLLC) and area-efficient architectures in IoT and M2M applications. Optimizing these codes for performance and resource constraints is essential to meet the growing demand [11].
It is well-known that the girth of LDPC codes is one of the key factors influencing iterative decoding performance. Short cycles in the Tanner graph invariably introduce message correlations, slowing the convergence, and causing error floors. Increasing the girth to 8 or higher alleviates the aforementioned problems with improved bit error rate (BER)/frame error rate (FER) performance. Recent developments by researchers in girth-8 QC-LDPC code constructions have significantly improved the design of high-performance codes for modern communication systems, particularly those that require short block lengths and efficient code constructions. In pursuit of these goals, investigators have examined diverse construction techniques, incorporating Sidon sequences [12], [13], disjoint difference sets [14], two-direction column-scanning greedy strategy (TD), earliest sequences (ES) using exponent matrices with vertical symmetry [15], primes of the form t2+1 = p [16], and greatest common divisor (GCD)-based techniques [17]. These approaches primarily focus on regular girth-8 QC-LDPC codes with column weights ranging from 3 to 8, ensuring enhanced structural properties and improved error-correcting performance. Based on the above considerations, we focus on hardware-friendly QC-LDPC code designs with girth ≥ 8, suitable for arbitrary column weights.
Our contributions are as follows: First, we generalize Behrend sequences [18] and propose a framework for constructing (dv, dc)-regular LDPC codes with a girth ≥ 8 for arbitrary column weights. Here, dv and dc represent the column weight and row weight, respectively, in the parity-check matrix of the LDPC code. Second, we introduce an algorithmic framework to construct (dv, dc)-regular LDPC codes with girths of 6 or 8. We compare our results against [8], [15], [16]. The proposed designs offer superior performance, with shifts computable on-the-fly, facilitating efficient hardware implementation.

Consequently, to construct a (4, dc)-regular QC-LDPC code with girth greater than 6, we must generate a sequence S that does not satisfy any of the conditions specified in (17). To
achieve this, we propose the following algorithm.
Algorithm 1: This algorithm generates an increasing sequence of dc elements such that its any three elements do not satisfy any of the conditions in (17). It begins with an initial
sequence and iteratively modifies it by checking all possible triplets against the conditions C2, C6 and C7 defined in (17). If a condition is satisfied, the algorithm adjusts the elements
and sorts the sequence after each modification, continuing this
process for a predefined maximum number of iterations. Upon finding a valid sequence that does not satisfy C2, C6 and

C7, it returns the sequence with a valid flag. If, however, the maximum number of iterations is reached without identifying a valid sequence, it returns the final sequence with an invalid status. To reduce search time, the algorithm can start with an initial sequence where no three terms satisfy C2; otherwise, additional time may be needed to generate a suitable sequence. For a given maximum number allowed in sequence (predefined limit), the algorithm may fail to produce a valid sequence. In such cases the maximum number allowed in sequence should be increased before re-running the algorithm. When strict requirements on code rate and code length must be met, i.e., predefined limit cannot be increased, then the algorithm may fail to generate a valid sequence, necessitating a compromise: accepting a lower girth specifically, girth 6 with a reduced number of 6-cycles.
Algorithm 2: This algorithm constructs parity-check matrices for QC-LDPC codes based on sequences generated using Algorithm 1 and selects the matrix with the fewest higherorder cycles (specifically, 6-cycles or 8-cycles). Since multiple sequences are possible for a fixed size p, Algorithm 1 generates different sequences across iterations. In each iteration, M is generated using a sequence from Algorithm 1, according to (15). Algorithm 2 forms a set D of parity-check matrices, and analyzes their cycles of length up to 8 to assess girth properties. After the maximum number of iterations, the matrix in D with the fewest 8-cycles (if girth-6 free) or, otherwise, the fewest 6-cycles is selected and assigned to H.
After describing Algorithms 1 and 2, we are ready to present a search-guided code construction.
Let S = {a1, a2, . . . , an} be a sequence generated by Algorithm 1 and optimized according to Algorithm 2. Utilize this sequence to define the matrix M in (15) and its corresponding parity-check matrix H in (16). Then, a (4, dc)regular QC-LDPC code with girth 6 or 8 can be constructed from M , depending on the outcome of Algorithm 1 and the constraints applied during the construction of S. However, our primary objective is to construct a sequence that strictly avoids satisfying any of the conditions outlined in (17).
Similarly, by selecting the first three rows of the matrix M in (15), a (3, dc)-regular QC-LDPC code with girth greater than six can be constructed by ensuring that only condition C2 is avoided. The corresponding right circulant permutation matrix used in this construction will be of order p ≥ 2an + 1.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
We constructed a family of QC-LDPC codes with girth at least 8 using generalized Behrend sequences along with illustrative examples. Given the design payload parameters, the algorithm generates codes with girth exceeding 6 while minimizing 8 cycles or produce codes with the fewest number of 6 cycles, meeting the design specifications. Compared to CCSDS telecommand standards [8], [21], the short 5G-NR LDPC codes, and recent short length girth-8 codes given in [15], [16], the proposed codes exhibit superior performance

over the AWGN channel. The code structure is hardware-
efficient since the entire model matrix can be derived from its second row (store only dc elements), thereby reducing storage needs, have simpler memory access and routing compared
to other irregular codes. Combining high performance with
low resource demands, the proposed family of codes offers
flexibility catering to various data payloads and coding rates,
amenable for space applications.
