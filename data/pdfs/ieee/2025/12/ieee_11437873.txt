Abstract—In this paper, we propose an efficient noisy gradient descent bit-flipping (NGDBF) algorithm for decoding LDPC codes. The proposed algorithm introduces random noise with uniform distribution on the interval [−1, 1] into the inversion function of the GDBF algorithm. The proposed algorithm avoids the complicated operations in the noise generation of the conventional NGDBF algorithm. In addition, our proposed decoding algorithm does not rely on the SNR estimation and the scaling factor. Simulation results indicate that the proposed algorithm can achieve almost the same performance as the conventional NGDBF algorithm. Moreover, even the using of a linear feedback shift register with very small stage can generate sufficient randomness to reach such performance.
Index Terms—low-density parity-check codes, bit-flipping, gradient descent, noisy gradient descent bit-flipping, uniform distribution, linear feedback shift register
I. INTRODUCTION
Because of their excellent error correction performance, low-density parity-check (LDPC) [1] codes are widely used in modern communication and storage systems, which makes it important to develop efficient decoding algorithms for these codes. In the literature, belief propagation (BP) algorithms and bit-flipping (BF) algorithms are two main categories of approaches for decoding LDPC codes [2], [3]. The BP algorithm can achieve strong error-correction capability at the expense of high decoding complexity, which limits its use in real-time systems. In contrast, the BF algorithm has much lower complexity and is easier to implement. However, its decoding performance is worse than the BP algorithm in general. Therefore, how to improve the performance of BF decoding while maintaining its low complexity merit is a topic that deserves investigation.
The gradient descent bit-flipping (GDBF) algorithm [4] models the bit-flipping process as a nonlinear optimization problem and applies the gradient descent method to solve the problem. Compared with other earlier BF algorithms (e.g., WBF algorithm [5]), the GDBF algorithm achieves better decoding performance while keeping low complexity feature. However, the performance of the GDBF algorithm is still worse than that of the BP algorithm, which is mainly due to the fact that the nonlinear objective function contains multiple local minima. To tackle this problem, several variants of the

GDBF algorithm have been proposed, among which the noisy GDBF (NGDBF) algorithm [6] is one of the most representative. The NGDBF algorithm introduces random noise with Gaussian distribution into the inversion function and optimizes the noise variance according to the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) to improve the decoding performance. To date, several works have designed efficient decoders [7]–[10] based on the NGDBF algorithm. Although the NGDBF algorithm can effectively enhance the error-correction capability, it has certain drawbacks. First, it is difficult to generate Gaussian random numbers in general, especially in the hardware platform [11], [12]. Second, it is intractable to accurately estimate the SNR in practice. Third, the optimized noise variance is dependent on the specific code as well as the SNR [6], which affects the generality of the algorithm.
In order to address these problems, we propose an efficient NGDBF algorithm for decoding LDPC codes in this paper. The proposed method replaces Gaussian noise in the conventional NGDBF decoder by random noise with uniform distribution on the interval [−1, 1]. From the practical point of view, uniform noise can be generated based on a linear feedback shift register (LFSR) [13], which avoids the complicated operations in the generation of Gaussian noise. In addition, our proposed algorithm does not rely on the SNR estimation, making it more suitable for practical applications. Simulation results indicate that the proposed decoding algorithm can achieve almost the same performance as the conventional GDBF decoder that uses Gaussian noise. Furthermore, even an LFSR with very small stage (e.g., 3-5) can generate sufficient randomness to reach such performance. All these indicate that the proposed decoder is very suitable for practical purposes.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the background of the paper. Section III describes the proposed efficient NGDBF algorithm in detail. Section IV presents the simulation results as well as the analysis. Finally, Section V concludes the paper.

III. PROPOSED NGDBF ALGORITHM

As previously mentioned, the NGDBF algorithm proposed

in [6] introduces Gaussian noise into the inversion function to

help the decoder escape from local optima. However, the use

of Gaussian noise brings some practical problems. First, the

generation of Gaussian random numbers inevitably involves

nonlinear operations, which makes it difficult to implement in

hardware platforms. For details, the readers can refer to [11],

[12]. Second, the channel SNR is necessary for the variance

of Gaussian noise in (12). In practice, however, the accurate

estimation of the channel SNR is difficult. Third, as shown

in (12), the calculation of the variance of Gaussian noise also

involves a scaling factor η. In order to achieve satisfactory

performance improvement, this parameter should be adjusted

according to the specific code and the channel SNR. (See

e.g., [6] for details.) This will increase the parameter tuning

effort and affect the generality of the algorithm.

Now let us further analyze the mechanism of the NGDBF decoder. By (12), the variance of the noise qk is η2N0/2. Based on the results reported in [6], we conclude that η2N0/2
is a small value less than one under most SNR values.

Consider the random LDPC code PEGReg504×1008 in the

database [14] as an example. At SNR = 3dB, the variance

of the channel noise is about 0.5012, and the optimized

scaling factor η reported in [6] is about 0.7. Therefore, the

variance of qk is about 0.25. Figure 1 illustrates the cumulative distribution function (CDF) curves of Gaussian noises with

small variances. We can see from the figure that the curve

[−1, 1]. This observation motivates us to replace Gaussian

The proposed decoder has several advantages over the original NGDBF decoder. Our NGDBF decoder avoids the

estimation of the channel SNR as well as the parameter tuning effort. Hence, our NGDBF decoder has better generality than the original NGDBF decoder. From the implementation point of view, generating uniform random number uk on the interval [−1, 1] is simple and efficient. In fact, it can be obtained by transforming a uniform random number dk on the interval [0, 1] through the linear mapping uk = 2dk − 1. This avoids the nonlinear operations involved in the generation of Gaussian noise. Moreover, the random number dk can be easily generated using a maximum length linear feedback shift register (LFSR) sequence.

The rows in the parity-check matrix of C1 are linearly independent, whereas the parity-check matrix of C2 contains three redundant rows.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have proposed an efficient NGDBF decoder for LDPC codes. Unlike the conventional NGDBF decoders, the proposed NGDBF decoder uses uniform noise as the perturbation noise. Moreover, the proposed method avoids the SNR estimation and the use of scaling factor. Simulation results suggest that the performance of the proposed decoder is comparable with the conventional NGDBF decoder. Furthermore, even an LFSR with very small stage can generate uniform noise to achieve such performance. Therefore, the proposed decoder is suitable for low cost implementations.
