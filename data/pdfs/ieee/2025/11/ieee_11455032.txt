AbstractвҖ”Low-bitrate voice and telemetry systems require 
short LDPC codes with low decoding complexity. This paper 
presents ALEMS, an Adaptive Layered Enhanced Min-Sum 
framework for short codes operating under low SNR and bursty 
conditions. ALEMS combines layered scheduling for fast 
convergence, an improved Min-Sum core with normalized or 
offset correction, and adaptive control with an early-stopping 
policy. These techniques retain the simplicity of Min-Sum 
decoding while reducing message saturation and trapping effects 
common in short codes. Simulations using rate-1/2 short LDPC 
codes over AWGN and GilbertвҖ“Elliott channels show consistent 
BER and FER gains over normalized Min-Sum and sum-product 
algorithms, together with fewer iterations and lower stall 
probability. Under bursty conditions, modest interleaving further 
enhances robustness without added cost. Fully compatible with 
QC-LDPC structures and fixed-point realizations, ALEMS offers 
a balanced solution between theoretical reliability and hardware 
efficiency for short-block, low-rate applications such as digital 
voice and narrowband modems. 
KeywordsвҖ”LDPC, Min-Sum decoding, layered scheduling, 
NMS/OMS, early stopping, short blocklength, low-bit rate modem 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are widely 
recognized for their near-capacity performance and broad use in 
modern communication systems. However, most existing 
implementations are optimized for long blocklengths and 
moderate-to-high SNR conditions, where computational 
complexity can be amortized. In contrast, emerging scenarios 
such as digital voice transmission, embedded telemetry, and 
low-power wireless networks require short-block codes that 
operate reliably under strict power and latency constraints. 
These conditions often involve low SNR operation and burst-
error patterns, under which conventional decoders encounter 
significant limitations. 
A representative case is short-packet voice communication 
over HF/VHF links. Such systems face three key challenges: (1) 
short LDPC blocklengths, (2) operation near or below 2 dB SNR, 
and (3) frequent burst errors. In this regime, the sum-product 
algorithm (SPA) achieves high reliability but with prohibitive 
complexity, while the normalized Min-Sum (NMS) algorithm 
offers efficiency at the cost of robustness. The resulting 
performanceвҖ“complexity gap motivates the search for decoding 
methods that remain reliable under short, noisy, and burst-prone 
conditions. 
Recent research has explored several approaches to narrow 
this gap. Variants of the Min-Sum algorithm, such as normalized 
and offset Min-Sum [1], reduce computational load but require 
precise parameter tuning to avoid error floors. Layered decoding 
strategies [2] accelerate convergence, especially for short codes, 
though their integration with adaptive control remains limited. 
More recently, neural-aided and graph-neural decoders [3] have 
achieved notable gains, yet their hardware and energy demands 
exceed the budgets of embedded or low-power systems. 
Collectively, these studies reveal a persistent need for decoder 
architectures 
that 
achieve 
SPA-level 
reliability 
while 
maintaining Min-Sum-level simplicityвҖ”particularly in short-
packet, low-SNR applications. 
To address these challenges, we propose the Adaptive 
Layered Enhanced Min-Sum (ALEMS) framework. ALEMS 
combines layered scheduling, enhanced Min-Sum updates, and 
adaptive parameter control with a reliable early-stopping policy. 
Together, these techniques accelerate convergence, mitigate 
error floors, and dynamically adapt to channel variations while 
preserving low arithmetic complexity.

1) We introduce ALEMS, a unified decoding framework

codes. 
2) We evaluate ALEMS on both AWGN and GilbertвҖ“Elliott 
channels, comparing its performance with SPA and NMS in 
terms of BER/FER, iteration count, and normalized complexity. 
3) We demonstrate its engineering feasibility through 
compatibility with QC-LDPC structures and fixed-point

D. Design Requirements for Short-Blocklength Applications 
Short LDPC codes face distinct challenges including 
trapping sets due to limited graph expansion, rapid saturation in 
low SNR regimes, sensitivity to burst errors, and stringent 
latency/power constraints. These requirements drive the 
adoption of layered scheduling, adaptive parameter control, and 
reliable early termination вҖ“ forming the foundation of the 
ALEMS framework presented in Section III.

V. CONCLUSION 
The proposed Adaptive Layered Enhanced Min-Sum 
(ALEMS) decoder achieves a strong balance between reliability 
and complexity for short-block LDPC codes in low-SNR and 
burst-prone environments. Simulations show that ALEMS 
remains within 0.1 dB of SPA performance while reducing 
average iterations by 60вҖ“75% and normalized complexity to 
about 30% of SPA. Under burst conditions, modest interleaving 
further enhances robustness. Beyond numerical gains, ALEMS 
is lightweight and hardware-friendly. Its layered structure, 
adaptive control, and enhanced node updates are compatible 
with QC-LDPC architectures and fixed-point implementations, 
making it practical for embedded and low-power systems. 
Future work will extend ALEMS to longer codes and explore 
parameter tuning for time-varying channels.
