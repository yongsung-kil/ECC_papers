Abstract—The rapid development of 5G communications technology has triggered higher demands for efficient error correction techniques to meet the needs of ultra-reliable, low-latency, and high-throughput data transmission. Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC-LDPC) codes have been chosen as the 5G New Radio (NR) standard because they can overcome conventional error correction techniques. These codes are crucial to enable enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB) and Ultra-Reliable Low Latency Communication (URLLC) applications by providing strong data integrity with low processing requirements. Nevertheless, hardware implementation of QC-LDPC encoding is extremely challenging because of its high computational complexity and need for adaptability to enable a wide range of code rates and block sizes. This paper is focused on designing and implementing a low-latency, high-throughput QC-LDPC encoder based on FPGA technology that overcomes these issues through optimized algorithms and architectural advancements.
The approach encompasses the study of 5G NR encoding needs, the development of an extremely parallel QC-LDPC encoding algorithm, and implementation on a programmable hardware platform. The encoder features pipeline optimization and parallel cyclic shift networks for enhancing efficiency. The design is synthesized in Verilog HDL and implemented on Nexys 4 DDR FPGA board to support various base graph configurations and lift sizes needed by 5G NR requirements. FPGA testing shows significant improvements in encoding latency, throughput, and resource utilization. With the realization of a fully functional and adaptive encoding chain, this research offers a high-performance and scalable solution for 5G applications and facilitates the development of next-generation wireless communication systems.
Index Terms—QC-LDPC, FPGA, 5G NR, CRC, Encoder, Vivado
I. INTRODUCTION
Error correction coding is of prime significance in modern
digital communication systems since it ensures the reliable
transmission of data over channels that are inherently noisy.
With the speedy development of wireless technology, there is
increasingly high demand for high-speed, low-latency, ultra-
reliable communication. In this context, LDPC codes have
also received particular attention due to their outstanding error
correction capability and relatively low decoding complexity.

One special sub-type, QC-LDPC codes, also enjoys structural advantages that are hardware-friendly, thus becoming even more appropriate for modern communication standards. In today’s wireless era, 5G New Radio (NR) has played the role of a central enabling technology, supporting enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) and ultra-reliable low latency communication (URLLC). The 5G NR standard adopted QC-LDPC codes as the recommended forward error correction technique for data channels. However, hardware design for QC-LDPC encoders is extremely challenging with varied code rates, lifting sizes, and computational complexities. Existing encoder implementations usually make trade-offs among scalability, performance, and hardware efficiency.
Through the design of a high-speed, flexible, and lowlatency QC-LDPC encoder that strictly follows 5G NR standards. The objective is to enable real-time wireless applications such as the Internet of Things (IoT), smart cities, and multimedia streaming, which need both speed and flexibility. An FPGA-based solution also has the advantage of reconfigurability, which allows for rapid prototyping and testing of different configurations.
Environmental sustainability of the work was ensured by adhering to energy-efficient hardware design principles, which are designed to keep power usage low while the encoder is in operation. In addition, the utilization of FPGA-based prototypes also minimizes electronic trash because it facilitates multiple usage and iterations using a single hardware platform.
This work introduces a novel method of simulation and synthesis of the proposed QC-LDPC encoder structure in the designed architecture using System Verilog in the Xilinx Vivado development software. The structure contains parameterized hardware modules and parallel cyclic shift networks, thus improving the level of throughput and utilization of resources. Compared to conventional fixed architecture, the designed encoder supports all forms of 5G NR base graph and lifting sizes, thus offering flexibility at the cost of hardware utilization and speed.

II. LITERATURE REVIEW
A. Recent developments
The rapid progress in wireless communications has spawned the 5G New Radio (NR) standard, which is targeted at ultrareliable low-latency communication (URLLC) and improved mobile broadband (eMBB) applications, both of which demand efficient and scalable error correction techniques. LowDensity Parity-Check (LDPC) codes have become the best candidate for 5G NR because of their proximity to the Shannon bound, design flexibility, and decoding robustness.
Quasi-Cyclic Low-Density Parity-Check (QC-LDPC) codes, in particular, are used in 5G New Radio (NR) data channels because they offer structured parity-check matrices, for efficient hardware implementation. Various encoder structures have been proposed in the last few years with the aim of reducing the encoding delay, resource minimization, and throughput enhancement. For example:
Tian et al. (2021) proposed a multi-channel parallel encoder that reduces latency by processing parity bits concurrently with cyclic shift mechanisms. Nguyen et al. (2019) used a hardware-efficient encoder that reduces memory as well as gate usage to a minimum by storing the permutation matrix optimally. Other efforts have been directed towards integrating Cyclic Redundancy Check (CRC) encoding into the LowDensity Parity-Check (LDPC) encoding paradigm in a bid to raise data reliability.
B. Theorectical Basis
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes were initially suggested by Gallager during the 1960s and were reemphasized in the 1990s due to their very potential in iterative decoding methods. LDPC codes rely on a sparse parity-check matrix (PCM) and thus are well suited for scalable and parallel decoding methods. QC-LDPC codes form a particular subclass of LDPC codes in which the PCM is derived based on circulant permutation matrices, thereby making it simpler to design encoder hardware based on shift registers. 5G NR uses the 3GPP standard (TS 38.212), which specifies two Base Graphs (BG1 and BG2) to support varying payload sizes and code rates. Encoding involves: The generator matrix is obtained

from the underlying graph. Computation of the parity bits through matrix-vector operations. Often times, inserting a CRC for error detection. Richardson-Urbanke (RU) lowertriangular transformation method was highly prevalent in older hardware implementations but is associated with higher storage complexity and non-scalability. Modern methods now utilize cyclic shift networks and matrix partitioning that encourages pipelining to utilize hardware resources to the best extent and obtain maximum throughput.
