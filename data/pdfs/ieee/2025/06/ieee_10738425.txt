Low-Complexity Neural Belief Propagation Decoding Algorithm Based on Tensor Ring Decomposition
Yuanhui Liang , Member, IEEE, Chan-Tong Lam , Senior Member, IEEE, Qingle Wu , Benjamin K. Ng , Senior Member, IEEE, and Sio Kei Im , Member, IEEE

Abstract—Neural belief propagation (NBP) decoding can improve the performance of belief propagation (BP) decoding for high-density parity check (HDPC) codes, at the expense of higher memory storage requirement and computational complexity due to the addition of trainable weight coefficients. To reduce the high storage requirement of NBP, the cyclically equivariant neural BP (CENBP) algorithm makes full use of the cyclically invariant property of the cyclic code, optimizes and reuses the weight coefficients of the NBP algorithm, at the expense of further increasing the computational complexity of NBP. In this paper, we propose low-complexity, in terms of both memory storage requirement and computational complexity, NBP and CENBP decoding algorithms based on Tensor Ring (TR) decomposition. First, in order to reduce the memory storage and computational complexity of the NBP algorithm, we propose a TR-based compression algorithm to compress the messages and mathematical calculations in the NBP decoding algorithm, called TR-NBP algorithm. Second, to address the high computational complexity of the CENBP algorithm, we propose to apply TR decomposition-based compression to the odd layers of the CENBP decoding algorithm, called TR-CENBP, to reduce the computational complexity, and further reduce the required memory storage requirement of the CENBP algorithm. Furthermore, we use TR decomposition-based compression to simplify the mathematical computations associated with the tanh function in the NBP algorithm to further reduce the complexity of the hardware implementation. Experimental results show that direct compression of BP algorithm using TR decomposition results in significant performance degradation and our proposed low complexity TR-NBP algorithm and TR-CENBP algorithm can greatly reduce both the memory storage requirement and computation complexity, without significant performance degradation for typical BCH and LDPC codes.
Index Terms—Neural belief propagation decoding, tensor ring decomposition, high-density parity check code.

I. INTRODUCTION
D EEP learning has been applied to the belief propagation (BP) decoding of high-density parity check (HDPC) codes, called neural belief propagation (NBP) decoding, which outperforms traditional BP algorithm by about 1 dB [1], [2], [3]. However, compared with the traditional BP algorithm, NBP decoding algorithm increases the computational complexity and the required storage space. To lower the complexity, the authors in [4] and [5] introduced the pruning algorithm which combines with the expert knowledge of BP decoding, and proposed a pruning-based NBP algorithm, which reduces the complexity of the BP decoding algorithm. In [6], the authors proposed a weight sharing method for learning BP decoding algorithms, which can be divided into two types: temporal weight sharing and spatial weight sharing. The authors in [7] applied weight sharing to the decoding algorithm of long LDPC code and optimized the parameters according to the degree of nodes, and proposed a neural 2-dimensional normalized decoder. The authors in [8] proposed a parameter sharing mechanism for Protograph-LDPC, which reduces the training complexity and storage cost. However, the approaches to lower the complexity of the NBP algorithm proposed in [6], [7], [8] mainly aimed at lowering the complexity of the added learning weights, and the degree of complexity reduction is limited. The computational complexity of the compressed NBP algorithm is still higher than that of the BP algorithm, which is difficult to implement on communication hardware with low power consumption requirements.
Based on the NBP algorithm, the authors in [9] proposed a cyclically equivariant NBP (CENBP) decoding algorithm for cyclic codes, which makes full use of the cyclically invariant property of cyclic codes. Although the memory requirement of the CENBP algorithm is much smaller than that of the NBP algorithm, the time complexity is greater than that of the NBP algorithm. The main factor affecting the time complexity is the number of multiplications and additions required by the decoding algorithm. Therefore, reducing the number of multiplications and additions in the decoding process of the CENBP algorithm is currently the main issue to be solved. However, compared with the NBP algorithm widely used in the field of channel decoding, the CENBP algorithm is only suitable for cyclic codes, so it is necessary to find a more general NBP algorithm with both low memory requirements and low computational

In the 5th generation (5G) communication system, Massive Machine Type Communication (mMTC) is used to support machine-type communication for the future Internet of Things services [10], [11], and ultra-low power consumption is needed to extend the life of the machines. In the other hand, Ultra Reliable Low Latency Communication (URLLC) requires ultra-low latency, where the end-to-end (E2E) delay requirements for 5G and beyond 5G are 1ms and 0.1ms, respectively [12], [13]. In addition, very high energy efficiency (1pJ/bit) is required to reduce the energy consumption of the overall network. Due to its high computational complexity, the BP algorithm is often replaced by the simplified algorithms such as Min-Sum during deployment, which leads to a decrease in decoding performance. Compared with the BP decoding algorithm, the NBP decoding algorithm has higher computational and storage complexity, which makes it difficult to meet the requirements of URLLC and mMTC. The above two problems involving the complexity of channel neural decoding are the main motivations for our research in this paper.
Tensor train (TT) and tensor ring (TR) decomposition have been proposed in recent years [14], [15] and are widely used in model compression due to their ability to overcome the curse of dimensionality [16], [17], [18]. At present, the compression algorithms based on TT and TR decomposition are used to lower the complexity of the intelligent communication system [19], [20], [21], [22], [23], [24]. Compared with other compression methods in intelligent communication, the compression method based on tensor decomposition has better interpretability and rigorous mathematical principles [16], [20]. Low-rank tensor decomposition can be used to search for a series of factor tensors under a preset error using a mathematical optimization algorithm. The complexity of the compressed factor tensor can be measured by mathematical theory. The approximation error caused by the decomposition can also be measured using the Frobenius norm. The implementation steps of compression are to first tensorizing the learning weight matrix and convert it into a high-order tensor. Then tensor decomposition is performed on the obtained high-order tensors, and the factor tensors with lower order obtained after decomposition is used as new learning weights, thus reducing the computational complexity and memory storage. However, to the best of our knowledge, TT and TR decompositions have not been applied to NBP decoding and CENBP decoding.
In this paper, we propose a TR based approach to lower the time complexity and storage complexity of the NBP and CENBP algorithms. First, in order to reduce the memory storage and time complexity of the NBP algorithm, we propose to reduce the number of messages and mathematical calculations in the NBP decoding algorithm. Different from only compressing the learning weights in [6], [7], [8], [9], we also compress the messages transmitted between the check nodes (CNs) and the variable nodes (VNs) during decoding [6], [7], [8], [9]. Different from the NNMS+ algorithm proposed in [20], which only needs to compress the learning weights and messages [20], [24], the NBP algorithm also requires highly complex mathematical calculation, such as the implementation

of tanh function, making it difficult to implement in resourceconstrained communication hardware. The tanh function, the cascaded multiplication of the tanh functions and the tanh−1 function in the NBP algorithm require particularly high computational complexity and memory storage resources when implemented in hardware. Specifically, we apply TR decompression on the variable of the approximated function of the tanh function to reduce their computational complexity and required storage resources. Furthermore, we also propose a joint-way compression for tanh-related mathematical computations in the NBP algorithm, by combining the TR-based compression with the conventional compression methods for the tanh functions.
To address the high time complexity of the CENBP algorithm [9], we propose to apply TR decomposition-based compression to the odd layers of the CENBP decoding to reduce the time complexity, and at the same time further reduces the required memory storage resources. Compression based on TR decomposition for CENBP algorithm can be considered as a joint-way compression for the NBP algorithm. The learning weights are first compressed using the shift-invariant property of cyclic codes, followed by the TR decompositionbased compression is performed on the messages in odd layers. The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
• We propose to use TR decomposition to reduce the memory storage requirements and computational complexity of the NBP algorithms (called TR-NBP algorithms) without significantly degrading bit error rate (BER) and frame error rate (FER) performance. Specifically, the learning weights and message matrices in the NBP algorithm are decomposed into the product of small-scale factor tensors by TR decomposition, which greatly reduces the number of multiplications between the original matrices. In addition, the number of operations related to the tanh function in the NBP algorithm is also reduced after the cascade multiplication related to the tanh function is compressed by TR decomposition. Moreover, we use TR decomposition to reduce the complexity of tanh function calculation of the proposed TR-NBP algorithm by exploiting higher-order function approximation to implement the tanh function.
• We propose to use TR decomposition to reduce the number of messages in the odd layers of the CENBP algorithm, called the TR-CENBP algorithm, which greatly reduces the time complexity and further reduces the required memory storage. Specifically, the message matrix in the odd layers of the CENBP algorithm is decomposed into the product of small-scale factor tensors by TR decomposition, which reduces the number of messages and also reduces the number of multiplications between the message matrix and the learning weight matrix.
• We show from experimental results that direct compression of the BP algorithm during the CN update process using TR decomposition results in significant performance degradation. This also shows that the learning weights play an important role in maintaining the

A. TR-Based Compression for NBP (TR-NBP)
In the NBP decoding algorithm, the excessive number of learning weights and messages requires a lot of storage resources. In addition, a large number of multiplication calculations are required during the CN update process, which leads to high computational complexity. The complex mathematical calculations in the CN update equation during the iterative decoding process lead to excessive computational complexity of the algorithm. According to different compression objects, the NBP decoding algorithms based on TR decomposition can be divided into four categories: 1. TRw-NBP - TR decomposition for the learning weight matrix only; 2. TRm-NBP TR decomposition for the message matrix only; 3. TRwmNBP - TR decomposition on both the learning weight and the message matrix; 4. TRmc-NBP - TR decomposition for mathematical operation on the approximated tanh functions.
1) TRw-NBP Algorithm: Based on the BP algorithm, the trainable learning weights are added to the NBP algorithm, resulting in a large number of multiplication operations. This is also the focus of the current compression method for NBP decoding, which reduces the complexity by reducing the number of learning weights [6], [7], [8], [9]. We use TR decomposition to reduce the number of learned weights, which reduces time complexity and required memory storage. IV. COMPLEXITY REDUCTION USING JOINT-WAY COMPRESSION
A. TR-Based Compression for CENBP (TR-CENBP)
The authors in [9] proposed a cyclically equivariant NBP (CENBP) decoder for cyclic codes, which improves the performance of NBP decoding with smaller memory requirement. Therefore, CENBP decoding can be regarded as an advanced version of NBP decoding, but it is only suitable for cyclic codes. However, the number of multiplications and additions in CENBP is more than that in NBP, resulting in a higher time complexity than NBP. The compression based on TR decomposition can reduce the additions and multiplications in neural decoding, which reduces the time complexity of neural decoding. Different from NBP decoding, the learned

weights of CENBP decoding are multiplexed in odd layers according to the shift-invariant structure. Therefore we can no longer perform TR decomposition on the learned weights to reduce memory requirements. Because this will destroy the multiplexing structure of the learning weights of CENBP decoding. In view of the limitation of learned weights in odd layers, we propose to perform TR decomposition-based compression on messages in odd layers while preserving the reuse of learned weights. After compression, the number of messages is greatly reduced, thereby reducing the number of additions and multiplications in the odd layer, which reduces the time complexity of the CENBP decoding.

VII. CONCLUSION
In order to reduce both the memory storage and computational complexity of the NBP algorithms, including the CENBP algorithm, we have proposed low-complexity NBP and CENBP decoding algorithms based on TR decomposition, called TR-NBP and TR-CENBP algorithm. The TR-NBP algorithm compresses the messages and mathematical calculations associated with the tanh function in the NBP decoding algorithm. We have also proposed to apply TR decompositionbased compression to the odd layers of the CENBP decoding algorithm to reduce the computational complexity, and further reduce the required memory storage requirement of the CENBP algorithm. By applying the TR decomposition-based compression to the mathematical computations associated with the tanh function in the NBP algorithm, the complexity of the hardware implementation can be further reduced. It was also shown that, experimentally, direct compression of BP algorithm using TR decomposition results in significant performance degradation. Experimental results have shown
