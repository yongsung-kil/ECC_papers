A 4.86-pJ/b Energy-Efficient Fully Parallel Stochastic LDPC Decoder With Two-Stage Shared Memory
Yakun Zhou , Jienan Chen , Yizhuo Zhou, Zihan Xia , Chuan Zhang , and Runsheng Wang

Abstract— The complex calculations of the low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoder result in significant energy and hardware consumption. To solve the challenge, this brief describes a fully parallel stochastic LDPC decoder with a two-stage shared memory (TSM) variable node (VN). To enhance cost efficiency, our design incorporates a shared low-cost random number generator (RNG) for all 2160 channels. We introduce a TSM VN function, which demonstrates faster convergence and reduced hardware overhead in comparison with the existing methods. We have taped out the (2160, 1760) stochastic LDPC decoder in the 55-nm process. The measure results exhibit that the proposed design achieves a throughput of 57.6 Gb/s, an efficiency of 33.68 Gb/s/mm2, and a power efficiency of 4.86 pJ/bit, underlining superior performance in terms of decoding throughput, hardware efficiency, and energy conservation.
Index Terms— Fully parallel, low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoder, shared memory, stochastic computing (SC).
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have gained significant attention for their outstanding error correction performance [1], [2]. Because of their ability to support high data rates, LDPC codes have been chosen as the channel decoding standard for 5G new radio (NR) [3].
The ever-increasing demand for higher data transmission rates presents significant challenges for current LDPC decoding methods to achieve both high throughput and high energy efficiency [4]. One common approach to boost throughput is to increase the clock frequency. However, this requires adding pipeline stages to the decoding hardware [5], which can lead to complications, such as stalling cycles. Another approach is to increase parallelism [6], but highly parallel hardware significantly increases the need for hardware resources and power consumption [7], which is especially problematic in mobile communications [8]. Recent research, such as the study in [9], introduced a five-stage deep pipeline aimed at shortening the critical path, though it significantly increases flipflop usage. Another notable contribution is the time-domain-based minimum value and index generator described in [10], which offers reduced latency and a more compact design. Stochastic computing (SC) [12] is increasingly recognized as a promising technology for efficient hardware designs [13], [14], [15]. Stochastic number generators generate random bitstreams to encode numbers into sequences of “1”s and “0”s. In a random bitstream, each bit has the same weight, which differs from the two’s complement system (TCS). Simple stochastic logics perform basic bitwise operations. The operation results are sent to probability estimator units by converting the number to TCS. Fig. 1(a) compares the stochastic multipliers with the conventional counterpart. Here, binary information is transformed into stochastic binary streams through random integer comparisons, enabling complex arithmetic operations to be executed with basic logic gates. Such characteristics render SC particularly suitable for probability-centric tasks, exemplified by parity check and nodes in LDPC decoding, such as variable nodes (VNs) and check nodes (CNs), as delineated in Fig. 1(b) and (c). A significant challenge in stochastic LDPC decoding is the latch issue associated with VN, where the existence of cycles in the code graph correlates with the stochastic streams stuck into fixed states for several decoding clocks. While numerous studies have tackled this challenge [13], [14], achieving convergence for the VN function often proves time-intensive and resource-heavy. For instance, Tehrani et al. [14] introduce a tracking forecast memory (TFM), realized via a 7-bit up/down saturation counter, bolstering data throughput in the stochastic LDPC decoder. However, we contend that current LDPC decoder designs offer sufficient room for enhancement. In the 5G LDPC codes explored in this brief, a large number of VNs have low degrees, necessitating specific optimizations for efficient hardware implementation.
This brief presents a fully parallel stochastic LDPC decoder featuring a two-stage shared memory (TSM) VN. Initially, we factorize the grouped VNs function using an approximated shared probability and updating probability function. Subsequently, we decompose the stochastic VN function into two stages: the shared internal memories (IMs) and stochastic counters (SCNTs). IV. STRUCTURE OF FULLY PARALLEL STOCHASTIC LDPC DECODER
In conventional designs, each binary-to-stochastic (B2S) converter operates with its unique random number generator (RNG), leading to considerable B2S conversion expenses. In this section, we introduce the structure of the stochastic LDPC decoder. Then, an entire iterative decoding dataflow is exhibited.
To enhance cost efficiency, our design incorporates a shared lowcost RNG for all 2160 channels. This strategy largely curtails the overhead associated with bitstream generation while still preserving decoding performance. Fig. 4 illustrates the stochastic LDPC decoder architecture. The input buffer stores initial binary channel information. The stochastic bitstream generator module uses only one channel data-specific random number linear feedback shift register (LFSR). The stochastic iterative decoding module consists of a stochastic VN cluster, a stochastic CN cluster, and a V2C/C2V routing network. The stochastic VN cluster includes 240 stochastic VNs with 2◦, 80 stochastic VNs with 4◦, 80 stochastic VNs with 1◦, 80 stochastic VNs with 5◦, and 1680 stochastic VNs with 3◦. The stochastic CN cluster consists of 80 stochastic 3◦ CNs and 320 stochastic 1◦ CNs. The bit-flipping postprocessing module consists of 2160 bit-flipping units and 400 check-sum units. The codeword checking module checks the bit-flipping decoded codeword and SC-decoded codeword. The output buffer will receive the final codewords.
Parallel B2S converters convert the 2160 7-bit channel data into 2160 stochastic bitstreams. These stochastic bitstreams are originated from parallel B2S converters using the LLR to represent channel data. The B2S converter sends the stochastic channel information sequences to a stochastic iterative decoding module. We then feed this bitstream to the VN computation and CN computation modules for probabilistic confidence propagation. During random decoding, the VNs share a VN-specific random number, LFSR. V. EXPERIMENTAL PERFORMANCE
A. Performance Analysis
To demonstrate the decoding performance of HS decoding, we apply it to decode an (2160, 1760) LDPC code from the 5G NR standard. The word length of fixed-point min-sum LDPC is 6 bit. The maximum number of decoding iterations is set to 100, and the decoding process will be stopped as soon as the right code is found or the process exceeds the maximum decoding iteration.
We modeled the SNR (signal-to-noise rate) and BER (bit error rate) of floating-point SPA LDPC, fixed-point min-sum LDPC, floatingpoint offset min-sum LDPC, and our own design, as seen in Fig. 6. The BER of the proposed stochastic LDPC achieves 10−6 when SNR is 4.63 dB. The SPA algorithm has excellent performance with high-complexity decoding for LDPC codes. The BER of the floatingpoint SPA LDPC decoder achieves 10−6 at 4.6 dB. The min-sum algorithm is a simplified method that can greatly reduce decoding complexity compared with SPA, but it may introduce substantial performance degradation in terms of BER. VI. CONCLUSION
This brief demonstrates a fully parallel stochastic LDPC decoder (2160, 1760) with a TSM VN. When benchmarked against prevailing models, the proposed design realizes a throughput of 57.6 Gb/s, an efficiency of 33.68 Gb/s/mm2, and a power efficiency of 4.86 pJ/bit, underlining superior performance across decoding throughput, hardware efficiency, and energy conservation. Our design reduces the hardware implementation and speeds up the convergence.
