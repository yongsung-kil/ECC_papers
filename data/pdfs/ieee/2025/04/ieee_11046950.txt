Integrated high-performance LDPC decoder for Continuous-Variable Quantum Key Distribution System

1. Introduction

Based on the principle of quantum mechanics, quantum key distribution (QKD) can share unconditional security keys between two communication parties under insecure channels [1]. Because of its compatibility with optical communication networks, continuous-variable quantum key distribution (CV-QKD) has attracted much attention and the secure key rate (SKR) of CV-QKD has been greatly improved by robustly increasing the repetition rate of modulation and detection of coherent states and more than hundreds of Mbps SKRs have been demonstrated [2,3]. However, the reported high-speed SKRs are only obtained by theoretical estimation or off-line post-processing, where the throughput of post-processing, especially the throughput of error correction is currently the major bottlenecks to realize practical high-performance CV-QKD. Realizing an integrated decoder with high decoding throughput, high reconciliation efficiency and low frame errors rate (FER) is strongly required for practical high-performance CVQKD.
Field programmable gate arrays (FPGA) is a kind of cost-effective large-scale programmable integrated devices with small volume, which is currently the most favorite choice for integrated high-performance post-processing of CV-QKD. Besides, well designed low density parity-check (LDPC) codes with low code rate and long code length is one of the most promising scheme for CV-QKD to realize high reconciliation efficiency [4]. Therefore, FPGA-based LDPC decoders for CV-QKD are studied very recently [5,6], where the currently reported error-correction throughput is very limited to maintain low FER. It is a great challenge to realize integrated error-correction with high-throughput and low FER based on FPGA. On the one side, the on-chip hardware resource of FPGA is very limited, and a finite width (precision) of the fixed-point number is required to process raw data for a high-speed decoder rather than floating-point number, which limits the decoding throughput. On the other hand, the signal to noise ratio (SNR) for a CV-QKD under a typical transmission distance is much less than 0 dB, so the decoding performance is strongly associated with the decoding accuracy. However, the limited precision decreases the decoding accuracy, leading to the existence of residual bit errors and high FER after decoding.
In this work, fixed-point number with limited precision is adopted to design the decoder to achieve higher decoding throughput. Besides, a residual bit errors correction method is used to erase the residual bit errors. Implementation results show that the decoding throughput of the integrated decoder can achieve 1.59 Gbps and 1.17 Gps for typical rate 0.2 and 0.1 LDPC codes and the FER < 0.1, which can support 95.55 Mbps and 16.84 Mbps realtime SKR under typical transmission distances of 25 km and 50 km, correspondingly. The implemented decoder in this work paves the way for large-scale deployment of high-rate integrated CV-QKD systems in metropolitan quantum secure network

In the design of the decoder, module sub_decoder implements the function of the Layered BP decoding algorithm and module error_bits_erase implements the function of the residual bit errors correction method. To make full use of hardware resources and get higher decoding throughput, nine sub_decoder modules and three error_bits_erase modules are designed. The architecture of the integrated decoder is shown in Fig. 1. Because of the running time of iterative decoding is longer than that of erasing residual bit errors, three sub_decoder modules are connected to one error_bits_erase module with a pipeline structure to save hardware resources.

4. Conclusion
An integrated LDPC decoder with limited precision and high-performance for CV-QKD has been implemented in this work. The implementation results show that the decoding throughput can achieve 1.59 Gbps and 1.17 Gps for typical rates 0.2 and 0.1 LDPC codes and FER < 0.1, which can support 95.55 Mbps and 16.84 Mbps real-time SKR under typical transmission distances of 25 km and 50 km, correspondingly. The integrated decoder paves the way for largescale deployment of high-rate integrated CV-QKD systems in metropolitan quantum secure network
