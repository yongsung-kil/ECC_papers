Asynchronization-Aided Ultra-Reliable Receiver
for SCMA in Satellite-Terrestrial Communication
Chunjie Li1,2, Ke Zhang∗,2, Ye Wang2, Jian Jiao2,3, Xiao Ma1 and Qinyu Zhang2,3 1School of Computer Science and Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou 510006, China
2Pengcheng Laboratory, Shenzhen 518055, China 3Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen), Shenzhen 518055, China {lichj, zhangk, wangy02}@pcl.ac.cn, {jiaojian, zqy}@hit.edu.cn, maxiao@mail.sysu.edu.cn

Abstract—This paper proposes an asynchronization-aided iterative detection and decoding (AIDD) scheme for sparse code multiple access (SCMA) in satellite-terrestrial communication scenario, where the messages between the detector and decoder are iteratively exchanged with an additional degreesof-freedom (DoF) in terms of delay. We first propose a parallel expectation propagation algorithm (P-EPA) for asynchronous multiuser detection, where a new initialization method is introduced by efficiently utilizing the prior information to enhance the performance of the detector. Furthermore, a universal soft-output decoder (S-OLD) is proposed based on the ordered likelihood decoder (OLD), which can generate the soft information with high reliability and serve as the input of P-EPA in the proposed AIDD. The iteration between the P-EPA and S-OLD is terminated when the maximum iteration number of the outer loop is achieved or the decoding results of all the users are converged. Simulation results show that the proposed P-EPA has better performance and lower latency compared to its counterparts, and the proposed AIDD also has better performance and fewer iterations than the synchronous IDD and joint detection and decoding (JDD) schemes.
Index Terms—Sparse code multiple access (SCMA), asynchronous, expectation propagation algorithm, ordered likelihood decoder (OLD).
I. INTRODUCTION
Sparse code multiple access (SCMA) [1] is one of the powerful candidates of multiple access techniques for satellite-terrestrial communication [2] to enhance its spectral efficiency and improve user capacity. In satellite-terrestrial communication, due to oscillator variations and differences in propagation time caused by different locations of the users, signals transmitted by the non-orthogonal users may not arrive at the satellite at the same time, which results in asynchronous reception of the signals [3]. The results in [4] show that asynchronous transmission enlarges the capacityregion of multiple access channels and can achieve better performance compared to synchronous transmission.

However, most of the existing work on SCMA detection and decoding is based on the assumption that all users are perfectly synchronized at the receiver. For example, the classical message passing algorithm (MPA) [5] and its improved algorithms, such as the expectation propagation algorithm (EPA) [6] and discretized message passing algorithm [7], are developed with synchronous transmission model. To further enhance the performance of synchronous SCMA, several iterative detection and decoding (IDD) schemes have been proposed by iteratively exchanging messages between the SCMA detector and channel decoder [8]–[10]. Moreover, some joint detection and decoding (JDD) schemes are also proposed by merging the synchronous SCMA detection and low-density parity check (LDPC) decoding under a joint sparse graph [11], [12].
There are few studies on the detection and decoding of asynchronous SCMA. The first detection algorithm for asynchronous SCMA was proposed in [13], where three MPA-based algorithms were proposed, i.e., the belief propagation message passing algorithm, belief propagation with equal gain combining message passing algorithm, and partial phase-coherent algorithm. As shown in its simulation results, however, the performance of these three algorithms is worse than that of the synchronous SCMA. Besides, a sample selection-based message passing algorithm was proposed for asynchronous SCMA [14], where its performance is still worse than that of MPA in synchronous SCMA. Moreover, for asynchronous code division multiple access system, a multi-user detection scheme was proposed in [15], where a three-dimensional factor graph was first constructed and an EPA detection was then developed. This EPA is performed from the head to the tail of the entire frame in a zigzag, which may result in high detection latency. Nevertheless, these methods only consider the detection of asynchronous SCMA, which is not combined with the softoutput decoder to further enhance the performance.
In this paper, we design an ultra-reliable receiver for SCMA in satellite-terrestrial communication by considering the additional DoF in terms of delay. We first propose a parallel EPA (P-EPA) detector based on the developed extended factor graph, and a new initialization method

II. SYSTEM MODEL
As shown in Fig. 1, we consider an uplink asynchronous SCMA-based satellite-terrestrial communication system with J users sharing K resource elements (REs). All users take the same code C(n′, k′) in channel coding, where n′ and k′ are the coded bit length and message length, respectively. For the j-th user, the transmitted message sequence uj = (u1,j, u2,j, . . . , uk′,j) is encoded as cj = (c1,j, c2,j, . . . , cn′,j). Then, the coded bit sequence cj is interleaved by πj and mapped to complex domain SCMA codewords by the SCMA encoder. For clarity of notation, we do not differentiate variables before and after interleaving. Assume that the size of SCMA codebook for each user is M , and every Q = log2M coded bits are mapped to a length-K SCMA codeword xj = (x1,j, x2,j, . . . , xK,j)T ∈ Xj, where Xj is the codebook of the j-th user, |Xj| = M , and xk,j represents the symbol of the j-th user transmitted on the k-th RE. Because of the sparsity of SCMA, the K-dimensional SCMA codeword xj consists of dv < K non-zero elements, whose positions are determined by a length-K binary vector fj = (f1,j, f2,j, . . . , fK,j)T , where fk,j = 1 denotes the jth user transmitting the signal xk,j over the k-th RE. The entire structure of the SCMA can be represented by a K ×J factor graph matrix F = [f1, f2, . . . , fJ ]. Correspondingly, we denote ϕk = {j : 1 ≤ j ≤ J, fk,j = 1} and φj = {k : 1 ≤ k ≤ K, fk,j = 1} the index set of users contributing to the k-th RE, and the index set of REs occupied by the j-th user, respectively. For a regular factor graph matrix, we have |ϕk| = df and |φj| = dv. After SCMA encoding, the overall transmitted symbol of

the j-th user is expressed as Xj = [xj1, x2j , . . . , xjF ], where xji = (x1i ,j, x2i ,j, . . . , xiK,j)T is the i-th transmitted SCMA codeword of the j-th user and F = n′/log2M is the frame length.

III. ITERATIVE DETECTION AND DECODING FOR ASYNCHRONOUS SCMA TRANSMISSION

In this section, we first develop an extended factor graph to characterize the interference in asynchronous SCMA transmission system. Based on this graph, a parallel EPA (PEPA) detector is given with a new initialization method. In addition, we propose a universal soft-output decoder that can be combined with the proposed P-EPA detector to exchange messages iteratively between them, as shown in Fig. 1.

Moreover, it can be seen that the proposed AIDD can approach the performance of single user scheme. The average iteration number of the outer loop for the synchronous IDD and the proposed AIDD, and the equivalent average iteration number of these two JDD schemes are also given in Fig. 5. As shown, the average number of iterations of the proposed AIDD is much less than the other schemes at low SNRs and converges to 2 with the increasing of SNRs.
V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have proposed an ultra-reliable receiver for SCMA in satellite-terrestrial communication, where the messages between the detector and decoder are iteratively exchanged by considering the delay. At first, we propose a parallel expectation propagation algorithm (P-EPA) based on the developed extended factor graph, and a new initialization method is given by utilizing the prior information to improve the performance. Then, a universal soft-output decoder called S-OLD is proposed based on the ordered likelihood decoder to generate the soft information efficiently and serve as the input of the proposed P-EPA in AIDD. The detection and decoding process will be terminated when the maximum number of iteration is achieved or the decoding results of all the users are converged. Simulation results show that the proposed P-EPA has better performance and less detection time than its counterparts, and the proposed AIDD has better performance and requires less iteration than that of the synchronous IDD and JDD schemes.
