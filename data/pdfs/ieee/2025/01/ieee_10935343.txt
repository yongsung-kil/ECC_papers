ABSTRACT This paper investigates the design of protograph low-density parity-check (PLDPC) codes and Gray-labeling codebooks (CBs) for the sparse code multiple access systems with iterative detection and decoding receiver (IDD-SCMA). Specifically, leveraging the protograph extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) analysis, we first propose two types of PLDPC codes (i.e., an unpunctured Code-W and a punctured Code-V) which achieve the lowest decoding thresholds for the IDD-SCMA systems over AWGN and Rayleigh fading channels. To further enhance system performance, we focus on the critical role of Gray-labeling in the PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA systems, which are regarded as specific types of bit-interleaved coded modulation with iterative decoding (BICM-ID) systems. Subsequently, we devise a simple SCMA CB design scheme, referred to as the M PSK-based Gray-labeling CB design. This scheme is composed of the factor graph matrix design, the constellation set design, and Gray-labeling for the constellation set. Furthermore, with the aid of the PEXIT algorithm, the proposed CBs exhibit superior compatibility with the PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA systems. Analytical and simulation results demonstrate significant performance gains from the proposed PLDPC codes and CBs compared to existing counterparts. Consequently, the PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA systems with the proposed design emerge as a promising solution to satisfy the high-reliability requirements for SCMA applications.
INDEX TERMS Gray-labeling, iterative detection and decoding (IDD), protograph low-density parity-check (PLDPC), protograph extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT), sparse code multiple access (SCMA).

I. INTRODUCTION Orthogonal multiple access (OMA) allows multiple user elements (UEs) to efficiently communicate by accessing the same kind of orthogonal resource elements (REs), such as time slots, frequency bands, and power levels [1], [2]. However, with the explosive growth of devices and the increasing demand for higher quality of service in massive connectivity (e.g., vehicle-to-infrastructure and network infrastructures) [3], [4], [5] and higher spectral efficiency, OMA struggles to address these challenges [2], [6], [7]. Unlike OMA, non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA)

allows multiple UEs to access the same REs, increasing the number of connected devices and improving the spectral efficiency [1], [2]. Therefore, NOMA has emerged as a key solution for the above challenges and demands [8], [9]. As one of the innovative NOMA schemes, sparse code multiple access (SCMA) has attracted much attention in recent years due to its high spectral efficiency [10]. At the SCMA transmitter, several bits of each UE can be directly mapped into a sparse multi-dimensional complex vector (i.e., an SCMA codeword) [10]. Each SCMA codeword is selected from a corresponding codebook (CB) that has been carefully designed to ensure excellent performance [10], [11]. maximum a posteriori (MAP) algorithm can be employed for multi-user detection as an optimal approach [12]. To achieve lower computational complexity with only slight performance degradation, the message passing algorithm (MPA) has been proposed for SCMA detection [10], [11].
Generally, researchers have focused on two primary directions in the SCMA systems: CB design and the optimization of the SCMA receiver [12]. CB design has been extensively studied by researchers due to its decisive role in determining the performance of SCMA systems [1], [12]. As a complex non-concave optimization problem, it has been approached from various perspectives, resulting in the development of numerous highly effective CBs [13], [14], [15], [16], [17], [18], [19]. For example, some works have focused on implementing rotation and interleaving techniques on the mother CB [14], [16]. Some researchers have optimized the process of CB design utilizing some specific algorithms (i.e., genetic algorithm and differential evolution) [15], [17]. Moreover, some CBs have been specifically designed for different channels (i.e., AWGN and Rayleigh fading channels) [18], [19]. On the other hand, significant advancements have been developed to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of the SCMA receiver [20]. Although the message passing algorithm (MPA) outperforms other algorithms such as successive interference cancellation (SIC), it still suffers from a huge computational overhead [20]. Inspired by the log-domain MAP (i.e., log-MAP and MAX-log-MAP [21]), two simplified versions namely log-MPA [22] and MAXlog MPA [23] have been proposed to reduce computational overhead while maintaining satisfactory performance.
To further enhance transmission reliability, errorcorrection codes (ECCs) are commonly employed in the SCMA systems. In particular, low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes, a highly efficient type of ECCs, have been widely adopted in SCMA research [24], [25], [26]. Especially, a type of structured LDPC codes called protograph LDPC (PLDPC) codes [27], [28], [29], [30], have emerged as a more desirable option for SCMA systems, as they maintain superior performance while offering simpler implementation [31]. Besides, the LDPC decoder can be integrated with the SCMA detector to form a turbo-like iterative detection and decoding (IDD) receiver, which significantly enhances system performance by exchanging the information such as the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) between the detector and decoder [24], [26], [31].
As is well known, a given PLDPC code generally demonstrates different performance behavior in different communication scenarios [27]. Specifically, a PLDPC-coded SCMA system with an IDD receiver (PLDPC-coded-IDDSCMA) is actually a bit-interleaved coded modulation with iterative decoding (BICM-ID) system [32], [33] with multiuser. Therefore, existing PLDPC codes may not perform well in such systems, as they have not been specifically designed for multi-user interference and the IDD scenario. Similar to channel coding, CBs also significantly influence the performance of the SCMA systems. Although
51430

numerous advanced CBs have been developed, they may not be well-suited for PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA systems, potentially resulting in performance degradation. In addition, Gray-labeling plays a crucial role in determining the performance of BICM-ID systems, as it enhances the capacity of such systems [12]. Moreover, CB design is naturally compatible with Gray-labeling, as it can be applied to each dimension of the multi-dimensional constellation (i.e., CBs). However, existing CB design may not fully consider Gray-labeling schemes, especially for the PLDPC-codedIDD-SCMA systems. Notably, channel coding design and constellation design (i.e., CB design) are strongly correlated. However, to the best of our knowledge, no existing work has combined these two schemes for PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA systems.
Inspired by the above discussion, we first propose two types of PLDPC codes (i.e., an unpunctured PLDPC code and a punctured PLDPC code) for the PLDPC-coded-IDDSCMA system by exploiting the protograph extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) algorithm [33]. Subsequently, based on the constellation rotation and Gray-labeling, we propose a three-step CB design scheme named the M PSK-based Graylabeling CB design, which consists of the factor graph matrix design, the constellation set design, and Gray-labeling for the constellation set. In particular, the CB design scheme utilizes the PEXIT algorithm to search the CB for the IDD-SCMA systems with the proposed PLDPC codes. Analysis and average BER (AVE-BER) simulations demonstrate that the proposed PLDPC codes and CBs significantly outperform their state-of-the-art counterparts.
The remainder of this work is organized as follows. In Section II, we present the system model of the traditional PLDPC-coded SCMA and PLDPC-coded-IDDSCMA. In Section III, we propose the PLDPC codes and SCMA CB design for the PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA system. The analyses and simulation results performed in Section IV demonstrate the superiority of our proposed design. The conclusions are presented in V.
II. SYSTEM MODEL OF PLDPC-CODED SCMA In this section, we first introduce the concept of PLDPC codes. Then, we describe the structure of the traditional PLDPC-coded SCMA system and the PLDPC-coded-IDDSCMA systems in detail, respectively.
A. PLDPC CODES A PLDPC code can be described by a small Tanner graph (i.e., protograph) consisting of the nc check nodes (CNs), nv variable nodes (VNs), and some edges connecting the CNs and VNs [27]. A protograph with a code rate R = (nv −nc)/nv can be represented by a base matrix B = (bi,j) of size nc × nv, where bi,j represent the number of edges connecting CN ci and VN vj. By using the modified progressive-edge-growth (PEG) algorithm [34], we can extend a base matrix to a parity check matrix (i.e., derived graph) of size Lnc × Lnv, where

C. THE PLDPC-CODED-IDD-SCMA SYSTEMS
Although the log-MPA algorithm can effectively detect interuser interference, the performance of traditional receivers is not sufficiently excellent due to the non-orthogonal property of SCMA. For better system performance, researchers have proposed the LDPC-coded SCMA systems with the IDD receiver [24]. The block diagram of the IDD receiver for the PLDPC-coded SCMA system is shown in Fig. 3. The LeS,j and LeD,j denote the extrinsic bit-level LLR sequence generated by the SCMA detector and PLDPC decoder, respectively, where j ∈ {1, 2, · · · , J }. The NS and NL denote the maximum iteration numbers of the SCMA detector and PLDPC decoder, respectively. As we can see, after the first round of SCMA detection, all UEs independently perform BP decoding to obtain the extrinsic LLR sequences, which enhances the performance of SCMA detection. Subsequently, more accurate extrinsic LLR sequences are sent to the PLDPC decoder. This turbo-like IDD receiver scheme achieves a significant performance gain with nearly the same complexity as the receiver without the IDD scheme [24], [26], [31]. III. DESIGN OF PLDPC CODES AND CBS In this section, we first propose two types of PLDPC codes for PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA. Then, we propose a simple M PSK-based CB design scheme, namely the M PSK-based Gray-labeling CB design, which exhibits excellent performance and low design complexity. In particular, the proposed PLDPC codes and CB design scheme exploit the PEXIT algorithm to analyze the systems convergence behavior [33]. This algorithm has attracted significant attention because it allows system performance to be accurately analyzed and predicted without simulation. A detailed explanation of the PEXIT algorithm can be found in [33].
A. THE PROPOSED PLDPC CODES As is well known, a PLDPC code that demonstrates excellent performance in one communication scenario may not perform well in another scenario [27]. To be specific, the existing PLDPC codes may not be suitable for the PLDPC-coded-IDD-SCMA systems. Therefore, using the PEXIT algorithm, we design two types of PLDPC codes (i.e., an unpunctured PLDPC code and a punctured PLDPC code) for the mentioned systems [33].
1) UNPUNCTURED PLDPC CODE DESIGN We first design an unpunctured PLDPC code with the aid of the PEXIT algorithm. In general, the larger base matrix allows higher design flexibility, which facilitates our search for better PLDPC codes with lower decoding thresholds [27], [36]. However, the excessive search space will lead to high search complexity. As we all know, a construct containing some low-degree VNs (i.e., degree-2 VNs) is more likely to search a protograph code with lower decoding thresholds [34], [36]. B. THE MPSK-BASED GRAY-LABELING CB DESIGN
As is well known, bit-labeling plays an important role in various channel coding scenarios, such as BICM systems, because it effectively enhances the capacity of these systems [32], [40], [41]. Gray-labeling, a widely used bitlabeling scheme, is regarded as one of the most effective schemes to enhance the BICM-ID system capacity [32], [41]. Furthermore, as a multi-dimensional complex constellation, an SCMA CB allows different Gray-labeling schemes to be implemented for each dimension (i.e., each RE). Inspired by the above discussion, we propose a simple CB design scheme, namely the M PSK-based Gray-labeling CB design. V. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK In this paper, we have investigated the proposed PLDPC codes and CBs for PLDPC-coded IDD-SCMA systems over AWGN and Rayleigh fading channels. To be specific, utilizing the PEXIT algorithm, we have first conceived two types of PLDPC codes (i.e., Code-W and Code-V), which have the lowest decoding thresholds in the mentioned systems. Subsequently, by leveraging Gray-labeling, we have proposed a three-step CB design scheme named the M PSK-based Gray-labeling CB design, which consists of the factor graph matrix design, the constellation set design, and Gray-labeling for the constellation set. With the aid of the PEXIT algorithm, the resulting CBs have exhibited strong compatibility with PLDPC-coded IDDSCMA systems. Numerical analyses have demonstrated that our proposed designs significantly outperform the state-ofthe-art counterparts. Therefore, our designs stand out as a prospective scheme for massive connectivity scenarios with high reliability and spectral efficiency requirements, especially for emerging IoT applications. Future work will focus on designing Gray-mapping constellations for various modulation schemes or studying other NOMA systems. Additionally, we will explore the design of PLDPC codes for other scenarios and investigate rate-compatible PLDPC codes with R > 1/2 for high stability communication scenarios. We also consider the joint design of constellation mapping and code design to further enhance system performance.
