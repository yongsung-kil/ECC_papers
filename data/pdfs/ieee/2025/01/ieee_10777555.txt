Improved Free-of-CPP ADMM-Based Iterative Decoding Algorithm of Binary LDPC Codes
Jing Bai , Zedong An , Yuhao Chi , Senior Member, IEEE, Guanghui Song , and Chau Yuen , Fellow, IEEE

Abstract—Iterative decoding algorithms based on the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) decoding of low density parity check (LDPC) codes has emerged as an alternating decoding method and bringed a boom of research on drawing upon mathematical optimization to LDPC decoding. Improving error-correcting performance is a key issue to enhance the superiority of ADMM decoding. In this letter, we investigate an efficient ADMM-based iterative decoder for binary LDPC codes. First, we build an mathematical programming equivalence of the maximum likelihood (ML) decoding problem by transforming parity-check constraints to multiple equivalent linear constraints and eliminating check-polytope projection (CPP). Then, an iterative algorithm based on ADMM technique is developed to solve this free-of-CPP (FCPP) equivalence and each ADMM update can be computed efficiently. Moreover, the proposed ADMM-FCPP decoding algorithm is analyzed to display a linear complexity to the length of the LDPC code at each iteration. Finally, simulation results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed decoder in error-correcting performance compared with the state-of-the-art ADMM-based decoders.
Index Terms—Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM), checkpolytope projection (CPP), mathematical programming.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have gained considerable attentions because of its fantastic error-correcting performance and hence widely employed in modern communication applications [1]. Besides traditional belief-propagation (BP) decoding and its improved versions [2], authors in [3] first proposed an alternative decoding method by relaxing the maximum-likelihood (ML) decoding problem to a linear programming (LP) version. The ADMM method brings LDPC decoding to the realm of convex optimization and thus it can be analyzed and improved by existing mathematical technique, which motivates lots of interested researchers towards improving LDPC decoding from an mathematical optimization standpoint.
In ADMM decoding, a main computational cost lies in a complex projection of check ploytope (CPP), which is represented by the convex hull of all binary vectors of the same even weight. To address this issue, numerous studies have been conducted to reduce the complexity of the CPP projection [5], [6], [7], [8], [9]. Moreover, authors in [10], [11] proposed an ADMM-based decoder without CPP by decomposing a high-degree check node into several three-degree check nodes but at a cost of introducing extensive auxiliary variables.
In addition, another focus for the research of ADMM decoding is on optimizing problem models to further improve error correction performance. Of particular relevance is [12] where a class of so-called ADMM penalized decoders were suggested by adding a non-convex term to LP decoding objective to penalize non-integer solutions. Subsequently, several improved versions based on different penalized strategies have been put forward to further lower error rate of ADMM penalized decoding [13], [14]. Besides, authors in [15] proposed an ADMM-based decoder by transforming the ML problem to an p-box equivalence but CPP operations are required.
In this letter, by jointly taking the reduction of decoding complexity caused by CPP projection and the improvement of error-correcting performance caused by LP relaxation into account, we investigate an efficient free-of-CPP ADMM-based (FCPP-ADMM) decoder for binary LDPC codes. First, we eliminate CPP operation by transforming all parity-check constraints into equivalent linear constraints and propose an mathematical programming (MP) equivalence of the ML problem. Moreover, an ADMM-based iterative algorithm is customized to solve this MP decoding problem and each ADMM update is computed efficiently. Finally, simulation results demonstrate the proposed decoder achieves better error-correcting performance compared with the state-of-the-art ADMM decoders.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose an efficient ADMM-FCPP decoding algorithm for binary LDPC codes. Moreover, simulation results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed ADMMFCPP decoder in terms of FER performance compared with the state-of-the-art ADMM decoders. However, the convergence of the proposed ADMM-FCPP decoder has been not theoretically guaranteed yet. Thus, it would be well worthy of further study on exploring how to optimize the structure of the proposed decoding problem for further enhancing convergence guarantee of the ADMM algorithm meanwhile with attaining excellent decoding performance in future.
