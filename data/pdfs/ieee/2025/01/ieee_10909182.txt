On the Gap to Channel Capacity of Generalized Low-Density Parity-Check Codes
DONGXU CHANG 1, QINGQING PENG1, GUANGHUI WANG 1, GUIYING YAN2, AND DAWEI YIN 1
1School of Mathematics, Shandong University, Jinan 250100, Shandong, China 2Academy of Mathematics and Systems Science, Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing 100190, China

ABSTRACT In this study, the gap to channel capacity of generalized low-density parity-check (GLDPC) codes under the a posteriori probability (APP) decoder on the binary input additive white Gaussian noise (BI-AWGN) channel is analyzed. Building on the density evolution for LDPC codes, we extend this to GLDPC codes by generalizing the properties of concentration, symmetry, and monotonicity to accommodate the characteristics of GLDPC codes. Specifically, we propose a methodology to simplify the computation of density evolution for GLDPC codes under APP decoding over BI-AWGN channels. Firstly, we identify a class of subcodes that can greatly simplify the performance analysis and practical decoding of GLDPC codes, which we refer to as message-invariant subcodes. Secondly, based on the characteristics of GLDPC codes, we develop a Gaussian mixture approximation algorithm to approximate the message distributions in density evolution. Compared to Gaussian approximation, the proposed Gaussian mixture approximation approach can greatly enhance accuracy while maintaining low computational complexity. Based on the above techniques, we demonstrate that with an appropriate proportion of generalized constraint (GC) nodes, despite the rate loss when single parity-check (SPC) nodes are replaced by GC nodes, GLDPC codes can reduce the original gap to capacity compared to their original LDPC counterparts. Our simulation experiments validate the performance analysis.
INDEX TERMS A posteriori probability decoding, density evolution, Gaussian mixture approximation, generalized low-density parity-check codes.

I. INTRODUCTION
A S A VARIANT of the low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes introduced by Gallager [1], generalized lowdensity parity-check (GLDPC) codes were first proposed by Tanner [2]. While preserving the sparse graph representation, GLDPC codes replace single parity-check (SPC) nodes with error-correcting block subcodes, known as generalized constraint (GC) nodes. The degrees of GC nodes match the length of their associated subcodes. Various linear block codes, including Hamming codes [3], [4], Bose-ChaudhuriHocquengham (BCH) codes [5], Hadamard codes [6], and others, can serve as subcodes in constructing GC nodes. GLDPC codes provide several advantages, including the capability to utilize more advanced decoding algorithms at the GC nodes, which leads to enhanced decoding

performance [7], accelerated convergence [8], and a lower error floor [9], [10].
However, when a check node is replaced by a GC node, the overall code rate decreases. Therefore, although the decoding performance can be easily improved when check nodes are replaced by GC nodes, the gap between the decoding threshold of the code and the channel capacity may actually increase. The construction of GLDPC codes that can, based on LDPC codes, further reduce the gap between the code rate and channel capacity has not yet been fully explored.
In [7], the tradeoff between code rate and asymptotic performance of a class of GLDPC code ensembles with fixed degree distributions constructed by including a certain fraction of GC nodes in the graph is analyzed over the binary erasure channel (BEC). Through the analysis of the

probabilistic peeling decoder (P-PD) algorithm, Liu et al. [7] accurately predict the performance of GLDPC codes under maximum likelihood (ML) decoding on GC nodes. The analysis in [7] demonstrates that when the proportion of GC nodes is appropriate, compared to their original LDPC counterparts, GLDPC codes can reduce the gap to capacity on the BEC. However, for a wider range of channels, such as binary input additive white Gaussian noise (BI-AWGN) channels, there is still a lack of sufficient analysis of the performance of GLDPC codes. While the extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart has been applied to GLDPC codes as a tool for performance analysis [6], [11], [12], the Gaussian approximation employed in EXIT charts can result in considerable inaccuracies in performance analysis, as we discussed in Section IV.
In this paper, we introduce a systematic methodology of performance analysis of GLDPC codes over the BI-AWGN channels. Specifically, for GLDPC codes under the a posteriori probability (APP) decoder, we adopt a general approach analogous to density evolution in LDPC codes [13], and the concentration, symmetry, and monotonicity properties are demonstrated on GLDPC codes to demonstrate the feasibility of applying the density evolution algorithm to GLDPC codes.
Based on the aforementioned properties, we conduct the density evolution analysis for GLDPC codes on the BI-AWGN channels. However, due to the computational complexity associated with APP decoding on the GC nodes, both practical decoding and theoretical analysis face considerable challenges. In this paper, we identify a class of error-correcting block codes in which the propagation of messages from GC nodes to each connected variable node follows a uniform and consistent pattern when they are used as subcodes on GC nodes. Consequently, the performance analysis and practical decoding of GLDPC codes can be significantly simplified. We refer to such subcodes as message-invariant subcodes.
Considering the complexity of computing the message distribution of GC nodes on the BI-AWGN channel, similar to the Gaussian approximation used for LDPC codes [14], we propose a technique for efficiently computing the channel parameter threshold. Specifically, we improve the traditional Gaussian approximation approach by approximating the distribution of messages using Gaussian mixture distributions. Different from the Gaussian mixture distribution approximation used in LDPC codes in [15], [16], [17], in this study, the proposed Gaussian mixture approximation algorithm is designed based on the structural characteristics of GLDPC codes. This can significantly reduce the errors introduced by Gaussian approximation while maintaining low computational complexity.
By selecting a (6,3)-linear block code and a (7,4)Hamming code as two message-invariant subcodes on the GC nodes, which were also used in [7] to illustrate that a suitable proportion of GC nodes can reduce the gap to capacity compared to the base LDPC code, our density evolution results reveal consistency with the findings in [7].

When the proportion of GC nodes is appropriate, GLDPC codes, under the APP decoder, exhibit a reduced gap to capacity compared to the base LDPC codes on the BI-AWGN channels. Our simulation results also confirm this conclusion, where a GLDPC code with performance surpassing that of the 5G new ratio (NR) LDPC code is constructed.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In Section II, we introduce GLDPC code ensemble, the message-passing decoder, and the density evolution on LDPC codes. Section III presents the properties of density evolution on GLDPC codes, where the concentration, symmetry, and monotonicity properties are demonstrated. In Section IV, we provide a simplified density analysis for GLDPC codes over the BI-AWGN channels, together with the simulation results. The paper concludes in Section V.

For LDPC codes, by showing the properties of centrality, symmetry, and monotonicity for LDPC codes under belief propagation (BP) decoding, the performance analysis of LDPC codes with block length tending to infinity can be greatly simplified. III. PROPERTIES OF DENSITY EVOLUTION ON GLDPC CODES The density evolution algorithm proposed in [13] provides an efficient framework for analyzing the asymptotic performance of LDPC codes. In their seminal work [13], Richardson and Urbanke demonstrated that the behavior of individual instances converges closely to the average performance, considering both the code structure and the noise characteristics. As the code length increases, the average behavior approximates that of a cycle-free LDPC code. The validity of this centrality in LDPC codes is essentially determined by the ‚Äúlocal‚ÄĚ decoding nature of BP decoding in LDPC codes. Since the nature of decoding locally also holds for ensembles of GLDPC codes, the concentration property remains applicable to GLDPC code ensembles. Consequently, the asymptotic performance analysis of GLDPC codes can be conducted on cycle-free graphs, and the incoming information to a node in GLDPC codes can be reasonably considered as mutually independent.
In this section, We theoretically justify the rationality of density evolution on GLDPC codes. We introduce and establish the symmetry conditions extended for GLDPC codes. We establish that, under the extended symmetry conditions, the error probability of GLDPC codes is unaffected by the choice of the transmitted codeword. Moreover, we show that GLDPC codes, when decoded using the APP decoder, exhibit the desirable property of monotonicity, which ensures the existence of a channel threshold.

[23], it was proven that for a given binaryinput memoryless output-symmetric channel, the density functions of messages exchanged between variable and check nodes during BP maintain symmetric. This property is established by illustrating that the density of channel messages exhibits symmetry, and this symmetry persists as messages traverse between variable and check nodes. According to [22, Th. 4.29], this symmetry also holds when messages are exchanged at GC nodes using the APP decoder. Consequently, we derive the following lemma.
Lemma 3: For a binary-input memoryless outputsymmetric channel, the density functions of messages sent from variable nodes are symmetric for GLDPC codes under the APP decoder.

IV. REDUCED GAP TO CAPACITY In this section, we will use density evolution to analyze the asymptotic performance of GLDPC codes over the BIAWGN channels. However, the complexity of the APP decoding makes the direct computation of message distributions in density evolution challenging and computationally intensive. To address this difficulty, we identify a class of error-correcting block codes in which the propagation of messages from each connected variable node follows a uniform and consistent way when they are used as subcodes on GC nodes. Consequently, the performance analysis and practical decoding of GLDPC codes can be greatly simplified. We refer to such subcodes as messageinvariant subcodes.
By employing two message-invariant subcodes as examples, we investigated the impact of varying proportions of GC nodes on the threshold. Over the BI-AWGN channel, we propose a Gaussian mixture approximation algorithm for rapid threshold computation. As an improvement to the Gaussian approximation method for LDPC codes [14], the Gaussian mixture approximation significantly reduces the loss of accuracy introduced by Gaussian approximation while retaining its low complexity characteristics.
