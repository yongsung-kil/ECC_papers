AbstractвҖ”In optical communication systems, channel coding is required to exhibit high code rates, superior performance, high throughput, and low complexity. While LDPC codes, adopted as the channel coding standard for 5G, deliver commendable performance, they often struggle to simultaneously achieve high code rates and low bit error rates. In contrast, DGLDPC codes, as an extension of LDPC codes, have been demonstrated to maintain excellent performance even at high code rates. This paper investigates a type of DGLDPC code and integrates it with layered scheduling decoding which is a technique originally developed for LDPC codes. The aim is to leverage their shared advantages: high error-correcting performance, strong structural regularity, and rapid convergence speed.
KeywordsвҖ”DGLDPC, Layered scheduling , SPC-PC
I. INTRODUCTION
With the advancement of technology, the demands for communication systems have increasingly intensified. As one of the most widely used communication technologies in recent years, optical communication requires channels with high speed, high precision, and high code rates. This implies the need to reduce both the bit error rate (BER) and computational complexity вҖ” a challenge that conventional channel coding schemes struggle to meet. Although traditional Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes exhibit commendable performance, they often fail to maintain low BER under high code rate conditions.Generalized LDPC (G-LDPC) codes, as an enhancement to LDPC codes, replace the standard paritycheck constraints with more sophisticated ones, specifically by substituting ordinary check nodes with super check nodes. This generalization allows for greater flexibility in the design of check node operations, enabling the exploitation of advantages offered by various component codes to meet specific requirements of communication systems.Furthermore, double generalized LDPC (DGLDPC) codes extend the concept of single G-LDPC codes by also generalizing variable nodes into super variable nodes, thereby introducing an additional layer of structural and functional enhancement. Many types of short-

length component codes have been considered for GLDPC/DGCLDPC codes. They include Hamming component codes[1]; Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) component codes[2]; Reed-Solomon (RS) component codes[3]; ReedMuller component codes [4]; and hybrid component codes [5].
DGLDPC codes were first proposed by Ying Wang et al. in 2006. In their work, Hamming codes and single parity-check (SPC) codes were employed as component codes to construct a type of DGLDPC code, which demonstrated significantly superior performance compared to LDPC codes and GLDPC codes of the same code rate [6].Furthermore, in the study by Yige Wang et al.[7], a hybrid approach was adopted for the component codes of the super check nodes (SCNs), combining both Hamming codes and single parity-check (SPC) codes. Similarly, the super variable nodes (SVNs) utilized a mixture of repetition codes and SPC codes. Simulation results demonstrated that this code construction achieves promising performance over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels with large block lengths.Building on this foundation, Y. Min et al. replaced the component codes of the super check nodes (SCNs) with two-dimensional single parity-check product codes (SPC-PCs) and employed a turbo iterative structure as the local decoder. Comparative analysis with the previous approach demonstrated that the proposed scheme achieves superior performance when the number of local iterations is set to 5 or 6 [8вҖ“9].Yejun He et al. conducted further in-depth research on SPC-PC codes, specifically investigating the use of three-dimensional SPC-PC codes as component codes for super check nodes (SCNs). Their findings revealed that the three-dimensional product codes exhibit superior performance compared to their two-dimensional counterparts. Subsequently, they also performed simulations on different types of SPC codes employed at the super variable nodes (SVNs), demonstrating that the cyclic form of SPC codes achieves the best stability and the highest decoding threshold [10вҖ“11].

B. Decoding algorithm for DG-LDPC
The decoding method of DG-LDPC corresponds to its construction method. When the decoding module receives the LLR information from the channel, the LLR information first enters the encoder of the super variable node (SVN). The SVN encoder utilizes the generator matrix to encode the LLR information into channel information to be passed to the super check node (SCN). This channel information is then combined with the extrinsic information fed back from the SCN to the SVN in the previous iteration to compute the extrinsic information to be passed to the SCN in the current iteration. For a DG -LDPC code with basic check matrix Ha, there are Ma SCNs and Na SVNs.In tanner graph, if an element in the fundamental matrix Ha is "1", it indicates a link between the SVN and the SCN.As shown in Fig. 2, for the v-th SVN, the received channel information bits are kv,1, kv,2, ..., kv,a.And dv,1, dv,2, ..., dv,b are the output after being processed by the encoder of this SVN, and they are transmitted to the SCNs that are linked to SVNv.
The extrinsic information dx,y (The y-th information output by the x-th SVN) passed to the SCN is fed into the local decoder of the component code. The output of the local decoder vr,1,vr,2,...,vr,c is then used as the extrinsic information passed from the r-th SCN to the connected SVN.

III. LAYERED DGLDPC WITH COMPONENT CODE SPC-PC
Single parity-check product (SPC-PC) code is a special type of product codes, which is constructed by binary and systematic SPC codes.Prior research has demonstrated that DGLDPC codes with SPC-PC component codes exhibit significantly improved performance compared to conventional LDPC codes, Turbo codes, and GLDPC codes, achieving optimal performance when the number of local decoding iterations is set to 5[9]. Owing to the mathematical and spatial characteristics of SPC-PC codes, DGLDPC codes utilizing SPC-PC component codes possess high structural regularity and accelerated decoding convergence. Noting that layered decoding, a scheduling strategy for LDPC decoding, similarly exhibits these attributes, we integrate the two approaches to further enhance performance and decoding convergence speed.
A. SPC-PC code local decoder
Suppose the (nspc, nspc вҲ’ 1)D SPC-PC is used as the constituent code in each SCN. Each (nspc, nspc вҲ’ 1) component SPC code in each dimension is considered as an independent code and uses an independent decoder. Fig.3 illustrates the decoding process of a (3,2)2 SPC-PC code. The core mechanism involves performing a parity check along each dimension. The information obtained after the check in Dimension 1 is utilized to update Dimension 2. The completion of updates in Dimension 2 constitutes one decoding iteration. Based on the number of iterations, the decoder either outputs the decoding result or proceeds to the next iteration. SPC-PC codes exhibit high structural regularity, which facilitates hardware implementation, while also delivering relatively satisfactory error-correction performance.

B. Layered DG-LDPC codes
Layered decoding, as a scheduling strategy for LDPC decoding, enables variable nodes to be updated multiple times within a single iteration, thereby significantly accelerating decoding convergence. In the traditional flooding schedule, all check nodes are updated simultaneously, and variable nodes are updated only in the next iteration after all check nodes have been processed вҖ” meaning variable nodes are updated only
once per iteration. In contrast, the layered schedule updates the LDPC code in a layer-by-layer manner. After each layer is processed, the variable nodes are updated immediately, resulting in multiple updates per iteration for each variable node. The Fig.4 shows the Layered Decoding Process of DGLDPC. Consequently, layered scheduling promotes more thorough message propagation and improves decoding performance for the same number of iterations. Moreover, this approach exhibits high structural regularity, which facilitates hardware implementation, a characteristic shared with DGLDPC codes. In this letter, each row block of the DGLDPC code is treated as one layer for layered decoding.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS AND ANALYSIS
In the simulations, (4, 3)2 SPC-PC code was employed as the component code for the SCNs in the DGLDPC, while a (4, 3) SPC code was used as the component code for the SVNs. The basic parity-check matrix H dimensions of 1500 Г— 2000, with row weight 4 and column weight 3.The channel model isan additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel. Theinformation bits are randomly generated by the source and thenencoded by the channel coding. The encoded sequence will bemodulated by Binary Phase Shift Keying (BPSK) and thenenter the AWGN channel.The signal received from the channel is demodulated using BPSK and then decoded using different methods. The decoding results will be used for bit error rate calculation.
A. Simulation of Layered DG-LDPC codes
We conducted a comparative simulation by varying the number of iterations for both layered and flooding DGLDPC decoding schemes. The results indicate that when the number of iterations is set to 5 for both schemes, the layered scheduling method significantly outperforms the flooding scheduling method, as shown in Fig.5. It is the same when the number of iterations is 10. When the number of iterations for the layered schedule remains fixed and that of the flooding schedule is increased, the performance of the latter gradually approaches that of the former,which is shown in Fig.7 and Fig.8. When the number of flooding iterations reaches 20, the performance shows almost no further improvement with additional iterations, indicating that it has reached an error floor. In contrast, the layered schedule already attains its error floor after only 10 iterations as shown in Fig.9. This demonstrates that the layered scheduling method accelerates decoding convergence and reaches the error floor more quickly at a lower iteration count.

V. CONCLUSION
A layered scheduled DGLDPC code is proposed in this letter. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed code exhibits excellent decoding performance, rapid convergence speed, and high structural regularity, which facilitates hardware implementation. Furthermore, this study explores treating a hybrid DGLDPC code as a two-layer DGLDPC structure, achieving reduced complexity and increased code rate while maintaining decoding performance.
