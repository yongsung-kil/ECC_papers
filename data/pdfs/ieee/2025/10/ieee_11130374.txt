Decoding-Aided Channel Estimation Based on the GC-LDPC Codes in Time-Varying MIMO Systems
Hongmei Kang , Student Member, IEEE, Lin Zhao, Ming Jiang , Member, IEEE, and Chunming Zhao , Member, IEEE

Abstract— Massive multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) has been widely adopted in next-generation wireless systems to enhance spectral efficiency and reliability. However, channel aging poses a significant challenge, leading to the outdated channel state information and degraded system performance. To address this issue, we propose a decoding-aided Kalman filter channel tracking algorithm based on the globally-coupled lowdensity parity-check (GC-LDPC) codes. The proposed approach leverages locally decoded symbols to optimize channel estimation and employs global decoding to further reduce the error floor. Additionally, protograph-based extrinsic information transfer analysis and the error minimization progressive edge-growth algorithm are utilized to optimize the compatible GC-LDPC codes. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm significantly improves channel tracking performance in time-varying MIMO systems.
Index Terms— Multiple-input multiple-output, globally coupled low-density parity-check codes, Kalman filter.
I. INTRODUCTION
TO MEET the ultra-high reliability, low latency, and high data rate requirements of the fifth-generation (5G) wireless systems, massive multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) technology has garnered significant attention in recent years [1]. However, its effectiveness is considerably impacted by channel aging and various system imperfections [2]. In particular, the time-varying nature of the channels between the base station (BS) and mobile user equipments (UEs) leads to outdated channel state information (CSI), exhibiting non-ideal characteristics. This results in performance degradation for MIMO detectors with higher error floors.
To mitigate the effects of channel aging, numerous channel estimation techniques have been proposed. In [3], a robust multi-user channel tracking scheme based on turbo-compressive sensing iteratively estimates the multi-user channel while simultaneously compensating for system imperfections until convergence. Pilot-based channel estimation methods have also been explored in [4], [5], where the

In particular, [4] introduces a modified decision-directed semi-blind estimator that periodically updates the channel estimation using only the most recent detected data symbols, while [5] applies a detection-aided Kalman filter (Det-Aided KF) to improve channel estimation accuracy. Furthermore, in [6], we incorporate low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes into a decoding-aided (Dec-Aid) KF algorithm, further enhancing the channel estimation quality.
To further enhance system performance, the coupled LDPC codes, which establish interconnections among multiple independent LDPC codes, have been proposed. Among these, the globally-coupled (GC) LDPC codes and spatially-coupled (SC) LDPC codes have attracted considerable interest [7], [8], [9]. GC-LDPC codes, in particular, employ a two-phase decoding strategy, where local decoding enables parallel processing while global decoding ensures ultra-high reliability. However, the conventional GC-LDPC codes constructed using algebraic methods, as discussed in [8], lack design flexibility and make them less adaptable to MIMO wireless systems. To address this limitation, in [9], the authors propose a superposition-based construction method for rate-compatible GC-LDPC codes, improving their applicability to dynamic environments.
Inspired by the work in [10], which combines rate-compatible protograph LDPC codes with feedback decoding, this paper presents a unified decoding and channel estimation framework for time-varying MIMO systems. First, we propose a Dec-Aided KF algorithm that leverages both hard and soft decision based outputs, and incorporates the reconstruction error into channel tracking. Second, we design a scalable GC-LDPC coding framework compatible with 5G LDPC codes, where the global structure is optimized via protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (PEXIT) analysis and a simplified error minimization progressive edge-growth (EMPEG) algorithm [9], [11], [12], [13]. Finally, we develop a joint estimation and decoding strategy that lowers the error floor via global decoding. Simulation results confirm the effectiveness of our proposed method in dynamic MIMO scenarios.

III. DEC-AIDED KF ALGORITHM BASED ON THE GC-LDPC CODES
A. Construction of GC-LDPC Codes
To construct the rate-compatible GC-LDPC codes, we select the first m0 rows and n0 columns as the local codes Blocali and Plocali , adhering to the 5G standard, which defines two base graphs (BG1 and BG2) and 16 exponent matrices [14]. The primary challenge in constructing GC-LDPC codes lies in designing the global base matrix Bgp = Bgp1 , · · · , BgpI and optimizing the exponent matrix Pgp.
Firstly, the PEXIT algorithm is employed to optimize Bgp. In contrast to the superposition construction in [9], which limits to the optimization for I = 2 and reuses the result for larger I, our approach separately optimizes Bgp for each desired number of code blocks I, thereby better adapting to system requirements. Furthermore, instead of employing an exhaustive search in [9], we reduce the design space by leveraging the standard base matrices (BG1 and BG2) of 5G LDPC codes. Specifically, if the condition r ≤ mmax − m0 is satisfied, where mmax = 46 for BG1 and mmax = 42 for BG2, we construct BGC by selecting r rows from the remaining

Secondly, for GC-LDPC codes, the protograph size is typically large, making the application of the standard EMPEG algorithm computationally prohibitive [12]. To enhance efficiency, a simplified EMPEG algorithm is adopted to optimize Pgp, considering both the cycle length and the extrinsic message degree (EMD) value. This approach effectively lowers the error floor while maintaining computational feasibility. The set V = {v1, · · · , vIdc } comprises all columns in Bgp that contain nonzero elements, where dc denotes the row weight of Bgpi . For clarity, the case when r = 1 is considered, while other values follow a similar procedure. Given an element vj in V , the algorithm determines whether the corresponding variable node can reach the target check node c through existing Tanner graph connections. If a valid connection cannot be established, the lifting factor pgpvj in Pgp is randomly selected from {0, 1, · · · , Z − 1}. Otherwise, pgpvj is assigned based the following priority order: first, pgpvj maximizing the minimum cycle length is chosen, i.e., pgpvj = arg maxpm,n Lmin. If multiple factors yield the same Lmin, the one with the highest EMD value is selected, i.e., pgpvj = arg maxpm,n EMD. If both criteria remain identical, the factor minimizing the number of short cycles is chosen, i.e., pgpvj = arg maxpm,n NLmin . Here, Lmin and NLmin denote the minimum cycle length of the GC-LDPC code under the current Tanner graph and the number of cycles of length Lmin, respectively. Besides, to limit the complexity of EMD computation, a relatively large maximum cycle length is considered to truncate the search space during lifting. Longer cycles are ignored and the corresponding nodes are treated as unreachable during lifting, as their impact on decoding performance is minimal.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS

In this section, we assess the frame error rate (FER) of

the proposed algorithms through simulations. We set K =

Lp = 4, M = 12 and I = 12, representing a small-scale uplink MIMO scenario with temporal correlation. The same

configuration logic can be extended to the larger-scale systems

by increasing the number of users or antennas while preserving

the temporal correlation. Besides, the maximum number of

iterations is set to 30 for both local and global LDPC decoding.

The performance of the “Dec-Aided KF GC” algorithm is

evaluated by comparison with the following schemes:

• Dec-Aided KF Lo: This approach uses the same channel

estimation algorithm as “Pilot Dec-Aided GC”, but the

second decoding step still relies solely on local decoding.

As a result, the global decoding capability of GC-LDPC

codes is not utilized.

• Det-Aided KF Lo/GC: This scheme adopts Algorithm 4

in [5] for channel estimation, which leverages only

detected information for channel tracking without incor-

porating any hard or soft decision decoded results.

• Without-Aided Lo/GC: This scheme only utilizes pilot

symbols and considers channel correlation but lacks chan-

nel optimization.
