Efficient Shuffled Sliding Window Decoding of
SC-LDPC Codes
Qin Du, Lisha Luo, Xuan He
Information Coding and Transmission Key Lab of Sichuan Province, Southwest Jiaotong University, China
qindu@my.swjtu.edu.cn, lisaluo@my.swjtu.edu.cn, xhe@swjtu.edu.cn
Abstract—Spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC-
LDPC) codes provide excellent performance and low latency
under sliding window decoding (SWD). To achieve a low-power
and low-complexity SC-LDPC decoder, we propose a variant of
SWD called size-λ monotonic double-ended queue SWD (λMDQ-
SWD), where λ refers to the storage complexity of the update
at a check node (CN). Our λMDQ-SWD employs the shuffled
schedule in SWD and uses the MDQ structure to implement
the CN update. Moreover, it considers using only the minimum
magnitude from target symbols for the CN update to avoid
redundant computations. Simulation results show that, although
under λ = 2, the proposed algorithm achieves comparable
error correction performance to the state-of-the-art algorithms.
Moreover, a detailed hardware implementation for the CN update
is designed. Analysis using the 90nm CMOS technology library
shows that, compared to the state-of-the-art algorithms, the
λMDQ-SWD with λ = 2 reduces the area and increases the clock
frequency of CN update by 63.7% and 107.6%, respectively.
I. INTRODUCTION
Spatially coupled low-density parity-check (SC-LDPC)
codes [1], [2] have been attractive for various applications
due to the excellent capacity approaching performance and
low latency characteristic under the sliding window decoding
(SWD). Featuring a diagonally banded parity-check matrix,
SC-LDPC codes are considered to be a type of LDPC con-
volutional code (LDPC-CC) [3]. In particular, with iterative
decoding algorithms, SC-LDPC codes have been shown to
reach the maximum a posteriori (MAP) probability threshold
of the underlying LDPC block code [4].
However, achieving the MAP threshold with full block
decoding (FBD) based on flooding schedule using the belief
propagation (BP) algorithm incurs prohibitively high latency
and complexity. In contrast to FBD, SWD [2], [5] decouples
the decoding process from the coupling length L, allowing the
decoding to proceed while awaiting subsequent message trans-
missions, significantly reducing latency. In order to further
reduce the complexity, two primary approaches are available.
The first approach is to change the message passing (MP)
algorithm used in SWD. Although the BP algorithm offers ex-
cellent error correction performance [6], its high computational
complexity results in slow convergence and high hardware
costs. To accommodate the constraints of practical hardware
circuits, suboptimal MP algorithms, such as the min sum (MS)
algorithm and its variants [7]–[9], can be employed to replace
BP. The second approach is to apply efficient information
schedule schemes to the MP algorithm in SWD. Specifically,
the layered schedule [10] and the shuffled schedule [11] can

be employed to accelerate decoding convergence and reduce
hardware costs. Analytical studies [12] show that for SC-
LDPC codes, both schedules exhibit faster convergence and
better performance compared to the flooding schedule [6].
Building on the established advantage of shuffled schedule
over flooding schedule, as demonstrated in FBD contexts
[13], [14], this paper specifically investigates its application
within the inherently low-latency SWD framework. We term
this direct integration as shuffled normalized min sum SWD
(SNMS-SWD), aiming to harness the convergence speedup
per decoding window. However, while SWD addresses the
latency issue inherent to FBD, the computational complexity
of the check node (CN) update in SNMS-SWD remains a
significant challenge. To address this complexity challenge, we
propose a shuffled size-λ monotone double-ended queue SWD
(λMDQ-SWD), which is inspired by the implementations of
shuffled decoding based on the MDQ data structure [15],
[16]. Simulation results show that the proposed SWD variant
has comparable performance to the state-of-the-art variants.
Furthermore, the results of the implementation demonstrate
that, for λ = 2, our proposed algorithm reduces the area of
CN update by 63.7% and increases the clock frequency by
107.6% compared to the SNMS-SWD.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Sec-
tion II illustrates the SC-LDPC codes and the SNMS-SWD.
Section III presents the λMDQ-SWD and simulation results.
Section IV describes the hardware architecture and resource
consumption of the CN update under the proposed algorithm.
Finally, conclusions are drawn in Section V.

different SWD algorithms using one clock cycle on both
ASIC and FPGA platforms. For fair comparison, all schemes
with 4-bits magnitude and 5-bits column index are designed
and synthesized. The SNMS-SWD is implemented by finding
the minimum value [23] based on external information. The
hardware architectures presented in Section IV-A are coded

Both ASIC and FPGA implementation results demonstrate
the advantages of the λMDQ-SWD scheme. Additionally, λ =
2 is more cost-effective in terms of hardware implementation
resource consumption.
V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed a variant of SWD called λMDQ-
SWD for SC-LDPC codes. Our λMDQ-SWD employs a
monotone double-ended queue structure and preserves the
minimum magnitude of target symbols to reduce the complex-
ity of the CN update. We also presented a detailed hardware
architecture for the CN update. Extensive simulations and
hardware implementations show that, the λMDQ-SWD with
λ = 2 achieves comparable decoding performance but much
lower resource consumption than the SNMS-SWD. Thus, we
recommend λMDQ-SWD with λ = 2 as the preferred choice
for SWD under the shuffled schedule.
