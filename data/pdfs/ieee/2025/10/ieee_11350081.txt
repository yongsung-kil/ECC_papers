Abstract—In this paper, we introduce a class of dualcontaining 2-generator quasi-cyclic codes over GF (4). By Hermitian construction, we show computation constructions of binary quantum codes with good parameters from quaternary quasi-cyclic codes. Furthermore, 3 new quantum codes [[42, 10, 9]], [[74, , 36, 9]] and [[78, 40, 9]] are derived from Hermitian dual-containing quasi-cyclic codes by an exhaustive search, along with 24 newly derived codes that enhance the previously best-known lower bounds on minimum distance.
Index Terms—quantum code, Hermitian construction , cyclotomic coset , quasicyclic codet
I. Introduction
Quantum error-correcting codes (QECCs), which safeguard delicate qubits against noise, are crucial for enabling quantum computing and quantum communication. The theory of quantum error-correcting codes (QECCs) has greatly advanced since the pioneering contributions of Shor [1] and Steane [2]. Ref. [3] establishes a fundamental link between quantum codes and classical quaternary codes. Later, In Refs. [4]–[6], there is a correspondence to self-orthogonal additive codes over Fq2 . In recent years, the Calderbank-Shor-Steane (CSS) construction and the Hermitian construction have become well-known methods for quantum codes. Many excellent quantum codes are constructed from self-orthogonal codes (also known as dual-containing codes) over finite fields. These self-orthogonal codes can be efficiently obtained using algebraic codes, including cyclic codes, constacyclic codes, quasi-cyclic codes, and algebraic geometry (AG) codes, and so on [7]–[10].
Quasi-cyclic code has a rich algebraic structure and excellent properties, which is a natural extension of cyclic code. [11]–[15]. If d is the greatest known value for which

Since many new classical bestknown codes are constructed from quasi-cyclic codes, much works has been focused on constructing quasi-cyclic codes [11], [16], [17]. Due to these excellent properties, scholars have considered whether quasi-cyclic codes can be applied to the construction of quantum codes. In 2011, Hagiwara et al. [19] introduced quantum LDPC codes derived from quasi-cyclic codes. The initial approach for building quantum codes from quasi-cyclic codes using symplectic, Euclidean, and Hermitian inner products is detailed in [20].
In [21], Ezerman and colleagues employ the quantum construction X to create quantum codes derived from quasi-cyclic codes that have larger Hermitian hulls. In Refs. [22], [23], Lv et al. derived several new quantum codes from dual-containing one- or two-generator quasicyclic codes by applying the symplectic construction followed by the Hermitian construction. One of difficult problems is that these codes needed to calculate the exact dual distances of these quasi-cyclic codes. In [24], Guan et al. determined the dimension of 2-generator quasi-cyclic codes under certain specific conditions; however, they did not establish the dimension of 2-generator quasi-cyclic codes in the general case.
Building on the aforementioned studies, we introduce a novel approach for creating quantum codes using 2generator self-orthogonal (or dual-containing) quasi-cyclic codes of index 2. The quantum codes developed in this work exhibit improved parameters compared to those currently documented in [26]. Here is a brief overview of the structure of this paper. Section 2 discusses the fundamentals of quasi-cyclic codes and quantum codes. In Section 3, a class of 2-generator quasi-cyclic codes is presented, along with a sufficient condition for their selforthogonality concerning the Hermitian inner product.

B. Binary Quantum codes
A K-dimensional subspace of 2n-dimensional Hilbert space (C2)⊗n is called a binary quantum code Q of length n, where C denotes the complex field and (C2)⊗n is the n-fold tensor power of C2. Such a binary quantum code Q is typically represented as [[n, k, d]]2, where k = log2K.
Hermitian construction is a well-known method for creating quantum codes, which connects quantum codes with classical self-orthogonal codes using the Hermitian inner product.
Lemma 2.1 (Corollary 19 [6], Hermitian construction) : Let [n, k, d]4 code C is C⊥h ⊂ C. Then there exists a pure quantum code with parameters [[n, 2k − n, d]]2 correspondence to d, where there are no vectors of weight ≤ d − 1 in C⊥h \ C.
Quantum codes can be derived from other quantum codes by the following propagation rules.
