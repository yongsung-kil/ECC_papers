Joint LDPC Code and Spreading Optimization for Multi-User Communications
Meilin He , Zheng Xing , Zhaoyang Zhang , Senior Member, IEEE, Rui Guo , and Haiquan Wang

Abstract— Closed-form expressions of the degree distribution of regular low-density parity-check codes and spreading length are presented over a Gaussian multiple access channel. Specifically, given a specific variable node degree dv, the theoretical expressions of check node degree dc and spreading length m are derived by fixed point equation array. Then, a reliable region where our multi-user system can decode successfully is obtained by solving uncertainty of the inverse problem. Finally, the optimal parameters (doc , dov, mo) of LDPC code degree distribution and spreading length in the reliable region are selected to maximize the transmission rate. Numerical results show that our derivation is effective and efficient, and exhibits much lower complexity than EXIT chart.
Index Terms— Multi-user system, degree distribution, spreading, maximum transmission rate.
I. INTRODUCTION
THE sixth generation (6G) mobile communication system has put forward unprecedented requirements for high speed, low delay and high reliability [1]. In the 6G vision, multi-device connectivity is one of its signature features [2]. The demand of this connection poses a severe challenge to the multi-access capability of the communication system.
In a multiple access channel (MAC), coding and spreading are important factors affecting its performance. Therefore, selecting suitable coding and spreading can greatly improve the performance of MAC to meet challenges. For coding, low-density parity-check (LDPC) [3] code is capable of outperforming other codes at reasonable complexity because of its low complexity and high efficiency [1]. For spreading, Song et al. [4] gives the optimal spreading sequence in Interleave-Division Multiple-Access (IDMA). Therefore, it is desired to optimize coding and spreading jointly for better performance.
Nowadays, many methods are employed to optimize multiuser systems, such as density evolution (DE) [5], [6] algorithm, the extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) [7], [8] chart and so on. In [9], LDPC codes are designed by using the row-combining and row-extending methods in rate-diverse MAC, with EXIT chart optimizing the degree-distribution to obtain the best decoding performance. They evaluate the iterative decoding performance of the multi-user system under different signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) with EXIT chart. Lim et al. [11] designs analog fountain codes and proposes a DE based framework to optimize weight sets and improve the bit error rate performance of both users. Zhang et al. [12] develops a multiedge-type DE-aided EXIT analysis to to calculate the decoding threshold for 5G-NR LDPC coded IDMA. These works above use DE, EXIT chart or their related improvement methods to design the multi-user system in MAC. However, these works generally require a large number of iterative processes. Therefore, it is worth trying to derive the closed-form expressions of the system parameters for optimizing the system performance.
Song et al. [13] and He et al. [14] derives the closed-form expressions of the repeat number of repeat-accumulate code and the spreading length to optimize the sum rate. In [15], the analytical expressions for the repeat number of repeat code and the number of convolutional codes in a parallel concatenated code are presented to achieve the maximum sum rate. Zhang et al. [16] designs spatially coupled repeat-accumulaterepeat (SC-RA-R) codes and obtains the maximum belief propagation threshold by adjusting the system parameters. Wang et al. [17] derives the expression of achievable rate in a Gaussian-approximate IDMA system and proves that the system can achieve the entire Shannon capacity region. The works above focus on closed-form expressions in multiuser systems to optimize different performance with different parameters. It is well known that LDPC codes exhibit excellent performance and great potential in diverse scenarios. Thus, it is a challenge to effectively design LDPC codes matching various system parameters for optimizing performance of multi-user systems.
In this letter, we give the closed-form expressions of the degree distribution of LDPC codes and spreading length for a specific variable node degree dv over a Gaussian MAC by fixed point method. Specifically, firstly, we track the output MI of each node and establish the fixed point equation array. Based on this equation array, we derive theoretical expressions of the check node degree dc and spreading length m. Secondly, we solve uncertainty of the inverse problem through mathematical set theory, and get a reliable region where the multi-user system can decode successfully. Finally, we select the optimal parameters (dvo, doc, mo) of LDPC code degree distribution and spreading length in the reliable region to maximize the transmission rate of the multi-user system. Numerical results show that our derivation is effective and efficient. All phenomena observed from our fixed point method coincide with the EXIT chart. Moreover, the complexity of our fixed point method is much lower than that of the EXIT chart. It is because that our method is not an iterative trial.

II. PRELIMINARY
In this section, we introduce the K-user regular LDPC code cascaded spreading system for the Gaussian MAC.
Firstly, the k-th user, 1 ≤ k ≤ K, sends an information bit vector to the (dv, dc)-LDPC encoder, where dv and dc denote variable and check node degree, respectively. Then the encoded vector is concatenated with a length-m spreading sequence. After spreading, an interleaver is employed to produce the transmitted signal. The transmission rate of the k-th user is r = (1 − dv/dc) /m.
Since each user uses the same (dv, dc)-LDPC code and the same length-m spreading sequence, the total transmission rate of the K-user system is R = (1 − dv/dc) K/m.
Note that our objective is to maximize the total transmission rate R of the system. In fact, it can be equivalent to maximizing the transmission rate r of a single user.
In the process of iterative decoding, the elementary signal estimation performed at the sum node provides an initial estimation of the target user’s transmitted symbol by treating interference from the remaining K −1 users as Gaussian noise. Then the k-th user performs single-user decoding by message passing on its own factor graph. Based on iterative decoding, we track the mutual information (MI) through the EXIT chart. At the l-th iteration, the final MI from variable node is

Note that Theorem 1 gives the explicit theoretical analytic

formulas of check node degree dc and the spreading length m. Based on the explicit expressions of dc and m, we avoid

the iterative process of MI between nodes through iterative

decoding algorithm compared with EXIT chart. It is because that Eq. (4) directly uses converged MI I∗ and Iv∗c as the independent variables of the functions f1(I∗, Iv∗c) and f2(I∗, Iv∗c). By contrast, EXIT chart requires a large number of iterations

to make the MI tend to convergence.

Obviously, when the decoding of the K-user system is

successful, I∗ and Iv∗c should be 1. Bringing I∗ = 1 and Iv∗c = 1 into Eq. (4), we can theoretically get the exact dc and m. However, due to J−1(1) = ∞, we can’t directly substitute Iv∗c = 1 and I∗ = 1 into the analytical expression of dc and m. Then we have to consider the case of decoding failure where I∗ and Iv∗c ∈ [0, 1). According to Eq. (4), all dc and m with the situation of decoding failure are obtained. We refer

to the area where these (dc, m) are located as the unreliable

region and fit its boundary line. Based on coding theory

and mathematical set theory, the other side of the boundary

line defines the reliable region. According to coding theory, decoding succeeds if I∗ = 1, and fails if I∗ ∈ [0, 1). Set theory

further supports this interpretation by treating the interval I∗ ∈ [0, 1] as a universal set, which is partitioned into two

disjoint subsets representing decoding success and decoding

failure, respectively. Then the pairs of dc and m which enable

successful decoding of multi-user systems in the reliable area

are clearly identified. Given a specific dv, the locally optimal dc∗ and m∗ (the term “locally” referring to the situation under the premise of a given dv rather than under all circumstances)
which achieve the maximum system transmission rate R are

obtained. Then according to the above method, by traversing dv, we pick out the optimal degree distribution (dov, dco) of LDPC code and spreading length mo from dv, dc∗ and m∗ to obtain the maximum transmission rate Ro.

It’s worth noting that dv, dc and m are all restricted to

integer values that adhere to practical system constraints.

As a result, the number of pairs (dc, m) is typically on the

order of 10 to 30, yielding a sufficiently small search range.

This enables straightforward exhaustive enumeration without

compromising analytical rigor and optimality.

In the reliable region, we selected the optimal parameters (dvo, doc, mo) of LDPC code degree distribution and spreading length, which maximize the transmission rate of the multi-user system. In this letter, we only focused on regular LDPC codes over Gaussian MAC. Extending our proposed method to irregular LDPC codes is challenging, since the non-uniformity of node degrees results in a complex system of strongly coupled parameters, making it analytically intractable to derive closedform solutions. Nevertheless, there exist other structured LDPC codes, such as sparse regression LDPC codes [18],
