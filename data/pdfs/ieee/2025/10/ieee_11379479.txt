Abstract-Soft-decision decoding plays a crucial role in 
achieving near-capacity error correction performance for 
modern communication standards such as 5G and 6G. 
However, existing soft-decision decoders often suffer 
from high complexity, large area overhead, and limited 
throughput, making them less suitable for high-speed 
applications. This paper proposes a high-throughput VLSI 
architecture for soft-decision decoding based on the 
Ordered Reliability Bits Guessing Random Additive 
Noise Decoding (ORBGRAND) algorithm. The novelty 
of the proposed approach lies in its pipelined and 
parallelized noise-guessing engine, lightweight reliability 
sorting mechanism, and optimized memory hierarchy, 
which collectively minimize latency and improve energy 
efficiency. The overall methodology involves algorithmic 
optimization, architecture design in HDL, simulation 
under 
AWGN 
channels, 
FPGA 
synthesis, 
and 
benchmarking against existing GRAND-based decoders. 
Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed 
architecture achieves multi-Gbps throughput, lower 
decoding 
latency, 
and 
superior 
error-correction 
performance with reduced area and power consumption. 
These results highlight its potential as an efficient solution 
for next-generation high-speed communication systems. 
 
Keywords— Error-Correction Performance, FPGA/ASIC 
Implementation, High-Throughput VLSI Architecture, 
ORBGRAND, Soft-Decision Decoding. 
 
 
I.INTRODUCTION 
 
Error correction coding is vital in the contemporary 
communication systems, which guarantees reliable 
data communication over noisy media [1]. The 5G 
and 6G demand very high reliability and low-latency 
connections of communication systems to support the 
autonomous vehicles, industrial IoT, and real-time 
multimedia streaming applications [2][3]. Decoding 
soft-decision which makes use of probabilistic 
information provided by the channel has been 
demonstrated to be able to provide almost capacity 
error correction performance relative to its hard-
decision counterparts [4][5]. But the traditional soft-
decision decoders such as belief propagation based 
on LDPC codes and successive cancellation list 
decoding based on Polar codes are computationally 
complex, 
highly 
memory 
intensive 
and 
low 
throughput in hardware implementation [6][7]. The 
challenges render it hard to attain multi-Gbps 
decoding rates within severe area and power limits, 
which is essential to next generation communication 
systems [8][9]. 
 
To overcome these constraints, the GRAND family 
of algorithms has recently attracted much interest due 
to its codebook-agnostic near-maximum likelihood 
(ML) 
decoding 
approach. 
Specifically, 
the 
ORBGRAND variant enhances performance through 
the reduction of guesses required to resolve the 
transmitted code word by prioritizing noise patterns 
according 
to 
reliability 
ordering. 
Although 
ORBGRAND demonstrates encouraging decoding 
performance, current hardware implementations are 
unable to realize complete parallelism or incur the 
bottleneck of latency caused by sequential noise-
pattern generation and sorting overhead. The paper 
suggests a new high-throughput VLSI architecture to 
soft-decision code (using ORBGRAND), and is 
particularly designed to support short and medium 
block length codes. The design proposed is a 
pipelined parallel noise-guessing engine, a hardware 
friendly and lightweight reliability sorting system, 
and an optimized memory hierarchy that can easily 
store and retrieve soft information. The design 
methodology entails algorithmic optimization, HDL 
data path and control logic modelling, heavy 
simulation with AWGN channel models, FPGA 
synthesis, and complete resource and power analysis. 
Experimental results illustrate that the architecture 
attains multi-Gbps throughput, lower latency, and 
better 
error-correction 
capability 
than 
current 
GRAND-based decoders, whereas having lower area

II.RELATED WORKS 
 
The Low-Density Parity-Check codes have emerged 
as the solution that can greatly improve the reliability 
of data in the contemporary NAND flash memory. 
The process of decoding may be done either through 
hard-decision or through soft-decision methods of 
decoding but both methods have the disadvantage of 
having lower error correction capabilities when the 
LLRs are erroneous. To address this issue, Cui et 
al.[10] introduced The optimization methods of the 
two types of decoding are more or less the same: 
LLR. They start with developing a threshold voltage 
distribution model of 3D floating-gate triple level cell 
NAND flash. They suggest a scheme of quantization 
of LLR based decoding (soft- and hard-decision) 
using this model. They also selectively amplify small 
LLR values, which is important to layer min-sum 
decoding, to obtain more accurate representation of 
the LLR. In hard-decision decoding, the new modes 
significantly increase the ability of the mode to 
correct errors compared with traditional ones. Hard-
decision decoding fails, which leads to soft-decision 
decoding, is examined in the study; the influence of 
reference voltage arrangement on the calculation of 
LLR is studied and the quantization scheme is 
applied. The results of the simulation show that the 
given approach brings several orders of magnitude in 
terms of FER. 
 
Soft-decision decoding algorithm of the cyclic codes 
and specifically the maximum likelihood decoding 
algorithm is associated with a significantly improved 
performance in comparison to the traditional 
algebraic decoding, but has high computational 
complexity. To address this challenge, Chen et 
al.[11] propose a better soft-decision decoding 
algorithm with cyclic information set, and a good 
implementation architecture. The algorithm exploits 
the cyclic nature of the codes to form a number of 
cyclic information sequences through the use of 
circular shifting to form the cyclic information set. 
Few 
candidate 
information 
sequences 
are 
subsequently created efficiently in terms of an 
iterative computation process, and candidate code 
words are acquired in terms of a brief cyclic code 
encoding process. In order to become even more 
practical, candidates sequences are generated and the 
best code word is chosen with the help of a systolic 
array-based hardware architecture. To measure the 
performance of the error correction and optimize the 
decoder parameters, a platform of emulating is built. 
It is shown that in the right choice of parameters 
proposed algorithm will achieve a bit error rate 
comparable to that of ML at low complexity.

decoding algorithm of the ReedMuller codes has

HIGH-THROUGHPUT 
ORBGRAND-BASED 
VLSI 
DECODING FRAMEWORK 
The proposed methodology will be to design and 
implement a high-throughput VLSI architecture of 
soft-decision decoding (using the ORBGRAND 
algorithm). It starts with the algorithm selection and 
optimization, in which the noise-guessing order and 
reliability sorting is customized to be hardware 
efficient 
yet 
with 
near 
maximum 
likelihood 
performance. Second, a parallelized and pipelined 
VLSI architecture is designed with optimized 
memory hierarchy for the storage of soft information 
and syndrome updates, and lightweight control logic 
for pattern noise generation and termination of 
decoding. 
The 
architecture 
is 
subsequently 
implemented in HDL (Verilog/VHDL) and simulated 
based on standard channel models (AWGN) and 
code types (LDPC, Polar) to ensure functionality and 
gather BER/FER performance at different SNR 
levels. After functional verification, the synthesis of 
FPGA or ASIC is conducted to measure area, power 
consumption, and maximum clock frequency. Lastly, 
the overall performance analysis and benchmarking 
is performed to compare the decoding throughput, 
latency and error-correction ability to the current 
GRAND-based decoders, where it is noted that it has 
improved the speed, energy-efficient, and overall 
hardware performance.

ORBGRAND Algorithm Integration

of decoding that utilizes the information about the 
reliability of bits to direct the search of the 
appropriate code word. As opposed to classic 
decoders, which implement an iterative or exhaustive 
search mechanism, ORBGRAND pays attention to 
the production of probable error patterns relying on 
the credibility of received bits and thus greatly 
decreases the complexity of the computation but still 
provides a high error-corrected capability.

VLSI Architecture Design 
The ORBGRAND VLSI architecture proposed is to 
fulfill the goal of high-throughput soft-decision 
decoding by taking advantage of pipelining, 
parallelism, and effective memory management. The 
main block of the architecture is a set of parallel 
noise-guessing engines which are tasked with the 
role of generating and testing candidate error 
patterns in parallel. Since the production of error 
patterns is shared among multiple engines, the 
design decreases the decoding latency of sequential 
processing of logistic weight partitions. Given a 
received code word of length n and a maximum 
logistic weight LWmax , the number of parallel

Simulation and Hardware Implementation 
To confirm the high-throughput VLSI architecture 
suggested to be used in ORBGRAND, the steps of 
extensive simulation and hardware implementation 
are carried out. The first test is functional 
verification of the design, to ensure that the design is 
functionally correct, the standard test vectors are 
produced by inputting code words when the 
simulated channel, e.g. AWGN or Rayleigh fading, 
is present. These test vectors are the equivalent soft-
decision LLRs that are inputted to the decoder. 
Functional simulations are done with industry tools 
like ModelSim or Vivado, which enable verification 
of both error-pattern generation logic and the code 
word validation units. Timing simulations are also 
done to confirm that the pipeline stages, parallel

IV.RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
 
The proposed high throughput VLSI architecture of 
ORBGRAND has much to be desired in both, 
decoding and hardware performance. The simulation 
has shown that a decoder can support multi-Gbps 
throughput with a latency per code word that is 
inversely proportional to the number of parallel 
noise-guessing engines that it has. The error-
correction rate is large, and the frame error rate 
(FER) of the architecture is below 10−7 with short 
and moderate length codes at moderate SNR, such as 
LDPC and Polar codes. The power and energy 
analysis indicates that the energy per bit reduces 
significantly 
with 
the 
effective 
pipelining, 
parallelism and efficient memory access. The gain of 
the proposed design is at least 2 dB FER when 
compared to hard-decision GRAND variants, and 
the decoding complexity of the proposed design is 
less by a factor of at least an order of magnitude 
compared to existing GRAND-based decoders and 
more traditional soft-decision algorithms. Overall, 
the results confirm the statement that the architecture 
can achieve a balance between high throughput and 
low latency on the one hand and, on the other hand, 
energy-efficient decoding and be applicable in the 
next-generation of communication systems such as 
5G and 6G.

V.CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORKS 
 
This paper introduces a high throughput VLSI 
design of ORBGRAND soft-decision decoding 
which combines parallel noise-guessing engines, 
pipelining and an efficient memory hierarchy to 
provide efficient and low latency error correction. Its 
simulation and hardware performance indicate that 
the proposed design can enable multi-Gbps 
throughput, 
lower 
latency, 
and 
high 
energy 
efficiency and yet exhibit high FER and BER of the 
design with respect to different SNR levels. The 
architecture 
demonstrates 
high 
performance 
improvement with reduced complexity over hard-
decision GRAND and traditional soft-decision 
decoders, and this could be used in the next-
generation communication systems, such as 5G and 
6G networks. 
 
Future studies can be aimed at elaborating the 
architecture by covering longer block-length codes 
and more complicated channel models, e.g. MIMO 
fading channels. Adaptive pipeline depth, dynamic 
parallelism optimizations may also enable an 
increased 
energy 
efficiency 
with 
different 
throughput demands. By incorporating new low-
power devices and approximate computing methods, 
the area and energy use can be decreased and the 
design can fit the IoT and edge communication 
devices. Prediction of error-patterns assisted by 
machine-learning might be investigated, leading to 
an increase in the decoding accuracy and a decrease 
in the latency.
