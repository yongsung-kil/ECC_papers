Efficient Design of MET-LDPC Code Ensembles via Multinomial Parameter Optimization
Linh Nguyen and Tuy Tan Nguyen* Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering FAMU-FSU College of Engineering & Center for Advanced Power Systems
Florida State University Tallahassee, FL 32310, USA ltn25d@fsu.edu, tuy.nguyen@fsu.edu

Abstract—Designing multi-edge type low-density parity-check (MET-LDPC) code ensembles that satisfy structural constraints while achieving near-capacity performance remains a challenging problem. Existing methods, including those based on differential evolution, have improved flexibility but still face limitations such as high computational cost and the need for manual configuration of edge patterns. We propose an optimization-based method that explores diverse, structurally feasible edge distribution candidates and optimizes node distributions via a constrained linear system formulation. A dual annealing optimizer is then applied to identify node profiles that maximize the decoding threshold. Evaluations on both the binary erasure channel and the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise channel across multiple code rates show that our method consistently produces ensembles with strong threshold performance and low edge complexity, highlighting its practicality and adaptability.
Index Terms—MET-LDPC code ensembles, dual annealing, near-capacity performance, optimization, decoding threshold
I. INTRODUCTION
Multi-edge-type low-density parity-check (MET-LDPC) codes, originally proposed by Divsalar through protographbased constructions and irregular repeat accumulate (IRA) structures [1], extend regular and irregular LDPC codes by incorporating multiple edge types and diverse degree profiles. This flexibility enables ensembles with improved thresholds and adaptability, making MET-LDPC codes particularly attractive for modern systems compared to classical block codes such as Golay [2] and Hadamard [3]. METLDPC codes have since been deployed in next-generation wireless systems, deep space communication, and hardwareefficient decoding. Recent research has investigated decoding techniques optimized for fifth-generation new radio (5G NR) [4], cross-layer scheduling methods for resolving memory contention in pipelined decoders [5], unequal error protection strategies [6], and applications in coded modulation systems [7]. Furthermore, advancements have expanded MET-LDPC design to network-coded multi-source relaying systems [8], and to quantum key distribution [9], [10].
Despite their promising applications, designing METLDPC codes remains a challenging task. A key difficulty lies in identifying edge and node degree distributions that satisfy critical structural constraints such as socket count equality,

non-negativity, and target code rate, while achieving nearcapacity decoding thresholds [11]. The space of feasible distributions is both highly constrained and sparsely populated, making random sampling and brute-force search impractical.
Early design methods addressed this by imposing simplifying assumptions, such as fixing the number of edge types [6] or limiting check node degrees. While reducing complexity, these constraints restrict connectivity diversity and limit threshold performance. To overcome this, authors in [12] proposed fixing the edge-distribution matrices and optimizing the node distribution via constrained differential evolution (DE) within the nullspace of the feasibility system, using Gaussian-approximated thresholds. Though efficient, this method lacks structural flexibility. As presented in [13], authors later introduced a joint DE-based optimization of both edge and node distributions, evaluated via exact density evolution. While more general, their method is computationally intensive and often requires manually constrained edge patterns.
To address these limitations, we propose a new method that optimizes node distributions given a fixed edge distribution. Structurally diverse and feasible edge matrices are first generated, and feasibility constraints are reformulated as a linear program for fast evaluation. We replace differential evolution with dual annealing (DA), a global optimizer well-suited for high-dimensional, non-convex spaces and requiring minimal tuning. This yields a flexible pipeline capable of discovering high-threshold MET-LDPC ensembles.
We evaluate the proposed framework on two representative channel models: the binary erasure channel (BEC) and the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise (BI-AWGN) channel. Across both channels and a wide range of code rates, our method consistently produces MET-LDPC ensembles with competitive thresholds and moderate decoding complexity. Compared to prior designs, the ensembles derived using our approach achieve smaller capacity gaps and better structural efficiency without requiring manual degree selection or predefined node types.

B. Finding Strictly Positive Solutions
To ensure the feasibility of the MET-LDPC ensemble construction, it is critical to determine whether there exists at least one node distribution vector ζ ≻ 0 that satisfies all the linear equality constraints. In other words, we seek to verify whether the intersection K ∩ Rn>v0+nc ̸= ∅, where K = {ζ ∈ Rnv+nc : Aζ = 0}, contains any strictly positive vectors.
To address this question, we invoke a classical result from convex geometry, specifically a variant of the Farkas lemma [16]. This result establishes a fundamental duality for linear systems: given any matrix A ∈ Rm×n, exactly one of the following two statements holds:
1) ∃ u ∈ Rn, u ≻ 0 such that Au = 0, 2) ∃ v ∈ Rm such that vT A ≻ 0.
This mutually exclusive condition provides a practical way to determine whether the feasible set K ∩ Rn>v0+nc is nonempty, and thus whether a valid MET-LDPC node distribution can be constructed.
To explicitly check whether such a strictly positive solution exists, we formulate the following linear program:

If this linear program is feasible, then K ∩ Rn>v0+nc ≠ ∅, meaning that a valid node distribution exists that satisfies the SCE and rate constraints. This solution can subsequently be normalized to satisfy the total bit-weight constraint, completing the construction of a feasible MET-LDPC ensemble.

1) Threshold θ∗: the maximum channel parameter for which successful BP decoding can be guaranteed asymptotically.

E. BER Performance Evaluation
To assess the practical decoding performance of the proposed MET-LDPC ensemble, we compare its bit error rate (BER) performance against the widely used accumulaterepeat-4-jagged-accumulate (AR4JA) code [17] and spatially coupled LDPC (SC-LDPC) code under both BEC and BIAWGN channel conditions, as shown in Fig. 1.
Fig. 1(a) shows the BER performance over the BEC. All three codes exhibit similar slopes in the waterfall region, indicating comparable threshold behavior. However, in the high signal-to-noise ratio SN R = Eb/N0 region (> 3.5dB), the proposed ensemble achieves lower BERs. Notably, AR4JA exhibits an early error floor at 10−6, likely due to its structural limitations such as stopping sets or limited minimum distance. SC-LDPC performs better and remains closer to the proposed code but still lags slightly in the ultra reliable region. The proposed ensemble shows a steeper decline and no observable floor, suggesting better erasure correction at high reliability.
In the BI-AWGN channel as illustrated in Fig. 1(b), performance gaps widen. The proposed code consistently achieves the lowest BER across the entire SNR range, with an earlier onset of the waterfall region and a significantly steeper slope. It maintains error free decoding down to BER < 10−6, without signs of a floor. SC-LDPC code again performs competitively and outperforms AR4JA, benefiting from its spatial coupling, but its curve flattens slightly at higher SNRs. AR4JA shows the weakest performance, with a visible error

This work presents an automated and scalable approach
for designing MET-LDPC code ensembles using constrained
optimization. Our method jointly explores the edge distribu-
tion space and node allocation to construct codes with strong
decoding performance. Evaluations on both BEC and BI-
AWGN channels show that the proposed ensembles achieve
thresholds close to capacity while maintaining moderate
edge complexity. Compared to prior designs, our approach
yields consistent improvements across various code rates,
demonstrating its effectiveness and adaptability for modern
communication systems.
