QC-LDPC Codes with Girth Eight Based on Independent Row-Column Mapping Sequence
Lei Wang, Xing Zhang, Feng Yu, Yaoxian Fu, and Yingguan Wang

Abstract—Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes are known to have algebraic structure, which suits hardware implementation. However, low girth makes their performances worse than random structured LDPC codes. Based on independent row-column mapping sequence, this work proposed a generalized method of constructing QC-LDPC codes with girth eight, which enlarges the class of girth eight QC-LDPC codes to a fairly general series. Simulation results show that the bit error rate of the constructed codes is close to that of progressive edge growth (PEG) based QC-LDPC codes under iterative decoding.

II. CONSTRUCTION MODEL

I. INTRODUCTION
T HERE are two major categories of methods emerged for the construction of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes after Gallager’s discovery [1] and MacKay’s rediscovery [2]. One set of methods is known as random construction [3], [4], while the other one is based on structured algebraic construction.
Random construction is not easy to implement in hardware, due to considerable storage space needed for the irregular connections between check and variable nodes in the Tanner graph [5]. In actual implementations, structured constructions have been strongly preferred because they result in much less storage space and more straightforward parallelism in the decoders. Of all the structured LDPC codes, Quasi-Cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes [6], [7] belong to one particular class. Codes from this class have a parity check matrix consisting of sub-matrices with circulant structures.
Due to the nature of “belief propagation” (BP) [8], the presence of short cycles in the Tanner graph of a given LDPC code has normally a negative effect on the code performance. Therefore, in designing LDPC codes, it is desirable to construct codes free of short cycles.
Zhang et al. presented a GCDg8 algorithm for construction of girth-eight QC-LDPC codes from greatest common divisor in [9]. However, GCDg8 focuses on the case that the exponent matrix entries have the structure ai × (j − 1), where ai is the ith element of a designed sequence and j is the column index. V. CONCLUSION
Based on independent row-column mapping sequence, a new generalized method for the construction of QC-LDPC codes with a girth of eight has been presented. This method has its advantage that not only row and column weight, but also their mapping sequences can be chosen arbitrarily, which enlarges the class of structured QC-LDPC codes. Moreover, the size of CPM can be any number fulfilling the lower bound proved in Theorem 1.
