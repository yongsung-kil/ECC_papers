Decoding Algorithm of LDPC Codes Based on Genetic Algorithm

Sheng Huang, Kai Tian, Fangfang Tian, Jianguo Yuan
Key Lab of Optical Fiber Communication Technology, Chongqing University of Posts and Telecommunications, Chongqing 400065, China
E-mail: huangs@cqupt.edu.cn

Abstract—In order to improve the decoding performance of low density parity check (LDPC) codes, a sum-product genetic decoding (SPGD) algorithm based on genetic algorithm (GA) is proposed. By means of the classical decoding algorithm of sum-product algorithm (SPA) of LDPC codes, variable node information generated by each hard decision of iteration is regarded as individuals that constitute initial population of GA. The decoded optimal solutions are produced by a series of genetic operations. Simulation results show that under the premise of almost no increase in complexity, the proposed SPGD algorithm has the superior decoding error correction performance, better practicability and higher net coding gain (NCG).
Keywords-sum-product genetic decoding (SPGD) algorithm; genetic algorithm (GA); sum product algorithm (SPA); net coding gain (NCG)
I. INTRODUCTION
Low density parity check (LDPC) codes, first discovered by Gallager in 1962 [1], are kind of linear block codes based on sparse parity check matrix. The bit error rate (BER) performance of LDPC codes can approach Shannon limit. In recent years, LDPC codes have become the most effective coding technology of error control coding, which is widely used in many fields [2-4]. After a phase of decoding LDPC codes by use of hard decision, soft iterative decoding scheme is developed, which has better decoding performance than former. Therefore, it is necessary to research in-depth on the iterative decoding algorithm that can improve the error correction performance of LDPC codes.
Genetic algorithm (GA) is a global optimization and optimal search algorithm first proposed by Holland [5]. It simulates natural selection and evolution by a series of genetic operations. Many researchers have been studying the decoding algorithm combined with GA for many years, and decoding performance is greatly improved [6-8]. In this paper, a sum-product genetic decoding (SPGD) decoding algorithm of LDPC codes are proposed, which is combined with SPA and GA. The initial population is generated by each hard decision of SPA iteration. Then, the decoded optimal solutions are generated after a series of genetic operations, including selection, crossover and mutation. The proposed SPGD algorithm has better decoding performance than SPA. Furthermore, there is almost no increase in

complexity. The organization of this paper is as follows. Section II
gives a brief introduction of SPA and GA. Section III presents a new decoding algorithm which is called SPGD algorithm. Simulation results and performance analysis are given in Section IV. Section V is the conclusion of this paper.

II. SUM-PRODUCT ALGORITHM AND GENETIC ALGORITHM
Currently the most popular decoding algorithm is message passing algorithm (MPA), one of which is SPA which has good error correction performance. SPA implements the parallel decoding by information transfer and sharing of iteration between variable nodes (VN) and check nodes (CN). What’s more, GA can be regard as global optimization and intelligent search algorithm implemented by information transmission.

B. Genetic algorithm (GA)
As a probabilistic optimization algorithm with inherent parallelism and global searching ability, GA can be used to solve the problems in which the objective function is non-differentiable, discontinuous, stochastic and highly nonlinear [10].
GA begins with the generation of initial population. According to the principle of survival of the fittest, each individual has a fitness function which reflects the individuals’ ability of adapting to the environment. In the iterative process of populations, some individuals with good adaptability survive, while some individuals with poor adaptability are eliminated. Then, the new individuals are generated after the genetic operations which include natural selection, crossover and mutation. The best fitted individuals will be copied to the next generation as the parent of the iterative population via natural selection operation. The crossover operation exchanges a section of genes of two selected individuals to generate new individuals. Mutation operation changes gene of selected individuals in order to generate new individuals. The purpose of genetic operations is to retain excellent individuals in the population, to generate new individuals among other suboptimal individuals, and to improve the overall adaptability of populations. After a series of loop

iterations, the adaptability of individuals in the population is continuously improved, after that, the optimal solution is generated eventually [11].
III. THE NOVEL SUM-PRODUCT GENETIC DECODING ALGORITHM
Based on the above analysis, it is effective to take advantage of the high parallelism of GA to obtain the optimal solutions. By means of the proposed SPGD algorithm, decoding problem is transformed into the problem of searching for the optimal solution. Therefore, the decoding algorithm of LDPC codes combined with GA is feasible.
In SPGD algorithm, the received sequence from channel is sent to SPA decoder. Then, a series of candidate sequences will be generated by multiple information iterations of SPA. Finally, the optimal solution is obtained after multiple genetic operations and population iteration by use of these candidate sequences. Flowchart of the novel SPGD algorithm is shown in Fig. 2.

IV. SIMULATIONS AND PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
A. Analysis of simulation results
This section will discuss the decoding simulations of (841, 756) regular QC-LDPC code and (3465, 3187) regular QC-LDPC code under the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel and BPSK modulation. For performance comparison, the performance curves of BPSK without coding and SPA are also simulated. The simulation curves are shown in Figs. 4 and 5. Parameters are set as follows: the size of population is 20; the iteration number of GA is 20; crossover probability is Pc=0.8, mutation probability is Pm=0.001.
As descripted in Fig. 4, at the BER of 10-5, 10-6 and 10-7 for LDPC (841,756), SPGD algorithm can increase 0.14dB, 0.2dB and 0.25dB net coding gain (NCG) respectively compared with SPA having good decoding performance.

V. CONCLUSIONS
This article presents a novel SPGD algorithm of LDPC codes based on GA. By means of the classical decoding algorithm SPA of LDPC codes, variable node information generated by each hard decision of iteration is regarded as

individuals that constitute initial population. The decoded optimal solutions are generated after a series of genetic operations. Under the premise of almost no increase in complexity, the proposed SPGD algorithm has good decoding error correction performance and NCG is increased. The simulation results show that SPGD algorithm has good decoding performance than classic SPA.
