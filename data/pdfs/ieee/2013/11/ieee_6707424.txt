I. 
INTRODUCTION 
For decoding low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1], the 
belief-propagation (BP) decoding have been shown to achieve 
near Shannon limit performance using iterative decoding [2], 
[ 3] but often with heavy decoding complexity [3], [4]. The 
bit-flipping (BF) decoding is simple but it often incurs higher 
decoded bit error rate (BER) than BP decoding. The weighted 
BF (WBF) decoding [5] offers a good trade-off between the 
decoding complexity and the BER performance. A modified 
WBF (MWBF) algorithm [6] was presented as a further 
improvement of WBF decoding. An improvement of MWBF 
(IMWBF) algorithm was also proposed to improve the 
decoded BER of MWBF [7]. 
In [5]-[7], the flipping functions of the WBF, MWBF and 
IMWBF algorithms are developed by concept and experiments 
without theoretic deduction; thus it is difficult to find a 
systematic method to optimize these decoding algorithms. In 
[9], Wu et at. explored the relationship between WBF decoding 
and BP-like decoding. The relationship can help us to develop 
a WBF algorithm from one type of BP-like decoding algorithm 
and vice versa. In this contribution, we reveal that the 
relationship between WBF decoding and BP decoding explored 
in [9] is not consistent at the specified hard-decision mapping. 
Then, a theoretic approach to the belief-propagation-based 
weighted bit-flipping (BP-based WBF) algorithm [9] is 
proposed. The BP-based WBF flipping function is a general 
form of that flipping function in well-known bit-flipping 
algorithms, such as WBF, MWBF and IMWBF algorithms. 
Moreover, the theoretic approach can help us to develop an 
efficiency WBF algorithm to improve decoding performance or 
offer a flexible decoding algorithm for performance and 
complexity trade-off.

VI. 
REMARKS AND CONCLUSIONS 
The BP-based WBF decoding algorithm has been derived 
in [9] by exploring the relationship between BP-like decoding 
and weight bit-flipping decoding, however the exploration is 
not very careful. In this letter, a theoretic approach to the same 
algorithm is introduced. Since the decoding algorithm can be 
easily simplified to the form of IMWBF, MWBF, WBF and 
BF algorithms, there is an open issue for the application: 
Using the idea of bootstrap decoding [8], the BP-based WBF 
decoding algorithm could offer a high flexible 
performance/complexity trade-off because the algorithm can 
be programmed into any reduced version by setting different 
parameters. Thus the implementation of a hybrid decoder only 
requires a single BP-based WBF decoder.
