Energy-Efficient LDPC Layered Decoder

Jianjun Zhang, Da Wang and Ye Jin
State Key Laboratory ofAdvanced Optical Communication Systems & Networks School ofElectronics Engineering and Computer Science Peking University, Beijing, China {zhangjianjun206, wangda, jinye}@pku,edu.cn

Abstract - It is very important to reduce the energy consumption of low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoders, especially for the intelligent and mobile terminals in green communications. This paper presents a novel energy-efficient layered decoder (EELD) to save the power consumption of quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) decoders based on the layered belief propagation (LBP) algorithm. The EELD forces a check-node process into asleep, when all the reliabilities of its neighboring variable nodes exceed the lazy threshold. The EELD also adopts the lower limit of the beliefs, rather than all the beliefs, of the neighboring variable nodes to compare with the lazy threshold in order to decide the state of check-node process. The EELD can skip the useless check-node processes to save energy consumption with slight additional complexity. Simulation results show that the EELD can improve the power efficiencies of the decoders by up to 28% for WiMAX codes without loss of error performances.
Keywords - Low-density parity-check codes, energy efficient, layered beliefpropagation, WiMAX.
I. INTRODUCTION
One of the main challenges for future green communications is the reduction of power consumption [1]. With the increasing miniaturization and multi-function of consumer electronic terminals, it is very important to reduce the energy consumption of each part in terminals [2]. In terminals, the channel decoder is an indispensable module, which should save its energy consumption as far as possible to achieve better energy efficiency for green communications [3]. Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes [4] have been widely used in many communication systems, such as 802.l6e (WiMAX) [5] and 802.lln [6], due to its capacity­ achieving performance and the parallel-processing capacity [7]. Therefore, it is very meaningful to research about the energy­ efficient decoding algorithm for QC-LDPC codes [8].
The most popular LDPC decoding algorithm is the layered belief propagation (LBP) algorithm [9]. The LBP is a sequential schedule, and its convergence rate is twice as fast as the flooding schedule [4]. However, the energy consumption of the LBP decoder also cannot satisfy the demand of the future communications.Recently, a residual belief propagation (RBP) [10] and its improved algorithms [11]-[13] are proposed. All of these RBP algorithms have significant less iteration times and lower error floors than the sequential schedules. However, they increase the computation complexity due to excessive storages, residual computations, sorting operations and look-up table operations.
Therefore, this paper proposes a new dynamic schedule, referred to as the energy-efficient layered decoder (EELD ),

based on the LBP to save the energy consumption of the useless processes without increasing the complexity. The EELD forces the check-node processes into asleep when the check nodes satisfy the lazy condition, in which all the beliefs of its neighboring variable nodes exceed the lazy threshold (L'h)' For simplifying the lazy schedule, the EELD adopts the normalized min-sum algorithm to obtain the lower limit of the beliefs of the variable nodes that are connected with the check node. Then the EELD compares the lazy threshold with the lower limit directly, rather than with every variable-node belief. The EELD skips the check-node processes that have little effect to the belief propagation. Therefore, the EELD can save the energy of the useless processes and reduce the energy consumption of LDPC decoders.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. The QC­ LDPC codes and the LBP are reviewed in Section II. Section III proposes the EELD based on the LBP. The simulation results are presented in Section IV. Finally, Section V draws the conclusions.

A row of H is also called a layer, and it corresponds to a check-node process in the LBP. A row of Hb is called a block row, and it contains p layers in H.

The mean value of noise is zero, and the average power is No/2, where No is the noise power spectral density. V. CONCLUSION
For green communications, a novel EELD was proposed to reduce the power consumption of LDPC decoders. In the EELD , the lazy condition could decide whether the layer should be forced into sleep or not. The lazy condition was satisfied when the lazy threshold was smaller than the lower limit of the beliefs of the variable nodes connecting to the check node. The EELD could reduce the average iteration times and improve the power efficiency PE. Simulation results indicated that the EELD can reduce the energy consumption of the decoders by up to 28% for WiMAX codes. Simulation results also showed that the EELD does not decrease performances compared with the traditional LBP algorithm. The proposed EELD is very useful for decreasing the power consumption of consumer electronic terminals and base stations in the future wireless communication systems.
