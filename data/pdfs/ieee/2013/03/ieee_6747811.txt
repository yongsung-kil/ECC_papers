Third International Conference on Information Science and Technology March 23-25, 2013; Yangzhou, Jiangsu, China
An Efficient LDPC Encoder Based on Block-Row-Cycle Structure for CMMB
Lei Liu, Peng Zhang and Ziliang Lin

Abstract—Concerning the high encoding complexity of LDPC codes, an efficient encoder based on block-row-cycle structure for CMMB is proposed, which is able to take full advantage of the characteristics of the sparse parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of row weight. Experimental results show that the proposed encoder can reduce dramatically the memory requirements in hardware implementation, and improve the encoding rate at the same time.
I. INTRODUCTION
LOW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes were first invented by Gallager in 1962 and rediscovered by Mackay and Neal in 1996 [1], [2]. In the past few years, LDPC codes had been attracting more and more attentions due to their excellent error-correcting ability and low decoding complexity. For instance, LDPC codes were used as channel coding in DVB-S2, IEEE 802.11n/16e, DMB-TH, and DTTB, etc. In Chinese Mobile Multimedia Broadcasting (CMMB) standard, LDPC code was also used as Forward Error Correction Code combined with Reed-Solomon (RS) code [3].
LDPC codes exhibit an asymptotically better performance than turbo codes and they admit a wide range of tradeoffs between performance and decoding complexity, since they can approach the Shannon limits for suitably large block length. One major criticism concerning LDPC codes has been their apparent high encoding complexity. Whereas turbo codes can be encoded in linear time, a straightforward encoder implementation for an LDPC code has complexity quadratic to the block length.
Lots of efforts had been made to develop computationally efficient encoders by many coding investigators. One of encoding methods with linear complexity, named as RU method or approximately lower triangular (ALT) method, was proposed by Richardson and Urbanke [4]. Richardson and Urbanke point out that linear time encoding is achievable through careful linear manipulation of LDPC codes, and methods based on greedy algorithm to preprocess the parity-check matrix H and a set of matrix operations to perform the actual encoding are proposed at the same time. However, it is not easy to find a good sparse LU decomposition for arbitrary H matrices, and especially some matrices cannot be decomposed. Other various encoding methods include MacKay’s lower triangular method [6], Haley’s iterative encoding method [7], and Johnson’s quasi-cyclic method [8].
For the ALT encoding method, the parity-check matrix after elementary permutations is divided into a few submatrices, which lose its characteristics. In allusion to the shortcoming, an improved ALT encoding method is proposed. According to this method, an efficient LDPC encoder based on block-row-cycle structure is designed for CMMB, which is capable of making the best of inherent characteristics of the parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of nonzero elements’ number per row. This encoder can reduce dramatically the memory requirements in hardware implementation, and improve the encoding rate at the same time.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. The next section describes the improved ALT method. In Section III, a LDPC encoder based on block-row-cycle structure for CMMB is given. Experiments are presented in Section IV. Finally, Section V draws the conclusion of this paper.

III. A LDPC ENCODER BASED ON BLOCK-ROW-CYCLE STRUCTURE FOR CMMB
There are two binary LDPC codes with different rate 1/2 and 3/4 in CMMB system. The former is a (9216, 3, 6) regular code, and the latter is a (9216, 3, 12) regular one. In the standard, the sparse parity-check matrices are specified, which are cyclic but not quasi-cyclic. For the rate 1/2 (3/4), the entire parity-check matrix H can be derived from the first 18(9) rows, which shift 36 columns cyclically every 18 (9) rows. We denote consecutive 18(9) rows as a block row, then H is divided into 256 block rows. The parameters of two LDPC codes are shown in Table I.

Since H is cyclic and has equal row weight, it is reasonable to believe that the improved ALT method is quite suitable for the two LDPC codes. According to this method, an efficient LDPC encoder based on block-row-cycle structure is proposed. This LDPC encoder consists of ten parts such as controller, vector memory (VM), destination address table (DAT), high-density matrix table (HDMT), matrix row mapping table (MRMT), first block row expansion table (FBRET), address generator (AG), reordering unit (RU), horizontal accumulator (HA), and parallel accumulator (PA). VM is used to store q and c, DAT is used to store the location of s and p1 in c, and HDMT is used to store all column vectors of Φ. AG uses MRMT and FBRET to generate the read-write address that HA needs. RU makes use of DAT to reorder s and p1, and then places them in the corresponding positions of c. The implementation of this LDPC encoder can be described in the following steps.
Step 1. Each bit of s is placed in c according to DAT by RU. Then, clear corresponding bits of pALT in c.
Step 2. Calculate p0 and p2 by HA according to the row mapping between HALT and H. The calculation can be achieved in several steps. Firstly, AG inquiries the first l+t cells of MRMT and the whole FBRET so as to calculate the read addresses of ρ-1 source operators and the write address of the destination operator. Then, controller reads ρ-1 sources from c in accordance with the address given by AG. Finally, the sum of ρ-1 sources is written into c in accordance with the destination address.
Step 3. Calculate q by HA according to the row mapping between HALT and H. The calculation can also be achieved in several steps. Firstly, AG inquiries the last d cells of MRMT and the whole FBRET so as to calculate the read addresses of ρ source operators. Then, controller reads ρ sources from c in accordance with the address given by AG. Finally, the sum of ρ sources is written into q.
Step 4. Multiplication of Φ and q is achieved by PA. Then, update p1 in c by DAT.
Step 5. Notice that p2 in step 2 is a temporary result. The computation of final p2 is similar to step 2 except that AG

inquiries the lth to (l+t-1)th cells of MRMT. Obviously, s, p0, p1 and p2 are placed in c in steps 1, 2, 4 and 5, respectively. There is no need to reorder them.

V. CONCLUSION
Combining with the improved ALT encoding method, we propose an efficient encoder based on block-row-cycle structure for CMMB, which is capable of making the most of the characteristics of the sparse parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of row weight.
Experimental results show that, the proposed encoder is extremely memory-efficient, improving the encoding rate at the same time. This encoder is of practial value because of low cost. Our work solves the technical difficulty of encoding LDPC codes and is expected to be helpful to the research and development of CMMB base station.
