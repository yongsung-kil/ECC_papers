A New Stopping Criterion for Fast Low-Density Parity-Check Decoders
Tian Xia, Hsiao-Chun Wu, Senior Member, IEEE, and Scott C.-H. Huang, Member, IEEE

Abstract-Nonbinary low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes can lead to excellent error performance while the codewords are of short or moderate length. However, the high decoding complexity of nonbinary LDPC codes inevitably depreciates their practical values. The computational bottleneck arises from the check node processing in the iterative message passing (MP) algorithms which terminate when either all parity checks are satisfied or the maximum iteration number is reached. We have observed that for undecodable blocks, the MP algorithms always run up to the maximum iteration limit and therefore cannot generate the correct codeword. Thus, it would be better to terminate the algorithms early so as to save the unnecessary computational time and reduce the extra power consumption when undecodable blocks are experienced. In this paper, we propose a new T-tolerance stopping criterion for LDPC decoders by exploiting the fact that the total a posteriori probability (APP) should increase as the iteration number grows. Simulation results demonstrate that our proposed new T-tolerance criterion can greatly reduce the average iteration number (complexity) while restricting the decoding performance degradation within 0.1 dB in low bit-energy-to-noise ratio scenarios.
Index Terms-Iterative decoding, nonbinary low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, message passing algorithms, stopping criterion.

Usually, a maximum iteration number is preset. The MP algorithms terminate when either the estimated codeword satisfies all parity checks or the maximum iteration number is reached. This conventional stopping criterion is not efficient since it can be observed that when the MP algorithms reach the maximum iteration number, the corresponding block is undecodable, and the MP algorithms should have halted earlier to save computational time and power consumption. Therefore, new stopping criteria need to be devised such that the MP algorithms could determine if the block is undecodable just in a few iterations. In [7], a stopping criterion was proposed for binary LDPC codes using the variable node reliability for undecodable codeword blocks. In [8], the iterative decoding process would be terminated when a soft-word cycle appears. In [9], [10], stopping criteria were developed by examining the number of unsatisfied parity checks.
However, the aforementioned stopping criteria either require a lot of memory storage space or involve some threshold parameters which are hard to optimize systematically in practice. In this paper, we propose a new stopping criterion for nonbinary LDPC decoding algorithms to remove these limitations. As a matter of fact, the maximum a posteriori probability (APP) for each variable node should increase when an MP algorithm is executed in progress across iterations [5]. Consequently, one may simply use the total APP as the essential metric to check if an MP algorithm converges to a correct solution. In particular, we propose a T-tolerance criterion such that the decoding process terminates when the total APP decreases exactly for T+1 times in aggregate during the execution of an MP algorithm.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. The existing nonbinary LDPC decoding algorithms are presented in Sec­ tion II. We provide the statistical analysis to evaluate the iter­ ation number distributions for decodable blocks in Section III. Based on this analysis, we design a new T-tolerance stopping criterion in Section IV. Monte Carlo simulation results are shown in Section V to demonstrate the tradeoff between the error performance and the complexity for our proposed stopping criterion. Conclusion will be drawn in Section VI.

IV. NEW STOPPING CRITERION
The conventional QSPA for decoding LDPC codes termi­ nates if either the maximum iteration number is reached or the estimated codeword satisfies all parity checks (the syndrome is a zero vector). The maximum iteration number is usually chosen large enough so that the codeword can be correctly restored before the iterations run out. On the other hand, for undecodable blocks, no matter how large the maximum iteration number is, the QSPA will always use up all iterations but fail to converge to the correct codeword, especially in the low signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) condition. That is, the conventional stopping criterion is not robust. Indeed, for any iterative algorithm, it usually needs a sophisticated stopping criterion which exploits the statistical algorithmic behavior for better termination control. In this section, we propose a novel stopping criterion such that QSPA can monitor the decoding process at each iteration and then identify the undecodable blocks at an early stage.

A. Total A Posteriori Probability

If the QSPA can converge to the correct codeword, the distribution of APP Pj would concentrate more and more around the correct symbol j as the iteration number in­ creases [5]. Such a frequency of a decodable block is usually much larger than that of an undecodable block. Using this new feature, we devise a new stopping criterion for decoding nonbinary LDPC codes so that QSPA would stop at an early stage whenever an undecodable block is identified.
B. New T-Tolerance Criterion
As mentioned before, the decrease in the total APP indicates the temporary ill-behavior of QSPA at a particular iteration. For an undecodable block, QSPA may often lead to ill-behaved iterations and the algorithm should terminate as early as possible once the situation is recognized. Thus, we propose a new T-tolerance criterion to stop QSPA, where T is the number of the cumulative times (frequency) that the total APP decreases from one iteration to next as discussed in Section IV-A.

V. SIMUL ATION
The performance of the QSPA using our proposed new T­ tolerance criterion is investigated via numerous Monte Carlo simulations. Again for all simulations, we use a (147,108) LDPC encoder over GlF(64), whose parity-check matrix is generated according to [14]. The maximum iteration number is defaulted to 50. The codeword is modulated by 64-QAM and transmitted over the AWGN channel.
Figure 4 depicts the frame error rate (FER) performance
versus Eb/No for different T values compared to the per­
formance of the conventional q-ary sum-product algorithm (labeled as "QSPA" in the figure). It can be observed that the larger the value of T, the better the FER performance. When T is too small, such as T = 0, 1, the T-tolerance criterion is more likely to terminate the algorithm too early before the decodable block can be recovered. However, the FER performance of the QSPA using the T-tolerance criterion approaches the performance of the conventional scheme very quickly as T increases. It is shown that the FER performance

degradation due to the 5-tolerance criterion (T = 5) is within 0.1 dB compared to the conventional QSPA.
Since our proposed stopping criterion is to reduce the itera­ tion numbers (thus reducing the complexity), we plot Figure 5
to show the average iteration number (AIN) versus Eb/NO
for different T values compared to that of the conventional QSPA. It can be observed that the smaller the value of T, the less the value of AIN. The AIN gap between the QSPA using the 5-tolerance criterion and the conventional scheme
is large in the low Eb/NO scenarios. However, when Eb/NO
increases, the AINs for the conventional method and the QSPA using different T-tolerance criteria converge altogether
as Eb/No > 10.5 dB. It is because when Eb/No is low, undecodable blocks are often found but when Eb/NO is
large, hardly can any undecodable block be found instead. Note that the corresponding FERs are still different when
Eb/No > 10.5 dB. It is due to the fact that the T-tolerance
criterion may terminate the QSPA too early before a codeword can be correctly restored since the total APP for an arbitrary decodable block may not always increase iteration by iteration as mentioned in Section IV-A.
The FER-complexity tradeoff can be maneuvered simply by adjusting T. Our simulation results suggest that it is favorable
to adopt the T-tolerance criterion in the low Eb/NO situations
where undecodable blocks are often found.
VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose a novel T-tolerance stopping crite­ rion for decoding LDPC codes. It allows the total a posteriori probability to decrease iteration by iteration at most T times when a message passing algorithm is executed. Monte Carlo simulation results demonstrate that the 5-tolerance criterion (T = 5) can greatly reduce the average iteration number
(complexity) in the low Eb/No conditions while maintaining
the frame-error-rate performance degradation within 0.1 dB. It is desirable to adopt our proposed new stopping criterion
