Abstract—In this paper, we propose reliability adjustment weighted bit-flipping (RA-WBF) decoding algorithm for lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes. By adjusting the reliability of received symbols during iterative decoding, the proposed algorithm can offer better tradeoff between performance, convergence speed and computational complexity. Furthermore, this improvement can easily be extended to other variations of weighted bit-flipping decoding algorithm and obtain noticeable improvement in error performance. Simulation results are shown to verify our conclusions.
Keywords-LDPC; iterative decoding; weighted bit-flipping (WBF); reliability adjustment;
I. INTRODUCTION
In 1960’s, LDPC codes [1] are firstly proposed by Gallager in his PhD thesis. Because of limitations in computational resources, LDPC codes were largely forgotten until they were rediscovered independently by Mackay [2] and Luby [3]. Because of their outstanding performance, LDPC codes have attracted a great deal of attention. And many decoding algorithms are proposed, including hard-decision, soft-decision, and hybrid decoding schemes, such as standard bit-flipping (BF) decoding [4], weighted bit-flipping (WBF) decoding [5], belief propagation (BP) decoding [6] and the variations of BP decoding [7].
Among these decoding algorithms, WBF algorithms provide an effective trade off between performance, convergence speed and computational complexity. That is why various modification schemes of WBF have been proposed. In [8], J. Zhang and M. Fossorier proposed a modified weighted bit-flipping (M-WBF) algorithm, which also considers the reliability information of the received symbols besides the check sums. In [9], an improvement on the modified weighted bit-flipping (IM-WBF) decoding algorithm is presented, which considers that the reliability of check sums involving this bit should exclude the bit itself. In addition, there exist many algorithms called hybrid iterative decoding such as

[10] and [11], which combine several different algorithms to generate better decoding algorithms.
In this paper, we propose reliability adjustment weighted bit-flipping (RA-WBF) decoding for low-density paritycheck codes via reliability adjustment of received symbols, which offers better performance with a little complexity increase. This improvement is based on the fact as follows. During iterative decoding, if the check sums don’t equal zeros, there must exit some error bits in the sets of bits participating in the check sums. Conversely, if the check sums are satisfied, the bits involving these check sums are more reliable. For the Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel, the reliability of a received symbol can be measure by its magnitude. Base on this principle, we can update the reliability values of these bits involving these check sums before each iteration. In our study, this idea can be implemented on the standard WBF, M-WBF and IMWBF algorithms.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows: In section II, the standard WBF, M-WBF and IM-WBF algorithms are briefly reviewed. In section III, the main principle of reliability adjustment of received symbols is depicted, then we apply this idea to standard WBF, M-WBF and IM-WBF algorithms and obtain corresponding modified algorithms. Simulation results are presented and discussed in section IV. Finally, conclusions are drawn in section V.

III. MODIFICATION: RELIABILITY ADJUSTMENT OF RECEIVED SYMBOLS
In this section, we present the principle of improvement and the corresponding modified standard WBF, M-WBF and IM-WBF algorithms.

A. Principle of the improvement
For the AWGN channel, the magnitude |yn| of a received symbol yn is used to measure its reliability. Hence, the larger the magnitude |yn| is, the larger the reliability of the corresponding hard-decision digit zn is. For m = 1, 2, . . . , M , wm defined by (4) is viewed as a measure of the reliability of the syndrome component sm. During iterative decoding, we observe the fact that, if a parity check equation is not satisfied, there must exit some error bits in the sets of bits participating in the check sum. Hence, the bits corresponding to the check sum is less reliable, especially for the smallest magnitude |ymin|. Conversely, if a parity check equation equal 0, the bits involving this check sum are more reliable. Based on this principle, a modification scheme adjusting reliability of received symbols is introduced. For each iteration, we firstly examine the check sums. If the check sums don’t equal to 0, we reduce the reliability of the smallest received symbols involving the check sums, else we increase the smallest received sysmbols. So the error bits are much easier to be found during iterative decoding.
The proposed idea can be extended to other variations of weighted bit-flipping decoding algorithm. In this paper, we only present this improvement to standard WBF, M-WBF and IM-WBF, respectively.

B. Standard WBF, M-WBF and IM-WBF algorithms combined with reliability adjustment of received symbols
In standard WBF and M-WBF algorithms, we uses the minimum absolute value of the received symbols participating in a check to measure the reliability of the check equation. Therefore, if the check equation is not satisfied, the minimum absolute value of the received symbols participating in this check is less reliable. Hence, we use a factor δ (0 < δ < 1) to reduce its reliable value. Conversely, if

Use standard WBF for example. In Step 1, after bit n is selected and flipped at the previous iteration, sm’s that correspond to the dv checks in which bit n participates should be updated. Then, for each m ∈ M(n), we update En for all n ∈ N (m) in Step 2. Then the computational complexity of Steps 1 and 2 is dv · dc additions. Next in Step 3, one bit of the length-N bit vector z is flipped. To select this bit based on En, (N − 1) times of comparison are needed. Then the computational complexity of Step 3 is (N − 1) since comparisons can be considered as additions. In total, we need dv · dc + N − 1 additions.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
A. Choice of the introduced factor δ
In this paper, we only apply this improvement to the standard WBF, M-WBF and IM-WBF algorithms. The other variations of WBF algorithms can be improved with a similar way. When different factors are given, we can get different performances by modified algorithms. For obtaining the best possible performance, a proper factor δ need to be determined. However, it is very difficult to get the optimal factor δ by a theoretic approach. Here, we resort to simulations. Fig. 1 shows the effect of δ word error rate (WER) of the (273, 191) finite geometry LDPC code (FGLDPC) [5], and the maximum number of iterations is set to be 20. We can observe that the word error rate is not very sensitive to the value of δ at low SNRs and the optimal value of δ nearly remains 0.4 at different SNRs. Base on above observation, factor δ can be fixed at some constant value for decoding a given LDPC code at different SNRs. In the following simulations, the optimal value of factor δ is given by simulations.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose a new improved weighted bitflipping decoding algorithm named RA-WBF for LDPC codes. By adjusting the reliability of received symbols during iterative decoding, the proposed algorithm can offer better tradeoff between performance, convergence speed and computational complexity. Furthermore, this RA-WBF algorithm can easily be extended to other variations of weighted bit-flipping decoding algorithm and obtain noticeable improvement in error performance. Simulation results are shown to verify our conclusions.
