Abstract-Concerning the high encoding complexity of LDPC codes, an efficient encoder based on block-column-cycle structure for CMMB is proposed, which is able to take full advantage of the characteristics of the sparse parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of column weight. Experimental results show that the proposed encoder can reduce dramatically the memory requirements in hardware implementation, and improve the encoding rate at the same time.

INTRODUCTION

Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were first invented by Gallager in 1962 and rediscovered by Mackay and Neal in 1996 [1], [2], In the past few years, LDPC codes had been attracting more and more attentions due to their excellent error-correcting ability and low decoding complexity. For instance, LDPC codes were used as channel coding in DVB-S2, IEEE 802.11nI16e, DMB-TH, and DTTB, etc. In Chinese Mobile Multimedia Broadcasting (CMMB) standard, LDPC code was also used as Forward Error Correction Code
combined with Reed-Solomon (RS) code [3].

LDPC codes exhibit an asymptotically better performance than turbo codes and they admit a wide range of tradeoffs between performance and decoding complexity, since they can approach the Shannon limits for suitably large block length. One major criticism concerning LDPC codes has been their apparent high encoding complexity. Whereas turbo codes can be encoded in linear time, a straightforward encoder implementation for an LDPC code has complexity quadratic to
the block length.

Lots of efforts had been made to develop computationally efficient encoders by many coding investigators. One of encoding methods with linear complexity, named as RU method or approximately lower triangular (ALT) method, was proposed by Richardson and Urbanke [4]. Richardson and Urbanke point out that linear time encoding is achievable through careful linear manipulation of LDPC codes, and methods based on greedy algorithm to preprocess the parity­ check matrix H and a set of matrix operations to perform the actual encoding are proposed at the same time. Another encoding method with linear complexity was proposed by Neal

[5], He used LU decomposition to free the encoding process of dense inverse operation. Its principle is very simple, and its key is to decompose a matrix into the product of a lower and upper triangular matrix as sparse as possible. However, it is not easy to find a good sparse LU decomposition for arbitrary H matrices, and especially some matrices cannot be decomposed. Other various encoding methods include MacKay's lower triangular method [6], Haley's iterative encoding method [7], and Johnson's quasi-cyclic method [8],
For the ALT encoding method, the parity-check matrix after elementary permutations is divided into a few submatrices, which lose its characteristics. In allusion to the shortcoming, an improved ALT encoding method is proposed. According to this method, an efficient LDPC encoder based on block-column­ cycle structure is designed for CMMB, which is capable of making the best of inherent characteristics of the parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of nonzero elements' number per column. This encoder can reduce dramatically the memory requirements in hardware implementation, and improve the encoding rate at the same time.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. The next section describes the improved ALT encoding method. In Section III, a LDPC encoder based on block-column-cycle structure for CMMB is given. Experiments are presented in Section IV. Finally, Section V draws the conclusion of this paper.

There are two binary LDPC codes with different rate 1/2 and 3/4 in CMMB system. The former is a (9216, 3, 6) regular code, and the latter is a (9216, 3, 12) regular one. In the standard, the sparse parity-check matrices are specified, which are cyclic but not quasi-cyclic. For the rate 1/2 (3/4), the entire parity-check matrix H can be derived from the first 36 columns, which shift 18 (9) rows cyclically every 36 columns. The former uses 62 blocks of M9K block RAM while the latter uses 147 blocks. The former admits choosing cheap FPGA.
Under 100 MHz, pure encoding rate can achieve 20.54
Mbps (the rate 112) and 22.03 Mbps (the rate 3/4), which satisfy sufficiently the specifications in CMMB standard: 10.852 Mbps and 16.243 Mbps correspondingly.

V. CONCLUSION
Combining with the improved ALT encoding method, we propose an efficient encoder based on block-column-cycle structure for CMMB, which is capable of making the most of the characteristics of the sparse parity-check matrix, such as cyclicity and equality of column weight.
Experimental results show that, the proposed encoder is extremely memory-efficient, improving the encoding rate at the same time. This encoder is of practial value because of low cost. Our work solves the technical difficulty of encoding LDPC codes and is expected to be helpful to the research and development of CMMB base station.
