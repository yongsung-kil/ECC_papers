I. 
INTRODUCTION 
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) 
codes were first 
introduced by Gallager [1], and then rediscovered in 1996 [2], 
[3]. In Gallager's original scheme, LDPC codes were 
constructed using sparse regular, random bipartite graphs, 
which are called regular LDPC codes. Their performance over 
A WGN channel is only slightly inferior to that of Turbo codes. 
Later, Luby et at. in [4] showed that irregular codes based on 
irregular graphs perform better than regular ones, which have 
different variable and check node degrees in the corresponding 
bipartite graphs. Extending Luby's analysis, Richardson et at. 
[5] developed a numerical method to optimize the degree 
profiles of the variable and the check nodes, which constructed 
powerful irregular LDPC codes that first beat Turbo codes on 
A WGN channels with unlimited number of iterations at long 
block lengths of more than 104, but for moderate and short 
lengths, the results were negative. Then, in [6], the 
performance was further improved where on A WGN channel 
the threshold for a rate 1/2 LDPC code is within 0.0045 dB of 
the Shannon limit and simulation results are within 0.04 dB of 
the Shannon limit at a bit error rate of 106 using a long block 
length of 107• 
Since then, the optimization design of irregular LDPC for 
various different channels and special applications has been the 
subject of much interest [7], [8], [9], [10], [11], [12], [13], [14]. 
Among the previous studies, most focus on the optimized 
irregular LDPC codes' capability of approaching the Shannon 
capacity limit with very long block size, and an important tool, 
so-called density evolution [15], was used frequently, which is 
a numerical technique for determining the thresholds, i.e. 
performance limits for LDPC codes with certain degree 
profiles 
under 
message 
passing 
decoding.

B. Density Evolution with Gaussian Approximation 
Density evolution is a powerful tool to evaluate the 
performance of LDPC codes and to aid in designing capacity­
approaching degree distribution pair. By tracking the 
probability density functions of the messages v and c passed 
in the bipartite graph from iteration to iteration, it can calculate 
a threshold value for a LDPC code ensemble specified by 
certain degree distributions which determines the boundary of 
the error-free region asymptotically as the codeword length n 
tends to infinity [5], [6], [15]. Compared with the full version 
of density evolution, the Gaussian approximation method 
proposed in [16] further approximates the message densities as 
Gaussians and Gaussian mixtures for regular LDPC codes and 
irregular LDPC codes, respectively, and only need to track a 
one-dimensional quantity, namely, the mean of a Gaussian 
density in iterations, which can significantly reduce the 
computational complexity in the code optimization process.

Gaussian approximation as follows.

V. 
CONCLUSIONS 
In this paper, we have studied the optimization design 
method for irregular LDPC codes. We develop a code 
optimization algorithm based on the combination of the 
Gaussian approximation of density evolution and a specialized 
differential evolution approach. The simulation results show 
414 
that even at moderate block length the irregular LDPC codes 
optimized by our algorithm can also beat the Turbo code, 
which demonstrates that the proposed algorithm is effective.
