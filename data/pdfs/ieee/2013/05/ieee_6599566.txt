A New Hybrid Decoding Algorithm for LDPC Codes Based on the Improved Variable Multi Weighted Bit-Flipping and BP Algorithms

* Young Researchers Club and Elites, Mashhad Branch, Islamic Azad University, Mashhad, Iran, e.olyaei@mail.sbu.ac.ir ** Dept. of Electrical & Computer Engineering, Shahid Beheshti University, Tehran, Iran, sharifi@aryasatel.com

Abstract: In this paper, a new two-stage hybrid algorithm for decoding Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes is proposed that incorporates two iterative decoding. For the first stage of decoding, we offer a new decoding algorithm called Improved Variable Multi Weighted Bit-Flipping (IVMWBF) algorithm. Since the IVMWBF algorithm is based on the idea of flipping variable multi bits in each iteration, both improvement in performance and reduction in iteration number and computational complexity are observed in this algorithm. The proposed hybrid algorithm which is a combination of the IVMWBF and BP algorithms, while having a performance exactly similar to that of the BP algorithm, has less complexity compared to that algorithm.
Keywords: Belief Propagation (BP), Modified weighted bit-flipping decoding, Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes, Hybrid iterative decoding.
1. Introduction
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes were
originally invented by Gallager [1], and brought back
into prominence by Mackay and Neal [2]. Given the increasingly need for channel codes with higher performance in the presence of noise channels, the LDPC codes and their associated concepts have been attracting a great deal of research interest in coding theory due to their very high performance, which is very much close to the Shannon limit. In addition to their near Shannon limit performance, the interest in these codes stems from their simple descriptions and implementations, and their amenability to rigorous theoretical analysis [3], [11].
There have been wide variety methods for decoding of LDPC codes, ranging from low to high complexity and from reasonably good to very good performance. These decoding methods may be classified into three categories: hard-decision, soft-decision and hybrid decoding schemes. These decoding algorithms include: One-Step Majority-Logic (OSMLG) decoding, Gallager’s bit flipping (BF) decoding, weighted MLG decoding (WMLG), weighted BF decoding (WBF), and other modified versions of WBF algorithm like improved weighted bit flipping (IWBF) and Modified Weighted bitflipping, A Posteriori Probability (APP) decoding, iterative decoding based on belief propagation (IDBP)

(commonly known as sum-product algorithm (SPA)), and approximations of the IDBP like Min-Sum, Normalized BP and Offset-BP algorithms. OSMLG and BF decoding belong to hard-decision decoding methods. APP, IDBP/SPA decoding and its approximations are softdecision schemes. WBF, IWBF and Modified WBF algorithms are hybrid decoding methods [3], [9].
Each iterative decoding algorithm provides a distinct trade-off between complexity and performance. It goes without saying that the soft-decision algorithms in which message passing is used to carry out their decoding procedures can achieve better performance compared with those based on hard-decision. However, due to their higher complexity in terms of calculations and the hardware implementation of the relevant decoders, the use of these algorithms is unfeasible. The performance of hard-decision algorithms is approximately 3dB lower than that of soft-decision algorithms.
Hybrid decoding algorithms which are in fact based on both soft and hard decisions have been developed to bridge the performance gap between these two groups of algorithms (those based on soft and hard decisions). Results obtained from analytical simulations and studies indicate that there is still a wide performance gap between hybrid algorithms and those based on soft decision[10], [12].
The present work is an effort to bridge the performance gap between hybrid decoding algorithms on the one hand, and algorithms based on soft decision on the other. In order to achieve this, we shall first propose a new hybrid decoding algorithm called the IVMWBF algorithm, which while having the capacity for flipping several bits per iteration, offers a faster convergence rate and less hardware complexity compared to the modified WBF algorithm. Then, having introduced the innovative idea of combining this algorithm with the BP algorithm, we shall introduce a new and highly efficient algorithm with a performance equivalent to that of the BP algorithm and less complexity and fewer iterations required compared to that algorithm.

2.2 Main Idea

Most existing algorithms used for decoding LDPC codes are based on the assumption that the iterative algorithm can flip only one bit of the received sequence in each iteration. The Standard WBF decoding algorithm along its improved versions such as IWBF and Modified

WBF algorithm are among these algorithms, which have a more or less high performance. In addition to flipping only one single bit in each iteration, these algorithms have slow convergence rate, and require numerous iteration to reach an acceptable performance. Furthermore, there is still a significant difference between these algorithms and those based on soft decision in terms of performance.
In this paper, a significant improved version called the IVMWBF algorithm with a much higher performance rate close to that of the SPA algorithm is presented based on the idea of flipping several bits in each iteration and making other fundamental changes in those iterative algorithms. However, it should be born in mind that making mistakes in flipping even a single bit in each iteration extends the chain error to the whole algorithm, and ultimately leads to its divergence. Therefore, the need for using a high-precision mechanism for determining the number and position of the bits to be flipped in each iteration becomes obvious. The idea of flipping bits in parallel in each iteration has many advantages over flipping only one single bit in each iteration, including rapid increase of the convergence rate of the algorithm and a decrease in the number of iterations needed. Another interesting characteristic of the algorithms in which bits are flipped in parallel fashion is that they are capable of avoiding errors in iterative decoding prevalent in algorithms, particularly in lower SNRs due to the limited number of iterations, although the frequency of such errors may be very low. For instance, if the received sequence for decoding an LDPC code consists of 2000 bits, and if the maximum number of iterations is 100, this iterative algorithm in which a single bit is flipped in each iteration will be capable of detecting and correcting 100 bits of errors from the received sequence in the best-case scenario. Therefore, the use of such algorithms for received sequences in which the number of errors is greater than the maximum number of iterations is practically unfeasible, because they cannot correct all the errors in such sequences.

2.3 IVMWBF Algorithm
Among the algorithms based on the idea of flipping several bits in each iteration, The Parallel WBF algorithm [5] shows a good performance. Therefore, it is used as a base for the development of a new algorithm called the IVMWBF algorithm. In the proposed algorithm, two major modifications have been made compared with the original parallel WBF algorithm, improving its performance. First, this algorithm uses a new and completely different mechanism to calculate the number of bits to be flipped in each iteration. The second major modification concerns the use of a new loop-detection mechanism in this algorithm. The loop-detection mechanism was first developed in [6] for algorithms in which only a single bit was flipped in each iteration. But the loop-detection we have used here is completely different from that, and is especially designed for algorithms in which several bits are flipped in each iteration. Therefore, this mechanism is highly dependent on the number of bits to be flipped in each iteration. The procedures of the IVMWBF algorithm are as follows:

3. Hybrid Iterative Decoding Based on IVMWBF and BP Algorithms

Our other innovation in this paper concerns the development of a two-stage hybrid iterative decoding algorithm which combines two iterative decoding algorithms to reduce calculation complexities of the LDPC codes. This hybrid iterative decoding may include one or two stages. This is how these two algorithms work: first, the IVMWBF decoding algorithm is implemented. If all the parity check equations are satisfied, the decoding process will be completed successfully, and the output will be assumed to be the decoded vector. Otherwise, the decoding process enters the second stage and continues using the robust BP algorithm. The new hybrid decoding algorithm reduces calculation complexity to a great extent, while having the same level of performance as the BP algorithm.
For the second stage of the decoding process, the standard BP algorithm can be replaced by a number of its simplified versions to achieve a good trade-off between decoding complexity on the one hand, and error performance on the other. Furthermore, other versions such as normalized BP or offset BP may be used to improve performance.
It may seem in the first glance that the idea of combining the IVMWBF and BP algorithms increases the amount of necessary calculations, thus adding to the complexity of the decoding process. This, however, is not the case, because the BP decoding algorithm contributes very little to the combinatory hybrid decoding process. The amount of this contribution is approximately equal to the WER of the IVMWBF decoding.
A word error occurs when a parity check remains unsatisfied or the decoded vector be a code-word different from the transmitted code-word (undetectable error). But since the undetectable error is rare particularly for lengthy codes, word error rate is approximately equal to the likelihood of the failure of parity checks. The need for the development and implementation of the IVMWBF algorithm for the first stage of the hybrid iterative

decoding process is very obvious, because the better the performance in the first stage of decoding, and the more strong algorithm is used for this purpose, the less the need for the implementation of the BP algorithm for the second stage of the hybrid algorithm would be. In regions with high SNR, the WER of the IVMWBF algorithm decoding is low, that is, the BP algorithm is rarely activated. Therefore, the calculation complexity of the developed hybrid algorithm is reduced. As for situations where the IVMWBF algorithm is capable of detecting and correcting errors, the BP algorithm can carry out the same function. But in situations where the first-stage algorithm fails, the Hybrid algorithm passes through the filter of the BP algorithm. Thus, it can be expected that the hybrid decoding algorithm should have the same level of performance as the BP algorithm. Since the complexity of the IVMWBF decoder is less than that of the BP decoder, the proposed hybrid decoder has a significant complexity, comparable to that of the BP decoder.

Based on the existing PWBF algorithm for LDPC codes, in this paper, we have proposed its significantly improved version, namely IVMWBF. This algorithm uses a new equation to determine the number of bits to be flipped in each iteration. This equation is based on the observation that there is a strong correlation between the number of bits to be flipped and the hamming weight of the syndrome vector in each iteration. Compared with the standard and Modified WBF, this algorithm has better performance and faster rate of convergence and requires a smaller number of iterations. Then we have combined this algorithm with the BP algorithm and presented the new two-stage hybrid iterative decoding algorithm that has the same performance as, and lower complexity compared with the robust BP algorithm.
