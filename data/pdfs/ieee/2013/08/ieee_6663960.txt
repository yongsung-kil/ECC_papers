Abstract—It’s common to use high-precision floating-point operations on theoretical study of the Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes, but it is difficult to implement it in practical application. Therefore, there is a great need to design effective quantitative method which enables us to implement it in hardware easily. In this paper, the min-sum decoding algorithm of LDPC codes for 8PSK modulation is studied in detail, and the method of the uniform quantization is analyzed and deduced. With this method to quantify the initial message, the variable messages and check messages are converted into integers after each iteration. Then the min-sum decoding algorithm based on integer arithmetic for 8PSK modulation is implemented. Simulation results demonstrate that the performance of the minsum decoding algorithm with the uniform quantization is close to that of the high-precision floating-point sum-product decoding algorithm. Meanwhile, all the variables in the algorithm are fixed-length integers, so it is convenient for hardware implementation.
Keywords—Low Density Parity Check Codes (LDPC); 8 phase-shift keying modulation; min-sum decoding algorithm; uniform quantization.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes, out of its near Shannon limit performance and relatively simple decoding structure, achieves increasing concern among the scholars of the channel coding. It has important significance in practical application.. In fact, many practical digital communication systems often adopt M-ary digital modulation. Motivated by the facts talked above, this paper studies the min-sum decoding algorithm based on integer operations for 8PSK modulation.
Sum product decoding algorithm under the probability measure is not suitable for quantization[1]. The quantitative methods of sum product decoding algorithm under likelihood

Reference [2] also quantifies the initial message under 3 dB SNR and 1/2 code rate and a better performance is achieved, and the resulting quantization table are applicable to different SNR and any rate of LDPC decoding. Reference [3] quantifies not only the received signal in the sum product decoding algorithm, but also some intermediate variables, and the maximum number of iterations is set to 200. Based on the studies of hyperbolic curve, reference [4] designs a correction factor to make the decoding performance of the rule codes and irregular codes improved significantly under different SNR.
With a method of uniform quantization to quantify the received signal, this paper designs a quantized min-sum decoding algorithm for 8PSK modulation whose variable is integer actually represented in 9 bits. When the maximum times of iterations is 20, its performance is little worse than that of high-precision floating-point sum product decoding algorithm. And this quantitative method only requires once table look-up. So it is convenient for hardware implementation, and the decoding speed is faster.

V. CONCLUSIONS M-ary modulation brings the system a significant

performance, i.e., it provides higher information transmission rate and reduces the impact resulting from inter-symbol interference determined by channel characteristics. In this paper, we quantify the initial message in min-sum decoding algorithm for 8PSK modulation with the method of uniform quantization. Then the original information is directly mapped to the corresponding integer value via a table. This initial message is converted into integer value and then participates in the decoding operation. According to equation (2) and equation (5), variable message and check message will become integer owing to the fact that the initial message is integer (the variable message corrected by the correction factor in equation (7) just need to be rounded). Finally, the improved min-sum decoding algorithm for 8PSK modulation is obtained. This algorithm is applicable to different SNR and any rate of LDPC decoding, and its performance is slightly worse than that of the high-precision floating-point LLR decoding algorithm for 8PSK modulation, however, it enjoys the advantages of faster decoding speed and easy of hardware implementation.
