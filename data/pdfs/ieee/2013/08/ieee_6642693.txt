Abstract—Based on the properties of pell sequences, hanoi sequences and lucas sequences, three methods are proposed to construct new families of quasi cyclic low density parity check codes. Furthermore, we will prove that the designed QC-LDPC codes have no circles shorter than six. The performance of the codes is also investigated.
Keywords- QC-LDPC codes; parity check matrices; basis matrices; pell sequences; lucas sequences; hanoi sequences;

I.

INTRODUCTION

In 1962, Gallager introduced low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes in his seminal work [1,2]. Having been almost completely forgotten for over 30 years, LDPC codes were rediscovered by MacKay and Neal. They showed that if decoded by using the sum–product algorithm (SPA), the performance of LDPC codes can approach the Shannon limit [3,4]. Ever since their rediscovery, a great deal of research effort has been expended in design, construction, structural analysis, efficient encoding and decoding, performance analysis, decoder architecture, and implementation, generalizations and applications of LDPC codes. Numerous papers have been published on these subjects. Many LDPC codes have been chosen as the standard codes for various next-generation communication systems and they are appearing in recent data storage products.
LDPC codes can be classified into two general categories: (1) random or pseudorandom codes that are constructed using computer-based algorithms or methods; and (2) algebraic codes that are constructed using algebraic or combinatorial tools such as finite fields [5,6], finite geometries [7], and experimental designs. Codes in these two categories can be classified into two types: codes whose parity-check matrices possess little structure and codes whose parity-check matrices have structure. A code whose parity-check matrix possesses no structure beyond being a linear code is problematic in that both encoding and decoding implementations become quite complex. A code whose parity-check matrix has structure beyond being a linear code is in general more easily implemented. Two desirable characteristics for hardware implementation of encoding and decoding of LDPC codes are cyclic and QC structures. A cyclic LDPC code can be efficiently and

systematically encoded with a single feedback shift-register with complexity linearly proportional to the number of parity-check symbols [8]. Encoding of a QC-LDPC code can also be efficiently implemented but requires multiple shift-registers [9]. It is, in general, more complex than encoding of a cyclic code but still enjoys linear complexity. However, QC-LDPC codes enjoy some advantages in decoder hardware implementation in terms of wire routing [10]. Furthermore, the QC structure allows partially parallel decoding [11] which offers a tradeoff between decoding complexity and decoding speed, while the cyclic structure allows either full parallel or serial decoding.
In this paper, we present three methods to construct basis matrices of parity check matrices for quasi cyclic low density parity check codes using properties of lucas sequences, hanoi sequences and pell sequences. Cyclic shift coefficients of the basis matrix could be expressed in simple analytic expressions. So the parity check matrix needs little storage space, reduces the cost of the practical application. This construction method with various lengths, rates and girth at least 6 can be flexibly constructed. The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In section 2, basis matrices of parity check matrices for QC-LDPC codes are introduced. In section 3, three methods are presented to construct basis matrices of parity check matrices for quasi cyclic low density parity check codes using the properties of lucas sequences, hanoi sequences and pell sequences. We will prove that the designed QC-LDPC codes have no circles shorter than six. The performance of the codes is also investigated. In section 4, we analysis the performance of the codes constructed comparing with regular randomly constructed LDPC codes of similar rates and lengths. The results show that the QC-LDPC codes constructed here perform well.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
For comparison, we construct QC-LDPC codes with the same length and rate. The results are compared with regular randomly constructed LDPC codes of similar rates and lengths. The simulation results are as follows.

Figure 1. random: The randomly constructed LDPC codes
lucas: QC-LDPC codes on the lucas sequences
hanoi: QC-LDPC codes on the hanoi sequences
pell: QC-LDPC codes on the pell sequences
The performance of the (3, 6) regular [387, 774]
codes of Example 3.3, Example 3.6 and Example 3.9 is shown in Fig.1. At the high BER, the simulation shows that the proposed codes have almost the same performance, and perform slightly better than the randomly constructed LDPC
codes. At the BER of 10−4 , QC-LDPC codes on the hanoi sequences have a gain nearly 0.1 dB more than the
randomly constructed LDPC codes. QC-LDPC codes on the pell sequences have almost the same performance with the randomly constructed LDPC.
Comparing with the randomly constructed LDPC
codes, QC-LDPC codes on the lucas sequences have 0.3 dB
performance decrease. But cyclic shift coefficients of the basis matrix could be expressed in simple analytic expressions. So the parity check matrix needs little storage space, reduce the cost of the practical application, and compensates the influence brought by the performance gap to some extent.
