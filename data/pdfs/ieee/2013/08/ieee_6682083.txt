IC Design of a Low-power Analog LDPC Decoder Employing New Stopping Iteration
Method

Wen-Ta Lee, Sheng-Sung Chiu and Yu-Shi Ke
Graduate Institute of Computer and Communication Engineering, National Taipei University of Technology Taipei, Taiwan, R.O.C. wtlee@ntut.edu.tw

Abstract—This paper proposes an analog LDPC decoder employing new stopping iteration method. It is based on the min-sum algorithm and by checking parity H-matrix to decide iteration termination. The proposed method not only can increase the decoding throughput but also decrease the power consumption. Experimental results show that this decoder can save 90% power consumption speed ratio compared with traditional decoders. Finally, an analog (32,8) min-sum decoder with new stopping iteration method is implemented by TSMC 0.18μm 1P6M CMOS technology. When the data throughput and supply voltage is 216 Mb/s and 1.8V respectively, the power consumption is only 4.98 mW. This analog decoder has low power and small area characteristics that can be applicable to green communication devices.
Keywords—stopping iteration, LDPC, analog decoder.
I. INTRODUCTION
Error control codes are used in a variety of applications to reduce or effectively eliminate the occurrence of errors in information which is transmitted or stored. In 1962, R .G. Gallager proposed Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes on communication theory [1].
Compare to digital decoders, the analog decoders have memory free and less static power consumption in many different decoding algorithms. Therefore, the analog decoders can reduce power consumption and chip area effectively. The sum-product algorithm (SPA) and min-sum algorithm (MSA) are two well known decoding schemes for LDPC codes. Even though the decoding performance of MSA is slightly inferior to SPA, the MSA has low complexity requirement for hardware implementation. Because the analog MSA decoder has the advantages of low power consumption and high speed resulting from the strong inversion operation of transistors, in this paper, we present a VLSI architecture for analog decoder based on MSA [2]. Meanwhile, a new analog stopping iteration method is also proposed. Analog decoder with the stopping iteration method can further bring lower power consumption and higher throughput. The implemented circuit was successfully used for designing and fabricating an analog min-sum decoder chip for a (32,8) LDPC code.

A. New stopping iteration method for analog LDPC
According to the theory of error-correcting code, the LDPC decoder needs to be constantly iterated until it gets a correct codeword. But the iterative computing cannot be unlimited for hardware implementation, this is not only a waste of time, and it cannot guarantee that every message can be decoded correctly. In a typical iterative decoder, such as turbo or LDPC decoders, they usually have early termination for the iteration mechanism, and it improves both throughput and power saving. Therefore, many researches apply early termination algorithm to digital decoders. But few proposed related researches do for analog decoders. Digital decoders use the number of iterations as termination option, but analog decoders not. For this reason, we propose two early termination options that are applied to the analog LDPC decoders:

3) Early stopping iteration control circuit
The proposed new early termination control circuit is shown in figure 5. When the decoding codeword goes toward the legal codeword, we can directly determine the final result. The termination of LDPC correct decoding is determined by checking the H · CT . If H · CT = 1, which means that the codeword is not correct yet, so decoder continues iterating. On the contrary, H · CT = 0 represents the correct codeword is obtained, so it directly determines the final codeword without waiting for the steady-state circuit. Thus it can also avoid decoding error that is caused of the approximation of the min-sum algorithms non-convergence phenomenon.
In another way, when the code is transmitted among channel with lots of noise. It may cause continually decoding, and can not get the correct codeword. This not only wastes power consumption, but also decreases the throughput. Thus to set a proper Maximum iteration time is needed for stopping iteration. When the time exceeds the maximum, the decoder is forced to stop iteration and output the result directly. Therefor, by checking H · CT and setting the iteration threshold time, the decoding time and power consumption can be saved effectively.

V. CONCLUSION
A new low-power analog LDPC decoder employing early termination is proposed. By checking H · CT and setting threshold iteration time, the new decoder can effectively reduce 90% power consumption speed ratio with simple early termination control hardware. Experimental results show that the new analog LDPC decoder with early stopping termination has better performance, whether the chip area or energy efficiency. This characteristic can be applied to green communication LDPC and SOC designs.
