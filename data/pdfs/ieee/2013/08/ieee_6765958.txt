EXIT-chart-based threshold calculation of LDPC code in 2D ISI channels

Lingjun Kong, Yong Liang Guan, and Guojun Han School of Electrical & Electronic Engineering
Nanyang Technological University, 639798 Singapore Email: {LJKong, EYLGuan, GJHan}@ntu.edu.sg

Kui Cai and Kheong-Sann Chan Data Storage Institute (DSI), Agency for Science, Technology, and Research (A∗STAR), 117608 Singapore Email: {Cai kui, CHAN Kheong Sann}@dsi.a-star.edu.sg

Abstract—An algorithm based on the fitting of modified Extrinsic Information Transfer (EXIT) chart is proposed to calculate the thresholds of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes optimized for the two-dimensional (2D) intersymbol interference (ISI) channels encountered in high-density magnetic recording, such as bit-patterned magnetic recording (BPMR) and two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR). The modified EXIT chart algorithm determines the LDPC threshold by fitting the EXIT curve of the variable node decoder (VND) combined with 2D detector (2DDET) to the EXIT curve of the check node decoder (CND). Both the optimal Bahl-Cocke-Jelinek-Raviv (BCJR) 2D-DET and reduced-complexity Gaussian-approximated iterative rowcolumn 2D-DET are considered.
I. INTRODUCTION
Due to continued strong demand for higher storage areal densities, bit-patterned magnetic recording (BPMR) and twodimensional magnetic recording (TDMR) have recently been extensively researched [1]. One way to boost the storage density is to reduce the separation between recording tracks. However, this results in more cross-talk between the tracks, called inter-track interference (ITI). The read-back signal of both the BPMR and TDMR can be modeled as a signal corrupted by two-dimensional (2D) inter-symbol interference (ISI) which is constituted by both one-dimensional (1D) ISI and ITI [2]-[4].
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) code exhibits a threshold when it is decoded by belief-propagation (BP) algorithm. Density evolution (DE) [5], which iteratively calculates the message densities to determine the LDPC thresholds, is an algorithm for evaluating the capacity of LDPC codes under BP decoding. Determining the LDPC thresholds and optimizing the LDPC degree distributions using DE are computationally intensive task, and are difficult for most channels other than binary-erasure channels (BECs). Even for the AWGN channels, DE is also complicated to be analyzed. Threshold calculation by DE was simplified by Chung et at. [6] on memoryless binary-input continuous-output AWGN channels with BP decoding, where the propagation message densities are approximated as Gaussians or Gaussian mixtures for LDPC codes.
Threshold calculation for 1D ISI channel was reported in [7]-[11]. DE has been extended to binary-input ISI channels [7]. Gaussian approximation methods for partial response

(PR) channels with additive Gaussian noise have been outlined in [8]-[9].
In our previous work [12]-[13], new LDPC codes optimized for 2D ISI channels detected using symbol-based BCJR 2D detector (2D-DET) have been designed based on Extrinsic Information Transfer (EXIT) curve fitting of modified EXIT charts, which can achieve better performance than codes optimized for AWGN channels. However, the computational complexity of the BCJR 2D-DET is prohibitive, hence iterative row-column soft decision feedback algorithm (IRCSDFA) was proposed in [16] as a reduced-complexity 2D-DET. To further cut down the complexity, Gaussian approximation (GA) is utilized to replace the strip detection in IRCSDFA by a single row or column detection, the resultant simplified detector is referred as “IRCSDFA-GA” [17].
In this paper, we propose to calculate the thresholds of LDPC codes optimized for 2D ISI channels based on the EXIT curve of the combined 2D-DET and variable node decoder (VND). In addition to using optimal 2D-DET based on symbol-based BCJR algorithm, the IRCSDFA-GA 2D-DET is also investigated in both threshold calculation and coded bit error rate (BER) performance evaluation.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the 2D ISI channel model. Then in Section III, thresholds of LPDC codes in 2D ISI channels are calculating based on the modified EXIT chart. Simulation results are presented in Section IV. Finally, Section V renders some conclusions.

III. THRESHOLD CALCULATION FOR 2D ISI CHANNELS
Following the notations used in [18], we define an ensemble of LDPC codes with the variable and check node degree sequences λ = (λ2, λ3, · · · , λDL ) and ρ = (ρ2, ρ3, · · · , ρDR ), where λi and ρj denote the fraction of edges connected to variable nodes of degree i and check nodes of degree j, respectively. And let DL and DR be the number of different variable node degrees and check node degrees, respectively. IA and IE quantities at various locations denote the a-priori mutual information and a-posteriori mutual information of the corresponding signals at those locations.
The thresholds of LDPC codes are evaluated by finding the minimum channel Eb/No to fit the EXIT curve of the combined 2D-DET and VND as closely as possible to the EXIT curve of the check node decoder (CND), while ensuring that they do not cross over.

IRCSDFA-GA 2D-DET has much lower complexity compared with the optimal symbol-based BCJR 2D-DET and IRCSDFA 2D-DET whose complexity is still exponent in the 2D ISI size [19]. V. CONCLUSION
Optimization and threshold determination of LDPC code can be performed by EXIT curve fitting of the applicable EXIT charts. In this paper, we apply the EXIT chart method to calculate the thresholds of LDPC codes optimized for 2D ISI channels and detected by different 2D-DETs.
