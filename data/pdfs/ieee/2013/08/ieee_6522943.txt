An EXIT-Based Design Method for LDPC-Coded Schemes without Gaussian Assumptions
Yen-Ming Chen, Member, IEEE, Yeong-Luh Ueng, Member, IEEE, and Hau-Jung Shiau

Abstract—It is known that low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are widely used to provide capacity-approaching performances in various transmission systems. However, the Gaussian assumption for soft messages in conventional EXIT (extrinsic information transfer)-chart-based optimization algorithms result in prediction gaps in the decoding threshold for certain transmission systems. In this letter, we propose an EXIT-chart-based optimization method, in which all the Gaussian assumptions for soft messages can be avoided. Compared to the conventional design methods, the proposed method provides a more accurate threshold prediction and can be used to design LDPC-coded schemes with better error performances.
Index Terms—Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart.
I. INTRODUCTION
R ECENTLY, extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) charts have become a popular tool for predicting the convergence behavior of iterative receivers [1]-[3]. Through the oneparametric Gaussian approximation for the soft messages, the EXIT curve can be drawn for both the variable node decoder (VND) and the check node decoder (CND), and then curve-fitting procedures can be used to choose a set of degree distributions, i.e., choose an ensemble of lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes, which maximizes the area between the EXIT curves of the two modules. Although another technique, known as density evolution [4], is capable of providing more accurate prediction for the convergence behavior through analyzing the probability density function (p.d.f.) of the exchanged soft messages, EXIT charts are still attractive and widely adopted as a design tool because of their simplicity and better visualization for the iterative decoding process.
The conventional EXIT-based analysis and design methods for LDPC codes is based on the assumption that the p.d.f. of soft messages iteratively processed at the receiver is approximately one-parametric Gaussian distribution. However, it was pointed out in [5] that although this assumption is reasonably accurate for the messages generated from the VND, the p.d.f. of the messages generated from the CND is far

from Gaussian. Consequently, a more accurate EXIT-based analysis and design method was proposed in [5], in which the Gaussian assumption for the CND outputs is removed. It was verified that when compared to the conventional EXITbased design methods, the design method investigated in [5] is able to provide a better code search result and a more accurate threshold prediction, under a single-input single-output (SISO) additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel.
On the other hand, in [2], EXIT charts were generalized to optimize the performance of coherent LDPC-coded coherent multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) systems. The signal detector combined with the VND can be regarded as an integrated variable node decoder (IVND), and hence similar curve-fitting procedures can be performed. Using the method presented in [2], capacity-approaching MIMO schemes can be devised.
Actually, the accuracy of Gaussian approximation for soft messages may be lost in various transmission schemes, e.g., the coded noncoherent transmission schemes, in which the knowledge of accurate channel state information is not required at the receiver. In addition, based on the observations for soft messages, it is verified in this letter that under Rayleigh block fading channels, which is a different environment from the case reported in [5], not only may the CND outputs have a p.d.f. which is far from Gaussian, but also the CND inputs (or equivalently, the IVND outputs). Hence, in this letter, we propose a simple but effective EXIT-based design method which is able to avoid the Gaussian assumption for the soft messages from both the CND and the IVND. Two LDPC-coded transmission schemes, which consider the SISO and MIMO cases, respectively, are adopted to demonstrate the performance of the proposed design method. Compared to the design methods presented in [2] and [5], the proposed method can provide a more accurate threshold prediction for both cases, and can also be used to search outer LDPC codes, thereby achieving improved error performances.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we have presented a new method for generating EXIT curves, in which the Gaussian assumption for output soft messages from both the CND and the IVND can be avoided. Two LDPC-coded schemes are adopted to demonstrate the performance of the proposed design algorithm. Simulation results show that the resultant prediction of the decoding thresholds are consistent with those obtained from the actual BER curves. Consequently, using the proposed design method can be considered when designing coded transmission schemes with iteratively processed non-Gaussian soft messages.
