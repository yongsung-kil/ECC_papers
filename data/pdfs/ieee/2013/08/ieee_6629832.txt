The Tenth International Symposium on Wireless Communication Systems 2013

Defining Turbo Codes as Irregular LDPC Codes
J.Bas1
Centre Tecnològic de Telecomunicacions (CTTC), Av. Carl Friedrich Gauss s/n, Parc de la Mediterrània, Castelldefels,(Barcelona), Spain jbas@cttc.es

Abstract— Turbo Codes (TC) and Low-Density ParityCheck (LDPC) codes share similar structures in their respective error correction process (e.g. two-stage iterative decoding, Log-Likelihood based, etc.). These relationships can be used to express TCs in terms of the parity-check matrix of LDPCs but constrained to be: i) of low-density and, ii) without cycles of 4th order [1][2]. Otherwise the performance of Believe Propagation (BP) algorithm, the decoding strategy of LDPC codes, degrades notably [3],[4]. The introduction of constraints in TC’s parity-check matrix determines the active shift registers of its convolutional mother codes and its permutation matrix. Simulation results show that the Bit Error Rate (BER) of Turbo Codes decoded by means of Believe Propagation (BP) algorithm is quite similar to that offers the BahlCocke-Jelinek-Raviv (BCJR), the classical algorithm for decoding Turbo Codes.
Keywords- LDPC, Turbo-Code, Reconfigurable Physical Layer.
I. INTRODUCTION
Error Correction Codes (ECC) based on Turbo-Codes and LDPC are used to obtain high level of reliability in noisy communication channels. However, the practical implementation of these coding schemes is critical when multiple code rates and/or large code word lengths have to be used. In order to overcome these drawbacks this paper focuses on the integration of both coding schemes in a single reconfigurable codec. A set of constraints are introduced in the parity-check matrix of TCs that allow them to be considered as irregular LDPCs. These constraints condition the architecture of the convolutional mother codes and the permutation matrix of TCs. The remainder of the paper is divided in the following sections. First Section II presents the Signal Model. Next, in Section III and IV are derived the constraints on the parity-check matrix of a Convolutional Code (CC) and the interleaver for having parity-check matrices free of cycles of 4th order. Then, simulation results and Conclusions come.

IV. INTERLEAVER DESIGN OF TURBO CODES.
After computing the generator matrix of Gcc1 free of cycles of 4th degree is necessary to append it to a permuted version of Gcc2 to create the TC’s parity-check matrix. This process can introduce cycles of 4th degree between Gcc1 and ȆGcc2 . For this reason it has been devised a recursive algorithm that determines the correct rows of Gcc2 to use. The algorithm starts searching the positions of the zeros and ones in the rows of the generator matrices of Gcc1 and, Gcc2 .Next, it selects a
random row from Gcc2 for being the first row of ȆGcc2 . At that point, to obtain the second row of ȆGcc2 , only it is necessary to add zeros in the columns of the second row of ȆGcc2 which are equal to one for its first row. However, for selecting the following rows of ȆGcc2 is necessary to introduce an additional constraint: the third or next rows of ȆGcc2 cannot contain an one in a given position of the row if for this column of ȆGcc2 there is an one that can generate a cycle of 4th degree with a column of Gcc1 . After obtaining the constraints for
choosing a row of ȆGcc2 the algorithm picks up one of them randomly. The process continues until all rows of Gcc2 have been permuted. It is possible that in some iteration there is no row in Gcc2 that satisfies the constraints for selecting the rows
of ȆGcc2 . In this case, the previous row of ȆGcc2 has to be changed by another possible row that satisfies the two constraints for being a row of ȆGcc2 .
