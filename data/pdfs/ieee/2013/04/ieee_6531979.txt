Error-Prediction Analyses in 1X, 2X and 3Xnm NAND Flash Memories for System-Level Reliability
Improvement of Solid-State Drives (SSDs)

Shuhei Tanakamaru1, 2, Masafumi Doi1, and Ken Takeuchi1
1Department of Electrical, Electronic, and Communication Engineering, Chuo University, Tokyo, Japan 2Department of Electrical Engineering and Information Systems, University of Tokyo, Tokyo, Japan
Phone: +81-3-3817-7374
E-mail: tanakamaru@takeuchi-lab.org

Abstract—The system-level reliability of solid-state drives (SSDs) is investigated with 1X, 2X and 3Xnm NAND flash memories. The reliability degradation of NAND with scaling is an serious issue. Advanced ECC with signal processing such as errorprediction low-density parity-check (EP-LDPC) and error recovery (ER) scheme will be needed in the future SSDs. In this paper, the NAND reliability information used for EP-LDPC and ER is examined. System-level reliability with conventional ECC and EP-LDPC is measured.
Keywords-NAND flash memory; error-correcting code; ECC; low-density parity-check; LDPC

I. INTRODUCTION
Solid-state drives (SSDs) which consists of NAND flash memories are replacing hard-disk drives thanks to its highperformance, low-power operation. To increase the SSD capacity, the design rule of the NAND flash memory has reached to 19nm or sub-20nm as a result of continuous, aggressive scaling [1-3]. However, a number of reliability issues such as cell-to-cell interference and hot-carrier injection (HCI) are becoming more severe with scaling [4-6]. Therefore, the capacity of the NAND flash memory has been increased at the sacrifice of reliability. The block lifetime of EP-LDPC is determined by whether the BER exceeds the acceptable BER in [8] (0.59% and 4.8% for CR=9/10 and 2/3, respectively) or not. Moreover, when EP-LDPC with CR of 2/3 is applied to 1Xnm NAND, the block lifetime greatly exceeds that of 2Xnm NAND with 44bit correction ECC (Fig. 14(b)).

EP-LDPC scheme compensates the reliability degradation with scaling.
III. CONCLUSION
Advanced ECCs and reliability improvement techniques such as EP-LDPC and ER are essential techniques for future SSDs due to the significant reliability degradation with scaling. Measurement data for reliability table used in EP-LDPC are shown with 1X-3Xnm NAND flash memories. Reliability degradation of the NAND with scaling and high temperature is clearly observed. Time dependences of PD and DR error are analyzed for ER scheme. Although there was no obvious trend in tCrossPoint with scaling, ER scheme adaptively detects the error mode. Then, the block lifetime which represents the systemlevel reliability is analyzed with various ECC strengths. The code rate of EP-LDPC for the future SSD is considered to maintain the reliability with scaling. EP-LDPC with CR of 9/10 is needed to keep the reliability comparable to 3Xnm NAND with 20bit correction BCH ECC.
