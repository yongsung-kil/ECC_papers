AN EFFICIENT LDPC ENCODING SCHEME FOR CMMB BASED ON IMPROVED LU DECOMPOSITION
Guoche Qin, Ming Yan, Guorong Hu
GxSOC Lab, Communication university of China No.1 Dingfuzhuang East Street, Chaoyang District, Beijing, 100024, P.R. China

Keywords: LDPC, CMMB, FEC.
Abstract
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are widely applied in modern communication system for its capability of approaching channel capacity at low complexity. We design an efficient encoding scheme based on LU decomposition for China mobile multimedia broadcasting (CMMB). We search for element with lightest row-multiplying-column weight from the k th row and k th column, and exchange it with diagonal element of k th row. Then we operate Gaussian elimination to generate triangular matrices. We improve classic LU decomposition by performing both row and column permutations to obtain triangular matrices with lower density and demonstrate procedure of the improved algorithm. Encoding complexity of the proposed method is linear in codeword length with small coefficient. We show that the proposed method can significantly reduce computing operation and cost no extra hardware.
1 Introduction
China mobile multimedia broadcasting (CMMB) is Chinese industrial standard in mobile multimedia broadcasting since 2006. CMMB system provides users with audio, video and data services to mobile, portable receiver using terrestrial or/and satellite transmitters in 30MHz~3000MHz [2]. CMMB system employs coded orthogonal frequency division multiplexing (COFDM) technology to guarantee reliability and high capacity. Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are adopted in CMMB system for its capability of achieving a significant fraction of channel capacity at a moderate implementation effort. LDPC codes were originally invented by Gallager [7] in 1962. Gallager investigated how to design regular LDPC codes and proposed iterative decoding algorithms in his paper, which showed that LDPC codes were capable of approaching channel capacity at low complexity. However, LDPC codes did not capture peoplesвҖҷ attention for the next 30 years. With the success of turbo code, LDPC codes evoked peoplesвҖҷ interest because it and turbo code were both based on a similar philosophy. In 1996, Mackay rediscovered LDPC codes and proved it to be high-performance codes [1]. After that, LDPC codes were extended to include irregular codes [1], [5], [6] and new methods for the investigation of

message-passing decoders (or iterative decoding algorithm) were demonstrated [5], [6]. At present LDPC codes were widely utilized in communication systems for its wide range of tradeoff between performance and decoding complexity. Since LDPC codes apparently demand for high encoding complexity, many researches concerning LDPC codes now is about how to decrease encoding complexity. Richardson and Urbanke provided an efficient encoding method called RU algorithm [11]. They performed row and column permutations to transform parity-check matrix into approximate lower triangular form. They proved that the complexity of RU algorithm was linear in the codeword length. Neal proposed a new encoding scheme which prevented encoder from processing dense matrix [8]. He generated parity-check codeword based on a lower triangular and an upper triangular matrix; therefore, we name this encoding method as LU algorithm. LU algorithm showed ability to reduce encoding complexity, which was linear in codeword length and had small coefficient. This paper is devoted to seeking an efficient encoding scheme for LDPC codes applied in CMMB system.
We first introduce a brief review of basic theory about LDPC codes and illustrate structure of LDPC codes specified by CMMB in Section 2. We analyze parity-check matrix  of LDPC codes in CMMB system and design an efficient encoding algorithm according to property of  in Section 3. The proposed encoding method is based on LU decomposition. We explicitly demonstrate the procedure of decomposing parity-check matrix  and explain why LU decomposition can bring in lower complexity. Furthermore, we improve LU decomposition to obtain minimum encoding complexity by exploiting distinctive structure of LDPC codes in CMMB. At the end of Section 3, we provide simulation results to prove our theory. Finally, in Section 4, we draw a conclusion that encoding algorithm based on improved LU decomposition is more suitable in CMMB system than that based on classic LU decomposition.

Su illustrated an extended LU decomposition algorithm by perform row permutation and Gaussian elimination [3]. Classic LU decomposition algorithms do not perform both row and column permutation in one decomposition, because multiplying by permutation matrix increase complexity. Moreover, row or column permutations are implemented without considering its effect on density of matrix after Gaussian elimination.

Gaussian elimination may increase density of matrix if there are too many 1 in the row that is used to eliminate other rows. WeвҖҷd better choose row with the least row weight to perform Gaussian elimination. Since LDPC codes in CMMB are designed to interleave original information word and paritycheck word after encoding. It means parity-check word will be reorder in column according to certain pattern. Therefore, performing row and column permutations in the same decomposition procedure will no demand extra cost in storage and operation. We can exploit this property to devise an improved LU decomposition algorithm, which can find
out matrix L and U with lower density.

4 Conclusion
In this paper we propose an efficient encoding scheme for LDPC encoding in CMMB. The proposed encoding method is based on improved LU decomposition. Encoding complexity is linear in codeword length with small coefficient. We can draw a conclusion that the proposed scheme can significant reduce computing operation without costing extra hardware. We preprocess a large amount of matrices and store them in memory. We will use paritycheck matrix in decoding. Since matrix  and  contains information about parity-check, further research should focus on how to exploit those processed matrix to simplify LDPC decoding procedure.
