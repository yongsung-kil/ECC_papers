Evaluation of the Low Error-Rate Performance of LDPC Codes over Rayleigh Fading Channels Using Importance Sampling
Seok-Ki Ahn, Kyeongcheol Yang, Senior Member, IEEE, and Dongsoo Har, Member, IEEE

Abstract—In this paper we propose a novel importance sampling (IS) scheme to estimate the low error-rate performance of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes over Rayleigh fading channels. The proposed scheme exploits the structural weakness of LDPC codes due to trapping sets (TSs). The Rayleigh fading distribution on the bits belonging to a TS is biased by parameter scaling (PS), while the noise distribution on them is biased via mean translation (MT) according to their fading coefficients. The biases in PS and MT are determined so that the variance of the proposed IS estimator is minimized. The proposed IS scheme is compared with the Monte Carlo (MC) simulator and other IS schemes modified from the conventional IS scheme employed for performance estimation of LDPC codes over an AWGN channel. Numerical results show that it provides much more accurate performance than other IS schemes. Furthermore, the proposed IS estimator is even more efficient than the MC estimator and other IS estimators from the viewpoint of the number of required simulation runs.

Importance sampling (IS) has been widely employed to enhance the occurrence of rare error events so that the low error-rate performance of LDPC codes can be efficiently estimated [5-10]. Recently, there have been several trials on the performance estimation of LDPC codes over an AWGN channel by IS in which the mean translation (MT) of the noise on the bits belonging to a TS is employed [7-10]. These researches have focused mainly on a search for TSs in LDPC codes. In order to maximize the advantage of IS over MC simulation, it is required to find an efficient method to generate rare error events due to TSs. In particular, it is indispensable to develop an IS scheme over a Rayleigh fading channel, since it may be a basis for performance estimation of an LDPC-coded system such as DVB-T2 [11] in fading environments.

I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes, discovered by Gallager [1], are a class of linear codes that yield excellent error-correction performance over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) or Rayleigh fading channels [2], [3]. Their performance at a given signal-to-noise power ratio (SNR) can be perfectly estimated by density evolution when the code length goes to infinity [2]. However, it is not easy to predict the performance of moderate-length LDPC codes in the low error-rate region because a phenomenon called an ‘error floor’ may result from their structural weakness caused mainly by trapping sets (TSs) [4]. The design of an IS scheme for performance evaluation of LDPC codes over a Rayleigh fading channel is much more complicated than that over an AWGN channel. The difficulty comes from the fact that noise and fading realizations causing error events should be carefully generated to maximize the virtue of an IS scheme. For this reason, there have been no elegant approaches on the former problem based on TSs. Most of the known results on IS are restricted to IS for LDPC codes over an AWGN channel [6-10] or IS for estimation of the error rate of a uncoded system over a Rayleigh fading channel [12], [13]. Unfortunately, these results cannot be directly applied to IS for LDPC codes over a Rayleigh fading channel. The only IS employed for LDPC codes over a Rayleigh fading channel is dual adaptive IS (DAIS) in [14], [15], but it can not be applied to moderate-length or long-length LDPC codes. Therefore, it is a challenging problem to design an IS scheme for LDPC codes over a Rayleigh fading channel.
In this paper, we propose a novel IS scheme for performance estimation of LDPC codes over a Rayleigh fading channel. We first consider the conventional IS scheme employed for performance estimation of LDPC codes over an AWGN channel and study its performance over a Rayleigh fading channel. We then analyze two simply modified IS schemes and exploit their strengths to propose a new IS scheme. In the proposed IS scheme, the parameter of the Rayleigh distribution on the bits belonging to a TS is scaled as well as the mean of the noise distribution on them is translated according to their fading

B. Conventional IS Estimator for LDPC Codes over an AWGN Channel
Let C be a binary LDPC code of length N defined by a sparse parity-check matrix H [1]. Every codeword c (c0, c1, ..., cN−1) ∈ C satisfies HcT = 0. Since C is linear, we can assume without loss of generality that the all-zero codeword is transmitted for a simple analysis. When the SNR increases, the decoding failures of an LDPC code under iterative decoding are mainly caused by its near-codewords associated with trapping sets (TSs) of small size as well as its codewords of minimum weight [4]. Therefore, performance estimation of an LDPC code in the error-floor region depends on estimation of the contribution of each TS to the error probability. For two nonnegative integers a and b, an (a, b)-TS S is a subset of a variable nodes (or bit nodes) such that the subgraph induced by S and its neighbors has b check nodes of odd degree. It is well-known in [7] that the error contribution of an (a, b)-TS gradually increases as b deceases, when a is small.
Cole et al. [7] presented a three-step method for performance estimation of LDPC codes over an AWGN channel, which consists of a search for TSs, a measurement of the error boundary for each TS, and IS with given TSs. At the first step, dominant TSs are searched by exploiting the local

V. CONCLUDING REMARKS
We proposed a novel IS scheme for performance estimation of LDPC codes over a Rayleigh fading channel. The proposed IS scheme biases both Rayleigh fading and noise distributions by PS and MT, respectively. The amounts of these biases are determined so that the variance of the proposed IS estimator is minimized. Approximate closed-form solutions for the parameters of the proposed IS scheme are derived. The proposed IS estimator provides a better performance than other modified IS estimators obtained from the conventional IS scheme. By employing the proposed IS estimator, it is possible to reduce the number of required simulation runs lower by several orders of magnitude than the MC simulator over a Rayleigh fading channel. As a further research, it may be challenging to extend the proposed scheme an IS scheme for high-order modulation or an IS scheme for LDPC-coded MIMO or OFDM systems.
