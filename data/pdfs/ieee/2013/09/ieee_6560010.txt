Several Explicit Constructions for (3, L) QC-LDPC Codes with Girth at Least Eight
Guohua Zhang, Rong Sun, Member, IEEE, and Xinmei Wang, Member, IEEE

Abstract—Some explicit constructions for exponent matrices which correspond to Tanner graphs with girth at least eight are proposed. From these results, for any row weight of L > 3, (3, L) quasi-cyclic LDPC codes with girth at least eight are constructed for any circulant permutation matrix (CPM) size P > L(L + mod(L, 2))/2.
Index Terms—Circulant permutation matrix (CPM), girth, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, quasi-cyclic (QC).

Q UASI-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes (binary and non-binary) with large girth have recently attracted much attention [1-14], since they have low encoding complexity and generally outperform random LDPC codes for short to moderate block lengths. A (J, L) QC-LDPC code is defined by a parity-check matrix (PCM) which is a J × L array of circulant permutation matrices (CPMs) or zero matrices of the same size, namely, P × P . Although many well-known methods to construct large-girth QC-LDPC codes have been proposed, most of these methods are based on combination of some algebraic techniques and computer search methods [1-8]. In contrast to these semirandom methods, some methods devoted to constructing QCLDPC codes with girth at least eight are explicit in the sense that no computer search is required [9-14]. One of the most important advantages of these explicit methods over semirandom ones, is that the former can be more easily used as a good starting point or a basic module to construct other types of binary [15] and non-binary codes [13-14], while the girth is guaranteed to be not decreased.
Throughout this paper, a girth at least g is denoted by Gg+. The contributions of this paper are as follows. (1) An important property in respect of CPM size for G8+ QC-LDPC codes is presented. (2) Without any computer search, three novel explicit constructions for G8+ (J = 3, L) QC-LDPC codes are proposed. (3) It is proved that G8+ (3, L) QCLDPC codes exist for any CPM size P ≥ L(L + 1)/2 + 1 and P ≥ L2/2 + 1 for L odd and L even, respectively.

IV. CONCLUSION
Three new classes of (3, L) QC-LDPC codes are explicitly presented with girth at least eight. From these new codes, it is proved that (3, L) QC-LDPC codes with girth at least eight always exist for any CPM size P > L(L + mod(L, 2))/2. These results can find application in the construction of binary and nonbinary QC-LDPC codes with girth at least eight.
