A Finite Length Performance Analysis of LDPC Codes Constructed by Connecting Spatially Coupled Chains

Pablo M. Olmos∗, David G. M. Mitchell†, Dmitri Truhachev‡, and Daniel J. Costello, Jr.† ∗Signal Theory and Communications Dept., University of Carlos III in Madrid, Spain, olmos@tsc.uc3m.es
†EE Dept., University of Notre Dame, Notre Dame, Indiana, USA, {david.mitchell, costello.2}@nd.edu ‡ECE Dept., Dalhousie University, Halifax, Canada, dmitry@dal.ca

Abstract—The finite length performance of codes on graphs constructed by connecting spatially coupled low-density paritycheck (SC-LDPC) code chains is analyzed. Successive (peeling) decoding is considered for the binary erasure channel (BEC). The evolution of the undecoded portion of the bipartite graph remaining after each iteration is analyzed as a dynamical system. It is shown that, in addition to superior iterative decoding thresholds, connected chain ensembles have better performance than single chain ensembles of the same rate and length.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) block codes, invented by Gallager in the 1960’s [1] and later rediscovered in the 1990’s, are of great interest due to their potential for near-capacity performance. However, the iterative decoding techniques generally employed for LDPC decoding are suboptimal compared to maximum likelihood (ML) decoding, which is prohibitively complex for the lengths typical of LDPC codes. As a result, the iterative decoding limits (thresholds) of LDPC block codes are below their ML decoding thresholds.
By contrast the asymptotic iterative decoding performance of LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CCs), proposed in [2], coincides with the ML decoding performance of closely related LDPC block codes. The reason for this behavior is the phenomenon of spatial graph coupling that defines the structure of LDPC-CCs. In spatial graph coupling, the Tanner graph of a block code is duplicated a number of times to produce a sequence of identical graphs. The neighboring copies of the initial graph are then connected by redirecting (spreading) certain edges. Iterative decoding progresses through the spatially coupled chain starting from each end. Parity check nodes located at the ends of the chain are connected to a smaller number of variable nodes than those in the middle. As a result, the nodes at the ends form stronger sub-codes and the resulting reliable information propagates through the chain. It has been shown that the iterative decoding thresholds of such spatially coupled LDPC (SC-LDPC) codes coincide with the ML decoding thresholds of the underlying graphs [3] [4], which can be close to the Shannon limit.
Spatial graph coupling need not be limited to a simple connection of graphs to form a single chain. In [5], more general ensembles, constructed by interconnecting more than one coupled graph chain, were proposed, and it was demonstrated that ensembles with improved decoding thresholds can

be constructed. A particularly interesting example of such a construction is the loop ensemble [5].
To analyze the finite-length performance of LDPC codes over the binary erasure channel (BEC), we consider an equivalent formulation to belief propagation called peeling decoding (PD) [6]. PD iteratively removes variable nodes from the Tanner graph whose value is known. This decoding process yields a sequence of graphs whose statistics define both the asymptotic and finite-length properties of the code [7]. An estimate of the PD error probability is based on the average presence of degree-one check nodes in the residual graph, as well as the variance around this average, as a function of the code length [8], as the decoding proceeds. Typically, the PD error probability is dominated by local minima in the expected evolution. We refer to these minima as critical points.
A finite-length analysis of single chain ensembles has recently been addressed in [9]. In this paper, we consider PD applied to connected SC-LDPC code chains. In particular, we study the finite-length properties of the loop ensemble and show that, in addition to having a threshold closer to capacity than a single chain ensemble, it has better performance. The mean graph evolution is analytically computed for both ensembles and we show that, in each case, the performance is dominated by a single critical point. We then demonstrate that the loop ensemble possesses superior performance compared to the single chain ensemble of the same rate and length.

Now we consider transmission over the BEC combined with PD [6]. We start the PD algorithm by removing from the graph all correctly received information, and then one degreeone check node and its linked variable node are removed per iteration. In [7], it is shown that if we apply PD to an LDPC code, then the sequence of graphs follows a typical path or

V. CONCLUSIONS
The connection of SC-LDPC code chains provides an approach to extending the spatial graph coupling phenomenon from simple (single chain) graph coupling to more general coupled structures. We have demonstrated that connecting coupled chains can result in code ensembles with better thresholds and improved finite-length decoding error probability compared to single chain SC-LDPC code ensembles of the same length and rate.
