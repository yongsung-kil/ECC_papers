Abstract— In this paper, we propose a high-speed LDPC encoder architecture for the DVB-S2 standard. The proposed LDPC encoding architecture is based on parallel 360 bit-wise operations. The key issues for realizing a high speed are two kinds of index addresses and making efficient use of memory. We implemented a half-rate LDPC encoder on an FPGA, and confirmed that its maximum throughput is up to 10 Gbps with a 100 MHz clock.
Keywords—DVB-S2, LDPC encoder, Memory
I. INTRODUCTION
DVB-S2 is the first digital video broadcast standard in which LDPC codes were adopted as forward-error-correcting codes. In this standard, twenty-one variations of LDPC codes are defined by structured parity check matrices, with a code length of 16200 or 64800, and the code rate varies from 1/4 to 9/10. Some researchers have already reported their LDPC decoder for DVB-S2 [1], [2]. And to improve the waterfall and error floor performance of LDPC codes the combination of Chase-2 and sum-product algorithm is proposed[3]. One of them also implemented an LDPC encoder[4][5]. However, no mention is made about their hardware encoder architecture. However, implementing LDPC codes in hardware is by no means a trivial task, even from the point of view of today’s VLSI technology. An efficient encoding method for DVB-S2 LDPC codes is presented in this paper. We show that the encoding of DVB-S2 LDPC codes is very simple owing to the special property of the parity check matrices. This is done without reference to the code generation matrices. The memory requirement is only the length of the codewords and some index addresses. In this paper, we discuss the proposed encoding architecture, which achieves high speed and easy operation.

IV. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed a high-speed LDPC encoder architecture for the DVB-S2 standards. In addition, the fact that the proposed encoder works on a single clock, and provides parallel 360 bit-wise operations. We implemented a half-rate encoder circuit for DVB-S2 codes on an FPGA. The synthesis results show that it occupies 36801 flip-flops and 90 register blocks with 360 bits and two RAMs for the index memory, and works with a 100 MHz clock. We confirmed that its maximum throughput is up to 10 Gbps.
