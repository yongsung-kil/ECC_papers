Abstract In MLC NAND flash system, the hybrid hard-decision
/soft-decision LDPC decoder prefers a high throughput bit-flipping decoder. Therefore, the high-efficiency silicon implementation of bit-flipping decoder becomes a practically relevant topic. This paper presents a so-called fuzzy-decision bit-flipping decoding algorithm to reduce the hardware consumption and average iteration numbers. Simulations and VLSI design show that the proposed design solution can improve upto 10% higher decoding throughput, and meanwhile reduce upto 40% less silicon cost, without performance reducing.
1. Introduction As the semiconductor industry continues pushing the
technology scaling envelope, the raw storage reliability of NAND flash memory decreases accordingly, especially in the case of revolution from SLC to MLC architecture [1, 2]. In MLC NAND flash application, BCH algorithm, adopted by conventional NAND flash memory system, cannot protect data well. Because of their proven good performance, LDPC codes have attracted many attentions in the scenario of MLC NAND flash system [3]. There are two major kinds of LDPC decoding methods: hard-decision algorithm and soft-decision algorithm. The soft-decision decoding algorithm, whose performance is approaching to the Shannon limit, has enough error correction strength in MLC NAND flash application [4]. To get better performance of error correcting ability, LDPC codes require finer grained (i.e., soft-decision) sensing, which results in obvious reading latency because of the repeated flash cell sensing to get soft information[5]. Therefore, if we only use the soft-decision LDPC decoder, the throughput would be significantly limited, and the read latency would be much longer. Fortunately, the hard-decision LDPC decoder could reach high throughput when the reliability of NAND flash is high, which is related with the program and erase (P/E) cycling. When P/E cycling is low, the raw bit error rate (RBER) is also low. A hybrid hard-decision/soft-decision LDPC decoder [6] could reduce the latency of read response, keep the high throughput by hard-decision decoder, while maintain the good performance of error correction by soft-decision decoder.
The hard-decision decoder and soft-decision decoder

could use the same algorithm. In this condition, the consumption of hard-decision decoder could be saved by hardware reuse. In NAND flash application, we typically use 3-bits soft-decision layered min-sum decoder, which can be also set as 1-bit hard-decision min-sum decoder. But if we want to improve the throughput of the hard-decision Min-sum decoder, it would consume more hardware consumption because of the complicated architecture of min-sum algorithm. There are many kinds of concise and elegant hard-decision decoding algorithm which could reach high throughput with less hard-ware consumption. Therefore, a heterogeneous hybrid LDPC decoder is considered better than the one using same algorithm for both hard-decision and soft-decision decoder [6].
The bit-flipping (BF) decoding algorithm was suggested by Gallager [7] as a simple and powerful hard-decision decoding method. Many improved approaches pay attention to decoding failure rate, such as weighted bit-flipping (WBF) [8], candidate bit based bit-flipping (CBBF) [9]. But the throughput is the key point for hard-decision decoding algorithm in NAND flash application, for the decoding failure rate could be guaranteed by the soft-decision decoding algorithm. Reducing the average iteration number could increase the throughput, so did increasing the parallel degrade by reducing the unite hardware consumption. In this paper, we propose a so-called fuzzy-decision bit-flipping (FDBF) algorithm to reduce the hardware consumption and the average iteration number. The FDBF algorithm reduces the consumption of internal memory and simplifies the architecture of global comparison circuit. During the simulation and VLSI design, the FDBF decoder could reduce the hardware consumption upto 40%, increase the throughput upto 10%, without performance reducing, comparing to the traditional BF decoder.

results of the BF and FDBF decoders. We constructed the decoders with information length of 2KB, code rate of 8/9 and column weight as 4, 5 and 6, respectively. Fig.5 shows the simulated decoding failure rate vs. bit error rate for these three different column weight decoders. LDPC decoding throughput depends on two factors, including (i) the number of clock cycles per decoding iteration, and (ii) the average number of decoding iterations. Both of the BF and FDBF decoders need 77 clock cycles per iteration when the column weight is 4. Selecting the clock frequency as 200 MHz, we could estimate the average throughput of the two decoders, shown as Fig.6, which shows that the proposed algorithm improves the throughput upto 10% than before.

6. Conclusion This paper presents an improved LDPC bit-flipping
decoding algorithm and its VLSI architecture, which could be used in the hybrid hard-decision/soft-decision decoding system for NAND flash application.
