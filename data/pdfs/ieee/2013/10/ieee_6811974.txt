Abstract The layered scheme is famous for its efficiency and high throughput structure in decoding of LDPC codes, especially the quasi-cyclic LDPC codes, whose parity check matrices are made of cyclically-shifted identity matrices, which can be used as natural partitions of decoding layers. However, for many QC-LDPC codes, it leads to serious routing congestion if the cyclically shifted identity matrix size is taken directly as the layer partition and parallel factor in decoding. In this paper, a parity-check matrix reordering method is introduced, which can lower the decoding parallelism while keeping the layered decoding structure at the same time. The LDPC codes in DVB-S2/T2 standards are taken as an example to illustrate the proposed method, simulation and FPGA implementation shows that the method is effective in reducing the total decoder cost.
Index terms Quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes, Layered Decoding, Matrix Reordering, VLSI implementation
1. Introduction Low Density parity-check (LDPC) codes are known to have remarkable error correcting capabilities and highly structured VLSI architectures. Among all the published works on LDPC, the approach introduced in [1] has led to the concept of architecture aware construction of LDPC codes, especially the quasi-cyclic codes, which are now employed in many wireless communication standards such as the DVB-S2/T2[2] in digital video broadcasting, wireless local area networks (WiFi) (IEEE 802.11n), wireless metropolitan area networks (Wimax) (IEEE 802.16e)[3]. These standards all feature certain kinds of quasi-cyclic LDPC codes in their forward error correction scheme. QC-LDPC codes can be easily tailored to accommodate applications with flexible code rates and efficiently implemented with partial-parallel structure for high throughput. LDPC code’s layered decoding is essentially a grouped horizontal node scheduling scheme. The concept was first introduced by Mansour in [1] as the turbo message passing and then referred to as the layered decoding by Hocevar [4].

Besides the implementation efficiency, layered decoding can reduce the number of iterations required by two compared to the traditional flooding schedule, further more the soft-output (SO) based check node processor presents a distinct reduction in memory size[6][7][8]. Quasi-cyclic LDPC codes have parity check matrices that are made up of cyclically-shifted identity matrices, which can be used as natural layer partitions. The DVB-S2 and DVB-T2 use typical QC-LDPC codes, in which the cyclically-shifted identity matrices are of size 360*360. The problem is that if we use 360 as the parallel factor and layer partition, there will be serious congestions in routing and challenges in silicon area. In this paper, a parity check matrix reordering method is presented to divide the sub-matrices in QC-LDPC codes into smaller ones, so that parallelism is reduced while the quasi-cyclic structure is kept at the same time, and the layered decoding method can still be carried out in a traditional way. The rest of the paper is organized as follows: section 2 analyzes the QC-LDPC codes in DVB-S2/T2 as well as the problems and challenges in its layered decoder; section 3 describes the matrix reordering method as a pre-processing technique to the QC-LDPC codes; section 4 presents the simulation and FPGA results of the QC-LDPC decoders in DVB-S2/T2; section 5 is the overview and summary.
2. QC-LDPC codec in DVB-S2/T2: the problems and challenges
The 2nd generation digital video broadcasting standards (DVBS2/T2) are the pioneers to use QC-LDPC codes. In this section, an overview of the QC-LDPC codes in DVB-S2/T2 will be presented first, then followed by the layered decoding principle, then we will point out the problems and challenges in those codes and decoders.
2.1 Overview of the QC-LDPC codes in DVB-S2/T2 The QC-LDPC codes in DVB-S2/T2 have two types of code lengths, 64800 bits for long frame and 16200 bits for short frame, supports 11 different code rates from 1/4 to 9/10. 2.3 Problems and challenges The DVB-S2/T2 standards require a throughput of about 90Mbps~120Mbps, so under a digital processing clock of 180Mhz~240Mhz, parallel processing of a fraction of 360 check nodes is enough. In [5], the authors have used only 45 check node processors which reduce area significantly. However, splitting of the 360 check nodes destroys the layer structure and brings extra complexity in memory access.

The 45 parallelism in check nodes achieves the best BER performance because no double shifted identity matrices are left after reordering. Simulation shows that the more double identity matrices are left, the worse the performance will be. For DVB-S2/T2, 45 is the minimal required parallelism for a 200Mhz pipelined layered decoder to reach the expected 90Mbps throughput. 5. Summary The matrix reordering method presented in this paper serves as a preprocessing technique to the QC-LDPC codes. It can effectively lower the parallelism in SISO check node processors while keep the layer structure at the same time. In addition, the matrix reordering method proposed here can obviously lower the number of double identity matrix in QC-LDPC codes of DVB-S2/T2. Simulations and FPGA implementation of a DVB-T2 LDPC decoder with 60 parallel SISO check nodes shows that the matrix reordering method can lead to distinct resources reduction.
