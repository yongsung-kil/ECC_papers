Construction of Girth-Eight QC-LDPC Codes from Greatest Common Divisor
Guohua Zhang, Rong Sun, Member, IEEE, and Xinmei Wang, Member, IEEE

Abstract—For any column weight J and any row weight L, a novel framework is proposed such that a girth-eight (J,L) quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) code with any block length above a lower bound can be constructed via a simple inequality in terms of greatest common divisor (GCD). The main advantage is that the construction of a class of (J,L) girtheight QC-LDPC codes is transformed into a rather simple task, searching for J integers satisfying the so-called GCD constraint for L. Combining the new method with masking matrices, a class of type-1 QC-LDPC codes is presented with girth at least eight. Simulation results show that the type-1 codes perform better than the random QC-LDPC codes and quadratic-congruencebased QC-LDPC codes for moderate block lengths and low code rates.
Index Terms—Girth, greatest common divisor, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, quasi-cyclic.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have recently attracted much attention due to their capacity approaching performance and low decoding complexity. A (J,L)-regular LDPC code is defined by a parity-check matrix in which each column has weight J and each row has weight L. Quasicyclic (QC) LDPC codes [1] are a special class of LDPC codes whose parity check matrices consist of circulant permutation matrices (CPMs) of the same size, namely,P × P . Compared to general LDPC codes, QC-LDPC codes are favorable for the purpose of hardware implementation. For iterative sumproduct decoding of QC-LDPC codes, a part of performance loss is usually due to small values of girth. Moreover, as pointed out in [2], the bit-flipping algorithm can decode up to the random error-correcting capability over binary symmetric channel for a girth-eight LDPC code. Therefore, QC-LDPC codes with girth at least eight have become a focal point of research [1-10].
In this letter, we present a simple but efficient approach to construct girth-eight QC-LDPC codes from greatest common divisor. VI. CONCLUSION
Based on greatest common divisor, we have proposed a general construction for girth-eight QC-LDPC codes with any column weight J and any row weight L. The main advantage of Theorem 1 is that it transforms the construction problem into a rather simple task, searching for J integers satisfying the GCD constraint for a fixed L. Several existing methods and a new method for girth-eight QC-LDPC codes can be directly deduced from Theorem 1.
Although many algorithms, such as [1,8-10], have been proposed to construct large girth QC-LDPC codes, the advantage of the proposed GCD-based method over others (for the case of girth eight) is that (1) search of such codes has very low computational complexity since the number of code parameters is small; and (2) the CPM size P can be an arbitrary integer above the lower bound in Theorem 1, instead of a fixed integer.
