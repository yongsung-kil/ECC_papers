An Efficient Methodology for Parallel
Concatenation of LDPC Codes with Reduced
Complexity and Decoding Delay
R. Pravin Kumar and Rakhesh Singh Kshetrimayum,
Department of Electronics and Electrical Engineering,
Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, Guwahati – 781039, India.
E-mail: {pravin.kumar, krs}@iitg.ernet.in
Abstract—Parallel concatenated Gallager codes (PCGC) is a
class of concatenated codes based on LDPC component codes.
Conventional attempts for concatenating LDPC in parallel using
the well known ‘Turbo code structure’ without interleavers
have not been widely successful, because of its performance
limitation and decoding delay. In this paper we present an
efficient methodology for parallel concatenation of LDPC codes.
We show that proposed methodology for PCGC outperforms
existing PCGC in terms of BER performance in both AWGN
and flat fading Rayleigh channels. We also present that proposed
PCGC outperforms existing PCGC in terms of complexity as well
as decoding delay.
Index Terms—LDPC, PCGC, Turbo-decoder, SPA, Min-Sum,
LLR.
I. INTRODUCTION
S
INCE the discovery of Turbo codes [1], parallel concate-
nation of codes has become one of the hottest topics in
code theory. Parallel concatenated Gallager codes (PCGCs)
were introduced in [2] as a class of concatenated codes in
which two low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [3] interact
in parallel through the Turbo code structure without inter-
leavers, we call it as conventional PCGC. But the conventional
PCGC fails to fully exploit the coding gain provided by the
concatenation of two components LDPC and has more decod-
ing delay. In this paper we present an efficient methodology
for parallel concatenation of LDPC, to make use of the coding
gain it provides. We present the simulation results to show
proposed PCGC outperforms the existing PCGC in terms
of BER. We also present that proposed PCGC outperforms
existing PCGC in terms of complexity as well as decoding
delay.
The remaining of this paper is organized as follows. In
section II we simply introduce the conventional PCGC and
define its limitations. In Section III we present the proposed
approach for the parallel concatenation of LDPC and indicate
the advantages of proposed PCGC over the conventional
PCGC. In Section IV simulation results for the BER perfor-
mance of conventional PCGC and proposed PCGC in AWGN
and fading channels are presented. Conclusions are drawn in
Section V.

fixed number of local iterations in each component decoder,
the reliability(magnitude of LLR values) of the systematic
message bits (including the error bits) is increased, so it
becomes difficult for next component decoder to correct the
maximum number of errors. Thus it fails to fully exploit the
coding gain provided by concatenation of codes.

In the proposed PCGC, decoder1 and decoder2 performs
local iteration simultaneously and exchanging of information
between both codewords takes place right after each local
iteration. Hence the convergence of proposed PCGC is quicker
than conventional PCGC.
In proposed PCGC, we can make use of the symmetric
structure of decoder to reduce its complexity. Proposed de-
coder for PCGC can be replaced by the simple LDPC decoder
(defined for the same parity-check matrix), with additional
registers in each variable node. These additional registers are
used to hold the LLR values of the corresponding bit in both
codewords as well as the intermediate values it received from
checknodes. But reduction in complexity of decoder leads
to increase in decoding delay by a factor of 1
3. But yet the
decoding delay is less than conventional PCGC. Thus the
complexity of the proposed PCGC (both encoder and decoder)
is only half the complexity of the conventional PCGC.
