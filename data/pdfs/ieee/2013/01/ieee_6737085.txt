Abstract‚ÄĒIn this paper, we develop an iterative multiuser joint decoding of code-division multiple-access (CDMA) signals based on a distributed optimization algorithm. For the joint decoding problem, decoding algorithm based on the turbo principle is widely used. The algorithm consists of soft-input soft-output (SISO) channel decoder and SISO multiuser detector and it can be derived as an application of the sum-product algorithm. On the other hand, in the research area of error correcting codes, the decoding algorithm based on convex optimization has been attracting a great deal of attention. Decoding algorithm based on linear programming (LP) has decoding error rate which is comparable with sum-product algorithm with stronger theoretical guarantees. We formulate the joint decoding problem of CDMA signals as an convex optimization problem and we present a relax form of the problem. Moreover, we propose a distributed algorithm which efficiently solves the relaxed optimization problem. The proposed algorithm is based on alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM). We also see the performance of the proposed decoder through numerical simulations.

I. INTRODUCTION
Over the several years, Multiuser detection (MUD) for interference suppression in code-division multiple access (CDMA) communication systems have been studied deeply [1]. Prohibitive computational complexity of the optimal multiuser detectors has motivated the study of a number of low-complexity suboptimal multiuser detectors.
Most of the previous work on MUD focused on uncoded CDMA systems. More recently, research has been focused on multiuser detection for coded CDMA systems. One can view the configuration of the system as a serially concatenated code, in which the spectrum spread code is the inner code, and the convolutional code is the outercode. The complexity of optimal detection and decoding is possibly quite high. This complexity can be mitigated by appealing to the turbo principle for decoding concatenated codes. A large number of works have been studied in this direction [2],[3],[4]. The basic building of a turbo multiuser detector are a soft-input soft-output (SISO) multiuser detector and a bank of singleuser SISO channel decoders. Boutros et al. show that those algorithms are derived in a unified way from the perspective of factor-graph and sum-product algorithm [4].
On the other hand, in the research community of error correcting codes, the decoding algorithm based on Linear Programming (LP) has been attracting a great deal of attention

[5]. It is known that the bit-error-rate performance of the LPbased decoder is comparable to that of sum-product decoder. Furthermore it is known that the LP-based decoder has a desirable property named ML-certificate that guarantees that the output of the algorithm is optimal if it is integer value. Many computationally efficient decoders that solve the LP decoding problem have also been developed [7][8][9]. The decoding algorithm proposed in [9] is based on augmented Lagrangian method and alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) is used to solve the dual problem. It is shown that the decoder based on ADMM works very well for large scale LDPC codes.
We can expect that the convex optimization approach can work well for other decoding problems. In fact, Taghavi proposed a decoding algorithm for the decoding problem on ISI channel and show the effectiveness of the convex optimization approach [6]. In this paper, we present a linear programming framework for multiuser joint decoding of CDMA. We also present an efficient algorithm for the decoding problem based on augmented Lagrangian method and ADMM.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In section 2, coded CDMA signal model is described. In section 3, we describe the transformation of the decoding problem to the linear programming problem. In section 4, we derive the dual formulation of decoding problem. ADMM based decoding algorithm for our problem is also presented in section 4. Performance analysis of the proposed algorithm based on numerical simulation is made in section 5. We summarize our conclusions in section 6.

Fig.1 shows the bit error rate curves of the proposed ADMM decoder and turbo decoder proposed in [3]. Both algorithms are iterative and the numbers of iterations are set to 20. SISO channel decoder exploited in the turbo decoder and Euclidean projection onto a fundamental polytope exploited in the proposed decoder are also iterative algorithm. We can see that the proposed ADMM decoder outperforms the turbo decoder in terms of bit error rate.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed the iterative multiuser joint decoding of CDMA signals based on a distributed optimization algorithm. We derived optimization formulation of the decoding problem and then derived an efficient algorithm that solves the optimization problem based on ADMM.
For the multiuser joint decoding problem, the decoding algorithm based on turbo principle has been widely used and it is known that it works well. We showed that the proposed ADMM decoder also works well for the problem through computer simulation.
