Column-Weight Three QC LDPC Codes with Girth 20
Mohammad Gholami, Mehdi Samadieh, and Ghaffar Raeisi

Abstract—In this paper, some well-structured block designs are presented whose incidence matrices can be considered as the mother matrices of some regular and irregular column-weight three quasi cyclic low-density parity-check codes of girth 20. The class of LDPC codes derived from our construction possesses much more flexibility in terms of code length and rate. Moreover, the length of the regular constructed codes with rate at most 0.5 is at least 4.9 times smaller than the length of the voltage graph LDPC codes with the same rate and girth. Simulation results show that the constructed codes outperform progressive edge growth, repeat accumulate and random LDPC codes.
Index Terms—LDPC codes, Tanner graph, girth.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] are one of the most promising class of linear codes due to their ease of implementation and excellent performance over noisy channels when decoded with message passing algorithms [2]. Based on methods of construction, LDPC codes can be divided into two categories: random construction [13] and structured codes [7]-[11]. Although randomly constructed LDPC codes of large length provide excellent bit error rate (BER) performance [1], structured LDPC codes can lead to much simpler implementations, particularly for encoding. Among the most promising structured LDPC codes, repeat accumulate (RA) codes [3] are a low complexity class of structured LDPC codes whose ensemble weight distributions are easy to derive.
Each LDPC code is specified by a sparse parity-check matrix H and its corresponding Tanner graph TG(H) [6]. An important parameter affecting the performance and determining the efficiency of iterative decoding algorithms for LDPC codes, is the girth which determines the number of independent iterations [1]. The girth of an LDPC code with the parity-check matrix H, denoted by g(H), is the length of the shortest cycle in TG(H).
The overall performance of an LDPC code is generally described by two different regions, i.e., the waterfall region and the error-floor region [5]. The performance in the waterfall region is heavily affected both by the girth and by the variable and check nodes degree profiles for irregular LDPC codes. This is because, for example, in erasure channels every stopping set contains cycles [14], so that increasing the girth turns out to increasing the stopping distance (i.e., the smallest stopping set size [4]) of the LDPC code. Short cycles in the Tanner graph, lead to inefficient decoding and prevent the sum-product algorithm from converging to the transmitted codeword [12], penalizing the performance of the LDPC decoders. Accordingly, the design of LDPC codes with high girth is of great interest.
The progressive edge growth (PEG) [15] is one of the most successful random approaches for the construction of LDPC codes with large girth. PEG algorithm builds a Tanner graph by connecting the graphs nodes edge-by-edge provided the added edge has minimal impact on the girth of the graph.
In the construction of structured LDPC codes, there has been considerable interest in the design of quasi-cyclic (QC) codes. Recently, several methods for constructing good families of QC LDPC codes with large girth have been proposed. One area of recent interest is protograph LDPC codes [7], which are codes based on graphs obtained by taking random lifts of a suitably chosen base graph, or protograph. Voltage graph [16] is another framework having the ability to analyze the resulting derived base graph algebraically, even when the voltages themselves are assigned randomly.
Kim et al. [7] defined some incidence matrices, denoted by P12, P14, . . ., such that for a given integer g, if the binary matrix H does not contain any row-column permutation of P2i and its transpose P2Ti, for all i < g, then the maximum achievable girth of QC LDPC codes with mother matrix H will be at least 2g. Ensuring that P2i and P2Ti, i < g, are not submatrices of H, especially when g grows, needs a high computational complexity because H may in principle include any row-column permutation of P2i or P2Ti, as its submatrices. Therefore, attempts were made to construct some specific mother matrices to obtain QC LDPC codes with large girths. For example, the incidence matrices of Steiner triple systems (which have girth 6) used as the mother matrices of some QC LDPC codes with maximum girth 18 [9]. In addition, Bocharova [11] used voltage graphs to construct some girth20 QC LDPC codes having mother matrices with girth 8.
In this paper, we propose some new well-structured block packing designs whose incidence matrices Mm have girth 6 and QC LDPC codes with mother matrices Mm have maximum girth 20. This implicit construction results in base matrices of smaller size than the ones in Bocharova [11]. Moreover, the class of LDPC codes derived from our construction, possesses much more flexibility in terms of code length and rate, as shown in Tables I and II. As a remarkable result, the length of the regular girth-20 constructed codes with rates at most 0.5 is considerably smaller than the length
