An Adaptive Weighted Multibit-Flipping Algorithm 
for Decoding LDPC Codes 
Tso-Cho Chen', Kuan-Liang Lin2 
Department of Avionics, China University of Science and Technology, Hsinchu, 31241, Taiwan I 
Graduate Institute of Aircraft System Engineering, China University of Science and Technology, Hsinchu, 31241, Taiwan2 
Abstract-An adaptive weighted multi bit-flipping (A WMBF) 
decoding algorithm which flips multiple bits in each iteration is 
proposed 
for 
low-density 
parity-check 
(LDPC) 
codes. 
The 
multibit-flipping criterion is devised based on the least reliable 
positions in which reliability measure threshold is dynamically 
adapted during the decoding process. The proposed decoder 
converges faster and provides better performances than the 
conventional weighted bit-flipping and multibit-flipping decoders. 
Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed algorithm 
achieves a good tradeoff between performance and complexity. 
Index Terms-adaptive multibit-flipping decoding, low-density 
parity-check (LDPC) codes, weighted bit-flipping decoding. 
I. 
INTRODUCTION 
The family of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] 
can be decoded using various decoding schemes such as soft­
decision, hard-decision and hybrid schemes. The soft-decision 
belief-propagation (BP) decoding algorithms, such as sum­
product (SP) algorithm [1] and min-sum (MS) algorithm [2], 
can achieve excellent performance but often with heavy 
implementation complexity. The hard-decision bit-flipping 
(BF) decoding algorithms, such as weighted bit-flipping 
(WBF) [3], modified WBF (MWBF) [4] and improved 
MWBF (lMWBF) [5] offer a good tradeoff between 
performance and complexity. However, the standard WBF 
algorithm [3] and its variants [4], [5] flip only one bit in each 
iteration. If the algorithm is able to flip several bits per 
iteration, the probability of correcting more than one error per 
iteration is increased. A class of multiple bit flipping (MBF) 
algorithms, which are referred to as gradient descent bit 
flipping (GDBF) algorithms [6], was proposed. The GDBF 
algorithms are derived from an objective function based on 
gradient descent formulation. In [7], the decoders based on the 
group replica shuffled BF (GRSBF) decoding schemes were 
presented to reduce the required number of iterations of BF 
decoding and provide better performance than the WBF 
decoder. 
Nevertheless, there still exist noticeable performance gaps 
between those BF algorithms and SP algorithm. In addition, 
the parameters utilized in the flipping threshold of the GDBF 
algorithm or GRSBF algorithm were chosen by empirical 
testing [6], [7]. The contribution of this letter is to present an 
efficient adaptive weighted multibit-flipping (A WMBF) 
algorithm which flips mUltiple bits in each iteration for regular 
LDPC codes. 
The flipping threshold per iteration is 
dynamically adapted to the current status of the decoding 
393 
process, while avoiding any preprocessing such as finding the 
optimal thresholds of the GDBF and GRSBF algorithms, 
which is very important in practical implementation. As a 
result, average number of iterations (ANI) can be reduced 
significantly, while good error performances can be achieved 
but with lower computational complexity.

IV. 
SIMULATION RESULTS AND COMPARISONS 
To examine the true efficiency of the proposed algorithm, 
we perform simulations with (N, K) - regular LDPC codes. We 
focus on the Euclidean geometry (EG) LDPC codes [3] as 
various WBF decoding algorithms perform extremely well for 
this special class of LDPC codes. Two-dimensional (N, K) 
EG-LDPC codes are decoded with various schemes to show 
the performance of the proposed scheme. The performances of 
the proposed WMBF decoding algorithm are evaluated in 
terms of bit error rate (BER), frame error rate (FER) and ANI. 
Fig. 1 shows the error performance comparisons between 
the AWMBF algorithm and the IMWBF, WBF, GRSBF and 
SP algorithms for decoding the (4095, 3367) EG-LDPC code 
with a = 2.5. The A WMBF algorithm (with kmax = 10 ) 
performs better than the IMWBF algorithm (with kmax = 50 ) 
and the GRSBF algorithm (with kmax = 10 ) [7]. For example, 
at BER=1O-5, the A WMBF algorithm achieves approximately 
0. 9dB and 0. 7dB coding gain over the IMWBF algorithm and 
the GRSBF algorithm, respectively. The A WMBF algorithm 
is only 0. 6dB away from the SP algorithm for BER::; 10-4.

CONCLUSION 
To accelerate the rate of convergence, we have proposed an 
A WMBF algorithm, in which the flipping threshold per 
iteration is dynamically adapted to the current status of the 
decoding process while avoiding empirical testing to fmd the 
optimal threshold. 
For EG-LDPC codes, the A WMBF 
decoding 
converges 
much 
faster 
and 
achieves 
better 
performances 
compared 
with 
the 
conventional 
MBF 
algorithms. The A WMBF algorithm achieves a good trade-off 
between 
the 
associated 
decoding 
complexity and the 
achievable performance compared with the SP algorithm and 
the other BF algorithms. Hence, the advantage of the proposed 
A WMBF decoding on convergence and complexity can be 
well employed to design high throughput decoder.
