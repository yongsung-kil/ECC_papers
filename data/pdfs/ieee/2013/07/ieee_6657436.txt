I. 
INTRODUCTION 
The remarkable performance of low-density parity-check 
(LDPC) codes [1] has positioned themselves as part of many 
emerging digital communication systems, such as DVB-S2, 
WLAN (802.l1n) and WiMAX (IEEE 802.16e) [2, 3]. The 
iterative belief propagation (BP) algorithms for decoding 
LDPC codes, such as sum-product algorithm (SPA) [1] and 
min-sum algorithm (MSA) [4], have shown remarkable 
performance [5]. However, the decoding algorithms may 
require hundreds of iterations before the convergence because 
of the cycles in the graph. The decoding complexity and delay 
increase linearly with the number of decoding iterations. The 
hard-decision bit-flipping (BF) decoding algorithms, such as 
weighted bit-flipping (WBF) [6], modified WBF (MWBF) [7] 
and improved MWBF (IMWBF) [8] offer a good tradeoff 
between performance and complexity. 
A variety of excellent algorithms which adaptively adjust 
the number of decoding iterations to the instantaneous channel 
condition and decoding status have been proposed [9-12]. The 
convergence of mean magnitude (CMM) [11] criterion is 
based on the evolution of the average magnitude of the 
variable node log-likelihood ratio (LLR) messages along the 
decoding process. This approach can effectively detect the 
undecodable block and reduce average number of iterations. 
Shin et. al [12] propose to use the number of satisfied parity­
check constraints (NSPC) as a measure of decoder behaviors 
and a decision rule to predict the decoding failure of LDPC 
codes. However, they may cause considerable performance 
389 
loss at high SNRs. In the above early stopping schemes, they 
are all applied to determine when to stop decoding but can't 
make convergence faster and therefore cannot improve 
decoding performance. 
The contribution of this paper is to present a hybrid adaptive 
weighted belief propagation decoding (HA WBP) algorithms 
of LDPC codes for fast convergence, and hence reducing the 
decoding complexity. It has been found that the output 
information produced by a soft-in soft-out (SISO) decoder is 
too optimistic, and thus a correction of the output information 
is necessary [13]. To accelerate the convergence of decoding, 
we multiply the extrinsic information at the output of a BP 
decoder by a weighting factor. The adaptive weighting factor 
is based on the average weighted reliability measure (A WRM) 
which is obtained from the BF function. 
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In Section 2, a 
brief review of the BP and BF decoding algorithm. The 
proposed HA WBP decoding algorithm which integrates the 
BP algorithm with the BF function is presented in Section 3. 
Simulation results and performance comparisons are presented 
in Section 4. Finally, the paper is concluded in Section 5.

V. 
CONCLUSION 
Taking advantage of the convergence characteristics of the 
A WRM at the output of the decoder, we have proposed an 
efficient algorithm for the iterative BP decoding of LDPC 
codes. The proposed algorithm incorporates the SPA with the 
392 
adaptive weighted iteration for decoding LDPC code, and 
results in reducing the number of decoding iterations and 
improving the error performances. The scheme is very 
efficient especially when the channel SNR is lower.
