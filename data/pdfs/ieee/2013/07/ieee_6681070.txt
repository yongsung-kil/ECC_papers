An Optimizing Strategy Research of LDPC Decoding Based on GPGPU

Luechao Yuan, Zuocheng Xing, Yang Zhang, Xiaobao Chen
Department of Computer National University of Defense Technology
Changsha, Hunan, China zhuangyillm@gmail.com, {zcxing, zyang, chenxb} @nudt.edu.cn

Abstract— As powerful error correcting codes, Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes have been adopted as a fundamental building block by dirty paper coding (DPC), which indicates that lossless precoding is theoretically possible at any signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), and is a promising strategy in future communication systems. However, to achieve this performance gain demands huge computation complexity. For its lower cost and better flexibility, the GPU-based LDPC decoder is an emerging research subject. Based on the perspective of GPU hardware architecture, a multi-stage optimizing mapping strategy (MSOMS) is proposed and implemented to accelerate LDPC decoding. The performance is boosted significantly by balancing the memory access and computation load, optimizing execution configuration and the memory access pattern, and fully utilizing the on-chip high speed resources. Proposed decoders can achieve 383- and 442speedup compared to CPU-based decoder for LDPC and RA code (another ensemble of LDCP code), and the achieved throughput is comparable to existed GPU-based decoders, which confirm the efficiency of the MSOMS strategy.
Keywords-Dirty Paper Coding, Low Density Parity Code, Repeat Accumulate Code, Graphic Process Unit
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, multiuser networks equipped with multipleinput multiple-output (MIMO) antennas have gained much attention. Tremendous capacity benefits can be achieved by simultaneously transmitting to multiple users or having multiple users simultaneously transmit without any separation in time, frequency, or code domain [1]. The optimal transmit strategy that can make such benefits available is DPC [2]. It has been shown that DPC can achieve the full capacity region of the Gaussian MIMO broadcast channel[3], cognitive radio [4] and digital watermark [5]. However, the main drawback of DPC is its high complexity, which makes it hard to implement. The most computational parts of DPC are the decoding algorithm of LDPC or RA and the BCJR algorithm [6-13].
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes are proposed by Robert Gallager [14] in 1962, and Mackay and Neal [15] rediscovered it in 1996. Recently, it have been adopted by new emerging standards for digital communication and storage applications, such as the DVB-S2 standard, WiMAX, Wi-Fi (802.11n) and others. RA codes [16] are a subset of both LDPC and Turbo, which is an ensemble of codes with (a) linear-time encoding algorithm and (b) can be decoded reliably in linear time [17]. Both of LDPC and RA can be decoded based on sum-product algorithm (SPA).

SPA is based on message-passing algorithm and demands very intensive computation. Several dedicated hardware implementations of the LDPC decoder have been proposed in the last few years[18]. However, hardware solutions are expensive, non-flexible, and have long time-tomarket. Its low-precision representation compromises the capacity several decibels away. Compared to hardware approach, the GPU-based decoder is a recently active research subject for its lower cost and better flexibility.
The earliest implementation of LDPC decoding on GPU was challenged by Falcao et al.[8, 19]. Falcao presented a detailed GPU-based SPA and achieved hundreds speedup compared to CPU-based decoder, but they focused only on off-chip memory. Chang et al. [6] employed CUDA stream to overlap computation and data transfer. Soonyoung Kang [11] proposed the unbalanced memory coalescing (UMC) scheme and optimized the performance by utilizing on-chip high speed memory. We will explain more thoroughly about UMC and show how to fully utilize those hardware resources.
In this paper, we proposed a systematic optimizing mapping strategy (MSOMS) to accelerate LDPC and RA decoding by fully utilizing resources integrated on the NAVIDIA GTX280 GPU. We implement LDPC and RA codes with different length based on MSOMS. A GPU-based BCJR algorithm has been implemented in [12], but the discuss about BCJR is out of the scope of this work, we will present it in our later report.
This paper is organized as follows. Section ė briefly introduces DPC and its practical schemes. In section Ę, detailed decoding algorithms of LDPC and RA code are described. The optimizing strategy is presented in section ę. The main results and discussions are given in section Ě. We conclude this paper in section ě.

B. Unbalanced memory coalescing (UMC)
To save the transfer time between CPU (the host) and GPU (the device), the same type data such as float or integer is packed and transferred together rather than separately. At the beginning of our optimization, experimental results show that the speedup of GPU versus CPU is improved linearly with the codes scale.
But, the execute time percentage of v2c_kernel is around 30%, which does not match its computation load since c2v_kernel take the most computation task. The reason that leads to this situation lies in the memory access. In the

original implementation, both of them load irregularly (long delay) but store regularly, while assign most of the computational load to c2v_kernel who computes the f(x) function. The mismatch between memory access and computation leads to a situation that v2c_kernel waits for its operator to be ready while c2v_kernel waits for its computation results to be stored, which leads to an inefficient computation and memory access overlapping pattern.
A method named unbalanced memory coalescing (UMC) [11] can be adopted to balance this load mismatch. It means that make consecutive memory access pattern in both load and store operation for v2c_kernel and assign irregular memory access via the interleaver in both directions for c2v_kernel. Thus, c2v_kernel takes the major memory access and computation load and both memory access delay and computation delay can be overlapped efficiently. Regular memory access pattern alleviates the bottleneck of the load latency of v2c_kernel. Experimental results show that the execution time of v2c_kernel decreases 40+%, while c2v_kernel increase less than 20%. The overall execution time decreases 10+%. The performance gain increases with larger matrix scale.
C. Block-size optimization The employed GPU device contains 30 stream multi-
cores (SM), each SM consists of 8 scalar processors (SP). It is the key element of GPU that there are sufficient threads or blocks ready to be scheduled at any time to achieve the best performance. Since the number of blocks counteracts threads number contained per block, so that we should make some compromise between block-level parallelism and threadlevel parallelism. We test different block-size configurations and the experimental results show that the best block-size for matrix A, B and C are 16, 32 and64 or 128, respectively. For RA code D, E and F, 128 are the best.
D. On chip resources
Figure 8. GPU memory hierachy
To release the bandwidth shortage of global memory, multi-level memory spaces are integrated on GPU to fully utilize the locality property of memory accesses, including shared memory, registers, texture and constant memory, as shown in Fig.8. In the rest of this section, we focus on how to utilize those on-chip resources to speed up SPA decoding.
Since the size of shard memory is limited, it is not suitable to handle large data. Therefore we only store the

interleaver into shared memory which will be used twice in c2v_kernel. Besides, another important property that can benefit from shared memory is that it can transform the access pattern to global memory from strided into coalesced which can improve the effective bandwidth significantly. Strided access occurs when successive threads access memory locations that have non-unit strides, which appears in v2c_kernel. In our experiment with shared memory, the decrease of v2c_kernel is more significant than c2v_kernel, which achieves more than 50% for matrix C.
The texture memory space resides in off-chip memory and is cached in the texture cache. It is optimized for 2D spatial locality, which benefit irregular memory access pattern. A texture load costs one memory read from off-chip memory only when a cache miss occurs; otherwise it just costs one read from texture cache, which can reduce the access load of global memory. The shortage of texture memory is that its data cannot be modified during a kernel call. Since there is no data reuse within SPA kernels, so it doesn’t affect kernel execution. We put c2v_array and v2c_array into texture memory. Our experiment results show that both of the execution time of c2v_kernel and v2c_kernel decrease, but the schedule time increases impressively which cancels out the benefit obtained from two kernels for matrix A, B and D. The reason why the schedule time increase may be that the data located in the on-chip cache have to be updated among kernel calls, which consumes the additional schedule time, to keep the macro-consistency of the entire algorithm. Consequently, only large-scale data can benefit from texture memory such as matrix C.
It is widely recommended that higher occupancy is beneficial to hide arithmetic and memory latencies. However, Vasily Volkov [32] find that to allocate more registers for each thread can achieve better performance rather than to optimize occupancy. Under this enlightenment, we try to decode multi-word simultaneously with aggressive usage of register. The best performance obtained when to decode 2word simultaneously for all matrices except D (4 is the best). In this case, matrix C uses 39 registers per thread rather than 13 as before, and achieve 19% performance enhancement, while the corresponding occupancy is 0.375 compared to 0.625 as before for c2v_kernel. The performance gain achieved is around 30% for other matrices. The insight of this significant performance gain is that the instruction level parallelism (ILP) per thread can be improved by decoding multi-word simultaneously.
E. Early termination
The early termination stage can avoid unnecessary computation, which can improve the throughput performance. However, the early termination stage of SPA is a multiplication-addition-reduction algorithm, which is not as efficient as horizontal and vertical processing stages for its low computation to memory access ratio. We adopt a similar early termination scheme described in [9] and an optimal reduction algorithm[33] is implemented to achieve the best performance. The experimental results show that the throughput increase while the speedup decreases for LDPC, but for RA, it does not bring out any performance gain.

Experiments platforms are listed in Table Ę. The CPU code is written in C and uses single precision floating-point arithmetic operations without stream SIMD instructions[8]. In Ref.[7], since they did not utilize on-chip memory, performance declines when a certain dimension of LDPC code is over passed because of the bandwidth bottleneck between CPU and GPU. Although UMC was first proposed in [11], they did not point out the relation between the performance improvement and latency overlapping scheme.

Therefore, we focus on low SNR region and achieve an inferior throughput performance. The low-SNR performance of those existing works is not good as well as shown in Table ę. In low SNR region, they can only achieve 22.5 and 24.5Mbps in 1.5dB and 2.7dB, respectively. The more powerful devices GTX470 [9] and GTX480 [11] they used is also contribution to their high throughput. The achieved throughput is 15, 24 and 29.3Mbps for LDPC A, B and C in this work. Additionally, we can achieve better throughput performance on RA than LDPC.

VI. CONCLUSION
Based on the analysis of DPC, parts of its computation intensive algorithms including LDPC and RA decoding are implemented in this paper. An efficient MSOMS strategy is presented to fully utilize GPU resource. The experimental results show that maximum speedups achieved are 383-x and 442-x for LDPC and RA decoding. Compared to existed methods, our strategy achieves comparable throughput performance in low SNR region on an inferior device, which confirms the efficiency of the proposed MSOMS strategy. Actually, the chosen LDPC and RA codes are simplified ones compared to those codes employed in DPC [22-26]. To achieve the promised benefit of DPC on GPU, some compromises have to be made between performance and feasibility. How to design a feasible practical DPC scheme based on GPU with acceptable performance is a big challenge in our future work.
