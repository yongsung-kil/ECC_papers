Adjustable Ordered Statistic Decoder for Short Block Length Code towards URLLC
Fei Wang1,2, Jian Jiao‚ąó,1,2, Ke Zhang1,2, Shaohua Wu1,2, and Qinyu Zhang1,2 1Communication Engineering Research Centre, Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen), Shenzhen 518055, China

Abstract‚ÄĒThe fifth generation (5G) ultra-reliable low latency communications (uRLLC) needs short block length codes to ensure the low latency requirement, which also brings the challenge in the design of maximum likelihood decoder (MLD) with practical complexity. In this paper, an adjustable-ordered statistics decoder (OSD) algorithm is proposed for short block length code to achieve the MLD performance with much lower decoding complexity, by finding the reasonable thresholds of the test error patterns (TEP) likelihood and weighted Hamming distance (WHD) in OSD decoding. In addition, we drive the closedform expressions to the TEP likelihood and WHD thresholds, respectively, and construct the adjustable-OSD parameter table of reasonable thresholds of TEP and WHD according to the required block error rate (BLER). Simulation results validate the accuracy of our theoretical derivations, and also show that our adjustable-OSD can achieve 10‚ąí5 BLER at 4 dB, where the decoding complexity is reduced up to 80% compared with the full OSD.
Index Terms‚ÄĒuRLLC, short block length codes, ordered statistics decoder.
I. INTRODUCTION
The fifth generation (5G) mobile communications defined the ultra-reliable low latency communications (uRLLC) as an important service [1]. By ensuring ultra-high reliability (10‚ąí5 ‚ąľ 10‚ąí9 block error rate (BLER)) and low end to end (E2E) latency (‚Č§ 1 ms) simultaneously, uRLLC has become the foundation for a variety of mission critical communications (MCC) [2]. Therefore, it has been widely concerned to meet the requirements of both low latency and high reliability [1]. In the MCC applications, the transmitted block length ranges from tens to hundreds of bits [3], which is much shorter than in traditional communications. Therefore, as an important scenario of 5G applications, it is necessary for uRLLC to design an efficient and robust communication scheme for short packets [1]. In this condition, Polyanskiy, Poor, and Verdu (PPV) meta-converse (MC) bound [4] has become a benchmark in the short block length channel coding design.

Among the existing 5G uRLLC candidate coding schemes, the short block length Bose Chaudhuri hocquenghem (BCH) coding scheme with ordered statistics decoder (OSD) has the smallest difference compared with PPV MC bound [5].
OSD [6] is a suboptimal decoder with asymptotic maximum likelihood decoding (MLD) performance, and it is considered to be a general decoding algorithm for linear block short length codes. The complexity of the OSD is related to its order m, which denotes the maximum Hamming weight of the test error patterns (TEP) processed during decoding. Previous works on improving the efficiency of OSD decoding have made some progresses. In [7], a fast OSD algorithm is proposed which can reduce half of the computational complexity at high signal-noise ratio (SNR). Yue et al. proposed a segmentationdiscarding decoding (SDD) algorithm in [8] to reduce the complexity, and the distribution of weighted Hamming distance (WHD) can be described as Gaussian mixture model, which can help infer the BLER upper bound. An efficient OSD (EOSD) is designed in [9], which can significantly reduce the decoding complexity and remain the BLER performance.
Motivated by the above, an adjustable-OSD algorithm is proposed to reduce the decoding complexity and the design method is given. Firstly, we derive the probability density function (pdf) of likelihood corresponding to the correct TEP, and all TEPs, respectively. Next, we derive the pdf of WHD corresponding to the correct codeword and all codewords, respectively. According to the above theoretical analysis, we provide a calculation method and construct an adjustable-OSD parameter table of TEP likelihood threshold ŌĄT EP and WHD threshold ŌĄW HD according to required BLER under different channel conditions. Simulation results on C(128, 64) agrees well with our derived theoretical closed-form expressions. Finally, we compare several existing OSD schemes with our proposed adjustable-OSD scheme.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we proposed a novel adjustable-OSD for short block length communication for uRLLC applications. We derived the pdf about likelihood of correct TEP, the pdf about likelihood of all TEPs, the pdf about WHD of correct codeword and the pdf about WHD of all codewords, respectively, and the Monte Carlo simulations agreed well with the theoretical expressions. Based on the above theoretical analysis, we proposed a method to set the TEP likelihood threshold and WHD threshold for different channel conditions, and obtained the parameter Table I corresponding to C(128, 64). Then we compared the full OSD, fast OSD, SDD and our adjustable-OSD with OSD order = 3 and LDPC C(128, 64). Simulation results showed that our adjustableOSD can achieve 10‚ąí5 BLER at 4 dB, and the decoding complexity was reduced up to 80% compared with the full OSD. Finally, we compared the adjustable-OSD scheme with other 5G uRLLC candidate schemes, and showed that order m = 3 adjustable-OSD with LDPC has high reliability and can approach the PPV MC bound.
