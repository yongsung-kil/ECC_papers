Joint Activity Detection and Data Decoding for Grant-Free Massive MIMO Systems
Roberto B. Di Renna and Rodrigo C. de Lamare Center for Telecommunications Studies (CETUC)
Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro, RJ, Brazil Email: {robertobrauer, delamare}@cetuc.puc-rio.br

Abstract—This work addresses the problem of joint active device detection and data decoding in an uplink grant-free massive multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) scenario. We develop a message-passing solution that uses channel decoder beliefs to refine the activity detection and data decoding. In order to improve the detection and decoding performance, we introduce a dynamic message-scheduling concept, where we apply scheduling techniques according to the activity user detection (AUD) and the residual belief propagation (RBP). Simulation results show that our proposed schemes outperform state-ofthe-art algorithms in terms of frame error rate (FER), false alarms (FAR) and missed detection rates (MDR), requiring a small number of iterations for convergence and lower complexity.
I. INTRODUCTION
Part of the 5th generation wireless technology (5G), massive machine-type communications (mMTC) strongly differs from the human-type communications. Designed for specific applications, mMTC devices (MTCDs) exhibit a sporadic data traffic, where small packets are transmitted at low rates [1]. mMTC traffic is concentrated on the uplink and aims to provide massive connectivity to different types of devices that behave differently. Due to these unique features, in recent years new random access (RA) procedures as the Grant-Free Random Access (GFRA), have been proposed [2]. In GFRA, MTCDs simply transmit their packages to the base station (BS) directly, without the need to wait for a specific uplink grant from the BS. The main advantages of GFRA are the reduced transmission latency, smaller signalling overhead and improved energy efficiency (battery life) of MTCDs [3].
Due to random transmissions of many MTCDs, the load in the base-station (BS) is increased. From a physical-layer perspective, the BS has no knowledge which MTCD is active at a given time instant such that the physical-layer task is to jointly estimate the channels and detect the activity and the data of the devices [1]. Recently, several joint activity and data detection techniques based on compressed sensing (CS) [4] have been proposed to address this. By exploiting the sparsity of the scenario, the works in [5]–[7] propose regularized versions of the well-known maximum likelihood (ML), sphere decoder (SD) and minimum mean squared error (MMSE) algorithms. Greedy solutions have also been proposed, in which variations of orthogonal matching pursuit (OMP) and orthogonal least squares (OLS) [8], [9] have been conceived. Iterative and adaptive solutions that employ

channel coding are also present, in [10]–[12]. Focusing on computational cost reduction, approximate message passing (AMP) [13] variations as [14]–[16] have been reported.
In this work, we propose a novel scheme that jointly performs active device detection and data decoding in a grantfree massive multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) scenario. Our scheme is a message-passing solution based on the generalized approximate message passing (GAMP) [17] algorithm, that uses channel decoder beliefs to refine the activity detection and data decoding. We also exploit the fact that the decoding of Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes is based on message-passing. In order to improve the detection and decoding performance, we introduce a dynamic messagescheduling concept [18]. Unlike most of the message-passing works in the literature that consider a completely parallel update of the messages, we apply sequential scheduling schemes for message updates in mMTC. In particular, the proposed message-scheduling GAMP (MSGAMP) updates the messages according to the activity user detection (AUD) and the residual belief propagation (RBP). In order to assess the performance of the proposed active device detection and data decoding scheme and MSGAMP we measure the frame error rate (FER), false alarm rate (FAR) and missed detection rate (MDR) results and compare them to state-of-the-art approaches.
Firstly, in Section II we present the GFRA system model and formulate the problem. The proposed joint activity detection and data decoding structure is presented in Section III. Section IV explains the dynamic scheduling strategies used in the factor graph along with their cost. Numerical results in terms of BER, FAR and MDR are shown in Section V. Lastly, Section VI draws the conclusions.

IV. MESSAGE-SCHEDULING TECHNIQUES

As studied in our previous work [18], the use of message-

scheduling techniques dramatically reduces the computational

cost of the algorithm since it does not require update of all

messages per iteration. Moreover, depending on the applica-

tion, it is possible to reach convergence faster than that of the

parallel approach. Thus, in order to refine the joint activity

detection and signal decoding, we devised two message-

scheduling techniques in MSGAMP.

The main idea is to only update the messages related to a

defined set S of devices. In this work, we use two different

criteria to define a new set: RBP and AUD. The MSGAMP-

type algorithm that uses RBP as a metric, MSGAMP-GRBP,

considers the norm (defined over the message space) of the

difference between the values of a message before and after an

update. The focus on the devices with largest residual values is

because as the factor graph approach converges, the differences

between the messages before and after an update diminish.

Thus, if this part of the graph is more frequently updated, the

algorithm should converge faster. When the activity detection

is used as criterion, MSGAMP-AUD defines S as the group of

devices in which the estimated activity probability computed

in (24) is larger than a threshold. Since the GAMP algorithm

exhibits an accuracy activity detection rate, this metric intends

to concentrate on updating the messages of the active devices

reaching better FER results. For the AUD metric the stopping

criterion in (27) considers only the devices judged as active.

In both cases, with each new iteration i, the sequence S

is updated. After the first iteration, where all messages are

updated, we have the first residuals and activity probability

values. As in the mMTC scenario it is expected at most 10%

active devices per observation window [22], both criteria limit

the size of S as the N/10 nodes with largest residuals or with

ρˆ closest to one. From the second iteration, all messages that

belong to S(i), except for s(1i) will be updated. Then, the index

that refers to the group of messages that had been updated is

V. NUMERICAL RESULTS
We consider an uplink under-determined mMTC system with N = 100, M = 32 as main setup parameters and average the results over 104 runs. All the simulated schemes experience the block-fading channel model as described in Section II. In each observation window, the number of active devices vary, but it is limited by 10% of N . If the n-th device is active, it transmits L = 128 QPSK symbols. Under these assumptions, we consider the well-known algorithms AMP [13] and GAMP [17] and two state-of-the-art algorithms, HyGAMP [23] and Joint-EM-AMP [15]. HyGAMP includes a loopy belief propagation (LBP) part for user activity detection before the GAMPs factor graph, refining the AUD. The JointEM-AMP uses expectation maximization (EM) algorithm to perform the activity detection, while the means and variances for signal detection are provided by the AMP. As a lower bound, we consider the oracle HyGAMP (OHyGAMP), a version of HyGAMP with perfect activity detection. Since the goal of this work is to compare the joint activity detection and signal decoding, we considered perfect channel estimation. In order to give a fair comparison, all evaluated algorithms used the LLR conversion presented in Section III-C. We considered as maximum N/2 iterations for AMP and Joint-EM-AMP, 30 for GAMP and 15 for HyGAMP and MSGAMP-type schemes.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this work we have presented a new formulation that
perform joint activity detection and data decoding for mMTC.
By exploiting the channel decoder beliefs, we refine the
activity detection of the proposed MSGAMP algorithm. Using
message-scheduling techniques, MSGAMP-type schemes sur-
passes other solutions in terms of FER, FAR and MDR while
requiring a much lower computational cost.
