AbstractвҖ”In this paper, we propose a two-stage decoding algorithm for spatially-coupled algebraic low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes with linear computational complexity, which consists of a sliding-window belief propagation (BP) decoding algorithm and a majority logic decoding algorithm. Simulation results are presented, showing that the error rate performance of SC-LDPC codes can be further improved by the majority logic decoding algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
LOW-density partiy-check (LDPC) codes [1] have attracted much research attention for their capacity-approaching capability under belief propagation (BP) decoding and low decoding complexity. One of the important parameters for LDPC code designing is the girth of the Tanner graph. A part of performance loss of LDPC codes is attributed to the small girth of their Tanner graph [2]. To construct LDPC codes with girth larger than four, a series of algebraic methods have been proposed. These methods can be decomposed into two catagories: the construction based on finite geometry [3]вҖ“ [5] and the construction based on cyclic permutation matrix (CPM) array [6]вҖ“[8]. The LDPC codes constructed based on CPM array are usually quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes. The advantages of QC-LDPC codes include good structural property that facilitates its hardware implementation for decoding and low error floor [9].
For better error rate performance in the waterfall region, spacially-coupled (SC) QC-LDPC codes can be constructed. It is shown in [10] that the SC-LDPC codes have superior error rate performance in comparation with their constituent underlying LDPC block codes (BCs). Besides their outstanding error performance, the threshold of SC-LDPC codes under BP decoding is close to the threshold of the original ensembles under maximum a posteriori (MAP) decoding. This phenomenon is called threshold saturation [11].
In this paper, we proposed a class of spatially-coupled algebraic LDPC codes and their two-stage decoding algorithm, which consists of the sliding-window BP decoding algorithm and the majority logic (MLG) decoding algorithm. The computational complexity of the proposed algorithm is analyzed, and numerical results are presented to compare the performance with the underlying LDPC block codes.

D. Computational Complexity Analysis
By construction, the proposed SC-LDPC codes are systematic, hence they are easily enocoded compared with their underlying LDPC-BCs. For decoding, the proposed SC-LDPC codes can be decoded either by the conventional slidingwindow BP algorithm or by the proposed two-stage decoding algorithm.
In the case when the conventional sliding-window BP algorithm is implemented, the main computational loads are the message-updation at the VNs and the CNs. The messageupdation at the VNs and the CNs in each window region involves ndОі2 floating-point additions and (n вҲ’ k)dПҒ(ПҒ вҲ’ 1) floating-point multiplications per iteration, respectively. For the caculation of total information, nd(Оі + 1) floating-point additions are needed per iteration. In each window region, the computational complexity of decoding is O(ndIavg), where Iavg denotes the average number of iterations.
For the two-stage decoding algorithm, we need extra computational loads, which include the check-sum calculation and the syndrome calculation. The check-sum calculation requires nОіПҒ binary additions and nОі integer additions per sub-codeword, while the syndrome calculation requires (n вҲ’ k)ПҒ + m binary additions per sub-codeword.
From above analysis, the operation number of the two-stage algorithm grows linearly with the code length of the subcodeword. Thus, the two-stage algorithm is an algorithm with linear computational complexity.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have proposed a two-stage decoding algorithm for spatially-coupled algebraic LDPC codes, where the results of sliding-window BP decoder are updated by the MLG decoder. The computational complexity of the two-stage algorithm is analyzed, showing that the computational loads of the proposed algorithm grow linearly with the length of sub-codeword. Choosing their underlying LDPC codes as the benchmark, the numerical results exhibited that the SC-LDPC codes apparently perform lower error rate in the waterfall region. The simulation also show that the two-stage docoding algorithm can lower down the error rate of SC-LDPC codes compared with the conventional sliding-window BP decoder. While the two-stage decoding algorithm is presented based on the algebraic LDPC codes constructed from the prime fields, it can also be applied to other LDPC codes with girth greater than four and relatively heavy column weight.
