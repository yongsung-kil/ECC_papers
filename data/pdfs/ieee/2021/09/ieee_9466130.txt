Design of LDBCH Codes for Ultra Reliable Low Latency Communications

Zhen Sun , Graduate Student Member, IEEE, Zhao Chen , Member, IEEE, Liuguo Yin , Member, IEEE, and Jianhua Lu, Fellow, IEEE

Abstract— In ultra reliable low latency communications, the demanding requirements on high reliability and low latency make it a challenge to design capable channel codes. In this work, a candidate named low-density BCH-constraint (LDBCH) code is introduced, which generalizes low-density parity-check codes by replacing the single parity check code constraints with the BCH code constraints. Specifically, a design methodology is introduced to construct efficient LDBCH codes of short information lengths and low coding rates. Moreover, the message passing decoding of LDBCH is enhanced by max-log approximation and weighted scaling when updating the BCH check nodes. Numerical results show that, with tolerable increase of decoding complexity, LDBCH codes with the proposed decoding enhancement outperforms CA-Polar and 5G LDPC by about 0.3 dB and 0.5 dB in SNR, respectively, for the information length of 104 and the block error rate of 10−6 over an AWGN channel with QPSK modulation.
Index Terms— Ultra reliable low latency communications, generalized low-density parity-check codes, low-density BCHconstraint (LDBCH) codes, scaled max-log BP decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
U LTRA reliable low latency communications (URLLC) is one of the three scenarios in 5G. It should satisfy two exacting requirements: ultra-high reliability and ultra-low latency. Most URLLC cases require end-to-end packet error rates down to 10−6 and packet latency between 1 ms to 10 ms, such as tele-surgery, autonomous driving, etc [1], [2]. On one hand, a low coding rate is the key to achieve high reliability. But, traditional coding design mostly focuses on moderate-tohigh coding rates. On the other hand, a short block length is necessary for low latency, which, however, usually causes a severe loss in coding gain. Therefore, more attention needs to be paid to construct channel codes with sufficiently good performances at short block lengths and low coding rates.
Recently, different candidate codes with short block lengths have been evaluated for URLLC [2], [3]. Bose-ChaudhuriHocquenghem (BCH) codes with soft decision decoding have good performance at low coding rates because of their large minimum distances. However, their block length and

information length can not be selected flexibly and the decoding complexity increases rapidly with the block length. As for Turbo codes, although they are able to approach Shannon’s limit at large blocks, the performance will be degraded when the coding rate and block length decreases. More recently, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes and Polar codes are selected for 5G enhanced Mobile Broad Band (eMBB) data and control channels, respectively. Two types of LDPC codes, referred to as base graphs 1 and 2, have been standardized and base graph 2 is designed for lower rates [4]. Nevertheless, it has been demonstrated in [5] that the performance of a short block-length LDPC code constructed from base graph 2 is still not satisfactory for URLLC, since the existence of many short cycles on the Tanner graph impacts the error-correcting performance significantly [2]. Meanwhile, Polar codes equipped with the cyclic redundancy check aided successive cancellation list (CA-SCL) decoder have been demonstrated to outperform LDPC codes at short block lengths and low code rates [6]. But, compared to the finite-length performance benchmark, normal approximation (NA) [7], [8], the gap to CA-Polar should be further narrowed [9]. More importantly, serial decoding of Polar codes will bottleneck the delay performance.
In order to further improve the performance of channel codes at short block lengths and low coding rates, we would like to suggest a class of generalized LDPC (GLDPC) codes based on BCH code constraints, named as low-density BCH-constraint (LDBCH) codes [10], as a new candidate for URLLC. By extending a single parity check node (SPCN) of the original LDPC to a BCH check node (BCN), the errorcorrecting capability of the check node (CN) will be enhanced due to the large minimum distances of BCH codes and the negative effect of short loops on the iterative decoding will be alleviated. The performance of LDBCH codes can be guaranteed even if the original LDPC codes are short. In this way, efficient low rate LDBCH codes can be constructed by using BCH codes with low coding rates for BCNs. Large blocklength LDBCH codes have been applied in several scenarios and demonstrated advantages including superior performance and low error floor compared to LDPC [10]–[12]. But there is still lack of studies on short block-length LDBCH codes.
In this letter, we propose a methodology for designing LDBCH codes of short information length and low coding rate for URLLC and enhance the message passing decoding of LDBCH [10] by using max-log approximation and weighted scaling when calculating the extrinsic log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) passed from the BCNs to the variable nodes (VNs). Simulations demonstrate that the constructed LDBCH code with the enhanced decoding algorithm outperforms 5G LDPC and CA-Polar at short information length. Besides, compared to the conventional message passing decoding, the proposed decoding algorithm has better performance at high signal to noise ratio (SNR) with lower computational complexity.

II. PRELIMINARIES

A. Construction and Encoding of LDBCH Codes

LDBCH codes are constructed by replacing the SPCNs of
the original LDPC with BCNs [10], which are realized by
extending the single parity check (SPC) code constraints to
the BCH code constraints. Here, a sequence of bits satisfying
a BCH code constraint means that those bits form a valid
BCH codeword. BCH codes are a class of powerful cyclic
block codes. However, BCH codes are not able to offer flexible
choices of the block and information lengths. On the contrary,
LDPC codes have excellent performance at large block lengths
as well as more flexible coding rates. Hence, by involving
different types and proportions of BCNs, LDBCH can achieve
good performance at different code lengths and coding rates.
The Tanner graph of an LDBCH code based on its original
LDPC code and a group of BCH codes is shown in Fig. 1. There are N LDPC VNs and M SPCNs in the original LDPC code. For each p ∈ {1, 2, · · · , M }, the p-th SPCN has degree ip and is replaced by the (nip , ip−1) BCN, which is connected to ip LDPC VNs and nip − ip BCH VNs. A (nip , ip − 1) BCN means a (nip , ip −1) BCH code constraint, whose information bits are the first ip − 1 LDPC VNs connected to it and nip = 2ip−2 − 1. The last LDPC VN connected to the (nip , ip − 1) BCN along with the ip − 1 information bits still satisfies an SPC constraint. It is worth noting that an LDBCH code allows
the SPCNs to be partially replaced. That is, if an SPC code
constraint is not extended to a BCH code constraint, it is con-
sidered as a special case in which the SPCN is replaced by a generalized (ip, ip −1) BCN by setting nip = ip and this BCN is only connected to ip LDPC VNs. Denoting the information length of the original LDPC code as K and the coding rate as RL, the overall coding rate of LDBCH can be written by

III. LDBCH CODE DESIGN FOR URLLC
A. Design Principles
The decoding performance of LDBCH depends on the connections between LDPC VNs and BCNs, namely H of its original LDPC. For large block-length LDBCH, the iterative decoding performance can be estimated by the decoding threshold, which is the required minimum SNR for the convergence of iterative decoding. Similar to LDPC codes, extrinsic mutual information transfer (EXIT) charts are usually used to analyze the threshold of LDBCH codes [11], [13]. Searching the optimal threshold of LDBCH has been demonstrated to be effective for the performance in the waterfall region with large block length [11], [13]. However, URLLC requires short

block lengths and low error floors, which indicates that directly searching LDBCH with the optimal threshold is not sufficient. We have to adapt the above design principles correspondingly. Note that the CNs and VNs of H are referred to as the SPCNs and LDPC VNs in Section II, respectively.
The design principles of H are summarized as follows. On one hand, the CN distribution of H should be optimized to achieve the lowest decoding threshold of LDBCH. This is due to the fact that the types and proportions of BCNs have a dominant impact on the iterative decoding performance, which is determined by the CN distribution of H. On the other hand, in order to improve the error floor performance of LDBCH at short block-lengths and lower coding rates, the VN distribution of H should be as uniform as possible. Short regular codes have been proved to have larger minimum code distances than short irregular codes [14], which is a key factor to achieve low error floor for short LDPC codes. Meanwhile, variable-regular short LDPC codes usually have longer girths than variableirregular short LDPC [15]–[17], which will be also beneficial to further lower down the error floor.
Moreover, in order to enlarge the search space for better distributions of CNs and VNs, we relax the code rate constraint on the LDPC once given a target overall coding rate Rt and block length. Then, we generate H of the original LDPC code by performing the mixed progressive edge growth - approximate cycle extrinsic message degree (PEG-ACE) algorithm [18] to maximize the girth. It will be demonstrated in Section V-B that the LDBCH code constructed according to the above design principles achieves a lower error floor than that obtained by searching the optimal decoding threshold.

VI. CONCLUSION This letter studies a new competitive candidate channel code for URLLC. We first introduce a novel class of GLDPC codes, referred to as LDBCH codes, which replaces the SPC code constraints of tanner graphs with BCH code constraints.
