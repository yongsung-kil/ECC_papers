A 33.2 Gbps/iter. Reconfigurable LDPC Decoder Fully Compliant with 5G NR Applications
Chieh-Yu Lin, Li-Wei Liu, Yen-Chin Liao, Hsie-Chia Chang Institute of Electronics, National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University, Hsinchu, Taiwan
Email: jfish12301230@gmail.com

Abstract—This paper presents a reconfigurable LDPC decoder implementation fully compliant with all the configurations in the 5G NR standard. Based on the row-based layered normalized Min-Sum (NMS) algorithm, the optimization approaches are proposed to solve the data dependency hazard in the pipeline process. The proposed instruction-level reordering diminishes the redundant latency of our pipelined decoder architecture. Moreover, the proposed data-level rescheduling optimizes the decoding sequence to remove the remaining pipeline stalls in the highthroughput design without decoding performance degradation. Evaluated in Xilinx VCU1525 FPGA, our design achieves a throughput of 6.7 Gbps per iteration. Implemented in TSMC 28nm CMOS process at the post-layout stage, a 33.2 Gbps, in one iteration, throughput can be achieved at a clock rate 556 MHz with the core area 1.97 mm2.
Index Terms—5G NR, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, instruction-level reordering, data-level rescheduling
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, defined by Gallager in the 1960s [1], are rediscovered by Mackay and Neal in the 1990s [2], [3]. Due to the superior decoding performance, high-degree parallelization, and achievable peak throughput, LDPC codes have been adopted in the fifth-generation (5G) new radio (NR) standard [4]. There are two base graphs (BGs), 8 set indices, and 51 lifting sizes (total 102 paritycheck matrices) in 5G NR LDPC codes to support various requirements. BG1 is most used for longer code lengths and higher code rates; on the contrary, BG2 has better performance for shorter code lengths and lower code rates. Furthermore, the wide ranges of code size and row/column degrees introduce challenging hardware implementation in latency reduction and throughput enhancement.
In consideration of decoding throughput and hardware cost, the partially parallel architectures with the NMS algorithm are adopted to reduce the hardware overhead by sharing process units with proper scheduling - row-based layered scheduling [5] and column-based shuffle scheduling [6]. The main difference is that column-based shuffled scheduling vertically divides the columns, and row-based layered scheduling horizontally divides the rows. Besides, both update approaches increase the frequency of information exchange to improve decoding convergence.
The parity check matrices in 5G NR LDPC code are irregular, and both the row and column degrees have a wide range (e.g., in BG1, the row degree distribution ranges from 3 to 19; the column degree distribution ranges from 1 to 30).

Regarding this, architecture selection becomes a complicated issue to balance the required bandwidth and affordable hardware complexity. The incurring data dependency issue further lengthens the latency, which lays down a challenge in highthroughput design. By the proposed optimization techniques in the following sections, they not only resolve the above concerns but also sustains its reconfigurability.
Section II illustrates the optimization for the pipeline process, and several optimization techniques are proposed to improve the hardware performance. The hardware architecture of the proposed decoder is illustrated in section III, and the implementation results are presented in section IV. The conclusion is summarized in section V.
II. PROPOSED SCHEDULING AND OPTIMIZATION APPROACHES
In general, column-based shuffled scheduling in highthroughput LDPC decoders consumes more hardware resources due to the data dependency issues. Regarding this, row-based layered scheduling, featuring pipeline architecture, becomes a cost-effective alternative for 5G NR LDPC decoders. The corresponding decoding flow divides the whole parity check matrix into multiple layers such that one iteration is divided into 46 and 42 sub-iterations in BG1 and BG2, respectively.
Moreover, the conventional layer-parallel implementation of row-based layered scheduling suffers from high routing complexity in memory and signal control due to the irregular degree and nonzero circulant permutation matrix (CPM) distribution. Consequently, block-parallel architecture is adopted.
A. Conventional Row-based Layered NMS
In conventional row-based layered NMS hardware implementation, variable node units (VNUs) first operate to obtain messages from variable nodes to check nodes (V2C); similarly, the check node units (CNUs) compute the messages from check nodes to variable nodes (C2V). Finally, a posteriori LLR (Lpost) values are updated. However, a posteriori LLR calculation depends on the neighboring C2V messages recovered from the global min and sign (GMS) in the corresponding layer.

C. Data-level Rescheduling
Because the row-based layered decoding utilizes the updated messages layer by layer, there are data dependency hazards between the adjacent layers. According to the example shown in Fig. 2, q0,0 and r0,0 are needed for recovering Z0 (i.e., Z0 = q0,0 + r0,0) in the pipeline process of layer 1. The recovery of Z0 in layer 1 stalls the pipeline until the pipeline processing for q0,0 and GMS in layer 0 is completed.
If block (1, 0) is processed in the first three cycles of layer 1, the data dependency issue happens. The data-level rescheduling method is proposed with the following rescheduling requirements in 5G NR LDPC codes to overcome the data dependency issue:

III. RECONFIGURABLE 5G NR LDPC DECODER ARCHITECTURE
To comply with 5G NR LDPC codes, the proposed rowbased layered NMS decoder equipped with instruction-level reordering adopts flexible block-parallel architecture to support all the lifting sizes. After data-level rescheduling, the modified decoding sequence supports all base graphs and code rates.
The hardware architecture of the decoder is shown in Fig. 8. The whole design contains four main blocks, including

Fig. 8. Overview of decoder architecture
The VNU is divided into PipeStage1 and PipeStage2. Due to the instruction-level reordering, the Lpost update is performed in PipeStage1. The recovered Lpost values are propagated into PipeStage2 to calculate the V2C value, and the updated values are written back into the PreLpost memory. The CNU process is performed in PipeStage3, which consists of accumulated sorters and sign operations. The results of PipeStage3 are written back to the old V2C sign (OVS) and GMS memories. The other combinational modules, such as SyndromeUpdate and EarlyTermination, is for syndrome check when the early termination is enabled.

V. CONCLUSION
A reconfigurable decoder is proposed to support irregular QC-LDPC codes with wide ranges of base graphs, lifting sizes, and code rates for 5G NR applications. Based on the row-based layered NMS decoding in block-parallel architecture, this work proposes instruction-level reordering to re-structure the pipeline architecture to reduce the data dependency hazards. Moreover, data-level rescheduling reorganizes the decoding sequence to eliminate the remaining stalled cycles in the pipeline process.
Evaluated in Xilinx VCU1525 FPGA, the proposed decoder achieves 6.7 Gbps/iter. throughput at clock rate 111MHz. Implemented in TSMC 28nm CMOS process, the proposed LDPC decoder can achieve a throughput of 33.2 Gbps/iter. at a clock rate 556 MHz with the core area 1.97 mm2.
