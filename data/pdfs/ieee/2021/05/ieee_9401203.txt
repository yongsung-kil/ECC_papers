Abstract— This paper proposes BER evaluation system that evaluates BER of TLC and QLC NAND flash memories with reliability information such as write and erase (W/E) cycle and data-retention time by combining SSD model emulator and device characteristics of NAND flash memories. Proposed system decides which ECC type should be used in TLC and QLC NAND flash in SCM/TLC/QLC NAND flash tri-hybrid SSD, corresponding to various applications and memory capacity ratio. For hm_0 (writecold application), BCH ECC is enough to correct bit errors in TLC NAND flash. On the other hand, for prxy_0 (write-hot application), LDPC ECC must be applied to TLC NAND flash in case of small SCM capacity, large W/E cycles and high BER in TLC NAND flash. In contrast, this paper concludes that QLC NAND flash needs LDPC ECC regardless of application and memory capacity.
Keywords—TLC NAND flash memories; QLC NAND flash memories; ECC; tri-hybrid SSD; SSD emulator; Error evaluation
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, by development of internet of things (IoT), data amount stored in data center has been growing. Error occurred in solid-state drives (SSDs) with storage class memories (SCMs) and NAND flash memories is unacceptable for important user data at data center. However, if error correction code (ECC) with high correction capability is used to all memories to protect data, SSD performance is decreased. Thus, to apply optimal ECC is critical to understand not only performance and energy but also error information in SSD for various applications and memory types. In previous works [1][2], though the system evaluating bit-error rate (BER) of ReRAM only SSD has been proposed by combining characteristics of ReRAM and storage emulator, the system cannot evaluate BER of NAND flash memories. Additionally, in previous work [3], optimal ECC for NAND flash based SSD is evaluated for real workloads. However, [3] does not evaluate BER of NAND flash memories because dataretention time used in [3] is not the actual time but presumed time defined by average overwrite. Therefore, this paper newly proposes BER evaluation system evaluating BER of the worst page type in NAND flash memories from write and erase (W/E) cycle and actual data-retention (D.R.) time for SSD using NAND flash memories as shown in Fig. 1. By evaluating simulated BER with SSD model emulator [4], both ECC type and strength is decided for various applications. This paper focuses on emerging SCM/NAND flash/NAND flash tri-hybrid SSD that achieves higher performance compared with NAND flash only based SSD by intelligently allocating hot (frequently accessed), cold (infrequently accessed) and frozen (hardly overwritten) data to each memory [5] as shown in Fig. 1. In

addition, triple-level cell (TLC) and quadruple-level cell (QLC) NAND flash memories are considered as the primary storage.

Fig. 3 shows Vref sensing differences between BCH ECC [8] and soft-decoding low-density parity-check code (LDPC) [9] in TLC NAND flash memories. BCH ECC requires only 1 Vref sensing between the VTH because of using hard information during the read. In contrast, soft-decoding LDPC requires soft information, which is obtained by one hard information and extra 6 Vref between each VTH state.

Thus, soft-decoding LDPC corrects more errors than BCH ECC. However, soft-decoding LDPC largely decreases SSD performance due to extra read operation. Therefore, ECC type should be properly optimized according to NAND flash memory type and applications to avoid SSD performance degradation.

III. EVALUATION SETTING
In this paper, a transaction-level-modeling based emulator [4] and block-level real storage workloads [10] of 1-week are used to evaluate the reliability of NAND flash in SCM/TLC/QLC NAND flash tri-hybrid SSD. As shown in Fig. 4, real storage workloads are divided into write-hot and write-cold [11]. In this paper, proposed BER evaluation system is evaluated with prxy_0 (write-hot workload) and hm_0 (writecold workload). In addition, characteristics of SCM and NAND flash memories shown in Table I are used. Additionally, SCM capacity ratio is assumed (two scenarios) as 1% and 10% of the total SSD capacity. On the other hand, QLC NAND flash capacity is set to over 50% of the total NAND flash capacity to be used as main storage.

IV. EVALUATION RESULT AND DISCUSSION
In NAND flash memories, the reliability is degraded by increasing W/E cycle and D.R. time. In this section, the reliability of TLC and QLC NAND flash are evaluated in SCM/TLC/QLC NAND flash tri-hybrid SSD for 2 types of workloads.
A. Evaluation of W/E cycle in TLC and QLC NAND flash memories
Fig. 6 shows W/E cycle of TLC and QLC NAND flash for prxy_0 and hm_0 when SCM and NAND flash capacity are changed. As shown in Fig. 6, the difference of W/E cycles between TLC and QLC NAND flash is up to 23 times for prxy_0. On the other hand, that is up to 2.0 times for hm_0. This is because the amount of data evicted from TLC to QLC NAND flash is few and thus the amount of hot data is large for prxy_0 (write-hot workload). In contrast, for hm_0 (write-cold workload), TLC NAND flash works just as first-in first-out (FIFO) non-volatile buffers because of huge amount of frozen data for hm_0. Therefore, most of data are evicted from TLC to QLC NAND flash. For these reasons, prxy_0 stresses on TLC NAND flash compared with hm_0.

B. Evaluation of data-retention time in TLC and QLC NAND flash memories
Fig. 7 shows definition of D.R. time in this work. As shown in Fig. 7, first write time (t1) and erase time in a block (t2) are recorded, and D.R time is defined as t2 - t1. t2 is also defined as the time when all pages in the block become invalid pages by overwrite access and GC. Fig. 8 shows maximum D.R. time per block of TLC and QLC NAND flash for real storage workloads of 1-week. The maximum D.R. time of TLC NAND flash becomes shorter as TLC NAND flash capacity is decreased. This is because W/E cycle per block becomes larger as shown in Fig. 6 when TLC NAND flash capacity becomes smaller. In contrast, maximum D.R. time per block of QLC NAND flash is different by workloads. For prxy_0 where D.R. time of QLC NAND flash is shorter because TLC NAND flash capacity ratio is 10 %. For hm_0, maximum D.R. time of QLC NAND flash is saturated by decreasing TLC NAND flash capacity. This is related to W/E cycle per QLC NAND flash capacity. Fig. 9 shows the relationship between maximum D.R. time of QLC NAND flash and W/E cycle per QLC NAND flash capacity ratio. When TLC NAND flash capacity ratio is 10% for prxy_0, W/E cycle per QLC NAND flash capacity ratio is large regardless of SCM capacity. Thus, maximum D.R. is shorter because interval between each W/E cycle becomes shorter. In contrast, for hm_0, W/E cycle per QLC NAND flash capacity ratio is saturated by decreasing TLC NAND flash capacity. Thus, maximum D.R. time of QLC NAND flash is saturated because interval of W/E becomes constant. From these results, the maximum D.R. time of TLC NAND flash is short compared with that of QLC NAND flash regardless of workloads and memory capacity.

VI. CONCLUSION
This paper proposes BER evaluation system that evaluates BER of TLC and QLC NAND flash memories with W/E cycle and D.R. time by combining SSD model emulator and device characteristics of NAND flash. Proposed system can output BER of the worst page type in NAND flash memories that occurs in SSD. Table II shows recommended ECC that can apply to TLC and QLC NAND flash memories in SCM/TLC/QLC NAND flash tri-hybrid SSD after 1 year by using BER outputting in proposed system. For hm_0 (write-cold workload), BCH ECC that can correct a few bit errors is enough to TLC NAND flash because BER of TLC NAND flash keeps low in 1 year. On the other hand, for prxy_0 (write-hot workload), TLC NAND flash in case of small SCM capacity needs to switch from BCH ECC to LDPC ECC that can correct more bit errors because BER of TLC NAND flash exceeds BCH ECC limit over 33 weeks. In contrast, QLC NAND flash always needs LDPC ECC regardless of application and memory capacity.
