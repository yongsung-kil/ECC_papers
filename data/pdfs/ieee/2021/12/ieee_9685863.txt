Abstract—We consider large multi-user detection (MUD) via deep unfolding-aided belief propagation (BP) in coded multi-user MIMO (MU-MIMO) systems. A BP detector optimized (trained) by data-driven-tuning of embedded internal parameters achieves low-complexity and high-accuracy MUD while compensating practical imperfections. However, in actual implementation, these parameters should be optimized according to system parameters, e.g., modulation and coding scheme (MCS). In particular, when channel coding is used, it is vital not only to minimize the mean square error (MSE) but also to enhance the Gaussianity of the output log-likelihood ratio (LLR), in order to maximize the error correction capability of the subsequent soft-decision decoder. To that end, a novel loss function based on a weighted average of negentropy, which is a key measure to evaluate the Gaussianity, and MSE of the detector output is proposed. Simulation results show that the trainable Gaussian BP (TGaBP) detector optimized with the proposed negentropy-aware loss function significantly improves the bit error rate (BER) performance of the decoder output and substantially outperforms the T-GaBP optimized with the typical MSE loss function.
Index Terms—Large MIMO detection, deep unfolding, belief propagation, negentropy, channel coding, data-driven-tuning
I. INTRODUCTION
Large multi-input multi-output (MIMO) will continue to be one of the most promising technologies in the fifth generation and beyond (5G+) and sixth generation (6G) networks, in order to achieve high spectral efficiency and massive connectivity [1]. Increasing the dimensions of MIMO channels provides a significant amount of the spatial degrees of freedoms (DoFs), which can be exploited to solve multi-user detection (MUD) in uplink scenarios with low-complexity [2]. However, the detection capability is severely degraded owing to the physical limitation of the receiver and the spatial fading correlation between base station (BS) antenna elements [3]. It is thus worth considering a practical methodology to enhance the detection reliability in various MIMO channel conditions. Also, in coded MIMO settings, the detector output is input to the soft-decision channel decoder; and therefore, it is also a vital requirement of MUD that the error correction capability of the subsequent decoder can be maximized.
A message passing (MP) algorithm on the basis of belief propagation (BP) has been proposed [4]–[10] as a detector that achieves a reasonable trade-off between complexity and performance. A notable method using this approach is approximate MP (AMP) [6], which is systematically derived from a rigorous approximation of Gaussian BP (GaBP) [4],

[5] in the large-system limit, where the input and output dimensions, M and N, respectively, are infinity for a given compression rate ξ = N/M. These BP detectors consist of only tensor products whose computational cost is proportional to M N for each iteration, which is much lower than those of orthogonal AMP (OAMP) [7], vector AMP (VAMP) [9], and expectation propagation (EP) [10]. However, they are guaranteed to converge to the Bayes-optimal solution only when the measurement matrix entries follow an independent identically distributed (i.i.d.) with zero mean in the large-system limit; this assumption is not reasonable in practical MUD scenarios, and severe performance degradation is inevitable.
A promising emerging approach to tackle this issue is datadriven-tuning of BP detectors [11]–[13], where the internal parameters embedded in the iterative process to control the convergence speed and possibly fixed point are optimized via deep learning (DL) techniques; this methodology is referred to as deep unfolding (DU) [14]–[16]. To elaborate, it has been shown in [17] that DU-aided trainable GaBP (T-GaBP) can achieve comparable detection performance to the EP detector [8] in correlated MUD, with lower computational cost.
In the above literature, mean square error (MSE) and binary cross-entropy (BCE) are often employed as loss functions with the aim of maximizing the stand-alone performance of the BP detectors. It is worth noting here that maximizing the detection capability does not necessarily mean minimizing the BER of the decoder output. As the forward error correction (FEC) is usually optimized for additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels, their error correction capability is maximized when the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) input to the soft-decision decoder follows a Gaussian distribution [18]. That is, in coded MUD, the detector should be trained with a loss function that can enhance the Gaussianity of the output LLR, while improving the convergence property of iterative detection.
In light of the above, we propose a novel loss function based on a weighted average of negentropy and MSE to optimize the trainable BP detector for coded MUD via datadriven tuning. A key ingredient to evaluate Gaussianity of the output LLR is the negentropy, which is utilized as a measure of distance to normality [19]. In addition, the proposed loss function allows us to optimize the trainable BP detector more flexibly for various communication conditions by adjusting the weight parameter according to the modulation and coding scheme (MCS). II. CUTTING-EDGE DU-AIDED ALGORITHMS
A series of optimization methods using the DU technique is a powerful framework for designing elaborated iterative algorithms that work under less-than-ideal conditions while benefiting from the advantages of “model-driven” and “datadriven” approach simultaneously [15], [16]. It is also worth mentioning that the DU technique can drastically reduce the number of internal trainable parameters, compared to the typical deep neural network (DNN)-based approach, which leads to a scalable and stable training process. For these reasons, the DU technique has been applied to various problems especially in signal processing and wireless communications, e.g., sparse signal recovery [14], [20], MIMO signal detection [11]–[13], decoding of FEC codes [21], [22], and multi-cast beamforming design via unsupervised DU-aided learning [23].
While these studies show the effectiveness of DU-aided algorithm design, maximizing the individual performance of each module does not necessarily mean improving the entire performance, especially in wireless communication systems. However, due to the mechanism of the backpropagation and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) methods, it is also difficult to stably and simultaneously optimize multiple modules with different operation levels, such as the detector and decoder. Within the context outlined above, this study focuses on the design of the loss function by taking into account the nature of the later modules. We emphasize that, to our best knowledge, the loss function design for DU-aided detector optimization considering the subsequent channel decoders, which is the key contribution of this paper, has not yet been proposed.

The T-GaBP consists of three modules: soft interference canceller (Soft IC), belief generator (BG), and soft replica generator (Soft RG). Soft IC is performed for each RX symbol by subtracting the inter-user interference with the soft replica generated in the previous iteration. In BG, the effective noise in the output belief (information to be propagated) of soft IC is approximated as i.i.d. Gaussian noise as a result of the central limit theorem (CLT); this behavior is referred to as scalar Gaussian approximation (SGA) [3]. After computing the likelihood information under SGA, Soft RG approximately calculate the conditional expectation of the TX symbol.

Given the above, T-GaBP can always select an appropriate strategy at each iteration step according to the channel model and loss function in the training process.
2) Belief scaling: The adaptively scaled belief (ASB) of [5] is introduced with scaling parameters {µk, ∀k} in (A1-16) and (A1-17), in order to control the convergence speed by adjusting the softness of denoiser (projection function from beliefs to expectations). The belief scaling is performed after normalizing the belief with its effective gain. This normalization allows us to remove the belief fluctuation due to randomness of H, enabling us to design the dynamics of the scaling parameter regardless of the instantaneous channel realization. This stability is attractive as a trainable parameter.

IV. NEGENTROPY-AWARE LOSS FUNCTION DESIGN

In the internal process of the soft-decision decoder, e.g., sum-product algorithm (SPA), the bit probabilities are calculated on the assumption that the input LLR follows a Gaussian distribution that satisfies the consistency condition (mean : variance = 1 : 2) [25]; and thus, it is vital to enhance the Gaussianity of the input LLR to improve the end-performance, especially when using strong channel codes with low coding rate. Under the ideal channel assumption of very large systems, the output LLR of GaBP follows a Gaussian distribution that satisfies the consistency condition. However, in practical finite-size correlated MIMO channels, a low-precision SGA significantly impairs the Gaussianity of the output LLR, which in turn severely reduces the error correction capability of the subsequent soft-decision decoder.
To iron out this issue, we propose a novel negentropy-aware loss function for coded MIMO systems.

A. BER performance
Fig. 3 shows the BER performances of different detectors in correlated (N , M ) = (32, 32) MU-MIMO systems, where the correlation coefficient is ρ = 0.6. As a baseline performance, the curve of typical linear MMSE filter “Linear MMSE” is drawn, in which the inverse matrix operation is performed for each subcarrier; and therefore, its computational cost is higher than that of GaBP. Under such high-loaded MIMO systems in correlated channels, the typical GaBP fails to detect MIMO signals reliably (BER> 10−1) and the subsequent decoder does not work. “GaBP w/ sched. + ASB” is the performance of GaBP with the fixed scheduling of (20) and belief scaling of (21), which can significantly improve the detection capability compared to “GaBP”, but there is still a 1.0 [dB] degradation at BER = 10−5 comped to “Linear MMSE”.

B. Brief analysis of weight parameter
Let shift our focus to the weight parameter w. Fig.4 shows the Es/N0 required to guarantee a BER of less than 10−5 according to w, where the simulation condition are the same as in the case in Fig. 3. The characteristic curve is convex downward, and takes a minimum Es/N0 around w = 0.6. This result suggests the necessity to optimize the DU-aided BP detector by considering both MSE and negentropy, not just one or the other, in order to improve the performance of the decoder output. As mentioned in Section IV-D, the optimal value of w varies depending on the MCS, but the detailed analysis will be pursued in the following works.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed a novel negentropy-aware loss function to optimize (train) the DU-aided BP detector for large MUD in coded MIMO systems. The proposed loss function is given by a weighted average of negentropy and MSE, and the weight parameter can be adjusted according to the MCS to optimize the trainable detector for various communication conditions. Computer simulation demonstrate the proposed scheme can substantially improve the operating point in terms of BER performance in coded MIMO-OFDM systems, as compared to the typical counterpart.
