AbstractÔÇöIn this paper, we propose an optimization scheme of quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes based on an evolutionary algorithm, the Genetic Algorithm (GenAlg). By changing the positions and shift values of non-negative entries in the base matrix, the optimized codes can achieve better decoding performances. The optimized construction of the base matrix compatible for multiple lengths with different lifting sizes is discussed and a statistic metric used to measure the effectiveness of optimization is introduced to quantitatively evaluate the decoding performances with different code lengths. Simulation results of the proposed GenAlg optimized QC-LDPC codes with different lengths can significantly outperform those of the codes in various existing standards.
KeywordsÔÇöquasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes, scalable code lengths, genetic algorithm
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, which were first proposed by Gallager in the 1960s [1] and were rediscovered by MacKay after thirty years [2], have become an important research topic of modern error correcting codes in the last two decades. Due to their high throughput [3], near capacityapproaching performance, low encoding and decoding complexity, they have been widely used in various communications systems, such as 5G NR [4], WiMAX [5], WiFi [6], ATSC [7] and DVB [8].
One of the most promising families of LDPC codes, quasicyclic (QC) LDPC codes [9]-[11] with structured parity-check matrices are widely adopted, such as the NR-LDPC codes used in 5G, which are suitable for the hardware implementation of encoder and decoder. The block-wise QC structures with different lifting sizes support length scalable construction of LDPC codes. For example, since the lifting sizes of NR-LDPC codes provided in the 5G NR protocol [12] are varied from 2 to 384, the length of information bits for LDPC encoding is scalable ranging from 20 to 8448 according to different base graphs in 5G NR, BG1 and BG2, respectively.
QC-LDPC codes usually can be constructed by two steps [13], design of base matrix, and the lifting extension. Base matrix can be optimized by the thresholds calculated with numerical method, such as extrinsic information transfer analysis for protograph-based LDPC codes [14], [15] or progressive edge growth algorithm [16]-[18]. While the lifting operation can be implemented by random search subjected to

some constraints like algebraic constructions [19]-[21] or hillclimbing algorithm [22], [23].
Taking the NR-LDPC codes as an example, their constructions can also be carried out by the two steps mentioned above. Firstly, according to the numbers of row and column calculated by code length and rate, the base matrix can be obtained from the BG1 or BG2 in 5G NR, which both have raptor-like and rate-compatible structures. Moreover, there are eight sets of lifting sizes and shift values supported by the protocol each for the two base matrices. The lifting operation is conducted by replacing every shift value by a Z ´é┤Z cyclic permutation matrix (CPM) or all-zero matrix. Since a base matrix with specific shift values is applied to different code rates and code lengths, there is inevitable trade-off among the decoding performances of different codes at high or low signal to noise ratio (SNR) regions. For example, all the performances of candidate coding schemes in NR proposals are evaluated and compared by the frame error rates (FER, also defined as ´üž
in the following) at about 1%. Therefore, most of the low rate short NR-LDPC codes suffer from an irreducible error floor at high SNR region, which severely limit their applications to the future evolutions of 5G, such as ultra-reliable and low-latency scenarios. This problem is quite difficult to be tackled by regular construction methods of QC-LDPC codes for multiobjective optimization of length-scalable coding schemes.
Recently, the genetic algorithm (GenAlg) [24] has been used for the optimized construction of LDPC codes [25], [26] and significant performance gain has been observed in the design for short codes. A. Elkelesh et al. [27], [28] gave a flexible framework on constructing short length LDPC codes using GenAlg scheme without any graph structure. In this paper, we extend the GenAlg based optimization to the construction of LDPC codes with QC structure, where a multiobjective optimization framework is proposed for the length scalable QC-LDPC codes with different lifting sizes.
The main contribution of our work is an efficient optimization scheme of length scalable QC-LDPC codes with different lifting sizes. The proposed scheme is used to obtain performance improvements on the base matrix with different lifting sizes by changing the positions and shift values of CPMs in condition of keeping the row weight constant. Relevant simulations have been conducted on NR-LDPC codes based on BG1 and BG2 as well as WiMAX LDPC codes,

The code rate of a QC-LDPC code usually can be given by R ´é│ 1´Çş M / N where M ´ÇŻ JZ and N ´ÇŻ LZ are defined as the number of parity-check equations and code block length, respectively. Due to the equivalence of the Tanner graph and the parity-check matrix, the parameters M and N also denote the numbers of check nodes and variable nodes. When the lifting size Z changes, the coded block length also changes.

NR-LDPC codes are typical QC-LDPC codes, whose base matrices BG1 and BG2 can be unified into one common structure, also called raptor-like structure, as shown in Fig. 1. The entire matrix with a raptor-like structure is composed of

five parts, C, D, E, F and G. Here, C and D form a high-rate core matrix consisting of four rows with larger weights, where C is the information part of the core matrix, and D is the parity part with dual diagonal block structure. The submatrix E is an all-zero block matrix. Then, F and G form the low rate extension matrix for rate compatible structure, where F is a sparse block matrix of information part, G is the parity part with a single diagonal block structure.
According to the 5G NR protocol [12], the maximum numbers of rows and columns of BG1 are J ´ÇŻ 46 and L ´ÇŻ 68 , while those number are different for BG2, J ´ÇŻ 42 and L ´ÇŻ 52 . Besides, in the information part of BG1 and BG2, the column numbers of C are set to 22 and 10, respectively, in which, the first two columns filling non-negative entries with orange color correspond to the part of puncture bits. Therefore, the rate of NR-LDPC codes can be adapted from 1/3 up to 11/12 as for BG1 and from 1/5 up to 5/6 for BG2, respectively.

III. GENALG BASED OPTIMIZATION

A. Flowchart of GenAlg optimization
We develop a genetic algorithm to obtain an optimized base matrix for one or multiple lifting sizes. Based on the structure of QC-LDPC codes, the optimization can be performed from two aspects, the positions and the shift values
of the non-negative entries d j ´ÇŻ {p j ,e j} , j ´ÇŻ 1,´é╝, J in the
base matrix. The optimization on positions of non-negative entries can be carried out row by row in the base matrix, in which the primary target of each row optimization is to get better decoding performance. The optimization result of each row will also be updated to the base matrix for the optimization of the next row to achieve the performance improvement of the entire matrix. While the optimization on the shift values of non-negative entries is to find the more suitable values for the whole base matrix. Moreover, the joint optimization on the positions and shift values of non-negative entries is the combination of the above two aspects. The following discussions will focus on the joint optimization scheme in detail, where the specific optimization on positions or shift values of non-negative entries can be viewed as a special case of the joint one.

The rate-compatible structure of QC-LDPC codes gives some constraints on the optimization problem. Apart from the essential concepts code rate R, lifting size Z and dimension of base matrix J ´é┤ L , the weight of each row ´ü▓ j , j ´ÇŻ 1, , J ,
should also remain constant for the position optimization, while the column weights can be varied from 1 to J.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In our simulations, the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, binary phase-shift keying (BPSK) modulation, and the layered normalized min-sum (LNMS) decoding algorithm [29] with 50 iterations are assumed. And the normalized factors of LNMS decoding are optimized specifically for each code involved. The single instruction multiple data (SIMD) instruction sets[30] provided by Intel and near hundred threads of parallel computation units (x86 cores) are used to speed up the decoding simulation, achieving the FER results at the design SNRs within a few seconds.
The simulations on the original NR-LDPC codes and GenAlg optimized ones based on BG1 and BG2 have been done. Our GenAlg optimization process is carried out in the F region shown in Fig. 1 without destroying the dual-diagonal structure in the D region and the identity matrix G. We also provide some results of the optimized QC-LDPC codes according to the WiMAX protocol, where only the information parts of the base matrices are taken into the optimization.
For the optimization of LDPC codes with multiple lengths, the codes are selected with different lifting sizes for BG1 and BG2 of NR-LDPC codes, respectively. The codes with R=1/2 for BG1 and R=1/3 for BG2 have been chosen and their lifting sizes are in the set Z={6,12,24,48} and the set Z={4,8,16,32}, part of the second set i1 and the first set i0 in Table Ôůá ,
respectively. For these tests, the GenAlg process sets K=50 generations and each population has Q=20 individuals in it.

When the proposed GenAlg process is applied to the optimization on the other QC-LDPC codes, the performance improvements of the GenAlg optimization are more obvious. V. CONCLUSION
Since the constructions of NR-LDPC codes are compatible with various code lengths and code rates, there is inevitably a trade-off among decoding performances of different codes. GenAlg scheme is an effective tool in designing and improving code structure and has got some beneficial effects on design of short codes. Therefore, we focus on improving the construction of the base matrix of QC-LDPC codes using the proposed GenAlg by changing the positions and shift values of the nonnegative entries. The simulations of GenAlg optimized codes from the base matrices of NR-LDPC codes or WiMAX LDPC codes have been conducted. From the FER results of optimized codes and original codes, we can see that the optimization of muliple codes can achieve noticeable performance gains at different code lengths based on the same base matrix. The cycle profile, the girth and the degree distribution and other characteristics of the optimized codes will also be evolved, which will be considered in our future research studies.
