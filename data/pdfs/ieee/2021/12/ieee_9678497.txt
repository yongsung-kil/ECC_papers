Abstract— In this paper, we present three construction methods of systematic regular LDPC-CCs based on randomly or algebraically structured sparse matrices. We provide an overview of LDPC-CCs and compares between Gallager’s, Mackay-Neal and Quasi-Cyclic (QC) construction methods, which are a good choice for many wireless applications due to their errors correction efficiency and low decoding complexity.
At a rate- ⁄ , for the same size of the parity matrix to obtain the same encoding and decoding complexity, and using the Bit Flipping (BF) decoding algorithm over Additive White Gaussian Noise (AWGN) channel, we simulated the BER performance of a (3,6) regular LDPC convolutional codes, where the results showed that Gallager’s construction method (random) outperforms Mackay-Neal and QC ones.
Index Terms— LDPC block codes (LDPC-BCs), LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CCs), Gallager, Mackay-Neal, Quasicyclic (QC).
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes can provide excellent error-correcting abilities and have achieved performance close to Shannon limit. Moreover, their ability to design codes that facilitate high parallelization in hardware, and their support of high data rates argued their use in many communication standards. LDPC block codes (LDPC-BCs) offer rather limited flexibility in dynamic wireless communication systems [1], where the channel conditions and the data frame size are continuously changing.

construction methods for LDPC codes are extended in this paper for LDPC convolutional codes under the same parameters.
The construction of the parity-check matrix for LDPC-BCs or LDPC-CCs, using equivalent parameters, such as row and column weights, coding rate and girth (shortest cycle in the Tanner graph), influence the bit-error rate (BER) performance. The parity-check matrices of an LDPC-CC in this paper has exactly the same form as the parity-check matrices proposed for LDPC-BCs for Gallager [6], Mackay-Neal [7] and quasi-cyclic [8] constructions, except that for LDPC-CCs they are unwrapped for a number of periods.
The paper is organized as follows: Section II introduces fundamentals of LDPC convolutional codes and their main parameters, and the unwrapping technique of the parity-check matrix with a predefined set of design rules based on the Tanner graph. The pseudo-random construction methods proposed by Gallager and Mackay-Neal, as well as the quasi-cyclic algebraic construction method are provided in summarized way in Section III. Then in Section IV, we analyze and measure the performances of these methods via simulation results. Finally, conclusion is derived for construction methods of LDPC convolutional codes in Section V.

Random and structured constructions are the two major categories of LDPC and LDPC-CCs construction. Connections between rows and columns of a parity-check matrix or between check and variable nodes of the corresponding Tanner graph define the type of construction.

Without any structure or predefined connection pattern, random constructions connect rows and columns of a matrix. To fulfill requirements, they are actually pseudo-random connections done by computer searching algorithms.

III.2. Structured (Algebraic) Constructions

Because it is difficult to guarantee an asymptotically optimal performance in random constructions, structured constructions are used. To achieve a desired or predefined pattern, structured construction methods impose limits on row- column connections. Since randomly constructed codes have some disadvantages, such as requiring extra memory for encoding/decoding, also the randomness of the construction has an impact on the computational efficiency, which is more important than the code’s BER performance, the main objectives are to achieve good efficiency.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, LDPC-BCs and LDPC-CCs construction methods are analyzed based on different parameters. The obtained results considered under the same conditions, it is evident that the performance of the three construction methods differs significantly. Gallager’s, Mackay’s and QC construction methods of LDPC-CC have shown better performance in term of BER than the counterpart LDPC-BC.
In addition to, Gallager construction gives the best performance compared to the other methods, and this is valid for LDPC-BCs as well as for LDPC-CCs.
We conclude that increasing the size of the syndrome-former memory for LDPC convolutional code using Gallager’s construction, gives an important performance enhancement. Simulation results have also shown that variable and check node degrees are also considered as important parameters with decreased values, that an LDPC-CC can achieve higher performance, due to the matrix sparsity.
