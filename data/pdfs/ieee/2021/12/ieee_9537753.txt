Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)-Based Detection for Multi-Level-Cell NAND Flash Memory

Zhifang Shi, Yi Fang , Member, IEEE, Yingcheng Bu , and Guojun Han , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract— With the increase of program/erase (PE) cycles and retention time, it is difficult to predict the threshold-voltage distributions for detection in NAND flash memory. To accurately acquire the log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) without the knowledge of threshold-voltage distributions, a convolutional neural network (CNN)-based detection algorithm is proposed for the multi-levelcell (MLC) flash memory. The CNN-based detection algorithm employs the trained CNN to accurately calculate the LLRs for each threshold-voltage region. Furthermore, we develop a CNN-aided read-voltage design scheme to optimize the read voltages by maximizing the mutual information between the coded bits and their corresponding LLRs. Exploiting the proposed scheme, we first design three hard-decision read voltages, and then formulate more soft-decision read voltages to further improve the detection performance. Simulation results demonstrate that the CNN-based detection algorithm can achieve performance approaching that of the optimal detection algorithm.
Index Terms— NAND flash memory, convolutional neural network, read voltages, detection.
I. INTRODUCTION
T HE NAND flash memory is widely applied for the data storage in many computer systems due to their attractive features, such as low power consumption and fast accessible speed. Nowadays, to store the big data, the manufacturers devoted to increasing the storage capacity of flash memory through scaling down the cell size and applying multiple levelcell techniques [1], [2]. However, with the storage capacity increasing, the flash memory suffers from severer circuit-level noise, leading to lower reliability and shorter lifetime.
To combat the noises and interference in the flash memory, error-correction codes (ECCs) are required. With the advantages of low complexity and capacity-approaching performance, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes were applied for flash memory to boost its data-storage reliability [3]–[5]. To achieve satisfactory error-correction capability, accurate log-likelihood ratio (LLR) information are required for LDPC decoding. Thus, it is indispensable to optimize the read voltages so as to guarantee the accuracy of LLR calculation.

Another solution is to maximize the mutual information (MMI) of flash-memory channel to design desirable read voltages [7]. However, the above read-voltage design schemes assume that the threshold-voltage distributions are perfectly known by the detector, which requires an additional channel-estimation algorithm [8].
To overcome this drawback, a back-propagation neural network (BpNN) was proposed in [9] to predict the thresholdvoltage distributions for flash memory. Furthermore, a recurrent neural network (RNN)-aided detector was developed in [10] to design the read-voltage without the knowledge of threshold-voltage distributions. Nonetheless, the RNN-aided detector can only generate the hard-decision metrics. The authors in [10] also proposed integer-based reliability mappings scheme to generate the LLR information. Nevertheless, the integers were used to represent the LLRs corresponding to all the threshold-voltage regions, which are indeed inaccurate.
The goal of this letter is to optimize the LLR accuracy for data detection without the knowledge of threshold-voltage distributions, thus improving the reliability of the multi-levelcell (MLC) flash memory. Our main contributions are as follows:
1) We propose a CNN-based detection algorithm, in which the CNN architecture is trained to predict the a-posteriori probabilities of four threshold-voltage states. Furthermore, the CNN-based detection algorithm employs the average a-posteriori probabilities to calculate the accurate LLR information for each voltage region.
2) We develop a CNN-aided read-voltage design scheme to optimize the read voltages by maximizing the mutual information between the coded bits and their corresponding LLRs. Especially, the proposed CNNaided read-voltage design scheme can optimize both the hard-decision as well as soft-decision read voltages for the flash memory.
3) Analyses and simulations are carried out to demonstrate that the proposed CNN-based detection algorithm not only outperforms the RNN-aided detection, but also performs approaching the optimal detection algorithm.

VI. CONCLUSION
To improve the reliability and extend the lifetime of the MLC flash memory, we presented a CNN-based detection algorithm to accurately calculate the LLRs without requiring the threshold-voltage PDFs. Based on the proposed CNN-based detection algorithm, we further developed a novel CNN-aided read-voltage design scheme to optimize the read voltages via maximizing the mutual information between the coded bits and corresponding LLRs. The errorrate simulations demonstrated the superiority of the proposed CNN-based detection algorithm and CNN-aided read-voltage design scheme.
