Construction of Algebraic-Based Variable-Rate QC-LDPC Codes
Huaan Li∗, Baoming Bai∗, Hengzhou Xu†, and Chao Chen∗ ∗State Key Laboratory of ISN, Xidian University, Xi’an, P. R. China †School of Network Engineering, Zhoukou Normal University, Zhoukou, P. R. China Email: liha16@stu.xidian.edu.cn, bmbai@mail.xidian.edu.cn, hzxu@zknu.edu.cn, cchen@xidian.edu.cn

Abstract—In this paper, we concentrate on one algebraicbased quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) code constructed from two subsets of a finite field and generalize it to propose a class of variable-rate QC-LDPC (VR-QC-LDPC) codes, whose parity-check matrices are nested horizontally and have constant number of rows. Thus the proposed codes are significant at least in terms of storage complexity and can be simply implemented. The constructed codes also inherit the original algebraic-based QC-LDPC codes and their exponent matrices can be obtained from two subsets of the given finite field. We hereby analyze the structural properties from the isomorphism perspective, and present some rules to significantly prune the size of search space and determine the non-isomorphic exponent matrices. By distinguishing the smaller quantities of non-isomorphic matrices with cycle property metric, we can easily construct a series of nested exponent matrices with better cycle distributions and obtain the VR-QC-LDPC codes. Numerical results demonstrate that the constructed codes have better iterative decoding performance within a range of code rates and decoding iterations.
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes [1] have better iterative decoding performance and lend themselves to simple implementations, and thus have been extensively studied and successfully applied in various communication and storage systems [2]–[11]. In particular, QC-LDPC codes have been accepted by 3GPP as the coding scheme of both the enhanced mobile broadband service and ultra-reliable low-latency communication scenarios in 5G communication [12].
There are many construction methods for QC-LDPC codes. Based on these methods, it is not hard to obtain a single good QC-LDPC code. This provokes the design of QC-LDPC codes with flexible code parameters, e.g., QC-LDPC codes supporting multiple lifting sizes [11], [12] and variable-rate LDPC (VR-LDPC) codes. VR-LDPC codes are one class of codes with various code rates and play an important role in most communication systems. Two representatives of such codes are rate-compatible LDPC codes [12] with fixed information length and multi-rate LDPC codes [13] with fixed codeword length. In this paper, we focus on the remaining one, i.e., the null spaces of the parity-check matrices for all constituent codes have identical size.
The decoding of LDPC codes can in general be depicted using a graphical model called factor graph [14]. Cycle structure, which shall affect the performance of an LDPC code, is defined from the graph-theoretic point of view. So there

is a body of work dedicated to design LDPC code from the perspective of graph theory, e.g., isomorphism of graph theory. A. Tasdighi et al. [7] and C. Sun et al. [8] first expounded the isomorphism of LDPC codes when studying the optimal girth of fully-connected (3, dc)-regular QC-LDPC codes. Then isomorphism was incessantly used to design LDPC codes, e.g., short-block nonbinary LDPC (NB-LDPC) codes [9], NBLDPC cycle codes [10], and finite field based QC-LDPC codes [15]. In theory, isomorphic LDPC codes have same structural properties, e.g., cycle distribution, connectivity, and distance property, so they have similar performance when decoded with the iterative algorithms. In [7], the authors were directly to study the fully-connected exponent matrices with arbitrary shift values. Such arbitrariness of shift values may bring huge search complexity for larger values of matrix size and lifting size. One way to reduce the complexity of exhaustive search is to prune the size of search space by considering some structural exponent matrices.
Algebraically constructed QC-LDPC codes based on two subsets of a finite field [4]–[6] have been verified to possess significant error performance and wonderful structural properties. Motivated by this observation, we in this paper generalize such codes and propose one class of variable-rate QC-LDPC codes, which are capable of supporting various code rates. The constructed codes keep inheriting the original algebraic-based codes and all supported exponent matrices can be represented by two subsets of the given field. Thus, all resulting constituent codes have girth at least 6. Numerical results show that the constructed codes have better iterative decoding performance. In detail, the main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
• We propose one class of algebraic-based VR-QC-LDPC codes to support various code rates. All parity-check matrices of constituent codes have constant number of rows and are nested horizontally, which can achieve lower storage complexity and significantly simplify the hardware implementation with only the continuation of variable-node processors.
• For the given design parameters, we formulate the specific values for all code rates of desirable codes.
• By combining the structural property of the constructed codes with the isomorphism of QC-LDPC codes, we present some rules as the theoretical foundation for

II. PRELIMINARY
A. Algebraic-Based QC-LDPC Codes
Consider the prime field Fq, i.e., q is fixed to a prime. For 1 m < n q, let S 1 = {i0, i1, . . . , im−1} and S 2 = {j0, j1, . . . , jn−1} be two arbitrary subsets over Fq, where ik, jt ∈ {0, 1, 2, . . . , q − 1} for 0 k < m, 0 t < n. Then we have matrix
P = [pk,t]m×n = [ik × jt (mod q)]0 k<m,0 t<n (1)
where “mod” denotes the modulo arithmetic, pk,t ∈ Fq for 0 k < m and 0 t < n. We represent each entry of P by the corresponding CPM of size q × q and obtain the m×n array H = [Qq(pk,t)]0 k<m,0 t<n, which is an M ×N sparse matrix, where M = mq and N = nq, and acts as the parity-check matrix of the desirable algebraic-based QCLDPC code. If H has constant column weight m and row weight n simultaneously, the resulting code is (m, n)-regular. Moreover, we say a QC-LDPC code is fully-connected if all shift values in its exponent matrix are negative.
B. Cycle and Girth
The parity-check matrix H of any LDPC code can be represented one-to-one by a bipartite graph called Tanner graph [16]. One of important notations in the Tanner graph is cycle, which plays an important part in the iterative decoding. One cycle refers to a closed path that begins and ends at the same node, and its length is defined as the number of edges on this path. Since the Tanner graph is a bipartite graph, so the length of any cycles is even number no less than 4. Another important concept is the girth, denoted by g and defined as the length of the shortest cycles. We symbolize the numbers of cycles of various lengths as the vector (g, g + 2, g + 4, . . .) or polynomial C(x) = Cgxg + Cg+2xg+2 + Cg+4xg+4 + · · · , termed as cycle distribution. For the sake of discussion, we use notation C(x) to denote the cycle distribution that has largest girth and fewer short cycles throughout the paper.
For a QC-LDPC code Cqc, the mapping of its parity-check matrix and exponent matrix (lifting size is given) is one-to-one, thus in the following, the cycle distributions of its parity-check matrix H and exponent matrix both refer to that of the Tanner graph associated with H.
The cycle structure of an LDPC code shall affect its iterative decoding performance, especially the short cycles of length 4 will degrade the error performance. The well-known row&column constraint (RC-constraint) [17] can ensure that the Tanner graph of designed LDPC code has no cycles of length 4 and obtains girth at least 6. In this paper we mainly

concentrate one class of QC-LDPC codes derived from the algebraic-based QC-LDPC codes in the previous subsection. Those codes can meet the RC-constraint and thus have girth at least 6.

C. Isomorphism of QC-LDPC Codes

Definition 1 (Graph Isomorphism [18]): Let G(V, E) be

a bipartite graph with vertex set V and edge set E. Graph

G1(V1, E1) is isomorphic to graph G2(V2, E2), denoted by G1(V1, E1) ∼= G2(V2, E2), if there is a bijection “f : V1∪E1 → V2 ∪ E2”: for each edge between two vertexes v1 and v2 in

G1(V1, E1), if and only if there exists an edge between vertexes

f (v1) and f (v2) in G2(V2, E2).

The parity-check matrix of an LDPC code and associated

Tanner graph are one-to-one, so isomorphic LDPC codes are

straightforward to define as follows.

Definition 2 (Isomorphism of LDPC Codes): Consider two

LDPC codes C1 and C2 specified by parity-check matrices H1 and H2, respectively. If the Tanner graphs of two codes are isomorphic, we say C1 is isomorphic to C2, denoted by C1 =∼ C2 or equivalently, H1 =∼ H2.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, motivated by the significant performance of QC-LDPC codes whose exponent matrices are constructed based on two subsets of a prime field [4]–[6], we generalized such codes and proposed one class of algebraic-based variable-rate QC-LDPC (VR-QC-LDPC) codes. The exponent matrices of constructed codes have nested structure and thus are of significance at least in terms of storage complexity.

To make the search realizable, we presented some rules from the isomorphism perspective to efficiently determine the non-isomorphic exponent matrices. By distinguishing these non-isomorphic matrices with cycle distribution, we finally obtained a series of constituent codes with better cycle distributions. Numerical results show that all constituent codes can obtain better performance over the AWGN channel with the iterative decoding algorithms.
