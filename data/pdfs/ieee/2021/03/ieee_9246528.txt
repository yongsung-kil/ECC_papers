Low Complexity Generalized-LDPC Decoder Based on Trellis Splicing
Xuan Zhou , Zheng Ma , Member, IEEE, Li Li , and Ming Xiao , Senior Member, IEEE

AbstractŌĆö The high reliability short length low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes for Ultra Reliable Low Latency Communication (URLLC) are particularly important to future wireless communication systems. Even though the error floor region performance can be improved by using generalized-LDPC (G-LDPC) decoder, its penalty of increasing complexity can not be ignored. In this letter, we invoke the early stop criterion and the trellis splicing (TS) method to G-LDPC decoder. Hence, a G-LDPCTS decoder is proposed. The proposed method can effectively reduce the number of variable nodes (VNs) involved in BCJR (Bahl-Cocke-Jelinek-Raviv) decoding of G-LDPC; thus, it yields a lower computational complexity. The results confirm that our G-LDPC-TS decoder can achieve a significant complexity reduction without sacrificing the performance.
Index TermsŌĆö Generalized-LDPC (G-LDPC) decoder, error floor region, trapping sets, early stop criterion, trellis splicing.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes have been adopted as error control codes for the fifth generation (5G) mobile communications. The Ultra Reliable and Low Latency Communication (URLLC), which requires a low error rate (below 10ŌłÆ6) and short coded packets, is one of major application scenarios in 5G and beyond 5G (B5G) mobile communication systems. However, MacKay and Postol discovered that LDPC code was limited by its error floor in moderate region of signal to noise ratio (SNR), and a set of nodes termed as trapping sets [1] accounted for this phenomenon.
A considerable amount of literature has been published on evaluating performance for LDPC codes in error floor region. Richardson [2] described the design and implementation of predicting error floor performance of a given LDPC code by using a computational technique. An efficient algorithm was

Generally speaking, the schemes lowering error floors can be divided into two aspects: one is to remove some of harmful trapping sets during the construction of LDPC codes [5]ŌĆō[10]. Amirzade and Sadeghi [9], [10] proposed an edge-coloring technique to reveal trapping sets, which results in a range of brand new LDPC codes, such as spatially-coupled codes, QC-LDPC codes, etc. Moreover, the tightest lower bounds on the size of trapping sets associated with different girths were shown in [8]. The other is to focus on decoder-based strategies which are more flexible and can be applied to any LDPC codes. For latter case, a post-processing scheme which compared unsatisfied check nodes (CNs) with a look-up table constructed by trapping sets was used in [5]. A backtracking post-processing scheme was proposed in [11]. This scheme has a simple decoding architecture. However, its decoding latency is not stable. Yang Han and William Ryan [12] proposed three different post-processing decoders, namely, bi-mode decoder, bit-pinning decoder and generalized-LDPC (G-LDPC) decoder. Among them, G-LDPC decoder is the best decoder in terms of performance. However, it is criticized due to its high computational complexity.
In this work, we focus on reducing computational complexity of the G-LDPC decoder via early stop criterion and trellis splicing [13], [14]. The original scheme in [13] is for Turbo codes (convolutional codes based) and not suitable for G-LDPC decoder (block codes based). In order to determine which trellis could be spliced, a novel early stop criterion is proposed before trellis splicing to terminate unnecessary high computational decoding.
The contributions of this letter can be summarized as follows: (1) An early stop criterion is designed to determine the reliable variable nodes (VNs) while performing G-LDPC decoding; (2) A block-code-based trellis splicing method is proposed to lower the computational complexity of G-LDPC decoder.

A. Trapping Sets
The error floors of LDPC codes are highly related to trapping sets [1]. An (a, b) trapping set is a set of a VNs, which induces a sub-graph with b odd-degree CNs and arbitrary number of even-degree CNs. Trapping sets with small a and b, also known as dominant trapping sets, dominate the error floor performance [3]. Because trapping sets usually consist of several small cycles, the associated log-likelihood ratio (LLR) values will lose independence gradually along with the increasing number of decoding iterations.

B. G-LDPC Decoder
Each SPC in traditional belief propagation (BP) decoding is locally optimal, while the global optimization can be achieved by combining all SPCs together. However, the complexity of such combination is extremely high, which almost gets close to the complexity of maximum likelihood (ML) decoding and hence becomes prohibitive in practice. G-LDPC decoder combines several SPCs into a generalized check process (GCP) to provide a sub-optimal solution. As described in Algorithm 1, the main difference between G-LDPC decoder and traditional decoder (e.g., min-sum) is the way of calculating extrinsic information of CNs (line7-9). For GCPs, the extrinsic information can be calculated by BCJR (Bahl-Cocke-JelinekRaviv) algorithm.
For G-LDPC decoder, odd-degree CNs in a trapping set are combined into a GCP. Then, the CNs in any pair of GCPs will not subject to the same SPC constraint. This requirement is called as ŌĆ£non-overlapped ruleŌĆØ. Because trapping sets only depend on the structures of codes, the construction of GCPs can be done before decoding. After obtaining the channel information LLRch, each VN calculates the LLRs from VN to CNs (LLRv2c), and each SPC calculates the LLRs from SPC to VNs (LLRspc2v). Then, each GCP employs BCJR algorithm to calculate the LLRs from GCP to VNs (LLRgcp2v) instead.

III. PROPOSED SCHEME
A. Algorithm Description
Our proposed G-LDPC-TS scheme is described in Algorithm 2. Because the computational complexity of BCJR algorithm increases as the GCP becomes large, original G-LDPC decoder cannot afford the increased complexity. We invoke the trellis splicing method into BCJR to decrease the entire decoding complexity. In trellis splicing method, when a bit has been determined by early stop criterion, the branches between this VN and its neighbouring bits will be omitted. Normally, the early stop requires a range of iterations(usually larger than 10) to converging. Similarly, we prune the branches connected with reliable bits in the BCJR trellis. These reliable bits are determined by an associated early stop criterion.
Every GCP is related to a trapping set. Then bits belong to a trapping set are more error prone than other bits. Because inaccurate judgement of reliable bits may negatively affect decoding performance. Thus we jointly take the LLR values and trapping sets into account to determine the set of reliable bits (or VNs) in BCJR process.

B. Early Stop Criterion
The design target of early stop criterion is to find the most reliable bits within as few as possible decoding iterations.
Firstly, we divided all the VNs included in GCP into two sets, namely reliable bits set C1 and unreliable bits set C2. Hence, it is reasonable to allocate the VNs in trapping sets to C2. Then, in each iteration, the remaining VNs in GCP will be arranged in descending order of their output LLR magnitudes. As a result, an ordered list of VNs is saved. After a number of Imax iterations, the top n elements in the ordered VN list are regarded as C1. Other elements in the ordered VN list are allocated to C2.
After determining the reliable bits set C1, the early stop criterion could be applied to the G-LDPC decoder. Consequently, during the ensuing iterations of the associated BCJR detection process, the LLR magnitudes of the VNs pertaining to C1 are fixed to a large number. According to our various experiments, normally only 2-3 iterations are required to find the reliable bits set C1. In contrast, the original early

V. CONCLUSION
This letter proposes an early stop criterion and a trellis splicing method based G-LDPC decoder. Our early stop criterion algorithm and trellis splicing method further bit from the trapping set technique. Without loss of performance, the proposed decoder could significantly reduce the complexity of previous G-LDPC decoder.
