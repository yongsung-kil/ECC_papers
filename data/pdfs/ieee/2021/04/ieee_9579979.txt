A Study on Iterative Decoding by Neural Network Detector in SMR System
M. Nishikawa1, Y. Nakamura1, Y. Kanai2, H. Osawa1, and Y. Okamoto1
1 Graduate School of Science and Engineering, Ehime University, Matsuyama, 790-8577, Japan 2 Department of Engineering, Niigata Institute of Technology, Kashiwazaki, 945-1195, Japan
We study the low-density parity-check (LDPC) coding and iterative decoding system, as a signal processing system for the shingled magnetic recording (SMR) in two-dimensional magnetic recording (TDMR). Previously we reported that a waveform equalization using a two-dimensional finite impulse response (TD-FIR) filter or an inter-track interference (ITI) canceler reduced the influence of ITI. We also proposed a neural network detector (NND), and evaluated the performance of the first decoding by the NND. In this paper, we propose the NND which iteratively calculates the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) as the decoding reliability using the returned sum-product (SP) decoder output sequence in addition to the TD-FIR filter output sequence. Furthermore, we evaluate the performance of the iterative decoding system using an NND, and compare it to that of the system using a soft-output Viterbi algorithm (SOVA) detector with the signal-dependent noise predictor.
Index Terms—Neural network detector, shingled magnetic recording, iterative decoding.

I. INTRODUCTION
HARD disk drive (HDD) used as “near-line storage” in data centers will be required larger capacities in the future due to the expansion of telework caused by COVID-19 and the spread of the 5th generation mobile communication system (5G).
We study the low-density parity-check (LDPC) [1] coding and iterative decoding system to realize high densities in HDD with a shingled magnetic recording (SMR) [2]. Among them, we have proposed the waveform equalization by a two-dimensional finite impulse response (TD-FIR) filter [3], [4] and an intertrack interference (ITI) canceller [5] to reduce the influence of ITI caused by narrowing the track width. Furthermore, it was clarified that the application of a neural network (NN) is effective not only in reducing the influence of ITI but also in reducing the influence of signal-dependent noise [6], [7].
In this paper, we propose a neural network detector (NND) for the iterative decoding and evaluate the performance by computer simulation.

II. READ/WRITE SYSTEM
Figure 1 shows the read/write (R/W) system of LDPC encoding and iterative decoding with the NND. The input sequence is encoded by a 128/130(0, 16/8) run-length-limited (RLL) [8] encoder and a (3, 30)-regular LDPC encoder [1]. The recording sequence is recorded on a granular medium model [5] under the specification of 4 Tbit/inch2. Figure 2 shows the granular medium model, the writer, and the reader readers in an array head. The magnetic grains with the magnetization of “0” or “1” are indicated in white or black, where the average grain size, the grain diameter dispersion, and the average nonmagnetic grain boundary width in the granular medium model are set to 5.0 nm, 1.0 nm, and 1.0 nm, respectively. The bit length lc and the track pitch ltp are 7.3 and 22.1 nm, respectively. In the writing process, we assume that the medium switching field distribution follows a Gaussian distribution with the mean of 12 kOe and the standard deviation of 5% and

In the reading process, the decoding target track and the adjacent tracks are read by the array head with three readers [3], [4], and the different additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) is added to each waveform as the system noise. We assume that these parameters are set to xh = 0.4 and Nt = 15. The output waveform from the PR1 channel is iteratively decoded by the turbo equalization performed between an NND and a sum-product (SP) decoder [11]. The SP decoder iteratively decodes using the constraint of LDPC code until the maximum iteration number isp. Furthermore, the SP decoder outputs the posterior probability sequence for the recording sequence including the parity bits of LDPC code which is returned to the NND as the reliability information to perform the turbo equalization for the target track until the maximum iteration number iglobal. Here, the SP output sequence is normalized in the maximum value for each sector. After the given number of iterations in the turbo equalization, the output sequence is obtained from the posterior probability sequence except parity passing through a hard decision unit and the RLL decoder. Then, the bit error rate (BER) is calculated by comparing the input sequence with the output sequence. Also, the channel error rate (CER) is provided by comparing the recording sequence and the hard decision sequence without parity composed of “0” or “1” obtained by the NND output.

pattern compared to the SOVA detector.

V. CONCLUSION
In this study, we have proposed the NND for the LDPC encoding and the iterative decoding. We have evaluated the iterative decoding performance in the NND and the SOVA detector by computer simulation. It is clarified that the NND realizes the more effective iterative decoding without depending on the recording pattern compared to the SOVA detector. As the result, the NND improves the required SNR by about 4.5 dB to achieve no-errors compared to the SOVA detector.
