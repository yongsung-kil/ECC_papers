Abstract—Motivated by the success of constructing quasicyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes based on ReedSolomon codes, a construction method for QC-LDPC codes is presented in this paper, using the check matrices of quadratic residues codes. Three QC-LDPC codes are obtained by utilizing two quadratic residues codes of lengths less than 100, and the resultant QC-LDPC codes are regular, high-rate and free of length-4 cycles. Simulation results show that the proposed QC-LDPC codes achieves similar performance as the random LDPC codes, but they have less short cycles. In addition, the proposed (279, 158) QC-LDPC code outperforms its counterpart constructed from Reed-Solomon codes at high signal-to-noise ratios.
Index Terms—Quasi-cyclic LDPC, quadratic residue code, check matrix, cycle.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have been applied in many applications such as 802.11n, DVB-S2 and 5G [1] due to their excellent error-correction performance [2]. In terms of different construction methods, LDPC codes can be generally classified into random LDPC codes and structured LDPC codes. Therein, structured LDPC codes are more preferred in practical systems because of low implementation complexity. For example, the authors of [3] presented several construction methods of structured LDPC codes, and the resultant LDPC codes are quasi-cyclic (QC) and free of length-four cycles.
Based on the codewords of Reed-Solomon (RS) codes, one class of algebraically-structured LDPC codes, called RSLDPC codes, can be constructed [4]. These codes exhibit good performance and converge fast under iterative decoding. However, the parity check matrix of this RS-LDPC code has random rather than QC structures, which requires quadratic encoding complexity [5] as opposed to the linear complexity for encoding QC codes [6].

Recently, Liu et al. [7] developed a QC representation of RS-LDPC codes, and most part of the parity-check matrix of a full-length RS-LDPC code consists of circulant permutation matrices and zero matrices under such a representation. More recently, several approaches were presented for constructing RS based QC-LDPC codes [8], [9], [10], referred to as RSQC-LDPC codes, and the resultant codes have good errorcorrecting performance.
Inspired by RS-QC-LDPC codes, we want to investigate the performance of QC-LDPC codes based on the binary quadratic residue (QR) codes [11], since this class of code has large minimum distances. Generally speaking, the QR codes of lengths less than 100 may have practical applications, since longer codes need to be constructed on Galois fields with very large size, leading to prohibitly high computational complexity. In this paper, we present a construction method of QC-LDPC codes by utilizing the parity check matrices of QR codes, and the obtained QC-LDPC codes are called QR-QCLDPC codes hereafter. The new QC-LDPC codes have high rates, and are regular, and their corresponding Tanner graphs are proved to be free of length-4 cycles. Simulation results show that QR-QC-LDPC codes perform as well as random LDPC codes constructed by the PEG algorithm in terms of error-correcting performance, but require less encoding complexity. In addition, a QR-QC-LDPC code outperforms an RS-QC-LDPC code with the same length and rate, which reveals the potential of proposed codes.
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section II introduces the background of QC-LDPC codes. Section III presents a design strategy of QR-QC-LDPC codes. Section IV provides the performance comparison between QR-QC-LDPC and random LDPC codes. Finally, this paper concludes with a brief summary in Section V.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we construct a new type of quasi-cyclic lowdensity parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes, utilizing the parity check matrices of quadratic residue (QR) codes. The resultant codes have high rates, regular structures and no cycles of length-4. Simulation results show that the proposed QR-QCLDPC codes achieves almost the same performance as the PEG LDPC codes while requiring less encoding complexity. In addition, the proposed QC-LDPC codes are potential to perform better than RS-QC-LDPC codes, and hence may have a good application prospect. For future work, we will construct QR-QC-LDPC codes by taking into consideration the degree distribution optimization and the masking technique.
