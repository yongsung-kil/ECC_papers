Design of Protograph LDPC Codes for the Unequal Power Two-User Downlink System
Wei Zhang, Pingping Chen, Senior Member, IEEE, Zhaopeng Xie and Feng Chen

Abstract—In this paper, we design protograph low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes for two-user downlink nonorthogonal multiple access (NOMA). In the downlink system, we consider successive interference cancellation (SIC) decoding by exchanging the external information of two users, referred to as the E-SIC. To improve the performance of downlink NOMA, 1) we use a two-user protograph-based extrinsic information transfer chart (TU-EXIT) analysis for two-user downlink system. The analysis takes into account the protograph structure, allowing an accurate and simple prediction of the decoding threshold; 2) we then design protograph LDPC codes of different code rates with the help of the TU-EXIT. Simulation results show that, in the downlink system, the proposed protograph LDPC codes can obtain significant performance gains over the optimized irregular LDPC codes for both the high and low code rates.
Index Terms—NOMA, protograph LDPC, code design, decoding threshold
I. INTRODUCTION
Non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA) has been recognized as one of the key technologies of the fifth generation (5G) wireless network [1]-[2]. Compared with the traditional Orthogonal Multiple Access (OMA) technology that can only transmit one signal on the same frequency, NOMA provide higher spectrum efficiency of system and user fairness in the same time and frequency [3]-[4]. However, the received at the users are non-orthogonal superpositon of serious interuser interference, which leads to decoding difficulty of the receiver [5]-[7]. In NOMA, the receiver uses continuous interference cancellation (SIC) decoding to avoid co-channel interference and obtain the desired signal. To achieve better performance, most of the existing researches are focused on resource allocation and SIC implementation. Ref. [8] investigated a subgradient based resource allocation algorithmto maximize the energy efficiency for a downlink NOMA system. It provides better performance in terms of the energy efficiency and sum rate. In order to reduce the inter-user interference, [9] introduced a multi-decision SIC strategy, which does not select one codeword for each user like traditional SIC, but introduces multiple codewords as the candidate object against error propagation. However, this strategy suffers the higher computational complexity as compared to traditional SIC.
Traditional low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have shortcomings, such as high complexity of encoding and decoding. These shortcomings limit further application in communication systems. Recently, a kind of LDPC codes, i.e., protograph LDPC codes were proposed [10]-[12]. The protograph LDPC codes have very simple structure and can realize linear encoding and decoding. The performance of protograph LDPC

codes outperforms better than traditional LDPC codes. As two typical protograph LDPC codes, AR3A code and AR4JA code have excellent performance over the AWGN channels [10,13]. To analyze the performance of protograph LDPC codes in the low SNR area, [14] and [13,15] respectively proposed the Protograph EXIT (P-EXIT) algorithm and the Asymptotic Weight Distribution (AWD) function. In addition, [16] presented rate-adaptive protograph LDPC codes for multimevel-cell NAND flash memory. The proposed codes with optimum mapping outperform the irregular LDPC codes with a faster decoding convergence.
In this paper, Section II introduces the downlink NOMA system model. In Section III, we consider successive interference cancellation (SIC) decoding by exchanging the external information of two users, referred to as the E-SIC scheme, and then derive a two-user protograph-based EXIT (TUEXIT) analysis. The design of protograph-based LDPC codes guided by the TU-EXIT and their performance evaluations are presented in Section IV and Section V, respectively. Finally, Section VI concludes the paper.
II. SYSTEM MODEL
Fig. 1 shows a downlink NOMA system with a base station (BS) and two users U E1 and U E2. Each user is equipped with a singe antenna. Moreover, we assume that U E1 and U E2 are respectively the near user and the far user from the BS. We denote hl as the channel gain from the BS to U El, where l ∈ {1, 2}. In the system, we assume that the channel gain of U E1 is greater than the channel gain of U E2, |h1|2 > |h2|2. We further denote P as the total power of the BS. Moreover, we denote the power that the BS transmit to U E1 and U E2 as P1 and P2, respectively. Since near users have better channel gains than far users, we have P2 > P1.
In the BS, we randomly generate blocks of k information bits of the users and then generate codewords of n bits with the rate of R = k/n. The encoded bits are translated into a bit stream by using BPSK modulation. Then, the generated bits are sent to the users. THE PROPOSED ALGORITHM The paper [17] propose a new extrinsic information assisted SIC scheme to improve the decoding performance, referred to as the E-SIC scheme, which exchanges the extrinsic decoding information of the signals for two users. In the section, we introduce the details of E-SIC scheme. In the prior work [18], the theoretical performances of the irregular LDPC code are evaluated by EXIT chart [19], and it proved that the error probability for both users becomes vanishingly small. However, in the work, we consider the LDPC codes constructed from the protograph. The protograph is a tanner graph with relatively few nodes, connected by few edges, which also allows parallel edges. Moreover, the P-EXIT was proposed to predict decoding threshold for protograph-based LDPC codes [14]. The decoding threshold of the protograph refers to the minimum channel SNR that supports reliable iterative decoding of LDPC codes constructed from the protograph. However, the P-EXIT analysis cannot be directly applied to the protograph-based two-user downlink systems. Therefore, some equations and calculation steps of EXIT analysis given in [14] and [18 ] must be modified. Based on the papers [14,18], we present a two-user protograph-based EXIT (TU-EXIT) analysis for two-user downlink system by modifying the P-EXIT analysis. In the next section, guided by the TU-EXIT analysis, we will design protograph-based LDPC codes that different code rates for two-user downlink system. IV. DESIGN OF PROTOGRAPH LDPC CODES
In the process of designing protograph-based LDPC codes for two-user downlink system, we summarize design rules as follows.
• Design the protograph with low decoding threshold guided by the proposed TU-EXIT. The codes with good performance can still maintain the performance advantage when extending to the high-rate one.
• There is one high degree variable node and less degree2 variable node. This because that the degree-2 variable nodes can improve the performance in the waterfall area, also lead to error floor in the high SNR region. The proportion of degree-2 variable nodes should be properly controlled.
• To preserve the linear growth of minimum distance of the protograph codes, we should ensure that some variable node have degree at least 3 [20].
In this part, firstly, we design the protograph (or the base matrix B) of LDPC code for two-user downlink system. The size of matrix B is set to 4 × 8. The matrix B has one high degree variable node and only one degree-2 variable node. In addition, other variable nodes have degree at least 3. The protograph has only few nodes, connected by few edges. In our design procedure, we follow the rules that we summarized.

A. Performance of Low-rate LDPC Codes
We set P1 = 0.55 and P2 = 0.75. The channel gains are set as h1 = h2 = 1.0. The rate of U E1 and U E2 are 1/4 and 1/3, respectively. The number of outer iteration of E-SIC is set to 5. From Fig. 3, we can see that the proposed protograph codes outperform irregular LDPC codes by more than 0.3 dB for both U E1 and U E2, at the BER of 10−5. According to TU-EXIT, we calculate the decoding thresholds of protograph LDPC codes. As the Tab. I shows, decoding thresholds of protograph with rate-1/4 and rate-1/3 are 0.572 dB and 1.919 dB, respectively. From Fig. 3 and Tab. I, we can see that the BER is less than 10−5 at an SNR mostly equal to the design SNR for both users.
B. Performance of High-rate LDPC Codes
We set P1 = 1.8 and P2 = 3. The rate of U E1 and U E2 are 1/2 and 2/3, respectively. From Fig. 4, we can see when the proposed protograph LDPC codes achieve significant gains of 0.8 dB over irregular LDPC codes for both U E1 and U E2. In addition, the performance of protograph LDPC codes are better than that of the traditional AR3A code and AR4JA code. Moreover, we find that AR3A code performs worse in the high SNR region. This because that degree-2 variable nodes in AR3A code leads to error floor. This means that the protograph LDPC codes can yield large gain over the traditional codes at high SNR region. As Tab. I shows, the decoding thresholds of rate-1/2 protograph and rate-2/3 protograph are 1.779 dB and 3.997 dB, respectively.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
This paper investigates the performance of protograph LDPC codes in two-user downlink NOMA system. First, we develop a two-user protograph-based TU-EXIT to analyze iterative decoding of two-user downlink system. Guided by the proposed EXIT, we design the protograph codes from low-rate to high-rate. The simulation results show that the proposed protograph codes can achieve significant performance gains over the traditional optimized irregular LDPC codes, especially at high SNR region.
