Abstract: In roder to adjust the log-likelihood ratios (LLRs), we design a hierarchical min-sum algorithm based on dynamic scaling factor (DSF-HMS). The results show that the proposed algorithm can improve the decoding performance.
1. Intrduction
In 2016, low-density parity-check (LDPC) has been formally established as the medium and long code coding standard of the Fifth-Generation mobile communication and ushered in the golden period of commercial development. LDPC coding was proposed by Robert Gallager in 1962 [1], which is close to the Shannon limit code, so it has a broad application prospect. The minsum (MS) algorithm, as a simplified algorithm of LDPC decoding, has attracted much interests [2]. Many researchers propose various improved algorithms for MS algorithm. They mainly focus on two aspects. On the one hand, there are improvements around the scaling factor. For instance, direct addition of fixed 2-D factor [3] and use suppression factor that independent of the channel model [4]. On the other hand, the calculation methods of variable nodes (VNs) and check nodes (CNs) are improved. For example, minimum value of check node is directly replaced by the second-order minimum value [5]. However, LDPC decoding that has fewer iterations and higher algorithm accuracy is still required in the new era.
In this paper, we propose hierarchical min-sum algorithm based on dynamic scaling factor (DSF-HMS) algorithm to solve the above problems. It designs a set of dynamic scaling factors and modifies the log-likelihood ratios (LLRs) of the CNs. The simulation results show this algorithm reduces iterations and improves the decoding accuracy. At the same time, it retains the advantages of MS algorithm which is easy to implement in hardware.
2. Simulation System and Results
2.1. Simulation System
Since MS algorithm reduces the computational complexity at the cost of enlarging LLR value, the DSF-HMS algorithm adds a group of dynamic scaling factors α = [α1, α2, . . . , αn] when calculating the CNs. The physical meaning of the scaling factors is to adjust the step size of the LLR values at the CNs. If adjustive step of LLR values of CNs keep large, the LLR values of CNs will likely to go straight over the exact LLR values that decodes the correct codeword. Oppositely, If they keep adjusting the small step length, the iterations will be increased, and they are likely still far from the accurate LLR values. Therefore, the dynamic scaling factors of DSF-HMS algorithm can firstly set LLR values of CNs near the exact LLR values with strides and then can accurately correct LLR values of CNs to the exact LLR values with small steps. We use grid search algorithm and Monte Carlo criterion to obtain a large number of simulation data and analyze these data and get the dynamic scaling factors. We use the grid search algorithm to change the scaling factor with 0.01 as the step size within the range of (0,1). Then we record the decoding results of each iteration under different scaling factors, and get the bit error rate. At the same time, we follow the Monte Carlo criterion in the acquisition process of simulation data to Avoid getting data in extreme cases. We draw the all data and obtain relation curve between the iterations and BER is obtained under 100 groups of different scaling factors. When the scaling factor is within the range of (0,0.59), the BER almost does not change with iterations, and the decoding performance is also poor. This situation is caused by the fact that the scaling factor is too small, that is to say, the adjustment step of LLR value is too large. Therefore, this part of the scaling factor does not fit into the shared dynamic scaling factor. When the scaling factor is within the range of (0.59,1), the BER changes significantly with iterations. In the first and second iterations, the BER drops fastest when the scaling factor is selected as 0.59. The relationship between the 3rd to 14th iterations and BER show that BER drops fastest when the scaling factor is 0.72. In this paper, we propose the so-called DSF-HMS algorithm aided by a set of dynamic scaling factors and layered iterations. According to the formula derivation, the CVs of DSF-HMS algorithm execute card(V (i) \ j) + 1 multiplications in every iteration as the same as MS algorithm indicating that the complexity of DSF-HMS algorithm does not increase. The simulation results show that the DSF-HMS algorithm can achieve a maximum gain of 0.437 dB compared with the MS algorithm when the BER is 9.115 × 10−8. Compared with MS algorithm, the DSF-HMS algorithm outperforms in less iterations but higher computational accuracy and results in lower BER.
