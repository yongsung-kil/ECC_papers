Analysis of Binary and Ternary Message Passing Decoding for Generalized LDPC Codes
Emna Ben Yacoub∗, Gianluigi Liva† ∗ Institute for Communications Engineering, Technical University of Munich, Munich, Germany † Institute of Communications and Navigation, German Aerospace Center (DLR), Wessling, Germany
Email: emna.ben-yacoub@tum.de, gianluigi.liva@dlr.de

Abstract—Binary and a ternary message passing decoding algorithms for generalized low-density parity-check codes are defined and analyzed. In all algorithms, the variable nodes exploit soft channel information. However, the exchanged messages between variable and check nodes are binary or ternary. Two types of local decoders are assumed at the check nodes, namely an optimum a-posteriori probability soft-input soft-output decoder, and a more practical bounded distance decoder. A density evolution analysis for irregular code ensembles is developed for all the introduced algorithms, which allows obtaining the scaling coefficients needed for the variable node operation. Finite-length simulation results confirm the validity of the asymptotic analysis.
I. INTRODUCTION
The rising requirement for high data rate makes the design of low-complexity and high throughput decoding algorithms crucial. The study of low-complexity message passing algorithms for low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes originates from the seminal work by Gallager [1]. In [1], Gallager introduced two decoding algorithms, known as Gallager A and Gallager B, where the variable and check nodes exchange binary messages. Another class of message passing algorithms for LDPC codes, referred to as binary message passing (BMP), was introduced in [2]. The messages exchanged between variable and check nodes are binary and the variable nodes (VNs) exploit soft information from the channel. Extensions of BMP to ternary and quaternary message alphabets were presented in [3] and [4] and are referred to as ternary message passing (TMP) and quaternary message passing (QMP) respectively.
A more general class of LDPC codes called generalized low-density parity-check (GLDPC) codes was introduced by Tanner in [5]. The codes associated to the check nodes (CNs) of a GLDPC code are more general than single parity-check (SPC) codes. They can be any linear block code and will be referred to as component codes. GLDPC codes offer a compromise between error floor and waterfall performance due to their good distance properties and the powerful block codes used at the CNs (compared to SPC codes employed by the CNs of LDPC codes). This comes at the cost of increasing decoding complexity, especially if optimum soft-input softoutput (SISO) decoding is performed at each CN. Due to their excellent error floor performance, specific classes of GLDPC codes are currently considered in the context of high-speed fiber optic links [6]–[9]. Several works studied reducing the decoding complexity of GLDPC and product-like codes [6],

[7], [10]–[12]. In [6], it has been proven that iterative hard decision decoding using extrinsic bounded distance decoding (BDD) at the component codes of spatially coupled GLDPC codes, where all VNs have degree 2, can approach capacity at high rates. The exchange of binary messages between VNs and CNs is particularly appealing for high-throughput applications, since it allows reducing the internal decoder data flow. The method was extended in [7], [8] such that the VNs exploit the channel reliabilities while the exchanged messages are still binary. In particular, the BMP decoding algorithms introduced in [7], [8] make use of BDD decoding at the check nodes.
We are interested how the sub-optimality of local CN decoders affects the overall performance. In particular, we consider low-complexity decoding algorithms for GLDPC codes that represent instances of BMP and TMP decoding while exploiting soft channel information at the VNs. The exchanged messages between VNs and CNs are binary or ternary. At the CNs, the binary and ternary messages are obtained either by using BDD or by using any a posteriori probability (APP) SISO algorithm to obtain extrinsic likelihood ratios and map them to messages from the desired alphabet. For the latter, we used the Johansson-Zigangirov (JZ) algorithm from [13]. The analysis is limited to GLDPC codes whose CN component codes admit a simple trellis representation and the results help to shed some light on the performance loss incurred by BDD decoding of the component codes
The paper is organized as follows. In Section II, we discuss the system model and introduce notation. Section III presents BMP and TMP decoding for GLDPC codes with various decoding methods at the CNs. The density evolution (DE) analysis of these algorithms for unstructured irregular GLDPC ensembles is provided in Section IV. Numerical results are discussed in Section V.

D. GLDPC Codes

V. NUMERICAL RESULTS

Consider the rate R = 0.625 regular GLDPC ensemble with VN degree dv = 2, where all the CNs correspond to the (32, 26) extended Hamming code. We designed a length n = 8000 code from this ensemble by generating its Tanner graph via the progressive edge-growth (PEG) algorithm [17]. For the simulations, we set the maximum number of iterations at max = 50. The simulation results for BMP (dashed lines) and TMP (solid lines) are depicted in Fig. 1 in terms of bit error rate (BER) versus Eb/N0. The results are provided by employing JZ decoding (blue lines) and BDD (green lines) at the CNs, where BDD is implemented in the extrinsic message passing setting. The waterfall performance of the different decoders is in agreement with the thresholds derived via DE. The performance gap between the TMP decoder employing optimum APP decoding at the CNs and the one using BDD is around 0.6 dB at a BER= 10−4, in good agreement with the DE results.
