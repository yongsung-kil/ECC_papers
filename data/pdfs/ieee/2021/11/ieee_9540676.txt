In [1]–[3], the belief propagation (BP) and the min-sum (MS) decoders using the DNNs are shown to improve the decoding performance of short length linear block codes. II. THE PROPOSED NEURAL LAYERED MIN-SUM DECODER
A. Notations
The LDPC matrix H used in the 5G-NR standards is defined by a protograph base matrix B (base graph BG1, BG2 [8]) of size Mˆ × Nˆ and an expansion factor Z. The matrix H with M = Z · Mˆ rows and N = Z · Nˆ columns can be represented by a bipartite TG with N VNs vn, n ∈ [N ] , where [N ] denotes the set {0, 1, · · · , N − 1}, and M CNs cm, m ∈ [M ]. Corresponding to each row index mˆ ∈ [Mˆ ] in B, the set of Z row indices in H, Lmˆ = {Z · mˆ , Z · mˆ + 1, · · · , Z · mˆ + Z − 1} is called the mˆ -th row-layer of H. In each row-layer Lmˆ ,, the local neighborhood of the VN vn, n ∈ [N] , is captured by an index set Vm nˆ = {m, m ∈ Lmˆ | hm,n = 1} (this has the indices of the CNs in the set Lmˆ connected to vn; hm,n here denotes the entry in the m-th row and the n-th column of H). Similarly, the neighborhood of the CN cm, m ∈ Lmˆ , is a set Cm mˆ = {n, n ∈ [N ] | hm,n = 1} consisting the VNs connected to cm. The nth encoded bit at the output of the LDPC encoder is denoted as yn ∈ {0, 1}. We take that the encoder output is modulated using the binary phase shift keying (BPSK) scheme to obtain xn = 1 − 2yn ∈ {−1, +1}. Finally, rn = xn + un is the output of the additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, where un ∼ N (0, σ2).

III. THE ADAPTIVE NEURAL LAYERED MIN-SUM DECODER
To improve the convergence speed and the decoding performance of the NLMS decoder, we propose the ANLMS decoder with adaptive suppressing parameters that are learnt by the DNN. The motivation behind the ANLMS is as follows. A nonzero syndrome vector St in the t-th iteration implies existence of at least one CN PE whose parity check is not met. Consequently, the belief updates at such CN PEs are unreliable and a design improvement is obtained by suppressing their output [10]. The novelty of our proposal is to apply the DNN for learning the suppressing parameters. In our parameter sharing framework, we suppress the belief updates of all the CN PEs in layer l2mˆ +1, mˆ ∈ [Mˆ ] of the DNN if the parity check at least one CN PE belonging to l2mˆ +1 is not met. IV. THE FILTERED NEURAL LAYERED MIN-SUM DECODER

Our third proposal for improving the performance of NLMS decoder uses a first-order discrete-time linear infinite impulse response (IIR) filter. Although the memory requirement increases in this technique (known as relaxation [3]), the benefit is that the jitter in the beliefs sent by the VNs to CNs reduces due to the filtering. Our proposal is to deploy the DNN to learn the location of the pole of the IIR filter for improved layered mode decoding. VI. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we propose various neural layered decoders, viz. (i) NLMS, (ii) ANLMS, and (iii) FNLMS. These decoders use several parameters which are learnt by the proposed DNN. We have applied our proposals to the 5G-NR LDPC codes with different information length and code rates. The simulation results show that the decoding performance of our proposed decoders is significantly better than the existing MS based decoders, and it approaches that of the computationally more expensive LBP decoder. A future extension of our work is to adopt hyper-graph neural network decoding [12] for the layered mode decoding of LDPC codes.
