LLR Metrics for 1024-QAM Soft-Decision: Implementation in IEEE 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6)

Roger Pierre Fabris Hoefel
Electrical Engineering Department Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS)
Porto Alegre, RS, Brazil roger.hofel@ufrgs.br

Abstract—The IEEE 802.11ax amendment (Wi-Fi 6) has defined modulation code schemes that implement uniform Gray mapping rectangular 1024-QAM constellation. In this paper, we derive approximate log-likelihood ratio (LLR) metrics for softdecoding of binary convolutional codes (BCC) using Viterbi decoding and low-density parity-check codes (LDPC) using the message-passing algorithm, implemented as the log-domain sum-product algorithm. The proposed low-complexity softdecision metrics are validated by simulating the 802.11ax physical layer that implements transmit beamforming transceivers over frequency selective channels.
Keywords— Wi-Fi 6, 802.11ax, 1024-QAM, soft-decision, LLR, BCC, LDPC, transmit beamforming.
I. INTRODUCTION
The Standards Board of IEEE Standards Association (SA) approved the 802.11ax amendment (Wi-Fi 6/6E) in May 2021 [1]. Wi-Fi Alliance® certification for Wi-Fi 6E was launched in January of 2021 to deliver interoperability for devices operating in the 6 GHz spectrum. Wi-Fi 6E can utilize up to 14 additional 80 MHz channels or seven additional 160 MHz channels in 6 GHz for applications such as ultra-high-definition video streaming and virtual/ augmented reality. Regarding to the continuous Wi-Fi momentum, it is interesting to observe that the Wi-Fi Alliance® has predicted 5.2 billion Wi-Fi 6 product shipments by 2025, 41% of which will be Wi-Fi 6E devices.
The 2021 802.11ax amendment (a.k.a. High Efficiency amendment) has introduced the uniform Gray mapping rectangular 1024-QAM constellation, which allows 25% higher-data rate in relation to the 256-QAM modulation scheme specified in the 2014 802.11ac amendment. Other notable novel features introduced in the Wi-Fi 6/6E include: multiband operation in the 2.4, 5 and 6 GHz bands; long orthogonal frequency division (OFDM) symbols to reduce the cyclic prefix (CP) overhead and to improve outdoor performance; orthogonal frequency division multiple access (OFDMA) for both uplink and downlink; introduction of uplink multi-user multiple input multiple output (UL MUMIMO) technology; 160 MHz channels; target wake time (TWT) to improve both network efficiency and device battery life. The Wi-Fi 6/6E maximum peak data rate is of ~9.6 Gbps when it is implemented transceivers with a bandwidth (BW) of 160 MHz, 1024-QAM, and 8x8 MIMO [1,2].
Previous Works: The 1999 802.11a amendment, operating at Unlicensed National Information Infrastructure (U-NII) 5 GHz band, and 2003 802.11g amendment, operating at Unlicensed Industry Scientific and Medical (ISM) 2.4 GHz band, have specified the rectangular Gray mapping 16-QAM and 64-QAM modulation schemes [3]. The physical layer

(PHY) of 802.11a and 802.11g amendments are based on orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM). Both amendments, which specify a BW of 20 MHz, allow a maximum peak data rate of 54 Mbps when it is used modulation and code scheme 6 (MCS6), which implements 64-QAM, and binary convolutional code (BCC) with code rate ¾. A simplified soft-decision demapper for Gray mapping 16-QAM and 64-QAM constellations was proposed in [4]. Simulation results for HIPERLAN/2 have shown no performance losses compared to the theoretical ones when it is implemented simplified log-likelihood ratio (LLR) metrics for soft-decision Viterbi decoding [4].

The 2009 802.11n amendment (Wi-Fi 4) has introduced single-user multiple-input multiple-output (SU-MIMO) with a maximum of four spatial streams (SS) [5, p.74]. Wi-Fi 4 has not introduced a high-order QAM constellation. However, it has defined a MCS that implements 64-QAM and BCC with code rate of 5/6. The High Throughput (HT) amendment has a maximum peak data rate of 600 Mbps when it is implemented 4x4 MIMO transceivers with a BW of 40 MHz.

The 2014 802.11ac amendment (Wi-Fi 5) has introduced the 256-QAM modulation scheme for MCS9 (256-QAM, BCC with code rate ¾) and MCS10 (256-QAM, BCC with code rate 5⁄6) The implementation of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is optional in the Very-Hight Throughput (VHT) amendment. The peak data rate is of ~6.9 Gbps when it is scheduled MCS10 with transceivers that implement 8x8 SU-MIMO using a BW of 160 MHz [5, p. 213].

Finally, we observe that that a low-complexity 256-QAM soft demapper for 5G New Radio (NR) was proposed in [6]. The validation was carried out by simulating the single-input single-output (SISO) transmission mode with zero-forcing (ZF) equalization in the frequency domain.

To accomplish our goals, this paper is organized as follows: Section II contains a mathematical description of the TxBF transceivers implemented in this paper. TxBF schemes can improve substantially the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR), allowing scheduling of MCS with high cardinality signaling schemes and, consequently, improving the performance and coverage of the wireless local area networks (WLANs). Third, increasing the number of transmit antennas from 8 to 16 allows power gains of 4 dB for both MCS10 (►◆) and MCS11 (▲■) when it is implemented BCC. The performance improvement of 4 dB gains is also observed due to the increase of the number of transmit antennas with LDPC for both MCS10 (◄★) and MCS11 (▼●). Finally, we observe that this figure also shows results with 16 transmitting antennas since the novel amendment under development (802.11be - Wi-Fi 7) has defined a maximum number of 16 antennas ports.

V. CONCLUSIONS

In this industry-oriented paper, we have derived lowcomplexity soft-decision metrics for uniform Gray-coding rectangular 1024-QAM constellation. We also have presented simulation results for 802.11ax PHY transceivers, which implement TxBF transceivers with either BCC and LPC coding schemes, that allow a first-order validation of the proposed LLR metrics. Finally, we have verified that the scheduling of 1024-QAM modulation scheme in the 802.11ax PHY is possible for feasible SNR regimes if TxBF transceivers are implemented.
