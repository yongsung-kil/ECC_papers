LLR Metrics for 4096-QAM Soft-Decision: Implementation in IEEE 802.11be (Wi-Fi 7)

Roger Pierre Fabris Hoefel
Electrical Engineering Department Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS)
Porto Alegre, RS, Brazil roger.hofel@ufrgs.br

AbstractвҖ”The IEEE 802.11be Task Group (TG) is currently developing the 2024 802.11be amendment (Wi-Fi 7), targeting a maximum throughput of at least 30 Gbps and at least one mode of operation with enhanced worst case latency and jitter. The 802.11be is the first wireless standard to define 4096-QAM modulation scheme. In this paper, we derive and provide a first order validation of low-complexity log-likelihood ratio (LLR) metrics suitable for soft-decoding rectangular Gray-coding 4096-QAM modulation scheme with either binary convolutional codes (BCC) or low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes.
KeywordsвҖ”802.11be, Wi-Fi 7, 4096-QAM, soft-decision, LLR, binary convolutional codes, LDPC, transmit beamforming.
I. INTRODUCTION
The 2021 IEEE 802.11ax amendment (Wi-Fi 6), the sixth generation of wireless local area networks (WLANS), was published in May 2021 [1]. This High Efficiency (HE) amendment has introduced technical enhancements (e.g., orthogonal frequency division multiple access, OFDMA, and uplink multi-user multiple-input multiple-output, UL MUMIMO) to improve the area capacity in order that the physical layer (PHY) throughput be effectively observed as goodput at the top of medium access control (MAC) layer.
The 2024 IEEE 802.11be amendment (a.k.a. Extremely High Throughput) improves the 802.11ax maximum peak data rate from 9.6 Gbps to at least 30 Gbps targeting applications as virtual reality/augment reality (VR/VR), and cloud computing [2]. The technical features specified in the 802.11be Specification Framework include [3]: (i) 320 MHz bandwidth (BW) and more efficient utilization of noncontiguous spectrum; (ii) multi-band/multi-channel operation at 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz and 6 GHz; (iii) OFDMA with allocation of multiple resource units (RUs) for a single user (SU); (iv) downlink (DL) and UL SU-MIMO and MU-MIMO technologies with up 16 spatial streams (SS); (v) multi-access point (AP) coordination (e.g., coordinated scheduling and joint transmission); (vi) enhanced link adaptation with implementation of Hybrid Automatic Repeat Request (HARQ) protocols.
The 802.11ax amendment has specified the Gray mapping 1024 quadrature amplitude modulation (1024-QAM) constellation [1]. The 802.11be amendment has introduced 4096-QAM modulation scheme [4], which allows 10 bps/Hz/spatial stream when it is implemented with 5/6 rate low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [5].
Previous Works: A simplified soft-decision demapper for Gray mapping 16-QAM and 64-QAM constellations was proposed in [6]. More recently, a low-complexity 256-QAM soft demapper for 5G New Radio (NR) was derived in [7].

Main Contribution: The 802.11be amendment is the first wireless standard to specify the 4096-QAM modulation scheme. For the best our knowledge, this is the first paper that derive low-complexity approximate log-likelihood ratio (LLR) metrics suitable for soft-decoding rectangular Graycoding 4096-QAM modulation scheme with either binary convolutional codes (BCC) or LDPC codes.
The remaining of this paper has the following layout. Section II presents the derivation of a low-complexity 4096QAM demapper suitable for both soft-decision convolutional Viterbi decoding and LDPC decoders. Section III presents a first-order validation of the derived LLR metrics by carrying out a simulation of 802.11be PHY layer that implements transmit beamforming (TxBF) transceivers with 4096-QAM signaling scheme with either BBC or LDPC codes. Finally, Section IV concludes this paper.

III. FIRST-ORDER VALIDATION OF 4096-QAM LLR METRICS

Table III shows the main parameters of our 802.11be PHY simulator. It implements the BCC and LDPC codes originally specified in IEEE 2014 802.11ac amendment, which supports block lengths of 648 bits, 1296 bits and 1944 bits [5]. The BCC decoder implements soft-decision Viterbi decoding. The LDPC decoder implements the log-domain sum-product algorithm with a maximum number of iterations set to 50. It is assumed TxBF transceivers that implement minimum mean squared error (MMSE) precoder and interference cancellation (IC) MMSE MIMO detector [9].

IV. FINAL REMARKS
In this paper, we derive and validate approximate linear low-complexity soft-demapping LLR metrics for 4096-QAM modulation schemes suitable for both BCC and LDPC codes. We also shown that scheduling of 4096-QAM in the 802.11be PHY is possible for feasible SNR regimes if TxBF transceivers are implemented.
