LAEPS: LDPC LLR Adaptive Estimation Based on Pattern Set Distribution Variation in 3D Charge Trap NAND Flash Memories
Qianhui Li1,2, Yiyang Jiang1,2, Qi Wang1,2, Liu Yang1,2, Zexia Wang2,1, Xiaolei Yu1,2, Jing He1,2, Zongliang Huo1,2* 1Institute of Microelectronics of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing 100029, China 2University of Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing 100049, China *Email:huozongliang@ime.ac.cn.
Abstract—Low-density parity-check (LDPC) code with the soft decoding algorithm has been widely used in 3D NAND flash memories to improve the system reliability, because of its strong error correction ability. However, the accuracy of the soft information represented by the Log-Likelihood Ratios (LLRs) is an important factor which affects the error correction ability of the LDPC decoder. To improve the accuracy of LLRs, a novel LLR Adaptive Estimation scheme based on Pattern Set distribution variation (LAEPS) in 3D charge trap NAND flash memories is presented in this paper. LAEPS estimates the LLR based on not only the information proved by multi-read but also the information proved by adjacent cells in the same channel. Experimental results by silicon test illustrate that LAEPS increases the correction capability of LDPC soft decoding to 1.4x, and decreases the decoding iteration and RBER to about 0.7x and 0.8x.
Keywords—LDPC, LLR estimation, lateral charge spreading, soft information, 3D NAND flash memories

According to the characteristics of the lateral charge spreading, the ”pattern set type” of a storage cell will affect the charge loss rates(Fig.1 (b)) [5]. Therefore, the data pattern distributions constituted by the storage cells with the same ”pattern set type” is different from the distribution consisting of all memory cells. This phenomenon can be used to estimate more accurate LLRs based on ”pattern set type” of storage cells.

With the development of Word-Line(WL) stacking and multibit per cell storage technology, the capacity of 3D NAND flash memories grows continuously, which leads to more data reliability issues [1]. Low-density parity-check (LDPC) code with the soft decoding algorithm has been adopted to ensure the reliability of the data stored in the NAND flash memories. In order to improve the error correction ability of the LDPC soft decoding, some LLR estimation methods are proposed in previous papers. Peng J et al. proposed a Dynamic LLR (DLLR) scheme based on Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm to estimate the LLRs [2]. Tokutomi T et al. proposed the burst-error masking (BEM) and program-disturb merged LLR estimation (PMLE) method to estimate LLRs in data-retention time [3]. Nakamura T et al. proposed an Artificial Neural Network-Coupled (ANN) LDPC, which estimating the LLR by trained ANN [4].
These methods proposed in previous papers can estimate the changes of the LLRs caused by Program/Erase (P/E) cycles, data retention times, and process variations. However, none of these approaches take into account the impact of data patterns to further improve the accuracy of the LLR. This paper will introduce a more accurate LLR estimation scheme considering the affects of data patterns and lateral charge spreading for 3D NAND flash memories.
II. BACKGROUND AND MOTIVATION

B. Motivation
Based on the threshold voltage distribution of ”pattern set type”, we proposed a LLR Adaptive Estimation scheme based on Pattern Set distribution variation (LAEPS) in this paper. LAEPS estimates the LLRs based on not only accurate reliability information such as P/E cycles, retention time, and vertical charge de-trap (state) effects but also the data patterns of adjacent cells which is related to the lateral charge spreading.

EXPERIMENTAL ANALYSIS In order to verify the effectiveness of the proposed LAEPS, 3D charge trap NAND flash memories with 1K P/E cycles fabricated with a 64 stacked WLs by Yangtze Memory Technologies Co., Ltd. (YMTC) had been used in the experiments. In addition, QC-LDPC(8272, 8763) constructed based on Latin squares over GF(128) [6] is used in the experiments and the maximum iteration limit of sum-product algorithm (SPA) for LDPC soft decoding is set to 60. A. RBE decrease by LAEPS As shown in Fig.3 (a), the Raw Bit Error (RBE) of pages read by LAEPS scheme is lower than conventional estimation scheme expect scheme 1. And Fig.3 (a) shows that W Ln−1 has little effect on W Ln in LAEPS scheme than W Ln+1. As a result, even both sides of W Ln is referred, there is no more RBE decrease in scheme 3 and 5 compared with scheme 2 and

4. The RBE of scheme 2 to 5 is 0.86 times the conventional scheme.
B. Decoding iteration cycles decrease by LAEPS
In Fig.3 (b), the Decoding Iteration Cycle (DIC) of the proposed LAEPS scheme is reduced compared with the conventional scheme because of more precise LLR estimation. The DIC of scheme 1 is about 0.96 time of the conventional scheme (Refer to W Ln−1). The DIC of scheme 2 and 4 is about 0.86 time of the conventional scheme (Refer to W Ln+1). The DIC of scheme 3 and 5 is about 0.71 time of the conventional scheme (Refer to W Ln ± 1).
C. Correction capability improvement by LAEPS
Fig.3 (c) is the correction capability analysis of the proposed LAEPS scheme. As shown in Fig.3 (c), the 5 schemes based on LAEPS proposed in subsection III-B perform 1.11, 1.29, 1.38, 1.27 and 1.37 times of Correction Capability(CC) improvement compared with the conventional LLR estimation scheme.
V. CONCLUSION
LAEPS proposes a method which estimates LLR based on adjacent WordLine cell information and time. It analyses the deviation trend of pattern set distributions and hence estimating the LLR more precisely. It shows good performance in the size of LLR table, the decrease of RBER and LDPC decoding iteration and the improvement of correction capability.
