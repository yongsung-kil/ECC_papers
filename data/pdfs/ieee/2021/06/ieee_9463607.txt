Abstractâ€”In this paper, we propose a Byte-Parallel Configurable Cyclic Shift (BP-CCS) algorithm, which converts the cyclic shift into a byte-parallel form. This method alleviates the low efficiency of cyclic shift calculation in Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) encoding under the 5G protocol effectively. Besides, we also expand the instruction set of self-developed DSP Universal Communication Processor (UCP) to cope with the hardware implementation bottleneck of BP-CCS algorithm. This allows the implementation of a BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder on UCP, which has been verified on the taped-out chip. Experimental results show that the time consumed by the BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder is about 1/50 of the time specified in 5G communication protocol standard, and the encoder architecture can be easily reconstructed in real time through parameter configuration.

I. INTRODUCTION
The fifth-generation communication system, referred to as 5G, is a new generation of mobile networks following the fourth-generation communication system [1], [2]. Its application scenarios include Enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB), UltraReliable Low-Latency Communication (uRLLC), and Massive Machine-Type Communication (mMTC). As the primary application scenario of 5G, eMBB needs to meet performance requirements such as high reliability, high mobility, and high data transmission rate. In order to meet these performance requirements, each key technology in communication system needs to be optimized systematically. Wherein, channel coding and decoding technology is a key technology of 5G, and the optimization of its performance plays a vital role in the improvement of the transmission rate and bit error rate of the 5G communication system [3]. At the 87th meeting of 3GPP RAN1 held in Las Vegas in November 2016, Low Density Parity Check (LDPC) codes were selected as the data channel coding scheme in the eMBB scenario [4]. Therefore, the research of LDPC encoding and decoding technology is of great significance for breaking the performance bottleneck of 5G communication systems.
Regarding the implementation of low-complexity encoders for LDPC codes, a lot of researches have been conducted in related fields. Ruslan Goriushkin et al. [5] developed a pipeline architecture for FPGA implementation of a QC-LDPC encoder. The results are provided for implementation on a FPGA ZYNQ-

7 ZC706 Evaluation Board for code with rate 5/6 and block lengths from 576 to 2304. Steffy Johnson et al. [6] presents the straightforward method and the lower triangular modification approach, which are the two encoding schemes for LDPC codes and compares the area utilization of both on FPGA platform, and observed that the device utilization by the straightforward method is considerably very small as compared to that of lower triangular modification approach. An efficient multi-rate encoder for IEEE 802.16e LDPC codes was proposed by WANG Xiumin et al. in [7], which outperforms current single rate encoders with acceptable hardware consumption and efficient memory consumption on the Altera FPGA Cyclone II EP2C70F896C6 device. V. Nandala et al. [8] proposed An efficient compact encoding process with the pipelining design of LDPC Encoder with a two stage, three stage and Maximal Rate Pipelining structures. The designed architectures are synthesized in SYNOPSYS tool and implemented in the XC3S250E FPGA device. Literature research shows that currently LDPC encoders are mostly implemented using FPGAs. When faced with scenarios with multiple code lengths in practical applications, FPGA-based encoders cannot quickly reconstruct the computing architecture, and the parallelism of key computing steps is low, which greatly restricts the coding rate in practical applications.
To solve these problems, we designed a high-speed parallel LDPC encoder based on our self-developed DSP (Universal Communication Processor, UCP), taking full advantage of the multi-granularity parallel architecture of UCP to achieve higher encoding rate. Compared with other existing work, the innovative identity of this paper is primarily reflected in three aspects:
â€¢ We developed a Byte-Parallel Configurable Cyclic Shift (BP-CCS) algorithm, which effectively improves the computational efficiency of cyclic shift in LDPC encoding.
â€¢ We customized the BP-CCS algorithm dedicated instruction on UCP, which effectively solved the bottleneck of BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder on UCP.

III. BYTE-PARALLEL CONFIGURABLE CYCLIC SHIFT SCHEME
Since the bit length of the subblock is N , the byte length of the sub-block is Nbyte = N / 8 . We assume that the shift number
is S . An intuitive algorithm of cyclic shift is to perform S shifts. The input of each shift is based on the output of the previous shift, and there is a data dependency between them. This kind of shifting algorithm is often adopted on FPGA. A shift register of length N is used to store the input data, and the register is shifted S times to obtain the output result. 5) The instruction allocation and load balancing of functional units
UCP has many parallel functional units, including four multiplication and addition units IMA0~3, four bus interface units BIU0~3, and four interleaving units SHU0~3. Each functional unit has unique functions and instructions, but some of the same functions and instructions are also shared between functional units. Therefore, after mapping each key operation of BP-CCS to instructions, it is also necessary to consider how to allocate each instruction to functional units reasonably. In addition, if the number of instructions of each functional unit in a loop body is not equal, the functional unit with a small number of instructions will inevitably consume extra cycles to synchronize the functional unit with a large number of instructions, resulting in great performance loss. Therefore, we also need to consider load balancing, that is, try to make the number of instructions of each functional unit equal, thereby reducing the performance loss. B. Hardware Implementation of BP-CCS-Based LDPC Encoder
After completing the design of the key operation BP-CCS, we can use this design to conveniently construct an BP-CCSbased LDPC encoder. Take the calculation of the first subblock of the parity bits as an example. We store the information bits and HBG in memory. The BIU functional unit reads the information bits and the non-zero elements of the first subblock row of HBG from the memory, and stores them in the M registers. The BP-CCS operator sequentially reads each nonzero element of HBG,c and the information bits subblock
corresponding to each non-zero element from the M register, and performs a cyclic shift operation on the information bits subblock according to the element value of HBG,c , then
accumulates the result. After that, the BP-CCS operator reads the non-zero element of HBG,w from the M register, and
performs reverse cyclic shift on the result accumulated in the previous step according to the element value of HBG,w , so that
the first subblock of the parity bits is obtained, and the subblock is written back to the memory through the BIU functional unit. The complete flow chart is shown in Fig 9.

V. EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
In this section, we will verify the performance of the BPCCS-based LDPC encoder compared with the 5G protocol standard, and show the feature that the encoder can be flexibly configured.
In order to evaluate the performance of the BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder, we implemented the encoder using UCP's selfdeveloped assembly language, and used UCP's own tool chain to carry out RTL simulation, and then carried out hardware verification on the taped-out UCP chip. UCP chip adopts TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company) 16-nm tapeout process, and the main frequency can work stably at 1.2GHz.
We selected the four scenarios with the largest amount of computation in the 5G protocol for performance verification, as shown in Table â… , and Table â…، lists the performance of BP-CCSbased LDPC encoder in these scenarios. According to the 5G protocol[10], a complete encoding process needs to be completed within 0.5ms. As can be seen from Table â…،, the time required for the BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder with Splicing Shift instruction to complete the encoding process in these four scenarios is from 0.0125ms to 0.0143ms, which is about 1/40 of the time specified in the 5G protocol. According to our analysis, its excellent performance is mainly due to the following two aspects:
First, the excellent performance benefits from the algorithm design of BP-CCS. The BP-CCS algorithm does not adopt the traditional serial cyclic shift scheme, but transforms the cyclic shift into a byte-parallel calculation process through a series of designs, reducing the time complexity of the cyclic shift from
O ( N ) to O (1) . When the subblock length is relatively long,
the performance acceleration ratio is particularly significant.
Second, the excellent performance benefits from UCP's architectural advantages. The powerful computing power and high-speed main frequency of UCP are important guarantees for excellent performance. The efficient parallel architecture of UCP enables the calculations in the BP-CCS algorithm to be executed in parallel on UCP, giving full play to the advantages of byte parallelism of the BP-CCS algorithm. The easyexpandable instruction set of UCP allows us to easily customize the special instruction "Splicing Shift" of the BP-CCS algorithm, thereby solving the key performance bottleneck of the algorithm in hardware implementation. As can be seen from Table â…،, when the Splicing Shift instruction is used, the processing time is about 1/5 of that when the Splicing Shift instruction is not used.

In addition to performance advantages, another advantage of BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder is configurability. In the BP-CCS algorithm, the length of the subblock is specified by the variable N . In the BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder, we can easily modify N through configuration data, thereby switching the subblock length of the encoder, so that the encoder can flexibly deal with multiple scenarios with different subblock lengths. However, for FPGA-based LDPC encoders, switching the subblock length of the encoder usually requires re-programming, which consumes a lot of time. VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we have discussed a Byte-Parallel Configurable Cyclic Shift (BP-CCS) algorithm, which converts the cyclic shift into a byte-parallel form to solve the lowefficiency problem of cyclic shift calculation in LDPC encoding. In addition, to break the bottleneck in the hardware implementation of the BP-CCS algorithm, we expanded the instruction set of the self-developed DSP Universal Communication Processor (UCP), thereby implementing a BPCCS-based LDPC encoder on UCP, and verifying it on the taped-out chip. The experimental results show that the time consumed by the BP-CCS-based LDPC encoder is about 1/50 of the time specified in 5G communication protocol standard, and the encoder architecture can be easily reconstructed in real time through parameter configuration.
Even though we only evaluated the performance of the BPCCS-based LDPC encoder on UCP, the encoder design is also applicable to other SIMD architectures. In future research, we will continue to explore parallelization schemes for other 5G physical layer algorithms such as LDPC decoding, and explore efficient hardware implementation schemes for these algorithms on UCP.
