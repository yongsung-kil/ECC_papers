ABSTRACT
In this paper, layered min-sum (MS) iterative decoding is formulated as a customized neural network following the sequential scheduling of check node (CN) updates. By virtue of the lifting structure of protograph low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, identical network parameters are shared among all derived edges originating from the same edge in the protograph, which makes the number of learnable parameters manageable. The proposed neural layered MS decoder can support arbitrary codelengths consequently. Moreover, an iteration-wise greedy training method is proposed to tune the parameters such that it avoids the vanishing gradient problem and accelerates the decoding convergence.
Index Terms— Neural network, protograph LDPC codes, layered decoding, min-sum (MS)
1. INTRODUCTION
Protograph low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1] have been intensely investigated because they possess not only excellent error performance but also low complexities. The LDPC coding scheme in 5G NR [2] adopts the protograph structure involving two base graphs (BG1 and BG2).
LDPC codes are typically decoded via sum-product (SP) or belief propagation (BP) [3] with high complexity. The min-sum (MS) algorithm [4] and its variants, such as normalized min-sum (NMS) and offset min-sum (OMS) [5], have been then developed to reduce the computational complexity and are therefore more suitable for practical use. As a modified version of the conventional SP algorithm, the turbo-decoding message-passing (TDMP) [6] algorithm, also referred to as layered decoding [7], was introduced to serialize the LDPC decoding process thus accelerating the decoding convergence by a factor of two in terms of iterations.
Due to the advances in deep learning, neural networks have also attracted more attention in coding and signal processing communities [8–12]. In [9], the neural SP decoder was introduced by adding learnable weights to edges in the Tanner graph [13] and

This weighted SP decoder can mitigate the detrimental impacts of short cycles thus presenting improvements over the plain SP decoder for various high-density parity-check (HDPC) codes. Later, more hardware-friendly neural NMS/OMS decoders [10, 11] were proposed to reduce the computational burden.
One limitation of existing works [9–11] is that the size of neural networks increases rapidly with codelengths and iterations, incurring excessive training complexity. For this reason, current neural decoders support only short to moderate codelengths. A valid method was put forward in [14] to tackle this issue. In this paper, we extend the method to design a neural layered MS decoder for protograph LDPC codes by exploiting their lifting structure. We unfold layered MS decoding as a customized neural network where messages are delivered in a feed-forward fashion. Each decoding iteration corresponds to one layer in the neural network. Each layer is further split into variable node (VN) sublayers, check node (CN) sublayers, and decision sublayers in accordance with the sequential scheduling of CN updates. Finely tuned weights and biases are added to per edge per iteration in the protograph. Identical parameters are shared among all derived edges originating from the same edge in the protograph. Therefore, the number of parameter pairs always equals the number of edges in the protograph and is manageable. Consequently, the proposed neural layered MS decoder can support arbitrary codelengths. Meanwhile, to avoid the vanishing gradient problem in neural decoding caused by multiple iterations, we propose an iteration-wise greedy training method to update the weights and biases. The proposed training, along with the sequential scheduling, tends to optimize neural decoders as early as possible, i.e., achieving faster convergence.

3. NEURAL LAYERED MIN-SUM DECODING
The NMS and OMS algorithms are two enhanced versions of the MS algorithm. The normalizing and offset factors (denote by α and β) can be viewed as assigned weights and biases to edges in the Tanner graph, respectively. With the aid of decoding neural network, these parameters can be automatically learned to approach optimal performance. To this end, we can construct a customized sparse neural decoder with a not-fully connected structure that is a trellis representation of layered iterative decoding following the sequential scheduling of CN updates.

3.2. Training the Neural Layered MS Decoder
In this paper, we employ mean square error (MSE) between the transmitted codeword x and the output of every decision sublayer o as the loss function. Given particular weight and bias arrays α and β, the loss function can be estimated. The weights and biases can be tuned to minimize the loss function by gradient-based optimization methods that are widely used in deep learning techniques, e.g., Adam [15].
To avoid the vanishing gradient problem incurred by the too deep network structure, a multi-loss function is employed in [10, 11] to inject the gradients directly to every layer. Here, we propose a different way: the neural network is built and trained in a greedy fashion. Once a layer is trained, its corresponding weights and biases remain constant in the process of training subsequent layers. The neural decoding network grows progressively, one layer after another. Each time, only the weights and biases of the last layer are learnable.
An early termination mechanism is usually applied to reduce average decoding latency: the iterative decoder immediately halts once the target performance is achieved. The proposed greedy training method tends to optimize the performance of the neural layered MS decoder as early as possible and achieves faster convergence, along with the sequential scheduling. It inherently aligns with the early termination mechanism. Furthermore, for different configurations of iterations, the optimized weights and biases in the neural decoder can be reused instead of retraining.

The high performance of our proposed neural layered MS decoding benefits from two aspects: one is that well-learned weights and biases enable the plain MS decoding to be a better approximation of the SP algorithm, which accounts for the primary gain; the other is that well-learned parameters can partially mitigate the adverse impacts of short cycles in the Tanner graph, as stated in [9], especially for short codes.
5. CONCLUSION
We have presented a neural layered MS decoder for protograph LDPC codes by adding finely tuned weights and biases to edges in the base graph. By the lifting structure of protograph LDPC codes, identical parameters are shared among all edges derived from the same edge in the base graph. Furthermore, an iteration-wise greedy training method has been proposed to combat the vanishing gradient problem. Simulation results show that the proposed neural layered MS decoder can achieve impressive error performance and faster decoding convergence.
