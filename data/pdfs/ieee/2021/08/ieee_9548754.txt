Improved Belief-Propagation Decoding with Virtual Channel Outputs for LDPC Convolutional Codes with Rational Parity-Check Matrices

Chung-Hsuan Wang and Jo-Han Lu Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Institute of Communications Engineering,

Abstract—Previous studies revealed that low-density paritycheck convolutional codes (LDPC-CC) with rational parity-check matrices may outperform LDPC-CC with ordinary polynomial parity-check matrices. However, such a performance improvement relies on a dynamic scheduling-aided decoding scheme with high processing latency which may prevent LDPC-CC with rational parity-check matrices from practical applications. In this paper, we find that conventional belief-propagation algorithms suitable for high-speed parallel implementation can still provide the satisfactory performance gain for LDPC-CC with rational parity-check matrices as long as some virtual channel outputs which can accelerate the convergence of iterative decoding are properly supplied. Criteria for generating virtual channel outputs are investigated from the viewpoint of Tanner graph. Simulation results are also provided for performance verification.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with iterative belief-propagation (BP) decoding based on Tanner graph have been shown to achieve capacity approaching performance [1]-[5] and attracted a flurry of research efforts. Recently, a variant class of LDPC codes, called LDPC convolutional codes (LDPC-CC), were proposed in [6], [7]. With quasicyclic LDPC codes [8]-[10] as their counterpart, LDPC-CC possess the structural property on their parity-check matrices, which lends themselves well to an efficient hardware implementation. Various algebraic constructions of LDPC-CC were also developed in [11]-[13] to provide significant improvement of code performance.
Previous works on LDPC-CC were focused on polynomial parity-check matrices. To decode LDPC-CC with polynomial parity-check matrices (LDPC-CC-PPCM), a special type of pipeline decoder suitable for practical implementation was proposed in [12], which behaves similarly to the slidingwindow decoder for ordinary convolutional codes but performs the iterative BP decoding on Tanner graph. Design guidelines for LDPC-CC-PPCM with monomial/binomial terms were also reported in [11]-[13] to guarantee the remarkable biterror-rate (BER) performance. However, there exists almost no literature on LDPC-CC with rational parity-check matrices (LDPC-CC-RPCM) due to the following reasons. First, from the viewpoint of conventional pipeline decoder, LDPC-CCRPCM will incur an infinite memory order, thereby resulting in unaffordable decoding latency and complexity. Moreover, author in [12] demonstrated that Tanner graph of LDPC-CCPPCM with trinomial (or higher order) terms will have a girth

less than or equal to 6. Such a result implies that a direct series decomposition of rational entries in a parity-check matrix may generate length-4 cycles on the Tanner graph so as to ruin the decoding performance of LDPC-CC-RPCM.
In [14], we discovered an alternative viewpoint to tranform the Tanner graph of LDPC-CC-RPCM into an equivalent one which keeps the same parity-check constraints specified by RPCM but has a finite memory order and excludes the length4 cycles due to rational entries in RPCM. The equivalent graph was further transformed by a procedure in [15] to eliminate all the undesired short-cycles but with the guarantee of good properties on the stopping sets. Decoding based on the transformed graph thus gives LDPC-CC-RPCM a chance to be reconsidered for possible use. In addition, after the graph transformation, the original LDPC-CC-RPCM can be viewed as a child code punctured from the mother code corresponding to the transformed graph. As suggested in [16] that a proper scheduling can improve the decoding performance of punctured LDPC codes, we devised a dynamic schedulingaided decoding scheme for LDPC-CC-RPCM in [15] which not only achieves a satisfactory performance gain over the conventional BP decoding with flooding schedule [5] in both the waterfall and error-floor regions but also helps LDPC-CCRPCM outperform LDPC-CC-PPCM in some cases. However, the employement of dynamic scheduling usually comes along with long processing latency which may prevent LDPC-CCRPCM from practical applications with the desire of high decoding throughput.
In this paper, we find that some punctured variable nodes on the transformed graph can not be successfully recovered by the BP algorithm with flooding schedule in the first few iterations. Those unrecovered nodes will disable their neighboring check nodes from message updating, thereby degenerating the overall decoding performance. Suppose some virtual channel outputs which are generated from other unpunctured nodes can be properly supplied to the punctured nodes before the iterative decoding starts. We observe that the flooding schedule which allows for high-speed parallel implementation can then provide the satisfactory decoding performance for LDPC-CCRPCM and even a better BER performance than the dynamic scheduling-aided scheme in some cases.

III. IMPROVED BELIEF-PROPAGATION DECODING WITH VIRTUAL CHANNEL OUTPUTS
As shown in Section II, every LDPC-CC-RPCM can be viewed as a child code punctured from a lower rate mother LDPC-CC whose Tanner graph not only satisfies the same parity-check constraints specified by the original RPCM but also guarantees the advantage of finite memory order and a large girth. Decoding LDPC-CC-RPCM on the transformed graph can hence be regarded as a special case of the decoding of punctured codes. Previous works on decoding punctured LDPC codes suggest that a proper scheduling, rather than the flooding schedule [5] which updates all the messages of variable and check nodes simultaneously at each iteration for decoding, can improve the BER performance of BP decoding. In [15], we adapted the efficient dynamic scheduling originally invented for LDPC block codes to the window-based decoder for LDPC-CC. The residual function of the efficient dynamic scheduling was also modified such that reliable nodes on the Tanner graph can have a higher priority than unreliable nodes to spread their messages. Together with two extra improvements: the signal perturbation and error cancellation, the proposed dynamic scheduling-aided decoding scheme was verified to provide a better BER performance than the flooding schedule under the same decoding complexity.
However, the dynamic scheduling requires that the message on each edge on the Tanner graph is serially updated according to the corresponding residual value. Compared with the flooding schedule which is suitable for high-speed parallel implementation, the dynamic scheduling-aided decoding scheme usually comes along with a long processing latency which may prevent LDPC-CC-RPCM from practical applications with the desire of high decoding throughput. By a careful investigation of the decoding process of LDPC-CC-RPCM, we discover the following factors which may impair the decoding performance of flooding schedule. First, we find that some punctured variable nodes on the transformed graph can not be successfully recovered by the flooding schedule in the first

few iterations. Those unrecovered nodes will disable their neighboring check nodes from message updating so as to degenerate the decoding performance. In addition, some punctured variable nodes are observed to connect to check nodes whose neighboring variable nodes are almost all punctured nodes. Those nodes are likely to be incorrectly recovered due to the possible unreliable recovery of other punctured nodes, therey resulting in a severe error propagation on the Tanner graph.
To mitigate the above undesired phenomenon, a simple and straight-forward solution is to supply each punctured node on the transformed graph G(H¯ (D)) with some extra check nodes, called supporting nodes, which are expected to satisfy the following conditions:

1) All the supporting nodes should define the equivalent parity-check constraints as in (2).
2) For each supporting node of a punctured node v, all the neighboring nodes besides v must be unpunctured nodes.
3) Each supporting node should have neighboring nodes as few as possible.
4) The supporting nodes should avoid introducing extra short cycles and small-size trapping sets on the union of G(H¯ (D)) and the effective Tanner graph consisting of the supporting nodes and their neighboring nodes.

V. CONCLUSIONS
In this work, a new BP decoding scheme with the aid of virtual channel outputs is presented for LDPC-CC-RPCM. By introducing the so-called supporting nodes on the Tanner graph of LDPC-CC-RPCM, virtual channel outputs recovered from real channel outputs of the unpunctured nodes can be supplied to the punctured nodes to enhance the decoding performance. Significant improvements are verified by the simulation results. The proposed scheme can also be directly integrated into the BP decoding with flooding schedule and is hence suitable for high-speed parallel implementation.
