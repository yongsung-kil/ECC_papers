III. PROPOSED IS-JDD ALGORITHM
It is well known that the receiver is important for communication systems because it determines two key performance metrics (i.e., high reliability and low latency). The MAP-based JDD algorithm achieves optimal system performance, but has high computational overhead and then cannot be used in scenarios with low-latency requirements. To address this problem, researchers have proposed sub-optimal detection algorithms with low complexity at the expense of performance, such as ZF and MMSE algorithms [9]. In fact, by redesigning the MAP-based JDD architecture to achieve low complexity without sacrificing performance, this may be a more promising technique. Thus, we propose an MAP-based IS-JDD algorithm, its block diagram is shown in Fig. 3 and detailed principle is given as follows.
• Preliminary: Given a codeword with n coded bits, it is divided into g groups and each group is composed of k SpD symbols and NAk SiD symbols (i.e., ηk coded bits), where n = gηk and k > 0 is a positive integer. To recover the received signals, we use the Max-Log-Map algorithm and Log-BP algorithm to implement the detector and decoder,

respectively, while introduce a group-based IS principle into
the JDD architecture. The proposed group-based IS principle
is elaborated in the following. • Parameter Initialization: Initialize an outer iteration flag
Fout,w = 0 and an inner iteration flag Fin,w = 0, where w = 1, 2, . . . , g. Meanwhile, set the appropriate detection threshold Ddet and decoding threshold Ddec. • Outer Iteration: Assuming current outer iteration number is t1 ∈ {1, 2, . . . , Tout}, we define the w-th (w = 1, 2, . . . , g) group as an active group if Fout,w = 0. The received signals within active groups will be detected with the aid of their
corresponding a-priori LLRs in a sequential group order.
Afterwards, the detector outputs the extrinsic LLRs for all active groups, and each of which has ηk extrinsic LLRs ldwe,tz,t1 corresponding to ηk coded bits during the t1-th outer iteration, where z = (w −1)ηk +1, (w −1)ηk +2, . . . , wηk. Then, we can calculate the average extrinsic LLR ldaveet,w,t1 for each active group, as

ldavee. ,w,t = ||lwde,.t||1/(ηk).

(5)

where ||·||1 is an L1-norm and ldwe,.t is an LLR vector with ηk elements for the w-th active group. In particular, t denotes

the outer (resp. inner) iteration number t1 (resp. t2) and de. indicates the abbreviations of detection (i.e., det) and de-

coding (i.e., dec). The IS-JDD algorithm decides whether to

decode the w-th group of soft information by comparing the detection difference (i.e., Ddet,w = |ldaveet,w,t1 −ldaveet,w,(t1−1)|) between two adjacent outer iterations. To be specific, when

Ddet,w > Ddet, Fout,w and Fin,w are set to be 0, while BP decoding procedure will be performed for the w-th group;

Otherwise, Fout,w = 1, Fin,w = 1, and the BP decoding procedure for the w-th group is terminated. It is important

to note that the detection termination in one group does

not affect the detection procedure in other groups, because

the detection process is carried out in a sequential group

order and the received signals within different groups are

independent of one another.

• Inner Iteration: Considering the group-based structure of detector, the BP decoder also uses a group-shuffled rule (i.e., follows a sequential group order) to process the LLRs. Assuming current inner iteration number is t2 ∈ {1, 2, . . . , Tin}, the decoder first identifies Fin,w, and then performs the BP decoding procedure (i.e., check-node (CN)/variable-node (VN) update) for ηk LLRs within the w-th active group if Fin,w = 0. During each inner iteration, we also compute the average a-posterior LLR ldaveec,w,t2 for each active group by (5). When decoder completes the BP procedure for the last active group, it immediately makes a decoding judgement. If current decoding is successful, the IS-JDD process ends; Otherwise, computing the decoding difference Ddec,w = |ldaveec,w,t2 − ldaveec,w,(t2−1)|: i) when Ddec,w > Ddec, Fin,w is reset to be 0 and the inner iteration for the w-th group will continue, ii) when Ddec,w < Ddec, Fin,w is reset to be 1 and the corresponding inner iteration is terminated. Assuming decoding fails when t2 = Tin or Fin,w = 1 for all groups, the decoder will output extrinsic LLRs of the groups with Fout,w = 0 to detector for next outer iteration.

A. Convergence Performance for Different JDD Algorithms
In Fig. 4, we analyze the BER/FER performance of (3, 6) P-LDPC code, and also calculate its maximum BP iterations required for convergence under two different JDD algorithms (i.e., conventional JDD and proposed IS-JDD algorithms). As observed from Fig. 4(a), one can notice that the proposed IS-JDD algorithm exhibits almost the same BER/FER performance as conventional JDD algorithm. Furthermore, referring to Fig. 4(b), the proposed IS-JDD algorithm requires fewer BP iterations than the conventional JDD algorithm to achieve convergence at different OSNRs, especially in the low-to-medium OSNR region (i.e., 6.2 ∼ 7 dB).

B. Unequal-Convergence Characteristic of IS-JDD Algorithm
In the proposed IS-JDD algorithm, a P-LDPC codeword is divided into multiple groups, and each of which can be detected and decoded individually. Then, for an irregular P-LDPC code, its different groups may be formulated by VNs with different degrees, this will lead to different convergence performance. Fig. 6 analyzes the convergence difference between groups 1 and 2 of ERJA P-LDPC code with proposed IS-JDD algorithm. It is obvious that groups 1 and 2 have different numbers of BP iterations required to achieve convergence at different OSNRs. V. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we have investigated P-LDPC-coded MIMOVLC systems with GSM over an indoor LoS channel. To be specific, inspired by the characteristic that different VNs within a P-LDPC codeword have different convergence performance, we have introduced a novel IS principle into the conventional MAP detector-based JDD framework, and then developed an IS-JDD algorithm. The proposed IS-JDD algorithm divides a P-LDPC codeword into several different groups, and each of which is detected and decoded individually, which helps to reduce the computational overhead (i.e., including the BP iterations and the number of calculations) without sacrificing system performance. Simulated results have demonstrated that the proposed IS-JDD algorithm has low-latency superiority, and thus is a promising alternative to existing benchmark schemes.
