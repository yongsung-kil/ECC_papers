Abstract‚ÄĒThe performance and complexity of low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoders have strict requirements since they are widely demanded in both communication and storage systems. Information storage bit flipping (ISBF) decoder and layered ISBF (LISBF) decoder demonstrate excellent decoding performance and throughput in which the global maximumfinding operation can be calculated in parallel to other decoding operations. However, their error-correction performance is not satisfactory. In this paper, we consider a variety of optimizations based on LISBF and propose a new algorithm named multiple flipping strategy LISBF (MLISBF). First, a high probability is adopted to flip those bits with high energy values. Second, strict flipping with a lower threshold is triggered when the syndrome weight is negligible. Then, we introduce perturbation flipping at specific iterations to help the decoder overcome trapping sets. We designed an efficient hardware implementation which realizes the layered calculation for BF decoders and reduces hardware resources for the MLISBF algorithm.
Index Terms‚ÄĒLow-density parity-check codes, bit flipping, flipping strategy, layered decoding, trapping set
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) has been intensively studied in the past decades because of its wide application in communication and storage systems [1]. The decoding algorithms of LDPC codes can be divided into two main categories: soft-decision decoding and hard-decision decoding. On the one hand, soft-decision decoding algorithms such as the belief-propagation (BP) algorithm [1] have high performance in decoding ability but at the cost of increased computational complexity. Therefore, some optimization algorithms, such as the min-sum (MS) algorithm [2] and offset MS (OMS) algorithm [3], were developed to simplify the decoding complexity. On the other hand, hard-decision decoding algorithms such as the bit flipping (BF) algorithm have low complexity, but their performance is worse than that of soft-decision decoding algorithms. To increase the error-correction performance of the BF algorithm, some soft information-aided BF algorithms, such as the gradient descent BF (GDBF) algorithm [4] and noisy GDBF (NGDBF) algorithm [5], have been proposed. In some application scenarios with high complexity requirements,

the high complexity generated by using soft information leads to a limit on the application of soft-decision LDPC decoder. Therefore, this paper focuses on BF algorithms based only on hard information.
The differences between different BF decoding algorithms are mainly the computation of the inversion function (or energy function) and the operation to obtain flipping threshold or flipping mechanism at each iteration [6]. The probabilistic GDBF (PGDBF) algorithm [7] was proposed for LDPC decoding outperforms the GDBF algorithm by a wide margin due to the deliberate introduction of random perturbations. Unlike the GDBF algorithm, which flips all bits whose energy values are equal to the maximum value, the PGDBF algorithm flips these bits with a certain probability. A tabu-list randompenalty GDBF (TRGDBF) algorithm [8] was proposed. On the one hand, the TRGDBF algorithm introduces a tabu-list to overcome the repeated flip problem between iterations. On the other hand, it adds an additional random penalty term in calculating the energy function. Compared with the PGDBF algorithm, the TRGDBF algorithm has a better ability to overcome trapping sets and higher error-correction performance. However, those algorithms must perform a global maximum search operation to obtain the flipping threshold. This serial calculation increases the critical path and dramatically affects the achievable hardware frequency, especially in applications with long codeword lengths. The probabilistic parallel BF (PPBF) algorithm [9], the information storage BF (ISBF) algorithm [10], and the layered ISBF (LISBF) algorithm [11] have been proposed to reduce the critical path. Instead of calculating the flipping threshold, the PPBF algorithm flips with a certain probability according to its energy values. However, it does not have satisfactory error-correction performance. In the ISBF algorithm, the energy values of the previous iteration are stored and used to calculate the flipping threshold of the current iteration. Thus, the ISBF algorithm can compute bit energy values and flipping thresholds in parallel. In the LISBF algorithm, the column-layered strategy is used to speed up the convergence of ISBF. To further improve the decoding performance, we study some undecodable codewords in detail. Many will fall into trapping sets with only a few error bits left. Still, the ISBF and LISBF algorithms cannot overcome those trapping sets very well. Fig. 2 shows one of those cases. It can be observed that all unsatisfied CNs are connected to only one error bit in VNs, which causes the Sk of the error bits to be not larger than 1. It is difficult to proceed with decoding based on the previous algorithms once the decoder falls into those trapping sets. Then, we propose another new flipping strategy called perturbation flipping to help the decoder to escape from the trapping sets. The perturbation flipping is an iteration with a low flipping threshold T h = T hper at the certain number of iterations t ‚ąą Tper. The perturbation brought by this flipping strategy should not exceed the decoding ability of the decoder, so we limit the number of flipped bits by flipping with a low probability p3.

The number of clock cycles for one iteration can be considered Nc = L. For LDPC codewords with long code lengths, a large amount of combinational logic and register resources will be used when one iteration is calculated in one clock cycle. However, if the calculation of one iteration is completed with multiple clock cycles, the traditional algorithm with global maximum-finding needs to wait for all Ek calculation to have been completed before starting the bits inversion operation, which significantly affects the throughput of the decoder. It is possible to solve this problem effectively using the MLISBF decoder, which calculates flipping thresholds and inversion indicators in parallel. Therefore, the MLISBF decoder has good application potential, especially in long code-length LDPC decoders.

VI. CONCLUSION
In this work, we propose a new algorithm for LDPC decoding: multiple flipping strategy LISBF. We adopt three flipping strategies to handle different states of decoding. The proposed MLISBF algorithm improves the decoding performance by increasing the decoding convergence speed and the ability to overcome trapping sets. Simulation results demonstrate that the FER performance and the average number of iterations of the MLISBF algorithm are better than other BF algorithms. Then, layered hardware architecture is proposed for the MLISBF decoder. Some combinational logic and registers are multiplexed between layers and the decoder use inversion information to update syndrome in time. Therefore, it has potential application in the decoding structure of LDPC codes with long code lengths. Synthesis results show that the MLISBF decoder offers lower hardware resource cost with comparable area efficiency. In conclusion, the proposed MLISBF decoder provides excellent decoding performance with high throughput and low complexity, and it has the application potential on long-code-length LDPC decoders. Hence, it has strong competitiveness and potential in practical decoding applications.
