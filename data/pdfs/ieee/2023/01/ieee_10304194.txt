Achievable Rates of Generalized Linear Systems With Orthogonal/Vector AMP Receiver
Lei Liu , Senior Member, IEEE, Yuhao Chi , Member, IEEE, Ying Li , Member, IEEE, and Zhaoyang Zhang , Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract—In signal processing and wireless communications, the generalized linear system (GLS) has been widely used to evaluate the impact of nonlinear preprocessing on receiver performance. Although generalized approximation message passing (GAMP) is a state-of-the-art algorithm for GLS signal recovery, it is only applicable to measurement matrices with independent and identically distributed (IID) elements. To overcome this limitation, the generalized orthogonal/vector approximate message passing (GOAMP/GVAMP) algorithm for unitarily invariant measurement matrices was developed and shown to be replica Bayes optimal in uncoded GLS. However, due to its complicated state evolution (SE), the information-theoretic limit of GOAMP/GVAMP remains an open problem for arbitrary input distributions. In this paper, we provide a rigorous achievable rate analysis of GOAMP/GVAMP in GLS, establishing its information-theoretic limit (i.e., maximum achievable rate). Specifically, GOAMP/GVAMP involves a dual-input-dual-output linear detector (LD) paired with two nonlinear detectors (NLD), making the achievable rate difficult to analyze. To address this issue, we convert the fully unfolded state evolution (SE) of a multi-layer iterative algorithm into an equivalent singleinput single-output variational SE (VSE). I. INTRODUCTION

I N signal processing and wireless communication applications, a generalized linear system (GLS) has been widely used to solve the recovery problem of an unknown signal x from a nonlinear noisy observation y in the following form:

y = Q(Ax, n),

(1)

where A is the measurement matrix, n is an additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN), and Q(·) is a nonlinear function. In comparison to the most commonly used standard linear system (SLS), i.e., y = Ax + n, GLS can better account for the effect of nonlinear preprocessing on signal recovery in practical transceivers [1], [2], [3], [4]. For example, an analog-todigital converter (ADC) is necessary for the receiver to reduce the hardware cost and power efficiency of massive multipleinput multiple-output (MIMO) systems [1], where Q(·) is a quantizer in GLS. To suppress the high peak-to-average power ratio (PAPR) caused by multi-carrier signal transmission in orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM), signal pre-distortion such as peak-clipping operation Q(·) is used to reduce envelope fluctuations of OFDM signals [2]. Furthermore, low-cost wireless devices commonly employ passive envelope detectors or have limited accuracy in-phase and quadrature components [3], i.e., Q(·) = | · | or | · |2 (elementwise), causing the signal phase information to be completely lost or severely degraded. As a result, the GLS-based phase retrieval problem is non-convex and has been determined to

A. Linearized Approximate Model
Despite the widespread use of GLS, an exact analysis is difficult due to the nonlinear nature of Q(·). Several linearized approximation models have been developed over the last decade to convert the GLS into a simplified SLS. In [5], quantization noise is assumed to be additive and independent using the additive quantization noise model (AQNM) and the effect of low-resolution ADCs and bandwidth on the achievable rate of MIMO is investigated. Meanwhile, in [6], AQNM is employed to calculate achievable rates or analyze the spectral efficiency of the maximum ratio combining (MRC) receiver for massive MIMO with Gaussian signaling and low-resolution ADCs. For 1-bit massive MIMO, linear minimum mean-squared error (LMMSE)-based channel estimation and performance analysis are developed under AQNM [7]. Similarly, a linearized clipping model is developed, and an iterative soft compensation method is provided to reduce the clipping distortion [8]. Although linearization can greatly simplify performance analysis and algorithm design for GLS, it is inherently suboptimal, resulting in considerable degradation of bit-error-rate (BER) or achievable rate performance.
B. Bayesian Algorithms
Various generalized approximate message passing (AMP)type algorithms based on the Bayesian framework have been developed for GLS in recent years, with continued development of AMP-type algorithms in SLS [9]. Because of its high efficiency and low complexity, AMP has attracted extensive research interests for high-dimensional linear systems with arbitrary input distributions [10], [11]. Based on simple state evolution (SE) analysis [11], AMP is proven to be Bayes optimal [12], [13]. However, AMP is only valid for independent identical distribution (IID) matrices A. This severely limits the application of AMP in more practical systems.
To solve this limitation, Turbo-CS, a compression-sensing (CS)-based signal recovery technique, is developed for SLS with the partial discrete Fourier transform (DFT) measurement matrix [14], [15]. Orthogonal/vector AMP (OAMP/VAMP) is presented [16], [17] for SLS with a broad range of right-unitarily-invariant matrices, utilizing a high-complexity LMMSE to reduce linear interference and orthogonalization to address the correlation problem. The Bayes optimality of OAMP/VAMP is demonstrated using the replica methods in [18], [19], and lately rigorously for a general class of signal priors and any unitarily-invariant A under a “high-temperature” condition that restricts the range of eigenvalues of AT A in [20], [21]. A low-complexity convolutional AMP (CAMP) is proposed to tackle the high-complexity difficulty of OAMP/VAMP by replacing the Onsager term in AMP with a convolution of all preceding messages [22]. However, CAMP has a relatively slow convergence speed and may fail to converge, especially for measurement matrices A with high condition numbers.

Independently, a low-complexity memory AMP (MAMP) is developed by replacing the LMMSE of OAMP/VAMP with a long-memory matched filter [23]. In addition, MAMP is shown to be Bayes optimal for SLS with the right-unitarily-invariant matrices. Furthermore, inspired by the damping optimization in [23] and its statistical interpretation in [24], sufficient statistical memory AMPs (SS-MAMPs) are presented in [25] to theoretically guarantee the convergence of all MAMP-type algorithms. In addition, AMP-Type algorithms are combined with deep learning techniques in [26], [27] to improve detection performance in small-scale systems.
The AMP-type algorithms (for SLS) discussed above have been extended to GLS. A generalized AMP (GAMP) is proposed for GLS in [28]. Like AMP, GAMP is low-complexity but is limited to IID matrices. To overcome the limitation of GAMP, generalized Turbo-SR [29] and generalized VAMP (GVAMP) [30] are proposed for GLS with DFT and unitarilyinvariant matrices, respectively. Meanwhile, GVAMP is proved to be replica Bayes optimal [31]. Due to the equivalence of GVAMP and generalized OAMP (GOAMP), they are referred to as GOAMP/GVAMP in this paper. Similar to OAMP/VAMP, GOAMP/GVAMP has high computational complexity. Recently, a low-complexity generalized MAMP (GMAMP) is proposed for GLS with unitarily-invariant matrices [32], in which the Bayes optimality of GMAMP is also proven via SE. However, the results in [28], [29], [30], [31], [32], [33] are limited to the uncoded GLS, where error-free performance is not guaranteed. To the best of our knowledge, a rigorous investigation of the information-theoretic limit of generalized AMP-type algorithms in the coded GLS is yet lacking.
Uncoded Systems vs Coded Systems: MSE measurement is widely used in signal processing and detection of un-coded systems, so MMSE is commonly used to denote the Bayes optimality (MMSE optimality) of an un-coded system. In contrast, the achievable rate is a key measurement for coded systems with error-free recovery. Thus, the maximum achievable rate is commonly used to denote the information-theoretical limit of GOAMP/GVAMP in a coded GLS. It is worth noting that Bayes optimality does not guarantee error-free performance. This can be seen in the numerical results of this paper, where there is a significant performance loss of Bayes optimal GOAMP/GVAMP with well-designed P2P low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes.
C. Information-Theoretical Limits of AMP-Type Algorithms in Coded SLSs
The information-theoretical (i.e., constrained capacity) optimality of AMP for a coded SLS with an arbitrary input distribution and IID matrices is demonstrated in [34]. That is, the achievable rate of AMP has been rigorously proven to be equal to the constrained capacity of SLS as derived in [12], [13]. The optimal coding principle is also provided for AMP based on the matching principle between the transfer functions of the linear detector (LD) and nonlinear detector (NLD), on which a kind of practical LDPC code is properly designed to approach the associated constrained capacity. However, the achievable rate analysis of the AMP-type algorithms [34], [35], [36], [37] in SLS cannot be straightforwardly applied to the generalized AMP-type algorithms in GLS. The main reason is that the achievable rate analysis of the AMP-type algorithms relies on the single-inputsingle-output (SISO) transfer functions in their SEs. In contrast, the LD transfer function of generalized AMP-type algorithms is dual-input-dual-output (DIDO) and is coupled with those of two NLDs, making the achievable rate analysis much more complicated.
D. Related Information-Theoretical Analysis for GLS
Recently, the achievable rate analysis of quantized MIMO based on Gaussian signaling and a linear model with additive independent Gaussian noise is presented in [38], with a unitary transform on the transceiver side and linear analog precoding on the transmitter side. The achievable rate analysis in [38] cannot be directly applied to quantized MIMO with practical constraints. First, the unitary-transform-based modulation may introduce interference among symbols, which is unfavorable for practical MIMO system design. Second, the theoretical analysis is limited to Gaussian signaling, which does not apply to arbitrary input distributions. Last but not least, the effects of channel coding and decoding are not considered. To the best of our knowledge, the information-theoretical analysis of coded GLS with arbitrary input distributions and unitarily-invariant measurement matrices is still an open issue.
E. Contributions of This Paper
In this paper, we present the information-theoretical (i.e., achievable rate) analysis of GOAMP/GVAMP in the coded GLS. To circumvent the difficulty of SE analysis of GOAMP/GVAMP, a multi-layer information matching principle is proposed to analyze the asymptotic performance of GOAMP/GVAMP. Specifically, a transfer function of the enhanced LD (ELD) is established for GOAMP/GVAMP. Then, GOAMP/GVAMP can be asymptotically characterized by a variational SE (VSE) composed of the transfer functions of ELD and NLD. The achievable rate of GOAMP/GVAMP is calculated using the VSE based on the mutual information and MMSE (I-MMSE) lemma [39]. Meanwhile, an optimal coding principle is presented for GOAMP/GVAMP to maximize the achievable rate. Furthermore, based on the VSE principle, the achievable rate and optimal code design of the more general multi-layer AMP-type algorithm under a high-dimensional neural network can be similarly derived. As an example, we investigate the achievable rates of GOAMP/GVAMP in the coded GLS with clipping and quantization. In addition, a kind

of practical LDPC code is designed for GLS with clipping and quantization. The main contributions of this paper are summarized below.
1) The VSE is proposed to analyze the achievable rate of GOAMP/GVAMP in GLS, based on which the optimal coding principle is developed to maximize the achievable rate. The proposed VSE can be extended to analyze achievable rate and code design for multi-layer AMP-type algorithms under high-dimensional neural networks.
2) The maximum achievable rate of GOAMP/GVAMP in GLS is discussed in depth for clipping and quantization. To validate its advantages, the GOAMP/GVAMP with optimized coding is compared to the conventional schemes, i.e., maximum ratio combining (MRC) receiver based on the linearized model and cascading GOAMP/GVAMP with separate LD and NLD.
3) A kind of irregular LDPC code is designed for GOAMP/GVAMP, whose theoretical detection thresholds are about 0.2 dB away from the theoretical limits corresponding to the maximum achievable rates.
4) Numerical results show that the finite-length performances of the optimized LDPC codes and quadrature phase-shift keying (QPSK) modulation are within 2.2 dB from the threshold limits. They also significantly outperform GOAMP/GVAMP with P2P regular and welldesigned irregular LDPC codes in GLS.
This is the first work to study the achievable rate of GOAMP/GVAMP in the coded GLS. Since the constrained capacity of GLS with arbitrary input distribution and unitarilyinvariant matrices is still not rigorously proven, we are still unable to prove the capacity optimality of the coded GOAMP/GVAMP proposed in this paper. Nevertheless, based on the Bayes optimality of GOAMP/GVAMP in uncoded GLS [30], [31] and our previous studies in [34], [35], [37], we conjecture that GOAMP/GVAMP is also constrained-capacity optimal for the coded GLS. The rigorous proof of this conjecture will be meaningful for future work.
Part of the results in this paper has been published in [40]. In this paper, we provide more detailed analysis and numerical results.

VI. CONCLUSION
This paper focuses on the achievable rate analysis of GOAMP/GVAMP for a coded GLS with unitarily-invariant matrices and arbitrary input distributions. To solve the difficulty of analyzing the DIDO transfer function of LD in SE, we propose the equivalent variational SISO transfer functions and prove that their convergence performances are the same. Based on the SISO transfer functions and I-MMSE lemma, the achievable rate analysis of GOAMP/GVAMP is presented, based on which the principle of optimal code design is obtained with the aim of maximizing the achievable rate. Moreover, the principle of equivalent SISO transfer functions is still valid for analyzing the achievable rate and optimal code design of a multi-layer AMP-type algorithm in a high-dimensional neural network. Furthermore, we compare the advantages of the achievable rate of GOAMP/GVAMP to the conventional MRC-based linearized model and CAS-GOAMP/GVAMP, using clipping and quantification as examples. Numerical results show that GOAMP/GVAMP with the optimized LDPC code can approach the theoretical decoding thresholds and finite-length performances are presented to verify its near optimality.
