Joint-Way Compression for LDPC Neural Decoding Algorithm With Tensor-Ring Decomposition
YUANHUI LIANG , CHAN-TONG LAM , (Senior Member, IEEE), AND BENJAMIN K. NG , (Senior Member, IEEE)
Faculty of Applied Sciences, Macao Polytechnic University, Macao SAR 999078, China
Corresponding author: Chan-Tong Lam (ctlam@mpu.edu.mo)

ABSTRACT In this paper, we propose low complexity joint-way compression algorithms with TensorRing (TR) decomposition and weight sharing to further lower the storage and computational complexity requirements for low density parity check (LDPC) neural decoding. Compared with Tensor-Train (TT) decomposition, TR decomposition is more flexible for the selection of ranks, and is also conducive to the use of rank optimization algorithms. In particular, we use TR decomposition to decompose not only the weight parameter matrix of Neural Normalized Min-Sum (NNMS)+ algorithm, but also the message matrix transmitted between variable nodes and check nodes. Furthermore, we combine the TR decomposition and weight sharing algorithm, called joint-way compression, to further lower the complexity of LDPC neural decoding algorithm. We show that the joint-way compression algorithm can achieve better compression efficiency than a single compression algorithm while maintaining a comparable bit error rate (BER) performance. From the numerical experiments, we found that all the compression algorithms with appropriate selection of ranks give almost no performance degradation and that the TRwm-ssNNMS+ algorithm, which combines the spatial sharing and TR decomposition of both weight and message matrix, has the best compression efficiency. Compared with our TT-NNMS+ algorithm proposed in Yuanhui et al. (2022), the number of parameters is reduced by about 70 times and the number of multiplications is reduced by about 6 times.
INDEX TERMS Joint-way compression, weight sharing, tensor ring decomposition, LDPC neural decoding.

I. INTRODUCTION Channel decoding algorithms based on model-driven deep learning, also known as channel neural decoding algorithms, have been widely developed recently [1], [2], [3], [4], [5]. At present, most of them are based on the unfolding method [6], which expands the iterative decoding algorithm into neural network decoding. In view of the poor decoding performance of the belief propagation (BP) algorithm in short and moderate length of Bose-Chaudhuri-Hocquenghem (BCH) codes, the authors in [1] proposed the Neural BP algorithm, which improves the decoding performance of the BP algorithm in high-density parity check (HDPC) codes. In order to reduce the complexity of channel neural decoding, [7] and [8] introduced the pruning algorithm combined with the expert knowledge of BP decoding, and proposed a pruning-based neural BP (NBP) algorithm, which reduces the complexity of the BP decoding algorithm. The weight sharing algorithm for neural channel decoding can be considered as a pruning algorithm. When the pruning algorithms are used for channel neural decoding, expert knowledge of the decoding algorithm is often combined to improve the compression efficiency.
In addition to weight sharing methods, Tensor-Train (TT) and Tensor-Ring (TR) decompositions have been widely used in model compression in recent years because they can overcome the curse of dimensionality [12], [13], [14], [15]. In [16], TT was applied to a deep neural network (DNN) based channel estimator for the first time to address the challenge of time-varying channels in MIMO systems. In [17], we applied TT decomposition to the model-driven deep learning-based LDPC decoding algorithm, which significantly reduced the parameter storage requirements and computational complexity of the decoding algorithm. Compared with TT decomposition, TR decomposition has the property of permutation without deformation, and it is more flexible and convenient in the selection of rank. TR decomposition can be considered as an extension of TT decomposition.
In machine learning, the parameters of DNN can be decomposed by low-rank decomposition algorithm before and after pruning to further improve the compression efficiency [18]. This low-complexity technique using multiple compression methods is called joint-way compression [12]. In this paper, we propose to use a joint-way compression technique, by combining the weight coefficient sharing algorithm and the TR decomposition, to further improve the compression rate of the LDPC neural decoding algorithm, without significant performance degradation. • We apply TR decomposition to the NNMS+ decoding algorithm proposed in [17] to further reduce its computational complexity, called TR-NNMS+ algorithm.

B. TENSOR-RING DECOMPOSITION
The authors in [15] proposed TR decomposition. The main advantage of TR decomposition is the circular dimensional permutation invariance, by employing the trace operation and treating the latent cores equivalently. Denote T ∈ RI1×I2×...×In as a d-dimensional tensor. After TR decomposition, T can be represented as a sequence of three-dimensional tensors Z ∈ Rrk ×nk ×rk+1 , k = 1, 2, . . . , d, given as [15],

T (i1, i2, . . . , id ) = tr {Z1(i1)Z2(i2) . . . Zd (id )} (3)

where T (i1, i2, . . . , id ) represents the (i1, i2, . . . , id )th element of the tensor T , tr is the matrix trace operation. Zk (ik ) represents the ik th slice matrix of the tensor T . We can write TR decomposition in the index form [15] as,

Zk (ak , ik , ak+1) (4)

where k is the index of tensor dimensions, ak is the idex of latent dimensions, ik is the idex of data dimensions. We can also write (4) in tensor format as

r1,...,rd

T=

z1(a1, a2) ◦ z1(a2, a3) ◦ . . . ◦ zd (ad , a1) (5)

where ◦ represents the outer product of vectors, Zk (ak , ak+1) ∈ Rnk represent the (ak , ak+1)th mode-2 fiber of tensor Zk . The spatial complexity of the TR format is O(dnr2), which is linear to the order of the tensor d. The difference between TR decomposition and TT decomposition is that TR satisfies the property of circular demensional permutation invariance [15].
A series of small tensor products obtained after TT decomposition must maintain a strict order, which results in that the cores for representing the same tensor with a circular dimension shifting cannot keep invariance. Therefore, we must choose an optimal dimensional permutation when using the TT decomposition. The application of TR decomposition does not have such strict requirements because it satisfies the property of circular demensional permutation invariance, which solves the difficulties of finding the optimal TT representation.

VI. IMPLEMENTATION A. COMPLEXITY ANALYSIS Since the compression algorithms proposed in this paper are all performed on the weight coefficient matrix and the message matrix in the NNMS+ algorithm, we mainly analyze the effects on the two matrices and the operations between them after the compression.

1) WEIGHT SHARING
For the Temporal weight sharing algorithm, since different network layers share the same weight coefficients, assuming that the maximum number of layers is T , the number of parameters of the weight coefficient matrix is reduced to 1/T times of the original after compression. For the Spatial weight sharing algorithm, different edges share the same parameter in the same layer of neural decoding, so the number of parameters of the weight coefficient matrix is T . Therefore, the number of parameters after weight sharing is smaller than that of the algorithm before compression, because the number of edges of each layer of the network is much greater than the number of layers T of the network. Compared with the ts-NNMS+ algorithm, the ss-NNMS+ algorithm has fewer parameters.

EFFECT OF APPROXIMATION ERROR The message matrix in the LDPC neural decoding algorithm corresponds to the Tanner graph, and each non-zero value in the message matrix corresponds to an edge between the VNs and the CNs in the Tanner graph. The compression algorithm based on TR decomposition decomposes not only the weight coefficient matrix, but also the message matrix, which means that the TR decomposition changes the original Tanner graph in actual operation. The matrix is first tensorized, then compressed by the TR decomposition, and decomposed into the product of a series of smaller tensors

The approximation error between the decomposed message and the original message will lead to a decrease in the accuracy of the message transmitted between the VNs and the CNs, thereby affecting the decoding performance.
Different TR ranks will lead to different errors in TR decomposition. The compression of the channel neural decoding algorithm through TR decomposition will bring errors to the trainable weight coefficient matrix and message matrix. These errors, especially the error of the message matrix, will affect the performance of channel neural decoding. Usually the Frobenius norm is used to describe the approximation error of the TR decomposition [15], meaning that replacing the original tensor A with its approximation B in the TR-format such that ∥A − B∥F ≤ ε∥B∥F holds. In the following section, we show that different ranks result in different performance degradation for the TR-NNMS+ algorithm.

VIII. CONCLUSION We have applied TR decomposition for more convenient choice of the ranks, as compared to the TT decomposition, for the NNMS+ decoding algorithm proposed in [17], called TR-NNMS+ algorithm. For the proposed TR-NNMS+ algorithm in this paper, not only the weight coefficients in the decoding algorithm are decomposed, the message matrix passed between the VNs and the CNs are also decomposed. We then proposed joint-way compression algorithms, by applying temporal and spatial weight coefficient sharing algorithms to the TR-NNMS+ decoding algorithm to further lower the complexity, without significantly degrading the BER performance. We found that all the compression algorithms with appropriate selection of ranks give almost no BER performance degradation and that the TRwmssNNMS+ algorithm, which combines the spatial sharing and TR decomposition of both weight and message matrix, has the best compression performance. Compared with our proposed TT-NNMS+ algorithm in [17], the number of parameters is reduced by 71 times and the number of multiplications is reduced by about 6 times.
