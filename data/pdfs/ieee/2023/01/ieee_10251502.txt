ABSTRACT 5G new radio (5G-NR) enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB) scenario demands a high data throughput of up to 20Gb/s, leading to an urgent need for low complexity and high throughput decoding algorithms for 5G-NR low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, the coding scheme of 5G eMBB data channel. Quantization of input and intermediate signals enables the data throughput enhancement and the computation and storage overhead reduction of LDPC decoder, but confronts the problem of performance degradation. In addition, conventional analysis tools show limitation on the accurate asymptotic performance prediction of quantized and normalized LDPC decoding algorithms. In this paper, we first introduce modified multi-edge type density evolution (MET-DE) to the asymptotic performance prediction of quantized and adaptive normalized min-sum algorithm (ANMSA). Then we adopt adaptive asymmetric quantization strategy for MET LDPC codes, combine it with conventional adaptive normalization technique, and further propose adaptive quantized and normalized min-sum algorithm (AQNMSA), which significantly alleviates the performance degradation of fixed quantized and normalized min-sum algorithm (NMSA) with negligible increment of computational complexity. Concurrently, we provide a novel look-up table design algorithm for AQNMSA based on the analysis tool of modified MET-DE. Finally, we apply AQNMSA to 5G-NR LDPC codes with a very low quantization bit-width of 4 bits for intermediate signals, and observe a comparable or superior decoding performance compared with its float-point non-adaptive counterparts under multiple code rates.

INDEX TERMS 5G-NR, adaptive quantization, adaptive quantized and normalized min-sum algorithm (AQNMSA), low-density parity-check (LDPC) code, modified MET-DE.

I. INTRODUCTION Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [1], first introduced by Gallager in 1962, is a class of linear block codes featuring sparse parity-check matrix (PCM). Irregular LDPC codes, whose PCMs have irregular row weights and column weights, could achieve Shannon-limit approaching performance under iterative belief propagation (BP) [2] decoding, and have low encoding and decoding complexity proportional to code length. Thus, they have been widely used

Multi-Edge Type (MET) LDPC codes [8], [9] are a kind of irregular LDPC codes with highly irregular row weights and column weights, and their PCMs contains special features such as some variable nodes punctured and some with degree-1.

channel of the 5G eMBB scenario. 5G-NR enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB) scenario requires the data throughput up to 20Gb/s, the support of incremental redundancy for hybrid automatic repeat request (HARQ), and the feature of code rate compatibility and code length scalability. To meet the demand of high data throughput and flexibility, 5G-NR LDPC codes are designed with many beneficial structural and functional features, including typical MET base graph or PCM, raptor-like base graph structure [11], quasi-cyclic (QC) lifting matrices [12], code rate compatibility with PCM nesting, code length scalability with compatible lifting matrices, and variable nodes puncturing [13], etc. These beneficial structural and functional features enable 5GNR LDPC codes achieve an excellent decoding threshold performance, relatively high decoding throughput compared to Turbo codes, and the support of incremental redundancy for HARQ as well as a wide range of code rate and code length.
The sum-product algorithm (SPA) [14], which could provide near optimal decoding threshold performance, is usually used for iterative LDPC decoding algorithms. However, it involves tanh and atanh operations, and therefore requires relatively high computational resources and shows sensitivity to the estimation error of channel-parameter. By comparison, the min-sum algorithm (MSA) [15] uses min instead of tanh and atanh operations, leading to low computation and storage overhead, and robustness to the estimation error of channelparameter, but degraded threshold performance.
To improve the threshold performance of MSA, offset MSA (OMSA) [16] and normalized MSA (NMSA) [17] are proposed, and the latter retains the robustness of MSA. However, conventional NMSA uses fixed normalization factor, leading to a performance tradeoff between the decoding threshold and the error floor [18]. Many efforts have been made to enhance the decoding performance of OMSA and NMSA. In [19], the min operation is modified by additionally preserving the third minimum value. In [20], [21], and [22], deep learning method is introduced to the optimization of normalization factor. As for the analysis tools, 3D extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart is applied in [23], and density evolution (DE) is applied in [24] and [25] in the decoding parameter optimization. Considering 5G-NR LDPC codes, the edge classification method based on node degree is usually utilized in the optimization of decoding parameter [22], [23], [26].
Fan et al. propose an improved NMSA called adaptive NMSA (ANMSA) [18], which utilizes a preconfigured look-up table (LUT) between the normalization factor α and the unsatisfied check nodes rate p, also referred to as checkratio, and uses Monte Carlo simulation to guide the LUT design. Based on Fan et al.’s work, Liu et al. introduce multiple LUTs for different class of edges or check nodes of irregular LDPC codes [27]. Zhou et al. propose checkratio-aided layer-based rescaling adaptive normalized MSA (CL-RANMSA) [28] and apply it to 5G-NR LDPC codes,

in which multi-edge type density evolution (MET-DE) [29], [30] analysis is utilized as the LUT design tool instead of Monte Carlo simulation.
The input signal and intermediate signals of practical LDPC decoders are generally quantized to fixed-point signals with limited bit-width. The conventional quantization strategy [31] is symmetric and fixed, which means that the input signals and intermediate signals use the same quantization method with fixed quantization parameters, i.e., the quantization parameters unchanged throughout the decoding process. Since the dynamic range of log-likelihoodratio (LLR) changes significantly throughout the iterative decoding process, it is difficult to achieve good balance between the decoding performance and data throughput, which both greatly depends on the quantization bit-width of LLR signals.
To meet the growing demand for data throughput, the bit-width of intermediate signals of LDPC decoder needs to be minimized with high-priority. An important improvement on quantization strategy is to apply different bit-width to the input LLRch and the intermediate LLRs exchanged between the variable node unit (VNU) and check node unit (CNU), and choose a very-low bit-width as the message passing bit-width, e.g., binary message passing (BMP) decoding [32], ternary message passing (TMP) decoding [33], and quaternary message passing (QMP) decoding [34], etc. From a similar perspective, dual quantization-domain (DQD) [35], [36] and reconstruction-construction-quantization (RCQ) [37], [38] scheme are proposed with larger message passing bit-width, e.g., 4-6 bits. Another potential improvement on quantization strategy is adaptive quantization parameters including the bit-width of intermediate signals [39], quantization intervals and reconstruction levels [40], [41], etc. Similarly, a LUT is preconfigured for the adaptive quantization parameters.
However, the aforementioned quantization strategies show noticeable performance degradation when the quantization bit-width is extremely low, e.g. 1 or 2 bit. Besides, they generally adopt iteration number as the LUT index, which limits their capability in iterative decoding process owing to different codes, fading channel models and channel conditions. Furthermore, these strategies are generally applied to LDPC codes with quasi-row-regular PCMs, and shows limitation on their application to typical MET LDPC codes such as 5G-NR LDPC codes. Thus, there is still a lot of room for improvement on high performance LDPC decoding algorithms with low quantization bit-width.
Efficient performance analysis tools are indispensable to the design of LDPC decoding algorithms. Monte Carlo simulation could provide accurate decoding performance results, but the computation complexity is unacceptable. Gaussian approximation or reciprocal-channel approximation (RCA) [42] is incapable of non-BP operations, e.g., min operation, quantization, and thus unable to predict the decoding performance of improved MSA as well as other deeply quantized LDPC decoding algorithms. Conventional

The main innovation of our work is that we propose a novel check-ratio based adaptive asymmetric quantization strategy, which, to the best of our knowledge, is not seen in existing literature. The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
1) We introduce modified MET-DE, which could provide accurate asymptotic performance prediction results for ANMSA and quantized ANMSA.
2) We propose a novel adaptive asymmetric quantization strategy for MET LDPC codes, whose quantization parameters are closely adaptive to the iterative decoding process.
3) Combining the proposed adaptive quantization for MET LDPC codes with adaptive normalization in previous work, we propose adaptive quantized and normalized MSA (AQNMSA), which significantly alleviates the performance degradation of fixed quantized and normalized MSA (NMSA) with negligible increment of computational complexity.
4) Aiming at the proposed AQNMSA, we provide a novel LUT design algorithm based on the analysis tool of modified MET-DE, which could simplify the design process of LUT and provide accurate prediction of asymptotic decoding performance, ensuring the effectiveness of the LUT design result.
5) We apply AQNMSA to 5G-NR LDPC codes with a very-low quantization bit-width of 4 bits, and observe a superior threshold performance and lower error floor compared with its non-adaptive counterparts.

ADAPTIVE ASYMMETRIC QUANTIZATION STRATEGY FOR MET LDPC CODES In typical hardware implementation, regardless of the parallel architecture, a shift network is always utilized to route the intermediate messages between VNUs and CNUs [45], and it is the key factor that determines the reconfigurability, parallelism, area, and routing delay of a decoder [46]. Typical MET LDPC codes, e.g., 5G-NR LDPC codes, owns relatively

When the LDPC code structure and the parallel decoding architecture are specified, the routing complexity is positively correlated with the quantization bit-width of the intermediate signals [47]. Generally an 8-bit or higher quantization bit-width is chosen for the intermediate signals [48] to guarantee an acceptable decoding threshold performance. In [49], for 5G-NR BG1 LDPC codes, a moderate performance gap of 0.25dB is observed between the float-point and 8-bit quantized SPA, while another 0.70dB performance degradation is observed when the quantization bit-width is reduced to 5-bit, which is unacceptable. Thus, it is necessary to improve conventional quantization strategy so as to alleviate the performance degradation caused by quantization.
Similar to the adaptive normalization strategy, potential improvements of quantization strategy could be made on the adaptation of quantization parameter, i.e., how the quantization parameters adapt to the iterative decoding process. As for the quantization parameters, since it is inconvenient to change the bit-width of intermediate signals throughout the decoding process in hardware implementation, this parameter

A. ASYMMETRIC QUANTIZATION We apply the asymmetric quantization strategy to design the bit-width of input and intermediate signals, which means that the input signals and intermediate signals use different quantization parameters. As the computation in VNU involves both LLRch, i.e., the input a-priori soft information signal from the channel, and LLRc→v, i.e., the intermediate signal delivered to VNU from CNU, we choose the same bit-width for LLRch and LLRc→v. Thus, we determine wL as the low bit-width of LLRv→c, and wH as the corresponding high bit-width of LLRch and LLRc→v. Note that only the low bit-width signals needs to be transmitted between VNUs and CNUs, which could significantly reduce the design complexity of routing and speed up the decoding process.
Since VNU and CNU utilize different bit-width for the input, there must be a function module attached to VNU to convert the high bit-width signals into the low bit-width signals and vice versa. We introduce the quantization conversion module (QCM), whose function is to transform a LLR with bit-width w1 and quantization interval T1 into a LLR with bit-width w2 and quantization interval T2. We define function QCM [w2, T1, T2](·), and it can be described by the predefined quantization function QUAN (·) accurately, where LLR(w,T ) refers to a LLR with bit-width w and quantization interval T :
LLR(w2,T2) = QCM [w2, T1, T2](LLR(w1,T1)) = QUAN (w2,T2)(LLR(w1,T1) · T1). (19)
Note that in the quantization conversion step, we are not trying to perfectly reproduce the original LLR from LLR(w1,T1), but to carry out the conversion in a simple and efficient way. The performance loss from this simple quantization method is marginal thanks to the proposed adaptive quantization strategy, which will be presented in the following paragraph.
B. ADAPTIVE QUANTIZATION BASED ON CHECK-RATIO AND EDGE-TYPE As for the adaptive method, we propose adaptive quantization strategy based on check-ratio and edge-type, in which the quantization intervals of each iteration are determined by check-ratio, which is a direct indication of the iterative decoding state, and edge-type. Since the bit-width of VNU’s input is high, there is little room for optimization of the quantization intervals of VNU’s input. Therefore, we only apply adaptive quantization intervals to CNU’s input, and

In practical implementation, the quantization parameters LUT ((check-ratio, edge-type), T ) is preconfigured and stored in the decoder. Before each iteration, the current unsatisfied check-ratio check-ratiocur is calculated according to the specific edge-type. Then check-ratiocur and edge-type serve as the indices of the LUT for the decoder to attain the current quantization interval Tcur of CNU’s input. After Tcur is obtained, wL , T0, and Tcur are send to QCM 1 to convert VNU’s high bit-width output LLRvH→c into CNU’s low bit-width input LLRvL→c. Concurrently, wH , T0, and Tcur are send to QCM 2. After the CNU’s process is done, CNU’s low bit-width output LLRLc→v are converted into VNU’s high bit-width input LLRHc→v. The check-ratiocur is initialized to 0.5 before the start of decoding process. The above quantization conversion process is illustrated in Fig. 4.
IV. ADAPTIVE QUANTIZED AND NORMALIZED MIN-SUM ALGORITHM FOR MET LDPC CODES To further improve the decoding performance and maintain the robustness, we combine the proposed adaptive asymmetric quantization strategy with adaptive normalization for MET LDPC codes, named adaptive quantized and normalized min-sum algorithm (AQNMSA). Since we utilize LUT based on check-ratio and edge-type for quantization parameters, it is also naturally to apply normalization factor based on these two indices. In this instance, besides the quantization parameters LUT, the normalization factor LUT ((check-ratio, edge-type), α) also needs to be preconfigured and stored in the decoder. Since the above two tables could use the same index, they can be stored in one table named quantization and normalization parameters LUT ((check-ratio, edge-type), (T , α)).
A. QUANTIZATION CONVERSION AND NORMALIZATION As the normalization operation is carried out directly after the CNU’s operation, the quantization conversion module QCM 2 can be modified accordingly to include the normalization operation. Thus, we propose the quantization conversion and normalization module (QCNM), whose function is to multiply a normalization factor α to a LLR with bit-width w1 and quantization interval T1, and then transform it into a LLR with bit-width w2 and quantization interval T2. We define function QCNM [w2, T1, T2, α](·), and it can be described by the predefined quantization function QUAN (·),

V. LOOK-UP TABLE DESIGN ALGORITHM BASED ON MODIFIED MULTI-EDGE TYPE DENSITY EVOLUTION We propose a LUT design algorithm based on modified MET-DE for AQNMSA. Based on the channel model, input LUT, and input signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) SNRcur , modified MET-DE could provide the accurate prediction of AQNMSA’s decoding performance, i.e., the output bit error ratio (BER) BERcur . As previously mentioned, thanks to the fast and accurate analysis process of modified MET-DE, we are able to tell the subtle difference in the decoding performance between AQNMSA with two very close LUTs.

The proposed LUT design algorithm is shown in Alg. 1, in which modified MET-DE is denoted as mMETDE. The function AdjustLUT(·) receives a LUT and adjust each parameter, i.e., increasing or decreasing each parameter by one unit. Then it check and adjust the obtained LUTs to retain the monotonicity of the adjusted LUTs, i.e., to ensure that T and α decrease monotonically as the check-ratio increases. It returns a candidate LUT set which consists of all the LUT adjustment. The function POP(·) receives a set, chooses an element and deletes it from the received set, and returns the chosen element.
The target LUT is referred to as targetLUT , and the candidate LUT is referred to as candidate. In the initialization step, targetLUT is generated by check-ratio bit-width W , quantization intervals bit-width wT , and normalization factor bitwidth wα. The {SNRcur , BERcur } located in waterfall region generally means that the BERcur lies in (10−6, 10−2). To finetune SNRcur means that if {SNRcur , BERcur } lies to the left of waterfall region, then SNRcur is scaled up by a small amount, otherwise it is scaled down.
Since we utilize modified MET-DE as the asymptotic performance prediction tool, the computation complexity is significantly reduced compared to Monte Carlo simulation, and the precision of decoding performance results is improved as well. Based on modified MET-DE, the proposed parameter design algorithm provides a low-complexity and efficient approach to attaining the final LUT with excellent threshold performance.

VII. CONCLUSION In this paper, we first introduce modified MET-DE, which could provide accurate asymptotic performance prediction results for ANMSA and quantized ANMSA. Then we propose adaptive asymmetric quantization strategy based on check-ratio and edge-type for typical MET LDPC codes, which is independent of iteration number and thus compatible with multiple code rates.
Combining the proposed quantization strategy with adaptive normalization technique, we further propose AQNMSA for typical MET LDPC codes, which significantly alleviates

the performance degradation of fixed quantized ANMSA. Concurrently, we propose a LUT design algorithm for AQNMSA based on the proposed modified MET-DE tool, which could attain good design results with low design complexity. Finally, we apply AQNMSA to 5 code rates of 5G-NR BG1 LDPC codes under BI-AWGN channel, and observe a superior threshold performance and lower error floor compared to float-point NMSA with optimal normalization factor. We also illustrates that AQNMSA shows stronger robustness to SNR estimation error comparable with SPA.
Although we have shown that AQNMSA could achieve good decoding performance with very low message passing bit-width of 4-bit, the proposed quantization strategy can be combined with other min-sum algorithms, and the quantization bit-width (wH , wL ) could be customized. Therefore, a tradeoff exists between the quantization bit-width and decoding threshold performance, and it will be studied in our future work.
In conclusion, AQNMSA shows good decoding performance and universality to multiple code rates with low quantization bit-width, which indicates that it could achieve good balance between decoding performance and implementation complexity, and has the potential to be widely utilized in scenarios with low implementation complexity and high data throughput demands.
