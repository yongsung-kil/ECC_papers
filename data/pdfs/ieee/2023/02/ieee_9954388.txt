Model-Driven Deep Learning ADMM Decoder for Irregular Binary LDPC Codes

Xiaomeng Guo , Member, IEEE, Tsung-Hui Chang , Senior Member, IEEE, and Yongchao Wang , Senior Member, IEEE

AbstractвҖ” In this letter, a model-driven deep learning (DL) decoder for irregular binary low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes is proposed via the alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) technique. Our technical contributions three twofold: 1) we formulate the maximum likelihood decoding problem as a non-convex quadratic program with a new penalty term and present an ADMM based decoder; 2) to alleviate hyper-parameter tuning, we employ the deep unfolding strategy to derive a model-driven ADMM-DL decoder; and 3) to save the number of learning parameters, we propose an initialization strategy which can apply to different codeword structures. Specifically, the designing procedure for the DL network structure and DL network parameters training algorithm are presented in detail for efficient implementation. Numerical results demonstrate that the proposed model-driven ADMM-DL decoder for irregular binary LDPC codes is competitive in comparison with the state-of-the-arts.
Index TermsвҖ” Model-driven, deep learning (DL), ADMM, LDPC codes, unfolding.
I. INTRODUCTION
I RREGULAR low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are used widely in modern wireless communication systems, such as LANs, WiMax, and DVB-S2 [1]. The most popular decoders for irregular LDPC codes are based on the belief propagation (BP) algorithm or its variants [2]. There are two concerns on BP-like decoders: one is so-called error-floor effect in the high SNR regime and the other is the intractability of analyzing their decoding performance in theory [3], [4].
In recent years, linear programming (LP) techniques are exploited for LDPC decoding, which attracts a lot of attention from researchers in the coding/decoding community. Unlike BP decoders which compute marginal probability, LP decoders are designed to obtain information bits by solving the relaxed maximum-likelihood (ML) decoding problem, which usually suffers from a high computational complexity. To tackle this issue, alternating direction method of multipliers (ADMM) techniques have been investigated and the

Generally, the ADMM-based decoder can be applied to both regular and irregular LDPC codes. Recent research works [8], [9] show that the error-correction performance of the ADMM-based decoders with proper chosen hyper-parameters can be significantly improved, especially for irregular LDPC codes. However, tuning hyper-parameter and evaluating their quality is time-consuming and computationally expensive.
Recently, to enable efficient determination of hyperparameters in the optimization model, some researchers exploit the iterative structure of the solving algorithm to construct the so-called model-driven deep learning (DL) network and design the corresponding learning algorithm [10], [11]. A combination of the unfolding and the ADMM method is proposed and applied to compress sensing in [10]. The authors in [11] also employ the unfolding technique to decode LDPC codes. However their approach is limited by the code length and doesnвҖҷt take advantages of the codeword structure. Inspired by these works, in this letter, we propose a model-driven ADMM-DL decoder for irregular LDPC codes. Specifically, the procedures of constructing the DL network and designing the parameters learning algorithm are presented. Furthermore, a model-driven initialization strategy based on the codeword structure is obtained to save the learning parameters. Simulation results demonstrate its effectiveness in comparison with state-of-the-art LDPC decoders.

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we have proposed a model-driven DL decoder
for irregular binary LDPC codes via the ADMM technique. Specifically, the ML decoding problem for irregular binary
LDPC codes is formulated as a non-convex quadratic program
with a new penalty factor vector. Moreover, the deep unfolding strategy has been applied to transform the ADMM iteration
algorithm to a structured deep-unfolding network. The corresponding network parameters training algorithm has been
delineated for efficient implementation. An initialization strat-
egy applied to different codeword structures is also proposed to reduce the number of the learning parameters. Simulation
results demonstrate its effectiveness in comparison with the
state-of-the-art LDPC decoders.
