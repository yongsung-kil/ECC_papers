Abstract— In this paper, we propose an improved Low-
Density Parity-Check (LDPC) code design scheme based on the 
existing genetic optimization-based LDPC code design scheme 
proposed in [1]. In particular, we perform the removal of the 
girth-4 property of the parity check matrix (H-matrix) and 
utilize the min-sum decoding algorithm instead of the Belief 
Propagation (BP) algorithm in order to enhance the 
performance of the LDPC code. Furthermore, an (32,64) LDPC 
code is considered in this paper. Finally, we evaluate the block 
error rate (BLER) of the LDPC code over white Gaussian noise 
channels. By means of evaluation results using Matlab, we 
indicate that our proposed approach can achieve a gain of more 
than 11% in terms of BLER compared to the existing schemes 
without significantly increasing the complexity of the decoding 
scheme. 
Keywords—LDPC, genetic algorithm, short-length block code, 
internet of things.

Our proposed scheme has two improvements compared 
to the scheme in [1]. First, we added the "Detection and 
Removal of Girth 4 of Parity Check Matrixes" block, which 
plays a role of a penalty function, eliminating worse fitness 
cases (in this case, identifying and removing cycle 4 in each 
matrix H), to save time and cost of the optimization process. 
Second, for the LDPC decoding algorithm, we apply the 
Min-sum decoding algorithm instead of the BP decoding 
algorithm in [1] to improve decoding performance. 
 
According to [18], in the parity check matrix H, the 
existence of a square with 4 sides of 1s is called Girth 4. As 
abovementioned, Girth 4 reduces the decoding efficiency of 
LPDC codes. Therefore, identifying and removing the Girth 
4 in the parity check matrix before encoding and decoding is 
necessary. To eliminate the Girth 4 in the parity check 
matrix, we remove the square with 4 sides of 1s by 
reorganizing some components of the square while 
maintaining the remaining components of the matrix. The 
"Detection and Removal of Girth 4 of Parity Check 
Matrices" block is detailed in Fig. 4. To be specific, the steps 
can be explained as follows: (1) determine the occurrence of 
Girth 4 in the matrix (Detection of Girth 4); (2) if “yes”, the 
Girth 4 will be removed; (3) otherwise, the matrix H will be 
transmitted without going through the Girth 4 removal step.

V. CONCLUSION 
In this paper, we proposed an improved LDPC code 
design scheme based on the existing genetic optimization-
based LDPC code design scheme proposed in [1]. Our 
method involves algorithmic modifications to enhance the 
system performance in terms of BLER for LDPC code. 
Specifically, we removed the girth-4 of the H-matrix and 
utilized the min-sum decoding algorithm instead of the BP 
decoding algorithm in [1]. Simulation results in Matlab 
indicated that our proposed LDPC code design scheme 
could obtain an improvement on the block error rate of more 
than 11% when compared to the original scheme in [1]. 
Moreover, our proposed scheme also demonstrated its 
effectiveness while reducing the LDPC matrix size down to 
32 × 64 . 
Finally, 
with 
4-byte 
information-length 
effectiveness transmission, our LDPC code design scheme 
shows potential use in IoT applications or other short-block 
transmission applications. 
As part of future work, we intend to focus on evaluating 
the performance of the proposed scheme over different 
channels such as Rayleigh and Rician with the presence of 
burst errors, and the applications of the proposed LDPC 
code design scheme in IoT.
