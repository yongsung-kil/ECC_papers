Abstract-This study introduces a construction method for QC-LDPC codes, ensuring their absence of four-cycles by utilizing Whiteman's generalized cyclotomic (GC) classes. An analysis of the cycle structure within the Tanner graph for the proposed codes, specifically focusing on cycles with length six, is conducted. Moreover, this paper presents the necessary and sufficient conditions for generating GC-QC-LDPC codes with a minimum girth of eight, based on the analysis. Simulation results demonstrate the superior bit error rate (BER) performance of the constructed QC-LDPC codes over the AWGN channel. Compared to other QC-LDPC codes, QC-LDPC codes constructed using this method have a reduced base matrix size for long code lengths. This attribute augments their suitability for integration within storage systems.
Index Terms-QC-LDPC code, Whiteman's generalized cyclotomy, girth, Tanner graph
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes are renowned for their remarkable capacity to approach the Shannon limit, making them widely applied in various domains, including 802.1 ln, DVB-S2, optical communication system ,cryptography and storage system [1]-[3]. Furthermore, LDPC codes have attracted considerable attention from researchers and remain a vibrant and active area of research to this day [4], [5]. These codes can be categorized into two types based on their parity-check matrix generation methods. The first type is random LDPC codes, obtained through computer search as mentioned in [6]. The second type is structured LDPC codes, constructed using mathematical tools and techniques cited in references such as [7]-[12]. The structured LDPC codes, particularly quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes, have found widespread practical applications due to their lower implementation complexity and superior error-correcting capabilities. Specifically, the BER performance of an LDPC code is strongly influenced by the girth of its corresponding Tanner graph [13]. As a result, it becomes imperative to avoid the occurrence of short cycles, especially four-cycles, within the parity-check matrix of an LDPC code. In this paper, the

Currently, many research studies in constructing QC-LDPC codes are founded on CPMs (Cyclic Permutation Matrices). In [14], the essential and comprehensive criteria for establishing QC-LDPC codes with specific girths are derived. Xiao et al. [7] utilized Reed-Solomon (RS) codes to construct three different varieties of QC-LDPC codes. In [8], structured QC-LDPC codes are constructed using cyclotomic cosets. Other related investigations can also be found in [9].
This paper presents an innovative approach to constructing four-cycle-free QC-LDPC codes by leveraging Whiteman's generalized cyclotomy (GC). Through the utilization of Whiteman's generalized cyclotornic cosets, the CPM has a larger size, resulting in a more "sparse" parity-check matrix. Additionally, by analyzing the cycle structure in the Tanner graph, six-cycle free GC-QC-LDPC codes are also constructed. A large CPM size implies the potential to construct longer structured QC-LDPC code with a sparser parity-check matrix. This characteristic enhances their applicability within storage systems.
The remainder of this paper is structured as follows. Section II presents relevant definitions and preliminary concepts. Basic constructions of four-cycle free GC-QC-LDPC codes are introduced in Section III. Section IV analyzes the cycle structure of length six in the underlying Tanner graph, and based on this analysis, GC-QC-LDPC codes with girth at least eight are constructed. In conclusion, Section V provides some remarks, summarizing key findings and contributions of this paper.

V. CONCLUSION
This study introduces an innovative approach to construct QC-LDPC codes that are free of four-cycles by using Whiteman's generalized cyclotomy. The larger size of the CPM in our method leads to a more "sparse" parity-check matrix, resulting in enhanced performance and a reduced base matrix size. This characteristic enhances their applicability within storage systems. Furthermore, The cycle structure of length six in the Tanner graphs for the proposed codes is analyzed, providing necessary and sufficient conditions are provided for constructing six-cycle free GC-QC-LDPC codes. The approach introduced in this paper for QC-LDPC code construction can be expanded to other generalized cyclotornic classes.
