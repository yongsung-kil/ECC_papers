A Study on Accelerating SP Decoding by Neural Network in SMR System
Madoka Nishikawa 1, Yasuaki Nakamura 1, Yasushi Kanai 2, and Yoshihiro Okamoto 1
1Graduate School of Science and Engineering, Ehime University, Matsuyama 790-8577, Japan 2Department of Engineering, Niigata Institute of Technology, Kashiwazaki 945-1195, Japan

We have previously studied low-density parity-check (LDPC) coding and iterative decoding in the shingled magnetic recording (SMR) system as a signal processing method to realize ultrahigh-density hard disk drives (HDD). In addition, we have proposed the application of the neural network (NN) to improve decoding performance and realize the automation of iterative decoding. In this study, we apply an NN to accelerate iterative decoding in the sum-product (SP) decoder. As the result, the SP decoder with the NN realized “no errors” at the fewest times of the iterative decoding compared to our previous studies.
Index Terms— Iterative decoding, low-density parity-check (LDPC) code, neural network (NN), shingled magnetic recording (SMR).

I. INTRODUCTION
T HE promotion of digital transformation (DX) on a global scale requires the rapid addition of new data centers and cloud storage. Therefore, hard disk drives (HDDs) are further expected as storage devices to realize large capacity.
We have previously studied the low-density parity-check (LDPC) coding and iterative decoding system in the shingled magnetic recording (SMR) system [1] to realize ultrahighdensity HDD. We have also proposed a log-likelihood ratio (LLR) modulator using a neural network (NN) and realized efficient iterative decoding [2], [3], [4]. However, the problem with [2], [3], and [4] is that the computational efficiency becomes complicated due to the structure of the NN and the large number of iterative decoding.
In this study, we apply an NN to accelerate iterative decoding in the sum-product (SP) decoder as a new approach for iterative decoding and evaluate the performance of the new SP decoder in the iterative decoding system by computer simulation.

II. READ/WRITE (R/W) SYSTEM

Fig. 1 shows the R/W system. The input sequence is encoded by a 128/130 (0, 16/8) run-length limited (RLL) [5] encoder and a (30, 3)-regular LDPC encoder [6]. After that, the sequence is recorded on a granular medium consisting of the discrete Voronoi diagram [7]. In this study, we assume the areal recording density of 4 Tbit/in2. Fig. 2 shows the relationship between the granular medium, the writer, and the array head with three readers on three SMR tracks. III. SP DECODER USING NN
Fig. 3 shows the block diagram of the SP decoder using an NN. In this study, we use the parity check matrix with a code length of 4096 bytes, a column weight of 3, and a row weight of 30. Unlike the parity check matrix created by Gallager’s construction method [6], it does not have a run of “1” in not only the second and third sub-matrices but also the first submatrix to reduce the correlation due to the partial response (PR) channel. In the figure, the blocks for the first sub-matrix are shown in yellow, those for the second sub-matrix are shown in green, and those for the third sub-matrix are shown in pink, respectively. Also, the blocks in the blue gray area are those for the iterative decoding in the SP decoder.

V. CONCLUSION
In this study, we have applied an NN to accelerate iterative decoding in the SP decoder. Also, we have evaluated the iterative decoding performance in the SP decoder with and without NN by computer simulation. As the result, the SP decoder with the NN achieved no errors at SNRS = 21.5 dB, isp = 7, and iglobal = 13. Furthermore, it is clarified that the SP decoder with the NN realized accelerating iterative decoding in the SP decoder.
