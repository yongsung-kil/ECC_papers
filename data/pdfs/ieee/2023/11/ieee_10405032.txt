RIS-Assisted Coded Relay Cooperation Based on LDPC Product Codes with Finite Code Length
Jin Wang∗, Shunwai Zhang∗, Zhonghui Mei∗, Hao Cheng†, Rongfang Song∗ ∗College of Telecommunications and Information Engineering,

Abstract—Reconfigurable intelligent surfaces (RIS) is a kind of metamaterial with the ability to regulate and control wireless channels, which can provide additional links for wireless communication systems. Meanwhile, it has low hardware cost, low energy consumption, flexible deployment, and intelligent reconfiguration merits. By combining the RIS technology with coded relay cooperation, we establish the RIS-assisted coded relay cooperation based on low density parity check (LDPC) product codes, and further propose the LDPC product code encoding method and efficient joint iterative decoding algorithm. Then, we theoretically derive the approximate solutions of the outage probability and the channel capacity with finite code length. Theoretical analysis and simulation results show that the proposed system obviously outperforms the traditional coded relay cooperation, and the more number of RIS elements, the more significant advantage can be obtained. Simulation results also demonstrate that the channel capacity with finite code length approaches the ideal channel capacity with the code length of LDPC product codes increasing.
Index Terms—Reconfigurable intelligent surface, LDPC product code, coded relay cooperation, outage probability, channel capacity with finite code length.
I. INTRODUCTION
With the rapid development of metamaterial technology, reconfigurable intelligent surfaces (RIS) [1], a cutting-edge technology that has the potential to regulate and control wireless channels, has attracted extensive attention from the wireless communications field. The authors of [2] statistically characterized the cascaded fading channels of RIS-assisted wireless communication systems under Rayleigh fading, and derived the exact theoretical symbol error rate (SER) and closed form solution of outage probability. Reference [3] analyzed the coverage of RIS-assisted system under Rayleigh fading. In addition, compared to both direct-link and relayassisted wireless systems, the RIS-assisted wireless system can obtain greater coverage distance. In [4], the authors investigated the impact of RIS location on the coverage of cellular network, where the cell coverage was further maximized by

The relay cooperation technology [5, 6] was inspired by the multiple input multiple output (MIMO) technology on the basis of the relay channel and the multiple access channel. Specifically, single antenna users share each other’s antennas through cooperation to form a virtual MIMO system, achieve space diversity gain, and improve the performance of the entire system. Unlike existing traditional relay cooperation technologies, RIS can improve wireless communication quality without additional transmission power and complex signal processing.
Though RIS shares many similarities with relay, they have fundamental differences and may complement each other. In [7], the authors showed that compared to amplify and forward (AF) relay systems, RIS-assisted wireless systems can greatly improve energy efficiency. Reference [8] compared RIS technology with classic decode and forward (DF) relay systems, and the results showed that the performance of small-scale RIS-assisted systems is inferior to DF relay systems. However, with the number of RIS units increasing, the performance of RIS-assisted systems can gradually outperform DF relay systems. In [9], the authors proposed a new hybrid relay and RIS auxiliary system based on relay and RIS technology, and proved that for practical situations with limited transmission power and limited number of reflection units, the performance of RIS systems can be significantly improved by using a simple DF relay.
Low density parity check (LDPC) code is an efficient channel coding technology with performance approaching Shannon limit, which can support high throughput and low delay communication[10]. In [11], the authors combined RIS technology with LDPC code to study the performance of RIS-assisted wireless systems with LDPC-coded signals, and derived the analytical upper and lower bounds on the bit error probability of the system. Besides, the authors of [12] proposed an iterative LDPC product code, whose performance is better than that of a simple LDPC product code in both random error and burst error scenarios.

II. SYSTEM MODEL
The RIS-assisted coded relay cooperation based on LDPC product codes is shown in Fig. 1. It consists of a source(S) with single antenna, a relay (R) with single antenna, a RIS (I) with M reflection units, and a destination (D) with single antenna. The direct link between S and D is assumed to be blocked due to unfavorable obstacle, and the same applies to the link between RIS and R. Assuming that R operates in halfduplex (HD) mode and can decode the signals perfectly. In the first time slot, R receives the direct signal sent by S, and D receives the transmission signal reflected by the RIS. In the second time slot, D receives the direct signal sent by R.
Assuming that the channel between S and the mth reflection unit of RIS is hS(mI ) = hS(mI ) e , −jϕ(SmI ) where hS(mI ) and ϕS(mI ) represent the amplitude and phase of channel coefficients hS(mI ), respectively. Similarly, the reflection channel between the mth reflection unit of RIS and D is hI(mD) = hI(mD) e , −jϕI(m D) the channel between S and R is hSR = |hSR| e−jϕSR , and the channel between R and D is hRD = |hRD| e−jϕRD . All channels are assumed to be independent, identically distributed (i.i.d), and their envelops follow Rayleigh distribution, i,e., we have hS(mI ), hI(mD), hSR, hRD∼ CN (0, 1).

Fig. 4 shows the BER performance of the proposed system and the traditional relay cooperation system (D receives direct signals from S and forward signals from R) [16] with M =6, 15, 24 and itermax=5. It is shown that the BER decreases as the number of RIS reflecting units M grows. When the number of RIS reflection units is small, the BER performance of the proposed system is similar to that of the traditional relay cooperation system, and with the increase of the number of RIS reflection units, the performance of the proposed system is significantly better than the traditional relay cooperation system. This is mainly because with the number of RIS reflection units increasing, the system provides more additional links and more space diversity gain can be obtain, which significantly improves the BER performance.
Fig. 5 shows the BER performance of the proposed system with M =24 and different joint decoding iterations. It can be

seen that the BER decreases with the joint decoding iterations increasing, and the joint iterative decoding algorithm can quickly converge. It almost converges when the number of iterations is 10, It can be attributed to the fact that one joint decoding iteration actually completes one row decoding and one column decoding in the proposed algorithm. Hence, the extrinsic information is exchanged sufficiently and more coding gain can be achieved.
Fig. 6 depicts the outage probabilities obtained by theoretical analysis and simulations with the equivalent code rate r=1/3. It is observed that the theoretical analysis value of outage probability is consistent with the simulation results, and the outage probability decreases as M increases. This indicates that with a given outage probability requirement, we can reduce the transmission power by increasing M , which further improves the system energy efficiency.

VI. CONCLUSION
Based on RIS technology and the coded relay cooperation, this paper established a RIS-assisted coded relay cooperation system based on LDPC product codes, and proposed an efficient joint iterative decoding algorithm. We further derived the approximate theoretical solutions of the outage probability and the channel capacity with finite code length. The superiority of the proposed system was verified by simulations from the viewpoints of BER, outage probability, and channel capacity. The results demonstrated that when the number of RIS reflection units reaches a certain level, the performance of the proposed system is better than that of the traditional relay cooperation system, and as the number of RIS reflection units increases, the advantage becomes more obvious. Simulation results also showed that with the increase of LDPC product code length, the channel capacity of the proposed system with finite code length gradually approaches the ideal capacity. Designing LDPC product codes used in the proposed system to further improve the coding gain will be one of our next research directions.
