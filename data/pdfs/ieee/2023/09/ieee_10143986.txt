High Throughput and Hardware Efficient Hybrid LDPC Decoder Using Bit-Serial Stochastic Updating
Shuai Hu , Kaining Han , Member, IEEE, Yubin Zhu , Guodong Shen, Fujie Wang, and Jianhao Hu , Member, IEEE

Abstract— Hybrid low-density parity-check (LDPC) decoding combines conventional Belief-Propagation (BP) algorithm with stochastic decoding to achieve high performance and low complexity simultaneously. However, lossy and inefficient stochasticto-binary (S2B) conversion brings extra performance degradation and decoding latency. In this paper, a bit-serial stochastic updating based hybrid decoding (BSSU-HD) is proposed, which employs fully correlated stochastic (FCS) check nodes (CNs) and probability tracers assisted variable nodes (VNs) to accomplish accurate and efficient S2B conversion. Two strategies, including random source selection and tracing speed switching, are proposed to further improve performance and convergence. A BSSU LDPC decoder for IEEE 802.3an is designed in a 65-nm CMOS process, which occupies 4.6 mm2 silicon area and achieves a throughput of 200.8 Gb/s at Eb/N0 = 4.4 dB with 500 MHz clock frequency from a 1.2 V supply voltage. The power and energy efficiency are 2.933 W and 14.61 pJ/bit, respectively. To the best of our known, it achieves the best decoding performance, the highest throughput and hardware efficiency among state-of-the-art IEEE 802.3an LDPC decoders. We also verify that the BSSU-HD can achieve better performance for multi-rate 5th generation (5G) New Ratio (NR) LDPC codes than conventional algorithm, which greatly extends the application of the stochastic decoding.
Index Terms— LDPC code, probability tracer, fully correlated stochastic, min-sum algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
LOW density parity-check (LDPC) codes are a kind of linear block code which are discovered to achieve near-capacity decoding performance when Belief-Propagation (BP) algorithm is adopted [1], [2]. The BP algorithm is an iterative decoding algorithm [3], which iteratively exchanges and updates belief probability messages between Variable Nodes (VNs) and Check Nodes (CNs). Due to the parallel nature of the BP algorithm, LDPC decoders have the potential to provide high-throughput decoding. Thus, LDPC codes have been adopted in many communication standards, such as the

IEEE 802.3an and the 5th generation (5G) New Radio (NR) standard.
The main implementation challenge of the BP-based LDPC decoder comes from the extremely high hardware consumption of the complex updating functions in CNs and VNs. The Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA) [4], and its approximation, Min-Sum Algorithm (MSA) [5], performed in log-likelihood domain (LLR) have relative simple VNs, but the CN updating function of MSA requires to search second minimum value and brings significant critical path and storage resources, which limits the throughput and hardware efficiency of LDPC decoder. Some literature is devoted to reducing the complexity cost of CN in MSA. In [6], all CNs were divided into multiple groups and second minimum values within each group replaced the global second minimum values. This method not only reduces the hardware resource cost but also reduces the wiring pressure. However, it causes significant performance loss. In [7], an approximate method for finding the second minimum value was proposed by adding a fixed constant to the minimum value to approximate the second minimum value, but it also cause significant performance loss. In [8], a Normalized Probabilistic Min-Sum Algorithm (NPMSA) was proposed, which randomly selects the second minimum value with a certain probability, resulting in relatively small performance loss and low complexity.
Stochastic decoding [9], inspired by stochastic computing [10], is an attractive alternative approach to provide low complexity decoding. In stochastic LDPC decoding, the probability messages are implicitly expressed by stochastic bits. The VN and CN functions in original BP algorithm can be realized by extremely simple logic gate circuits. Stochastic decoding also alleviates the routing problem among CNs and VNs since only one bit is exchanged in each interconnection. However, early stochastic decoding algorithms suffer from the latch-up problem and are hence unsuccessful to be applied to practical LDPC codes [9]. This issue can be alleviated by using re-randomization units, such as edge memory (EM) [11] and tracking forecast memory (TFM) [12], to the output of the stochastic VNs. To save the hardware consumption caused by re-randomization units, a Delayed Stochastic(DS) decoder is proposed to eliminate probability tracers [13], but results in big performance loss. In [14], a Check-to-Variable (C2V) based stochastic decoding is proposed which directly uses the C2V messages to update the re-randomization units to save

the critical path and area consumption. With the strategies introduced in [15] to lower down the decoding cycles, the author in [16] analyses the impact of input grouping, which achieves relative high performance and fast convergence. However, due to the inaccurate stochastic CNs and VNs, existing stochastic decoding algorithms still suffer from performance loss and slow convergence compared with conventional decoding algorithms. Besides, the stochastic VNs will become too inaccurate to converge for low-degree VNs, such as for low rate 5G NR LDPC codes. It is also not suitable for partially parallel designs, such as layered decoding [17] and multi-length and multi-rate compatibility [18], which limits the wide application of stochastic decoding.
Recently, hybrid decoding algorithms are introduced to achieve high performance and low complexity simultaneously by combining conventional VNs with stochastic CNs [19], [20]. The Relax Half-Stochastic (RHS) decoding transforms stochastic messages in probability domain to LLR domain through piece-wise linear approximation [19]. This transformation requires several cycles in each decoding iteration to guarantee enough precision due to huge dynamic range difference between two calculation domains. To avoid calculation domain transformation, we propose the fully correlated stochastic (FCS) CN in [20] to directly realize MSA CN updating in LLR domain with stochastic messages. However, the stochastic-to-binary (S2B) conversion in [20] is based on bit streams, which also requires several cycles in one iteration. These lossy and inefficient S2B conversions result in extra decoding latency and performance loss, which limits the throughput and hardware efficiency of the hybrid LDPC decoders.
In this paper, we propose a bit-serial stochastic updating based hybrid decoding (BSSU-HD) to achieve high performance, fast convergence and low complexity LDPC decoding at the same time. The FCS CN is employed to provide more accurate CN updating than conventional stochastic CN and to avoid precision loss caused by calculation domain transformation. Then, the successive relaxation (SR) [21] based probability tracers (PTs) are introduced to efficiently accomplish S2B conversion of C2V messages with only one cycle in each decoding iteration. We further analyze how to simultaneously improve the tracing speed and accuracy of the probability tracer. Based on this, two efficient techniques, including random source selection and tracing speed switching, are proposed to speed up convergence and improve decoding performance of BSSU-HD.
To demonstrate the proposed algorithm, we apply it to decode the LDPC codes from the IEEE 802.3an and the 5G NR standard. The BSSU-HD can achieve the best performance for IEEE 802.3an (2048,1723) LDPC code among existing stochastic LDPC decoding algorithms, which is very close to conventional float point Normalize Min-Sum (NMS) algorithm [22]. We also verify that BSSU-HD works well with multi-rate 5G NR LDPC codes. For low rate 5G NR LDPC codes, the BSSU-HD even achieves much better decoding performance than float point NMS benefiting from more frequent and fine-grained message exchanging in BSSU-HD. A. Conventional BP-Based LDPC Decoding

The CN cm corresponds to the m-th row of H and VN vn corresponds to the n-th column. Then each ’1’ located in (m, n) in H represents an interconnection between cm and vn. The LDPC code can be decoded on this graph structure using BP-based algorithms, which iteratively exchange and update messages between CNs and VNs. B. Preliminaries of Stochastic LDPC Decoding
Stochastic computing (SC) was firstly introduced in the 1960s as a novel numerical representation method. The numbers in SC are represented by stochastic bit streams, called stochastic numbers (SNs), with their expectations equal to the value of them. Typically, a stochastic number X is modeled as a Bernoulli sequence with each bit in it having a probability pX to be one. Then X can be generated by comparing pX with a random number sequence (RNS) generated from uniform distribution in [0,1], as shown in Fig. 2. Although an SN can only explicitly represent the value in [0,1], the real representation range can be extended by changing the range of the RNS. For example, when the RNS is generated from [0,Lcap], then the generated SN with pX of bit ’1’ actually represents the value pX · Lcap. The representation range of the SN can also be extended to [−Lcap,Lcap] by adding a sign bit with it.
In SC, some complex calculation operations, such as a multiplication, can be realized by simple logic gate circuits, such as an AND gate, under the condition that the SNs are independent from each other, as shown in Fig. 3(a). The representation precision of an SN is determined by the variance of it, that is, how fast it converges to its expectation. Then the calculation precision of an SC unit is determined by both the expectation and the variance of the output SN. Although some special random number sequences, such as Sobol sequence, can be used to speed up the convergence and thus improve the representation precision of an SN [24], they usually have trouble satisfying the independent condition and lead to incorrect expectation of the output. On the other hand, the correlation can also be utilized to design SC units with specific functions [25]. For example, when the input SNs are fully correlated, an AND gate will behave as a minimizer because for all bit ’1’s in a small SN, there must be bit ’1’s in a bigger SN, as shown in Fig. 3(b).

A. Overall Architecture of BSSU-HD
The block diagram of the BSSU-HD, including a pair of neighboring CN unit (CNU) and VN unit (VNU), is shown in Fig. 7. The messages from other nodes are omitted for the sake of simplicity. The BSSU-HD is based on the MSA and its enhancements. The VNU includes a conventional MSA VN and its corresponding probability tracers and SNGs, which are responsible for S2B and Binary-to-Stochastic (B2S) conversions, respectively. The CNU employs the FCS CN to realize MSA CN updating with stochastic messages. Since the messages in BSSU-HD are all in LLR domain, a sign bit and a stochastic bit are jointly used as a stochastic message to represent an LLR value. Only stochastic messages are exchanged in each interconnection between CNU and VNU to achieve low routing resource. In one decoding iteration, the FCS CN receives the stochastic messages from its neighboring VNs and realizes the CN updating of the MSA, which will be described in section III-B. Then the probability tracers convert the stochastic C2V messages to binary representation, which will be described in section III-C. The updated V2C messages are then converted to stochastic messages in stochastic number generators (SNGs), and the next decoding iteration begins. Only one cycle is required in each decoding iteration due to efficient and accurate S2B conversion schemes, which will be detailed in section III-D.

The hybrid stochastic (HS) decoding in [20] also employs FCS CNs to achieve more accurate CN updating. The precision of the messages is determined by the stream length in each decoding iteration. So it requires several cycles in one iteration to convert stochastic bit streams to binary numbers, which seriously increases the decoding latency and decreases the message exchanging efficiency. While in BSSU-HD, we introduce the probability tracer to trace the C2V messages in each decoding cycle, which results in high throughput. Besides, the successive relaxation based probability tracer can significantly improve the message exchanging efficiency, which brings performance gain with respect to conventional algorithms [21].

D. Improved Probability Tracer

The performance of the probability tracers has a significant influence on the decoding performance and convergence of the BSSU-HD. For the successive relaxation based probability tracers, the value of the relaxation factor α determines the tracing speed and the accuracy. As the value of α increase, the probability tracer converges more faster toward the traced value, but after convergence, it will suffer from more fluctuations, which makes a trade-off between tracing speed and accuracy. Generally, relatively small α (2−4 in [12]) is selected in conventional stochastic LDPC decoding to avoid error propagation caused by inaccurate updating, which also leads to slow convergence. While in BSSU-HD, since both FCS CN and MSA VN are more accurate than conventional stochastic CN and VN, the larger α can be used to achieve a faster convergence of the decoding. Besides, we propose two techniques to further improve the speed and the accuracy of the probability tracer, which will be detailed in section III-D.1 and section III-D.2, respectively.
1) Random Source Selection: The probability tracers extract the moving average probability of a stochastic stream based on successive relaxation method. Assuming that the traced probability Qr changes slowly in recent T iterations, and the prior stochastic bits have little influence on the traced probability, then the tracing accuracy and speed are determined by the number of bit ’1’s and their distribution in these T iterations for a constant α. In order to minimize the average error between Qt and Qr for all probability tracers, the number of bit ’1’s in T iterations should be r ound(Qr · T ) and they are uniformly distributed in these T iterations, and this holds true for any T . To achieve this, one possible way is to use Sobol sequence as the RNS. Sobol sequence is a kind of low discrepancy sequence which tries to generate a uniformly distributed sequence. That means, all numbers of a Sobol sequence are uniformly distributed in [0, 1], and the number located in the most sparse part in [0, 1] will be generated in every new cycle. Please see [24] for more details about Sobol sequence. To be noticed, Sobol sequence has trouble satisfying the independent condition required in conventional stochastic decoding, but it has good compatibility with BSSU-HD since all SNGs share the same random source.
Fig. 11 shows the convergence speed and accuracy of a probability tracer with different α and RNSs, characterized by the Mean-Absolute-Error (MAE) between Qt and Qr , which are uniformly generated from [0, 1], As shown in Fig. 11, for the same α, the probability tracer with Sobol sequence converges faster than that with Bernoulli sequence, and it also has a smaller MAE after convergence. As a result, we can use bigger α to achieve faster convergence and maintain acceptable tracing accuracy at the same time, which leads to fast convergence of the BSSU-HD.
2) Tracing Speed Switching: The input LLR value has a range in [− inf, inf] and is usually saturated in range [−Lin f , Lin f ] in practical situation, where the Lin f is properly selected as the smallest value that doesn’t influence the decoding performance to save bit width and improve representation precision of the binary fixed-point numbers. Generally, the C2V and V2C messages should have a similar representation range with the channel inputs, that is, the Lcap should be equal to Lin f , and too small Lcap will lead to decoding failures of some codewords, which results in higher error

floor. On the other hand, considering the tracing accuracy we
discussed in section III-D.1, the MAE of the probability tracers is actually enlarged by Lcap, so we expect smaller Lcap for better tracing accuracy. We address this issue heuristically by
using a tracing speed switching (TSS) strategy. Specifically, for the first few iterations of the BSSU-HD decoding, small Lcap and big α are used to speed up convergence with acceptable tracing accuracy. Then, the Lcap and α are switched to bigger and smaller values, respectively, to figure out the residual
unsuccessfully decoded codes.
Fig. 12 demonstrates the convergence process of BSSU-HD decoding with different values of Lcap and α for a (2048,1723) LDPC code. In low SNR region, such as Eb/N0 = 3.6dB in Fig. 12, the BSSU-HD decoding with relative small Lcap = 4 and big α = 2−2 have much faster convergence, which leads to higher BER performance in limitted decoding iterations. While in high SNR region, such as Eb/N0 = 4.4dB and 4.6dB, although BSSU-HD with small Lcap and big α still has faster convergence in the begining of the decoding process, it suffers an inferior BER performance to bigger Lcap = 8 and smaller α = 2−3. This indicates that a too small Lcap is not enough to correct the residual errors. By apppling TSS strategy, which switches Lcap and α from 4 and 2−2 to 8 and 2−3 at proper iteration, the BSSU-HD can achieve both fast
convergence and low error floor.
Generally, the timing to switch the tracing speed can be
chosen when the decoding convergence speed slows down, such as the 30th and 20th iteration in Figure 12 for Eb/N0 = 4.4dB and 4.6dB, respectively. Different switching timings will have different impact on the final decoding performance
and convergence speed. The optimal TSS strategy can be
obtained by simulating different code at different SNRs. Sim-
ilarly, a more refined multi-segment TSS strategy can also be
developed through simulation to achieve the best convergence
speed and performance, but this will bring higher design costs
and corresponding implementation difficulties. According to
the experimental experience of us, for the multiple tested
LDPC code at different SNRs, using a two-segment TSS
strategy can achieve good enough convergence speed and
performance.

IV. PERFORMANCE COMPARISON
To demonstrate the proposed BSSU-HD, we apply it to decode a (2048,1723) code from IEEE 802.3an standard to compare its decoding performance and convergence with state-of-the-art stochastic LDPC decoding. We also verify the performance of the BSSU-HD in 5G NR LDPC codes with different code length and rate, where conventional stochastic decoding has trouble converging. For all results in this section, the Sobol sequence is used as random source to achieve high performance and fast convergence. The maximum number of decoding cycles is set as 100.
A. The IEEE 802.3an Standard LDPC Code
The (2048,1723) LDPC code from the IEEE 802.3an standard is a regular Reed-Solomon based code with degree6 VNs and degree-32 CNs. The constant and medium size degree of VNs makes it pretty suitable for conventional stochastic decoding. We design a (2048,1723) LDPC decoder based on BSSU-HD to compare its performance and hardware efficiency with existing IEEE 802.3an LDPC decoders. Early termination unit is employed to achieve high throughput. The implementation details about the proposed decoder will be introduced in section V-A. A delayed stochastic (DS) decoder with no re-randomization units [13], a state-of-theart stochastic decoder, called as C2V-based stochastic decoder [14], a hybrid stochastic (HS) decoder [20], and the FP conventional NMS algorithm are included for comparison. The maximum decoding iterations of FP NMS is set as 20, while the maximum decoding cycles for other stochastic decoders can be found in Table II.

B. 5G NR LDPC Code
To verify the decoding performance of the BSSU-HD for LDPC codes with various code length and code rate, we apply it to decode LDPC codes from 5G NR standard with different code rate 0.32, 0.5, and 0.81 and K = 8448. The maximum iteration number of FP NMS and HS decoding is set as 20, where the HS decoding requires 15 cycles in each iteration.
As shown in Fig. 16, for high code rate 0.81, the BSSU-HD and HS decoding achieve a similar BER performance with FP NMS, which is consistent with the results for (2048,1723) code. But with the code rate decreases, the BSSU-HD turns to achieve better BER performance than FP NMS. This is because for low-degree VNs, especially for degree-1 VNs, the updated messages are more possible to be incorrect than high-degree VNs, which brings error propagation in iterative decoding. While in BSSU-HD, only one bit (excluding sign bit) is exchanged and the extrinsic messages are updated gradually in each decoding cycle with SR updating, which improves the message exchanging frequent and accuracy, thus results in a better decoding performance.
V. IMPLEMENTATION
In order to demonstrate the hardware efficiency of the BSSU-HD, we implement a (2048,1723) LDPC decoder in a 65nm CMOS process and compare it with existing IEEE 802.3an LDPC decoders. The details of the BSSU-HD LDPC decoder design are firstly introduced in section V-A, then the implementation results are included and compared in section V-B.
A. Decoder Design
We implement the (2048,1723) decoder in a fully parallel manner. Fig.17 gives the overall block diagram of the proposed decoder. All binary numbers only have integer bits with bit-width identified in Fig.17 except for the values in probability tracers with extra 2 bits fraction. The structure in Fig. 10(b) is applied in variable node units (VNU). The early termination unit receives the hard decision of the codeword from VNUs and performs the parity check to decide whether the decoding should be terminated. Although all VNUs share the same random source so only one random number generator (RNG) is needed, we employ 64 copies of the RNG with each of them responding for 32 VNUs from the consideration of fan-out and the hardware consumption of them is trivial.
We add two pipelines before and after the check node units (CNUs) to cut off the critical path of the whole decoder to achieve a higher clock frequency. Combined with the memories in the probability tracers, the decoder has totally three-stage pipelines. Pipeline interleaving is applied in the

VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose the BSSU-HD, which is a novel kind of stochastic decoding based on fully correlated stochastic computing. Benefiting from more accuracy FCS CNs and probability tracer based MSA VNs than conventional stochastic decoding, the BSSU-HD achieves decoding performance and convergence speed close to or even better than that of conventional NMS decoding with much lower hardware consumption. We also verify that the BSSU-HD can achieve excellent decoding performance for 5G NR LDPC codes with various code length and code rate, which greatly extends the application of the stochastic decoding. The implemented (2048,1723) decoder demonstrates that the BSSU-HD decoder can achieve the highest performance, highest throughput among state-ofthe-art stochastic decoders with he best hardware efficiency. Thus, the BSSU-HD decoder can be used in high throughput, high performance and low complexity applications.
