Superposition Construction of Globally-Coupled
LDPC Codes for MIMO Communication
Jian Fang∗, Baoming Bai∗, Ruimin Yuan∗, Rongchi Xu∗, Xiao Ma†, and Chung-Li Wang‡ ∗State Key Laboratory of ISN, Xidian University, Xi’an 710071, China
†School of Computer Science and Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou 510006, China ‡Alibaba Group, Hangzhou 311121, China E-mail: bmbai@mail.xidian.edu.cn

Abstract—This paper presents a globally-coupled low-density parity-check (GC-LDPC) coding scheme with high throughput for multiple-input and multiple-output transmission. To design rate-compatible length-scalable GC-LDPC codes, a novel superposition construction of GC-LDPC codes is proposed. To reduce the complexity of the two-phase decoding (TPD) method, we propose a modified TPD method based on partial check matrices. Numerical results show that our coding scheme can divide the decoding of a large code into 4 and 5 parallel decodings of short codes and increase throughput by 4 and 5 times at the frame error rate of 10−5 for code rates of 0.49 and 0.66, respectively. Besides, the modified TPD method reduces the global decoding complexity to almost 1/4 and 1/5 of the TPD method at the frame error rate of 10−5 for code rates of 0.49 and 0.66, respectively.
Index Terms—Globally-coupled low-density parity-check code, multiple-input and multiple-output, two-phase iterative decoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
According to the sixth generation (6G) mobile communications vision [1], future 6G networks are expected to provide much higher data rates (up to Tbps), ultra-high reliability, and larger code lengths for integrated space and ground networks. Thus, for physical layer techniques, high parallelism of decoding is required to achieve such high throughput. It is noticed that low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes [2] for 5G new radio have a base matrix with 22 information columns and a maximum lifting size of 384, which can not support such high throughput and large code length. The expansion of the lifting size would make it challenging to design exponent matrices, especially when the exponent matrices need to be compatible with multiple lifting sizes. Therefore, it is necessary to consider new coding schemes and decoding algorithms.
High throughput coding schemes have been widely studied in optical communications. In [3], a block Markov superposition transmission scheme with a coupling structure is proposed, in which multiple quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes are used in parallel to constitute the basic code. The scheme supports parallel encoding and decoding. However, high complexity makes it difficult to implement. In [4], a low-complexity Reed-Solomon-single-parity-check (RS-SPC) product coding scheme is proposed, where RS codes can be decoded in parallel. Unfortunately, the use of simple RS codes with hard-decision decoding would result in limited performance for wireless communications. You et al. [5]

proposed a spatiotemporal 2-D channel coding scheme for multiple-input-multiple-output (MIMO) wireless systems in an attempt to achieve low latency and high-reliability transmission, which has a product-like coding structure. Inspired by the work in [5], we consider the globally-coupled LDPC (GC-LDPC) codes [6] for high throughput transmission. A GC-LDPC code is characterized by local and global decoding properties, similar to bilayer LDPC ensembles [7], [8]. The disjoint local codes support parallel decoding and can be well incorporated with MIMO channels [9], which can enhance system throughput. The global decoding ensures ultra-high reliability. However, the existing GC-LDPC codes based on algebraic construction in [10], [11] are not optimally designed for wireless communications. The message passing between local and global decoding in [12] does not consider the optimization of the check matrix in the global decoding.
In this paper, we study the GC-LDPC coded MIMO system and propose a novel method for superposition construction of GC-QC-LDPC (SP-GC-QC-LDPC) codes with flexible code rates and a modified two-phase decoding (TPD) method. To be compatible with 5G LDPC codes, we take 5G LDPC codes as local codes and design global constraints with flexible code lengths and rates. These local codewords are transmitted over different antennas and decoded in parallel at the receiver for high throughput. Moreover, the global decoding is performed based on the partial check matrix, resulting in lower decoding complexity with no performance loss. Numerical results show that our coding scheme can achieve improved throughput and reduced decoding complexity. Besides, it has higher reliability than BCH-LDPC concatenation codes in the digital video broadcasting-satellite second generation (DVBS2) standard [13].

III. CONSTRUCTION AND DECODING OF SP-GC-QC-LDPC CODES

We first introduce the superposition construction, and then propose the construction of SP-GC-QC-LDPC codes and a modified TPD method.

A. Superposition Construction and Base Matrices
Consider an LDPC code. Let Bsp = [bi,j ]0≤i<mb,0≤j<nb be an mb × nb matrix, where bi,j are nonnegative integers. For each entry bi,j, we replace it with a binary sparse circulant matrix of size Z × Z or an all-zero matrix of the same size, resulting in the PCM Hsp with QC structure. This construction method is known as superposition construction [14]. The matrix Bsp is called the base matrix. In this paper, we

C. Decoding
Based on r delivered by the MAP detector, TPD is carried out to estimate information uˆ. The TPD method includes two phases of local and global decoding. In the local decoding, each received ri is decoded using a local iterative decoder based on the SPA. Once one local decoder fails, the global decoding based on the entire parity-check matrix is activated to decode r [6].

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, we evaluate the decoding performance and the iterations of the GC-LDPC codes for MIMO system with NT = NR = 2. Assume that quadrature phase-shifting keying modulation, and MAP detection over MIMO channels are used. We consider two groups of parameters, Z = 384, kb = 22, m = 24, n = 46, s = 1, t = 4, p = 2 and Z = 384, kb = 22, m = 13, n = 35, s = 1, t = 5, p = 2. Based on these two groups of parameters, we construct two GC-LDPC codes Cgc,24,46,1,4 and Cgc,13,35,1,5. The maximum iteration numbers of the local and global decoding are both 30. Code parameters are given in Table III.
In Fig. 3, we provide the frame error rate (FER) performances of GC-LDPC codes over MIMO channels. In the legend, we use “ Part.” to represent the modified TPD method based on the partial check matrix. The performance of the modified TPD method is similar to that of the TPD method. Considering integrated space and ground networks, we also compare GC-LDPC codes with BCH-LDPC concatenation codes in the DVB-S2 standard. Two BCH-LDPC concatenation codes, denoted by CBCH−LDPC,1 and CBCH−LDPC,2, are chosen from the DVB-S2. Cgc,24,46,1,4 is shortened to have the same code length and rate as CBCH−LDPC,1. Figure 3 shows that our GC-LDPC codes have performance gains of 0.05 dB and 0.15 dB compared with BCH-LDPC concatenation codes at an FER of 10−5 for rates 0.49 and 0.66, respectively.
Let IL and IG denote the average iteration numbers of local and global decoding, respectively. The average iteration numbers are shown in Fig. 4. We can see that the global iteration number IG goes down quickly with the increase of the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR). At an FER of 10−5, IG approaches zero, which implies that system throughput is mainly dominated by the local decoding. In other words, our coding scheme divides the decoding of a large code into t parallel decoding of short codes at high SNRs. Cgc,24,46,1,4 and Cgc,13,35,1,5 enhance the throughput by 4 and 5 times at high SNRs, respectively.

V. CONCLUSION In this paper, the construction and efficient decoding of GC-LDPC codes for MIMO systems are studied in order

to achieve high throughput. With the help of the structure
based on 5G LDPC codes, our proposed GC-LDPC codes
have flexible lengths, rates, and excellent compatibility with
5G LDPC codes. Numerical results show that the proposed
coding scheme ensures high throughput and low complexity
over MIMO channels. Therefore, the proposed coding scheme
can be a candidate scheme for future wireless communications.
