Using Partial Orthomorphisms to Construct Short Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes with Girth at Least 6
Henry Chimal-Dzul and Anthony Gómez-Fonseca Department of Mathematics, University of Notre Dame, Notre Dame, IN 46556 USA
{hchimald, agomezfo}@nd.edu

Abstract—Since the publication of Fossorier’s work on the necessary and sufficient conditions for a (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code to have a desired girth g, many researchers have been attracted to the problem of determining the smallest lifting degree Nmin for which such a code exists. For some values of k, ℓ and g, either an explicit algebraic formula for Nmin has been given or its value has been determined by exhaustive computer search. For most cases, however, only lower bounds are known. In this paper, we translate the problem of determining Nmin into one about the existence of mutually adjacent partial orthomorphisms of ZN with certain deficit d, and determine its value for certain parameters. These particular orthomorphisms can also be used to determine, for a fixed ℓ and N , the maximum number kmax for which there exists a (kmax, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code with girth g.
Index Terms—Lifting degree, Low-density parity check (LDPC) codes, Quasi-cyclic (QC) LDPC codes, girth, Tanner graph, Partial Orthomorphisms.
I. INTRODUCTION
In the early 1960s, Gallager [1] developed a class of errorcorrecting codes, called low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, that have been shown to be capacity-approaching. The capacity-approaching performance of many members of this class has made them suitable for applications such as the Deep Space Network and satellite communications. The subclass of quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes is attractive for implementation purposes since they can be encoded in linear time with shift registers [2] and their simple structure leads to efficiencies in decoder design [3]. Moreover, it has been shown that QC-LDPC codes perform well compared to random LDPC codes for short to moderate block lengths [4], [5]. These are some of the reasons why QC-LDPC codes appear in many industry standards, including those developed by the Consultative Committee for Space Data System (CCSDS) [6].
QC-LDPC codes have been constructed using many strategies, including algebraic and combinatorial designs, depending on the target application [7]–[10]. The application taken into consideration influence the desired values of certain parameters of the codes being used, including their block length and dimension, the girth of their corresponding Tanner graphs and their minimum distance, for example.
In this paper, we only consider (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC codes specified as the kernel of a kN ×ℓN block parity-check matrix, where the blocks are circulant permutation matrices of size N × N . The parameter N is called the lifting degree of the code. For these codes, it has been shown that the girth, the

number of short cycles and other graphical structures in their Tanner graphs [11] affect their iterative decoding performance [12]–[14]. In consequence, and in an attempt to improve the performance of the code under iterative decoding, it is desirable to have a large girth.
The girth of a (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code depends, among other factors, on the choice of the circulant permutation matrices and the lifting degree N ; the latter determines in turn, along with k and ℓ, the length of the code and the number of rows in its parity-check matrix. As a consequence of this, it is an interesting problem to determine, for fixed values of k and ℓ, the smallest lifting degree N for which there exists a (k, ℓ)regular QC-LDCP code with a given girth g. In [15] and [16], under the requirement of having g ≥ 6, a complete answer has been given for (3, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC codes, whereas a partial answer has been given in [15] for (4, ℓ)-regular QCLDPC codes.
In this paper, we offer an algebraic approach to translate the problem involving the construction of (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC codes with girth g ≥ 6 into the existence of mutually adjacent partial orthomorphisms of ZN with certain deficit d. Associated with these mutually adjacent partial orthomorphisms, we consider the partial orthomorphism graph of ZN . The clique number of this graph provides an answer to the closely related problem of determining, for fixed ℓ and N , the maximum number kmax for which there exists a (kmax, ℓ)-regular QCLDPC code. We use constructions of partial orthomorphisms existing in the literature, and in particular of orthomorphisms, to construct (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC codes for k = 4 and k = 5 and ℓ satisfying certain conditions (see Theorem 16). The results presented in this paper can become part of the toolbox to design regular QC-LDPC codes. They also provide a lower bound for the lifting degree when QC-LDPC codes of girth g ≥ 8 are designed.

B. The girth of QC-LDPC codes

A (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code can also be represented by a bipartite graph called the Tanner graph [11]. The length of the shortest cycle(s) in the Tanner graph of such a code is called the girth of the code, and it is often denoted by g. It is known that the girth of a (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC can only be even and in the range 4 ≤ g ≤ 12 provided that k ≥ 2 and ℓ ≥ 3 [18]. A necessary and sufficient condition for a QCLDPC code with parity-check matrix (1) to have girth g ≥ 6 is to satisfy

hi1,j1 − hi2,j1 + hi2,j2 − hi1,j2 ̸= 0 (mod N ) (3)

1) h∗i has nonzero and pairwise distinct entries in ZN , and 2) h∗i −hj∗ has nonzero and pairwise distinct entries in ZN ,
where 2 ≤ i < j ≤ ℓ.

If k = 2, then Theorem 1 establishes that in order to construct a (2, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC with girth g ≥ 6 we must choose N ≥ ℓ. Indeed, in light of Theorem 1, if N = ℓ and π : ZN → ZN is a permutation, then any (2, ℓ)-regular QCLDPC code with parity-check matrix
1 1 ··· 1 H = 1 xπ(1) · · · xπ(N−1)
has girth g ≥ 6. Thus, the smallest lifting degree N for which there exists a code with parity-check matrix H with girth g ≥ 6 is Nmin = ℓ.
Remark 2. The lower bound N ≥ ℓ on the lifting degree is necessary for a (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code to have girth g ≥ 6. Due to this, in the rest of this paper, we assume that N ≥ ℓ.
III. PARTIAL ORTHOMORPHISMS AND THE MINIMUM LIFTING DEGREE
In this section, we study the connection between the conditions in Theorem 1 and partial orthomorphisms of the additive cyclic group ZN . First, we consider the following definition.
Definition 3. A partial orthomorphism of ZN of deficit d ≥ 1 is an injective map σ : S → ZN , where S ⊆ ZN and |S| = N − d, for which σ(x) ̸∈ {0, x} and the map σ∗ : S → ZN defined by σ∗(x) = x − σ(x) is also injective.
It is worth mentioning that partial orthomorphisms were introduced by Wanless [20] without the condition σ(x) ∉ {0, x}. Nevertheless, we impose it in Definition 3 as it will be essential in establishing Theorem 8. Moreover, this condition was also used in [21] to derive various results concerning to the number of partial orthomorphisms and divisibility properties for the number of Latin rectangles.
Theorem 4 ([21, Example 1.1]). For each N ≥ 3 and 1 ≤ d ≤ N − 1, there is a partial orthomorphism of deficit d .
It has been observed in [21, Theorem 1.2] that if N ≥ 3 is odd, then every partial orthomorphism σ of ZN of deficit 1 has domain S = ZN \ {0}. Therefore, every such partial orthomorphism gives place to a map θ : ZN → ZN defined by θ(0) = 0 and θ(x) = σ(x) for every x ∈ S = ZN \ {0}. The map θ is a (canonical) orthomorphism of ZN , that is, θ is a permutation of ZN that has 0 as its only fixed point (see [22] for more details). Conversely, when N ≥ 3 is odd, every orthomorphism of ZN gives place to a partial orthomorphism of ZN of deficit 1 by restricting its domain to S = ZN \ {0}.
Remark 5. In light of the previous discussions, by convention, we will refer to an orthomorphism of ZN as a partial orthomorphism of deficit d = 0.
Proposition 6 ([22]). There is an orthomorphism of (ZN , +) if and only if N ≥ 3 is odd.
We now introduce the notion of adjacent partial orthomorphisms, which extends the concept of adjacent orthomorphisms (see [22, Page 1]).

In light of Theorem 8, constructing a (k, ℓ)-regular QCLDPC code with matrix of exponents given as in (2), girth g ≥ 6, and lifting degree N , is equivalent to finding a set of k − 2 mutually adjacent partial orthomorphisms of ZN with domain S = {h2,2, h2,3, . . . , h2,ℓ}, where h2,j ∈ ZN \ {0}. Clearly, this problem depends on the parameters k, ℓ, the lifting degree N , and the selection of the domain S. When k and ℓ are fixed, the problem reduces to determine a lifting degree N such that we can construct a set of k − 2 mutually adjacent partial orthomorphisms of ZN of deficit d. We are interested in the minimum lifting degree Nmin for which this construction can be achieved. Alternatively, we can fix the parameter ℓ and choose a lifting degree N ≥ ℓ, and then determine the maximum number kmax of mutually adjacent partial orthomorphisms of ZN of deficit d = N − ℓ that can be constructed for this choice of ℓ and N . This construction will give a (kmax, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code with girth g ≥ 6 and lifting degree N . As we can observe, the solution of one problem provides information on the solution of the other.

In the rest of this paper, we will discuss these two related problems.
The problem of constructing a set of mutually adjacent orthomorphisms of ZN has been studied in [22] via the clique number of the orthomorphism graph of ZN . Motivated by this, we now introduce the following definitions.
Definition 9. The graph of partial orthomorphisms of ZN of deficit d, denoted by POrth(ZN , d), is defined as the undirected graph whose set of vertices is the set of all partial orthomorphisms of ZN of deficit d and two vertices are connected by an edge if the corresponding partial orthomorphisms are adjacent.
The graph of partial orthomorphisms of ZN of deficit 0 is called the orthomorphism graph of ZN and it is denoted by Orth(ZN ) (see [22] for more details). Recall that a graph is said to be complete if all its vertices are pairwise adjacent. The number of vertices in a graph is its order.
Definition 10. The clique number of POrth(ZN , d), denoted by ω(POrth(ZN , d)), is the largest order of a complete graph that can be formed from vertices and edges of POrth(ZN , d).
If N ≥ 3 is even, then Orth(ZN ) has no vertices and no edges by Proposition 6, so ω(Orth(ZN )) = 0. However, by Theorem 4, ω(POrth(ZN , d)) ≥ 1 for N even and d ≥ 1. On the other hand, if N ≥ 3 is odd, then Orth(ZN ) contains at least one vertex by Proposition 6. In this case, ω(Orth(ZN )) ≥ 1. Furthermore, we have also observed that if N ≥ 3 is odd, then every orthomorphism of ZN uniquely defines a partial orthomorphism of ZN of deficit 1, and vice versa. In consequence, two orthomorphisms of ZN are adjacent if and only if the corresponding partial orthomorphisms of deficit 1 are adjacent as well. Therefore, ω(Orth(ZN )) = ω(POrth(ZN , 0)) = ω(POrth(ZN , 1)) ≥ 1 for all N ≥ 3 odd. In general, determining the clique number of POrth(ZN , d) is not an easy task and we will discuss some lower bounds for it in Section IV.
Theorem 11. Let N and ℓ be integers such that N ≥ ℓ. The maximum number kmax for which there is a (k, ℓ)-regular QCLDPC code with girth g ≥ 6 and lifting degree N is
kmax = ω(POrth(ZN , N − ℓ)) + 2.
We now proceed to establish a connection between the lifting factor N of a (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code, the girth of the code, and the clique number of POrth(ZN , d). Recall that the number ℓ of columns of the matrix of exponents (2) is a lower bound for N , i.e., N ≥ ℓ.
Theorem 12. Let ℓ ≥ k ≥ 3 be integers. The following statements are equivalent.
1) There exists a (k, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code with girth g ≥ 6 and lifting degree N .
2) ω(POrth(ZN , N − ℓ)) ≥ k − 2.
As an immediate consequence of previous the theorem, we have the following criterion for the minimum lifting degree.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper we established a connection between partial orthomorphisms of ZN and the lifting degree N of a (k, ℓ)regular QC-LDPC code so that the code has girth at least 6. The connection has been established via the clique number of the graph of partial orthomorphisms of ZN of deficit d. Using this approach and bounds for the clique number of the orthormorphism graph of ZN , we obtained the minimum lifting degree for (4, ℓ)-regular and (5, ℓ)-regular QC-LDPC code. Some constructions for these codes have been also provided.
