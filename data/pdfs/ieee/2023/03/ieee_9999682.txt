IV. SIMULATION RESULTS
In this section, several simulation results are presented to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed Algorithm 1. We also compare the simulation results with those under the state-of-the-art efficient ADMM-based decoders. The considered competing decoders include the ADMM-based VNP decoder [4], the ADMM-based CNP decoder [5], the ADMM-based p-box decoder [6] and the check-polytopefree ADMM-based quadratic programming (QP) decoder [13]. Moreover, the decoders [4], [5], [6] are carried out with efficient check-polytope projection algorithm [7]. In addition, two LDPC codes from different communication applications are considered in the simulations. They are a (2640,1320) ‚ÄùMargulis‚Äù regular LDPC code C1 in [15] and a (2048,512) irregular LDPC code C2 from the recent 5G standard code [16], respectively.
The simulations are performed over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel with binary phrase shift keying (BPSK). The simulation parameters are set as follows: parameter Œº = 5 for both the considered LDPC codes; penalty parameter Œ± is chosen as 1 and 2.5 for C1 and C2, respectively; parameter (œÅx, œÅz) is set to be (6, 1) for the selected two codes; the stopping criterion is  = 10‚àí5 and the maximum number

A. Comparisons of Frame-Error-Rate (FER) Performance
Fig. 1 shows the comparisons of FER performance among the proposed G-BADMM decoding algorithm and the competing ADMM-based decoding algorithms [4], [5], [6], [13] for codes C1 and C2. It should be noted that all data points in Fig. 1 are obtained by collecting 100 error frames. From Fig. 1(a), it can be observed that the proposed G-BADMM decoder displays slightly better FER performance than the ADMM-based CNP decoder [5] and achieves much more better error-correction performance than the decoders [4], [6] [13]. Besides, Fig. 1(b) shows that the FER performance of the proposed G-BADMM decoder performs superior to the competing ADMM-based decoders [4], [5], [6], [13] for C2. Therefore, it can be concluded that our proposed G-BADMM decoder achieves a more favourable performance gain compared with the state-of-the-art ADMM-based decoders [4], [5], [6], [13].
