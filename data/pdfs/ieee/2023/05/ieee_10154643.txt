Abstract—In the paper, we propose a novel construction method for quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes with lower error floor. This approach aims at eliminating the most harmful substructures by choosing proper shift values for cyclic permutation matrices (CPM). To search these harmful substructures, we introduce a high-efficiency searching algorithm, based on cycle combination and leaf cutting. In addition, a high throughput decoding platform based on multi-core CPU with SIMD instruction sets are implemented and utilized to reach the error floors of different LDPC codes efficiently. Simulation results show that for given base matrices, our method can achieve excellent performance in the high SNR region.
Index Terms—QC LDPC codes, absorbing sets, error floor, cyclic permutation matrix
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes [1], as an important category, have been widely adopted in communication systems, such as WiMAX [2] and 5G-NR [3]. For the ultra-high reliability application scenarios in the future 6G networks [4], better construction and more efficient performance evaluation method of the LDPC codes need to be investigated. The parity-check matrices of most QC-LDPC codes are designed to have lower column weights in order to achieve low threshold and high throughput. As a consequence, the error floor becomes a serious problem due to the generation of many harmful substructures, such as small size trapping sets (TSs) [5], which are considered to be the main culprit in the error floor region in early works [6] [7]. Then the leafless elementary trapping sets (LETSs) [8] and absorbing sets (ASs) [9] are discovered to be more harmful.
For a given structure of the base matrix, properly assigning shift values of cyclic permutation matrices (CPMs) is the most effective way to keep the error floor as low as possible. There exist many ways to search for harmful substructures by the shift values of CPMs. The maximization of the average loop length can be used to optimize the girth of QC-LDPC codes and the proposed code can be designed for better

girth and degree profile with conventional progressive edge growth (PEG) and extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart techniques [10]. Reference [8] gives a layered superset (LSS) search algorithm, which is simple to implement, but the variety of intermediate results are huge and the search efficiency is very low. A systematic search method called DQL with controllable complexity is proposed in [11] by making up the shortcomings of LSS method. However, the DQL algorithm is not suitable to directly generate QC-LDPC codes, because it requires hundreds to thousands of such searches.
In this work, we combine the structure features of LETSs and ASs to get a new class of target substructure called leafless elementary absorbing sets (LEASs), and select LEASs as the optimization object for constructing the base matrices of QCLDPC codes to achieve lower error floor. In the first stage, the construction of target substructures is achieved through a specially designed process called cycle combination and leaf cutting. In the second stage, we choose the best one by analyzing the distribution of target substructures in each matrix got form stage one.
Multi-threads technology refers to the implementation of multiple threads executing simultaneously in software or hardware, and the processors that support it are called multi-core processors. To verify the lower error floor of LDPC codes, a high throughput simulation platform based on multi-cores which offer single instruction multiple data (SIMD) operations is used to process encoding and decoding in parallel. The simulation results show that our method always ensure a low error floor performance.

III. PROPOSED ALGORITHM
For getting the optimized QC-LDPC codes in error floor region, it is necessary to eliminate the harmful substructures. Some previous works mainly focus on the recursive search algorithms proposed for the harmful substructures. However, these methods are less efficient and require a lot of storage space. To overcome the above issues, we propose a novel algorithm based on the combination of cycles and the cutting of leaves to find harmful substructures. Based on this algorithm, we present a method for constructing QC-LDPC with good performance in high SNR region, whose basic idea is to eliminate LEASs by choosing better shift values for CPMs.

A. The Framework of Construction and Searching
The framework of the proposed construction algorithm is
outlined as follows, and the optimization on the positions of
the nonzero elements can be carried out one by one by column. Step 1 . Shift values are generated randomly on nonzero
element positions in the first column of Hb, and randomly fill a shift value pcur for the current location corresponding a nonzero element of Hb and pcur ∈ {0, 1, 2 · · · , Z − 1} from the second column to the last one. Then, exploit the Algorithm 1 to find the set S of GN (S) for LEAS in the sub-matrix so far of the Hb, where, the values after the current position are zero overall. If the number of all LEASs in a ≤ amax are zero, the current location accept the pcur as the selected shift value. Move to the next location to process the Step 1, otherwise, execute the Step 2.
Step 2 . Traverse pnxt, where pnxt = pcur + 1(modZ), then repeat Algorithm 1. Compare the increased types of LEASs in pcur with pnxt, select the one that has the maximum size of τam,bin, which is the optimization goal of construction algorithm. If the size of (amin, bmin) is identical for both pcur and pnxt, the one that have the smallest number with τam,bin is selected to become pcur. B. Cycle Combination and Leaf Cutting

By comparing the harmful substructures mentioned above, we find that all GN (S) contain at least one cycle. IV. SIMULATION RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS
We have applied the proposed search algorithm to find LEASs of irregular QC-LDPC codes on WiMAX and 5GNR standard with different rates and blocklengths. Additive white gaussian noise (AWGN) channels, quadrature phase shift keying (QPSK) modulation and the layered normalized minsum (LNMS) decoding algorithm with 30 iterations and 0.75 normalized factor are assumed. The simulation platform of encoding and decoding is composed of an Intel(R) Xeon(R) CPU E7-8867 v4, which is running at 2.4GHz and contains 72 physical cores and supporting hyper-threading technology. The SIMD instruction sets provided by Intel are used to simulate 32 frames in parallel AVX mode. For example, the overall decoding throughput of 5G-NR LDPC codes of rate 11/14 and lifting factor Z = 224 can achieve 2Gbps in our simulation using total 72 cores with SIMD/AVX instructions.

V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we proposed a novel approach for constructing QC-LDPC codes with lower error floor, which is to eliminate the LEAS based on choosing proper shift values for CPMs. Cycle combination and leaf cutting are used to enumerate LEASs, which greatly reduces the search complexity very well.
