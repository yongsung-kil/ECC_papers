A Study on Accelerating SP decoding by Neural Network  
in SMR System 
 
Madoka Nishikawa1, Yasuaki Nakamura1, Yasushi Kanai2, and Yoshihiro Okamoto1 
 
1 Graduate School of Science and Engineering, Ehime University, Matsuyama, 790-8577, Japan 
2 Department of Engineering, Niigata Institute of Technology, Kashiwazaki, 945-1195, Japan 
 
We have previously studied low-density parity-check (LDPC) coding and iterative decoding in shingled magnetic recording (SMR) 
system as a signal processing method to realize ultra-high-density hard disk drive (HDD). In addition, we have proposed the application 
of the neural network to improve decoding performance and realize the automation of iterative decoding. In this study, we apply a neural 
network to accelerate iterative decoding in the sum-product (SP) decoder. As the result, the SP decoder with the neural network realized 
“no errors” at the fewest times of the iterative decoding compared to our previous studies. 
 
Index Terms— Neural network, iterative decoding, low-density parity-check (LDPC) code, shingled magnetic recording (SMR). 
 
I. INTRODUCTION 
HE PROMOTION of digital transformation (DX) on a global 
scale requires the rapid addition of new data centers and 
cloud storage. Therefore, hard disk drives (HDDs) are further 
expected as storage devices to realize large capacity.  
We have previously studied the low-density parity-check 
(LDPC) coding and iterative decoding system in the shingled 
magnetic recording (SMR) system [1] to realize ultra-high- 
density HDD. We have also proposed a log-likelihood ratio 
(LLR) modulator using a neural network and realized efficient 
iterative decoding [2]-[4]. 
In this study, we apply a neural network to accelerate iterative 
decoding in the sum-product (SP) decoder as a new approach 
for iterative decoding. 
II.  READ/WRITE SYSTEM 
Figure 1 shows the read/write system. The input sequence is 
encoded by a 128/130(0, 16/8) run-length limited (RLL) [5] 
encoder and a (30, 3)-regular LDPC encoder [6]. The recording 
sequence is recorded on a granular medium model [7] under the 
specification of 4 Tbit/inch2. In the reading process, the 
decoding target track and the adjacent tracks are read composed 
by the array head composed of three readers simultaneously [8].  
In addition, system noise defined by the signal-to-noise ratio 
(SNR) is added at the reading point and is defined by SNR𝑆=
20 log10(𝐴/𝜎𝑠)[dB], where 𝐴 is the positive saturation level of 
the waveform reproduced from an isolated magnetic transition 
and 𝜎𝑠 is the root-mean-square (RMS) value of the system noise 
in the bandwidth of the the channel bit rate 𝑓𝑐. A channel bit 
response including read/write (R/W) process on the decoding 
target track is equalized to the partial response class-I (PR1) 
target [9] by the two-dimensional equalizer composed of three 
low-pass filters (LPFs) having cut-off frequency 𝑥ℎ normalized 
by the 𝑓𝑐 and the two-dimensional finite impulse response (TD-
FIR) filter with 𝑁𝑡 taps. Therefore, the total number of taps for 
a reader is 3 × 𝑁𝑡 [8].  We assume that these parameters are set 
to 𝑥ℎ = 0.4 and 𝑁𝑡 = 15. The turbo equalization is performed 
between an a posteriori probability (APP) decoder and an SP 
decoder using a neural network. Here, 𝑖𝑠𝑝 and 𝑖𝑔𝑙𝑜𝑏𝑎𝑙 stand for 
the maximum number of iterations in SP decoder and turbo 
equalization, respectively. Then, the bit error rate (BER) is 
calculated by comparing the input sequence with the output 
sequence. In this study, we focus on improving the decoding 
performance of the SP decoder with the neural network and 
performing effective iterative decoding. 
III. SP DECODER USING NEURAL NETWORK  
Figure 2 shows the block diagram of the SP decoder using a 
neural network. In this study, we use the parity check matrix 
with a code length of 4,096 bytes, a column weight of 3, and a 
row weight of 30, so 3 LLRs each for message nodes, check 
nodes, and prior information is calculated. The extrinsic 
information for the APP decoder is also calculated. Here, the 
LLR for message nodes is calculated using gamma, alpha, and 
beta shown in the logarithmic by the Max-log-MAP algorithm 
[10] based on the constraint of the LDPC code. Gamma means 
the transition probability from the previous point to the current 
point on the trellis diagram and is calculated using the SP 
decoder input and the prior information. In this study, in order 
to increase the decoding gain, we propose an update of the 
reliable gamma chosen by the neural network and realize 
effective iterative decoding. The configuration of the neural 
network is 3-5-1 neurons as shown in the figure. In the training 
process, by the back-propagation algorithm, we set the training 
signal to the recording sequence in order to obtain the neural 
network connection weight. Also, alpha and beta mean the 
forward and backward probabilities on the trellis diagram, 
respectively. In the cases that the parity check detects errors and 
the iterative decoding of the SP decoder is within 𝑖𝑠𝑝, the LLR

Figure 3 shows how to update the gamma. As a simple 
example, the parity check matrix in the cases of a code length 
of 12, a column weight of 3, and a row weight of 4 is shown in 
the figure. If the neural network output is greater than 0.9 or 
less than 0.1, it is considered that the recording information is 
“1” and “0”, respectively, and the gamma is updated by 
multiplying it by 1.34. However, in other cases, the gamma is 
not updated. In this way, the system realizes effective iterative 
decoding by weighting according to the neural network output 
to increase the decoded gain. 
IV. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION AND CONCLUSION 
Figure 4 shows the BER performance for 𝑖𝑠𝑝  and 𝑖𝑔𝑙𝑜𝑏𝑎𝑙  at 
SNR𝑆= 21.5  dB. The colors mean the BER performance as 
shown in the color bar on the right side. Also, the figures (a) 
and (b) correspond to the SP decoder with and without the 
neural network, respectively. As can be seen from the figures, 
the fewest iteration times in which the SP decoder with the 
neural network achieves “no errors” are 𝑖𝑠𝑝= 7 and 𝑖𝑔𝑙𝑜𝑏𝑎𝑙=
13 , but the SP decoder without the neural network does not 
achieve “no errors” by increasing the iteration times. 
Furthermore, the SP decoder with the neural network realizes 
“no errors” at the fewest 𝑖𝑠𝑝 compared to our previous studies 
[2]-[4].  
From the above, it is clarified that the SP decoder with the 
neural network realized accelerating iterative decoding in the 
SP decoder.
