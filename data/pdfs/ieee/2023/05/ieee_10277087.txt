An adjustable decoding scheme for robust and efficient transmission in FTTR C-WAN architecture

Zhihao Chen1, Fuzhou Peng1, Xiang Chen1,* 1School of Electronics and Information Technology, Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou 510006, China
Email: chenxiang@mail.sysu.edu.cn

Abstract—This paper investigates an effective adjustment scheme of the LDPC decoding algorithm according to the channel states in the Fiber-to-the-Room (FTTR) system with Centralized/Cloud Wireless-optical Access Network (C-WAN) architecture. The family scenarios of Distributed-MIMO (D-MIMO) channels of 5GHz Wi-Fi are measured and analyzed. According to the proposed scheme, the gain brought by the C-WAN architecture is transformed to the decrease of computation and time complexity of LDPC algorithm, which matches burst transmission character of Wi-Fi system. Simulations and experiments show that D-MIMO technology certainly improves the performance of family network system and an average 3dB gain of Signal-to-Noise ratio (SNR) is achieved. When using the Offset Min-Sum (OMS) algorithm, the LDPC decoding performance can approach the Belief Propagation (BP) algorithm at lower complexity, which also can improve the throughput of LDPC by reducing the number of iterations.
Index Terms—FTTR C-WAN, distributed MIMO, LDPC, offset min-sum algorithm.
I. INTRODUCTION
Recently, the promising Fiber-to-the-Room (FTTR) with Passive Optical Network (PON) solution for extending the coverage of Wi-Fi, has been widely deployed and has enormously improved user experience. The existing deployment of the FTTR network uses Point to Multi-point (PTMP) topology, which takes advantage of low construction cost, easier deployment, scalability, etc. [1] However, the access points (APs) deployed in different rooms are independent, which doesn’t fully utilize the networking collaboration technologies, causing serious disordered competition in air interface and poor performance of insensible roaming. Hence, researchers proposed a Centralized/Cloud Wireless-optical Access Network (C-WAN) architecture, in which the data of different spatial streams are collected for central processing. [2] For higher robustness and lower computation complexity, this paper proposes to apply distributed MIMO (D-MIMO) technology [3] in C-WAN architecture, which will bring additional signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) gain.
Due to the burst transmission property in Wi-Fi frames, the receiver does not have the prior information ahead of time. Only after correctly synchronizing and decoding the control information of the frame, the spatial flow can be decoded to ensure the subsequent operation of the system, which requires the receiver algorithm to be low complexity and high robustness.

However, the Wi-Fi system adopts LDPC as optional coding scheme. Sometimes, the decoding algorithms of LDPC are computable especially when a lower bit error rate (BER) performance is required. The problem to transform the extra SNR gain into the decoding margin in this structure is still open.
When the channel condition is poor or the system load is heavy, higher dimensional MIMO can be applied to coordinate several slave devices. And LDPC decoding algorithm can be configured dynamically, such as reducing the iteration number to maintain stability and improve throughput of LDPC without loss of BER performance. Therefore, this paper proposes an adjustment scheme of the LDPC decoding algorithm in the CWAN D-MIMO structure and achieves the trade-off between computation complexity and SNR gain of the D-MIMO.
The rest of the paper is organized as follows. First, the architecture description and system model are introduced in section II. The experimental scenario and results are illustrated in Section Ⅲ while the conclusion and future work are given in Section Ⅳ.

In the traditional FTTR architecture, each AP receives data and completes the receiving process separately, which will cause inter-AP interference. In the C-WAN architecture, the FTTR scenario consists of a master device, several slave devices and many optical fibers. Data received by each slave device will be transmitted to the master device for unified processing, making the D-MIMO structure possible, thus obtaining the diversity gain brought by the higher-dimensional MIMO.

B. LDPC Decoder Algorithm

LDPC decoding algorithm has been relatively mature, which

makes researchers focus on the trade-off between computation

complexity and decoding performance. The Bit Flipped (BF)

algorithm is a hard decision algorithm, which is simple in the

calculation but has poor performance. The Belief Propagation

(BP) algorithm is a soft decision algorithm, which makes the

decoding performance close to the Shannon limit by iterating

the message of the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) be-tween the bit

nodes and the check nodes. The BP algorithm has good decod-

ing performance, but it requires logarithmic calculation. Hence

it is of high computation complexity and is difficult for hard-

ware implementation. Furthermore, the error of noise estima-

tion will greatly affect the decoding performance of the BP al-

gorithm.

In order to reduce the complexity, the Minimum Sum (MS)

algorithm is proposed, which converts the logarithmic calcula-

tion into comparison and sum. However, it will expand the mes-

sage of the check node, resulting in the loss of decoding perfor-

mance. In order to compensate for the performance loss of the

MS algorithm relative to the BP algorithm, while preserving the

characteristics of the MS algorithm, the Normalized Minimum

Sum (NMS) algorithm [5] is proposed, which corrects the am-

plitude of check node information by introducing a normaliza-

tion factor. Another optimization algorithm is the Offset Mini-

mum Sum (OMS) algorithm, which introduces the offset factor.

III. EXPERIMENTAL SCENARIO AND RESULTS
In this section, an FTTR C-WAN D-MIMO test scenario is designed. Then the experimental results are given, which analyzed the SNR gain brought by the D-MIMO structure in the CWAN architecture and the performance of the proposed scheme.
A. Experimental Scenario

As shown in Fig.3, the test scenario is a typical indoor singleuser D-MIMO scenario with FTTR C-WAN architecture. Two slave devices are located in different positions. The station (STA) is configured as two transmit antennas mode and each slave device is configured as two receive antennas mode, which forms a 2 ×4 D-MIMO system. In the experiment, several groups of measurement data in five test points are collected, and the received data are processed to analyze the performance of LDPC algorithms and the proposed scheme.

As the result, the BP algorithm has the best decoding performance, while the MS algorithm has the worst decoding performance for the expansion of message. The OMS and NMS algorithms can approximate the BP algorithm with about 0.1dB loss. Therefore, considering the SNR gain brought by the C-WAN D-MIMO architecture, the OMS and NMS algorithms can approach or even exceed the decoding performance of the BP algorithm while reducing the computational complexity.

IV. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
This paper introduces the D-MIMO technology into the indoor FTTR network with C-WAN architecture. To make full use of the SNR gain caused by the system architecture, this paper proposes an adjustable decoding scheme. With the scheme, the trade-off between time constraints and computation complexity can be achieved. According to the experiment results of channel analysis with the indoor test scenario, the efficiency and robustness of the proposed scheme are verified. In future work, we will strengthen the investigation of various channel modeling for improving the attraction when introducing this architecture into millimeter waves (mmWave). Moreover, the CWAN D-MIMO structure will contribute to the realization of senseless roaming, which is also worth investigating.
