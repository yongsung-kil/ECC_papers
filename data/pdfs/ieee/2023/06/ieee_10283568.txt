Protograph-Based LDPC-Coded Orbital Angular Momentum Systems with Index Modulation

Zhaojie Yang*, Yufei Zhao*, Yao Ge*, Yi Fang†, and Yong Liang Guan* *School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Nanyang Technological University, Singapore †School of Information Engineering, Guangdong University of Technology, Guangzhou, China
Email: *{zhaojie.yang, yufei.zhao, yao.ge, eylguan}@ntu.edu.sg; †fangyi@gdut.edu.cn

Abstract—This paper investigates the joint design of protographbased low-density parity-check (PLDPC) codes and orbital angular momentum (OAM) systems with index modulation (IM) over lineof-sight (LoS) channels. To begin with, we analyze the probability density function (PDF) of extrinsic log-likelihood-ratios (LLRs) for coded bits output from channel detector, and propose a customized Box-Cox transformation (CBCT) to make it achieve symmetry and Gaussian characteristics. We also develop a CBCT protographbased extrinsic information transfer (CBCT-PEXIT) algorithm to predict the convergence performance of PLDPC-coded OAM-IM systems. Furthermore, with the aid of such an algorithm, we construct two new types of improved PLDPC codes tailored for OAMIM systems, including unpunctured codes and rate-compatible punctured codes. Both theoretical analyses and simulation results demonstrate that our proposed PLDPC-coded OAM-IM systems can significantly outperform the state-of-the-art counterparts.
Index Terms—Orbital angular momentum, index modulation, extrinsic information transfer, protograph, low-density parity-check.
I. INTRODUCTION
Multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) is a physical-layer solution to achieve high data-transmission reliability and spectral efficiency for wireless applications. However, conventional MIMO systems cannot ensure hardware-friendly implementation and desirable capacity gains over line-of-sight (LoS) channels, which are considered as a common model for various communication scenarios, such as LoS-dominant intelligent reflecting surface (IRS) [1], unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) [2], satellite [3] and backhaul [4]. To obtain the inherent advantages of MIMO and overcome its drawbacks, great research efforts have been dedicated to exploring an emerging alternative technique, namely orbital angular momentum (OAM)-aided mode division multiplexing (MDM) transmission [5].
OAM-aided MDM systems are implemented using the electromagnetic (EM) waves with different OAM modes having low correlation. Also, the OAM mode multiplexing/demultiplexing is realized using the analog processing technologies (e.g., phase shift networks), which are beneficial to circumvent the inter-channel interference (ICI) and reduce the computational overhead. Owing to these appealing characteristics, a flurry of research activities have been conducted to design and optimize the methods for generating OAM EM waves. In particular, the uniform circular array (UCA) [6] is one of the most competitive alternatives due to its relatively low hardware cost and implementation complexity. Recently, some research works related to the joint design of OAM and multiple access technology have been proposed to realize high-capacity MDM systems [5]. However, the OAM systems usually suffer from severe inter-mode interference due to the misalignment between the transmitting and receiving UCAs. To

alleviate the performance degradation, previous researchers have designed the OAM receiver [6], the power allocation scheme [7] as well as the precoding scheme [8].
Coded modulation is another solution to realize the high reliability-and-throughput OAM communications. For instance, the multidimensional OAM coded-modulation scheme has been proposed to use all available resources (e.g., the amplitude, phase and OAM mode) to carry information [9]. A joint design of OAM and trellis-coded modulation has been presented to improve both performance and spectrum efficiency [10]. Also, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes and index modulation (IM) [11] have been considered for OAM systems to further enhance the data-transmission reliability over LoS channels [12]. Actually, the capacity-approaching LDPC codes always have irregular structures, which lead to unacceptable coding complexity [13]. To address this issue, a type of structured LDPC codes, namely protograph-based LDPC (PLDPC) codes [14], has emerged as a more promising alternative due to its simple design and easy implementation. Motivated by the above discussion, the joint design of PLDPC codes and OAM-IM framework has great potential to be a feasible multiplexing solution, which can acquire desirable communication reliability and throughput in LoS scenarios. To the best of our knowledge, this is a first attempt to investigate the PLDPC-coded OAM-IM systems over LoS channels. Our contributions are summarized as follows:
1) We analyze the conditional probability density function (PDF) of the extrinsic log-likelihood-ratios (LLRs) for coded bits output from channel detector, and find that it follows a nonGaussian distribution. We then propose a customized BoxCox transformation (CBCT), which can transform the LLRs to attain symmetric Gaussian features for facilitating the lowcomplexity performance analysis and code design.
2) We develop a CBCT protograph-based extrinsic information transfer (CBCT-PEXIT) algorithm for CBCT-aided OAM-IM systems (i.e., a transformation model). Such an algorithm possesses low computational complexity and can evaluate the convergence-performance trends of different PLDPC codes in the OAM-IM systems with typical inter-mode interference.
3) We construct a rate-1/2 unpunctured improved PLDPC code and two rate-1/2 punctured improved PLDPC codes by using the proposed CBCT-PEXIT algorithm. We further extend these two punctured codes to be rate compatible.

B. Proposed CBCT
The channel characteristics should be considered to develop capacity-approaching coding schemes. However, no work discussed the LLR features of coded bits transmitted over LoS channels with or without the inter-mode interference. To fill this gap, we analyze the extrinsic LLRs of coded bits output from channel detector based on Monte Carlo simulations. Specifically, the rate-1/2 regular-(3, 6) PLDPC code with a codeword length ne = 8000 is adopted, while the quadrature phase-shift keying (QPSK) modulation is considered. We define bi as the i-th coded bit in a frame (codeword) and li as the corresponding extrinsic LLR value output from detector. Also, f (li|bi = 0) (f (li|bi = 1)) represents the conditional PDFs of li for bi = 0 (bi = 1). We give an ideal symmetric Gaussian distribution N (u, 2u) (i.e., N (u0, 2u0) and N (u1, 2u1)) for comparison, where u is the mean, 2u is the variance, and u0 (u1) denotes the average extrinsic LLR value for bi = 0 (bi = 1). In Fig. 1(a), an OAMIM with NA = 1 is considered for θ = 0 (i.e., perfect alignment between the transmitting and receiving UCAs). It is observed that f (li|bi = 0) and f (li|bi = 1) (i.e., scattered points) are consistent with N (u0, 2u0) and N (u1, 2u1) (i.e., solid lines), respectively. However, when the transmitting and receiving UCAs are misaligned (i.e., θ = 0), f (li|bi = 0) and f (li|bi = 1) may not exhibit such PDF characteristics. An example (θ = 0.8) is given in Fig. 1(b), where f (li|bi = 0) and f (li|bi = 1) no longer tend to be symmetric Gaussian distributions, but skewed distributions (see scattered points). Similar phenomenons can be observed for an OAM-IM with NA = 2 (related results are omitted here due to the space limitation). Thus, the theoreticalanalysis tools and coding schemes tailored for channels with symmetric Gaussian characteristics cannot be directly applied to OAM-IM systems with typical inter-mode interference.

III. PROPOSED CBCT-PEXIT ALGORITHM FOR PLDPC-CODED OAM-IM SYSTEMS
A. PLDPC Codes
A protograph Gp = (Vp, Cp, Ep) is formulated by a variablenode (VN) set Vp = {v1, v2, . . . , vnv }, a check-node (CN) set Cp = {c1, c2, . . . , cnc } and an edge set Ep = {ei,j}, where ei,j denotes the edge connection of the i-th CN ci (i = 1, 2, . . . , nc) and the j-th VN vj (j = 1, 2, . . . , nv). A protograph with a code rate Rp = (nv − nc)/nv corresponds to a base matrix Bp = (bi,j), whose rows (columns) denote the CNs (VNs) and bi,j denotes the number of edges connecting ci to vj. For a given protograph, one can perform a lifting operation on it and then attain its derived graph, which is used to generate the PLDPC code. Specifically, a length-ne PLDPC code can be generated from a protograph with nv VNs, where ne = nv × f and f is a lifting factor. The lifting operation is implemented by a modified progressive-edge-growth (PEG) algorithm [17] in this paper.

B. Proposed CBCT-PEXIT Algorithm
PEXIT algorithm is an asymptotic-performance analysis tool, which can predict the decoding threshold of a PLDPC code by tracing its mutual information (MI) evolution. The evolution process is divided into two parts. The first part is the calculation of MI output from the detector, while the second part is the MI update between the VN decoder and CN decoder. Remark 2:
• In this algorithm, the CBCT is an essential link between the detector and VN decoder, which allows to use (12) for calculating the transformed extrinsic MIs of OAM-IM systems with inter-mode interference.
• The transformed extrinsic LLRs have symmetric and Gaussian characteristics, but they are output from a transformation model (i.e., CBCT-aided OAM-IM systems with intermode interference) instead of AWGN channels.
IV. PROPOSED PLDPC CODES FOR OAM-IM SYSTEMS
A. Proposed PLDPC Codes
Even a PLDPC code with excellent performance over a specific channel, it may not be a promising candidate for other channels due to the different fading characteristics. In AWGN scenarios, the accumulate-repeat-by-4-jagged-accumulate (AR4JA) code is a competitive code since it can realize a good performance tradeoff between the waterfall and error-floor regions [19]. For the OAM-IM systems over LoS channels, the transmitted signals will suffer from inter-mode interference and their extrinsic LLRs

output from detector may follow a skewed distribution. In this scenario, the PLDPC-code design is a new challenging issue, which deserves to be addressed. In the following, we use the CBCT-PEXIT-aided computer-search method to construct two types of rate-1/2 improved PLDPC codes tailored for our proposed transformation model. They are also promising alternatives for OAM-IM systems without using CBCT.
To begin with, we set a small-size (i.e., nc × nv) all-zero base matrix B0. The search space equals (1 + bmax)nc×nv − 1 if an exhaustive computer-search method is used to find the resultant base matrix Breul of a PLDPC code with the optimal decoding threshold. In particular, bmax is the maximum integer element in Breul. To reduce the search space and lower the encoding/decoding complexity, one can first assume that bi,j within Breul satisfies 0 ≤ bi,j ≤ 3 (i ∈ {1, 2, . . . , nc} and j ∈ {1, 2, . . . , nv}). Meanwhile, two fundamental characteristics of a good PLDPC code should be taken into account [19], i.e.,

• Adding an appropriate proportion of degree-1 and degree-2 VNs into the protograph, this can help the resultant PLDPC

codes exhibit a relatively low decoding threshold;

• Using a CN to connect a degree-1 unpunctured VN and a highest-degree punctured VN, this formulates a precoding

structure that can further improve the threshold performance

for a punctured PLDPC code.

VI. CONCLUSIONS
This paper has studied the PLDPC-coded OAM-IM systems over LoS channels. Specifically, we have first analyzed the characteristic of extrinsic LLRs output from the channel detector and proposed a CBCT to formulate a transformation model. We have also conceived a CBCT-PEXIT algorithm, which has low computational complexity and can predict the convergenceperformance trends of PLDPC codes in OAM-IM systems with inter-mode interference. By exploiting a CBCT-PEXIT-aided computer-search method, we have constructed a rate-1/2 unpunctured improved PLDPC code and two rate-compatible punctured improved PLDPC codes, which exhibit desirable decoding thresholds. Simulation results have shown that the OAM-IM systems with the use of proposed PLDPC codes can achieve better performance than the existing counterparts.
