Matryoshka Globally-Coupled LDPC Code

Hao Liu, Graduate Student Member, IEEE, and Qiyue Yu , Senior Member, IEEE

AbstractвҖ” This paper proposes a new low-density paritycheck (LDPC) code called Matryoshka globally-coupled (MGC) LDPC code. We give the definition of N -fold MGCLDPC code which consists of N layers Matryoshka-style codes, i.e., Cmgc (1) , Cmgc (2) , . . . , Cmgc (N ). Moreover, the N Matryoshka-style codes can form a Matryoshka chain Cmgc (1) вүә Cmgc (2) вүә В· В· В· вүә Cmgc (N ), which indicates that a high-layer Matryoshka-style code contains a low-layer Matryoshka-style code just like the Matryoshka dolls. Thus, the proposed N -fold MGC-LDPC code naturally has rate-compatible feature. Based on the superposition (SP) construction, we present truncating and masking methods to construct the base matrices (BMs) of MGC-LDPC codes. More importantly, we introduce the double lower triangular (DLT) form of parity-check matrix for further developing a recursive encoding scheme for MGC-LDPC codes. With the help of the proposed encoding scheme, the local codes of MGC-LDPC code can be independently encoded, and the nth layer Matryoshka-style codeword can be recursively obtained by the (n вҲ’ 1)th layer Matryoshka-style code and the local codes in nth layer Matryoshka-style code for 2 вүӨ n вүӨ N . Simulations show that MGC-LDPC codes can perform well over both the additive white Gaussian noise channel (AWGNC) and binary erasure channel (BEC), and provide a flexible rate-compatible feature to satisfy the requirements of different scenarios.
Index TermsвҖ” Globally-coupled LDPC, nested codes, superposition, masking matrix, rate-compatible, recursive encoding.
I. INTRODUCTION
LOW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes have been proved to be one of the most promising codes to approach the Shannon capacity [1], [2], [3]. Due to the fascinating performance, LDPC codes have been considered in many industry scenarios or standards, including the fifth generation (5G) mobile communications [4], digital video broadcasting satellite second generation (DVB-S2) [5], IEEE 802.11ax [6], flash memories [7], [8], etc. Many works are interested in constructing well-designed LDPC codes for different channels, e.g., additive white Gaussian noise channel (AWGNC) and binary erasure channel (BEC) [9], [10], [11], [12], [13], [14], [15].
In general, there are graph-based and algebra-based construction methods. The most popular graph-based method is

To satisfy the girth constraint, progressive-edge-growth (PEG) [17] and some modified algorithms [18], [19], [20] are proposed to construct the derived Tanner graph. The first comprehensive algebra-based construction was introduced in [9], and then various algebra-based constructions have been proposed [8], [21], [22]. In [23], the authors presented a general superposition (SP) method for constructing LDPC codes. Actually, both graph-based and algebra-based methods can be unified by the SP construction [24]. The SP construction method decomposes the design of LDPC codes into the design of the base matrices (BMs) and the replacement sets. Many novel LDPC codes can be derived based on the SP construction, e.g., spatially-coupled (SC) LDPC codes [25], globally-coupled (GC) LDPC codes [26], doubly-cyclic LDPC codes [27], etc.
The GC-LDPC code is one of the most promising LDPC codes, which consists of a group of local LDPC codes that are connected by some global check nodes (CNs) or variable nodes (VNs). The introduced global CNs (or VNs) enlarge the connection of local codes, which provides fast convergence in iterative decoding and well-behaved performance over both AWGNC and BEC. More importantly, the global CNs (or VNs) connection structure brings the flexibility to decoding, which supports various applications. Many researchers have investigated the GC-LDPC codes from construction, decoding scheme to applications. In [26], the authors first present some methods to construct GC-LDPC codes and introduce a two-phase decoding scheme. Reference [28] extends the constructions of GC-LDPC codes with more flexible parameters. The authors in [29] present the tail-biting (TB) GC-LDPC codes by introducing a TB structure into the global CNs, and further analyze the asymptotic performance of the ensembles. In [30], the authors introduce the finite geometry (FG) GC-LDPC codes and the corresponding encoding scheme. Reference [31] proposes the PTG-based GC-LDPC code for burst-erasure channel. In [32], the authors investigate the replacement sets according to balanced incomplete block design (BIBD). In [33], GC-LDPC codes based on free-ride coding [34] are proposed for reducing the rate loss due to the global CNs. Additionally, some literatures focus on the applications of GC-LDPC. For example, the authors in [35] apply GC-LDPC codes to the multiple-input and multipleoutput (MIMO) systems for improving the network reliability.
In this paper, we extend the structure of GC-LDPC codes, and propose a new type of LDPC codes named as Matryoshka globally coupled (MGC) LDPC codes. The MGC-LDPC codes introduce the matryoshka chain to the classical GC-LDPC codes, so that the degree distribution, cycle distribution and

In addition, MGC-LDPC codes consist of a sequence of subcodes called Matryoshka-style codes that can provide different blocklengths and code rates, and these Matryoshka-style codes are special GC-LDPC codes by developing the conception of local codes. Therefore, MGC-LDPC codes have the rate-compatible feature. More importantly, we propose a recursive encoding scheme, in which the local codes can be encoded independently and the Matryoshka-style codes are obtained in a recursive manner. The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
1) We first give the definitions of the N -fold MGC-LDPC code and the nth layer Matryoshka-style code which is nested in the N -fold MGC-LDPC code in order for n = 1, 2, . . . , N . Then, we prove that an N -fold MGC-LDPC code can form a Matryoshka chain, which can naturally realize the rate-compatible feature to support different blocklengths and code rates via the N Matryoshka-style codes.
2) We propose truncating and masking methods for constructing the BMs of N -fold MGC-LDPC codes. The MGC-LDPC codes constructed by the truncating method are more efficient, which is easy to implement. In contrast, the masking method is more flexible. The masking method can further optimize the cycle distribution and degree distribution to achieve better performance.
3) We introduce the double lower triangular (DLT) form of parity-check matrix to develop the recursive encoding scheme of MGC-LDPC codes. With the help of the DLT form, local LDPC codes can be independently encoded and then form a codeword of the MGC-LDPC code. Moreover, the codewords of higher-layer Matryoshkastyle code can be obtained recursively from the lowerlayer Matryoshka-style code.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section II reviews the basic features of GC-LDPC codes. In Section III, we give the definition of MGC-LDPC codes and analyze the structural properties. We present truncating and masking methods to construct BMs of MGC-LDPC codes in Section IV. The DLT form and recursive encoding scheme for MGC-LDPC codes are given in Section V. Some examples of MGC-LDPC codes are illustrated and analyzed in Section VI. Some conclusions and future works are given in Section VII.

Let GF (q) be a Galois field with q elements, and Оұ be a primitive element of GF (q). Then, the powers of Оұ, i.e., ОұвҲ’вҲһ, Оұ0, Оұ1, . . . , ОұqвҲ’2, give all the elements of GF (q). For an element of GF (q), we can define a mapping between Оұi and the q-ary CPM of size (q вҲ’ 1) Г— (q вҲ’ 1) where the first row has single nonzero component at position i and the rest of rows are right cyclic-shift of the row above it. The zero element ОұвҲ’вҲһ is mapped to the zero matrix (ZM) of size (q вҲ’ 1) Г— (q вҲ’ 1). It can be verified that the mapping between elements of finite field and CPMs or ZMs is one-toone. Then, we can derive an array Hgc of CPMs or ZMs over GF (2) by replacing each entry of Bgc with the corresponding CPMs or ZMs, denoted by Hgc = CPM (Bgc). The null space of Hgc gives a CN-based CPM-dispersion superposition quasi-cyclic GC-LDPC (CPM-D-SP-QC-GC-LDPC) code. For a CN-based CPM-D-SP-QC-GC-LDPC code, the girth and the cycle distribution are highly related to Bgc. If Bgc satisfies the 2Г—2 submatrix (SM) constraint [27], the grith of the CN-based CPM-D-SP-QC-GC-LDPC code is at least 6. In the following discussion, we use вҖңGC-LDPCвҖқ instead of вҖңCN-based CPMD-SP-QC-GC-LDPCвҖқ for simplicity.

Theorem 1 reveals the most important feature of the N -fold MGC-LDPC code. The high-layer Matryoshka-style code contains the low-layer Matryoshka-style code. Then, the N different layers Matryoshka-style codes form a chain as (8) just like the Matryoshka dolls, which is the motivation of the proposed MGC-LDPC codes. More importantly, the Matryoshka chain naturally brings the rate-compatible feature. We can construct the MGC-LDPC code with different code lengths and rates for each Matryoshka-style code, so that the N Matryoshkastyle codes can dynamically fulfill different channel conditions and Quality of Service (QoS) requirements. In Section VI, we construct a 4-fold MGC-LDPC code with rate-compatible feature and evaluate its performance over AWGNC and BEC.

V. RECURSIVE ENCODING FOR MGC-LDPC CODES
In this section, we propose an encoding scheme for MGCLDPC codes. Based on the Theorem 1, the recursive encoding scheme is implicit in MGC-LDPC codes. In order to ensure that the local code can be independently encoded, we first introduce the conception of double lower triangular (DLT) form of parity-check matrix. Then, we apply the DLT form to GC-LDPC codes and MGC-LDPC codes. Finally, we propose the recursive encoding scheme for MGC-LDPC codes.

A. Double Lower Triangular Form

Let u, p and v denote the information vector, paritycheck vector and codeword respectively. We consider the systematic form, i.e. v = [u, p]. Generally, there are two approaches for encoding. One is to obtain v according to the generator matrix G, i.e., v = uG, and the other is to solve the equations HvT = 0. The following proposed encoding scheme can be viewed as a combination of the aforementioned two approaches. VII. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we present an extension of GC-LDPC codes called the N -fold MGC-LDPC code which consists of N Matryoshka-style codes that form a Matryoshka chain. Two methods are proposed to construct the N -fold MGC-LDPC code. One is the truncating method, and the other is the masking method. Moreover, we define the DLT form and recursive encoding scheme so that the local codes can be independently encoded, and the codewords of different layers Matryoshka-style codes can be recursively generated. Simulations show that the MGC-LDPC codes can further improve the cycle distribution, degree distribution, threshold and performance compared with the GC-LDPC codes.
A valuable research topic of MGC-LDPC codes is to design a low-complexity decoding scheme. This paper only shows the performance in terms of flooding-schedule decoding without considering the structural features of MGC-LDPC codes. It is very interesting to design a low-complexity decoding algorithm for the practical application of MGC-LDPC codes based on the feature of Matryoshka chain. A possible direction is to design a sliding-window decoding scheme [41], and the core difficulty is to deal with the property of global coupling. Another possible direction is to modify the twophase decoding, which decodes the GC-LDPC codes by local decoder or global decoder. However, the latency of directly using two-phases decoding is still very high due to the nested structure. Therefore, it is a challenging topic to design a decoding scheme for MGC-LDPC codes. Furthermore, this paper only considers the binary MGC-LDPC codes, construction of non-binary MGC-LDPC codes is an interesting issue, as well as the low-complexity encoding scheme and decoding scheme.
