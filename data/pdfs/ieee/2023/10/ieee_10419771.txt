Abstract—In this paper, we propose two new scheduling algorithms to address the issues of existing informed dynamic scheduling (IDS) decoding algorithms of short low-density paritycheck (LDPC) codes for the fifth-generation (5G) new radio (NR). The first issue is the complexity problem, where the high complexity of IDS makes hardware implementation difficult. By considering the impact of puncturing for the first two columns of base matrices, the proposed least-punctured and sum-residualbased (LPSRB) algorithm can reduce decoding complexity. The second issue is the significant degradation of decoding performance when the code length is short. By fully utilizing residuals, the proposed novel residual belief propagation (NRBP) algorithm allocates a significant amount of decoding resources to the less convergent edges.Simulation results demonstrate that NRBP achieves better frame error rate (FER) performance for short LDPC codes.
Index Terms—5G, layered decoding, LDPC codes, LPSRB, NRBP, scheduling scheme
I. INTRODUCTION
In order to meet the high throughput requirements of the fifth-generation (5G) new radio (NR) wireless systems, the 3rd Generation Partnership Project (3GPP) has adopted low density parity check (LDPC) code [1] as data channel coding technology [2]. Long-blocklength LDPC codes are commonly employed as channel coding in many communication standards [3]–[5] due to their remarkable performance approaching the Shannon limit. However, it is difficult to achieve both high reliability and low latency for long-blocklength LDPC codes. In order to meet the requirements of high-reliability and real-time communication systems, short code length error correction code schemes are usually used. Due to the impact of short cycles, how to improve the decoding performance of short LDPC codes has become a key issue.
The belief propagation (BP) algorithm [6], a flooding scheduling algorithm that updates all check nodes (CNs) in a single iteration, is the most widely used decoding algorithm.

Flooding schedule can effectively achieve greater parallelism, but it has a slower convergence speed. A sequential scheduling scheme is suggested as a solution to this issue. Layered BP (LBP) [7] is the common sequential scheduling method for NR LDPC codes. In the LBP decoding iterations, the posterior probability of variable nodes (VNs) is updated layer by layer in a fixed order by the parity check matrix (PCM). However, employing suitable scheduling methods can effectively decrease the frame error rate (FER) of LBP and enhance the convergence speed. Based on the difference in obtaining the order of updates, scheduling algorithms can be classified into two categories: static scheduling and informed dynamic scheduling (IDS).
In terms of the static scheduling, there are least-punctured and highest-degree (LPHD) proposed by in [8] and base graph based static scheduling (BGSS) proposed in [9]. These two kinds of static scheduling take into account the unique properties of NR LDPC, which are based on the punctured non-zero elements in the base graph (BG).
In terms of IDS, residual belief propagation (RBP) scheme proposed in [10] and node wise RBP (NW-RBP) scheme proposed in [11], which have a fast rate of convergence, need a lot of computation. In [8], residual-based LBP (RB-LBP) reduces additional complexity on this basis with only a small performance loss. [12] introduces IDS into adaptive belief propagation (ABP) [13] decoding of RS codes. ABP decoding systematizes low reliability bits, making it difficult to accurately recover. The layered residual BP (LRBP), introduced in [12], can allocate a greater amount of decoding resources to low reliability bits.
In this paper, we propose least-punctured and sum-residualbased (LPSRB) that is based on sorting by residual and the number of non-zero elements in the punctured block column. We prioritize updating rows with fewer punctured non-zero elements as our first optimization rule (OR). Under this OR, by further sorting the rows with the same update priority

II. PRELIMINARIES
A. LDPC codes in 5G NR
Two base matrices with various structures are defined for the NR LDPC codes in the 5G NR channel coding system, namely BG1 and BG2, where the BG1 size is 46 × 68 and the BG2 size is 42×52. The base matrix’s fundamental structure is depicted in Fig. 1. The high rate portion of the matrix, A B , equates to Hcore of the Raptor-like LDPC (RL-LDPC). D E equates to Hext, corresponds to the extended check rows, and is connected to the degree one variable nodes for rate matching. Wherein, submatrix A corresponds to information bit of codeword. All the elements in submatrix C is zero matrix. The submatrix E represents the connection between the extended check node and the degree one variable node, which is an identity matrix. The submatrix B is square matrix with size of 4 × 4, which is a double diagonal structure. The base matrix element corresponds to a unit shift matrix or a zero matrix (obtained by right cyclic shift of the unit matrix) in PCM H. Define Z as the lifting size, then the size of the unit shift matrix is Z × Z. The first two columns in BG are large column weights, which can enhance the information flow in decoding and increase the efficiency of information transmission between check equations. V. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we propose two novel IDS algorithms, LPSRB and NRBP, which provide significant improvements in complexity reduction and decoding performance, respectively. The proposed LPSRB utilizes a matrix structure to reduce decoding complexity while maintaining high decoding performance. By utilizing the sum of residuals, the proposed LPSRB improves the convergence speed and ensures that the decoding

performance is not significantly compromised compared to RB-LBP algorithm. On the other hand, the proposed NRBP introduces two prominent advantages. Firstly, it fully utilizes C2V residuals to update the information of non-converged nodes more frequently, thereby achieving a better FER and accelerating convergence speed. Secondly, the parallelism of NRBP facilitates efficient hardware implementation. When applying these two scheduling methods to 5G short LDPC codes, both can achieve performance improvement, making short LDPC codes more valuable in practical applications.
