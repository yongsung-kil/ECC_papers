Exploiting the Single-Symbol LLR Variation to Accelerate LDPC Decoding for 3-D NAND Flash Memory
Yingge Li, Guojun Han , Senior Member, IEEE, Chang Liu , Meng Zhang , and Fei Wu , Member, IEEE

Abstract—Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes have been widely adopted to guarantee data reliability in 3-D NAND flash memory. However, the iterative LDPC decoding algorithm leads to high-decoding latency due to the iterative message transfer mechanism. Using a field-programmable gate array (FPGA) testbed, we first present the binary channel in NAND flash and analyze the single-symbol log-likelihood ratio (LLR) variation with the decoding iterations. Subsequently, we investigate the raw bit error ratio (RBER) characteristics of intrapage frames. To reduce the number of iterative decoding, we propose a frame feedback information aware decoding algorithm (FFIA-DA), combined with the single-symbol LLR variation and the similar error characteristics among intrapage frames. The proposed method uses the decoding feedback information of one frame to decrease the number of decoding iterations of other frames with similar RBER. Experiments show that the proposed approach can improve the decoding performance of LDPC and speed up decoding convergence.
Index Terms—3-D NAND flash memory, binary channel, low-density parity-check (LDPC), raw bit error rate (BER), single-symbol loglikelihood ratio (LLR) variation.
I. INTRODUCTION
With multiple types of noise bringing more challenges to the reliability of 3-D NAND flash memories [1], strong error-correction codes (ECCs), such as low-density parity check (LDPC) codes, have been used in 3-D flash memories to address reliability challenges as well as to extend the memory lifespan [2]. However, the LDPC decoding algorithm is an iterative probability decoding algorithm, which iteratively calculates the posterior probability of each bit in the codeword, and updates the log-likelihood ratio (LLR) information to correct bit errors. The decoding requires several iterations to obtain accurate posterior information, resulting in a significant decoding delay [3]. Thus, it is urgent to accelerate the iterative decoding process to reduce decoding iterations.
In [4], a method has been proposed for detection and decoding in flash memory by exploiting the correlation or errors in neighboring

For TLC NAND flash, the method first decodes two lower-raw bit error ratio (RBER) pages (e.g., the center significant bit (CSB) page and the most significant bit (MSB) page), and stores their corresponding channel LLRs and posterior LLRs. Then these LLRs are read out to optimize decoding operations for the higher-RBER page (e.g., the least significant bit (LSB) page). In [9], pair-bit errors aware LDPC decoding scheme has been proposed to reduce the number of decoding iterations based on pair-bit error characteristics of MLC NAND flash pages.
The aforementioned joint-page decoding optimization methods mainly use the error characteristics among pages or cells at one wordline to accelerate the convergence and reduce the number of iterations, which are more suitable for sequential read operations [8]. For random read operations, excessive dependence on decoding operations among pages at the same wordline will not only lead to unnecessary reading or decoding operations but also increase the storage overhead for storing the channel LLR or posterior LLR information of the auxiliary decoding page. Therefore, this article focuses on the error characteristics of frames within one page, rather than the error characteristics among pages. We first introduce the binary channel from the perspective of page, and analyze the LLR variation, as well as the variation of the mean value of single-symbol LLR and the number of error bits with the number of decoding iterations. Subsequently, the RBER variation of the intrapage frame is analyzed. Then, we propose a frame feedback information aware decoding algorithm (FFIA-DA) to improve the LDPC decoding process based on the characteristics of single-symbol LLR variation and intrapage frame similarity error, which can be suitable for sequential read operations and random read operations.

IV. EXPERIMENTS AND DISCUSSION
This section evaluates the proposed FFIA-DA on Micron B17A 3-D TLC NAND flash memory chips under various noise conditions. The baking time of 2.64 h in a high-low temperature test chamber under temperature 70 ◦C is equivalent to 1 month under room temperature of 25 ◦C [15]. The LDPC code utilized in our experiment is a regular code [14] (4000, 3600) created by progressive edge-growth. The row weight dc is 30, and the column weight dv is 3. The random sequence (i.e., bit 0 and bit 1 are randomly distributed in the flash page) is encoded. The maximum decoding iteration is set to 10 [3], [16]. The BER= (N2/N0) of each frame is calculated, where N2 represents the number of error bits after soft decoding.
Fig. 6(a) and (b) show the block convergence and BER/FER performances of the conventional decoding algorithm and the proposed FFIA-DA under various noise conditions. Referring to Fig. 6(a), to achieve a block BER of 10−5, under PE = 5K and T = 12 months (i.e., the RBER of 9.5 × 10−3), the average number of iterations using the conventional LDPC decoding algorithm is 10, while the average number of iterations using the proposed FFIA-DA is 5. At the an RBER of 9.5 × 10−3, conventional LDPC decoding algorithm can achieve a BER of 1.1 × 10−5 and FER of 4.6 × 10−3, while the proposed FFIA-DA accomplishes a BER of 2.9 × 10−6 and FER of 1.7 × 10−3 [see Fig. 6(b)]. From the above observations, one can observe that the proposed FFIA-DA accomplishes better performance than the conventional decoding algorithm. Moreover, we analyze the computational complexity and space overload. With regard to the computational complexity, one decoding iteration of the adaptive decoding frame requires N(n/2 − 1) addition, N(q + 1) multiplications, and N(2) division. q denotes the column weight, n denotes the length of the codeword. On the other hand, for the space overhead, it requires to store the step size λ.
Since the EMAL algorithm optimizes the decoding of the CSB page by reading the channel LLR and decoding results of the LSB and MSB page [8], we only conduct comparative analysis on the performance of CSB pages, as shown in Fig. 7. Referring to Fig. 7(a), to achieve a BER of 10−5 for CSB pages, under PE = 5K and T = 12 months (i.e., the RBER of 1.55 × 10−2), the average number of iterations using the EMAL algorithm is 9, while the average number of iterations using the proposed FFIA-DA is 6. Referring to Fig. 7(b), at the RBER of 1.55 × 10−2 for CSB pages, the EMAL algorithm can achieve a BER of 7.7 × 10−6 and FER of 4.1 × 10−3, while the proposed FFIA-DA accomplishes a BER of 5.3×10−6 and FER of 2.6 × 10−3. As a consequence, the proposed FFIA-DA can achieve a better performance than the EMAL algorithm. Additionally,

V. CONCLUSION
In this work, we first present the page binary channel and analyze the variation of single-symbol (e.g., bit 1) LLR and BER of iterative decoding under page binary channel. Then, considering unnecessary reading and decoding operations being introduced over multipage joint decoding optimization algorithm for random read operations, we propose a decoding optimization algorithm based on intrapage frame feedback information, which takes page rather than wordline as the optimization unit. Experimental results show that the algorithm is effective in convergence and error correction performance. Furthermore, the proposed algorithm can be extended to frame decoding optimization of page groups with similar characteristics according to the RBER quantization of frames.
