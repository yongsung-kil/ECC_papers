Abstract- A new approach is proposed for constructing regular Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes based on Tensor product of matrices. In this paper, first a general construction method of regular LDPC codes exploiting permutation matrices is described. Constructed codes have a quasi-cyclic structure with no short cycles of length 4 in their Tanner graph, hence simple encoding while maintaining good performance is achieved. The paper also demonstrates a generalized design, which covers a large family of LDPC codes and number of other construction methods. The new generalized LDPC codes are defined by a small number of parameters and cover a large set of code lengths and rates. Using these codes, LDPC matrices of any column weight and row weight can be constructed. Performance of these codes under iterative decoding compares well with other wellstructured as well as random LDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION Several Low-Density Parity-Check (LDPC) codes have been designed achieving performance very close to the Shannon limit [1]. During the past few years, research has been focused on pseudo-random (or briefly, random) LDPC codes with the aim of closing the gap between the Shannon limit and error correction performances of LDPC codes [2]. Despite their excellent error correcting properties, complexity issues tend to dominate system architecture and design considerations. The high complexity of random LDPC codes [3] is a direct consequence of the fact that for these codes a large amount of information is necessary to specify positions of the nonzero elements in the parity-check matrix. The complexity drawbacks of random LDPC codes have triggered a new path of using structured LDPC codes. Several classes of structured LDPC codes are known, including Quasi-Cyclic (QC) LDPC codes based on combinatorial designs such as Balanced Incomplete Block Design (BIBD) [4, 5], finite geometries [6] and mutually orthogonal Latin squares [7]. QC LDPC codes [8], as a major class of structured LDPC codes, have encoding advantages over random LDPC codes as they can be encoded using simple shift-registers with complexity linearly proportional to their code length [9]. In this paper we present and investigate a new generalized approach for implementing good QC LDPC codes based on permutation matrices. It is fascinating to observe that

our proposed design covers a large family of LDPC codes and number of other construction methods. For example, as will be shown later, Vandermonde [10] and Array [11] LDPC codes are derived as a special case of the proposed design by employing permutation matrices based on the prime fields. The proposed family of codes also covers the recent construction method provided by Tanner, et al in [12]. This is because any GF(pn), where p is a prime number and n is an integer, provide set of permutation matrices, which are used in our construction method. We compare the performance of these codes with random LDPC codes and show that excellent performance results, comparable with random LDPC codes can be achieved. The paper is organized as follows: Section II provides a background on LDPC codes and their properties. In addition, few definitions and concepts, which are going to be used later on, are presented in Section II. In Section III, our construction method is described in details. Performance results for a range of code lengths and rates are provided in Section IV. Section V, concludes the paper.
II. BACKGROUND AND BASIC CONCEPTS
a. Regular LDPC Codes Binary regular LDPC code of length N is defined as the null space of an MuN parity-check matrix H over GF(2) and has the following structural properties: 1) each row consists of U ‘ones’, referred to as the row weight (Rw); 2) each column consists of J ‘ones’, referred to as the column weight (Cw); 3) the maximum number of ‘ones’ in common between any two columns (or any two rows), is denoted O d 1 ; and 4) both U and J are small compared to the code length N. The performance of LDPC codes with iterative decoding depends on a number of structural properties of the code. One of the most important structural properties is the girth of the code. The girth of an LDPC code is defined as the length of the shortest cycle in the code's Tanner graph [13]. An LDPC code with girth g (gt 4), is free of cycles of length less than g in its Tanner graph. Well-designed LDPC codes without short cycles, using iterative decoding algorithms such as the sum-product algorithm (SPA) [14], achieve good error correction performance. In fact, property

V. CONCLUSION In this paper, we have presented a general construction method for regular QC LDPC codes based on permutation matrices. The presented designs provide a large family of LDPC codes with various lengths and rates. Using these codes, LDPC matrices of any column weight and row weight can be constructed. The performance of these codes under iterative decoding algorithm compares well with pseudo-random LDPC codes.
