An Efficient Girth-Locating Algorithm for Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes
Xiaofu Wu∗†, Xiaohu You∗, and Chunming Zhao∗ ∗National Mobile Commun. Research Lab.,
Southeast University, Nanjing 210096, CHINA Email: xfuwu@ieee.org, xhyou@seu.edu.cn, cmzhao@seu.edu.cn †Nanjing Institute of Communications Engineering, Nanjing 210007, CHINA

Abstract— The parity-check matrix of a quasi-cyclic code can be represented by a polynomial parity-check matrix with a significantly lower dimension. By using this compact representation, we can develop an efficient method for locating the girth of the quasi-cyclic code. The proposed girth-locating algorithm can be well employed to generate quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check codes with large girth.
I. INTRODUCTION
Quasi-cyclic low-density parity-check (QC-LDPC) codes are a special class of LDPC codes whose parity-check matrices consist of circulant matrices. Particular examples of circulant matrices include circulant permutation matrices, sums of two or more circulant permutation matrices, and also the zero matrix. If the parity-check matrix of a QC-LDPC code consists of circulant matrices of the same size, namely, L×L, the required memory for storing the parity check matrix can be reduced by a factor 1/L, as compared to randomly structured LDPC codes. The encoding of QC-LDPC codes can be efficiently implemented by simple shift-register circuits. Therefore, QCLDPC codes are more favorable for hardware implementation.
A (J, K) regular LDPC code is defined by a parity-check matrix H in which each column has weight J and each row has weight K. In [1]–[3], the authors considered a special class of regular QC-LDPC codes whose circulant matrices are circulant permutation matrices. It has been shown in [1], [3] that a (J, L)-regular QC-LDPC code of this class has minimum distance always upper bounded by (J + 1)!, and the girth upper bounded by 12.
The minimum distance of QC-LDPC codes may be improved if circulant matrices other than circulant permutation matrices are allowed in H. This was first observed in [4]. By composing a parity-check matrix not only with circulant permutation matrices but also with sums of two circulant permutation matrices and with zero matrices, one can increase the minimum distance while keeping the same regularity. Further allowing the sums of more than two circulant permutation matrices leads to cycle-6, thus are not of interest for iterative sum-product decoding. This new class of QCLDPC codes are called type-II QC-LDPC codes in [4], while the codes whose parity-check matrices are composed of only circulant permuation matrices and the zero matrix are of typeI. Specifically, for (3, 4) -regular codes in the type-I class, the

III. SEARCH OF GOOD QC-LDPC CODES
The theorem 4 can be employed to locating the girth of general QC-LDPC codes, whose parity-check matrices can be composed by any circular matrix. Under standard sum-product decoding, it is of interest to only consider type-II QC-LDPC codes [5]. Note that type-I QC-LDPC codes can be considered as a special class of type-II QC-LDPC codes. In [5], Smarandache and Vontobel derived a new upper bound on the minimum distance of the type-II QC-LDPC code, which is directly related to the so-called weight distribution matrix of the polynomial parity-check matrix H(x).

IV. CONCLUSION
We have proposed an efficient girth-locating algorithm for QC-LDPC codes. The proposed algorithm can detect all possible loops in a systematic way. Therefore, it can be well employed in search of good QC-LDPC codes with large girth.
