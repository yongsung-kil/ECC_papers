A Combining Method of Structured LDPC Codes from Affine Permutation Matrices

Seho Myung and Kyeongcheol Yang Dept. of Electronics and Electrical Engineering Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH)
Pohang, Kyungbuk 790-784, Korea Email: {bighead, kcyang}@postech.ac.kr

Dong Seek Park Advanced Research Lab. Global Standards and Research Team Samsung Electronics Co., LTD, Korea Email: dspark@samsung.com

Abstract‚Äî In this paper we present a class of structured low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes from affine permutation matrices, called the APM-LDPC codes, which are a generalization of quasi-cyclic LDPC codes. We give a necessary and sufficient condition under which an APM-LDPC code has a cycle and introduce a simple method to construct APM-LDPC codes of large length by combining those of small length based on the Chinese Remainder Theorem. In particular, we show that the girth of APM-LDPC codes obtained in this method is always larger than or equal to those of given APM-LDPC codes.
I. INTRODUCTION
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, first discovered by Gallager [6], were rediscovered to be Shannonlimit-approaching codes over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels [9], [14]. It has been well-known that LDPC codes perform extremely close to the Shannon capacity for sufficiently large code length [1], [8], [9], [13]. However, the performance of LDPC codes of small length may be strongly affected by their cycle properties such as girth and stopping sets [2], etc. Here the girth is the minimum length of cycles in the Tanner graph of a given parity-check matrix of an LDPC code. In particular, cycles of length 4 (or 4-cycles, for short) in their Tanner graph may cause fatal errors when a message-passing decoding algorithm is employed. Recently, coding theorists have made efforts to find efficient methods for constructing the parity-check matrices with large girth. There are two typical approaches to construct an LDPC code with no short cycles. One approach is to find efficient algorithms for removing short cycles from a given random LDPC code [5], [10] and the other is to construct an LDPC code with no short cycles in an algebraic way [3], [4], [7], [8], [15].
Randomly constructed LDPC codes need a significant amount of memory to store their parity-check matrices. It may also be hard to access the memory and encode data efficiently. For these reasons, algebraically structured LDPC codes are more attractive for their commercial use and implementation purpose. Several coding theorists proposed some classes of quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes from circulant permutation matrices and analyzed their properties [3], [4], [15]. QCLDPC codes may be a good candidate to solve the memory problem due to their algebraic structures. In fact, the required memory for storing their parity-check matrices can be reduced

by a factor 1/L, when L √ó L circulant permutation matrices are employed. It is also easier to control their cycle properties in their construction process, as compared with random LDPC codes.
The main contributions of the paper are as follows. First, we present a class of structured LDPC codes from affine permutation matrices, called the APM-LDPC codes. They are a generalization of QC-LDPC codes and are different from the LDPC codes studied by O‚ÄôSullivan and Smarandache [12]. We show a necessary and sufficient condition under which an APM-LDPC code has a cycle and introduce a simple method to construct APM-LDPC codes of large length by combining those of small length based on the Chinese Remainder Theorem. An APM-LDPC code of large length can be obtained by combining two or more APM-LDPC codes of smaller length. We show that the girths of the APM-LDPC codes obtained in this method are always larger than or equal to those of given APM-LDPC codes.
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section II, we present APM-LDPC codes and introduce some definitions for our presentation. We give a necessary and sufficient condition under which an APM-LDPC code has a cycle and propose a simple method to construct APM-LDPC codes of large length from those of smaller length in Section III. We verify the performance of several APM-LDPC codes by simulations in Section IV. Finally, we give some concluding remarks in Section V.

IV. NUMERICAL RESULTS
The performance of several combined APM-LDPC codes was analyzed by computer simulations over an AWGN channel. They were decoded by the sum-product algorithm with 50 iterations.
Figure 1 shows the bit error rate (BER) performance of (4,9)-, (4,12)- and (5,12)-regular APM-LDPC codes with girth 8 which are obtained by the similar method to Example 6. They are based on 144 √ó 144, 153 √ó 153, and 464 √ó 464 affine permutation matrices and have 4√ó9, 4√ó12, and 5√ó12 mother matrices, respectively. The (4,9)-regular APM-LDPC code is obtained by combining two function matrices defined over A16 and A9, respectively. In the same manner, the (4,12)- and (5,12)-regular APM-LDPC codes are obtained by combining those defined over (A17, A9) and (A16, A29), respectively. There are no serious performance degradations at BER=10‚àí6 due to the constraints on their construction, as compared with randomly constructed regular LDPC codes with girth 6.

V. CONCLUDING REMARKS
We discussed an extension method in which APM-LDPC codes of large length can be efficiently constructed. First, we gave a clear description of the condition under which an APM-LDPC code has a cycle. Second, we proposed a simple combining method based on the Chinese Remainder Theorem in order to construct APM-LDPC codes of large length from those of small length without sacrificing their cycle properties. In the case that the column weight is larger than or equal to 4, simulation results show that a serious performance degradation is not introduced in this method.
