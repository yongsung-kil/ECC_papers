Abstract - This paper presents bit splitting methods to apply multilevel modulation to iterative codes such as turbo code, low density parity check code and turbo product code. Loglikelihood ratio method splits multilevel symbols to soft decision
symbols using the received in-phase and quadrature component based on Gaussian approximation. However it is too complicate to calculate and to implement hardware due to exponential and logarithm calculation. Therefore this paper presents Euclidean, MAX and Sector method to reduce the high complexity of LLR method. Also, this paper proposes optimal soft symbol split method for three kind of iterative codes.

Concatenate coding schemes are considered as being the

best solution for powerful protection of digital information

against nonlinear and fading noise. However, the performance

of concatenate coding scheme is away from the Shannon's

limit. Recent channel codes to approach the Shannon's limit

are turbo code, proposed by Berrou and al in 1993 [1][2],

LDCP(Low Density Parity Check) code proposed by Gallager

in 1962[3] and re-established by Mackay in 1996[4], and

TPC(Turbo Product Code)[5] which is effective for the high

coding rate due to 2-dimensional product code. These

algorithms are employed by Portable Internet System

Standard 802.16e[6] as channel coding technique. For high-

speed transmission, recent communication systems guarantee

quality of service (QoS) using adaptive modulation and

coding (AMC) rather than power control. Therefore

multilevel modulation has become an essential field of

research in digital communications. However, all those

advantage of iterative codes should be accompanied with

accurate estimation of soft demapping This would be more important in the
