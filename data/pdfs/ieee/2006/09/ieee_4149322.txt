Cycle Properties Analysis of Matrix-Filled LDPC Codes

Jianquan Liu, Youyun Xu, Yueming Cai Institute of Communications Engineering, PLAUST, Nanjing 210007, P. R. China
jianquanliu@gmail.com, yyxu@vip.sina.com, caiym@vip.sina.com

Abstract
In this paper, we present the upper and lower bounds of the girth of MF-LDPC codes which are determined by the shift times of cyclic shift matrices by analysis the properties of cyclic shift matrices in cycle, we also propose a concrete conditional-filling construction, the simulations prove that sufficient utilization of the properties can reduce the amount of least cycle of the MF-matrix remarkably and improve the performance to a larger extent.
Keywords: MF-LDPC codes, cyclic shift matrix, girth
1. Introduction
Low-density parity-check (LDPC) codesŌĆöfirst discovered by Gallager [1], [2] and rediscovered by MacKay and Neal. [3]ŌĆöhave created much interest recently since they are shown to have a remarkable performance close to the Shannon limit over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels [4]. LDPC codes possess many advantages including parallelizable decoding, self-error-detection capability by syndrome check, an asymptotically better performance than turbo codes, etc. However, there are some challenging issues relating to application of LDPC codes in practice: how to design an LDPC code in the case of a finite block length and how to reduce the complexity in encoding and storing the parity check matrix. Richardson introduced a method to design LDPC codes that perform extremely close to the Shannon capacity for sufficiently large code length under the assumption of no cycles [5]. The density evolution method was proposed to optimize LDPC codes in an asymptotic sense. However, there is no guideline for constructing parity check matrices of the short block length.
Presently, some joint LDPC code-encoder-decoder design approaches have been proposed [6], [7], [8], [9]. In these approaches, a design method of the LDPC code using cyclic shift matrices is present to obtain both the reduced memory and practical allowable encoding complexity. As a loss, they should have a large block length to obtain good performance.
LDPC codes can be described by a bipartite graph called a Tanner graph [10]. The length of the shortest cycle in a

Tanner graph is referred to as its girth g. Since large girth improves the bit-error performance of the codes, leads to more efficient decoding [2], and guarantees large minimum distance [10], LDPC codes with large girth are particularly desired.
The main contribution of the paper is to propose the upper and lower bounds of the girth of a check matrix which is determined by the shift times of cyclic shift matrices, and present a concrete conditional-filling construction of LDPC codes.
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section 2, we review the matrix-filled (MF) LDPC codes. We describe the properties of cyclic shift matrices in cycle and propose the upper and lower bounds of the girth of MF-matrix based on cyclic shift matrices in Section 3 and present a concrete conditional-filling construction and simulations in Section 4. Finally, we give concluding remarks in Section 5.

4.2. Numerical results
Computer simulations were done to analyze the performance of MF-LDPC codes constructed by our conditionalfilling over an AWGN channel, each code is modulated by BPSK and decoded with the sum-product algorithm. We construct 245├Ś 343 regular mother matrix by geometrical approach, and adopt cyclic shift matrix of length 5. Both the mother matrix and the MF-matrix have column weight 5 and row weight 7. The mother matrix has 2058 cycles of length 6, which is the girth. We can obtain 1225├Ś1715 MF-matrix constructed by conditional-filling which has 30 cycles of length 6 and 10260 cycles of length 30. The number of cycles with length 6 of the code constructed by random- filling is at most 10290.

5. Conclusion
We discussed how to construct MF-LDPC codes for good performance. We analyzed the cycle properties of MF-matrix, and showed that their girth is upper-bounded and lowerbounded by two certain number which is determined by the shift time of cyclic shift matrix, respectively.
In order to construct good MF-LDPC codes, we present a new conditional-filling construction. Simulation results show that the proposed conditional-filling construction outperforms the random-filling construction.
