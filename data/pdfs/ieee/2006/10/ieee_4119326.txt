Abstract--When mobiles cannot support multiple antennas due to size or other constraints, cooperative diversity is an efficient way to provide uplink transmit diversity. This paper presents a novel cooperative coding scheme in use of rate-compatible parallel concatenated LDPC codes. We evaluate its performance in terms of bit error rate. Simulation results show our scheme provides significant performance gains for a variety of channel conditions. By allowing different code rates and partitions, parallel concatenated LDPC coded cooperation provides a great degree of flexibility to adapt to channel conditions. *
I. INTRODUCTION
Transmit diversity, a powerful technique for combating fading in wireless systems, is not directly applicable to many mobiles due to their small size. User cooperation [1] provides uplink transmit diversity in the face of this limitation. T. Hunter [2] developed coded cooperation in which cooperative signaling is integrated with channel coding. Coded cooperation works by sending different portions of each user's codewords via two independent fading paths. The basic idea is that each user tries to transmit incremental redundancy for his partner. Coded cooperation can use a wide variety of channel coding methods. The examples in [2] employ rate-compatible punctured convolutional (RCPC) codes [3]. In [4], turbo codes are applied to this framework.
Parallel Concatenated LDPC (PC-LDPC) codes are class special LDPC codes [5] and have good error correcting capabilities in both AWGN and Rayleigh fading channels. PC-LDPC codes [6] are rate-compatible codes which the parity bits of higher rate codes are

embedded in those of lower rate codes. We here consider a cooperative system in use of PC-LDPC codes. The PC-LDPC coded cooperative system transmits a codewords over the source-relay channel and only requires simple bit-flipping (BF) decoding[7] at the partner node due to the better quality of the source-relay (inter-user) link, which means shorter decoding delay and less power consumption. On the other hand, LDPC codes and sum-product iterative decoding have a more powerful error correction capability than convolutional codes and lower decoding complicity than turbo codes.

The setup consists of two mobiles, User 1 and User 2, communicating with a base station in a cellular system. The basic idea is to construct a system such that the codeword transmitted by each user arrives at the base station through two independent fading paths, while maintaining the same overall information rate and average power as a comparable non-cooperative system. VI. CONCLUSIONS
This paper presents a novel cooperative coding scheme in use of PC-LDPC codes. We presented the performance in terms of bit error rate (BER) with various inteiruser channel conditions. Simulation results show that Coded cooperation using PC-LDPC codes provides significant performance gains for a variety of channel conditions. By allowing different code rates and partitions, parallel concatenated LDPC coded cooperation provides a great degree of flexibility to adapt to channel conditions.
