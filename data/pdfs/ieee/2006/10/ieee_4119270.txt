Fast Weighted Bit-Flipping Decoding of Finite-Geometry LDPC Codes
Xiaofu Wu, Ming Jiang, Chunming Zhao, and Xiaohu You

Abstract- In this letter, we establish a natural relationship between the improved modified-weighted-bit-flipping (IMWBF) decoding and min-sum decoding. This insight helps us to develop a fast weighted bit-flipping (FWBF) decoding algorithm. For decoding of finite-geometry low-density parity-check codes, we demonstrate through examples that the FWBF decoding can provide a further improvement to the IMWBF decoding on both convergence and performance.
Index Terms- Bit-flipping decoding, min-sum decoding, lowdensity parity-check (LDPC) codes.

II. ON THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN IMWBF DECODING AND MIN-SUM DECODING

I. INTRODUCTION
I TERATIVE decoding of finite-geometry (FG) low-density
parity-check (LDPC) codes has received much attention in
recent years [1]. The standard sum-product algorithm (SPA) decoding can achieve excellent performance but often with heavy implementation complexity. Bit-flipping (BF) decoding is simple, but it often incurs considerable performance loss compared to the SPA decoding. To bridge the performance gap between BF decoding and SPA decoding, weighted BF (WBF) decoding and its variants were proposed [1]-[5], among which, the improved modified-WBF (IMWBF) decoding may perform best so far [5].
Like all the other WBF decoding algorithms, the IMWBF decoding algorithm flips only one bit per iteration. This sequential BF scheme often results in slow convergence. In this letter, we show that by flipping multiple bits only in its first iteration, the convergence of the IMWBF decoding can be accelerated significantly.

B. Initiatives
To speed the decoding convergence, multiple-bit flipping is allowed in the first decoding iteration of the new algorithm. Based on the justification of (7), it is reasonable to flip all bits {Zn} for which En(a) > 0. As seen from (7), the flipped bits are just those bits for which the decision after the first iteration of min-sum decoding is made against the original hard decision from the channel.
For the subsequent iterations, the IMWBF decoding diverges from the min-sum decoding. Hence, a sequential bitflipping strategy is again adopted in order to minimize the probability of error-flipping.
In some cases, the multiple-bit-flipping strategy employed in the FWBF decoding in its first iteration contributes to a fraction of performance loss compared to the IMWBF decoding. To remedy this performance loss, one can reset the decoder with a reduced number of bits to be flipped once it fails. There are two mechanisms that should be made clear. One is to detect decoding failure, and the other is to select a reduced number of bits to be flipped once the decoder restarts.
In [3], the authors show that decoding failure occurs if a non-codeword vector is encountered twice in the decoding process, since after that the decoding process is trapped in an infinite loop. Unfortunately, this requires storing all the indexes of the bits which have been flipped in the previous iterations. A simplified method has been introduced in [4], where the number of unsatisfied checks in each iteration is used. Let Uch(k) denote the number of unsatisfied checks in the kth iteration. Whenever Uch(k) > Uch(k -1), decoding failure is declared. Clearly, this simplified method is much simpler to implement as it only requires to store the number of unsatisfied checks in the previous iteration. In simulations, we notice that in many cases, decoding failure may occur when
Uch(k) = Uch(k -1). Therefore, in the proposed algorithm,
decoding failure is declared whenever Uch(k) > Uch(k -1). Once decoding failure is detected, the FWBF algorithm
resets the decoder and restarts the decoding process with about half of the candidate bits to be flipped in respect of the previous resetting-decoder operation. Here, we do not declare the optimality of this flipping scheme. However, simulations show that it works well. Further improvement may be not easy.

V. CONCLUSION
We have proposed a fast WBF decoding algorithm for FGLDPC codes. The FWBF decoding improves the IMWBF decoding in both convergence and performance. Compared to the IMWBF decoing, the advantage of the proposed FWBF decoding on convergence is remarkable.
