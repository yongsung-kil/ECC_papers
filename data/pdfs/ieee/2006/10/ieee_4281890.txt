Normalized Switch Schemes for Low Density Parity Check Codes

GONG Chen, LIU Qi, CUI Huiluan, and TANG Kun
State Key Laboratory on Microwave and Digital Communications, Department of Electronic Engineering Tsinghua University,
Beijing, 100084, P.R.China
Phone: +086-010-62774265, E-mail: gongc5gmai hua.edu.cn

Abstract - This paper addresses switch schemes for switch type hybrid hard decision iterative algorithms decoding finite length low density parity check (LDPC) codes. After piecewise analysis for extrinsic transfer information (EXIT) chart family and exploration of fixed points, one general switch scheme named normalized switch scheme, for which majority based (MB) algorithms and Gallager's decoding algorithm B (GB) are extreme cases, is proposed. Several specific normalized switch scheme, named expanded optimal switch scheme and tail-cut (TC) optimal switch scheme, are given and their convergence properties are analyzed. Simulation shows that compared with GB that holds optimality for infinity length codes, proposed switch schemes show performance improvements while enjoying less sensitivity to channel parameter underestimations for finite length codes.
Key Words: low density parity check (LDPC) codes, extrinsic transfer information (EXIT) charts, optimal switch, Gallager's algorithm B (GB), finite length codes
I. INTRODUCTION
LDPC codes [1], since rediscovered [2] in 1990's, have become the subject of extensive research for both theorists and engineers. As linear block codes fully described by check matrices, LDPC codes enable random [3] and structured constructions [4]-[12]. Long structured codes, although with relatively poorer decoding performance than long random codes, enjoy linear time encoding complexity, while small length structured codes exhibit nearly the same decoding performance as and holding lower encoding complexity than small length random codes. Quasi-cyclic (QC) codes [7]-[12] are attractive for low complexity encoding using shift registers and XOR gates. Iterative decoding of LDPC codes is performed by exchanging messages, soft or hard, between variable nodes and check nodes. A series of message passing iterative decoding algorithms, belief propagation (BP) algorithm [2], uniformly most powerful belief propagation (UMP-BP) algorithm [13], weighted bit flipping (WBF) algorithm [5] and bit flipping (BF) algorithm [17]-[27], constitutes a good tradeoff between performance and complexity. BF decoding algorithms, with simple operations of XOR and count at both variable and check nodes, serve as good candidates in future very-high-speed communication systems. Firstly introduced by Gallager in his original thesis [1], BF algorithms, with binary message traversing on edges

in Tanner graphs, are explored, generalized, and improved in literature [17]-[27]. Extrinsic information transfer (EXIT) chart, originally invented by S. ten Brink to describe the transfer of mutual information in iterative decoding [14]-[16], is generalized to describe the transfer of average error probability of binary traversing message [18]-[20] [22]-[24] [28]. For the latter, an exact analysis of EXIT charts to determine threshold and describe convergence behavior for BF algorithms [24] becomes possible. Design of irregular codes under BF decoding algorithm based on EXIT charts for average error probability of traversing message is addressed in literatures [18]-[24]. Gallager's decoding algorithm B (GB) [1], with optimal switches among constituent majority based (MB) algorithms, features larger threshold and fast convergence [17] [18] for infinite length codes, and is generalized as one instance of gear shift decoding algorithm [20][28]. [25][26] present time-invariant hybrid hard decision algorithms which not only exhibits nearly the same threshold as GB for infinite length codes and comparable performance improvements over GB for finite length codes, but also holds less sensitivity to channel parameter underestimations. [34] proposes a probabilistic schedule for the flipping of binary traversing message. This paper addresses switch schemes which exhibit performance improvements over GB for finite length codes. After piecewise analysis of EXIT chart families including switching points and fixed points for MB algorithms, this paper proposes one general switch scheme called normalized switch scheme and explores its convergence behavior. Then two specific normalized switch schemes, named expanded optimal switch schemes and tail-cut (TC) optimal switch schemes, are proposed and their convergence behaviors are analyzed. Simulations for finite length codes show that compared with GB the optimal switch scheme for infinite length codes, proposed schemes exhibit performance improvements and enjoy less sensitivity to channel underestimations. Compared with probabilistic flipping schedules that exhibit significant performance improvements for regular codes with variable nodes degree 3 yet rapidly decreasing improvements for larger degrees [27], proposed schemes by this paper characteristic meaningful performance improvements for regular codes with variable nodes degree larger than 4. V. CONCLUDING REMARKS
Gallager's decoding algorithm B, which operates on optimal switches according to EXIT charts, may not stay optimal for codes with finite length. One general switch scheme named normalized switch scheme is proposed in this paper as one framework for the design and analysis for switch type hybrid hard decision decoding algorithms. Expanded optimal switch scheme, TC optimal switch scheme and its expanded form are proposed as instances of normalized switch schemes. Convergence properties for proposed schemes are analyzed using EXIT function
as a tool. Examples of normalized switches scheme show
meaningful perfornmance improvements over optimal
