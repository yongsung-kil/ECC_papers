High Performance LDPC Decoder Using a Simplified Robust 
Algorithm in the Categorized Tanner Graph 
Tsuguo Maru 
System Platform Research Laboratories, NEC Corporation 
1753 Shimonumabe, Nakahara-Ku, Kawasaki, Kanagawa 211-8666, Japan

Abstract 
This paper presents an LDPC decoder design method for avoiding a high error floor caused by the approxiยญ
mation in the Max-Log domain known as the min-sum algorithm in the categorized tanner graph. This decoder, 
which doesn't need any knowledge of multi-path channels, has the benefit of robustness over level mismatch 
between demodulator and decoder. To overcome error floor, we apply some individual coefficients to LLR valยญ
ues in every category. Simulation results in AWGN and multi-path fading channels show that even though cateยญ
gorized low profile and approximations are used, good performance is achieved, which cannot be achieved by 
ordinary single coefficient methods (e.g., normalized BP algorithm). Unlike the recently developed simplified 
algorithms which use the noise variance of the channel estimate, simulation results show the proposed multiยญ
coefficient method which doesn't use the noise variance is robust over the varying fading conditions.

Introduction 
LDPC codes, which have the best known theoretiยญ
cal performance (0.0045dB to Shannon limit) [1], are 
great candidates for mobile communication systems. 
Though the performance of irregular type LDPC is 
closest to the Shannon limit, this performance is obยญ
tained at the expense of very large frame lengths (106-
10 7) and high degree orders (102), which means that 
the complexity of the LDPC decoder is very high and 
infeasible for mobile communication equipment. 
Some categorized methods which specifY the 
numbers of ones in disjointly divided square subยญ
matrices of the parity check matrix have been proยญ
posed as in Fig.l [2], and the recent advent of a new 
framework brings the possibility to achieve good perยญ
formance while featuring a low complexity with the 
help of a socket model using categorized methods as 
in Fig.l [4] and Fig. 2 [3]. 
From the tuning process of this socket model usยญ
ing the sum-product algorithm, the simulation result 
shows that the performance of these categorized 
methods outperforms the well tuned 3GPP turbo deยญ
coder with the several thousand frame length and a 
few degree orders. 
Instead of the sum-product algorithm, the greatly 
approximate algorithm in the Max-Log domain 
known as the min-sum algorithm is needed to achieve 
a significant reduction of complexity for mobile 
communication system; however error floor appears. 
To combat this error floor and to achieve very 
good performance, we propose a new method using 
the categorized multi-coefficients. 
These multiยญ
coefficients are obtained not only by simulation, but 
also roughly by GA based method.

Conclusion 
Multi-coefficient methods provide large coding 
gain and a low degree profile LDPC decoder which is 
suitable for mobile communication systems to inยญ
crease user capacity and peak data rate through low 
complexity. Since this method does not require any 
knowledge of the channel noise, it brings good perยญ
formance in the fading conditions. This method can 
not be used by the standard irregular LDPC because 
of its randomly distributed degree profile. Hence, this 
method is suitable for the categorized tanner graph.
