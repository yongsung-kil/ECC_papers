Low-complexity LDPCC decoder based on layered decoding and 
MIN-SUM approximation 
Stefano Valle, STMicroelectronics srI, Italy 
Abstract 
LDPCC are becoming a valid alternative to Turbo Codes. They have been incorporated into the specifications of 
several real systems, but often the resulting decoders compose a significant fraction of the digital transceivers. As 
a consequence, a lot of research is currently focused on their simplification. The bulk of an LOPCC decoder is 
comprised of memories and check-node processing unit(s). The present work introduces a memory efficient deยญ
coding algorithm that jointly exploits the simplification of check-node processing by means of the MIN-SUM 
approximation, and a recently introduced scheduling of the Sum-Product Algorithm (SPA), namely layered deยญ
coding, that shows a twice faster convergence of the iterative process. The performances of the proposed algoยญ
rithm are comparable with the SPA, but 2 70% less memory is required. Further potential advantages in real imยญ
plementations are briefly discussed. 
1. Introduction 
Low-Density Parity-Check Codes (LOPCC), invented 
by Gallager in 1963 [1], are now recognized a valid 
alternative to turbo-codes (TC). Nevertheless, iterative 
decoders still represent a challenging design issue, as 
they often occupy a significant part of a digital transยญ
ceiver. For this reason, a lot of research effort is diยญ
rected to reduce their implementation complexity. The 
complexity issue can be faced on different, and often 
complementary, fronts. The check-node processing 
represents the more computationally intensive part of 
the decoder. The simplification approach is conceptuยญ
ally very similar to that adopted for approximating the 
Log-MAP operator in MAP decoders of Convoluยญ
tional and Turbo Codes [2]. Generally, these elegant 
approximations of the algorithm, originally proposed 
by Gallager, don't lead to performance degradation in 
the context of a fixed-point implementation. Alternaยญ
tively, when the design trade-off tends to prefer even 
more simple implementations at the cost of some perยญ
formance degradation, the so-called MIN-SUM (MS) 
approximation is adopted. The reader can find an inยญ
teresting review of the most effective MS implementaยญ
tions in [3]. 
The decoder complexity also originates from the large 
memory requirements. Memory represents the bulk of 
serial decoders that instantiate a single check-node 
processor. In high-speed parallel implementations, 
memory still represents a significant fraction of the 
decoder. Moreover, memory accesses are generally 
complicated by clashes, so that sophisticated memory 
paging strategies are necessary. It was indicated in [4], 
and now unanimously recognized, that the LDPCC 
design cannot omit consideration of these memory 
conflicts without incurring intractable problems durยญ
ing the decoder design (see for example [5], [6] and 
references therein). In [5] the reader can find an interยญ
esting presentation of the most practical approaches to 
reduce the memory requirements and to structure the 
code in order to simplifY conflicts in memory address-
ing. In [7] it is demonstrated that the MS operator can 
be conveniently exploited to drastically reduce the 
memory requirements of a serial decoder. 
It is worth noting that the encoding complexity issue, 
not considered in this work, represents the other drivยญ
ing factor that determines the code structure choice 
[9]. 
The convergence speed of the decoding algorithm is 
another degree of freedom to explore, when lowยญ
complexity decoders are searched. It has been obยญ
served that significant improvements of convergence 
speed can be obtained with some scheduling variaยญ
tions. The reader is referred to the papers [5] and [8] 
and references therein for a complete presentation of 
these concepts. The scheduling algorithm proposed in 
[8], namely layered decoding, will be presented in deยญ
tails in the following section. 
The present work is a contribution in the direction of 
low-complexity LDPCC decoders. More specifically, 
it focuses on the joint exploitation of the MS apยญ
proximation and the concept of layered decoding that 
is conveniently applicable to certain classes of 
LDPCC. It will be demonstrated that the resulting deยญ
coding algorithm requires a smaller amount of memยญ
ory when compared to the commonly used approach 
(ยฅ75% less is achievable) with comparable performยญ
ance. Moreover, it provides a potential simplification 
of some memory-related design issues that one incurs 
during the design of high speed LDPCC decoders. 
The second section reviews the SPA algorithm and inยญ
troduces the merged schedule and layered schedule 
algorithms. The third section introduces the proposed 
algorithm, and its performance is demonstrated in the 
fourth section. The last section is dedicated to analyze 
some implementation aspects of the proposed soluยญ
tion.

Intuitively, the reliability of the messages coming out 
of a check-node update is dominated by the less reliยญ
able incoming message. It has been observed that the 
MS outputs are, in modulus, slightly larger than those 
output by a non-approximated check-node processor. 
This fact results in a significant error rate degradation. 
For this reason, a Normalized-MS (N-MS) has been 
proposed to partially compensate for these losses [3]. 
N-MS consists of a simple multiplication of the output 
messages by a scaling factor. The factor can be optiยญ
mized through simulations or, more elegantly, with 
density evolution [3]. This very simple approach reยญ
covers most of the performance gap caused by MS 
and makes MS a valid alternative to a full processing 
approach. An almost equivalent alternative to the Nยญ
MS is the Offset-MIN-SUM (O-MS) that performs 
slightly worse than N-MS [3]. 
It can be demonstrated that the adoption of the MS 
decoder does not require knowledge of the noise variยญ
ance, which is of great interest when the noise variยญ
ance in unknown or hard to be determined. 
More sophisticated approximations are able to perยญ
form nearly the same as a full precision approach, but 
generally require a data dependent correction term that 
makes the check-node processor more complex and 
thus misses the target of the present work (see for exยญ
ample [14]). In fact, parallel or partially parallel archiยญ
tectures require the instantiation of a multiplicity of 
check-node processors. For this reason any simplificaยญ
tion of this computation kernel is of particular interest. 
When MS is adopted the modulus of messages outgoยญ
ing from a check-node update processor is the smaller 
one among the incoming, except for the bit correยญ
sponding to the smaller incoming modulus, for which 
the second smaller is selected. Hence, the minimum 
check-to-bit information to be stored results to be 
much less than in the approaches described so far. In 
fact, it is sufficient to store: (i) two moduli; (ii) the 
signs of all the outgoing messages; (iii) the position of 
the less reliable message. A more detailed analysis of 
the required storage capability will be given in the last 
section with a detailed comparison with the other 
cases.

6. Conclusions 
In this work MIN-SUM approximation and layered 
decoding are put together to provide a memory effiยญ
cient decoding algorithm whose performance is, in 
some cases, comparable or better that the conventional 
SPA. The algorithm becomes more appealing when 
high rates or small codeword sizes are to be supยญ
ported. Memory requirements are drastically reduced 
down to ยฅ30% of conventional SPA implementations. 
Degradation in fixed-point implementations is visible, 
but strongly depends on the code choice. The degradaยญ
tion ranges between 0.05 and 004 dB in the presented 
examples when 8-4 quantization strategy is adopted. 
Further potential simplifications in high-speed decodยญ
