Construction of Good Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes*

Abstract: The BER performance of presented quasi-cyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LOPC) codes is not good as that of randomly constructed LDPC codes, while the lack of structure of randomly constructed LOPC codes implies serious disadvantages in terms of storing and accessing a large parity-check matrix . To solve the problems, this paper proposes a design of good quasicyclic (QC) low-density parity-check (LOPC) codes, the obtained algebraically structured codes have large minimum distances and good BER performance. The proposed design is based on index matrices, which determines the shifts of the circulant matrices of the sparse parity-check matrices . Compared with the presented design of QC LOPC codes, the designed QC LOPC codes are free from girth 4, sometimes free from girth 6, and have much larger minimum distances. Based on the algebraic code structure, the conditions of the girth and minimum distance of the codes are found. The BER performance of the designed QC LOPC block codes compares with that of randomly constructed LOPC codes for any block lengths. Better BER performance is obtained by increasing the circulant size of the base QC code. Simulations verify the construction of the QC LOPC codes to be valid. Key words: Channel coding, circulant matrices, low-density parity-check codes, quasi-cyclic (QC) codes.
1. Introduction
Low-density parity-check (LOPC) codes in [I] have attracted considerable attention [2-11], because they can achieve nearcapacity performance with iterative message-passing decoding and sufficiently long block sizes. The parity check matrix of quasicyclic (QC) LOPC codes the code is composed of blocks of circulant matrices, giving the code a QC property, which can potentially facilitate efficient encoder implementation. Further, the algebraic structure of the code allows for an efficient high-speed very large scale integration (VLSI) implementation [5-11].
Most methods for designing LOPC codes are based on random construction techniques; the lack of structure implies serious disadvantages in terms of storing and accessing a large paritycheck matrix, encoding data, and analyzing code performance (e.g., determining a code's distance properties) . If the codes are designed with algebraic structure, then some of these problems can be overcome. However , the girth, distance and BER performances presented QC codes are not good as random codes. The main contribution of this paper is to provide a construction of QC LDPC codes and obtain a large class of QC regular LOPC codes with large distances, which ensure that the QC codes can be of good BER performance similar to random codes. We show that the QC LOPC codes can not perform well compared to random regular LOPC codes if the parameters of index matrices to be selected correctly.

distance. At high SNRs, the maximum-likelihood decoding perfor-

5. Conclusions
An algebraic construction based on circulant matrices for designing LDPC codes has been proposed. The girth, distance and BER properties of the LDPC codes resulting from this constr uction have been examined. Simulations show that given the different parameters of a, band M, the nature of the codes can have differe nt girth, distance and BER properties. The proposed codes perform com parably to random LDPC codes at short to long block lengths . The algebra ic struct ure of the codes makes them particularly suitab le for high-speed VLS I implementation. The good girth, distance and BER performances of these proposed codes have been obtai ned and illustrated .
