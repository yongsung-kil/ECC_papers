Improvement of Burst Error Correction Using a Look-Up Table with Low Density Parity Check Coding for Compressed Ultraspectral Sounder Data
Bormin Huang1, Y. Sriraja1, Hung-Lung Huang1, and Mitchell D. Goldberg2
'Space Science and Engineering Center, University of Wisconsin-Madison, Madison, WI 53711, USA
2NOAA NESDIS, Room 810, 5200 Auth Road, Camp Springs, MD 20746, USA E-mail: bormingssec.wisc.edu

Abstract - Low density parity check (LDPC) codes have the ability to transmit data at rates approaching channel capacity with very low error probability. Our study of LDPC codes on
JPEG2000-compressed ultraspectral sounder data shows that for an LDPC encoder designed with certain input parameters, the
LDPC decoder is able to correct all single errors and two-bit burst errors. Furthermore, our analysis on a massive variety of 3- and 4-bit burst error scenarios indicates that the locations of LDPC-uncorrected errors for 3- and 4-bit burst errors appear to be fixed, regardless of the content of the input data. We propose a
look-up table based error correction method to correct errors surviving from the LDPC decoder for 3- and 4-bit burst errors.
Keywords - Low density parity check (LDPC), burst error, look-up table, JPEG2000, ultraspectral sounder data, AIRS.
1. Introduction
Contemporary and future ultraspectral sounders (e.g. AIRS
CrTS, JASI, GIFTS, and HES [l]) are expected to improve
weather and climate prediction. Given the huge volume of three-dimensional data generated by an ultraspectral sounder each day, the use of robust data compression techniques will be beneficial for data transfer and archival. The physical
retrieval of these geophysical parameters, involving the
inverse solution of the radiative transfer equation, is a mathematically ill-posed problem [2], i.e. the solution is sensitive to the error or noise in the data. Therefore, there is a need for lossless or near lossless compression of ultraspectral sounder data to avoid potential retrieval degradation of geophysical parameters due to lossy compression. Robust error-correcting codes are also desired for satellite data transmission.
In this paper we study the performance of an LDPC code on ultraspectral sounder data compressed with JPEG2000. LPDC codes have been shown to perform close to the Shannon limit by Mackay and Neal [3,4]. LDPC codes also feature low error floor and linear decoding time. They are suitable for satellite communication applications because of their performance at high code rates in the presence ofburst errors [5].
Satellite channels have errors due to the noise from ground receiving terminal equipment and the attenuation of the atmosphere (water vapor, fog, clouds, rain) [6]. Errors in satellite channels have a burst characteristic. In addition burst

errors are also caused by extraterrestrial effects such as solar

flares that drown out the transmission from a satellite for a

2. Ultraspectral Sounder Data

The test granules for ultraspectral sounder data compression study are obtained from the NASA AIRS digital counts collected on March 2, 2004. The data is publicly available via anonymous ftp [8]. It consists of 10 digital count granules, five daytime and five nighttime, selected from representative geographical regions of the Earth [1]. The AIRS digital count data ranges from 12-14 bits for different channels. More information regarding the AIRS instrument may be acquired from the NASA AIRS website [9]. To make the selected data more generic to other ultraspectral sounders, 271 bad channels identified in the supplied AIRS infrared channel properties file are excluded, assuming that they occur only in the AIRS sounder. Each resulting 3D granule consists of 2107 channels, 135 scan lines, and 90 cross-track footprints per scan line.

3. Low Density Parity Check Codes

Low density parity check (LDPC) codes are linear block
codes defined by a parity check matrix H that is sparse, i.e. the matrix has a low density of l's. They were first introduced in 1960 by Gallagher but were ignored for a long time until being reintroduced by Mackay and Neal [3,4]. In this paper we adopt their LDPC encoding and decoding codes. performed as explained below following the terminology given in [4]. With a source input of K bits and channel coded

5.2 Burst Error Analysis
The interleaving and deinterleaving process is a common
mitigation technique to spread out long burst noises into random errors or shorter manageable burst errors [7]. The
channel decoder errors might still come in bursts even at a low BER of 10-1 [10]. For simulation purposes we consider a binary symmetric channel and introduce burst errors of up to length 4 in each block to observe and analyze the error performance of both channel and source decoders.
The JPEG2000 compressed stream is divided into fixed-length blocks (K=3906 for R=0.954) and the burst errors are introduced at all possible locations within a block. For the LDPC implementation with fixed input parameters (M= 190, N = 4906, t = 4, seed = 1) we observe that the LDPC is able to correct all 1-bit random errors and 2-bit burst errors. For 3and 4-bit burst errors, the locations of uncorrectable errors after the LDPC decoding appear to be fixed for all the tested blocks (-30000) from 4 different granules. Only two locations

LDPC decoder fails.

6. Conclusions
We performadetaileduanalysisofthe effects ofburst errors
on compressed ultraspectral sounder data using an LDPC code and show the error patterns surviving from the LDPC decoder for 3- and 4-bit burst errors appear to occur in a deterministic manner. We propose a look-up table method to correct such errors thus avoiding significant error propagation due to JPEG2000 source decoding.
