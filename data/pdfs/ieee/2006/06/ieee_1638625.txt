Lifting Methods for Quasi-Cyclic LDPC Codes
Seho Myung, Kyeongcheol Yang, Member, IEEE, and Youngkyun Kim, Senior Member, IEEE

Abstract— In this paper we present two simple methods to construct a quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) code of larger length by lifting a given QC-LDPC code. We show that its girth is always larger than or equal to that of the given QC-LDPC code. Applying the methods recursively, it is possible to construct a sequence of QC-LDPC codes generated from a single exponent matrix by proper floor or modulo operations. Simulation results show that they have no serious performance degradation, as compared with randomly constructed LDPC codes.
Index Terms— Block-cycle, circulant permutation matrix, lifting, low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, quasi-cyclic.
I. INTRODUCTION
L OW-DENSITY parity-check (LDPC) codes, first discovered by Gallager [5], were rediscovered to be Shannonlimit-approaching codes over additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channels [1], [8], [9], [13], [14]. Recently, some coding theorists have made efforts to find efficient methods for constructing algebraically structured LDPC codes since they are more attractive for their commercial use and implementation purpose [2], [3], [4], [7], [8], [15]. Quasi-cyclic LDPC (QC-LDPC) codes may be a good candidate to meet these requirements due to their algebraic structures.
The main contributions of the paper are as follows. We present two simple methods to construct a QC-LDPC code of larger length by lifting a given QC-LDPC code, which are different from the method studied by Miladinovic and Fossorier [10]. We show that its girth is always larger than or equal to that of the given QC-LDPC code. Applying the methods recursively, it is possible to construct a sequence of QC-LDPC codes generated from a single exponent matrix by proper floor or modulo operations.
The outline of the paper is as follows. In Section II, we review QC-LDPC codes and introduce some definitions for our presentation. We propose two simple lifting methods for QCLDPC codes in Section III. The performance of several QCLDPC codes is verified by simulations in Section IV. Finally, we give some concluding remarks in Section V.

V. CONCLUDING REMARKS
We discussed two simple lifting methods by which QCLDPC codes of large length can be efficiently constructed. Applying the methods recursively, it is possible to construct a sequence of QC-LDPC codes with larger lengths from a given QC-LDPC code without sacrificing their cycle properties. Furthermore, they can be generated from the exponent matrix of the QC-LDPC code having the largest length in the lifting procedure. Numerical results show that there are no serious performance degradation as compared with random LDPC codes.
