The parity matrix H was defined in a nonsystematic form; each column of H had a small weight (e.g., 3) and the weight per row was also uniform; the matrix H was constructed at random subject to these constraints. In spite of their promising properties, it appears that GL codes have been generally forgotten, the assumption perhaps being that concatenated codes were superior for practical purposes (R.G. Gallager, personal communication).
