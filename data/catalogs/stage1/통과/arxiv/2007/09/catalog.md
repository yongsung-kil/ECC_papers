## Serially Concatenated IRA Codes

- **Status**: ✅
- **Reason**: 직렬연접 IRA로 error floor 저감, stopping-set 전파 방지 인터리버 설계 — 바이너리 LDPC error floor 기법 이식 가능(E)
- **알고리즘 기여**: ✅ 알고리즘/코드 기여
- **ID**: arxiv:0709.4466v1
- **Type**: preprint
- **Published**: 2007-09-27
- **Authors**: Taikun Cheng, Krishnamoorthy Sivakumar, Benjamin J. Belzer
- **PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/0709.4466v1
- **Abstract**: We address the error floor problem of low-density parity check (LDPC) codes on the binary-input additive white Gaussian noise (AWGN) channel, by constructing a serially concatenated code consisting of two systematic irregular repeat accumulate (IRA) component codes connected by an interleaver. The interleaver is designed to prevent stopping-set error events in one of the IRA codes from propagating into stopping set events of the other code. Simulations with two 128-bit rate 0.707 IRA component codes show that the proposed architecture achieves a much lower error floor at higher SNRs, compared to a 16384-bit rate 1/2 IRA code, but incurs an SNR penalty of about 2 dB at low to medium SNRs. Experiments indicate that the SNR penalty can be reduced at larger blocklengths.
