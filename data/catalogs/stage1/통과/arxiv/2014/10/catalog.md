## Design of LDPC Code Ensembles with Fast Convergence Properties

- **Status**: ✅
- **Reason**: 유한 반복수 최적화 LDPC/GLDPC 앙상블 설계(E: 코드 설계, degree distribution 최적화) — 바이너리, 디코더 반복수 제약 하 구성 기법 이식 가능
- **알고리즘 기여**: ✅ 알고리즘/코드 기여
- **ID**: arxiv:1410.4086v2
- **Type**: preprint
- **Published**: 2014-10-15
- **Authors**: Ian P. Mulholland, Enrico Paolini, Mark F. Flanagan
- **PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1410.4086v2
- **Abstract**: The design of low-density parity-check (LDPC) code ensembles optimized for a finite number of decoder iterations is investigated. Our approach employs EXIT chart analysis and differential evolution to design such ensembles for the binary erasure channel and additive white Gaussian noise channel. The error rates of codes optimized for various numbers of decoder iterations are compared and it is seen that in the cases considered, the best performance for a given number of decoder iterations is achieved by codes which are optimized for this particular number. The design of generalized LDPC (GLDPC) codes is also considered, showing that these structures can offer better performance than LDPC codes for low-iteration-number designs. Finally, it is illustrated that LDPC codes which are optimized for a small number of iterations exhibit significant deviations in terms of degree distribution and weight enumerators with respect to LDPC codes returned by more conventional design tools.

## New Results on the Pseudoredundancy

- **Status**: ✅
- **Reason**: 바이너리 LDPC 유한길이 분석의 pseudocodeword/pseudoweight·pseudoredundancy(E: error floor 관련 유한길이 구성 분석) — 애매하나 살림
- **알고리즘 기여**: ✅ 알고리즘/코드 기여
- **ID**: arxiv:1410.1627v1
- **Type**: preprint
- **Published**: 2014-10-07
- **Authors**: Zihui Liu, Jens Zumbrägel, Marcus Greferath +1
- **PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/1410.1627v1
- **Abstract**: The concepts of pseudocodeword and pseudoweight play a fundamental role in the finite-length analysis of LDPC codes. The pseudoredundancy of a binary linear code is defined as the minimum number of rows in a parity-check matrix such that the corresponding minimum pseudoweight equals its minimum Hamming distance. By using the value assignment of Chen and Kløve we present new results on the pseudocodeword redundancy of binary linear codes. In particular, we give several upper bounds on the pseudoredundancies of certain codes with repeated and added coordinates and of certain shortened subcodes. We also investigate several kinds of k-dimensional binary codes and compute their exact pseudocodeword redundancy.
