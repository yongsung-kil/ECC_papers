## Optimized Rate-Adaptive Protograph-Based LDPC Codes for Source Coding With Side Information

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: Slepian-Wolf 소스코딩(side info)용 LDPC rate-adaptive — 채널 ECC 아닌 소스코딩, 원칙 제외
- **ID**: ieee:8662618
- **Type**: journal
- **Published**: June 2019
- **Authors**: Fangping Ye, Elsa Dupraz, Zeina Mheich +1
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8662618
- **Abstract**: This paper considers the problem of source coding with side information at the decoder, also called Slepian-Wolf source coding scheme. In practical applications of this coding scheme, the statistical relation between the source and the side information can vary from one data transmission to another, and there is a need to adapt the coding rate depending on the current statistical relation. In this paper, we propose a novel rate-adaptive code construction based on LDPC codes for the Slepian-Wolf source coding scheme. The proposed code design method allows to optimize the code degree distributions at all the considered rates, while minimizing the amount of short cycles in the parity check matrices at all rates. Simulation results show that the proposed method greatly reduces the source coding rate compared to the standard low density parity check accumulated (LDPCA) solution.

## Secret Key Generation With Precoding and Role Reversal in MIMO Wireless Systems

- **Status**: ❌
- **Reason**: MIMO 보안키 생성(precoding/role reversal) — 보안·무선, LDPC 무관
- **ID**: ieee:8692757
- **Type**: journal
- **Published**: June 2019
- **Authors**: Saygın Bakşi, Dimitrie C. Popescu
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8692757
- **Abstract**: Secret key generation based on channel measurements has emerged as a meaningful approach to enhance the security of wireless links. The key generation mechanism takes advantage of wireless channel reciprocity and uses the mutual information between legitimate users' channel measurements. However, the rate at which key bits are generated depends on the channel coherence time. Furthermore, eavesdroppers may obtain information to generate the secret key bits if their channels become correlated to the legitimate channels. In this paper, we propose a new approach for key generation that uses MIMO precoding and role switching by legitimate users during the key generation process in order to prevent an eavesdropper whose channel is correlated with the legitimate channel to acquire information that can be used to generate the encryption key, while also ensuring that the rate at which uncorrelated key bits are generated does not depend on the channel coherence time. The proposed approach is supported by an information theoretic analysis and is illustrated with numerical results obtained from simulations showing that, even when the legitimate and eavesdropper channels are correlated, the bit mismatch rate at the eavesdropper does not improve with increasing SNR, while that at the legitimate users improves.

- **Type**: journal
- **Published**: June 2019
- **Authors**: Yassine Mouchtak, Faissal El Bouanani, Daniel Benevides da Costa
- **PDF**: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8651330
- **Abstract**: In this paper, the upper bound and asymptotical approximation for the bit error rate (BER) as well as for the frame error rate of linear block codes under maximum-likelihood decoding operating over exponentially correlated (EC) generalized-fading channels are proposed. - **Abstract**: Adaptive transmission schemes are a crucial aspect of radio design for future wireless networks. This paper studies the performance of two classes of adaptive transmission schemes in cellular downlink.
